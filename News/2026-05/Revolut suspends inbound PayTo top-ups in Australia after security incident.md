# Revolut suspends inbound PayTo top-ups in Australia after security incident

> [!info] 2026-05-13 · 1 упоминаний · 1 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: Connecting the Dots in Fintech

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[Connecting the Dots in Fintech] 🇦🇺 Revolut has suspended inbound PayTo top-ups in Australiafollowing a reported security incident involving suspected mule accounts and potentially millions in misdirected customer funds. The issue has raised concerns across the PayTo ecosystem, including among Banking Circle and major Australian banks.

Revolut has moved from enthusiastically launching PayTo in Australia as recently as mid-March, to suspending it for inbound top-ups in early May, following an undisclosed “security incident.”

That security incident is believed to involve some 250 sleeper mule accounts and the misdirection of up to $3 million of inbound customer funds.

Revolut’s BSB – facilitated since late 2024 by Banking Circle (the trading name for Australian Settlements Limited) – is being blocked on risk grounds by major banks such as ANZ, unless customers specifically take action to whitelist it.

Revolut launched PayTo in March as a "smarter, safer way to move money”.

Just a few short weeks ago, Matt Baxby, CEO of Revolut Australia, stated the launch of PayTo was a "significant milestone" in their mission to become the "ultimate financial super-app."

The fintech highlighted PayTo as a digital, real-time alternative to traditional, slower direct debits, allowing instant authorisation of payment mandates, and a "smarter, safer way to move money."

It promised customers the ability to see, manage, and cancel recurring payment agreements or account top-ups in real-time within the Revolut app.

However, an important nuance was unclear, given Revolut’s intended use of PayTo was two-pronged.

Firstly, to enable customers to pay in funds, for example, from a big four bank, and secondly, for customers to pay out funds, for example, for a software subscription.

Barely two months live, something has gone very wrong for the inbound use case.

Scammers have infiltrated the PayTo chain of command

The inbound use case has been attacked by bad actors.

The most likely explanation is that Revolut customers have been scammed, into mistakenly authorising a PayTo mandate at their bank, to pay their funds into the wrong Revolut account.

Industry insiders are speculating there has been a data breach somewhere along the line of Revolut, Banking Circle, the NPP, or elsewhere.

While Revolut cites “proactive security measures” during the time of its investigation, industry chatter points to issues involving third-party institutions connected via Banking Circle in its capacity as Revolut’s NPP provider, though details remain scarce.

Customers left in the dark as Revolut investigates

Revolut customers received an email last week advising Revolut had disabled its freshly launched PayTo top-up functionality in Australia following an undisclosed “security incident.”

All existing PayTo agreements to add money had been cancelled, and new ones were blocked while Revolut investigates.

However, outgoing PayTo payments remain untouched.

Revolut insisted "no Revolut customer accounts have been compromised" which we understand to mean the stolen funds were not pulled directly from Revolut accounts.

While Revolut is actively working with its partners and the wider industry to review this disruption, payments industry professionals are scratching their heads, trying to reverse engineer what has happened.

Commentators take to social media to speculate

Melbourne-based entrepreneur Simon Lee, co-founder of Assembly Payments which rebranded to Zai in 2021, took to LinkedIn on Friday to share his recent experience as a Revolut business customer.

“When we tried to transfer our own money from ANZ to our own Revolut account, ANZ instantly locked ALL of my accounts. Personal. Business. Everything. It took 3 days to get them unlocked,”
