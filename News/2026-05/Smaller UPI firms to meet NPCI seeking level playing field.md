Smaller UPI Firms to Meet NPCI, Seek Level Playing Field with PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm

Smaller Unified Payments Interface (UPI) firms plan to meet National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI). They seek fair competition in the payments ecosystem. The move reflects concerns over dominance of leading apps.
 Author: V.Gayathri 
 Published: April 30, 2026
Smaller players in India’s Unified Payments Interface ( UPI ) ecosystem are preparing to meet the National Payments Corporation of India ( NPCI ) to raise concerns around competition. The development comes as these firms look for a more balanced environment in the rapidly growing digital payments space. Smaller UPI Firms Plan NPCI Meeting A group of smaller UPI application providers is expected to engage with NPCI to discuss issues related to market dynamics. These firms are seeking measures that can help them compete more effectively with larger players in the ecosystem. The discussions are expected to focus on ensuring fair access and opportunities for all participants within the UPI framework. Concerns Over Market Dominance The move comes amid concerns that a significant share of UPI transactions is concentrated among a few large platforms such as PhonePe, Google Pay, and  Paytm . Smaller firms believe that this concentration creates challenges in scaling their operations and acquiring users, impacting overall competition in the sector. Push for Level Playing Field The firms are expected to raise the need for a level playing field during their interaction with NPCI. This may include discussions around policies and frameworks that can support a more competitive environment. The objective is to ensure that smaller players are not disadvantaged in terms of growth opportunities and visibility within the ecosystem. Focus on Ecosystem Balance The upcoming meeting is likely to focus on maintaining a balanced UPI ecosystem that supports both established and emerging players. Ensuring fair competition is seen as important for the long-term sustainability and innovation within India’s digital payments landscape. The planned meeting between smaller UPI firms and NPCI highlights ongoing concerns about competition in the digital payments space. The outcome of these discussions could play a role in shaping a more inclusive and balanced UPI ecosystem. Source - The Economic Times
