## Ant Group digital bank adds stock trading to AlipayHK

Share

Add TIA as a preferred source

Ant Bank, Ant Group’s digital bank in Hong Kong, has added stock trading to its AlipayHK app.

The feature allows users to buy Hong Kong and US shares and ETFs as the company expands into the city’s retail brokerage market.

The service also covers warrants and callable contracts through the app’s wealth section, with zero commission for Hong Kong stocks and no custody fees.

The launch follows Ant’s March purchase of a 50.55% stake in Hong Kong-listed Bright Smart Securities for HK$2.81 billion (US$358 million).

The deal was completed through its wealth management affiliate, Wealthiness and Prosperity Holding.

_🔗 Source: South China Morning Post_

## 🧠 Food for thought

Implications, context, and why it matters.

### Ant’s zero-fee trading enters Hong Kong’s fee war

*   Ant’s zero-commission trading looks less like a shake-up and more like the cost of joining Hong Kong’s brokerage market 1.
*   Futu, a Hong Kong-based online brokerage platform, has waived commission on Hong Kong stocks for years. Some rivals started cutting fees in 2020 2, 3.
*   The fight has squeezed older brokerages. Nearly 200 stockbrokers have shut since 2020, while the top 14 firms took 70.9% of trading turnover 4.
*   The trading tool sits inside a broader super app plan. Ant wants it to work with existing services and keep AlipayHK’s more than 4.2 million users engaged 5, 6.

### Payment apps are turning into wealth-management channels

*   Ant’s move offers a clear example of how payment apps with large user bases can branch into wealth management through in-app distribution 1.
*   One route is buying control of a licensed local brokerage such as Bright Smart Securities. Ant’s wealth-management affiliate bought a 50.55% stake in the Hong Kong-listed broker for HK$2.81 billion (US$358 million) 1.
*   The shift can turn investing into a built-in app feature used alongside everyday payments. That puts pressure on standalone fintech firms and traditional banks that no longer own the main customer relationship.

Ant Group’s bank adds stock trading to Alipay app in Hong Kong 
 Move comes weeks after Ant Group strengthened foothold in city’s brokerage market through acquisition of stake in Bright Smart Securities 
The service allowed users to trade Hong Kong and US stocks and exchange-traded funds, as well as structured products including warrants and callable contracts, through the app’s wealth-management section, according to its promotional materials.
By integrating trading with existing functions such as payments, savings and fund investments, the app aimed to offer “one-stop” services for personal finance, the company said. The offering featured zero-commission trading for Hong Kong equities, no custody fees and free real-time market data access, the materials said.
The company also touted seamless fund allocation across consumption, savings and investment, as well as simplified account opening, leveraging AlipayHK’s existing user base.
In late March, Wealthiness and Prosperity Holding, Ant’s wealth-management affiliate, completed the acquisition of a 50.55 per cent stake in the Hong Kong-listed broker for HK$2.81 billion (US$369 million), highlighting its ambition to expand beyond payments and digital banking into full-fledged investment services.
