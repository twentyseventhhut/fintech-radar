Coinbase briefly went offline after an AWS data‑center outage.

JAKARTA – Cryptocurrency exchange platform Coinbase went down for several hours following a data centre service disruption at Amazon Web Services (AWS) in Northern Virginia.

The disruption came just days after Coinbase announced plans to lay off around 14% of its workforce, equivalent to approximately 700 employees.

The AWS outage was first reported on Thursday evening US time and affected a number of platforms, including Coinbase.

AWS said the disruption stemmed from a cooling issue in one of the main US-East-1 regional zones.

Coinbase stated that disruptions across several AWS zones caused transactions on its platform to become inaccessible for a considerable period.

Coinbase Chief Executive Officer (CEO) Brian Armstrong said on his personal X account that the disruption was “completely unacceptable”.

However, he maintained that the issue did not affect the company’s crypto exchange operations. “Our exchange has a unique architecture optimised for network latency and client colocation,” Armstrong explained on Saturday (9/5).

Earlier this week, Coinbase released its first-quarter results, reporting a loss of US$394 million.

In response to the losses, Coinbase plans to undertake major efficiency measures through Artificial Intelligence (AI) technology. As part of the effort, the company will also cut hundreds of jobs, despite having to bear restructuring costs worth IDR 60 million, according to a Bloomberg report.

“The market is currently weakening and we need to adjust our cost structure so the company can become leaner, move faster, and operate more efficiently as we prepare for the next phase of growth,” Armstrong said on Tuesday (5/5).

Despite the pressure, Coinbase shares traded on the Nasdaq rose 4.25% on Friday (8/5). However, since the start of the year, the share price has fallen by around 11%. (KR/LM)
