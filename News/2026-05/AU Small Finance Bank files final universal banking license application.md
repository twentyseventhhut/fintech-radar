# AU Small Finance Bank files final universal banking license application

Regulatory Changes Smooth Path
The bank's submission for a universal banking license simplifies the transition, following the Reserve Bank of India's (RBI) recent regulatory flexibility. Executive Director Vivek Tripathi confirmed the final application was submitted in March, shortly after the RBI's March decision to remove the non-operative holding company requirement. This regulatory change, applicable unless the bank opts for mutual fund or insurance subsidiaries, allows AU Small Finance Bank to focus on preparing its technology and products for universal banking without needing immediate corporate restructuring.
Leadership Team Strengthened
Concurrent with its licensing efforts, AU Small Finance Bank has reinforced its leadership pipeline. Vivek Tripathi was appointed Executive Director on April 24, after serving as chief credit officer. This move enhances operational oversight. Gaurav Jain joined as chief financial officer on April 27, strengthening financial governance. Managing Director Sanjay Agarwal was reappointed for three years from April 19, ensuring leadership continuity. Deputy Chief Executive Officer Uttam Tibrewal will continue to lead retail business growth and geographic expansion. This leadership lineup prepares the bank for universal banking operations.
Valuation and Market Performance
AU Small Finance Bank's P/E ratio is about 18 times trailing twelve-month earnings. This valuation is competitive within the small finance banking sector, where peers like Equitas Small Finance Bank and Ujjivan Small Finance Bank typically trade around 20 times earnings. The bank's market capitalization is around $5 billion. The stock currently trades around ₹700, reflecting market anticipation of its universal banking status and growth plans. The stock briefly dipped after the initial license announcement but has since trended upward, showing investor confidence.
Challenges Ahead
Despite these positive steps, AU Small Finance Bank faces significant challenges. Moving to universal banking means facing tougher competition from larger institutions with more resources, which could challenge its market capitalization. Unlike larger banks with diverse revenue, AU Small Finance Bank's balance sheet is closely watched, particularly its net interest margins and reliance on retail lending, which are sensitive to interest rate changes and economic downturns. Also, while the RBI has been flexible, future capital requirements for universal banks could lead to substantial financial obligations. Its retail-focused loan portfolio is vulnerable to credit cycles, requiring close monitoring of asset quality, especially considering the credit experience of its new Executive Director.
Future Outlook
Analysts generally view the move to universal banking favorably, expecting it to open new revenue streams and broaden product offerings. Brokerage consensus forecasts continued growth in net interest income and improvements in operational efficiency as the bank expands. Analysts watch for sustained asset quality, strong deposit growth, and smooth integration of new business lines. They believe these could help its P/E multiple move closer to higher industry averages.
