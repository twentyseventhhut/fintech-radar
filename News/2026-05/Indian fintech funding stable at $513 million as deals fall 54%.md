Highlights
India's fintech sector attracted $513 million in Q1 2026, showing a 2% year-on-year increase from Q1 2025, but deal count plummeted by 54%.
Capital is now concentrated in fewer companies, leading to a significant increase in average cheque sizes as investors become more selective.
Mumbai dominated funding, securing 61% of total capital, driven by strong activity in online lending and housing finance segments.
Late-stage investments surged by 126% from the previous quarter, accounting for $273 million, indicating a preference for mature businesses with proven models.

Implications for Founders and Investors
Founders seeking funding should demonstrate robust unit economics and a clear path to profitability to attract increasingly selective investors.
Investors should continue to monitor the performance of late-stage fintech companies and assess the long-term implications of capital concentration in specific sub-sectors and geographies.
