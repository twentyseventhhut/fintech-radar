Exclusive: Scripbox plans Rs 170 Cr debt and equity raise; gears up for IPO
 
The proceeds will be used to support Scripbox’s accelerated growth strategy, strengthen its balance sheet, and prepare for its targeted initial public offering (IPO). 
 
 Follow Us
 Bengaluru-based wealthtech startup Scripboxis planning to raise up to Rs 170 crore through a mix of equity and debt as it looks to acquire the mutual fund distribution business of a Delhi-based Independent Financial Advisor (IFA). 
 According to its regulatory filing reviewed by Entrackr, the company’s board passed a resolution to approve a proposal to raise up to Rs 60 crore from a select group of friends and family investors through equity shares, preference shares, convertible instruments, or other securities.  
 The filing noted that the proceeds will be used to support Scripbox’s accelerated growth strategy, strengthen its balance sheet, and prepare for its targeted initial public offering (IPO).  
 Separately, Scripbox also secured approval to avail debt facilities of up to Rs 110 crore from banks, financial institutions, NBFCs, or other lenders. The debt will primarily be used to finance the acquisition of an Independent Financial Advisor (IFA).  
 However, the filing did not disclose the name of the Delhi-based Independent Financial Advisor (IFA) whose mutual fund distribution business is being acquired. 
 As part of the company’s expansion strategy, the company approved a draft business transfer agreement to acquire the mutual fund distribution business of a Delhi-based IFA through the purchase and transfer of its AMFI Registration Number (ARN), along with associated client relationships and obligations.  
 Founded in 2012, Scripbox is a Bengaluru-based digital wealth management platform that helps retail investors invest across mutual funds, fixed deposits, US stocks, ETFs, and NPS, while offering personalised investment and financial planning solutions. 
 Scripbox has raised over $55 million to date and holds a valuation of around Rs 1,150 crore (approximately $137 million). Its investors include Accel, LetsVenture, DMI, and others. 
 While the company is yet to file its FY26 results, Scripbox turned profitable in FY25 with a profit of Rs 12.7 crore, while its operating revenue grew 27% year-on-year to Rs 107.2 crore. 
According to data compiled by Entrackr, Indian wealthtech startups raised over $634 million across 51 deals involving 39 startups during 2024 and 2025. In the ongoing calendar year, Centricity is in talks to raise $30 million round, AssetPlus secured $19.3 million, Wint Wealth raised $28 million, Sahi bagged $33 million, Bachatt closed a $12 million round, while Jiraaf is also in the process of raising additional capital through an extended Series B round.
