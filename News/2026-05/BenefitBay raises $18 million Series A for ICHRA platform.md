# benefitbay Raises $18M Series A Led by Ten Coves Capital as ICHRA Adoption Reshapes Employer Healthcare Benefits

May 22, 2026

Brandy Thompson, Founder & CEO - benefitbay

**benefitbay**has **raised an $18 million Series A round led by Ten Coves Capital** as the company positions itself at the center of one of the fastest-growing structural shifts in employer-sponsored healthcare: the movement from traditional group health plans toward defined-contribution benefits models built around Individual Coverage Health Reimbursement Arrangements, or ICHRAs.

The financing reflects growing investor conviction that rising healthcare costs, increasing employee demand for flexibility, and mounting pressure on employers to control benefits spending are accelerating adoption of consumer-directed healthcare infrastructure.

benefitbay said the new capital will fund expansion of its payments infrastructure, direct carrier and payroll integrations, and broker-focused enablement tools that support large-scale ICHRA deployment.

The company also announced the appointment of healthcare executive Kevin Mullins as president and chief financial officer alongside the financing.

The raise comes at a critical moment for the health benefits market. Traditional employer-sponsored group plans have faced sustained cost inflation for years, with many businesses increasingly struggling to absorb annual premium increases while employees demand more personalized coverage options.

That dynamic has pushed ICHRA — a regulatory structure introduced in 2020 that allows employers to provide tax-advantaged reimbursement funds employees can use to purchase individual health insurance — from a niche alternative into a rapidly emerging benefits strategy.

“Traditional group premiums have outpaced what employers can absorb, and employees are demanding greater flexibility in their coverage,” the company said, describing a broader “structural shift from defined benefit to defined contribution healthcare.”

The model mirrors broader trends already seen in retirement markets, where employers largely transitioned from defined-benefit pensions to defined-contribution plans over previous decades.

benefitbay has built its platform specifically around the broker ecosystem, a strategic choice that appears increasingly important as large brokerages establish dedicated ICHRA advisory practices and bring larger employer groups into the market. The company said it currently supports more than 40,000 covered lives through a national network of brokerage partners.

“We built benefitbay because we believe employees deserve more than a one-size-fits-no-one health plan,” said founder and CEO **Brandy Thompson**. “Brokers are how we make that vision real at scale.”

Ten Coves Capital framed the investment as part of a longer-term evolution toward consumer-directed healthcare infrastructure. The firm previously backed HealthEquity, one of the most prominent health savings account administrators in the market.

“ICHRA represents the next chapter of that story, and benefitbay has built the infrastructure to lead it,” said Ten Coves Managing Partner Dan Kittredge.

Importantly, investors highlighted benefitbay’s payments and reconciliation architecture as a core differentiator — a signal that operational infrastructure, not just insurance distribution, is becoming a major competitive battleground in next-generation healthcare benefits administration.

As ICHRA adoption scales, employers and brokers increasingly need enterprise-grade systems capable of handling compliance, carrier integration, payroll synchronization, enrollment workflows, reimbursement reconciliation, and employee education at scale.

That complexity has created opportunities for infrastructure-focused healthcare fintech companies positioned between insurers, employers, payroll systems, and broker networks.

The addition of Mullins further suggests benefitbay is preparing for a larger-scale operational growth phase. Prior to joining the company, he held senior leadership positions at LifeStance Health and Zarminali Pediatrics, bringing experience scaling healthcare organizations through rapid expansion and public-market growth environments.

The broader ICHRA market itself remains relatively early-stage but is drawing increasing attention from insurers, brokers, payroll providers, and healthcare technology firms seeking to capitalize on changing employer benefits economics.

As healthcare affordability pressures continue mounting and employers look for more predictable cost structures, defined-contribution healthcare models could become a far more significant component of the employer-sponsored insurance market over the next decade.
