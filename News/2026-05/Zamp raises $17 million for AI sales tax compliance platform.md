# city biz+ Zamp Raises $30M to Scale AI-Driven Sales Tax Compliance Platform

May 1, 2026

### New York-based Zamp has raised $30 million in funding led by Acrew Capital, with participation from Thomson Reuters Ventures and others. Founded in 2022, the company provides AI-powered sales tax compliance software that combines automation with human expertise. Zamp has remitted over $300 million in taxes, completed 100,000+ filings, and handled 75,000 notices with 99.97% accuracy. Its platform helps firms scale amid an impending accountant shortage and rising compliance demands.

Rohit Bhadange

New York-based **Zamp**, which makes software to handle clients’ sales tax compliance, has **raised $30 million in funding** led by Palo Alto, Calif.’s **Acrew Capital** with participation from New York’s **Thomson Reuters Ventures**.

The company said the round was also backed by Chicago, Ill.’s **Valor Equity Partners**, London’s **20Growth** and Menlo Park, Calif.’s **Friends & Family Capital**.

Since the company’s founding in 2022 by former financial analyst **Rohit Bhadange**, Zamp said it has remitted over $300 million in sales tax, more than 100,000 on-time filings, and handled over 75,000 notices with 99.97% filing accuracy by combining AI-powered automation with experienced tax professionals to handle the compliance lifecycle.

“We source our own tax data. We own every workflow. We deploy agents across every step with tax professionals there when it matters. That’s the part people get wrong about AI. It doesn’t replace the team. It gives them leverage,” said Bhadange, Zamp’s CEO.

Before Zamp, Bhadange worked in private equity and venture capital as an analyst at Insight Venture Partners and Trident Capital. The University of Pennsylvania alumnus also previously worked at Pareto Holdings, handling real estate investing to build early-stage companies.

Zamp’s agents replace the time spent on tasks such as data gathering by automatically ingesting historical filing data to clean and validate it, helping firms manage more clients with fewer personnel.

The company said Zamp OS is reshaping the country’s accounting industry with 75% of accountants expected to retire over the next 15 years while compliance demands are on the rise.

The vertically integrated sales platform enables accounting firms to offer fully managed compliance services without building dedicated practices. The platform is used by more than 120 firms and claimed a 97.8% client retention rate in 2025. Zamp has sales tax coverage in more than 100 countries.

Zamp: $30 Million Raised To Scale AI-Powered Sales Tax Operating System Globally
Zamp, a vertically integrated sales tax compliance platform, has raised $30 million in total funding and announced the global launch of Zamp OS, which it describes as the Operating System of Sales Tax. The latest round was led by Acrew Capital with participation from Thomson Reuters Ventures and several of Zamp’s accounting firm partners.
Zamp OS is designed to address a compounding crisis in the accounting profession: 75% of U.S. accountants are expected to retire in the next 15 years, while compliance workloads continue to grow. The platform enables accounting firms to offer fully managed sales tax compliance to their clients without building a dedicated sales tax practice. More than 120 accounting firms currently use Zamp, including Withum, Anchin, Frank Rimerman, and Larson Gross, with 97.8% client retention in 2025.
What distinguishes Zamp OS from other compliance platforms is its end-to-end ownership of the entire process. The company sources its own tax data across more than 12,000 jurisdictions, owns every workflow from registration to filing to notice management, and pairs AI agents with in-house tax and legal professionals at each step. Because Zamp controls data, agents, human judgment, and execution end to end, the company offers a penalty guarantee: if Zamp gets it wrong, Zamp covers the cost.
Since launch, the platform has remitted more than $300 million in sales tax, completed more than 100,000 on-time filings, handled more than 75,000 notices with 99.97% filing accuracy, and increased output per compliance resource by 12 times. Zamp now provides sales tax coverage in more than 100 countries.
The platform integrates into firm workflows through three access points: embedded directly into a firm’s own product under their brand, through Zamp’s partner dashboard with full client visibility, or via Zamp’s Model Context Protocol, which gives firms access to compliance status inside their existing tools and AI workflows.
