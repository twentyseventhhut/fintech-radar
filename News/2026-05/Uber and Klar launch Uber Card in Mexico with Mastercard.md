 Uber and Mexican financial technology firm  Klar , in partnership with Mastercard, announced the launch of the "Uber Card operated by Klar." This strategic alliance makes Mexico the first market globally to introduce a financial solution fully integrated into Uber’s mobility and delivery ecosystem. The co-branded credit card aims to convert Uber’s 25 million active annual users in Mexico into financial services customers by offering cashback rewards, interest-free installments, and credit lines of up to MX$250,000 (US$14,453). 
 The partnership represents a significant convergence between the gig economy and digital banking. According to Stefan Möller, CEO and co-founder, Klar, the initiative is designed to embed financial tools into daily consumer habits. 
 “The arrival of the Uber Card operated by Klar will demonstrate how financial innovation can be naturally integrated into people’s daily lives,” Möller said. He emphasized that the collaboration provides “tangible value and access both inside and outside the platform” while leveraging Klar’s infrastructure to improve payment experiences. 
 Product Tiers and Financial Specifications 
 The Uber Card will be issued in two distinct versions on the  Mastercard global network: Standard and Platinum. Both tiers are structured to incentivize spending within the Uber app while providing broader utility for external purchases. 
 The Standard version is positioned as an entry-level product with no annual fee. It allows users to accumulate cashback on Uber trips and Uber Eats orders, as well as rewards in categories such as telecommunications, subscriptions, and supermarkets.  
 The Platinum version targets a higher-spending demographic, offering increased cashback rates, access to airport VIP lounges through  LoungeKey , and a credit limit of uo to MX$200,000 (US$11,562). While the Platinum card carries an annual fee, that cost is waived if the user meets a minimum spending threshold within the first three months of the first year. 
 Beyond rewards, the card also functions as a broader financial tool. Users can access interest-free installment plans (MSI) at more than 140,000 affiliated merchants and utilize investment features offering returns of up to 15% annually. 
 Strategic Market Expansion 
 For Uber, the launch is a tactical move to deepen user loyalty and increase transaction frequency. Daniel Colunga, General Manager, Uber Eats Latin America, described the card as a “natural step in the evolution” of the company’s ecosystem. 
 “Today, we connect more than 25 million active users every year in Mexico, and we want every trip and every order to represent a real advantage for them, especially for Uber One members,” Colunga said. He added that the card optimizes spending by integrating “mobility, delivery, and rewards into a single experience.” 
 The partnership also addresses financial inclusion challenges in Mexico, where many consumers remain cautious about traditional banking institutions. During an interview with MBN, executives highlighted that the potential market extends far beyond their current combined user base. 
 While Klar currently serves 7 million users, Möller said the total addressable market includes approximately 90 million adults in Mexico. 
 “There are still many users, particularly in certain age groups, who may not yet have the confidence to experiment with a digital financial institution. Products with a value proposition like this help us build that confidence, allowing the market to grow from seven million to a potential 90 million adults,” Möller said. 
 Operational Integration and Regulatory Compliance 
 To maintain a seamless user experience, the application process is hosted directly within the Uber and Uber Eats apps. Beginning in late May and continuing through June 2026, users will see a dedicated section in their profiles where they can request the card. 
 However, the technical and regulatory backend remains under Klar’s jurisdiction. Colunga clarified that while the signup process begins within Uber’s apps, the regulated credit experience is managed through the Klar app to comply with Mexican financial regulations. 
 “The regulated experience, the credit experience, is still within the Klar app because it is imperative that authorities require it of us,” Colunga said. He described the transition between apps as “quite simple, where one does not necessarily realize they are moving between one or the other, but always respecting the regulatory framework.” 
 When asked about security protocols, Colunga said Uber conducted a rigorous evaluation of Klar’s technological capabilities before finalizing the partnership. 
 “Part of the evaluation we did was to identify the partner with the strongest technological capabilities. There are security considerations, user experience issues, and many other factors. We basically left what Klar knows how to do best to Klar rather than trying to build something that is not our core,” he said. 
 Risk Management and Inclusivity 
 A central theme of the partnership is the “democratization” of credit. Möller noted that between 30% and 35% of Klar’s users are first-time credit card holders, while 80% earn interest on their accounts for the first time through the platform. 
 By expanding into higher-tier products such as the Platinum card, Klar aims to strengthen and diversify its risk profile. 
 “Moving upward actually allows us to move further downward because it allows us to distribute the risk across our balance sheet,” Möller said. 
 He argued that maintaining a diverse product portfolio is a strategy traditionally used by banks to withstand macroeconomic volatility, a depth that digital financial institutions are increasingly being encouraged to replicate by regulators. 
 From a business perspective, the integration of cashback also serves as a secondary payment mechanism designed to drive additional consumption. Colunga noted that accumulated rewards can often cover the cost of entire orders. 
 “The cashback becomes that other form of payment. Suddenly you say, ‘Hey, the month is over and I have X amount here—my order is free,’” he said. 
 While the companies declined to provide specific signup targets for the first quarter, they characterized the current phase as only the “first or second minute of the match.” 
 Mariana Casar, vice president of fintech, digital partners, and enablers for Mexico, Mastercard, said the program combines “the simplicity and trust that people expect from Uber, Klar, and Mastercard,” creating an opportunity for Mexicans to extract more value from their daily digital interactions. 
 The rollout will continue through the end of June 2026, as both companies monitor conversion rates and user feedback to refine the offering.

# Uber, Klar y Mastercard lanzarán tarjeta de crédito en México

27 de mayo, 2026

·_La tarjeta, Uber Card operada por Klar, brindará mayor valor para viajes, reuniones y cualquier otro momento de consumo._

·_Disponible en versiones Standard y Platino en la red global de Mastercard, la tarjeta ofrecerá cashback, meses sin intereses, promociones y muchos otros beneficios a los usuarios de Uber._

Uber y Klar anuncian el próximo lanzamiento en México de Uber Card operada por Klar en la red de Mastercard. Nuestro país se convertirá en el primero en el mundo en introducir esta solución financiera enfocada al usuario, integrada al ecosistema de movilidad y _delivery_ de Uber. La nueva tarjeta está diseñada para transformar la forma en que los usuarios pagan sus viajes, pedidos y compras diarias, ofreciendo recompensas exclusivas en cada transacción.

_“La llegada de Uber Card operada por Klar, demostrará cómo la innovación financiera puede integrarse de forma natural en la vida cotidiana de las personas. Con el respaldo de Klar, estamos ofreciendo herramientas que generan inclusión, mejoran la experiencia de pago, valor tangible y acceso dentro y fuera de la plataforma”,_ explicó Stefan Möller, CEO y cofundador de Klar.

La tarjeta estará disponible para todos los usuarios de Uber y Uber Eats en dos versiones: Standard y Platino, ambas con propuestas diferenciadas que permiten acumular _cashback_ en viajes y pedidos, obtener recompensas en categorías como telecomunicaciones, suscripciones y supermercados, y acceder a meses sin intereses en más de 140 mil comercios, además de ofrecer soluciones como pago de servicios, líneas de crédito de hasta 250 mil pesos e inversiones con hasta 15% de rendimiento anual, entre otros.

La versión Platino sumará accesos a salas VIP en aeropuertos con _LoungeKey,_ un _cashback_ aumentado en una mayor categoría de comercios y un límite de crédito de hasta 200 mil pesos. Asimismo, la Standard no tendrá cuota anual, mientras que la Platino la eliminará al cumplir con un gasto mínimo en los primeros tres meses del primer año.

_“Uber Card operada por Klar es un paso natural en la evolución de nuestro ecosistema. Hoy, conectamos a más de 25 millones de usuarios activos cada año en México y queremos que cada viaje y cada pedido representen una ventaja real para ellos, especialmente para los miembros de Uber One. Esta tarjeta integrará movilidad, delivery y recompensas en una sola experiencia, optimizando el gasto_”, comentó Daniel Colunga, director general de Uber Eats Latinoamérica.

Finalmente, por su parte, Mariana Casar, vicepresidenta de Fintech, Digital Partners and Enablers para México en Mastercard, agregó: “_Este es un programa único que combina la simplicidad y confianza que las personas esperan de Uber, Klar y Mastercard. Ahora, existe una oportunidad para obtener más valor de cada clic, toque y app en la vida diaria de los mexicanos_”.

Para obtenerla, el usuario deberá abrir su aplicación de Uber o Uber Eats, ir a su perfil, y ahí, próximamente encontrará la sección que lo llevará al proceso para obtener su tarjeta _Uber Card operada por Klar._ A partir de hoy y hasta finales del mes de junio del presente, los usuarios de la plataforma de Uber comenzarán a visualizar el despliegue y acceso al proceso de solicitud de su _Uber Card operada por Klar_ dentro de la app
