Business EconomyPosted at: May 2 2026 9:07PM New Delhi, May 2 (UNI) Flipkart, Axis Bank and PayU today announced the launch of biometric authentication for card payments to tackle frauds like SIM-Swap or OTP-based replaces.

Flipkart is India's homegrown eCommerce marketplace and Axis Bank is country's leading Private sector Bank. 

On the other hand, PayU is India's leading diversified fintech platform.

This rollout offers users to authenticate transactions using their face ID on any compatible device either Android or iOS. It eliminates the need of One-Time Password (OTP).

This service is initially available to Axis Bank cardholders through their issuer-level biometric authentication.

This renewed function also reduces the risk of SIM-swap or OTP-based fraud. This technology replaces SMS-based OTPs with a faster and more secured mechanism from which smartphone users are already familiar. The biometric authentication is set to deal with India’s alarming digital payment fraud landscape. 

According to RBI data, fraud values surged more than five-fold to cross Rs 1,400 crore in FY2024.Taking cognisance of this matter, RBI issued a circular 'Authentication Mechanisms for Digital Payment Transactions Directions 2025', encouraging the adoption of biometric and risk-based authentication over SMS OTPs.

Speaking on the launch, Gaurav Arora, Vice President Payments and Supercoins at Flipkart said, "At Flipkart our approach to innovation is anchored around robust compliance and customer trust. The integration of the biometric authentication feature reflects our commitment to building safety to consumer payments at a time when digital fraud risks are becoming increasingly sophisticated."

"By leveraging secure biometric credentials, we are delivering a payment experience that is intuitive and seamless, while meeting the highest standards of consumer protection. As digital commerce continues to scale, our focus remains on enabling faster transactions while reinforcing the regulatory rigour and ecosystem-wide trust that secure payments demand" he added.

Undoubtedly, this launch is a step forward in India's transition away from OTPs, offering a more smoother to pay while following high standards of compliance and control.

Echoing same view, Hemang Dattani, Chief Business Officer, PayU Payments, said, “As digital commerce continues to scale in India, simplifying payments without compromising on security is critical. At PayU, we are proud to partner with Flipkart to enable a biometric authentication experience for card payments, helping customers move past long-standing friction associated with OTP-based verification. 

"By combining biometric authentication with enhanced security controls, this partnership marks a meaningful step towards building a faster, more secure and frictionless digital payments experience for Indian consumers" he added.

Arnika Dixit, President & Head – Cards, Payments and Wealth Management, Axis Bank, said, “At Axis Bank, we are constantly working to reinforce trust and security across every digital touchpoint."

She noted that customers today expect digital payments to be not only safe but effortless, and biometric authentication delivers exactly that by enabling a secure, one‑touch approval experience. 

"We are delighted to introduce this enhanced capability for our cardholders through our collaboration with Flipkart and PayU.

Dixit believed that "this rollout reflects the strength of our long‑standing partnership and our shared commitment to elevating industry standards in both security and customer experience." 

"As digital commerce continues to evolve, we remain focused on bringing innovations that make payments simpler, stronger and future‑ready for our customers" she concluded.
