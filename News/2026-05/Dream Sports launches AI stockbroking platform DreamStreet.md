 Dream Sports, the parent company of Dream11, has officially launched an AI-powered stock broking platform called DreamStreet.  
 The platform is designed for first-time investors and aims to simplify stock market participation through AI-led insights and expert-backed recommendations.  
 The development follows a restructuring exercise at Dream Sports. The firm reorganised operations into multiple independent units after over 100 executives exited.  
 At launch, DreamStreet allows users to invest in stocks and ETFs, with features such as futures and options (F&O) and IPO investments expected to be added in the coming weeks.  
 The platform includes an AI assistant called “Veda,” which provides insights, analysis, and guidance, along with access to SEBI-registered advisors and research analysts.  
 DreamStreet is led by Rahul Mirchandani as CEO, along with Karan Bansal and Nikhil Lalvani in leadership roles.  
 The company is targeting India’s growing base of retail investors, especially users who have stayed away from markets due to complexity or lack of guidance.  
 The push came after a ban on real money gaming in August 2025 disrupted revenues and led the company to focus on investments and wealth products.  
Following the ban, Dream Sports reorganised into multiple startups including Dream11 (pivoted), FanCode, DreamSetGo, DreamCricket, Dream Play, Dream Money, and Dream Horizon.
