## Eleutheria Closes $5M Round

Eleutheria, a Web3 project that hasn't shared much about what it actually does, announced a **$5 million** strategic funding round on May 13, 2026. The company didn't specify its category or main product focus. The raise comes as Web3 projects continue attracting capital despite regulatory pressure and price swings in crypto markets.

**Alpha Capital** participated in the round, though the announcement listed them under "other investors" rather than as a lead. No lead investor was named, and the company didn't disclose its valuation—a common choice for early-stage Web3 companies that want room to negotiate future terms. Without stated use of funds, it's unclear where the $5 million will go.

## What the Deal Tells Us

**Alpha Capital's** involvement suggests continued interest in decentralized tech, though we don't know how big their check was or what terms they got. The deal landed during a relatively calm stretch for crypto markets in early 2026, which may have helped.

Here's what we actually know:

*   **Alpha Capital** participated. They've backed other decentralized projects, but their specific role here wasn't detailed.
*   It's labeled a **strategic** round, which usually means the money targets specific growth goals rather than broad experimentation.
*   No valuation disclosed, giving Eleutheria flexibility in later fundraising.
*   The announcement mentions "other investors" but doesn't name them—there may be additional backers we don't know about.
*   **$5 million** is a typical size for Web3 rounds focused on team growth or protocol development, though Eleutheria didn't confirm either.
