Kissht crosses Rs 600 Cr revenue in Q4 FY26; profit jumps 52%
 
Kissht’s revenue from operations increased to Rs 619 crore in Q4 FY26 from Rs 369 crore in Q4 FY25, according to its consolidated financial statement sourced from NSE.
 
 Follow Us
 Digital lending platform Kissht delivered a strong financial performance in Q4 FY26, shortly after making its stock market debut earlier this month. The company’s operating revenue surged 68% year-on-year to cross the Rs 600 crore mark during the quarter, while its profit rose 52%. 
 Kissht’s revenue from operations increased to Rs 619 crore in Q4 FY26 from Rs 369 crore in Q4 FY25, according to its consolidated financial statement sourced from NSE. 
 
 Founded in 2015 by Ranvir Singh and Krishnan Vishwanathan, provides small-ticket consumer loans and has scaled aggressively through partnerships with merchants across electronics, fashion, travel, and other categories. The company primarily earns interest income from these loans, although a detailed revenue breakup for the quarter was not disclosed. 
 The company also earned Rs 6 crore from non-operating sources, which took its overall revenue to Rs 625 crore. For the full financial year, its operating revenue jumped 63% year-on-year to Rs 2,179 crore from Rs 1,337 crore in FY25. 
 For the fintech platform, impairment on financial instruments remained one of the largest expense heads, accounting for over 22% of the overall cost base. This expense grew 50% year-on-year to Rs 114 crore in Q4 FY26, while finance cost rose 48% to Rs 77 crore in Q4 FY25. 
 Employee benefit expenses stood at Rs 65 crore, while other overheads including marketing, legal & professional, and IT expenses pushed the company’s overall expenditure up 70% year-on-year to Rs 515 crore in Q4 FY26 from Rs 303 crore in the corresponding quarter last year. 
 In the end, strong revenue growth helped the Mumbai-based increase its profit by 52% year-on-year to Rs 82 crore in Q4 FY26. For the full fiscal year, the company’s profit jumped nearly 75% to Rs 281 crore from Rs 161 crore in FY25. 
 Earlier this month, Kissht made a strong stock market debut in India, with its shares listing at a 12% premium over the issue price. Since then, the stock has continued to gain momentum, closing at around Rs 266 in the last trading session, over 56% higher than its issue price of Rs 171. The company’s current total market capitalization stands at Rs 4,482 crore or $472 million. 
According to its RHP , Kissht raised around Rs 926 crore through its initial public offering (IPO), which included a fresh issue of equity shares worth Rs 850 crore and an offer for sale (OFS) of 4.4 million shares aggregating to Rs 76 crore.
