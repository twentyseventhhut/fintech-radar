Monzo has become one of the first banks to receive regulatory approval from the Financial Conduct Authority to provide targeted support. 
This comes as a Freedom of Information request by FT Adviser revealed the FCA has only had seven firms apply for targeted support , as of March 26.
Since then, it has been revealed that Royal London , Vanguard , Quilter , L&G and Zopa Bank have received approval to deliver targeted support.
Targeted support went live on April 6 and allows firms to give recommendations to groups of consumers with similar characteristics.
Jo Phillips, general manager for wealth at Monzo, said: “At Monzo, our mission is to make money work for everyone. 
“That means helping customers not only manage their money day-to-day, but also build long-term financial security and wealth.

“We’ve seen first-hand that many people want to start investing, but often feel unsure where to begin.”
Monzo launched investments in 2023 and said a third of customers were first time investors when it launched. 
“Today, more than half a million customers invest with Monzo,” Phillips said. “Targeted support has the potential to help customers make informed financial decisions with greater confidence.
“We see this as an important step in helping more people engage with investing and longer-term financial planning in a way that feels simple, responsible and accessible.”
