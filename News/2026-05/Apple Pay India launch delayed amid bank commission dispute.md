Apple Pay India launch delayed amid commission dispute with major banks
Negotiations between Apple and leading Indian lenders over transaction fees and commercial terms continue to hold back the payments service rollout.
ADVERTISEMENT
Apple’s plans to launch its mobile payments platform Apple Pay in India have reportedly been delayed as negotiations with major banks over transaction commissions remain unresolved.
According to a report by Moneycontrol, the technical infrastructure required for the rollout is largely ready, and the service could potentially be launched immediately if commercial agreements are finalised.
Apple is currently in discussions with several leading credit card issuers in the country, including HDFC Bank, SBI Cards, ICICI Bank, Axis Bank and Kotak Mahindra Bank.
The report stated that Apple is seeking a commission of 20 basis points per transaction from partner banks, while lenders are reportedly willing to offer only 15 basis points, similar to the fee structures followed by Apple Pay in several global markets, including the United States.
Also read: Elon Musk loses lawsuit against OpenAI as jury rules claims were filed too late 
Apple is said to be positioning Apple Pay as a premium payments offering in India, where Android smartphones continue to dominate the mobile market with an estimated share of nearly 90%.
Sources cited in the report said Apple wants to launch the service alongside the country’s largest card issuers, which has contributed to delays in finalising agreements.
Apart from commercial negotiations, regulatory compliance has also posed challenges for Apple’s India entry strategy. One of the key hurdles involved the Reserve Bank of India’s data localisation requirements and mandatory system audit regulations for payment operators.
To comply with local rules, Apple is reportedly planning to store tokenised payment credentials on users’ devices and through card network partners rather than mirroring payment data overseas. Global card networks such as Visa, Mastercard and American Express have already aligned with India’s data localisation framework.
The company has also been working with card networks to enable tokenisation support for Apple Pay transactions in the country.
Apple’s efforts to introduce Apple Pay in India date back nearly three years. However, the company reportedly faced difficulties securing regulatory approvals earlier, while India’s mandatory two-factor authentication process for card payments was viewed as inconsistent with Apple Pay’s streamlined global user experience.
Also read: Anthropic eases restrictions on cybersecurity AI model sharing under Project Glasswing 
The company had also avoided integrating Apple Pay with India’s Unified Payments Interface (UPI) ecosystem because of the zero Merchant Discount Rate (MDR) structure applied to UPI payments.
Industry experts believe the situation improved after the RBI updated its additional factor authentication guidelines last year to permit biometric authentication for card-based transactions. The move is expected to allow Apple Pay users in India to authenticate payments through Face ID and similar biometric systems, bringing the experience closer to what Apple offers in Western markets.
Currently, most Indian Apple users rely on UPI-based payment methods for transactions related to iCloud subscriptions and other Apple services.
