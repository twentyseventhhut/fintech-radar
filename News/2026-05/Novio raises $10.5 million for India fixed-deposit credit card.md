Fintech Startup Novio Raises Rs 100 Crore In Series A Round
Team StartupCity
 Novio has raised ₹100 crore in a Series A round led by Cornerstone Ventures, with participation from multiple investors and venture debt providers. 
 The startup offers FD-backed RuPay credit cards to users without credit history, enabling instant access to formal credit without income proof or credit checks. 
 Novio plans to expand into smaller cities, scale partnerships with banks, and target 5 million card issuances in the next three years. 
 Novio , a young fintech startup aiming to widen access to credit in India, has raised Rs 100 crore (about $10.5 million) in a Series A funding round led by Cornerstone Ventures, with participation from Shepherd’s Hill Private Equity, ESV-Arthya AIF, and Roots Ventures. The round also included venture debt from Innoven Capital and Alteria Capital, signaling strong investor confidence in the company’s early traction and long-term vision.
Founded in 2024 by Aditya Gupta, Sandeep Ghule, Anand Kapadia, and Manish Sinha, Novio is tackling a persistent gap in India’s financial ecosystem millions of consumers who lack a formal credit history and are therefore excluded from traditional credit products.

Its solution is relatively simple but powerful: users can open a fixed deposit through the app and, in return, receive a RuPay credit card within hours, without income documentation or credit checks. The deposit both secures the card and continues to earn interest, making it an accessible entry point into the credit system.
The company plans to use the fresh capital to expand its reach beyond major cities into tier II to tier V markets, where access to formal credit remains limited. It also intends to deepen partnerships with banks such as SBM Bank and DCB Bank, while continuing to build out its product offerings. This includes integrating UPI payments and rolling out AI-driven credit advisory tools designed to help users better manage and build their financial profiles.
In just about a year since launch, Novio says it has crossed 100,000 active cardholders a milestone that underscores the demand for alternative credit solutions. Looking ahead, the startup has set an ambitious goal of issuing 5 million cards over the next three years, positioning itself as a key player in India’s evolving fintech landscape.
