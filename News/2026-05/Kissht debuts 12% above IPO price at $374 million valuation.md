Kissht makes stock market debut at 12% premium over IPO price

 Digital lending platform Kissht, operated by OnEMI Technology Solutions, made a strong debut on the Indian stock exchanges, with its shares listing at Rs 191 apiece on the BSE, nearly 12% higher than the issue price of Rs 171. 
 The shares debuted at Rs 190 on the National Stock Exchange (NSE). Following the listing, the stock gained over 10% to trade around Rs 210 as of 11:20 AM today. At the current price, the company commands a market capitalization of approximately Rs 3,532 crore ($372 million). 
 The company received strong interest from the investors during the subscription period, which was subscribed over 9 times. According to exchange data , the QIB segment subscribed 24.9X, followed by Non-Institutional Investors (NIIs) at 6.5X, while the retail investor quota saw relatively muted interest at 1.83X. 
 Kissht’s initial public offering (IPO) was open for subscription from April 30 to May 5, with a price band of Rs 162–171 and a minimum investment of Rs 14,094 for a lot size of 87 shares. 
 According to its RHP , Kissht raised around Rs 926 crore through its initial public offering (IPO), which included a fresh issue of equity shares worth Rs 850 crore and an offer for sale (OFS) of 4.4 million shares aggregating to Rs 76 crore. 
 The OFS participants included early investors in Kissht who offloaded their partial stake such as Vertex Venture, Ammar Sdn Bhd, Ventureast Proactive Fund, Endiya Seed Co-creation Fund, and AION Advisory. 
 Ahead of the IPO launch, Kissht secured Rs 278 crore (around $30 million) from 22 anchor investors. The anchor round saw participation from a mix of prominent domestic mutual funds and global institutional investors, including HDFC Mutual Fund, ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund, WhiteOak Capital, Goldman Sachs, Citigroup, and BNP Paribas. 
 For the nine-month period ended December 2025, Kissht reported an operating revenue of Rs 1,560 crore and a net profit of Rs 199 crore. In FY25, the company recorded Rs 1,337 crore in operating revenue and a net profit of Rs 160 crore.
