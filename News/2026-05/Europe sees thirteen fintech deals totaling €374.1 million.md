BlackFin Tech Weekly — May 18th, 2026
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity.
Hello FinTech Friends,
Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry!
 Over the last week, there were thirteen fintech deals in Europe, totaling €374.1m in disclosed funding, including six transactions in the UK, two in France, one in Italy, one in Spain, one in Poland, on in the Netherlands and one in Slovenia. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Paymentology , a UK-based cloud-native issuer processing platform, has raised €150.1m in an investment round co-led by Apis Partners and Aspirity Partners . 
 Elliptic , a UK-based blockchain analytics and digital asset compliance platform, has raised €103.1m in a Series D round led by One Peak . 
 Embat , a Spain-based AI-powered treasury management platform, has raised €30m in a Series B round led by Cathay Innovation . 
 Let’s dive! 
 Paymentology 
 Paymentology , a UK-based cloud-native issuer processing platform for banks and fintechs, has raised €150.1m in an investment round co-led by Apis Partners and Aspirity Partners . The company provides real-time card and payment processing infrastructure, enabling financial institutions to launch and scale card programs across multiple markets while modernizing legacy payment systems. The funding will support international expansion, product development, and growth across areas including credit, stablecoin infrastructure, tokenization, and AI-driven financial services, as Paymentology looks to strengthen its position in global issuer processing and embedded finance infrastructure.

 Elliptic 
 Elliptic , a UK-based digital asset compliance and blockchain analytics platform, has raised €103.1m in a Series D round led by One Peak , with participation from Nasdaq Ventures , Deutsche Bank , and the British Business Bank . The round values the company at €575.7m . Elliptic provides AI-native on-chain analytics and compliance infrastructure for banks, fintechs, exchanges, and government agencies, screening more than one billion blockchain transactions per week across 65+ blockchains. The funding will support product development and global expansion as the company scales real-time compliance and risk monitoring infrastructure for stablecoins, tokenized assets, and institutional digital asset adoption. 
 Read more 
 Embat 
 Embat , a Spain-based AI-powered treasury management platform, has raised €30m in a Series B round led by Cathay Innovation , with participation from existing investors Creandum , Samaipata , 4Founders Capital , and VentureFriends . The company provides treasury and cash management software for mid-market and enterprise clients, using agentic AI to automate reconciliation, cash visibility, forecasting, and liquidity management processes. The funding will support international expansion, team growth, and continued investment in AI-native treasury tools, as Embat looks to scale its presence across Europe and strengthen its position in corporate finance automation.

 In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 NAB , an Australia-based banking group, has acquired Banked , a UK-based Pay by Bank and account-to-account payments platform. The acquisition strengthens NAB’s real-time payments and digital checkout capabilities, enabling merchants to receive funds directly from customer bank accounts through faster and lower-cost payment flows. Founded in 2018, Banked provides account-to-account payment infrastructure and orchestration technology for businesses and developers globally. NAB plans to integrate the platform into its broader payments infrastructure as it expands its real-time and account-to-account payment services for business customers.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 The Bank of England is preparing to soften its stablecoin restrictions after deputy governor Sarah Breeden said initial proposals, including ownership limits and a 40% backing deposit requirement, may have been “overly conservative.” The crypto industry warned the rules could undermine the UK’s digital assets hub ambitions. Breeden said the BoE is “genuinely open” to alternatives, including lowering the deposit requirement, which is far stricter than US rules. Separately, Breeden signalled no rush to raise rates, despite markets pricing in hikes this year, saying “we don’t need to do it in June or July.” 
 Read more 
 UK fintechs raised $741m across 41 funding rounds in Q1 2026, according to the UK Fintech Quarterly Funding Report from data intelligence platform Tracxn . This represents a 43% decline from the $1.3bn raised in Q4 2025. Tracxn describes the decline as a “strategic rebalancing by investors”, with the report highlighting significant structural shifts including a clear divergence across funding stages. Capital is being repositioned to back companies at earlier stages and with greater conviction. Early stage investment rose to $276m, up 35% quarter-on-quarter and 177% year-on-year. Seed funding also grew by 46% quarter-on-quarter to $54.2m. 
 Read more 
 Klarna reported $1mn in net income in Q1 2026, its first break-even since the $15bn NYSE listing last September. Revenue rose 44% to $1bn, driven by debit card growth and 119mn active users, up 21% year-on-year. The company is pivoting from buy now, pay later towards a broader digital bank model. However, credit loss provisions rose 40% to $186mn and shares have fallen 70% since listing, valuing the company at just $5.2bn. Klarna continues to lean on AI, with a chatbot handling two-thirds of customer service and CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski claiming the tech has allowed it to halve its workforce. 
 Read more 
 Germany’s BaFin warned that cyber risks are “growing” and “substantial” due to AI advances, particularly Anthropic ’s Mythos model, which has triggered a scramble across global banking to test the technology. BaFin President Mark Branson said new AI models can identify vulnerabilities in IT systems with “remarkable speed” and exploit them ever more rapidly, calling cybersecurity strengthening an “urgent and essential investment”. BaFin announced a new division conducting targeted “IT spotlight” inspections at financial firms, enabling faster assessments than full reviews and a more effective response to emerging threats.
