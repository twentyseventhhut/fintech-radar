# Tamara's Q1 profit surges 378% to SAR 123 million

Tamara posted strong financial results for the first quarter of 2026, with net profit rising to SAR 123.4 million, compared to SAR 25.8 million in the same period of 2025, achieving growth exceeding 378%.
Profits also rose compared to the previous quarter, as the company had recorded around SAR 101 million in the fourth quarter of 2025, reflecting continued operational improvement and accelerated growth in the company's business.
These results come at a time when the Saudi fintech sector is witnessing increased competition and pressures related to funding and profitability, making this growth an important indicator of the evolution of the company's business model.

 Revenue continues to grow 
The financial data showed a continued rise in the company's operating revenue during the first quarter of 2026, driven by an increase in the volume of purchases through the platform and a widening user and merchant base.
According to the report, total revenue rose to SAR 529.8 million, compared to SAR 349.4 million in the same period last year, an increase of about 51.6%.
This growth reflects the continued expansion of “buy now, pay later” services in the Saudi market, alongside the rising reliance on digital payments and e-commerce.
The company also continued to expand its partnerships with merchants across multiple sectors, including:
E-commerce
Retail
Fashion
electronics
Travel
Digital services
The figures indicate that the company has managed to maintain high growth rates despite new competitors entering the regional market.
 Clear improvement in margins 
One of the most notable indicators in the results was the marked improvement in the profitability margin.
With profit rising 378% versus revenue growth of only 51.6%, the results show that the company has begun achieving higher operational efficiency compared to previous periods.
This reflects Tamara's ability to better control:
Funding costs
Operating expenses
Risk management
Customer acquisition costs
Collection efficiency
The current improvement also indicates the company's gradual transition from a phase of heavy spending on expansion and user acquisition to a phase more focused on achieving returns and financial sustainability.
 Digital finance continues to expand 
The results reflect the continued strong growth of the “buy now, pay later” solutions market in Saudi Arabia and the region.
In recent years, digital installment payments have become one of the fastest-growing fintech sectors, supported by rising e-commerce usage, changing consumer behavior, and increased reliance on flexible payment solutions.
The Saudi regulatory environment has also contributed to supporting the expansion of fintech companies, with an acceleration in license issuance and the development of the digital infrastructure for payments and financial services.
Market data indicates that Saudi Arabia has become one of the largest markets for digital payments and electronic installments in the region.
 Profitability becomes a priority 
Tamara's results also reflect a broader shift within the global fintech sector, where companies and investors are increasingly focusing on profitability and operational sustainability after years of focusing solely on growth.
In the recent period, global fintech companies have faced increasing pressures due to:
rising interest rates
Rising funding costs
Slowing investments
Higher cost of capital
Market tightening against loss-making companies
But Tamara's results indicate the company's success in improving its balance between growth and expansion and achieving profits.
This also gives the company greater flexibility for future regional expansion compared to companies that still rely primarily on external funding to sustain their growth.
 Competition intensifies 
Despite the strong performance, the company still operates in a market witnessing rapid competition from local, regional, and global companies.
Banks, digital payment platforms, and other fintech companies have also begun expanding strongly into the installment and flexible payment sector, alongside rising demand for short-term financing.
However, a large customer base and extensive local presence give Tamara a strong competitive edge in the Saudi market.
Observers believe the next phase may see market concentration around companies capable of achieving:
Sustainable growth
Risk management
Operational efficiency
Long-term profitability
 Saudi fintech matures 
Tamara's results indicate that the Saudi fintech sector has begun entering a more mature phase, with companies shifting from focusing solely on user acquisition to building business models capable of generating profits and sustaining operations.
The results also reflect the evolution of the Saudi digital environment, which now supports the growth of fintech companies faster than many regional markets.
Analysts believe that continued improvement in profits and operational efficiency may drive Saudi fintech companies to expand their regional presence in the coming years, especially with the continued growth of the digital economy and modern financial services in the Kingdom.
