# Apple Pay drives 30-fold surge in EMV payments at Hyundai Card

Transactions using the EMV contactless payment standard have been steadily growing in South Korea.
Spending on foreign-issued Visa and Mastercard rose 17-fold in the third quarter, compared with a year ago. The surge was particularly evident when Hyundai Card introduced Apple Pay in the country, with transactions up nine times in June from a year earlier.
"EMV contactless transactions are at least 75% faster than other existing payment methods,” a Visa Korea spokesperson explains. “They are also more secure as a payment standard accepted around the world, and have excellent versatility. EMV contactless payment is already fairly common globally, and people visiting Korea from overseas have been making increasing use of EMV contactless during their time here.”
Short for Europay, Mastercard and Visa, EMV contactless is an international NFC ( near-field communication ) payment standard, which accounts for approximately 90% of the total non-contact card payment market.
In contrast to the MST ( magnetic secure transmission ) standard which requires card swiping or QR code scanning, EMV contactless payment can be completed by just holding the card or mobile device near an NFC terminal. Security is also high as transaction data are sent to payment providers in a private code.
During the Covid-19 pandemic, EMV transactions grew substantially as they are considered hygienic with no need for physical contact between buyer and merchant.
Hyundai Card was the first to issue EMV contactless credit cards in South Korea in 2017 and has since been building the country’s contactless payment ecosystem.
Currently, all cards issued by Hyundai Card support EMV contactless. “Korea must shift from using MST, which only Korea uses, to the EMV+NFC system,” says Hyundai Card vice-chairman and chief executive officer Ted Chung.
Korean consumers are now realizing the convenience of adopting the EMV contactless standard. A survey commissioned by Visa in March shows that 70% of 1,000 adult respondents in the country are “willing to use a contactless card within one year”.
However, a Hyundai Card representative admits that “the road will be long until EMV contactless becomes as popular in Korea as in other countries”, noting that the standard already accounts for more than 96% of card and mobile payments in markets such as Australia, Singapore, the United Kingdom, and Hong Kong.
