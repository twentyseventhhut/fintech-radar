InCred posts Rs 1,849 Cr revenue in first 9 months of FY26
 
InCred company also reported strong financial performance for the first nine months of FY26, with revenue rising 39% year-on-year, while profit remained steady at Rs 290 crore during the period.

 
 Follow Us
 InCred Holdings, the parent company of NBFC arm InCred Financial Services, has filed its updated draft red herring prospectus (UDRHP) with SEBI to raise Rs 1,250 crore through a fresh issue in its IPO. The company also reported strong financial performance for the first nine months of FY26, with revenue rising 39% year-on-year, while profit remained steady at Rs 290 crore during the period. 
 According to its updated DRHP, the Mumbai-based company’s revenue from operations rose to Rs 1,849 crore during the first nine months of FY26 from Rs 1,334 crore in the corresponding period of FY25. 
 
 Founded by Bhupinder Singh in 2017, InCred Finance is a retail-focused diversified NBFC offering lending products across five verticals, including personal loans, student loans, secured business loans, specialised MSME loans, and loans to financial institutions. 
 Interest income from loan disbursements formed the bulk of InCred’s revenue, accounted for over 91% of the total operating income at Rs 1,689 crore, and rose 39% year-on-year. Fees and commission income stood at Rs 150 crore, while the remaining operating income came from net gains on fair value changes in financial assets. 
 The firm also made Rs 21 crore from other income, tallying its total revenue to Rs 1,849 crore during the period. 
 For the fintech firm, finance costs formed 46% of the overall expenditure and rose 51% to Rs 673 crore during the nine-month period. Employee benefit expenses also increased 28% to Rs 311 crore from Rs 243 crore in the corresponding period of FY25. 
 Losses from impaired financial instruments, including loan write-offs during the period, jumped 86% to Rs 234 crore. 
 Depreciation along with legal, collection expenses, advertising, traveling, and other expenses took the firm’s overall cost up 52% to Rs 1,471 crore in 9M FY26 from Rs 966 crore in the same period of FY25. 
 Despite strong revenue growth, the company’s profit remained steady at Rs 290 crore during the period due to higher losses from loan write-offs. On a unit level, it spent Rs 0.8 to earn every rupee in 9M FY26. 
 During the first 9 months of FY26, InCred had total financial assets worth Rs 14,269 crore which included healthy cash and bank balance of Rs 931 crore. 
 According to its UDRHP, the IPO comprises a fresh issue of Rs 1,250 crore along with an offer for sale (OFS) of up to 9.90 crore equity shares. Existing shareholders including KKR, MNI Ventures Mohandas Pai Family, Moore Strategic, V’Ocean Investments, and others will participate in the OFS.
