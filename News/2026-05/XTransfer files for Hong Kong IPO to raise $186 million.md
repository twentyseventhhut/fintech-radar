# XTransfer Files for IPO on HKEX, Seeking $186 Million

 GuruFocus News 

 04/27/2026 02:00 

B2B cross-border payment platform XTransfer has submitted an IPO application to the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (00388.HK), aiming to raise $186 million. Underwriters include UBS and CICC, with pre-IPO support from investors like China Merchants Venture and Alibaba (09988.HK). The company utilizes a weighted voting rights (WVR) structure, granting special shares ten votes each, controlled entirely by founder and CEO Deng Guobiao.

XTransfer targets small and medium-sized enterprises, facilitating efficient and secure cross-border payment transactions. By 2025, it aims to become the largest B2B cross-border payment platform globally, projecting a transaction volume exceeding $60 billion and a market share of 5.1%. Forecasted revenues for 2023 to 2025 are $115 million, $162 million, and $248 million, with adjusted net profits of $11.2 million, $5.27 million, and $47.67 million respectively. Established in 2017, XTransfer has completed seven funding rounds, the latest in March, raising $69 million at a post-money valuation of $3.02 billion.
