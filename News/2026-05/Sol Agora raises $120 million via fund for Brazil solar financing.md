Brookfield fintech raises US$120mn for Brazil distributed solar 
 Sol Agora, a fintech of Brookfield, raised 600 million reais (US$120mn) in the Sol Agora 4 Credit Receivables Investment Fund (FIDC), bringing the total amount raised since 2022 to 3 billion reais. 
 The capital from Fund 4 is expected to enable 30,000 new solar installations in Brazil, including hybrid projects with batteries, expanding access beyond conventional photovoltaic generation. 
 Eduardo Solamone, Sol Agora’s investor relations director, told BNamericas that the focus is on distributed generation projects aimed at small consumers. 
 BNamericas: Will the funds be used to finance distributed or centralized generation projects?
 Solamone: The resources of FIDC Sol Agora 4 will be directed exclusively to financing distributed generation projects, with a focus on families and small businesses throughout Brazil.
The structure follows the company’s strategy of expanding access to solar energy through distributed, high‑impact solutions in the energy transition.
 BNamericas: What led to the decision to include hybrid installations (with batteries)?
 Solamone: The inclusion of hybrid projects with batteries reflects the evolution of the market and the expansion of Sol Agora’s portfolio of energy solutions. More mature markets, such as the United States and Australia, highlight this trend, which is expected to be reflected in Brazil over the course of this year and the coming years.
We have observed a growing demand for projects that deliver greater energy efficiency, autonomy, and predictability for consumers, in line with the sector’s technological maturity.
 BNamericas: Isn’t there concern about the difficulty new solar generation projects have in accessing the transmission and distribution grid? Even so, do you still see demand and room for new projects?
 Solamone: We closely monitor the infrastructure and connection challenges in the sector, but we continue to see a market with significant structural demand, especially in distributed generation focused on small consumer projects, which is not affected by the discussions on flow reversal and project approvals.
 BNamericas: Does Sol Agora intend to carry out new fundraising rounds in the coming months? Is there a financial target?
 Solamone: New capital raisings this year should occur only opportunistically, given that the company already has funding for its next investment cycle.
 BNamericas: Who are Sol Agora’s main partners in the projects (integrators/suppliers of solar panels, inverters and batteries)?
 Solamone: Today there are approximately 20,000 integrators qualified to work with us through around 50 different equipment distributors.
We have a range of financeable equipment available through our platform as a way to better tailor projects to customers’ needs and after-sales experience.
