BlackFin Tech Weekly — May 11th, 2026
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity.
Hello FinTech Friends,
Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry!
 Over the last week, there were seven fintech deals in Europe, totaling €107.8m in disclosed funding, including three transactions in Italy, two in the UK, one in France, and one in Sweden. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 LegalPlace , a France-based legaltech platform for business creation and management, has raised €70m in a funding round backed by XAnge , Eurazeo , and Move Capital . 
 OpenTrade , a UK-based stablecoin yield infrastructure platform, has raised €14.4m in a strategic funding round led by Mercury Fund and Notion Capital. 
 Swimbird , a Sweden-based fintech company providing digital financial system solutions, has raised €7.9min a Series Around led by 7RIDGE . 
 Let’s dive! 
 LegalPlace 
 LegalPlace , a France-based legaltech company, raised €70m in a funding round from investors including XAnge , Eurazeo , and Move Capital , alongside the acquisition of rival Legalstart . The company provides a digital platform for company formation and business administration services for SMEs and entrepreneurs. The funding and acquisition will support product expansion and market consolidation, as LegalPlace aims to build an integrated one-stop platform combining legal, administrative, and accounting services for businesses.

 OpenTrade 
 OpenTrade , a UK-based stablecoin yield infrastructure platform, raised €14.4m in a strategic funding round led by Mercury Fund and Notion Capital , with participation from a16z Crypto , AlbionVC , and CMCC Global . The company provides infrastructure enabling fintechs, neobanks, and exchanges to offer dollar- and euro-denominated yield products backed by real-world assets through plug-and-play stablecoin solutions. The funding will support platform expansion, development of its institutional-grade Curation+ services, and global team growth, as OpenTrade aims to scale secure and compliant yield infrastructure for stablecoin-based financial products. 
 Read more 
 Swimbird 
 Swimbird , a Sweden-based fintech company providing digital financial system solutions for banks, brokers, exchanges, and asset managers, has raised €7.9m in a Series A funding round led by 7RIDGE . The company combines financial expertise with modern technology to deliver business-critical infrastructure and digital transformation solutions for financial institutions. The funding will support product scaling and international expansion, as Swimbird enters its next phase of growth alongside strategic partners focused on accelerating its global development.

 In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 Norvestor , a Norway-based private equity firm, has agreed to acquire a majority stake in Debtist , a Germany-based AI-driven receivables management and digital debt collection platform, in a deal reportedly valuing the company at more than €100m . The transaction will support the companies’ ambition to build a regional leader in technology-enabled debt collection across Europe, with Debtist’s co-founders Matteo Benedetti, Tony Zabel, and Brandon Baumgarten retaining a substantial minority stake. Founded in 2023, Debtist provides a fully digital platform covering the entire receivables management lifecycle, using AI to analyse debtor behaviour, optimise outreach strategies, and improve recovery rates while maintaining regulatory compliance and transparency. Backed by Norvestor , the company plans to accelerate its expansion across Europe and further scale its AI-native infrastructure for receivables management and collections. 
 Read more 
 Deel , a US-based global payroll and workforce management platform, has acquired Sastrify , a Germany-based AI-powered SaaS procurement and management platform. The acquisition will strengthen Deel IT by adding software procurement, license management, spend benchmarking, renewal workflows, and compliance reporting to its existing hardware provisioning and device management capabilities. The transaction supports Deel’s strategy to become a broader workforce operating system, enabling companies to manage employee onboarding, offboarding, devices, and SaaS spend through a single platform, while Sastrify brings its SaaS spend optimization technology and pricing benchmark data into the combined offering. 
 Read more 
 Ramp , a US-based financial operations and corporate spend management platform, has acquired Midday , a Sweden-based open-source business management platform focused on invoicing, time tracking, and financial insights. The acquisition will strengthen Ramp’s AI-native product capabilities and design-led user experience, while bringing Midday’s co-founders, Pontus Abrahamsson and Viktor Hofte , into the company. Ramp plans to integrate Midday’s product expertise into its broader financial automation platform as it continues expanding tools for finance teams and business operators.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 PayPal , Mastercard and Visa are facing a UK competition probe after the FCA opened an investigation into alleged anti-competitive conduct linked to the funding and usage of PayPal ’s digital wallet, in a rare use of the regulator’s competition enforcement powers. All three companies confirmed cooperation with the probe. Digital wallet usage in the UK surged to 29% of card transactions from 8% in 2023, prompting calls for stronger competition oversight. The probe follows a joint FCA - PSR report on digital wallets and a separate CMA inquiry into Apple and Google ’s mobile ecosystems launched in January 2025.

 The EU’s Instant Payments Regulation requires all eurozone banks to send and receive instant euro payments by July 2027. SEPA Instant is projected to account for 18% of eurozone payments by 2035, with some institutions investing up to €100m in infrastructure. President Macron backed Wero as Europe’s homegrown account-to-account payments model, pushing for independence from US payment firms. ECB president Lagarde reinforced the sovereignty push, dismissing euro stablecoins and backing central bank money as the anchor for tokenised settlement via Pontes in September.

 Allianz Commercial will transfer its standalone cyber insurance portfolio to Coalition , combining Allianz ’s scale with Coalition ’s data-driven underwriting model in a long-term partnership. Coalition will handle pricing, product design, risk mitigation and claims handling. Allianz receives equity, board representation and performance-linked components tied to growth. Coalition ’s “Active Insurance” model combines coverage with continuous threat monitoring. Rollout targets the US, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, Denmark and Sweden.

 Anthropic launched pre-built AI agents for AML, KYC, and capital markets, designed to automate compliance and banking workflows with full audit trails. The AML agent, co-built with FIS , compresses investigations from days to minutes. BMO and Amalgamated Bank are among the first to deploy. Additional agents cover pitchbook generation, earnings analysis, and credit decisioning. The rollout follows a $1.5bn joint venture with Blackstone , Goldman Sachs , and others to embed Anthropic engineers directly within enterprise clients. Separately, Goldman Sachs restricted Claude access in Hong Kong due to licensing limits.
