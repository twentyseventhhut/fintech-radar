# Bithumb enters Vietnam crypto license race with SSI Digital deal

Latest NewsPublished May 7, 2026

Bithumb’s tie-up with SSI Digital positions it for Vietnam’s strict crypto exchange pilot as competition for scarce licenses heats up and scrutiny mounts at home.

Copy link

South Korean crypto exchange Bithumb is expanding its overseas push through a new partnership in Vietnam as the country moves toward licensing its first domestic digital asset trading platforms.

Bithumb said it signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with SSI Digital (SSID), a subsidiary of Vietnam’s largest securities company, SSI Securities, to cooperate on establishing and operating a virtual asset exchange in the country.

The agreement, signed in Hanoi in March but announced Thursday, also leaves the door open for Bithumb to make a strategic equity investment in an SSID-designated entity, subject to Vietnam’s regulatory approvals.

The move positions Bithumb for Vietnam’s pilot program for licensed domestic digital asset trading platforms, creating a potential entry point into one of Southeast Asia’s fastest-growing crypto markets.

## Vietnam’s tight crypto pilot race

Vietnam’s five-year crypto asset pilot, unveiled in September 2025, requires exchange operators to be Vietnamese entities with at least 10 trillion dong ($380 million), in charter capital and caps foreign ownership at 49%. Authorities are also drafting rules that could restrict trading on unlicensed overseas platforms.

_Bithumb and Vietnam's SSID Collaborate. Source:__Bithumb_

Competition for licenses is already intensifying. A Finance Ministry document cited by Reuters in March said five firms, including affiliates of private banks Techcombank, VPBank and LPBank, along with broker VIX Securities and conglomerate Sun Group, have already cleared an initial qualification round.

_**Related:**__**Bank of Korea floats crypto ‘circuit breakers’ after Bithumb blunder**_

One of the most advanced bids is VPBank-linked CAEX, which secured backing from OKX Ventures and HashKey Capital in April to help meet Vietnam’s minimum charter capital threshold for the pilot. Bithumb’s Vietnamese partner, SSI Securities, is one of the country’s largest brokers and set up SSI Digital Technology JSC to spearhead its push into digital assets in 2022. Vietnam was ranked fourth in global crypto adoption in 2025 by blockchain forensics firm Chainalysis.

According to Bithumb’s press statement, the company and SSID plan to collaborate across exchange technology, wallet and custody systems, security and risk management, regulatory support and institutional business development. No timeline has been given for a formal license application or final investment decision, and Vietnam has not yet approved any fully licensed crypto exchange under the pilot.

Cointelegraph reached out to Bithumb for comment but had not received a response by publication.

## Korea headwinds raise stakes

The expansion plan comes as Bithumb faces heightened scrutiny in its home country following a high-profile payout error and a delayed listing timeline.

The exchange has pushed back its initial public offering (IPO) to sometime after 2028, with management saying it needs to strengthen accounting policies and internal controls after earlier regulatory sanctions.

In February, Bithumb mistakenly credited customers with 620,000 Bitcoin instead of 620,000 won during a promotional event, briefly creating more than $40 billion in notional balances and triggering sharp price swings on the platform. The firm says it recovered 99.7% of the funds and is now pursuing legal action to reclaim the remaining 7 BTC.

# Bithumb Joins Vietnam’s Crypto Licensing Push Through SSI Digital Partnership

*   By Damilola Esebame
*   May 7, 2026

*   Share

South Korean cryptocurrency exchange Bithumb has signed a memorandum of understanding with SSID, the digital asset subsidiary of Vietnam’s largest securities firm, SSI Securities, to jointly build a licensed virtual asset exchange in the country.

Bithumb publicly announced the agreement on May 7, although the MOU was originally signed at SSI’s Hanoi office on March 2. Bithumb CEO Lee Jae-won and SSI CEO Nguyen Khac Hai attended the signing ceremony, which was later joined by SSI Securities Chairman Nguyen Duy Hung, who reaffirmed the partnership.

The deal covers cooperation across exchange operations, including technology architecture, wallet and custody systems, security and risk management, regulatory compliance, business development and institutional services.

The companies also said they may pursue an equity investment in an SSID-designated entity, subject to regulatory approval and Vietnam’s foreign ownership limits, which generally cap overseas stakes in financial firms at 49%.

## Capitalising on Vietnam’s New Pilot Framework

The deal lands as Vietnam moves to formalise its long-unregulated crypto sector. The State Securities Commission began accepting applications for licensed digital asset exchange operators on January 20 under the Ministry of Finance’s Decision No. 96/QD-BTC, which implements Resolution 05/2025/NQ-CP authorising a five-year pilot program.

Under the framework, applicants must hold a minimum charter capital of 10 trillion Vietnamese dong, equivalent to nearly $400 million, and meet strict shareholder and infrastructure requirements. At least 65% of charter capital must come from institutional investors, with more than 35% contributed by qualifying banks, securities firms, fund managers, insurers or technology companies.

According to Vietnam News, around 10 securities firms and banks have publicly signalled intentions to apply. SSI Securities established SSID in 2022 and has since announced separate strategic agreements with Tether, U2U Network and Amazon Web Services to build a blockchain and cloud-based digital finance ecosystem in Vietnam.

### Cross-Border Strategy

A Bithumb spokesperson said the agreement reflects international recognition of the exchange’s compliance posture.

“This partnership reflects international recognition of Bithumb’s operational capabilities and transparency,” the spokesperson told The Korea Herald. “We will prioritise full compliance with Vietnamese regulations as we work to build a safe virtual asset trading infrastructure.”

Bithumb is South Korea’s second-largest cryptocurrency exchange and the latest Asian operator to seek a foothold in Vietnam’s emerging market.

MBBank previously signed a technical cooperation agreement with Dunamu, the operator of South Korea’s largest exchange Upbit, while Techcom Securities and VIX Securities have launched their own digital asset platforms ahead of the licensing window.

A Chainalysis report, cited by Vietnamese state media, estimated that the country received approximately $220 billion in crypto value between July 2024 and June 2025, placing it third in the Asia-Pacific region behind India and South Korea.

The Vietnamese government has tasked agencies with launching pilot exchanges as soon as conditions allow under the new licensing regime.
