Usage-based billing startup Flexprice has raised $1.5 million in a seed funding round led by Shastra VC, with participation from TDV Partners and People Group founder Anupam Mittal.
The fresh capital will be used to expand in the US and Europe and build products beyond billing, including metering, revenue recognition, and financial reporting tools.
Founded in 2024, Flexprice builds open-source billing infrastructure for AI-native and API-first companies. Its platform is being used by AI companies to manage billing across API calls, token usage, GPU hours, and hybrid pricing models.
According to the firm, its revenue grew 6X in the last quarter, while event processing volume increased 20X over the past year to more than 20 billion events per month.
The company is headquartered in New Delhi and has teams in Bengaluru and San Francisco.
Venture capital firm Shastra VC (formerly Veda VC) recently launched a $100 million fund focused on early-stage startups in deeptech, artificial intelligence, climate tech, spacetech, defence, and renewable sciences.

## Indian AI billing startup Flexprice raises $1.5m seed

Share

Add TIA as a preferred source

Flexprice, a startup based in New Delhi that provides billing software for AI-native and application programming interface (API)-first businesses, has raised US$1.5 million in seed funding led by Shastra VC.

Founded in late 2024, the company said it will use the capital to expand in the US and Europe and build products beyond billing, including metering, revenue recognition, and financial reporting.

The company said its platform processes more than 20 billion events a month across billing models tied to tokens, API calls, graphics processing unit (GPU) hours, and hybrid pricing.

Flexprice also uses a mix of open-source and commercial software licensing.

Expanding into usage-based billing could also create additional accounting and reporting complexity for software companies.
