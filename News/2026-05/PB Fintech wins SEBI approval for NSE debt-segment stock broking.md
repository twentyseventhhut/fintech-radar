PB Fintech enters debt broking space with SEBI approval
 
PB Fintech, the parent company of Policybazaar, has received approval from the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) to operate as a stock broker in the debt segment of the National Stock Exchange (NSE).
 
 Follow Us
 PB Fintech, the parent company of Policybazaar, has received approval from the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) for its wholly-owned subsidiary PB Marketing and Consulting Pvt Ltd to operate as a stock broker in the debt segment of the National Stock Exchange (NSE). 
 The company disclosed the development on a stock exchange filing today. 
 Unlike a full-fledged stockbroking license that permits equity trading, derivatives, commodities, and other market activities, the approval received by PB Fintech is limited to the debt segment. This means the company can facilitate transactions in instruments such as corporate bonds, government securities, treasury bills, and other fixed-income products, but cannot offer equity or F&O trading without obtaining separate approvals. 
 The move marks PB Fintech’s continued expansion in financial services beyond insurance aggregation. Over the past few years, the company has been steadily building its presence across lending, payments, and wealth-related offerings. 
 In the latest development around the company, Chinese internet giant Tencent recently offloaded a 1.05% stake in PB Fintech through a block deal worth Rs 805 crore. 
On the financial front, PB Fintech reported a 37% year-on-year increase in operating revenue to Rs 2,061 crore during the quarter, while its profit rose 54% to Rs 261 crore. At the end of Friday’s trading session, shares of PB Fintech closed at Rs 1,647.9, taking the company’s total market capitalization to Rs 76,247 crore (around $8 billion).
