## Indian microfinance startup Sindhuja raises $5m

Share

Add TIA as a preferred source

Sindhuja Microcredit, a Noida-based microfinance lender serving rural India, has raised US$5 million in a pre-series D round from existing investors Abler Nordic, GAWA Capital, and Oikocredit.

The funds to strengthen its capital base and expand the business.

The company added that it has disbursed microloans to more than 500,000 self-employed women and micro-entrepreneurs across 12 Indian states over the past eight years.

Sindhuja also lends to traders, shopkeepers, and farmers, and said it now operates 366 branches with about 11 billion rupees (US$116 million) in assets under management.

## 🧠 Food for thought

Implications, context, and why it matters.

### Existing backers stayed with Sindhuja through tougher markets

*   Sindhuja raised a US$5 million pre-series D round from current investors, which suggests they still back the company through a weaker economy 1.
*   The company has raised money in rough periods before, including its series A during India’s IL&FS (Infrastructure Leasing & Financial Services) crisis and its series B during the first phase of the COVID-19 pandemic 1.
*   This round is smaller than the US$14.5 million (1.2 billion rupees (US$12.6 million)) series C from March 2024, so it reads more like follow-on funding than a fresh step up in financing 2.

### Sindhuja’s board brings finance and technology depth

*   Sindhuja’s leadership combines financial services experience with senior technology know-how, which gives the company an edge as it grows 3.
*   The co-founders held leadership roles at Utkarsh Small Finance Bank, an Indian lender focused on underserved customers, where they built experience in scaling microfinance plus MSME (micro, small, and medium enterprises)/agri lending 3.
*   The board includes an independent director who was group CIO (Chief Information Officer) at Access Bank, a large African commercial bank, and president & CTO at Reliance Capital, an Indian financial services company 3.
*   That mix of finance and tech leadership fits Sindhuja’s focus on technology-enabled solutions and could help it build a model that reaches markets often left out by larger institutions 3.
