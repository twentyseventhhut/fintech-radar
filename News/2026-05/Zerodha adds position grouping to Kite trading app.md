Introducing position grouping on Kite app 
When you have just one or two trades, the positions page is easy to read. But once you have trades across different stocks, indices, and expiries, it becomes harder to make sense of everything in one view.
Instead of reading everything as one long list, you can now group your positions on the Kite app. This feature was earlier available only on Kite web and helps you view related positions together and track your P&L more clearly.
 
By default, all positions still appear in a single list with a combined P&L at the top. But now, you’ll also see a new “Group” option on the positions page.
Underlying
Selecting “Underlying” groups all positions of the same instrument together. For example, if you trade Nifty and Sensex F&O contracts across multiple expiries, each underlying will appear under its own group with a combined P&L beside it.
 
Underlying and Expiry
If you want a more detailed view, choose “Underlying and Expiry.” This groups positions by both instrument and expiry date, which is helpful when trading multiple expiries of the same stock or index.
Additionally, you can collapse or expand groups to focus only on the positions that matter.
 
We’ve also improved multi-selection. Earlier, long-pressing a position opened a separate screen. Now, you can select positions directly on the main page while your P&L continues updating in real time, making it easier to manage and exit multiple positions without losing context.
You can also exit positions group-wise. Just long-press a group name and tap on exit to exit all positions in that group at once.
 
Your grouping preference stays saved unless you log out, after which it resets to the default view. Filters, however, reset automatically at the end of each trading day.
Multi-selection improvement is also available for Orderbook, GTT, and Alerts, allowing you to quickly cancel multiple open orders or delete GTTs and Alerts.
