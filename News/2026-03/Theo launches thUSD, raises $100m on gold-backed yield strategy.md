Gold Tokenization News
Tokenization platform Theo has launched thUSD, a dollar-pegged stablecoin that generates yield through a gold futures trading strategy. The token is supported by a $100 million structured facility called the Genesis Vault, which closed at its cap within 24 hours of opening.
The capital in the facility goes toward buying tokenized gold. At the same time, Theo shorts gold futures on the CME to hedge against price swings. That combination, known as a cash-and-carry trade, captures the spread between spot and futures prices without carrying full exposure to gold's market value.
Theo recorded an average annual return of 8.27% in 2025 using this approach. Co-founder Ari Pingle said the firm targets returns between 5% and 12%, depending on gold market conditions. Chief Investment Officer Iggy Ioppe said the firm also plans to short gold futures on crypto-native platforms, including decentralized exchange Hyperliquid, which he estimates could push annualized yields toward 10%.
The stablecoin maintains its dollar peg through reserves of thGOLD, a token Theo launched in January, which is backed by secured lending agreements with gold retailers, including Singapore-based Mustafa Gold. Ioppe said retailers like Mustafa benefit from the setup because it allows them to hedge gold price exposure while continuing normal production.
The launch falls within a broader regulatory debate in Washington over stablecoin yield. The GENIUSAct bars issuers of payment stablecoins from distributing yield on reserves such as Treasury bills. Pingle said thUSD does not fall under that restriction because its returns come from the trading structure itself, not from the issuer passing on reserve income.
"Products structured around tokenized assets or separate financial primitives can generate yield differently," Pingle said. Lawmakers and banking groups remain divided over whether third parties should be allowed to offer yield on stablecoin holdings at all.
Theo's investors include Hack VC and Anthos Capital, as well as angel investors from Jane Street, Optiver, and JPMorgan. The firm's first product, thBIll, a tokenized money-market fund, launched last July. In DeFi settings, thUSD is designed to work with lending protocols like Morpho that support tokenized real-world assets.
Users must complete identity verification to access Theo's products. Once the token is on-chain, Ioppe said it functions permissionlessly within decentralized finance. The firm said thUSD is available to users in 200 countries.
