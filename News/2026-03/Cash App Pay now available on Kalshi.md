Cash App Pay Now Available on Kalshi 
Kalshi integrates Cash App Pay, becoming its first prediction market payment partner and giving customers more flexibility when trading 
NEW YORK, March 13, 2026 – Kalshi, the world’s largest regulated prediction market, today announced that Cash App Pay is now available as a payment option on its platform. This integration gives Kalshi and Cash App’s next generation customers a new way to fund their accounts when trading on real-world events.
Cash App serves more than 59 million monthly active customers, making it one of the most widely used financial apps in the U.S. ¹ This integration enables its customers to securely link their accounts to Kalshi, making their Cash App balance available for trading on Kalshi and creating a seamless path from funding to participation. Kalshi is widely credited with legalizing prediction markets and running a platform for millions of traders in America. ² 
“We’re thrilled to bring Cash App Pay’s payment experience to Kalshi’s users,” says Tanuj Parikh, Head of Revenue, North America, Cash App and Afterpay. “Their approach to prediction markets aligns with our commitment to providing reliable payment solutions across diverse use cases.”
“We started Kalshi to expand access to financial markets,” said Max Crowley, VP of Business Development. “This integration makes it easier for tens of millions of Cash App customers to participate in regulated prediction markets using a financial tool they already know and trust.”
Cash App Pay on Kalshi provides: 
 Quick account funding using Cash App balance 
 Streamlined checkout without repeatedly entering payment information 
 Secure payment funding through Cash App’s trusted payment infrastructure 
 Seamless integration with existing Kalshi trading workflows 
 About Kalshi 
Founded in 2018, Kalshi is the world’s largest prediction market. Kalshi believes prediction markets harness the power of the wisdom of the crowds, creating data that can effectively complement polling and expert opinion. Kalshi offers financial markets on the outcome of real world events such as elections, award shows, sports, and more. It’s widely credited with legalizing prediction markets and building a safe, legal, regulated platform for millions of traders in America.
