 FirstRand shells out almost R1.5bn to up its stake in Optasia to 26%
 
 FirstRand bought more than 74 million ordinary shares in Optasia at a price of R20 per share on 25 March. 
 That’s a slight premium to its previous closing price of R19.02 and works out to a little over R1.48 billion in total. 
 The additional stake was acquired from an entity owned by Optasia founder and non-executive director Bassim Haidar.

South Africa’s largest banking group, FirstRand, has upped its stake in Optasia to the extent that it now owns more than a quarter of the JSE-listed fintech platform.
FirstRand, which acquired a 20.1% stake in Optasia just before it listed last year, has bought an additional 6% stake from an entity owned by founder and non-executive director Bassim Haidar. A statement issued by Optasia on Thursday said FirstRand bought 74 103 711 ordinary shares in Optasia at a price of R20 per share on 25 March 2026, a slight premium to its closing price of R19.02 on that day.
That works out to a little over R1.48 billion and leaves Haidar with a remaining indirect aggregate shareholding of 1.5% of the total issued shares in Optasia. The fintech said Haidar remains on its board and will continue to actively support the company’s strategic direction.
“We are delighted that FirstRand has increased its investment in Optasia,” Salvador Anglada, Optasia’s CEO, said in the statement. “The exceptional performance reported for full-year 2025 demonstrates how well-positioned Optasia is to address the substantial market opportunity and advance its strategic pursuit of disciplined, responsible scaling to create long-term sustainable value for all stakeholders.”
Optasia reported a more than 57% jump in normalised net income to $57.8 million for the 12 months ended 31 December 2025, up from $36.8 million in the prior financial year. That would appear to have justified FirstRand’s initial R4.7 billion investment in Optasia in October last year, ahead of its JSE listing.
The fintech was founded in 2012 by Haidar, who was born in Nigeria, and uses AI and other technologies to evaluate customer creditworthiness to enable the provision of small, short-term loans and airtime credit through mobile and financial services providers. Former FNB CEO Michael Jordaan is the independent chairman of Optasia, having joined its board in April 2025.
