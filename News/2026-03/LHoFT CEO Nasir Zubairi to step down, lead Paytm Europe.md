After successfully positioning the Lhoft within Luxembourg’s financial ecosystem, Nasir Zubairi will now focus on launching the European strategy of Indian success story Paytm.   (Photo: Matic Zorman / Paperjam / Archives) 
After nearly a decade at the helm of the Lhoft, its CEO and founder, Nasir Zubairi, will step down in the summer of 2026 to take the helm at Paytm Europe, marking both a turning point for Luxembourg’s fintech ecosystem and the arrival of an ambitious Indian player on the European market.
After nearly a decade at the helm of the Lhoft, its CEO and founder, Nasir Zubairi, will step down in summer 2026 to take up the leadership of Paytm Europe, marking both a turning point for Luxembourg’s fintech ecosystem and the arrival of an ambitious Indian player on the European market.
The CEO and founder of the Luxembourg House of Financial Technology (Lhoft), Nasir Zubairi, will leave his position in summer 2026 after nine and a half years leading the institution. A transition process will begin in the coming months, with the launch of a selection procedure to ensure his succession.
“It has been an honor and a great source of pride to serve Luxembourg and the financial ecosystem in this role,” he said. “Helping to build the Lhoft from its inception and working alongside committed colleagues, partners, and public stakeholders has been one of the most rewarding chapters of my career. I am deeply grateful to the Ministry of Finance, the Lhoft Board of Directors, our strategic partners, and the Foundation’s team for their trust and collaboration over the years.”
During his tenure, the Lhoft supported the growth of hundreds of technology companies, strengthened skills across the financial industry, launched public-private initiatives to promote digital innovation, and contributed to enhancing Luxembourg’s international visibility as an innovation-driven financial center.
“The time has come, both for the Lhoft and for me personally, to open a new chapter,” added Nasir Zubairi.
The Minister of Finance, Gilles Roth, praised “an exceptional contribution to Luxembourg’s financial center.” “Under his leadership, the Lhoft has helped position Luxembourg as a recognized hub for fintech, digital transformation, and forward-looking financial services. I would like to express my sincere appreciation for his commitment, vision, and the remarkable work accomplished over the past nine years.”
Solutions for SMEsAt the end of his mandate, Nasir Zubairi will join Paytm, a leading digital payments player in India, as Chief Executive Officer of Paytm Europe Payments, a Luxembourg-based entity currently in the process of obtaining a license from the CSSF. He will be responsible for driving the development of the group’s European operations as part of its international expansion strategy.
Paytm plans to deploy in selected European markets its model—proven in India—of merchant payments and financial services distribution. The company, a pioneer in mobile payments, QR codes, and Soundbox devices, is targeting small and medium-sized enterprises by offering low-cost payment acceptance solutions and access to digital credit.
“We have built a strong model that enables small merchants to accept mobile payments at low cost and access digital credit. We believe it can work just as effectively for small and medium-sized businesses in Europe, starting with Luxembourg. Nasir’s experience in business and finance makes him the ideal choice to lead this expansion and drive financial inclusion in Europe,” said Vijay Shekhar Sharma, founder and CEO of Paytm.
Mr. Sharma is part of the “dream team” recently brought together by the Minister of Finance.
“I am excited about this new challenge,” concluded Nasir Zubairi. “Luxembourg has established itself as an open and international financial center, with strong expertise in financial services and innovation. It is a natural anchor point to build a European platform for a global payments company.”

Nasir Zubairi, founding Chief Executive of the Luxembourg House of Financial Technology ( LHoFT ), will step down this summer to become CEO of Paytm ’s newly established European entity.
According to the Luxembourg Times , Zubairi will leave LHoFT after nine and a half years, having helped build the institution from the ground up and establish Luxembourg as a recognised hub for fintech and digital financial services.
The board will begin a formal search for his successor in the coming months.
Zubairi will lead Paytm Europe Payments from Luxembourg, overseeing the company’s European operations.
Paytm, formally One97 Communications, serves around 450 million registered users and 45 million merchants in India and trades publicly with a market capitalisation of roughly US$9.3 billion.
The company incorporated its Luxembourg entity on 12 January as part of its European expansion.
Speaking on his appointment, Paytm founder Vijay Shekhar Sharma said:
 “We have built a strong business model that helps small merchants accept mobile payments at low cost and access digital credit. We believe it can work equally well for small and medium businesses in Europe, starting with Luxembourg.” 
Zubairi added:
 “Luxembourg has built a reputation as an open, international financial centre with strong expertise in financial services and innovation. It is a natural location from which to build a European platform for a global payments company.” 
To operate in Europe, Paytm Europe Payments will require authorisation from Luxembourg’s financial regulator, the CSSF.
The company is preparing for this process, including hiring a Chief Compliance Officer to establish a compliance framework, implement AML/CFT controls, and liaise directly with the regulator.
