*   **_The system combines conversational AI and human oversight to improve the user experience in the digital recruitment process._**
*   **_The first AI agent is already operational to assist customers who wish to take out a pre-approved consumer loan via the mobile application_**

*   **_CaixaBank will present the new service and its strategy for deploying AI agents at the Mobile World Congress_**

CaixaBank has launched a new Artificial Intelligence agent system designed to improve its customers' experience during digital onboarding processes via the bank's mobile app. The new service combines conversational AI and human support to optimise digital recruitment and represents a key step in the strategy to continue driving digital sales, in a context in which the CaixaBank app is consolidating its position as one of the main channels for customer relations, with more than 12 million users, the largest digital customer base in Spain. CaixaBank's AI Agents project will be presented next week at the Mobile World Congress in Barcelona.

The innovation is based on the implementation of AI agents that operate exclusively within the app's chat channel.The AI agent can hold a conversation with the customer to help them specify their needs, provide further information and resolve any queries that may arise. When it comes time to sign the contract, the AI agent refers the customer to a specialist at the organisation, ensuring that none of the information processed up to that point is lost.

The first AI recruitment support agent, which is already operational, is aimed at customers who have started the pre-approved loan application process through the app and are encountering difficulties in the procedure. In these cases, the agent helps them resolve any queries, simulate and define the ideal configuration for their loan, instalments and terms, and facilitate a smooth contracting process.

The AI recruitment assistance project, based on Salesforce's Agentforce technology, will evolve over the coming weeks until it is integrated into all recruitment chats on the bank's application, enabling them to obtain information efficiently, quickly and flexibly. The AI Agent system will be complemented by specialists in each type of product, from consumer loans to mortgages and savings products.

The application of Artificial Intelligence in the entity's app aims to improve the user experience for customers, who can obtain comprehensive information more quickly and easily. Customers can request to speak to a specialist at any time, and the tool will immediately put them in contact with a professional from the organisation.

**AI agent specialising in cards**

Last summer, CaixaBank launched a generative AI-based agent to help users of its mobile banking app inform customers about card-related issues. This feature, developed with Google Cloud's artificial intelligence technology, differs from the one presented today in that it is designed exclusively to inform customers, not to guide them through the hiring process.

The AI agent for cards has the ability to answer questions about the features of the cards available in the app's catalogue, compare the different options, and present the one that best suits the customer's needs. They can also hold a conversation with the AI agent to ask questions and obtain further information.

A few months earlier, CaixaBank had activated an AI agent to assist employees who provide support for remote recruitment. This AI agent, developed with Salesforce platform tools, was created specifically for a team of more than 300 professionals at the organisation who are dedicated to guiding customers who request assistance in searching for products through digital channels.

In addition to AI agents for customer service, CaixaBank is developing AI agents to improve the bank's internal processes.

**Commitment to generative AI**

The development of AI agents is part of CaixaBank's commitment to the application of generative AI included in the 2025-2027 Strategic Plan. In this regard, the organisation seeks to develop a technological strategy over the coming years based on four main objectives: increasing the agility and commercial capacity of its business areas; to develop new services thanks to cutting-edge capabilities and the simplification of processes; to promote operational excellence improving efficiency and, finally, to strengthen and develop the current technological platform with the highest resilience security standards.

Specifically, this includes plans to revamp existing channels and develop new commercial and customer service capabilities using generative artificial intelligence.

With experience since 2014, CaixaBank has stood out for being one of the pioneering banks in analysing the use of Artificial Intelligence applied to financial services. Artificial Intelligence is already being applied in multiple services and projects within the Group, with a cross-cutting and multidisciplinary approach that includes various perspectives, from technology to ethics.

**CaixaBank, leader in innovation**

Technology and innovation are key for CaixaBank, currently Spain's leading bank and one of the most important in Portugal. With the largest digital customer base in Spain's financial sector, exceeding 12 million, the institution is at the forefront of developing new models and systems to meet its customers' demands.

The organisation has its own technology subsidiary, CaixaBank Tech, and multidisciplinary teams to bring innovation to all areas of the organisation. Through cutting-edge technology, CaixaBank is driving forward an ambitious digital transformation plan. The aim is to put technology at the service of people, with services and solutions that facilitate more personalised advice, better commercial offerings and engagement, new financial services and a more agile decision-making model.

 CaixaBank Rolls Out AI Agent in App as Spanish Digital Banking Adoption Surges 
CaixaBank has launched an artificial intelligence (AI) agent inside its mobile app that assists customers in selecting and purchasing financial products, marking a notable advance in the use of generative AI in mainstream European retail banking.
 
 
The new AI assistant designed to interact conversationally with users helps guide customers through complex product choices and supports them in completing applications directly within the bank’s flagship digital channel. The system combines automated conversational responses with human oversight to ensure clarity and accuracy throughout the journey, particularly when customers reach the later stages of contractual commitment.
Operational within the app’s chat interface, the AI agent is already assisting users with processes such as pre-approved consumer credit applications and related queries. By interpreting user intent and providing tailored explanations of product features, the assistant streamlines decision-making and reduces friction in digital sales funnels — a key focus for banks as digital adoption climbs.
CaixaBank’s rollout comes at a time of rapid digital transformation across the Spanish banking sector. According to data from financial observers, more than 80% of internet users in Spain now actively use mobile or web banking, significantly above the European average of around 64%. This trend is expected to continue, with projections suggesting that almost 95% of customers could be banking digitally by 2030 .
CaixaBank itself has maintained a leading position in this shift. With over 12 million digital customers, the bank remains the largest digital banking provider in the country and consistently reports higher digital engagement than its peers. In June 2025 alone, CaixaBank customers executed more than 100 million mobile and wearable device payment transactions, a jump of more than 34% compared with the previous year, with nearly 5 million customers using mobile payment services. In addition, the bank has recently created a dedicated AI office to ensure responsible deployment of the smart innovations in its banking flows.

While many banks already offer chatbots for basic inquiries , CaixaBank’s implementation is distinguished by its integration into product discovery and onboarding paths, moving beyond informational assistance toward commercial enablement. Instead of merely explaining products, the AI agent helps customers compare options and move toward purchase decisions, then smoothly transitions to human support where appropriate.
This approach aligns with growing customer expectations for personalised, conversational interactions that mirror experiences found in mainstream AI platforms, such as large-language-model assistants, while remaining embedded in regulated banking environments.
The introduction of AI-powered guidance tools also raises questions about governance and transparency. Spanish customers broadly appreciate the convenience of digital channels, with surveys indicating that over 90% of users feel safer and more satisfied using mobile and web banking but there are ongoing concerns around data privacy and accuracy when it comes to automation.
CaixaBank has emphasised that the new agent operates within existing security and compliance frameworks, and that final contractual steps always involve human verification. This hybrid model aims to balance efficiency gains with consumer trust — a critical factor as regulators in Europe increasingly focus on AI governance in financial services.
CaixaBank plans to showcase its AI initiative at this year’s Mobile World Congress in Barcelona , signaling its intent to scale such technologies across product lines and customer segments. As digital channels continue to consolidate their role as the primary point of engagement between banks and customers in Spain, innovations like AI-driven purchasing assistants could help traditional institutions defend market share against digital challengers.
