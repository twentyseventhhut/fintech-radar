# Bank of Baroda error inflates home loan EMIs

A technical malfunction at Bank of Baroda has reportedly caused inflated Equated Monthly Instalments (EMIs) for several home loan borrowers, raising serious concerns over financial accuracy and credit reporting integrity.
According to multiple reports, the issue surfaced in March 2026 when customers noticed unusually high EMI deductions—significantly exceeding their scheduled repayment amounts. The discrepancy is believed to have stemmed from a system error within the bank’s loan processing infrastructure.
 Borrowers Face Double Impact: Financial Strain and Credit Damage 
The glitch has not only resulted in excess payments but has also potentially affected borrowers’ credit scores. Since credit bureaus rely heavily on repayment records, any mismatch—such as inflated dues or perceived underpayment—can adversely impact a borrower’s creditworthiness.
Financial institutions evaluate credit behaviour based on timely EMI payments, and even minor inconsistencies can lower scores or flag accounts as irregular.

 Bank Initiates Corrective Measures 
In response, the bank has acknowledged the issue and stated that remedial steps are underway. While specific details of compensation or recalibration mechanisms remain unclear, affected customers are expected to see corrections in their loan accounts.
However, the absence of immediate clarity on whether credit scores will be restored automatically has left borrowers anxious.
 Systemic Risks in Automated Lending Infrastructure 
The incident highlights a broader vulnerability in automated banking systems, where even minor technical errors can cascade into significant financial consequences. EMI calculations are based on standardised formulas involving principal, interest rate, and tenure, making accuracy critical to both lenders and borrowers.
Any deviation in these computations can distort repayment schedules and financial records, potentially leading to disputes and reputational damage.
 What Borrowers Should Do 
Experts advise affected customers to:
Immediately check loan statements and EMI deductions
Raise formal complaints with the bank
Monitor credit reports for discrepancies
File correction requests with credit bureaus if needed
Borrowers are also encouraged to maintain documentation of excess payments and communication with the bank for future claims or disputes.
 Regulatory and Consumer Protection Angle 
The episode may draw regulatory attention, particularly regarding accountability in digital banking systems. As India’s financial ecosystem becomes increasingly tech-driven, ensuring robust safeguards against system errors becomes essential.
