 Western Union, Sasai launch money transfer mobile app 
Western Union and Sasai Fintech have launched a new international money transfer mobile app tailored to South African consumers.
March 6, 2026
 Western Union and Sasai Fintech, a business of Cassava Technologies, have launched a new international money transfer mobile app tailored to South African consumers. 
 According to a joint statement, the app is designed to meet the needs of an increasingly connected and mobile population, offering a reliable way to send money abroad.  
 "South Africa is home to a growing population of digitally savvy consumers, many with deep ties across borders, that seek ways to move money internationally with flexibility and confidence," explained Western Union's Head of Africa Mohamed Touhami el Ouazzani. 
 He said the partnership with Sasai Fintech enables consumers in South Africa to connect with the world, unlocking opportunity and helping communities and families to move money in an increasingly globalized economy. 
 The collaboration combines Western Union's global network spanning 200 countries and territories with Sasai Fintech's Payments-as-a-Service (PaaS) technology – a remittance platform that includes both technology infrastructure and the required licensing. 
 Related: Western Union, Zoona, Chipper Cash launch money transfers in Zambia 
 The companies said customers can transfer funds to billions of bank accounts and wallets worldwide, or for cash pick-up at hundreds of thousands of retail locations abroad. 
 Users can send money via the money transfer service, with multiple funding options that include Sasai's retail network of over 150,000 outlets, debit or credit cards, and electronic funds transfers from their bank account.  
 "Our partnership with Western Union accelerates financial inclusion and delivers real value to Africans in the diaspora and their families across the continent," said Cassava Technologies' fintech and digital platforms CEO, Darlington Mandivenga.  
 He said the company was contributing to Africa's payments infrastructure via its integrated tech stack, super-app platform, and innovations like stablecoins .  
Fintech boom in Africa 
 There has been an influx of African banks and financial services companies either partnering with or investing in fintech in recent times.  
 In December 2025, South African bank Capitec entered into a binding agreement to acquire 100% of fintech innovator Walletdoc , subject to regulatory approvals.  
 South African financial services provider Nedbank Group in August 2025 entered a binding agreement to acquire fintech innovator iKhokha for 1.65 billion South African rand (US$94 million at the time). 
 In July 2025, Western Union, Zoona Transactions Zambia and Chipper Cash partnered to launch international money transfer services in the Chipper Cash app to enable customers in Zambia to send and receive money globally.

 The same month Airtel Money partnered with the Tanzania Cooperative Development Commission (TCDC) to expand digital financial services to thousands of farmers . 
 In March 2025, Mastercard partnered with Airtel Africa to launch the Airtel Money GlobalPay Card , enabling users to make international online transactions.

Western Union partners Sasai Fintech to expand digital remittances in Africa
 
 Global money transfer service Western Union and Sasai Fintech, a unit of Cassava Technologies, have launched an international money transfer mobile app targeting South African consumers, the companies said on Wednesday. 
 The app is intended to tap into growing demand for digital cross-border payments in one of Africa’s most connected markets, allowing users to send money abroad via smartphones. 
 “South Africa is home to a growing base of digitally savvy consumers with strong cross-border ties who are seeking flexible and reliable ways to move money internationally,” said Mohamed Touhami el Ouazzani, Head of Africa at Western Union. He said the partnership would extend the company’s global network to mobile-first customers and help families and communities stay connected across borders. 
 The collaboration combines Western Union’s network spanning more than 200 countries and territories with Sasai’s payments infrastructure, which includes licensing and a fully integrated remittance platform. 
 Through the co-branded app, customers can send funds to bank accounts and digital wallets worldwide or opt for cash collection at retail locations abroad. Funding options include debit and credit cards, electronic bank transfers and payments through Sasai’s retail network of more than 150,000 outlets. 
 Darlington Mandivenga, CEO of Fintech and Digital Platforms at Cassava Technologies, said the partnership would support financial inclusion and strengthen payment flows between Africans in the diaspora and recipients across the continent.
