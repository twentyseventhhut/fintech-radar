BlackFin Tech Weekly — March 9th, 2026
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity.
Hello FinTech Friends,
Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry!
 Over the last week, there were ten fintech deals in Europe, totaling €151.8m in disclosed funding, including five transactions in the UK, two in Germany, one in Italy, one in the Netherlands, and one in Spain. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Rent2Cash , an Italy-based fintech platform providing rental advance financing to landlords, has raised over €100m in a funding round led by an international investment fund. 
 Silverflow , a Netherlands-based cloud-native card payment processing platform, has raised $34.6m in a Series B round led by Picus Capital . 
 Wealthyhood , a London-based wealthtech app focused on helping younger investors build wealth, has raised €6m in a funding round led by Bank of Cyprus . 
 Let’s dive! 
 Rent2Cash 
 Rent2Cash , an Italy-based fintech providing rental advance solutions for property owners, raised €100m in a funding round led by an international investment fund specialized in structured finance and real estate. The company enables landlords to receive upfront payments based on future rental income, converting rental contracts into immediate liquidity while managing rent collection and repayment flows. The funding will support the scaling of its rental advance platform and expansion across Europe, starting with Spain, France, and Portugal.

 Silverflow 
 Silverflow , a Netherlands-based fintech providing cloud-native card payment processing infrastructure, raised €34.6m in a Series B round led by Picus Capital , with participation from Rabo Investments and existing investors Inkef , Global PayTech Ventures , Crane Venture Partners , and Coatue . The company offers a single API platform connecting payment providers directly to card networks, enabling PSPs, acquirers, and merchants to process transactions while accessing enriched payment data and real-time insights. The funding will support global expansion, product development, and team growth, while strengthening its presence in North America and Southeast Asia.

 Wealthyhood 
 Wealthyhood , a London-based wealthtech platform focused on helping younger investors build wealth, raised €6m in a funding round led by Bank of Cyprus , with participation from Genesis Ventures . The company provides a mobile investing app combining financial education, automated investing, and fractional share trading, alongside AI-powered insights and savings tools. The funding will support European expansion, enhancement of its AI capabilities, and development of its B2B offering for banks and financial institutions seeking to modernise digital investment services.

 In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 Flowpay , a Czech-based fintech providing short- and medium-term financing solutions for SMEs, has acquired Germany-based Tapline , a platform offering revenue-based financing to SaaS and technology companies. The acquisition strengthens Flowpay’s presence in Germany and the UK and expands its offering into financing for high-growth tech businesses. The deal will enable the integration of Tapline’s technology, expertise, and team into Flowpay’s platform, supporting its European expansion and positioning ahead of a new investment round.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 Revolut has applied for a US national bank charter with the OCC and FDIC , citing the Trump administration’s openness to new entrants. The move would allow the $75bn fintech to offer insured deposits and eliminate reliance on partner banks. Revolut has about one million US customers and plans to offer personal loans and credit cards. The application comes while Revolut still lacks a full UK banking licence, remaining in the mobilisation phase since 2024. In December, the OCC approved five licence applications including two for crypto firms Circle and Ripple . Last year saw 14 OCC applications from fintechs, almost matching the total of the preceding four years.

 A study published by the European Central Bank on 3 March 2026 warns that widespread stablecoin adoption in the euro zone could reduce monetary policy effectiveness, drain bank deposits and limit lending to the real economy. The ECB ‘s key concern is that most stablecoins are dollar-denominated, meaning foreign monetary conditions could be “imported” into the euro area, diluting the central bank’s control over financial conditions. While global stablecoin markets (around $300bn) remain small versus euro area deposits (around €17trn), the ECB calls for stronger regulation including transparency requirements, robust redemption guarantees and adequate capital buffers.

 Raisin has launched its B2C savings platform in Italy , targeting a market where households hold €1.27 trillion in deposits, with 90% sitting in low-yield overnight accounts despite the Eurozone’s high-rate environment. The platform launches with fixed-term deposits from partner banks in Germany , France and Belgium , protected under EU deposit guarantee schemes up to €100,000. Raisin ‘s cross-border distribution model gives Italian savers access to competitive rates via a single digital interface. With Italy , Raisin now operates in eleven markets, continuing its European expansion strategy focused on digital-first deposit aggregation and addressing limited pass-through of interest rates to retail savers.

 The European Union has shortlisted fund managers from Sweden , France and the UK to oversee its planned €5 billion Scaleup Europe Fund, targeting artificial intelligence, quantum computing and other deep tech investments, with a decision expected in April 2026. The candidates include Sweden ‘s EQT and Northzone , France ‘s Eurazeo , and UK -based Atomico and Vitruvian Partners . German insurer Allianz has signed on as a founding investor alongside the European Commission . The fund aims to raise €25 billion and will focus on strategic technologies, requiring recipients to maintain headquarters in Europe. The move addresses concerns over tech losses such as Silo AI to AMD and Oxford Ionics to IonQ .
