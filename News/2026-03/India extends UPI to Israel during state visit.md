India Extends UPI To Israel, Expands Digital Payments Partnership
 # India 
 # UPI 
 # Digital Payments Partnership 
 # Israel 
India has officially extended its Unified Payments Interface (UPI) to Israel, marking a significant step in digital finance collaboration and signalling a broader push to deepen technology-driven bilateral ties. The announcement was made during Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s two-day state visit to Israel, where he held talks with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu on strategic, technological and defence cooperation.
Addressing a joint press briefing, Modi said the two countries have agreed to strengthen cooperation in critical and emerging technologies, with UPI integration forming a key component of the partnership. “We have decided to establish a critical and emerging technology partnership. I am happy that an agreement has been signed for the use of UPI in Israel. We will further our work in civil nuclear energy and space fields. Together, we will develop future-ready farming solutions. We will work on creating villages of excellence,” he said.
The adoption of UPI in Israel is expected to enable seamless digital payments for businesses and consumers across both countries, expanding India’s fintech footprint globally while supporting trade, tourism and investment flows between the two economies.
 Broader Strategic Cooperation The joint statement outlined multiple areas of collaboration beyond digital payments, reflecting the widening scope of India-Israel relations. These include joint development and manufacturing of defence equipment, efforts to finalise a bilateral trade agreement in the near term, and expansion of cooperation under the I2U2 framework for regional development initiatives.
Both sides also agreed to deepen partnerships in civil nuclear energy, space exploration and emerging technologies, underscoring a shared focus on innovation-led growth.
Modi emphasised the countries’ shared security outlook, stating that terrorism cannot be accepted in any form and reaffirming continued cooperation against terrorism and its supporters. He added that both nations would move forward together on initiatives such as the India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor (IMEC) and I2U2, while describing bilateral ties as a “special strategic partnership” reflecting the aspirations of both countries.
Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu welcomed the strengthening of cooperation and praised the efficiency of government-level engagement between the two nations. Highlighting the growing alignment, he said the “meeting of minds and hearts” between India and Israel would further boost collaboration and unlock greater mutual benefits across sectors.
