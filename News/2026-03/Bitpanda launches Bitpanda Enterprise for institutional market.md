Crypto broker Bitpanda bets on banks and tokenization to expand globally ahead of IPO plans
The Vienna-based crypto broker eyes emerging markets, teaming up with institutions rather than competing with local exchanges, said Vishal Sacheendran, VP of global markets strategy and operations.
Bitpanda is expanding globally by bringing crypto rails to banks and institutions, said Vishal Sacheendran, VP of global markets strategy and operations.
The firm launched Bitpanda Enterprise, a platform offering crypto infrastructure, custody and tokenization services for banks and fintechs.
Emerging markets in Asia, Latin America and the Middle East are a focus for expansion, where regulators are increasingly open to crypto adoption, Sacheendran said.
Vienna, Austria-based crypto broker Bitpanda is leaning into a strategy it has been quietly building for years: keep its retail business anchored in Europe while expanding globally by supplying crypto infrastructure to banks and financial firms.
The company’s next phase of growth will focus less on raw user numbers and more on geographic reach, Vishal Sacheendran, vice president of global markets strategy and operations, told CoinDesk in an interview.
"It’s about having a footprint in more markets," Sacheendran said.
That expansion is building on its steady growth. The company, which boasts more than 7 million users, reported this week €371 million ($430 million) in adjusted revenue for 2025, up 16% from the previous year, while its registered user base increased 25% to 7.4 million.
The firm is also weighing a public listing. Bitpanda is reportedly preparing for a potential IPO on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange as early as the first half of 2026, targeting a valuation between EUR 4 billion and EUR 5 billion. The plan comes as multiple crypto exchanges and infrastructure firms have either gone public or are planning to do so. 
Bringing crypto to banks
The exchange spent the past decade largely focused on the European Union, where its app allows retail users to trade cryptocurrencies and other assets. But outside Europe, Sacheendran said the strategy needs to change. In some markets — especially smaller ones or those already dominated by global exchanges — launching a consumer app may not make sense.
Instead, Bitpanda wants to work through banks and financial institutions that already have distribution. "We don’t want to compete with exchanges everywhere," he said. "There’s a big segment of the market that still trusts banks."
The company formalized that approach earlier in March with the launch of Bitpanda Enterprise, a new institutional offering that packages the firm’s infrastructure for banks, brokers, asset managers, fintechs and corporate clients.
The unit builds on Bitpanda’s existing B2B business, previously known as Bitpanda Technology Solutions, and bundles several services into a single platform. These include API-based investment infrastructure for financial brands, institutional-grade custody, trading liquidity and settlement tools, and payment rails for crypto and stablecoins. The platform also includes token infrastructure for stablecoin issuance and systems designed to support tokenized assets.
UAE launchpad
One early example of that model came in July, when RAKBANK, one of the United Arab Emirates' oldest lenders, launched crypto trading for retail customers through a partnership with Bitpanda. Instead of building its own infrastructure, the bank plugged into Bitpanda’s platform.
Sacheendran said deals like that often open doors elsewhere. Once one major bank adopts crypto services, others tend to follow. "When a top-tier bank starts offering it, the rest of the market takes notice," he said.
Bitpanda’s pitch to institutional partners rests heavily on its regulatory positioning. The company has been operating under strict licensing requirements, including the European Union’s MiCA framework, widely seen as one of the most comprehensive crypto regulatory regimes.
Regulatory moat
That regulatory credibility travels, Sacheendran said, especially in emerging markets where regulators are still shaping their approach to digital assets. In many of those regions — including parts of Asia, Latin America and the Middle East — authorities are eager to develop the sector but want partners that already operate within strong compliance frameworks.
Asia-Pacific illustrates the complexity. The region is "very fragmented," he said, with different rules in jurisdictions such as Hong Kong, Singapore, Japan and South Korea. Bitpanda’s approach there will be gradual: start small, test demand and scale where the regulatory and commercial conditions align.
On the product side, Bitpanda is evaluating derivatives trading, though Sacheendran noted that regulations differ widely across jurisdictions. He also expects tokenization to become a bigger theme in the coming years, particularly for assets such as bonds, money market funds and real estate.
Those markets could benefit from blockchain’s ability to enable around-the-clock trading and broader investor access, he said.
One area Bitpanda is unlikely to enter directly is stablecoin issuance. "We don’t build a stablecoin," Sacheendran said, noting that the company prefers to provide infrastructure and operational support for institutions that want to launch their own.

Bitpanda has launched Bitpanda Enterprise, a new institutional platform designed to provide financial institutions with integrated access to digital asset infrastructure. The offering consolidates several services including custody, liquidity, payments, tokenization and stablecoin management within a single technology environment.

The new platform represents an evolution of Bitpanda’s previous business-to-business infrastructure offering, which operated under the name Bitpanda Technology Solutions.

Through the new structure, Bitpanda Enterprise functions as the institutional infrastructure division of the company, providing technology used by banks, brokers, fintech companies and asset managers seeking to integrate digital assets into their services.

Financial institutions have increasingly explored digital asset infrastructure as part of broader efforts to incorporate cryptocurrency and tokenized assets into financial products and services.

## Shift from Investment-as-a-Service to Institutional Infrastructure

The launch of Bitpanda Enterprise reflects a shift in the company’s institutional strategy from individual technology components toward a broader infrastructure platform.

Bitpanda previously provided digital asset infrastructure through its Investment-as-a-Service model, which allowed financial brands to offer cryptocurrency trading to retail clients through application programming interfaces.

The new platform expands this approach by combining multiple infrastructure components within a single system.

This structure is designed to allow financial institutions to access custody, trading and payment functions without integrating separate vendors.

Institutions exploring digital asset services often face fragmented infrastructure environments in which custody, liquidity, payments and tokenization capabilities are provided by different providers.

The company said the new platform is designed to address that fragmentation by offering a unified technology stack.

Lukas Enzersdorfer-Konrad, chief executive of Bitpanda, said the institutional market requires integrated digital asset infrastructure rather than multiple independent tools.

Enzersdorfer-Konrad commented, “The institutional market doesn’t need more fragmented tools. It needs a comprehensive, integrated technology stack.”

## Modular Infrastructure Components

The Bitpanda Enterprise platform combines several infrastructure components designed for institutional use.

These components include application programming interfaces that support digital asset trading services for financial brands.

The platform also includes custody services designed to store digital assets for institutional clients.

Custody infrastructure is a central element of digital asset markets because financial institutions often require regulated storage solutions for cryptocurrency holdings.

The platform also includes trading infrastructure designed to provide access to liquidity and standardized execution processes.

Execution services are intended to support trading firms and brokers handling large transaction volumes.

Post trade settlement functions are also included within the trading infrastructure.

The company said the platform includes payment systems designed to support transactions involving cryptocurrencies and stablecoins.

These payment capabilities are intended for both business-to-business and business-to-consumer transaction environments.

## Stablecoin and Tokenization Capabilities

Another component of the platform includes infrastructure designed for stablecoin issuance and management.

The system includes a token management environment that allows organizations to issue and manage their own digital tokens.

Stablecoins are digital assets designed to maintain a stable value relative to reference assets such as fiat currencies.

Financial institutions have explored stablecoins as potential tools for payments and settlement processes.

The platform also includes technology designed to support asset tokenization.

Tokenization refers to the process of representing financial assets on blockchain networks.

Tokenized assets can include financial instruments such as securities, funds or other asset classes.

Institutions exploring tokenization often require infrastructure capable of managing the lifecycle of digital assets on blockchain networks.

The Bitpanda Enterprise platform includes infrastructure designed to manage these tokenized asset processes.

## Institutional Demand for Digital Asset Infrastructure

Financial institutions across several regions have increased their focus on digital asset services in recent years.

Banks, asset managers and trading firms have explored ways to provide cryptocurrency trading, custody and tokenized asset products to clients.

Building internal infrastructure for these services can require significant investment and regulatory expertise.

Technology providers have therefore developed platforms that allow institutions to access digital asset capabilities through external infrastructure providers.

Bitpanda Enterprise aims to provide such infrastructure through a modular system that can be integrated into existing financial platforms.

The company said the system is designed to support institutions that want to add digital asset services without developing their own infrastructure.

## Institutional Partnerships

Bitpanda Enterprise provides digital asset infrastructure used by financial institutions operating across multiple regions.

The platform currently supports digital asset services for end users in the European Union, the United Kingdom, the United Arab Emirates and Latin America.

The company said the institutional infrastructure is used by a range of partners across banking, fintech and asset management sectors.

These partnerships include collaborations with financial institutions and trading infrastructure providers.

Institutions working with the platform use its infrastructure to provide digital asset access to clients while operating within regulatory frameworks in their respective jurisdictions.

By combining trading infrastructure, custody services and payment systems, the company said Bitpanda Enterprise is intended to provide institutions with a unified environment for operating in digital asset markets.

**Takeaway**

Bitpanda Enterprise represents an effort to consolidate several elements of digital asset infrastructure into a single institutional platform. Financial institutions exploring cryptocurrency trading, custody and tokenized assets often rely on multiple specialized providers to operate these services. By combining investment infrastructure, liquidity access, custody systems, payment rails, stablecoin management and tokenization tools within a single environment, the platform reflects a broader trend toward integrated digital asset infrastructure designed for institutional participation in emerging financial markets.
