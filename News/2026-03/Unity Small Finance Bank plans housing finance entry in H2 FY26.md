Unity Small Finance Bank plans entry into housing finance market amid broader strategic shifts
Unity Small Finance Bank is preparing to enter the housing finance sector in the latter half of the upcoming fiscal year as part of a broader strategic repositioning following its unsuccessful bid to acquire Aviom India Housing Finance under the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT)-monitored resolution process. The bank's executive chairman said that Unity SFB would prioritise strategic options rather than pursue acquisitions at any cost, signalling a cautious approach to expansion. Internal preparations are ongoing, with a focus on affordable housing finance and micro-loan products such as micro home loans and micro-LAP, rather than prime city lending. Unity SFB also aims to leverage its strong capital adequacy and explore organic growth opportunities, while planning for a potential public listing in approximately two to two-and-a-half years. Partnerships in digital products and diversified lending portfolios form part of the bank's evolving strategy. 
Representative image
Unity Small Finance Bank, majority-owned by Centrum Group with a substantial minority stake held by fintech partners, has confirmed plans to enter the housing finance business later in the upcoming fiscal year, even though its bid to acquire Aviom India Housing Finance was not successful. The bank's leadership has emphasised that it intends to keep strategic options open rather than pursue acquisitions at any cost, signalling a shift towards measured growth.

Unity Small Finance Bank Emerges Victorious in ₹977.5 Crore Battle for Aviom India Housing Finance
 2025-12-24
 1547
 0

 In a landmark development within India's housing finance sector, Unity Small Finance Bank has reportedly secured the acquisition of Aviom India Housing Finance through a highly competitive three-way challenge auction. The bank's winning bid of ₹977.5 crore, offered as an upfront payment, has positioned it to make a strategic entry into the affordable mortgage lending segment.
The transaction, conducted under the insolvency resolution process supervised by the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) , marks one of the most significant recoveries witnessed in a distressed housing finance case in recent times.
Competitive Auction Results and Final Standings
According to sources, Unity SFB's offer emerged as the frontrunner after an intense 13 rounds of bidding, outpacing competitors by a significant margin.
Strategic Implications of the Acquisition
The acquisition carries substantial strategic significance for multiple stakeholders. For lenders, Unity's winning bid reportedly offers creditors approximately 71 per cent recovery on admitted claims, which industry experts consider a remarkable outcome for a financial institution undergoing insolvency proceedings.
The deal is expected to provide Unity SFB with direct access to the mortgage and housing finance sector , a segment where the bank has had limited presence until now. Currently, Unity SFB's operations have been primarily focused on digital banking and MSME lending services.
Notably, Unity Small Finance Bank is backed by Centrum Group, which holds a 51 per cent stake, and Resilient Innovations, the parent company of BharatPe, controlling the remaining 49 per cent. This strong promoter backing is expected to enhance the bank's capacity to scale the acquired housing finance business effectively.
Transaction Timeline and Regulatory Approvals
Industry observers suggest that the deal is expected to be fully formalised by the first quarter of FY26, subject to necessary regulatory approvals from the Reserve Bank of India and final NCLT sanction.
Background of Aviom's Insolvency Proceedings
Aviom India Housing Finance was admitted into insolvency in February 2025 following defaults on its financial obligations. The case was reportedly initiated through a referral from the RBI under the provisions of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). Since then, the company has been operating under the supervision of an appointed administrator.

Market Implications and Industry Outlook
This acquisition represents more than just a distressed asset recovery; it serves as a strategic gateway for Unity SFB into the affordable housing finance sector . This segment has witnessed substantial growth potential, bolstered by government housing initiatives under PMAY and strong demand from underserved borrower segments.
The transaction also reflects renewed investor appetite for housing finance companies undergoing insolvency, particularly where the underlying loan book and asset quality demonstrate revival potential. Similar to how Max Estates took over delayed projects after NCLT approval , this deal showcases the effectiveness of the IBC framework in facilitating distressed asset resolution.
The banking sector's growing interest in real estate continues to shape investment patterns, with institutions increasingly viewing housing finance as a high-growth opportunity.
Deal Summary at a Glance
Conclusion
Unity Small Finance Bank's ₹977.5 crore acquisition of Aviom India Housing Finance represents a transformative milestone in its growth trajectory. The deal establishes a significant precedent in India's housing finance insolvency landscape and demonstrates the viability of resolution mechanisms under the IBC framework.
Once completed, the transaction is expected to deliver substantial recovery to lenders while fundamentally reshaping Unity SFB's presence in the lending ecosystem, particularly within the affordable housing segment. For prospective homebuyers, this development could translate into expanded financing options as the bank leverages its new capabilities to serve the growing housing finance market .
