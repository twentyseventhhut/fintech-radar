# PayU, CoRover.ai to Enable AI Voice Payments in 100+ Indian Languages

PayU and CoRover.ai will work across e-commerce, travel, OTT services, and banking and financial services sectors.

Merin Susan John

MARCH 11, 2026, 9:33 AM

Key Takeaways

### What Actually Matters.

PayU partners with CoRover.ai to enable voice-based digital payments in over 100 Indian languages.

Integration allows users to make transactions via AI assistants in e-commerce, travel, and banking sectors.

More

Bengaluru-based fintech platform PayU has partnered with CoRover.ai, the creator of BharatGPT, to enable agentic, voice-based digital payments across more than 100 Indian languages and dialects.

The collaboration aims to integrate PayU’s payment infrastructure with CoRover’s conversational AI platform to create multilingual, voice-first payment experiences. The companies said the partnership will allow AI agents to initiate and complete transactions directly within chat or voice interactions.

The companies say the goal is to simplify digital commerce for the next 500 million users in the country, many of whom primarily interact with technology through voice and regional languages.

# Google Cloud, Brahma AI Team Up to Scale ‘Digital Humans’ for Global Enterprises

These digital humans replicate the likeness of real individuals, enabling firms to communicate across languages and geographies while maintaining the authenticity of face-to-face interactions.

Pallavi Chakravorty

MARCH 11, 2026, 8:51 AM

Before Article Content · 728×90 Advertise Here

Listen 5 min

SHARE Save

FollowPreferred SourceGoogle NewsWhatsAppTelegram

Key Takeaways

### What Actually Matters.

Google Cloud partners with Brahma AI to create high-fidelity digital humans for enterprises.

Developed digital identities, called 'ATMANS', enhance global communication and authenticity for organizations.

More

Google Cloudhas entered a multi-year strategic partnership with Brahma AI to develop and deploy high-fidelity digital humans, designed to transform how organisations communicate with global audiences.

Announced on March 10, the collaboration aims to enable enterprises to convert complex audiovisual data into interactive, human-centric experiences through hyper-realistic digital identities known as ‘ATMANS’. These digital humans replicate the likeness and persona of real individuals, enabling organisations to communicate across languages and geographies while maintaining the authenticity of face-to-face interactions.

Under the partnership, Brahma AI will build its platform natively on Google Cloud’sinfrastructure while integrating its advanced generative AI technologies, including the Gemini multimodal model and the Veo video generation system. The combination is designed to produce what the companies describe as ‘movie-quality’ interactive digital humans at enterprise scale.
