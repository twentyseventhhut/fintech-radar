Cambodia and Singapore Complete Full Interoperability of Cross-Border QR Payment Linkage

 
 Paid Content
 

 

 
 
 March 30, 2026 ,
 
 3:12 PM 
 
 
 PHNOM PENH— The National Bank of Cambodia (NBC) and Liquid Group officially marked the Phase 2 launch of the cross-border QR payment linkage between Cambodia and Singapore under the support of two sponsoring banks in Cambodia—ACLEDA Bank and Phillip Bank—at the Raffles Hotel Le Royal. 
 
 
 
The launch of Phase 2 follows the successful rollout of Phase 1 in November 2025, which allowed Cambodian travelers to make QR code payments via Liquid Group’s RoamQR network in Singapore using Bakong-linked Khmer riel accounts.
Presided over by Chea Serey, Governor of the NBC, the ceremony marked the full operationalization of a digital corridor that allows travelers to “pay like a local” in both nations.
 
Under the banner of “Scan Local, Pay Global," Phase 2 enables Singaporean visitors to seamlessly experience Cambodia like a local by scanning KHQR codes at over 4.5 million merchant touchpoints across the Kingdom. Travelers can pay using their home mobile payment apps through the cross-border QR payment linkage, with initial participating apps available at launch and additional banks and wallet partners across both markets being progressively enabled.
The service allows users to view transparent foreign exchange rates before confirming payment, ensuring clarity and confidence in every transaction. Payments are completed instantly without the need for cash exchange, with transactions automatically processed in Singapore dollars (SGD) and settled in Khmer riel (KHR), delivering a seamless and familiar payment experience.
Jeremy Tan, Chief Executive Officer of Liquid Group, said, “With the completion of Phase 2, Cambodia and Singapore have established a fully operational bilateral cross-border QR payment linkage. Travelers can now explore Cambodia without the need for cash—simply by scanning KHQR codes using their home mobile payment apps. This milestone reflects close collaboration between our payment ecosystems and supports the continued adoption of seamless cross-border payments as part of everyday travel.”
 
In Channy, President and Group Managing Director of ACLEDA Bank Plc., expressed profound honor at ACLEDA's appointment by the National Bank of Cambodia (NBC) as the sponsoring bank for a landmark cross-border QR payment initiative with Singapore, spearheaded by the NBC. He highlighted that in Phase 1, ACLEDA has successfully implemented cross-border QR payments, enabling Cambodian users to scan Singapore SGQR codes at RoamQR merchants across Singapore.
 
This advancement promotes the use of the Khmer Riel across ASEAN, fosters financial inclusion, and advances integration within the ASEAN Economic Community. It builds upon ACLEDA's established successes in cross-border QR interoperability with other ASEAN nations, involving key partners such as ACLEDA Bank (Cambodia) and Liquid Group (Singapore) supported by intermediary banks such as United Overseas Bank Limited and DBS Bank Ltd. in Singapore. The initiative establishes a robust, interoperable KHQR-SGQR infrastructure under the supervision of the NBC.
For today, the Phase 2 launch will empower Singapore users to scan Cambodian KHQR codes directly through their mobile banking applications. This will streamline payments for Singaporean travelers purchasing goods and services in Cambodia, thereby enhancing convenience, security, tourism, business efficiency, the strength of the local currency, transaction speed, and overall cost savings.
Mach Chan, Chief Executive Officer of Phillip Bank Plc., said, “Phillip Bank is honored to be part of this journey connecting people, businesses, and economies across our two homes—Singapore, where our group was founded, and Cambodia, where we have grown.”
 
 Building ASEAN Connectivity and Empowering MSMEs 
The launch underscores a greater shift toward frictionless and cashless international travel and commerce. By scanning ubiquitous local QR codes, Singaporean tourists and business travelers can settle transactions instantly in Cambodia without the need for physical currency exchange.
Beyond convenience, the linkage serves as a catalyst for broader regional integration. The project is a primary building block for advancing the ASEAN Regional Payment Connectivity framework, promoting a unified digital economy. The expected seamless flow of funds will also boost bilateral trade and tourism, providing a secure, transparent, and real-time payment alternative to cash.
Additionally, by allowing Cambodian shop owners, especially small businesses, to accept international digital payments using KHQR codes, the system helps more people access financial services and lowers costs for local sellers.
 About KHQR 
KHQR is the standardized QR code system launched by the National Bank of Cambodia (NBC) in 2022 to unify mobile retail payments across Cambodia. It allows customers to use any participating bank or wallet app to scan a single QR code for payments in Khmer Riel or US dollars. It enhances financial inclusion and enables seamless, fast, secure, and cross-border transactions for merchants and consumers.
 About RoamQR 
RoamQR™ is an interoperable QR payment network, developed and operated by Liquid Group. It connects banks, wallets, non-bank financial institutions, and acquirers, enabling seamless domestic and cross-border QR payments.
As the network expands beyond Singapore, RoamQR™ acts as a network linking national QR systems such as KHQR and others across the region, enabling users to scan local and pay global.
 About Liquid Group 
Liquid Group is a Singapore-based fintech company specializing in cross-border digital payments and digital trade infrastructure. Through its involvement in the RoamQR network and partnerships in the region, Liquid Group makes it easy and safe for people to make payments across Asia, linking different national payment systems and helping to boost international trade and travel.
 About ACLEDA Bank 
ACLEDA Bank Plc. is a leading commercial bank in Cambodia and the first to list on the Cambodia Securities Exchange (CSX). ACLEDA Bank has four subsidiaries, including ACLEDA University of Business Co., Ltd., ACLEDA Securities Plc., ACLEDA Bank Lao Ltd., and ACLEDA MFI Myanmar Co., Ltd. and a representative office in the Republic of the Union of Myanmar. As of the end of September 2025, ACLEDA Bank had USD 11.94 billion in total assets and USD 9.28 billion in total savings deposits, with more than 6.18 million customers.
 About Phillip Bank 
Phillip Bank is a member of Phillip Capital Group, a Singapore-based financial institution established in 1975. As part of a network spanning more than 15 countries, the Group offers a full spectrum of quality and innovative financial services to retail, corporate, and institutional clients worldwide. In Cambodia, Phillip Bank stands as a medium-sized commercial bank, operating over 70 branches nationwide. Together with its sister companies, Phillip Trustee, Phillip General Insurance, and Phillip Life Assurance, Phillip Bank connects Cambodia’s financial landscape to the world.
