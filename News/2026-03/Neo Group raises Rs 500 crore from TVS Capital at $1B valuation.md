Neo Group enters unicorn club with Rs 500 crore investment from TVS Capital
 
 Follow Us
Mumbai-based wealth and asset management firm Neo Group has raised Rs 500 crore (about $53 million) from private equity investor TVS Capital and its affiliates, in a deal that values the company at Rs 10,000 crore ($1.08 billion).
The funding round formally places Neo Group among India’s growing list of unicorns, privately held companies that are valued at over $1 billion.
The company said the fresh capital would be used to accelerate its expansion and strengthen its offering of “knowledge-driven solutions” for clients.
TVS Capital described the investment as a bet on the evolving wealth management landscape in India.
“India’s wealth management industry is at a structural inflection point. Neo combines the two hardest things to build — trust and talent — into a high-quality, institutional franchise. We’re excited to partner with Neo’s team to build a category-defining platform,” said Suraj Majee, principal at TVS Capital.
The deal also marks TVS Capital’s first investment in the wealth management sector. The private equity firm, which is currently raising its fourth fund, has previously backed financial services and fintech companies including PhonePe, InsuranceDekho and Five-Star Business Finance.
Neo Group’s chairman and managing director, Nitin Jain, said the latest round underscored investor confidence in the business.
“Our objective has always been to build an enduring institution with strong governance, disciplined capital allocation and client-first alignment. The continued participation of high-quality investors reinforces confidence in our platform and our long-term strategy,” he said.
Founded in 2021 by Nitin Jain, a former executive at Edelweiss Wealth Management, Neo Group provides advisory and yield-based investment products to high-net-worth and ultra-high-net-worth individuals, as well as family offices. The firm says it manages close to Rs 1 trillion in client assets.
The company has raised funds multiple times over the past year. In February 2025, it secured $20 million from investors including MUFG and Peak XV Partners at a valuation of about $640 million. This was followed by a $19 million round in August and a further $25 million investment in November led by Crystal Investment Advisors.
Neo’s asset management arm has also been expanding. It announced the first close of a Rs 2,000 crore private equity secondaries fund at Rs 750 crore last year, with plans to invest in 12 to 15 companies across sectors such as financial services, healthcare and technology.
