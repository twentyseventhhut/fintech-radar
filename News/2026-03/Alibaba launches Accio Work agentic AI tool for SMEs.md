Accelerating Agentic Business:   A proactive enterprise-grade AI Agent team built for secure global operations 
 NEW YORK , March 23, 2026 /PRNewswire/ -- Alibaba International unveiled Accio Work, a plug-and-play enterprise AI agent that equips businesses with an immediate, no-code taskforce. Requiring zero setup, the platform deploys specialized agents to execute complex, long-horizon operations from day one, signaling a significant shift toward "Agentic Business"—a new era where AI moves beyond passive Q&A tools to become active, autonomous executors.

 First introduced in November 2024 as an AI-powered B2B sourcing engine, Accio has since rapidly evolved into a full-scale AI agent, now serving over 10 million monthly active users globally. The launch of Accio Work marks a new phase in this evolution—moving beyond sourcing to a full-scale enterprise AI agent designed to support the diverse operational needs of SMEs worldwide.
"Our vision is to democratize enterprise-grade AI," said Kuo Zhang, President of Alibaba.com and VP of Alibaba International. "We want every entrepreneur—regardless of team size—to access an intelligent workforce that operates with the scale of a major corporation. Small businesses will find Accio Work especially useful."
 A Proactive, Ready-to-Use "Agent Team" 
For non-technical entrepreneurs, building a custom AI workforce usually means navigating complex engineering and risky deployments. Accio Work removes these barriers with a pre-configured team of agents designed for the entire SME lifecycle—from initial market analysis, design, and sourcing to long-term store optimization and inventory monitoring.
Its secret lies in dynamic orchestration: upon receiving a goal, the system instantly assembles a cross-functional "squad" of specialized agents - analysts, creators, logistics experts - to work in parallel.
Beyond handling long-term operations, the agent team proactively provides strategic insights and suggestions. This enables SMEs and solo founders to run end-to-end global commerce workflows with enterprise-grade precision, requiring no code or setup.
 Autonomous Execution and Global Connectivity 
Accio Work enables entrepreneurs to run online businesses or physical stores with a new level of automation, specifically designed to alleviate the heavy operational burden faced by SMEs. Users can deploy a customizable fleet of AI agents to handle critical tasks, including:
 Automated Compliance : Managing real-time VAT filings, tax refunds, and customs documentation across more than 100 markets.
 Autonomous Sourcing : Executing Request for Quotations (RFQs) and conducting multi-round negotiations with suppliers to secure optimal terms.
 Operational Integration : Driving marketing automation and overseeing logistics through tools like Telegram and WhatsApp.
Additionally, Accio Work allows users to encapsulate their unique processes into reusable "skills," enabling them to standardize, share, or even monetize their expertise.
 Alibaba Ecosystem-Driven Reliability and Security 
Built on Alibaba Group's proprietary ecosystem, Accio Work is structurally engineered to minimize AI hallucinations. Unlike models reliant on general knowledge prone to inaccuracies, Accio Work draws directly from real-time consumer trends and actual business transaction records across Alibaba's e-commerce platforms. This foundation ensures every output is specific, accurate, and commercially relevant.
Recognizing the sensitivity of delegating critical tasks to AI, Accio Work employs a security-first approach featuring sandboxed environments and granular permission management. High stakes actions involving finances or file access require explicit user approval, ensuring the AI operates strictly within defined boundaries. Furthermore, the system respects data sovereignty, allowing users to choose not to save any data on servers.
Accio Work will be available on www.Accio.com by the end of March.

Alibaba International Digital Commerce Group (Alibaba International) is dedicated to supporting the development of global digital trade with AI-powered technology. It operates various platforms with distinctive business models, covering multiple countries and regions around the world.

The New Frontier: Agentic AI in Global Commerce
The landscape of enterprise technology is undergoing a seismic shift. As generative AI transitions from simple text generation and basic chatbots to complex, task-oriented execution, the industry is witnessing the dawn of the "Agentic AI" era. At the forefront of this evolution is Alibaba International Digital Commerce Group, which has officially launched Accio Work , a specialized enterprise AI platform designed to empower small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) with a new tier of digital labor.
For businesses operating in the competitive arena of cross-border e-commerce , the barrier to entry has historically been high. Managing logistics, navigating international customs, optimizing marketing spend, and delivering localized customer service often require significant headcount and operational expertise. Accio Work aims to dissolve these barriers by deploying "virtual employee squads"—autonomous AI agents programmed to handle distinct, complex operational tasks. This development signals a critical move by Alibaba International to integrate advanced Enterprise AI deep into the workflow of global merchants.
Deconstructing Accio Work: The Virtual Employee Squad Model
Unlike traditional software-as-a-service (SaaS) platforms that provide tools for humans to operate, Accio Work functions as a delegated operational system. It leverages the agentic nature of modern AI to not just assist, but to execute.
The platform is structured around the concept of " virtual employee squads ." These are not singular, monolithic AI models. Instead, they represent a collection of specialized agents, each fine-tuned for specific e-commerce functions. When an SME onboards with Accio Work, they are essentially deploying a digital workforce capable of running the business around the clock.
The core functionality encompasses several high-stakes areas:
 Market Analysis & Product Selection: Agents analyze global consumer trends to identify high-potential products, reducing the reliance on speculative inventory investments.
 Storefront Operations: Automated systems handle the technical aspects of setting up multi-language, multi-currency storefronts, ensuring compliance with local e-commerce standards.
 Marketing & Customer Engagement: AI agents manage social media campaigns, generate ad creative, and provide real-time, multilingual customer service that mimics human interaction.
 Supply Chain & Logistics: By integrating with shipping and logistics networks, agents can monitor inventory levels and optimize shipping routes in real-time.

Empowering SMEs in a Borderless Economy
The strategic intent behind Alibaba International 's launch of Accio Work is clear: to commoditize the operational expertise that usually costs a fortune to acquire. By lowering the barrier to entry, the platform aims to capture a vast market segment—tens of millions of potential users—who have the products and the ambition but lack the institutional knowledge to scale globally.
From a business perspective, this aligns with the broader goals of Alibaba's international commerce division. By creating an ecosystem where merchants can grow effortlessly, the platform generates a virtuous cycle of increased transaction volume and deeper platform reliance. For the SME owner, this is not just about cost-cutting; it is about scaling. A small shop in a regional market can suddenly compete on a global stage, supported by a digital infrastructure that operates with the sophistication of a large enterprise.
However, the efficacy of this platform hinges on the quality of its AI agents. The industry is moving toward a standard where "agentic" capabilities—the ability to reason, plan, and execute multi-step tasks—are the gold standard. If Accio Work can consistently deliver high-quality, reliable outcomes without human intervention, it will likely become an indispensable tool for the next generation of global entrepreneurs.
Strategic Implications for the Enterprise AI Industry
The introduction of Accio Work is a bellwether for the future of Enterprise AI . We are moving away from the "co-pilot" era—where AI simply suggests actions—toward the "autonomous agent" era, where AI executes entire business processes.
For competitors and industry observers, Alibaba's move highlights several critical trends:
 Specialization over Generalization: Broad, general-purpose LLMs are losing their luster for enterprise use cases. Instead, the market is pivoting toward highly specialized, task-specific agents that can integrate directly into business workflows.
 The "Agentic" Shift: The effectiveness of an AI solution is now being measured by its autonomy. Businesses are looking for systems that require minimal "human-in-the-loop" oversight.
 Vertical Integration: Platforms that combine infrastructure, data, and execution are winning. By housing the agents within the same ecosystem where the e-commerce transaction occurs, Alibaba minimizes latency and maximizes the utility of its proprietary data.
Looking Ahead: The Future of Autonomous Commerce
As Accio Work continues to roll out, the metrics of success will be twofold: the number of SMEs successfully leveraging these agents, and the tangible impact on their cross-border revenue growth.
Looking forward, we can expect the capabilities of these virtual employee squads to expand. As multimodal models become more sophisticated, these agents will likely begin to handle more complex tasks, such as automated video production for advertising, advanced financial forecasting, and perhaps even predictive supply chain management based on global geopolitical events.
For the readers of Creati.ai, the launch of Accio Work serves as a practical, real-world case study on how Agentic AI is disrupting traditional commerce. It is no longer a question of if AI will manage the back-office of global businesses, but how quickly businesses can adapt to this new paradigm of autonomous, agent-led operations. The era of the digital workforce has arrived, and it is democratizing access to global markets one SME at a time.
