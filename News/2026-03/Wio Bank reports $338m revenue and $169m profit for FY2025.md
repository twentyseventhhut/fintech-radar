Wio Bank reported strong financial performance for FY2025, driven by continued customer adoption and the expansion of its digital banking platform across the UAE.

In its third full year of operations, the bank recorded revenue of AED1.24bn ($338m), marking a 55 per cent year-on-year increase, while net profit rose 57 per cent to AED622m ($169m).

The bank’s growth was supported by rising customer engagement, with more businesses, individuals and families choosing Wio as their primary financial partner.

This was reflected in a Net Promoter Score (NPS) of 75 for Wio Business and 76 for Wio Personal, described as the highest in the UAE banking industry.

Wio’s balance sheet also expanded significantly, with assets reaching AED61bn ($16.6bn) as of December 31, 2025, up 64 per cent year-on-year. Customer deposits surpassed AED57bn ($15.5bn), increasing by 66 per cent.

#### Wio Bank results

Lending activity grew strongly, supported by increased demand for supply chain finance, term loans and an expanded SME credit suite, underscoring the bank’s transition into a full-service digital financial platform.

Mansour AlMulla, Chairman of the Board, said: “These strong results reinforce our commitment to empowering the UAE’s digital economy and supporting the ambitions of individuals, businesses and communities across the nation.

“Wio’s growth reflects the trust we have earned, with a growing number of customers choosing Wio as their primary banking partner and entrusting us with a greater share of their financial lives.

“Our exceptional balance sheet strength provides a strong foundation to continue serving our customers and investing in a future focused on meeting their evolving financial needs.”

Wio continued to strengthen its position in the business banking segment in 2025, serving a large and diverse base of enterprises across the UAE. The bank expanded its capabilities to support business growth, including supply chain finance, point-of-sale lending, SME credit cards and integrations with accounting and taxation platforms.

#### Digital supply chain

Its digital supply chain finance platform delivered more than AED1bn ($272m) in financing during the year, contributing to improved access to capital. More than 120,000 businesses now use Wio as their primary banking partner.

The bank also reported strong growth in its retail segment, with more than 270,000 individuals choosing Wio as their primary bank, alongside a growing focus on family banking solutions.

In the fourth quarter, Wio launched Wio Family, a fully integrated platform designed to help households manage spending, plan finances and build wealth collectively. Within the first three months, more than 10,000 families adopted the platform.

Wioalso saw increased adoption of its payroll proposition, with nearly 25 per cent of customers shifting their salaries to the platform.

The bank continued to invest in its operating infrastructure, enhancing customer service, governance capabilities and operational resilience.

#### Wio forecast

Looking ahead, Wio said it is focused on expanding its offering in 2026, including plans to develop an Islamic banking proposition, subject to regulatory approval.

The bank is also advancing sector-specific solutions targeting industries such as healthcare, education, food and beverage, trade and digital businesses, alongside continued development of its Wio Family platform to support long-term financial planning.

The bank said it is scaling its artificial intelligence capabilities to deliver more personalised financial tools and experiences, while maintaining strong governance, customer privacy and security standards.
