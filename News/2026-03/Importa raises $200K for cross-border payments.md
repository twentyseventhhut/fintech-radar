Led by Toronet Blockchain, this raise marks a bold new chapter for ImportaPay — opening corridors to 20+ countries, launching stablecoin infrastructure, and going all-in on B2B.

Today, we are sharing the news that has been a long time in the making. **ImportaPay has closed a $200,000 funding round**, led by **Toronet Blockchain** and supported by a growing network of angel investors. This follows the annoucement of our initial $15,000 angel round in October 2025 — and it sets us firmly on the path toward our target of**$2 million in Pre-seed funding.**

This is more than a financial milestone. It is a declaration of intent. We are building the payment infrastructure that businesses across Africa and beyond will rely on — and this raise gives us the runway to do it right.

## **What We Are Building With This Capital**

This round is earmarked as**liquidity funding** — capital to expand our infrastructure, open new payment corridors, and power our B2B pivot. Here is exactly where it goes:

## 1. Opening USD Rails to 20+ Countries

We are expanding our USD payment capabilities to more than 20 countries. Whether you are a business sending payments to suppliers in China, or Wholesaler sending payment to manufacturers in the EU region, our infrastructure will make it possible — faster and cheaper than the status quo.

## 2. Chinese Yuan Settlements — Fast & Affordable

One of our most exciting infrastructure milestones is the ability to send **Chinese Yuan (CNY) directly into Chinese bank accounts.** For African importers and traders dealing with Chinese suppliers, this has historically meant expensive wire transfers, slow settlement times, and opaque fees.

We are changing that. CNY payments through ImportaPay will be fast and significantly cheaper — giving African businesses a real, reliable way to pay suppliers in China without the usual friction.

## 3. Stablecoin Infrastructure for Businesses

We are also launching a **stablecoin exchange layer for businesses**. This means companies will be able to seamlessly convert their local currency into stablecoins — including **USDT, USDC, and other major stablecoins**— directly through ImportaPay's infrastructure.

For businesses operating in volatile currency environments, this opens up a powerful new tool: the ability to hold value in stable digital assets, move money across borders on-chain, and settle with counterparties anywhere in the world. This is programmable money for real business needs.

## Built on World-Class Blockchain Infrastructure

ImportaPay's infrastructure is built on and deployed across some of the most capable blockchain networks in the world. Our architecture leverages the strengths of each chain to deliver speed, security, and low transaction costs to our users.

## Going All-In on B2B

Perhaps the most significant signal from this raise is what it says about our direction.**ImportaPay is sharpening its focus on B2B payment infrastructure**. This means building specifically for businesses — importers, exporters, platforms, marketplaces, and enterprises that need reliable, programmable, multi-currency payment flows at scale.

**What B2B Infrastructure Means for Our Customers**

1.   API-first payment rails that businesses can plug into their own systems
2.   Multi-currency accounts and stablecoin exchange for treasury management
3.   USD, CNY, and local currency settlement to 20+ countries
4.   Transparent pricing with no hidden FX markups or opaque fees
5.   Compliance-ready infrastructure built for business-grade volume

We believe the biggest opportunity in African cross-border payments is not at the consumer level. It is in the infrastructure that powers commerce — and that is exactly where we are planting our flag.

## A Word from the Team

We started ImportaPay because we experienced firsthand how broken cross-border payments are for African businesses and individuals. The fees are too high. The process is too slow. The experience is too painful. And for businesses trying to trade internationally, the options are even worse.

We are building the fix. Not as a flashy consumer app, but as the unglamorous, essential infrastructure that makes money move the way it should — across borders, across currencies, across chains.

This raise is fuel. The work is just beginning.
