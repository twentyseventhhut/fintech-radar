Blockchain opens Malta office to anchor European operations
The new office strengthens our presence in Malta as we scale regulated retail and institutional services across Europe
 With the implementation of the Markets in Crypto Assets regulation, the European Union has introduced one of the most comprehensive regulatory frameworks for digital assets anywhere in the world. For companies building long-term infrastructure in crypto, this clarity represents a major step forward. 
 
 Today we are announcing the opening of a new Blockchain office in Malta. We didn’t pick Malta by accident. Malta has taken a thoughtful and forward-looking approach to digital asset regulation, 
 
 This week, we opened our Malta office alongside Prime Minister Robert Abela and Minister Silvio Schembri, along with regional clients and partners. Marking a shared commitment to build a tangible, regulated, and permanent home for digital assets in the heart of the Mediterranean. 
 
 This office will serve as a strategic hub for our European operations as we expand regulated services across the region under the MiCA framework. We are excited to roll out our institutional business soon and are actively engaging with some of the region’s most reputable licensed institutional businesses, including hedge funds, market makers, and corporates increasingly deploying capital into digital asset markets. 
 
 How we’re building in Malta 
 
 Malta has played a leading role in the development of digital asset regulation in Europe. Its commitment to clear regulatory frameworks and responsible innovation has created an environment where companies can build with long term confidence. 
 
 For Blockchain, establishing a presence in Malta allows us to deepen our engagement with regulators, expand our European team, and continue building trusted infrastructure for customers across the region. 
 
 Building the next phase of digital finance 
 
 Over the past decade, crypto has evolved from an emerging technology into a rapidly expanding global financial system. 
 
 Institutions, corporations, and individuals are increasingly exploring digital assets as part of the broader financial landscape. As adoption grows, regulatory frameworks like MiCA provide the foundation for responsible innovation and global participation. 
 
 We believe regulated access to digital assets is essential for the next phase of adoption. 
 
 Our Malta office will support a wide range of initiatives including regulatory engagement, operational development, and institutional services across Europe. 
 
 A global mission 
 
 Since 2011, Blockchain.com has built one of the most widely used platforms in the digital asset ecosystem. Today more than 90 million wallets have been created on our platform and over 40 million users have completed identity verification. 
 
 Across our global network we have processed more than $1 trillion in crypto transactions while operating in more than 70 jurisdictions worldwide. 
 Our expansion into Malta reflects the same principle that has guided our company from the beginning: building secure, compliant infrastructure that connects people everywhere to the future of finance. 
 
 Europe is an important part of that future. 
 
 And Malta is helping lead the way.

The new office strengthens Blockchain.com's presence in Malta as the company scales regulated retail and institutional services across Europe 
 VALLETTA, Malta , March 25, 2026 /PRNewswire/ -- Blockchain.com announced today the official opening of its Malta office, marking the next step in the company's European expansion and its long-term commitment to building regulated digital asset infrastructure across the region.
The new office will serve as a strategic hub for Blockchain.com's operations across Europe following the company securing its Markets in Crypto Assets (MiCA) license from the Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA). The license enables Blockchain.com to passport regulated crypto brokerage services across the European Economic Area under the EU's first comprehensive digital asset regulatory framework.
"We didn't pick Malta by accident. Malta has taken a thoughtful and forward-looking approach to digital asset regulation," said Nic Cary, Co-founder and Vice Chairman at Blockchain.com . "At Blockchain.com, we've spent over a decade building a brand of trust and integrity, and we're bringing that same professional-grade standard to users across Europe. This office represents our commitment to building trusted, compliant infrastructure for the crypto market."
Blockchain.com's Malta office will support a range of functions including regulatory engagement, operational development, and retail client services across the European market. The company will soon launch its institutional business, partnering with some of the region's leading licensed firms.
Blockchain.com operates in more than 70 jurisdictions worldwide. Since 2011, Blockchain.com has processed over $1.2 trillion in crypto transactions, created more than 90 million wallets, and verified over 40 million users. As a global crypto brokerage, Blockchain.com provides individuals and institutions with secure access to digital assets while maintaining a strong commitment to compliance, transparency and security.
