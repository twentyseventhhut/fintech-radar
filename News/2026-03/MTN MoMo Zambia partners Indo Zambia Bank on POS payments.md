 MTN Mobile Money Zambia and Indo Zambia Bank (IZB) have unveiled a strategic partnership that will allow MTN MoMo customers to seamlessly pay for goods and services across all IZB Point of Sale (POS) terminals nationwide. This collaboration marks a significant milestone in Zambia’s digital transformation journey, strengthening interoperability, enhancing customer convenience, and accelerating the shift toward a cashless economy. 
 Through this integration, customers can now authorise payments directly on their mobile phones and receive instant confirmations, while merchants benefit from real ‑ time payment validation on IZB POS machines. This advancement ensures faster, safer, and more secure transactions — delivering a superior checkout experience. 
 Building on a long ‑ standing relationship between the two institutions, IZB currently supports MTN MoMo with liquidity and float management for agents across the country. Additionally, through the National Financial Switch (NFS), customers are already able to move funds seamlessly between IZB accounts and MoMo wallets. 
 With 40 branches across all 10 provinces, a network of more than 1,800 POS terminals, and over four decades of uninterrupted service, I ZB continues to play a critical role in strengthening Zambia’s digital financial infrastructure. 
 Driving Digital Innovation Across Zambia 
 Speaking at the launch event, the Chief Executive Officer of MTN Mobile Money Limited, Mr. Komba Malukutila, highlighted the company’s dedication to advancing financial inclusion and fostering innovation in the digital finance sector. 
 “MTN MoMo continues to evolve beyond traditional mobile money. We are reshaping the digital payments landscape by offering smarter, more inclusive, and more integrated services for our customers and businesses across Zambia. Enabling MoMo payments on IZB POS terminals is another bold step toward a fully interoperable and cashless ecosystem.” 
 Mr. Malukutila also pointed to recent MTN MoMo innovations, including the enhanced MoMo App, MoMo Virtual Card, international transfers to Europe and Canada, Village Banking, and a growing Insurance and Loans Marketplace. 
 Echoing the commitment to a more integrated and efficient payments ecosystem, the IZB Managing Director, Mr. Brajesh Kumar Singh, reaffirmed the bank’s dedication to driving digital transformation and enhancing customer convenience. 
 “This integration reinforces our mission to promote secure, convenient, and accessible electronic payments across Zambia. Customers can now pay effortlessly using their MTN MoMo wallets on any IZB POS terminal—enhancing convenience, reducing dependency on cash, and delivering faster, safer checkout experiences.” 
 Mr. Singh noted that the Bank has significantly strengthened its digital infrastructure through upgrades to the Core Banking System, the IZB NXT Digital Banking suite, Agency Banking, ERP integrations, QR payment capabilities, and the fully operational Indo Wallet. These developments improve convenience for customers and enable merchants to process payments more efficiently. 
 He further reaffirmed IZB’s commitment to financial inclusion through its continued support of key Government programmes such as the Social Cash Transfer, Cash for Work, FISP, and Sustainable Agricultural Financing (SAF). These efforts underscore the Bank’s dedication to providing practical financial solutions that uplift communities, boost productivity, and promote inclusive economic growth. 
 It was emphasized that t he partnership directly supports Zambia’s national priorities of strengthening financial inclusion, boosting digital commerce, and promoting secure and interoperable payment infrastructure.

Bank of Zambia Warns on Public Workers Loan Defaults, Orders Stricter Lending Rules
Published
on
By
THE Bank of Zambia has raised alarm over rising loan defaults by public workers, saying payroll-based lending practices are creating “growing risks” to financial stability.
In a notice issued seen by Mwebantu, the central bank said non-performing loans tied to civil servants have hit elevated levels. It blamed practices that “deliberately or calculatedly circumvent” prescribed debt-service-ratio, DSR, and affordability rules.
Recent financial stability assessments found more public workers taking “off-payroll credit” beyond permissible limits. The BoZ cited misrepresentation, non-disclosure of existing loans, and multiple borrowing across institutions as key drivers.
Lenders were also faulted for weak underwriting, including inconsistent use of the Credit Reference Bureau, CRB, or ignoring Bureau data altogether.
The Bank of Zambia reminded banks, microfinance institutions, employees, and payroll administrators that DSR limits and CRB checks are mandatory under the Banking and Financial Services Act. The 2020 Credit Data Directive requires all regulated lenders to use CRB data.
“These tools form part of a critical safeguard for responsible lending, consumer protection, prudent credit underwriting, and financial stability,” the BoZ said.
It directed lenders to strictly adhere to DSR limits and adopt responsible lending practices. Payroll administrators were told to strengthen controls, improve recovery mechanisms, and take disciplinary action where payroll deduction arrangements are abused.
The central bank said it will continue monitoring compliance and will take “all necessary measures” to enforce prudential standards.
(Mwebantu, Friday, 12th June, 2026)

NAPSA: We Weren’t Forced to Fund Lusaka-Ndola Road
Published
on
By
THE National Pension Scheme Authority (NAPSA) has rejected claims it was directed by government to finance the Lusaka-Ndola dual carriageway, saying the $300 million investment was approved under strict guidelines to protect members’ funds.
NAPSA Head of Corporate Affairs Cephas Sinyangwe clarified June 11, 2026, that the concessionaire approached the authority with a financing proposal as part of its broader structure for the project.
“NAPSA considered the proposal in line with its investment guidelines and approved participation only after satisfying itself with the loan recovery mechanisms and collateral arrangements that would be put in place,” Sinyangwe said.
He dismissed assertions that NAPSA should have been given toll gates to recover its investment, calling them “inaccurate and misrepresent how the financing arrangement was structured.” Under the security framework, all revenues from allocated toll gates go into an escrow account, with lenders paid according to a predetermined hierarchy. As senior lender, NAPSA enjoys first right of repayment.
NAPSA committed $300 million of the $650 million total financing package during the March 26, 2024 signing. The 13-year facility carries 9.5% interest per annum, with a 3-year construction moratorium followed by 10 years of principal and interest repayment. To date, NAPSA has received $5.9 million in interest.
Sinyangwe said the US dollar-denominated facility protects against kwacha depreciation and inflation, and is expected to generate more than $220 million interest over its life while supporting infrastructure and job creation.
(Mwebantu, Thursday, 11th June, 2026)
