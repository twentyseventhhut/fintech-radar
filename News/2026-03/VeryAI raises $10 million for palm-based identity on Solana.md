Solana News
VeryAI has secured $10 million in seed funding to build a palm-based identity verification system on the Solana blockchain. Polychain Capital led the round, with participation from the Berggruen Institute and Anagram. Anatoly Yakovenko, co-founder of Solana, also joined as an angel investor.
Palm biometrics were selected because they are highly distinctive and less publicly visible than facial features. The conversion process is irreversible, meaning the original scan cannot be reconstructed from the stored data. Identity attestations are recorded on Solana, removing the need for a centralized data repository.
The platform targets a growing problem in the crypto industry: AI-generated accounts. Founder and CEO Zach Meltzer said crypto platforms are exposed to sybil attacks during onboarding, fake accounts farming token rewards, and impersonation scams. He described the core mission as confirming that a real person is acting, not just that a human identity technically exists somewhere.
VeryAI is already working with MEXC, Colosseum, Clique and Talus. Additional centralized exchanges and wallet providers are preparing to integrate the system, according to Meltzer.
World, co-founded by Sam Altman, takes a similar approach using iris scans to confirm human uniqueness on a blockchain network. Ethereum co-founder Vitalik Buterin has separately advocated for verification systems that confirm specific attributes, such as uniqueness or eligibility, without exposing a user's full identity.
