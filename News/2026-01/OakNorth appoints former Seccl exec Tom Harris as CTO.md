UK challenger business bank OakNorth has appointed Tom Harris as chief technology officer (CTO), effective immediately.

Harris moves from Octopus-owned embedded investment platform Seccl, where he has served as CTO since September 2024. He previously spent six years at ClearBank, advancing from head of engineering to CTO in 2021.

Announcing his move on LinkedIn, Harris states: "After spending the best part of the last 10 years building and scaling banking infrastructure at ClearBank and Seccl...it felt like the right moment to step into the front-of-house bank itself."

In his new position, Harris will oversee OakNorth's various technical initiatives, including its partnership with OpenAI, inked last year, and ongoing US expansion, with the new CTO stating: "I will be expanding the engineering team and am keen to hear from people at all levels who are interested in building and innovating."

OakNorth struck a deal to acquire Michigan-based Community Unity Bank in March last year. While the acquisition has yet to close, OakNorth previously described the move as "a solid foundation" for its ongoing growth in the US.

OakNorth hires new CTO
 
Tom Harris has joined OakNorth as chief technology officer, stepping into the role with immediate effect. 
 
Harris moves from Octopus‑owned Seccl, where he served as CTO for two years. Before that, he spent six years at ClearBank, progressing from head of engineering to CTO in 2021. At ClearBank he led work across the core platform, data infrastructure and operational systems for customer service and financial crime prevention. 
 
Announcing his appointment on LinkedIn, Harris said it felt like the right moment to move into a front‑of‑house bank after a decade building and scaling banking infrastructure at ClearBank and Seccl. 
 
At OakNorth, Harris will oversee technology and AI across the bank’s lending, business banking and savings platforms. He said he will expand the engineering team and is keen to hear from candidates at all levels. 
 
His remit includes OakNorth’s technical initiatives and its partnership with OpenAI, signed last year, as well as supporting the bank’s continued expansion in the US. OakNorth agreed a deal in March 2025 to acquire Michigan‑based Community Unity Bank, describing the move as a foundation for its growth in the market. 
 
“The opportunity at OakNorth is hugely exciting as we continue to grow, innovate and expand, including into the US,” Harris said.
