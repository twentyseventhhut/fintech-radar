Kbank cuts valuation by 20% in third IPO push
 
South Korean app-based lender Kbank has taken a bold step by lowering its valuation by around 20 percent as it makes a third attempt to go public on the main stock market.
According to financial industry sources, Kbank filed a securities registration statement with the Financial Services Commission on Tuesday to officially launch its initial public offering (IPO) process for a listing on the main Kospi market.
NH Investment & Securities Co. and Samsung Securities Co. are serving as joint lead underwriters, sources said.
During a capital increase in 2021, Kbank promised its financial investors (FIs) that it would complete an IPO by July this year.
If it fails to do so, the investors would gain a drag-along rights, allowing them to sell not only their own stakes but also the shares of BC Card Co., Kbank’s largest shareholder, to a third party.
The latest IPO is seen as Kbank’s last chance to safeguard its management control, sources said.
Sources noted that the decisive factor behind the bank’s failure in its two previous attempts was the market’s cold reaction to its valuation.
At the time, Kbank drew criticism for clinging to a high valuation even as shares of its local rival KakaoBank were falling.
This time, however, Kbank has presented what is seen as a thoroughly market-friendly price. It has sharply lowered its indicative offering range compared with previous attempts.
The proposed price band is set at 8,300 won ($5.63) to 9,500 won per share, implying a total offering size of 498 billion won to 570 billion won. Market capitalization is projected at between 3.37 trillion won and 3.85 trillion won at listing.
Compared with its previous IPO attempt in 2024, Kbank has reduced its target market capitalization by about 20 percent.
It has also capped its expected valuation below 4 trillion won, adopting a pragmatic strategy that leaves room for upside in the share price after listing.
The bookbuilding session will run from February 4 to 10.
After the final offering price is set, the public subscription will take place for two days starting on February 20.
The listing is scheduled for March 5.
Following the IPO, Kbank plans to use the proceeds in expanding into the small and medium-sized enterprise (SME) market, strengthening its technological competitiveness, building a platform business foundation, and venturing into new businesses such as digital assets.
