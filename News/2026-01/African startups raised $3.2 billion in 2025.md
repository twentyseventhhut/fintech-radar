 African startups raised $3.2B in 2025, 40% more than in 2024 
New data from Africa: The Big Deal shows that startups on the continent raised $3.2 billion in 2025, more than in both 2024 and 2023.
January 7, 2026
 Last year was a strong year for African startup investment, with the continent's startups raising US$3.2 billion in 2025, excluding exits.  
 That is according to the latest data from Africa: The Big Deal , which showed that investment grew 40% year-over-year (YoY) compared to 2024.  
 The growth also came after two consecutive years of decline, with startup investment dropping 25% YoY in 2024 ($2.2 billion) and declining 35% YoY in 2023 ($3 billion).  
 The decline was on the back of strong investment numbers the two previous years, with $4.4 billion raised in 2021 and $4.6 billion in 2022, according to the deals database.  
 Almost 500 ventures raised at least $100,000 on the continent in 2025 (excluding exits), a number almost identical to both 2024 and 2023 levels.  
 Max Cuvellier Giacomelli, co-founder of Africa: The Big Deal, said what is notable however, is the higher number of ventures raising larger amounts: 215 ventures raised $1 million or more, which is 11% higher than 2024 and comparable to 2023.  
 Related: African startups raised $2.2B in 2024, 25% less than 2023 
 Meanwhile 69 startups pulled in more than $10 million in 2025, compared to just 40 in 2024 and 62 in 2023.  
 "As a matter of fact, this is the second-highest number since we started tracking numbers back in 2019 – the top was 2022, with 97," Cuvellier Giacomelli said.  
 Eight startups announced more than $100 million in funding in 2025, versus five in 2024 and four in 2023.  
 Most of those were either in the energy sector (Sun King, M-Kopa , d.light, and Spiro) or fintech (Wave, MNT-Halan, Moniepoint ). 
Investor interest remains strong
 Africa: The Big Deal's database identified at least 554 investors who got involved in a $100,000+ deal on the continent. 
 Cuvellier Giacomelli said this number is likely underestimated as it won't include small angel investors whose names are rarely listed, but it gives a good sense of the number of investors who have signed a sizeable check during the year, of tens of thousands of dollars.  
 He said this number is almost identical to the group's 2024 estimate, but lower than 2023 where there were over 650 investors identified. 
 The database showed that 31% of investors were involved in more than one deal in 2025 and 7% were involve in more than five deals. 
 The most active investor (excluding grant-making organizations) was Digital Africa with at least 23 investments announced in 2025. 
Investment over the years
 Looking at the longer term, Giacomelli said that since 2019, startups in Africa have raised almost $20 billion (excluding exits).  
 Over the seven-year period over 2,200 ventures raised at least $100,000. This includes over 1,000 which raised over $1 million, nearly 300 which raised over $10 million and 33 over $100 million.

 Egyptian fintech platform MNT-Halan tops the charts with over $1 billion in fundraising. 
 Meanwhile, more than 2,500 investors have participated in at least one deal since 2019.
