New Law on Banks and Digital Financial Assets Approved in Kazakhstan
The President of Kazakhstan signed a new law introducing regulation of digital financial assets, including stablecoins and cryptocurrencies, formalizing the status of the digital tenge (CBDC), and expanding banks’ capabilities to develop FinTech products.
 Kassym-Jomart Tokayev , President of the Republic of Kazakhstan, signed the law “On Banks and Banking Activities,” as well as the law “On Amendments and Additions to Certain Legislative Acts of the Republic of Kazakhstan on the Regulation and Development of the Financial Market, Communications, and Bankruptcy.” The documents were developed by the Agency of the Republic of Kazakhstan for Regulation and Development of the Financial Market (ARDFM). The key focus of the legislation is on the development of the FinTech sector, regulation of digital assets, modernization of financial infrastructure, and maintaining the stability of the banking system.
The adopted laws permit the circulation of digital financial assets and introduce their regulation as a separate asset class. There are three types of such assets, namely:
money-backed stablecoins;
digital assets based on financial instruments, property, or property rights;
financial instruments issued in digital form on platforms.
Requirements for stablecoins will be set by the National Bank of Kazakhstan (NBRK), while requirements for other types of digital assets will be established by the ARDFM.
A separate section is devoted to cryptocurrencies as unbacked digital assets. The law provides for the creation of licensed crypto exchanges, whose activities will be regulated by the NBRK. The regulator will have the authority to determine the list of cryptocurrencies permitted for circulation, as well as to set limits and restrictions on transactions to protect investors. Crypto exchanges and infrastructure solution providers will be included in the financial monitoring system to combat money laundering and terrorist financing.
To foster innovation, banks are granted the right to invest in FinTech companies and establish subsidiaries in the fields of digital technologies, artificial intelligence (AI), cybersecurity, biometrics, e-commerce, and telecommunications. This will enable the formation of banking ecosystems and accelerate the introduction of new digital financial products.
Moreover, the status of the digital tenge as a new form of the national currency was legally established. The procedure for its circulation and use will be determined by the NBRK. In parallel, a modern digital financial infrastructure is being developed, including open banking, mobile payments, a national payment system, and a centralized exchange of identification data.
Overall, the new laws are aimed at developing and ensuring the financial stability of the banking system and reflect Kazakhstan’s transition to modern international standards of regulation, competition, digitalization, and consumer protection.
In April 2025, the National Bank of Kazakhstan presented a comprehensive plan to legalize crypto-assets in the country. The initiative evolved into a package of legislative measures aimed at creating a full-fledged digital asset ecosystem and strengthening oversight of cryptocurrency circulation. Besides, a special CryptoCity zone was established in Kazakhstan, within which the use of cryptocurrencies in the real sector of the economy was tested, along with the launch of a state digital asset reserve and a pilot project for a national stablecoin.

 Kazakhstan has officially recognized the digital tenge as a legal form of national currency, according to the Kazakh National Bank. 
This change was formalized with the signing of the "Law on Banks and Banking Activities" and amendments to the "Financial Market Regulation and Bankruptcy" by President Kassym-Jomart Tokayev, News.Az reports, citing Trend .
The digital tenge is now officially recognized as a legitimate means of payment in Kazakhstan. The National Bank has been designated as the sole issuer, and transactions will be conducted through licensed financial market participants. The integration of smart contracts and programmability will enable the creation of innovative financial services and enhance transparency in government spending.
Furthermore, the legal framework strengthens the status of Kazakhstan’s National Digital Financial Infrastructure (NDFI), encompassing the National Payment System, Interbank Payment Systems, the Digital Tenge platform, Open Banking, and Biometric Identification Systems. These initiatives are designed to improve banking efficiency, expand mobile and QR payment options, and drive innovation in the financial sector.
Simultaneously, Kazakhstan has introduced a comprehensive regulatory framework for the issuance of digital financial assets (DFAs) and the supervised circulation of unsecured cryptocurrencies. This framework establishes the necessary infrastructure requirements and defines the regulatory powers of relevant authorities.
In 2022, the National Bank of Kazakhstan, together with market participants, the expert community, and international partners, began studying the necessity of implementing a digital tenge. By the end of the same year, based on the results of the research, a decision was made to implement the digital tenge in three phases. The first phase of the digital tenge rollout took place in 2023.
