Razorpay Gets RBI Nod to Power Offline Payments

 RBI grants Razorpay POS the offline payment aggregator license 

 Razorpay now holds online, offline, and cross-border payment approvals 

 License strengthens in-store digital payments for merchants nationwide 

 Razorpay POS, the offline payments arm of fintech major Razorpay, has received the Payment Aggregator-Physical (PA-P) license from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). The approval allows the company to officially onboard merchants and process in-store digital payments across India.

 With this clearance, Razorpay becomes one of the few fintech players in the country to hold all three major RBI payment licenses, online payment aggregator, offline payment aggregator, and cross-border payment aggregator . This places the company in a select group alongside firms such as Paytm, Pine Labs, PayU, Easebuzz, and Airpay that are authorized to offer end-to-end payment services across channels.

 The PA-P license enables Razorpay POS to scale its offline payments business and support a wide range of merchants, from large retailers and enterprises to small and medium businesses. The company already serves offline merchants through products such as mPOS terminals, smart POS devices , soundboxes, and pin-on-mobile solutions, which are widely used in stores across the country.

Razorpay POS receives RBI Payment Aggregator–Physical licence
 
 Follow Us
Razorpay POS, the offline payments arm of fintech firm Razorpay, has received the Payment Aggregator–Physical (PA-P) licence from the Reserve Bank of India, allowing it to operate as an authorised payment aggregator for in-store transactions.
With this approval, Razorpay now holds all three key RBI payment permissions covering online payment aggregation, offline or physical payment aggregation, and cross-border payment aggregation.
This places the company among a small group of fintech firms authorised to operate end-to-end across India’s payments ecosystem. In recent months, the RBI has cleared online, offline and cross-border payment aggregation licences for around half a dozen companies, including Paytm, Easebuzz, PayU, Pine Labs and Airpay.
The PA-P licence enables Razorpay POS to onboard merchants and process in-store digital payments at scale, covering large retailers, enterprises, and small and medium businesses. The company already operates across India’s offline commerce ecosystem through products such as mPOS terminals, smart POS machines, soundboxes and pin-on-mobile solutions.
Razorpay entered the offline payments segment in 2022 through the acquisition of Ezetap, which was later rebranded as Razorpay POS. Since then, the company has expanded its physical payments footprint alongside its core online payments business.
With the offline licence in place, Razorpay can now offer a fully compliant payments infrastructure spanning digital transactions, in-store merchant payments and cross-border payment flows. The company said it secured its cross-border payment authorisation in December 2025.
Commenting on the development, Shashank Kumar, managing director and co-founder of Razorpay, said the licence strengthens the company’s ability to provide compliant, high-volume in-store payment solutions as businesses expand their omnichannel presence. He added that the approval supports Razorpay’s plans to serve merchants operating seamlessly across both online and offline channels.
