Crypto.com Exchange Expands Collaboration with CryptoStruct to Deliver Advanced High-Frequency Trading Solutions
Integration Provides Normalized Low-Latency APIs, Tick-Level Orderbook Data, and Exclusive VIP Fee Access for HFT Clients.
 January 8, 2025 – Crypto.com Exchange today announced its expanded partnership with CryptoStruct, a leading provider of high-performance, low-latency trading software for algorithmic traders and market makers. This collaboration enables CryptoStruct clients to seamlessly deploy high-frequency trading (HFT) strategies on Crypto.com Exchange with normalized APIs across spot, futures, options, and perpetual markets*.
The partnership provides clients with tick-level orderbook data, normalized low-latency feeds, configurable rate-limit replication, and a parallelized runtime capable of supporting thousands of strategies. These features reduce technical overhead for firms expanding into Crypto.com Exchange and improve scalability and execution quality.
As part of this extended collaboration, CryptoStruct clients are eligible for 90 days of access to Crypto.com Exchange’s lowest VIP fee tiers. Additional trial programs are available, including a four-week SDK trial for prospective CryptoStruct clients and a two-week new-exchange trial for existing CryptoStruct users.
“This expanded partnership reflects our commitment to supporting the needs of professional trading firms,” said Karl Turner, Director, ETF Partnerships & Business Solutions of Crypto.com. “By extending VIP account access to CryptoStruct clients and enabling seamless API connectivity to our exchange, we are making it even easier for high‑frequency traders to engage with our markets at the speed and scale they require.”
“Crypto.com is an important part of our exchange ecosystem,” said Thomas Schmeling, Managing Director of CryptoStruct. “The existing integration provides a solid foundation for sophisticated HFT strategies, and we’re excited to bring this partnership into the spotlight together.”
* Product availability is subject to jurisdictional limitations
