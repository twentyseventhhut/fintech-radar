# Saudi Sudanese Bank taps Network International to power digital payment processing

Network International says it will provide the Sudanese bank with "a full suite of end-to-end digital payment processing services".

[](https://www.fintechfutures.com/author/francis-bignell)

UAE-based payment technology company Network International has cemented its standing in Sudan, following a new partnership with Saudi Sudanese Bank, formalised during Cairo ICT 2025.

Established in 1984 to strengthen ties between Sudan and the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, Saudi Sudanese Bank has expanded to a network of over 20 branches and more than 50 ATMs. With a workforce exceeding 500 employees, the bank offers a wide range of digital, investment, and international banking services.

Through the partnership, Network International says it will provide the bank with "a full suite of end-to-end digital payment processing services, including Mastercard Sponsorship, prepaid issuing, and a range of value-added services designed to support the bank's digital ambitions".

According to a statement from the UAE fintech, these new services will help to "modernise the local financial ecosystem, improve trade settlement efficiency, and create new investment channels between the two markets".

Related:May 2026: Top five fintech partnership stories of the month

With a presence in more than 50 countries and serving more than 250 financial institutions, such as Absa Business Banking and Ruya Bank, Network International describes the new collaboration as representing an "important milestone" in its expansion into Sudan.

Meanwhile, Osama Ali Ibrahim, CEO of Saudi Sudanese Bank, states that the agreement is a "major step in our journey to modernise Sudan's banking landscape". "Network's expertise and comprehensive digital payments platform will allow us to introduce faster, safer, and more accessible financial products to our customers", continues the CEO.
