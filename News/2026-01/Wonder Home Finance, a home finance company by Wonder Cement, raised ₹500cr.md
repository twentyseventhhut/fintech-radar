Rationale and key rating drivers 

CARE Ratings Limited ( CareEdge Ratings ) has upgraded ratings assigned to bank facilities and instruments of Wonder Home 

Finance Limited (WHFL) to “CARE A+; Stable” while factoring in its healthy portfolio growth and comfortable capitalisation levels, 

aided further by the recent capital infusion by Singapore -based Private Equity Fund “Growtheum Capital Partners (GCP) ”. Of the 

proposed capital raise of ₹500 crore, GCP has already infused first tranche of their investment in the form of compulsory 

convertible preference shares (CCPS ) of ₹250 crore in January 2026 , and second tranche is expected to be drawn unconditionally 

in H1FY2 7. The equity raise is expected to significantly strengthen WHFL’s capitalisation profile and aid the growth momentum 

of the company . The company’s asset under management (AUM) increased to ₹2,998 crore as on December 31, 2025, from 

₹2,389 crore as on March 31, 2025. 

The rating upgrade continues to derive comfort from strong promoters, with the Patni family holding ~83 % stake as on January 

31, 202 6, and infus ing capital at regular intervals . Apart from capital support, WHFL enjoys financial flexibility driven by resourceful 

promoter s, common shareholding, sharing common logo and brand name with the group’s flagship company, Wonder Cement 

Limited (WCL; rated ‘CARE AA; Stable / CARE A1+’). 

However, these strengths are offset by WHFL’s limited seasoning of the loan portfolio owing to the nascent stage of operations, 

modest experience of Wonder Group in the housing finance business, its moderate , despite improving, profitability metrics, and 

regionally concentrated operations. While CareEdge Ratings takes cognisance of significant uptick in business amid healthy as set 

quality and diversification, the company’s ability to demonstrate sustained improvement in profitab ility and franchise growth shall 

remain a key monitorable. Going forward, as economies of scale and operational efficiencies take effect, CareEdge Ratings expects 

WHFL’s profitability metrics to stabilise in the medium -term. 

Rating sensitivities: Factors likely to lead to rating actions 

Positive factors : Factors that could individually or collectively lead to positive rating action/upgrade: 

• Significant business growth with geographical diversification and improvement in profitability with return on managed assets 

(ROMA) remaining above 3.0 % on a sustained basis. 

Negative factors : Factors that could individually or collectively lead to negative rating action/downgrade: 

• Material dilution in the ownership by, expected support from, and strategic importance to the Wonder Group. 

• Deterioration in the credit profile of the Wonder Group. 

• Material decline in the asset quality, with gross non -performing assets (GNPA) exceeding 2.00% on a sustained basis. 

• Weakening capital structure with the overall gearing remaining above 3.5x on a sustained basis. 

Analytical approach: 

Standalone and factoring in linkages with the promoters of Wonder Cement Group. CareEdge Ratings has analysed WHFL on a 

standalone basis while factoring in the resourceful promoters and expectations of continued capital support. In addition to c apital 

support and management oversight, WHFL shares common brand name and logo with the group’s flagship company, WCL (rated 

‘CARE AA; Stable/ CARE A1+’).

Outlook: Stable 

The outlook is “Stable” considering expectation of continued need -based capital support from the promoters to drive the business 

growth, while keeping the asset quality under control. 

Detailed description of key rating drivers: 

Key strengths 

Strong parentage, shared brand name, and continued capital support from parent 

WFHL is a part of the Rajasthan -based Wonder group, which is promoted by the Patni family. As on January 31, 2026, promoters 

held ~83% stake in the company in their individual capacity and through family trusts. By virtue of its shareholding, WHFL benefits 

from its promoters’ strong support as demonstrated by capital infusion at regular intervals and articulation of the promoter’ s

intent to continue maintaining majority shareholding in the company. Apart from the capital support, WHFL enjoys financial 

flexibility driven by resourceful pro moters, common shareholding, logo and brand name sharing with the group’s flagship 

company, Wonder Cement Limited ( WCL; rated ‘ CARE AA; Stable / CARE A1+ ’). WHFL continues to be an integral part of Wonder 

group’s business, considering that the company is the group’s sole venture in the financial services domain. Since inception, 

promoters have infused equity to the tune of ~₹800 crore till December 31, 2025. 

CareEdge Ratings expects the promoters to continue maintaining majority shareholding in the company and provide need -based 

capital support to WHFL. 

Experienced management and board 

Ashok Patni, Chairman and Founder of the Wonder group has over three decades’ experience in the marble industry and over 10 

years’ experience in the cement industry. The Board comprises three Directors from Patni family, five Independent Directors and 

one whole -time director . Additionally, the Board will also have representation from incoming investor , GCP . The family has 

experience in industries/sectors which are ancillary to housing finance industry. While WHFL is promoted by the Patni Family, the 

company is run by professional management , led by Sanjay Singh Rajawat, who is the Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of WHFL 

and has n early three decades’ experience in the banking sector. He is assisted by a professional leadership team with relevant 

experience across business verticals .

Healthy asset quality 

Given the significant growth in portfolio growth in recent financial years , WHFL’s asset quality continues to be healthy as reflected 

by Gross Stage 3 (GS3) and Net Stage 3 (NS3) of 0.19% (PY: 0.17%) and 0.12% (PY: 0.12%), respectively, as on March 31, 

2025. In 9M FY26, while there was a marginal uptick in delinquencies led by GS3 and NS3 of 0. 47 % and 0.2 9%, respectively, this 

remains comfortable. In line with asset quality moderation witnessed in industry, the company’s on time portfolio declined to 

96. 63 % as on December 31 , 2025 , compared to 97.33% as on March 31, 2025 , led by higher delinquencies in the housing loan 

book. While the borrower class in affordable segment is relatively vulnerable, WHFL’s granular portfolio and low NS3 to tangible 

net worth (TNW) of 0. 86 % as on December 31, 2025, provides comfort. 

Given the company’s expansion in newer geographies and longer tenure of the product , asset quality metrics will continue to be 

a key monitorable. 

Comfortable capitalisation profile supported by recent capital infusion and group’s demonstrated financial 

flexibility 

The company’s capitalisation profile continues to be comfortable with recurring equity infusions from the promoters aggregating 

to ~₹800 crore and committed investment of ₹500 crore from GCP (a Singapore -based P rivate Equity Fund). GCP has already 

infused first tranche of its investment in the form of Compulsory Convertible Preference Shares (CCPS ) of ₹250 crore , and second 

tranche is expected to be drawn unconditionally in H1FY2 7.

With the said capital raise , WHFL ’s TNW grew to ~₹1,140 crore as on January 31 , 202 6, and its overall gearing is expected to be 

less than 2x as on March 31, 2026, against overall gearing of 2.2x as on December 31, 2025 . In addition, WHFL ’s capitalisation 

profile is expected to improve further in the near term given the infusion of second tranche in H1FY27 .

Benefiting from the promoter association and financial flexibility, the company has been able to raise debt at competitive ra tes 

from private and public sector banks. As on December 31, 2025, the company has established relationships with over 2 3 lenders.

Key weaknesses 

Low portfolio seasoning 

WHFL commenced lending operations in 2018 as a non -deposit -accepting housing finance company (HFC). The company’s AUM 

increased from ₹472.23 crore as on FY22 to ₹2,389.50 crore as on FY25 at a 3 -year compounded annual growth rate (CAGR) of 

72% driven by an enhanced disbursement momentum. The growth momentum continued in 9MFY26 with AUM growing to ₹2,998 

crore as on December 31, 2025. Majority company’s portfolio witnessed a healthy growth since FY2 2 onwards, and hence, the 

seasoning of the entire book stands limited. The disbursements have gained traction in the last few fiscals with ~ 70 % of the AUM 

having less than 2 -year track record since origination. As a result, its asset quality performance through different economic cycles 

and geographies is yet to be tested and the portfolio quality remains to be seen. 

Moderate , despite improving, profitability metrics 

The company’s disbursement momentum remained healthy with ₹1,210 crore disbursed in FY25 (₹834 crore in FY24; ₹492 crore 

in FY23). The momentum continued in 9MFY26 with disbursements aggregating to ~₹967 crore. However, the net interest margin 

was compressed in FY25 given the higher gearing levels in most of FY25. The company’s gearing levels reduced post equity 

infusion in March 2025. As the company continues to reduce it s borrowing cost and marginal uptick in yield considering change 

in accounting for disbursements, net interest margins ( NIMs ) for the company increased to 5.76% (annualised) in 9MFY26 from 

4.86% in FY25 (5.25% in FY24) .

The company added 35 new branches from April 2024 till December 2025 (32 branches added in FY24 ) and stood at 136 branches 

as on December 31, 2025. As a result, the employee headcount increased from 1,492 as on March 31, 2024, to 2,286 as on 

December 31, 2025 , driving up the opex costs. The opex as a percentage of average total assets (ATA) in 9MFY26 and FY25, 

although improv ing slightly, remained high at 5.51% and 5.59% , respectively. With operating leverage yet to kick in, the ROTA 

remained moderate at 1.98% in 9MFY26 (annualised) notwithstanding improvement from 1.15% in FY25 (PY: 1.05%). CareEdge 

Ratings expects the ROTA to remain above 2% in FY26 and FY27 , supported by the reduced leverage post equity raise and gradual 

reduction in opex costs with economies of scale. 

Sole financial services entity of Wonder Group promoters with exposure to weaker borrower profile 

The Wonder Cement group has no prior experience in the retail lending segment. Housing finance is an altogether different ass et 

class compared to conventional financing and requires a thorough understanding and experience in this domain. As on December 

31, 2025, the company’s product offering includes affordable housing loans (7 2% of AUM) and LAP (2 8% of AUM). The company’s 

customer base consisting of self -employed individuals (6 0% of AUM as on December 31, 2025), presents a higher credit risk due 

to potential income volatility. CareEdge Ratings opines, going forward, promoters’ ability to successfully establish their pr esence 

in the housing finance business shall be critical. 

Regionally concentrated operations 

WHFL commenced its operations from Rajasthan, with its target to stabilise itself in the Rajasthan market leveraging the grou p’s 

established presence in the geography. Resultantly , the company’s portfolio remains concentrated in Rajasthan. However, to 

enhance its PAN -India presence, the company has been expanding into newer geographies/locations. Consequently, portfolio 

concentration in Rajasthan continued to decline and stood at 4 1% of AUM as on December 31, 2025, from 44 % as on March 31, 

202 5 (52 % as on March 31, 20 24 ). WHFL’s ability to successfully expand its operations across geographies while maintaining its 

asset quality will remain a key monitorable.

 Khaitan, JSA act on Wonder Home Finance securing ₹500 crore from Growtheum Capital Partners 
 
 
 
 
 
 Copied 
Wonder Home Finance Limited has secured an investment of ₹500 crore from Growtheum Capital Partners, a Singapore-based private equity firm.
 Khaitan & Co advised Wonder Home Finance on this transaction. The Firm drafted, negotiated and finalised the transaction documents, including the share subscription agreement and the shareholders' agreement. The Firm also advised on other regulatory aspects of the deal.
The transaction team consisted of Kartick Maheshwari (Partner), Vidushi Gupta (Partner), Shriya Kamat (Senior Associate), Utsav Mitra (Senior Associate) and Aanya Desai (Associate)
 JSA Advocates & Solicitors advised Growtheum Capital Partners on this investment.
The transaction team was led by Sidharrth Shankar (Partner), with support from Nandini Seth (Partner), Aditya Vardhan Dayal (Principal Associate), and Associates Aditya Sinha and Vivek Vishwanathan.
Nabarun Ray (Senior Associate), and Associates Achint Johri, Rahul Nayyar and Aaryan Pandit assisted on the legal due diligence.
Competition related analysis was undertaken by Vaibhav Choukse (Partner), with support from Nripi Jolly (Principal Associate).
Wonder Home Finance, backed by the RK Group, is a leading housing finance company in the business of providing products such as home purchase loan, house construction loan, mortgage loans, and home loan balance transfer.
