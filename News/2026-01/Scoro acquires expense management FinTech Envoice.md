# Scoro acquires expense management fintech Enovice

London-headquartered fintech Scoro has acquired Envoice, a bill and expense management platform based in Estonia, for an undisclosed sum. The deal sees Scoro merge new automation capabilities from Envoice, with the integration "already in closed beta”, according to a statement.

Incorporated in 2014, Envoice provides over 15,000 businesses and accounting firms in 30 countries a bill and expense management platform which utilises AI to streamline manual tasks like categorisation, data extraction and approval workflows.

Scoro says this integration will make it "significantly easier to capture accurate cost data" through its professional services automation platform, which consolidates estimating, resourcing, project tracking, billing, and reporting functions for project managers.

The purchase will also be used by Scoro to bolster its AI strategy. The company currently has a chatbot called ELI in beta, and will use the AI technology from its latest purchase with the system to analyse and categorise cost data in real time.

Today, we’re excited to announce that Scoro has acquired Envoice , a category leader in AI-powered bill and expense management. 
 Scoro has always tracked project costs. You can log expenses, link them to projects, and see the impact on budgets and margins. But getting that external cost data into the system in the first place? That’s where a lot of the hurdles lie for many professional services firms. The receipt-chasing, manual data entry, approval routing, and data cleanup that happens before a cost ever reaches your project budget in Scoro. 
 Envoice is purpose-built to solve exactly that. And by integrating it directly with Scoro, we’re making it significantly easier to capture accurate cost data – so you see margins in real time, not two weeks later. 
Why This Matters: The Battle for Margins & Visibility
 Most service firms manage their projects and billable hours in one system, while project-related external costs, such as freelancer bills or travel expenses, sit in a separate accounting silo. This fragmentation forces leaders to wait until month-end to see their true profit margins. 
 But delayed visibility is only half the problem. The other half is the manual burden of getting the expense logged in the first place: chasing down bills, entering data, routing expenses for approval, making sure costs get coded to the right projects, and so on. It’s tedious, error-prone, and eats up valuable time.  
 By combining Scoro’s professional services automation platform with Envoice’s AI-driven bill and expense management system, we’re solving both of these problems at once:  
 Costs get captured and categorized automatically.  
 Approvals get routed to the right people without manual triage.  
 Every expense links to the correct project the moment it’s logged for live margin tracking. 
 For service businesses operating on tight margins, this means fewer surprises at month-end, faster decisions, and far greater control over profitability as work happens. 
 
A Shared Vision for AI
 This acquisition is a major step toward our broader vision: applying AI and automation across the full project lifecycle to solve real operational problems. 
 At Scoro, this journey is already well underway with our AI assistant, ELI , and our MCP server . Scoro’s AI is unique as it doesn’t rely on generic public knowledge. It works with your actual business data: projects, resources, timelines, budgets, and financials. 
 But AI is only as good as the data feeding it. And cost data has traditionally been the laggard. Arriving late, entered inconsistently, or sitting in disconnected systems. Envoice changes that. Their AI-powered extraction technology reads and categorizes bills and receipts with exceptional accuracy, ensuring cost data is digitized the moment it exists. 
 By bringing Envoice into Scoro’s ecosystem, we’re closing that gap – giving our AI a complete, real-time picture to work with. The result: more relevant recommendations, earlier warnings, and fewer blind spots. 
What This Means for Scoro Customers
 For Scoro customers, this acquisition introduces a purpose-built front end for managing external project costs. 
 Envoice will continue to operate as a standalone product with its own dedicated interface. It’s not a hidden feature inside Scoro, but a complementary system that integrates deeply with your project workflows. 
 Used together, Scoro and Envoice provide a comprehensive solution for managing projects, work, resources, and costs. 
 First, Envoice automates pre-accounting and the capture of external expenses: 
 Automated data extraction – forward PDF bills via email or snap a photo of a receipt and AI digitizes it with near-perfect accuracy, eliminating manual entry.
 Approval workflows – automatically route expenses and supplier invoices to the right people for review before they are finalized and paid out.
 Expense management – give your team a simple app to submit reimbursables in seconds to improve visibility into pass-through costs. 
 Then, the integration brings that data into Scoro in real-time: 
 Automated linking to projects and POs – all related expenses get linked to the correct projects and purchase orders at the point of capture. 
 Live margin tracking – approved costs flow directly into project budgets, allocated to the right budget lines and financial dimensions, so you see the impact on profitability immediately. This makes project cost reporting faster and even more reliable. 
 The Scoro–Envoice integration is already in closed beta. If you’re interested in the combined solution, register your early interest here . 
What This Means for Envoice Customers
 If you’re an Envoice customer, the short version is: nothing changes about the product you’ve come to love.  
 Same interface, same features, same hands-on support. Envoice will continue to operate as an independent product with the same stability and accuracy. 
 What will change is the speed of innovation. With the backing of Scoro’s resources and development capacity, Envoice will be able to accelerate its roadmap significantly. You can expect more features, deeper automation, and a stronger foundation for the tools you use every day. 
 What’s Next 
This is just the beginning. While Scoro and Envoice will continue to operate as separate products, we’ll be joining our forces to ensure a seamless data flow between the two systems.
Over the coming months, we’ll roll out further enhancements to the integration that brings bill and expense automation even closer to your project workflows.
We’re thrilled to welcome the Envoice team to the Scoro Group!

Scoro acquires Envoice to close the project cost visibility gap
Estonian-founded project management platform Scoro has acquired the Estonian AI-driven expense and bill management company Envoice .  
Professional services firms have long managed time and costs in separate, siloed systems. 
This fragmentation often forces businesses to rely on month-end reporting to understand their margins. 
By combining Scoro’s control over projects, budgets, time, and resources with Envoice’s control over spend, companies can bridge this visibility gap and capture every project cost as work is done, avoiding month-end surprises that impact the bottom line. Visibility and real-time insights are vital in an era of razor-thin margins and heightened competition as AI disrupts the very services these firms sell.  
The acquisition brings together two complementary solutions: Scoro’s comprehensive project management capabilities and Envoice’s world-class AI-powered bill and expense automation. 
While Scoro and Envoice will continue to operate as separate products, together they offer professional services firms a significantly more efficient way to manage project-related expenses, with real-time insights into profitability. Integration is already live between the two products. 
Receipts get automatically linked to both projects and purchase orders at the point of capture, making project cost reporting faster and more reliable. Further enhancements will be rolled out over the next two months to fully automate data exchange. 
Fred Krieger, Founder and CEO of Scoro, said: 
 "Service businesses live and die by their margins, and yet external expenses are not always accounted for in project tracking because they sit in disconnected systems.  Envoice has built a truly innovative solution for automating bookkeeping, bill capture and expense approvals. Together, we ensure no cost goes unlogged, fulfilling our vision of an AI-powered future where repetitive manual tasks are outsourced to AI and automation is applied across the full project lifecycle, from planning and delivery to financial control.” 
According to Jaanus Põder, Founder and CEO of Envoice: "
 Scoro is the perfect match for our next chapter. For our customers and partners, business will continue as usual, but with the backing of Scoro, we can now accelerate our roadmap and bring new capabilities to market much faster."
