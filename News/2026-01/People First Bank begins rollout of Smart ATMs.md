People First Bank has begun the roll-out of 80 new state-of-the-art ATMs.
 Gordon Peters | Published 5 Jan 2026 
The customer-owned bank People First Bank has started the roll-ot new ATMs, with the new machines scheduled to be operating 24/7 in all its branches across the country by the end of March 2026.
The bank says the Branded ‘Smart’ ATMs, new machines not only provide faster and more reliable service, they will also offer a range of enhanced options once fully enabled, including:
Selecting preferred withdrawal denominations across $20, $50 and $100 notes.
Receiving SMS or printed receipts.
Contactless withdrawals by swiping a card or mobile phone.
Deposit taking option (excluding cheque deposits).
People First says the ‘Smart’ ATMs have already been installed in its branches in Flinders Street and Norwood in South Australia, as well in Queensland in the Heritage Bank* branches in Maroochydore, Chermside, Toowoomba (Grand Central), Morayfield and Helensvale.
The new ATMs are being installed in partnership with Armaguard, Australia’s largest supplier of end-to-end ATM services.
“We have a very clear strategy of wanting to make banking easier and more secure for our customers at every stage, and our new fleet of ‘SmartCamilleri. said Chief Customer Officer, Maria-Ann Camilleri.
“We’re using digital technology to provide the fast, efficient services customers want without any of the complications.”
People First notes that as the roll-out continues, customers at each branch will be kept fully informed of installation dates to minimise any disruptions to their normal banking - and installation of the new ATMs will have no impact on customer access to other ATMs or networks.
The rebranding of Heritage Bank sites to People First Bank will begin in 2026.’ "ATMs is a clear example of us delivering on that goal,” said Camilleri.
