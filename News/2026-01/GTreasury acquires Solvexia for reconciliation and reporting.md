Ripple Treasury Acquires Solvexia to Revolutionize Reconciliation and Regulatory Reporting
‍
 Acquisition Extends "No Friction, No Boundaries" Vision with Enterprise-Class Automation for Treasury, Finance, and Compliance 
 CHICAGO, IL, SAN FRANCISCO, CA and SYDNEY, AUSTRALIA – January 6, 2026 – Ripple Treasury , powered by GTreasury, today announced the acquisition of Solvexia, a leading provider of no-code financial automation, data management, and analytics solutions. The acquisition expands Ripple Treasury's platform to automate reconciliation and regulatory reporting across treasury, finance, and compliance operations, addressing one of the most persistent challenges facing finance teams: manual, spreadsheet-driven processes that increase operational risk and audit exposure.
By combining Ripple Treasury's proven treasury management and digital asset infrastructure with Solvexia’s reconciliation and regulatory reporting automation, the platform delivers a unified approach to treasury, finance, and compliance operations, helping organizations reduce risk, improve accuracy, and meet growing regulatory demands.
"Today's acquisition of Solvexia removes the boundaries between treasury management, reconciliation, and compliance reporting," said Renaat Ver Eecke, CEO of Ripple Treasury . "Organizations shouldn't rely on manual processes that introduce fraud risk, disclosure weaknesses, and audit exposure when automation can deliver near-perfect accuracy and complete transparency. The integration of Ripple Treasury's capabilities with Solvexia's automation platform delivers unprecedented visibility and control across the entire finance function, protecting CFO reputation while ensuring governance and regulatory compliance."
Together, Ripple Treasury and Solvexia deliver a comprehensive finance platform that provides: 
 End-to-end reconciliation automation across payment gateways, banking systems, ERPs, and internal records, spanning both fiat and digital asset transactions, to identify fraudulent fund flows, detect revenue leakage, and verify intercompany settlements.
 Embedded governance and controls that bring clarity to approval processes and reduce risk from manual, spreadsheet-driven workflows.
 Enterprise-class regulatory reporting, transforming complex, multi-jurisdictional submissions from weeks to hours.
 Built-in audit readiness, with complete audit trails, version control, and approval workflows to help finance teams meet close deadlines and prevent material weaknesses in disclosure.
"Organizations are simultaneously managing traditional banking relationships and exploring digital assets, all while facing increasing regulatory scrutiny across multiple jurisdictions," said Adem Turgut, CEO of Solvexia . "The combination of Solvexia's automation capabilities with Ripple Treasury's infrastructure extends the platform to ensure it's not just ready for today's challenges: it's built for the future of finance with the governance and audit controls required to protect organizations from regulatory liabilities."
Trusted by more than 1,000 customers across 160 countries, Ripple Treasury brings four decades of treasury leadership, enabling finance teams to connect, consolidate, and manage mission-critical data to optimize cash flow and capital structures. With the acquisition of Solvexia, Ripple Treasury further advances its “No Friction, No Boundaries” vision, delivering a unified, enterprise-grade platform that automates reconciliation, strengthens compliance, and equips finance leaders with the controls and visibility required to operate with confidence.
‍
 About Ripple Treasury 
Ripple Treasury, powered by GTreasury, is the pioneer and global leader in Digital Treasury Solutions for the Office of the CFO. For nearly four decades, Ripple Treasury has delivered industry-leading solutions spanning cash, payments, debt, derivatives, investments, and exposures. Trusted by over 1,000 customers across 160+ countries, Ripple Treasury provides treasury and finance teams with the ability to connect, compile, and manage mission-critical data to optimize cash flows and capital structures while enabling instant, global liquidity execution through Ripple's digital asset infrastructure. Ripple Treasury was acquired by Ripple in 2025.

The platform enables organizations to combine, transform, and analyze all data to produce insights and analytics in minutes instead of days, reduce human error by up to 98%, and embed Enterprise-Class governance and audit capabilities across critical finance processes.

# GTreasury acquires reconciliation and regulatory reporting platform Solvexia

GTreasury will use the acquisition of Solvexia to provide customers with automated reconciliation across both digital asset and fiat payment gateways, banking systems, ERPs, and internal records.

GTreasury has acquired Australian financial automation platform Solvexia to integrate automated reconciliation and regulatory reporting services into its flagship treasury management system. The financial terms of the acquisition have not been disclosed.

Solvexia is a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) platform that specialises in automating financial, tax, and compliance processes for the banking, life insurance, and travel sectors. The company processes around one billion transaction each month, according to its website, and claims to reduce human error by up to 98%.

GTreasury says it will be able to provide customers with automated reconciliation across both digital asset and fiat payment gateways, banking systems, ERPs, and internal records as a result of the deal for Solvexia.

Additionally, the acquisition will see GTreasury embed governance and regulatory reporting controls into the treasury approval process, which will “bring clarity to approval process”, and transform “complex, multi-jurisdictional submissions from weeks to hours”, respectively.

Related:Barclays to buy UK money app GoHenry

Established in 1986 by husband and wife Orazio and Peg Pater, GTreasury now claims to have over 1,000 customers across 160 countries, optimising cashflows and capital structures through its treasury and finance solutions. In October 2025, the treasury management system was bought by digital asset infrastructure provider Ripple for $1 billion.
