Digital challenger bank Atom now has more than £1bn of commercial mortgages on its balance sheet.

‌

The Newcastle-based lender said the "important milestone" had come off the back of work delivered by its commercial lending team, with balances growing 24% year-on-year in the past 12 months. Atom said the final three months of 2025 saw it set a new record for commercial mortgage offers, which was achieved with a month still remaining within the quarter.

‌

At the end of the year, the bank also completed its largest ever Scottish transaction, worth almost £6m. It also pointed to a number of changes in 2025 that enhanced its commercial lending offer, including the introduction of a simplified stressed interest rate, developing a programme where energy efficient properties qualify for a rate discount and building on its Growth Guarantee Scheme which is designed to support access to finance for UK small businesses as they look to invest and grow.

Tom Renwick, head of business lending at Atom bank, said: "Surpassing £1bn of commercial lending on our balance sheet is an important milestone, and demonstrates the impact Atom bank has made in this market, developing from a start-up challenger to a genuine player in the commercial space. We have consistently invested in our technology to ensure brokers and their clients enjoy a faster, easier and more transparent experience, and this growth shows how those improvements have struck a chord with the intermediary community.

"To have achieved this milestone while maintaining strict credit discipline is unquestionably impressive. We have hit this figure by staying true to our core purpose of supporting quality SMEs, often overlooked by high street lenders, but who have the drive and insight to make their plans a reality.

"Atom bank’s commercial team has enjoyed an outstanding few years but we have no intention of resting on our laurels, and will continue to invest in the people and technology needed to bring our offering to a wider group of SMEs."

The commercial lending milestone for Atom follows most recent results which show growth in other areas of the business - including a 31% uplift in mortgage balances to £4.2bn in the year to the end of March 2025 and a 31% rise in its deposit base to £7.5bn. That came as total interest income rose from £441m to £442m but operating profit was down from £27m to £25m.

At the time, CEO Mark Mullen said it had been a successful 12 months for Atom - meeting financial targets and developing a high rating on Trustpilot. He said: "Atom isn’t just growing; we are growing in the right way. We are working hard to build a sustainable bank that challenges the status quo, one that champions competition and serves the real economy."
