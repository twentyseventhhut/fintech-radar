## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[Connecting the Dots in Fintech] 🇧🇷 Brazilian FinTech PicPay raises $434 million in US IPO. The digital bank sold 22.86 million shares after initially offering them in a range of $16 to $19. Financial results show PicPay’s growth trajectory, with the company reporting net income of 270.4 million reais ($52 million) on revenue of 7.26 billion reais for the nine months ending September 30.

[Connecting the Dots in Fintech] 🇧🇷 PicPay seeks investor confidence in the first IPO of a Brazilian company in 4 years. The company, controlled by the billionaire Batista family, is seeking a vote of confidence from investors after filing for an IPO in the United States this month, aiming for a market value of up to US$2.5 billion.

[Connecting the Dots in Fintech] PicPay disclosed a sharp jump in revenue and profit in new US IPO filings, as the Brazilian FinTech renews its push to list shares in New York.

[Connecting the Dots in Fintech] This marks PicPay’s second attempt at a US listing after pulling its IPO plans in 2021 amid weak market conditions. The timing now looks different as public markets slowly reopen.
