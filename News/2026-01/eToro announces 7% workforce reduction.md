 eToro, listed on Nasdaq as ETOR, is set to reduce its global workforce by approximately 7%, according to a letter from CEO Yoni Assia addressed to employees. The decision, part of a strategic realignment to enhance operational efficiency, comes as the company aims to align its resources with its business objectives and support long-term growth. The firm’s IPO prospectus indicated that, as of the end of 2024, eToro employed 1,501 individuals across multiple global locations. This reduction could impact more than 100 staff members, though specific roles and regions affected have not been disclosed.
The online trading platform, headquartered in Israel, has a diverse international presence with offices in the UK, Cyprus, Belgium, Germany, Denmark, the United States, Australia, Abu Dhabi, Singapore, Seychelles, Malta, and Gibraltar. Assia emphasized the necessity of resizing the company during periods of strength to ensure focus on key technological advancements and opportunities that will define eToro’s future trajectory. He further stated that despite the layoffs, eToro’s financial standing remains robust, citing a 28% increase in net contribution revenue, a 43% growth in adjusted EBITDA, and significant cash flow generation in the third quarter. The company held $1.2 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments as of September 30, 2025.
The announcement reflects a broader trend of workforce reductions among brokers. In recent times, firms like IG Group and CMC Markets have also implemented significant layoffs as they navigate changing market landscapes. IG Group reduced its workforce by 10% in 2023, while CMC Markets cut 17% of its staff. Additionally, the operator of FXCM and Tradu is reportedly preparing to lay off more than 100 employees, with AI being cited as a contributing factor in these adjustments.
Despite reporting strong financials, eToro’s shares have faced challenges in the market, losing over 50% of their value since listing. Goldman Sachs recently downgraded eToro from Buy to Hold, adjusting its price target. eToro’s projected 7% annual top-line growth for 2025-2027 lags behind the peer average of 8%, and its pre-tax margin of 36% is lower than the sector average of 54%.
As the industry experiences rapid changes, driven by technological advancements and evolving economic conditions, brokers are adapting by restructuring operations to maintain competitiveness. eToro’s move aligns with a broader strategic approach that includes leveraging process automation and artificial intelligence to improve efficiency. The company’s focus remains on capturing growth opportunities and delivering long-term value to users and shareholders.
The decision to reduce staff underscores eToro’s commitment to optimizing its workforce and prioritizing areas that will drive future success. As the financial services sector continues to evolve, companies are expected to navigate these transitions while seeking strategic growth opportunities through mergers, acquisitions, and other forms of inorganic expansion.
The workforce reduction is part of a strategic realignment that aims to position eToro optimally within the financial services industry. While operational adjustments are challenging, they are often necessary in maintaining alignment with market demands and ensuring sustainable growth. eToro remains focused on its long-term objectives and the evolving needs of its clients, as it seeks to maintain a competitive edge in the rapidly changing investment landscape.
Approval processes for financial products like Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) typically involve rigorous scrutiny by regulatory bodies. Regulators concentrate on factors such as market integrity and investor protection. These processes ensure that financial institutions operate under guidelines that promote transparency and maintain market stability. For firms like eToro, the pursuit of new product offerings is part of a broader strategy to meet growing client demand for diverse investment options.
The competitive landscape within the brokerage industry often sees multiple firms filing for similar financial products, leading to uncertain timelines and frequent amendments to initial proposals. Companies must remain agile, adapting to regulatory changes and market conditions to maintain their positions and capitalize on emerging opportunities.
As the situation evolves, stakeholders will closely watch for further developments, including potential amendments to eToro’s strategic plans or additional regulatory guidance. The workforce reduction may lead to efficiencies that support the company’s broader strategic goals, ensuring that it is well-positioned to leverage future growth opportunities in the dynamic financial markets.
