# Cetin Duransoy steps down as Raisin US CEO

Amid the leadership transition, Raisin US has also confirmed with FinTech Futures the appointment of former UBS executive Michael Blum as executive chairman of the regional division.

[](https://www.fintechfutures.com/author/tyler-pathe)

Tyler Pathe,Reporter, FinTech Futures
January 19, 2026

After nearly three years in the role, Cetin Duransoy has stepped down as CEO of Raisin US. The regional division of the German deposit fintech will now be led by former Rhino vice president Alastair Wood.

Announcing his departure on LinkedIn, Duransoy writes: "After an incredible almost three years of significant growth in revenue, people, capabilities and offerings at Raisin US, I will be stepping down as CEO with deep appreciation for everything we’ve accomplished together."

He goes on to describe his successor, who brings experience from Citi and Kindur, as "a thoughtful, strategic leader", affirming his "full confidence" that Wood will "guide the company successfully into its next chapter".

Raisin operates a platform that allows consumers to access savings and investment products, including fixed-term and overnight deposits, from over 300 partner banks.

Headquartered in Berlin, the company debuted its Savings-as-a-Service software in the US in May 2020, with regional operations led by Duransoy since April 2023.

Related:UK fintech Keel launches from stealth

In a statement to FinTech Futures, a Raisin spokesperson says: "We can confirm that Cetin Duransoy, CEO of Raisin US, will leave the company in January to pursue a new professional opportunity."

"To ensure continuity and stability during this transition, Alastair Wood, General Counsel US, will assume the role of CEO for the US," the statement continues. "He has been part of Raisin US since 2023 and is well-positioned to lead the organization forward."

The spokesperson also confirms the appointment of Michael Blum as executive chairman of Raisin US. Blum previously served as head of the US wealth management banking group of UBS, and also served as the bank's chairman.

This is the second executive Raisin has nabbed from USB in recent years, following the appointment of Axel Weber as an advisor and board member around two years ago.

"Together, Alastair and Michael bring deep industry expertise and strong leadership that will support Raisin US’s continued growth and expanding footprint," the statement concludes. "With this leadership team in place, Raisin remains well positioned for the future."
