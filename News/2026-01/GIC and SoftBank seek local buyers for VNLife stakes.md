GIC, SoftBank seek investors in Korea for stakes in VNLife
 
Global investment firms including Singapore’s sovereign wealth fund GIC Pte. and SoftBank Vision Fund are seeking South Korean investors as they look to sell their stakes in VNLife, a Vietnamese fintech firm.
According to multiple sources from the investment banking industry on Sunday, investors in VNLife have recently appointed UBS Vietnam as their financial advisor and begun the process of divesting their holdings.
As part of this effort, they are sounding out Korean financial holding companies and financial investors that may be interested in acquiring the stake.
The stake for sale amounts to about 40 percent of the company, currently held by GIC, SoftBank, and others. These investors initially invested around $300 million in VNLife in 2018.
In 2021, VNLife successfully raised an additional $250 million from global investors including General Atlantic Service Co and Dragoneer Investment Group.
VNLife’s enterprise value is estimated at $1 billion. The market value of the 40 percent stake is estimated at roughly 580 billion won ($401 million), sources said.
VNLife serves more than 35 million customers and partners with over 45 banks across Southeast Asia.
The Southeast Asian fintech market is projected to grow to about $76 billion by 2030, sources said.
