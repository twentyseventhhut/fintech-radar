BlackFin Tech Weekly - January 13th, 2026
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity
Hello FinTech Friends,
 Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry! Over the last week, there were two fintech deals in Europe , raising a total of €9.8 million , including one transaction in France and one in the UK. 
Congratulations to the two largest rounds announced last week:
 Quideos , a France-based platform providing hedging contracts for agriculture and agribusiness professionals to offset commodity price volatility, has raised €5.2m in a Venture round led by BREEGA and Demeter . 
 MAIA Technology , a UK-based cloud platform for asset management that centralises trading, portfolio management, risk, compliance, and reporting, has raised €4.6m in a Series A round led by Molten Ventures . Let’s dive in! 
 Quideos , based in France , provides hedging contracts for agriculture and agribusiness professionals, helping them offset commodity price volatility and manage market risk. The company has raised €5.2m in a Venture round led by BREEGA and Demeter , with participation from Groupe Crédit Agricole and Clint Capital . The funds will be used to support the company’s development and scale its solutions for agricultural and agribusiness stakeholders. Read more 
 MAIA Technology , based in the UK , provides a cloud platform for asset management, centralising trading, portfolio management, risk, compliance, and reporting for investors. The company has raised €4.6m in a Series A round led by Molten Ventures . The proceeds will be used to enhance product capabilities, deepen integrations, and expand commercial reach across the investment management sector. Read more In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 Diginex , a Hong Kong-based provider of sustainability RegTech and data management solutions, has signed definitive agreements to acquire Plan A , a Germany-based carbon accounting and decarbonization platform. The transaction is pending and valued at €55m, structured as €3m in cash and €52m in shares. The deal supports Diginex’s strategy to consolidate ESG capabilities by combining its sustainability reporting and data infrastructure with Plan A’s carbon measurement and decarbonisation tooling, while extending Plan A’s reach across a broader enterprise customer base. Read more 
 Zilch , a UK-based BNPL platform that offers a card for pay-later purchases online and in-store, has agreed to acquire Fjord Bank , a Lithuania-based digital bank offering consumer loans and term deposits (including savings) alongside insurance products, in a pending transaction valued at €32.7m. The deal is designed to secure a European banking licence and strengthen Zilch’s regulatory and operational footprint in Europe, providing a foundation to broaden its product set beyond BNPL and scale services across the region. Read more 
 Sage , a UK-based software provider helping companies manage invoicing, accounting, payroll, HR, and ERP systems, has announced the acquisition of Akao , a France-based company specialising in the digitalisation and automation of administrative processes, particularly invoice processing and document management. The transaction, which remains pending and for which no deal value has been disclosed, reinforces Sage’s position in banktech and strengthens its end-to-end digital invoicing and compliance capabilities for businesses, notably in the context of upcoming regulatory requirements around e-invoicing in France. Read more And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 Payhawk , the Bulgarian AI-powered spend management platform, is in early-stage talks to raise over $100 million at a valuation close to $2 billion , potentially doubling its 2022 Series B valuation when it became Bulgaria’s first unicorn. The company combines corporate cards, expense tracking, invoice processing, and supplier payments into a single platform targeting mid-sized and large multinationals. 
 Existing backers include Lightspeed Venture Partners and Greenoaks Capital . Payhawk recently launched four AI agents that automate finance operations, claiming 60% faster workflows , as it competes against Pleo , Spendesk , and US heavyweight Brex , which recently secured an EU licence. Read more 
 UK consumer payments firm Zilch has agreed to acquire Fjord Bank , a Lithuanian challenger bank with $120 million in assets specialising in digital consumer lending and savings. Financial terms were not disclosed. The acquisition secures Zilch a European banking licence , enabling it to passport payment services across the continent rather than pursuing multi-year local approvals, and to fund itself through retail deposits instead of relying solely on equity and private credit markets. 
 Zilch raised $175 million in debt and equity in November 2025 to help finance the deal and support IPO plans . The company recently secured a UK FCA payment services licence and plans to launch Zilch Pay in H1 2026. Read more 
 JP Morgan Chase is set to take over from Goldman Sachs as the issuer of Apple Card , with the transition expected to complete in approximately 24 months subject to regulatory approval. The deal is expected to bring over $20 billion in card balances to Chase, which has set aside $2.2 billion in provisions for credit losses in Q4 2025. 
 Goldman Sachs says the transaction will add $0.46 to its Q4 2025 earnings per share. Goldman Sachs CEO David Solomon said the transaction “substantially completes the narrowing of our focus in our consumer business,” while Apple confirmed Mastercard will remain the payment network. Read more 
 Lloyds Banking Group has completed what it claims is the UK’s first public blockchain transaction using tokenised sterling deposits, issuing digital tokens on the Canton Network to purchase a tokenised gilt from UK crypto exchange Archax . The bank ran its own validator node during the transaction, and Archax moved the underlying funds back into its regular Lloyds account, demonstrating seamless flow between blockchain and traditional banking systems. The deal builds on an existing Lloyds - Archax partnership involving tokenised money market funds and gilts as FX collateral, and signals to the UK government how tokenisation can bring traditional instruments into the digital ecosystem.
