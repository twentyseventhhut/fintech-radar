UnionPay expands QR payments to Kazakhstan's Halyk Bank

UnionPay International announced this month that Kazakhstan's Halyk Bank has officially launched its QR code payment service, making it the first financial institution in Central Asia to offer this feature and further expanding UnionPay's digital payment footprint in the region.
Through this partnership, Halyk Bank customers can now make convenient QR code payments across the global UnionPay network via their mobile banking app, which also supports scanning WeChat Pay collection codes within China.
Customers can also get a "one-code pass" for inbound and cross-border payments without switching payment tools, providing more comprehensive financial support for cross-border trade and personnel exchanges between China and Kazakhstan.
This year marks the 20th anniversary of cooperation between Union-Pay and Halyk Bank, a long-term partnership that has yielded fruitful results. Previously, Kazakhstan's largest financial group had enabled all-channel offline and online merchants to accept UnionPay cards, widely covering high-frequency consumption scenarios such as catering, accommodation, transportation, tourism, entertainment and shopping. By the end of 2025, the bank had issued millions of Union-Pay cards.
Central Asia is one of the key markets for UnionPay's international strategic layout. Currently, multiple Central Asian countries have launched UnionPay card issuance businesses. In 2025, the issuance volume of UnionPay cards in the region surged by over 70 percent year-on-year, with UnionPay gradually becoming a favored payment brand for daily consumption and cross-border travel.
In November 2025, UnionPay International also signed memorandums of understanding with local switching networks in countries such as Uzbekistan and Kyrgyzstan, continuously promoting payment interoperability in Central Asia and facilitating the innovative development of regional payment businesses.
Furthermore, in January, Union-Pay International officially signed a strategic cooperation memorandum with the Central Bank of the Republic of Azerbaijan, under which the two sides agreed to cooperate in building a local QR code system to promote the mutual acceptance of QR code payments between China and Azerbaijan.
With their low-cost and easy-to-promote features, QR code payments have become an emerging mainstream payment method globally. Standard QR code payment systems led by central banks in Southeast Asian countries, such as PromptPay in Thailand and QRIS in Indonesia, cover over 10 million local merchants. Brazil is accelerating its digital payment transformation relying on the Pix Payment system, and Turkiye has built a unified payment framework through the BKM national switching network. In these countries and regions, QR code payments demonstrate development characteristics of "official promotion, unified standards and rapid popularization," laying a solid foundation for cross-border payment integration.
"As payment barriers are gradually broken down, tens of millions of micro-, small — and medium-sized merchants worldwide can access the massive purchasing power of China and the world with zero threshold through the inclusive and ubiquitous UnionPay global payment network. This not only solves the difficulty of cross-border payments, but also injects vitality into the recovery and development of the global economy," said Dong Junfeng, chairman of China UnionPay.
UnionPay has reached or advanced QR code interconnectivity cooperation in about 50 countries and regions, gradually building a widely covered and two-way unimpeded cross-border payment network.
In terms of using domestic Chinese wallets overseas, UnionPay launched cross-network QR code partnerships in seven overseas markets in 2025, allowing domestic Chinese users' payment tools to be used smoothly abroad. Regarding the use of overseas wallets in China, the company has established QR code payment cooperation with over 200 overseas wallet institutions, helping visitors to China to conveniently use their wallets whilst in the country.

 Cai Jianbo (left 5th), CEO of UnionPay International, and Umut Shayakhmetova (left 4th), President of Halyk Bank, jointly attended the signing ceremony. 
 
 
UnionPay International is committed to providing diversified supports and services for the implementation of the "One Belt One Road" strategy. UnionPay International announced on May 27 it reached all-round cooperation with Halyk Bank of Kazakhstan, agreeing to deepen cooperation between the two sides in a wide range of fields including local card issuance, innovative services and foreign student support. Cai Jianbo, CEO of UnionPay International, and Umut Shayakhmetova, President of Halyk Bank, jointly attended the signing ceremony. 
 
Kazakhstan is located in the center of Central Asia and is of great importance to the implementation of the "One Belt One Road" strategy. Since last year, UnionPay International has rapidly increased the UnionPay card acceptance coverage to cover about 70% of local ATMs and over 50% of merchants and has also pushed forward large-scale UnionPay card issuance by major local institutions. The business developments have laid solid foundation for UnionPay International’s support of the "One Belt One Road" strategy in a broader area. As the first institution in Kazakhstan to carry out UnionPay card businesses locally, Halyk Bank has enabled UnionPay card acceptance by all its ATMs and merchants. The bank also issued the first UnionPay chip debit card in the local market at the end of last year and will also realize large-scale UnionPay card issuance. 
 
UnionPay has strong advantages in global acceptance network while Halyk Bank wishes to provide its clients with payment convenience during their international travels. These common grounds helped to realize the deepened cooperation. The cooperation involves the following aspects. First, card issuance will expand to cover corporate card, diamond card and CNY/Tenge dual-currency card to avoid exchange losses and meet the demands of different cardholders. The two sides will also cooperate on innovative services including online payment and cross-border remittance. Second, the two sides will jointly carry out the scholarship program for Kazakhstan students in China. Third, the two sides will carry out cooperation in the Khorgos Trade Zone located at the border of China and Kazakhstan. Fourth, the two sides will discuss to participate in the Astana EXPO-2017 in appropriate ways. 
 
Cai Jianbo stated that the implementation of the "One Belt One Road" strategy has helped to promote quick UnionPay card business development along the involved countries and regions. At the same time, UnionPay International also wishes to provide support to the economic, trade and personnel exchanges between China and those countries through more diversified means. The deepened cooperation with Halyk Bank provides a good example for UnionPay card business development along the "One Belt One Road" areas. In the future, we will continue to expand the fields of cooperation with local institutions to contribute to the implementation of the national strategy. 
 
Umut Shayakhmetova said the cooperation with UnionPay International is important. The global UnionPay card acceptance network is being increasingly optimized and it not only meets the personnel exchange demands between China and Kazakhstan but also provides payment convenience for Kazakhstan's exchanges with Russia, Europe and other surrounding regions. We are willing to carry out all-round cooperation with UnionPay International to provide our clients with more convenient payment services. 
 
The UnionPay card acceptance environment in the countries and regions along the "One Belt One Road" is rapidly improving. In Pakistan, UnionPay cards are accepted by 70% of ATMs and about 90% of POS terminals and a variety of UnionPay cards have also been issued locally to provide support for the construction of China-Pakistan economic corridor in the "One Belt One Road" flagship project. In the UAE, UnionPay cards have become accepted by all merchants and 100% of ATM acceptance is expected within this year. In Mongolia, UnionPay cards are accepted by almost all POS terminals and ATMs. The UnionPay card acceptance coverage is also expanding in countries including Russia, Turkey and Azerbaijan.

Halyk Bank becomes the first in Kazakhstan to launch international QR payments via UnionPay and Weixin Pay (WeChat Pay in mainland China)
Halyk Bank is expanding its QR payment service for traveling customers. Soon, UnionPay cardholders using Halyk Super App (for individuals) and Onlinebank (for entrepreneurs and legal entities) will be able to pay for purchases in China and in more than 40 countries where UnionPay QR is accepted, including Japan, South Korea, Sri Lanka, Thailand, Mongolia, and others.
 How the service works 
The new functionality allows users to scan the merchant’s QR code via the mobile app, generate their own QR code for payment, and instantly pay for goods and services in stores, restaurants, and service outlets that support QR acquiring. If a UnionPay card is not available at the time of payment, a digital card can be issued online. Users are also advised to update the Bank’s mobile app to access the service.
 Opportunities for businesses and travelers 
The new feature makes payments in China significantly more convenient for both tourists and entrepreneurs who regularly travel for sourcing or conduct business with Chinese partners.
For entrepreneurs, the service provides additional opportunities:
convenient settlements with suppliers and merchants in China;
payment for goods at markets and shopping centers where QR payments are widely used;
reduced need for cash during business trips.
For individual customers, the new feature simplifies everyday payments while traveling — from cafés and shops to transport and various services.
 Over 600,000 trips to China annually 
The Kazakhstan–China route remains one of the key destinations for both business and leisure travel. According to official data from the Ministry of Tourism, citizens of Kazakhstan make more than 500–600 thousand trips to China annually, and the introduction of a visa-free regime continues to drive growth in both tourist and business travel.
In 2025, China recorded over 150 million inbound trips by foreign nationals, representing a 17% increase year-on-year. Of these, 30.08 million trips were made under a visa-free regime, marking a 49.5% increase compared to the previous year.
An additional indicator of growing travel activity is the increase in passenger traffic at the Kazakhstan–China border in early 2026.
China is one of the most promising digital payment economies in the world
China is considered one of the most advanced digital payment economies globally. In major cities, more than 90% of retail payments are made via QR codes.
The main payment tools are QR acquiring systems, so in many retail outlets - from small shops and markets to restaurants and services - QR payments have actually replaced bank cards and cash.
This has often created challenges for foreign tourists and entrepreneurs, as many merchants accept only QR payments, while international bank cards may not be accepted everywhere.
 Over 20 years of partnership 
Halyk Bank and UnionPay have been collaborating for over 20 years, developing modern payment solutions and expanding international settlement capabilities for customers.
The launch of UnionPay QR and Weixin Pay QR support marks another step in the development of the Bank’s digital services and the expansion of cashless payment options for customers worldwide.
