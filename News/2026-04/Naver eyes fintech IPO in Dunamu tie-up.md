Dunamu, operator of South Korea's largest cryptocurrency exchange Upbit, will pursue a public listing by 2031 after merging with Naver Financial.
Dunamu and Naver Financial announced Thursday that they will immediately pursue Naver Financial's IPO following the completion of a comprehensive share exchange between the two companies.
Specifically, the companies plan to establish an IPO committee for Naver Financial within one year after the share exchange and will make their best efforts toward listing. If the listing is not completed within five years, the timeline may be extended by up to two years.
However, specific execution plans including whether to proceed with the listing, timing, and method have not yet been finalized. The companies said they will make decisions after comprehensively considering market conditions and relevant regulations.
Dunamu had previously announced plans to list within five years at its annual shareholders' meeting last month. "Once the deal with Naver Financial is finalized, we plan to actively pursue listing," Dunamu CEO Oh Kyung-seok said at the time. "We are keeping both domestic and overseas markets open."
Dunamu CFO Nam Seung-hyun also said, "The five-year timeframe represents the contractual final deadline. We will prepare for listing as soon as the deal is completed and make every effort to enter the market immediately."
Naver Financial and Dunamu decided in November last year to merge through a comprehensive share exchange. After the share exchange, Naver Financial, which will hold 100% of Dunamu's shares, was known to be planning a listing on the U.S. Nasdaq or other exchanges within five years.
However, the merger timeline has been delayed by approximately three months due to the government's prolonged merger review. Naver announced Wednesday that the shareholders' meeting date for the comprehensive share exchange between Naver Financial and Dunamu has been changed from May 22 to August 18. Accordingly, the share exchange and transfer dates have also been postponed from June 30 to September 30.
The companies had originally planned to complete the merger in the first half of this year, but the delay in the business combination review, combined with restrictions on major shareholder stakes in cryptocurrency exchanges, appears to have become variables. "Given the scale of the deal and its unprecedented nature, we understand that government authorities need time to establish a reasonable direction and conduct their review," CEO Oh explained. "The delay is not due to government approval issues, but rather the natural time required to go through the procedures."
Regarding Dunamu's future strategy, he said, "After the merger with Naver Financial, we will be able to pursue strategic collaborations with traditional financial companies as well as M&A. We are preparing AI-based agent wallet and trading services." He added, "We are actively considering expanding our corporate and foreign customer base, and overseas market expansion including Upbit Global and Vietnam."
The amended disclosure also specified that Naver will secure voting rights in Naver Financial through an investor agreement and maintain its status as a consolidated subsidiary as before.
The two companies plan to maintain their existing businesses after the share exchange while strengthening functional cooperation and reviewing additional restructuring options. However, specific restructuring plans and listing schedules will be finalized through board resolutions and other procedures.
