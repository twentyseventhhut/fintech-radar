Groww reports Rs 686 Cr profit on Rs 1,500 Cr revenue in Q4 FY26
 
Digital investment platform Groww on Monday reported its Q4 FY26 results. During the period, the company more than doubled its profit to Rs 686 crore, while at the same time its revenue from operations rose 88% year-on-year to cross Rs 1,500 crore.

 
 Follow Us
 Digital investment platform Groww on Monday reported its Q4 FY26 results. During the period, the company more than doubled its profit to Rs 686 crore, while at the same time its revenue from operations rose 88% year-on-year to cross Rs 1,500 crore. 
 The company’s revenue from operations increased to Rs 1,505 crore in Q4 FY26 from Rs 801 crore in the same quarter last year, according to its financial statement sourced from NSE. 
 
 Other income contributed an additional Rs 30 crore, which pushed total income to Rs 1,535.5 crore for the quarter. For the full year ended March 2026, Groww reported a 19% year-on-year increase in operating income to Rs 4,645 crore, while its profit crossed the Rs 2,000 crore threshold in FY26. 
 On the expense side, employee benefits formed the largest component, accounting for 29% of total expenses at Rs 173 crore in Q4 FY26. Finance costs stood at Rs 8 crore, while depreciation expenses rose to Rs 24.45 crore.  
 Overall, the Lalit Keshre-led firm reported a 38% increase in total expenditure to Rs 599 crore in the quarter, compared to Rs 435 crore in Q4 FY25. 
 Groww reported net profit of Rs 686 crore in Q4 FY26, more than double from Rs 309 crore in Q4 FY25, as revenue growth outpaced the rise in expenses by a notable margin. 
 Groww continued to add more new stock market users than the rest of the industry combined, even as market activity slowed due to geopolitical tensions. In February, the platform added 2.55 lakh clients, which took its total client base to around 1.27 crore (12.75 million) and pushed its market share to 28.03%. 
 During the quarter, Ashish Agrawal, former MD at Peak XV Partners, stepped down from the board of Groww as he plans to begin his entrepreneurial journey and set up a new venture capital firm, according to his resignation letter. 
 As of 1:55 PM (today), Groww’s share is trading at Rs 198.7, giving the company total market capitalization of Rs 1,24,707 crore (around $13.4 billion).
