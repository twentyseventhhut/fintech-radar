Imagine this. 
 A customer in Paris searches for Indian sarees. 
 She lands on your website after scrolling through Google Search. You’ve done everything right: SEO, ads, a clean website, beautiful products, sharp pricing. She loves what she sees and adds a saree to her cart. 
 Then she reaches the payment page. 
 She’s asked to enter her card number. 
 Then the expiry date. 
 Then the CVV. 
 Then, an unfamiliar authentication step. 
 She doesn’t see any payment method she uses every day . 
 She doesn’t immediately recognise the checkout flow. 
 And she doesn’t yet know your brand well enough to trust it blindly. 
 So she hesitates. 
 Or worse, she drops off. 
 Now imagine the same moment, differently. 
 She reaches checkout and sees a logo she recognises instantly. 
 A payment method she uses regularly. 
 A brand she already trusts. 
 One tap on her phone. 
 Biometric authentication. 
 Payment complete. 
 That moment of familiarity is often the difference between an abandoned cart and a successful international sale. 
 That’s exactly why Razorpay is launching the Google Pay digital wallet for international card payments – to help Indian exporters convert global customers with a checkout experience they already know and trust. 
 Digital wallets are shaping the future of international payments 
 The way the world pays online is undergoing a shift. 
 The 2026 Worldpay Global Payments Report forecasts that digital wallets will account for nearly 65% of global e-commerce transaction value by 2030 , as consumers increasingly favour faster, wallet-based checkout experiences over manual card entry. 
 For Indian exporters selling to customers in the US, UK, Europe, Australia, and other international markets, this matters enormously. These customers don’t just recognise digital wallets; they actively prefer them. 
 The challenges Indian exporters face with international payments 
 Despite strong products and global demand, many Indian exporters lose customers at the final step. 
 Low trust with first-time international buyers: An unfamiliar brand combined with a manual card flow can create hesitation-  especially in cross-border transactions. 
 Drop-offs due to checkout friction: Entering card numbers, expiry dates, CVV, and OTPs adds friction. On mobile devices, this friction compounds quickly. 
 A tougher climb for new brands: Established global brands benefit from reputation and recall. But for new or fast-growing Indian brands, who are still building trust, checkout becomes the first real test of credibility. 
 Together, these challenges translate directly into lower payment success rates, conversions, and revenue . 
 Why Google Pay is the answer 
 The Google Pay digital wallet addresses these challenges at their root: by offering a payment method global shoppers are familiar with. 
 A trusted, globally recognised payment method: With millions of users worldwide, Google Pay is immediately recognisable across key international markets. Customers don’t need reassurance; they already trust the payment experience. 
 One-tap, biometric checkout: No card entry. No OTPs. No friction. 
 Customers authenticate using biometrics and complete payments in seconds. 
 Built-in trust, even for new brands: When customers see the Google Pay digital wallet at checkout, trust transfers instantly. They may be new to your brand, but they already trust Google Pay and its security. 
 The impact businesses can expect 
 Early pilots on Razorpay International Payments demonstrate clear, measurable business impact on conversions, checkout experience, payment success rates!
 The outcome is simple: less friction, fewer drop-offs, and higher international revenue , without changing pricing, marketing, or product strategy. 
 Lower fraud risk by design 
 The Google Pay digital wallet also reduces fraud exposure through: 
 Tokenisation (actual card details are never exposed) 
 Device-level security 
 Biometric authentication 
 Compared to manual card entry, this significantly lowers the risk of card-not-present fraud. 
 
 How merchants can go live with Google Pay digital wallet 
 Already using Razorpay International Payments? No action is required. The Google Pay digital wallet will be enabled for eligible merchants in phases , automatically. 
 New to Razorpay? You can sign up for Razorpay International Payments to start accepting Google Pay digital wallet payments along with other global payment methods. 
 Familiarity that converts 
 With Apple Pay and now the Google Pay digital wallet , Razorpay International Payments brings the world’s most trusted digital wallets to Indian exporters, on one single, seamless checkout. 
 We’re building with one belief at the core: global customers should be able to pay the way they already love. 
 As international commerce becomes more competitive, familiarity will increasingly decide who converts and who doesn’t. 
 The future of international payments is wallet-first. 
 And that future is now live on Razorpay.

# Razorpay integrates Google Pay for cross-border payments by exporters

## Razorpay has integrated Google Pay for cross-border transactions, allowing Indian exporters to offer wallet-based checkout and improve payment success in global e-commerce

)

 premium 

Through this partnership, Razorpay said Indian businesses can now meet their customers where they already are — on their phones, with a single-tap experience, instead of relying only on card-led flows with CVV, OTP, and manual entry

Peerzada Abrar Bengaluru 

2 min read Last Updated : Apr 22 2026 | 8:11 PM IST

#### Listen to This Article

 Razorpay said it has integrated with Google Pay to enable international payments for Indian businesses, becoming the first domestic payment aggregator to offer a card-based wallet experience through the platform for cross-border transactions.

 With this partnership, Razorpay aims to bring Indian exporters closer to their international customers by removing long-standing payment checkout barriers. By enabling Google Pay at checkout, exporters can offer a fast, familiar, and trusted payment experience, eliminating manual card entry and unnecessary authentication steps.

 “Indian businesses are no longer thinking of building their presence domestically first. They want to create products and have a reach that is global,” said Rahul Kothari, chief operating officer, Razorpay. “We see our role as building the financial rails that make this possible.”
