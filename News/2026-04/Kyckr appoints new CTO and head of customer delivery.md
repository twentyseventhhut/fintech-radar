 UK RegTech firm Kyckr, a business verification and monitoring platform, has announced the appointment of Ian Jones as its new chief technology officer (CTO). 
According to FinTech Finance , Jones joins having accumulated nearly 30 years of experience across technology infrastructure and financial services. His previous roles include CTO at OpenPlay, director of technology at payments platform xMoney, and technical product director for EQ Pay, Equiniti’s global payments platform.
Kellet’s appointment represents a return to Kyckr, where he previously held a solutions engineer role between 2022 and 2024. The company says both hires are intended to accelerate product development as it looks to build on strong momentum from the second half of 2025, during which it recorded 60% revenue growth.
Established in 2022, Kyckr operates as a business verification and monitoring platform, focusing on anti-money laundering (AML), fraud prevention, and third-party risk assessment. The company has offices across the UK, Ireland, and Australia and counts more than 300 businesses on its network. Having reached profitability in 2024, Kyckr says it is now positioning itself for its next phase of growth.

# Kyckr makes double appointment

By

Steve Brooks

 - 

April 16, 2026

FacebookXWhatsAppLinkedinEmail

[](https://www.enterprisetimes.co.uk/2026/04/16/kyckr-makes-double-appointment/# "More")

Kyckr,[](https://www.kyckr.com/) the global business Register has announced the appointment of Ian Jones as its new Chief Technology Officer. The appointment follows that of its new CEO, Steve Lamb, in 2025, following the sudden departure of the previous incumbents in those roles.

Lamb, who was previous Kyckr’s Head of Product and then Chief Operating Officer for five years, is now building out a leadership team for the firm’s next stage of growth. The firm also announced the appointment of Andrew Kellett as Head of Customer Delivery. Kellet will be responsible for leading global customer support and delivery.

They joined after a very successful H2 2025 despite the change in leadership. The firm enjoyed 60% revenue growth in H2 2025, boosted by rising regulatory scrutiny of business verification, particularly Ultimate Beneficial Ownership (UBO) identification requirements. There has also been a rising demand for integrated compliance solutions.

Kyckr integrates with over 300 company registries across 100 countries and partners with leading technology companies, including LexisNexis, FirstAML, Experian, and Dun & Bradstreet.

Jones now has the remit to lead the technology charge in a world that is changing with AI reshaping compliance across KYC and anti-money laundering.

Steve Lamb, CEO, said: “_Bringing Ian on board signals the huge ambition we have for this business. Access to reliable company data has been a complex, expensive problem for obliged entities for too long.Kyckr is changing that. With record growth, profitability, and a strong team in place,we’re now competing at a different level.”_

## Who is Ian Jones

Ian Jones, CTO, Kyckr

Jones joins from OpenPlay, where he served as Chief Technology Officer for nearly 3 years. Before OpenPlay, he was Director of Technology at xMoney.comm part of the MultiversX group.

He also spent 3.5 years at Equiniti in product and engineering leadership roles for EQ Pay and EQ Data. With a background in software engineering and nearly two decades of experience, he has helped build scalable platforms in regulated markets. Expertise that will be useful at Kyckr.

Ian Jones, CTO at Kyckr, said: “_What attracted me to Kyckr was the clarity of the vision and the scale of the opportunity. Business verification is one of the most persistent unsolved problems in financial services, and Kyckr has the data, the platform, and the momentum to drive real change.”_

Jones has a degree in Automotive Electronics Engineering from Swansea Institute of Higher Education.

## Who is Andrew Kellett

Andrew Kellett, Head of Service Delivery, Kyckr

Kellett is no stranger to Kyckr, having worked there from 2022 to 2024 as a solutions engineer. He now returns as Head of Customer Delivery after a stint as an integrations Consultant at Thirdfort.

Kellett also spent nearly six years working as a consultant and in customer services at Premium Credit Ltd. He re-joined Kickr in January 2026.

Kellet has a BA in Graphic Design from the University for the Creative Arts, Surrey.

## Enterprise Times: What does this mean

The appointment of a new CTO should bring not only stability but also a resurgence of product development to Kyckr as it looks to cement its market position. Where will Jones lead Kyckr in its next chapter, and how will he seek to integrate AI into its platform?
