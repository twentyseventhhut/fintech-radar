The new offering is built on Visa’s global payments infrastructure, enabling seamless transactions both within Nigeria and internationally. It is designed to support customers who require flexible, secure, and reliable payment experiences across borders.
Beyond payments, the Visa Signature Card also provides a curated range of travel, lifestyle, and security benefits, aimed at enhancing convenience and value for high-end users.
According to the announcement, the partnership between Visa and Zenith Bank reflects a shared commitment to delivering secure and widely accepted financial solutions that align with the evolving needs of today’s international lifestyles.

Visa Inc. has partnered with Zenith Bank to introduce the Visa Signature Card, a premium payment product aimed at meeting the financial and lifestyle needs of affluent customers in Nigeria.
 The card was unveiled during a media briefing held at Zenith Bank’s headquarters in Victoria Island, Lagos. Executives from both organisations said the initiative is part of efforts to expand premium banking services and strengthen offerings for high-net-worth individuals and internationally mobile customers.
  
 The new card builds on Zenith Bank’s existing premium card portfolio, which already includes Gold and Platinum cards designed for customers with higher spending capacity. Bank executives said the Visa Signature Card represents the next phase of its strategy to deepen engagement with this segment.
 According to the bank, the product is designed to serve customers who frequently travel and require seamless international payment options alongside lifestyle benefits. Advertisement 
 
 
 
 Representatives of Visa explained that the card was developed after studying the lifestyle habits of affluent customers, particularly their preference for international travel, premium shopping experiences and convenience-driven financial services.
  
 The Visa Signature Card combines global and local lifestyle benefits through partnerships with merchants across major travel destinations as well as within Nigeria. Cardholders will have access to airport lounges worldwide, travel assistance services, and exclusive discounts at partner merchants in key travel corridors, including the United Kingdom, the United States, and South Africa.
 In addition to international benefits, the card will also offer special deals and discounts at selected restaurants, spas and lifestyle outlets in Nigeria through partnerships with local merchants.
   Advertisement 
 
 
 
 Executives from Zenith Bank described the bank’s affluent segment as customers with significant financial capacity and high spending patterns, many of whom maintain international lifestyles and require banking products that match their financial activity.
 They noted that beyond payment functionality, the Visa Signature Card includes concierge services, travel support, and other lifestyle assistance aimed at improving convenience for users.
  
 The card is denominated in foreign currency to allow seamless international transactions and is expected to be rolled out to the wider market later in March as part of Zenith Bank’s broader strategy to strengthen premium banking services for its high-value customers.
 For businesses in the travel, hospitality, and retail sectors, particularly premium service providers, the introduction of such payment products could create new opportunities to attract affluent customers who tend to spend more on lifestyle services and international travel experiences. The Yobe State Government is exploring the introduction of interest-free loans for small and medium-scale enterprises (SMEs) as part of efforts to strengthen local businesses and stimulate economic growth across the state.

 Governor Mai Mala Buni said supporting SMEs remains a key priority for his administration, recognizing the sector’s significant contribution to job creation, income generation, and local economic development.
 According to the governor, the state is exploring partnerships and innovative financial mechanisms that will make funding more accessible to entrepreneurs while reducing the financial burden often associated with traditional lending. Advertisement

 For many MSMEs, limited access to affordable credit remains one of the biggest barriers to growth. High borrowing costs often restrict the ability of small businesses to purchase equipment, expand operations, increase inventory, or invest in innovation.
 The proposed interest-free loan scheme is expected to provide relief to entrepreneurs by offering a more accessible financing option that can help businesses grow without the pressure of accumulating interest payments.
 If successfully implemented, the programme could strengthen Yobe’s SME ecosystem, improve business sustainability, encourage entrepreneurship, and create additional employment opportunities across various sectors of the economy.

 25 University of Abuja Students Receive ₦3.2m Grants for Food Security Research

on
By
Nigeria’s drive to strengthen food security through research received a boost as 25 final-year students from the Faculty of Agriculture at the University of Abuja were awarded research grants under the Nigeria Food Security Scholars program .
  
 The beneficiaries received project-based grants totaling ₦3.211 million alongside certificates of participation to support research focused on some of the country’s most pressing agricultural challenges. The research areas include food security, agricultural supply chains, climate resilience, post-harvest loss reduction, sustainable food systems, and related fields.
 The initiative, implemented by the Nigerian Food Security Project, seeks to encourage students and researchers to develop practical, research-driven solutions capable of improving food production, reducing losses across the value chain, and strengthening Nigeria’s agricultural sector.
   Advertisement 
 
 
 
 Speaking during the award presentation, the Director of the Nigerian Food Security Project, Ajibola Oladiipo, emphasized the importance of connecting academic research with real-world food security challenges.
 According to him, the programme was created to ensure that research conducted in higher institutions contributes directly to solving food security problems facing the country. He noted that the initiative is committed to supporting and scaling commercially viable solutions that emerge from student-led research.
  
 The Dean of the Faculty of Agriculture, Professor Akeem Oyerinde, described the programme as a significant step toward addressing food insecurity through innovation and research. He explained that the university selected 25 outstanding students to participate in studies covering key areas such as women and youth participation in agriculture and strategies for reducing post-harvest losses.
 The students have been organized into thematic research clusters and are already carrying out studies expected to generate practical recommendations for the agricultural sector. Advertisement 
 
 
 
 Oyerinde noted that the initiative is the first of its kind at the university and is expected to have a lasting impact on both the students and the institution by producing agricultural professionals equipped to drive innovation and contribute to national food security goals.
  
 Beyond financial support, the programme will provide participants with mentorship and academic guidance through faculty supervisors. The students will also benefit from quarterly coaching and review sessions organized by the Supply Chain Research and Innovation Hub.
 The Nigerian Food Security Project, an initiative of the Supply Chain Research and Innovation Hub, focuses on advancing agricultural research, innovation, and human capital development. Through partnerships with tertiary institutions and industry stakeholders, the project aims to promote practical solutions that improve agricultural productivity, strengthen food systems, and reduce post-harvest losses across Nigeria.
   Advertisement 
 
 
 
 For agribusinesses and MSMEs operating within the agricultural value chain, initiatives that support research and innovation remain critical to developing sustainable solutions capable of improving food availability, reducing waste, and enhancing productivity across the sector.

 Mata A Farka Incubator Launches to Transition 100 Women-Led Nano Ventures into Formal Structures 
 
 
 
 
 
 Published
on
By
More than 80 women entrepreneurs gathered in Abuja today for the official launch of the Mata A Farka Incubator Program, a six-week business development initiative designed to help women-owned micro and small businesses build stronger foundations for growth.
  
 The programme, whose name translates to “Women Arise,” is targeting 100 women entrepreneurs who either have business ideas or operate existing businesses generating less than ₦1 million in monthly revenue. Its primary objective is to support participants in formalising their businesses, improving financial management skills, strengthening business knowledge, and transitioning from nano enterprises into sustainable micro businesses.
  
 The launch received support from the Strong Women Network, which provided technical expertise and community-based implementation support, while the One Health and Development Initiative (OHDI) contributed financial support specifically aimed at women operating within agriculture and food systems. Advertisement 
 
 
 
 Participants were introduced to practical business development strategies during the opening sessions. Business and management consultant, GIZ-certified master trainer, and Tony Elumelu Foundation mentor Judith Nyior led an interactive session focused on helping entrepreneurs move beyond informal business practices and build structured, scalable enterprises.
 Another key session was delivered by Legal Space and Consult, which guided participants through the fundamentals of business formalisation, including the process of registering businesses with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC).
  
 Speaking at the launch, Team Lead Ajoke Adeola highlighted the importance of supporting women entrepreneurs who continue to navigate challenging business conditions.
 “It is truly a privilege and an honour to launch this programme and support the incredible work each of these women is doing. Running a business in Nigeria is no small feat, and we salute your courage, resilience, and commitment. We are excited to embark on this journey with you,” she said. Advertisement 
 
 
 
 The Mata A Farka Incubator Programme forms part of a capstone project developed by 16 students of the 2026 School of Politics, Policy and Governance (SPPG) Programme.
 Training sessions will be held every Saturday morning in a hybrid format and will cover essential business topics including financial literacy and money management, pricing and taxation, marketing and digital visibility, as well as business sustainability and resilience.
  
 Beyond training, the initiative offers participants access to mentorship opportunities, with every entrepreneur paired with a mentor throughout the programme. Upon completion, participants will also gain access to a pool of donated funds and will be encouraged to establish a voluntary cooperative savings group to support long-term business growth.
   Advertisement 
 
 
 
 For many women-led MSMEs, access to business knowledge, mentorship, formal registration, and financing remains a major barrier to growth. Programs such as Mata A Farka are increasingly emerging as important platforms for helping women entrepreneurs build stronger, more resilient businesses capable of creating jobs and contributing to local economic development.
