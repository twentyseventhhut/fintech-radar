Google Pay has introduced a new feature called “Pocket Money” under its UPI Circle framework, aiming to reshape how families manage digital payments and allowances. The feature is designed to let primary account holders—typically parents or guardians—extend controlled UPI access to secondary users such as children or dependents, without requiring them to have their own bank accounts.
With this setup, the secondary user can make UPI transactions using the primary user’s linked bank account, but within strict limits defined by the account owner. The system is built to bring structure and supervision into everyday digital spending while still offering a degree of independence to younger or first-time users.

The feature offers two distinct modes of control. In one, the primary user can set a monthly spending cap for the secondary user, reportedly up to Rs 15,000, allowing them to transact freely within that limit. In the other, a more tightly controlled approval-based system requires every transaction initiated by the secondary user to be approved in real time by the primary account holder before money is debited.
Once onboarded into a UPI Circle, the secondary user can scan QR codes, make merchant payments, and initiate transactions across UPI-supported platforms, with the primary user receiving instant notifications and oversight depending on the selected mode of control. The experience is designed to mirror real-world allowance systems, but in a fully digital and trackable format.

The launch reflects a broader shift in India’s digital payments ecosystem, where platforms like Google Pay are evolving beyond simple transaction tools into full-fledged financial management systems. By introducing controlled access features, the company is also tapping into a growing user base of teenagers and young users who are increasingly interacting with digital payments but may not yet have independent banking access.
Positioned within the UPI Circle framework, Pocket Money effectively turns everyday allowances into a monitored digital experience, reinforcing Google Pay’s push to blend convenience, safety, and financial learning within its ecosystem.
