# Nubank shortlisted to acquire Caixa Geral's Brazilian unit

Nubank is among four finalists bidding to acquire the Brazilian subsidiary of Caixa Geral de Depósitos (CGD), Portugal’s state-owned bank. The information was disclosed by the Portuguese government in its official gazette. The other bidders in the competitive process are Garantia Capital, MD Capital, and Sputnik. 
 Contacted for comment, Nubank said it has already informed the market of its intention to obtain a banking license in Brazil this year and, in that context, has been evaluating different alternatives to achieve that goal. 
 “These assessments do not imply a decision regarding any specific transaction. As a publicly listed company, we are committed to transparency and will inform the market when any relevant decision is made,” the company said in a statement. 
 Nubank has previously said it is considering either acquiring an existing banking license or applying for one from scratch, but no final decision has been made. 
 CGD had indicated last year that it expected to receive bids for its Brazilian unit by early 2026, with the transaction likely to be completed only in 2027, largely due to the time required for regulatory approvals. 
 According to IFData, a database maintained by Brazil’s Central Bank, the subsidiary reported total assets of R$1.964 billion as of September last year, including a loan portfolio of R$866.3 million. Shareholders’ equity stood at R$300.3 million.
