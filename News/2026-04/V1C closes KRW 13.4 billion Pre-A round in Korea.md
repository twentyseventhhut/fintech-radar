With participation from Base Ventures, DSC Investment, and others, it demonstrates the competitiveness of its B2B finance offerings, including ‘ClovAI’ and capital-raising solutions
V1C, a fintech startup that operates financial management and capital-raising solutions for businesses, said it has successfully closed a Pre-A funding round totaling KRW 13.4 billion.
In this round, major domestic venture capital firms such as Base Ventures and DSC Investment led the participation. The investors said they highly value V1C’s operational know-how in financial and accounting software, as well as its ability to strengthen capacity for stable loan disbursement and risk management through its financial platform. Despite being in the early stage, V1C secured funding of an unusually large size, earning recognition for its potential in the B2B finance market.
V1C operates ClovAI , a software-as-a-service for financial management that helps corporations and individual business owners manage their funds efficiently. ClovAI integrates companies’ various bank account, credit card, and tax invoice data into a single system, providing real-time cash-flow statements and profit-and-loss analysis features. Within a year and a half of launching, the service has secured more than 10,000 corporate customers and is showing a steep growth trajectory.
In addition, V1C is also smoothly expanding beyond simply providing financial management software into a comprehensive financial platform that addresses companies’ capital-raising challenges. Clov Finance , which V1C operates, has already surpassed a cumulative loan amount of 110 billion won, contributing to the seamless flow of funds for small business owners.
Meanwhile, V1C is stepping up efforts to improve the financial environment for small and mid-sized enterprises and self-employed individuals based on a business agreement it signed with Hana Financial Group in March 2026. The two sides are working closely not only to link financial products and conduct joint marketing, but also to develop advanced B2B financial services such as research into AI-based credit evaluation models.
Do Eun-uk, CEO of V1C, said, “Unlike the B2C market where innovation is happening actively, the B2B market still looks like a financial wilderness.” He added, “We believe that only when the overall operating environment for small businesses is transformed with innovation will growth in the Korean economy accelerate, and we will do our best to further enhance our services moving forward.”
