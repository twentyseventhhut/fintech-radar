BlackFin Tech Weekly — April 20th, 2026
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity.
Hello FinTech Friends,
Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry!
 Over the last week, there were ten fintech deals in Europe, totaling €31.2m in disclosed funding, including four transactions in the UK, two in Italy, one in Stockholm, one in Denmark, one in Ireland and one in Switzerland. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Spektr , a Denmark-based company, has raised €17m in a Series A round led by NEA . 
 Wamo , a Finland- and UK-based company, has raised €10.0m in a Series A round led by 3TS Capital Partners . 
 Round , a UK-based company , has raised €5.1m in a seed round led by Alstin Capital . 
 Let’s dive! 
 Spektr 
 Spektr , a Denmark-based company, raised €17m in a Series A round led by NEA , with participation from Northzone , Seedcamp , and PSV Tech . The company provides an AI-driven compliance platform that automates KYC and KYB workflows through specialized AI agents, enabling financial institutions to streamline onboarding and monitoring processes. The funding will support platform development and international expansion, as Spektr aims to modernize compliance operations in financial services. 
 Read more 
 Wamo 
 Wamo , a Finland- and UK-based company , raised €10m in a Series A round led by 3TS Capital Partners (via TCEE Fund IV), with participation from Oleka Capital and existing investors. The company provides a financial operating platform for SMEs, combining multi-currency accounts, payments, invoicing, and embedded lending within a unified system. The funding will support European expansion, product development, and the rollout of AI-driven financial tools, as Wamo aims to enhance financial infrastructure for small businesses. 
 Read more 
 Round 
 Round , a UK-based company , raised €5.1m in a seed round led by Alstin Capital , with participation from Backed VC , Love Ventures , Passion Capital , and angel investors. The company provides an AI-powered finance automation platform combining treasury, payments, and payroll infrastructure to streamline financial operations for businesses. The funding will support product development, team expansion, and platform scaling, as Round aims to automate repetitive finance workflows for modern finance teams.

 In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 Synctera , a US-based embedded finance platform, has acquired Cable , a UK-based compliance control testing and verification provider. The acquisition strengthens Synctera’s compliance capabilities by integrating Cable’s automated control-testing technology, enabling continuous monitoring and validation of regulatory processes across fintech partners. The transaction supports Synctera’s strategy to enhance oversight and risk management in embedded finance, as regulatory requirements increase for sponsor banks and fintech ecosystems. 
 Read more 
 Kivra , a Sweden-based digital mailbox and document platform, has acquired Tab , a Sweden-based provider of API-driven smart receipt infrastructure. The acquisition combines Kivra’s large-scale distribution platform, used by over 6 million users, with Tab’s technology enabling automatic, transaction-linked digital receipts. The transaction supports Kivra’s strategy to expand its digital document ecosystem and accelerate the adoption of fully digital, automated receipts at scale.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 SumUp is appointing Deutsche Bank , Goldman Sachs , Jefferies and JPMorgan for a potential London IPO, with STJ Advisors as financial adviser. The company could fetch a valuation of $10bn+, down from the $15bn initially floated. Advisers recommended a European exchange over the US given SumUp ’s limited North American footprint, bucking the trend of fintechs like Klarna listing stateside. Proceeds would fund acquisitions amid expected European payments consolidation. A successful listing would provide a rare boost to the LSE , still recovering from a prolonged IPO drought. SumUp serves over 4m customers across 36 countries and last raised €590m from Bain Capital in 2022. 
 Read more 
 Deutsche Börse Group has acquired a 1.5% stake in Kraken parent Payward Inc for $200m, valuing the crypto exchange at roughly $13.3bn. This marks a drop from the $20bn valuation Kraken reached last November. The investment deepens an existing partnership between the two firms. Kraken is already integrating with 360T , Deutsche Börse ’s FX trading subsidiary, with plans to expand into custody, settlement, trading, collateral management, and tokenised assets. The deal comes as Kraken prepares for a potential US IPO, having filed a Form S-1 with the SEC last November. For Deutsche Börse , the move follows its €5.3bn agreement to acquire wealthtech platform Allfunds .

 Hokodo , a European B2B BNPL provider founded in 2018, has announced it is shutting down after raising approximately USD 177 million across equity and debt from backers including Anthemis Group , Notion Capital , and Viola Credit . The company financed over USD 590 million in invoices and served 100,000+ buyers across ten countries, but scaled too early and took on excessive product complexity before sufficiently validating its model. The closure reflects broader challenges in B2B trade credit, where sustainable unit economics have proved harder to achieve than expected. The founders are now launching Liquidity Lab , a consulting firm focused on optimising trade credit and cash flow operations using AI.

 European FinTech deal activity reached 273 transactions in Q1 2026 , its highest level since Q1 2025, up 5% YoY and 13% QoQ, signalling renewed momentum even as funding volumes moderated. The UK reinforced its dominance as Europe’s leading FinTech hub, capturing 38% of all deals (103 transactions), up from 34% a year earlier. France moved into second with 30 deals, overtaking Germany (22 deals). While capital raised fell to $3.7bn (down 31% YoY), the rise in deal count points to a healthier pipeline of earlier-stage and mid-sized rounds. Among the quarter’s standout deals, French digital health insurer Alan raised $116m at a $5.8bn valuation, reporting ARR of $915m (up 53% YoY) and targeting $1.2bn for 2026.
