# CarePay Raises Funds to Solve Healthcare Financing Gap

**CarePay** is addressing a critical gap in **India’s healthcare system** by enabling patients to finance elective treatments through AI-driven monthly installment solutions.

The idea emerged after its founders engaged with over 200 patients and numerous doctors, discovering that **l**iquidity constraints—not lack of demand—were the primary reason patients delayed treatments.

In India, most elective procedures such as IVF, LASIK, and dental treatments are not covered by insurance, forcing patients to pay upfront.

This creates a significant barrier, leading to over 60% of middle-income patients postponing or skipping care. CarePay solves this by embedding an **AI-powered financing platform**directly within clinics, allowing patients to convert treatment costs into zero-cost EMIs at the point of care.

**Key Highlights**

*   _CarePay enables zero-cost EMIs for elective healthcare with AI-driven approvals_
*   _Targets $40–60B elective healthcare market with low NPAs under 1%_
*   _Achieved 7x growth in 9 months and raised $2.2M seed funding_

The platform offers **instant approvals within 42 seconds**, leveraging a **multi-lender marketplace** that aggregates financing options to achieve approval rates of around 40%. Its **healthcare-specific underwriting model**, which evaluates treatment type and clinic-level data, ensures better risk assessment and keeps **non-performing assets (NPAs) below 1%**.

India’s healthcare market is valued at $370 billion, with the elective segment representing a $40–60 billion opportunity. CarePay estimates a $13–15 billion annual financing market, driven by over 100,000 specialty clinics serving around 180 eligible patients annually at an average treatment cost of $720.

The founding team includes **Gaurav Gupta (CEO)**, **Nupur Khandelwal (COO)**, and **Nikhil Salkar (CPO)**, bringing expertise in healthcare, finance, and product development. The company has achieved a $9 million annualized run rate, with 7x growth in nine months, and monthly disbursals rising from $530,000 to $3.7 million.

Operating on a take-rate model (8–15% gross, ~4% net), CarePay differentiates itself from BNPL players like Simpl and LazyPay through its healthcare-focused approach. It has raised $2.2 million in**seed funding** and is preparing for a growth capital round.
