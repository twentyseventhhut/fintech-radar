# GoHenry to expand into Europe with acquisition of Pixpay

ByAlex Clere

July 12, 2022

2 mins

Share this article

**Prioritise Us** on Google

Share this article

**Prioritise Us** on Google

GoHenry, the money management app designed to teach children about finances, has entered Europe for the first time after acquiring French rival Pixpay.

Financial literacy app GoHenry has acquired the French fintech Pixpay, allowing it to expand into Europe for the first time.

Pixpay has established itself as a leader in the European teen banking space, accumulating 200,000 members. It launched in France two years ago before rolling out in Spain last year. It also plans to expand its product offering into Italy and Germany.

GoHenry, which uses smart banking to teach children about money, says that both companies would continue to operate under their separate brands and be led by their existing leadership teams. It promised to work together on product development and accelerating both GoHenry and Pixpay’s goal to transform financial education.

## Pixpay ‘excited about what the future holds’

Announcing the news in a statement, GoHenry says it is “excited to empower even more young people with the money management skills they need to thrive in today’s digital economy”.

Alex Zivoder, CEO at GoHenry, says: “Pixpay is the most developed player in Europe and we’re excited to combine our expertise in financial education to accelerate not only GoHenry’s growth but to accelerate the financial fitness of even more kids and teens globally.”

Benoit Grassin, CEO at Pixpay, adds: “We are delighted to be joining the GoHenry Group as we prepare to accelerate Pixpay’s expansion across Europe. GoHenry’s experience and heritage will only serve to strengthen the already strong proposition offered by Pixpay. We look forward to building a strong, long-term relationship with GoHenry and we are excited about what the future holds.”

## Scope to build out an already strong audience

GoHenry was launched in the UK ten years ago and has built an audience of more than 2 million members. In March, the app launched its first Junior ISA to allow parents to put money away for when their child turns 18. At the time, it said the new proposition would let parents save for their child’s future or set money aside for a first car or university expenses.

Its recent Youth Economy Report shows that young people are increasingly recognising the importance of financial education and money literacy. Three-quarters of those surveyed by GoHenry believe that having good money management skills will help with their future career.

# Re(AI)magine your Workforce with KPMG

By Lucy Buchholz

May 01, 2026

3 mins

Share this article

**Prioritise Us** on Google

Share this article

**Prioritise Us** on Google

The Shift to AI Moving Beyond Productivity and Toward a Total Business Reimagination

AI is a workforce redesign, not a tech rollout. HR must build human and digital teams to deliver value at scale

## From workforce enablement to business transformation, organisations are navigating a seismic shift in how work gets done

As AI becomes embedded into daily processes, it is moving beyond productivity tools and reshaping decision-making, collaboration and performance. This shift is pushing organisations beyond incremental improvements toward total business reimagination – where silos collapse and work centres on critical thinking, problem-solving and collaboration.

## What’s really happening

A gap exists between AI ambition and execution. Many organisations fall into common traps: vision paralysis, tools-first adoption and overwhelming employees with disconnected initiatives. AI that is not fully ingrained into workflows struggles to scale.

Progress requires clarity of vision and co-creation of employee experiences. Aligning AI to real work – where employees experience friction or decision overload – builds trust and reduces anxiety. Concurrently, CHROs face a dual mandate: enabling enterprise transformation while disrupting their own function to lead the way.

## Reimagine, Reinvent, Realise

Reimagine: Innovate work to drive growth

 Go beyond static roles. Clarify what differentiates your business and define the capabilities required across leaders, employees and AI. Redesign work itself – redefining performance, organisational structures and career experiences for a world where humans and AI collaborate. Data-driven insights from technology like KPMG Mystro can support modelling the evolving mix of human and digital labour as organisations design an AI × Human workforce aligned to strategic priorities.

Reinvent: Operate smarter to deliver results

 As agentic labour emerges, distinctions between employees and non-employees begin to break down. HR, payroll and learning must evolve into a cohesive Total Workforce Management capability—enabled by automation, personalisation and horizontal integration across legacy functions. This is not simply a mindset shift; it is a new operational muscle that must be built.

Realise: Accelerate change to capture value

 To begin, MUFG joined forces with KPMG LLP to modernise its core HR platforms and refine its operating model, thereby establishing the digital foundation capable of supporting ongoing workforce evolution.

In progressing that workforce evolution, MUFG and KPMG know what taking care of people in a time of disruptive change looks like. Every year, as MUFG moves its executives and their families to new countries around the world, it looks to KPMG to make it as seamless as possible and deeply human. We bring an empathetic and highly orchestrated approach so MUFG’s leaders can stay focused on the business, no matter where they call home.

Now we’ve turned that same capability on the next set of disruptive changes happening across the industry. We believe that winning with AI is not about platforms alone – it is about engaged people. A human-centric approach builds trust and momentum. Shifting focus from ROI to ROX (Return on Experience) helps leaders assess whether work is becoming better, easier and more meaningful, often a leading indicator of long-term value.

Value emerges when leaders articulate a clear business imperative for AI and co-create that vision with employees closest to the work. Empathetic, curious leadership – supported by practical, day-in-the-life guidance – sustains adoption.
