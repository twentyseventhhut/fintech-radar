# PayU India FY25 revenue rises 24% to $669 million

*   PayU India’s overall revenues increased by 24% to $669 million for the financial year ending March 2025, while adjusted EBIT (earnings before interest and taxes) deteriorated slightly to -7% as compared to -5.8% in the previous year. Adjusted EBIT loss increased by 38% to $44 million in FY25 from $32 million in FY24.

*   Revenue from payment services increased by 12% to $498 million in FY25. The company said its total payment volume (TPV) increased by 14%, which indicates that it has reached over $80 billion.

*   “India Payments witnessed steady progress in profitability despite competitive intensity and a higher unified payments interface (UPI) mix, resulting in lower take rates. Despite these headwinds, India Payments reached breakeven during 2H25 and improved its adjusted EBIT margin by 1% to -2% for FY25,” said Prosus, which owns PayU India completely, in its annual report.

*   To accelerate business growth, PayU India has “reorganised the payments business with dedicated teams focusing on key account management, acquiring new customers in existing segments, and forging new partnerships.”

*   PayU India’s lending business improved its income by 60% to $171 million during the year, much faster than last year's growth of 29%. The adjusted EBIT loss in this business also increased by 70% to $34 million compared to the previous year.

*   In FY25, the lending unit issued loans of $1.1 billion, registering a 23% rise. The loan book at the end of March 2025 stood at $558 million, a 19% increase compared to a year earlier. Prosus said that PayU India has pivoted the "credit strategy to house partnerships, lending at checkout (consumers) and diversification from unsecured consumer to SMB lending."

*   “Revenue grew 60%, supported by growth in AuM and diversification to SMB lending, while the aEBIT margin at -19% was driven by increased costs linked to higher financial leverage and larger losses from the consumer loan book. In response, the business strengthened underwriting practices, which resulted in improved performance in loans issued thereafter, better underlining the business’ adaptability and long-term potential,” said the company in the report.

*   PayU India also disclosed that it spent $68 million to acquire a 70% stake in Mindgate Solutions, which is expected to improve its checkout business.

*   PayU recently expanded its board of directors with three new appointments: former RBI deputy governor Subhash Mundra and Manoj Kumar Agarwal, co-founder and president of AI company DevRev, who will join as non-executive independent directors, while Ashutosh Sharma, head of investments and M&A for India at Prosus Ventures, will serve as a non-executive non-independent director.
