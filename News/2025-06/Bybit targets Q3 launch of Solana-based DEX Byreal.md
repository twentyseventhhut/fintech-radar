# Bybit targets Q3 launch of Solana-based DEX Byreal

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[Connecting the Dots in Fintech] 🇸🇬 Bybit targets Q3 launch of new Solana-based DEX Byreal. It aims to release its new Solana-based decentralized exchange, Byreal, with a testnet version coming out at the end of June. Byreal was designed with RFQ (Request for Quote) and CLMM (Concentrated Liquidity Market Maker) routing.

Bybit to launch Byreal, its first onchain DEX on Solana, on June 30 

 The firm aims to bridge centralized and decentralized finance for seamless, efficient trading experiences.

Crypto exchange Bybit announced Sunday that it will launch Byreal, its first on-chain DEX, by the end of this month. Powered by Solana, the new platform aims to deliver the best of centralized exchange and decentralized finance, which Bybit CEO Ben Zhou touts as “real hybrid finance.”

As noted in the announcement , Byreal will feature a unified liquidity model that utilizes requesting-for-quote (RFQ) and Concentrated Liquidity Market Maker (CLMM) routing to provide low-slippage trades and MEV protection while maintaining high transaction speeds.
The new DEX will also include features such as a fair launchpad model with Smart Price Ladder and Fairshare Engine, as well as curated yield vaults including bbSOL, a SOL-based Liquid Staking Token (LST).
 Bybit launched bbSOL last September in collaboration with Solana ecosystem partners, including Sanctum, Kamino Finance, Orca, and Solayer. The token was created to bridge Bybit’s CEX and web3 platforms, enabling users who stake SOL on Bybit Web3 to receive bbSOL tokens.
Bybit plans to launch Byreal’s testnet on June 30, with mainnet deployment expected in Q3 2025.
The platform represents Bybit’s entry into hybrid finance, with plans for future CEX and DEX project launches. The exchange will be accessible through byreal.io.
A known advocate of hybrid DeFi models, Zhou previously promoted a blend of traditional and DeFi systems, stating that the future of finance lies not at either extreme, but in the growing middle ground where conventional and blockchain-based systems increasingly converge.
The upcoming launch of Byreal follows  Bybit’s shutdown of numerous web3 services  last month, including its Cloud Wallet, Keyless Wallet, NFT marketplace, DEX Pro, and Swap & Bridge, with all closures scheduled for May 31.
The exchange also discontinued Web3 Points, its loyalty program, along with several other services such as the fiat-to-crypto on-ramp and initial DEX offering service.
Those terminations are part of Bybit’s strategy to refine its focus on core product quality, following a major $1.4 billion loss due to a hack.

Bybit will launch its first on-chain decentralized exchange, Byreal, on June 30 with a testnet rollout. The new platform will run on Solana and serve as a major part of Bybit’s shift to hybrid finance. The mainnet launch is scheduled for the third quarter of 2025.
Byreal Introduces Hybrid Model for Trading
 Byreal combines centralized exchange speed with decentralized finance features to create a unified trading platform. It will use a hybrid liquidity model based on RFQ and CLMM routing to enable efficient trading. This approach aims to reduce slippage and protect against MEV while ensuring fast transaction speeds.

The platform also introduces a fair launchpad and a set of curated vaults designed to support ecosystem participation. These include tools such as the Smart Price Ladder and Fairshare Engine, which help ensure equal opportunity access. Through this system, Byreal targets balanced rewards and stronger user engagement across multiple pools.
Bybit Expands Staking With bbSOL Token
Byreal integrates bbSOL, a liquid staking token built on Solana that Bybit launched in September 2024. Users who stake SOL on Bybit’s Web3 platform receive bbSOL, allowing cross-platform access and liquidity. The token’s design aims to link Bybit’s CEX users with DeFi tools while increasing SOL staking options.
Bybit developed bbSOL with support from Solana ecosystem partners including Sanctum, Kamino Finance, Orca, and Solayer. This collaboration reflects Bybit’s commitment to enhancing product interoperability within the Solana network. It also marks a strategic move to provide seamless staking solutions across centralized and decentralized environments.
Web3 Service Closures and Future Plans
The Byreal launch follows Bybit’s decision to end several Web3 services by May 31 as part of product refocusing. Services including Cloud Wallet, Keyless Wallet, NFT marketplace , DEX Pro, and Swap & Bridge were all shut down. Bybit also discontinued its Web3 loyalty program, on-ramp services, and initial DEX offerings.
The exchange is now aligning its focus on high-performance hybrid finance solutions with fewer but stronger product offerings. This transition comes after a reported $1.4 billion hack loss that forced a broader strategic pivot. Users can access the new exchange through its official portal, byreal.io.
