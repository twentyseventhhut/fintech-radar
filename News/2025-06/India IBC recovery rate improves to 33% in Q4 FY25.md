IBC Recovery Rate Inches Up To 32.76% In Q4FY25
 # IBC 
 # Recovery Rate 
The overall recovery rate under India’s Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC) rose marginally to 32.76 per cent in the fourth quarter of FY25 from 31.39 per cent in the previous quarter, according to data from CareEdge Ratings. While the uptick suggests progress in resolution outcomes, the average haircut remains steep, with creditors recovering less than one-third of their admitted claims.
A key indicator of IBC’s improving performance is the rising resolution-to-liquidation ratio, which increased from 0.20 in FY18 to 1.89 in Q4FY25. This shift implies that whereas only one company was resolved for every five liquidated in FY18, nearly two are now being resolved for every five liquidations. The trend underscores enhanced efficiency in the insolvency process, though long-standing delays in resolution and liquidation timelines continue to impact financial and operational creditors.
Despite signs of recovery, resolution remains elusive in many cases. As of March 2025, only 14.4 per cent of admitted Corporate Insolvency Resolution Processes (CIRPs) have culminated in approved resolution plans, while 33.2 per cent have ended in liquidation. Among those liquidated, nearly 78 per cent were defunct or legacy cases transferred from the erstwhile Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction (BIFR). About 13.9 per cent were withdrawn under Section 12A following settlements, and 15.4 per cent were closed through appeals, reviews, or settlements.
Case admissions under CIRP increased by around 10 per cent year-on-year in Q4FY25. Yet, total new admissions for the full fiscal year remained under 1,000—well below FY20 pre-pandemic levels. The number of cases initiated by corporate debtors has declined significantly, and even operational creditors are filing fewer cases compared to previous years.
Since the IBC’s implementation in 2016, over 8,300 cases have been admitted. Financial and operational creditors have each initiated nearly half of these, while corporate debtors account for the smallest share.
The total number of ongoing CIRPs remains stable at around 1,900 cases, a figure that has held steady for the past few years. In terms of sectoral distribution, manufacturing continues to lead with 37 per cent of cases, followed by real estate at 22 per cent, construction at 12 per cent, and wholesale and retail trade at 10 per cent.
