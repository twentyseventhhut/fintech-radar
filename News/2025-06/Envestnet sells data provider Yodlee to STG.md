Envestnet is offloading Yodlee to private equity buyer back in Silicon Valley, which values its 'trusted data access and intelligent analytics'
Under new CEO and new owner, the Berwyn, Pa,, wealth platform is liquidating the account aggregation unit and maintaining long term license agreements
 Envestnet , Inc.'s new CEO and new ownership group are finally selling Yodlee and ending a 10-year $600-million misadventure, bent on a new vision that hews more strictly to financial advice. 
The Berwyn, Pa., firm announced at noon ET it had agreed to sell the original account aggregation provider back to Silicon Valley ownership. It's the biggest strategic move by the company since Bain Capital took Envestnet private. 
 Yodlee was seen as an impediment to the deal that has now finally been resolved.See: Bain Capital may be set to buy Envestnet, Reuters reports; Sudden RIA industry mega PE player must find CEO, resolve Yodlee riddle to move forward 
STG, formerly Symphony Technology Group, a private-equity firm in Menlo Park, Calif., – just down Rt. 101 in Silicon Valley – is paying an undisclosed sum for Yodlee, based in Redwood City, the county seat of San Mateo County in the heart of the tech-heavy region. 
The purchase follows Yodlee's write-down to zero for accounting purposes; Envestnet acquired it in 2015.  See: With Bain deal [all but] locked down, Envestnet writes off last $96 million of Yodlee 'goodwill,' and the dwindling unit's fate now awaits 'board' verdict 
Trusted brand
STG, which manages $12 billion – including SurveyMonkey in its portfolio – still sees considerable value in Yodlee , not least its inroads into the financial industry, and seems to like to refer to it by its founding brand.
“ Yodlee sits at the intersection of trusted data access and intelligent analytics,” said STG Managing Director Marc Bala. “It has built deep integrations across the financial ecosystem and earned the confidence of the world’s leading banks, asset managers, and wealth platforms. 
"We are excited to partner with the team to accelerate their investment in product innovation, customer success, and long-term growth. We welcome Yodlee to the STG family.”
Creating value
Envestnet CEO Chris Todd  is no less excited about getting Yodlee off of his plate as he prepares to disclose later this summer his master post-Bain plan for the company.
“This transaction is the first step in our value creation plan as a private company and will allow Envestnet to focus more deeply on its core connected wealth management platform, advanced insights and comprehensive solutions,” he said in a release.
Yodlee remains a key account aggregation partner with Tamarac and MoneyGuidePro – typically to create a full wealth picture that includes assets “held-away” at other institutions.
 Envestnet need not own the Yodlee cow to get the milk of aggregated asset holdings data.
“Through our continued partnership with Yodlee , advisors will retain access to Yodlee data aggregation technology that helps power their business,” Todd adds.
Getting a lift
 Yodlee lost its edge as Plaid and other more modern aggregators came along. See: With Anil Arora gone, Jud Bergman comes clean with Wall Street about the need for cleaner, more API-friendly Yodlee data -- causing investors to pare Envestnet shares by 6% 
The private equity firm plans to give it a lift, the Envestnet release says.
“STG intends to leverage its transformation experience to help Yodlee deliver increased value to clients, attract and retain top talent, and achieve best-in-class business performance. 
"With a focus on customer-centric innovation, the firms aim to rapidly invest in technologies and deliver enhanced solutions to the financial sector.”
