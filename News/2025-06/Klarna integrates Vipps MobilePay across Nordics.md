Klarna blir nytt betalsätt i Vipps och MobilePay i hela Norden
November 11, 2025 - 3 min read
 Stockholm, 11 november 2025 – Klarna, den globala digitala banken och betalningstjänsten, breddar nu sitt nordiska samarbete med Vipps MobilePay. Från och med nu kan användare i Norge, Danmark och Finland välja Klarna som betalsätt direkt i Vipps och MobilePay - enklast beskrivet som de lokala motsvarigheterna till svenska Swish. 
 Samarbetet innebär att konsumenter kan lägga till Klarna som betalmetod i Vipps- och MobilePay-apparna. Betalningen sker sedan lika enkelt som med övriga betalsätt, och användaren kan välja att betala med Klarna inom 30 dagar eller dela upp köpet. Sedan tidigare har Vipps MobilePay integrerats som betalsätt genom Klarna både i appen och hos butiker i Norge, Danmark och Finland. 
 För handlare i de tre länderna krävs inga ändringar. Alla butiker som redan tar emot betalningar via Vipps eller MobilePay får automatiskt stöd för Klarna-betalningar – till samma pris och med samma snabba utbetalning som tidigare. 
 ”Det här är ett naturligt nästa steg i vårt samarbete. Vipps MobilePay är ett väldigt populärt betalsätt i våra nordiska grannländer, och det har redan integrerats som en del av Klarnas app och betalsätt i kassan hos våra handlare. Nu kan konsumenter som använder deras digitala plånbok betala med Klarna när de vill utan att de anslutna handlarna behöver göra ytterligare en integration”, säger Björn Bryngelson, Nordenchef på Klarna. 
 Vipps MobilePay, som idag har över 12 miljoner aktiva användare i Norden, ser samarbetet som ett sätt att ge användarna fler valmöjligheter. 
 ”För oss handlar allt om valfrihet. Vi vill bygga en digital plånbok som ger människor frihet att välja hur de vill betala. Det finns över 2,5 miljoner Klarna-användare bara i Norge, så det är naturligt att inkludera Klarna i Vipps. Vipps erbjuder inga egna kreditprodukter, men vi samarbetar med aktörer som Klarna och olika kortutgivare för att ge användarna fler alternativ och göra vardagen enklare för både konsumenter och handlare”, säger Rune Garborg, vd på Vipps MobilePay. 
 Lanseringen rullas just nu ut stegvis i Norge, Danmark och Finland.

 Europe Pushes For Seamless Payments: Klarna, BLIK And iDEAL→Wero Accelerate Interoperability 
Europe’s payments landscape is shifting from a patchwork of national systems toward greater interoperability, driven by commercial integrations, pilot projects, and a regulatory push for instant, cross-border payments. Recent moves: Klarna’s deeper integration with Nordic wallets Vipps and MobilePay, Poland’s BLIK launching pilot cross-border P2P transfers, and the planned transition of the Netherlands’ iDEAL into the pan-European Wero wallet, together mark a clear move toward a more connected payments fabric across the continent.
 
 
This week, we have witnessed plenty of intra-industry cooperation examples in the European payment sector, which hint at a gradual transition from a fragmented, siloed approach to more integrated payment networks in the region.
Klarna has expanded its presence inside the widely used Nordic digital wallets Vipps and MobilePay, making its payment option available directly in those apps and thereby widening merchant and consumer choice across Norway, Denmark, and Finland. By embedding Klarna as a payment method inside local wallets, merchants that already accept Vipps or MobilePay can offer Klarna payments without additional integration work, increasing convenience for shoppers and extending Klarna’s reach in the Nordics.
At the same time, Poland’s BLIK, the country’s dominant mobile payment system,  has taken a decisive step to operate beyond national borders. At Web Summit 2025 BLIK demonstrated the first test peer-to-peer transaction routed between Portugal’s MB WAY and a Polish recipient using a BLIK-registered phone number, and announced a pilot to link with other European mobile payment systems to enable phone-number transfers across several markets. This technical cooperation could make instant P2P transfers much simpler for end users and reduce reliance on card rails for small payments.
Parallel to these private initiatives, the European push for unified payments has a high-profile anchor: the transition of iDEAL to Wero, a digital wallet built around the European Payments Initiative. iDEAL’s phasing into Wero, set to begin in 2026, signals banks’ renewed appetite to build a European alternative to global card networks and to standardise on a wallet that can operate cross-border and across channels. Industry reports show Wero’s expanding footprint as several banks prepare launches and migrations into 2026.
Why Interoperability Matters, And Why Now
 Interoperability reduces friction for consumers and merchants, enables cheaper real-time settlement, and makes it easier for new entrants to scale beyond national borders. It also supports policy goals: regulators and industry bodies have been accelerating instant payments standards and rulebooks to make real-time euro transfers ubiquitous and secure across SEPA, creating both the technical baseline and regulatory impetus for cross-border cooperation. The European Payments Council’s 2025 SEPA Instant rulebook and recent EU initiatives highlight that standardisation is becoming a legal and operational priority. 
Challenges on the Road to a Single Payment Fabric
 Despite momentum, meaningful obstacles remain. Europe’s payments market is fragmented by legacy infrastructure, national governance models and competing commercial incentives: local schemes protect domestic user bases while banks and card networks weigh the economics of switching rails. 
 Technically, aligning messaging standards, settlement windows, fraud-prevention measures and confirmation-of-payee systems across many providers is complex and costly. Regulatory requirements such as the Instant Payments Regulation and mandatory verification checks add compliance overhead and require system upgrades across thousands of PSPs and banks. Finally, cross-border UX consistency and data-privacy considerations (e.g., differing national interpretations of data sharing and consent) must be solved to avoid friction for consumers. 
What to Watch Next
Momentum from Klarna, BLIK and the iDEAL to Wero migration suggests interoperability will proceed as a mix of bottom-up commercial linkages and top-down standardisation. Early pilots and wallet integrations will test the user experience and fraud controls. However, their commercial success will hinge on seamless UX, clear settlement mechanics and convincing cost economics for merchants and banks. If pilots convert into live, scaled services and regulatory rulebooks align, consumers across Europe could soon see simpler, faster and cheaper payments, whether they’re using a local mobile app or a pan-European wallet.
