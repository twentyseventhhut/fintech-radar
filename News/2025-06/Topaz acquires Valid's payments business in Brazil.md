Topaz acquires Valid's financial transaction operation in Colombia 
 This press release was published in English using an automatic translation system 
By Stefanini 
Bogotá and São Paulo, May 2025 - Crossing borders and influencing the adoption of new banking transaction systems in Latin America, Topaz , one of the largest technology companies specializing in digital financial solutions in the world, announces the purchase for US$7 million (R$39.8 million) of 100% of Valid's payment business unit in Colombia, focused exclusively on payment solutions and digital financial transactions.
With over 65 years of experience, the Brazilian multinational Valid is present in 15 countries and has over 3,000 employees. It operates in several segments such as ID & Digital Government, Payment Methods and Secure Connectivity. With the sale of Valid Colombia's payment operation to Topaz, Valid will continue to operate normally by offering solutions for other business areas.
Topaz, a Stefanini Group company, is intensifying its efforts to incorporate new solutions into its already robust platform and is in line with its main objective: to offer the financial market technology to promote the evolution and growth of its customers, supporting them at each stage of their digital transformation journeys, through a more complete platform specialized in digital financial solutions. In addition, with the acquisition of Valid's payments business unit in Colombia, Topaz adds even more value to its performance in the evolution of business in an increasingly competitive segment hungry for innovation.
Created in 2015, Valid Colombia specializes in highly customizable products and software for payment and transaction solutions. With a select number of clients in the banking, transportation, real estate and restaurant sectors, the division has served some of the most important companies in Colombia. Banco Davivienda is one of its main clients in the instant payments operation in that country.
"The acquisition of Valid Colombia's payments business unit, which transacts 3 million payments per month, marks our entry into instant payments in Latin America. Following Brazil, which is at the forefront of this movement with the successful consolidation of Pix, created by the Central Bank, Colombia is experiencing a moment of disruption in the financial market in which instant payments have great potential for growth. We are fostering the regional financial ecosystem to make digital banking services more accessible to everyone," says Jorge Iglesias, CEO of Topaz.
According to the Banco de La República (BanRep), considered the country’s central bank, there are 56 financial institutions that can participate in the instant payments system. When it was launched in 2019, Transfiya, also known as YA Transfers, had a low transaction volume (3.7%) compared to cash payments. With QR-based B2P and P2P functionality added to the system in 2023, Colombia is expected to become the second country in the region with the highest growth in real-time payments volume in Latin America (Spanish-speaking countries only), after Peru, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 42.6%.
With the acquisition, Topaz will expand its offering and customer base, strengthening its leadership position in the financial solutions market in Latin America, in addition to the geographic expansion, part of Topaz's growth plan, and the expansion of the company's portfolio. Revenue from Valid Pay solutions is expected to grow by approximately 20% by the end of this year. "With the possibility of expanding to other countries, such as El Salvador, Mexico, Panama and Peru, this percentage could be even higher," says Iglesias, highlighting that Topaz will announce other global acquisitions in the second half of 2025, which will be essential for its projected growth for this year. "I am very familiar with Topaz's solutions and the quality of its deliveries. That is why I feel completely confident about the sale of our business unit in Colombia. The integration of Valid's unit into Topaz will enhance the company's full banking platform, a leader in the region, in addition to contributing to a market that is advancing in its interoperable instant payment system, while we focus our efforts on consolidating the Secure Identity platform in the Brazilian market", highlights Ilson Bressan, CEO of Valid. In Brazil, Topaz is present throughout the Pix transaction journey, with a portfolio of innovative products focused on increasing the security of transfers. According to Jorge Iglesias, the company is already testing automatic Pix features, with implementation scheduled for June 2025. "These steps are carried out by our systems, including our end-to-end anti-fraud security solution. Topaz is well ahead of these issues. Currently, large traditional and digital banks in the region use Topaz solutions," adds the executive, who is targeting R$1 billion in revenue this year. According to Topaz's CEO, there is growing collaboration and exchange of experiences between Latin American countries with the aim of establishing regional standards for instant payments. The idea is that interoperability protocols will facilitate integration between different instant transfer methods as a way of promoting and fostering the regional financial ecosystem.
 About Topaz 
With over three decades of experience, Topaz is one of the largest technology companies specializing in digital financial solutions, standing out as a leader in the Brazilian and Latin American markets. As part of the Stefanini group, it operates in over 25 countries with over 300 clients of different sizes and segments. Topaz developed Topaz One, the world's first full banking platform, offering a complete ecosystem of integrated, secure and high-availability solutions such as full banking core and core for microfinance, fraud prevention and combat, physical and digital channels, AML/FTP and compliance, instant payments, regulatory, investments and customer engagement for banks and financial institutions in general, impacting over 550 million end customers every day. Topaz positions itself as a reference in technology for digital business and is recognized by Gartner®, FORRESTER® and Celent.
