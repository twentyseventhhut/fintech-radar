# Pine Labs earns 71% of revenue from digital infrastructure

> [!info] 2025-06-28 · 1 упоминаний · 1 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: This Week in Fintech

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[This Week in Fintech] In the first nine months of FY25 till Dec, 2024, Pine Labs generated 71% of its revenue from its Digital Infrastructure and Transaction Platform. The remaining 29% came from its Issuing and Acquiring Platform.

*   Indian fintech firm Pine Labs filed its draft red herring prospectus (DRHP) with the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) on Wednesday, 25 June 2025, for its upcoming initial public offering (IPO), according to an exchange filing. The draft papers, released on Thursday, indicate that the IPO will comprise a fresh issue of shares worth Rs 2,600 crore and an offer-for-sale (OFS) of over 14.78 crore shares.

*   Pine Labs is a fintech company that offers full-stack payment solutions, including point-of-sale (POS) machines for card payments, and competes with players like Paytm and Walmart-owned PhonePe.

*   Pine Labs was last valued at $5 billion in 2022 when it raised funding from Alpha Wave Global and Vitruvian Partners. Since then, various reports have pegged the company is targeting a valuation of $6-8 billion for the IPO.

*   Peak XV is set to offload around 3.9 crore shares, making up approximately 26.3% of the total OFS. This marks a significant partial exit for one of Pine Labs’ earliest and most prominent backers. Actis Pine Labs Investment and Temasek will sell 1.49 crore and 1.48 crore shares, respectively, each accounting for around 10% of the total OFS.

*   Mastercard Asia Pacific, which joined the cap table in 2020, is offloading 1 crore shares, accounting for roughly 6.76% of the OFS. In May 2018, PayPal and Temasek Holdings invested $125 million in Pine Labs, acquiring minority stakes. PayPal is now selling 1.15 crore shares, representing around 7.7% of the OFS. Other institutional investors participating in the offer include AIM Investment Funds (Invesco) with 54.4 lakh shares, Madison India Opportunities IV with 51.1 lakh shares, and SBI-FMO Emerging Asia Fund, which is offloading 25.4 lakh shares.

*   Peak XV holds the largest external stake in Pine Labs at 20.35%, followed by Temasek with 7.1% and PayPal with 6%. Actis Pine Labs Investment owns 5.78%, while Mastercard and Alpha Wave hold 5.24% and 3.39%, respectively. Among individual shareholders, Amrish Rau holds 2.35%, and Lokvir Kapoor owns 1.97%, according to the DRHP.

*   Pine Labs plans to use Rs 870 crore from the fresh issue to repay or prepay borrowings. It will invest Rs 60 crore in its overseas subsidiaries and has allocated Rs 760 crore towards IT assets, including cloud infrastructure and other technology initiatives. The remaining proceeds will go towards general corporate purposes.’

*   As of the nine months ended December FY25, net profit stood at Rs 26.1 crore, compared to a loss of Rs 151.6 crore in the same period last year. Revenue from operations grew 23% year-on-year to Rs 1,208 crore during the same period. The company’s total expenses rose by 2.7%, to Rs 1,238.41 crore in the nine-month period ended December 31, 2024.

*   In the nine-month period ending December 31, 2024, Pine Labs processed payments worth Rs 7,53,105 crore in gross transaction value (GTV) across 3.97 billion transactions on its platforms. The company had 915,731 merchants, 666 consumer brands and enterprises, and 164 financial institutions.

*   Morgan Stanley, Citi Global, Axis Capital, JPMorgan India, and Jefferies are acting as the book-running lead managers for the offering. At the same time, KFin Technologies has been appointed as the registrar to the issue.

*   Pine Labs generates its revenue from two primary sources within its business. First, through its Digital Infrastructure and Transaction Platform, it provides tools for merchants to accept payments in-store and online, offer EMI and Buy Now Pay Later options, and utilise other services such as transaction processing and financial software.

*   Second, through its Issuing and Acquiring Platform, it helps brands and merchants create and manage prepaid products, such as gift cards, to boost sales. It also provides banks and financial institutions with the technology to offer credit, debit, and prepaid cards to their customers and helps them handle merchant payments.

*   In the first nine months of FY25, Pine Labs generated 70.6% of its revenue from its Digital Infrastructure and Transaction Platform, amounting to Rs 853.79 crore. The remaining 29.3%, or Rs 354.3 crore, came from its Issuing and Acquiring Platform.

*   **Diversifying revenues:** Since Amrish Rau was appointed CEO of Pine Labs in March 2020, the company has consciously sought to diversify its revenue and brand beyond the flagship POS terminals business—an offline play. It offers integrated payment solutions—card-based payments, QR codes, e-wallets, and UPI—and has recast itself as a merchant commerce platform.

*   A significant part of the diversification has come from acquisitions like Fave, a consumer payments and savings platform in Southeast Asia, in 2021, Setu (API infrastructure) to boost its presence in digital payments, and Mosambee in 2022. The latter owned Benow, a buy-now, pay-later (BNPL) startup. Consequently, Pine Labs’ online businesses—Plural (payment gateway), Setu (digital payments), and QFix (smart invoicing and billing tools)—continue to scale.

*   **Top-level changes:** In late August last year, Pine Labs announced an organisational restructuring. Chief Transformation Officer Sumit Chopra was promoted to COO – Pine Labs, Digital Payments, while Chief Business Officer Kush Mehra, who built the digital payments business from inception, is now responsible for expanding existing and new business opportunities in India and overseas markets such as Southeast Asia and the Middle East. Pine Labs’ Chief Revenue Officer Navnit Nakra quit towards the end of last year. Nakra’s stint lasted for one and a half years, following his role as the India CEO of Chinese smartphone brand OnePlus.

*   Qwikcilver, Pine Labs’ issuing business, is already present in 12 countries, notably in the Middle East and Southeast Asia. Pine Labs’ subsidiary, Pine Payment Solutions, expanded its BNPL technology offering to Southeast Asia through a strategic partnership with Mastercard.
