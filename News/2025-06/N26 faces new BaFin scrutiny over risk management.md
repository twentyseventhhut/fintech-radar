BaFin tightens supervisory grip on fintech N26
Claudia Pincovski
16 Dec 2025 / 4 Min Read
 BaFin has imposed a new set of supervisory measures on online bank N26 , intensifying regulatory pressure on the fintech. 
 The actions include restrictions on parts of N26’s business in the Netherlands and the appointment of a special monitor to oversee compliance and governance improvements. The move represents the second time since 2021 that BaFin has mandated direct supervisory oversight of the digital bank. 
 N26, which operates across multiple European markets under a German banking licence, has experienced a period of operational and governance instability. Over the past year, the lender has undergone several changes at both the management and supervisory board levels, reflecting ongoing efforts to address regulatory expectations and internal control challenges. The bank has indicated that it is maintaining active cooperation with supervisory authorities and the appointed special representative as part of the remediation process. 
 As part of the latest measures, BaFin has barred N26 from offering new mortgage products in the Netherlands. The restriction follows the findings of a special audit conducted in 2024, which identified significant weaknesses in several core areas of the bank’s operations. According to the regulator, deficiencies were particularly evident in risk management, complaint handling, and the organisational structure supporting the lending business. These shortcomings were deemed serious enough to constitute breaches of Germany’s Banking Act. 
 Regulatory scrutiny and fintech risk exposure 
 The intervention highlights broader regulatory concerns surrounding fast-growing digital banks operating across borders in the European Union. N26 serves more than 8 million customers across roughly 24 countries, according to company disclosures, and reached a valuation of approximately USD 9 billion during the peak of fintech investment in 2021. Rapid customer growth has historically placed pressure on internal systems, particularly in areas such as anti-money laundering controls, credit risk oversight, and customer service infrastructure. 
 BaFin has increased its supervisory intensity across the financial sector in recent years. In 2023 alone, the regulator conducted hundreds of special audits and ongoing inspections, with a particular focus on IT resilience, governance frameworks, and consumer protection. European banking authorities have similarly tightened oversight, reflecting lessons learned from high-profile compliance failures and the increasing complexity of digital financial services. 
 For N26, the renewed appointment of a special monitor signals that previous remediation efforts have not fully satisfied regulatory expectations. The monitor is tasked with overseeing the implementation of corrective measures and ensuring sustained compliance. While the restrictions apply specifically to certain business lines and jurisdictions, the case underscores the challenges faced by fintech banks as they scale within Europe’s highly regulated financial system.

BaFin hits N26 with additional oversight
 
The German neobank has run into compliance issues before, in 2021. On Monday, BaFin appointed a special monitor to keep tabs on the neobank’s compliance arm.

 German neobank N26 is no longer permitted to lend mortgages in the Netherlands after regulator BaFin found lapses in the firm’s compliance framework. 
 The order, which also prohibits N26 from securitizing mortgage claims, follows a special audit conducted last year which revealed N26 “lacked proper business organization” and had “serious deficiencies … in risk and complaint management and in the organization of the lending business,” the German financial regulator said Monday . 
 N26 violated the provisions of the German Banking Act, BaFin said. 
 This is the second time BaFin has ordered special oversight of N26 since 2021. Issues then involved deficiencies in its anti-money laundering controls, and cost the neobank €4.25 million in BaFin penalties . 
 Also in 2021, N26 shuttered its U.S. operations . Then-Chief Growth Officer Alex Weber said the firm was “sharpening our strategic focus on our core business in Europe for the time being.” N26 counted 3.7 million “revenue-relevant” customers in 2021 ; and counts 4.8 million “revenue-relevant” customers in 2025 . 
 As part of Monday’s order, BaFin appointed a special monitor to track N26’s progress in fixing its compliance issues; and imposed additional capital requirements and business restrictions on N26. 
 Separately, BaFin imposed a €15,000 fine on N26 in March, the regulator said, after the bank failed to inform BaFin that the supervisory board had improperly approved a loan granted to an executive. 
 A spokesperson for N26 did not immediately respond to a request for comment.
