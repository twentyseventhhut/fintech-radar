FAQs
 How does RfP differ from traditional payment methods? 
Unlike traditional methods that "pull" funds automatically, RfP operates on a "push payment model" where the payer explicitly authorizes each transaction, creating a more secure environment with no unexpected debits. This extends Cross River's payment expertise from instant payouts to secure, instant pay-ins. 
 Does Cross River support RfP on both RTP® and FedNow® networks? 
Currently, Cross River’s RfP functionality is only available through The Clearing House’s RTP® network and is not supported on FedNow®. 
 Can partners enable RfP functionality for all their merchants at once? 
No. Each merchant originating RfP through Cross River and The Clearing House (TCH) will have to be registered, reviewed and approved. What details from the RfP are shown to end users in the banking app? 
An RfP includes the requested amount, the payment originator and account number, the requested execution date, and the RfP expiry date.
 How quickly are funds available after authorization? 
Funds are received instantly upon authorization by the RfP receiver.
