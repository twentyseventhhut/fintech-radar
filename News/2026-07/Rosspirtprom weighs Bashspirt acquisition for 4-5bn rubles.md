 «Росспиртпром» может приобрести «Башспирт» за 4–5 млрд рублей 
Крупнейший производитель спирта в России «Росспиртпром» рассматривает покупку принадлежащего правительству Башкирии АО «Башспирт» (100% акций), рассказали три источника “ Коммерсанта ” на рынке алкоголя. По словам одного из них, представители «Росспиртпрома» на минувшей неделе посещали заводы «Башспирта». По данным другого собеседника издания, весь бизнес «Башспирта» оценивается в 4–5 млрд руб. Глава минземимущества Башкирии Наталья Полянская заявила, что к ним обращение о покупке «Башспирта» не поступало. В ноябре 2025 года глава региона Радий Хабиров, отвечая на вопрос о возможной продаже «Башспирта», говорил: «Не вижу необходимости».
АО «Башспирт» — крупный производитель спирта и алкоголя, созданный в 2000 году, объединяет четыре ликероводочных завода и одно спиртовое предприятие. В его портфель входят бренды «Сталковская», «Белебеевская», «Дикий мед» и Fenomen. По собственным данным, компания способна производить в год до 20 млн литров спирта, 50 млн литров алкогольной продукции и 278 тыс. литров уксуса. Согласно СПАРК, в 2025 году выручка АО «Башспирт» выросла на 24,14%, до 11,57 млрд руб., чистая прибыль — на 0,84%, до 266,5 млн руб.
«Росспиртпром» создан в 2009 году на базе одноименного Федерального государственного унитарного предприятия. В его периметр входят восемь спиртовых заводов по всей стране. Весной 2024 года за 8,289 млрд руб. компанию приватизировало ООО «Бизнес Альянс», принадлежащее бывшему топ-менеджеру Тройка-Д банка Владимиру Акаеву. Как следует из ЕГРЮЛ, сейчас в его собственности находится 49% в ООО «Бизнес Альянс». 51% бизнеса с июля прошлого года через ООО «Опал» принадлежит ЗПИФ «Батман», пайщики которого не раскрываются.
Старший юрист «Рустам Курмаев и партнеры» Владислав Гейтс говорит, что если продавцом будет правительство региона, то продажа актива должна проходить на открытых торгах. Но если республика сначала переведет акции на баланс своего холдингового акционерного общества, то дальнейшая продажа бизнеса возможна уже вне закона о приватизации, поясняет он. Именно по такой модели прошла аналогичная сделка с «Татспиртпромом»: он принадлежал Татарстану не напрямую, а через холдинг «Связьинвестнефтехим» и был продан без аукциона.
«Башспирт» интересен «Росспиртпрому» в первую очередь как крупный производитель спирта, считает президент гильдии «Алкопро» Андрей Московский. По его оценке, компания входит в топ-5 производителей такой продукции. По мнению эксперта, «Росспиртпром» сегодня ориентируется прежде всего на наращивание объемов производства алкоголя, для чего нужно укреплять собственную сырьевую базу. Эксперт алкогольного рынка Сергей Гладыщук говорит, что покупка «Башспирта» — серьезный шаг к консолидации рынка крепкого алкоголя.

# Context-enrichment: Rosspirtprom weighs Bashspirt acquisition for 4-5bn rubles
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Kommersant (~2026-07-10) reports, citing **three anonymous alcohol-market sources**, that Rosspirtprom — Russia's largest spirit producer, just off closing the Tatspirtprom deal — is weighing the purchase of 100% of Bashspirt, a distiller wholly owned by the Bashkortostan government, at a whole-business estimate of **4–5bn rubles**. The **one** concrete data point: Rosspirtprom reps visited Bashspirt plants "last week." Everything else is inference.
- **This is NOT a signed deal, and it is officially denied.** Bashkortostan land/property minister Natalya Polyanskaya says no purchase request was received; neither company commented; the 4–5bn figure comes from a single source.
- **The governor ruled it out 8 months ago.** On his 11 Nov 2025 Direct Line, Radiy Khabirov said he saw "no need" to sell Bashspirt, noting it's top-5 in Russian strong alcohol and growing. **Why it can still be real:** Tatarstan followed the same denial-then-deal arc — the Tatspirtprom sale was never pre-announced and closed anyway (SINKH signed 30 Dec 2025, transfer 23 Jan 2026). A regional "no need" is not a veto; budget pressure flips it.
- **Why the 4–5bn number is soft:** it is a market estimate, not a negotiated price. On Bashspirt's 2025 numbers (revenue 11.57bn +24.14%, net profit 266.5M +0.84%) it implies **EV/Revenue ≈ 0.35–0.43x** (cheap for a growing branded producer) but **P/E ≈ 15–19x** (rich, because net margin is only ~2.3%). The discount-on-revenue / premium-on-profit tension means a buyer is paying for **plants + brands + captive spirit supply**, not standalone earnings. EV/EBITDA can't be computed (no EBITDA disclosed).

## [1] Competitors / peers
Russian strong-alcohol producers by Forbes-2025 production revenue: **Novabev/Beluga #1** (~34bn, ~6.2% share; group revenue 149.3bn), **Alvisa #2** (~24.8bn), **Alcohol Siberian Group (ASG) #3** (~23.2bn), **Tatspirtprom #4** (now Rosspirtprom's), with **Ladoga** the fastest grower (+23.4%). Rosspirtprom's own strength is upstream **spirit** (~30–42% of Russian output, >40% with Tatspirtprom), not bottled brands.
- **Position:** Rosspirtprom is consolidating from the spirit end downward. Tatspirtprom (vodka #1 by volume) gave it a branded finished-goods flagship; Bashspirt (top-5 strong alcohol, 20M L spirit / 50M L product capacity) would add both **captive spirit raw-material base** and mid-tier brands (Stalkovskaya, Belebeevskaya, Dikiy Med, Fenomen).
- **Why the roll-up now:** top-10 producers' share rose to ~35% (+1.1pp) in Jan–Nov 2025 as excise/MRP hikes squeeze small regional players. Consolidation isn't opportunistic — it's the structural response to a shrinking (~-4% in 2025), higher-taxed market where scale in spirit self-supply is the margin lever.

## [2] Company history / fit
Rosspirtprom = ex-FGUP (created 2000, Zivenko era, historically linked in media to Rotenberg-adjacent structures). First privatization auction (Jan 2024, 10.61bn start) **failed — no bids**; the repeat auction (19 Apr 2024) sold 100% for **8.289bn rubles** to OOO "Business Alliance," beneficiary **Vladimir Akaev** (ex-Troika-D Bank). Ownership then restructured (Jul 2025): Akaev 49% + OOO "Opal" 51%, Opal held by **ZPIF "Batman" with undisclosed unitholders**.
- **Ultimate control is opaque by design** — the ZPIF wrapper shields the true beneficiary. Media infer a Rotenberg-adjacent hand; **no source ties Gazprombank specifically** to the current structure (treat "state-linked" as implied, not documented).
- **Fit is logical and demonstrated:** post-privatization Rosspirtprom moved fast — Tula bottling (Prazdnichnaya) + Brand Management (trademarks) 51% stakes (Jul–Aug 2025), an Itkul spirit-plant stake, then **Tatspirtprom (closed Jan 2026)**. Bashspirt is the next brick. **Why:** a bulk-spirit supplier earns a commodity take-rate; integrating downstream into branded vodka + securing captive spirit demand is the structural push toward a defensible, higher-margin national champion.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Genuinely a **new, distinct development** — no prior note covers Bashspirt deal talks. Related in the corpus: [[X5 prepares to acquire alcohol distributor Region 50]] (different acquirer/segment — retail-side alcohol distribution, not producer consolidation) and the macro backdrop in [[Sber forecasts surge in Russian M&A activity this autumn]] (distressed/subscale assets changing hands — exactly this pattern). The register-privatization theme also echoes [[Bank of Russia to value NSPK ahead of possible stake sale]] and [[Rosatom decides to buy out Shishkarev's stake in Delo Group]] as state-asset reshuffles.
- **Traction so far = one plant visit.** That is real (buyers doing site DD) but thin; everything above it (price, intent, structure) is chatter. **Where the value is:** for Rosspirtprom, captive spirit supply + brand portfolio; for Bashkortostan, a one-off 4–5bn cash injection plus offloading a subscale, thin-margin asset before consolidation erodes its position.
- **Who captures the margin:** the acquirer, via vertical integration — Bashspirt standalone earns a ~2.3% net margin; folded into a spirit-to-shelf integrated group, the same volume carries more of the value chain in-house.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Deal legality is the gating question.** A direct sale of a 100%-region-owned JSC must go via **open tender** under privatization law. The bypass — used for Tatspirtprom — is to first park shares in a **regional holding JSC** (Tatarstan used Svyazinvestneftekhim / SINKH), then sell the holding's asset **without auction**. **No evidence Bashkortostan has created such a holding for Bashspirt** (it appears held directly by the property ministry). Until it does, a quiet no-auction 4–5bn sale isn't legal — a reason for skepticism.
- **Tatspirtprom precedent frames price:** sold to Rosspirtprom via SINKH, no auction; price undisclosed, estimated **~9bn (Business Online) to 20–25bn (Infoline)**, reportedly staged. Tatspirtprom earns ~10x Bashspirt's net profit, so a 4–5bn Bashspirt tag is roughly proportionate to scale.
- **Regulatory tailwind for consolidation:** from 1 Jan 2026 excise on >18% alcohol rose 740→824 rub/L pure alcohol; vodka MRP up to 409 rub/0.5L. Higher taxes + falling volumes squeeze regional producers → more forced sellers.
- **Counterintuitive second-order:** the more Rosspirtprom concentrates spirit + vodka nationally under an opaque ZPIF-wrapped owner, the more it invites FAS/antitrust and political scrutiny — concentration makes the champion **regulatorily fragile**, not safe. And Khabirov's public reversal (if it happens) would be a governance signal that regional "strategic" assets are now sale candidates under fiscal stress.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian strong-alcohol (spirit/vodka) production is a shrinking, tax-squeezed market: 2025 vodka output fell 4.2% to 79.3m dal, and vodka/cognac sales are described as plateaued (per TAdviser/Vinetur, as of 2026). Spirit production is structurally *fragmented* — no producer holds >5% share (per IBISWorld) — but is consolidating fast under state/oligarch-linked hands: since its 2024 privatization Rosspirtprom (8 distilleries) acquired Tatspirtprom from Tatarstan in Dec 2025, and The Insider ties the ultimate buyer to Arkady Rotenberg (via the opaque "Batman" ZPIF that holds 51% of Business Alliance). "Why now": excise on ethanol rose ~15% in Jan-2025 (to ₽740/l) and again ~11% to ₽824/l for 2026, plus a jump in minimum vodka retail price (to ₽409/0.5l, +17%); rising input/excise cost rewards vertical integration into own ethanol supply — exactly the stated rationale for buying a top-5 spirit producer (per Alkopro guild). Entry barriers are high: licensing, EGAIS tracking (mandatory for >9% ABV), excise-stamp control.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: ethanol/alcohol production capacity (dal/yr), excise-adjusted gross margin, EGAIS-registered volume. Bashspirt = 4 liquor plants + 1 distillery, up to 20m l ethanol + 50m l alcohol/yr, top-5 ethanol producer (per Alkopro's Moskovsky); 2025 revenue ₽11.57bn (+24.1%), net profit ₽266.5m (+0.84%), 6.015m dal sold (+4.1%, a record). Named market peers in the Association of Russian Vodka Producers: Rosspirtprom, Soyuzplodoimport, Novabev Group, Ladoga, Stellar. Recent moves: Rosspirtprom→Tatspirtprom closed Dec 30 2025 (direct sale via Svyazinvestneftekhim holding, price undisclosed, "market price/independent valuation"); X5 buying distributor Region 50 (2026-07) shows retailers integrating downstream. Rosspirtprom's position: consolidator/roll-up leader chasing raw-material self-sufficiency; moat = scale + state relationships (analysis), not brand.

**Comps & multiples.** Deal size ₽4-5bn for 100% of Bashspirt:
- EV/Revenue = ₽4-5bn / ₽11.57bn = **0.35x-0.43x** (thin, but a low-margin ethanol/vodka producer, not a growth asset).
- P/E = ₽4-5bn / ₽0.2665bn = **~15x-19x** — rich vs the revenue multiple because net margin is only ~2.3%; the price is anchored to assets/capacity, not earnings.
- Internal comp — Rosspirtprom's own 2024 privatization: ₽8.289bn for 8 distilleries, assets valued ₽22.5bn end-2024 (auction cleared 23% below the ₽10.6bn start price, single bidder — widely called under-priced). [[Rosatom decides to buy out Shishkarev's stake in Delo Group]] and [[Bashkiria sells part of Bashneft stake for 14.8bn rubles]] are same-vintage Russian state-asset disposals; [[X5 prepares to acquire alcohol distributor Region 50]] and [[Russia weighs selling up to 5% of NSPK to single investor]] show the consolidation/state-sale wave.
- Verdict: in-line to cheap on revenue; no clean EBITDA available → EV/EBITDA "no data". Peer public multiples for RU spirit assets are largely `[UNSOURCED]` (thin float, opaque deals).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Deal not confirmed — sourced from "market sources" only.** Bashkiria's property minister (Polyanskaya) says no offer received; regional head Khabirov said in Nov-2025 he "saw no need" to sell. Announced ≠ live; the ₽4-5bn is one anonymous estimate.
2. **Privatization-mechanics / governance risk.** A regional-government seller must use open auction; the workaround (transfer to a regional holding first, then sell off-auction — the Tatspirtprom/Svyazinvestneftekhim template) invites under-pricing and opacity, mirroring Rosspirtprom's own criticized 2024 auction. Second-order: valuation may not reflect fair market, and beneficial ownership (Rotenberg-linked, per The Insider) raises concentration/political risk.
3. **Excise/regulatory margin squeeze.** Sequential excise hikes (ethanol +15% then +11%), minimum-price resets and EGAIS compliance compress an already ~2.3% net-margin business; a debt-funded buyer overpaying into a declining-volume market carries execution risk.
