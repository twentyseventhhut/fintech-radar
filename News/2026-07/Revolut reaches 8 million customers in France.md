## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** Revolut announced it has reached **8 million customers in France**, calling it the neobank's fastest-growing European market. It is pairing the milestone with a new **Western Europe headquarters in Paris** (10-year lease, ~2,417 m² across six floors near the Bourse/Sentier district), set to open in early 2027, on the back of a previously announced **€1bn+ investment** in the region. (Source: [Connecting the Dots in Fintech](https://www.connectingthedotsinfin.tech/r/49ff91c5); [Revolut UK newsroom](https://www.revolut.com/news/revolut_announces_new_western_europe_hq_based_in_paris_and_will_apply_for_french_banking_licence_to_support_regional_growth/); [Retail Banker International](https://www.retailbankerinternational.com/news/revolut-to-open-western-europe-headquarters-in-france/).)

**[1] Trajectory of the France number.** France has ramped fast: Revolut reported **~5M French customers in April/May 2026** (per its FY2025 results and the [[Revolut surpasses 70 million customers globally]] retelling), then **"more than 7M"** around the Paris-HQ/French-licence announcement, and now **8M**. Management has publicly targeted **10M French users by end-2026**. France is described as Revolut's largest EU market and part of a Western Europe region of **25M+ customers** (its largest/fastest-growing region). (Source: [Global Banking & Finance](https://www.globalbankingandfinance.com/revolut-aims-secure-french-us-licences-year-western-europe/); [Invezz](https://invezz.com/news/2026/04/22/revolut-targets-france-us-banking-licences-to-drive-growth/).)

**[2] Why it matters — the licence angle.** The France push is inseparable from Revolut's plan to apply for a **full French banking licence with the ACPR**. A local licence would let it lend (loans, mortgages, credit) and offer deposit-guaranteed products tailored to French customers, deepening monetisation beyond the current EEA passporting model. CEO Nik Storonsky flagged talks with ACPR as "advanced" but declined to guarantee 2026 approval. (Source: [Finextra](https://www.finextra.com/newsarticle/46003/revolut-to-invest-1-billion-in-france-and-apply-for-banking-licence); [Yahoo/Reuters](https://uk.finance.yahoo.com/news/revolut-aims-secure-french-us-163729804.html).)

**[3] Global backdrop.** Revolut ended 2025 with **68.3M retail customers globally** (+30% YoY) and reported **$6.0bn revenue (+46%)** and **$2.3bn pre-tax profit** — fifth consecutive year of net profit (see [[Revolut surpasses 70 million customers globally]], now 70M+ and targeting 100M by mid-2027). France is one of the biggest single-country contributors alongside the UK (~13M) and Spain (~6M).

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
1. **Is 8M France genuinely new vs the 5M / 7M figures already on file?** Yes — internal notes show 5M France (May 2026) and "7M+" around the HQ announcement; 8M is a fresh, higher milestone. New. → **fresh**.
2. **Is the Paris HQ / French licence itself new, or a re-report?** The €1bn investment + Paris-HQ-by-2027 + ACPR licence intent were announced earlier (Apr/Nov 2025). This item bundles that context around the new 8M number, so the licence/HQ part is *re-stated* backdrop, not new news. Note this in the write-up.
3. **Registered vs active customers?** Revolut's headline counts are cumulative registered retail customers; active/engaged share is lower and not disclosed per-country. Treat 8M as registered. Open.
4. **Does "fastest-growing European market" hold up?** Plausible given 5M→8M inside ~2 months of reporting, but "fastest-growing" is a company claim; Italy (5M), Spain (~6M) also growing fast. Company-sourced superlative — flag.
5. **10M-by-end-2026 target credible?** From 8M mid-July 2026, +2M in ~5.5 months is roughly the observed run-rate; achievable but ambitious. Open.
6. **Does the milestone change financials materially?** Marginally — France is one of several large markets; monetisation gated on the French licence (lending). No standalone revenue disclosed for France.
7. **Regulatory risk?** Revolut's EU/EEA entities have faced heightened scrutiny (see [[Revolut EU unit’s capital buffer raised the most by the watchdog]]); a French licence adds a second EU banking regulator (ACPR) alongside the Lithuanian ECB-supervised bank. Open.
8. **Is Paris HQ a substitution for or addition to the Lithuania bank base?** Addition — RBUAB (Lithuania) remains the EEA banking entity; France would be a separate licensed entity. Confirmed by IR (RHE/RBUAB annual reports).
9. **Competitive read?** France pits Revolut against domestic neobanks (Boursorama/BoursoBank, N26, local incumbents' digital arms). 8M is a strong share signal in a ~67M-population market.
10. **Any risk the number is inflated/PR-timed?** Milestone PR is routine for Revolut (Italy 5M, global 70M). Directionally consistent with 68.3M global YE2025; low fabrication risk.
11. **Does 8M France fit the global 68.3M base?** Yes — France ~12% of global retail base, consistent with it being a top-3 country.
12. **Jobs/hiring angle?** HQ tied to €1bn regional investment and hiring, but this note gives no France-specific headcount; separate from Portugal 250-jobs note.
13. **Deposit-guarantee / consumer angle?** Until French licence lands, French deposits sit under the Lithuanian entity's scheme, not French FGDR — a localisation gap the licence would close. Open.
14. **Second-order: US charter parallel.** Revolut filed a US national bank charter (Mar 2026); France + US licences are twin 2026 localisation bets. Context only.
15. **Duplicate risk with [[Revolut surpasses 70 million customers globally]]?** No — that is the global count; this is country-specific. Distinct.

Importance: 3/5 — a solid, credible country-milestone that reinforces Revolut's Western-Europe strategy and French-licence narrative, but the headline number is incremental (5M→8M) and the HQ/licence context is previously announced; not a strategy-changing event on its own.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector.** European neobanking / digital-banking super-app. Revolut is the region's scale leader by customers, using a country-by-country milestone playbook (Italy 5M, Spain ~6M, UK ~13M, France now 8M) to signal share gains while pushing toward a global 100M-customer goal by mid-2027.

**In-base comps.** [[Revolut surpasses 70 million customers globally]] (68.3M retail YE2025, +30%); [[Revolut reaches 5 million customers in Italy]]; [[Revolut opens Matosinhos sales hub, plans 250 Portugal jobs]] and [[Revolut passes €600 million deposits in Portugal, adds savings products]] (Western-Europe deepening); [[Revolut completes share sale at $75B valuation]]; [[Revolut EU unit's capital buffer raised the most by the watchdog]] (regulatory scrutiny of the EEA entity).

**Market position / thesis.** France (~8M of ~67M population) is now one of Revolut's top-3/4 single-country bases and its stated fastest-growing EU market, backed by a €1bn+/$1.1bn regional investment, a Paris Western-Europe HQ opening early 2027, and a pending full French banking licence with the ACPR. The licence is the strategic prize: it unlocks local lending (loans/mortgages/credit) and French deposit-guarantee coverage (FGDR), lifting per-customer monetisation vs the current EEA-passporting/Lithuania-bank setup. Management targets 10M French users by end-2026.

**IR grounding (PRIMARY — Revolut Group Holdings Ltd Annual Report 2025, FY ended 31 Dec 2025, published 2026-04-29; [PDF](https://assets.revolut.com/pdf/annualreport2025.pdf), and [results release](https://www.revolut.com/en-US/news/revolut_reports_record_profit_of_2_3bn_for_2025_as_revenue_surges_to_6bn/)):**
- Group revenue **$6.0bn (£4.5bn), +46% YoY** (from $4.0bn/£3.1bn in 2024).
- **Pre-tax profit $2.3bn (£1.7bn)**, PBT margin **38%** (up from 35%) — **5th consecutive year of net profitability**.
- Retail customers **68.3M (+30%)**; business customers **767K (+33%)**.
- Customer balances **$67.5bn (£50.2bn), +66%**; lending book **$2.9bn (£2.2bn), +120% YoY**.
- **11 product lines each >~$135m (£100m)** revenue — diversified base.
- Licensed bank in **30+ of ~40 markets**; France is *not yet* a licensed-bank market (French licence pending, hence the strategic weight of this note); US national bank charter filed Mar 2026.
- Per prior IR-linked reporting, France stood at **~5M** at FY2025 results time → the 8M figure implies continued fast H1-2026 growth. Also relevant: separate EEA entity reports exist (RHE, RBUAB-Lithuania, RSE) — France would add a new ACPR-supervised entity.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Regulatory execution risk** — French banking licence with ACPR is "advanced" but not granted; timing (2026) uncertain per CEO. Adds a second EU banking supervisor alongside ECB-supervised RBUAB (Lithuania).
2. **Company-sourced superlatives / registered vs active** — "fastest-growing market" and the 8M count are cumulative registered customers; per-country active/engagement and France-specific revenue are undisclosed.
3. **Prior-scrutiny overhang** — [[Revolut EU unit's capital buffer raised the most by the watchdog]] shows heightened EEA capital scrutiny; a French entity increases regulatory surface area.
4. **Re-announced context** — HQ/€1bn/licence elements are previously disclosed backdrop, so the incremental news value is the 8M milestone itself.

## Earnings Review

Verdict: **beat / strong** (record year, well ahead of prior year on every headline line).

**Reported FY2025 figures vs FY2024:**
- Revenue **$6.0bn (£4.5bn)** vs $4.0bn (£3.1bn) — **+46% YoY**.
- Pre-tax profit **$2.3bn (£1.7bn)** vs ~$1.4bn prior; **PBT margin 38%** vs 35%.
- **5th consecutive year of net profitability.**
- Retail customers **68.3M, +30%**; business customers **767K, +33%**.
- Customer balances **$67.5bn (£50.2bn), +66%** — deposit gathering accelerating.
- Lending portfolio **$2.9bn (£2.2bn), +120% YoY** (personal loans, credit cards, nascent mortgages).
- Revenue diversification: **11 product lines each >~$135m (£100m)**.
- Valuation **$75bn** (secondary share sale, see [[Revolut completes share sale at $75B valuation]]).

**Western-Europe / France context (ties to this note):**
- Western Europe is Revolut's **largest and fastest-growing region (25M+ customers)**; France ~5M at results time → **8M** now (this note), targeting **10M by end-2026**.
- Licensed bank in **30+ of ~40 markets** — but **France is NOT yet licensed**; the pending **ACPR French banking licence** + €1bn/$1.1bn France investment + Paris HQ (2027) are the growth-unlock levers. A French licence would enable local lending and FGDR-covered deposits, materially raising French monetisation.
- Parallel: **US national bank charter filed Mar 2026** — twin licence bets underpin the 100M-by-mid-2027 customer goal.

**Thesis flags:** Positive — rapid, profitable, diversifying growth with deposits and lending compounding; France is a live proof-point of the Western-Europe deepening. Watch: French licence *approval and timing* (not guaranteed for 2026) and EEA regulatory scrutiny ([[Revolut EU unit's capital buffer raised the most by the watchdog]]). No France-specific P&L is disclosed, so the 8M milestone is a scale signal rather than a booked-revenue event. No public consensus estimates (private company) — comparison is vs prior-year actuals.
