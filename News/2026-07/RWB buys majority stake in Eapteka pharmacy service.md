 RWB купила мажоритарную долю в сервисе «Еаптека» 
Группа RWB, в которую входят маркетплейс Wildberries и рекламная компания Russ Outdoor, купила долю в онлайн-аптеке «Еаптека», сообщили CNews  представители RWB. Стороны не раскрывают сумму сделки, равно как и долю, которую получила RWB, но в группе отмечают, что «Еаптека» вошла в ее состав. «Решение о приобретении актива является частью стратегии RWB по развитию направления «RWB Здоровье» и созданию цифровой платформы здоровья», – заявили представители RWB.
Покупка доли в «Еаптеке» даст группе возможность «существенно нарастить ассортиментную матрицу лекарств и товаров для здоровья» на Wildberries, отмечают в RWB. Помимо этого, группа получит партнерство с сетью из более 25 тыс. аптек по всей России, которые используются для выдачи заказанных на «Еаптеке» лекарств.
«Ожидается, что это существенно повысит доступность лекарств и товаров для здоровья даже в отдаленных регионах страны», – добавили в RWB.
«Цифровая платформа здоровья, которую мы создаем, объединит в себе медицинский и фармацевтический маркетплейсы, – и присоединение «Еаптека» к нашей экосистеме – новый шаг к реализации этих планов, – сказала CNews управляющий директор «RWB Здоровье» Анастасия Карпова. – Продажа лекарств и товаров для здоровья уже доступна на Wildberries, однако благодаря объединению экспертизы мы рассчитываем существенно расширить как ассортимент товаров на площадке, так и доступ покупателей к лекарственным препаратам. Партнеры же – производители фармпрепаратов – получат новые возможности инфраструктуры RWB Здоровье для контакта со своим потребителем».
«Еаптека» – это компания ООО «Еаптека», основанная в июне 2014 г. с уставным капиталом около 1,86 млрд руб. Генеральный директор – Дмитрий Боков, основной вид деятельности – «торговля розничная лекарственными средствами в специализированных магазинах (аптеках)» (код ОКВЭД – 47.73).
Компания была убыточной. Согласно сервису «Контур.Фокус», по итогам 2024 г. она получила выручку в размере 32,9 млрд руб. и чистый убыток на уровне 4,1 млрд руб.
Финансовые данные за 2025 г. компания до сих пор не опубликовала, но это не единственная тайна, которая ее окружает. К моменту выхода материала была засекречена информация о ее владельцах – она исключена из ЕГРЮЛ.
По информации « Ведомостей », группа RWB получила в «Еаптеке» именно «мажоритарную долю». Точные ее размеры издание не приводит.
До сделки «Еаптекой» владели структуры, связанные с предпринимателем Алексеем Репиком, утверждают «Ведомости», а до этого значительная часть сервиса принадлежала Сбербанку. Осенью 2025 г. эти структуры выкупили у Сбербанка 45% акций «Еаптеки», доведя свою долю в ней до 100%.
По оценке опрошенных «Ведомостями» экспертов, покупка RWB сервиса «Еаптека» – «одна из наиболее заметных сделок на российском рынке электронной коммерции и цифрового здравоохранения за последнее время». На этом настаивает, в частности, руководитель проектов практики «Потребительский сектор и АПК» Strategy Partners Роман Самойлов.
У RWB здесь двойная выгода. По словам Самойлова, в результате сделки RWB получила крупный бренд наряду с полностью готовым бизнесом, который заранее наладил логистику, решил вопрос с лицензиями и партнерскими аптеками, а также создал собственную ИТ-инфраструктуру. Все это дает RWB возможность не строить собственный бизнес в этом направлении с нуля, а быстро масштабировать полученные активы.
По оценке директора по развитию аналитической компании RNC Pharma Николая Беспалова, бизнес «Еаптеки» стоил примерно 7-12 млрд руб. без учета долгов. Он сообщил «Ведомостям», что покупка «Еаптеки» – наглядное доказательство того, что RWB расценивает свой фармацевтический бизнес как стратегический.

# Context-enrichment: RWB buys majority stake in Eapteka pharmacy service
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS/DUPLICATE VERDICT: FRESH (but low-weight; a confirmation follow-up).** The internal corpus already carries [[Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka]] (2026-06-24), which reported the deal as a **leak-sourced, unconfirmed "agreed to buy"** — first surfaced by the Telegram channel «Свободная касса», confirmed only by anonymous Kommersant investment-market sources, with **both WB and Eapteka declining comment**. This 2026-07-01 note is materially different: it is the **official confirmation** — RWB representatives told CNews the stake was bought and «Еаптека» «вошла в её состав», with an **on-record quote from RWB Health MD Anastasia Karpova**, the «RWB Здоровье» strategy framing, the 25k-pharmacy pickup network, and RNC Pharma's Bespalov valuing the business at **7-12 bln RUB ex-debt**. That is a genuine "talks (rumor) → confirmed close" follow-up, hence **fresh**, not a reprint. It is NOT stale — but it is the SAME deal, so treat it as a confirmation update to the June note, not a new strategic event. (If the pipeline prefers to suppress same-deal repeats, the June note is the anchor: `duplicate_of` candidate = [[Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka]].)

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
RWB Group (the merged Wildberries + Russ Outdoor; operating entity ООО «РВБ») **confirmed** it bought a **majority ("мажоритарную") stake** in online pharmacy «Еаптека» (ООО «Еаптека»). De-PR'd, the two numbers that matter are **both undisclosed**: the **exact stake %** and the **price**. RWB will not name either; the only figures are third-party estimates — RNC Pharma's Nikolai Bespalov puts the business at **~7-12 bln RUB ex-debt**, and Kommersant's June source said **"minimum 8 bln RUB"** — these are valuations, **not a transaction price**.
- Eapteka 2024 (Контур.Фокус/СПАРК): revenue **32.9 bln RUB (+12%)**, **net loss 4.1 bln RUB** (loss narrowed ~8%). **2025 financials unpublished.** End-2025 (DSM Group, per the June note): **15.5k pickup points, ~1.42% online-pharma share — only #4**, behind Аптека.ру, Ютека, Здравсити. Note the corpus discrepancy vs the ownership-register opacity: at press time Eapteka's owners were **secret / struck from ЕГРЮЛ** — an unusual info-blackout the primary source itself flags.
- **Why framed this way (analysis):** the PR anchors to the **strategy story** («цифровая платформа здоровья», assortment expansion, 25k-pharmacy reach) precisely because the **asset economics are ugly** — a chronically loss-making (~12% net-loss margin) #4 player whose previous strategic owner **Sber exited** (autumn 2025, its 45% to Repik-linked structures). A controlling strategic walking away is the loudest fact; the "digital health platform" wrapper is the anti-PR tell that the value isn't Eapteka's P&L.

## [1] Competitors / peers
**E-pharma leaders (all ahead of Eapteka):** Аптека.ру (Катрен) #1, Ютека #2, Здравсити (Протек-adjacent) #3 — Eapteka is a distant #4 at 1.42%. **Marketplace rivals entering pharma:** Ozon registered «Ozon Аптека»/«Ozon Ветаптека» trademarks (spring 2026) for OTC home delivery. Offline roll-up runs in parallel: see [[Neofarm acquires Astrakhan pharmacy chain Na Zdorovye]] (2026-06-30). Online-pharma penetration is only ~15.9% of the ~367 bln RUB e-pharma turnover (DSM, 2025) — the "headroom" thesis.
- **Position & why (analysis):** buying Eapteka makes RWB the **largest marketplace in pharma by acquisition**, leapfrogging Ozon/Yandex — but in the absolute e-pharma ranking it stays **#4**. What RWB actually buys is a **pharma license + 15.5k pickup points + compliance/logistics stack + 25k-pharmacy partner network** — a regulatory shortcut, not technology. That is why pure marketplaces (heavily constrained by Rx-delivery rules and licensing) buy a licensed operator rather than build.

## [2] Company history / fit
RWB is on an aggressive **vertical + embedded-finance roll-up**: cosmetics chain Рив Гош, a leasing subsidiary, a consumer-credit MCC, **WB Bank**, and (May 2026) a strategic partnership with state-owned **VTB** taking minority stakes across RWB's fintech companies. Eapteka slots into the new **«RWB Здоровье»** division (announced ~SPIEF June 2026), led by **Anastasia Karpova** (ex-CEO of Erkapharm/«Эркафарм», joined Jan 2026) — uniting a medical + pharmaceutical marketplace. CEO of Eapteka is **Dmitry Bokov** (appointed late Jan 2026). Adjacent RWB↔VTB moves in the corpus: [[VTB to sell former Otkritie office to Wildberries-Russ]] (2026-06-30).
- **Why the company acts this way (analysis):** marketplace take-rates are commoditized and thin. RWB's logic = move into **high-frequency, high-margin, sticky, regulated categories** (pharma, cosmetics, БАД) that drive repeat visits, then monetize the traffic + 80M+ customer base through **finance** (WB Bank credit/savings/BNPL). Eapteka is **frequency + regulatory-license + data feedstock** for the embedded-finance flywheel, not a standalone profit center.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Low novelty — a confirmation, not a new bet.** Eapteka was already on WB's storefront via an **Aug-2025 partnership**; the buy-rumor circulated in **summer 2025 (WB denied it then)** and again in June 2026. This July note is the **official close of a year-plus-old, on-again relationship**. Eapteka's own traction is *declining relevance* (#4, persistent losses, lost its Sber backer). The genuinely NEW content vs the June note is: on-record confirmation, Karpova's quotes, the «RWB Здоровье» platform framing, the 25k-pharmacy network claim, and the 7-12 bln valuation.
- **Why the value-add is thin (analysis):** value isn't Eapteka's P&L (loss-making) but (a) the **license + pickup footprint** as a compliance shortcut and (b) feeding **WB Bank's** flywheel. **Who captures the margin?** Likely **finance, not pharma retail** — drug retail is low-margin and price-regulated. Skeptical note: the **"25,000+ pharmacies"** is the *partner network RWB expects to access*, not owned points — Eapteka's own footprint is ~15.5k; don't conflate reach with control.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Analyst sentiment is mildly positive on the **logic** (Strategy Partners' Roman Samoylov: RWB gets a ready-made bicnes with logistics/licenses/IT solved, so it can scale fast rather than build from scratch; Bespalov: proof RWB treats pharma as strategic) — **but no one defends Eapteka's standalone economics.** RWB was also a bidder for pharmacy chain «Апрель» (per the June note; a source said it's "not interested now").
- **Why the market goes this way / second-order (analysis):** the durable story is **embedded finance + a state-aligned super-app, not pharma**. The concurrent VTB tie-up (state bank into a private marketplace's fintech, 80M customers) reframes the central question from "can RWB win e-pharma?" to "**is RWB building a state-aligned financial-conglomerate super-app** using verticals like pharma as customer-frequency feedstock?" Counterintuitive downside: deep state-bank entanglement + roll-up of distressed assets = **concentration/balance-sheet risk, not safety**. Open regulatory overhang: the Rx remote-sale experiment (Moscow/MO/Belgorod) was set to run only to **1 Mar 2026** — its extension status directly governs any high-margin Rx upside and is **not confirmed** in the corpus.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian pharma e-commerce reached ~283bn RUB in 2024, ~14% of the total pharmacy retail market (per Statista topic page, as of 2024); the channel was on track to roughly double 2022→2024, legalized off the COVID delivery rules. Structure: consolidated at the top and value-chain-integrated — the same players that own the online storefront also own the pharmacy fulfilment network (>25k pharmacies used by Eapteka for order pickup). Barriers are regulatory (pharma licensing, Rx-dispensing rules, cold-chain/OKVED 47.73) plus logistics scale — exactly the assets RWB says it bought pre-built rather than building from scratch. "Why now": the deal is the M&A leg of a marketplace-ecosystem land-grab — after Wildberries/Ozon captured ~47% / ~34% of Russian e-commerce (2025, per Statista/UNIMALL), the next margin pool is category depth (Rx + health goods) and embedded fintech attached to it. `(analysis)`

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: GMV, take rate, order volume, fulfilment-network reach. Players: Apteka.ru (channel leader, ~32% of the digital pharmacy channel per Statista), Eapteka (#2), Ozon (#3), plus horizontal marketplaces Wildberries and Yandex Market pushing into the vertical. Basis of competition: assortment breadth + last-mile/pickup density + price, not product novelty. Recent moves: Wildberries began selling Eapteka's assortment on-platform from Aug 2025 (partnership → acquisition path); autumn 2025 Repik-linked structures bought Sber's remaining 45% of Eapteka, taking it to 100% before flipping the majority to RWB (per Vedomosti/CNews, Jul 2026). Protagonist position: RWB moves from a standalone marketplace to an integrated "RWB Health" platform — catching up to Apteka.ru in pure e-pharmacy but with a far larger distribution funnel (2025 GMV 6.1trn RUB, +49%; net profit 175bn RUB — per T-Investments/SelSup). Moat: distribution scale + ecosystem lock-in (WB Wallet, BNPL, ad platform) rather than pharma expertise. `(analysis)`

**Comps & multiples.** Deal terms undisclosed (stake size and price not released). Third-party value estimate: 7–12bn RUB ex-debt (Nikolai Bespalov, RNC Pharma, via Vedomosti). Against Eapteka FY2024 revenue of 32.9bn RUB (Kontur.Focus), implied **EV/Revenue ≈ 7–12bn / 32.9bn = 0.21x–0.36x** — a distressed-looking revenue multiple, consistent with a loss-making target (FY2024 net loss 4.1bn RUB; FY2025 financials unpublished). EV/EBITDA and P/E = no data (negative earnings; no EBITDA disclosed). This is a majority — not 100% — stake, so the effective cash outlay is below even that estimate. Internal comps: no directly comparable Russian e-pharmacy / marketplace-health M&A in base; nearest analogues are commerce-fintech platform build-outs — [[Bazaar acquires Keenu to build commerce-fintech platform in Pakistan]] and [[PB Health acquires Amazon-backed Fitterfly]] (health-commerce roll-up), both without disclosed multiples → qualitative only, distribution not computed.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Opacity / undisclosed economics** — neither price, stake size, nor Eapteka's current owners are public (excluded from EGRYUL; FY2025 unfiled). Buying into an ownership-obscured, previously Sber- then Repik-linked asset raises governance/related-party and sanctions-adjacent diligence risk; the 0.21–0.36x "cheap" multiple may reflect that, not a bargain.
2. **Loss-making target, integration execution** — 4.1bn RUB FY2024 loss on 32.9bn revenue; RWB must fund and turn around fulfilment economics while grafting Rx-licensing onto a general marketplace — second-order margin drag before any health-platform synergy lands.
3. **Regulatory / Rx-dispensing dependence** — the value rests on the >25k pharmacy pickup network and pharma licences; any tightening of Rx-online rules or the pharmacy-partnership terms directly disintermediates the thesis.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Signals consolidation of Russian digital health under the two marketplace ecosystems (RWB vs Ozon), turning e-pharmacy into an embedded-finance + ad-attach funnel rather than a standalone vertical.
