# Context-enrichment: Mergers.ru — Russian tech-asset M&A market 2025 study, part two
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Mergers.ru published **part two of a multi-author study of the Russian tech/IT-asset M&A market for full-year 2025**. Authors: M&A lawyer Anastasia Nerchinskaya together with VERBA LEGAL, ALRUD, Denuo and ADVANCE CAPITAL (B1 also cited as an organizer in the broader project). The study is split into three sections: (1) trend review + deal-structuring specifics of Tech M&A in 2025; (2) due diligence of software and other IT assets; (3) legal structuring of deals in AI-based assets, plus a dedicated analysis of the AI-asset M&A market.

De-PR'd: **this is a downloadable practitioner/legal report, not a transaction and not a data release with fresh headline figures inside the note itself.** The note text is essentially the publisher's announcement blurb — it carries no numbers. The substance lives in the downloadable PDF and in the underlying market data (already circulated by B1/Forbes/Kommersant in March–April 2026). Part two's own value-add is the **legal-technical** layer (DD methodology, IP title, infosec risk, AI-asset structuring), not the market-size headline.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the report is a **law-firm consortium marketing artifact** (five advisory brands co-branding a study) as much as an analytical one — the point is to signal expertise in tech/IT/AI deal execution to a buy-side/sell-side audience in a shrinking market. The "unique AI-asset M&A analysis" framing is the differentiator vs. generic M&A reviews. That a five-firm consortium invests in a three-part study while deal count is collapsing is itself a second-order signal: advisors are competing for a smaller pie and positioning on the one growth-adjacent niche (AI).

The underlying 2025 market it describes (from external coverage): **Tech M&A deal count -54% YoY to ~85 deals** (vs 186 in 2024), only **5 deals >10bn RUB**; aggregate volume **incl. the largest deals ~315bn RUB vs 770bn RUB (-59%)**; ex-largest ~44.5bn RUB (down from ~90bn+). **AI-asset deals: ~30 in 2025 (VC+PE+M&A), -39% YoY.** These are external/prior figures, not stated in the note.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Comparable Russian tech-M&A studies for 2025: **B1's "M&A market trends" survey (2026, part 1)** and B1's broader Russia M&A 2025 review; **Kommersant/Forbes coverage** ("compressed air: tech-deal market fell more than twofold", March 2026); **ABN/IF24** retrospectives. Mergers.ru itself ran the "Russian M&A market 2022–2024 with 2025 forecast" (mid-2025). Positioning: **catching up / parallel** — the headline 2025 numbers were already public via B1/Forbes in Q1 2026; this Mergers.ru consortium report differentiates on the **legal-structuring + DD + AI-asset** angle rather than being first with the data.

**Why the landscape is this way:** in Russia's post-2022 closed market, tech-M&A "research" is produced by advisory firms (B1 ex-EY, plus law-firm consortia) as business development, not by banks. The premium accrues to whoever owns the **execution niche** — DD of IT/IP and AI-asset legal risk — because in a distressed, low-volume market advisors monetize complexity, not deal flow.

## [2] Company history / fit
Mergers.ru (part of the Cbonds group) is a long-running Russian M&A information portal — deal database, ratings, monthly M&A bulletin. Publishing a co-branded expert study fits its role as an **aggregator/platform** that hosts advisor content and ratings. The recurring cadence (2022–2024 review in 2025 → tech-asset 2025 study in three parts in mid-2026) shows a steady editorial franchise. **Why:** the portal's model is attention + advisor relationships; hosting flagship studies reinforces both.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Genuinely new in **part two**: a structured, sourced treatment of **IT-asset due diligence** (IP title, information-security risk, regulatory/team-resilience checks now "deeper and more operationally oriented") and **legal structuring of AI-based-asset deals** — an area with little prior formalized Russian-language practice guidance. That is the real value-add: **methodology, not market size.** The market-size story (-54% deal count) is not novel — it was reported months earlier.

**Traction / what underpins it:** value is real but narrow — a reference document for practitioners, no adoption metric applies. Who captures the margin: the five co-authoring advisory firms (lead-generation for DD/structuring mandates). What breaks the value: the numbers cited are **whole-market and mostly pre-published**, so for a news reader the incremental information is low; the note is a pointer to a PDF behind a download.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Sentiment is clearly bearish-turning-selective: 2025 was a phase of **strategic internal consolidation** (closing product gaps, consolidating critical competencies, strengthening own tech perimeter), driven down by high key rate, geopolitics, tax reform, and a buyer/seller price-expectation gap. Forecast for 2026–2027: shift **from growth-expectation deals to synergy-backed deals**, with buyers prizing fast integration into existing stack/customer base over standalone product promise. AI remains the one narrative pocket but even AI deals fell 39%.

**Why / second-order:** in a high-rate, sanctioned market, tech-M&A becomes **defensive consolidation by domestic strategics** rather than growth/VC-style buying — which structurally favors large incumbents (fewer, bigger, integration-driven deals) and squeezes standalone startups toward acqui-hire economics. The advisor consortium's bet on DD/AI-structuring content is a rational read of where the few remaining mandates concentrate.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is a research report on Russian technology-asset M&A (Tech M&A) FY2025, authored by Anastasiya Nerchinskaya with VERBA LEGAL, ALRUD, Denuo, B1/ADVANCE CAPITAL — part two covers software/IT due diligence and AI-asset deal structuring. Market contracted sharply in 2025: closed Tech M&A deals **-54% to 85 (from 186 in 2024)**; total value **~315bn RUB incl. mega-deals vs 770bn RUB in 2024 (-59%)**, and only ~44.5bn RUB excluding the largest deals; mega-deals (>10bn RUB) **fell 8→5** (per the study, via b1.ru / abn.agency / if24.ru, as of 2026-03/04). AI-asset segment: **30 AI-company deals in 2025 (VC+PE+M&A), -39% YoY**, disclosed value on 21 of them **~6.2bn RUB** (per DSight, via b1.ru). Structure: highly fragmented on volume (TMT keeps #1 by deal count) but concentrating in value; barriers are regulatory (state software mandates, critical-infrastructure rules) more than capital. **Why now:** the "market turned to a phase of strategic internal consolidation" — foreign exit-driven distressed M&A of 2022–24 has largely cleared, and the 2025 driver is domestic import-substitution + tightening IT regulation, with **cybersecurity the most active consolidation vertical** (rising cyber-threats + regulatory pressure pull strategic buyers).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs run on deal count, aggregate deal value, mega-deal count, and disclosure rate (only 21/30 AI deals disclosed value — thin transparency). This note's protagonist is not an operating company but the *report producers* — a consortium of Russian M&A legal/advisory firms (VERBA LEGAL, ALRUD, Denuo, ADVANCE CAPITAL/B1) competing on M&A-advisory league-table position; basis of competition is deal-flow access + structuring expertise (esp. AI-asset and software DD niches). Recent moves: peer B1 published its own "Tech M&A investment landscape / trends 2026" study (b1.ru, 2026-03); ALRUD flagged a first *joint* study of Russian M&A (alrud.com). Position: Mergers.ru (Cbonds group) is the incumbent data/registry venue (База сделок, M&A Monthly) — niche moat is the proprietary Russian deals database, a switching-cost/data asset rather than a scalable franchise.

**Comps & multiples.** No transaction multiples disclosed — report is aggregate market stats, not per-deal EV/Revenue; **multiples = no data** (Russian private tech deals rarely disclose revenue/EBITDA). Internal comps (recent RU tech/asset M&A in-base, 2026-07): [[Rosatom decides to buy out Shishkarev's stake in Delo Group]] · [[Bank of Russia to value NSPK ahead of possible stake sale]] · [[VIM Sberezheniy fund buys Poklonka Place business quarter]] · [[Belaya Skala buys 47.2bn ruble asset portfolio]] — all point to the same theme: state/quasi-state and large domestic buyers dominating, foreign strategics absent. Rich/cheap read: not computable; qualitative — buyer pool is narrowing to state-adjacent players, which typically compresses seller pricing power `(analysis)`.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Buyer concentration / state dominance** — commercial deal count is shrinking while state-participation deals rise (mergers.akm.ru); a thinning strategic-buyer pool depresses valuations and exit optionality for tech founders `(analysis)`.
2. **Disclosure & data reliability** — only 21/30 AI deals had disclosed value and the 44.5bn-vs-315bn spread hinges on a handful of mega-deals; aggregate figures are highly sensitive to one or two transactions, so "-59%" overstates precision.
3. **Regulatory dependence** — the entire consolidation thesis rests on import-substitution mandates and IT-regulation staying in force; a policy loosening or budget cut (import-substituted IT reportedly returned only ~1.6bn RUB on ~187bn RUB spend, 2022–25) would remove the main deal driver.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** 2025 marks the pivot from foreign-exit fire-sales to **domestic strategic consolidation**, led by cybersecurity and infrastructure software `(analysis)`. Falsifiable thesis: if the driver is genuinely regulatory-consolidation (not just a cyclical dip), 2026 mega-deal count rebounds in cybersecurity/critical-infrastructure software even as total count stays low; trigger to watch — a named cyber/infra-SW roll-up above 10bn RUB. Thesis breaks if 2026 deal count keeps falling across the board, implying capital-market stress, not consolidation.
