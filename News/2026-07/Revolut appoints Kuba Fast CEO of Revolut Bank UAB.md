# Context-enrichment: Revolut appoints Kuba Fast CEO of Revolut Bank UAB
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS VERDICT: STALE (duplicate).** This is a re-report of the SAME appointment already covered in
[[Kuba Fast takes over Revolut's Europe expansion]] (2026-05-13, "Kuba Fast takes over for Revolut in
Europe"). Same person, same role, same company. The July 10 version only formalizes what was already
flagged in May: it names the exact legal entity (Revolut Bank UAB, the Lithuania-licensed EU bank),
confirms the appointment is now effective following ECB / Bank of Lithuania sign-off, and adds that
incumbent Joe Heneghan moves up to CEO of Revolut Holdings Europe UAB (the EEA holding company). Those are
incremental governance details, not a new event → `duplicate_of: [[Kuba Fast takes over Revolut's Europe expansion]]`.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- Revolut appointed **Kuba Fast**, former CEO of Chase UK (JPMorgan's UK digital retail bank), as **CEO of
  Revolut Bank UAB** — the Lithuania-registered entity that holds Revolut's EU banking licence, supervised
  by the ECB and Bank of Lithuania. He also becomes Revolut's **CEO for Europe**.
- He **replaces Joe Heneghan**, who ran Revolut Bank UAB since 2021 and now becomes **CEO of Revolut
  Holdings Europe UAB**, the holding company for the group's EEA operations / regulatory alignment.
- Appointment took effect ~mid-2026, **pending/following ECB and Bank of Lithuania approval** (regulator
  sign-off on a bank CEO is standard "fit-and-proper" process, not a special endorsement).
- Fast's background: ~7 years at JPMorgan Chase (helped launch Chase UK "from a PowerPoint slide", CEO of
  Chase UK from May 2024); earlier ~3 years at Poland's mBank and ~6 years at McKinsey.
- **Why framed this way / what it reveals:** the note's own text is pure PR ("agility of FinTech + rigorous
  regulatory standards of Tier-1 institutions"). The un-PR'd reality is a *governance hire under regulatory
  pressure*: the ECB restricted Revolut's EEA product launches in 2025 over control deficiencies and set the
  group's Lithuanian entity the highest Pillar 2 capital add-on of any bank it directly supervises (4.5% for
  2026, up from 3.7%). Hiring a Tier-1-bank operator to run the licensed entity, and splitting the role into
  a "bank CEO" (Fast) plus a "holding CEO" (Heneghan), is a **compliance/governance build-out** dressed as a
  growth story. (analysis)

## [1] Competitors / peers
- Neobank governance professionalization is an industry pattern: **Monzo, Starling, N26, Wise** all layered
  in ex-incumbent-bank compliance/risk leadership as they scaled into regulated banking. Revolut is late to
  this relative to its size (75M+ customers, ~$115bn share-sale valuation).
- N26 is the closest cautionary comp: BaFin capped its customer growth in 2021–2024 over AML/controls — a
  direct precedent for what a regulator-imposed "fix your controls first" phase costs a fast neobank. Revolut
  is now in an ECB-supervised version of the same dynamic.
- **Position:** catching up on governance, not pioneering. The hire signals Revolut is answering supervisory
  concerns rather than getting ahead of peers. (analysis)

## [2] Company history / fit
- Fits a clear 2025–2026 arc: full **UK banking licence** granted 11 Mar 2026 after a 5-year process and an
  18-month mobilisation (longest for a new UK bank); **French banking-licence** plans + €1bn Paris hub
  ([[Revolut plans second EU bank in Paris with French licence]]); ECB product restrictions and the highest
  Pillar 2 add-on ([[Revolut EU unit’s capital buffer raised the most by the watchdog]]).
- **Why Revolut acts this way:** Storonsky's "self-guided missiles" ship-fast culture is exactly what the ECB
  told Revolut to rein in. To reach the $200bn listing ambition it must be supervised like a systemic bank,
  which requires credible bank-grade leadership of the licensed entity. Installing an ex-Chase-UK CEO at the
  UAB level is the structural answer to that pressure. (analysis)
- Same-day sibling items confirm the theme: [[Revolut strengthens AML and fraud controls in Lithuania]] (the
  compliance side of the very same entity) and [[Revolut names Daisuke Aranami CEO of Japan unit]] (parallel
  regional-CEO professionalization). This item is specifically the **Bank UAB CEO** appointment.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty: low.** The appointment itself was already reported in May; a personnel move is not a product or a
  capability. No new customers, licences, or numbers attach to it.
- **Real value-add (if any):** a credible, regulator-acceptable CEO for the entity carrying Revolut's entire
  EU licence could de-risk the ECB relationship and unblock the paused EEA product pipeline — that is the
  substantive upside, but it is *governance de-risking*, not growth. It plays out over quarters and is not
  measurable today. (analysis)
- **Who captures value:** the licensed EU bank is the choke point for all EEA product launches; whoever runs
  it credibly with the ECB controls Revolut's continental expansion speed. That makes the seat important
  structurally even if this specific news is thin. (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Watch whether the ECB **lifts the EEA product-launch restrictions** and whether the Pillar 2 add-on eases
  in the 2027 SREP cycle — those, not the hire, are the real signals that the governance fix worked.
- The dual-hub structure (Lithuania UAB + planned French bank) means Fast's remit may narrow if/when the
  French licence lands; the Paris entity could absorb Western-Europe growth. (analysis)
- **Counterintuitive second-order effect:** professionalizing the licensed entity may *slow* Revolut's famed
  launch cadence — the tech-startup speed that built the valuation is precisely what supervision constrains.
  The open question (unchanged from the ECB-restriction note) is whether Revolut can keep its pace once run
  like a systemic bank chasing a $200bn listing.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** EU neobanking is scaling from "wallet" to full-stack licensed banking, and the choke point is now governance/regulatory build-out, not product. Revolut group reported FY2025 revenue up 46% to $6.0bn (£4.5bn) and pre-tax profit up 57% to $2.3bn (£1.7bn, 38% PBT margin), with retail customers +30% to 68.3m and business customers +33% to 767k (per Revolut FY2025 Annual Report, published 2026-04-29 — [group PR](https://www.revolut.com/en-US/news/revolut_reports_record_profit_of_2_3bn_for_2025_as_revenue_surges_to_6bn/); PDF cited in `ir_latest.json` -> https://assets.revolut.com/pdf/annualreport2025.pdf). Structure: the EEA business runs on a single Lithuanian banking licence (Revolut Bank UAB) held under Revolut Holdings Europe UAB — a deliberately consolidated hub, which is exactly why the CEO seat matters. "Why now": Revolut is mid-transition to a **dual-hub** model, adding a Paris HQ + a French banking-licence application to ACPR (>EUR 1bn / 200+ jobs pledged over 3 years), so the UAB seat is being professionalised as one of two EEA anchors rather than the sole one (per [Revolut Paris HQ PR](https://www.revolut.com/news/revolut_announces_new_western_europe_hq_based_in_paris_and_will_apply_for_french_banking_licence_to_support_regional_growth/)).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs for licensed neobanks: customer count, deposits/balances (funding base), revenue per user, and PBT margin. Basis of competition has shifted from UX to **regulatory reach + capital + trust**. Peers' recent moves: Monzo ~12.2m customers, GBP 60.4m PBT, eyeing a 2026 IPO at ~GBP 6-7bn ([Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/monzo/)); N26 ~4.8m active customers, ~EUR 440m 2024 revenue, reportedly weighing a down-round Series F ([Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/n26/)); Starling profitable since 2021. Revolut's position: **clearly ahead** on scale (68.3m vs Monzo's 12.2m) and profitability ($2.3bn PBT dwarfs peers'). Moat (analysis): scale + multi-market licence stack + switching costs from salary/primary-account penetration; the Fast hire — ex-Chase UK CEO, ex-mBank, ex-McKinsey — buys Tier-1 regulatory credibility into the licensed entity, the one asset money can't quickly replicate.

**Comps & multiples.** Internal comps from base:
- [[Revolut completes share sale at $75B valuation]] — Nov-2025 secondary at **$75bn** (up from $48bn Aug-2025).
- [[Revolut EU unit’s capital buffer raised the most by the watchdog]] — ECB lifted Revolut Holdings Europe UAB's Pillar 2 Requirement to 4.5% of RWA (from 3.7%), the largest hike among peers.
- [[Revolut share sale draws NVentures, a16z, Franklin Templeton]] — investor base on the $75bn round.

Multiple (group, private-round basis): P/S = $75bn / $6.0bn ~= **12.5x** revenue. Rich vs public/quasi-public neobanks (Monzo ~GBP 6-7bn on ~$1.25bn 2024 revenue ~= 4-6x), but Revolut grew revenue 46% YoY at a 38% PBT margin, so the premium tracks the growth-multiple relationship rather than flagging over-valuation on its own (analysis). EV/EBITDA and forward multiples: no data (private, no free clean figures). RBUAB standalone net profit/deposits: [UNSOURCED] — PDF ([RBUAB FY2025](https://assets.revolut.com/pdf/RBUAB_Annual_Financial_Report_2025_EN.pdf)) not parsed here.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Single-entity concentration + AML track record at the very entity Fast now runs.** In Apr-2025 the Bank of Lithuania fined Revolut Bank UAB EUR 3.5m (Lithuania's largest-ever AML penalty) for shortcomings in transaction/relationship monitoring; a EUR 70k late-filing fine preceded it (2022). ECB simultaneously raised the EEA unit's Pillar 2 buffer the most among peers — regulators signalling compliance hasn't kept pace with growth. Fast's mandate is effectively a remediation-and-scale job; execution risk is real. ([thepaypers](https://thepaypers.com/fraud-and-fincrime/news/revolut-fined-eur-35-million-for-aml-failings-in-lithuania))
2. **Audit credibility overhang.** BDO flagged IT-control weaknesses that put 2021 revenue at risk of material misstatement; Revolut only moved to EY as global auditor in Aug-2025. Governance repair is ongoing, not closed. ([kyc360](https://kyc360.com/knowledge-hub/resources/revolut-fined-for-aml-deficiencies))
3. **Dual-hub transition risk.** Splitting EEA anchoring between Lithuania and a not-yet-granted French licence (ACPR pending) adds coordination and regulatory-approval risk; a delayed French licence leaves UAB carrying disproportionate load.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a governance re-rating, not a product move: hiring a Tier-1 incumbent banker (ex-Chase UK) to run the licensed entity — while Heneghan moves up to lead Revolut Holdings Europe UAB — signals Revolut is buying regulatory legitimacy ahead of the French/US licence pushes and a likely future listing (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if the professionalisation sticks, expect no repeat AML enforcement at UAB and ACPR approval within ~12 months; trigger to watch — French banking licence grant and the next Bank of Lithuania / ECB supervisory action. What breaks it: a fresh AML/prudential penalty at UAB post-appointment.

> Note: the underlying story is a **leadership appointment** (Kuba Fast, ex-Chase UK / JPMorgan, as CEO of Revolut Bank UAB — the group's licensed EU credit institution in Lithuania), **not** an earnings release. Framed below: Revolut's latest reported financials (FY2025, published 2026-04-29) as the backdrop for the governance/scaling thesis this appointment supports. Latest reported period = FY ended 31 Dec 2025 (consolidated, Revolut Group Holdings Ltd).

**Verdict (headline read).** BEAT / strong growth — revenue **$6.0bn (£4.5bn), +46% YoY** · profit before tax **$2.3bn (£1.7bn), +57% YoY** · net profit **$1.7bn (£1.3bn), +70% YoY** (up from $1.0bn/£790m) · 5th consecutive year of net profitability. Private company, no analyst consensus → beat/miss is qualitative vs prior year. Key drivers: fee-income diversification + retail base growth to 68.3m. No formal forward guidance issued (private); management trajectory framing points to ~100m customers by mid-2027.

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Revenue: **$6.0bn / £4.5bn, +46% YoY** (from $4.0bn / £3.1bn in 2024).
- Profit before tax: **$2.3bn / £1.7bn, +57% YoY**; **PBT margin 38%**, up from 35% in 2024 (operating leverage).
- Net profit: **$1.7bn / £1.3bn, +70% YoY** (from $1.0bn / £790m).
- Retail customers: **68.3m, +30% YoY** (16m new retail added in 2025).
- Business customers: **767K, +33% YoY**.
- Total customer balances / deposits: **$67.5bn / £50.2bn, +66% YoY**.
- Fee-based revenue = **76% of turnover** (+4.2pp YoY), i.e. mix shift away from interest-rate dependence.
- 11 product lines each exceeded c.$135m (£100m) in revenue.

**By segment / driver (FY2025 revenue mix).**
- Card payments / interchange: £1.0bn ($1.3bn), 22.2% of revenue, +45% YoY — still the single largest stream.
- Interest income: £974m ($1.3bn), 21.6%, +23% YoY — grows slowest, hence the deliberate fee-diversification narrative.
- Subscriptions: £708m ($936m), 15.7%, +67% YoY — fastest-growing major line (premium tiers).
- Wealth (equities + crypto): £663m ($876m), 14.7%, +31% YoY.
- FX: £606m ($800m), 13.4%, +43% YoY.
Read: growth is broad-based, not one-line; the highest-momentum lines (subscriptions +67%, card +45%, FX +43%) are fee-based, corroborating the 76% fee-mix and the falling reliance on net interest as rates normalize.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public sell-side consensus (Revolut is private, unlisted) → variance is measured vs prior year only, not vs Street. Every headline line accelerated or held: revenue +46% (no large-base deceleration), PBT +57% with margin expansion to 38%, net profit +70%, deposits +66%. This is the 5th straight profitable year — a consistent trajectory, not a one-off print. [No consensus available → any beat/miss vs estimate is UNSOURCED.] No prior Revolut earnings note found in the corpus to `[[wikilink]]`.

**Guidance / forward.** No formal financial guidance (private issuer — none given). Management's forward framing (FY2025 results + subsequent commentary): 75m+ retail customers "as of today", ~1m new customers every 17 days, targeting 100m customers by mid-2027; licensed-bank operations live in 30+ of 40 markets (incl. UK, Mexico), plus a U.S. national bank charter filing in March 2026. **Thesis-flags (why this appointment matters against these numbers).**
1. **Governance/scaling of the licensed EU bank.** Revolut Bank UAB (Lithuania-licensed credit institution; specialised licence Dec-2018, full banking licence Dec-2021) is the legal vehicle behind the £50.2bn/$67.5bn deposit base and the EEA banking rollout. Installing an ex-Chase UK / JPMorgan operator as its CEO → why: signals a shift toward Tier-1 regulatory/risk rigor at the deposit-taking entity → why it matters: as customer balances grow +66% YoY, supervisory scrutiny (capital, liquidity, AML) rises non-linearly → second-order: de-risks the group's path to 100-market licensed-bank coverage and the pending U.S. charter, and supports IPO-readiness credibility.
2. **Deposit-led model needs bank-grade leadership.** Deposits +66% now materially outpace revenue (+46%); the licensed bank is becoming the balance-sheet engine, not just a compliance wrapper. A career banker at RBUAB aligns leadership with where the risk/regulatory weight actually sits. Second-order: strengthens the case for credit-product expansion (lending against that deposit base) without regulatory missteps.
3. **De-PR caution.** The appointment PR emphasizes pedigree ("agility of FinTech + Tier-1 rigor") but is silent on remit scope, board reporting lines, and whether it addresses any specific supervisory feedback. Treat as a strengthening signal, not evidence of a resolved issue — no financial figure attaches to the appointment itself.
