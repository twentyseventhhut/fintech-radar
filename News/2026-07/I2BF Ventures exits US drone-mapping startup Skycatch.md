## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
[0] TL;DR
I2BF Global Ventures (founder Ilya Golubovich) exited its stake in US drone-mapping / mine-site-intelligence startup Skycatch when Caterpillar (NYSE: CAT) acquired the company. Announced 7 July 2026; financial terms undisclosed. Skycatch's drones + AI produce a near-real-time 3D "digital twin" of active mine sites, which Caterpillar will feed into its RPMGlobal mine-planning and MineStar autonomous-haulage stacks.

[1] What happened
- Caterpillar acquired Skycatch, Inc. (spatial data capture, processing, analysis for mining). Announcement 7 July 2026; terms not disclosed.
- I2BF was a Skycatch backer — it participated in the ~$25M Series C (25 Mar 2021, alongside Gaingels, Wavemaker, Falkon, WND, ADB Ventures, Wealthing VC Club). The Caterpillar deal is the exit event for I2BF's holding.
- Positioning: second "intelligence-layer" M&A by Caterpillar in 2026, following its RPMGlobal acquisition. Skycatch's spatial layer becomes the perception feed unifying mine planning (RPM) and physical execution / autonomous haulage (MineStar).

[2] Key numbers
- Deal terms: undisclosed (no price/multiple public).
- Skycatch lifetime funding: ~$73.8M over 6 rounds (Seed + 5 early-stage). Largest round: $27.4M Series B (Aug 2017). Last round: ~$25M Series C (Mar 2021). Third-party (StartupHub) est. valuation ~$288M — not a transaction price.
- Notable prior investors: Google Ventures (GV), Autodesk (Series A, Nov 2015), Bee Partners, Avalon Ventures, Wavemaker, I2BF.

[3] Market context
- Drone analytics market: ~$22.8B (2026) → ~$115B (2034) at ~22% CAGR (Fortune Business Insights). Mining & quarrying ≈15% of drone-analytics demand (stockpile measurement, pit mapping, excavation monitoring).
- Drone-for-mining niche: ~$4.4B (2024) → ~$10.2B (2032), ~11% CAGR (Verified Market Research).
- Structural driver: digital-twin + drone pairing (continuous physical-vs-virtual comparison) is spreading in mining/construction; strategic OEMs (Caterpillar, Trimble, Komatsu) are vertically integrating perception + autonomy data layers.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Drone/geospatial analytics — spatial data capture + processing that turns UAV imagery into a near-real-time 3D "digital twin" of a site — is a small but fast-growing layer of the broader construction/mining site-intelligence stack. Market size estimates diverge widely by source: ~$5.5bn (2025) → ~$18.4bn (2030) at ~27% CAGR (Mordor, via researchandmarkets/mordorintelligence); Allied Market Research puts 2030 at ~$28.6bn at ~29% CAGR; a lower-bound estimate lands at ~$13bn/2030 at ~24.7% CAGR (per press releases via globenewswire) — treat the level as "no data / wide range", the direction (20-30% CAGR, construction the fastest sub-segment at ~32% CAGR) as the durable read. Structure: fragmented at the software/analytics layer (DroneDeploy, Pix4D, Propeller Aero, Skycatch) but the *value capture* is consolidating into heavy-equipment OEMs (Caterpillar, Komatsu) who own the fleet, the customer relationship and the downstream planning stack (MineStar, RPMGlobal). Why now: OEMs are racing to bundle spatial data into autonomous/optimized material-movement workflows — Caterpillar bought RPMGlobal (mining software) shortly before Skycatch (announced 2026-07-07), signalling a build-out of an integrated mine-data platform where standalone analytics vendors become features, not franchises (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs (analysis): data-capture cycle time (speed to a usable digital twin), spatial accuracy/precision, and integration depth into planning/execution systems; for the vendors, ARR + logo concentration among large miners/contractors. Key players: standalone analytics/mapping — DroneDeploy (~$178M raised, ~$610M valuation May-2025, reached break-even Sep-2025), Propeller Aero (~$57.5M raised), Pix4D (bootstrapped, ~$14.7M ARR); strategic acquirers — Caterpillar and Komatsu (the two mining-equipment incumbents). Basis of competition: accuracy + processing speed + distribution via the OEM/fleet channel. Recent moves: Caterpillar acquired RPMGlobal, then Skycatch (2026-07-07, terms undisclosed) to fold spatial data into MineStar/RPM; Propeller acquired Spacesium (2026-02). Protagonist positioning: Skycatch was a niche leader in high-precision mine spatial data (digital-twin of the pit) — its moat was intangibles/proprietary processing rather than scale; as a ~$74M-raised company it exits into a strategic that already owns the customer, i.e. absorbed rather than scaled independently.

**Comps & multiples.** Deal terms undisclosed → EV/Revenue, price-per-ARR = **no data**; no public multiple can be computed for this exit. Reference points (private, sourced): Skycatch raised ~$71.8-75M total over 6 rounds (34 investors), largest a $27.4M Series B (Aug-2017), last a $25M Series C (2021-03-25); notable investors GV/Google Ventures, Avalon, WaveMaker, Autodesk, ADB Ventures — and, tellingly, **Komatsu** (Caterpillar's chief rival) led a 2017 round. Peer valuation anchors: DroneDeploy ~$610M (2025, ~$178M raised) is the closest scaled comp — implies a software-analytics business can reach mid-hundreds-of-$M as an independent, so an undisclosed Skycatch price is plausibly a modest strategic tuck-in well below that (analysis, `[UNSOURCED]` on the actual figure). Internal comps in-base are thin (fintech corpus): nearest adjacencies are hardware/defense-drone financings [[Stark raises $569 million at $4 billion valuation for defense drones]] (different sub-vertical — attack drones, not analytics) — not a valuation comp. Distribution not computed; qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disintermediation of the analytics layer** — Skycatch's outcome is the base case for the sub-sector: OEMs that own the fleet + planning stack (CAT, Komatsu) can acquire or in-house spatial analytics, capping the standalone TAM and the exit ceiling for the remaining independents (DroneDeploy, Propeller). Second-order: future rounds for pure-play analytics vendors get harder to price.
2. **Customer/end-market concentration** — value is tied to a handful of large miners and to commodity-cycle capex; a mining-capex downturn compresses both the vendors' ARR and OEM appetite for tuck-ins.
3. **Undisclosed terms = weak exit signal** — an unpriced sale of a ~$74M-raised, 13-year-old company (Series C in 2021, none since) reads as a soft/strategic exit rather than a marquee return; for I2BF the realized multiple is unverifiable (`[UNSOURCED]`) and plausibly modest.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a consolidation signal, not a re-rating: heavy-equipment OEMs are assembling end-to-end mine-data platforms and treating drone analytics as a feature to own, not a market to license from (analysis). Watch for the next OEM tuck-in (Komatsu response, or CAT buying more of the planning stack) as the trigger; the thesis breaks if a standalone analytics vendor (e.g. DroneDeploy) instead scales into an independent platform IPO/large round — that would show the layer can capture value on its own rather than being absorbed.
