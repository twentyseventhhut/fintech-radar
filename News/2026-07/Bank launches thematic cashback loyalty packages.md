На полях Финансового конгресса Банка России Россельхозбанк представил обновленный формат программы лояльности –  Тематический кешбэк. Банк предлагает готовые тематические подборки, объединяющие наиболее востребованные категории покупок в зависимости от интересов, привычек и образа жизни. 
 
Новый подход делает использование программы лояльности более простым и понятным. Вместо того чтобы анализировать множество категорий и выбирать наиболее подходящие, клиенту достаточно определить тему, которая соответствует его повседневным расходам. Все необходимые категории уже объединены внутри выбранной темы.
 
Тем, кто часто путешествует, подойдет тема «Путешествия». Любителям встречаться с друзьями в кафе и ресторанах – «Вкус жизни». Для тех, кто проводит много времени за городом, занимается домом или садом, создана тема «Дачная». Молодым клиентам банк предлагает тему «Молодежная», а для представителей старшего поколения – «Надежная», включающую категории наиболее востребованных повседневных расходов.
 
Каждая тема объединяет несколько категорий повышенного кешбэка, сформированных на основе предпочтений клиентов с похожими интересами и стилем жизни. Благодаря этому пользователи программы лояльности получают дополнительную выгоду именно за те покупки, которые совершают регулярно.
 
Новый формат стал очередным этапом развития программы лояльности Россельхозбанка и отражает общий тренд на персонализацию банковских сервисов. Банк делает акцент на том, чтобы финансовые продукты были не только выгодными, но и максимально удобными в использовании.
 
«Мы стремимся создавать продукты, которые учитывают реальные потребности клиентов. Тематический кешбэк позволяет получать больше выгоды от привычных покупок без сложных настроек и необходимости каждый месяц выбирать отдельные категории. Достаточно определить тему, которая соответствует образу жизни клиента, а мы уже подобрали внутри неё самые актуальные категории», – отметила заместитель Председателя Правления Россельхозбанка Елена Батурова.
 
Обновленная программа уже доступна в мобильном приложении Россельхозбанка. Клиенты могут выбрать наиболее подходящую тему и получать повышенный кешбэк за покупки, которые действительно соответствуют их повседневным привычкам и образу жизни.

# Context-enrichment: Rosselkhozbank (RSHB) launches "Thematic Cashback" loyalty format
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**Who/what (the generic title decoded):** The bank is **Россельхозбанк / Rosselkhozbank (RSHB / Russian Agricultural Bank)** — 100% state-owned, systemically important, ~5th-largest RU bank by assets (~5.5tn RUB, mid-2025), whose core franchise is agricultural lending (~80% share of ag credit); retail is a secondary line. The product is **"Тематический кешбэк" ("Thematic Cashback")** — bonus categories grouped into ready-made themed packages by customer lifestyle: Travel, "Taste of life" (cafes/restaurants), "Dacha", "Youth", "Reliable" (everyday for seniors). Live in the RSHB mobile app; announced ~2026-07-03 around the CBR Financial Congress.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Not a new product — a **rebrand + expansion of an existing theme-selection cashback**. RSHB already ran quarterly "тема" selection with 4 themes (Возможно всё / Путешествия / Дачная / Вкус жизни) live by spring 2026 (selection deadline 30 Jun 2026). July's "Thematic Cashback" = same mechanic, new name, + two demographic packages (Youth, Seniors). Zero economics disclosed in the PR: no rates, no caps, no fees. The only substantive claim is Deputy Chair Elena Baturova's "more benefit without complex settings."
**Why framed this way:** (a) timed to the CBR Financial Congress (1-3 Jul, St. Petersburg) for PR lift — but cashback/loyalty was **not** on the congress agenda (monetary policy / FX / crypto), so this is association, not a congress session or CBR endorsement (analysis). (b) "No complex settings / pre-set packages" is the sector's euphemism for **removing the high-value wildcard categories customers self-select** → i.e. a cost-optimization dressed as convenience (analysis, consistent with peers' stated pivots).

## [1] Competitors / peers
"Curated themed category packages" is **table-stakes**, not innovation, and RSHB is a follower on economics:
- **Sberbank / СберСпасибо** — monthly category selection since 2024 simplification; up to ~10% category / up to 20% partner (by tier).
- **T-Bank (ex-Tinkoff)** — 4 rotating categories, 1-15% category / up to 30% partners; crucially pays **real rubles, not points**.
- **Alfa-Bank** — up to 30% category; caps 5k/7k/15k RUB by tier; Alfa Travel already cut to flat 3-4%.
- **VTB** — publicly forecasts loyalty spend falling ~1.5x, pivoting to "personalization."
RSHB's underlying program (per May 2026 disclosures) pays **points at a haircut (~0.85:1 non-premium)**, up to 15% in points, 5k RUB/mo cap, ~10k RUB/mo min spend to redeem, premium gated at 3m RUB balance. → **Behind peers**: points-with-haircut vs T-Bank's cash.
**Why:** everyone landed on category-cashback + monthly rotation years ago; the only room left to "differentiate" cheaply is packaging/branding, which is exactly what RSHB did.

## [2] Company history / fit
An ag-focused state bank whose retail base skews rural/regional/older. **Дачная** (gardeners) and **Надежная** (seniors) map neatly onto that demographic — the one mildly defensible, on-brand differentiator vs urban fintech rivals. Retail loyalty PR matters optically to RSHB precisely because retail is its growth-secondary line, not its identity.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Low novelty (rebrand of a live product; peers did category-packs first). **No traction metrics disclosed** (no user counts, no uptake). Value-add for the customer is questionable: pre-set bundles reduce optionality, and the underlying economics (points, haircut redemption, min-spend gate) are below-market. **Who captures the value:** by bundling categories the bank likely trims payout vs open self-selection → margin stays with the bank while the framing implies a customer gift (analysis).

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Sector-wide **2026 cashback retreat** is the real story this item illustrates: (a) VAT exemption on card acquiring removed + VAT raised 20%→22%, both from 1 Jan 2026, directly taxing the interchange pool that funds cashback; (b) key rate down 21%→15% (Mar 2026) compressing margins. Aggregate bank cashback payouts (~500bn RUB in 2025) are expected to reverse. Regulatory pressure on cashback is **fiscal, not prudential** — CBR has **no standing interchange cap** (only a temporary 1% acquiring cap Apr-Aug 2022, not renewed) and no loyalty-program rules. So "thematic/personalized" is the industry's cover story for spending less while appearing generous (VTB frames it explicitly). Reading RSHB's move as cost optimization dressed as benefit is the most likely interpretation.

3. **Cash or points?** — **Points**, redeemed at ~0.85:1 (non-premium) / up to 1:1 (premium). Materially worse than T-Bank's real-ruble cashback. (From May program; July unconfirmed.)
4. **Fees / spend minimums?** — Existing program requires ≥10k RUB/mo spend to redeem; premium tier gated at 3m RUB balance. July terms **open/unconfirmed**.
5. **Was it really "presented at the CBR Financial Congress"?** — Congress ran 1-3 Jul 2026 but cashback/loyalty was **not** on the published agenda (monetary policy, FX, crypto). Dateline = PR association, not a congress session. Partly open.
6. **Live or announced?** — **Live** ("уже доступна" in-app), per the PR.
7. **Does "thematic" cut costs while looking generous?** — Highly plausible: pre-set packages remove the high-value wildcard categories customers self-select and cap optionality. Fits VTB's stated ~1.5x loyalty-spend cut and the "personalization" euphemism. Directionally yes; not admitted.
8. **Has CBR capped interchange / restricted cashback in 2024-2026?** — **No standing cap.** Only precedent: temporary 1% acquiring cap (Apr-Aug 2022, socially-significant goods), not renewed. No loyalty-program rules found.
9. **So what's the real pressure on cashback?** — **Fiscal, not prudential:** VAT exemption on acquiring removed + VAT hike to 22% (both 1 Jan 2026) tax the interchange pool; key rate 21%→15% compresses margins.
10. **Are Russian banks cutting cashback in 2025-2026?** — **Yes, broadly.** VTB projects ~1.5x cuts; "end of the cashback race" narrative; Alfa Travel flattened to 3-4%. Aggregate ~500bn RUB (2025) payouts expected to reverse.
11. **Is RSHB competitive on economics?** — **No.** Points-with-haircut, 15% ceiling, low caps vs T-Bank (30% partner, cash) and Alfa (30%, 15k cap top tier).
12. **Any user / traction numbers?** — **None disclosed. Open.**
13. **Does it fit RSHB's franchise?** — Yes; Dacha/Seniors packages match its rural/regional/older base — the one defensible differentiator.
14. **Could July rates be better than the old program?** — **Open/unconfirmed;** no evidence, and sector direction is downward, so upside unlikely.
15. **Is CBR endorsing this?** — **No.** Announced during the CBR congress ≠ CBR endorsement.

Importance: 2/5 — Retail-loyalty rebrand by the retail-secondary arm of a state ag bank: rebrand of a live product, table-stakes mechanics peers deployed earlier, no economics disclosed, below-market redemption (points at a haircut). Above a pure "1" only because (a) it's a clean data point in the sector-wide 2026 cashback retreat (VAT-on-acquiring + falling key rate), and (b) the Dacha/Seniors packaging is mildly on-brand/differentiated for RSHB's demographic. ## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian retail-banking card-loyalty/cashback. Market size (spend, sourced): total cashback paid by Russian banks rose from ~RUB 99bn (2019) to ~RUB 141bn (2021) to ~RUB 400bn (2024) (per market press, via mn.ru/banki.ru, as of 2026); for 2026 banks guide to a *cut* — VTB analysts estimate leading banks reduce loyalty spend ~1.5x (per VTB, via mn.ru, Mar 2026). Structure: consolidated, state-adjacent — top-5 banks dominate; RSHB is #5 by assets, a systemically-important, 100%-state-owned agro-focused lender (per banki.ru/RAEX, 2026). Cashback is funded largely out of acquiring/interchange income, so its economics track the rate cycle. **Why now:** CBR key rate fell from a 21% peak (end-2024) to 15% (Mar 2026), compressing net-interest margin and the yield on the deposits that subsidize rewards; simultaneously tax/cost pressure on acquiring squeezes the interchange pool (per mn.ru, Mar 2026). So the sector-wide move is de-generosity — "the end of the cashback race" — and RSHB's "thematic bundles" is best read as simplification + cost-containment framed as personalization (analysis). De-PR: the release says "announced/available in-app" and is silent on cashback *rates*, caps, and category economics — the numbers that actually determine customer value.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: reward-payout as % of card turnover, acquiring/interchange take, active-card MAU, category attach. Basis of competition = breadth/rate of cashback + UX of category selection + breadth of partner network. Recent peer moves (dated): T-Bank launched "Т-Банк Таргет" — algorithmic *personalized* cashback replacing fixed pick-your-category (9 Apr 2026); Alfa-Bank kept payouts up (+13%) but capped the number of selectable categories (Apr–Jun 2026); Sber tightened the spend threshold for its SberSchet rate boost from RUB 30k to RUB 50k (5 May 2026); RSHB itself reworked base loyalty tiers ("Standard" 5 categories / "Prestige" 7 categories) effective 1 Jul 2026 (per rshb.ru/vbr.ru). Protagonist position: **catching up / niche follower** — Sber, Alfa and especially T-Bank lead on AI-personalization; RSHB's pre-packaged lifestyle themes ("Путешествия", "Дачная", "Молодежная", "Надежная") are a *curation* layer, not per-user ML like T-Bank Target (analysis). Moat: distribution into under-banked rural/agro geographies + state ownership — a captive-base moat, not a product-tech one (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** RSHB is unlisted/state-owned — no market cap, no traded multiple (no data); EV/Revenue, P/E = not applicable. Financial anchors (sourced): equity RUB 576bn, +14.7% YoY (FY2025); agro loan book >RUB 2tn; credit rating AA+.ru stable (NKR) (per rshb.ru/ratings.ru). Internal comps (base, `[[wikilink]]`, filename w/o .md): [[T-Investments launches loyalty platform paying shares for purchases]] (RU, shares-for-purchases loyalty, Jul 2026 — different mechanic, same "loyalty as retention lever" theme); [[Klarna launches global membership program with cashback and perks]] (Nov 2025); [[Nubank launches Vantagens Nu rewards hub with Shopee deals]] (2026); [[Venmo launches Stash debit card rewards program]] (Nov 2025). Distribution not computed (comps are qualitative product analogs, not valuation peers). Flag: in-line with a global shift from open-ended cashback to curated/bundled/membership rewards; RSHB is a *late-cycle* mover in a *shrinking* domestic reward pool.

**Risk flags.**
1. Value-erosion / undisclosed economics — release omits rates and caps while the whole sector is cutting payouts ~1.5x; "themes" can mask a lower effective cashback rate, risking customer disappointment and churn once rates are compared (second-order: reputational, in a price-sensitive segment).
2. Personalization gap vs T-Bank/Sber — static curated bundles are weaker than per-user ML targeting; if peers' algorithmic cashback wins share of wallet, RSHB's simpler product may retain only its captive rural base (second-order: loyalty spend delivers less incremental engagement per ruble).
3. Margin/funding dependence — reward budget is levered to interchange + the falling key rate; a further rate cut or acquiring-cost rise forces another trim, making the new format hard to sustain at attractive rates (second-order: pro-cyclical loyalty that thins exactly when competition heats up).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Confirms the 2026 Russian loyalty theme: from "cashback arms race" to cost-disciplined, curated/personalized rewards — a defensive re-tooling, not expansion (analysis). Watch: whether RSHB discloses actual thematic-bundle rates/caps and whether it follows T-Bank into ML personalization. Falsifiable thesis: if RSHB's effective cashback rate is materially below Alfa/T-Bank, the "themes" are a payout cut in disguise and won't move retail share; thesis breaks if RSHB pairs bundles with competitive rates and grows active retail cards (trigger: next RSHB retail metrics / independent rate comparisons).
