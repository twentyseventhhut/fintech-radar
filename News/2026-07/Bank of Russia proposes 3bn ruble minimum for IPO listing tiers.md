# Context-enrichment: Bank of Russia proposes RUB 3bn minimum IPO size for tier 1/2 listing
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- On **6 July 2026**, on the sidelines of the CBR Financial Congress, **Ekaterina Abasheeva**, director of the CBR's corporate-relations department, told Interfax the Bank of Russia will propose a **minimum IPO placement size of RUB 3bn** as a condition for inclusion in the **first and second quotation lists** (tier 1/2 listing) on the Moscow Exchange (MOEX).
- Second, distinct lever: a **differentiated free-float scale** for tier 1/2, keyed to the issuer's **IFRS equity (own capital)**:
  - Capital **up to RUB 30bn** → minimum free float **10%, but not less than RUB 3bn** in market value.
  - Capital **from RUB 30bn** → free float via a formula where **RUB 1tn of capital ⇒ 5% free float and a RUB 50bn offering**.
- **Timeline:** public consultation **late July / early Aug 2026**; the note's primary source (Interfax) says "come into force from next year"; corroborating Russian coverage (Vedomosti, Kommersant, Mail.ru/Finance) states **effective from 2027** — treat 2027 as the operative date.
- **The de-PR'd point:** framed as a "free-float requirement," but the operative change is a **hard RUB 3bn size floor** for *quality* listing tiers. It does NOT ban small IPOs — a sub-3bn issuer can still list, but only in **tier 3** (the uncategorised list), which excludes it from index inclusion, many institutional mandates, and the "quotation-list" prestige. This is a **maturity gate**, not a prohibition.
- **Why structured this way (analysis):** the CBR's stated goal — "make the IPO market *qualitative*… limits will foster liquidity and market pricing" — targets the real 2025-26 pathology: micro-floats with no post-listing liquidity, poor price discovery, and retail losses. Anchoring to *free-float value in rubles* (not just %) is deliberate: a 10%-of-a-tiny-company float can still be a symbolic, illiquid stub, so the RUB 3bn absolute floor forces a **minimum tradable pool** regardless of company size.

## [1] Competitors / peers (policy peers & the affected issuer cohort)
- **The affected cohort is concrete and already visible in the corpus.** 2026 YTD MOEX had **only two IPOs, both below RUB 3bn**: **B2B-RTS** (Sovcombank group, ~RUB 2.43bn incl. stabilisation, landed **tier 2**) and **Incab Holding** (~RUB 2.04bn, only **tier 3**) — see [[Mergers.ru Incab Holding raises 2 billion rubles in IPO]]. Under the new floor, B2B-RTS's tier-2 slot would have been **disallowed**. 2025's cohort: **DOM.RF** (RUB 31.7bn, tier 1), **Basis** (RUB 3bn, tier 2), **GloraX** (RUB 2.1bn, tier 2) — GloraX would also have been **excluded** from tier 2.
- **Regulatory prior art / direction:** this is the tightening leg of a two-part CBR-driven listing overhaul. Earlier the CBR (and Putin's directive) flagged **differentiating free-float by company size**; separately, **MOEX itself loosened** tier-2 free-float requirements from **1 Nov 2025 (favourable regime to 30 Jul 2027)** because it saw *insufficient demand* for big IPOs. So the exchange loosened to keep the pipe open; the CBR is now imposing a *quality floor* on top — **partially offsetting** MOEX's easing (analysis).
- **Position:** Russia's listing-quality bar is being **raised specifically at the small end**, at a moment when the *entire* primary market is thin. The peer signal from the corpus — [[Russian pre-IPO platform Rounds shuts down]] (standalone pre-IPO operators structurally loss-making) and [[VIM Investments Pre-IPO funds pay investors 9.2bn rubles]] (exits harvested only via the one domestic MOEX window) — is that the RU new-issue funnel is **narrow and fragile**; the CBR is choosing quality over volume in that funnel.

## [2] Regulator/market history & fit
- **Trajectory:** the CBR has spent 2025-26 tightening the IPO chain — mandatory independent research, forward-guidance rules in prospectuses, allocation disclosure, curbs on inflated forecasts (noted in [[VIM Investments Pre-IPO funds pay investors 9.2bn rubles]] market-research). A RUB 3bn listing-tier floor is the **next logical step**: after policing *disclosure*, police *structure/liquidity*.
- **Why the CBR acts this way (analysis):** its own **March 2026 review** found **only 7 of 19** RU IPOs of the prior two years stayed above offer; 2025 IPOs fell ~5.2% day-1 / ~8.7% within a month; **retail participation fell ~4.5x YoY** (per [[Mergers.ru Incab Holding raises 2 billion rubles in IPO]]). A string of small, illiquid, underwater IPOs is a **retail-trust problem** for the regulator. The structural pressure is: **protect a captive domestic retail base** (foreign capital is sanctioned-out; MOEX is the only exit venue) from a low-quality primary market — because if retail sours, the *entire* onshore equity-financing channel that replaced Western capital dries up.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty: moderate.** A *minimum ruble IPO size* tied to quotation tiers is a **genuinely new structural rule** for MOEX (prior gates were % free-float and capitalisation, not an absolute placement floor). It is **still a proposal**, not law — consultation opens end-July, effect **2027**. So: *announced, not in force.*
- **Real bite (the substance):** unlike vague "we aim to improve quality" statements, this has a **hard, quantified threshold (RUB 3bn) that would have excluded 3 of the last 5 RU IPOs** from the quality tiers — i.e. it is a **material, testable constraint**, not PR garnish.
- **Where value/margin shifts (analysis):** the floor **pushes the small end from tier 2 to tier 3** (or off-exchange to pre-IPO platforms — connecting to [[Russian pre-IPO platform Rounds shuts down]], whose economics already failed). Second order: it **advantages larger issuers and underwriters of size** (Sber, VTB, sovereign names like DOM.RF) and disadvantages small industrials doing balance-sheet-repair listings (the Incab/GloraX archetype). It **improves average listing quality but shrinks tier-1/2 *count*** in an already-thin market — a quality-vs-volume trade the CBR is consciously making.
- **What breaks it:** if the RU primary market fails to recover on rate cuts, a RUB 3bn floor could **choke the tier-1/2 pipeline entirely** (few issuers clear it), turning a quality gate into a de-facto freeze on quotation-list IPOs.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Process:** public consultation **late Jul / early Aug 2026** → effect **2027**. Expect pushback from small-cap issuers, VC/pre-IPO players and possibly MOEX (which just *loosened* free-float to keep deals flowing).
- **Macro backdrop:** CBR cut the key rate to **14.25% (19 Jun 2026)** — a cautious 25bp, below the 50bp some expected — signalling gradual easing. The RU IPO recovery is widely forecast to be **back-loaded to H2 2026**, contingent on rate cuts; brokers project **up to ~20 IPOs / RUB 50-100bn in 2026** (a ~5x jump in count vs 2025's ~3-4). Market Power analysts note the **2025 median IPO was ~RUB 2.5bn — below the new floor**, so a large share of the small-deal flow would be pushed out of the quotation lists.
- **Counterintuitive second order (analysis):** raising the quality bar *during* a fragile recovery could **suppress the very volume the market needs to re-broaden** — but the CBR appears to judge that **another wave of illiquid, underwater small-caps would do more damage to retail trust** than a smaller, higher-quality pipeline. If rate cuts revive demand and issuers up-size to clear RUB 3bn, the rule could **healthily concentrate** liquidity; if demand stays weak, it **gates the market shut** at the top tiers. Watch: consultation feedback, whether the H2-2026 pipeline actually fires, and whether issuers restructure deals to clear the floor.

## Top challenge / red-team questions

1. **Is this a ban on small IPOs or a tier gate?** A **tier gate**. A sub-RUB-3bn issuer can still IPO, but only into **tier 3** (uncategorised), losing index inclusion, many institutional mandates and quotation-list status. Not a prohibition. — answered.

2. **How many recent RU IPOs would have failed the RUB 3bn floor?** At least **3 of the last 5**: Incab (~2.04bn, was tier 3 anyway), B2B-RTS (~2.43bn, would lose its tier-2 slot), GloraX (~2.1bn, would lose tier 2). Both 2026 YTD IPOs were sub-3bn. The **2025 median IPO was ~RUB 2.5bn — below the floor.** — answered; this is the rule's real bite.

3. **Is it in force?** No — **proposal only.** Consultation late Jul / early Aug 2026; effect **2027** (Vedomosti/Kommersant/Mail.ru say 2027; Interfax on-note says "next year"). Announced, not live. — answered.

4. **Does the RUB 3bn refer to total placement or free-float value?** Two linked thresholds: a **RUB 3bn minimum placement (IPO) size** for tier 1/2, AND a free-float rule where for capital <RUB 30bn the float must be ≥10% **and ≥RUB 3bn in value**. The absolute-ruble anchor is the point — a % float alone can still be illiquid. — answered.

5. **Why anchor to IFRS equity for the large-cap formula?** To scale the free-float requirement down for very large issuers (RUB 1tn capital ⇒ only 5% float / RUB 50bn offering) so mega-caps aren't forced to sell impractically large stakes, while still guaranteeing a large absolute tradable pool. — answered (mechanism).

6. **Does this contradict MOEX's own Nov 2025 free-float easing?** Partly. MOEX **loosened** tier-2 free-float (regime to 30 Jul 2027) citing weak big-IPO demand; the CBR now **imposes a size/liquidity floor**. Exchange wants volume; regulator wants quality — a genuine tension. — answered.

7. **Who is silent about what?** The proposal is silent on **transition/grandfathering** for issuers mid-pipeline, on whether the RUB 3bn floor is inflation-indexed, and on how tier-3 (sub-floor) issuers' liquidity/investor protection is handled. — open.

8. **Does raising the bar during a fragile recovery risk freezing the top tiers?** Real risk: if few issuers clear RUB 3bn (median was 2.5bn) and demand stays weak, tier-1/2 IPO count could **collapse**. The CBR is betting rate cuts up-size deals. — open (depends on H2-2026 pipeline + rates).

9. **Who benefits / who loses?** Benefits: **large issuers, sovereign names (DOM.RF), size underwriters (Sber/VTB), and retail** (better average liquidity). Loses: **small industrials doing balance-sheet-repair listings** (Incab/GloraX archetype), which get pushed to tier 3 or to pre-IPO venues. — answered (analysis).

10. **Does it push volume to pre-IPO platforms?** Plausible second-order — but those are themselves weak: [[Russian pre-IPO platform Rounds shuts down]] shows standalone pre-IPO operators are structurally loss-making, and exits still funnel through the single MOEX window ([[VIM Investments Pre-IPO funds pay investors 9.2bn rubles]]). So displaced small issuers may have **no good alternative**. — answered (analysis).

11. **Is the CBR's "quality/liquidity" rationale credible or cover?** Credible: its **March 2026 review** found only 7/19 recent IPOs above offer, ~4.5x drop in retail participation — a genuine retail-trust problem the floor addresses. — answered.

12. **Effective date — 2027 or "next year" (i.e. 2027 from a 2026 statement)?** These reconcile: statement made mid-2026, "next year" = **2027**, matching Vedomosti/Kommersant. — answered.

13. **Is there prior art / a directive behind it?** Yes — builds on **Putin's directive to differentiate free-float by capital size** and the CBR's ongoing IPO-chain reform (research mandates, prospectus guidance rules). Not a standalone surprise. — answered.

14. **What's the macro read — will it matter in practice soon?** Only if the forecast **H2-2026 IPO wave** (up to ~20 deals / RUB 50-100bn) actually fires on rate cuts (key rate now 14.25%). If the market stays paused, the rule is moot until issuers can clear the floor. — open.

15. **Freshness/duplicate?** **Fresh** — no prior corpus note on this specific proposal; adjacent notes are RU-primary-market context/peers, not the same event. No duplicate_of. — answered.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is a listing-standards / market-microstructure reform in Russian capital markets, not a company deal. Size/structure: the Russian IPO market is small, retail-dominated and cyclical — 2024 was a local peak with 15 IPOs (19 issuers offered incl. SPOs) raising >RUB 102bn (per Bank of Russia, cbr.ru, 2024 review; MOEX itself counts 13 IPOs + 4 SPOs, moex.com FY2024), after 8 IPOs in 2023. Activity then collapsed: 3 IPOs in 2025 (DOM.RF RUB 31.7bn; Glorax RUB 2.1bn; Bazis RUB 3bn — per note primary/Interfax) and only 2 YTD-2026, both below the proposed RUB 3bn floor (B2B-RTS RUB 2.43bn incl. stabilisation → tier-2; Inkab Holding RUB 2.04bn → tier-3) (Interfax, 6 Jul 2026, via note). Barriers are regulatory (MOEX listing tiers A1/A2/B, CBR prospectus rules) and — critically — liquidity/demand: retail fragmented demand can clear RUB 3-5bn deals but not the RUB 50bn+ placements foreign funds used to underwrite (Izvestia/secondary, via WebSearch). Why now: the 2023-24 "parade of small- and mid-caps" left retail badly burned — after six months 2024-vintage IPOs averaged roughly -25%, and only ~5-7 of ~18-19 names traded above issue (Izvestia, united24media, via WebSearch), a direct political/prudential embarrassment against Putin's 2030 target to double market cap/GDP (Moscow Times, Feb 2026). Second order: the CBR is reframing "small IPO" as a quality/liquidity defect, using the free-float rules as a maturity gate rather than a pure disclosure lever.

**Competitive landscape.** The "KPIs" here are placement size, free float (share of stock actually tradable), post-IPO liquidity and price discovery. Key actors and basis of competition: **Bank of Russia (regulator/gatekeeper)** vs **MOEX (venue, revenue-motivated to keep listing flow up)** vs **issuers/underwriters (want easier access)**. Notably, MOEX has pulled the OTHER way — earlier in 2026 it proposed *easing* free-float requirements citing insufficient demand for big IPOs (Interfax, via WebSearch) — so the CBR's minimum-size proposal is in visible tension with the exchange's growth incentive. The CBR's own free-float scale (proposed): capital <RUB 30bn → min 10% float but ≥RUB 3bn; capital ≥RUB 30bn → formula (at RUB 1trn capital → 5% float / RUB 50bn offering) (note primary). This sits alongside a wider CBR tightening arc: qualified-investor asset threshold raised RUB 6m→12m, and →RUB 24m from 1 Jan 2026 (Denuo/CBR, via WebSearch), plus the 2025 "IPO in new format" disclosure reform (cbr.ru id=23304). Protagonist (the regulator) position: firmly in the driver's seat — this is a consultation (public discussion late-Jul/early-Aug 2026, in force from 2027), so *announced, not adopted*. Moat: statutory authority; MOEX cannot overrule listing-eligibility rules set by the CBR. (analysis)

**Comps & multiples.** No equity multiple is computable — the subject is a rule, not an issuer, so EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E = n/a. The load-bearing arithmetic is the *reach* of the threshold: on the record, **both 2026 IPOs (100%) and 2 of 3 2025 IPOs would fail the RUB 3bn floor** — Glorax (RUB 2.1bn) and Bazis (RUB 3bn, exactly at the line) join B2B-RTS (2.43bn) and Inkab (2.04bn) below or at the bar; only DOM.RF (RUB 31.7bn) clears it comfortably (note primary). So on 2025-26 data ~4 of the last 5 placements would have been pushed out of quality tiers A1/A2. Internal corpus comps (via grep) — Russian capital-markets / listing-reform context: [[Russian pre-IPO platform Rounds shuts down]] (same theme: sub-scale retail-equity venues are structurally troubled; MOEX START consolidating pre-IPO flow), [[Russia weighs selling up to 5% of NSPK to single investor]] and [[VTB takes large Ozon share stake as collateral]] (Russian equity-market plumbing). Distribution not computed — no comparable valuation set; qualitative only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Chokes off the small/mid-cap pipeline (composition risk).** With ~4 of the last 5 placements below RUB 3bn, a hard floor for tiers A1/A2 relegates small issuers to tier-3 (Inkab precedent) — thinner index inclusion, less institutional mandate eligibility, weaker demand. Why it matters: it could shrink an already-collapsing IPO count (3 in 2025, 2 YTD-2026) precisely when the state wants MORE listings for the 2030 cap/GDP target — a policy contradiction.
2. **Regulator-vs-exchange incentive conflict.** MOEX simultaneously proposed *easing* free-float rules for weak demand; the CBR is *tightening* size/float. Why it matters: the final rule is a negotiated outcome, so the RUB 3bn number and the free-float formula may soften in consultation (late Jul-Aug 2026) — treat the threshold as a proposal, not settled.
3. **Doesn't fix the demand-side root cause.** The retail-heavy, low-float, poor price-discovery problem that produced ~-25%/6mo returns is a *demand and float* issue; a minimum ticket raises quality of *who lists* but does nothing to bring back the institutional/foreign underwriting that could actually absorb larger, more liquid deals. Why it matters: absent that, larger mandated deals may simply not happen rather than happening better.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Read this as the CBR trading *quantity* of IPOs for *quality/liquidity* — a deliberate re-rating of what counts as an "investable" listing, consistent with the qualified-investor threshold hikes and 2025 disclosure reform (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if adopted near RUB 3bn, expect 2027 to show FEWER but LARGER tier-1/2 IPOs, with a visible migration of sub-RUB-3bn names into tier-3 or into pre-IPO/OTC venues (MOEX START). What breaks the thesis: the number gets watered down in consultation (MOEX pushback) or a rate cut revives retail demand enough that small IPOs regain liquidity on their own. Triggers to watch: the late-Jul/early-Aug 2026 public consultation draft, and whether the free-float *formula* (not just the RUB 3bn floor) survives intact.
