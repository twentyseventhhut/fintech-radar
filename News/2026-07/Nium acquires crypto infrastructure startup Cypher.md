## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** On 2026-07-08 Nium (Singapore-based, Temasek/GIC-backed cross-border payments infrastructure) announced it has **acquired Cypher**, a San Francisco crypto-native, non-custodial wallet and card-issuing startup, to expand its "fiat-to-on-chain money movement infrastructure." Terms were **not disclosed**. This is an acqui-hire/tech deal: Cypher is **sunsetting** (mobile app, dApp and business platform shut down **2026-09-06**); founder **Kuberan "Kube" Marimuthu** joins Nium as **VP of Digital Assets** reporting to CEO Prajit Nanu, and Cypher's engineering team joins with him. (nium.com/newsroom/nium-acquires-cypher; prnewswire 302821241; technode.global 2026-07-09.)

**[1] Who / what is Cypher.** Founded ~4 years ago, backed by **Y Combinator** and **Coinbase Ventures**; built non-custodial wallet + stablecoin-backed card issuance connecting on-chain assets to traditional banking. Kube previously held payments/risk engineering leadership roles at **Coinbase, Amazon and Zenefits**. Nium gains "deep operational knowledge of how to build and scale products for crypto-native users." (dealstreetasia 488465; cypherhq.io blog.)

**[2] Why it matters / strategic fit.** Nium already has a heavy stablecoin/on-chain buildout: [[Nium debuts stablecoin card issuance platform with Visa]] and [[Nium launches stablecoin card issuance across 190+ countries]] (Mar 2026, dual-network Visa+Mastercard), [[Nium partners with Coinbase for USDC stablecoin payments]] (Apr 2026), [[Nium and Circle connect USDC settlement with global payouts]] (May 2026, joined Circle Payments Network), and [[Nium joins Visa's stablecoin settlement pilot]] (Nov 2025). Cypher adds crypto-native wallet/on-chain expertise and talent to that fiat-crypto bridge. Nium is IPO-pathed (US listing targeted end-2026, delayed from 2025).

**[3] Market frame.** Consolidation wave in stablecoin/crypto-payments infra: Stripe→Bridge ($1.1B, 2024/25); Mastercard→BVNK (up to $1.8B, Mar 2026, largest to date); Coinbase↔BVNK $2B talks collapsed (Nov 2025). Stablecoin volumes hit ~$33T in 2025 (+72% YoY); B2B stablecoin payments ~$226B (+733% YoY). Nium's Cypher deal is a small tuck-in/acqui-hire by comparison — talent+tech, not a headline-price platform buy.

**[4] Caveats.** Undisclosed (likely small) terms; Cypher's own product is being killed, so value is people + IP, not an operating book of business. Nium remains unprofitable per its FY2024 filing ([[Nium, Singapore-based fintech, narrowed annual loss to S$88M ($69M) with 13.3% revenue]]: rev S$167.2m/$124m +13.3%, net loss S$88.1m). Integration/retention risk on a small acquired team.

**Deal in context (M&A comps).** Nium/Cypher is a small, undisclosed **acqui-hire** at the tail of a consolidation wave dominated by far larger deals:
- Stripe → **Bridge**, ~$1.1B (2024/25).
- Mastercard → **BVNK**, up to **$1.8B** (Mar 2026) — largest stablecoin acquisition to date.
- Coinbase ↔ BVNK, ~$2B talks **collapsed** (Nov 2025).
- Stripe/Visa/Mastercard reportedly building a joint stablecoin platform (Jun 2026), with Coinbase weighing participation.
Read: platform networks are locking up stablecoin infra via multi-billion buys; Nium's move is a **talent+IP tuck-in**, not a scale play.

**In-base comps (internal).** Nium's own stablecoin build-out is well-documented: [[Nium debuts stablecoin card issuance platform with Visa]], [[Nium launches stablecoin card issuance across 190+ countries]] (dual-network, Mar 2026); [[Nium partners with Coinbase for USDC stablecoin payments]] (Apr 2026); [[Nium and Circle connect USDC settlement with global payouts]] (May 2026, joined Circle Payments Network); [[Nium joins Visa's stablecoin settlement pilot]] (Nov 2025).
