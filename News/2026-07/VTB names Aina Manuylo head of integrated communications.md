## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
### [0] What happened
VTB (Russia's second-largest, state-controlled bank) appointed **Aina Manuylo** as head of its integrated communications department, effective around 2026-07-09/10. She moves from **SberMarketing** (the in-house agency of rival Sber), where she was Director of Client Service. At VTB she will own media planning and buying, develop attribution and media-effectiveness measurement, and run media partnerships — i.e. she gains influence over one of the largest advertising budgets in Russia. (Sources: [AdIndex 2026-07-09](https://adindex.ru/news/hr/2026/07/9/347054.phtml), [AdIndex 2026-07-10](https://adindex.ru/news/hr/2026/07/10/347104.phtml))

### [1] Who is Aina Manuylo / why it matters
Manuylo's career includes Alkasar Group (2012–2014), Okkam and Rore Group, then **SberMarketing**, where from 2024 she was Director of Client Service (previously Director of External Client Relations). She brings agency-side, media-buying expertise from Sber's marketing arm into VTB — a notable talent move between the two dominant state banks. The role is a build-out of in-house media-planning and business-outcome (attribution) capability rather than a C-level or business-line change.

### [2] Scale of the budget she'll touch
Per AdIndex, **VTB is the 7th-largest advertiser in Russia**. Its spend across the five key media rose >25% in 2025 to **~14bn RUB**, of which **9bn RUB went to TV** (2nd place after Sber) and **4.3bn RUB to internet** (7th). Aggregate spend of Russia's top-30 advertisers reached 338.8bn RUB in 2025, with Sber, VTB, Yandex and Ozon leading. (Sources: [Sostav rating 2025](https://www.sostav.ru/publication/rejting-krupnejshikh-reklamodatelej-rossii-2025-74192.html), [AdIndex market 2025](https://adindex.ru/news/ad_budjet/2026/03/26/343600.phtml))

### [3] Strategic backdrop — why a comms reshuffle now
- VTB is executing a medium-term strategy targeting **19–20% ROE** and a **retail push**: growing the mass-market base toward **~35mn active clients in 2026** via compact offices, regional penetration, loyalty investment, a "family service" product and federal courier delivery. Heavier marketing/attribution capability directly serves this customer-acquisition goal. ([bne IntelliNews](https://www.intellinews.com/strategy-of-russian-vtb-bank-to-focus-on-retail-315513/))
- Financial backdrop: **9M 2025 IFRS net profit ~380.8bn RUB (+1.5% y/y)**; Q3 2025 ~100bn RUB. Lending income was under pressure (regulatory curbs on consumer loans hit VTB harder than Sber), pushing capital toward corporate/investment banking. ([AKM](https://www.akm.ru/eng/news/vtb-group-s-net-profit-under-ifrs-increased-by-1-5-in-9-months-/), [VTB IR](https://www.vtb.com/ir/financial-results/ifrs-financial-results/))
- Ownership: in Sep 2025 VTB ran an **SPO of ~1.264bn shares (~24% of the class, ~$1.02bn)**, cutting the state stake toward 50%+1 and enlarging free float — a partial-privatization dynamic that raises the stakes on brand/investor perception.

**Sector & drivers.** Russian retail banking, viewed through the marketing/brand-building lens of a state incumbent. VTB is Russia's #2 bank after Sberbank and, per AdIndex (via the note text), the country's #7 largest advertiser; its 2025 spend across five key media rose >25% to ~RUB 14bn (RUB 9bn TV, RUB 4.3bn internet) — the note's own figures, unverified against AdIndex primary. "Why now": VTB is in an aggressive retail land-grab — the group is running an SPO of up to ~49% of ordinary shares at RUB 87 to fund a Wildberries-Russ (RWB) retail partnership, while the state keeps >50% (interfax.com, marketscreener.com, 2025). A comms/media-planning hire fits a bank trying to convert a state-heavy brand into a retail-consumer one to feed marketplace/retail products. This is a **soft, low-materiality personnel event** — analysis kept proportionate.

**IR backdrop (primary).** VTB Group FY2025 IFRS (12M 2025), reported 2026-02-25 (results) / discussed on the FY2025 analyst call 2026-03-03 (transcript, per pipeline/ir/ir_latest.json): net profit **RUB 502.1bn (~$6.5bn), −8.9% YoY** off the 2024 record of RUB 551.4bn; **Q4 2025 net profit RUB 121.3bn, −31.2% YoY** (tass.com). Interim: 9M 2025 net profit RUB 380.8bn, +1.5% YoY (tass.com); earlier-2025 monthly disclosures put ROE at ~17–18% and a compressed **net interest margin of ~0.6–0.8%** vs ~2.2–2.4% a year prior (vtb.com.mx). Thin capital buffers: at 1-Jan-2025 total-capital N20.0 was 9.1% vs 8.75% minimum incl. buffers, CET1 (N20.1) 6.2% vs 5.25% (vtb.com.mx). Read-through: profit is holding near record levels but **falling**, margins are squeezed by high rates, and capital headroom is slim — which is exactly why the SPO and the retail/marketplace deposit push matter, and why brand/media efficiency (Manuylo's remit — attribution, media ROI) is a rational cost-discipline move rather than a strategy shift.

Key players: **Sberbank** (dominant, own ad + ecosystem; Manuylo joins from SberMarketing — VTB is importing Sber-school agency expertise), **T-Bank** (digital-native brand), **Alfa-Bank**, and marketplace-banks (Ozon Bank, WB Bank). - **Cost-to-income (CIR) 47.3%** for the year — efficiency under pressure; relevant given the item is about scaling the media/marketing spend (opex line).

**2026 guidance.** VTB targets a **new record net profit of RUB600–650bn** with **ROE ≥20%**, and a more moderate payout of 25–35% of profit. Implies management expects the NIM recovery to carry into 2026.

**Read for this appointment.** Strategically, VTB is defending profitability into 2026 off a compressed 2025; a bigger, better-attributed media budget (the Manuylo mandate) is an opex/customer-acquisition lever against a thin-capital, margin-recovering backdrop — no direct P&L read-through, but it sits on the CIR line the bank needs to hold near ~47%.
