## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)

At SPIEF-2026 (5 June 2026), Sberbank CEO German Gref told journalists Sber **plans** to conduct "the full spectrum of crypto operations that will be permitted by the new law," including exchanging fiat for crypto "in the usual, convenient client channels." This is an **intent statement, future-tensed and law-contingent — not a launch.** The trigger is Duma bill №1194918-8, which passed **only its first reading (21 April 2026)** and originally set legal retail crypto purchases (via CBR-registered exchangers, brokers, trustees) from **1 July 2026**.

**Why framed this way / what it reveals:** Gref is pre-positioning Sber as the default rail the moment retail crypto is legal — the value is *distribution* (existing app, 100M+ clients), not a new product. The scope is deliberately narrow by law: qualified investors buy freely (except anonymity coins); **non-qualified investors capped at ₽300k/yr per intermediary**; domestic crypto payments **remain banned**. So "full crypto operations" ≠ retail-wide free crypto — it's gated custody/exchange for a qualified/capped audience.

**Freshness caveat (verify before publish):** external research (Financial Congress, reported 2026-07-02) indicates the law's effective date **slipped from 1 July to 1 Sept 2026** (digital-ruble mass rollout also Sept 2026). The note's own source (Interfax, 5 June) still says 1 July. If confirmed, Sber cannot legally start before Sept 2026 — reinforcing "announced, not live."

## [1] Competitors / peers

Russian banks are racing to claim the same not-yet-open lane:
- **VTB** (3 June 2026, board member Sergeychuk): preparing to obtain the necessary CBR licenses to offer "the full range" of crypto services to retail + corporate once the law is in force — same posture as Sber (this is the item's second half). VTB earlier (Dec 2025) said it aimed to be **first with spot BTC/ETH** trading. See [[Russia to legalize crypto for cross-border trade payments]].
- **T-Bank (Tinkoff):** cited in the aggregated text as having the same ambitions; live BTC-linked ЦФА since 29 May 2025 (qualified investors, real BTC reserved via Atomyze).
- **Alfa-Bank:** BTC/ETH ЦФА live 29 May 2025 (qualified only).
- **Moscow Exchange:** BTC futures live 4 June 2025; multi-coin indices/futures added ~May 2026; spot targeted end-2026.

**Why the lay of the land:** everyone is announcing readiness for the *same* law → the differentiator is licensing speed + distribution, not product. Sber's edge is its client base; VTB's play is "first to spot." Nobody has a durable moat yet because the regulated market does not legally exist. (analysis)

## [2] Company history / fit

Sber is not starting from scratch — it has the deepest Russian digital-asset stack: CBR-licensed **DFA/ЦФА platform** (first DFA July 2022; 2025 issuance ₽408bn / ~$5.3B, ~5.6× vs 2024 — see [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]]); BTC-linked structured bonds + BTC futures via SberInvestments on MOEX (June 2025); a pilot **bitcoin-backed loan** and plans to expand crypto-backed lending to corporates (Feb 2026 — [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]], [[Cantor Fitzgerald and Sberbank join crypto-backed lending push]]); member of the digital-ruble pilot.

**Why Sber acts this way:** it already monetizes tokenized rights (ЦФА) and BTC exposure for the wealthy; retail fiat↔crypto exchange is the missing consumer-facing piece that keeps flows inside SberBank rather than leaking to offshore exchanges. Structural driver: onshore capture of crypto demand a sanctioned bank cannot serve via Western rails.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction

**Genuinely new vs prior notes:** yes — this is Gref's *first* public commitment to retail fiat↔crypto exchange under the pending law (prior Sber notes cover crypto-backed **lending** and DFA, not retail exchange). But **traction = zero**: no operational retail crypto service, contingent on a law at first reading only. Value-add is real *if* Sber wins the license race and its distribution converts — but margin capture is unproven: intermediary economics on capped (₽300k/yr) retail flow are thin, and the qualified-investor segment is small.

**Second-order:** because domestic crypto payments stay banned and Western stablecoins/exchanges are off-limits to a fully-sanctioned bank (OFAC SDN since Apr 2022), any Sber crypto service is walled into onshore ruble settlement — investment/custody, not payments. The "who captures the margin" question is muted until volumes exist. (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment

Watch: (1) 2nd/3rd Duma readings + signing on the (slipped?) **Sept 2026** schedule; (2) CBR licensing of Sber/VTB; (3) whether "qualified/super-qualified" thresholds get lowered (MinFin pushing softer, CBR restrictive — regulator split persists). Risks: repeated Russian digital-asset timeline slippage (digital ruble delayed to Sept 2026; crypto law July→Sept 2026); EU 20th package (crypto measures effective 24 May 2026) sectorally bans EU persons transacting with any Russia-established crypto exchange, capping any Sber venue's reach by construction.

**Why the market goes this way:** a sanctioned economy needs onshore crypto rails; the state is legalizing investment while banning payments to keep monetary control. Counterintuitive second-order: the more banks pre-announce, the more the *law's timing* — not any bank's product — is the real variable.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russia is building a bank-intermediated crypto market from a de-PR standpoint — announced framework, not yet live. The Bank of Russia's proposed regime must be legislated by ~Jul 1 2026, takes effect Sep 1 2026, with a transition to Jul 1 2027 (per CoinDesk/Cryptonews, Dec 2025). Structure is deliberately incumbent-gated: banks/brokers may run crypto exchanges via a light "notification" path on existing licenses rather than a new license, but are capped at ~1% of capital in crypto-related activity, and retail buys are cashless-only (no crypto-to-cash) with non-qualified investors limited to RUB 300k (~$3.3k) per intermediary/year after a risk test (per CoinDesk, Dec 2025). This is a state-orchestrated oligopoly by design — the barrier is regulatory access, not technology, which structurally favors Sber/VTB/T-Bank over crypto-native players. Two adjacent, distinct rails: (a) the ЦФА/DFA market (tokenized on-chain claims, legal since 2021) — the Bank of Russia estimates it grew ~a third to a ~$13bn market in 2025 (per DL News); cumulative placements since inception reached ~RUB 1.35tn by Sep 2025 (per tadviser/secondary); and (b) the cross-border crypto-payments EPR/ELR for sanctioned foreign trade, live since Sep 2024 for selected exporters/importers (per Bank of Russia via Cointelegraph/Cryptoslate). "Why now": the trade-settlement need under sanctions is the political driver pulling the ЦБ РФ from prohibitionist toward a tightly-fenced, bank-only opening.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs here: capital allocated to crypto (hard 1% cap), retail client base as a distribution moat, ЦФА issuance volume, and cross-border settlement throughput. Basis of competition is distribution + regulatory access, not price. Recent dated moves: Gref at SPIEF (ПМЭФ) said Sber plans the full range of crypto operations the new law permits, including fiat→crypto exchange in existing client channels (agg source, 2026-07-02); Sber issued a pilot bitcoin-collateralized loan to Intelion Data (Dec 2025) and is scaling crypto-backed lending to businesses (per CoinDesk 2026-02-06 [[Russias-Sberbank-plans-crypto-backed-loans-for-corporate-clients]]); Sber expanded ЦФА issuance to ~RUB 408bn in 2025 and offers BTC/ETH-linked structured bonds (per bitcoinmagazine/crypto-economy). T-Bank has parallel ambitions; VTB (Dec 2025) said it plans to let clients buy crypto instruments and act as intermediary, and is preparing to obtain ЦБ licenses (agg source). Protagonist position: **ahead** — Sber's ~111mn retail clients (per Sber Q2-2025 IR) plus the largest ЦФА franchise make it the default beneficiary of a distribution-gated regime. Moat = scale + distribution + regulatory relationship (analysis); it does not remove sanctions/custody risk.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation/round/deal metrics attach to this announcement — Sber is a listed incumbent adding a product line, so bank trading multiples (P/E, P/B, P/S) are not the right frame for a not-yet-live crypto unit; revenue contribution is "no data." Internal corpus comps (bank/crypto-service entry) as reference points: [[Russias-Sberbank-plans-crypto-backed-loans-for-corporate-clients]] (2026-02-09, same protagonist, upstream event); [[Citi-plans-to-offer-crypto-custody-services-in-2026]] (2025-10-20); [[Deutsche-Bank-to-launch-crypto-custody-service-in-2026]] (2025-07-02); [[Ripple-and-Absa-Bank-launch-digital-asset-custody-in-South-Africa]] (2025-10-17). IR anchor (see IR section): Sber FY2025 net profit RUB 1,705.9bn (+7.9% y/y), ROE 22.7% — i.e. the crypto push is bolted onto a highly profitable core, and the 1% capital cap means near-term P&L impact is immaterial. Multiple distribution not computed (comps are qualitative product-launch precedents, not valuation comps).

**Risk flags.** (1) **Regulatory dependence / timing** — the entire opportunity is contingent on a bill not yet finalized (Duma first reading only, Apr 2026); effective date Sep 2026 could slip and the 1% cap throttles scale, so "full range" is aspirational, not committed. (2) **Sanctions & custody rails** — even where legal domestically, cross-border usefulness depends on foreign exchanges, custodians, stablecoin issuers and liquidity providers outside Russian control and subject to Western sanctions/wallet-freeze screening (per Cryptoslate); Sber cannot underwrite counterparty access. (3) **Ring-fenced economics** — cashless-only, retail caps, and no crypto-to-cash mean the domestic retail wedge is narrow; monetization skews to custody/spread/ЦФА fees, and the announcement is silent on take rate, fraud liability and AML burden.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Signals a regulator-blessed, incumbent-captured crypto market forming in Russia — a landgrab where distribution (Sber, VTB, T-Bank) beats crypto-native product (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if the law lands by Sep 2026 on the drafted terms, Sber captures the majority of licensed retail crypto flow within 12 months on client-base gravity. Trigger to watch: final law text + ЦБ РФ licensing of the first bank exchanges; thesis breaks if the 1% cap or a crypto-to-cash-style clampdown makes the retail unit sub-scale, or if sanctions cut off custody/liquidity rails.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**No full earnings report in this news** — the item is a crypto-strategy signal (Gref at SPIEF-2026, 5 Jun: Sber will run the full range of crypto operations allowed by the new law, incl. fiat↔crypto exchange in familiar client channels). Below: Sber's OWN latest reported results as the financial backdrop, to size the base from which a full crypto build-out can be funded. Prefer filing over web.

**Backdrop — Sber's scale/profitability (latest filing: FY2025 IFRS, released 2026-02-26).**
- **Net profit** RUB **1,707.4bn** (FY2025) vs RUB **1,581.6bn** (FY2024), **+8.0% YoY** — profit attributable to shareholders of the Bank, straight from the Summary Consolidated IFRS Financial Statements 12M 2025. (Press coverage rounds this to +7.9% / RUB 1.7trn / ~$22bn.)
- **ROE 22.7%** (FY2025, per Sber's own results slides / press), i.e. profitability held above 22% through a Russian macro slowdown.
- **Total equity attributable to shareholders ~RUB 8,351bn** (retained earnings RUB 8,107.9bn), on **total assets RUB 60,468.0bn** (from the FY2025 balance sheet).
- **Net interest income** RUB **3,556bn** FY2025 (**+18.5% YoY**); **net fee & commission income** RUB **833.7bn** (**−1.1% YoY**). Trend is consistent with the 9M/Q3 2025 print: 9M NII RUB **2,567.8bn (+18.0% YoY)**, 9M fee income RUB **614.7bn (+0.5% YoY)**, 9M operating income before provisions RUB **3,078.7bn (+17.4% YoY)** — a NII-led year with flat-to-soft fee income.
- Distribution/franchise reach: **>111.2mn** retail clients (Q2 2025), **21.2mn** SberPrime subscribers — the "familiar client channels" Gref points to for crypto exchange.
- Capital return: dividend recommended at **50% of IFRS net profit** (~RUB 850bn) — the buffer above that is what can self-fund a crypto build-out.

**Why it matters for the crypto plan (analysis).** Fact: FY2025 net profit RUB 1,707.4bn at 22.7% ROE. Why: earnings are NII-driven (+18.5%) while fee income is flat (−1.1%). Why it matters: licensed crypto brokerage/exchange under bill No. 1194918-8 (in force 1 Jul 2026) is a *fee/commission* line — precisely the segment currently stalling — so Sber can fund the build-out from a RUB 1.7trn profit base without stressing capital or diverting NII. Second-order: with 111mn+ clients and existing SberBank Online rails, a full-stack crypto offer is a marginal add-on, not a bet — the constraint is regulation (CBR registry, licences) and retail caps (RUB 300k/yr per intermediary for non-qualified investors), not funding. De-PR: Gref gave *intent* ("we are preparing", "will do the full range"), not live product, licence, or any crypto P&L target — no numbers to score.
