## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**What happened (2026-07-09/13).** UK fintech **Lendable** priced its **first public asset-backed securities (ABS) deal**, raising **£500m (~$671m)** off a portfolio of **unsecured UK personal loans**. The vehicle is named **Hoxton Consumer Loan Funding 2026-1**. Books were finalised **9 July 2026**. Demand was strong: reportedly **~2x oversubscribed overall**, with lower-rated tranches **~8x** oversubscribed. **Moody's** modelled a **7.9% lifetime default rate** and **20% recovery** on the collateral. Proceeds fund lending growth ahead of a planned **US and Mexico** expansion.

**Why this is a signal, not just a funding round.** Lendable's core model is a **forward-flow / marketplace originator**: it does **not take deposits**, originates and services loans on behalf of **institutional investors** (private credit lines, forward-flow agreements), earning a platform/servicing fee rather than holding loans on balance sheet. Tapping the **public** ABS market for the first time is a graduation step — it diversifies funding away from bilateral private facilities toward **capital-markets term funding**, typically at scale and (if oversubscribed) at tighter spreads. It also imposes public rating-agency scrutiny (Moody's) and market-price discovery on the loan book's credit quality. This is the **second UK consumer ABS debut of 2026** (after Citi's Montgomery Square), signalling a reopening/deepening of the UK consumer-ABS channel.

**Company scale & trajectory.** Lendable claims to be the **largest UK personal-loan originator by volume in 2025** (ahead of large incumbent banks) and #2 in new credit cards; **>£10bn** cumulative personal loans issued since inception. **FY2024 revenue £446m (+90% YoY)**; **2025 profit ~£11m (+120%)**. Backed by **Goldman Sachs** and **Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan** (via Teachers' Innovation Platform), which led a **£210m round in 2022 at a >£3.5bn (~$4.6bn) valuation**. Note: the target-prompt figure of "$4.5B / 2021 Ontario Teachers'" is close but imprecise — the OTPP round was **March 2022 at £3.5bn**; the £1bn valuation was March 2021.

**Market backdrop.** UK unsecured consumer credit balances hit ~£260bn in Q1 2026 (+5.7% YoY); unsecured gross lending forecast to grow ~8.1% CAGR through 2026-27. Rising competition and a broadly resilient (but rate-/credit-sensitive) consumer environment underpin origination growth.

**Related prior notes (context, not duplicates).** Prior coverage frames Lendable as a **creditor to emerging-market fintechs** — [[Mexico's Mattilda secures $50M credit line from Lendable]] (2025-09) and [[CrediSmart secures up to $48M from Lendable for Colombia expansion]] (2026-04) — and as a **consumer-product operator** via [[Lendable launches first UK fintech mobile plan]] (2025-10, Zable Mobile). This ABS deal is a **distinct, funding-side event** about Lendable's own UK loan book, not those credit-line stories.

**Competitors / peer set.** UK consumer-lending & securitization peers: **Zopa** (closed the UK's first marketplace consumer-loan securitization; now a bank), **Oakbrook**, **Fund Ourselves**, plus bank-side incumbents. Lendable's public-ABS debut narrows the funding-sophistication gap with bank peers.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
**Red-team questions**

1. Is £500m the **final priced size** or the marketed target? Some outlets say "raising," implying pre-pricing — confirm settlement.
2. Was pricing actually **tight** (evidence of the "2x/8x oversubscription" beyond a single trade-press claim)? What were the AAA tranche spreads vs comparable UK consumer ABS?
3. **Retention/skin-in-the-game**: does Lendable retain the equity/junior tranche (risk-retention rules)? If not, who holds first-loss?
4. Moody's **7.9% lifetime default / 20% recovery** — is that benign or stressed relative to Lendable's realised loss history? A 20% recovery on unsecured loans is low; net loss assumption is high.
5. Does a public ABS debut **replace or merely supplement** private forward-flow funding? If institutional forward-flow demand were strong, why access public markets now?
6. Vintage/seasoning risk: are the securitized loans recent 2024–25 originations underwritten during rapid **90% revenue growth** — i.e., is there adverse selection or unseasoned credit risk?
7. **Valuation staleness**: the £3.5bn (2022) mark is 4 years old and pre the 2022–24 rate shock. Is that still a fair reference, or materially marked down?
8. Profit of only **£11m on £446m revenue** (~2.5% margin) — is Lendable genuinely profitable at scale, or is thin profit a funding-cost/credit-cost warning?
9. **US and Mexico expansion**: capital-intensive and credit-risky in unfamiliar markets — is the ABS raise really "for growth" or to shore up funding as private lines tighten?
10. Confusion risk: Lendable's own UK ABS vs its LatAm credit-line lending (Mattilda, CrediSmart) — are these the **same balance sheet** or separate funds? Governance/consolidation question.
11. Is "largest UK personal-loan originator by volume 2025" a **cherry-picked** metric (volume not balances; excludes mortgages/secured)? Verify source.
12. Concentration: single-issuer, single-country, single-asset-class (unsecured UK consumer) — how exposed to a **UK consumer-credit downturn**?
13. Naming — "Hoxton Consumer Loan Funding 2026-1" implies a **programme** (more deals to come). Is this a one-off or the start of regular issuance (repeat-funding dependence)?
14. Does the "£500m / $670m / $671m" figure conflate the ABS with any concurrent equity/debt? Some headlines say "$670m raise" — ensure it's one transaction.

**Importance: 4/5** — A credible, well-oversubscribed public-ABS debut by the (claimed) largest UK personal-loan originator is a genuine capital-markets milestone: it validates the forward-flow model at term-funding scale, reopens the UK consumer-ABS channel (2nd 2026 debut), and de-risks a US/Mexico expansion. It falls short of 5/5 because it is a funding/structuring event (not a strategic pivot, M&A, or regulatory shift), the headline size is modest, and thin margins plus a stale valuation temper the read-through.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** UK unsecured consumer credit balances reached ~£260bn in Q1 2026, +5.7% YoY, inside a ~£1.88tn total consumer credit stock, +3.7% YoY (per UK Finance / industry data, via Financial IT, as of Q1 2026). Structure: the personal-loan segment is contested between high-street banks and a growing cohort of data-driven specialist/fintech lenders; Lendable is described as the largest UK personal-loan originator by new-loan volume in 2025 and has issued >£10bn cumulatively since inception (per Bloomberg, via Finextra/PYMNTS, 2026-07-09). Why now: fintech originators lack deposit funding, so their growth is capped by wholesale funding access — public ABS/securitization is the maturation signal that opens deep, repeatable, cheaper capital vs. private warehouse/forward-flow lines. The deal (branded *Hoxton Consumer Loan Funding 2026-1*, backed by unsecured personal loans) was ~2x oversubscribed overall and ~8x on lower-rated tranches (per Bloomberg, 2026-07-09) — evidence of live investor appetite for UK consumer-ABS risk, not just an announcement.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: loan-book / origination volume, cost of funds, net interest margin, and loss/arrears rates. Key players & basis of competition: deposit-funded neobanks ([[Zopa Bank doubles profit before tax to £65m|Zopa]], Monzo) compete on cheap retail-deposit funding + full-bank licence; non-bank specialists (Lendable, Oakbrook) compete on underwriting/data and must fund via wholesale markets. Recent peer moves: Zopa posted £65m PBT in 2025 (2x YoY), ~2m users, loan book >£3bn, funded largely by ~£5.5bn deposits (per Zopa, 2026-03); [[ClearScore and Oakbrook partner on debt consolidation loans|Oakbrook]] scaled via a ClearScore distribution partnership (2025-06); Lendable itself signed the Post Office to power personal loans (2025-07, per IR). Protagonist position: scale leader in origination but structurally disadvantaged on funding vs. deposit-funded rivals — this ABS debut is Lendable catching up on the funding axis (analysis). Moat: proprietary underwriting/data + originator scale (intangibles + scale); no deposit moat, unlike Zopa/Monzo.

**Comps & multiples.** Lendable financials (per Bloomberg/GuruFocus, 2026-07): 2024 revenue £446m, +90% YoY; >£10bn cumulative originations. Last equity valuation ~£3.5bn at the 2022 Ontario Teachers-led £210m round (per OTPP/AltFi, 2022) — a round valuation, not a live mark, and now stale. P/S = £3.5bn / £0.446bn = **~7.8x** on 2024 revenue, but the numerator is 2022-dated and the denominator 2024, so treat as indicative only (`[UNSOURCED]` for a current mark). Internal comps (base): [[Zopa Bank doubles profit before tax to £65m]] (deposit-funded, profitable, >$1bn valuation Dec-2024); [[ClearScore and Oakbrook partner on debt consolidation loans]] (specialist forward-flow/distribution); [[Kroo Bank enters forward-flow funding partnership with Glenhawk]] and [[Octane closes $350 million forward-flow agreement with Nuveen]] (2026-05, comparable non-deposit wholesale-funding structures); [[Klarna sells $6.5B loan portfolio in US agreement]] (2025-11, loan-sale funding). Distribution not computed — comps differ on funding model/geo; qualitative comparison only. Flag: at ~7.8x trailing P/S the 2022 valuation looks rich for a balance-sheet lending model (vs. Zopa ~>1x book), but +90% revenue growth partly justifies it — the real question is whether the £3.5bn mark survives a 2026 re-rating of unprofitable-vs-profitable lenders.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Funding-model dependence / cost-of-funds cyclicality** — as a non-deposit lender, Lendable's growth is hostage to open ABS/warehouse markets and spread levels; a credit-market shutdown (as in 2022–23) would throttle origination in a way it does not for deposit-funded Zopa/Monzo. Second-order: NIM compresses exactly when consumer losses rise.
2. **Consumer-credit loss cycle** — the book is unsecured personal loans to UK consumers; a UK unemployment/affordability shock lifts arrears, hits ABS tranche performance and future deal economics. The 8x oversubscription on junior tranches also signals reach-for-yield that can reverse fast.
3. **Stale/rich valuation + execution risk on US/Mexico expansion** — the £3.5bn mark is 2022-vintage; simultaneously entering the US and Mexico (per Bloomberg) adds underwriting-in-new-geographies risk with no proven track record, raising the odds of a down-round if funding tightens.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** A successful, oversubscribed public ABS debut is a funding-market validation that lets the largest UK originator scale origination without dilutive equity — watch whether this becomes a repeat programmatic issuer (Hoxton 2026-2+) and whether peers (Oakbrook, other specialists) follow into public ABS, which would signal a broadening of UK consumer-ABS supply (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if a second public deal prices within ~12 months at tighter spreads, Lendable's funding moat is real; the thesis breaks if arrears on 2026-1 collateral run above pricing assumptions or the next deal stalls — either would re-expose the deposit-funding disadvantage vs. Zopa.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Grounding note.** Lendable is **private** (no listed equity, no quarterly reporting). Statutory numbers come from **Companies House** filings for *Lendable Operations Ltd* (group consolidated, co. 09011579) and *Lendable Ltd* (co. 08828186); the latest on file is the **FY ended 31 Dec 2024** accounts (filed 2025-10-04). ### Latest disclosed financials (full-year)
- **Revenue: ~£446m, up ~90% YoY** — Lendable's latest reported full year (accounts released/summarised around the **25 Jun 2025** full-year results). This is roughly a **doubling** of the top line in a single year.
- **Profitability: profit up ~120% to ~£11m** (net/after-tax profit). Lendable is a **rare profitable UK consumer-lending fintech**; margins are thin relative to revenue because the model is high-volume, fee/servicing-led.
- **Originations / market position:** per **Experian** data, Lendable issued **more new consumer-credit loans by volume than any other UK lender in 2025** — ahead of the largest incumbent banks — and ranked **#2 in new credit-card issuance**. Cumulative **>£10bn** in personal loans issued since inception.
- **Valuation (last primary round):** **>£3.5bn (~$4.6bn)** at the **March 2022** £210m round led by **Ontario Teachers' (Teachers' Innovation Platform)**, with **Goldman Sachs** also a backer. (The prompt's "$4.5B / 2021 Ontario Teachers'" is imprecise: the ~£1bn valuation was **March 2021**; the £3.5bn OTPP round was **March 2022**. No newer round has re-priced the equity.)

### Why the £500m ABS matters to the trajectory
- **Business model:** Lendable is a **marketplace / forward-flow originator** — it does **not take deposits**; it originates and services unsecured personal loans funded by institutional capital (private credit lines, forward-flow agreements), earning platform/servicing economics. Funding capacity is therefore the direct throttle on origination growth (and thus revenue).
- **The deal:** **£500m (~$671m)** debut **public** ABS — **Hoxton Consumer Loan Funding 2026-1** — backed by unsecured UK personal loans; books closed **9 Jul 2026**, reportedly **~2x oversubscribed** overall (junior tranches ~8x). **Moody's** modelled a **7.9% lifetime default** / **20% recovery** assumption on the pool.
- **Strategic read:** moving from **bilateral private facilities → public term ABS** is a funding **graduation** — larger, more diversified, potentially tighter-spread capital-markets funding, and it subjects the loan book to **public rating-agency + market credit scrutiny** for the first time. Proceeds fund origination growth ahead of planned **US and Mexico** expansion.

### Thesis flags
- **Bull:** profitable, capital-light originator; #1 UK by loan volume; new public-ABS channel de-risks the funding side and supports continued ~90% revenue growth; strong sponsors (OTPP, Goldman).
- **Bear / watch:** absolute profit (~£11m) is small vs. revenue (~£446m) → **thin margin, credit-cycle sensitive**; a **7.9% modelled default rate** signals meaningful loss content in an unsecured book if UK consumer stress rises; valuation last struck in **2022** (rate regime since repriced fintech comps sharply) — the £3.5bn mark is stale; expansion into **US/Mexico** adds execution and cross-border credit risk.
- **Disclosure caveat:** as a private company Lendable reports only annual statutory accounts with limited operational KPIs; exact loan-book size, NIM, and cohort loss rates are **not publicly broken out**.
