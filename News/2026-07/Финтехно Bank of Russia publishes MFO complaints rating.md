# Context-enrichment: Bank of Russia publishes first MFO complaints ranking (2025)
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 2026-07-13 the Bank of Russia (CBR) published, **for the first time**, a "ренкинг" (ranking) of microfinance organisations (МФО / MFOs) by the *relative* number of **justified** consumer complaints for full-year **2025**. De-PR'd specifics:
- **Metric:** justified complaints per **100,000 microloan contracts** — i.e. scale-adjusted, not raw counts. "Justified" = complaints where, after CBR verification, an actual violation of consumer rights was confirmed. Complaints filed to **both** the CBR **and** the Financial Ombudsman (финуполномоченный) are pooled. MFOs enter the table only if they had **≥2 confirmed complaints** in 2025.
- **Two cohorts** (to avoid mixing scale): **large MFOs** (≥1m microloan contracts) and **small MFOs** (<1m).
- **Large-cohort worst:** "Джой Мани" (Joy Money), "Срочноденьги", "ЭЙРЛОАНС"/AIRLOANS, "Лайм-Займ"; then MKK UF, Webbankir, Turbozaim, OTP Finance, MigCredit, Bystrodengi. **Best in large cohort:** "Корона", **"Займер"/Zaymer (20th, i.e. among the cleanest)**, "Русинтерфинанс", "Т-Финанс".
- **Small-cohort worst:** "Микро Резерв", "Диджитал Мани", "Мобикредит".
- **Key magnitude fact:** even the *worst* large MFO sits at ~2 justified complaints per 100k contracts — orders of magnitude below the small-MFO leaders. → The dispersion is almost entirely a *small-vs-large* story.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the CBR deliberately (a) normalises by contract count and (b) splits by size so a mega-lender's absolute complaint volume isn't penalised. The design converts a naming-and-shaming instrument into a *per-customer conduct* metric. The reveal: scale correlates with *cleaner* conduct (large MFOs = the branded, listed, compliance-heavy names), while the long tail of tiny lenders concentrates the abuse. This is a transparency/regtech lever, not an enforcement action — no fines, no licence moves attach to placement.

## [1] Competitors / peers (regulatory instrument peers, RU)
This is a **direct clone of the CBR bank-complaints ренкинг** applied to MFOs. Timeline of the parent instrument: CBR first published a **bank** complaints ranking in **April 2025** (on 2024 data); moved to a **semi-annual** cadence; in H1-2025 the worst large banks were MTS-Bank, Gazprombank, Sovcombank, VTB, T-Bank; methodology later updated to pool CBR + Financial-Ombudsman complaints — **exactly the methodology now inherited by the MFO table.** So the MFO ranking is the *third* CBR "sunlight" metric after (i) bank complaints rankings and (ii) the 2025 payment-service **satisfaction** metric (see corpus). Position of this item: **catching up / mechanical extension** — MFOs are simply the next asset class folded into an already-live disclosure regime, not a novel tool.

**Why the landscape is this way / second-order:** the CBR is building a *ladder of conduct-transparency instruments* (satisfaction survey → bank ranking → MFO ranking) as a cheaper substitute for case-by-case enforcement. Second-order: publishing per-100k conduct scores hands *comparison shoppers and aggregators* (e.g. Sravni-type marketplaces) a machine-readable quality signal → conduct becomes a distribution variable, pressuring the worst small lenders on customer-acquisition, not just on regulation.

## [2] Company / issuer history & fit
Issuer = the CBR's consumer-protection (защита прав потребителей) function, same unit behind the bank ranking and the 2025 payment-satisfaction metric. Fit is tight: the MFO ranking lands in the same quarter (Q3 2026) as a wave of MFO conduct rules — the 1 July 2026 new MFO standard (bans on imposed services / dark patterns), tightened PDN (debt-load) discounting, near-prohibitive macroprudential limits on high-PDN retail lending, and mandatory biometric verification of microloan borrowers (corpus). **Why the CBR acts this way:** structural pressure — MFO complaint inflow reportedly rose ~50% into 2025/2026 and the CBR is steering the sector through a deliberate **contraction** (its own forecast: MFO portfolio −40% in 2026 to ~₽313bn, −55% more in 2027 to ~₽235bn). Sunlight metrics are the low-cost complement to the hard caps: shape behaviour and consumer choice while the balance-sheet squeeze does the rest.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Genuinely new:** first-ever public per-MFO conduct scorecard in Russia. **But** the *instrument* is not new (bank version live since Apr-2025) and the *methodology* is copied — so novelty is "new asset class, old tool," ~medium.
**Value-add — real but bounded:**
- Real: exposes that abuse is concentrated in small MFOs; validates that the large listed names (Zaymer 20th = clean; T-Finance clean) run tighter conduct → a *reputational asset* for the consolidating incumbents.
- Bounded: it is disclosure only — **no fine, no licence consequence** ties to rank; a "justified complaint" depends on consumers actually escalating, so it under-counts silent harm and favours lenders whose customers don't complain (not necessarily those who behave best). Placement can be gamed by suppressing complaints as much as by improving conduct.
**Traction:** it is *published/live*, but there is no evidence yet that borrowers use it or that acquisition shifts — traction is "announced disclosure," not measured behaviour change.
**Who captures value:** large, brand-name MFOs (Zaymer, T-Finance) — a clean score is a moat in a sector the CBR is actively shrinking; the long tail of small MFOs is squeezed simultaneously by the ranking, the caps, and the portfolio contraction → **accelerant of consolidation.**

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Cadence:** the bank ranking went semi-annual; expect the MFO ranking to become **recurring** (likely semi-annual/annual) — a standing pressure gauge.
- **Regulatory trajectory (hard rails, dated):** from **1 Jan 2027** — max **one** microloan per borrower at a time, PSK (all-in cost) capped at **≤100%**, and a **3-day cooling-off period**; PDN moves to officially-confirmed income only from **1 Jul 2027**. As of 30 Jun 2025, 27.8% of MFO borrowers held ≥2 loans at PSK>100% (43.2% of portfolio) — precisely the segment the caps target.
- **Market:** 2025 quarterly issuance ~₽0.5tn (≈₽2tn/yr); Q2-2025 issuance +53% YoY to ₽533bn, portfolio ~₽739bn (SRO "MiR": FY25 portfolio ₽520.4bn, issuance +37.7%). Leaders by issuance H1-2025: T-Finance (₽47.67bn), A Dengi (₽31.74bn), Zaymer group (₽30–55bn). **Sentiment:** managed shrinkage — CBR forecasts portfolio −40% (2026) then −55% (2027); Zaymer's profit reportedly −73.2% — the sector is pivoting off "fast payday" models toward installment/IL and diversification.
- **Why the market goes this way / counterintuitive second-order:** the CBR's mix of caps + transparency deliberately thins the sector. **Counterintuitive effect:** a "good" (low) complaint rank does *not* shield a lender from the portfolio contraction — the caps hit the profitable high-PSK book regardless of conduct. So a clean ranking is a *marketing* win, not an *earnings* shield; the real driver of who survives is who can re-underwrite at PSK≤100% with one-loan-per-borrower, i.e. the balance-sheet/tech-heavy incumbents. The central question is less "who behaves well" than "who can still make money once the caps bite."

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
### Red-team / challenge questions

1. **Is it actually new, or a re-run?** New *asset class* (MFOs), but the *instrument* and *methodology* are copied from the CBR bank complaints ранкинг live since Apr-2025. → New event (first MFO table, 2025 data), not a reprint. **Fresh.**
2. **Does any prior corpus note cover THIS MFO ranking?** No — closest ([[Финтехно Q3 brings debt-load and installment-market rule changes]], [[Russian central bank measures payment-service satisfaction metric]]) cover adjacent instruments, not this table. Not a duplicate.
3. **What does "justified complaint per 100k contracts" actually measure — conduct or complaint propensity?** Open/critical: it measures *escalated-and-upheld* complaints; silent harm and low-escalation customer bases flatter a lender. A low rank ≠ clean conduct.
4. **Can placement be gamed?** Yes — suppressing/settling complaints before they reach CBR/ombudsman lowers rank as effectively as improving conduct. No independent audit disclosed.
5. **Why does Zaymer (largest/most-visible) rank 20th = clean while tiny MFOs are worst?** Consistent with scale = compliance investment + brand risk; but also large lenders' customers may escalate less if disputes are resolved in-house. Both effects plausible.
6. **Does the ranking carry any enforcement teeth?** No — pure disclosure. No fine/licence action attaches to rank. Weight comes only from reputation + aggregator/marketplace surfacing.
7. **Is the ~2-per-100k ceiling for large MFOs a sign the sector is clean, or that the metric is too coarse?** Coarseness risk: with justified-only + ≥2-complaint threshold, the large cohort compresses near zero, limiting discriminatory power at the top.
8. **How does this interact with the 2027 caps (1 loan/borrower, PSK≤100%, 3-day cooling-off)?** Decisive: caps hit *earnings* regardless of complaint rank → a clean rank is a marketing, not survival, asset. Central question shifts to re-underwriting economics.
9. **Is the +50% complaint-inflow figure about MFOs specifically and same period?** Reported (knews, May-2026) as MFO complaints +~50%; needs cross-check vs CBR primary; treat as directional.
10. **Portfolio-contraction forecast (−40% 2026, −55% 2027) — CBR's own or third-party?** Attributed to CBR forecasting; if accurate, the ranking is a sunlight tool over a *shrinking* sector — its market impact is muted by the contraction itself.
11. **Do borrowers actually consult such rankings?** Open — no adoption data. Payday/MFO borrowers are often distressed/rate-insensitive; behaviour-change traction unproven.
12. **Who benefits most?** Consolidating branded incumbents (Zaymer, T-Finance) — clean score = moat while regulator thins the tail. Long-tail small MFOs squeezed by ranking + caps + contraction simultaneously.
13. **Is the two-cohort split (1m contracts) arbitrary?** Threshold choice affects who looks worst; a small MFO with 2 justified complaints on a tiny base spikes dramatically — the small-cohort table may over-punish low-volume lenders.
14. **Semi-annual or annual cadence for MFOs?** Bank version went semi-annual; MFO cadence not yet confirmed — open.
15. **Second-order fragility:** does forced consolidation into a few clean incumbents raise systemic conduct-concentration risk? (analysis) — fewer, larger MFOs = fewer complaints but higher single-name exposure for borrowers.

**Importance: 3/5** — A genuinely first-of-kind per-MFO conduct scorecard and a clean read of the RU microlending regulatory arc, with real consolidation signal. Capped at 3 because it is disclosure-only (no enforcement teeth), a mechanical extension of an existing bank-ranking instrument, and its market impact is dominated by the far harder 2027 rate/PDN caps rather than by the ranking itself. Fresh, non-duplicate, region-specific.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian microfinance is a small, tightly-regulated adjunct to bank lending, now mid-consolidation. MFO microloan issuance was ~₽506bn in Q3 2025, down 5% q/q — the second quarterly decline of 2025 as the market cools (per Bank of Russia, cbr.ru, as of 2025 Q3). The registry is shrinking: ~902 active MFOs at Jan 2025 → ~856 by end-Oct 2025 (per Bank of Russia registry, via investment commentary, as of Oct 2025); the top-20 by loan portfolio hit a multi-year-high 65% share in Q2 2025 (per CBR, via smart-lab, as of Q2 2025) — two-thirds of the market in 20 hands, ~800+ small players splitting the rest. **Why now:** a sustained regulatory tightening cycle is squeezing thin-margin players out. Overpayment cap cut from 130% → 100% (from Jul 2025), a further cut effective 1 Apr 2026 (per CBR press releases); a "one-loan rule" barring new microloans before the old is repaid plus a cooling-off period land in 2026; and a new MFO conduct standard from 1 Jul 2026 targets imposed add-on services and dark patterns (see [[Финтехно Q3 brings debt-load and installment-market rule changes]]). Growth is shifting to bank/marketplace-affiliated MFOs (marketplace-seller business lending +29% yoy in H1 2025) — an estimated ~50–60% of 2026 issuance expected from such players `(analysis, per market commentary)`.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: net loan portfolio, interest income, cost of risk / provisions, ROE, and — the news's own metric — justified-complaints per 100k contracts (a conduct/reputation KPI). H1 2025 issuance leaders: **Т-Финанс** ₽47.67bn (T-Bank-affiliated), **А деньги** ₽31.74bn, **ГК Займер** ₽30.06bn — all tech-scale players with scoring infra and bank-linked funding (per market commentary, H1 2025). The CBR complaints ranking sorts by conduct, not size: worst among large (1m+ contracts) MFOs — Джой Мани, Срочноденьги, ЭЙРЛОАНС, Лайм-Займ; best — Корона, Займер, Русинтерфинанс, **Т-Финанс**. Notably the two scale/bank-affiliated leaders (Т-Финанс, Займер) sit in the LOW-complaint group — scale + bank-grade compliance correlates with cleaner conduct `(analysis)`. Basis of competition is shifting from speed-of-cash to compliance, scoring quality and distribution (affiliated bank/marketplace funnels). Recent move: Sravni launched a SaaS to embed mandatory unified-biometric ID checks into MFO onboarding, live Aug 2026 (see [[Sravni helps microlenders embed mandatory biometric verification]]) — a compliance-as-a-service layer that small MFOs will lean on.

**Comps & multiples.** Only Займер (MOEX: ZAYM) is public and comparable. FY2025 (IFRS): net profit ₽4.35bn (+11% yoy), interest income ₽20.5bn (+10%), fee income ₽2.4bn (+4.5x), ROE ~32%; provisions rose to ₽8.68bn, compressing net interest margin-after-provisions to ₽11.74bn (from ₽13.02bn) — margin pressure from the tighter caps is already visible (per Cbonds/Market Power, IFRS FY2025). P/E, EV/Revenue: no data (market cap not verified in this pass) → `[UNSOURCED]`. Distribution not computed — only one public comp; qualitative comparison only. Internal comps: [[Финтехно Q3 brings debt-load and installment-market rule changes]] (the regulatory package driving the cycle), [[Sravni helps microlenders embed mandatory biometric verification]] (compliance rails). Peer set otherwise private/affiliated (Т-Финанс under T-Bank, А деньги) — no standalone multiples.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Regulation-driven consolidation / margin squeeze.** Successive overpayment-cap cuts (130→100%, then lower Apr 2026), the one-loan rule and cooling-off periods shrink per-loan economics and volumes; even leader Займер's after-provision margin already fell yoy. Second-order: sub-scale MFOs exit, accelerating the ~902→856 decline and pushing share to bank/marketplace-affiliated players.
2. **Reputational / transparency pressure from public complaint disclosure.** The CBR ranking makes conduct a public, comparable metric — small MFOs with complaint rates "orders of magnitude" above the ~2/100k-contract top of the large group face funding/partnership and licensing scrutiny. Second-order: conduct becomes a competitive moat for scale players, a survival threshold for the tail.
3. **Demand / credit-quality deterioration.** Complaints to CBR rose ~47% yoy in Q1 2026 (~14k), concentrated on add-on-service refunds and document disclosure — signalling both borrower stress and aggressive selling; combined with rising provisions, this raises asset-quality risk as the macroprudential-limit filter pushes the riskiest borrowers out of formal lending (disintermediation into illegal lenders).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Public conduct-ranking + caps + one-loan rule = a structural consolidation catalyst, not a one-off `(analysis)`. Thesis: the market bifurcates into a handful of compliant, bank/marketplace-affiliated scale MFOs (Т-Финанс, Займер, А деньги) capturing rising share, and a shrinking, reputationally-exposed tail. Watch: further registry attrition below ~856, top-20 share above 65%, and whether Займер's ROE holds ~30% as the Apr-2026 cap cut bites — a break below would falsify the "scale-players-are-insulated" thesis.
