Trump memecoin investors lost $3.8 billion, analysis finds
Nearly 1 million people have lost a total of $3.8 billion after buying President Donald Trump’s $TRUMP memecoin, according to cryptocurrency analytics firm Nansen.
 The New York Times reports that Nansen’s analysis is based on transactions that are publicly visible on the blockchain, showing that 988,905 accounts had lost money on the memecoin as of the end of June. That represents around two out of three $TRUMP buyers.
On Sunday, $TRUMP was trading at $1.69 , down nearly 98% from its high of $75.35.
Trump announced the memecoin three days before his inauguration in 2025. He’d previously co-founded a crypto startup, World Liberty Financial, with his sons. The $WLFI coin has also declined significantly in value.
In a recent financial disclosure, the president revealed that he made $636 million from the $TRUMP memecoin , accounting for nearly half of the $1.4 billion that the president made from the crypto industry last year.
Under the Trump administration, the Securities and Exchange Commission has said it will not regulate memecoins as securities and has dropped a number of lawsuits against crypto companies . A White House spokesperson told the NYT, “President Trump proudly made the United States the crypto capital of the world.”

# Context-enrichment: TechCrunch — Trump memecoin investors lost $3.8 billion
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On-chain analytics firm **Nansen** (reported by the **NYT**, relayed by TechCrunch 2026-07-05) tallied wallet-level P&L on the **$TRUMP** memecoin: **988,905 accounts** — "about two of every three buyers" — were underwater as of end-June 2026, for **~$3.81 billion** in aggregate losses. Method is straightforward blockchain forensics (public transaction ledger, realized + unrealized). $TRUMP traded ~**$1.69** on the Sunday cited, **down ~98%** from a **~$75** peak set right after the Jan-2025 launch.

The de-PR'd point is the **mirror-image distribution**, not the loss headline: the same dataset shows **~492,285 wallets in profit holding ~$4.04bn of gains** (per CoinDesk) — overwhelmingly early sub-$1 buyers and insiders. This is not "a coin that fell"; it is a **wealth transfer from ~1M late retail buyers to a small early cohort**, with the issuer capturing fee/licensing rents on top. Separately, Trump's **927-page OGE financial disclosure (2026-06-30)** shows **$636M** from $TRUMP (via CIC Digital LLC, mostly a ~$635M licensing agreement) — **~half of ~$1.4bn** in 2025 crypto income (the rest largely World Liberty: ~$526M token sales + ~$65M equity). Why it's structured this way: Trump-affiliated **CIC Digital LLC / Fight Fight Fight LLC control ~80% of supply** and earn on issuance/fees, so the issuer's economics are **decoupled from — and adverse to — buyer P&L**. That is the whole story.

## [1] Competitors / peers
This is the archetype of the **celebrity/political memecoin bust**: **$MELANIA** (−~99%), **$LIBRA** (Milei-linked, ~$4.5bn to −80%+, triggered an Argentine political scandal), **$HAWK** (Hailey Welch, ~$490M→~$50M in minutes, federal fraud suit). DL News pegged four such coins at **~$17.5bn combined losses**. $TRUMP is the **largest and most systemically loaded** case because the issuer is a sitting president. The structural analog inside our own corpus is [[WSJ finds 0.1% of Polymarket accounts capture 67% of profits]] — the same power-law: a tiny sophisticated cohort extracts nearly all gains while the 99%+ lose. (analysis) The differentiator vs a normal exchange/prediction market: here the **counterparty who profits from issuance is the policymaker who also sets the rules** — a conflict absent from Polymarket or a Coinbase listing.

## [2] Company history / fit
$TRUMP launched **2025-01-17**, three days pre-inauguration; Trump had earlier co-founded **World Liberty Financial ($WLFI)** with Eric Trump and Donald Trump Jr. — $WLFI has also slid heavily (CoinDesk: ~85% of secondary wallets underwater, single-sourced). Fits a deliberate trajectory: monetize the brand at peak political attention via a supply the family controls, harvest launch-window liquidity, and rely on the regulatory posture the same administration sets. The **May-2025 $148M gala for the top 220 holders** (top holder: Justin Sun; majority foreign) shows the design **rewards concentrated, often overseas, whales** — i.e., a channel for access/influence, not a consumer product.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
No product novelty — a memecoin with zero utility. The "traction" is the **loss/gain distribution itself as evidence**: ~988,905 losers vs ~492k winners quantifies front-loaded insider supply + peak-FOMO retail. Value capture in the stack sits entirely with **the issuer (fees/licensing) and early wallets**; late retail is the exit liquidity. (analysis) What makes it durable vs a random rug: the token trades near ~$400M cap 18 months on, so it is a **slow bleed monetized continuously**, not a one-shot rug — which is arguably worse for the ~2-of-3 who keep averaging down.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Regulatory backdrop is the pivot: the **SEC staff statement (2025-02-27)** deems memecoins non-securities / unprotected (Peirce reaffirmed May 2025), and the SEC **dropped/paused cases** vs Coinbase, Binance/CZ, and Justin Sun. So the losses have **no securities-law remedy** by design. The live tension is ethics: [[Senate Banking Committee advances crypto Clarity Act]] stalled precisely on **"ethics guardrails for government officials,"** and [[Trump signs executive order to modernize fintech rules]] shows the same administration widening crypto/fintech access. (hypothesis) Second-order effect: the $3.8bn figure becomes **ammunition in the Clarity Act ethics fight** and for a proposed memecoin/president-token ban — the number's weight is political-regulatory, not market-moving. Counterintuitive read: the more the token bleeds, the **stronger the conflict-of-interest narrative**, so the durable risk to the arrangement is legislative, not price.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Sector = celebrity/political memecoins, a value-destructive corner of the broader memecoin market. Size/growth (sourced): the memecoin category market cap collapsed from ~$93.1bn (Jan-2025) to ~$36.5bn (Jan-2026), ~-61%, with trading volume falling from ~$20bn (mid-2025) to <$3bn by Dec-2025 (per CoinLaw / The Defiant / KuCoin, via web). Memecoins' share of the altcoin market dropped to ~3.7%, the lowest since early-2024 (per CoinLaw, via web). $TRUMP specifically: peaked ~$15bn market cap Jan-2025, now trading ~$1.69, down ~98% from a $75.35 high, ~$407m cap (per Nansen/NBC/TechCrunch, via web + in-note). Structure: extreme concentration + power-law failure — per Binance Research, ~97% of launched memecoins have died or effectively gone to zero, average lifespan ~1yr (via web); Dogecoin alone (~$14bn) is roughly half the whole category. Barriers to entry are near-zero (anyone can mint), so the "moat" is purely attention/distribution, which is why insider-launched celebrity coins extract value at issuance and decay after. Why now: the driver is a permissive US regulatory stance — the SEC Division of Corporation Finance stated (Feb-2025) that memecoins are generally NOT securities (akin to collectibles, no Howey investment contract), and the SEC dropped ~10 crypto enforcement actions (Coinbase/Binance/Kraken) in early 2025 (per SEC / Harvard Corp Gov / McDermott, via web). That removed the main deterrent to celebrity-launch memecoins exactly as a sitting president became a direct issuer.

**Competitive landscape.** "KPIs" for a memecoin are attention/liquidity proxies, not fundamentals: market cap, on-chain holder count, share of buyers underwater, insider-vs-retail P&L split. There is no revenue/margin to compete on. Peer set = other celebrity/political launch coins: **$LIBRA** (Argentina, Milei-promoted, Feb-2025) spiked to a ~$4.5bn market cap then crashed within hours — ~74,000 investors lost ~$251m while ~9 founding accounts extracted ~$87m; marketer Hayden Davis/Kelsier Ventures implicated, an Interpol red-notice sought (per Wikipedia/CoinDesk/DL News, via web). **$WLFI** (Trump-family governance token) is down ~81% YoY, trading ~$0.06 (per CoinMarketCap, via [[World Liberty Financial sues Justin Sun for defamation]]); Nansen finds ~85% of WLFI secondary buyers underwater (in-note context). Basis of "competition" = celebrity reach + launch timing, not product. Protagonist position: $TRUMP is the largest and most-scrutinized instance of the pattern — not "ahead" in any durable sense, but the reference case that defines the "insider extracts, retail loses" template. Its only moat is the presidential brand and a permissive SEC, both reversible with the political cycle `(analysis)`.

**Comps & multiples.** No equity/EV multiples apply — this is a token, not a company; EV/Revenue etc. = no data. The meaningful comparison is the **insider-gain-vs-retail-loss ratio and drawdown depth**:
- **$TRUMP:** ~$3.8bn retail losses across 988,905 losing wallets (~2/3 of all buyers) vs ~$636m to Trump (issuance royalties, insulated from price); insiders reportedly extracted ~$4bn total (per Nansen/TechCrunch/CoinDesk/Blockchainreporter, via web). Drawdown ~-98% from $75.35 → ~$1.69.
- **$LIBRA:** ~$251m retail losses / ~$87m insider gain, ~-99% collapse in hours (per CoinDesk/DL News, via web) — same template, ~15x smaller.
- **$WLFI:** ~-81% YoY, ~$0.06, ~85% of secondary buyers underwater (in-note + CoinMarketCap).
Internal comps (grep of News/): [[Trump earned ~$1.4bn from crypto ventures last year]] (2026-07, the OGE disclosure this note quantifies; $636m of the $1.4bn is the memecoin line), [[World Liberty Financial sues Justin Sun for defamation]] (2026-05, WLFI insider dispute), [[Trump signs GENIUS Act stablecoin law]] (2025-07, the regulatory-legitimization backdrop). Distribution not computed (only 3 celebrity-coin datapoints, non-comparable scale) → qualitative: every instance shows deep drawdown + insider>retail asymmetry; $TRUMP is the outlier only in absolute size.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Conflict-of-interest / regulatory-capture tail risk to the whole crypto industry.** A sitting president's own administration ruled memecoins outside SEC jurisdiction while he personally cleared ~$636m from one — the SEC "formally declared the arrangement outside its jurisdiction" (per Silicon Canals, via web). Second-order: hands crypto critics a concrete "insiders extract from retail" case that could trigger a future re-tightening of memecoin/token rules for everyone, not just Trump.
2. **Retail-harm / reputational contagion for the memecoin category.** ~988,905 wallets down ~$3.8bn on a single token, plus ~97% of all memecoins failing, cements the "memecoins = wealth transfer to insiders" narrative. Second-order: accelerates the ~61% category-cap decline and undermines the industry's legitimacy push, feeding the regulatory-backlash risk above.
3. **Structural asymmetry is unfixable, not a one-off.** $TRUMP royalties accrue on issuance/trading, so insiders win regardless of price — the same mechanic as $LIBRA and $WLFI. Second-order: as long as SEC treats these as non-securities, the template repeats (new celebrity launches), each producing fresh retail losses and fresh political/legal exposure; there is no product improvement path that changes the outcome.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This crystallizes memecoins as a *structurally* extractive asset class rather than a speculative-but-fair one: the same insider>retail, ~98% drawdown pattern recurs across $TRUMP, $LIBRA and $WLFI, so the class-level thesis is "celebrity/political memecoins are a wealth-transfer wrapper, not an investment" `(analysis)`. Falsifiable thesis: the category cap keeps bleeding (already ~-61% YoY) and no celebrity launch sustains value — watch whether any 2026 celebrity coin holds >50% of its launch cap after 90 days (a survivor would break the thesis) and watch for any SEC/Congress reversal of the "memecoins aren't securities" stance (the catalyst that would re-price the whole category). What would break the thesis: a durable, community-owned memecoin with sustained liquidity and a non-insider holder base — none observed so far.
