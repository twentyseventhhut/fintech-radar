SpaceX's IPO honeymoon seems to be over.

Five weeks after the company's record-breaking debut, about $1 trillion in market value has evaporated — and the selling isn't letting up.

The stock first slipped below its $135 IPO price on Wednesday, touching $132.75 before recovering to close above it.

There was no recovery on Thursday: shares closed at $131.11 — their first finish below the IPO price — and fell by 5% on Friday. The stock was trading below $125 just before midday ET.

It means the rocket and AI company has lost roughly $320 billion in market value since IPO day and is down about $1 trillion since its June 16 peak.

The latest drop came after SpaceX called off its first Starship launchsince going public, which had been planned for Thursday at the company's Starbase facility in South Texas.

"Some of the engines didn't start, triggering an automatic launch abort," CEO Elon Musk said in a post on X, adding that the company would try again, with a targeted launch timing of "early next week."

# Context-enrichment: SpaceX shares slide 5%, deepening drop below IPO price (2026-07-17)
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**FRESHNESS: fresh.** This is a specific new trading-day event — the first *sustained close* below the $135 IPO price (Thu $131.11) plus a 5% Friday slide to ~$124 — crossing the "~$1T lost since the June 16 peak" threshold. It is NOT a re-run of the prior price notes: [[SpaceX stock tumbles 16.4%, shaving off most IPO gains]] (2026-07-07) was a drop off the peak while still *above* IPO; [[SpaceX falls to $135 IPO price ahead of Starship launch]] (2026-07-15) was the first *touch* of $135 that still *closed above* it ($135.27). The market-price consequence here is also distinct from the launch-mechanics note [[SpaceX aborts second Starship V3 launch after ignition]] (2026-07-17), which is the scrub itself, not the price event.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
De-PR'd facts: SpaceX (NASDAQ: SPCX) fell **~5% Friday July 17 to ~$124** (trading below $125 midday ET), the **6th straight down day**. It **first closed below its $135 IPO price on Thursday** ($131.11) after dipping to $132.75 Wednesday and recovering. Cumulative: **~$320B market value gone since IPO day**, and **~$1T gone since the June 16 intraday peak** (~$225.64 / ~$2.64T close → ~$1.63T). IPO was **June 12, 2026 at $135/share, raising >$85B** (largest public debut in history). Immediate catalyst: SpaceX **scrubbed Thursday's Starship launch** ("some engines didn't start," Musk; automatic abort), targeting a retry "early next week."
→ Why it matters: this is the moment the IPO went **underwater for day-one buyers** — a psychological line that turns "profit-taking off a euphoric top" into "loss realization," which changes holder behavior. → Second order: the $1T-in-a-month figure is a *headline* off the June 16 close; the more durable frame is that even at ~$1.63T the stock still trades at **~90x+ trailing sales** (FY25 revenue ~$18.7B) — so the real question is not "how far has it fallen" but "why is it still this expensive going into a supply cliff."

## [1] Competitors / peers (comparable mega-IPO post-debut slumps)
Not a product-competitor story; the relevant peers are **record-size, thin-float, single-controller IPOs that broke issue price fast**:
- **Saudi Aramco** (Dec 2019, ~$1.7T, prior record): held for weeks, then broke below issue decisively **Mar 2020** on the OPEC price-war crash — a controlled, thin-float mega-IPO breaking issue on a catalyst. Closest structural analog.
- **CoreWeave** (Mar 2025, priced $40 *below* range): broke issue on **Day 1**, later rallied hard — the "broke within days" analog, though driven by weak *demand* pricing, not a euphoric top.
- **Arm** (Sep 2023, $51): +25% Day 1 and *rose* into year-end — the **counter-example**; quality mega-IPOs don't always break issue.
→ Why the lay of the land: SpaceX's break is **faster and steeper in percentage terms** than either Aramco or CoreWeave, and — unlike Aramco (macro shock) or CoreWeave (soft demand) — is driven by its **own ~4% float mechanics** plus a self-inflicted supply cliff, not a market-wide event. → Second order: thin float means the price is a poor fair-value signal in *both* directions — it over-shot up (Day-3 $2.64T) and can over-shoot down into the August unlock.

## [2] Company history / fit
Private-mark ladder (analysis, negotiated tenders not market-clearing): **~$350B** (Dec 2024 tender) → ~$400B (Jul 2025) → ~$800B (Dec 2025) → IPO ~$1.77T open → peak ~$2.64T → now ~$1.63T. Recent corpus trajectory shows the run-up was already contested: [[Linas Newsletter Why a $1.75 trillion SpaceX IPO may be uninvestable]] flagged the valuation pre-debut; [[SpaceX let some Chinese investors into IPO via back channels]] and [[SpaceX falls to $135 IPO price ahead of Starship launch]] chart the slide. The AI-adjacency narrative ([[SpaceX to acquire Cursor for $60 billion in stock]], [[SpaceXAI releases Grok 4.5, an 'Opus-class model']], [[SpaceX in talks to sell AI compute to Pentagon]]) inflated the multiple beyond a pure launch/Starlink base.
→ Why the company/stock behaves this way: an ultra-thin ~4% float was floated into peak euphoria; the structure **guaranteed** violent mean-reversion once momentum turned. Even after losing $1T the public mark is **~2x the Dec-2025 private mark and ~4.7x the Dec-2024 $350B**, so the reset is compressing an extreme multiple *toward — but still well above —* the last private prints.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction — reset vs fundamental
This is a **valuation reset, not a fundamental break** (analysis). Starlink — the cash anchor — shows **no disclosed deterioration**: ~$11.4B FY25 revenue (+~50% YoY), ~61% of total, ~63% EBITDA margin, FCF-positive since 2024, ~10.3M subscribers (Mar 2026), 2026 est. ~$15.5–16B revenue (per third-party estimates; **unverified in a public filing until the first quarterly report ~early August**). Nothing in the numbers changed — only the price. 
→ Why the reset may not be done: at ~90x+ sales with an **August lockup cliff**, downside is asymmetric. **911.5M shares unlock unconditionally** on the 2nd trading day after the first quarterly report (~Aug 6) — **~$123B at current prices, larger than the entire ~$86B public float** and 64% bigger than the offering. A *second* 455.8M tranche unlocks only above **$175.50** for 5 of 10 pre-earnings days — currently far out of the money, so **dormant**. → Who captures the margin / what breaks the multiple: the market is **front-running a supply cliff** that exceeds the float; the binary is whether locked holders (huge paper gains) actually sell. Overhang ≠ realized selling (open).

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Near-term the two events collide: the **first public quarterly report (~Aug 6)** and, two days later, the **911.5M-share unlock**. Starship retry targeted "early next week"; the May V3 flight was *largely successful* but the booster crashed (5/33 Raptors failed to reignite; FAA closed the probe Jul 13). 
→ Why the market may keep sliding (analysis): the August unlock is a mechanical supply shock the market discounts in advance; a clean earnings beat + orderly selling could mark capitulation, while a miss + heavy unlock selling could cascade. → Counterintuitive second order: the *scrubbed* launch is sentiment noise, not fundamentals — but because the float is thin and buyers are now underwater, sentiment-driven selling has outsized price impact, so "noise" catalysts move the tape far more than they should. Governance overhang (single controller, Musk ~60% locked to mid-2027) caps free float and adds a fragility premium.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** SpaceX straddles two markets: space launch and satellite broadband (plus a nascent AI/compute leg). Starlink is the cash engine: ~10.3M subscribers across 164 countries with >9,600 satellites deployed by Q1 2026, projected ~$15.5bn 2026 revenue vs an estimated ~$11.8bn in 2025 (per valueaddvc citing S-1/estimates, as of 2026). Group S-1 disclosed ~$13.5bn revenue at ~36% EBITDA margins (per valueaddvc, 2026). Launch is a scale/reuse business: SpaceX ran 165 orbital launches in 2025 (record), but only ~43 for outside customers — nearly three-quarters are internal Starlink deployment (per search of 2026 sources). Structure: broadband is capital- and spectrum-intensive with strong scale/network barriers (self-funded constellation via own launch); launch is near-monopolistic on reusable heavy-lift. Why now: the whole thesis re-rates on Starship — the next-gen fully-reusable vehicle needed to launch V3 Starlink sats and NASA's Artemis lunar lander — so program slips hit the growth narrative directly. Note the ARPU tension: prospectus showed ARPU fell ~18% (to ~$81/mo) 2023–25 as subs quadrupled, then SpaceX raised plan prices up to $10/mo in May 2026 — a shift from land-grab to monetizing the installed base (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: Starlink — subscribers, ARPU, net adds, EBITDA margin; launch — cadence, cost/kg, reuse count. Launch peers: no true peer at SpaceX's cadence/reuse; Blue Origin, Rocket Lab, and legacy (ULA/Arianespace) trail on reusable heavy-lift. Broadband: Amazon's Kuiper is the emerging challenger; GEO incumbents (Viasat, Eutelsat/OneWeb) are the displaced. Recent moves with dates: Starship V3 debut in May 2026 ended in a booster mishap; the first post-IPO Starship launch (planned ~Jul 16, 2026) was scrubbed on an ignition abort ("some engines didn't start"), retargeted to "early next week." Position: dominant and ahead on both launch cadence and LEO broadband scale; moat = scale + vertical integration (own launch subsidizes own constellation) + intangibles/regulatory spectrum — a genuine wide moat, but one whose *future* value is levered to Starship execution (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** IPO priced Jun 12, 2026 at $135/share = ~$1.77tn valuation, raising ~$85–86bn (largest IPO ever). At the $135 IPO mark on ~$13.5bn revenue, implied P/S ≈ $1.77tn / $13.5bn ≈ **131x** — an extreme multiple even for hyper-growth (~14–30% group growth does not classically justify triple-digit sales multiples; the number prices Starship/Mars optionality, not current cash flows) (analysis). Post-IPO: closed ~$161 day one (+~19%), peaked mid-June, then bled to $131.11 (first close below IPO, Jul 16) and traded <$125 midday Jul 17; that is ~$320bn lost since IPO and ~$1tn off the June 16 peak. Internal comps (base): [[Linas Newsletter Why a $1.75 trillion SpaceX IPO may be uninvestable]] (2026-06-21, flagged the valuation ex-ante), [[SpaceX stock tumbles 16.4%, shaving off most IPO gains]] (2026-07-07), [[SpaceX falls to $135 IPO price ahead of Starship launch]] (2026-07-15) — a clear multi-week de-rating sequence. External IPO precedent: CoreWeave (Mar 2025) priced *below* range and traded flat/soft for days, then rallied >250% by Jun 2025 — i.e. a below-offer open is not destiny; the driver is execution, not the tape. Clean EV/EBITDA / forward multiples → [UNSOURCED] (private-history, no reliable free net-debt/consensus).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Lockup overhang (near-term, largest).** ~911.5M class-A shares (~$123bn) unlock unconditionally in the days after the ~Aug 6 Q2 report, and up to ~1.37bn shares total could hit the tape — roughly quadrupling float vs the ~639M sold at IPO. Mechanical supply shock into a stock already below issue price; why it matters: forced/eligible selling can overwhelm demand regardless of fundamentals.
2. **Starship program / execution risk.** May V3 booster mishap + scrubbed post-IPO launch. Why: Starship underpins V3 Starlink economics and the NASA Artemis contract; each slip erodes the growth optionality that the ~131x P/S depends on.
3. **Valuation / multiple risk.** Triple-digit P/S leaves near-zero margin for error; a re-rating toward "growth-justified" multiples alone implies large downside even if the business executes (analysis).
4. **Concentration.** Revenue and narrative heavily tied to Starlink (which itself consumes ~3/4 of launch capacity) and to a single founder-CEO whose posts move the stock — key-person + single-product concentration.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a post-IPO air-pocket, not (yet) a thesis break: the honeymoon multiple is deflating into a supply-heavy window. Falsifiable read (analysis): the Aug lockup + Q2 print is the pivotal catalyst — a successful Starship return-to-flight plus in-line/above Starlink monetization (post price-hike ARPU) into the unlock could mark the bottom (CoreWeave-style snap-back); conversely another Starship failure *and* heavy insider selling would confirm a structural re-rating from "optionality" toward "cash-flow" multiples. Watch: Starship next attempt (early wk of Jul 20), the Aug 6 report/ARPU trend, and lockup-window print/volume.

**Verdict (post-IPO valuation check, NOT an earnings print).** MISS on the "growth-justifies-the-multiple" thesis · no fresh quarterly results — SpaceX's first post-IPO report is only due in August. The 5% Friday drop (to <$125, first close below the $135 IPO price at $131.11 on Thu) is a valuation de-rating, not a reaction to new financials. The only hard numbers in the market are the S-1/prospectus figures below, and they cut against the ~90x price-to-sales multiple: revenue growth is decelerating while per-subscriber economics and losses deteriorate. A scrubbed Starship launch (engine ignition abort) and looming lockup expirations are the proximate triggers.

**Key figures (from the S-1 prospectus — no fresh print).**
- FY2025 revenue $18.7bn; net loss $4.9bn; operating loss $2.6bn; adjusted EBITDA +$6.6bn. Accumulated deficit since founding $41.3bn.
- Q1 2026 (3 months to Mar): revenue $4.69bn, +15% YoY — a marked deceleration; net loss $4.3bn in the quarter.
- Segments FY2025: Connectivity (Starlink) $11.4bn (~61% of revenue), the only profitable division at +$4.42bn income; Space/launch $4.0bn (loss –$657m); AI (xAI) $3.2bn (loss –$6.36bn, consuming most capex).

**By driver — the ARPU problem.** Starlink subscribers scaled 2.3m (2023) → 4.4m (2024) → 8.9m (2025) → 10.3m (Mar 2026), but ARPU fell $99/mo (2023) → $86 (Q1 2025) → $66 (Q1 2026) as growth shifts to price-sensitive markets (Africa, SE Asia, LatAm). Subscriber adds are increasingly offset by falling revenue per user — the crux of the bear case: at ~90x sales, the multiple needs total revenue to outrun the per-sub decline, and Q1's +15% suggests it is not.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public quarterly consensus exists yet (first report pending), so no beat/miss variance can be computed [UNSOURCED for consensus]. vs prior period: revenue growth decelerated to +15% YoY in Q1 2026, and net loss widened at the group level (AI drag). **Guidance / forward.** None given (no earnings call yet). Forward catalysts flagged: (1) first quarterly report expected August, immediately after which lockups lift on 911.5m shares (rank-and-file + some early investors) — a structural supply overhang cited as weighing on the stock; (2) Starship V3 return-to-flight, scrubbed Thursday on an engine ignition abort, retargeted "early next week" per Musk — Starship underpins next-gen Starlink launch cadence and the NASA Artemis lander.
