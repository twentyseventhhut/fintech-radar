BENGALURU, India , July 9, 2026 /PRNewswire/ -- Kapture CX , the verticalized full-stack agentic AI platform, today announced the successful closing of its $10 Mn pre-Series B funding round. The round was led by Bajaj Finserv Ventures ( BFSV ), part of Bajaj Finserv, one of India's leading AI-driven financial services groups, marking the firm's first investment in the AI landscape. Existing investors Cactus Venture Partners and India Alternatives also participated in the round.

 The funding comes at a pivotal moment. Despite pouring resources into AI, most large enterprises are yet to see real returns. The market is cluttered with point products from multiple providers, making AI adoption a high effort exercise. What enterprises need is a full-stack agentic AI player, one that understands their industry deeply and delivers custom solutions for their most complex workflows. This is exactly the gap Kapture was built to close.
When an enterprise works with Kapture, everything comes together on a single agentic OS platform. By owning and optimizing the full technology stack, from models to agentic layer to user interface, Kapture enables enterprises to deploy AI at scale without the complexity of stitching together multiple solutions. The platform is deeply verticalized, backed by deep-tech capabilities that allow Kapture to fine tune models. At a time when traditional SaaS players are struggling to prove their worth, Kapture is transforming complex enterprise operations in entirety.
Today, Kapture is trusted by over 1,000 enterprises across 18 countries, including market leaders such as Bajaj Finance, and multiple companies within the Tata Group and Reliance Group. Some of the world's largest consulting firms and hyperscalers are also working with Kapture as partners.
Commenting on the investment, Lakshmi Iyer, Group President - Investments and MD & CEO, Bajaj Alts , said: "We are currently in the process of launching the AI Opportunities Fund to invest across the emerging AI ecosystem. As we evaluated opportunities in the space, we found in Kapture a solution that demonstrably works at scale. Kapture's deep-tech capabilities, including model training, combined with its strong understanding of enterprise workflows and robust partner ecosystem, position the company strongly within the evolving AI landscape." 
 Sheshgiri Kamath, Co-founder and CEO of Kapture CX , said: "Since the Series A round in 2023, Kapture has grown 4x and achieved what remains rare in the AI economy - profitability. Having an AI-first organization like Bajaj Finserv Ventures lead this round is powerful validation of our value proposition. For enterprises whose experimentation phase with AI is behind them, Kapture is where real value begins. With an infrastructure that already processes massive volumes and drives measurable impact for customers around the world, Kapture CX is the right partner for enterprises ready to truly transform with AI." 
The fresh capital will fuel Kapture's expansion into global markets and power continued investment in product development. The company is targeting 5x revenue growth over the next few years as it moves to seize the immense opportunity in today's enterprise landscape.

## Контекст

### [0] What happened
Kapture CX — a Bengaluru-based, ~2014-founded ("12-year-old") verticalized full-stack **agentic AI** platform automating enterprise customer-support / CX & CRM workflows with industry-specific AI agents — closed a **$10M pre-Series B** round on 2026-07-09. **Bajaj Finserv Ventures (BFSV)** led (its *first-ever AI investment*), with existing backers **Cactus Venture Partners** and **India Alternatives** participating. Proceeds fund global-market expansion and product/R&D; company targets **5x revenue growth** "over the next few years." Co-founder/CEO **Sheshgiri Kamath**; co-founder/CPO **Vikas Garg** (a third co-founder, Pearl Tewari, is also cited externally).

### [1] Numbers / traction (own claims unless noted)
- Round: $10M pre-Series B. Total raised now **>$20M** (Tracxn/CB Insights: ~$11.3M over 7 rounds pre-round; prior Series A = $4M led by Cactus, Jul 2023, + $4M extension led by India Alternatives, Dec 2023).
- Traction claims: **1,000+ enterprises across 18 countries**; named customers Bajaj Finance, Tata Group & Reliance Group entities. Claims **4x growth since 2023 Series A** and **profitability** (rare for AI-era startups). FY23 revenue reported externally at **~$3.64M** (CB Insights) — implies a still-modest revenue base even after 4x.
- Product KPI claims (marketing): +22% first-contact resolution, −45% response time, +10% CSAT.

### [2] Why it matters
Data point in two converging theses: (a) **Indian vertical SaaS pivoting to "agentic AI"** as classic SaaS multiples compress, and (b) **Bajaj Finserv's aggressive move into AI/tech venture capital**. BFSV frames this as the anchor of a forthcoming **"AI Opportunities Fund"** (Lakshmi Iyer, Bajaj Alts). It slots into Bajaj's broader ₹1,500–2,000cr new-age-ventures / "Finserv Intelligence" program. Strategic angle: existing customer **Bajaj Finance** is a Kapture reference account — investor-as-customer, a common but conflict-worthy pattern.

### [3] Internal precedents (news DB)
- [[Bajaj Finserv unit to invest up to ₹2,000 crore in startups]] — the ₹1,500–2,000cr ($175–234M) AI/fintech/cyber venture mandate; Kapture is a direct instance of this program deploying.
- [[BFSI AI platform Lumiq raises ₹50cr from Bajaj Finserv, Info Edge]] — prior BFSV India AI bet (₹45cr lead, ~$46M post); shows BFSV's BFSI-adjacent AI pattern, though Lumiq is data-platform vs Kapture's CX-agents.
- [[Razorpay confidentially files for roughly $600 million IPO]] — India fintech/SaaS exit-market backdrop for late-stage rounds.
- Comparable-round framing: India SaaS/AI funding cadence in the same window (see 2026-06/07 funding notes).

### [4] Competitive landscape
Crowded CX-automation / support-AI field: **Zendesk** (~15% CX market share), **Freshworks/Freshdesk** (Indian-origin peer, ~21% FY24 growth), **Sprinklr** (unified-CXM), **Yellow.ai** (omnichannel bots), plus newer agentic entrants (Sierra, Kore.ai, Decagon, NiCE/Cognigy). Kapture differentiates on **verticalized, full-stack "agentic OS"** (owning model→agent→UI stack, fine-tuning) and enterprise India logos — but is sub-scale vs. these players on revenue and geography.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
### Red-team questions
1. **Related-party optics:** Bajaj Finance is both a named Kapture customer *and* affiliated with lead investor BFSV. How much of Kapture's traction/revenue depends on the Bajaj/Tata/Reliance ecosystem vs. independent wins?
2. **"Profitability" claim** is unaudited and self-reported — profitable on what basis (operating? PAT? cash?), and at what revenue scale? FY23 revenue was only ~$3.64M externally.
3. **"4x growth since 2023"** — off a very small base (~$3.6M → ~$14M?), so the absolute ARR may still be modest for a "pre-Series B" at $10M. Where's the confirmed current ARR?
4. **Round size vs. hype:** $10M pre-Series B, 12 years after founding, total <$25M raised — is this a modest bridge/insider round dressed as an AI milestone rather than a growth inflection?
5. **Insider-heavy round:** both non-lead participants (Cactus, India Alternatives) are *existing* investors. Was there genuine new outside institutional demand, or mostly pro-rata/insider support?
6. **BFSV's "first AI investment"** — is Kapture a conviction bet or a strategic/relationship placement to seed the forthcoming AI Opportunities Fund with a marquee logo it already banks with?
7. **Agentic-AI positioning:** claims of owning "model→agent→UI" full stack and "model training/fine-tuning" — verify vs. reality (are they truly training models, or wrapping foundation-model APIs?).
8. **Competitive moat:** against Zendesk, Freshworks, Sprinklr, Yellow.ai, Sierra, Decagon, Kore.ai — what is defensible beyond India enterprise relationships? CX-automation is commoditizing fast.
9. **Valuation undisclosed** — no post-money given. What dilution did a $10M raise imply, and does it signal a flat/down round given the small base?
10. **Geography risk:** 18-country / 1,000-enterprise claims — how many are paying, active, multi-seat vs. logo-count inflation? Global expansion (the stated use of funds) is unproven for this company.
11. **5x revenue target** has no timeframe ("next few years") — non-falsifiable guidance.
12. **Churn / net-retention** undisclosed — critical for a CRM/CX SaaS claiming scale.
13. **Coverage quality:** most sources are PR-newswire reprints (Morningstar, Yahoo, Analytics Insight, Khaleej Times) of the same company release — near-zero independent reporting/verification.
14. **Freshness overlap:** BFSV's venture push is already in the DB ([[Bajaj Finserv unit to invest up to ₹2,000 crore in startups]], [[BFSI AI platform Lumiq raises ₹50cr from Bajaj Finserv, Info Edge]]) — is this genuinely new signal or an incremental deployment of a known mandate?

**Importance: 3/5** — A clean, well-verified $10M round that is a concrete instance of two tracked themes (Bajaj Finserv's AI-VC push + Indian SaaS→agentic-AI pivot) and adds a named comparable to the DB. Capped below 4 because the check size is small, the company is sub-scale (~single-digit-millions revenue), traction/profitability figures are self-reported, the round is insider-heavy, and coverage is almost entirely PR reprints. Distinct enough from prior BFSV notes to count as fresh, not a duplicate.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Kapture sits in AI customer-experience / contact-center automation SaaS. Sizing depends heavily on scope: the AI-in-CX segment is ~$14.78bn (2025) → $147.62bn by 2035 at ~26.0% CAGR (per InsightAce, via insightaceanalytic.com); the narrower call-center-AI slice is ~$2.98bn (2026) at ~20.8% CAGR (per P&S Market Research). The broad contact-center-software base is ~$78–92bn (2025–26) growing ~16–18% (per Fortune Business Insights / EIN). Take the 20–26% AI-CX growth as the relevant driver, not the raw contact-center TAM. Structure: fragmented — global suite incumbents (Salesforce, Zendesk, Freshworks, Sprinklr, ServiceNow), AI-native disruptors (Intercom/Fin, Decagon, Sierra, Lorikeet), and regional/vertical players (Kapture, Yellow.ai in India). Barriers are moderate: enterprise switching costs + data/integration depth, low regulatory moat. **Why now:** the defining 2026 shift is agentic support collapsing seat-based pricing → outcome/per-resolution pricing. Salesforce Agentforce launched pay-per-resolution (available Jul '26), Zendesk shifted to $1.50–2.00 per automated resolution, Intercom Fin at $0.99/resolution (per salesforceben.com, futurumgroup.com, fin.ai). This re-prices the whole category and pressures seat-heavy incumbents.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: ARR/revenue growth, NRR, gross margin, and increasingly automated-resolution rate / cost-per-resolution. Kapture's positioning: 12-yr-old Bengaluru vendor, verticalized full-stack "agentic OS" (owns model→agent layer→UI), 1,000+ enterprises across 18 countries, anchor clients Bajaj Finance, Tata & Reliance group cos. It claims 4x revenue growth since 2023 Series A and — notably — profitability, rare among AI-CX peers (analysis: this is its strongest differentiator vs cash-burning AI-natives). Recent peer moves (2026): Agentforce per-resolution pricing; Zendesk Autonomous Service Workforce; Rep AI raised $6.2M with Zendesk as strategic investor (see internal comp). Position: regional catching-up player, niche-vertical + India-anchored, NOT a global category leader. Moat (analysis): switching costs + India enterprise relationships + full-stack ownership; weak on scale vs $800M-revenue incumbents. Undisclosed: ARR, NRR, automated-resolution rate, valuation — all `[UNSOURCED]`.

**Comps & multiples.** Kapture's own round carries NO disclosed valuation → its EV/Revenue is "no data"; $10M pre-Series B is small and revenue undisclosed. Peer trading comps (public, TTM, early Jul 2026):
- Freshworks (FRSH, India-origin CX/ITSM): mkt cap ~$2.72bn; rev ~$228.6M Q1 → ~$914M annualized; EV/Rev ≈ 2.72 / 0.914 ≈ **~3.0x** on +16% growth (per stocktitan.net, macrotrends).
- Sprinklr (CXM): mkt cap ~$1.27bn; TTM rev ~$871M → EV/Rev ≈ **~0.9x**, EV/EBITDA ~4.7x, on only +7.6% growth (per multiples.vc, stockanalysis) — cheap, reflecting stalled growth.
- Yellow.ai (private India peer): last priced ~$500M valuation on ~$78M Series C in **2021**, stale — not a current mark (per Tracxn/getlatka). 
Internal comps: [[Flashpoint backs US startup Rep AI in $6.2M round]] (CX/ecommerce AI, Zendesk strategic backer) and thematic [[Fintech Blueprint Are AI employees more expensive than humans]]. Distribution not computed (only 2 clean public marks + 1 stale private). Read: public CX-suite multiples have compressed to ~1–3x revenue (in-line/cheap), so any private "AI premium" for Kapture would need growth to justify it; profitability + claimed 4x growth is the offset. Kapture's implied multiple = no data.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Pricing-model disintermediation** — the same agentic shift Kapture rides also lets any full-stack rival (Salesforce, Zendesk) undercut on per-resolution economics; if resolutions get commoditized, Kapture's full-stack build cost may not out-earn cheaper API-layer entrants (second-order: margin captured by model providers upstack).
2. **Client & geo concentration** — anchor logos are Bajaj/Tata/Reliance and the lead investor (Bajaj Finserv Ventures) is also a customer (Bajaj Finance); related-party revenue + India concentration make the "1,000 enterprises / 18 countries" claim worth de-PR'ing (global expansion is the stated use of funds precisely because the base is India-heavy).
3. **Small round vs global ambition** — $10M pre-Series B is thin capital to fund global GTM against incumbents spending 10-figure revenue on R&D; execution/dilution risk if the 5x revenue target requires a much larger Series B.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) Not a re-rating event — it's a small validation round; the read-through is that even profitable, India-native CX vendors can raise on the agentic thesis while public CX-suite multiples sit at ~1–3x rev. Falsifiable thesis: if Kapture's per-resolution/agentic model sustains profitable 4x-class growth, it is a credible Series-B / eventual IPO candidate; trigger to watch = a larger priced Series B with a disclosed valuation and ARR. What breaks it = margin erosion once incumbents' pay-per-resolution pricing (live Jul '26) reaches Kapture's mid-market, or churn among the marquee anchor accounts.
