A fast-growing Edinburghfintech has raised £10 million and gained a new investor as it pushes ahead with its international expansion plans.

Payments technology specialist BR-DGE has seen a 15-fold increase in platform volumes in under two years, with transactions set to exceed 100 million per month by year-end.

Having established a leadership position in gaming payments, the capital tech venture is now expanding into adjacent enterprise sectors, with recent customer wins including major e-commerce business The Hut Group and Betfred.

Perry Blacher, chairman at BR-DGE; Pauline Bradley, CEO at Greyfriars (BR-DGE’s long-term investment advisors); Thomas Gillan, CEO at BR-DGE; Jake Kleiner, partner at Bettor Capital. Picture by Euan Cherry

The financial technology firm’s latest funding round has seen Bettor Capital, a specialist US-based investor in the gaming industry, join as a new growth partner, alongside continued backing from existing investors.

Thomas Gillan, chief executive at BR-DGE, which was founded in Edinburgh in 2018, said: “This investment gives us additional backing at a pivotal stage of international expansion. Payments have become a much bigger strategic priority for enterprise merchants, particularly in complex, regulated sectors where resilience, competitive advantage, customer experience and compliance are critical to success.

“The next generation of payment infrastructure is not about moving money; it’s about optimising every transaction.

“This new funding will enable us to further develop our platform, power our growth plan and help more merchants solve the payment infrastructure problems that matter most in large-scale businesses.”

The firm has also appointed Perry Blacher as chairman, bringing more than 25 years’ experience across technology and financial services, including as a founder, investor, advisor and board member.

Blacher, a graduate of both the University of Cambridge and Harvard University, said: “Merchants need infrastructure that helps them make better decisions, use data more effectively and respond quickly as markets, providers and customer expectations change. BR-DGE has the technology, sector knowledge and customer focus to solve those problems at scale, and is well positioned to help define the next generation of enterprise payments.”

Jake Kleiner, partner at Bettor Capital, said: “BR-DGE gives operators a smarter way to manage payments performance, resilience and data-driven decisions across a highly complex provider and regulatory landscape. Its growing positioning in gaming, combined with significant opportunity in adjacent verticals, made this a highly attractive investment opportunity.”

# Context-enrichment: BR-DGE raises £10m to accelerate global expansion
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- BR-DGE (Edinburgh payment-orchestration provider, founded 2018) closed a **£10m growth round** announced 30 Jun 2026. New backer: **Bettor Capital** (US, gaming-specialist investor), alongside "continued backing from existing investors" (long-term advisor Greyfriars; earlier cheques from Gloag Investment Group, Tech Nation). StrictlyVC framed it as a **"$13.3M round led by Bettor Capital"** — that is the **same round in USD**, not a second raise. Also appointed **Perry Blacher as chairman**.
- Round type is **not explicitly labelled** (Series A/B). Press calls it a "funding round"; no valuation, no dilution, no cap-table split disclosed. (analysis) £10m is small for an "8-year-old" company still scaling — consistent with a modest growth/insider-led top-up rather than a marquee institutional Series B.
- Traction claims are the real signal: **15x platform-volume growth in <2 years**, **>100m transactions/month by year-end** (targeted, not yet hit), named customer wins **The Hut Group (THG)** and **Betfred**; existing gaming roster William Hill, LiveScore Bet, Tombola.
- **Why framed this way:** the anchor is deliberately gaming-heavy — Bettor Capital is a gaming investor, and BR-DGE's defensible niche is **regulated gaming payments** (a vertical with real routing/resilience/compliance pain). The "global expansion / adjacent enterprise sectors / THG" narrative is the *aspiration*; the *proof* is still concentrated in UK gaming. The de-PR'd read: a niche gaming-payments orchestrator raising modest capital to attempt vertical diversification, not a horizontal orchestration challenger at scale.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Payment orchestration is a **crowded, commoditizing** layer:
- [[Apexx Global raises $10M for payments orchestration platform]] — near-identical: UK orchestrator, **$10m** (Feb 2026, Finch Capital), same "route across the ecosystem → raise acceptance, cut cost" pitch. Direct stage/geo peer.
- [[Solidgate launches Routing 2.0 payment orchestration in beta]] — UK; ships routing as a *product feature*, competing on engine depth.
- [[This Week in Fintech payment intelligence redefines orchestration]] — Praxis Tech; theme note: orchestration is shifting from routing plumbing to **"payment intelligence"** (data/decisioning) — exactly where BR-DGE positions ("optimising every transaction").
- [[CellPoint Digital launches One Source Orchestration platform]] — travel/airline vertical orchestrator; shows the **vertical-specialist** playbook BR-DGE is running in gaming.
- Also in corpus/space: Yuno, Pay.com, Ecommpay, plus Gr4vy/Spreedly (not in corpus) and hyperscaler-adjacent Checkout.com/Stripe adding native orchestration.
- **Position:** parity-to-behind on scale/capital vs global orchestrators; **ahead only within regulated UK/EU gaming**. (analysis) Second-order: orchestration's value is being squeezed from *above* (acquirers/PSPs bundle routing for free) and *below* (merchants build in-house once volume justifies) — so the winners are vertical specialists with compliance moats, which is why BR-DGE leans on gaming.

## [2] Company history / fit
- 2018 founded Edinburgh; early capital incl. **£2.5m from Gloag Investment Group** + Tech Nation accelerator; Greyfriars as long-term advisor. → the £10m is the largest disclosed round but the company has raised modestly overall — a **slow-burn, capital-light** trajectory, not a blitzscaler.
- Fit: raising *now*, on the back of THG + Betfred wins and 15x volume, is logical — capital to convert a gaming beachhead into "enterprise/e-commerce" before the orchestration window commoditizes further. **Why this way:** a UK niche orchestrator can't out-spend Stripe/Checkout.com horizontally, so it must (a) deepen a defensible vertical (gaming compliance/resilience) and (b) take an insider-friendly, non-dilutive-ish top-up with a strategically relevant investor (Bettor) rather than a large priced institutional round it may not command.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Nothing technically new.** Routing + tokenisation + data insight + smart controls is table-stakes orchestration (Apexx, Yuno, Solidgate, CellPoint all claim it). The CEO line "not about moving money; it's about optimising every transaction" is the industry's standard **"payment intelligence"** repositioning.
- Real value-add is **distribution + vertical depth in regulated gaming**, evidenced by named, credible operators (William Hill, Betfred, LiveScore, Tombola) and now THG. THG is the notable **non-gaming** proof point.
- Traction quality: **15x volume growth** and **100m tx/month target** are the load-bearing facts, but base is undisclosed (15x from a small base is easy) and the 100m figure is a **year-end target, not achieved**. (analysis) **Who captures the margin:** orchestration take-rate is thin and pressured; durable margin accrues to whoever owns the merchant relationship + compliance workflow in a regulated vertical, not to generic routing. BR-DGE's moat is only as strong as its gaming-compliance stickiness — commoditization breaks it if PSPs bundle equivalent routing free.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Plans: platform build-out, GTM, geographic + adjacent-vertical expansion, "significant new market and product launches" in **H2 2026**. Watch whether non-gaming logos (beyond THG) actually land — that's the thesis test.
- Backdrop: orchestration funding stayed active in 2026 (Apexx $10m Feb; multiple product launches), signalling a **consolidating, capital-hungry** layer where sub-scale players either specialise or get squeezed. Bettor Capital's involvement telegraphs continued **gaming-first** focus and possible portfolio cross-sell.
- Risks: (analysis) modest raise limits international ambitions vs well-capitalised rivals; regulatory dependence (gaming compliance is both moat and concentration risk); no disclosed valuation/round label suggests investors priced conservatively. **Counterintuitive second-order:** the gaming specialisation that makes the story fundable is also the ceiling — it caps the multiple and makes the "global enterprise" narrative aspirational until proven outside gaming.

**Sector & drivers.** BR-DGE sits in payment orchestration — a routing/tokenisation/data layer that sits *above* PSPs and card networks, giving merchants one integration to many providers. Market sizing is fragmented and analyst estimates diverge widely: per Mordor Intelligence, ~$3.13bn in 2026 at ~18.3% CAGR to 2031; per SkyQuest, $2.05bn (2025) → $9.34bn (2033) at ~20.9%; Research and Markets cites a much larger $10.39bn 2026 base — the 3–5x spread signals no consensus TAM and a category still defining its own boundaries (via marketreports/mordor/skyquest, as of 2026). Treat any single TAM as "no data / directional only". Structure: fragmented, many sub-scale orchestrators (Primer, Payrails, Gr4vy, Spreedly, Apexx, Yuno, MoneyHash, IXOPAY, Solidgate Routing 2.0) competing on approval-rate uplift and cost. Value in the stack is captured by whoever owns routing intelligence and data — but the underlying rails (Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, Trustly) sit below, so orchestrators are a thin margin layer riding others' economics. Why now: enterprise merchants in regulated/complex verticals (gaming, marketplaces) increasingly treat payments as a resilience+optimisation problem, not plumbing — CEO framing "not about moving money; it's about optimising every transaction" (per Scotsman, 2026-06-30).

William Hill, LiveScore Bet, Tombola (per next.io/businesscloud, 2026-06-30).
