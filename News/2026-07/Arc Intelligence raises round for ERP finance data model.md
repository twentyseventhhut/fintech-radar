# Arc Intelligence raises round for ERP finance data model

*   ARC Intelligence has secured €4 million in seed funding, led by 42CAP, with 468 Capital and IBB Ventures also participating.
*   In just 18 months, the Berlin-based startup shifted from offering a data tool for SMEs to building a finance operating system aimed at CFOs.
*   The company has grown its revenue tenfold since its December 2024 pre-seed round. This year, it plans to do the same.

Most seed rounds take months of pitching, follow-up calls, and diligence before a term sheet lands. ARC Intelligence took about a week.

The Berlin-based startup, founded in 2023 by CEO Clemens Wessendorff and CTO Simon Zimmermann, builds an AI layer that connects a company’s scattered ERP systems into a single data model. It has now closed a €4 million seed round led by 42CAP, with existing investors 468 Capital and IBB Ventures returning.

“It was a very smooth process,” Wessendorff, co-founder and CEO of ARC Intelligence, tells _Tech Funding News_, describing the round that moved from opening conversations to a signed term sheet in roughly a week. He credited part of that speed to 42CAP’s focus on the exact niche ARC operates in and to general partner Moritz Zimmermann‘s background in building enterprise software.

That speed says something about appetite in a category where the money is real: companies globally spend well over $50 billion a year on ERP software, much of it on systems that were never built to talk to each other across a group’s different entities, and investors are increasingly betting AI is the fastest way to fix that without a rip-and-replace.

A fast round is not, on its own, news. What makes it worth a second look is what ARC is asking investors to believe happened over the 18 months since its previous raise, and what its founder said about it when _TFN_ asked directly.

## **An ontology layer instead of replacing existing systems**

Wessendorff explained that ARC’s product serves as an ontology layer on top of a client’s existing ERP systems, whether they are older or newer platforms such as Business Central. For companies with multiple ERP systems, ARC collects data via standard ETL pipelines, loads it into a central model, and uses AI to automate reporting and analysis.

“It’s just ingesting the data,” Wessendorff said, highlighting the platform’s practical use. For example, ARC helps groups with multiple subsidiaries combine consolidation and margin reporting, removing the need for manual reconciliation.

Wessendorff traced the idea back to Zimmermann’s earlier work in financial due diligence and mergers and acquisitions at Deloitte, where the two kept running into the same problem: ERP systems full of inefficiency, with no clean way to fix them short of tearing them out.

That framing puts ARC alongside a wider group of startups betting AI belongs on top of the existing finance stack rather than replacing it.

Zalos, a San Francisco-based CFO-agent startup built on the same premise, has drawn attention from tech companies and private equity firms for improving operational efficiency across portfolio companies without forcing costly system overhauls.

ARC’s own press release points to a similar dynamic, naming Auctus Capital and GENUI as investors enabling the scaling of its platform across their portfolio companies. Light, a Copenhagen-based rival, takes the opposite approach, rebuilding the general ledger from scratch rather than connecting to legacy ERP.

Wessendorff pointed to Lucanet for financial consolidation and OneStream for the larger enterprise segment, positioning ARC in between the two, focused on mid-market and upper-mid-market companies. He didn’t mention that both now belong to the same portfolio.

Hg, the private equity firm formerly known as Hg Capital, acquired Lucanet in 2022 and completed a $6.4 billion take-private acquisition of OneStream in April 2026, just months before ARC’s own round closed.

An eight-person startup, in other words, is stepping into a category where its two named rivals are now funded and directed by a single consolidator.

## **A week-long round, a year-long revenue question**

ARC’s speed with investors contrasts with the more ambiguous pace of its own growth story. The company has grown its revenue tenfold since its December 2024 pre-seed round. This year, it plans to do the same.

The round brings ARC’s total disclosed funding to roughly €5 million. Most of the new capital is earmarked for go-to-market work, starting in the Netherlands, then the UK, with the U.S. described by Wessendorff as a longer-term ambition. The team, currently eight people, is expected to grow to around 25 within the next year.

42CAP’s Zimmermann, who co-founded Hybris, the German e-commerce company sold to SAP for around $1.5 billion in 2013, called ARC’s team extraordinary for the customer enthusiasm it has generated at such an early stage, and said he expects the next generation of finance software to be a genuine operating system rather than a collection of point solutions. 42CAP has backed several other European B2B software startups at similar stages over the past two years.

# Context-enrichment: ARC Intelligence raises €4M seed for an ERP finance data model
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **De-PR'd fact.** Berlin-based **ARC Intelligence** (founded 2023, CEO Clemens Wessendorff, CTO Simon Zimmermann) raised a **€4M seed** led by **42CAP**, with existing backers **468 Capital** and **IBB Ventures** returning. This brings **total disclosed funding to ~€5M** (a ~€1M pre-seed was in Dec 2024, IBB-led). The StrictlyVC digest wrote "$4.6M"; the primary source (Tech Funding News) and every other outlet say **€4M** — treat €4M as correct, the USD is a rounding/FX artifact.
- **Product (de-PR'd).** Not a new ERP and not an agent. It is an **"ontology/semantic layer" on top of existing ERPs** (e.g. MS Dynamics Business Central and older systems). Mechanism: standard **ETL pipelines ingest scattered ERP data → a central data model → AI automates consolidation, margin reporting and analysis** for multi-entity groups. In plain terms: **a middleware/data-plumbing product with an AI reporting front-end**, sold as a "finance operating system / AI CFO layer."
- **Why framed this way.** The "finance OS" and "one-week round" framing is narrative. The substance is that ARC pivoted in 18 months from an **SME data tool** to a **mid-market CFO consolidation** product — the raise is a **go-to-market bet, not a technology milestone**. Capital is earmarked for GTM (Netherlands → UK → US later); team 8 → ~25.
- **Disambiguation (confirmed).** This is **ARC Intelligence** (arc-intelligence, Berlin, ERP data model) — NOT arc.tech (SaaS treasury/lending) and NOT Circle's "Arc" L1 blockchain. All three are unrelated.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Same "AI on top of the finance stack" thesis:** **Zalos** (SF, $3.6M seed Mar-2026, computer-agents for CFOs on NetSuite/SAP/Sage — cited in ARC's own note); **Datarails** ([[Datarails raises $70M Series C led by One Peak]], FP&A/Excel-native, far larger and later-stage); **UnifyApps** ([[UnifyApps raises $50 million Series B for enterprise AI automation]], enterprise system-connection + AI, $50M Series B).
- **Adjacent finance-automation/reporting:** **GTreasury/Solvexia** ([[GTreasury acquires Solvexia for reconciliation and reporting]], automating reconciliation/reporting — an incumbent buying the capability ARC sells greenfield); **Ramp** ([[Ramp raises $300M at $32B valuation]], the CFO-stack/AI-spend giant — different wedge, same buyer).
- **Opposite architecture:** **Light** (Copenhagen) rebuilds the general ledger from scratch rather than layering on legacy ERP.
- **Consolidated incumbents (the real competitive fact):** ARC positions between **Lucanet** (mid-market consolidation) and **OneStream** (enterprise CPM). Both are now owned by **Hg** — Lucanet since 2022, **OneStream via a $6.4B take-private completed 1-Apr-2026** (verified: $24/share, 31% premium, 17 months after its 2024 IPO). So an **8-person startup is entering a category where its two named comparables are funded and directed by one PE consolidator** — that consolidator can bundle, cut price, or acquire.
- **Position:** clearly **behind on scale/traction**; the only "ahead" claim is architectural (layer, not rip-and-replace), which is not differentiated (Zalos, UnifyApps, incumbents' own AI all claim it).

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Trajectory:** 2023 founded → Dec-2024 ~€1M pre-seed (IBB) as an SME data tool → 18-month pivot to a CFO "finance OS" → Jul-2026 €4M seed. Revenue claimed **10x since Dec-2024**, with a plan to 10x again this year — off a tiny base (8 people, ~€5M total raised), so "10x" is low-signal.
- **Why this pivot.** The founders' origin (Zimmermann's Deloitte M&A/financial-due-diligence work) repeatedly hit ERP fragmentation across group entities. The pivot from SME to mid/upper-mid-market CFO is a **move up-market toward higher ACVs and a defensible wedge (consolidation)** — logical, because an SME data tool is a commodity while multi-entity consolidation has stickiness and switching costs.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new:** essentially nothing at the primitive level — ETL + a central model + LLM reporting is a well-populated 2025-26 pattern. The **value-add, if real, is the finance-specific ontology** (mapping heterogeneous charts-of-accounts/entities into one consolidatable model) so the AI outputs are audit-grade. That is the hard, defensible part — and it is **unproven from public info** (no named customers, no client count, no GMV/ARR figure).
- **Traction reality:** the only disclosed numbers are **8 people, ~€5M raised, "10x revenue" off an undisclosed base, one-week round.** ARC's PR namechecks **Auctus Capital and GENUI** as investors scaling ARC across portfolio companies — that is the **traction to watch (PE-portfolio roll-in)**, but it is announced, not quantified.
- **Who captures the margin (analysis).** The buyer (the CFO/consolidation budget of the >$50B/yr ERP spend) is real, but the **margin sits with whoever owns the system of record.** ARC is deliberately a **layer**, i.e. it does NOT own the ledger — so it risks being a **feature ERPs/Hg-owned incumbents absorb**, not a platform. Owning the ledger (Light's bet) is harder but more defensible; owning only the layer is faster but commoditizable.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Plans:** GTM Netherlands → UK, US later; scale to ~25 people; possible push into PE-portfolio deployments.
- **Sentiment:** category tailwind is real — >$50B/yr global ERP spend, strong VC appetite for "AI on top of finance without rip-and-replace" (Zalos, Datarails, UnifyApps all funded in the same window). 42CAP GP Moritz Zimmermann (ex-Hybris, sold to SAP ~$1.5B) betting on a "finance OS" gives the round enterprise-software credibility.
- **Counterintuitive second-order (analysis):** the same tailwind that funds ARC also funds the incumbents' AI and empowers Hg's consolidation — so a **hot category can be more dangerous, not safer, for an 8-person layer play**: the window to prove a defensible ontology + lock in PE-portfolio distribution before an incumbent bundles it is short. The central question is not "is there an AI-finance story" (yes) but **"can ARC own something ERPs/Hg can't cheaply replicate before it's commoditized."**

Sizing: financial-consolidation software is ~$2.7bn in 2024, projected to ~$6.4bn by 2032 at ~11.4% CAGR (per Credence Research, as of 2026) — a mid-sized, not hyperscale, niche; the adjacent global ERP spend cited by TFN is ">$50bn/year" [UNSOURCED beyond TFN's own phrasing].
