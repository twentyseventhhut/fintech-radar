## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **July 13, 2026**, REPAY Holdings (NASDAQ: RPAY) appointed **Zachary F. Sadek** to its board, expanding it from 6 to 7 members; term runs to the 2027 AGM and the board deemed him "independent" under Nasdaq standards. The digest's PR framing ("two decades investing across payments/fintech") buries the load-bearing fact: **Sadek is a Senior Partner at Parthenon Capital Partners, an affiliate of PCP Managers II L.P. — one of REPAY's largest stockholders** (his portfolio history includes BillingTree, Cayan, Payroc, Zelis, KBRA, Official Payments — a deep payments PE resume).
- **Why this is structured this way / what it reveals:** the appointment was announced **the same day the board unanimously REJECTED Forager Capital's revised $5.25/share (~$1B) unsolicited bid** as "significantly undervaluing" the company. Seating a director drawn from a large existing holder is a textbook **defensive-governance move**: it hard-wires a friendly, aligned insider onto the board precisely as an activist presses for a sale. The "independent" label is technically true under Nasdaq rules but economically the seat aligns the board with a status-quo holder, not with the $5.25 exit. So this is not a routine board refresh — it is a chess move inside a live takeover fight.

## [1] Competitors / peers
REPAY is a sub-scale US integrated-payments processor (~$300m revenue, ~$130B combined annual volume post-KUBRA) competing below Fiserv, Global Payments, FIS and Shift4. Board-level activist pressure on sub-scale payments names is a **sector pattern**: cf. [[Fiserv names JPMorgan veteran Adam Hyde Merchant Solutions COO]] (governance/leadership churn at a payments giant) and the broader consolidation drumbeat ([[Euronet to acquire CrediaBank merchant acquiring in Greece]], [[US Bancorp to acquire BTIG in up-to-$1 billion deal]]). Position: REPAY is a **take-private candidate** — small-cap, low multiple (~$4.80–5.25 bids vs a ~$2.75 30-day VWAP pre-bid), exactly the profile PE/activists target. Adding a PE-partner director is how boards signal they intend to fight for a higher standalone multiple rather than accept the first bid.

## [2] Company history / fit
Timeline: [[REPAY agrees to acquire KUBRA]] (Mar 30, 2026, $372m) → [[Repay attracts $1B takeover offer from Forager Capital]] (Apr 24; Forager criticized REPAY's "defensive measures" and questioned its commitment to shareholder value) → [[Repay completes $372 million acquisition of Kubra Data Transfer]] (Jun 8; funded with a $500m term loan, net leverage ~4.0x) → Forager revises bid to $5.25 → board rejects + appoints Sadek (Jul 13). **Why the company acts this way:** REPAY is running a dual-track defense — (a) a debt-funded KUBRA deal to claim a *higher standalone value* while (b) tightening board alignment. Piling ~4.0x leverage on the balance sheet is itself a poison-pill-adjacent tactic (a levered target is harder/costlier to acquire). The Sadek seat is the governance leg of the same defense.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Nothing operationally new — no product, no revenue. The *real* content is the **shift in the control contest**. Value-add of the appointment (analysis): it lends the board a credible payments-PE voice to argue "we can extract more value ourselves," and it consolidates support from a large holder against Forager (~13%) and the separate anti-KUBRA activist Veradace (~8.4%) — together ~21% of shares are activist-controlled. Traction to watch is not adoption but **the proxy math**: does Parthenon's stake + the new seat give management enough votes to hold the line, or does it inflame the "entrenchment" narrative Forager is already pushing?

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Expect escalation: Forager may go hostile (tender/consent solicitation) or run a proxy fight into the 2027 AGM; REPAY will lean on KUBRA integration ($15m+ run-rate synergies, 25% FCF accretion by 2028 targets) as its standalone-value case. Sell-side (DA Davidson) held Buy after the rejection. **Second-order (analysis):** the appointment could *backfire* — installing a director tied to a large holder while rejecting a 75%-premium-to-VWAP cash bid gives Forager fresh "board entrenchment / poor governance" ammunition. The central question is not "is a board seat meaningful" but **"does this move the sale closer or further"** — it is designed to push the price up or the sale away, and it materially raises the odds of a public proxy war.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** US integrated payments / merchant acquiring — a fragmented, consolidating stack where value migrates from pure processing toward embedded, software-led verticals. REPAY Holdings (RPAY) is a sub-scale specialist, not a broad acquirer: it clears card/ACH in two niches — Consumer Payments (loan/debt repayment, auto, receivables, credit unions) and Business Payments (AP automation / B2B). Full-year 2025 revenue was $309.3m, actually *down* from $313.0m a year earlier (per REPAY FY2025 8-K, via Globe & Mail / StockTitan) — i.e. the top line stalled while larger integrated peers kept compounding. FY2026 guidance is $340–346m revenue with $136.5–141.5m Adjusted EBITDA (guidance trimmed from $141–146m), framed as 10–12% reported growth aided by the KUBRA acquisition (REPAY FY2025 8-K / Q1 2026 release). Why now: the payments cohort is in a take-private / consolidation wave (sub-scale, EBITDA-rich, cheaply-valued names are being pulled off-market), and RPAY sits squarely in that crosshair.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: card-payment volume / TPV, gross-profit take rate, and Adjusted EBITDA margin. REPAY runs ~40%+ Adjusted EBITDA margin (Q1 2026: revenue $80.8m, ~4% YoY; AdjEBITDA ~$33.8–34.3m, ~42% margin; EPS $0.22 vs $0.21 est — Q1 2026 release / Quiver). Basis of competition is distribution + software integrations into vertical workflows, not price. Peers/incumbents: Shift4 (FOUR), Priority Technology, Fiserv/Clover, Global Payments, plus the Nuvei/Paya cluster in the same debt-repayment niche. Position: **niche, catching-up-at-best** — RPAY's revenue is roughly flat-to-declining while Shift4 keeps double-digit growth; its moat is switching costs inside sticky verticals (credit unions, receivables), not scale or network effects `(analysis)`. Most consequential recent move is not a product launch but a control contest (see risk flags).

**Comps & multiples.** RPAY: EV ~$594.1m, net debt ~$321.2m, market cap ~$239m (as of ~Apr 2026, per stockanalysis/companiesmarketcap).
- EV/Revenue (FY2025): $594.1m / $309.3m = **1.9x**
- EV/Revenue (FY2026E, mid $343m): $594.1m / $343m = **1.7x**
- EV/EBITDA (FY2026E, mid $139m): $594.1m / $139m = **4.3x**
- Peer anchor — Shift4 (FOUR): ~9.2x FY26E EV/EBITDA, ~4.1x EV/Revenue (multiples.vc / valueinvesting.io).
RPAY at ~4.3x EV/EBITDA vs FOUR ~9x is a **cheap / discounted** print — but the discount is *earned*, not an anomaly: RPAY has flat-to-negative revenue growth vs FOUR's double-digit, so the low multiple reflects the growth gap, not hidden value. Forager Capital (largest holder) put a control price on it: bid raised to $5.25/share (from $4.80), a ~91% premium to the ~$2.75 30-day average — the board **unanimously rejected** it on 13 Jul 2026 as undervaluing the company (BusinessWire / StockTitan). That rejection is the live valuation signal: management/board mark fair value well above ~4x EBITDA. Internal comps (payments/acquiring in-base): [[Top US banks weigh buying debit network from Fiserv]], [[Visa introduces Stablecoin Platform for banks and fintechs]], [[Mollie posts 29% net revenue growth to €147 million]] (a growth acquirer, contrast to RPAY's flat top line). No EDGAR entry in ir_latest.json — figures are web-sourced (SEC 8-K / IR releases).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Control overhang / contested take-private.** Forager (largest shareholder) has an open, twice-raised, board-rejected bid; a rejected offer from your biggest holder invites a proxy fight, revised bid, or forced strategic review — the Sadek board appointment (payments/fintech PE-investing background, 15 Jul, two days after the rejection) reads as governance/defensive reinforcement, not routine `(analysis)`. Second-order: strategy and capital allocation get hostage to the M&A process, not the operating plan.
2. **Stalled growth + trimmed guidance.** FY2025 revenue *fell* YoY and FY2026 AdjEBITDA guide was cut — a sub-scale processor losing top-line momentum is exactly the profile that gets disintermediated or acquired at a discount. Growth is now leaning on M&A (KUBRA) rather than organic.
3. **Leverage vs equity.** Net debt ~$321m against ~$239m market cap — debt is larger than the equity value, so small EBITDA slips hit equity holders disproportionately and constrain the board's ability to say "no" indefinitely.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a consolidation/take-private setup, not a re-rating on fundamentals `(analysis)`. Falsifiable thesis: with the board having rejected $5.25 and reinforced its board, either (a) Forager returns with a higher/hostile bid or a proxy contest, or (b) RPAY runs alone and must prove the KUBRA-driven 10–12% growth reaccelerates the top line. Watch: any revised Forager proposal, 13D activity, or a formal strategic-review announcement — that is the trigger that resets the stock from the ~4x EBITDA standalone level toward a control price. If FY2026 organic growth again disappoints, the "undervalued, reject the bid" stance gets harder to defend.
