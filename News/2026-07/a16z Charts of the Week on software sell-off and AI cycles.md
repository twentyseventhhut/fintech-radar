# Context-enrichment: a16z — "Charts of the Week: Software's Selective Sell-Off"
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5. Freshness: fresh._

Framing correction up front: this is an **a16z "Charts of the Week" note by Moses Sternstein** (a16z.news, ~2026-07-17). a16z is a VC structurally **long software + AI** — the two load-bearing claims ("selective sell-off, not a bloodbath" and "little to no evidence AI kills jobs") both happen to be the bullish reads for their book. Treat as book-talking and gate accordingly. The essay bundles two threads its own note-title flags: (a) the software valuation sell-off, and (b) the "cycles, different but the same" AI-demand argument (token-cost decline → Jevons → PC-adoption analogy).

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **Thesis:** publicly traded software keeps selling off, but it is a **selective/differentiated** sell-off, not an "undifferentiated bloodbath." Cost of intelligence is falling fast; a **Jevons-ish** dynamic (cheaper AI → more demand) supports AI demand; PC-adoption analogy says mass adoption is slow and scales with utility + cost; and (most aggressively) "little to no evidence" AI is actually eliminating jobs.
- **The sell-off is real and deep — not spin.** IGV (software ETF) returned only **+5.56% in 2025 vs +22.78% for the S&P 500**, and is **down ~13.8% YTD 2026**. For the **first time ever, software trades at a discount to the S&P 500** — software forward P/E ~22.7x fell below the market multiple (SaaStr).
- **Multiples have fully round-tripped below the pre-bubble baseline.** Median public SaaS **EV/TTM revenue ~3.3x (3/31/2026)**, down from 4.9x (YE2025) and 6.2x (YE2024); BVP EMCLOUD ~6.3–7.4x EV/revenue index-level. Versus the **~40x median EV/NTM peak of Nov 2021**, this is a full correction to/below the ~5–6x long-run drift (valueaddvc; saasvaluationmultiple).
- **Why framed this way / what it reveals:** a16z's own cuts — "software FCF multiples at 2014 levels," a "~50pp spread between top- and bottom-quartile IGV names," "revenue growth basically uncorrelated with stock performance" — are **proprietary, un-reconstructable** (universe/weighting/window unstated). The framing does real work: by anchoring on **dispersion** it recasts a brutal asset-class de-rating as a stock-pickers' rotation → "buy the winners," not "software is impaired."

## [1] Competitors / peers (whose thesis, not whose product)
- **The "selective" claim is the most defensible part and is independently corroborated:** cyber/observability and data-moated **vertical SaaS held up; horizontal/seat-based workflow SaaS, cloud/infra, marketplaces, ad-tech lagged** (Seeking Alpha; a16z's own earlier "rotation not sell-off" pieces). Dispersion is genuine, not narrative.
- **Bear camp — terminal-value impairment, not sentiment:** the sell-off reprices the **per-seat model** (one agent replaces N seats → enterprises buy 100 seats not 500), and **AI gross margins ~50–60% vs mature SaaS 70–90%** structurally cap multiples. IDC: pure seat-pricing **obsolete by 2028**; **Thoma Bravo's ~$6.4B Medallia → debt-for-equity swap ≈ $5.1B equity wipe** is real capital destruction (SaaStr; mindstudio; IDC).
- **Marquee voice cuts both ways — a16z uses only the friendly half:** **Satya Nadella** invoked Jevons (Jan 2025, "a commodity we just can't get enough of") — the a16z-friendly quote — but ALSO said **"SaaS is dead"** (Dec 2024), business apps are "CRUD databases with business logic" migrating into an agent tier. a16z cites optimistic Nadella, skips dead-SaaS Nadella (Fortune; Outlook Business).

## [2] Company history / fit
- Fits a16z's running 2026 arc cleanly. Compare **[[a16z Charts of the Week on cycles and AI-native startups]]** (2026-06-26): "rotation to atoms is temporary/cyclical → capital-heavy AI infra eventually flows to the software/app layer." The July piece is the **next beat of that same optimistic cycle model** — same author franchise, same "this too shall rotate back to software" reflex applied to the sell-off.
- Also rhymes with **[[a16z The Next AI Goldrush - Tokens, Loops, and Neofirms]]** (2026-07-14, enriched, imp 3): token-abundance / cost-of-intelligence collapse framing. And with **[[a16z speedrun The rise of the solo founder in the AI era]]** (AI → lean orgs) — all point one way: **AI as demand-expander, not disruptor of a16z's software book.** Structural pressure: a fund long software/AI must argue the de-rating is fear (cyclical, buy) not fundamentals (structural, sell).

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Token-cost decline is well-supported, not PR.** a16z's own ~10x/yr "LLMflation" estimate roughly held: GPT-4-class output **~$30/M tokens (Mar 2023) → <$0.50/M (2026)**, >60x at frontier quality; GPT-3.5-level ~280x (valueaddvc; agentmarketcap). So the **falling-cost-of-intelligence premise is solid.**
- **"Token-maxxing → token-optimizing" is a real 2026 meme, not an a16z coinage** — "token-maxxing" popularized ~Apr 2026 (Gizmodo/Mike Pearl; Meta "Claudeonomics" leaderboard); market's rival coinage is "valuemaxxing" (IBM). a16z is **framing** an existing trend, adding little original.
- **PC-adoption analogy checks out directionally:** BLS/Census show household computer ownership 15%→35% over 1990–1997; ~90% households today (~97% incl. smartphones) is consistent with Census ACS. The exact "~45% of adults 35–54 owned a PC in 1997" line is plausible but **not directly quotable from Census excerpts (open)**. Analogy is fair for "adoption is slow, scales with utility+cost."
- **Where the value-add breaks (who captures margin):** Jevons only rescues software revenue if demand elasticity > per-unit price collapse. **Token volume ↑ ≠ SaaS ARR ↑** — a cheaper agent can grow raw token consumption while *cutting paid seats*. And 20%→60% YTD open-weight token share (a16z's own figure) **commoditizes the model layer** the same deflation is supposed to bless. So the multiple that breaks is precisely the seat-based app SaaS a16z says to "buy the winners" within.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Sentiment genuinely bifurcated/bearish on software as an asset class** (discount to S&P, 3.3x median) but the market is discriminating — a16z's "selective" call is the right read.
- **The "no job impact" claim is the essay's weakest, most book-talking link, and is contradicted by the best evidence:** **Stanford "Canaries in the Coal Mine"** (Brynjolfsson et al., Aug 2025, 25M-worker ADP payroll) — workers **22–25 in AI-exposed roles down ~13%** relative; entry-level software/support ~20%. **AI = #1 cited layoff reason, ~13% of Q1 2026 cuts** (~87.7k AI-attributed cuts in 5 months); **Salesforce cut ~4,000 support roles**; Amazon ~14,000 (Stanford; Tom's Hardware; TechCrunch).
- **The one internal note that partly rescues a16z:** **[[Report heavy AI adopters grew headcount 10.2%]]** (Ramp/Revelio) — "high-intensity AI adopters" grew headcount 10.2%, entry-level +12% — i.e., AI as firm-*expander* in tech-forward firms. But that note is itself flagged stale and its authors admit it doesn't show AI "universally creates jobs." **Honest read:** AI is measurably hitting young/entry-level cohorts even as some AI-adjacent hiring recovers → a16z **overstates** with "little to no evidence."
- **Counterintuitive second-order:** if 3.3x medians are the *new fair value* (terminal-value impairment), then "multiple compression from fear" is a mis-diagnosis and the "buy the dip on winners" call is a value trap for the punished quartile.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is macro/sector commentary, not a company event — the "sector" is public application software / SaaS, and a16z's charts frame two things: a *selective* (not undifferentiated) software sell-off, and the token-maxxing → token-optimizing shift driving a falling cost of intelligence with Jevons-style demand. The 2026 backdrop is severe: the median public SaaS EV/TTM-revenue multiple fell to ~3.3x at 31-Mar-2026, down from ~4.9x at YE2025 and ~6.2x at YE2024, and software forward P/E dropped to ~22.7x — below the S&P 500 for the first time on record (per multiples.vc / SaaStr, as of Mar–Jul 2026). The iShares software ETF **IGV was down ~11% YTD and ~17% since 1-Jun-2026, erasing its YTD gains while the S&P 500 (VOO) was up ~+10%** (per Seeking Alpha / PortfoliosLab, Jul-2026). Driver / "why now": investors are re-pricing *terminal* per-seat revenue, not the current quarter — if one AI agent replaces N human seats, seat-expansion (the SaaS growth engine) is at risk, so multiples compress even against in-line results. Second-order: pricing is migrating off seats — per-seat adoption reportedly fell 21%→15% in a year, with outcome/consumption-based terms in ~40% of enterprise contracts (per Chargebee / L40, 2026) `(analysis on causality)`.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: ARR growth, net revenue retention (NRR), Rule of 40, and now *seat-count durability* / share of consumption-priced revenue. The "AI-vs-SaaS" debate splits the tape, which is exactly a16z's "selective sell-off" point:
- **Losers (seat-exposed):** ServiceNow (NOW) ~-40% YTD (also on Middle-East on-prem deal slippage), Atlassian (TEAM) ~-45–50% YTD (agents auto-managing Jira tickets), HubSpot (HUBS) ~-45% YTD (per AOL / Sherwood / Yahoo Finance, Jul-2026).
- **Contested / re-rating up:** Salesforce (CRM) ~-31% YTD but Agentforce reported ~$800M ARR; Atlassian later *beat and raised* FY26 guidance, which Sherwood framed as "blowing a hole in the software AI bear thesis" — evidence the sell-off is not uniform.
- **New premium tier:** AI-native platforms reportedly commanding ~25–30x EV/Revenue vs ~3x for legacy SaaS (per multiples.vc/Oliver Wyman) — dispersion, not a blanket "bloodbath," corroborates a16z.
Position: a16z is the commentator, not a protagonist; as a large late-stage/crossover holder of both private software and AI-native names, it is not a neutral observer (see Risk flags).

**Comps & multiples.** Qualitative grounding on IR-covered public-software names — no clean free EV/EBITDA source, so multiples kept qualitative:
- **Salesforce (CRM, covered)** — mega-cap, re-rating candidate on Agentforce; trades as the "agentic winner" case.
- **DocuSign (DOCU, covered), Shopify (SHOP, covered), Xero, Wix (WIX)** — a spread of app-software names IR-covered here, illustrating the same seat-vs-consumption fault line.
- Sector anchor arithmetic (external): median SaaS EV/TTM-rev `3.3x` = the ~55% de-rate from `6.2x` (YE2024) → `3.3x`, i.e. multiples roughly halved in ~15 months while ARR mostly kept growing — the compression is a *multiple* story, not a revenue collapse (per SaaStr/multiples.vc). In-base comps: [[Backbase acquires Kasisto to advance agentic banking]], [[Salesforce moves to acquire usage-based billing platform M3ter]] (usage/consumption-billing tooling — the pricing-model pivot in fintech-SaaS), [[Rapyd restructures with hundreds of layoffs in AI-driven shift]], [[StrictlyVC US stocks fall as semiconductor shares sell off]]. **Distribution not computed** (mixed disclosure); qualitative comparison only. Rich/cheap flag: legacy SaaS now *cheap vs its own history and vs the S&P*; AI-native tier *rich* — but the range breach is growth/terminal-value driven, not an automatic mispricing.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Author conflict / selection bias** — a16z is a large holder of AI-native and private software; a "selective, not a bloodbath" framing plus "Jevons → more AI demand" is directionally favorable to its book. Charts-of-the-week curate which series to show; the *un*differentiated part of the tape (IGV -17% since June, TEAM/HUBS ~-45–50%) is the harder fact. Treat as data, not thesis.
2. **Thesis fragility — Jevons ≠ software revenue.** Falling token cost + rising usage supports *AI compute* demand; it does not mechanically support *seat-based software revenue*. Cheaper intelligence is precisely what threatens the seat model. The bull "Jevons" chart and the bear "seat-compression" chart can both be true simultaneously — a16z's own bifurcation admits this.
3. **"Token cost illusion" / cost paradox.** Blended token price fell ~67% YoY (~$18.40→$6.07/M tokens) yet enterprise AI *bills tripled* as agentic workflows multiply token counts 50–500x and ~72% of cost sits outside the model invoice (per Artefact/Bain/Navya, 2026). "Cost of intelligence falling" is a real but partial fact — total spend is rising, which cuts against a clean "cheaper → free adoption" read.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` The re-rating is a *bifurcation*, not a sector-wide de-rating: the market is repricing terminal seat economics, rewarding names that convert to consumption/outcome pricing and own proprietary data + workflow lock-in, and punishing pure seat-expansion stories. Falsifiable watch: if Q2–Q3-2026 prints from seat-heavy incumbents (NOW, TEAM, HUBS) show NRR stabilizing and successful consumption-pricing migration (à la Atlassian's beat-and-raise), the "AI eats SaaS" bear thesis weakens and legacy multiples re-rate off ~3.3x; conversely, another leg down in IGV on NRR erosion would confirm structural, not cyclical, compression. Trigger: enterprise-software Q2-2026 earnings + any acceleration in per-seat→consumption pricing disclosure.
