# Gorizont Industries seeks to buy Louis Dreyfus Russian assets

> [!info] 2026-07-08 · 1 упоминаний · 1 источника(ов) с текстом

[Mergers.ru] 🌾 Российские активы международного зернотрейдера Louis Dreyfus рассчитывает выкупить АО «Горизонт Индастриз»

АО «Горизонт Индастриз» через «дочку» ТОО «Темиртек» заключило предварительные договоры купли-продажи российских структур Louis Dreyfus Company (LDС). Об этом говорится в сообщении компании, направленном в Минсельхоз. В периметр сделки, заключенной 25 мая, вошли ООО «Волгоградский элеватор», компания «Луис Дрейфус Восток» и Русская элеваторная компания («РусЭлКо»), уточняет "Коммерсант".

АО «Горизонт Индастриз» зарегистрирована в Москве в октябре 2024 года. Учредители компании не раскрываются. Александр Поцелуев — бывший глава и совладелец инвестфонда Russian Friends Capital, среди активов которого были комплекс по производству атлантического лосося «Тулома Салмон Дальний Восток» и поставщик оборудования для предприятий горнодобывающей промышленности «Буллит». Последним господин Поцелуев сейчас владеет сам.

Собеседник издания на аграрном рынке предполагает, что стратегия «Горизонт Индастриз» направлена на перепродажу активов. Инвестиционный директор BGP Capital Юрий Левицкий поясняет, что в перспективе российский бизнес LDC может быть интересен крупным экспортерам зерна и агрохолдингам, которые нуждаются в линейной элеваторной сети и собственной перевалке на Дону. Другой источник “Ъ” говорит, что господин Поцелуев действует в интересах миллиардера Искандара Махмудова. В подконтрольном его структурам холдинге «Просторы» "от комментариев отказались.

Louis Dreyfus занимается торговлей сельхозпродукцией. Компания существует с 1851 года, ее головная структура находится в Нидерландах. В России LDС долго была одним из крупнейших экспортеров зерна. В первой половине 2021–2022 сельскохозяйственного года компания заняла девятую позицию, отправив за рубеж 559 тыс. тонн продукции. Но затем работа LDC в России фактически прекратилась.

Продажа российских активов LDС обсуждается с 2024 года. Тогда Владимир Путин разрешил реализацию 100% долей в «РусЭлКо», одного из активов холдинга. Покупатель в постановлении не фигурировал, хотя на рынке потенциальным интересантом называли «Био-Тон». Сделка в итоге не состоялась, и в августе 2025 года стало известно о планах ликвидировать компанию «Луис Дрейфус Восток» и «РусЭлКо». Контроль над активами компании рассчитывал получить топ-менеджмент российского Louis Dreyfus, начавший взыскивать задолженность по зарплате.

На балансе российских структур LDC — 12 элеваторов и зерновой терминал в порту Азов. Это одна из самых мощных сетей в России, которая могла бы стоить десятки миллиардов рублей, поясняет Юрий Левицкий. Но сейчас, по его словам, продается проблемный бизнес с накопленными долгами, а сделка предполагает обязательный дисконт. В итоге стоимость активов составит 1–3 млрд руб., дополнительно придется вложиться в выплату долгов и перезапуск, констатирует эксперт.

Сейчас «Горизонт Индастриз» уже занимается восстановлением платежеспособности структур LDC. В середине мая погасила долги «РусЭлКо» по налогам и сборам. Для возобновления работы элеваторов, следует из текста, планируется рассчитаться с сотрудниками и ресурсоснабжающими организациями. Политика «Горизонт Индастриз», по мнению господина Левицкого, может быть направлена на то, чтобы компании избежали банкротства, а регулятор получил позитивный сигнал, уверившись в серьезности намерений покупателя.

«Горизонт Индастриз» только предстоит получить разрешение властей на приобретение активов. Предварительные договоры купли-продажи заключены с этим условием, следует из уведомления в Минсельхоз. Подать заявления в правкомиссию по контролю за иностранными инвестициями компания планирует к августу 2026 года. Партнер юрфирмы «Алимирзоев и Трофимов» Илья Алещев поясняет, что, получив обращение потенциального покупателя LDC, Минсельхоз перенаправит его в Минфин, сопроводив отзывом.

ТОО «Темиртек», через которое планируется заключение сделки по покупке LDC, зарегистрировано в Казахстане. Это нейтральная юрисдикция, замечает господин Левицкий. Партнер Stonebridge Legal Роман Прудентов в то же время замечает, что вопросы, связанные со структурой расчетов, прозрачностью владения и наличием иностранного элемента, могут послужить причиной отказа правкомиссии на одобрение сделки.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **25 May 2026**, JSC **Gorizont Industries** (Горизонт Индастриз), acting through its Kazakhstan-registered subsidiary **TOO Temirtek**, signed **preliminary** sale-and-purchase agreements for the Russian structures of **Louis Dreyfus Company (LDC)** and filed notice with the Ministry of Agriculture. The perimeter: **OOO Volgogradsky Elevator**, **Louis Dreyfus Vostok**, and the **Russian Elevator Company (RusElKo)** — together **12 elevators + a grain terminal at the port of Azov**, one of the strongest storage/transshipment networks in Russia.

- **This is NOT a closed deal.** It is a *conditional preliminary contract*. Gorizont still needs approval from the government Commission on Control of Foreign Investment; it plans to file the application only by **August 2026**. Ministry of Agriculture will forward it to Ministry of Finance with an opinion. So the operative status is "talks → conditional signature", not "sold".
- **Price is ambiguous and worth flagging.** Kommersant's headline (doc 8798465) frames it as **~13bn rubles**; but BGP Capital's Yuri Levitsky estimates the *clean* asset value at only **1–3bn rubles** because the business is distressed (accumulated debts, mandatory exit discount), plus buyer must fund debt repayment and restart. The gap likely = enterprise value / gross-of-debt headline (~13bn) vs. net clean-asset value (1–3bn). Treat the 13bn as unconfirmed/gross; the note's own primary text anchors to 1–3bn.
- **Why structured this way:** the deal routes through a **Kazakh SPV (Temirtek)** — a "neutral jurisdiction" — to soften the "foreign element" for the Commission. Ownership of Gorizont itself is **undisclosed**. Gorizont is a shell registered in **Moscow, Oct 2024**; front man **Alexander Potseluyev** (ex-Russian Friends Capital). Sources say he **acts in the interests of billionaire Iskander Makhmudov** (whose agri holding "Prostory" declined to comment). So the real buyer is masked behind two layers (Kazakh SPV + undisclosed shell) — precisely to get a sanctioned-adjacent forced-sale past the regulator.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **The Big-4 grain-trader exodus.** LDC ceased Russian grain exports from **1 Jul 2023**, alongside **Cargill** and **Viterra** (all announced Apr 2023). In Jul 2022–Mar 2023 Viterra shipped ~2.4Mt of Russian wheat, Cargill ~1.6Mt, Dreyfus ~0.8Mt. State pressure to hand exports to domestic players drove the exit.
- **Rival bidders for LDC's carcass:** earlier **Bio-Ton** was floated as a buyer of RusElKo (Putin's Nov 2024 decree authorised a RusElKo sale to an unnamed buyer; that deal fell through). LDC's own **Russian top-management** tried to seize the assets in 2025 via salary-debt lawsuits. **UralChem** offered to buy Cargill/Viterra local assets. So Gorizont/Makhmudov is one of several domestic circling parties.
- **Position:** Gorizont is *ahead* of rival claimants (it has signed preliminary contracts and is already paying down RusElKo's tax debts), but *behind* the regulatory finish line.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **LDC:** founded 1851, HQ Netherlands; once a top-9 Russian grain exporter (559kt in H1 2021/22 season) before winding down to near-zero. Aug 2025: plans surfaced to *liquidate* Louis Dreyfus Vostok and RusElKo — so Gorizont is buying out of a near-liquidation.
- **Gorizont fit:** the corpus context (analysis) is that this is a **classic distressed forced-sale flip**. A market source explicitly says Gorizont's strategy is **resale**: LDC's linear elevator network + Don-basin transshipment would appeal to large exporters/agroholdings. Gorizont is pre-cleaning the assets (paid RusElKo tax arrears mid-May, plans to clear payroll + utilities) — **why?** To avoid bankruptcy and signal seriousness to the regulator, raising approval odds and the eventual flip price.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Not a fintech/capital-markets innovation** — it is a pure Russian-market distressed M&A of Western-owned agri infrastructure. No product, no technology, no financial-services angle beyond deal structuring.
- The only "new" element vs prior corpus items on Russian forced-sales of foreign assets ([[Belaya Skala buys 47.2bn ruble asset portfolio]] — Raven Russia warehouses; [[Ekto Prime fund becomes owner of Top Lubricants plant]] — likely-LUKOIL buyer; [[VTB to sell former Otkritie office to Wildberries-Russ]]; [[Ping An and Taikang weigh buying Russian life insurers]]) is the **sector (grain/agri infrastructure)** and the **named oligarch beneficiary (Makhmudov)**.
- **Traction = none yet.** Preliminary contract only; approval not filed until Aug 2026; ownership opaque. Value-add (who captures the margin): if the resale thesis is right, Gorizont/Makhmudov captures the arbitrage between the **mandatory ≥60% forced-sale discount** (regulator-imposed) and the network's intrinsic worth (Levitsky: "tens of billions of rubles" if healthy) — i.e., the margin is **regulatory-arbitrage rent**, not operational value creation.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Regulatory gate is the whole story.** Russia's 2024 tightening: exit transactions require a **≥60% discount** to appraised market value, a **≥35% "voluntary" exit-tax** on undiscounted value, and Presidential sign-off if assets exceed **50bn rubles** (LDC's are far below, so no Presidential decree needed this time — unlike the 2024 RusElKo decree). Stonebridge Legal's Roman Prudentov warns the **opaque ownership, payment structure and foreign element** could still trigger a Commission refusal.
- **Second-order (analysis):** the Kazakh-SPV + undisclosed-shell structure is a **double-edged** signal — it eases the "unfriendly-jurisdiction" optics but is exactly the kind of opacity the Commission has been rejecting. Approval is *not* a formality.
- **Sentiment:** market reads this as a **speculative distressed flip by a well-connected buyer**, not a strategic agri play. Grain-market conditions make a comparable-scale strategic buyer unlikely near-term (per analysts), which is *why* a flipper stepped in.

3. **Who really owns Gorizont Industries?** — *Open/masked.* Founders undisclosed; front man Potseluyev; sources tie it to billionaire Iskander Makhmudov (holding "Prostory"), unconfirmed on record.
4. **Why a Kazakh SPV (Temirtek)?** — Neutral-jurisdiction optics to reduce "foreign element" friction at the Commission; but also a transparency red flag the Commission has rejected before.
5. **What is the exit thesis — operate or flip?** — Market source says **flip/resale**; Gorizont has no agri-operating track record, supporting the flip read.
6. **Does the deal need Presidential sign-off?** — *No* (assets << 50bn ruble threshold), unlike the 2024 RusElKo decree. Only Commission (Minfin/Minselkhoz route) approval needed.
7. **What discount/exit-tax applies?** — ≥60% mandatory discount to appraised value + ≥35% "voluntary" exit tax (2024 rules). This is the margin source for a flipper.
8. **Could the Commission refuse?** — *Yes* (Stonebridge/Prudentov): opaque ownership, payment structure, foreign element. Approval is not a formality.
9. **Are there rival claimants?** — Yes: Bio-Ton (earlier RusElKo interest, failed), LDC's own Russian top-management (2025 salary-debt suits), UralChem (Cargill/Viterra assets). Gorizont is ahead but not sole.
10. **Is there any fintech / capital-markets substance?** — *No.* Pure distressed Russian M&A of agri infrastructure; only tagged capital-markets by convention.
11. **Is this new vs. the corpus?** — *Fresh.* No prior LDC/Gorizont note; corpus has analogous forced-sale flips ([[Belaya Skala buys 47.2bn ruble asset portfolio]], [[Ekto Prime fund becomes owner of Top Lubricants plant]]) but not this deal/period.
12. **What breaks the thesis?** — Commission refusal; buyer's opacity; distressed debts exceeding restart economics; weak grain-market demand leaving no downstream buyer to flip to.
13. **Why does LDC accept a fire-sale?** — Assets were near liquidation (Aug 2025); recovering *any* value beats a wind-down; exit rules cap upside anyway.
14. **Second-order significance?** — Illustrates the maturing mechanics of Russia's forced-exit regime (Kazakh SPV masking + oligarch beneficiaries capturing regulatory-arbitrage rent), but the individual deal is small and unconfirmed.

**Importance: 2/5** — Small (1–3bn ruble clean value), unconfirmed (conditional preliminary contract, approval pending to Aug 2026), opaque, and with **no fintech / financial-services substance** — a niche Russian agri distressed-M&A item. Weight comes only as another data point in the "foreign companies exiting Russia at forced discounts, bought by masked oligarch-linked shells" pattern; not a market-moving or novel event on its own.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is a Russia-market M&A / forced-exit deal in grain agribusiness, not a fintech round — sizing is qualitative. Russia's foreign-owner exit regime is the dominant structural driver: since 2022 sales by "unfriendly"-jurisdiction owners require Government Commission approval plus a mandatory discount and a budget "contribution" (exit tax), both repeatedly hiked — discount now ≥60% of appraised value and contribution ≥35% (per Reuters/Russia Post & Cleary Gottlieb, as of late 2024). The regime has all but frozen exits: only 15 foreign-exit deals in 9M-2025 worth ~$642.8m (2.5% of Russian M&A), down from ~40 deals/~$2bn in 9M-2024 (per Russia Post, 2025). "Why now": LDC — a grain trader operating since 1851, Dutch-headquartered — stopped Russian grain exports from 1 July 2023 alongside Cargill and Viterra (S&P Global, Moscow Times, 2023), idled its RusElCo elevators from mid-2024, and moved to liquidate «Луис Дрейфус Восток» + RusElCo on 6 Aug 2025 (AgroPages, 2025). Gorizont's pre-agreements (25 May 2026, filing to Minselkhoz) are an attempt to buy the distressed remnant before liquidation completes. Second-order effect: Western traders exiting handed the export chain to domestic/state-linked consolidators (Grain Gates, linked to Demetra/VTB, now ~33% share; Aston ~15%; OZK ~7.5% — Agricensus/Fastmarkets, 2025), so the physical elevator/port infrastructure they left is the contested prize.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs here: elevator storage capacity, port transshipment throughput, export quota allocation. Asset perimeter: 12 elevators + a grain terminal at Azov port — one of Russia's larger networks (note: Yuri Levitsky, BGP Capital). Structure is consolidating: quotas concentrate volume in a few state-backed shippers, and the 2026 tariff quota was doubled to 20mt to clear a record ~137mt harvest (Bloomberg/Tridge, Dec 2025) — i.e. rising domestic demand for owned throughput. Named intended acquirers per the note: Gorizont Industries (Moscow, reg. Oct 2024, undisclosed owners; principal Alexander Potseluev, ex-Russian Friends Capital) acting via Kazakh vehicle TOO Temirtek — a source alleges he acts for billionaire Iskander Makhmudov ("Prostory" holding declined comment; unconfirmed). Levitsky notes the buyer profile that ultimately fits: large grain exporters / agroholdings needing a linear elevator network and own Don-region transshipment. Gorizont's position: opportunistic distressed-asset intermediary, likely a flip vehicle ("стратегия направлена на перепродажу", per an agri-market source) — no operating moat; the moat is regulatory access (getting Commission approval) plus the physical scarcity of the elevator/port network.

**Comps & multiples.** No trading multiples — private distressed assets, no EV/EBITDA computable → no data. Deal-price anchor (from note, per Levitsky): the network "could be worth tens of billions of rubles" at fair value, but as a distressed, indebted business sold under mandatory discount the price is estimated at only RUB 1–3bn, plus debt repayment + relaunch capex — a heavy haircut consistent with the ≥60% mandatory-discount regime. Arithmetic can't be shown (no public appraised base value). Internal comps (Russia foreign-exit / forced-sale precedent in base):
- [[Citigroup to sell remaining Russian operations at $1.2bn loss]] — foreign owner books a $1.2bn loss to exit; magnitude of the exit haircut.
- [[Citi board approves sale of AO Citibank to Renaissance Capital]] — foreign→domestic buyer, discounted forced sale.
- [[Telegram's $500m Russian bonds frozen under sanctions]] — Russia-asset sanction/impairment risk.
- [[Rosatom decides to buy out Shishkarev's stake in Delo Group]] — state-linked consolidation of Russian infrastructure (same July window).
Comparison qualitative: like Citi, this is a distressed exit realized far below fair value; unlike Citi (clean regulator-approved bank sale), Gorizont still lacks Commission approval, so completion is not yet comparable.

**Risk flags.**
1. Approval risk — deal is conditional; Gorizont has yet to file with the foreign-investment Government Commission (planned ~Aug 2026). Stonebridge's Prudentov flags that opaque ownership, payment structure and a foreign (Kazakh) element could draw a Commission refusal — the Minselkhoz→Minfin referral chain adds veto points. Why: without approval the pre-agreements are void.
2. Opaque beneficiary / flip risk — undisclosed owners, alleged Makhmudov proxy, and a stated resale strategy mean the "buyer" may not be an operator; a flip raises re-approval and continuity risk and signals the assets may change hands again. Why: value accretion depends on relaunch, not resale.
3. Distressed/valuation & debt overhang — accumulated tax and wage debts (Gorizont already paid RusElCo tax arrears in mid-May 2026), idle elevators since 2024, and mandatory-discount pricing mean headline RUB 1–3bn understates true cash needs (debt + restart). Why: negative carry until throughput and export-quota access are restored.
4. Sanctions/geopolitical & concentration — single-country (Russia), sanctions-exposed sector; sold on the residue of the Western-trader exodus into a state-quota-gated export market where a few consolidators dominate. Why: quota allocation and sanctions on rails/banking can strand the asset's export utility.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) A late, low-value data point in the multi-year domestic consolidation of Russian grain infrastructure abandoned by Cargill/Viterra/LDC — the physical network survives even as the foreign brand exits. Falsifiable thesis: if the Government Commission approves (watch the ~Aug 2026 filing and any правкомиссия decision), expect the elevators/Azov terminal to be flipped to a large exporter/agroholding needing Don transshipment; if approval stalls on ownership opacity, LDC's liquidation completes and the network is broken up in bankruptcy instead. Trigger to watch: Commission decision + whether Makhmudov/"Prostory" involvement is ever confirmed.
