## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened (one line).** On 14 July 2026 Bizum launched a standalone consumer app — **Bizum Pay** — on the App Store and Google Play, letting its ~32M users tap to pay in physical stores directly from their bank account without opening (or depending on) their bank's own app. Framed by the source digest as "Bizum Breaks Free of the Bank App" (Connecting the Dots in Payments, 15 Jul 2026).

**[1] What Bizum is / the shift.** Bizum is Spain's bank-owned instant A2A payment rail (launched 2016, ~30–32M users, ~70%+ of the adult population), historically living *inside* member banks' mobile apps for P2P transfers, then e-commerce (100k+ merchants) and payments to public administrations. In-store was the missing leg. The account-to-account model settles funds to the merchant instantly, bypassing card networks — the pitch vs. Visa/Mastercard/Apple Pay is lower merchant fees and no card intermediary.

**[2] Dated timeline (distinguishing the two 2026 milestones — this is the crux for freshness).**
- 2016 — Bizum launches (P2P transfers via bank apps).
- 2025-03 — EuroPA interoperability live: Bizum + Bancomat Pay (IT) + MB WAY/SIBS (PT) → ~50M+ users cross-border P2P (see [[Bizum and European banks to enable cross-border payments in 15 countries]]).
- 2026-02 — EuroPA ↔ EPI/Wero MoU: hub linking Bizum, Bancomat, SIBS, Vipps MobilePay, EPI → ~130M users / 13 countries; P2P in 2026, e-commerce + POS in 2027.
- **2026-04/05** — **NFC in-store rollout begins (18 May)**: contactless pay-in-store via NFC, delivered *primarily through participating bank apps*, gradual bank-by-bank rollout, with a Bizum Pay wallet still ramping (see [[Bizum rolls out in-store NFC payments across Spain]] and [[Bizum to enable contactless in-store payments across Spain from May]]).
- **2026-07-14/15** — **THIS ITEM: the standalone Bizum Pay app goes live on both app stores**, decoupled from any bank app. Android: unlock + tap; iPhone: double-press side button + tap. >1M POS terminals (~60% of Spanish terminals) already accept it; merchants activate via their bank, no new hardware.

**[3] Ecosystem / competitive context.**
- **vs. cards & wallets:** direct competitor to Visa, Mastercard, Apple Pay, Google Pay at the physical POS — the largest slice of payment volume. Bizum's edge: instant A2A settlement, no card scheme fees, sovereign (Spanish bank–owned).
- **vs. Wero/EPI:** parallel "sovereign European payments" plays. Wero (EPI) is the pan-European bank wallet building merchant acceptance (see companion same-day item [[Adyen joins European Payments Initiative as principal member]] — Adyen joined EPI as principal member to bring merchant scale to Wero). Bizum and Wero are converging via the EuroPA↔EPI hub rather than competing head-on domestically. Related Wero context: [[Wero wallet reaches 44 million European users in year one]], [[Europe's local payment systems gain ground with Wero]].
- **Bizum international expansion / business rails:** [[Bizum appoints Fernando Rodriguez to lead international expansion]], [[Bizum to launch business payment platform in 2026]].

**[4] Why it matters.** Physical POS is where card schemes dominate; a standalone, card-independent, bank-owned A2A app aimed at 32M users is a direct challenge to Visa/Mastercard/Apple Pay interchange economics in Spain and a template for European "payment sovereignty" (mirroring Wero, BLIK, MB WAY). Execution risk: adoption depends on bank buy-in and merchant activation; the May NFC rollout was explicitly gradual/phased through end-2026.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Market research — Bizum standalone in-store app (Spain A2A / instant payments)**
_Stage [2] desk research, 2026-07-15. Skeptical read; no IR coverage for Bizum (private Spanish bank consortium — not in ir_coverage.json)._

### What's actually new here
Bizum (Spain's domestic instant-payment scheme, owned by a consortium of ~20+ Spanish banks) launched a **standalone consumer app** for in-store payment. This is an incremental UX step, NOT the initial in-store launch: the underlying NFC POS capability went live **18 May 2026**, initially routed through banks' own apps (CaixaBank, Sabadell, Bankinter first, phased to full-bank/merchant availability by end-2026). The standalone app matters because it removes the dependency on each bank's app — a play for a single, scheme-branded consumer entry point (the "Bizum Pay" brand) competing head-on with Apple Pay / Google Pay wallet real estate, not just card rails.

- Mechanism: NFC tap → **instant A2A transfer** account-to-merchant, bypassing Visa/Mastercard scheme + interchange. Domestic bank infrastructure, no scheme/interchange fees in the traditional sense; merchant MDR pitched materially below card (Spanish debit MDR ~0.3–0.8%, credit ~0.8–1.8%; EU IFR caps 0.2%/0.3% interchange).
- Scale: ~30M users (near-total Spanish adult banking population); ~3.4M instant transfers/day in 2025. This is a rare A2A scheme that already owns the consumer base BEFORE attacking POS — the inverse of most wallet launches.

### Sector context — European A2A / instant payments
- **The structural thesis (infrastructure sovereignty).** Bizum in-store is one node in a broader European push to displace US card rails with domestic/pan-European A2A, accelerated by SEPA Instant mandate (all euro-area PSPs must send/receive instant credit transfers, deadline Oct 2025). Bizum, Wero (EPI), BLIK, MB Way, Bancomat Pay are the national instances; PIX (Brazil) and UPI (India) are the proof-of-concept comps for A2A displacing cards at POS.
- **Two competing pan-European umbrellas, now converging.** (1) **EuroPA alliance** — Bizum + Bancomat Pay (Italy) + MB Way / SIBS (Portugal), cross-border P2P live since summer 2025 (Spain/Italy/Portugal/Andorra), Greece (IRIS) and Poland (BLIK) joining. (2) **EPI / Wero** — the German/French/Belgian bank-backed wallet, A2A live in DE/FR/BE, rolling to NL/LU, adding e-commerce 2026 and POS (QR first, NFC by 2027). Critically, EuroPA + EPI signed an **MoU (Feb 2026)** for interoperability by end-2026/early-2027 → claimed reach ~130M users / 13 countries / ~72% of EU population. Read: the "two rails" narrative is really one consolidating bloc.
- **Timing asymmetry.** Bizum is AHEAD of Wero on in-store: Bizum has NFC POS live now; Wero's POS starts QR in 2026, NFC only ~2027. In its home market Bizum's card-displacement clock is running ~12–18 months earlier than the pan-European rail.

- `News/2025-11/Bizum to launch business payment platform in 2026.md` (2025-11-26) — merchant/B2B platform ("to take on Visa and Mastercard"); the acceptance side of the same strategy.

- Displacement-precedent comps in base: `News/2025-11/Brazil's Pix processes R$15 trillion in first half of 2025.md`; UPI notes (NPCI). These show A2A-at-POS can reach scale but usually on the back of zero-MDR mandates / sub-scale bank economics.

### Risk flags
1. **Bank-consortium incentive conflict.** Bizum's owners are the same banks that earn card interchange/issuing economics. Cannibalising their own high-margin card revenue for a low-MDR A2A rail is a structural disincentive — historically the biggest brake on European A2A-at-POS. Watch whether banks actively push Bizum Pay or quietly keep it default-off.
2. **Merchant monetization vs. free-P2P legacy.** Consumers know Bizum as *free* P2P. Merchant MDR must be low to win vs. cards yet high enough to fund the scheme once bank subsidy tapers — a narrow band. Sustainable unit economics unproven at POS scale.
3. **Wallet UX / NFC access.** iOS NFC access historically gated by Apple; a standalone tap-to-pay app depends on host-card-emulation availability and OS defaults. Any Apple/Google friction blunts the "own the consumer entry point" thesis (the whole reason for a standalone app).
4. **Fragmentation / interop execution risk.** The EuroPA↔EPI interoperability (end-2026/2027) and EuroPA POS (2027) are roadmap, not shipped. Cross-border and pan-EU acceptance remain promises; today's utility is domestic-Spain only.
5. **Adoption ≠ usage.** 30M P2P users is the asset, but tap-to-pay habit vs. entrenched contactless cards / Apple Pay is a behavior-change problem. Card MDR displacement (bulls cite 25–35% of Spanish in-store volume over 2–3 yrs) is an analyst projection, not a run-rate — treat skeptically.
6. **Regulatory/data.** Bizum now reports P2P data to Bank of Spain; heavier scheme-level scrutiny (AML, systemic-payments oversight, potential interchange-style regulation of A2A) rises as volume shifts to POS.
