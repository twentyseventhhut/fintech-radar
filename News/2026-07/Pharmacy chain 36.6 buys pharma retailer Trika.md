 Сеть «36,6» выкупила фармритейлера «Трика» 
Компания «36,6» выкупила аптечную сеть «Трика», рассказали “ Коммерсанту ” в самой группе. В периметр сделки вошли 45 аптек и четыре юридических лица — ООО «Трика», «Вега», «Фармтун-6» и «Фармацевтическая гильдия». Сумму и другие условия сделки в компании не привели. Консультант по M&A Иван Пешков оценивает стоимость четырех компаний в 540 млн руб. с учетом стоимости долга. Оценка гендиректора DSM Group Сергея Шуляка — 700 млн руб.
Фармритейлер «36,6» основан в 1991 году Сергеем Кривошеевым и Артемом Бектемировым. Сейчас это одна из крупнейших в России аптечных сетей. В первом квартале 2026 года она заняла пятое место по обороту, следует из данных DSM Group. Он составил 31,3 млрд руб., увеличившись год к году на 7%. Компания сформировала 5% продаж в коммерческом сегменте. В общей сложности на конец первого квартала в группу «36,6» входили 2,8 тыс. аптек в 15 регионах России, работающих под брендами «36,6», «Горздрав» и «ЛекОптТорг».
Аптечная сеть «Трика» существует с 2002 года и позиционируется как сеть низких цен. Владельцами бизнеса, согласно СПАРК, выступают Тигран Шахбазян, Татьяна Петрова, Рубен Асатурян и Карапет Зананян. Бизнесом они владеют фактически в равных долях. Совокупная выручка четырех компаний, вошедших в контур сделки «36,6», в 2025 году составила 1,8 млрд руб., подсчитал господин Пешков. В рейтинге DSM Group по итогам первого квартала «Трика» на 45-м месте с оборотом в 0,8 млрд руб.
В «36,6» поясняют, что приобретение сети «Трика» позволит компании усилить свое присутствие в Москве, Подмосковье и Ярославской области, а также выйти в новый для себя регион — Орловскую область. Расширение географии за счет сделок слияния и поглощения — часть стратегии группы, добавили там. Сергей Шуляк предполагает, что аптеки «Трика» в перспективе будут переведены под бренд «36,6» или «Горздрав», что позволит группе укрепить позиции в регионе. Компания в этом году может договориться о приобретении и других сетей, не исключает он.

# Context-enrichment: Pharmacy chain 36.6 buys pharma retailer Trika
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS/DUPLICATE VERDICT: FRESH.** No prior corpus note covers this specific deal (36.6 / Trika). The adjacent RU-pharmacy notes are *different deals*: online-pharmacy roll-ups by Wildberries/RVB ([[Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka]], [[RWB buys majority stake in Eapteka pharmacy service]]) and Neofarm/Stolichki's Astrakhan tuck-in ([[Neofarm acquires Astrakhan pharmacy chain Na Zdorovye]]). This is a distinct *offline* bolt-on by a different buyer (36.6). 1 mention / 1 source in the corpus — a low-salience item. It fits the broader consolidation thesis already tracked in [[Sber forecasts surge in Russian M&A activity this autumn]].

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
De-PR'd: **ГК «Аптечная сеть 36,6» (PAO, MOEX: APTK)** — Russia's **#5** pharmacy retailer by turnover (DSM Q1-2026: RUB 31.3bn, +7% YoY, 5.02% commercial-segment share, ~2,803 pharmacies across 15 regions under «36,6» / «Горздрав» / «ЛекОптТорг») — has **acquired «Трика»**, a low-price chain founded 2002. **Perimeter: 45 pharmacies + 4 legal entities** (ООО «Трика», «Вега», «Фармтун-6», «Фармацевтическая гильдия»).
- **Price NOT disclosed by the parties.** Three third-party *estimates*, all rough: Ivan Peshkov (M&A consultant) **~540m RUB "с учётом стоимости долга" (incl. debt)**; Sergei Shulyak (DSM Group CEO) **~700m RUB**; and — not in the source note — Nikolai Bespalov (RNC Pharma) **480–550m RUB** (Vademecum). **De-PR'd read: ≈0.5–0.7bn RUB (~$6–9M).** No transacted figure exists; every multiple is indicative.
- **Why "incl. debt" matters:** Peshkov's tag implies **Trika carries debt** — so the numbers are closer to an *enterprise value* (equity + assumed liabilities) than a cash equity price. Debt quantum is **undisclosed/open**. Combined with Trika's turnover trend (see below), this reads as a **motivated seller of a fading, debt-laden sub-scale asset**, not a prized target.
- **Data conflict (flag):** source note says 4-entity combined **2025 revenue ~1.8bn RUB** and Trika **#45 in DSM Q1-2026** (~0.8bn RUB quarterly turnover, and per DSM that Q1 line was **−4% YoY**). RNC Pharma (via Vademecum) instead cites Trika **goods turnover 2.1bn RUB, #70** nationally, **#13 in Moscow (0.72% city share)**. Reconcilable (revenue vs goods-turnover base; different rankers), but rankings differ. Honest range: **~1.8–2.1bn RUB 2025, sub-1% national share, declining**.
- **Rationale (per 36.6):** strengthen Moscow + Moscow region + **Yaroslavl region**, and **enter a new region — Oryol (Орловская обл.)**; M&A-driven geographic expansion is "part of the group's strategy." Acquired stores to be rebranded **«36,6» or «Горздрав»** — but explicitly "**в перспективе**" (in future), no timeline/capex committed.

## [1] Competitors / peers
RU pharmacy retail is a large, fast-consolidating market: commercial drug segment **~1.85 trn RUB 2025 (+13%)**; whole pharmacy segment incl. parapharma ~2.3 trn (+14%) (DSM Group). **Concentration ~74–75%** (top-20/top-50), and **M&A deal count nearly doubled 91 (2024) → 189 (2025)** (DSM Group). DSM Q1-2026 leaders: **Rigla (Protek)** #1 (73.4bn, 11.77%, 7,148 pts) — the most aggressive vertically-integrated roll-up; **Апрель** #2 (60.5bn, **−4%**, 10,370 pts — most points in RU); **Планета Здоровья** #3 (49.0bn); then **«36,6» #5** (31.3bn, 5.02%); Neofarm/Stolichki #5–6. Online is a separate axis: **Apteka.ru (Katren)** #1, **Zdravsity** (Protek), **Eapteka** (bought by Wildberries/RVB, July 2026 — see internal notes).
- **Why the lay of the land / second-order:** per-pharmacy barriers are low (Roszdravnadzor licensing), but real scale barriers sit in **procurement leverage + logistics + private label** → the sector favours roll-ups, so leaders buy cheap sub-scale regional chains at **~0.2–0.4x sales** to add density/geography. The genuinely material 2026 action is **one tier above this deal**: **«Апрель» (#2) is reportedly for sale** (Izvestia/Vedomosti, Jun 2026; rumoured ultimate beneficiary Sergei Galitsky of Magnit; shopped ~4 years unsuccessfully, WB/Russ *not* interested), and **Eapteka just went to Wildberries**. 36.6 buying a **45-store minnow** while the #2 chain is on the block signals it is a **mid-pack consolidator of minnows, not a bidder for the crown** (analysis) — consistent with limited firepower.

## [2] Company history / fit
36.6 (founded **1991** by Sergei Krivosheev & Artem Bektemirov; first pharmacy 1997; **IPO on MMVB 2003**, ticker APTK) is a **historical serial acquirer** — by 2006 it had bought **18 chains / 352 pharmacies**; the A.V.E merger (2015) brought the «Горздрав» brand; «ЛекОптТорг» added later. But it then went through **near-death by debt**: **negative equity of −13.8bn RUB by 2017**, total debt ~30bn (mostly to МКБ / Московский кредитный банк), founders exited, and **МКБ became a shareholder (June 2019)** as the final restructuring stage; Walgreens Boots Alliance (15% in 2016) has since exited.
- **Current condition (much healthier than the legacy narrative):** Group 2025 IFRS revenue **117.4bn RUB (+21%)**, **EBITDA 8.5bn (+27%, 8.1% margin)**, LFL +7.9%, ~2,753 pharmacies (+8%). **Deleveraging is the key number: Net Debt/EBITDA 3.16x (2021) → 1.61 (2022) → ~1.3x (2023–Q2 2025)** — moderate, not distressed. PAO-level RSBU 2025 net profit is thin (43.5m RUB, −62%) but that is the holding shell, not the group; group IFRS net profit not disclosed (open).
- **Live overhang — Jan-2026 asset arrest:** Moscow Arbitration froze **RUB 3.675bn** across 4 operating structures + two ex-co-owners (Kintsurashvili, Saganelidze); plaintiff «Агро Строй+», claim tracing to legacy **Alliance Healthcare Russia** debts (36.6 bought it in 2016, it went bankrupt). Company says operations unaffected; risk is **reputational > financial**.
- **Ownership is murky/fragmenting (flag):** no single disclosed controller — cited blocks include **Altus Capital ~30%**, **МКБ ~17%**, plus 2025–26 stake-building by **Sergei Vorobyev (~15%)** and **Sergei Levin / АО «Тренд» (~16.6%)**. Whether this is consolidation of control or pre-exit positioning is **open**.
- **Fit:** Trika is a **return to the acquisition playbook** now that leverage is back to ~1.3x — a small tuck-in that adds Moscow density + Yaroslavl reinforcement + one new region (Oryol). Structurally logical, but tiny.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Not novel.** 36.6 is a historical serial acquirer resuming bolt-on M&A after years of debt repair. Trika is a **~45-store tuck-in vs ~2,803 (≈2% of point count; sub-1% of national turnover)**. Traction of the thesis is modest and confirmed only by the *announced* purchase — rebranding is "in perspective," unfunded and untimelined.
- **Why the value-add is thin / who captures margin:** drug retail is low-margin and price-regulated; the value here is **footprint/density + a first-time entry into Oryol**, not earnings. The **sellers (Trika's four ~equal owners: Shakhbazyan, Petrova, Asaturyan, Zananyan)** likely capture the cleaner value — a well-timed exit of a **declining (−4% Q1), debt-laden, sub-scale low-price chain** squeezed by discounters like «Апрель». For 36.6, absorbing a sub-scale low-price chain risks near-term margin dilution until rebranding/procurement synergies land. The "incl. debt" pricing suggests 36.6 mostly **assumed liabilities** rather than paying an equity premium — the shape of an opportunistic, not competitive, buy.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** rebrand of 45 stores to «36,6»/«Горздрав»; Oryol becomes the 16th region; possibly further small tuck-ins (Shulyak: 36.6 "may agree other chains this year"). With leverage ~1.3x, 36.6 has firepower for *small* deals only — **not** for a top-tier target like «Апрель».
- **Sentiment:** neutral-to-mildly-positive operationally (deleveraged, +21% revenue), shadowed by the **Jan-2026 RUB 3.7bn arrest** and opaque, fragmenting ownership. The Trika deal itself is **immaterial to sentiment** — a rounding error on a 31bn/quarter chain.
- **Why the market goes this way / second-order:** concentration climbing toward ~75% + the 91→189 deal-count jump (2025) mean cheap regional bolt-ons at ~0.2–0.4x sales are the leaders' default mode; Sber forecasts a further M&A surge in autumn 2026 as distressed, over-levered assets change hands ([[Sber forecasts surge in Russian M&A activity this autumn]]). **Counterintuitive:** the truly structural RU-pharma story in 2026 is the **marketplace land-grab** (Wildberries/RVB–Eapteka) and the **top-of-market «Апрель» sale**, not offline minnow tuck-ins like this — this deal matters mainly as one more data point on the consolidation trend. **Falsifiable trigger:** if disclosure later shows 36.6 paid materially above ~0.5x sales, the "opportunistic/attractive-price" read breaks and it looks defensive.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian retail drug market ≈₽1.89tn in 2025, +16% YoY (per DSM Group), with 2026 growth guided to ~7% toward ₽3.1tn for the broader drug market; online sub-channel is the fastest-growing leg (+28% YoY value in 2025 per AlphaRM; e-pharmacy was ₽283bn / ~14% of the market in 2024). Structure is **consolidating but still fragmented**: the top-10 chains held 56.9% of sales end-2025, and the top-20 share rose from ~60% (Q3'19) to 67.4% (Q3'24) — leaving a long tail of small regional networks (Trika ranked only 45th, ₽0.8bn Q1 turnover) that are prime roll-up fodder. "Why now": squeezed regional independents facing a high tax burden plus marketplace/e-pharmacy competition are choosing to sell; Sber's own analysts flag an expected **autumn-2026 surge in distressed M&A** as over-levered assets change hands (see [[Sber forecasts surge in Russian M&A activity this autumn]]). Consolidation compounds chains' buyer power over manufacturers (larger marketing-contract payments), a structural margin tailwind for scaled players.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: turnover/market share, store count, average check, LFL sales, online mix. Key players by 2025 sales: Rigla (Protek — vertically integrated with wholesale), April, Planeta Zdorovya (itself a 2017-era alliance of Planeta Zdorovya + Farmakor + Maksavit), Erkapharm/Neo-Pharm, Vita, and 36.6 (brands 36.6 / Gorzdrav / LekOptTorg). Basis of competition: price + density/location + increasingly online fulfillment; Apteka.ru (~32% of the e-channel) and marketplaces (Ozon, and Wildberries/RWB via its Eapteka buy) attack the higher-margin OTC/parapharma/dietary-supplement categories. Recent peer moves: RWB (Wildberries) acquired online pharmacy Eapteka (see [[RWB buys majority stake in Eapteka pharmacy service]], [[Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka]]); Neofarm rolled up Astrakhan chain Na Zdorovye ([[Neofarm acquires Astrakhan pharmacy chain Na Zdorovye]]) — i.e. sector-wide roll-up is the live pattern, not a one-off. **36.6's position:** 5th by Q1'26 turnover (₽31.3bn, +7% YoY, ~5% of the commercial segment; 2,753 pharmacies in 15 regions). M&A-led geographic expansion is explicit strategy (Trika adds Moscow/Podmoskovye/Yaroslavl density + entry into a new region, Oryol). Moat = scale + supplier bargaining power + multi-brand format coverage (analysis) — real but not dominant; it trails Rigla and is not the online leader.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Balance-sheet fragility at the acquirer.** 36.6 has a documented history of heavy leverage (share issuance to repay debt; Pharm-Logistic liquidated in early 2025 to resolve group debt obligations). Current net-debt/EBITDA is [UNSOURCED], but an M&A-led expansion strategy funded on top of an already-levered base is the core risk — second-order: rising rates or a demand slowdown could force distressed asset sales rather than more buys.
2. **Margin disintermediation by e-pharmacy/marketplaces.** Online (+28% value in 2025) is siphoning the highest-margin parapharma/OTC/supplement categories out of physical pharmacies; with Wildberries (Eapteka) and Ozon scaling drug delivery, 36.6's 2,753-store physical footprint risks becoming a cost center defending a shrinking-margin core. 36.6's own online is growing (+38.9%) but off a small base vs Apteka.ru's ~32% channel share.
3. **Integration / rebranding execution.** Trika is a *low-price* brand across four separate legal entities; folding it under 36.6/Gorzdrav (Shulyak's expectation) risks either losing Trika's price-sensitive traffic or diluting the premium banner — plus multi-entity M&A carries hidden-liability/debt risk (the ₽540m figure is explicitly "incl. debt").

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) Not a re-rating — it's another tick in the **grind of Russian pharmacy-retail consolidation**: distressed/sub-scale independents selling into federal chains, exactly what Sber flags for autumn 2026. Falsifiable thesis: 36.6 signs ≥1 more chain acquisition in 2026 (management itself did not rule it out; Shulyak expects further deals). Trigger to watch: the next distressed regional-chain sale and whether federal chains fund it with cash or fresh debt — if buyers lever up further into a demand slowdown, the roll-up shifts from offensive land-grab to defensive scale-or-die, and margins (not store count) become the real scoreboard.
