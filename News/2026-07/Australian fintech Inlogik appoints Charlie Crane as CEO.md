## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** Australian fintech **Inlogik** (Sydney; commercial-card management + expense/spend-management SaaS sold largely white-label to banks) named **Charlie (Charles) Crane** as **CEO, effective 1 July 2026**. Founder **Richard Eskell** — who led the company for 20–30+ years (sources vary; Inlogik founded 1993) — moves to **Founder & Board Director**. Crane is an internal promotion who "played a significant role in shaping Inlogik's long-term strategy" and brings a commercial-banking / payments / fintech background. (Primary: [inlogik.com press release](https://inlogik.com/inlogik-appoints-charles-crane-as-ceo-to-lead-its-next-phase-of-growth/); coverage [Australian FinTech](https://australianfintech.com.au/australian-fintech-inlogik-appoints-charlie-crane-as-ceo-to-lead-its-next-phase-of-growth/).)

**[1] Company.** Inlogik has built commercial-card / expenditure-management software since 1993. HQ Sydney; offices Melbourne, New York, London, New Zealand; ~51–200 staff (Tracxn/LinkedIn — unverified). Products: **Card Management Platform (CMP)**, **Card Reporting Platform (CRP)**, **Expense Management System (EMS)** / **ExpenseMe (ProMaster** legacy brand). PCI-DSS compliant, ISO 27001 certified. Sells **white-label to banks, issuers, enterprises and governments** — an infrastructure/B2B2B model, distinct from direct-to-SMB spend cards. Customers historically include banks and government/local-government bodies (FinPro case study). Funding/revenue: not disclosed; appears bootstrapped/founder-run, not VC-backed.

**[2] Strategy signal.** Framed around a thesis (PR-newswire thought-leadership, [prnewswire APAC](https://www.prnewswire.com/apac/news-releases/banks-risk-losing-the-commercial-spend-relationship-as-finance-moves-beyond-traditional-banking-302814283.html)): *"The real risk for banks is not losing the payment. It is losing the customer relationship around how commercial finance is managed, approved, controlled and understood."* Inlogik is repositioning its **Spend Platform** into a "connected commercial finance ecosystem" — payments, cards, policy controls, approvals, **AI-assisted decisioning**, financial insights — and pitching banks that embedded fintechs (Ramp/Brex/Airwallex-style) are capturing the workflow/experience layer while banks keep only the rails. AI framed as friction-reduction/compliance, not headcount replacement.

**[3] Why it matters (modest).** A CEO succession at a ~30-year, founder-run, non-VC Australian B2B SaaS vendor. Materiality is **low**: no funding, no financials, no M&A, no product launch, no customer numbers. Signal value = (a) generational handover with founder staying on the board (continuity, low disruption risk), and (b) confirms the strategic pivot to bank-enablement against spend-management challengers in APAC. Not market-moving; a "who's-who / sector-positioning" item.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
### Sector: commercial-card & spend management (APAC focus)

**Where Inlogik sits.** Inlogik is a **bank-facing infrastructure vendor** (white-label CMP/EMS), not a direct-to-SMB spend-management brand. Its buyers are banks/issuers/enterprises/governments who then serve their own commercial-card customers. This is a different economic model from the VC-funded challengers below — stickier, slower, but structurally exposed to disintermediation if its bank clients lose their commercial customers to fintechs.

**APAC / Australian competitive landscape.**
- **Weel (ex-DiviPay)** — Sydney, founded 2016; first virtual corporate cards in Australia (2018). Prepaid corporate cards + expense mgmt + AP + reimbursements; SMB-direct. Basic plan ~A$135/mo. (Airwallex AU comparison, Finder, Cape.)
- **Airwallex** — Melbourne-origin, ~$12B valuation ([[Airwallex raises new funding at $12 billion valuation]]); full financial-operations stack: spend management + multi-currency Global Accounts + cross-border payments + acceptance. The most credible platform threat Crane's thesis points at. Grow bundle ~A$99/mo.
- **ProSpend** (ex-expensemanager) — Sydney spend-mgmt platform, acquired by UK's ISH in Nov 2025 ([[ISH acquires Australian platform ProSpend]]); 1,000+ customers (Nike, KFC, Monash College). Direct Aussie comp now consolidating under a UK SaaS roll-up with UK/IE launch from 2026.
- **Cape**, **ApprovalMax**, **Expensify** — additional AU/NZ SME spend/approval tools.

**Global archetypes Crane references (implicitly).**
- **Ramp** — US spend-management leader, ~$32B valuation, $300M raise ([[Ramp raises $300M at $32B valuation]]); the "own the CFO workflow" playbook.
- **Brex**, **SAP Concur** (incumbent T&E), **Pleo** (EU; recently cut ~100 jobs — [[Spend management startup Pleo cuts up to 100 jobs]]), **Emburse/Amex** ([[American Express and Emburse expand expense automation]]), **Extend** (bank-issued virtual-card enablement — [[Extend and PNC launch virtual card platform for commercial clients]]).

**Sector dynamics.** (1) Commercial-card/expense is consolidating (ISH→ProSpend, Amex→Emburse partnerships) and margins are under pressure (Pleo layoffs). (2) The strategic battle has moved from the **payment rail** to the **finance workflow/experience layer** — approvals, policy, controls, AI decisioning — which is exactly Inlogik's pitch to banks. (3) AI-assisted decisioning is now table-stakes messaging across the category. (4) Banks' defensive posture (keep the commercial relationship) is a real tailwind for bank-enablement vendors like Inlogik vs. going direct.

### Risk flags (4)

1. **Un-quantified / promotional claims.** Everything sourced traces to Inlogik's own press release + a paid PR-newswire thought-leadership piece. **No** revenue, customer count, ARR, funding, or growth data disclosed; strategy claims are unverified and self-serving. Headcount (~51–200) and office list are third-party estimates (Tracxn/LinkedIn), not confirmed. Even the founder's tenure is inconsistent across sources ("20+" vs "30+" years).
2. **Disintermediation / structural exposure.** Inlogik's fortunes depend on banks retaining commercial-spend customers. If VC-funded challengers (Airwallex, Weel, Ramp) win the SMB/mid-market relationship directly, Inlogik's white-label demand shrinks regardless of product quality — its thesis is also its existential risk.
3. **Consolidation & scale gap.** Peers are being rolled up and capitalised (ISH/ProSpend; Airwallex $12B). A bootstrapped/founder-run ~30-year vendor risks being out-invested on AI and out-distributed on go-to-market. No evidence of fresh capital to fund the "connected ecosystem" ambition.
4. **Low newsworthiness / signal risk.** An internal CEO promotion with founder staying on the board is continuity, not transformation; easy to over-read as strategic momentum when it is a governance formality plus a marketing repositioning.

**Comps to watch:** Airwallex, Weel, ProSpend/ISH, Ramp, Pleo, SAP Concur, Extend.
