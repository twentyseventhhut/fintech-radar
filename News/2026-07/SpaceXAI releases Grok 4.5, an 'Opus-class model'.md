SpaceXAI releases Grok 4.5, which Elon describes as an ‘Opus-class model’
SpaceXAI has released its latest model, Grok 4.5 — the first since the company went public several weeks ago.
In a blog post published Wednesday, SpaceXAI characterized its new release as a workhorse that can tackle all of the typical tasks that the AI industry has sought to automate: coding and app-building, office and clerical work, research, writing, and other forms of routine knowledge work.
Grok can supposedly do all this for less spend, too, as SpaceXAI says that its model has “twice greater token efficiency” than other leading models. If it carries through to real-world use cases, that efficiency would be a big advantage for SpaceXAI, since the cost of tokens has been a growing concern for AI consumers.
The company released benchmark metrics Wednesday that appeared to show Grok’s competitiveness with other top models from SpaceXAI competitors, although just short of best-in-class:
In a post on his social media platform X (which is a subsidiary of SpaceXAI), founder Elon Musk compared the model to Opus, Anthropic’s LLM designed for intensive and complex tasks.
“Based on strong positive feedback from customers in our beta test program, @SpaceXAI will make Grok 4.5 available to the public tomorrow. It is an Opus-class model, but faster, more token-efficient and lower cost,” wrote Musk in his post on X.
 Musk later added : “Our internal assessment is that Grok 4.5 is roughly comparable to Opus 4.7, but much faster. The combination of capability, faster speed and lower cost is what makes it competitive.”
SpaceXAI says that its new model costs $2 per million input tokens and $6 per million output tokens. That’s quite competitive, if Grok’s capabilities match SpaceXAI’s rhetoric.
 Opus 4.7 , by comparison, costs $5 per million input tokens and $25 per million output tokens. OpenAI has tiered costs for different model versions: Sol, its most expensive, costs $5 for 1 million input tokens and $30 for 1 million output tokens, while its least expensive, Luna, costs $1 for 1 million input and $6 for 1 million output tokens.
It’s a big week for AI model releases. OpenAI is planning to release GPT 5.6, its latest, most powerful model, on Thursday. The release of that model had previously been limited by the Trump administration, due to concerns about its security implications. OpenAI has called it its “strongest model yet.”

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
SpaceXAI (the merged SpaceX + xAI entity; xAI absorbed Feb 2026 at a ~$250bn mark inside a ~$1.25tn combined entity) publicly released **Grok 4.5** on 2026-07-08, opening to the public 2026-07-09 — its first flagship since going public "several weeks ago." De-PR'd:
- **The claim is a *tier* claim, not a *beats-Opus* claim.** Musk: "an Opus-class model, but faster, more token-efficient and lower cost," with an internal assessment of "roughly comparable to **Opus 4.7**" — pointedly the *previous-gen* Anthropic Opus, not current Opus 4.8. TechCrunch's own read: benchmarks "appeared to show competitiveness… although just short of best-in-class." → Why framed this way: "Opus-class" borrows Anthropic's quality halo while the honest positioning is "last-gen frontier quality at a fraction of the cost." Independent indices put Grok 4.5 ≈54 on an Intelligence Index — level with Opus 4.7 (54), *below* Opus 4.8 (56) — confirming the tier claim but undercutting any "beats Opus" spin. On xAI's *own* released chart it wins DeepSWE 1.0 / Terminal-Bench 2.1 but **loses** DeepSWE 1.1 / SWE-Bench Pro to Opus 4.8 — a curated, mixed picture.
- **The real, verifiable edge is cost + token efficiency, not raw capability.** Priced **$2/M input, $6/M output** vs Opus 4.7's $5/$25 (and Opus 4.8's $5/$25). The "twice greater token efficiency" claim is directionally backed: on SWE-Bench Pro, Grok 4.5 uses ~15,954 output tokens/task vs Opus 4.8's ~67,020 (a 4.2x gap, richer than the 2x headline); ~14k output tokens per Intelligence-Index task, >60% fewer than Opus 4.8. → Why it matters: for token-bound coding workloads the *effective* cost gap (per-token price × tokens consumed) is far larger than the 2.5x sticker gap — this is the load-bearing selling point, and it is real if the efficiency holds in production (vendor benchmark — treat as claim, not audited).
- **First model trained on Cursor data.** Grok 4.5 runs a new "V9" foundation (reportedly ~1.5T params, unverified) and is the first flagship trained using data from **Cursor**, which SpaceX is acquiring ([[SpaceX to acquire Cursor for $60 billion in stock]]). It is the default in Grok Build. → Why: vertical integration of a leading coding tool's interaction data directly into model training — the same "own the data flywheel" logic as [[Base44 launches own AI model to seek defensibility]], but at frontier-lab scale.
- **Timing is competitive theater.** Released one day before OpenAI's GPT-5.6 (2026-07-09), whose flagship "Sol" prices at $5/$30. → The launch window is deliberately jammed against GPT-5.6 to own the "cheaper frontier" narrative before OpenAI's news cycle.

- **Anthropic Opus 4.7 / 4.8** — the explicit reference point. Opus 4.8 ($5/$25) is Anthropic's current, most-capable Opus; Opus 4.7 ($5/$25) the prior gen. Grok 4.5 targets 4.7-parity at ~40% of the input price / ~24% of the output price. Anthropic's own frontier tier (Fable 5, $10/$50) sits above on Claude-Code coding indices. *(Pricing verified against Anthropic's model catalog: Opus 4.7/4.8 = $5 in / $25 out; Fable 5 = $10/$50; Sonnet 5 = $3/$15 intro $2/$10.)*
- **OpenAI GPT-5.6** (2026-07-09) — three-variant family (Sol flagship $5/$30; Luna low-end $1/$6). Grok's $2/$6 slots *between* Luna and Sol on price while claiming Opus-4.7-class quality. GPT-5.6 had been gated by the Trump administration on security grounds (per TechCrunch) — a regulatory backdrop absent for Grok.
- **Google Gemini** — third pillar; Grok is #3 US chatbot by share (17.8% Jan 2026, up from 1.9% Jan 2025, per Reuters) behind ChatGPT and Gemini.
- **Position:** Grok 4.5 is **catching up on the frontier** (parity with *last-gen* Opus, below current Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.6 Sol) but **leading on price/efficiency** in its tier. → Why the landscape is this way (central-question shift): the frontier is bifurcating into "max-capability at premium price" (Opus 4.8, Fable 5, GPT-5.6 Sol) vs "good-enough frontier at commodity price" (Grok 4.5, GPT-5.6 Luna, DeepSeek). Grok's bet is that for high-volume coding, **cost-per-completed-task**, not peak benchmark, decides adoption — the same thesis the-decoder frames as "benchmark gaps may not matter much."

_Red-team / challenge questions — second-order, each answered or "open." Skeptical of vendor benchmark claims._

1. **Is "Opus-class" true, and which Opus?** Partly. Musk's own internal assessment pins it at "roughly comparable to **Opus 4.7**" (last-gen), not current **Opus 4.8**. All **vendor-reported** — not independently audited. Risk: efficiency may be benchmark-tuned (SWE-Bench-Pro-specific) and not generalize to production. **Open on real-world workloads.**

4. **Is the effective cost advantage as big as claimed?** Potentially larger than sticker: effective cost = price × tokens. $2/$6 vs Opus's $5/$25, times ~2–4x fewer tokens → effective coding-task cost could be 5–10x cheaper *if* both price and efficiency hold. But this compounds two vendor claims; verify with one independent production benchmark before believing it.

5. **Live/adopted or announced?** Live to public 2026-07-09, default in Grok Build. But **zero independent adoption metrics** — "strong positive feedback from beta customers" is unquantified. Capability, not proven usage.

6. **Anthropic Opus pricing in the article — accurate?** Yes. Verified: Opus 4.7 = $5/M in, $25/M out; Opus 4.8 same; OpenAI Sol $5/$30, Luna $1/$6 — all consistent with the note text. No pricing error to flag.

10. **Is the launch timing meaningful?** Yes — one day before GPT-5.6. Deliberate jamming to own the "cheaper frontier" narrative in OpenAI's news window. Marketing choreography > technical event.

11. **What's the Cursor-training dependency risk?** The coding-data edge rests on closing the [[SpaceX to acquire Cursor for $60 billion in stock]] deal and retaining that data pipeline. If the acquisition slips or Cursor talent leaves, the differentiator weakens. **Open.**

12. **Does the ~1.5T-param / "V9" architecture claim matter?** Unverified and largely irrelevant to the value case — the selling point is cost/efficiency, not scale. Treat param count as marketing color, not a validated spec.

13. **Fintech read-through — durable or hype?** Real but indirect: cheaper frontier coding models pressure inference COGS for every LLM-building fintech ([[Fintechs replace SaaS vendors by building with AI]]) and strengthen Grok's position in the broker-assistant/agentic-trading wave ([[Interactive Brokers connects Grok, Claude and ChatGPT to live trading]]).
