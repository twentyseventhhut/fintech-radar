 Банк России проведет оценку НСПК перед возможной продажей доли 
2 июля, выступая на Финансовом конгрессе Банка России, глава регулятора Эльвира Набиуллина заявила об обсуждении с банками частичной приватизации Национальной системы платежных карт (оператор карт «Мир»). Планируется провести рыночную оценку компании, для того чтобы «потенциальные участники тоже понимали, сколько может стоить пакет этих акций, как они смогут принять участие в возможном инвестировании в НСПК», отметила она. Об этом пишет " Коммерсант ".
По словам госпожи Набиуллиной, существует законодательное требование, которое разрешает продать пакет акций в рынок, но так, чтобы ЦБ сохранил более 50% плюс 1 акцию и одновременно передал не более 5% в одни руки сторонним инвесторам.
«Это инфраструктурная компания, и важно, если частные инвесторы соучаствуют в ней, чтобы не было концентрации этих пакетов»,— пояснила глава ЦБ.
Осенью прошлого года Банк России опубликовал консультационный доклад, в котором сообщил об оценке возможности и механизма передачи ряда сервисов НСПК в управление частного сектора. Среди возможных сценариев рассматривалась продажа миноритарного пакета или 100% отдельных сервисов, например платежной системы «Мир» или системы быстрых платежей (СБП).
Ряд банков уже выразил желание поучаствовать в приватизации НСПК. На Финконгрессе глава РСХБ Борис Листов заявил, что войти в ее капитал могут около 20 новых участников и Россельхозбанк готов стать одним из них. «Многие считают, что так НСПК привлекает дополнительный капитал для своего развития, но надо отметить, что сейчас компании хватает прибыли, чтобы развиваться самостоятельно»,— отметил он. В Совкомбанке сообщили, что также готовы участвовать в приватизации НСПК, а в Т-Банке отметили, что ждут конкретики и дальнейшей информации.
Уставный капитал НСПК составляет 4,3 млрд руб. и разделен на 43 тыс. акций. При этом последний раз компания публиковала отчетность по РСБУ лишь по итогам 2023 года. Тогда выручка НСПК выросла в 1,4 раза и составила 71,9 млрд руб. Чистая прибыль также выросла в 1,4 раза, до 35,1 млрд руб.
Эксперты считают, что компания может быть интересна инвесторам не только из банковской сферы. Выручка НСПК формируется от оказания услуг платежной системы «Мир» и системы быстрых переводов. Объем карточных платежей в 2025 году составил 154,3 трлн руб., это на 8% ниже, чем в 2023 году, однако доля карт «Мир» в этом сегменте значительно увеличилась. По данным НСПК, в первом квартале 2026 года она превысила 75%, тогда как три года назад была 53%. Более того, в 2025 году объем операций через СБП достиг 103 трлн руб., в 10 раз превысив показатель 2023 года. Такие показатели сказываются на финансовых результатах НСПК. Исходя из отчетности ЦБ, за 2022–2025 годы компания выплатила регулятору в виде дивидендов более 80 млрд руб., причем в 2025 году — почти 47 млрд руб.
Как считает глава правления ассоциации «Финансовые инновации» Роман Прохоров, НСПК — понятный доходоприносящий актив и спрос на его приобретение со стороны как кредитных организаций, так и других экономических субъектов будет неплохим. При этом, про его мнению, «рыночную оценку актива можно осуществлять различными способами, в данном случае вполне нормально использовать формулу нескольких EBITDA, конкретное количество и является предметом оценки».
Интерес к инфраструктурной компании могут проявить не только отечественные, но и зарубежные инвесторы из дружественных стран. «Это может придать новый импульс развитию компании за счет привнесения новых идей, а также новых возможностей, например, в сфере трансграничных расчетов»,— считает Роман Прохоров. Впрочем, по словам Эльвиры Набиуллиной, приватизация НСПК вряд ли завершится до конца этого года.

# Context-enrichment: Bank of Russia to value NSPK ahead of possible stake sale
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **2 July 2026**, speaking at the Bank of Russia's Financial Congress, CBR governor **Elvira Nabiullina** put the NSPK partial-privatization idea **on the record for the first time** and named a concrete next step: the CBR will run a **formal market valuation of NSPK, which Interfax reports it expects to complete by end-2026**, so that "potential participants understand how much the share package might be worth and how they could take part in investing in NSPK" (per Kommersant/Interfax, via web). Partial privatization would then follow — **most likely in 2027, not 2026** ("privatization is unlikely to be completed by year-end," Nabiullina). NSPK is the 100%-CBR-owned operator of the **Mir** card scheme, the **SBP/СБП** instant-payments rail and the universal QR.

The legal frame Nabiullina restated: a stake may be sold into the market **only if the CBR keeps >50%+1 share** and **no more than 5% goes into any single pair of hands** ("this is infrastructure; if private investors participate, there must be no concentration of these packages"). NSPK's charter capital is **₽4.3bn split into 43,000 shares**. Last published RAS accounts (**FY2023**): revenue **₽71.9bn** (+1.4×), net profit **₽35.1bn** (+1.4×); FY2024–25 statements **not published**. Scale disclosed at the congress: **2025 card-payment volume ₽154.3trn** (−8% vs 2023 in value, but Mir's share of that segment rose to **>75% in Q1 2026** from 53% three years earlier); **SBP volume ₽103trn in 2025** (10× the 2023 figure). Per CBR reporting, NSPK paid the regulator **>₽80bn in dividends over 2022–2025**, of which **~₽47bn in 2025 alone**.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the *new* substance versus the June anonymous-source balloon is a **process commitment** — a governor-on-record valuation with a deadline — not a deal. Anchoring privatization to a formal valuation is a **price-discovery / political-cover mechanism**: it lets CBR say "the market set the number" and lets ~20 would-be bank buyers self-select before any allocation, while the ≥50%+1 and ≤5%-per-hand caps hard-wire that **no buyer gets control or even a blocking position**. The "no concentration" language is aimed squarely at Sber/Alfa/T-Bank — the same banks whose independent-rail project CBR just killed (see [[Russia's central bank rejects Sber, Alfa, T-Bank payment system project]]). So the valuation is the machinery that turns the June "5%/2027" trial balloon into a governed, deniable process. (analysis)

## [1] Competitors / peers
National-scheme ownership models (the real comparison set for "state vs market control of the rails"):
- **India — NPCI (UPI, RuPay):** not central-bank-owned; a **not-for-profit owned by a consortium of banks**. The nearest template for "let banks into the cap table" — but bank-owned by design, not a 5% sliver carved from a state monopoly.
- **Brazil — Pix:** **100% Banco Central do Brasil**, no private equity — the regulator-owns-and-operates model NSPK has today.
- **Turkey — BKM/TROY:** the central bank (CBRT) became **controlling shareholder in 2020** — Turkey moved *toward* state control, the opposite vector.
- **Western private-control-of-rails backdrop:** [[Italy's CDP to raise Nexi stake, blocking CVC takeover]] and [[CVC Capital Partners weighs €9 billion bid to take Nexi private]] show the mirror-image fight — a *state* lender buying *up* to keep a payments operator out of PE hands — while [[Mastercard explores sale of Nets account-to-account unit for $3.2bn]] shows rails trading privately.

**+ Why the lay of the land is this way / second-order:** almost no peer sells a minority sliver of a state-owned national rail via public valuation; peers are either fully bank-owned (NPCI), fully state-owned (Pix), or moving to state control (Turkey). Nabiullina's design keeps NSPK a state monopoly with token private holders — closer to a *dividend-sharing* scheme than to NPCI-style governance. The comparison that matters: in Italy the state buys *in* to block private capture; in Russia the state sells a *sliver out* to co-opt its biggest banks. Both are about **control, not capital**; the valuation exercise is what lets Russia do the latter without a real market for corporate control. (analysis)

## [2] Company history / fit
NSPK founded **2014** (after Visa/Mastercard cut off sanctioned Russian banks over Crimea); launched **Mir** (Dec 2015) and **SBP/СБП** (Jan 2019); since 2015 it also switches **all domestic Visa/Mastercard** traffic. NSPK is **OFAC-sanctioned (Feb 2024)**. Privatization arc: **Jul 2025** Vedomosti — CBR open to investors in *individual NSPK services*; **Sep 2025** CBR advisory/consultation report lays out two scenarios (minority equity keeping ≥50%+1, or spin-off/sell a whole service, e.g. Mir or SBP); **Oct 2025** (Finopolis) Nabiullina restates both; **Feb 2026** CBR says **no clear market interest**; **May 2026** consultations "ongoing"; **22 Jun 2026** banks' rail rejected, equity offered ([[Russia's central bank rejects Sber, Alfa, T-Bank payment system project]]); **~29–30 Jun 2026** anonymous "5% / single investor / 2027" ([[Russia weighs selling up to 5% of NSPK to single investor]]); **2 Jul 2026** Nabiullina on-record + **valuation to finish by end-2026**.

**+ Why CBR acts this way:** the structural bind is a **regulator-owner conflict of interest** — CBR both regulates the payments market and owns its monopoly operator. Rather than cede a rival rail, it converts the governance complaint into a small, valuation-priced ownership concession while keeping control. The service-sale variant floated in 2025 has quietly dropped; the live option is **equity in NSPK itself**, priced by a formal valuation to give the allocation political and fair-value cover. (analysis)

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Moderate novelty; still no transaction.** Versus the June note, the genuinely new elements are: (i) **on-record governor** (not anonymous sources); (ii) a **committed valuation process with a deadline (end-2026)**; (iii) **named would-be buyers** — RSHB's Boris Listov floated "~20 new participants" and RSHB's own readiness, with **Sovcombank** ready and **T-Bank** awaiting specifics; (iv) fresh **scale disclosures** (₽154.3trn card volume, Mir >75% share, ₽103trn SBP, ₽80bn+ dividends 2022–25). But it remains **talks + a process, not a signed deal**: no valuation number, no price per share, no allocation, timeline explicitly slipping to 2027.

**+ Who captures the margin / what's real:** a ≤5% non-controlling stake transfers **dividends, not governance**. NSPK is a large, high-margin utility — FY2023 net margin ~49% (₽35.1bn/₽71.9bn); ~₽47bn dividend in 2025 alone implies a fat, growing coupon (SBP volume up 10× in two years). But the rails' *pricing* (fee-capped SBP, free universal QR) is CBR **policy**, so a 5% holder gets a dividend clip and soft tariff/strategy voice — **not** the ability to reshape the economics that motivated the banks' rejected project. Notably, RSHB's own Listov undercut the "capital-raising" rationale: "the company has enough profit to develop on its own" — i.e. the deal is about **access/influence**, not funding NSPK. The deeper question is therefore not "what is NSPK worth" but **"why would a buyer pay a formula-derived EBITDA multiple for a non-voting sliver of an OFAC-sanctioned state monopoly whose dominance rests on the forced 2022 Visa/MC exit"** — the answer is strategic seat-at-the-table access, not returns per se. (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Watch for: (1) the **valuation number / methodology** by end-2026 — Roman Prokhorov (Association of Financial Innovations) expects "a formula of several EBITDAs," with the multiple itself the object of the valuation; (2) **named buyers and an allocation** in 2027; (3) whether "friendly-country" foreign investors are actually admitted (Prokhorov floats cross-border-settlement upside — **speculative**, and hard to square with OFAC sanctions on NSPK). Sentiment among banks is **positive-but-cautious**: RSHB and Sovcombank keen, T-Bank waiting for specifics. Nabiullina herself dampened timing ("unlikely to complete this year").

**+ Why the market goes this way / second-order:** under sanctions, **payments are national-security infrastructure**, so CBR will not cede real control regardless of the banks' ~80%+ acquiring weight; the ≤5% cap and valuation-cover are precisely the tools to **monetize and co-opt without liberalizing**. Counterintuitive effects: (a) a formal valuation *creates a public reference price* for a state monopoly the CBR may prefer stayed opaque — once "several EBITDAs" is on paper, future dividend policy and tariff decisions get scored against it; (b) letting NSPK's **largest customers and just-rejected rivals** into its cap table (even at 5%) is structurally awkward on competition/governance grounds — the smallness is designed to dodge that, but a board-adjacent seat for a dominant acquirer remains a live conflict. Risk to the thesis: if the process stalls again (as it did Feb–May 2026 for "lack of interest"), the July on-record push reads as **congress rhetoric**, not a divestment. Treat "valuation by end-2026, sale 2027" as a **firmer-than-before signal, still not a deal**. (analysis/hypothesis)

## Challenge / red-team questions

1. **Is this a deal or just talk?** → Talk + a process. Nabiullina committed to a **market valuation (finish by end-2026)**, but there is no price, no allocation, no signed agreement; sale itself slips to **2027** and "unlikely this year."
2. **Is it genuinely new vs the June 30 note ([[Russia weighs selling up to 5% of NSPK to single investor]])?** → **Yes, incrementally.** June was an anonymous-source Interfax balloon on "5% / one investor / 2027." July 2 is the **governor on record** at the Financial Congress + a **committed valuation with a deadline** + **named would-be buyers** (RSHB, Sovcombank, T-Bank) + fresh scale numbers. A "trial balloon → on-record process" follow-up, not a reprint. **Fresh, tightly coupled.**
3. **Is it new vs the Sep-2025 CBR consultation report?** → The *minority-equity ≥50%+1* scenario already existed there; the new thing is the **valuation mechanism and deadline** and the governor putting her name to it. The service-sale variant (Mir/SBP spun out) appears effectively dead.
4. **What is NSPK actually worth?** → **No number disclosed.** Last public accounts FY2023: revenue ₽71.9bn, net profit ₽35.1bn (net margin ~49%). FY2024–25 unpublished. Prokhorov expects "a formula of several EBITDAs," multiple TBD. So even the implied 5% value is unknowable until the valuation lands. **Open.**
5. **Why anchor to a formal valuation now?** → Price-discovery + political cover: lets CBR say "the market set it," lets ~20 banks self-select, and hard-wires the ≤5%/no-concentration rule. It converts the June balloon into a governed, deniable process. (analysis)
6. **Who would buy a non-voting sliver of an OFAC-sanctioned state monopoly?** → Domestic banks. Named on record: **RSHB (Listov, "~20 participants")**, **Sovcombank** (ready), **T-Bank** (awaiting specifics). The rejected Sber/Alfa/T-Bank trio remains the logical core. Foreign "friendly-country" investors floated by Prokhorov are **speculative** and hard to reconcile with sanctions.
7. **What does a ≤5% holder actually get?** → Dividends (a fat, growing coupon — ~₽47bn paid in 2025) + soft tariff/strategy voice. **Not** control of pricing, which is CBR policy (fee-capped SBP, free universal QR). Governance terms unpublished.
8. **Is this a capital raise?** → **No — and RSHB's own Listov said so:** "the company has enough profit to develop on its own." The deal is about **access/influence**, not funding NSPK. That reframes it from "growth capital" to "co-opting rivals."
9. **What's the core conflict of interest?** → CBR both **regulates** the payments market and **owns** its monopoly operator; the buyers would be NSPK's **largest customers AND just-rejected rivals**. The ≤5% cap is designed to dodge the competition/governance problem — but a board-adjacent seat for a dominant acquirer stays awkward.
10. **What's the real trigger?** → The control fight over the payment interface/universal QR (law 248-ФЗ, phased from 2026-09-01) and the **22 Jun 2026 rejection** of the Sber/Alfa/T-Bank rail. The valuation/sale is the consolation-and-co-optation follow-through.
11. **How solid is the sourcing now vs June?** → **Much better.** June rested on unnamed Interfax sources. July 2 is Nabiullina on record at the congress, multi-outlet (Kommersant, Vedomosti, Interfax, 1prime), with the end-2026 valuation deadline reported by Interfax.
12. **Do the scale figures hold up / what do they hide?** → 2025 card volume ₽154.3trn but **−8% vs 2023 in value**, even as Mir's *share* rose to >75% — i.e. the pie shrank while Mir's slice grew (sanctions/import-substitution, not organic win). SBP ₽103trn (10× 2023) is real explosive growth. FY2024–25 P&L is **not disclosed**, so profitability trend is inferred from dividends, not statements.
13. **Does the comparable set support "privatization"?** → Weakly. Pix is 100% central-bank; Turkey moved TO state control (2020); only NPCI is bank-owned, by original design. A ≤5% sale leaves NSPK a state monopoly with token holders — dividend-sharing, not NPCI-style governance. (analysis)
14. **What breaks the "monetize-and-co-opt, don't liberalize" thesis?** → A valuation that triggers a **competitive auction**, a **genuinely independent/foreign** buyer at a market-clearing price, real **board seats**, or a stake creeping above 5% — any would signal a real governance opening rather than symbolic co-optation.
15. **Second-order effect of publishing a valuation?** → It **creates a public reference price** for a state monopoly CBR might prefer stayed opaque; once "several EBITDAs" is on paper, future dividend/tariff decisions get scored against it, and the ~₽80bn 2022–25 dividend take becomes a valuation input that outsiders can now model. (hypothesis)

Importance: 3/5 — A real, on-record escalation of the NSPK privatization story: the governor personally committing to a formal market valuation (by end-2026) with named bank interest is a firmer signal than the June anonymous balloon, and it is a rare, structurally interesting crack in Russia's regulator-owns-operator model tied to the consequential 22-Jun rejection. But it caps at 3: still **talks + a process, not a deal** — no valuation number, no price, no allocation; the sale slips to 2027; the ≤5%/no-concentration design guarantees no transfer of control; and the whole thing has stalled once already (Feb–May 2026 "no interest"). A meaningful development to track, not a market-moving transaction.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russia's national payments infrastructure is a state-built monopoly rail: NSPK operates both the Mir card scheme and SBP (faster payments), created 2014-19 after Visa/Mastercard sanctions cut off Russia's domestic switching. Scale (per the source, citing NSPK/Kommersant): card-payment volume Rub 154.3trn in 2025 (down 8% vs 2023, but Mir's share of that segment rose to >75% in Q1-2026 from 53% three years earlier); SBP volume Rub 103trn in 2025, ~10x the 2023 level. Drivers/"why now": (1) forced import-substitution — Mir is the only card rail left after Visa/Mastercard exit, so its share gain is structural, not competitive; (2) the "why now" for the deal is regulatory — the CBR published a Oct-2025 consultation report on transferring some NSPK services to private management, and Nabiullina (2 Jul 2026, Financial Congress) confirmed a market valuation is being commissioned so bidders "understand what the stake is worth". Structure: fully consolidated single-operator monopoly; entry barriers are absolute (regulation + network effects + it IS the national rail). Deal law caps: CBR keeps >50%+1 share, and no single outside investor may hold >5% — deliberately fragmented to avoid concentration (analysis: this suppresses any control premium).

**Competitive landscape.** Payments-infra KPIs: transaction volume/TPV, take rate per transaction, net margin, dividend payout. NSPK has no domestic competitor by design — Mir is the sole card scheme, SBP the sole interbank instant-payments rail. "Competition" is only latent (foreign schemes are sanction-blocked; A7-type cross-border rails [[Russia's A7 cross-border payments firm plans global expansion]] operate in a different, B2B/cross-border layer). Demand side of the deal: RSHB's Boris Listov said ~20 new participants could enter and RSHB is ready; Sovcombank confirmed interest; T-Bank is waiting for specifics (all 2 Jul 2026). Position: NSPK is not "ahead/behind" — it is the monopoly incumbent with a regulatory moat; the moat is 100% intangible/regulatory (state mandate), which is also its ceiling — value is politically administered, not market-set.

**Comps & multiples.** Financials NSPK actually disclosed (RAS, FY2023 — last published): revenue Rub 71.9bn (+1.4x), net profit Rub 35.1bn (+1.4x). Dividends to CBR Rub >80bn over 2022-25, of which ~Rub 47bn in 2025 alone (implies profit materially higher by 2025, but no post-2023 statements → [UNSOURCED] for current revenue/EBITDA). Charter capital Rub 4.3bn / 43,000 shares.
- Expert (Roman Prokhorov, "Financial Innovations" assoc.) frames valuation as "a multiple of several EBITDAs, the exact number being the subject of the valuation" — i.e. deal is likely priced on a low EBITDA multiple, not a Visa-style network multiple.
- External anchor (why the discount): Visa ~20.5x EV/EBITDA / ~14.4x EV/Revenue, Mastercard ~23-28x EV/EBITDA (as of May-Jul 2026, per multiples.vc/GuruFocus). Comparability FAILS: those are global, listed, multi-currency networks; NSPK is domestic-only, sanction-walled, state-controlled, with a 5% per-holder cap and no control on offer. A "several EBITDA" framing (~low-single-digit) is a >5-10x discount to global card networks — consistent with a captive-monopoly-but-illiquid-and-administered asset. Do NOT read the Visa/MA multiples across; distribution not computed (only 1 quasi-comparable, and the deal metric [EBITDA] isn't disclosed).
- Rough sanity, NOT a valuation: on FY2023 net profit Rub 35.1bn, "several EBITDAs" (say 3-6x on profit as a crude EBITDA proxy) → order-of-magnitude Rub ~100-210bn for 100%; a <5% slice → single-digit Rub bn. Flagged [UNSOURCED]/(analysis) — EBITDA undisclosed, 2025 profit undisclosed, and the CBR's own valuation isn't public. No usable in-base transaction comp for a national card scheme (semsearch surfaced only adjacent items: national-scheme launches in Pakistan/Singapore/UAE [[Singapore launches Singapore Payments Network entity]], not valuations).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Administered, not market, pricing** — CBR keeps control + caps holders at 5% and structures entry as cash-out; no control premium, minority illiquidity, and the "market valuation" is set by the seller-regulator. Why: buyers get economics without governance, so the clearing price will sit well below any Visa-analogy multiple.
2. **Disclosure gap / stale financials** — last public statements are FY2023; 2025 profit/EBITDA are undisclosed while dividends jumped (~Rub 47bn in 2025). Why: bidders and any valuation must rely on non-public CBR data, widening the information asymmetry and the range of defensible prices.
3. **Sanctions / geopolitical single-point dependence** — the entire investment thesis (Mir 75% share, SBP 10x growth) rests on the sanctions regime that removed Visa/Mastercard; cross-border ambitions ("friendly-country" investors, cross-border settlement) are the upside but also the most sanction-exposed, least-proven leg. Why: a change in the geopolitical setup is the main tail risk to both volumes and any foreign-buyer angle.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is a state monopoly monetization / partial-privatization, not a re-rating of a competitive asset — the read-through is "how cheaply will the state let banks buy captive payment-rail cash flows," a template other state infra (SBP-as-a-service, cross-border) could follow. Falsifiable thesis: if a headline valuation emerges, expect it at a low EBITDA multiple (deep discount to global card networks) precisely because of the 5% cap + no control + illiquidity — NOT at network-scheme multiples. Watch/trigger: publication of the CBR's commissioned valuation and the per-holder pricing; Nabiullina already said the deal is unlikely to close in 2026, so near-term catalyst is the valuation number, not a completed sale. Thesis breaks if the CBR loosens the 5% cap or offers a control block — then a premium could return.
