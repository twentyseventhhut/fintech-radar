INETCO launches agentic AI fraud investigation module
Diana Vorniceanu
16 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
INETCO has launched INETCO BullzAI Investigate, an AI-assisted fraud investigation module that applies agentic AI to fraud case management. The Canada-based fraud prevention company said the module is designed to help fraud teams handle growing investigation workloads and provide around-the-clock decision support, while keeping sensitive data secure and analysts in control of decisions.
Addressing alert fatigue
INETCO framed the launch around the operational strain on fraud teams as digital payment fraud and AI-driven cyber threats grow more sophisticated. Analysts spend time reviewing low-risk alerts and working through fragmented transaction data while higher-priority threats wait. The module combines agentic AI with analyst oversight, with the aim of producing faster investigations and more consistent decisions rather than replacing human reviewers.
How the module works
INETCO BullzAI Investigate runs on a proprietary small language model deployed on-premise, so transaction data does not leave a customer's environment, which INETCO notes is a requirement for institutions navigating data privacy, residency, and compliance obligations. Agentic AI fraud agents collate transaction data sets, analyse behavioural patterns, and automatically triage incoming alerts, prioritising the highest-risk cases for analyst review. The system delivers explainable risk scoring intended to give investigators auditable reasoning behind each recommendation, and it improves through a human-in-the-loop supervised machine-learning cycle in which analyst feedback refines the system after every case.
Reported results
According to INETCO, early results show investigations accelerating from between 10 and 30 minutes to roughly 20 seconds, a reduction in investigation time of 97% to 99%, reviews up to 90 times faster, and recommendation precision of approximately 95%. The company also pointed to a Chartis Research report featuring an INETCO BullzAI deployment at a South African bank, which it said found the platform combined explainable AI, adaptive machine learning, and AI-assisted investigation to improve detection accuracy and reduce operational noise.
Market context
The launch reflects wider adoption of agentic AI in fraud operations, where financial institutions are looking to automate triage and case preparation while retaining human accountability for decisions. On-premise deployment of a smaller, self-contained model also responds to concern among banks about sending sensitive transaction data to external AI systems. As per the official press release, INETCO reportedly monitors more than 100 billion transactions a year.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
INETCO (Vancouver, private, founded 1984) launched **BullzAI Investigate** on ~14–16 Jul 2026 — a fraud **case-investigation module**, i.e. a new SKU bolted onto its existing BullzAI fraud-detection/blocking platform (first launched Sep 2021), **not** a new detection engine or standalone product. It runs a proprietary **small language model on-premise** so transaction data never leaves the bank; "agentic AI agents" auto-triage alerts, collate transaction/behavioural data, rank high-risk cases and emit explainable risk scores for a human analyst; feedback loop retrains via human-in-the-loop supervised ML.

**Why the framing / what it reveals:** De-hyped, this is an **LLM investigation copilot with light triage automation, not autonomous agents taking actions.** The PR itself concedes "analysts in control of decisions" — agents *recommend*, humans *decide*. No evidence agents autonomously close cases, block accounts or file SARs. "Agentic" is doing marketing work. The headline metrics — investigations 10–30 min → ~20 sec (**97–99% cut**), reviews **up to 90× faster**, **~95% precision** — are **vendor-only, unaudited, from unnamed "early deployments"** and compare best-case automation vs worst-case manual (cherry-picked spread). The on-prem SLM / data-residency angle is the one genuinely substantive, market-relevant claim.

## [1] Competitors / peers
The AI fraud-investigation copilot category is 2–3 years old and crowded — INETCO is late:
- **Lucinity** "Luci" copilot — Jun 2023 (category pioneer), system-agnostic plugin Jun 2024.
- **NICE Actimize** — Xceed AI Agents + X-Sight ActOne agentic investigators, Apr 2025.
- **Hawk AI** — AML Investigative Agent, Mar 2026: automates data-gathering, case summarisation, typology ID and **SAR-narrative drafting** — broader automation than INETCO.
- **ComplyAdvantage** — agentic AML, claims auto-resolution of up to 85% of routine alerts.
- **SymphonyAI Sensa Agents**; **Feedzai IQ** network scoring ([[Feedzai opens fraud intelligence network with Feedzai IQ Score]], Jun 2026); **Featurespace** now inside **Visa** (~$946M, closed Dec 2024).
- Bank-built peers: [[Commonwealth Bank builds AI agent to detect fraud and build defences]] (agentic rule-generation, Apr 2026), [[Starling Bank adds AI agent to detect romance and investment fraud]] (Jun 2026).

**Position: catching up / parity on the copilot layer, behind Hawk/ComplyAdvantage/NICE on automation breadth.** Why the lay of the land: incumbents (NICE, SAS, SymphonyAI) own the case-management install base; INETCO's only defensible wedge is **on-prem SLM (data sovereignty) + coupling to its pre-authorization payments firewall** — a payments-infrastructure position AML-first rivals lack.

## [2] Company history / fit
INETCO Insight (real-time payment transaction monitoring, switch-independent) → BullzAI (2021, pre-auth fraud firewall, self-training ML) → BullzAI Investigate (2026, the investigation SKU). Monitors **100B+ transactions/year**. Named Chartis **Category Leader** in 2026 RiskTech Quadrants (enterprise/payment fraud). Reference client **African Bank** (South Africa) via partner NCR Atleos, Aug 2025; EMEA/Middle East channel. **Why this move:** a ~40-yr niche vendor extending down the funnel from detection into investigation to lift ARPU and defend accounts against broad-suite incumbents — logical, but into an already-contested adjacency.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Modest novelty.** Not first-mover (Lucinity 2023). Traction on Investigate specifically is **unproven at launch**: no named production customer for the module; metrics from unnamed early deployments; a 30 Jul 2026 demo webinar is the only proof point. Chartis validates the **base BullzAI detection platform**, **not** the Investigate module's 97–99% / 90× / 95% numbers — a co-marketed analyst quadrant is not an audit. **Where value could be real:** the on-prem SLM answers a genuine bank concern (not sending transaction data to cloud LLMs), especially in EMEA/Africa/Middle East — but that is a distribution/compliance edge, not a technical moat, and cloud peers can ship private-cloud/on-prem variants.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Tailwind is real: agentic-AI-in-fraud is the hottest sub-segment — ~$7.7B (2025) → ~$11.5B (2026), CAGR ~49%, forecast ~$55.7B by 2030 (GlobeNewswire, directional). But the space is crowded and consolidating (Visa/Featurespace). **Second-order:** as every fraud vendor and several banks ship "agentic" investigators simultaneously, the label commoditizes fast; differentiation shifts from "we have agents" to **deployment model (on-prem/data-sovereign) + integration depth into the transaction rail** — which is exactly INETCO's narrow bet. Risk: incumbents with deeper case-management footprints and broader AML coverage out-execute on breadth.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Real-time payment fraud detection & prevention. Market sizing varies widely by source: per Fortune Business Insights (via GlobeNewswire, Jul 2026) the broad fraud-detection & prevention market was ~$35.3bn in 2025 → ~$40.4bn 2026, CAGR ~18.1% to 2033; the narrower agentic-AI-in-fraud segment is quoted at ~$7.7bn (2025) → ~$11.5bn (2026), CAGR ~49% (secondary market-research firm, unverifiable methodology — treat as directional, not a hard TAM). Structure: fragmented, with a two-tier split — global scale players (Visa/Featurespace, FICO, NICE Actimize, SAS) vs a crowded field of AI-native challengers (Feedzai, Hawk, Oscilar, ComplyAdvantage on the AML side). Value concentrates at the transaction-scoring + case-management layers where switching costs and integration into card/RTP rails create stickiness. Why now: three converging drivers — (1) rising APP/scam and AI-driven attack sophistication shifting the problem from card fraud to real-time A2A/instant-payment fraud; (2) alert fatigue and analyst-labor scarcity making triage automation the acute pain point; (3) data-residency/compliance pressure pushing on-prem, self-contained models over cloud LLMs. INETCO's positioning (on-prem proprietary small language model, no data egress) is squarely aimed at driver (3).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: detection accuracy / false-positive rate, alert-to-case triage time, analyst-hours saved, transactions monitored/yr, and (commercially) ARR + net retention `[UNSOURCED]` for INETCO (private, undisclosed). Key players & basis of competition — competition is on model accuracy, explainability/auditability, latency, and deployment model (cloud vs on-prem), not price: **Visa/Featurespace** (ARIC Risk Hub, ~500 behavioural variables in <50ms, ~500m consumers / ~100bn payment events/yr per Visa; acquired Dec 2024); **Feedzai** ($2bn valuation Oct 2025, protects >$70bn annualized volume); **NICE Actimize** (launched Actimize Insights Network for cross-institution counterparty risk, 2026); **Hawk**, **Oscilar**, **ComplyAdvantage**. Recent moves: NICE Actimize Insights Network (2026) and Featurespace's Visa integration both attack the same cross-institutional/scam gap INETCO's Investigate module addresses. Protagonist's position: niche/specialist — INETCO monitors >100bn transactions/yr (per its own PR) and was named a Category Leader in Chartis's 2026 RiskTech Quadrants for Enterprise Fraud & Payment Fraud Solutions (third-party validation). Moat `(analysis)`: on-prem deployment + deep payment-network packet-level heritage (its legacy is transaction monitoring) gives switching costs at data-residency-sensitive banks; but it is sub-scale vs Visa/Feedzai on data-network effects, which are the dominant moat in fraud ML.

**Comps & multiples.** All named peers are private — no public trading multiples; use last-round valuations, not market caps. **Feedzai** — ~$2.0bn post-money (Oct 2025 round ~$75m; ~$364m raised total). **ComplyAdvantage** — ~$158m raised total (no fresh valuation). **Hawk / Featurespace** — Featurespace absorbed by Visa Dec 2024 (price undisclosed). Revenue not disclosed for any → **EV/Revenue / P/S = no data**; distribution not computed, qualitative comparison only. INETCO is private and disclosed no round/financials → own multiple = no data. Internal base comps (fraud/risk-intel deals & launches, all product/partnership not valuation events): [[MoneyGram partners Oscilar on AI risk intelligence]] (agentic risk, Nov 2025), [[Chargeflow raises $35M for chargeback automation]], [[Rased raises pre-seed from Wa'ed Ventures for fraud detection]], [[Unico launches Trust Network anti-fraud platform in Mexico]], [[Celent warns cross-institutional lending fraud evades single-lender controls]], [[Zest AI-Celent survey finds fraud hitting most lenders' credit losses]].

**Risk flags.**
1. **Agentic-AI reliability / false positives** — INETCO's own claimed ~95% recommendation precision and 97–99% time reduction are vendor-reported single-deployment figures (Chartis-cited South African bank); at ~95% precision, autonomous triage still mis-prioritises ~1-in-20 cases, so real-world false-negative risk on high-value fraud (the tail that matters) is where the thesis breaks, not the average speed metric. Human-in-the-loop is the mitigation but also caps the labor savings.
2. **Crowded field vs data-network incumbents** — the dominant moat in fraud ML is cross-institutional data (Featurespace/Visa ~100bn events, Feedzai >$70bn volume, NICE's new Insights Network). INETCO's on-prem, data-stays-local design is a compliance selling point but structurally forgoes the network-effect data that improves detection — a strategic trade-off that could cap accuracy vs scaled peers.
3. **Model-governance / regulatory scrutiny + long procurement** — agentic AI in bank fraud ops faces DORA-style AI-agent governance and explainability mandates (why INETCO leads with "explainable risk scoring"); bank procurement/model-validation cycles are long, so "launched/announced" is far from "adopted at scale" — no named launch customer for the Investigate module beyond the referenced deployment.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` Agentic case-management is becoming table-stakes across the fraud stack — the frontier is shifting from *detection* accuracy to *investigation* throughput (analyst-hours saved), which every major (NICE, Feedzai, Visa) is now targeting. INETCO's differentiated wedge is the on-prem small-language-model / data-residency angle for compliance-constrained and emerging-market banks. Falsifiable thesis: if INETCO converts the Chartis Category-Leader recognition + 100bn-tx footprint into named tier-1/RTP-scheme bank wins for Investigate within 2–4 quarters, it defends the niche; trigger to watch — a disclosed marquee customer or a Visa/Feedzai on-prem SLM counter-launch that erases the residency edge would break the differentiation.
