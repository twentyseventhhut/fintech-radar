 Сервис «Озон Инвестиции» на первом этапе будет встроен в приложение Ozon Банка
 
 Ozon планирует запустить брокерское направление осенью 2026 года, сообщил Ваэ Овасапян, генеральный директор OzonFintech,  в интервью Радио РБК на полях Финансового конгресса Банка России.

 По его словам, компания готовит собственное брокерское обслуживание с прямым доступом к Московской бирже. Осенью клиенты получат доступ к акциям и облигациям на Мосбирже, а также к продуктам управляющей компании Ozon.

 Брокер будет называться «Озон Инвестиции». На первом этапе сервис встроят в приложение Ozon Банка, а затем компания не исключает запуск отдельного приложения, рассказал Ваэ Овасапян.

 Он также отметил, что Ozon сделает ставку не только на доступ к биржевым инструментам, но и на собственные продукты коллективных инвестиций, в том числе ПИФы. Комиссию за сделки с ними компания планирует установить на нижней границе рынка.

_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Vae Ovasapyan, CEO of OzonFintech, told Radio RBK at the Bank of Russia's Financial Congress (early July 2026) that Ozon will launch a brokerage arm, "Ozon Investments" (Озон Инвестиции), in **autumn 2026**. De-PR'd, this is a **stated intention with a soft timeline, not a live product**: clients "will get" direct access to Moscow Exchange (MOEX) stocks and bonds plus Ozon's own asset-management (UC) products. Phase 1 is a window **inside the Ozon Bank app**; a standalone app is "not excluded" later — i.e. undecided.
- **Why structured this way:** Ozon already holds the two hard pieces — a MOEX presence and a licensed asset manager (UC "Ozon Asset Management," registered July 2025, CBR license Dec 2025 for PIFs/NPFs; five BPIFs registered, first — "Ozon Money Market", ticker OLIQ — trading from 25 June 2026). The brokerage layer is the missing distribution rail that turns captive Bank clients (~30M) and marketplace buyers (58M+) into a self-directed trading base. Bundling it into the Bank app (not a new app) minimizes CAC and rides existing KYC/deposits.
- **What it reveals:** Ozon plans to price PIF commissions "at the lower bound of the market" — a **volume/land-grab play**, not a margin play at launch. The real product is the fund factory (own PIFs) distributed through an owned broker, capturing both the brokerage fee and the management fee.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Russia's retail-broker market is large but shallow: **~40.1M unique MOEX clients (53% of the active-labor population), yet ~86% of accounts hold <RUB 10k**; only ~5.5M hold meaningful assets; qualified investors (~1M) control ~77% of retail assets and ~70% of the record RUB 2.5tn net inflows in 2025 (analysis of 2025 market data).
- **T-Investments (T-Bank, ex-Tinkoff):** the app-first retail leader, >10M users — the direct template Ozon is copying (broker embedded in a super-app).
- **Sber Investments / VTB My Investments:** bank-ecosystem incumbents with huge default distribution.
- **Also:** BCS, Alfa, Finam, Gazprombank Invest, Freedom Finance, Aton.
- **Position:** Ozon enters **late and near-parity in capability but behind in trust/track record.** Its edge is not product novelty — it is the same "embed the broker in the app you already open" model T-Bank proved. **Why the landscape is this way:** distribution, not product, wins Russian retail broking; the moat is the captive app + KYC + deposit float. Second-order: because ~86% of accounts are dormant, adding a broker to an ecosystem is cheap to launch but hard to monetize — the durable revenue sits with the few qualified whales the incumbents already hold.

## [2] Company history / fit
Ozon's fintech is the fastest-growing leg of the group: fintech revenue +164% YoY to RUB 46.2bn in Q2 2025; Ozon Capital net profit ~2.9x to RUB 16.2bn in 2024; active fintech clients ~30.3M (+1.6x); deposits ~RUB 216.6bn by Mar-2025 (~3x YoY); ~55% of card spend is off-marketplace. The sequence is coherent: cards → credit → deposits → DFA platform (registered June 2026) → asset management → **brokerage** now.
- **Why the company acts this way:** e-commerce take-rate is a commodity margin; Ozon needs higher-multiple, recurring financial revenue and to keep payment/savings flow inside its own rails. A broker + own PIFs converts marketplace attention into fee-bearing AUM and deepens deposit stickiness. (analysis)

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Novelty is low; this is an incumbent-model replication.** Nothing here that T-Bank/Sber/VTB don't already offer. Traction to date is thin and precursor-level: the only *live* asset is BPIF trading since 25 June 2026 (OLIQ); the broker itself is **announced, not launched — zero client-facing brokerage traction as of July 2026.**
- **Why value-add is real-but-modest:** the genuine leverage is the **own fund factory** (five BPIFs registered) distributed through an owned broker — Ozon captures management fee + brokerage fee + deposit float on the same client. But launching commissions "at the lower bound" signals it must **buy share on price**, so early economics are weak; the durable margin is the AUM in its own PIFs, not trade commissions. **Who captures the margin:** MOEX takes venue fees; the durable Ozon margin is AUM management on captive flows — which only compounds if it converts dormant marketplace users into funded, recurring investors (unproven).

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Watch for: (a) actual autumn-2026 go-live vs. slippage; (b) whether a standalone app ships; (c) the brokerage/depositary license issuance (fintech expected these in 2026); (d) funded-account and AUM numbers, not sign-ups. Backdrop: high CBR key rate keeps money-market PIFs (OLIQ) attractive — a natural on-ramp for cautious first-timers, which fits Ozon's low-fee, mass-market angle.
- **Why the market goes this way:** Russian retail investing is a distribution war among captive ecosystems; Ozon has the funnel and now the licenses, so entry is rational. **Counterintuitive second-order:** a 40M-account market that is 86% empty means "another broker" barely moves the needle — the scarce asset is *funded* investors, already concentrated at incumbents, so Ozon's real fight is activation of its own dormant base, not stealing whales. Note also the June-2026 VTB collateral stake in Ozon — a reminder Ozon's balance-sheet ties to incumbents it now competes with. (analysis)

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
1. Announced or launched? — **Open/announced only.** As of July 2026 it is a stated autumn-2026 intention by OzonFintech's CEO; no live brokerage, no UX, no client numbers.
2. Does Ozon hold a brokerage license yet? — Partly open. Asset-management license held (Dec 2025); brokerage/depositary licenses were *expected in 2026* — issuance not confirmed as live in the primary source. Confirm before publish.
3. Is this genuinely new vs. its own past? — No. It is the next step after the July-2025 UC registration and June-2026 BPIF launch; the broker is the missing distribution layer, not a new event. Fresh but incremental.
4. What is actually LIVE today? — Only BPIF trading (OLIQ) since 25 June 2026. The brokerage has zero client-facing traction.
5. Who captures the margin — Ozon or MOEX? — MOEX takes venue fees; Ozon's durable margin is AUM management on its own PIFs + deposit float, not trade commissions (set "at market lower bound").
6. Is the "low-fee" pledge a strength or a tell? — A tell: it signals Ozon must buy share on price, implying weak early unit economics and reliance on volume.
7. How does this differ from T-Investments/Sber/VTB? — It doesn't, functionally. Same "broker embedded in a super-app" model; Ozon is late and behind on trust/track record.
8. Where is the real TAM? — Open. 40.1M MOEX accounts but ~86% empty (<RUB 10k); only ~5.5M funded; ~1M qualified investors hold ~77% of assets. The scarce asset is *funded* investors, concentrated at incumbents.
9. Can Ozon activate its dormant base? — Unproven. Success hinges on converting marketplace/Bank users into funded, recurring investors, not on headline sign-ups.
10. Standalone app or forever a Bank-app window? — Undecided ("not excluded"). Signals product commitment is still tentative.
11. What's the deposit-float angle? — Ozon deposits ~RUB 216.6bn (Mar-2025, ~3x YoY); a broker deepens stickiness and cross-sell, arguably the real strategic prize.
12. Regulatory/timeline risk? — Autumn-2026 could slip; license issuance and CBR retail-protection rules (qualified-investor threshold doubled in 2025) can gate rollout.
13. Any conflict signal from the VTB stake? — Note June-2026 VTB collateral stake in Ozon; Ozon now competes with an incumbent tied to its own balance sheet. (analysis)
14. What metric proves this worked? — Funded brokerage accounts and PIF AUM growth, NOT app installs or "clients will get access."
15. Second-order market effect? — Adding one more broker to an 86%-empty market barely moves aggregate participation; the fight is intra-ecosystem activation, not net new investors.

Importance: 3/5 — Strategically coherent, credible (licenses + live BPIF + huge captive funnel + fast-growing fintech), and it extends a real ecosystem push. But it is an announced intention replicating an incumbent model in an already-saturated, whale-concentrated market, with low novelty and no brokerage traction yet. Solid mid-tier, not a market-mover.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian retail brokerage/investing. Structurally this is an *ecosystem-embedded* fintech play: Ozon is bolting a broker onto an existing marketplace + bank, not building a standalone broker. Sector participation is deep — MOEX reported ~31m unique broker clients (as of early 2024, per Moscow Exchange via web), though the active/funded base is far smaller: only ~5.1m accounts held >RUB 10k. Net retail inflows into brokerage accounts roughly doubled YoY to ~RUB 574bn, driven by falling deposit rates and expectations of monetary easing (per Ken Research / MOEX secondary citations, 2025) — the "why now": as CBR eases and deposit yields fall, retail cash rotates from deposits toward brokerage/PIFs, and every ecosystem wants to capture that flow. Structure is **consolidated at the top** (Sber, T-Investments/T-Bank, VTB, BCS, Alfa, Finam) but distribution is the real moat — barriers are less capital than a licensed rails + a large captive user funnel. Ozon already cleared the licensing barrier: it registered "Ozon Asset Management" in Jul-2025, got a CBR license (fund/PIF/NPF management) end-2025, and launched a money-market exchange fund "Ozon Money Market" in late Jun-2026 (per note source, bosfera.ru). Brokerage/depositary licenses were expected in 2026 (per Interfax/web).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: funded accounts, AUM/assets on brokerage accounts, net inflows, and commission take-rate on trades + fund management fees. Key players and basis of competition: **T-Investments** (T-Bank ecosystem) and **Sber** lead retail by client count and distribution; VTB, Alfa, BCS, Finam follow. Competition is on *distribution + product breadth + fee*, not price alone. Recent peer moves: T-Investments launched Russia's first issuer-loyalty platform paying shares for purchases (30-Jun-2026) [[T-Investments launches loyalty platform paying shares for purchases]] — a direct "shopping → shareholding" funnel that Ozon's marketplace-native model most closely mirrors. Ozon's stated positioning: direct MOEX access to equities/bonds + its own collective-investment products (PIFs), commissions "at the lower bound of the market" (per note), first embedded in the Ozon Bank app, then possibly a standalone app. Protagonist position: **late entrant but structurally advantaged** on distribution — its moat is the marketplace + Ozon Bank user funnel (network/scale + switching costs from an embedded wallet), not product differentiation `(analysis)`. Against T-Investments/Sber it is catching up on breadth and brand-in-investing.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation attaches to this launch — it is a product announcement, not a round/deal, so trading multiples are "no data" for the brokerage arm itself. Internal comps (ecosystem/fintech, RU): [[T-Investments launches loyalty platform paying shares for purchases]] (closest strategic comp — retail-broker-in-ecosystem, shopping-to-equity funnel); [[VTB takes large Ozon share stake as collateral]] (Ozon shares pledged to VTB, Jun-2026 — signals ownership/leverage complexity at parent level); [[Ozon Bank registers digital financial assets platform with CBR]] and [[Ozon Bank to double ATM network to 2,000 by end-2026]] (fintech build-out pace). **Risk flags.**
1. **Late-mover into a distribution-led, consolidated market.** T-Investments and Sber own the retail-investor mindshare and funded-account base; "commissions at the lower bound" (Ozon's stated lever) compresses the take-rate for everyone and risks a margin-thin business unless AUM scales fast — second-order: fee war erodes sector economics just as Ozon enters.
2. **Execution/licensing dependency.** Brokerage + depositary licenses were only *expected* in 2026 and direct MOEX access is not yet live — "announced" vs "adopted" gap; an autumn-2026 launch is a target, not a fact.
3. **Parent-level financial/ownership overhang.** VTB holds a large Ozon share block as collateral (Jun-2026) [[VTB takes large Ozon share stake as collateral]]; adding a licensed broker under a parent with pledged equity and an ongoing redomiciliation/ownership backdrop raises regulatory-scrutiny and governance risk for a regulated financial sub.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a *new entry* that pushes Russian ecosystem fintech further toward the full "super-app" template (marketplace + bank + broker + funds) — matching what T-Investments already does from the banking side. Falsifiable thesis: Ozon converts its >60m buyers / >30m fintech users into funded brokerage accounts faster than standalone brokers can, because the shopping funnel + embedded wallet lower CAC. Trigger to watch: actual autumn-2026 go-live with MOEX access + first disclosed funded-account / brokerage-AUM numbers in a 2026/2027 IR release. What breaks the thesis: launch slips past 2026, or fintech-segment growth decelerates in Q4'25/2026 IR prints, signaling the funnel isn't converting.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. Fintech is the profit engine, and the brokerage extends its highest-margin, fee-generating surface — fact: 9M fintech pre-tax profit RUB 41.8bn (+156%); why: 39m active clients + RUB 438bn deposits are a captive distribution base; why it matters: brokerage/mutual-fund fees add fee income (already 37% of fintech revenue) at near-zero incremental CAC; second-order: pushes Ozon further toward a full-stack financial super-app, raising switching costs and LTV per buyer.
2. Licence execution is the real gate, not demand — dealer licence in hand (05.11.2025), but broker + depositary licences were still "in process" at Q3; an autumn-2026 launch assumes CBR approval lands on time. De-PR: the deck flags the pipeline but is silent on licence timing risk. Watch for the broker licence grant as the true go-live trigger.
3. Growth is decelerating by design (GMV +53% Q3 2025 → +36% Q1 2026 → +25–30% guide), so fintech (and now brokerage/wealth fees) must carry an increasing share of incremental EBITDA to defend the ~4% margin trajectory; second-order: any credit-cycle stress (CoR already 10.0%) would pressure the interest-income leg just as fees are being built up.
