Robinhood accelerates expansion with Robinhood Chain Mainnet, stock tokens, agentic trading, and defi products
Sinziana Albu
08 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Robinhood has launched its Robinhood Chain public mainnet, introduced new stock tokens, and outlined agentic trading and DeFi expansion plans.
Robinhood Markets used an event in London to detail a package of decentralised finance (DeFi) and artificial intelligence (AI)-driven products, alongside plans to expand its services into additional markets. The announcements cover blockchain infrastructure, tokenised equities, lending, perpetual futures, and agentic trading tools.
Robinhood Chain and tokenised stocks
The company has launched the public mainnet of Robinhood Chain, a layer 2 blockchain built on the Arbitrum platform. According to Robinhood, the chain is designed to institutional standards and is intended to connect to the company's onchain user base. Partners deploying on the network from launch include Uniswap, which is setting up an automated market maker (AMM) to function as a public liquidity protocol, and Pleiades, which is deploying a separate AMM for proprietary trading. In addition, the company said the chain also integrates with Alchemy, BitGo, and Chainlink, and includes lending and borrowing functionality.
Alongside the chain, Robinhood has introduced new stock tokens, available through the Robinhood Wallet in more than 120 countries, subject to jurisdictional restrictions. The tokens can be traded around the clock and used within DeFi applications, including as collateral or within lending pools. Trading is supported through decentralised exchanges including Uniswap, Rialto, Lighter, Arcus, and 1Inch. The earlier version of the product, now referred to as Classic Stock Tokens, remains available through the Robinhood Europe app.
Lending, derivatives, and agentic trading
According to the official press release, Robinhood has begun rolling out Robinhood Earn to eligible US users, a self-custody lending product for the USDG stablecoin, offering an estimated 7% annual percentage yield (APY). The product uses the Morpho protocol for its lending infrastructure and includes insurance arranged through Lloyd's of London and RELM, intended to cover losses from cyber incidents or smart contract failures. The product was built with the support of category-defining partners like Steakhouse, Ethena, Spark, and Maple.
In the EU, Robinhood has extended its perpetual futures offering beyond crypto assets to include commodities, exchange-traded funds (ETFs), and foreign exchange (FX) pairs, such as gold, silver, and oil, with leverage of up to 10x. The company said crypto perpetual futures have been among its fastest-growing products in Europe since it expanded to 30 European countries last year. In the US, Robinhood is introducing maker order types for crypto traders, with fees as low as 0% depending on trading volume.
The company is also preparing to extend its agentic trading tools, which allow users to connect AI models to Robinhood's data and execution tools via its Trading MCP, from equities and options to crypto trading for eligible US users.

**Position:** Robinhood is **behind Kraken on live volume** and **behind Ondo/Coinbase on the ownership/regulatory story**, but **ahead on stack ownership** (own L2 + Bitstamp's 50+ licenses + retail distribution). It chose the synthetic/debt-wrapper pole for global speed, not legal quality. (Same analysis as the primary note — no new competitive fact in this reprint.)

## Sector & drivers — tokenized equities / RWA
- **Size/growth:** the tokenized-*equities* segment is small but hyper-scaling: ~$0.96bn value / ~2,878% YoY (Jan 2026), crossed **$1bn with 185k+ holders in Mar 2026** (up from ~$20m and <1,500 users Dec-2024), per RWA.xyz-style trackers via Yahoo Finance / Bitget. Broader *tokenized securities* market ~$35.8bn in 2026 → CAGR ~38% to ~$184bn by 2031 (Mordor Intelligence — secondary/analyst estimate; treat the 2031 forecast as vendor projection, not fact). Total on-chain RWA ~$33.7bn as of 15 May 2026 (theblock/RWA.xyz).
- **Structure:** fast-fragmenting land-grab, splitting on a **synthetic-exposure vs. true-ownership** fault line. Public-equity 1:1-custody tokens (xStocks/Backed, Ondo) sit apart from **SPV-derivative** exposure (Robinhood's EU stock tokens) and from issuer-sponsored registered on-chain shares (Superstate). Entry barriers = regulatory license + custody/transfer-agent rails + on-chain liquidity, not capital.
- **Why now:** (1) US regulatory thaw — SEC 28-Jan-2026 tokenized-securities statement + Nasdaq same-order-book approval + Atkins "innovation exemption" (announced 21-Apr, **delayed ~22-May-2026** after Nasdaq/NYSE/Cboe pushback), see [[US SEC poised to allow stock token trading]]; (2) first US-compliant launches (Ondo 1-Jul-2026, IVV/Micron via no-action route) — see [[Ondo Finance launches custodial tokenized securities with Broadridge]]; (3) DTCC production trades starting Jul-2026. The regulatory door is opening but throttled by design (volume caps + whitelists protect incumbents).

  - **xStocks (Kraken/Backed Finance)** — category leader: **>$30bn cumulative volume, 125k+ holders, 100+ tokens across 5 chains** (Jun-2026); ~47% share on Solana (Dune, via Kraken blog). 1:1 custody of *public* shares → sidesteps the private-company-consent problem. Comps: [[Kraken and Backed expand tokenized stocks to BNB Chain]], [[Kraken and Bybit launch tokenized US stocks]].
  - **Ondo Global Markets** — 100+ → target 1,000 tokenized US stocks/ETFs; ~37% Ethereum share; now US-compliant custodial model with proxy voting/Broadridge. Comps: [[Ondo launches 100+ tokenized US stocks across chains]], [[Ondo Finance launches custodial tokenized securities with Broadridge]], [[Ondo Finance and Pantera launch $250 million tokenization fund]].

- **Robinhood's position — catching up on scale, differentiated on stack ownership (analysis).** RH is *behind xStocks/Ondo on tokenized-equity holders/volume* but is the only player also shipping its **own L2** (Robinhood Chain on Arbitrum, mainnet ~2-8 Jul-2026, Uniswap/Pleiades AMMs, Chainlink/Alchemy/BitGo — [[Robinhood Chain launches and adopts Chainlink for onchain access]], [[Robinhood launches tokenized stocks and L2 blockchain]]) plus 24/7 tokens in 120+ countries, Robinhood Earn (7% USDG yield via Morpho), EU perps. **Moat = distribution + vertical integration** (brokerage funnel → wallet → own chain), an intangibles/switching-cost moat rather than a liquidity moat it does not yet have.

## Risk flags
1. **Structure/legal risk on the tokens themselves** — RH's stock tokens are **SPV-derivative exposure, not equity** (no vote/dividend/cap-table). This is exactly what triggered the **OpenAI/SpaceX disavowal + Bank-of-Lithuania inquiry (Jul-2025)** — see [[Analysis Why Robinhood and OpenAI clashed over onchain stock tokens]]. Naming/consumer-communication and MiFID-II (EU treats these as financial instruments, *outside* MiCA) exposure persists; the compliant-US door (SEC exemption) is **stalled**, so US retail rollout is gated.
2. **Behind on the KPI that matters (liquidity/holders)** — xStocks (>$30bn vol, 125k holders) and Ondo dwarf RH's tokenized-equity footprint; a proprietary L2 with thin third-party liquidity risks being an under-used app-chain. Second-order: without deep AMM liquidity, 24/7 tokens trade at wide spreads/discounts, undercutting the "same as the stock" promise.
3. **Disintermediation-by-incumbent / margin risk** — if DTC/DTCC stays the golden record and same-order-book (Nasdaq) tokenization wins in the US, on-chain rails become a UX layer and incumbents keep the margin; RH's chain captures little. Compounded by a *contracting* crypto segment (−47%) and a **10% workforce cut** ([[Robinhood to cut 10% of workforce in restructuring]]) signalling cost pressure.

## What this changes (idea-lens)
Robinhood is converting a **brokerage-distribution moat into a vertically-integrated on-chain stack** — the bet is that owning chain + wallet + tokens + yield beats being a listing on someone else's rails (analysis). **Falsifiable thesis:** if Robinhood Chain's tokenized-equity holders/volume don't visibly close the gap on xStocks within ~2–3 quarters *and* the US SEC innovation exemption stays delayed, RH's tokenization push is optionality-only, not a re-rating driver. **Watch/triggers:** on-chain holder & volume data for Robinhood Chain vs xStocks/Ondo; US SEC exemption timeline post-May delay; whether crypto/tokenization revenue reverses the −47% trend in H2-2026 prints.

- Adjusted EBITDA $534mn (+14%), margin ~50%; GAAP diluted EPS $0.38.
- Funded Customers 27.4mn (+1.7mn, +6% YoY).
- Total Platform Assets $307bn (+39%); net deposits $17.7bn (~22% annualized).
- Notional volumes: equities $638bn (+54%), options contracts 586mn (+17%), **crypto notional $66bn (-48%)** — the single figure most exposed to the tokenization thesis.

**By segment / driver.** The mix shifted hard away from crypto: crypto revenue went from ~$268mn in Q3 2025 (+300% YoY) to $134mn — a >50% sequential/comp collapse as 2025's boom lapped. Offsetting it: event contracts ($147mn, +320%) are now larger than crypto and near options; equities revenue +46% on +54% notional; net interest ($359mn) is the ballast, +24% on a doubled margin book and $307bn platform assets. So the business is de-risking off crypto notional even before Chain — but crypto is still the swing factor, which is exactly why tokenized stocks + Robinhood Chain matter to the model.

**vs expectations / prior period.** Miss vs public consensus (StockAnalysis/Investing.com aggregation, as of ~2026-04-28): revenue $1.07bn vs ~$1.14–1.17bn (miss ~$70–100mn, ~6–8%); EPS $0.38 vs ~$0.41 (miss ~$0.03). The entire miss is attributable to the crypto line; ex-crypto the print was strong. vs prior quarter: Q4 2025 was $1.28bn (+27% YoY) with record 8.5bn event contracts, so Q1 2026 also decelerated sequentially on the crypto/seasonal fade. Full-year 2025 crypto was a boom base that now creates tough comps through 2026.

**Guidance / forward.** No formal revenue guidance in the release (Robinhood guides opex, not revenue). Public forward consensus implied EPS recovery (~$0.45 Q2 / ~$0.50 Q3 2026 per aggregators [UNSOURCED as to Robinhood's own numbers]). Management tone was expansion-first — the London event package (Robinhood Chain L2 on Arbitrum, new stock tokens in 120+ countries, Robinhood Earn 7% USDG, EU perp futures on commodities/ETFs/FX, agentic trading via Trading MCP) is the forward story management is selling. What they stayed quiet on: the P&L path — none of tokenized stocks, Chain, or DeFi products carries disclosed revenue or take-rate, and there is no framework for when/if they offset the ~$130mn/quarter crypto volatility.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. **Crypto is the swing line and it just halved.** Crypto revenue -47% (notional -48% to $66bn) → the whole consensus miss is here → it matters because Robinhood's multiple is levered to crypto beta → second-order: management needs a *structural* onchain revenue source, hence Chain + tokenized stocks, to convert episodic crypto trading into recurring rails.
2. **Tokenized stocks are strategy, not yet numbers.** Stock tokens in 120+ countries, 24/7, usable as DeFi collateral (Uniswap/Rialto/Lighter/Arcus/1Inch) → but no disclosed revenue, volume, take-rate or user count → matters because the thesis "chain moves the model" is unproven → second-order: near-term this is optionality/narrative, not a modeled line; watch for Robinhood to start disclosing onchain metrics before crediting it in estimates.
3. **The durable core is holding.** Net interest $359mn (+24%), platform assets $307bn (+39%), event contracts $147mn (+320%), funded customers 27.4mn → these de-risk the model regardless of crypto → matters because it buys time for the Chain bet → second-order: even if tokenization underdelivers, the deposit/NII flywheel and event-contracts ramp support the base case; Chain is upside call-option, not the load-bearing wall.
4. **Comp risk through 2026.** 2025 was a crypto boom base → tough YoY comps for several quarters → matters because reported growth may stay noisy/soft even as the franchise expands → second-order: judge Chain/tokenized-stock traction on adoption and onchain volume, not on the crypto revenue print, which will be comp-distorted.
