[0] TL;DR
i3D, a Russian maker of industrial metal 3D printers, has raised 50m ₽ (~$0.6m) from the Moscow Venture Fund (Фонд развития венчурного инвестирования города Москвы). Proceeds go to production equipment purchases, leasing production space, and payroll. This is a small, all-domestic public-fund seed/growth cheque into an import-substitution hardware play. Fresh; not a duplicate.

[1] WHO / WHAT
- **i3D (ГК i3D, i3d.ru):** Russian developer/manufacturer of industrial 3D printers, focused on metal-powder printing via SLM/LPBF (selective laser melting / laser powder bed fusion), plus additive-tech equipment supply and domestic software. Per web sources the group was founded in 2016 by Mikhail Rodin. NOTE: multiple details below are from web-search summaries only and are NOT firmly corroborated — treat as tentative:
  - Reported subsidiaries/brands "AM.TECH" and "НПО 3Д-Интеграция" said to be part of the i3D group. UNVERIFIED — search engines conflated several entities (including unrelated foreign "IT3D Group" and US "i3D MFG"). Do not assert as fact.
- **Moscow Venture Fund (Московский венчурный фонд / Moscow Seed Fund):** a Moscow-city public fund run under the Moscow Innovation Cluster (i.moscow) to stimulate private VC activity and back tech startups. It is also the entity behind the widely-cited RU-VC market-stat studies. It is a municipal/public investor, not a private VC — cheque sizes are modest and policy-driven (import substitution, local jobs).
- The ₽ amount (50m) and use-of-funds match the source verbatim; the round type (seed/growth/debt vs equity) is not disclosed.

[2] WHY IT MATTERS
- Additive manufacturing is a strategic import-substitution priority in Russia after Western AM vendors (EOS, SLM Solutions, etc.) exited. Domestic metal-3D-printing capacity has direct defence/industrial-tooling relevance, which is why public money flows here.
- RU additive-tech market reportedly grew ~40% YoY to ~18.3bn ₽ (TAdviser) — a rare growth pocket inside an otherwise contracting RU venture market (2025 RU VC ≈ $159m / 12.2bn ₽, −10% YoY, deal count −42%, per Moscow Venture Fund/ФРИИ).
- 50m ₽ is tiny relative to sector peers: fund "Восход" (Voskhod, anchored by Potanin's Interros) put 250m ₽ into 3D-printer maker Am.Tech in Nov 2025 (round reportedly up to 600m ₽). If Am.Tech truly sits inside the i3D group, this small city-fund cheque is a minor top-up beside a far larger private round — a materiality caveat worth flagging.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** i3D (parent LLC "Ai 3D Group" / NPO 3D Integration, founded 2016 by Mikhail Rodin) is a Russian industrial 3D-printing group; its metal-printing arm Am.Tech builds SLM/LPBF (selective laser melting / laser powder bed fusion) machines. This is industrial hardware / additive manufacturing, not fintech. Market context: Russian additive-manufacturing market projected ~20.5% CAGR to 2030, with the metal segment the fastest-growing at ~30.8% (per nextmsc/databridge secondary market-research reports, unverifiable methodology — treat as indicative, not audited). Structure: concentrated on the demand side (aerospace, defense, energy state enterprises), fragmented on domestic hardware supply (PICASO 3D, Imprinta, Anisoprint, Am.Tech). "Why now": import-substitution — Western SLM vendors (EOS, SLM Solutions, TRUMPF) exited/restricted after 2022, and the RF Minpromtorg's additive-tech roadmap makes domestic metal printers a strategic priority. Barriers to entry: high (capital-intensive laser/optics R&D, metal-powder feedstock ecosystem, quality-certification for defense/aerospace parts). Datapoint: in 2024, 94 SLM systems were sold in Russia for ~3.1bn rubles total (~33m rubles avg per unit) (per Voskhod fund, via Forbes.ru).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: units shipped/yr, revenue per machine, installed base, in-house component share (import-substitution proof). Key players: Am.Tech (i3D) vs PICASO 3D, Imprinta, Anisoprint on domestic hardware; imported/gray-market SLM machines on the high end. Basis of competition: sovereignty/serviceability + price vs Western OEMs, laser-source and software capability. Recent moves: (2023) Am.Tech opened the "Moscow Digital Factory"; (Nov 2025) Am.Tech raised 250m rubles from the Voskhod fund (Potanin-linked) for additive tech (Forbes.ru, CNews); (this deal, 2026-07) i3D raised 50m rubles from Moscow Venture Fund (Moscow Seed Fund) for equipment purchase, facility rent and payroll (Русский венчур); planned Am.Tech/NPO factory in Innopolis SEZ (Laishevo, Tatarstan), targeting ~290 machines/yr by 2032. Position: domestic SLM leader `(analysis)` — Am.Tech 2024 revenue reported at ~1.2bn rubles (own printers + imported-equipment integration + services) per Voskhod; against a 3.1bn-ruble 2024 SLM market this implies a leading share `(analysis, revenue mixes hardware+integration so not a clean share figure)`. Moat: intangibles (laser-synthesis IP, QC software) + regulatory tailwind (import substitution) + scale ambition; switching costs once installed in certified production.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation/post-money disclosed for either round → EV/Revenue, P/S, price-per-machine = **no data**; multiple = **[UNSOURCED]**. Round-size comparison only (arithmetic on disclosed rubles):
- Moscow Venture Fund → i3D: 50m rubles (2026-07). Seed-scale; matches the fund's stated 2024 ceiling of up to 50m rubles/company (Interfax).
- Voskhod → Am.Tech: 250m rubles (Nov 2025) — **5.0x** the Moscow Venture Fund ticket (`250 / 50 = 5.0x`), and the group's Am.Tech arm alone reported ~1.2bn-ruble 2024 revenue. So this 50m round is small relative to group scale (revenue-to-ticket ≈ `1,200m / 50m = 24x`) — reads as top-up/facility-and-payroll working capital, not a growth round.
- Am.Tech revenue trajectory (context, not a comp): 30.2m (2019) → 233.6m (2022) → ~1,200m rubles (2024); 2022→2024 ≈ **5.1x** in two years (`1200 / 233.6`).
Internal corpus comps: **no data** — the news DB is fintech/payments-centric; grep found no Russian early-stage venture / additive-manufacturing peers to wikilink.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Ticket-vs-scale mismatch / signaling.** 50m rubles is ~24x below the group's own reported annual revenue and 5x below Am.Tech's 2025 Voskhod round; a state seed fund financing "rent + payroll" for an already-1bn+ revenue group raises a question of which entity actually received the money (i3D retail/distribution vs Am.Tech manufacturing) and why so small — possibly a subsidised/PR-driven municipal grant rather than a market-priced round.
2. **End-market concentration & sanctions coupling.** Demand skews to defense/aerospace/energy state buyers; the import-substitution tailwind that creates the opportunity also ties revenue to a sanctioned, budget-cyclical customer base and to continued restriction of Western OEMs — a policy reversal or budget cut hits demand directly.
3. **Feedstock & component dependence.** SLM economics depend on metal powders and laser/optics components; if these remain import-dependent, the "sovereign" positioning is partial and margins are exposed to FX / supply-chain shocks (second-order: undercuts the very import-substitution thesis funding the sector).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Signals continued state/municipal capital rotation into Russian metal-additive hardware (Moscow Venture Fund + Voskhod within ~8 months) — a policy-driven build-out, not a market re-rating `(analysis)`. Watch trigger: whether the Tatarstan/Innopolis factory reaches groundbreaking and the ~290 machines/yr target holds; thesis breaks if unit sales stall near the 2024 ~94-system market level or if Western SLM supply quietly returns and reprices domestic hardware.
