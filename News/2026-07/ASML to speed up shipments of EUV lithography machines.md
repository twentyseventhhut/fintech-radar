# ASML to speed up shipments of EUV lithography machines

> [!info] 2026-07-16 · 1 упоминаний · 0 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: MTS

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[MTS] ASML will work to ship their EUV machines faster. The Dutch company’s extreme ultraviolet lithography machines are critical for all advanced semiconductor manufacturing, and cost hundreds of millions of dollars each. Its cycle time, the time between beginning production and shipping to a customer, currently takes about 22 weeks, but the company is working on reducing it to 15-16. Thanks to the AI boom, ASML’s 2027 capacity is already close to booked, and they’re working on increasing capacity 30% year-over-year for the next two years. Intel is already using ASML’s most advanced machine, the EXE:5200B High NA EUV, to manufacture some chips. They also may raise prices.

# Context-enrichment: ASML to speed up shipments of EUV lithography machines
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**FRESHNESS: fresh.** This is a NEW event — ASML's **Q2 2026 earnings / operational disclosure (call on 2026-07-15; Bloomberg wrote it up 2026-07-15)** where CFO Roger Dassen said ASML is cutting EUV build-and-test *cycle time* from ~22 weeks toward ~15–16 weeks and expanding capacity ~30%/yr. It is NOT a duplicate of the prior ASML-tagged corpus notes, which are different subjects: [[US fears ASML's top chip tool ended up in China]] (s74ed343c, export-control allegation, 20 Jun) and [[xLight raises round to replace ASML's chip light source]] / [[US-backed chip startup led by ex-Intel CEO raises $350M]] (xLight FEL-source funding). Those cover the China scare and a challenger startup; none covers ASML's own shipment-acceleration / earnings. → cross-reference, not duplicate.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On its **Q2 2026 earnings call (2026-07-15)**, ASML disclosed it is working to **compress EUV "cycle time"** — the span from starting production of a system to shipping it to a customer — from **~22 weeks to ~15–16 weeks** (~a one-third cut), per CFO Roger Dassen (Bloomberg, 2026-07-15). Alongside: it is adding **~30% EUV capacity for 2027 vs 2026** and *investigating* a further ~30% for 2028; **2027 is "close to fully booked"** with a "substantial number" of 2028 orders already in — orders that far ahead are described as rare.
- **De-PR'd:** "speed up shipments" is **not new capacity from new fabs**; it is **manufacturing-throughput / lead-time optimization** — building the same tool faster so ASML can ship more units per year and pull revenue forward. It does not add net EUV supply beyond the capacity plan; it improves the *rate* of conversion of backlog to revenue.
- **The hard numbers behind it** (Q2 2026 print, from the call and coverage): **net sales €9.3bn**, **net income €2.9bn**, **gross margin 54.0%**, EPS €7.59 — all above guidance. Full-year 2026 revenue guidance **raised to €43–45bn**. EUV business growth guided ~**45%** for 2026; ~**65 Low-NA EUV** systems to ship in 2026, ~**80 in 2027**.
- **Demand driver = memory, not just logic.** The pull is now led by **HBM/DRAM** makers (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron) building for HBM3E/HBM4/HBM4E — for the first time memory EUV orders are reported to have *overtaken logic*. Concrete: **SK Hynix committed ~$7.9–8bn (₩11.9tn) through 2027 for ~30 EUV tools** (record order). 2027 Low-NA delivery mix cited: ~20 units SK Hynix, ~7 Samsung, ~5 Intel.
- **High-NA:** **Intel is running the EXE-class High-NA EUV in production** — Intel Foundry is using High-NA on its **18A** node for a subset of Core Ultra Series 3 chips (a production-readiness milestone); High-NA units (~$380M each) are also slated for Intel 14A and some SK Hynix memory.
- **"May raise prices":** flagged as *possible*, not announced — consistent with a supply-constrained monopoly, but treat as vague/forward-looking, not a committed price action.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals.** When you are the sole EUV supplier and 2027 is already sold out, you cannot grow near-term revenue by winning share (there is none to win) or by cutting price (demand is not price-elastic here). The only near-term levers are (a) **ship faster** (cycle-time cut → more units/yr from the same lines, revenue pulled forward) and (b) **raise price**. So the "speed up shipments" story is the operational expression of a **capacity-bound monopoly meeting an AI/HBM demand spike** — the interesting fact is not the 22→15 weeks per se but what it signals: backlog so deep and orders so far forward that ASML is optimizing throughput rather than chasing orders.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **EUV scanners: no competitor.** ASML is the sole supplier of EUV (and High-NA EUV) — 100% share; the "peers" are (a) its own **installed base of DUV** (multi-patterning as a partial substitute at trailing nodes), and (b) **challengers that have not shipped**: [[xLight raises round to replace ASML's chip light source]] (FEL source, prototype 2028) and Substrate (X-ray litho) — both pre-product, zero traction. See [[US-backed chip startup led by ex-Intel CEO raises $350M]].
- **The real "competitors" for this story are ASML's own customers as demand nodes:** memory (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron) vs logic (TSMC, Intel, Samsung LSI) fighting for scarce EUV *slots*. The news that **memory orders overtook logic** is the competitive shift — HBM for AI accelerators is now the marginal buyer setting the queue.
- **Position:** ASML is not "ahead/behind" — it is the **bottleneck**. The competitive dynamic is downstream: whoever secures EUV slots (SK Hynix's ~$8bn record order) gets HBM/advanced-DRAM capacity first.

**Why the lay of the land / 2nd order.** Because EUV is the single non-substitutable node, ASML's throughput decisions **allocate** who gets to build AI memory first. The cycle-time cut is quietly a **supply-allocation event** for the whole AI hardware chain: shaving ~6 weeks per tool × dozens of tools/yr materially advances when HBM4-class capacity comes online. So the second-order read is that ASML's factory floor, not any chipmaker's roadmap, is a gating input to the AI-memory buildout.

## [2] Company history / fit
ASML — 42 years, ~44,000 people, ~€4.5bn R&D/yr; Europe's most valuable listed company (mkt cap has traded ~$530–700bn across 2026). EUV tools cost ~$200–400M (High-NA ~$380M), size of a school bus, months to assemble, hundreds of suppliers. Trajectory into this event:
- **FY2025:** net sales ~€32.7bn; EUV systems sales +39% to ~€11.6bn; backlog ~€38.8bn.
- **H1 2026:** "very strong" bookings; **Q2 2026 €9.3bn sales**, guidance raised to €43–45bn FY.
- **Mar 2026:** SK Hynix record ~$8bn EUV order through 2027.
- **2026:** Intel takes High-NA EUV into production (18A).
- **2027:** ~"fully booked", ~80 EUV shipments planned, +30% capacity.

**Why the company acts this way.** ASML "follows its customers" (Fouquet's framing) and has openly said the world won't have enough chips "for years." Faced with a demand spike it did not forecast, and unable to expand a supplier-heavy, multi-month build overnight, the rational move is **industrial-engineering the cycle time down** — the fastest way to add effective supply without new buildings — while pushing suppliers to +30%/yr. It fits a monopolist optimizing yield-of-throughput rather than competing for demand.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**What's genuinely new here:** the *magnitude and forwardness* of the AI/HBM demand signal — 2027 booked out this early, memory orders overtaking logic for the first time, and ASML resorting to **cycle-time compression** as a supply lever. That is real operating news backed by a numbers print (Q2 beat, raised guide), i.e. **traction, not announcement.**
**What is NOT new / is vague:** the cycle-time cut is an ongoing operational program (target, not achieved 15–16 weeks yet); the +30% capacity for 2027 has been guided before; "may raise prices" is speculative. So the item mixes a hard, dated earnings fact with softer forward operational aspirations.
**Who captures the margin (2nd order).** ASML captures almost all of it: sole supplier, 54% gross margin, pricing power on a sold-out book. The cycle-time cut is **pure margin/velocity upside for ASML** — faster revenue recognition, more units/yr at monopoly prices, and optionality to raise price into scarcity. Downstream, the value of a secured EUV slot accrues to whichever memory maker locks HBM capacity first (SK Hynix's record order is essentially buying queue priority). The one thing that breaks this is **demand air-pocket risk**: if the AI/HBM capex cycle rolls over, a book that is "fully booked to 2027" can see cancellations/push-outs — the classic semi-cap whipsaw.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Watch:** whether cycle time actually reaches 15–16 weeks (execution), whether the 2028 +30% is confirmed, and whether a **price increase** materializes vs stays a hint.
- **Sentiment:** constructive — Q2 beat + guidance raise (€43–45bn) + 2027 sold out read as confirmation the AI chip boom is intact; ASML is the "picks-and-shovels" proxy for AI capex durability. Analysts frame EUV bookings as the tell on AI-capex sustainability.
- **Regulatory backdrop:** unrelated to this item's core, but the same franchise carries **China export-control overhang** (DUV-ban risk, ~20% of revenue) — see [[US fears ASML's top chip tool ended up in China]]. Not triggered here, but it's the standing tail risk on the name.

**Why the market goes this way / counterintuitive.** The bullish read is "sold out to 2027 = safety." The counterintuitive second-order: **a fully-booked backlog concentrated in one demand theme (AI/HBM) is a fragility, not a fortress** — ASML's near-term numbers are now hostage to hyperscaler/memory capex holding up, and a single-cycle correction in HBM would hit the most-booked years first. And the cycle-time cut, by pulling revenue *forward*, can steepen the eventual air-pocket. So the durable question is less "is demand strong now" (clearly yes) than **"how correlated is ASML's record backlog to a single AI-capex cycle that could inflect."**

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This sits in **EUV lithography / semiconductor capital equipment** — the single most capital- and IP-intensive node of the AI chip stack. Size: ASML's own FY2026 guidance, **raised to €43–45bn total net sales** at Q2 (from €39–40bn earlier), with 2026 gross margin **54–56%**, is the cleanest live gauge of the leading-edge-tool market it effectively *is* (ASML, Q2 2026 release, 15 Jul 2026); broader EUV-equipment TAM third-party estimates cluster ~$16–26bn in 2026 growing ~9–11% CAGR, but diverge widely by scope → order-of-magnitude only. Structure: **maximally consolidated / a monopoly** — ASML is the **sole EUV supplier** (5/3/2nm) and ~90% of broader litho; the moat is the deepest in tech (source alone took ~20yr, >€10bn cumulative R&D, ~44,000 staff, ~€4.5bn R&D/yr). **Why now (this event):** ASML is compressing EUV **cycle time from ~22 to ~15–16 weeks (~a third)** and adding **+30% NXE capacity for 2027** (~65→~85 systems), "investigating" a further +30% for 2028, because **2027 EUV is close to fully booked with a substantial 2028 order book** — a demand signal so far out ASML calls it "rare" (per Q2 2026 call / TheNextWeb, 15 Jul 2026). Root driver: hyperscaler capex (the four largest US clouds committing >$600bn to AI infra in 2026, per Fouquet) has made leading-edge chips **supply-constrained "for years"** — lithography is now the bottleneck, so ASML is optimizing throughput (reduced test protocols, clean-room reallocated from R&D to output, supply-chain de-bottlenecking) rather than just building more fabs.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: **throughput (wafers/hr & systems/yr)**, **cycle time (weeks)**, **source power (W)/wavelength**, and **cost-per-exposed-layer**. This news moves the first two directly. Key players & basis of competition: **ASML** (incumbent monopolist; competes on integration + reliability + a roadmap to a **1,000W source / +50% speed by ~2030**, plus now faster build cadence); challengers all **pre-revenue**: **xLight** (FEL/accelerator source, positioned as plug-in *partner*, prototype 2028 — see [[xLight raises round to replace ASML's chip light source]]) and **Substrate** (X-ray accelerator litho, ~$1bn val Oct 2025, "replace the scanner" stance). Recent dated moves: **Intel qualified High-NA EUV (EXE) on select Intel 18A layers — first high-volume Logic use of High-NA** (ASML Q2 2026, 15 Jul 2026), which the note flags ("Intel is already using… EXE:5200B"); ASML scheduled **56 low-NA + 10 High-NA EUV tools for 2027 delivery** (TechPowerUp). **Protagonist position: dominant, extending the lead** — the capacity/cycle-time push widens the gap the challengers must close before they even ship. Moat `(analysis)`: intangibles + 20-yr integration + installed-base service; the news reinforces it (faster fulfillment = higher switching cost / lock-in on a supply-constrained tool). The hinted **price increase** — "they also may raise prices" — is unconfirmed pricing power being tested; de-PR: *announced intent*, not booked.

**Comps & multiples.** ASML is a public monopoly with no true trading comp; the multiple *is* the benchmark.
- **ASML (ASML)** — mkt cap **~$686bn** (6 Jul 2026, Capital.com; ~$1,797/sh). On FY2026 guided sales **~€44bn (~$48bn)**: **EV/Revenue ≈ ~14x** (per multiples.vc); **P/E ~62x** (10 Jul 2026, fullratio), **fwd P/E ~49x** (GuruFocus), **EV/EBITDA ~42.5x** (valueinvesting.io). Q2 2026 actuals: **€9.3bn net sales, €2.9bn net income, 54% GM** (ASML/GlobeNewswire, 15 Jul 2026). Rich vs sanity ranges (P/E ~10–50x, EV/EBITDA ~8–25x — ASML sits *above* both), but that is **monopoly rent + AI supercycle**, not hyper-growth speculation: ~54–56% GM, sole-supplier pricing, multi-year booked backlog. P/E is ~70% above its own history (fullratio) — the risk is that the multiple already prices the acceleration.
- **Challengers** — **xLight** ($350M round reported, ~$200M prior; no revenue → **no multiple, "no data"**); **Substrate** (~$1bn round val Oct 2025 → round valuation, not market cap). Both pre-revenue → revenue/earnings multiples **not computable**.
- Internal comps: [[xLight raises round to replace ASML's chip light source]] (challenger cohort, ASML FY figures, monopoly rent thesis) · [[US fears ASML's top chip tool ended up in China]] (China exposure, DUV-ban overhang, monopoly structure).
Distribution **not computed** — one public comp; challengers private/pre-revenue. Qualitative read: this is the value pool the whole cohort is optioning against, and today's news makes the incumbent's numerator (throughput/backlog conversion) *bigger and faster*.

**Risk flags.**
1. **China / export-control overhang (largest quantified risk).** China guided to **~20% of 2026 net sales, down from ~33% in 2025** (Benzinga/CNBC, 2026); the **MATCH Act** would ban DUV *shipments and servicing* to China — that ~20% (and high-margin service) is politically revocable. Second-order: revenue quality now depends on US/Dutch political will, not just tech monopoly.
2. **Cyclicality / demand concentration on one theme.** The entire acceleration case rests on **hyperscaler AI capex staying elevated for years**; ASML explicitly follows customer capex. If AI capex normalizes, a 30%-expanded capacity base built into a supply-constrained peak becomes over-capacity into a trough — classic semi-cap whiplash. The "2027 booked / 2028 orders" cushion mitigates but doesn't eliminate.
3. **Customer concentration + execution.** A handful of foundries/IDMs (TSMC, Intel, Samsung) absorb nearly all EUV; Intel's High-NA ramp is load-bearing for the High-NA narrative, and Intel's own turnaround is unproven. Cutting cycle time by ~30% via "reduced test protocols" also carries a **quality/yield-escape execution risk** on ~$200–400M tools if throughput is pushed too hard.
4. **Multiple already prices it.** At ~62x P/E (~70% above own history) with the guidance raise already in the tape, the acceleration may be largely discounted — asymmetric downside on any China escalation or capex-cycle wobble.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` Not a re-rating trigger by itself — it's the incumbent **converting a demand bottleneck into faster backlog realization and (attempted) pricing power**, which *widens* the gap the xLight/Substrate cohort must close before 2028. Falsifiable thesis: *if* ASML lands the 22→15–16wk cycle time and +30% 2027 capacity without yield escapes, EUV supply loosens ~2027–28 and the litho bottleneck migrates downstream (foundry/packaging), pressuring the "chips short for years" narrative that supports the whole AI-capex multiple. **Triggers to watch:** (a) whether the mooted price increase actually books; (b) 2028 capacity decision (the second +30%); (c) MATCH Act progression / China-share trajectory; (d) High-NA (EXE) volume ramp beyond Intel's first 18A layers. **What breaks it:** an AI-capex air-pocket turns the capacity add into over-build, or DUV-servicing bans crystallize the ~20% China hit.
