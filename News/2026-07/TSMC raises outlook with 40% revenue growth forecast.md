# Context-enrichment: TSMC raises outlook with 40% revenue growth forecast
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

**Freshness: FRESH.** This is TSMC's actual Q2 2026 print (reported 16 Jul 2026) plus a raised FY guide and a new $100B US commitment. No prior TSMC earnings note exists in the corpus (only `company/tsmc` note is this one). Not a duplicate.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Q2 2026 (ended 30 Jun 2026), reported 16 Jul 2026:
- Revenue **US$40.20B** (NT$1,270.38B), **+33.7% YoY in USD** / +36.0% YoY in NT$, **+12.0% QoQ**.
- **Net income NT$706.56B, +77.4% YoY**; diluted EPS NT$27.25 (US$4.31/ADR). (The note's "77.4% net income" figure is the YoY EPS/net-income growth — correct.)
- **Gross margin 67.7%** (op margin 60.3%, net margin 55.6%) — up from 66.2% in Q1'26 and ~62% in Q4'25. Margin is still expanding despite the drag of ramping US fabs.
- **HPC (AI accelerators + CPUs + networking) = 66% of Q2 revenue**, up from 61% in Q1'26 and 60% a year ago → the mix shift to AI is the story, not the headline growth.
- **Guidance raised twice over:** FY2026 revenue now "slightly above 40%" YoY in USD (prior guide ">30%"; consensus ~35%). **Capex lifted to $60–64B** (from $52–56B). Q3'26 guide: $44.6–45.8B rev (+37% YoY / +12% QoQ), GM 65–67%.
- **+$100B additional US investment → $265B total**, funding ≥4 more Arizona fabs at 2nm and below.

**+ Why framed this way:** The digest anchors on the flattering "40% revenue growth" and the giant $265B number. The de-PR'd substance is (a) the +5pp jump in guidance (>30% → >40%) mid-year, which is the real signal of AI-demand acceleration, and (b) that margins are RISING even as TSMC absorbs dilutive overseas fabs — meaning pricing power > cost drag. The Arizona pledge is partly a geopolitical hedge (reduce Taiwan-concentration risk), not purely economics; US fabs run structurally lower margin.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Foundry:** TSMC held **~72.3% of the foundry market in Q1'26** (up from 70.4% QoQ); **Samsung Foundry ~6.5%**, a distant second. Samsung's 2nm yields lagged (~55–60% end-2025 vs TSMC's reported 70–90%). Intel Foundry is not a factor at 2nm/AI-accelerator scale in 2026. TSMC's lead is *widening*, not narrowing.
- **Upstream equipment:** ASML is capacity-constrained; 2027 EUV capacity nearly booked, cycle time being cut 22→15-16 weeks ([[ASML to speed up shipments of EUV lithography machines]]). TSMC's capex hike directly feeds ASML demand.
- **Memory peers riding the same AI wave:** Micron profit jumped 15-fold ([[Micron quarterly profit jumps 15-fold]]); AI memory demand is pushing consumer prices ([[AI memory-chip demand pushes Apple to raise iPhone prices]]).
- **Custom-silicon customers = also demand drivers:** OpenAI/Broadcom inference chip ([[OpenAI and Broadcom unveil AI inference chip]]), Anthropic/Samsung talks ([[Anthropic in talks with Samsung on custom AI chip]]) — all of these ultimately fab at leading-edge, and Broadcom/OpenAI chips go to TSMC.

**+ Second-order:** TSMC's moat is not just node leadership but **CoWoS/advanced-packaging capacity**, the true bottleneck for AI accelerators. The 72% share + margin expansion means the "premium" is name-specific (execution/yield), not merely sectoral — Samsung sits in the same booming market yet can't convert it to share or margin.

## [2] Company history / fit
- TSMC has compounded through the AI cycle: Q1'26 rev +35.1% YoY (GM 66.2%), Q4'25 GM ~62%. Q2'26 continues sequential margin and revenue gains — a clean acceleration, not a one-off.
- The Arizona escalation ($165B → $265B) fits a multi-year de-risking arc under US CHIPS/tariff pressure and customer (Apple, Nvidia) desire for US-made leading-edge supply.

**+ Why:** As the sole credible leading-edge/advanced-packaging supplier, TSMC captures pricing power and can pass on the cost of geographically diversifying. It builds in the US not because Arizona is cheaper (it isn't — it's margin-dilutive) but because customer + political demand for "not-only-Taiwan" supply is now a competitive requirement.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Not "new tech" — this is a **traction/guidance data-point**: the mid-year jump from >30% to >40% FY growth, capex +$8B, HPC now two-thirds of revenue. That is hard adoption evidence (booked wafers, not announcements) that the AI-capex build-out is still *accelerating* in H2 2026, echoing ASML's booked 2027 capacity.

**+ Who captures the margin:** TSMC sits at the choke point of the AI stack — every hyperscaler/custom-silicon design (Nvidia, Broadcom/OpenAI, AWS Trainium, Etched) must fab at TSMC leading edge + CoWoS. It captures margin regardless of which chip designer wins, which is why 67.7% GM is durable. The break-case: a demand air-pocket if AI-accelerator ROI disappoints (the "AI spending ceiling" question), or forced margin dilution as US/Japan/Europe fabs scale.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Q3'26 guide implies continued +37% YoY; FY >40% now the base case. Capex $60–64B signals confidence the demand is real, not speculative.
- Sentiment strongly positive; TSMC is read as *the* purest AI-infrastructure proxy. Watch signals: CoWoS capacity adds, 2nm ramp/yield, HPC mix trend.
- Risks: (i) AI-capex digestion / ROI doubt among hyperscalers; (ii) geopolitics — Taiwan concentration, China export curbs ([[Nvidia halves list of Asia buyers over China chip export curbs]]), US tariffs; (iii) margin dilution from overseas fabs; (iv) FX.

**+ Counterintuitive second-order:** The bigger and more indispensable TSMC becomes, the more it is a single point of failure for the entire AI economy — its strength concentrates systemic risk. And the $265B US pledge, framed as de-risking, also locks TSMC into a lower-margin geographic footprint that only pays off if AI demand stays structurally high for a decade.

**Importance: 4/5** — The single most important AI-supply-chain data-point of the quarter: TSMC mid-year raising FY guidance from >30% to >40% (vs ~35% consensus), lifting capex to $60–64B, with HPC now 66% of revenue, is the hardest evidence that AI-capex is still accelerating, not peaking. It sets the demand backdrop for the whole hardware stack. ## Top challenge / red-team questions

1. Is the "40% growth" genuinely a raise, or PR anchoring? — **Answered:** Real raise. Prior FY guide was ">30%", consensus ~35%; now "slightly above 40%" in USD. A ~+5–10pp mid-year lift is a substantive signal, not spin.

2. Is the note's "77.4% net-income increase" for Q2 or the whole year? — **Answered:** Q2'26 YoY net income and EPS both +77.4%. Correct figure, though margin-boosted by FX and utilization, not just volume.

3. Are margins actually rising, or masked by FX/one-offs? — **Answered:** GM 67.7% Q2 vs 66.2% Q1 vs ~62% Q4'25. Genuine expansion, but part is favorable NT$/USD FX + high utilization; strip FX and the underlying gain is smaller. Open: exact FX contribution to Q2.

4. How much of growth is AI vs the rest? — **Answered:** HPC = 66% of Q2 revenue (up from 60% a year ago). Growth is overwhelmingly AI/HPC-mix-driven; smartphone/other are flat-to-soft.

5. Is the $265B US number real capital or a headline? — **Partly open:** It's a *planned* cumulative pledge ($165B → $265B) for ≥4 more Arizona fabs at 2nm+, spread over years — announced intent, not deployed capex. Execution/timeline risk is real.

6. Does the US expansion hurt margins? — **Answered (analysis):** Yes, structurally — overseas fabs run below Taiwan margins; management has repeatedly flagged multi-point dilution as US fabs scale. The current 67.7% GM is before full dilution bites.

7. Is TSMC's foundry lead widening or is Samsung catching up? — **Answered:** Widening. TSMC ~72.3% share Q1'26 (up QoQ) vs Samsung ~6.5%; Samsung 2nm yields lag (~55–60% vs TSMC 70–90%). No credible parity at leading edge.

8. What is the true bottleneck — nodes or packaging? — **Answered (analysis):** Advanced packaging (CoWoS) is the binding constraint for AI accelerators; TSMC's CoWoS capacity adds are the number to watch more than wafer nodes.

9. Where does TSMC sit in margin capture across the AI stack? — **Answered (analysis):** At the choke point — Nvidia, Broadcom/OpenAI, AWS Trainium, Etched all must fab at TSMC. It captures margin independent of which chip designer wins → durable pricing power.

10. What's the downside trigger? — **Answered:** An AI-capex "digestion" / ROI disappointment among hyperscalers ("AI spending ceiling"), which would hit the most cyclical, highest-margin HPC mix hardest. Also China export curbs and Taiwan geopolitics.

11. Is guidance credible or aspirational? — **Answered:** Backed by $60–64B capex commitment and booked upstream capacity (ASML 2027 nearly sold out) → capital is being deployed against real orders, not hope.

12. Does concentration make TSMC safer or more fragile? — **Answered (analysis):** More fragile at the system level — as the indispensable node for AI, it concentrates systemic and geopolitical risk even as it strengthens the franchise.

13. Is this a duplicate of any prior TSMC note? — **Answered:** No. Only one `company/tsmc` note in corpus (this one); no prior Q2'26 coverage. FRESH.

14. How does this compare to memory peers riding the same wave? — **Answered:** Micron profit +15x, AI memory shortages lifting device prices — corroborates a broad, not TSMC-specific, AI-hardware upcycle. TSMC's edge is margin + share within it.

15. What would change the central question? — **Open/analysis:** If H2'26 order data shows deceleration despite the raise, the question shifts from "how big is the AI upcycle" to "is the raise the top". Watch CoWoS bookings and Q4 guide.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** TSMC is the pure-play foundry at the base of the AI semiconductor supply chain (all leading-edge accelerators from Nvidia/AMD/hyperscaler ASICs are fabbed here). Sector KPIs it moves on: leading-edge wafer output, advanced-packaging (CoWoS) capacity, node yields. Live signals from Q2 2026 (reported Jul 16, 2026, Taipei): Q2 revenue ~$40.2bn, gross margin 67.7%, EPS $4.31; net income +77% YoY (fifth consecutive record-profit quarter); FY2026 USD revenue-growth target raised to >40% (from >30%); Q3 guide $44.6–45.8bn (per TSMC 6-K / earnings call, via Investing.com & TradingKey, 2026-07-16). **Why now:** demand outstrips supply — 3nm lines running >100% utilization and CoWoS advanced packaging is the industry's primary bottleneck; the raise is a demand-signal for the whole AI-infra stack (compute cost feeds down into cloud, and ultimately into AI/fintech-infra unit economics — see [[Linas Newsletter Who actually makes money from AI]], [[Nvidia to take share of some clients' cloud revenue]]). Structure: extremely consolidated — barriers are capital ($60–64bn/yr capex alone) plus a multi-year process/yield lead; not contestable on price.

**Competitive landscape.** Basis of competition is process leadership + yield + packaging capacity, NOT price. Q1 2026 pure-foundry share (Counterpoint/TrendForce, via design-reuse.com): **TSMC 72.3%** (up from 70.4% QoQ), **Samsung 6.5%** (distant #2, TSMC ~11x its foundry revenue), SMIC 5.1%, UMC 3.9%, GlobalFoundries 3.3%; **Intel Foundry ~0.4%** of global (Q4'25, ~$174m external). TSMC holds >90% of leading-edge production. 2nm-node yield estimates: **TSMC ~65% vs Intel ~55% vs Samsung ~40%** (institutional estimates via FTC/ftcelectronics, 2026) — the yield gap is the moat and it is widening at the node that matters for AI. Intel Foundry still lossmaking: Q1 2026 operating loss ~$2.4bn (improving QoQ on 18A ramp, per Intel Q1'26, via alpha-sense). Recent peer moves: Samsung chasing large 2nm customers and a reported $73bn semi investment (2026); Anthropic reportedly in talks with Samsung on a custom AI chip [[Anthropic in talks with Samsung on custom AI chip]] — i.e. hyperscalers *want* a credible #2 to exist, but haven't yet moved volume off TSMC. **Position: dominant / structurally ahead** — moat = scale + intangible process/yield lead + switching costs (re-taping out a leading-edge design is slow and costly).

**Comps & multiples.** TSMC market cap ~$2.175tn; trailing P/E **33.25x**, forward P/E **20.00x**; stock +73% over the trailing 52 weeks (stockanalysis.com / companiesmarketcap, Jul 2026). The forward-vs-trailing compression (33x → 20x) is consistent with the >40% growth raise — the multiple is *justified by growth*, so NOT flagged as over-valuation. ROE 36.2%, ROIC 51.6% (stockanalysis.com) evidence pricing-power at the node. Cross-peer multiple comparison NOT computed: Samsung foundry is a non-broken-out segment inside Samsung Electronics, and Intel Foundry is loss-making (EBITDA-negative) — no clean like-for-like EV/EBITDA or P/E for the foundry business exists on a free source → **[UNSOURCED]**; comparison stays qualitative. Internal comps (grep fallback; sem broken) are fintech-adjacent, not foundry: [[Nvidia halves list of Asia buyers over China chip export curbs]], [[China orders tech giants to halt Nvidia AI chip purchases]] (demand/geo-risk), [[Nvidia to take share of some clients' cloud revenue]] (AI-infra economics).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Geographic/geopolitical concentration.** The core fab base remains in Taiwan; the raised US commitment ($265bn total: original $65bn + $100bn Mar'25 + new $100bn = 4+ Arizona 2nm fabs, per UPI/Tom's Hardware, 2026-07-16) is explicitly framed to "reduce reliance on Taiwan" — an admission the Taiwan-concentration tail-risk is real. Second-order: any cross-strait disruption is a single point of failure for the *entire* AI-infra stack, not just TSMC.
2. **Capex/margin drag from the US shift.** $60–64bn capex and US fabs carry structurally higher cost; Q3 gross-margin guide 65–67% (down from 67.7%) disappointed on 2nm-ramp + overseas dilution (BigGo, via 2026-07). US 2nm output at parity yield is not proven — execution risk on the diversification itself.
3. **Demand concentration in one cycle.** The >40% raise is levered to AI-accelerator demand; a hyperscaler capex pause (or a shift of volume toward a viable Samsung/Intel #2, which Anthropic-Samsung talks hint at) would hit the leading-edge/CoWoS book that is carrying the growth.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a demand *confirmation* for the whole AI-infra layer, not a re-rating of TSMC itself — the forward multiple already compressed into the growth (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: TSMC's leading-edge quasi-monopoly holds through 2026 as long as the 2nm yield gap vs Samsung/Intel stays >10pts. **Watch:** (a) whether any hyperscaler actually moves *volume* (not just talks) to Samsung/Intel 2nm — the first real dual-sourcing is the trigger that breaks the monopoly narrative; (b) US-fab yield parity milestones; (c) Q3 gross-margin trajectory as the tell on how costly the Taiwan-de-risking is.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Verdict (headline read).** BEAT · Q2 2026 (qtr ended Jun 30) revenue US$40.20bn / NT$1,270.38bn (+36.0% YoY, +12.0% QoQ; above own guide of $39.0–40.2bn and ~$39.9bn public consensus) · diluted EPS NT$27.25 / US$4.31 per ADR (+77.4% YoY; vs ~$3.77–3.83 consensus, a clear beat) · driver: AI/HPC demand + operating leverage · guidance action: raised FY2026 revenue-growth outlook to "slightly above 40%" (from "over 30%") and lifted FY capex to $60–64bn (from $52–56bn). Record net income for the 5th consecutive quarter.

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Revenue: NT$1,270.38bn / US$40.20bn, +36.0% YoY, +12.0% QoQ.
- Net income: NT$706.56bn, +77.4% YoY, +23.4% QoQ.
- Diluted EPS: NT$27.25 (US$4.31/ADR), +77.4% YoY.
- Gross margin 67.7%; operating margin 60.3%; net margin 55.6% — very strong profitability; margin expansion driven by pricing, utilization and operating leverage from AI-node demand (net income +77.4% far outpacing revenue +36.0%).

**By segment / driver.** High-performance computing (the AI-chip platform) = 66% of Q2 revenue; smartphones = 22%. Growth is disproportionately HPC/AI-led — this is the engine behind both the top-line acceleration and the margin outperformance. The mix shift toward advanced nodes (leading-edge N3/N5) is the structural driver, not a one-off.

**vs expectations / prior period.** BEAT on both lines. Revenue $40.20bn vs public consensus ~$39.9bn (as of pre-print, Jul 15–16 2026) — small top-line beat, and above the top end of TSMC's own $39.0–40.2bn guide. EPS US$4.31/ADR vs consensus ~$3.77 (Zacks) to ~$3.83 — a meaningful bottom-line beat, i.e. margin-driven upside, not just revenue. YoY momentum: revenue +36.0% and net income +77.4%; net income growth roughly 2x revenue growth signals sustained operating leverage rather than a one-time item. First TSMC report in this corpus (no prior quarterly note to wikilink); trajectory therefore assessed vs disclosed YoY/QoQ only.

**Guidance / forward.** RAISED. (1) FY2026 revenue-growth outlook lifted to "slightly above 40%" in US-dollar terms, up from the prior "over 30%" — a large upgrade mid-year, management framing it on structural (incl. agentic-AI) demand. (2) FY2026 capex raised to $60–64bn from $52–56bn — funds N3/leading-edge capacity (Taiwan, Arizona, Japan) plus a further $100bn US commitment (total US pledge to ~$265bn). (3) Q3 2026 guide: revenue $44.6–45.8bn (implies continued sequential acceleration); gross margin 65–67%; operating margin 56–58%. Note the Q3 gross-margin guide (65–67%) is a step DOWN from Q2's 67.7% and landed below some analyst hopes — management attributes ~2–3pp full-year dilution to the 2nm (N2) ramp. Management tone: confident/bullish on AI demand durability.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. AI-demand durability CONFIRMED for now — FY revenue-growth guide raised to >40% and HPC at 66% of mix. Why it matters: TSMC is the cleanest supply-side read-through on AI capex; a raised guide validates the "AI buildout still accelerating" thesis. Second-order: supports downstream Nvidia/AMD/hyperscaler capex narratives.
2. Margin ceiling flagged, not breached — Q3 GM guide 65–67% vs Q2 67.7%, with 2nm ramp diluting margins 2–3pp over FY. Why it matters: the de-PR read is that record top-line coexists with a near-term margin peak; watch whether N2 pricing offsets ramp cost. Second-order: cyclical margin softness could cap multiple even as revenue accelerates.
3. Capex step-up ($60–64bn) + $100bn extra US investment — geographic de-risking away from Taiwan reliance, but overseas fabs are structurally lower-margin. Why it matters: near-term FCF headwind and a longer-run margin drag traded for supply security and political goodwill. Second-order: elevated capex signals confidence in multi-year demand, but raises the bar on utilization.
4. Growth quality — net income +77.4% >> revenue +36.0% is genuine operating leverage, not a disclosed one-off; sustainability is the key watch-item into 2027 as N2 dilution and FX cut in.
