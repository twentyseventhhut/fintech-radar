# Cyber unicorn Aikido acquires Israeli startup Root for $70-$100 million

## The Belgian company expands its AI security push and will open a new development center in Israel following the deal.

Meir Orbach

17:00, 30.06.26 

Belgian cybersecurity unicorn Aikido Security is acquiring Israeli cybersecurity company Root, which developed an AI platform for securing open-source components. Following the acquisition, Aikido will open a development center in Israel that will absorb all of Root’s employees and is expected to further expand its local workforce. The value of the acquisition was not disclosed, but is estimated at between $70 million and $100 million.

The acquisition is the latest in a series of deals made by Aikido over the past year, following its purchases of AI-based code review company Trag and autonomous penetration testing companies Allseek and Haicker. The company serves more than 100,000 teams worldwide, including clients such as the Premier League, Revolut, SoundCloud, and Niantic. Earlier this year, Aikido completed a Series B funding round of $60 million at a valuation of $1 billion, bringing its total funding to approximately $85 million.

Root founders.

Root began as Slim.AI, the developer of the open-source Slim Toolkit. The company was founded by Benji Kalman, Mickey Gordon, Ian Riopel, and John Amaral. It employs about 25 people, of whom approximately 15 are based in its Tel Aviv development center, and has raised about $31 million to date from venture capital funds including Insight Partners, Decibel Ventures, Boldstart Ventures, Lema Partners, and TechAviv. Its clients include SiXworks (a subsidiary of IBM), DeleteMe, and Relay Networks.

“Open source needs patching, and it needs it fast. Today you have two options, and neither works for most companies: upgrade and likely break your application, or migrate to a vendor's locked-down replacement,” said Willem Delbare, co-founder and CEO of Aikido Security. “With Root, we fix what teams are actually running, generating hundreds of verified patches a day: no upgrades, no migrations, no breaking changes. That's how supply chain security gets solved for everyone, not just the 1%.”

# Context-enrichment: Aikido Security acquires Root (framed as "raises round")
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

> [!warning] Title/frame correction
> The note title says Aikido "raises round for developer security tools." That is inaccurate.
> The actual event (StrictlyVC / CTech, 30 Jun 2026) is an **M&A**: Aikido Security is **acquiring**
> Tel-Aviv startup **Root** for an estimated **$70M–$100M** (undisclosed). The "$60M Series B at $1B
> valuation" is *prior* background (announced Jan 2026, led by DST Global), not the subject of this news.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **The deal:** Aikido Security (Ghent, Belgium; cyber "unicorn," founded 2022) is acquiring **Root**
  (Tel Aviv, ~25 staff, ~15 in TLV), an AI platform that auto-patches open-source / container
  vulnerabilities **in place** — no version upgrades, no migrations, no forced code changes. Price
  **undisclosed**, estimated **$70–100M** by CTech. Aikido will open an Israeli dev center absorbing
  all Root staff. (Source: CTech/Calcalist, 30 Jun 2026.)
- **Root's real identity:** Root began as **Slim.AI** (maker of the open-source Slim Toolkit),
  founders Benji Kalman, Mickey Gordon, Ian Riopel, John Amaral. Raised **~$31M** (Insight Partners,
  Decibel, Boldstart, Lema, TechAviv). This is a **pivot-then-sell**: a repositioning from
  container-slimming to AI open-source patching, exited at a modest premium over capital raised —
  not a breakout outcome.
- **Mechanism claims (vendor-stated, verify):** AI agents ship individual patches in **15–40 min**,
  remediating **>80%** of analysed cases without manual code changes. Aikido says it will **backport
  fixes for critical/exploited OSS bugs to the community and upstream them** rather than paywall them.
  These are announced capabilities, not independently audited traction.
- **Why framed this way:** the PR anchors to the aspirational "supply-chain security for everyone, not
  just the 1%" and to speed metrics (15–40 min, 80%) — classic AI-remediation framing. The de-PR'd
  reality: a **~$1B unicorn using fresh Series-B capital and a $1B currency to roll up niche AI-security
  teams** (Root is the 4th tuck-in in ~12 months). The story is **consolidation strategy**, not a
  product breakthrough.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Developer-first AppSec / SCA:** **Snyk** (market leader in dev-centric AppSec; the incumbent Aikido
  positions against), **Semgrep** (default OSS SAST, aggressive pricing), **Endor Labs** (ASPM +
  reachability for OSS supply chain), **Checkmarx**, **Veracode** (legacy SAST/DAST), **Trivy** (OSS SCA).
- **Cloud side:** **Wiz** dominates cloud/CNAPP; Aikido's "code→cloud→runtime" pitch overlaps at the edges.
- **The differentiator Aikido is buying:** most tools **find** vulnerabilities; Root claims to **fix**
  them in place (backported patches without upgrades). That is a genuine mechanism delta vs Snyk/Semgrep,
  IF the auto-patching works at claimed reliability. **(analysis)** The market's stated 2026 pain is
  "too many tools reporting the same alerts, nobody remediates" — so a credible auto-fix layer is the
  scarce, valuable piece.
- **Position:** Aikido is a fast-follower consolidating capabilities (code review = Trag, pen-testing =
  Allseek/Haicker, OSS patching = Root) into one platform to undercut Snyk on breadth + price. It is
  **catching up on surface area, potentially ahead on remediation** — but far behind Snyk/Wiz on scale.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Trajectory:** Founded 2022 (Willem Delbare, CEO). Jan 2026: **$60M Series B at $1B valuation** (led
  by **DST Global**, w/ PSG Equity, Notion Capital, Singular) — billed as fastest European cyber co to
  unicorn; ~5x revenue growth, ~3x customer growth. Total raised **~$84–85M**. 100k+ teams; named
  clients: **Revolut** (see corpus), Premier League, SoundCloud, Niantic.
- **Acquisition cadence (last ~12 mo):** **Trag** (Aug 2025, AI code review) → **Allseek + Haicker**
  (Sep 2025, autonomous pen-testing → "Aikido Attack," targeting the ~$6B pen-test market) → **Root**
  (Jun 2026, OSS auto-patching). Four tuck-ins in a year.
- **Why it acts this way (analysis):** having raised at a $1B mark on a "one platform for the whole SDLC"
  thesis, Aikido must **justify the multiple by owning the full find→fix→attack loop** before Snyk/Wiz
  close the gap. With a unicorn currency and fresh cash, buying teams (esp. Israeli AI-security talent) is
  faster than building. The Israeli dev center is also a **talent-acquisition play** as much as a product one.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new:** in-place OSS patching that avoids the upgrade/migrate dilemma — the real
  operational pain in supply-chain security. If the "80% auto-remediated, 15–40 min" claims hold in the
  field, that shifts Aikido from a *scanner* to a *remediator* — the part of the stack that actually
  captures value.
- **What is NOT proven:** all remediation numbers are **vendor-stated**; no independent adoption/false-
  positive data. Root's own commercial traction was modest (~$31M raised, clients incl. an IBM subsidiary,
  DeleteMe, Relay) and it had already **pivoted once** (Slim.AI → Root). The exit at ~$70–100M on ~$31M
  raised is a **soft landing / acqui-hire-plus**, not a validation of standalone demand.
- **Who captures the margin (analysis):** value in AppSec is migrating from "detection" (commoditized —
  Semgrep/Trivy are free) to **automated remediation + platform correlation (ASPM)**. Aikido is betting the
  margin sits in auto-fix. The risk: **foundation-model vendors** and the LLM-coding tools (Copilot,
  Cursor) could commoditize "AI patches your code," compressing the moat to distribution + trust.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Plans:** stand up an Israeli R&D center, integrate Root's engine into the Aikido platform, and — per
  PR — **upstream/backport community fixes** for critical OSS bugs (goodwill + top-of-funnel).
- **Market backdrop:** 2026 AppSec is a **consolidation market** — buyers tired of tool sprawl and

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is developer-first application security (AppSec) / DevSecOps, and specifically the software-supply-chain / open-source (SCA) sub-segment — the layer that scans code, cloud and runtime and now patches third-party CVEs. DevSecOps market ~$8.9–11.7bn in 2026 depending on the definition, growing at ~13% CAGR toward ~$26bn by 2033 (per Coherent Market Insights / Precedence Research / Mordor Intelligence, via web, as of 2026; multiple secondary estimates, treat as directional). "Why now": (1) AI-generated code is exploding the volume of dependencies and vulnerabilities engineering teams must triage, shifting the bottleneck from *finding* CVEs to *fixing* them — Aikido's own thesis with the Root deal is "stop debating which vulns to fix, just fix the versions already running"; (2) regulatory tailwind on supply-chain provenance (SBOM mandates, EU/US executive-order pressure) keeps SCA a board-level line item. Structure: fragmented and consolidating fast — a long tail of point tools (SAST/DAST/SCA/secrets/pentest) is being rolled into single "code-to-cloud" platforms; barriers are distribution into developer workflows (GitHub/CI) and breadth of ecosystem coverage rather than capital.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: ARR, NRR, and (developer-tool-specific) teams/seats and ecosystem coverage; Aikido does not disclose ARR → `[UNSOURCED]`. Traction it does disclose: >100,000 teams, clients incl. Revolut, Premier League, SoundCloud, Niantic (per CTech, 2026-06-30; corpus confirms [[Revolut selects Aikido Security for software security]], 2025-11). Key players by tier: incumbents/scale — Snyk (~$300m+ ARR, last private valuation ~$7.4bn, IPO-watch in 2026), Semgrep ($100m Series D, Feb-2025, Menlo), GitHub Advanced Security; supply-chain specialists — Endor Labs (~$188m raised), Socket (SCA, ~$1,200/yr), RapidFort; cloud-security giant Wiz (acquired by Google for $32bn, closed 2026-03-11). Basis of competition: developer UX + all-in-one breadth + price (Aikido positions "no-BS", low-friction) vs. depth/enterprise (Endor at ~$20k+/yr). Recent moves: Aikido has run a roll-up — Trag (AI code review), Allseek + Haicker (autonomous pentest), now Root (in-place open-source patching, →"Aikido Libraries"), all inside ~12 months. Protagonist position: a fast-rising *challenger* — fastest-ever European cyber unicorn — carving a niche as the consolidated, developer-friendly SCA/AppSec suite; moat is distribution + product breadth and switching cost once embedded in CI (analysis).

**Risk flags.**
