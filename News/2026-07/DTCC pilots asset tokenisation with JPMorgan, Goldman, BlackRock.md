DTCC pilots asset tokenisation with JPMorgan, Goldman, BlackRock
Claudia Pincovski
17 Jul 2026 / 4 Min Read
The Depository Trust and Clearing Corporation (DTCC) has completed a day-long exercise involving tokenised versions of assets held at The Depository Trust Company, according to the organisation.
Trial brings together traditional and digital finance participants
The event involved more than 25 firms spanning traditional finance and digital assets. JPMorgan opened proceedings by converting the Invesco QQQ Trust into a tokenised asset. Goldman Sachs, BlackRock, Vanguard, and the New York Stock Exchange were among the other participants, a DTCC spokesperson confirmed.
A DTCC official described the initiative as an early step intended to demonstrate that established and emerging infrastructure can operate together, adding that the exercise is expected to lay the groundwork for a broader, scalable launch in October 2026.
Tokenisation refers to the process of issuing digital representations of publicly traded securities, real-world assets, or other forms of value on a blockchain network. Proponents of the approach point to potential benefits including lower costs, faster settlement, greater transparency around ownership and performance, and programmable terms, alongside broader accessibility for retail investors.
Established clearing infrastructure faces competition from crypto-native entrants
DTCC serves as the central post-trade organisation within the US financial system, processing, settling and safeguarding the majority of stock and bond transactions. Its subsidiaries processed USD 4.7 quadrillion worth of securities transactions in 2025.
Tokenisation has been debated within the financial industry for several years without widespread implementation. During that period, specialist firms from the crypto sector, including Ondo and Securitize, have entered the space, forming partnerships with established asset managers such as BlackRock and creating competitive pressure for incumbent clearing infrastructure.
A DTCC official said the tokenisation service is intended to connect traditional finance with decentralised finance on infrastructure that has underpinned capital markets for decades, citing potential gains in efficiency, liquidity, and asset mobility as objectives of the programme.
Demonstration spans multiple use cases
The exercise was designed to show how tokenised assets could function across collateral management, repo transactions, equities, margin and asset transfers, using existing financial infrastructure rather than parallel systems.
Assets expected to be tokenised during the demonstration include shares of Microsoft, Circle Internet Group, the Invesco QQQ Trust, the State Street SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust, and the iShares 0-3 Month Treasury Bond ETF, as well as Treasury securities of varying maturities.
The trial represents a tangible, if early-stage, step towards broader adoption of tokenised settlement within mainstream capital markets infrastructure, with DTCC positioning the October 2026 timeline as the next milestone for scaling the service beyond a single-day demonstration.

# Context-enrichment: DTCC pilots asset tokenisation with JPMorgan, Goldman, BlackRock
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **17 Jul 2026** DTCC ran a **single-day, live production demonstration** of its DTC Tokenization Service — tokenised versions of assets already custodied at The Depository Trust Company (DTC). **>25 firms** (some outlets: ~40–50+ onboarded) took part. JPMorgan opened by converting the **Invesco QQQ Trust** into a tokenised asset; Goldman Sachs, BlackRock, Vanguard, Citadel Securities, Société Générale, CME Group and NYSE among participants. Assets in scope: Microsoft, Circle, QQQ, SPY, iShares 0–3M Treasury ETF and Treasuries of varying maturities — i.e. **only highly-liquid Russell-1000 equities, major ETFs and Treasuries** (exactly the asset set the Dec 2025 SEC no-action letter permits). Use-cases shown: collateral, repo, equities, margin, asset transfers — notably **JPM pledging tokenised collateral to CME Group to meet CCP margin**. Platform: DTCC's **ComposerX** suite (on Azure), multi-ledger across **DTCC AppChain (Besu/Ethereum-compatible), Canton Network and Stellar**, with a patented **Compliance-Aware Token Framework (CATF)**. DTCC frames it as "an early step" ahead of a **broader, scalable launch targeted October 2026**.
**+ Why framed this way / what it reveals:** DTCC anchors on its scale halo (subsidiaries processed **$4.7 quadrillion** of securities transactions in 2025; ~$114T custodied) but discloses **zero tokenised transaction volume** — because there is essentially none yet. The token is a **DLT mirror of an existing DTC security entitlement**, not a re-plumbing of the settlement core: per the SEC no-action letter these tokens carry **NO settlement or collateral value for DTC's own risk-management**. So "JPM pledges to CME" happens at the CCP/bilateral layer over a compliance-wrapped representation — the DTC master record stays authoritative. This is a **pilot/demo, explicitly reversible and time-limited (3-yr no-action, ~to Dec 2028)** — "piloted," not "live at volume."

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Broadridge DLR (Distributed Ledger Repo)** — the incumbent by REAL volume: **~$7.5T in June 2026 (~$357B ADV)**, running ~$7–9T/mo — live production, not a demo. This is the benchmark DTCC's ~zero tokenised volume must be measured against.
- **JPMorgan Kinexys (ex-Onyx) / Tokenized Collateral Network (TCN)** — live tokenised-MMF collateral pledges, cumulative "in the trillions"; Dec 2025 launched a tokenised MMF on Ethereum. **JPM is simultaneously a DTCC participant AND a competing rail.**
- **Canton Network / Digital Asset** — the DTCC-selected chain (DTCC co-chairs the Canton Foundation with Euroclear); more partner than rival now.
- **BlackRock BUIDL (via Securitize)** — tokenised MMF, ~$2.9B AUM mid-2025, accepted as Binance off-exchange collateral (Nov 2025). BlackRock also a DTCC participant.
- **Fnality** (GBP settlement live), **HQLAx** (EU collateral mobility), **Ondo/Securitize** (tokenised Treasuries — now inside DTCC's tent) — see [[Ondo Finance launches custodial tokenized securities with Broadridge]].
**+ Second-order dynamics:** The competitive fault line isn't "can it be tokenised" — Broadridge already does trillions monthly — it's **who owns the authoritative registry**. DTCC's edge is incumbency: it IS the US post-trade utility, so its token can inherit legal finality and existing plumbing that a crypto-native (Ondo, Securitize) cannot. But that same incumbency plus the no-action's "no risk-management value" clause means DTCC is moving cautiously precisely where challengers move fast — the fight is over **standard-setting, not tech**, echoing DTCC's own message in [[Citi and DTCC urge regulators to keep pace with tokenized collateral]].

## [2] Company history / fit
Consistent, telegraphed roadmap: **Project Ion** (DLT settlement PoC, parallel-production since ~2022) → **Digital Launchpad** (Oct 2024 sandbox) → **Collateral AppChain / "Great Collateral Experiment"** (Apr 2025) → **SEC no-action letter to DTC** (Dec 11 2025) → **Canton Network selection** for DTC-custodied Treasuries, MVP H1 2026 ([[DTCC selects Canton Network for Treasury tokenization]], [[DTCC subsidiary receives SEC no-action letter for tokenized securities]]) → **Chainlink** for pricing on the AppChain (May 2026) → **"service advanced," 50+ firms** (May 2026) → **this Jul 2026 production demo** → **Oct 2026 launch**. Interoperability across chains is a stated priority ([[The DTCC is prioritizing interoperability across different blockchain networks to ensure that]]).
**+ Why DTCC acts this way:** As an industry-owned utility DTCC faces **disintermediation risk** if tokenised settlement migrates to crypto-native rails (Ondo, Securitize + BlackRock partnerships create exactly that pressure — the note text names this competitive threat explicitly). Building its own multi-ledger, compliance-wrapped service is a **defensive land-grab**: keep the authoritative record inside the utility while offering the DLT benefits (atomic DvP, 24/7, real-time mobility) that clients would otherwise seek elsewhere.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Genuinely new vs prior notes: this is the **first live, multi-firm production demonstration** (named counterparties, real tokenisation of specific instruments, JPM→CME margin flow) — an escalation from Dec-2025 announcements of *authority and platform choice* to *demonstrated multi-participant use*. The distinctive tech is the **multi-ledger ComposerX + CATF** (rules embedded in the token). **Traction, honestly: near-zero.** No tokenised volume disclosed; it is a one-day exercise; "expected to be tokenised" language on several assets = demo-ware, not throughput.
**+ Why value-add is real-but-capped:** The real prize is DTCC keeping the **registry + legal finality** while adding programmability — that's a durable moat if it converts. But the margin-capture question stays open: the no-action letter denies the token **risk-management/collateral value at DTC**, and the **cash leg is unspecified** (tokenised deposit? stablecoin? Fedwire bridge?) — without atomic tokenised cash, "atomic DvP" is aspirational. So the value-add is a **standards/registry play**, not yet a settlement-economics change; Broadridge's live $7–9T/mo shows the demand exists, but DTCC has not shown it will capture it.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Next milestone: **October 2026 "scalable launch."** Regulatory backdrop: SEC (Peirce, "Tokenization Trending") is permissive **via no-action, not a rule** — 3-yr runway (~Dec 2028), so a **permanent rulemaking is still required** for durability; GENIUS Act is adjacent context (stablecoin cash-leg), **not** the operative authority here.
**+ Counterintuitive second-order effects:** (analysis) The bull read is "DTCC blesses tokenisation → mainstream adoption." The skeptic read: DTCC's incumbency could **standardise challengers out** OR its caution (voluntary, reversible, no risk-management value, mainframe stays authoritative) could keep this in "parallel production" limbo for years — as Project Ion arguably was. The central question is not "is tokenisation happening" (it is, at Broadridge) but **"does the authoritative US registry move on-chain, and at the Oct-2026/Dec-2028 checkpoints does DTCC show real daily tokenised volume or stay demo-ware."**

## Top challenge / red-team questions (second-order)

1. **What is the actual tokenised daily/monthly volume?** — Open. DTCC disclosed none; this was a one-day demo. Measured against Broadridge DLR's live ~$7.5T/mo (Jun 2026), pilot volume is effectively zero.
2. **Is this new or a re-announcement of Dec 2025?** — Answered: genuinely NEW milestone. Dec 2025 = SEC no-action + Canton selection (authority/platform); Jul 2026 = first live multi-firm production demo with named flows (JPM→CME margin). Escalation, not reprint.
3. **Since tokens carry NO DTC risk-management/collateral value (per the no-action letter), what real efficiency does JPM→CME margin gain vs existing triparty?** — Open/skeptical. Likely marketing of a compliance-wrapped mirror at the CCP layer, not a settlement-core change.
4. **Where is the cash leg?** — Open. Atomic DvP needs tokenised cash (tokenised deposit, GENIUS-stablecoin, or Fedwire bridge). Not specified; without it "atomic settlement" is aspirational.
5. **Which of the three ledgers (AppChain / Canton / Stellar) actually gets institutional flow — and does multi-chain support fragment liquidity rather than unify it?** — Open. Interoperability is a stated priority but unproven at stress.
6. **Is JPM (Kinexys/TCN) and BlackRock (BUIDL) participating to ADOPT DTCC's rail or to DEFEND their own competing rails?** — Open. Both are simultaneously participants and competitors; dual-track hedging is the likely read (analysis).
7. **Does the tokenised security entitlement (UCC Art. 8) survive a DTC/participant insolvency with the same finality as the centralised ledger?** — Open; legal enforceability untested at stress (DTCC itself flagged legal-finality, not tech, as the barrier — see Citi/DTCC note).
8. **What happens at the ~Dec 2028 no-action expiry?** — Open. Authority is discretionary/revocable; a permanent SEC rulemaking is required for durability and is not yet on a public calendar.
9. **Does DTCC's incumbency crowd out challengers (Broadridge, Fnality, HQLAx, Ondo) or standardise them in?** — Open. DTCC controls the authoritative US registry → structural leverage, but caution could cede live volume to Broadridge in the interim.
10. **Is the Oct 2026 "scalable launch" a hard commercial launch or another framed milestone?** — Open. History (Project Ion sat in parallel-production for years) counsels skepticism that "launch" = real throughput.
11. **Why only Russell-1000 equities, major ETFs and Treasuries?** — Answered: exactly the asset set the SEC no-action letter permits; scope is regulation-bounded, not ambition-bounded.
12. **How many firms are REALLY active vs onboarded?** — Open. Note says ">25"; other outlets say ~40–50+. Onboarded ≠ transacting.
13. **Does this cannibalise DTCC's own $4.7-quadrillion legacy franchise, and is it therefore structurally incentivised to go slow?** — Answered (analysis): yes — as an industry-owned utility DTCC has disintermediation-defence incentives, which explains a reversible, low-stakes pilot design.
14. **Is Canton (where DTCC co-chairs the Foundation with Euroclear) a neutral rail or a governance-capture play?** — Open. DTCC's governance seat aligns incentives but raises concentration/neutrality questions.
15. **What breaks the thesis?** — If Oct 2026 ships without disclosed tokenised volume, or the no-action lapses without a rule, or the cash leg stays unsolved, this stays demo-ware while Broadridge/Kinexys keep the live flow.

Importance: 4/5 — DTCC is the authoritative US post-trade utility, and the FIRST live multi-firm production demo (JPM, Goldman, BlackRock, Vanguard, Citadel, CME, NYSE) tokenising real DTC-custodied assets is a materially significant, market-defining milestone with a concrete Oct-2026 launch date. Held below 5 because it is an explicitly early, reversible, zero-disclosed-volume pilot bounded by a discretionary 3-year SEC no-action letter, with the cash leg and true settlement economics unresolved — announcement weight, not yet adoption.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Institutional RWA tokenisation is small but inflecting: on-chain tokenised RWAs (ex-stablecoins) were ~$24bn by Feb 2026 (+266% in 2025) and ~$34bn by early July 2026 (per RWA.xyz, via CoinDesk/Canton). Forecasts diverge by an order of magnitude and should be read as scenarios, not TAM: McKinsey ~$2tn tokenised financial assets by 2030 (conservative); Standard Chartered up to ~$30tn by 2034 (bullish) — the spread comes from whether the "money layer" (deposits/stablecoins) is counted. Structure: the post-trade layer DTCC sits in is a regulated near-monopoly — DTCC subsidiaries settled USD 4.7 quadrillion of securities transactions in 2025 (per DTCC, via note) — with very high barriers (regulation, network effects, decades of incumbency). Why now: (1) the SEC's 11-Dec-2025 no-action letter gives DTC a ~3-year window to run a tokenisation service (via CoinDesk), removing the key regulatory blocker; (2) crypto-native entrants (Ondo, Securitize) partnering with BlackRock created competitive pressure the incumbent had to answer. Second-order: if settlement compresses toward atomic/T+0, the float and fails-management economics that fund parts of legacy post-trade shrink — DTCC is defending its rails, not just adding a feature.

**Competitive landscape.** KPIs this sub-sector runs on: on-chain settled volume / AUM tokenised, number of live (not "announced") institutional participants, and settlement finality (atomic DvP vs batch). Players & basis of competition (infrastructure control + network, not price): incumbent post-trade (DTCC — the pilot host); tokenised-settlement rails (Broadridge DLR; Fnality; Canton Network / Digital Asset; JPMorgan's Kinexys); tokenised-asset issuers (BlackRock BUIDL, Securitize, Ondo, Franklin Templeton). Recent dated moves: DTCC ran the day-long exercise 15–16 Jul 2026 with 25+ (some reports 40–50+) firms — JPMorgan tokenised Invesco QQQ, with Goldman, BlackRock, Vanguard, NYSE — targeting a scalable launch Oct 2026 (via note; CoinDesk 15-Jul-2026). Broadridge DLR processed ~$368bn/day and ~$7.4tn in Nov 2025, ~$9tn in Dec 2025, +457% YoY in Feb 2026 (per Broadridge). Kinexys is issuing JPMD deposit-token natively on Canton in a phased 2026 rollout (per Digital Asset/PRNewswire, Jan 2026); >$4tn annual tokenised volume reportedly flows through Canton. DTCC's position: **ahead on distribution / catching up on technology** — it owns the connectivity to essentially all US securities but is arriving after Broadridge/Canton already have live institutional volume. Moat = regulatory franchise + network effects (`analysis`); the risk is that the moat is in clearing, and settlement may migrate onto rails it doesn't own.

**Comps & multiples.** Protagonist (DTCC) is a member-owned utility — no market cap, no traded multiple → **valuation multiples: no data**. Reference points for the tokenisation stack:
- **Fnality** — Series C $136m (Sep 2025), >$280m raised since 2019, backers incl. Bank of America, Citi, WisdomTree, Temasek, Tradeweb (per The Block/Fnality). Round funding, not market cap.
- **Digital Asset (Canton)** — ~$499m raised across 11 rounds incl. a $355m Series C led by a16z crypto (per search); private, round valuation `[UNSOURCED]` (post-money not disclosed in results).
- **BlackRock BUIDL** — ~$2.9bn AUM, ~40% of the tokenised-Treasury market (2026); **Securitize** ~$3.9bn tokenised AUM H2-2025; **Ondo** ~$3.78bn TVL, >70% share of tokenised equities (per stablecoininsider/BlockEden). These are AUM, not valuations — sizing the demand DTCC is chasing.
- Internal comps (base): [[Ondo Finance launches custodial tokenized securities with Broadridge]] · [[JPMorgan Kinexys launches blockchain fund-flows tool]] · [[Citi and DTCC urge regulators to keep pace with tokenized collateral]] · [[Paradigm leads seed in M1X for tokenized sovereign debt]] · [[Franklin Templeton and DBS launch Singapore's first tokenised retail fund]] · [[Cyclops raises $20M for faster stablecoin settlement]].
Distribution not computed (mix of round valuations and AUM, not comparable) — qualitative only.

**IR grounding.** JPMorgan and Goldman are IR-covered (jp-morgan, goldman-sachs; latest EDGAR filings through mid-2026), but neither breaks out tokenisation-pilot economics in filings, so their reported figures are **not primary evidence for this story** — the pilot is a DTCC-run demo, and DTCC is a private utility that does not file. Fold-in is therefore contextual only, not a numbers ground.

**Risk flags.**
1. **"Announced" vs "live" gap / execution risk.** A one-day, 25-firm demo with a "broader launch" slated for Oct 2026 is a proof-of-concept, not production; the Oct milestone is the real test. Tokenisation has been "debated for years without widespread implementation" (per note) — repeated pilots, thin live volume.
2. **Disintermediation of the settlement layer.** DTCC's franchise is clearing/depository; if atomic on-chain settlement matures on rails it doesn't control (Canton, Broadridge DLR, Fnality), value could migrate down-stack and compress the incumbent's fee/float economics — it is defending, not expanding.
3. **Regulatory dependency / narrow runway.** The whole service rests on the SEC's Dec-2025 no-action letter with a ~3-year window and conditions; a policy reversal or failure to convert to permanent rules would strand the build-out.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is the incumbent post-trade utility co-opting tokenisation rather than being disrupted by it — a defensive consolidation of the standard onto DTCC's connectivity. Falsifiable thesis: if DTCC's Oct-2026 launch reaches sustained multi-hundred-billion daily tokenised volume (Broadridge-DLR scale), the "crypto-native settlement will bypass incumbents" narrative weakens and the base-case is re-centralisation around DTCC/Canton. Trigger/watch: the Oct-2026 go-live scope (live participants, asset classes, DvP finality) and whether Kinexys/Canton interoperate with, or route around, DTCC. What breaks the thesis: Oct slips again or launches with token participation and negligible volume.
