# Context-enrichment: ClearBank and Mambu discuss multi-rail modular core banking
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
This is **thought-leadership / co-marketing, not news.** The item (Future of Banking, 2026-07-08) is a joint discussion in which ClearBank and Mambu talk about how "modular core banking + multi-rail infrastructure" can help banks support both fiat and stablecoin payments. There is **no new deal, no product launch, no numbers.**
- **"Multi-rail modular core banking" is a marketing concept, not a named product.** It bundles two pre-existing real things: (a) Mambu's composable/modular core (ledger + product modules) and (b) ClearBank's licensed payment-rail connectivity (FPS/Bacs/CHAPS in the UK; SEPA/EUR via ClearBank Europe; and now stablecoin settlement).
- **The underlying tie-up is 5+ years old.** ClearBank and Mambu first announced Mambu's integration into ClearBank's ISO-20022 API on **9 June 2020**; Mambu maintains productized "ClearBank Retail" and "ClearBank Lenders" connectors on Mambu Process Orchestrator. So the plumbing is real and live — but old.
- **→ Why framed this way:** The "fiat + stablecoin / multi-rail" hook is 2026 zeitgeist bolted onto a 2020 integration. It maps onto ClearBank's genuine 2026 pivot (ClearBank Europe: MiCAR CASP status, EURC/USDC stablecoins, Digital Asset Rails) and Mambu's 2025 Payments Hub (built on its Numeral acquisition). The commentary is real strategy dressed as an "announcement." **→ Second order:** the value here is a signal of where both vendors are steering (stablecoin rails inside the core), not an event.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Composable / cloud-native core banking:
- **Thought Machine (Vault Core):** revenue >$100M in 2025 (+57% YoY); new ~£30m/$41m tier-1-bank funding reported ~6 Jul 2026; last headline valuation ~$2.7bn (Series D, 2022). Healthiest independent challenger.
- **Temenos:** #1 core-banking vendor (IBS League, 21st yr); publicly listed; SaaS core wins (e.g. Questrade). Cf. corpus [[VPBank moves to cloud-native core banking with Temenos, Red Hat]].
- **10x Banking, Tuum, Solaris, Finxact/Fiserv:** active peers; Tuum in corpus [[Tuum secures strategic partnership extension with Multitude Bank to power long-term growth and innovation]].
- On rails/BaaS side ClearBank sits alongside Engine by Starling, Solaris, Griffin. Corpus: [[Tangerine signs with Engine by Starling]].
**→ Why the landscape is this way:** cores (Mambu/TM) don't hold licences; licensed-rail players (ClearBank) don't own the ledger. Multi-rail modular banking is the natural "core + licensed rails" bundle — each side needs the other, hence recurring co-marketing rather than a merger.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **ClearBank:** UK clearing/agency/embedded-banking bank. FY2025 (reported 15 Apr 2026): **3rd consecutive year of UK profitability**, UK PBT £12.2m (+53%); group normalised revenue £121.6m (+34%); **statutory pretax LOSS £16.7m** (deliberate EU investment); deposits £17.8bn (+65%); S&P BBB-. EU build-out via Netherlands (ECB credit-institution licence, DNB-supervised) + first Dutch bank with MiCAR CASP status (Apr 2026). Corpus: [[ClearBank posts third year of UK profitability, revenue up 34%]], [[ClearBank Europe first Dutch institution to complete MiCAR notification]], [[ClearBank EU to offer EURC and USDC stablecoins]], [[ClearBank joins Circle's payment network]].
- **Mambu:** cloud-native composable core (founded 2011). Series E €235m at €4.9bn (Dec 2021, EQT-led); no fresh up-round since (valuation likely stale). Founder-CEO exit 2023 → Fernando Zandona CEO since Aug 2023. Live customers in corpus: [[Akbank's German unit goes live on Mambu core]], [[ABN Amro picks Mambu to power neobank Buut]], [[Mambu selected as core banking provider by Nyla]], [[Wio Bank leverages Mambu for composable banking]].
**→ Why they act this way:** both are pushing into stablecoin/multi-rail because their core/rails businesses are commoditizing; adding stablecoin settlement is the differentiation-and-multiple play. The joint messaging is a distribution tactic (each vendor's clients are the other's prospects).

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Low novelty as news** — the integration dates to 2020; the "fiat + stablecoin" angle is the only 2026 element, and it's asserted, not demonstrated (no joint stablecoin customer named).
- **Real traction sits elsewhere, separately:** ClearBank's genuine 2026 momentum is its own EU/stablecoin build-out (MiCAR, Digital Asset Rails, Circle/EURC-USDC); Mambu's is Payments Hub + core migrations (Akbank, ABN AMRO/BUUT, Nyla). Neither of those is a *joint* ClearBank×Mambu product.
- **→ Who captures the margin:** the licensed party (ClearBank) captures rail/settlement economics; the core (Mambu) captures per-account SaaS. A "multi-rail modular" bundle doesn't change that split — so this commentary adds narrative, not new unit economics.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Expect continued **co-marketing** of "composable core + multi-rail (incl. stablecoin) rails." The thing to actually watch for is a **named joint stablecoin/multi-rail customer** — that would upgrade this from thought-leadership to news.
- Sentiment: ClearBank positive (profitable UK unit, credible EU/stablecoin push); Mambu mixed (good product/customer news but a stale 2021 valuation and post-2021 restructuring history).
- **→ Second order:** stablecoin settlement embedded directly in the core banking stack (ClearBank rails inside Mambu) is a plausible structural shift; but until a joint client goes live it's positioning, not proof.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** The pick sits at the intersection of two markets: (1) **SaaS/composable core banking** — global SaaS core-banking software ~**$13.5bn (2025) → $16.2bn (2026)**, CAGR ~**20%** to 2035 (per Precedence/Grand View-type estimates, via researchandmarkets/grandviewresearch, as of Jan 2026); overall core-banking software CAGR ~**17–18%** 2026–2034 (Technavio/Fortune Business Insights). SaaS/hosted ≈**67.5% of core-banking spend in 2026** — the delivery shift is now the default, not the option. (2) **Embedded finance / BaaS rails** — platform+infrastructure revenue ~**$21bn (2021) → $51bn (2026)** in the US; embedded-finance revenue ~**$230bn by 2025**, 10–15% of banking revenue pools by 2030 (McKinsey, via multiple 2026 secondaries). **Why now:** the news is really a **stablecoin-rail** story dressed as core banking — ClearBank+Mambu pitch running fiat AND stablecoin (USDC/EURC) settlement through one modular stack, riding the 2025–26 EU/US stablecoin-regulation wave (MiCAR live, GENIUS-type US frameworks). Structure: **two-sided and disaggregating** — the old monolith (Temenos/FIS/Fiserv) is unbundling into a stack of {licensed clearing bank} + {composable core} + {payment rails}. Barriers: banking licence + scheme access + regulatory capital (ClearBank's moat) vs. platform lock-in + integration switching costs (Mambu's).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: for the core vendor — **ARR, NRR, tier-one go-lives, live-vs-signed ratio**; for the clearing bank — **fee income %, deposits, payment volumes, take on transactions**. This is a **partnership**, not a merger, so the two protagonists compete in different layers:
- **Core-banking layer (Mambu's peers):** Thought Machine, Temenos, 10x Banking, Engine by Starling, Tuum, Pismo (Visa), FIS/Finxact, SAP Fioneer. Basis of competition = cloud-native architecture + deal cadence + reference go-lives. Recent moves: Thought Machine crossed **$100m revenue (+57%) and $100m ARR** for FY2025 (6 Jul 2026, [[Thought Machine tops $100m revenue for FY2025, up 57%]]); Engine by Starling landed **Tangerine (Canada, 10-yr, Nov 2025)**; Temenos FY2025 ARR **$860m (+12% cc)**, non-IFRS EBIT margin 34.7% (Temenos Q4-25 results, Feb 2026).
- **Clearing/BaaS layer (ClearBank's peers):** Griffin, Solaris, Modulr, LHV, plus the neobank-BaaS (Starling Engine, Railsr). Basis = licence breadth, scheme membership, real-time clearing, stablecoin issuance.

**Position:** Mambu is a **weakened category-definer** — it *coined* "composable banking" (225M+ end users, 260+ FIs, 60+ countries per its own PR) but is being **out-executed on tier-one go-lives** and shows decelerating growth (co-founder CEO Eugene Danilkis stepped down; CTO Fernando Zandona now interim CEO; Tracxn-cited YoY revenue growth ~9%). Its bright spot is **Mambu Payments (7x YoY payments growth in 2025**, per Mambu IR 25 Feb 2026) — bought via the **Numeral acquisition (Dec 2024)** — which is exactly what this ClearBank tie-up monetizes. ClearBank is the **stronger, profitable partner** (see comps). Moat: ClearBank = licence + scheme + investment-grade balance sheet (switching costs + regulatory barrier); Mambu = installed-base + integration lock-in, but eroding `(analysis)`.

*Multiples:* Both protagonists are **private**; no market cap, so no clean EV/Rev. **Risk flags.**
1. **Partner-quality asymmetry / Mambu overhang.** ClearBank is profitable and IG-rated; Mambu is a **decelerating (~9%), CEO-in-transition, $5.5bn-2021-marked** vendor with no fresh round. Second-order: if Mambu stalls or takes a down round, ClearBank's rail strategy loses a distribution channel and the "modular core" narrative weakens — dependence on someone else's (wobbling) core.
2. **Stablecoin regulatory + reputational risk.** The whole pitch hinges on fiat+stablecoin settlement into a still-forming EU/US regime (MiCAR, US frameworks). A rule change, de-peg, or issuer failure (USDC/EURC dependency) hits the differentiating layer directly, and ClearBank carries the **licensed-bank fraud/settlement liability** the PR is silent on.
3. **Disintermediation / margin capture up-stack.** In a disaggregated stack the economics can be captured by whoever owns the client relationship (the neobank) or the scheme. Two "picks-and-shovels" vendors partnering doesn't guarantee either keeps the margin — take-rate compression risk as embedded-finance rails commoditize.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is **consolidation-by-partnership** in a disaggregating stack: the profitable licensed rail (ClearBank) bolts onto the composable-core installed base (Mambu) to sell fiat+stablecoin settlement as one product — a bet that banks want **one throat to choke** for multi-rail money movement. Falsifiable thesis: *if this is real, expect a named joint go-live (an FI running Mambu core + ClearBank stablecoin settlement in production) within ~2–4 quarters.* Trigger to watch: a dated production reference. What breaks it: it stays "commentary/webinar" with no live client — i.e. **announced ≠ adopted** — or Mambu raises a down round, signalling the composable-core side can't fund the integration. De-PR note: the source is a **Future of Banking discussion piece, not a product launch or contract** — treat as positioning, not traction.

**Frame.** This is a thought-leadership/commentary item (`type/commentary`), not an earnings release — no results are in the news itself. ### ClearBank — SCALING and (UK) PROFITABLE
Primary: ClearBank Annual Report & Accounts 2025 (published 2026-04-15) and the accompanying results release.
- **Group normalised revenue £121.6m, +34% YoY.** UK normalised revenue £117.7m, +32% YoY.
- **UK profit before tax £12.2m, +53% YoY** (from £8.0m in FY2024) — third consecutive year of UK profitability.
- **Group statutory pre-tax LOSS £16.7m** — the UK bank is profitable, but the group is loss-making due to planned investment in scaling ClearBank Europe (28 clients, 21 added in 2025).
- **Fee-based revenue +51% YoY**, now ~three-quarters of total revenue (normalised basis); statutory net fee income +38% on recurring platform fees (46% of group revenue statutory).
- **Client deposits £17.8bn, +65% YoY** (from £10.8bn). **279 live clients** (+61 added). **Payment volumes +57%** — 262m payments processed (from 167m in 2024).
- ClearBank UK earned an **Investment Grade (BBB-) rating from S&P** in 2025.
- **Thesis-flag:** genuine operating leverage — revenue +34% while UK PBT +53% shows the UK core scales profitably; the group loss is a deliberate, funded land-grab in Europe, not a broken model. Watch whether European losses narrow on schedule; the group return to profit hinges on Europe reaching scale.

### Mambu — SCALING (operating metrics), FINANCIALS NOT PUBLICLY FILED
Mambu GmbH is a **private company that does not publish audited annual results**; ir_latest.json carries only press releases and product updates, no annual report. Consequently there is **no primary-source revenue / profit / margin figure to quote** — third-party aggregator figures for FY2025 are inconsistent and unverified (e.g. ~€255m revenue with ~-6.8% adj. EBITDA margin per one synthesis vs ~$159.5m per another; Tracxn's filed-accounts line is garbled and unusable). Treat all Mambu financial magnitudes as **[UNSOURCED] / no data**.
- **Officially disclosed operating metrics (Mambu IR, own press releases):** 225M+ end users, 60+ countries (15-year-anniversary release, 2026-03-26); ~400+ customers / FIs across 65+ countries, 100M+ accounts (product materials). Mambu Payments reported **~7x YoY payments growth in 2025** and expansion beyond Europe into LatAm/APAC (release 2026-02-25). Numeral (payments) acquired Dec 2024 to add multi-rail — the exact capability this commentary is about.
- **Thesis-flag:** Mambu is clearly **scaling on adoption metrics** (users, geography, payments volume), but with **no filed P&L there is no evidence on profitability** — the open question (path to sustained profit / positive EBITDA) cannot be answered from primary sources. Contrast with ClearBank, whose regulated status forces disclosure. For a diligence view on Mambu, profitability is a genuine data gap, not a confirmed positive or negative.

**Verdict (standing, not a print).** ClearBank: scaling with a profitable, cash-generative UK core (rev +34%, UK PBT +53%) but a group loss funding European expansion — a credible growth-plus-profitability story. Mambu: scaling on user/geography/payments metrics but **financially opaque** — no audited results, profitability unverifiable.
