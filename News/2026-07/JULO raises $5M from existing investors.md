### [0] What happened (dated)

- **2026-07-14** — JULO, an Indonesian digital consumer lender, raised **US$5 million from existing investors**. Per the digest, JULO uses ML to power its credit platform and has disbursed **>IDR 28 trillion** in loans to **>3.3 million users** since launch. Source: [Connecting the Dots in Fintech](https://www.connectingthedotsinfin.tech/r/bbce8141).
- **2026-01 / reported 2026-03-26** — As of Jan 2026 JULO reported >IDR 27T disbursed, ~3.29M users, TKB90 (90-day repayment success) of **99%**, >25.6M app downloads, >25% of disbursements outside Java. ([PR Newswire / ANTARA](https://en.antaranews.com/news/411285/indonesias-p2p-lending-grows-25-julo-strengthens-credit-quality)).

### [1] What JULO is

- Indonesian **digital consumer credit / P2P lender** (fintech lending / P2P under OJK regime). ML-driven credit scoring; unsecured consumer loans for household, education, healthcare, business capital. Founded 2016; targets underbanked mass-market borrowers, incl. outside Java.

### [2] Funding history / round context

- **2018** — US$5M Series A led by **Skystar Capital** (East Ventures, Provident, Gobi, Convergence et al.); extended to **US$15M Series A2** (+$10M). ([DealStreetAsia](https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/indonesia-julo-skystar-97602), [TechCrunch 2019](https://techcrunch.com/2019/09/18/julo-indonesia/)).
- **2022** — **US$60–80M Series B** led by **Credit Saison**, reportedly at **~US$200M valuation**. Plus a 2022 debt round.
- Total disclosed equity raised historically ~US$105–155M across ~6 rounds (data providers vary). Backers: Credit Saison, Skystar, Saratoga Investama, East Ventures, Quona, Central Capital Ventura, MDI Ventures, Gobi.
- **2024** — Bloomberg reported JULO **working toward an IPO** ([Bloomberg 2024-02-26](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-02-26/indonesia-digital-lending-startup-julo-working-toward-ipo)).
- **The 2026-07 raise**: a small **US$5M top-up from EXISTING investors** — a bridge/extension, not a priced primary round; no new lead or valuation disclosed. Small relative to prior Series B, consistent with runway/pre-IPO topping-up rather than a growth round.

### [3] Market & regulation

- Indonesia fintech lending (P2P) outstanding loans **IDR 98.54T as of Jan 2026, +25.5% YoY** (OJK). See [[Indonesia P2P lending loans grow 25% to IDR 98 trillion]].
- Regulator **OJK** tightened P2P rules (rate caps, borrower-protection, min. equity, TKB90 disclosure). JULO's 99% TKB90 is a positioning signal on asset quality amid a sector cleanup.
- Comparable Indonesian lending raises: [[UangCermat raises $6.4M Series A for Indonesian lending]] ($6.4M equity + $20M credit facility, Jan 2026). Regional digital-banking scale: [[WeLab raises $220M Series D for pan-Asian expansion]] (SEA expansion). Bank-backed competition intensifying: [[Grab consolidates Superbank Indonesia with majority stake]].

### [4] Why it matters

- Small dollar amount; signal value > capital value. "Existing investors only, $5M" reads as **insider bridge financing** — supportive backers but no external validation / new marginal capital. In a market growing 25% with tightening regulation, a market leader (3.3M users, IDR 28T disbursed, 99% TKB90) taking only a $5M insider top-up is modestly negative on funding-momentum optics, though consistent with a pre-IPO holding pattern.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector context — Indonesia / SEA digital lending & consumer credit.** JULO is an OJK-licensed Indonesian P2P/consumer-credit lender (ML-driven underwriting) claiming >IDR 28tn cumulative disbursement to >3.3m users and TKB90 ~99% (Jan 2026). The backdrop is a maturing but sharply consolidating market: OJK enforced a minimum paid-up capital of IDR 12.5bn by end-June 2025 (12 platforms exited), and SEOJK 19/2025 (compliance deadline 1 Jan 2026) added funding caps — a base IDR 2bn per borrower, extendable to IDR 5bn for productive loans only if NPL ≤5% over six months and no OJK sanction — plus tighter governance, transparency and risk-management rules. Net effect: a higher regulatory floor that favours well-capitalised incumbents and squeezes marginal players; JULO's headline 99% TKB90 is above the 5% NPL threshold, so it sits on the compliant side.

**Deal read — bridge/insider signal.** The round is US$5M "from existing investors." Public trackers show JULO's last equity raise was the Feb 2022 Series B (~US$60M, led by Credit Saison; +US$30M corporate and ~US$50M debt from Credit Saison in 2022). A small insider top-up ~52 months later — against a 2024 Bloomberg report that JULO was "working toward an IPO" and reported ~18% YoY headcount decline — reads as a defensive bridge, not a growth/priced up-round. No new lead investor, no disclosed valuation. This is the classic profile of extending runway while an IPO/priced round stays out of reach in a tight SEA fintech funding climate. Treat any "growth capital" framing with skepticism.
