# Context-enrichment: BGFI Group taps SBS for centralised digital banking rollout
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
BGFIBank Group (Libreville, Gabon; largest bank in CEMAC/Central Africa) **renewed and expanded** its relationship with **SBS** (formerly Sopra Banking Software) via a **new subscription to the SBS Digital Banking Suite (DBS)** — a **digital-channels/experience layer** (mobile banking, digital onboarding, cross-border/corporate portals, data/AI, "seamless core connectivity"). This is **not** a core-banking replacement or a competitive vendor win.
- **The real story is "centralised":** BGFI is moving from per-market digital builds to **one Group-wide digital foundation**, delivered through **BGFI Services** (its central tech entity), starting with **2 pilot subsidiaries** then expanding across ~12 African markets + France.
- **Why framed this way:** the press frames a channels-layer subscription as a "digital banking rollout across Africa" and wraps it in a strategic plan (outlets disagree on the name — **"BGFI 30 (2026–2030)"** vs **"Dynamique 2025"**, an unresolved PR inconsistency → likely a re-issued/overlapping release). The PR anchors to breadth ("across Africa") because the actual disclosed scope is thin: **no contract value, no named pilot countries, no go-live date** (all open). The flattering "200+ banks / 50 countries" is SBS vendor marketing, not deal substance.
- **Why it reveals incumbency, not disruption:** BGFI has run SBS's **Amplitude** core for years (lineage: Delta-Bank / Delta Informatique, which Sopra absorbed). DBS layers on top of that existing core → this deepens a single-vendor stack rather than opening a bake-off.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Francophone / CEMAC-WAEMU:** SBS **Amplitude is entrenched and dominant** (Orabank across 12 countries, Coris Bank, Advans, KCB, QNB Egypt). So BGFI x SBS reflects the regional incumbent keeping its incumbent — no contest.
- **Global core leaders:** **Temenos (Transact)** — 950+ banks, "Best Core Banking" Banking Tech Awards 2025 (see [[Temenos launches embedded AI agents across core and digital banking]]); **Oracle FLEXCUBE** (won NBS Bank Malawi off T24); **Infosys Finacle, TCS BaNCS, Finastra**.
- **Cloud-native challengers:** **Mambu** (powers TymeBank SA), **Oradian** (MSME/MFI West Africa), **Thought Machine**, **Neptune**. These win neobanks/greenfields (cf. [[Attijariwafa Bank launches Simple, Morocco's first full neobank]]) — **not yet** pan-African incumbent groups like BGFI.
- **Why the map looks this way:** in Francophone Africa the switching cost off Amplitude (multi-country, multi-regulator, ex-Delta lineage) is enormous, so the digital-layer upsell is the natural expansion path, not a rip-and-replace. Second order: SBS defends its base by moving up the stack (channels + AI, cf. [[SBS embeds AI Foundation into core banking software]]) precisely because cloud-native players attack the greenfield/neobank flank it can't easily win.

## [2] Company history / fit
- BGFI: ~12 countries + France; FY2024 net income ~US$203m; NBI CFA175bn→302bn (2019–23), cost/income 79%→65%. **Listed on BVMAC 7 May 2026** (first CEMAC multinational on the equity board; ~45.3bn FCFA raised) after a contentious IPO (shareholder injunction dismissed Sep 2025). Chairman **Henri-Claude Oyima** was Gabon's Minister of Economy/Finance May 2025–Jan 2026 (governance flag).
- **Why BGFI acts this way:** a newly-listed pan-African group under cost-to-income and growth pressure needs to (a) show a modern digital story to the market and (b) cut duplication across 12+1 markets. Centralising digital via BGFI Services on a shared SBS foundation does both — a rational cost/consistency play, not a technology leap.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What is genuinely new:** the *centralisation* — a Group-wide shared digital foundation replacing market-by-market builds. That is a real (if unglamorous) architectural shift.
- **What is NOT new:** the SBS relationship, the Amplitude core, and SBS's digital ambitions. This is a renewal/upsell, not a first-time selection.
- **Traction = announced, not live:** only "2 pilot subsidiaries," phased, no go-live, no budget. By the anti-PR gate this is **framed, not adopted** — importance capped low until pilots produce metrics.
- **Who captures the margin:** SBS. Layering DBS on its own Amplitude core deepens lock-in (core + channels + AI all one vendor, now **Axway-owned** after the €330m Sept-2024 carve-out). BGFI trades vendor concentration risk for delivery speed/consistency.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Expect phased expansion from the 2 pilots across BGFI's network **if** pilots hit; watch for disclosed go-lives, named countries, cloud/SaaS deployment (not yet confirmed — open) as the real signal.
- **Structural driver:** African core/digital modernization wave — mobile-money interoperability, pan-African expansion, regulation, cost pressure. Centralisation is the efficient response for multi-market groups.
- **Counterintuitive second-order:** deepening the SBS/Amplitude+DBS stack makes BGFI faster now but **more exposed** to Axway's post-acquisition roadmap and to any cloud-native disruption of the channels layer later; single-vendor "efficiency" is also single-vendor fragility.
- **Risks:** vendor lock-in; Axway integration/roadmap risk; multi-regulator CEMAC rollout complexity; IPO/governance overhang; pilot→scale execution; zero disclosed economics.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** BGFI's central-Africa footprint sits in the fastest-growing core-banking demand pool globally. Sub-Saharan African account ownership rose from 34% (2014) to 58% (2024), leaving ~42% of adults unbanked — the widest formal-inclusion gap of any region, with mobile money (40% of adults, highest worldwide) the primary rail (per World Bank Global Findex 2025). The addressable software layer is scaling with it: the MEA core-banking software market is projected at $2.38bn (2025) → $5.53bn (2033), ~11.1% CAGR (per Straits Research). Structure: vendor-side consolidated among a few global suites (Temenos Transact, Infosys Finacle, Mambu, SBS/Sopra) selling into a fragmented, largely bank-owned demand base; barriers are high switching costs + regulatory/data-residency requirements, not capital. Why now: BGFI is running this as a Group-wide re-platform under its "BGFI 30" (2026-2030) plan via central entity BGFI Services — a shift from country-by-country modernization to one shared digital foundation, deploying to two pilot subsidiaries first, then across its Africa + Europe network (per Financial IT, ffnews, 2026). This is a renewal/expansion of an existing SBS relationship, not a competitive rip-and-replace win — the de-PR distinction that matters.

**Competitive landscape.** Core-banking vendor KPIs: installed-base count, ARR/subscription mix, SaaS-migration rate, multi-entity/multi-country deployment scale. Key players in Africa and basis of competition (product depth + regional install base, not price): **Temenos** — deepest continental footprint, and just partnered with Interswitch to scale digital banking across Africa ([[Interswitch partners with Temenos to scale African digital banking]], 2026-06); **Infosys Finacle** — powers Standard Bank across 16 African countries, plus GTBank and UBA; **Mambu** — cloud-native/composable, favoured by neobanks and Tier-2 leapfroggers; **SBS (ex-Sopra Banking Software)** — the protagonist's vendor, ~1,500 institutions across 80 countries, and separately just embedded its "AI Foundation" into that core stack ([[SBS embeds AI Foundation into core banking software]], 2026-07). SBS position: entrenched incumbent in Francophone/CEMAC Africa via long BGFI reference; catching up to Temenos/Mambu on cloud-native SaaS narrative. Moat here is the switching-cost + incumbency lock-in on BGFI's group core `(analysis)`.

**Comps & multiples.** Both principals are private-within-a-group, so market-cap multiples are not computable. **SBS** was carved out of Sopra Steria and sold to Axway (Dec 2024, ~€330m EV); SBS contributed ~€340m FY2023 revenue → the €330m deal EV implies ~1.0x EV/Revenue (`€330m / €340m`), a modest software multiple reflecting services-heavy, legacy-core mix and carve-out discount rather than a SaaS re-rating. The combined 74Software (Axway + SBS) targeted ~€700m 2025 revenue. **BGFI Bank Group** is a private CEMAC banking group — no public valuation, no data. Internal comps for African/EM core-banking deals: [[Interswitch partners with Temenos to scale African digital banking]] (2026-06), [[Peru's Mibanco selects Temenos SaaS for core banking upgrade]] (2026-02, Credicorp-owned microfinance, LatAm), [[SBS embeds AI Foundation into core banking software]] (2026-07). Distribution not computed (deal terms undisclosed for BGFI); qualitative read: SBS's ~1.0x carve-out multiple sits well below cloud-native peers (Temenos/Mambu command richer revenue multiples on SaaS growth) — in-line-to-cheap, consistent with a legacy-core, services-weighted vendor. Ticket size / contract value for the BGFI deal: `[UNSOURCED]`.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Execution / re-platform risk.** Group-wide core migration across multiple African + European subsidiaries under one operating model is the highest-risk pattern in banking IT; "pilot two subsidiaries then progressively expand" (2026-2030 runway) signals management already de-risking for slippage — a multi-year, back-loaded rollout where announced ≠ live across the network.
2. **Vendor-stability overhang.** SBS is itself mid-transition — carved out of Sopra Steria into Axway/74Software (Dec 2024) and only recently rebranded; a small vendor absorbing an M&A integration while committing to a group's decade-long core is a continuity risk for BGFI, and second-order pressure on SBS to keep the flagship African reference.
3. **Displacement risk from cloud-native peers.** SBS's ~1.0x carve-out multiple and legacy-core positioning leave it exposed if Temenos (now scaling Africa via Interswitch) or Mambu win the next Francophone/CEMAC accounts on SaaS economics — BGFI locks in, but SBS's broader African pipeline is contestable.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** For the sector this is incremental, not a re-rating: it validates that Francophone/CEMAC re-platforming is now moving group-wide, but on renewal/incumbency, not net-new competitive share `(analysis)`. Falsifiable thesis: SBS holds its Francophone-Africa core-banking base through 2026-2030 on switching-cost lock-in. Trigger/what breaks it: watch whether Temenos (post-Interswitch) or Mambu land a comparable Francophone group-core win in the next 12-18 months — that would signal SBS's regional moat is eroding and reprice the "cheap incumbent" read. Watch also for a signed live go-live (not pilot) as the first proof this deployment is real.
