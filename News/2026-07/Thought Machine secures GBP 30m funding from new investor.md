## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened (one line).** Thought Machine, the London cloud-native core-banking vendor, closed a ~£30m transaction from a new, undisclosed "tier 1" bank that is both investor and client — completed 27 May 2026 and disclosed alongside FY2025 results in early July 2026.

**[1] Deal mechanics & the money.** Per the reporting, the ~£30m breaks into ~£9m of PRIMARY funding (convertible loan notes) plus a ~£21m SECONDARY transaction letting employees sell share options for liquidity. It is NOT a headline priced equity round with a fresh valuation. Combined with a ~£44.8m round in July 2025, the company frames a broader ~£75–80m of capital raised across the two events. Note the currency/framing spread across outlets (£30m / $40m / $41m; Tech.eu, Finextra, Dealroom) — treat the exact USD figure as approximate FX conversion, not a distinct raise.

**[2] Why it matters / context.**
- Signals a shift from the 2021–22 "growth-at-any-cost" era toward capital efficiency: the raise is small, partly liquidity-driven, and pitched around commercial traction rather than a valuation step-up.
- The backer being a client-bank reinforces the "strategic bank equity" pattern that already defines Thought Machine's cap table (Series D investors included Temasek, Intesa Sanpaolo, Morgan Stanley, ING, JPMorgan, Lloyds, SEB).
- Capital earmarked for London headcount — 100+ engineering hires confirmed in 2026.

**[3] Numbers to anchor.**
- Raise: ~£30m (≈£9m primary CLNs + ≈£21m secondary), closed 27 May 2026.
- FY2025 revenue: £74.8m, +57% YoY; ARR crossed $100m in Q2 2026.
- Operating loss: £42m (FY2025) vs £69.4m (2024); total loss £45m vs £71.2m. (One outlet cited losses cut "to ~£12m" — inconsistent with the £45m filing figure; flagged, not adopted.)
- Positive free cash flow in H2 2025 → stated path to sustained operating profitability.
- Customers: 68 banks, 18 tier-1. Valuation: last public mark $2.7bn (Series D, May 2022, $160m led by Temasek); NO updated valuation disclosed. IPO pushed to ~2028.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
**Red-team / challenge questions:**

1. Is this actually a "funding round"? ~£21m of the ~£30m is a SECONDARY employee-liquidity sale — only ~£9m is new primary capital (convertible loan notes). Headline £30m overstates fresh money into the business.
2. The investor is undisclosed. Claims that it is a "tier 1 bank / client" come from the CEO, not a filing — unverifiable independently.
3. No new valuation was disclosed. Last public mark ($2.7bn, May 2022) is 4 years stale; a small CLN raise may imply a flat or down round the company chose not to price.
4. Convertible loan notes are debt-like with conversion terms unknown — dilution/discount to the next round is undisclosed.
5. Loss figures conflict across sources (£45m total loss per filing vs one outlet's "~£12m"). Which is the operating vs total vs adjusted number?
6. "Positive free cash flow in H2 2025" — is this a full-year sustainable figure or a one-off half driven by timing of large migration prepayments?
7. Revenue £74.8m is GBP; ARR ">$100m" is USD — mixing currencies flatters the growth narrative. What is ARR in GBP?
8. ARR crossing $100m in Q2 2026 is post-period vs FY2025 revenue — is the "growth" partly a comparison-window artifact?
9. IPO slipped to 2028 (from earlier ~2024/25 talk). Does a small strategic raise substitute for a real growth round because market appetite is weak?
10. Concentration risk: growth "driven by multi-year tier 1 migrations" — how many customers, and what share of ARR sits in the top 2–3?
11. Competitive pressure: Temenos, Finastra, Backbase, Stitch, plus incumbents modernizing — is 68 banks / 18 tier-1 defensible against price competition?
12. Is the £30m even new vs already reported in July 2025's £44.8m round framing? (Verdict: distinct — closed 27 May 2026.)
13. Duplicate check vs the FY2025 revenue note — same disclosure cycle; is the funding materially separate news or repackaging? (Verdict: separate funding event.)
14. Headcount "100+ engineering hires" while cutting losses — is aggressive hiring consistent with the stated path to profitability?

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Cloud-native core-banking SaaS. Market sizing (secondary research, treat as directional): SaaS-based core-banking software ~$12–14bn in 2025–26 growing to ~$50–53bn by 2033 at ~19–21% CAGR (per Persistence Market Research / SNS Insider, via GlobeNewswire, Jan 2026); the SaaS/hosted segment is ~68% of the broader core-banking market in 2026 (per Fortune Business Insights). Structure: **oligopolistic at the top, fragmenting at the cloud-native edge.** Scale incumbents (Temenos >950 banks, FIS, Finastra) own the installed base; a cloud-native cohort (TM, 10x, Engine by Starling, Mambu, Tuum, Pismo) contests greenfield + migration mandates. Barriers are high: mission-critical reliability, multi-year integration, regulatory certification → **switching costs are the moat, but they cut both ways** — they slow TM's own displacement of legacy cores (long sales cycles, "signed ≠ live"). Why now: (1) a wave of tier-1 core-modernization off legacy mainframes; (2) TM's FY2025 results (6 Jul 2026) reframe the narrative from "cash-burning, IPO slipping" to "at-scale, self-funding" just as this £30m round closes.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: **ARR, ARR growth, NRR, Rule of 40, gross margin** (SaaS infra). TM disclosed: revenue **£74.8m FY2025, +57% YoY**; **ARR crossed $100m in Q2 2026**; **68 banks signed, 18 tier-one**; **positive FCF in H2 2025** (per FintechFutures, Finextra, 6 Jul 2026). NRR / gross margin: `[UNSOURCED]`. Key players & basis of competition (product depth + cloud-native credibility + distribution): **10x Banking** — closest tier-1 rival, runs Chase UK + Westpac (note: Chase UK is NOT a TM client); **Engine by Starling** — momentum name, landed Tangerine (Canada, 10-yr, Nov 2025) — see [[Tangerine signs with Engine by Starling]]; **Temenos** — scale incumbent retrofitting to SaaS on AWS — see [[Temenos launches embedded AI agents across core and digital banking]], [[VPBank moves to cloud-native core banking with Temenos, Red Hat]]; **Mambu** — weakened (CEO change, layoffs); **Tuum, Pismo (Visa-owned), SAP Fioneer** round out the field. Recent peer moves: Temenos acquired additiv (wealth, Jun 2026) and partnered Interswitch (Jun 2026); Backbase acquired Kasisto (Jun 2026). Protagonist position: **catching up on scale vs Temenos, roughly at parity with 10x/Engine on cloud-native tier-1 credibility** (analysis). Moat = switching costs + tier-1 reference logos (intangible), but production go-lives lag signings.

**Comps & multiples.** TM last disclosed valuation: **$2.7bn (2022)**; this £30m round's valuation NOT disclosed (CEO Paul Taylor explicitly declined) — so a current TM EV/Revenue **cannot be computed = no data**. For scale only, the *stale* 2022 mark vs FY2025 revenue: `$2.7bn / ~$100m ≈ 27x` — a 2022 private mark against 2025 revenue, **not a current multiple**, cited only to show how far the top line has grown into the old valuation. Peers:
| Peer | Metric | Multiple (arithmetic) |
|---|---|---|
| **Temenos** (public, TEMN.SW) | mkt cap ~$6.26bn / TTM rev ~$1.08bn | **P/S ≈ $6.26bn / $1.08bn = 5.8x** (companiesmarketcap, May–Jun 2026) |
| **Mambu** (private) | last round €4.9bn (Series E, EQT) | round valuation, not mkt cap; revenue not disclosed → **EV/Rev no data** |
| Sector EV/ARR (private cloud-banking) | — | median ~6x; 10–15x only for 40%+ growth + 120%+ NRR (Aventis/consultefc, 2026) |

Read: at Temenos' ~5.8x P/S, TM's ~$100m revenue ⇒ `~$0.58bn` implied — **far below the 2022 $2.7bn mark**. TM grows faster (+57% vs Temenos low-single-digit), which supports a premium to 5.8x, but bridging to $2.7bn requires a persistent ~27x — **rich vs the ~6x sector median and not obviously justified by growth alone**. Internal comps: [[Thought Machine lands GBP 45M funding as losses widen]] (Oct 2025, existing investors, no lead, no valuation), [[Thought Machine tops $100m revenue for FY2025, up 57%]].

**Risk flags.**
1. **Down-round / stale-mark risk.** The refusal to disclose a valuation, plus a structure that is £9m primary (convertible loan notes) + £21m secondary (employee option liquidity) — not a headline priced equity round — signals TM is **avoiding a re-mark** of the 2022 $2.7bn. At sector multiples a fresh mark could print materially lower → dilution / morale / IPO-timing pressure (IPO now pushed to ≥2028).
2. **Signed ≠ live + revenue quality.** "68 banks / 18 tier-one" is cumulative *signings*, not go-lives; ARR is stated in USD while statutory accounts are GBP (£74.8m). "FCF-positive in H2" is a half-year cash metric, not statutory profit — TM still booked a **~£45m total loss FY2025** (down from £71.2m); operating loss £42m (from £69.4m). Path-to-profit is improving but **not yet breakeven, no dated breakeven disclosed.**
3. **Customer-as-investor concentration.** The new backer is an unnamed **tier-1 bank that is both investor and client** — aligns incentives but concentrates dependence; a strategic buyer's motives (option on the tech, cheap entry) differ from a financial lead's price discipline.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is a **bridge financing dressed as a milestone, not a re-rating.** Falsifiable thesis: TM's next *priced* equity round (or IPO prospectus) will value it **below the 2022 $2.7bn** unless ARR growth stays >50% with visible tier-1 go-lives; watch for a disclosed valuation, a lead investor, or confirmed production migrations (not signings) as the trigger that would break the down-round thesis. Sector-wise, the cloud-native cohort keeps taking greenfield share from Temenos/Mambu, but self-funding via customer-banks (vs VC leads) suggests **private-market appetite for core-banking at 2021–22 marks has cooled.**

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
> [!note] Earnings review (FY2025, private — press-reported alongside Companies House FY2024). Thought Machine is private; the news is a £30m raise, not a results release — but the raise lands with recently reported FY2025 accounts, so these are the financial context for the round. IR DB holds only the audited **FY2024** accounts (filed 2025-09-30); FY2025 accounts are **not yet in the corpus**, so the FY2025 £-loss lines below are **press-reported**, not primary-filed.

**Verdict (growth read).** Improving burn, not a growth stall. Revenue **£74.8m (+57% YoY)**, crossing **$100m**; operating loss **narrowed to £42m** (FY2024: £69.4m); total loss for the year **£45m** (FY2024: £71.2m); **positive free cash flow in H2 2025** (first time). The raise reads less as survival cash and more as topping-up while the burn curve bends — and the backer being a tier-1 bank that is *both* investor and client is the real signal.

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Revenue: **£74.8m, +57% YoY** (implied FY2024 ≈ £47.6m); crosses the **$100m** headline.
- Operating loss: **£42m vs £69.4m FY2024** — cut ~39%, on revenue scale + press-stated "improved cost management".
- Total loss for the year: **£45m vs £71.2m FY2024** (~37% narrower).
- Free cash flow: **positive in H2 2025** — the burn inflection.
- ARR: **crossed $100m as of Q2 2026** (a 2026 data point, not FY2025), on multi-year tier-1 migration commitments.
- Clients: **18 of the world's largest institutions; 68 banks signed globally**, 30+ countries.

**The raise vs the results (de-PR).** Two press framings — watch the gap. FinTech Futures/Finextra headline **£30m** = **£9m primary** (convertible loan notes) + **£21m secondary** (employee option sales), completed **27 May 2026**. Tech.eu/Dealroom frame a larger **~$40–41m (~£75m)** round. So only ~£9m is genuinely new primary money into the company; the bulk is secondary liquidity and/or a wider round the press sizes differently. "£30m raise" overstates the primary injection ~3x. Investor undisclosed in filings; CEO Paul Taylor says it is an unnamed tier-1 bank that is also a client.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public consensus (private). vs prior year the trajectory is unambiguously positive: **+57%** top line with loss down ~37–39% = operating leverage kicking in, not growth-at-all-costs. IR DB baseline is FY2024 accounts only; no prior FY2025 earnings note in the corpus ([[Thought Machine secures GBP 30m funding from new investor]] is the only corpus item). Implied FY2024 revenue ≈ £47.6m from the +57% math [analysis].

**Guidance / forward.** No formal guidance (private). Management signalled **IPO delayed to 2028** (London), citing a "difficult" listing environment — this raise + secondary partly bridges liquidity while the IPO window is pushed out. Hiring **100+ engineers in 2026** (Lisbon, expanded London, Miami) means continued spend even as burn narrows.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. **Burn inflection is the story** → operating loss ~39% narrower on +57% revenue → unit economics converging → materially de-risks the "perma-burn core-banking vendor" thesis; second-order: strengthens IPO-readiness for 2028.
2. **Tier-1 bank as investor + client** → validates product at the top of the market (hardest, stickiest, multi-year migrations) → but concentrates revenue in a few very large deals → ramp/churn risk if one tier-1 migration slips.
3. **"£30m raise" is mostly secondary** → only ~£9m new primary capital → company isn't cash-hungry (consistent with H2 FCF+), but the headline overstates fresh funding; verify true cash position in the FY2025 filing.
4. **IPO pushed to 2028** → longer private runway needed → is H2-2025 FCF+ durable through the 2026 hiring ramp, or does burn re-widen? That's the number to watch once FY2025 accounts are filed.
