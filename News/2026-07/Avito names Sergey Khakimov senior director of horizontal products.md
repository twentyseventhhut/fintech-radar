# Context-enrichment: Avito names Sergey Khakimov senior director of horizontal products
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Avito appointed Sergey Khakimov to a newly framed role, **senior director of horizontal products**, spanning advertising, monetization, **financial services**, products for private users and professional sellers, plus a "product office" that builds cross-team tooling/processes. Khakimov has been at Avito since April 2022 and since May 2024 was senior product director of the "Avito Goods/Товары" vertical, where he led Avito Delivery (self-pickup, large-cargo delivery), federal sales campaigns, the cart, "Avito Premium" and "Avito для бизнеса"; earlier he was a retail-sector business consultant at EY (per [Vedomosti](https://www.vedomosti.ru/technologies/personnel/news/2026/07/07/1211731-gorizontalnim-produktam), [Retail.ru](https://www.retail.ru/news/avito-naznachil-sergeya-khakimova-starshim-direktorom-po-gorizontalnym-produktam-7-iyulya-2026-279646/)).

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** This is an *internal promotion*, not an external fintech hire — the de-PR read is that Avito is consolidating previously siloed monetization levers (ads, payments, seller tools, financial services) under one "horizontal" owner. Financial services is one line item inside a broad product remit, NOT a dedicated fintech leadership hire. Note Avito already has a **director of financial services, Tatiana Zharkova** ([Sravni](https://www.sravni.ru/novost/2025/8/25/avito-podklyuchil-besproczentnuyu-rassrochku-do-100-tysyach-rublej/)), so Khakimov sits *above* the fintech function as a cross-vertical product exec, not as its hands-on head. The signal is organizational (monetization consolidation) more than a new fintech strategy launch.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Russian marketplaces are all racing to embed finance: **Ozon Bank** (registered a CBR digital-financial-assets platform, Jun 2026 [[Ozon Bank registers digital financial assets platform with CBR]]; brokerage arm "Ozon Investments" launching autumn 2026 [[Ozon to launch brokerage arm Ozon Investments in autumn 2026]]; ATM network doubling to 2,000 by end-2026 [[Ozon Bank to double ATM network to 2,000 by end-2026]]); **Wildberries/RWB → WB Bank** (leadership change same day [[WB Bank names Svyatoslav Emelyanov acting board chairman]], transforming marketplace data into credit/deposit/payment products); **Magnit** (Magnit Pay scaling to 33,000 stores [[Magnit Pay VTB scales to all 33,000 Magnit stores]]). Avito differs: it is a **classifieds/C2C** platform (not a goods marketplace with its own logistics-fulfilment margin), so its embedded-finance surface is instalments on C2C purchases, seller monetization and ad-financing rather than a full neobank. It runs finance via a **bank partnership (Sovcombank's "Plait" instalments)** rather than an owned bank ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.ru/tekhnologii/544457-a-by-vzal-castami-no-mne-nuzno-srazu-avito-podklucaet-besprocentnuu-rassrochku)) — meaning it is *behind* Ozon/WB, who own licensed banks.

**Second-order:** Avito's 50% owner since April 2025 is state **Rosselkhozbank (RSHB)** ([Moscow Times](https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2025/04/28/russian-state-bank-takes-over-leading-online-marketplace-avito-a88895), [AIM Group](https://aimgroup.com/2025/04/25/sanctioned-russian-bank-buys-50-of-avito-classifieds-giant/)) — so Avito has an obvious in-house banking partner to build owned financial services with, mirroring the Ozon-bank / WB-VTB model. Consolidating financial services under a "horizontal products" director is plausibly a step toward that build-out (analysis).

## [2] Company history / fit
Avito: Russia's dominant classifieds; 2023 revenue RUB 100.9bn (+57% y/y), net profit RUB 39.3bn (+230%) ([RBC via shoppers.media](https://shoppers.media/news/14122_vyrucka-avito-vpervye-prevysila-100-mlrd-rub)); Forbes valued it 4th among RuNet firms at ~$4.6bn (2024). Ownership arc: Naspers/Prosus → Kismet (Ivan Tavrin) for $2.4bn in 2022 → RSHB bought 50% from Kismet in April 2025 (terms undisclosed, includes credit RSHB extended for Tavrin's original purchase). Registered **"Avito Finance"** in the financial-platform operators register in March 2025; launched interest-free instalments up to RUB 100k via Sovcombank Plait ([Frank Media](https://frankmedia.ru/184900)). AI investment: A-Vibe / A-Vision models across verticals; targets RUB 21bn incremental profit from GenAI by 2028 ([ComNews](https://www.comnews.ru/content/242003/2025-10-28/2025-w44/1008/avito-podarit-razrabotchikam-polmilliarda-rubley)).

**Why the company acts this way:** classifieds monetize via listing-promotion take, a capped ad-like revenue pool. To grow beyond that, Avito must layer transactional monetization (payments, instalments, delivery, seller SaaS) on top of its traffic — hence "horizontal products" that turn one giant audience into multiple monetization surfaces. The RSHB tie-up gives it the banking rails to do so.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Novelty is **low**: this is a personnel reshuffle, not a product or capability launch. The real fintech traction sits in earlier events — Avito Finance registration (Mar 2025), Plait instalments (Aug 2025), with reported traction: average instalment cheque up 6x, instalment purchases in Clothing >3x and Auto-parts ~2.5x in H1 2025 (per fin-services director Zharkova, [Sravni](https://www.sravni.ru/novost/2025/8/25/avito-podklyuchil-besproczentnuyu-rassrochku-do-100-tysyach-rublej/)). Khakimov's appointment does not itself add value or traction; at most it centralizes ownership. **Who captures the margin:** on instalments the *bank* (Sovcombank/RSHB) holds the credit economics and risk; Avito captures a referral/marketing take and conversion uplift — so Avito remains the demand layer, not the balance-sheet layer, unless RSHB backing lets it internalize lending later (hypothesis).

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Watch whether Avito uses the RSHB relationship to launch *owned* financial products (wallet, own instalments/lending, CFA/DFA) rather than reselling partner-bank products — that would be the real strategic move this appointment sets up (analysis). Sector backdrop: a "race for finance" among Russian marketplaces (Vedomosti&, [Гонка за финансами](https://www.vedomosti.ru/partner/articles/2026/07/01/1210140-gonka-finansami), 2026-07-01), with Ozon, WB, Magnit and Yandex all embedding banking. Risks: sanctioned state-bank ownership limits any cross-border/IPO optionality; asset-quality risk as unsecured retail lending scales (the same flag Expert RA raised for WB Bank [[WB Bank names Svyatoslav Emelyanov acting board chairman]]); an appointment is a weak signal absent a subsequent product print.

## Market Research — Russian marketplace embedded finance

**Sector & drivers.** Sub-vertical: **embedded finance on Russian marketplaces / classifieds** (instalments, payments, wallets, marketplace-data-driven lending). Global embedded-finance context is large but not directly applicable to a sanctioned domestic market; no verifiable Russia-specific TAM in sources → **no data** on sector size. Structure: rapidly *consolidating around platform-owned or platform-partnered banks* — Ozon (own Ozon Bank), Wildberries/RWB (WB Bank, VTB partnership), Magnit (Magnit Pay with VTB), Yandex (Yandex Bank). Entry barrier = a banking license + capital + regulator (CBR) approval, so classifieds/marketplaces either buy/build a bank or partner with one. Driver / "why now": a documented "race for finance" among Russian marketplaces (Vedomosti&, "Гонка за финансами", 2026-07-01) as platforms convert captive audience + transaction/behavioral data into higher-margin financial products; second-order — whoever owns the bank captures the credit spread, whoever only refers (Avito today) captures thinner conversion economics.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: GMV, take rate, instalment/credit attach rate, and (for owned banks) net interest margin + cost of risk on unsecured retail. Key players & basis of competition: **Ozon Bank** — owned bank, brokerage arm "Ozon Investments" launching autumn 2026 [[Ozon to launch brokerage arm Ozon Investments in autumn 2026]], CFA/DFA platform registered with CBR Jun 2026 [[Ozon Bank registers digital financial assets platform with CBR]], ATM network doubling to 2,000 by end-2026 [[Ozon Bank to double ATM network to 2,000 by end-2026]]; **WB Bank / RWB** — marketplace-data neobank, leadership change 2026-07-07 [[WB Bank names Svyatoslav Emelyanov acting board chairman]]; **Magnit Pay** — VTB-backed, scaled to 33,000 stores [[Magnit Pay VTB scales to all 33,000 Magnit stores]]. Avito's position: **catching up / niche** — classifieds/C2C, no owned bank, finance delivered via Sovcombank "Plait" instalments + "Avito Finance" financial-platform registration (Mar 2025); moat = dominant classifieds audience & seller relationships (network effects), not a balance sheet. This appointment consolidates monetization ownership but does not close the owned-bank gap.

**Comps & multiples.** Avito is private and does not disclose a fintech-segment P&L → segment multiples **not computed / no data**. Company-level reference points (public press, not audited comps): 2023 revenue RUB 100.9bn (+57% y/y), net profit RUB 39.3bn; Forbes 2024 RuNet valuation ~$4.6bn; Kismet acquired it for $2.4bn (RUB 151bn) in 2022; RSHB bought 50% in Apr 2025, terms undisclosed → implied valuation **[UNSOURCED]**. On $4.6bn valuation vs ~$1.6bn (2023) revenue → EV/Rev ≈ `$4.6bn / $1.6bn ≈ 2.9x` (crude, mixing a 2024 valuation with 2023 revenue and no net-debt/EV adjustment → treat as indicative only, not a clean multiple). Internal comps: [[Ozon to launch brokerage arm Ozon Investments in autumn 2026]], [[Ozon Bank registers digital financial assets platform with CBR]], [[Magnit Pay VTB scales to all 33,000 Magnit stores]], [[WB Bank names Svyatoslav Emelyanov acting board chairman]] — all show peers moving faster into *owned* financial infrastructure. Distribution not computed (mixed public/private, no clean peer segment multiples).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Partner-bank dependence / related-party concentration** — finance economics ride on Sovcombank (instalments) and 50%-owner RSHB; Avito captures referral, not credit, margin, and RSHB ownership adds related-party concentration → structurally thinner and less controllable than owned-bank peers.
2. **Sanctioned state ownership** — RSHB is sanctioned; caps cross-border optionality and IPO timing, and ties Avito's fintech build to state-bank strategy.
3. **Unsecured retail credit risk (sector-wide)** — as marketplaces scale instalments/lending, asset quality is a latent risk (Expert RA flagged exactly this for WB Bank [[WB Bank names Svyatoslav Emelyanov acting board chairman]]); Avito is insulated while it only refers, but exposed if it internalizes lending.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** For the sector: minor — one internal reshuffle, not a re-rating or new entry (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: *the RSHB tie-up + consolidation of financial services under a horizontal-products director sets up Avito to launch owned financial products (wallet/lending/CFA) within ~12 months.* Trigger/catalyst = an owned-product announcement or an Avito Finance lending print; the thesis is wrong if Avito keeps reselling partner-bank instalments with no owned-balance-sheet move.
