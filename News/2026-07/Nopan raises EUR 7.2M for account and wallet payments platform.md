Nopan raises EUR 7.2 million to build account and wallet payments platform
Sinziana Albu
14 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Netherlands-based Nopan has raised EUR 7.2 million in a round led by Newion, with Crane and Seedcamp also contributing.
The latest round was led by Newion, with follow-on participation from Crane and Seedcamp, and support from a group of angel investors with backgrounds in merchant payments and fintech.
The company said in the official announcement that the capital will be used to expand its coverage of account and wallet payment methods across Europe, continue development of its optimisation capabilities, and grow its commercial presence among digital businesses and fintechs.
According to the company, the founders identified a recurring challenge during their time working on payments for large-scale digital businesses: connecting a payment method is comparatively straightforward, but sustaining its performance at scale is more difficult. The founding team said this merchant-side background informs Nopan's approach to performance, operational maturity and scale.
Market context
Account and wallet payment methods have been gaining traction across Europe, spanning pan-European initiatives such as Wero alongside established local payment methods in individual markets. Nopan said digital businesses increasingly expect these payment methods to match the reliability, scalability and operational maturity historically associated with card payments.
The company positions its offering as an infrastructure and optimisation layer addressing routing, bank connectivity, payment method behaviour, and operational performance for account and wallet payments. In addition, Nopan said this differs from infrastructure historically built primarily around card payments, and that its aim is to support digital businesses and payment service providers in launching, optimising and scaling these payment methods across Europe.
Commenting on the round, Konstantin Surkov , co-founder and CEO of Nopan, said launching a new payment method represents only an initial step, with the more significant challenge being consistent performance across banks, customer behaviours, and operational processes. Pieter Welten, Partner at Newion, said the investment reflects a growing need for infrastructure capable of supporting account and wallet payments at scale.
With backing from Newion, Crane, and Seedcamp secured, Nopan said it will continue developing infrastructure intended to help digital businesses and payment service providers capture value from account and wallet payment methods.

# Context-enrichment: Nopan raises EUR 7.2M for account and wallet payments platform
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS / DUPLICATE VERDICT: FRESH.** A distinct event from the corpus sibling [[Nopan and DIAS partner to expand IRIS Commerce internationally]] (02 Jul 2026) — that was a scheme-distribution *partnership*; this (14 Jul 2026) is a *funding* event. Same company, different news, no overlap → not a duplicate. Announced by The Paypers 14 Jul 2026; independently corroborated same day by Tech.eu, EU-Startups, TechFundingNews, ITdaily.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Netherlands-based (Amsterdam) Nopan disclosed a funding round **led by Newion**, with follow-on from **Crane** and **Seedcamp** plus unnamed angels from merchant-payments/fintech. Capital is for expanding account/wallet payment-method coverage across Europe, optimisation R&D, and commercial growth among digital businesses and PSPs.
- **The "EUR 7.2M" is CUMULATIVE, not the size of this round.** The Paypers headline reads it as a raise; but Tech.eu and EU-Startups both state Nopan has "raised €7.2M **to date**." So the new-money figure for *this* round is **undisclosed** — 7.2M is total funding across rounds, and the round stage (seed/pre-seed) is unstated. **Why this matters:** the headline inflates a modest, mixed-vintage total into a single-round number; the actual new capital and post-money are silent. Treat as an early-stage top-up, not a marquee raise.
- **What Nopan actually is (de-PR'd):** a specialist PSP / optimisation layer *on top of* account-to-account and wallet schemes (Wero, BLIK, Bizum, Satispay, IRIS) — routing, bank-connectivity, payment-method behaviour, MIT/recurring, conversion optimisation. It is **not a rail**; it sells "make A2A/wallet perform like cards." **DNB-licensed since Q4 2025**, **EPI Principal Member** (17 Jun 2026).
- **Correction to prior corpus note:** [[Nopan and DIAS partner to expand IRIS Commerce internationally]] listed Nopan as "Paris-based." The funding coverage consistently places it in the **Netherlands/Amsterdam** (DNB-licensed) — earlier "Paris" was wrong.
- **Traction claim:** the company/Tech.eu say Nopan is "live with initial customers" with positive feedback and "growing interest from enterprise merchants/PSPs" — but **no named merchant, no processed volume, no client count** is disclosed. Announcement-grade, not adoption-grade.

## [1] Competitors / peers
A2A / open-banking payments enablement in Europe is crowded, well-funded, and consolidating:
- **Larger-funded enablers/aggregators:** TrueLayer (~$322M raised, ~$1bn valuation), Volt (~$100M raised incl. $60M Series B, 80+ markets one API), Token.io (Series C $40M, HSBC/Mastercard backing), Trustly (pay-by-bank leader), Brite, Yapily/Tink, Banked (acquired by NAB, see [[National Australia Bank acquires open banking fintech Banked]]), Kevin.eu.
- **Rails/schemes Nopan sits atop:** EPI **Wero**, BLIK, Bizum, Satispay, IRIS, EuroPA.
- **Nopan's position:** small challenger, **catching up not leading**. Its differentiation is a narrow-but-real wedge — merchant-side *performance/optimisation* of A2A+wallet specifically, vs generic pay-by-bank connectivity — but the same slice is contested by far larger-funded incumbents. Distribution reach, not concept, decides the winner.
- **Why the landscape is this way (analysis):** the EU Instant Payments Regulation makes SEPA Instant free/ubiquitous → the rail commoditizes → value migrates to the enablement/conversion layer. That is precisely Nopan's chosen slice, but also exactly where Volt/Trustly/Token already sit with 10–40x the capital. A €7.2M-total challenger is under-capitalized for a distribution race.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Founded by **ex-Netflix payments leaders Konstantin Surkov (CEO) and Nick Ryabov** — pitch is "built by former merchants," i.e. buy-side payments operators who felt the "launching a method is easy, sustaining performance at scale is hard" pain first-hand. Founding year undisclosed.
- **2026 momentum is a logo/credential string, not disclosed volume:** DNB license (Q4 2025) → EPI Principal Member (17 Jun 2026) → DIAS/IRIS distribution deal (02 Jul 2026) → this raise (14 Jul 2026). **Why it acts this way (analysis):** a young enabler with no rail of its own must accumulate scheme memberships + method integrations to build a credible "one integration, many local methods" catalogue; capital funds that catalogue build-out and enterprise sales. Each credential is only worth its live volume — which remains undisclosed.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new here:** just the capital + confirmation of Netherlands base/DNB status. The *product* thesis (A2A/wallet optimisation layer) is not new to the market — it overlaps Volt/Trustly/Token.
- **Value-add is plausible but unproven:** the "cards were built for cards; A2A/wallets need their own optimisation stack (conversion, retries, bank-behaviour routing)" argument is real and topical, and being a Wero EPI Principal Member is a genuine credential few small players hold. But **zero named clients, zero volume** = no traction evidence.
- **Who captures the margin (analysis):** A2A has no interchange to skim and thin per-txn economics; Nopan earns an orchestration/optimisation fee. Its multiple rests on becoming the default A2A+wallet catalogue/optimisation layer for *enterprise* merchants before EuroPA interoperability + card-acquirers (Nexi, Worldline) offer the same natively — which would commoditize per-method integrators. With €7.2M total, the risk is being out-distributed before reaching that default status.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** watch for named enterprise merchants live, disclosed processed volume, and additional scheme/EPI wins. Absent those, this stays a small early-stage top-up.
- **Regulatory / structural tailwind:** EU Instant Payments Regulation (SEPA Instant receive since 9 Jan 2025, send + Verification of Payee since 9 Oct 2025) + PSD3/PSR (provisional deal Nov 2025) + Europe's "payment sovereignty" push (Wero/EPI/EuroPA reducing Visa/Mastercard dependence) all expand A2A. Market sizing cited by coverage: open-banking/A2A from ~$51bn (2026) to ~$287bn (2033); A2A projected to rise from ~24% to ~40% of European ecommerce by 2035 (Celent).
- **Second-order (analysis):** the real momentum is scheme-level (Wero going pan-European, EuroPA interlinking) — central-bank/regulation-backed. A private optimisation layer riding those rails is a beneficiary *if* A2A adoption outruns the commoditization of enablement. **The central question is not "is A2A growing" (clearly yes) but "can a €7.2M-total specialist win enterprise-merchant distribution against $100M+ incumbents before the enablement layer commoditizes" — and on live traction, the release is silent.**

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Nopan sits in European account-to-account (A2A) / bank-and-wallet payments infrastructure — an optimisation/orchestration layer on top of A2A and local wallet rails (Wero, iDEAL, Bizum, Greece's IRIS, etc.), distinct from card-centric infra. Size/growth: A2A e-commerce value in Europe is projected to exceed EUR 850bn in transaction value by 2026, with A2A already ~17% of European e-commerce transaction value in 2024 and volumes growing ~30% in 2025 (per Juniper/industry data, via eu-startups/mybank.eu, Jul 2026); Juniper puts consumer A2A at ~$5.7tn globally by 2029. Structure: the pan-European scheme layer is consolidating fast — the EPI–EuroPA agreement (Feb 2026) links Wero with Bizum, Bancomat, MB WAY and Vipps MobilePay into a ~130m-user / 13-country network — but the merchant-side integration/optimisation layer above these fragmented local schemes remains fragmented and immature. "Why now": the EU Instant Payments Regulation mandates 10-second SEPA settlement at no surcharge, and pay-by-bank runs ~30–70% cheaper than cards (via mybank.eu/European Business Magazine), pulling volume off card rails — but each market has its own scheme, so a cross-scheme performance layer is the emergent white space Nopan targets.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: authorisation/conversion rate, payment success rate at scale, number of methods/banks connected, TPV routed, per-transaction cost vs cards. Nopan does not disclose any of these — TPV, take rate, conversion uplift all `[UNSOURCED]`. Basis of competition = product/reliability and breadth of local-method coverage, not price. Named/adjacent players: Trustly (global A2A, ~$438m raised cumulatively), Volt ($60m Series B, ~$350m+ valuation in 2023, "orchestration"-style multi-network A2A), Token.io (Pay-by-Bank infra, ~$40m Series C 2022), Devengo (see internal comp below); kevin. (raised ~$65m/EUR61m Series A 2022) has since wound down — a caution that capital did not equal survival in this niche. Nopan's position: niche new entrant `(analysis)` — the differentiator is a "make payments *perform* at scale" optimisation layer (routing, bank connectivity, method behaviour) framed by ex-Netflix payments founders (Konstantin Surkov, Nick Ryabov). Credibility markers confirmed on web: DNB (Dutch central bank) licence secured Q4 2025, and Principal Member of EPI (the body behind Wero). Moat `(analysis)`: intangibles (founder domain expertise) + potential switching costs once merchants route production volume — thin and unproven at this stage, no network effects yet.

**Comps & multiples.** Private early-stage; no market cap and no disclosed revenue/TPV → EV/Revenue, P/S, price-per-user all "no data". This EUR 7.2M is total funding *to date* (not a single priced round) — post-money not disclosed, so no round-valuation multiple computable. Comparison is qualitative on round size, not multiples (distribution not computed):
- Internal: [[Devengo raises €2M for A2A payments in Europe]] — Madrid, EUR 2M debt+equity, Nov 2025, SEPA A2A infra (closest direct comp: same subvertical, similar stage).
- Internal: [[Nopan and DIAS partner to expand IRIS Commerce internationally]] — same company, Jul 2026; adds Greece's IRIS Commerce (e-com volumes +70% in 2025, 16x since 2020) to Nopan's method portfolio — evidence of live commercial traction, not just PR.
- External (cumulative raised, private, mark as round/total funding not market cap): Trustly ~$438m; Volt $60m Series B (~$350m+ post, 2023); Token.io ~$40m; kevin. ~$65m (defunct).
- Flag: Nopan's EUR 7.2M is small vs scaled A2A infra peers (Trustly/Volt) but broadly in line with early-stage European A2A rounds (~2–3x Devengo). No over/under-valuation read possible without post-money — in-line-to-early on round size.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disintermediation / margin capture by the scheme layer.** EPI/Wero and EuroPA are building a consolidated pan-European A2A network directly; if schemes + PSPs deliver adequate cross-border reliability natively, a thin third-party "optimisation layer" can be squeezed out — second-order: Nopan's value proposition erodes as the very rails it optimises mature. (kevin.'s exit shows this niche can strand well-funded players.)
2. **Sub-scale funding vs execution ambition.** EUR 7.2M total to fund "coverage across Europe" is modest against Trustly/Volt war-chests; multi-market bank connectivity is capital- and integration-intensive — runway/execution risk if commercial ramp lags. `(analysis)`
3. **Zero disclosed economics.** No TPV, take rate, conversion uplift, or customer count — "performance" claims are unverifiable, and the note is silent on unit economics and fraud/liability allocation on A2A (irrevocability shifts risk vs cards). De-PR: "expand coverage" is announced intent, only IRIS Commerce is a confirmed live method.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` Nopan is a bet that A2A's growth creates a durable *reliability/orchestration* gap above fragmented local schemes — a new-entry thesis, not a re-rating. Falsifiable: if within ~12–18 months EPI/Wero and incumbent PSPs standardise cross-scheme performance natively, the independent optimisation layer loses its reason to exist. Watch: additional local-method integrations beyond IRIS/DIAS, first disclosed TPV or named PSP customers, and whether a Series A follows quickly (signal of traction) — absence of any of these by 2027 would weaken the thesis.
