Pie Raises $23.7M to Bring AI-Powered Growth to Main Street Businesses 



 Led by Lightspeed Venture Partners, Pie’s $19.5M Series A brings total funding to $23.7M and will help the company scale its platform partnerships and AI product suite across growth, local discovery and customer conversion. 



 Pie , the AI-powered growth platform for small businesses, today announced a $19.5 million Series A led by Lightspeed Venture Partners, bringing its total funding to $23.7 million. The round included participation from Capital One Ventures, Max Levchin’s SciFi VC, F-Prime, Commerce Ventures, WEX Venture Capital, and existing investors. The company also announced its emergence from stealth and launched Front Desk, an AI product that answers calls for small business owners 24/7, takes bookings and reservations, and responds to customer questions.


This press release features multimedia. View the full release here: https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260630636582/en/ 
Syed Ali (left), co-founder and CEO of Pie, and Akhil Mantripragada (right), co-founder and CTO of Pie.

Founded by former Square and Toast operators Syed Ali and Akhil Mantripragada, Pie is building the growth infrastructure layer for Main Street businesses at a time when customer discovery is shifting across AI search, Google and digital channels. Ali and Mantripragada spent the last 15 years collectively building products for small businesses, where they heard the same pain point again and again: owners did not need another tool. They needed more customers. Pie was built to solve that problem directly, helping small businesses get found, capture demand and convert customer intent into revenue.



Since launching its first AI-powered growth product in late 2025, Pie has grown to reach thousands of small business customers while in stealth, largely through referrals and platform partnerships, and expanded its product suite across AI Search, Growth, and Front Desk.



Small businesses represent one of the largest and most underserved software markets, but most local merchants have historically been priced out of enterprise-grade growth tools or forced to rely on agencies that can charge $2,500 to $5,000 per month, require long-term contracts and offer limited transparency. Many existing tools focus on managing current customers, while Pie is focused on helping businesses generate net-new demand from sources like ChatGPT, Claude, Google Maps, Meta & Yelp. Pie gives SMBs access to an always-on growth platform that can optimize profiles, create content to improve local discovery across different channels and capture customer demand at a fraction of traditional agency costs, without requiring owners to manage multiple tools or channels themselves.



“Small business owners have been stuck with expensive, opaque agency models for decades,” said Syed Ali, Co-founder and CEO, Pie. “Every owner I talked to said some version of the same thing: ‘I need more customers, and I can’t afford an agency.’ We built Pie to fix that problem and bring enterprise-grade growth capabilities to the small businesses that power local economies.”



Pie’s platform is built around three products. AI Search helps businesses show up as local discovery moves into AI-powered search experiences like ChatGPT, Claude and Perplexity. Growth then helps businesses acquire customers across high-intent local channels like Google Maps, Yelp, Nextdoor and other digital platforms. Front Desk helps businesses capture demand once it comes in by answering calls, taking bookings and responding to customer questions. Together, the products are designed to help small businesses create demand, improve discoverability and convert customer intent into revenue.



“Pie is bringing AI to Main Street by starting with one of the biggest pain points for small business owners: finding new customers,” said Aaron Frank, Partner at Lightspeed Venture Partners. “Customer acquisition is a powerful entry point, but the broader vision is to build an AI platform that can support small businesses across more of their daily operations over time. Syed and Akhil have built a product that solves an immediate revenue problem today while laying the foundation for something much larger.”



Pie reaches small businesses both directly and through embedded partnerships with vertical software platforms, giving the company a distribution model designed to scale across categories like auto repair, pet care, fitness and beauty services. For SaaS partners, Pie turns their platform into an integrated growth suite that helps merchants acquire new customers while supporting retention, incremental SaaS revenue and double-digit increases in transaction-based revenue.



“Small businesses are notoriously hard to build for and even harder to reach,” said Max Levchin, Founder of Affirm and PayPal, “but the Pie team seems to have the rare combination of founder-market fit, distribution strategy, and product velocity to win this historically challenging market.”



For early customers, Pie has delivered measurable revenue impact by helping merchants generate more calls, bookings and new revenue. Across the platform, Pie has driven more than 100,000 phone calls to small businesses, and customers typically see a 15 to 20% increase in year-over-year sales. The traction reflects Pie’s broader thesis: AI is most valuable for small businesses when it is tied to outcomes they can measure, from calls and bookings to new revenue.



“Pie has made local marketing incredibly simple to manage across our 65 salons,” said Jason Siska, Vice President of Moxie Management Group, a major Supercuts franchise operator. “The platform’s intuitive UI allows us to launch campaigns quickly and get locations up and running in a fraction of the time it takes with traditional agencies. We maintain full control over our monthly spend, avoid expensive agency retainers, and consistently see measurable results. Most importantly, we’re driving double-digit year-over-year sales growth across our locations.”



“At Tekmetric, we’re focused on helping auto repair shop owners grow their businesses, not just run them,” said Sunil Patel, Founder & CEO, Tekmetric, a leading auto repair technology company serving over 15,000 auto repair shops. “Pie’s approach to digital growth marketing aligns closely with that vision by helping shops reach more local customers, generate demand, and drive incremental revenue in a way that is simple for businesses. Together, we’re bringing a practical, data-driven growth solution to the independent auto repair industry.”



The Series A will help Pie scale its platform distribution model, deepen its product suite and grow its team as it builds the growth infrastructure layer for Main Street businesses. By combining embedded distribution, local business data and AI-powered automation, Pie is positioning itself to serve a large, underserved market of merchants that need measurable growth without added complexity.

# Context-enrichment: Pie raises $19.5M to boost AI search visibility for SMBs
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

> Disambiguation (verified): this is **getpie.com** — a NYC AI growth platform for Main Street/SMBs (co-founders Syed Ali / Akhil Mantripragada, both ex-Square/Toast). It is **NOT Pie Insurance** (the workers'-comp insurtech). All facts below reference the getpie.com SMB platform and the 2026-06-30 stealth exit.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **$19.5M Series A led by Lightspeed** (partner Aaron Frank), announced 2026-06-30. Total funding **$23.7M** → implies a prior ~$4.2M seed, but seed size/date/investors are **not disclosed (open)**. **Valuation: not disclosed. Employee count: not disclosed.**
- Investors: Lightspeed (lead) + Capital One Ventures, SciFi VC (Max Levchin), F-Prime, Commerce Ventures, WEX Venture Capital, plus existing backers.
- Pie **emerged from stealth** with this round and **launched Front Desk** (a 24/7 AI voice receptionist that answers calls, takes bookings, answers questions) — live/GA at announcement.
- **The note's framing is misleading**: the title says "$19.5M to boost AI search visibility." The primary press release actually leads with **"AI-powered growth for Main Street" + the Front Desk launch**. "AI Search" is only **one of three products** (AI Search / Growth / Front Desk) — and the least-substantiated. Framing Pie as a "GEO-for-SMB" company overstates what the round emphasized. **Why this way**: "visible in AI search" is the fashionable hook (GEO is hot); the company is really a **bundled SMB customer-acquisition suite** using GEO as its most novel-sounding leg.
- **Traction claims are all PR-sourced, none independently verified**: 100,000+ phone calls driven, "15–20% YoY sales lift," "thousands of customers" (in stealth, via referrals + partners). Named references: Tekmetric (auto-repair SaaS "serving 15,000+ shops" — that is **Tekmetric's** base, not Pie's users) and Moxie/Supercuts franchisee (65 salons). → **why it matters**: the "15,000 shops" is a distribution-partner's installed base dressed up as reach; treat all metrics as vendor-reported.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **GEO / AI-visibility (AI Search leg)** — a well-funded, mostly **enterprise/brand** field: **Profound** ($96M Series C at **$1B valuation**, 2026-02, also **led by Lightspeed**; ~$155M total), **Scrunch AI** ($15M Series A, 2025), **Peec AI** (~$29M), **AthenaHQ/"Am I On AI"** ($2.2M seed, YC), **Otterly.ai** (bootstrapped, $29/mo, Gartner Cool Vendor 2025). GEO market sizing is early/speculative: ~**$1.09B (2026), ~40–45% CAGR** (Dimension / others; wide dispersion → treat as directional).
- **SMB local-marketing (Growth leg)**: Podium ($439M raised, 100k+ businesses, ships Podium AI), Birdeye (~$93M), Thryv, Weave, Broadly, Vagaro, Yelp — all racing to add the same AI.
- **AI receptionist (Front Desk leg)** — **crowded and well-capitalized**: Slang.ai ($68M total, restaurants), Numa ($48M, auto dealers), Goodcall, Beside ($32M), Rosie. Front Desk is **table-stakes tech, not novel**.
- **Position**: Pie is **catching up on tech in every category** but **differentiated on GTM** — one of very few aiming GEO at **SMBs vs enterprise**. **Second-order**: its own lead investor (Lightspeed) simultaneously funds Profound (enterprise GEO, $1B) — Lightspeed is hedging both sides of the theme, and Pie is the smaller, less-resourced bet.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Founded 2025, NYC. Founders spent ~15 years building SMB products at **Square and Toast** — genuine founder-market fit (Levchin quote leans on this). First AI growth product shipped late 2025; grew "in stealth" via referrals + embedded vertical-SaaS partnerships (Tekmetric, Supercuts franchisees).
- **Why bundle three products**: SMB owners "don't want another tool, they want more customers." Pie's structural bet is **embedded distribution through vertical SaaS** (auto repair, pet care, fitness, beauty) rather than direct sales — turning a SaaS partner's platform into an integrated growth suite (partner gets retention + "double-digit transaction-revenue" lift). → the bundle is the **distribution wedge**, not a tech moat.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Real novelty is positioning, not product**: GEO/AI-visibility aimed at **SMBs** (most GEO tools chase national brands) delivered via **embedded vertical-SaaS distribution**. That is a legitimately differentiated wedge.
- **What's NOT novel**: each leg sits in a crowded, better-funded field (Profound in GEO, Podium/Birdeye in local marketing, Slang/Numa in receptionists). The standout "AI Search" **mechanics are undisclosed** (monitoring? content generation? review/citation seeding?) — **open**.
- **Where the margin/moat is (analysis)**: not technology. If defensible, it's **SMB distribution + bundling + local-business data**. But **AI Search depends entirely on surfaces Pie doesn't control** (ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity/Google) — those can change ranking logic or absorb the function natively overnight. SMBs are also **high-churn, price-sensitive**, and single-shop GEO ROI is hard to attribute — a structurally tougher segment than enterprise brands.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Use of funds: scale embedded distribution, deepen the product suite, grow the team. Sentiment is positive (hot category, marquee investors, Levchin halo).
- **Regulatory**: AI-receptionist disclosure laws are real — **CA AB 2905** (AI phone disclosure, eff. 2025-01-01), **CA SB 243** and **Texas RAIGA** (both eff. 2026-01-01); *Taylor v. ConverseNow* shows **the AI vendor, not just the deploying business, can face liability**. Pie hasn't said whether Front Desk auto-injects disclosures — a manageable compliance-config flag, not existential.
- **Why the market goes this way (analysis)**: GEO demand is real (consumers increasingly ask AI for local recs), so capital is flooding in — but **capital concentration in Profound ($1B) makes SMB-GEO a race Pie may not win on its own**; the counterintuitive risk is that "hot category + marquee backers" invites a better-funded incumbent (or the platforms themselves) to squeeze the SMB niche.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Pie sits at the intersection of two adjacent martech sub-verticals: AI-search visibility / generative-engine-optimization (GEO / answer-engine optimization, AEO) and SMB local-growth automation. The secular "why now": Gartner (Feb 2024) projects traditional search-engine volume drops ~25% by 2026 as GenAI answer engines substitute for queries (per Gartner press release). Second-order effect — brand discovery migrates from Google's 10 blue links to the 2–7 domains an LLM cites per answer, so a new optimization budget line (GEO/AEO) opens up; the AEO software category on G2 grew >2,000% in a year (per Search Engine Land / Cintra, 2026). Structure: early, fragmented and land-grab — enterprise-brand GEO pure-plays (Profound, AthenaHQ, Peec.ai) on one side, SMB/local-growth suites on the other; barriers are distribution (reaching millions of long-tail merchants) and proprietary local-business data, not capital. Note: Pie is NOT a pure GEO play — "AI Search" is one of three products (Growth, AI Search, Front Desk); its de-PR'd positioning is an SMB growth-infrastructure layer, competing more with local-marketing agencies ($2,500–5,000/mo retainers, per PR) than with enterprise GEO vendors.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs for SMB growth-SaaS: ARR, net revenue retention, merchants live, and outcome metrics (calls/bookings driven, merchant YoY sales lift). Pie discloses outcome proxies — >100,000 phone calls driven and "15–20% YoY sales increase" for early customers (per PR; both `(analysis)`-grade, self-reported, no denominator). Key players and basis of competition:
- **Enterprise GEO pure-plays:** Profound (NYC), AthenaHQ, Peec.ai — compete on visibility analytics for large brands; basis = data depth + enterprise trust.
- **SMB local-marketing incumbents/agencies:** the $2.5–5k/mo agency model Pie explicitly targets; basis = price + transparency.
- **Vertical SaaS platforms** (Toast, Tekmetric, etc.) — both partners and potential build-vs-buy competitors; Pie's edge is embedded distribution INTO them.
Recent moves: Profound raised $35M Series B (Aug 2025, led Sequoia) then a $96M Series C at a ~$1bn valuation (Feb 2026, led Lightspeed) — the same lead investor as Pie, signalling Lightspeed is building a barbell across enterprise (Profound) and SMB (Pie) GEO. Pie's position: niche/early — differentiated by embedded vertical-SaaS distribution and an outcome (calls/bookings/revenue) framing rather than pure visibility dashboards. Moat `(analysis)`: distribution + local-business data flywheel; switching costs currently low.

**Comps & multiples.** Pie: $19.5M Series A, $23.7M total funding; post-money valuation NOT disclosed → EV/Revenue, P/S = no data. Round-valuation peers (GEO-adjacent, private):
- Profound — $96M Series C at ~$1bn post-money, $155M total (Fortune, Feb 2026); >700 enterprise customers, 10% of Fortune 500. Enterprise-tier, ~40x Pie's total funding — not a scale comp, only a thematic one.
- AthenaHQ, Peec.ai — GEO pure-plays; funding not publicly verifiable here → `[UNSOURCED]`.
- Internal corpus comps (AI × SMB/fintech-adjacent rounds, thematic not multiple-grade): [[Eloquent AI raises $7.4M seed for financial services]], [[Sequence raises $20M Series A for AI finance automation]], [[Perplexity launches AI shopping assistant with PayPal Instant Buy]] (answer-engine-as-discovery precedent).
No revenue disclosed for any private peer → multiples not computable; distribution not computed, qualitative comparison only. Sanity read: a $19.5M Series A for a ~1-yr-old, post-stealth SMB martech company with disclosed traction is in-line for the 2026 AI-funding environment `(analysis)`.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Regulatory / AI-disclosure exposure on Front Desk.** The AI receptionist that "answers calls 24/7" faces emerging state bot-disclosure laws that may require the caller be told they're talking to AI (per TechTimes, Jul 2026) — adds compliance overhead and could dent the always-on UX that is the product's selling point.
2. **Disintermediation by the AI platforms and vertical SaaS.** Pie's "AI Search" value depends on ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity keeping an openable optimization surface; the platforms (or embedded partners like Toast/Tekmetric) could internalize discovery/booking, capturing the margin — Pie rents someone else's rails.
3. **Unverifiable, self-reported traction + no disclosed valuation/ARR.** "15–20% YoY sales lift" and ">100,000 calls" lack denominators and independent audit; SMB churn and low switching costs are the classic execution risk for this segment (`[UNSOURCED]` on NRR/CAC payback).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` GEO is bifurcating: enterprise-brand visibility (Profound/AthenaHQ, unicorn-scale) vs. SMB outcome-growth suites (Pie) — Lightspeed backing both sides suggests it's a new martech category, not a feature. Falsifiable thesis: Pie wins if embedded vertical-SaaS distribution compounds faster than platforms/agencies can bundle GEO themselves; watch for (a) named additional vertical-SaaS embed partners beyond Tekmetric and (b) any AI-disclosure ruling that forces Front Desk redesign — either would be an early trigger for/against the thesis.
