 X5 готовится купить алкогольного дистрибутора «Регион 50» 
 X5 готовится купить алкогольного дистрибутора «Регион 50» 
X5 ведет переговоры о покупке алкогольной компании «Регион 50», рассказали три источника “ Коммерсанта ” на рынке. По словам одного из них, X5 намерен объединить этот бизнес со своей сетью магазинов «Около», развивающейся по системе франшиз.
X5 — один из крупнейших продуктовых ритейлеров в России, объединяет сети «Пятерочка», «Перекресток» и «Чижик». По собственным данным, на конец сентября 2025 года у компании насчитывалось более 29 тыс. магазинов разных форматов. Как следует из отчетности компании, ее консолидированная выручка в 2025 году увеличилась на 18,8% год к году, до 4,6 трлн руб., чистая прибыль снизилась на 13,9%, до 94,8 млрд руб.
«Регион 50» — основанная в 2004 году алкогольная компания, зарегистрирована в подмосковных Мытищах. Сейчас по 50% долей в бизнесе принадлежат Николаю Лаушкину и Сергею Полтеву. Компания занимается дистрибуцией в том числе импортного спиртного и собственным производством алкоголя. Среди ее партнеров: группа Ladoga, «Кубань-Вино», «Татспиртпром», Bacardi, Campari. По данным СПАРК, выручка «Региона 50» в 2025 году уменьшилась на 1,14% год к году, до 8,2 млрд руб., чистая прибыль увеличилась на 9,15%, до 55,26 млн руб.
Инвестбанкир Илья Шумов говорит, что с учетом Московского межреспубликанского винодельческого завода (ММВЗ) в Дмитрове, принадлежащего «Региону 50», 100% долей компании могут стоить до 1 млрд руб. По его словам, несмотря на высокую выручку, оценочная стоимость компании невысокая, так как ее ключевое направление — дистрибуция алкоголя, бизнес с низкой маржинальностью. По оценке одного из источников на алкогольном рынке, стоимость бизнеса «Региона 50» в рамках сделки может составить 600–700 млн руб. без учета долговой нагрузки. При наиболее оптимистичном сценарии актив может стоить около 800–900 млн руб., добавляет он.
Другой собеседник издания говорит, что ММВЗ в сделку, вероятно, не войдет. Косвенно об этом может свидетельствовать смена собственника у здания завода ММВЗ в Дмитрове на улице Промышленная, 46, стр. 1. Как следует из выписки ЕГРН, новым его собственников 4 февраля 2026 года стала компания «Корнет», чьими бенефициарами также являются Николай Лаушкин и Сергей Полтев.
Президент гильдии «Алкопро» Андрей Московский считает, что для X5 бизнес «Региона 50» может представлять интерес прежде всего как готовая логистическая инфраструктура. По его словам, эта платформа при необходимости может использоваться не только для поставок алкоголя, но и для других товарных категорий. Эксперт алкогольного рынка Сергей Гладыщук отмечает, что этот актив нужен Х5 как крупный алкогольный дистрибутор, работающий в Москве и Санкт-Петербурге. Также он может пригодиться для заблаговременного формирования стоков алкогольной продукции перед всплесками сезонных продаж.
Если X5 все же выкупит и ММВЗ, то сможет контролировать себестоимость алкоголя и предлагать более привлекательные цены на полке, рассуждает Андрей Московский. Для западных ритейлеров это нормальная практика — например, по такой схеме работает немецкая сеть дискаунтеров Aldi. Развитие собственного бизнеса в этой отрасли также может усилить переговорные позиции ритейлеров по отношению к поставщикам, заключает эксперт.

# Context-enrichment: X5 in talks to acquire alcohol distributor "Region 50"
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5. Low fintech relevance — retail supply-chain M&A, not a payments/lending event._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **Talks, not a signed deal.** Per Kommersant (3 sources), X5 Group is *negotiating* to buy alcohol company "Region 50" (Мытищи, Moscow region; founded 2004, owned 50/50 by Nikolay Laushkin and Sergey Poltev). Region 50's co-owner/CEO Laushkin told Kommersant "there are no agreements yet" — so this is **"talks →", not "signed"**. Frame it as intent, not a transaction.
- **Small ticket, low-margin target.** Region 50's 2025 revenue was RUB 8.2bn (**−1.14% YoY**, i.e. shrinking) but net profit only RUB 55.3mn (+9.15%) — a **~0.7% net margin**, the classic thin-margin distribution business. Deal value floated at **RUB 600–700mn ex-debt** (base case), up to **~RUB 800–900mn** optimistic, or "up to RUB 1bn" including the Moscow Interrepublican Wine Plant (MMVZ/ММВЗ) in Dmitrov. For X5 (2025 revenue **RUB 4.6trn**), this is a **rounding error (~0.02% of revenue)** — a bolt-on, not a strategic bet.
- **The MMVZ asset is probably being carved out.** A second source says MMVZ likely will NOT be in the deal — corroborated by the Dmitrov plant building changing owner (4 Feb 2026) to "Kornet", also beneficially owned by Laushkin/Poltev. → **Why this matters:** without MMVZ (production), X5 buys *distribution/logistics*, not manufacturing. That caps the "control your own alcohol cost / private-label" thesis — you can't set COGS if you don't own the plant. The analyst "vertical integration → cheaper shelf prices" narrative (Andrey Moskovsky) is **conditional on also buying MMVZ**, which most sources doubt.
- **De-PR'd core:** what X5 is really buying is a **ready-made alcohol-distribution logistics platform in Moscow & St. Petersburg** — the two highest-value metros — plus a licensed importer relationship (Ladoga, Kuban-Vino, Tatspirtprom, Bacardi, Campari). The stated plan: fold it into the **"Okolo" (Около) franchise convenience network**.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Direct Russian retail-consolidation comps (dated):** Magnit (the #2 grocer) is the aggressive consolidator — bought **Diksi** (2021) and a controlling stake in premium chain **Azbuka Vkusa** (~RUB 35–40bn, 2024–25). X5 (2025 rev RUB 4.6trn) still leads Magnit (2024 rev ~RUB 3trn). Top-10 grocers' market share roughly doubled 2011→2020 (~17%→37%). → X5's Region 50 move is **tiny by comparison** — a supply-chain tuck-in, not a market-share land grab like Azbuka Vkusa.
- **Vertical-integration into alcohol distribution — prior art:** Dixy started **direct import of alcohol to stores** years ago; direct import cuts product cost ~15%, and chains plan to lift direct alcohol imports ~20% and roughly double private-label wine share. Western analogue cited by the source: **Aldi** owning its supply chain to set shelf prices. → So the "own the distributor" playbook is **not novel** for Russian retail; X5 is catching up to an established pattern, not pioneering.
- **Position:** X5 is at parity/slightly behind on owned-alcohol-supply integration; the deal is defensive/efficiency-driven, not first-mover.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **X5 = ~29,000 stores** (end-Sep 2025: Pyaterochka, Perekrestok, Chizhik), **RUB 4.6trn revenue (+18.8%)**, but **net profit −13.9% to RUB 94.8bn** — top-line growth with **margin compression**. → **Why X5 acts this way (analysis):** with net income falling despite revenue growth, the structural pressure is on **cost/COGS and own-brand margin capture**. Buying a distributor is a lever to (a) cut middleman markup on a high-frequency, tax-heavy category (alcohol), (b) pre-build seasonal stock, (c) feed the fast-growing **Okolo** franchise.
- **Okolo fit is the real logic.** Okolo is X5's franchise convenience play: ~5,000–6,000 stores (5,000th opened Feb 2026), target **8,000 by end-2026** and **40,000 by 2030**; Okolo.Market served 392k customers in 2025 (RUB 66bn revenue), a St. Petersburg DC opening in 2026. → An owned alcohol distributor in Moscow/SPb gives Okolo franchisees a **captive, margin-friendly alcohol supply** — coherent with the network's logistics build-out. This is the one genuinely strategic thread.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty: low.** Owning a distributor to control alcohol supply is established Russian-retail practice (Dixy, direct import). No new capability, no fintech component.
- **Value-add — where the margin sits (analysis):** the durable value is **logistics + license infrastructure in the two richest metros**, reusable for non-alcohol categories (Okolo assortment) — the experts (Moskovsky, Gladyshchuk) stress the platform is category-agnostic. BUT: alcohol distribution is **structurally low-margin (~0.7% net)** and **revenue is already shrinking (−1.14%)**. The value is NOT in the target's P&L; it's in **removing a middleman markup from X5's own alcohol throughput** and in seasonal stock-building. Whether that markup capture exceeds the cost of running a licensed alcohol distributor (heavy regulation: EGAIS, excise, licensing) is **unproven — open**.
- **Traction: none yet** — the deal isn't signed. No integration, no numbers.
- **Fintech angle — honest assessment:** X5 does have fintech ambitions (X5 Bank / X5 Financial services, loyalty-card banking with Alfa-Bank; retail lending/BNPL adjacency). **This deal is NOT that.** It's alcohol-distribution supply-chain M&A. The only tenuous fintech link is second-order: a bigger owned-logistics + Okolo franchise base *could* deepen the merchant/embedded-finance surface — but that's speculative and not what's happening here.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** talks may or may not convert; MMVZ likely excluded → X5 gets distribution, not production, capping the price-control thesis. Watch whether X5 also buys MMVZ (would upgrade importance) or leaves it with Kornet (Laushkin/Poltev).
- **Sentiment:** experts frame it positively as logistics + bargaining-power gain vs suppliers (Aldi analogy), and seasonal-stock readiness. Skeptical read: a **sub-RUB-1bn tuck-in of a shrinking, thin-margin distributor** is immaterial to a RUB 4.6trn retailer's financials; its only real payoff is as **Okolo supply infrastructure**.
- **Regulatory backdrop:** Russian alcohol distribution is heavily regulated (licensing, EGAIS, excise) — owning it adds compliance overhead, another reason margins stay thin.
- **Second-order (analysis):** the broader signal is **continued Russian grocery vertical integration** (own the supply chain to defend margins as retail net income compresses). Region 50 is a small data point in that trend, not a driver.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** X5 is Russia's largest grocery retailer: FY2025 consolidated revenue RUB 4.6trn (+18.8% YoY), net profit RUB 94.8bn (−13.9%), >29k stores, adj. EBITDA margin ~6.1% pre-IFRS 16 (per X5 IR, x5.ru, FY2025 release). The target, Region 50, is a low-margin alcohol distributor: FY2025 revenue RUB 8.2bn (−1.14%), net profit RUB 55.26mn (+9.15%) → net margin ~0.67% (per SPARK, via Kommersant/Mergers.ru, 2026-07-03). Structure: Russian food retail is **consolidating** at the top (X5 + Magnit dominate) while the **alcohol-distribution tier is fragmented and thin-margin** — the classic setup for a large retailer to buy the distribution layer. "Why now": X5 is already **the country's largest alcohol importer**, having overtaken specialist distributors Luding Group and Simple Group (per TAdviser) — so it is buying scale in a channel it already leads, not entering cold. Secular driver = **retailer vertical integration** into wholesale/logistics to defend margin as net profit contracts.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: for a distributor — GMV/turnover, net margin (here ~0.67%), portfolio breadth (Region 50 carries Ladoga, Kuban-Vino, Tatspirtprom, Bacardi, Campari); for the retailer — EBITDA margin, private-label/vertical mix, store count. Key players: X5 (Pyaterochka/Perekrestok/Chizhik + the **Около** franchise, ~6,000 stores, targeting 8,000 by end-2026 per Forbes.ru); specialist distributors Luding, Simple; producer-integrators like Novabev (Russia's largest alcohol group). Basis of competition = **distribution + own supply**, not price at the shelf alone. Recent X5 moves in this vertical: 2024 strategic partnership with Bashspirt (reformatting Tantana outlets into "Tantana Около"), acquisition of PokupAlko stores via Tamerlan (per finance.mail.ru/Kommersant). Position: X5 is **ahead** — the deal buys ready-made Moscow/St. Petersburg logistics and import stock-building capacity, feeding the franchise network. Moat: **scale + owned distribution** (intangible import relationships + physical logistics). Fintech backdrop (analysis): X5 also runs an embedded-finance play — **X5 Bank** (JV with Alfa-Bank, ~49.99%/49.99%), loyalty-card-linked cards, cashback — so vertical integration here rhymes with its broader "own the whole customer journey" ecosystem thesis, though this deal is logistics-, not finance-, driven.

**Comps & multiples.** Deal value (source estimates, ex-debt): **RUB 600–700mn base case; 800–900mn optimistic; up to RUB 1bn incl. the ММВЗ winery** (banker Ilya Shumov, via Kommersant). Note ММВЗ likely **excluded** — its Dmitrov plant transferred to "Kornet" (same beneficiaries) on 2026-02-04. Arithmetic on the core distributor:
- EV/Revenue ≈ RUB 0.6–0.9bn / RUB 8.2bn = **~0.07x–0.11x** — extremely cheap on sales, but correct for a ~0.67%-net-margin logistics business (revenue is not value here).
- P/E ≈ RUB 0.6–0.9bn / RUB 0.0553bn = **~10.9x–16.3x** on trailing net profit — squarely mid-range for a low-growth, low-margin asset; **in-line**, not a bargain, once you price off earnings rather than turnover.
- The 0.07x–0.11x EV/Rev sits far below the ~0.5–20x sanity band; this is expected/justified, NOT an over-/under-valuation flag — it reflects margin, not mispricing.
Internal comps: no comparable Russian grocery/distribution M&A in-base; nearest adjacent (embedded-finance/retail M&A, different geo/model, weak comparability) — [[Zopa acquires payments firm Rvvup]], [[Omni acquires Jüsto and commits $100 million to scale grocery]]. Distribution not computed — <3 comparable figures; qualitative only. X5 own trading multiples vs this deal: no data (private/OTC context).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Margin trap / small strategic payoff** — Region 50 nets ~0.67%; the value is the logistics/import platform, not the P&L. If X5 overpays for "strategic optionality" (using it for non-alcohol categories, per Alkopro's Moskovsky), returns depend on integration it hasn't demonstrated. Second-order: distribution stays a cost center, not a profit driver.
2. **Regulatory / concentration** — Russian alcohol is heavily licensed and sales-restricted; a retailer owning both distribution and (potentially, if ММВЗ ever follows) production invites scrutiny and channel-conflict with the very brands Region 50 distributes (Bacardi, Campari, Ladoga) who may resent a retailer-owned distributor. Second-order: supplier partners could route around it.
3. **Unconfirmed, PR-thin deal** — sourced to three anonymous Kommersant sources; no price, no signed terms, ММВЗ perimeter unsettled. Treat as **"in talks," not done**; valuation range (0.6–1.0bn) is a spread, not a number.

**What this changes (idea-lens) (analysis).** Reads as **retailer-led vertical integration** of a fragmented distribution tier, not a re-rating — X5 converting its #1-importer position into owned rails to feed the Около franchise and build seasonal stock. Falsifiable thesis: if the deal closes ex-ММВЗ, expect X5 to reuse the platform for **multi-category logistics**, not just alcohol.
