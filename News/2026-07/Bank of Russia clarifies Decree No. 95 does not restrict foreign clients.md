# Context-enrichment: Bank of Russia clarifies Decree No. 95 does not restrict foreign clients
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
The Bank of Russia (CBR) issued the operational clarification that banks had been waiting for since June, resolving how the counter-sanction regime of Decree No. 95 applies to "unfriendly"-country individuals' bank accounts. The core message: **Decree No. 95 does NOT reach ordinary current/settlement accounts** — it applies only to the specific obligations enumerated in the decree, of which bank *deposits* (вклады) are now one (added by Decree No. 377, in force 1 June 2026). Concretely (per Финтехно, corroborated by Kommersant and legal commentaries):
- Money on a **current account** cannot be automatically treated as a deposit — which is exactly what some banks had done, over-freezing clients.
- A current account can be used under normal rules; it does not by itself fall under the type-C regime.
- The type-C constraint bites **only when a deposit becomes repayable** — i.e. at maturity or when interest is due. At that moment the bank cannot freely pay out: it needs a type-C account or a special permission.
- If the deposit's term has not yet come due, Decree No. 95 does not by itself require the bank to block or pre-emptively move funds.
- A deposit can be rolled over/extended (note text cut off, but points to continuation rather than forced freeze).

**Why this framing / what it reveals:** This is the CBR walking back bank over-compliance, not a new restriction. Decree 377 (1 June) extended type-C to deposits but gave no operating detail, so compliance desks — facing CBR risk — over-froze, catching current accounts and non-matured deposits. The clarification narrows the perimeter back to the statutory minimum: type-C is triggered by the *event of a payable obligation*, not by the mere existence of an "unfriendly" client's balance. The "does not restrict foreign clients" headline is real relative to the June panic, but it is a clarification of an *existing tightening*, not a liberalization.

## [1] Competitors / peers (regime comparison, not corporate)
Sovereign measure; "peers" = the counter-sanction toolkit and the CBR's own prior official clarifications (this is the latest in a long series):
- **Decree 95 (5 Mar 2022)** — ruble forced-settlement to type-C for "unfriendly" creditors.
- **Decree 377 (1 Jun 2026)** — extends 95's perimeter to bank deposits/savings; the trigger for the June freezes.
- **Prior CBR official clarifications (Official Explanations / ОР series, e.g. No. 1-ОР and 2-ОР 2024)** — the established mechanism by which the CBR narrows/operationalizes ambiguous decree wording. This July note is the same instrument applied to the deposit extension.
- **CBR easings (31 Jul 2025; 8 Dec 2025)** — narrow relaxations of cross-border transfers; consistent direction of "clarify/soften operational friction while keeping the frozen core."
See internal prior note [[Russia tightens counter-sanction limits on retail non-resident clients]] (2026-06-29) — the June tightening this clarification responds to.

**+ Second-order:** The clarification confirms the pattern predicted in the June note's [4]: "expect CBR/Minfin clarifications; the gap between decree and operational rules is the open question." That gap has now been partly closed on the current-account-vs-deposit axis and the timing axis (obligation must be payable). Still open: dual-nationals, residence, principal-vs-income cap.

## [2] "Company" history / fit (the regime's trajectory)
The counter-sanction architecture has ratcheted continuously since 2022 while keeping the "temporary" legal form. The characteristic cycle is: decree extends scope → banks over-comply into ambiguity → CBR issues an official clarification narrowing application. This July clarification is a textbook instance of the third phase for the June deposit extension. It fits the regime's dual signalling: trap legacy "unfriendly" money at the point of a payable obligation, but avoid a blanket de-banking that would contradict the parallel effort to court new foreign inflows (type-I / "Ин" accounts).

**+ Why now:** The June freezes generated visible client harm and press (Sber, VTB, Alfa, T-Bank clients), plus Nabiullina's Financial Congress signal that clarifications were coming. The CBR acted to stop banks from converting a targeted measure into indiscriminate account blocking.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Genuinely new:** the authoritative statement that (a) current accounts are outside the regime, and (b) the type-C trigger is the *maturity/payment event*, not client status. This directly answers two questions left OPEN in the June note (its Q9 "deposits only or also current accounts?" and the timing question).

**Traction:** high practical value — this is the rule banks will actually operate by, so it should unblock current accounts and non-matured deposits that were frozen in June. But it does not restore free access at deposit maturity (type-C still applies then), so it is a *scoping* win, not a reversal.

**+ Deeper:** the binding constraint shifts from "client nationality" back to "event of a payable obligation." That reduces the chilling/over-freeze effect but preserves the frozen core. Net effect for the affected individual is materially better for current-account/mid-term-deposit holders, unchanged at maturity.

## [4] What's next / sentiment
Expect: banks to unwind over-broad freezes; residual disputes on edge cases (dual citizens, residents of Russia, principal vs income, the 10M-ruble/month income cap that the decree text still does not spell out). Watch for a formal written CBR Official Explanation (ОР) codifying the current-account carve-out, and any further note on rollover mechanics. Risk vectors: scope-creep back if a future decree adds current accounts; inconsistent bank interpretation persisting despite the clarification. Severity for global fintech/markets: low (RU-domestic, no new cross-border rail); relevance is regulatory-precedent + affected-individual, and it is a *de-escalation* of the June story.

### Red-team challenge questions

1. **Is this a duplicate of the June 29 note [[Russia tightens counter-sanction limits on retail non-resident clients]]?** No — that note was the *tightening* (Decree 377 extends type-C to deposits; June freezes). This is the *clarification* that narrows over-broad bank application (current accounts out; type-C only at deposit maturity). The June note explicitly predicted "expect CBR clarifications"; this IS that follow-on event. FRESH, strongly linked. Answered.
2. **Is "does not restrict foreign clients" accurate, or spin?** Partial. It is accurate that current accounts and non-matured deposits are freed; but at deposit maturity type-C still applies. So it's a scoping clarification, not a full un-restriction. Answered (with caveat).
3. **Is the current-account carve-out official CBR or press interpretation?** Corroborated by Kommersant (CBR to explain to banks) and EN legal commentary (current accounts "in principle not affected"). Whether a formal written Official Explanation (ОР) has been issued vs. informal guidance is not fully confirmed. OPEN on the exact instrument.
4. **Does the 10M-ruble/month income cap still stand?** Yes, per legal commentaries — deposit *income* payable to unfriendly persons above 10M/month routes to type-C. The clarification does not remove this; it clarifies *when/what* the regime touches. Answered.
5. **Are dual nationals / Russian residents exempted?** Still not resolved by this clarification. OPEN (was OPEN in June note too).
6. **Principal vs income — which is capped?** Coverage attributes the 10M cap to income; principal treatment at maturity still routes through type-C but the cap's application to principal is unclear. OPEN.
7. **Does the clarification actually unblock the June-frozen clients?** It should unblock current accounts and non-matured deposits over-frozen by banks; it does not restore free payout at deposit maturity. Practical unwind depends on each bank re-screening. Partially answered.
8. **Which exact paragraph of Decree 95 governs the "obligation becomes payable" trigger?** Not confirmed to article level in sources. OPEN — would need the ОР / decree text.
9. **Could banks keep over-freezing despite the clarification?** Plausible — compliance desks over-comply to avoid CBR risk; a verbal/informal clarification is weaker than a written ОР. Enforcement/consistency risk remains. (analysis)
10. **Is there a CBR/Minfin permission route to release at maturity?** Yes historically for type-C (creditor applies to the bank for use per CBR-determined procedure; Lidings). Whether retail depositors can practically use it is unclarified. OPEN.
11. **Does this contradict the parallel "attract foreign inflows" (type-I / Ин accounts) effort?** No — it is consistent: narrow the freeze to the payable-obligation event to avoid indiscriminate de-banking that would poison the inflow pitch. (analysis)
12. **Global fintech/markets relevance?** Low — RU-domestic, no new cross-border rail, no Western counterparty exposure. Significance is regulatory-precedent + de-escalation of the June human-impact story.
13. **What's the real news value vs June?** It resolves two of June's OPEN questions (current-account scope; timing trigger). Incremental but concrete — moves the story from "banks freezing arbitrarily" to "regime is bounded to payable deposit obligations."
14. **Could a future decree pull current accounts back in?** Yes — scope-creep risk; the "temporary" regime has only ratcheted wider over 2022–2026. The current-account carve-out is a clarification of *present* wording, not a guarantee. (analysis)
15. **What would raise importance?** A formal written CBR Official Explanation codifying the carve-out, or confirmation that frozen June balances were actually released at scale, would firm it up; scope-creep back onto current accounts would make it a bigger (negative) story. OPEN.

**Importance: 3/5** — a concrete, real clarification that de-escalates the June retail-deposit freeze and resolves two previously-open scope questions (current accounts out; type-C only at deposit maturity), but it is a narrowing of an existing regime rather than a new event, RU-domestic, still leaves dual-national/residence/principal questions open, and is not systemic for global fintech.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian counter-sanction / capital-control regulation, not a product or deal. This note is the direct follow-on to [[Russia tightens counter-sanction limits on retail non-resident clients]] (29 Jun 2026): after Decree No. 377 (1 Jun 2026) extended Decree No. 95 (5 Mar 2022, "temporary procedure for fulfilling obligations to certain foreign creditors") to bank deposits, some banks began over-restricting "unfriendly"-national clients — freezing even ordinary balances. The Bank of Russia's clarification narrows that over-compliance: Decree No. 95 covers only the obligations expressly listed in it (bank *deposits/вклады* among them), so a **current/settlement account is NOT automatically equated with a deposit** and is not per se pulled into the Type "C" regime; restrictions bite only when a deposit falls due for return or interest payment, at which point payout to an "unfriendly" person routes through a Type C account or a special permission. The secular driver is unchanged: Russia's asymmetric response to the ~$300bn+ of frozen Russian reserves abroad, with Type C as the domestic "hostage pool." "Why now": the June retail freezes generated visible client harm and press (RBC/Forbes/Expert), and CBR head Nabiullina flagged at the July Financial Congress that the regulator would issue operational clarifications for banks — this note is that clarification landing. (analysis)

**Competitive landscape.** Not a competitive market — actors are the regulator (Bank of Russia, which owns the Type C operating rules) and the systemically important deposit-takers that must apply them: **T-Bank, Sberbank, VTB** (named in June RBC reporting), with similar client reports at **Alfa-Bank**. Basis of "competition" = compliance interpretation, not price/product: pre-clarification, banks read Decree 377 conservatively and blocked broadly (KYC re-screening of nationality/residency, account-type routing). CBR's move re-centralizes interpretation and should reduce the dispersion of bank-by-bank treatment. De-PR: the clarification is *guidance published* — its real-world effect depends on whether banks actually unfreeze current accounts they had swept in; adoption ≠ announcement, and the banks are silent on how many accounts were caught.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation/multiple content — regulation story, no deal, no disclosed metrics; trading multiples not applicable. Affected-pool size: aggregate Type C balances were reported above ~RUB 1tn (~$11–12bn) at the start of 2024 (The Bell, older figure, no fresh retail-deposit breakdown → no data on incremental retail exposure). Internal comps from the base (counter-sanction / frozen-asset / capital-control precedents): [[Russia tightens counter-sanction limits on retail non-resident clients]] (the June trigger this clarifies); [[Telegram's $500m Russian bonds frozen under sanctions]] (frozen Russian-issued debt, Jan 2026); [[Citigroup to sell remaining Russian operations at $1.2bn loss]] (forced foreign-bank exit, Jan 2026); [[Russian central bank revokes ALOR+ depository licence]] and [[Russia's A7 cross-border payments firm plans global expansion]] (parallel tightening of the domestic financial perimeter + build-out of alternative rails). Per-bank exposure = no data (unpublished).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Residual discretion / uneven application (regulatory).** The clarification draws a line (current account ≠ deposit) but the *trigger* — deposit maturity/interest date routing to Type C — still runs through bank compliance and the RUB 10m/month income cap; banks that over-froze may not fully reverse, and dual-citizen / relocated-resident edge cases stay unresolved. Second-order: the "your money is yours but not movable" chilling effect on foreign/dual-national depositors persists even where the account itself is technically usable.
2. **Policy contradiction / trust risk (systemic-lite).** The tightening regime coexists with Russia's parallel effort to court *new* non-resident money via guaranteed "In/Ин" investment accounts (reported near-zero uptake). Clarifying that legacy deposits still hit Type C at maturity keeps the two policies pulling in opposite directions — capital watches how the last cohort is treated, so the clarification only partially repairs the trust damage.
3. **Scope-creep optionality (hard to price).** The regime moved wholesale→retail in one decree; a guidance letter can be re-issued. Any non-resident/dual-national retail balance at a Russian bank now carries open-ended, discretionary freeze optionality that a favourable clarification narrows but does not remove.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** The clarification resolves the specific "current account vs deposit" ambiguity that was flagged OPEN in the June note — narrowing the freeze back toward the deposit-maturity event rather than blanket account blocking — but leaves the structural trap intact. Falsifiable thesis: this is damage-control that slows, not reverses, the exit of foreign/dual-national retail money from Russian banks; the "In-account" inflow scheme stays marginal. Watch: whether banks visibly restore swept current accounts, any published permitted-operations carve-out for retail Type C, fresh aggregate Type C balance data, or scope-creep to deposit *principal* (not just income) — the last would re-escalate this from "narrow, clarified" back to "de-facto blocking of unfriendly-national balances." (analysis)
