DNB goes live on nCino platform for corporate lending
Sinziana Albu
02 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Norway-based DNB has gone live on the nCino Platform to modernise its corporate lending operations, with SME lending to follow in 2027.
The implementation covers nCino for Commercial Lending, the platform's core lending management system, together with Banking Advisor, an AI-powered conversational interface designed to embed data and recommendations directly into banker workflows. The go-live followed what nCino described as a gold standard implementation, delivered with support from Deloitte. In addition, following the initial deployment, DNB has said it intends to continue rolling out the nCino Platform across branches in nine countries.
DNB framed the move as part of a broader effort to modernise core credit systems as a foundation for long-term business value. Cecilie Kirsebom Foyn-Bruun , Executive Vice President of Lending at DNB, said the bank had operated for 200 years and that the shift reflected its ongoing digital transformation. At the same time, it was added that the goal is focused on offering bankers the possibility to have what they need to do their best work: a single platform, connected data, and the intelligence to move faster for clients.
Wider market relevance
According to the official press release, the deployment adds DNB to nCino's roster of European financial institution clients as the company continues to expand across the EMEA region. Joaquín de Valenzuela, Managing Director of EMEA at nCino, said the partnership reflected the trust that financial institutions in the region are placing in the platform's agentic AI capabilities, positioning the tools as support for faster, data-informed lending decisions rather than a replacement for banker judgement.
For DNB, consolidating corporate lending onto a single platform is intended to standardise processes and connect data across markets ahead of the planned SME lending rollout in 2027. Furthermore, the phased approach, corporate lending first, followed by SME lending and further country rollouts, indicates a multi-year implementation programme rather than a single system switch, consistent with the scale of a bank operating across nine countries.
Both institutions will continue to focus on meeting the needs, preferences, and demands of clients and users in an ever-evolving market, while prioritising the process of remaining compliant with the regulatory requirements and laws of the industry as well.

# Context-enrichment: DNB goes live on nCino platform for corporate lending
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5. Freshness: FRESH (no prior DNB–nCino note in corpus; first public disclosure of the relationship)._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **LIVE, not just announced** — and that distinction matters here. Norway's DNB has gone into **production** on the nCino Platform for **corporate lending only**. Vendor PR dated **1 Jul 2026** (GlobeNewswire); The Paypers / fintech.global retellings 2 Jul.
- **Scope deployed:** **nCino Commercial Lending** (the core loan-origination/lifecycle system) **+ Banking Advisor** (nCino's agentic-AI conversational assistant embedding data/recommendations into banker workflows). Delivered with **Deloitte** as SI ("nCino gold standard implementation").
- **Everything else is roadmap, not live:** **SME lending → 2027**; rollout "across branches in nine countries" is a forward-looking plan, NOT confirmed live everywhere. → Why flag this: the headline says "live", but the load-bearing scope (nine countries, SME, full agentic-AI usage depth) is aspirational — classic PR framing where one live segment is stretched to imply platform-wide transformation.
- **Not disclosed (open):** original contract **signing date**, dollar/ACV value, loan volumes, user counts, who nCino displaced, independent DNB-IR confirmation. Sole primary source is **nCino's own press release** — single-vendor-sourced. → Why it matters: a corporate-credit core replacement + Deloitte gold-standard build implies a **multi-year program**, so "go-live" is the culmination of a quiet 2–3-year effort, not a fresh deal (analysis; signing date unconfirmed).

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Core-led bundled suites** (incumbency pressure): **Temenos**, **Finastra** (Fusion Loan IQ for corporate/syndicated), FIS/Fiserv.
- **Cloud-native SaaS:** **Mambu**, Thought Machine, Backbase, Sopra, Finacle.
- **Position:** nCino is Salesforce-native, origination/lifecycle-focused — not a full deposit core. It wins the *credit-workflow* layer, not the ledger. → Why: DNB explicitly modernises **corporate credit systems / loan origination**, NOT its deposit core (separately, DNB uses **TCS** for broader IT transformation). So this is nCino occupying the lending-workflow slot alongside, not replacing, the bank's core — the competitive fight is with Temenos/Finastra's lending modules, not with Mambu-style core migration.
- **Nordic head-to-head wins:** none surfaced for rivals — **open/unconfirmed**; do not assert Temenos/Mambu Nordic corporate-lending wins.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **nCino (NASDAQ: NCNO)** growth narrative = **AI (Banking Advisor / agentic) + international/EMEA expansion**. DNB slots directly into that thesis.
- **European footprint (dated):** Allied Irish Banks selected nCino **Sep 2019** (asset finance, SME, corporate, ESG); Santander International uses it (date unpinned). nCino claims ~2,700 institutions globally.
- **Nordic specifically:** DNB appears to be nCino's **marquee/flagship Nordic bank win** — no confirmed Nordea/SEB/Handelsbanken/OP/Swedbank deployments found (nCino was a MoneyLIVE Nordic 2024 sponsor, but sponsorship ≠ customer). → Why nCino leads with this logo: EMEA MD Joaquín de Valenzuela frames it as regional trust in "agentic AI" — a top-tier bank + Deloitte gives the sales team a reference logo to crack the rest of the Nordics.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Real, but thin on proof.** DNB (Norway's largest bank; ~2M retail, 200k+ corporate clients, 200-year history) is a **genuine production reference win** with a name-brand SI — strong for EMEA pipeline.
- **But:** no loan volumes, no user counts, no ACV, no cycle-time metrics, no displacement detail. Banking Advisor is "deployed alongside" — depth of live agentic-AI use **unverified**. → Why the value-add is only partly real: the durable value to nCino is the *reference*, not near-term revenue (one logo barely moves a ~$642M revenue base). The margin-capture question is whether nCino owns the **credit-decision workflow + data layer** long enough to upsell SME/other countries — the 2027 SME phase and nine-country rollout are where real ACV expansion (or execution risk) sits, and none of it is delivered yet.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Roadmap:** corporate → SME (2027) → further country rollouts; consolidate corporate lending onto one platform to standardise processes/data across markets.
- **NCNO context:** Q1 FY2027 (reported ~28 May 2026) revenue **$159.4M (+11% YoY)**, subscription **$140.9M (+12%)**, non-GAAP op margin ~28% (beat), but **EPS missed**; FY27 guide **$642–646M** (~8% growth midpoint). Analyst consensus Buy/Moderate Buy with wide target dispersion (~$17 low to ~$36 high) = uncertainty.
- **Risks:** decelerating growth (~8% guide), soft US mortgage exposure, SaaS multiple compression; **execution risk** on DNB's nine-country + SME phases. → Second-order: a marquee EMEA logo is a *sentiment* asset that helps the international story, but the market will judge nCino on whether these multi-year rollouts convert to recurring ACV — announcement-grade wins don't rerate a decelerating SaaS name; delivered expansion does.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Cloud loan-origination / core-lending SaaS. Market sizing (secondary, treat as indicative — vendor/consultancy reports, not audited): global loan-origination software ~$6.06bn in 2025 → ~$6.96bn 2026, with cloud-based LOS/servicing at ~13–15% CAGR to the mid-2030s (per openpr/Spherical Insights/GlobalGrowthInsights syndicated reports, via WebSearch, as of 2026 — ranges vary 13.4–15.45% by firm, so read as order-of-magnitude, not precise). Structure: fragmented, incumbent-heavy — ICE Mortgage Tech, Fiserv, Finastra, Temenos, MeridianLink, Pega on the enterprise side, plus cloud-native challengers (nCino, Mambu, Thought Machine, Backbase as the experience layer). Barriers are high: multi-year core-system replacement, regulatory/audit requirements, deep data-migration and integration cost — which is why deals are sticky and slow. **Why now:** two drivers — (1) tier-1 banks are unbundling legacy on-prem cores onto cloud platforms (cf. Danske–AWS extension, Finastra Universal Banking sale to Pollen Street, both Jun 2026); (2) the AI-in-workflow shift — nCino leads with "Banking Advisor," an embedded conversational/agentic layer, marketed as banker-augmentation, not replacement. DNB is the concrete EMEA reference: a 200-year-old bank (2m retail / 200k+ corporate clients) going live on commercial lending, SME to follow 2027, rollout across nine countries.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs (SaaS): subscription revenue growth, ARR/ACV, Rule-of-40, NRR. Key players & basis of competition: nCino competes on Salesforce-native commercial-lending depth + AI; Temenos on tier-1 scale and breadth of core (T24); Finastra/ICE/Fiserv on installed base; Mambu/Thought Machine on cloud-native flexibility; Backbase on the front-end/orchestration layer. Basis = product depth + reference credibility + AI, not price. Recent moves (dated): DNB go-live 01 Jul 2026 (nCino PR, Deloitte-delivered "gold standard"); nCino ProBanker launch for UK lenders (Sep 2025); nCino mortgage-AI innovations (Oct 2025); Temenos raised FY-28 targets on SaaS momentum (Q4-25 results). **Protagonist's position** (nCino = the vendor; DNB is the reference customer): ahead in Salesforce-based commercial lending, catching-up in raw core-banking breadth vs Temenos. Moat = switching costs (multi-year, multi-country implementation, data migration) + reference-customer network effects in EMEA `(analysis)`. DNB is one anchor logo in nCino's EMEA expansion, not a step-change in nCino's revenue base.

**Comps & multiples.** EV proxied by market cap (nCino/Temenos carry modest net cash, so true EV slightly lower — mark `(EV≈mktcap)`):
- **nCino (NCNO):** mkt cap ~$1.71bn (26 Jun 2026, $15.62/sh); FY2026 total-rev guidance $585–589M → EV/Rev ≈ $1.71bn / $0.587bn ≈ **2.9x**. Subscription growth ~15% YoY (Q2 FY26); FY27 total-rev guide $639–643M (~9% total growth). In-line-to-cheap for a ~9–15% grower — no premium re-rating priced in.
- **Temenos (TEMN):** mkt cap ~$5.05bn; FY-25 rev ~$1.09bn (+4.5%) → EV/Rev ≈ **4.6x**. Richer than nCino despite slower total-rev growth — reflects scale/ARR mix (>90% recurring) `(analysis)`.
- **Mambu (private):** last post-money $5.5bn (Series E, Dec 2021). STALE — 4.5yr old, pre-rate-hike-cycle; not a usable current mark → `[UNSOURCED]` for a live multiple.
- Distribution not computed (only 2 verifiable public multiples). Qualitative: nCino trades *cheaper* than Temenos on EV/Rev; nCino's higher subscription growth doesn't earn a premium — the market is discounting the modest total-revenue trajectory (services drag) rather than rewarding subscription momentum.
- Internal comps: [[Backbase five reasons banks unify onto single platforms]] · [[Fintech Wrap Up core banking platforms compared on features and pricing]] · [[nCino launches ProBanker by FullCircl for UK lenders]] · [[Finastra offloads Universal Banking division to Pollen Street Capital]].

**Risk flags.**
1. **Deal ≠ economics.** The PR discloses go-live, nine-country ambition and 2027 SME phase, but no contract value, ACV or migration cost. A single European logo is de-PR'd as a reference win, not a revenue inflection — nCino's FY27 total-rev growth guide is only ~9%, so no re-rating is warranted on this alone. Watch for silence on ticket size.
2. **Execution / implementation risk.** "Multi-year phased rollout across nine countries" = long, front-loaded services-heavy delivery (Deloitte-led). Core-lending replacements routinely slip; SME phase is a 2027 promise, not delivered. Services drag is exactly what is capping nCino's blended growth vs its subscription line.
3. **Displacement by tier-1 cores + AI-layer capture.** Temenos (broader core, NVIDIA GenAI partnership) and cloud-native rivals contest the same EMEA tier-1 budget; the "agentic AI" positioning is easy to claim and hard to differentiate — margin/value could migrate to whoever owns the AI orchestration layer rather than the LOS itself `(analysis)`.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** For the sector: another EMEA tier-1 validating cloud-native commercial-lending confirms the core-modernization cycle is live, not a re-rating catalyst for nCino by itself `(analysis)`. Falsifiable thesis: if nCino converts multi-country references like DNB into accelerating *subscription* growth (>17% and rising) and the SME phase lands on time in 2027, the current ~2.9x EV/Rev is too cheap vs Temenos's 4.6x. What breaks it: FY27 subscription guide already decelerating (~9% total, subs ~$569–573M vs FY26 ~$517M ≈ ~10%), so the burden of proof is on nCino to show the EMEA logos flow through to the top line — SME-phase slippage or services drag would confirm the discount. Trigger to watch: FY27 quarterly subscription-growth prints and any DNB SME-go-live date.
