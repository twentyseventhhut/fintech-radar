Elon Musk praises Mythos/Fable, promises not to ‘cut off’ Anthropic
Should Anthropic trust Elon Musk to host its models? After users on X implied that Musk could wake up one day and simply boot the AI lab from SpaceX’s servers as a way to kneecap a rival, Musk replied with glowing praise for the AI lab. He said that such a trick was “not my style.”
“I was clearly wrong about Anthropic,” Musk wrote on Thursday, referring to his September 2025 post on X in which he said, “Winning was never in the set of possible outcomes for Anthropic.” Of course, even at that time, Anthropic could already be considered a winner; the company was reported to have the biggest AI market share with enterprises .
It seems those anti-Anthropic days are behind Musk — and not just on X. As of July 2026, Anthropic is one of SpaceX’s largest customers. 
To recap: Anthropic signed a deal in May to buy 300 megawatts of compute, the entire output of xAI’s Colossus 1 data center near Memphis, Tennessee. (Musk’s xAI merged with SpaceX in February.) Anthropic agreed to pay $1.25 billion per month through May 2029, a deal worth about $40 billion in revenue for SpaceX’s xAI unit. Google, by the way, also signed a deal to rent SpaceX infrastructure through June 2029, for $920 million per month.
Musk insists that this wasn’t a dangerous decision by Anthropic and that he’s full of admiration for the rival.
“They are obviously currently the leader in AI. No company has released a model as good as Mythos/Fable and they will undoubtedly have Mythos 2 ready soon. And I would never cut them off in a way that hurt them badly, even as a competitor. That’s not my style,” he wrote.
He offered as proof of his don’t-squeeze-competitors style Tesla’s decision in 2014 (which was outlined in a now deleted company blog post and now housed under its patent pledge ) to not initiate patent lawsuits against anyone who, in good faith, wants to use its technology. He also noted that Tesla opened its Supercharger network and charging port design to competitors.
“SpaceX launches competing satellite systems with no increase in price or use of unfair terms. Even my worst enemies can attack me on this platform,” he wrote, listing another example.
Of course, Musk is not exactly above tactics aimed at rivals, especially those with whom he has a history. He sued OpenAI, for instance .
Anthropic doesn’t have to rely on Musk’s sticking to his “style,” though. There would certainly be contractual consequences if Musk suddenly shut down Anthropic’s infrastructure. Not to mention the massive benefits for SpaceX to keep that deal intact. Not only does Anthropic pay handsomely, but also SpaceX’s engineers may learn how to build for, and support, Anthropic’s rapidly growing AI , just like Amazon’s engineers do . 
That proximity might have other benefits as well. During his trial against OpenAI, Musk acknowledged that AI “distilling” was real — a process in which one model maker sets up many fake accounts to send prompts to a competitor in order to learn how it works. As the New York Times reported , when a lawyer asked him if xAI had ever distilled technology from OpenAI, Musk replied: “Generally AI companies distill other AI companies.”
Anthropic in February accused three Chinese model makers of doing this to Claude. Presumably, Anthropic and Google feel they have safeguards against SpaceX doing this while they are using its infrastructure. But hosting Anthropic’s compute could still give SpaceX greater visibility into how the company operates than most competitors would ever have.
There appears to be nothing but upside for Musk’s company in this partnership at the moment. As for tomorrow, and as the three-year contract ages, who knows?

# Context-enrichment: Elon Musk praises Mythos/Fable, vows not to "cut off" Anthropic
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5. Freshness: stale (sentiment re-run of the already-covered May compute deal)._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **2026-07-09**, on X, Elon Musk (a) recanted his Sept-2025 "Winning was never in the set of possible outcomes for Anthropic" post ("I was clearly wrong about Anthropic"), (b) called Anthropic "obviously currently the leader in AI," praising Mythos/Fable, and (c) pledged he "would never cut them off in a way that hurt them badly, even as a competitor." **This is X commentary, not a transaction.** No new terms, no new money, no confirmation compute went live.

The *substance* it references is the pre-existing **May 2026 deal**: Anthropic bought **300 MW — the entire output of xAI's Colossus 1 data center near Memphis** — for **$1.25B/month through May 2029**, ~**$40–45B** total to the SpaceX/xAI unit (xAI merged into SpaceX in Feb 2026). Google separately rents SpaceX compute for **$920M/month** through Jun 2029.

**Why framed this way / what it reveals:** The "cut-off" framing is theatre. Web reporting on the May terms notes **either party can terminate on 90 days' notice** ([TechCrunch, 2026-05-20](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/anthropic-will-pay-xai-1-25-billion-per-month-for-compute/)) — so Musk's "pledge not to cut them off" is nearly costless, and the real lever would be quiet degradation/deprioritization, not a hard kill. Musk's cited "proofs of style" (Tesla patent pledge 2014, Supercharger opening, Starlink pricing) are self-serving analogies, immediately undercut by his own OpenAI lawsuit. The genuine information here is a **sentiment/relationship marker**: a public de-escalation of Musk-vs-Anthropic hostility, protecting a ~$40B revenue line for SpaceX.

## [1] Competitors / peers (compute-hosting landscape)
Renting compute to a direct AI rival is now **normalized**, not novel:
- **SpaceX/xAI "neocloud":** Colossus 1 was reportedly stranded (~11% utilization) after Grok training moved to Colossus 2, so Musk is monetizing idle GPUs. Anthropic came first, then Google, then Reflection AI ($150M/month, GB300s, Colossus 2, from Jul 2026 — see [[SpaceX signs $150M month compute deal with Reflection AI]]), then Cursor (rent-then-acquire; [[SpaceX to acquire Cursor for $60 billion in stock]]).
- **Anthropic's real backbone is elsewhere:** primarily **AWS Trainium** (Project Rainier, >1M Trainium2, multi-GW) + a **Google TPU** deal — multi-gigawatt each. The 300 MW xAI leg is a **minority, opportunistic burst slice**, not the training core.
- **Peers who host whom:** OpenAI↔Oracle/Stargate; Amazon both invests in and hosts Anthropic; CoreWeave/Nebius host multiple labs.

**Why the landscape is this way / second-order:** In a compute crunch, cheap immediately-available capacity beats brand loyalty; ideology is set aside on both sides. The dynamic that matters is not "will Musk cut them off" but "who captures margin on stranded compute" — SpaceX turns an idle depreciating asset into ~$40B of contracted revenue, i.e. becomes an **AI landlord** with visibility into a rival's operations as a bonus.

## [2] Company history / fit
Anthropic (~$350B valuation, ARR climbing through 2026) is deliberately **multi-cloud** to avoid single-vendor dependence — hence AWS + Google + xAI. Musk's arc: Sept-2025 dismissal of Anthropic → Feb-2026 xAI/SpaceX merger → SpaceX AI division restructuring after deepfake controversies ([[SpaceX to acquire Cursor for $60 billion in stock]]) → May-2026 sells idle Colossus 1 to Anthropic → Jul-2026 public praise. **Why he acts this way:** SpaceX's AI unit is behind the frontier labs and needs revenue + a $26T-TAM AI IPO story; renting to (and praising) the sector leader is the fastest monetization of a lagging division. Anthropic's fit: it needs every megawatt it can source, even from a rival, and public warmth from Musk lowers headline risk on the contract.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Low novelty for THIS item.** The deal is old news (May), already surfaced across the corpus (Reflection, Cursor, Google parallels). Traction check: the compute is **contracted + ramping, NOT verified live at full 300 MW** — [TechCrunch 05/20](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/anthropic-will-pay-xai-1-25-billion-per-month-for-compute/) describes discounted first two months (ramp-up). So even the underlying deal is "signed, ramping," not "proven at scale."

**Who captures margin / what breaks it:** SpaceX captures landlord margin on a stranded asset; Anthropic captures optionality (substitutable capacity). The break risks are NOT Musk's whim but (i) **Memphis Colossus environmental/Clean Air Act litigation** (NAACP/SELC/Earthjustice suits over unpermitted gas turbines) that could threaten power delivery, and (ii) **distilling / IP-visibility** — Anthropic rents raw power+GPUs (not model access), and standard cloud isolation should protect weights/prompts, but hosting still gives SpaceX unusual proximity. Note the contrast: Anthropic's own Alibaba distillation accusation was via the **public API**, not hosting ([[Anthropic accuses Alibaba of stealing Claude]]).

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Watch: (1) whether Colossus 1 compute is confirmed **live** at capacity; (2) the Memphis turbine litigation as the credible "cutoff" vector; (3) Anthropic's export-control status — its top models went offline June 12 on NSA guardrail concerns and Fable 5 was in re-release negotiations ([[MTS Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 may return after export thaw]]), a bigger existential lever than any hosting spat. **Why sentiment shifts:** capital + compute concentration among a few AI landlords makes even leaders like Anthropic structurally exposed to a handful of counterparties (SpaceX, AWS, Google) — a second-order fragility that Musk's friendly tweet cosmetically papers over but does not remove. The 90-day mutual exit means the relationship is transactional and reversible on both sides.

**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: AI model hosting / GPU compute rental for frontier labs ("neoclouds" + hyperscaler-adjacent capacity). Market size: neocloud revenue ~$35.2bn in 2026, growing ~46% CAGR to ~$237bn by 2031 (per Mordor Intelligence/Synergy Research, via search, as of 2026); Gartner estimates neoclouds capture ~20% of a ~$267bn AI-cloud market by 2030 (via search). Structure: still fragmented and capital-gated — the moat is power + Nvidia GPU allocation, not software; entry barrier is multi-GW datacenter buildout and multi-billion capex (CoreWeave guides $30–35bn 2026 capex, via search). Why now: (1) frontier labs' compute demand outstrips their own datacenter buildout, forcing them to rent at scale; (2) SpaceX's xAI unit, whose own model effort "faltered" (per [[SpaceX signs $150M month compute deal with Reflection AI]]), pivoted its idle Colossus GPU stock into a rental business — turning a failed lab into a landlord. Second-order effect: the value in "frontier AI" is migrating from model IP toward whoever controls power + chips.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: contracted backlog / RPO, $/MW·month, power capacity (MW), GPU count, utilization. Landlords: SpaceX-xAI (Colossus 1 & 2, Memphis; 220k+ GPUs), CoreWeave (market leader — 2025 revenue $5.13bn, +168% YoY; $66.8bn backlog, via search), plus Crusoe, Nebius, Lambda, Nscale, Core Scientific; hyperscalers (AWS, which raised GPU reservation prices ~20% amid chip shortage — [[Amazon raises AWS GPU reservation prices about 20% amid chip shortage]]). Basis of competition: power availability, GPU generation (H100/H200/GB200 here vs GB300 at Colossus 2), price, contract flexibility. Recent moves: Anthropic–SpaceX signed May 2026 ($1.25bn/mo through May 2029); Google–SpaceX ($920M/mo through ~June 2029); Reflection AI–SpaceX Jun 2026 ($150M/mo, up to $6.3bn — [[SpaceX signs $150M month compute deal with Reflection AI]]); Meta entered AI-cloud market Jul 2026, hitting neocloud share prices (via search). Protagonist framing: this note's "news" is Musk's X commentary (de-PR'd: reputation management, not a new deal) — the substance is the pre-existing ~$40bn Anthropic contract. Anthropic's position: a compute *tenant* renting from a competitor's rails; its moat is model/enterprise share (reportedly biggest enterprise AI share), not infra ownership — deliberately not vertically integrated on this tranche.

**Comps & multiples.** No public equity multiple for the protagonists — both Anthropic and SpaceX-xAI are private; EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E = no data. Deal-economics comps (all via [[SpaceX signs $150M month compute deal with Reflection AI]] + search):
- Anthropic → SpaceX: $1.25bn/mo = **$15.0bn/yr** (1.25×12); ~$40bn total through May 2029.
- Google → SpaceX: $920M/mo = **$11.0bn/yr**.
- Reflection AI → SpaceX: $150M/mo = **$1.8bn/yr**; up to $6.3bn total.
- SpaceX rental annualized run-rate from these three ≈ **$27.8bn/yr** (15.0+11.0+1.8) — vs xAI's own reported revenue of $3.2bn (2025) and ~$818M Q1'26 (per SpaceX S-1, via search). The rental book is ~8x xAI's organic AI revenue → the compute-landlord business now dwarfs the model business.
- Concentration on the tenant side: Anthropic run-rate revenue ~$47bn (May 2026, Series H, via search); $15.0bn/yr to one vendor ≈ **32%** of run-rate revenue routed through a single competitor's datacenter (analysis; run-rate ≠ GAAP revenue, so treat as order-of-magnitude).
- SpaceX targeting ~$1.75–2.0T IPO valuation (S-1, via search) — flag: no clean revenue denominator disclosed, so any implied multiple = [UNSOURCED].

**Risk flags.**
1. **Dependence on a competitor's rails (single-vendor concentration).** ~32% of Anthropic's run-rate compute spend flows to xAI, a direct model rival. Why: gives SpaceX engineers unusual operational visibility into how Claude/Mythos runs; the note itself raises "distilling" (Musk conceded "generally AI companies distill other AI companies") — a proximity risk contract terms may not fully neutralize.
2. **Term/cancellation fragility.** Musk publicly downplays the 3-year term, stressing contracts "can be canceled at any time" (Reflection deal allows 90-day exit after month 3). Why: the "$40bn" headline is soft — revenue durability for SpaceX and supply security for Anthropic are both weaker than the sticker implies; a re-price or pull at renewal is a real tail risk.
3. **Counterparty/reputational (key-man) risk.** The entire "won't cut you off" assurance rests on Musk's stated "style," backed only by contract, not structural separation. Why: Musk has litigious history with rivals (sued OpenAI); reliance on goodwill from a competitor-landlord is a governance red flag regardless of current economics.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Signals a re-rating of "failed model lab → compute landlord": SpaceX-xAI monetizes stranded GPUs by renting to the very labs it competes with, and rivals (Anthropic, Google, Reflection) accept a competitor as landlord because power+chips are scarcer than pride (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: compute scarcity, not model quality, is the current binding constraint for frontier labs — watch for (trigger) any lab breaking a SpaceX contract early or self-building to exit, or (breaks-thesis) GPU/power glut compressing $/MW and pushing labs back in-house. Near-term catalysts: Meta's neocloud entry and the SpaceX IPO S-1 disclosures.
