Türkiye, Hong Kong central banks sign fintech cooperation deal
Paula Albu
02 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
The Central Bank of the Republic of Türkiye (CBRT) has signed a memorandum of understanding with the Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) to strengthen cooperation in financial technologies, according to a CBRT statement.. The agreement establishes a framework for collaboration between the innovation functions of the two institutions.
Scope of the cooperation framework
The memorandum is expected to facilitate the exchange of expertise on financial innovation between the CBRT and HKMA, as well as collaboration on future fintech projects. According to the CBRT, the agreement opens the way for closer coordination between the two authorities on initiatives related to financial technology, though specific projects under the framework have not yet been detailed.
Broader context of Türkiye-Hong Kong ties
The fintech memorandum follows the entry into force earlier this year of a double-taxation agreement between Türkiye and Hong Kong, originally signed in September 2024. The treaty, the first formal tax agreement between the two jurisdictions, is designed to prevent the same income from being taxed twice and to provide greater tax certainty for businesses operating across both markets. It will apply to Hong Kong taxes from the 2027/28 tax year and to Turkish taxes from 1 January 2027.
Expanding financial cooperation with China
The agreement with the HKMA comes as Türkiye deepens financial cooperation with both Hong Kong and mainland China, with a particular focus on local-currency settlement and cross-border payment infrastructure. The approach forms part of Ankara's broader strategy to diversify trade financing channels and attract increased investment from Asian markets.
In June 2025, the CBRT and the People's Bank of China (PBOC) signed two separate agreements. The first renewed an existing bilateral currency swap arrangement between the two central banks, while the second established renminbi clearing arrangements in Türkiye. The renewed swap line permits the exchange of up to USD 4 billion between the two central banks over a three-year period, with an option for extension, aimed at supporting bilateral trade settled in local currencies and deepening broader financial cooperation between the two countries.
Implications for regional fintech coordination
Taken together, the tax treaty, the renminbi clearing arrangement, and the newly signed fintech memorandum indicate a broader effort by Türkiye to formalise financial ties with Hong Kong and mainland China across taxation, currency settlement, and technology cooperation. For fintech firms and payment infrastructure providers operating across these markets, the agreement may signal increased regulatory dialogue between the CBRT and HKMA, though the practical scope of joint fintech initiatives under the memorandum has not yet been specified.

# Context-enrichment: Turkiye & Hong Kong central banks sign fintech cooperation deal
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
The **CBRT (Central Bank of the Republic of Türkiye)** and the **HKMA (Hong Kong Monetary Authority)** exchanged a **Memorandum of Understanding on financial-innovation cooperation**. Per the HKMA's own release, the MoU exchange was announced **30 June 2026** (The Paypers wrote it up 02 Jul; not two separate events). The MoU "establishes a framework for collaboration between the innovation functions of the two institutions" — i.e. between their fintech/innovation units, not a payments-linkage or supervisory-college deal.

De-PR'd substance: this is a **soft, non-binding cooperation MoU** — information/expertise exchange, referrals, and "joint exploration" of emerging tech. Both sides are explicit that **"specific projects under the framework have not yet been detailed."** There is **no** committed pilot, no cross-border payment link, no sandbox interoperability, no CBDC bridge named in the text.
- **Why structured this way:** an innovation-hub MoU is HKMA's standard, low-cost template for planting a flag in a new corridor (it has ~a dozen such fintech co-operation agreements — DFSA 2017, ADGM 2018/2023, MAS, etc.). It commits neither balance sheet nor supervisory authority; it's an option on a future relationship, not the relationship itself.
- **Why it matters (2nd order):** the real signal is directional, not operational — it slots into Türkiye's deliberate pivot toward Asian capital and settlement rails (see below), and gives HKMA a Middle-corridor node. The value is in *what it enables later*, and that is entirely unproven today.

## [1] Competitors / peers (prior art — the corpus is full of these)
This is a common, near-commoditized instrument. Directly comparable recent MoUs in the corpus:
- [[MAS and HKMA sign MoU on cross-border banking]] (2025-09) — HKMA's supervisory-side analogue.
- [[MAS and OJK sign MOU on fintech cooperation]] (2025-11) — near-identical "fintech + digital-asset cooperation" template (MAS↔Indonesia).
- [[CBUAE signs fintech cooperation MoU with Central Bank of Kosovo]] (2026-06) and [[UAE and Philippines sign MoU on cross-border payments]] (2026-04) — the CBUAE runs the same playbook aggressively.
- [[Rwanda and Cambodia central banks launch cross-border payments cooperation]] (2026-06), [[RBI and ECB sign updated MoU on central banking cooperation]] (2026-05) — the genre.

Externally, HKMA's *operational* partnerships (the ones with real substance) are a tier above this MoU: **Project Ensemble** cross-border tokenisation with **Banco Central do Brasil** and **Bank of Thailand** (Oct 2024), and its DFSA/ADGM fintech bridges. The CBRT MoU is at the **bottom rung** (framework-only) of HKMA's cooperation ladder.
- **Why the lay of the land:** central banks issue these MoUs cheaply and constantly; scarcity — and thus weight — lives in the *activation* (a named pilot, a live rail), not the signature. On that scale Türkiye–HK is "just signed," peer-parity with dozens of others.

## [2] Company history / fit
Türkiye's side fits a clear, documented strategy of **diversifying away from USD/EUR dependence toward Asian settlement**:
- [[China and Turkey renew currency swap, set up yuan clearing system]] (2025-06) — the exact CBRT–PBOC swap (up to USD 4bn, 3 yrs) + RMB clearing the note cites.
- [[Türkiye advances digital lira with framework and pilot tests]] (2025-11) — CBRT's digital-lira Phase 2 (launched Apr 2025), now inviting banks/PSPs/e-money firms to test programmable-payment use cases (external: TCMB, Central Banking).
- Broader Türkiye fintech momentum in corpus: [[Turkey pursues membership in Single Euro Payments Area]] (2026-07), [[Turkish fintech Midas files for e-money licence]] (2026-06), [[Garanti BBVA and Mastercard run first agentic transaction in Turkey]] (2026-03).
- The double-taxation treaty (signed Sep 2024, in force 2027) is the legal-plumbing precursor the note references.

For HKMA it fits its self-declared "**fintech bridge**" positioning and the **Fintech Promotion Blueprint / Fintech 2030** agenda ([[Hong Kong rolls out Fintech 2030 blueprint]], [[Hong Kong Monetary Authority launches Fintech Promotion Blueprint]]).
- **Why both act this way:** Türkiye needs non-Western capital and trade-finance channels under FX/inflation pressure; HKMA needs to be the default Asia node for every emerging corridor to justify its IFC ambitions. The MoU is mutually cheap flag-planting, not a costly commitment.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Novelty: low.** It is Türkiye's *first* formal fintech MoU with Hong Kong (that narrow "first" is true), but the *instrument* is entirely standard and the *content* is empty — expertise exchange + "future projects." No traction exists: **zero named pilots, zero live rails, zero numbers.** "Framed," not "live."
- **Where value could become real (hypothesis):** if it later plugs Türkiye's digital-lira pilot into HKMA's tokenisation/e-HKD work, or if it supports RMB-clearing/local-currency settlement corridors — but none of that is in the text. Today the value-add is **optionality only**.
- **Who captures the margin:** nobody yet — there is no product. For Türkiye's fintechs the practical upside (market-entry referrals, sandbox reciprocity) is speculative until a concrete workstream is named.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Watch for **activation signals**: a named joint pilot, a sandbox-referral arrangement, or linkage to Ensemble / digital-lira / RMB-clearing. The regulatory backdrop is favorable on both ends (HKMA very active on stablecoins/tokenisation; CBRT pushing digital lira Phase 2). Risk: like most such MoUs, it may **never activate** into anything operational and remain a press line.
- **Why the market may still care (2nd order):** the *cumulative* Türkiye–Asia stack (tax treaty + RMB clearing + swap line + this MoU) is a coherent de-dollarization / trade-finance diversification signal — the MoU is a small tile in that mosaic, meaningful as a trend marker rather than as a standalone event.

## FRESHNESS / DUPLICATE VERDICT
**FRESH.** No prior corpus note covers this specific CBRT–HKMA MoU. It is a genuinely new, distinct bilateral instrument (not a reprint of the PBOC swap or the digital-lira notes, which are related-but-different events). Low importance, but not a duplicate.
