## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- iGoDirect Group appointed **Greg Duncan as CEO of its Cards & Payments division** (a business-unit CEO, NOT group CEO). Announced ~mid-July 2026 (article dated ~3 days before the 2026-07-17 retrieval), via Australian FinTech / Connecting the Dots in Fintech.
- Duncan is to "lead continued expansion of the Cards & Payments division — strengthening issuer and partner relationships, driving innovation, enhancing customer outcomes." This is **pure PR boilerplate**: no numbers, no mandate specifics, no new product, no target metrics.
- **Why structured this way:** the group already named a **group CEO, Hamish Moline, in April 2025** (ex-ShopBack FS MD, Zip CCO, Visa APAC ventures) [external]. Carving out a *divisional* Cards & Payments CEO under him signals iGoDirect is trying to professionalize / scale the regulated card-issuing arm separately from the legacy rewards/promotions business → the appointment is an org-structure signal, not a market event.
- **On Duncan's background:** PR cites "deep expertise in cards, payments, customer acquisition, strategic partnerships" but names no prior employer. A "Gregory Duncan" listed as Acting Chief Product Officer at **ANZ Worldline Payment Solutions** appears in directory data — **plausibly but NOT confirmed** the same person (hypothesis; do not assert).

## [1] Competitors / peers
- iGoDirect's Cards & Payments = **prepaid / gift-card issuing** (Open Loop + Private Label Visa, physical + digital, Apple/Google Pay). ANZ prepaid/rewards competitors: **EML Payments (ASX:EML)**, **Prezzee**, **Blackhawk Network**, **Wildcard/Novatti**, plus incumbent bank-issued gift cards.
- Position: **niche follower**, not a category leader. iGoDirect's differentiator is being a **Visa Principal Member Issuer in Australia** (awarded ~2023) — removes the third-party BIN sponsor, improving margin and control. That is genuinely the most substantive fact about the franchise, and it predates this appointment.
- **Second-order:** in a market where EML has struggled (regulatory/AML issues in Europe) and margins on prepaid are thin, a principal-issuer license is a moat vs sub-scale rivals but not vs banks. This exec hire doesn't change that balance.

## [2] Company history / fit
- iGoDirect Group: **founded 1999, Melbourne**, rewards + promotional-tech (YourSayPays surveys-for-giftcards, Rewards Come True, True Rewards cashback/loyalty). ~25 yrs in gift cards/prepaid.
- Trajectory: promotions/loyalty vendor → obtained **Visa Principal Member Issuing Licence (Australia, ~2023)** → new **group CEO (Moline, Apr 2025)** → now a **dedicated Cards & Payments division CEO (Duncan, Jul 2026)**.
- **Why the company acts this way:** legacy rewards/survey revenue is commoditized and low-multiple; owning the issuing rail (principal member) is the higher-value, regulated pillar. Splitting out a divisional CEO is the logical step to run the regulated payments franchise with dedicated leadership while Moline drives the group. Fits — but it's incremental internal plumbing.

- **Traction: not disclosed.** No cards-issued volume, GMV, client count, or revenue. The only durable, citable asset is the pre-existing Visa principal-issuer license.
- **Who captures margin:** in prepaid, interchange + breakage + program fees; principal-issuer status lets iGoDirect keep more of it vs BIN-sponsored rivals. But this note adds zero incremental economics.

## Market Research

**Sector & drivers.** iGoDirect is an Australian (Melbourne/Fitzroy VIC, founded 1999) prepaid-card, gift-card, rewards and promotions provider operating across AU & NZ — NOT a Southeast Asian processor (the note's 🇦🇺 flag is correct). The relevant sector is AU/NZ **prepaid & incentive/gift cards**, not general acquiring. Market sizing (secondary analyst reports, treat as indicative): Australia gift & incentive-card market ~US$13.6bn in 2025 → ~US$20.6bn by 2031 at ~7% CAGR (per Mordor Intelligence, as of 2025); a separately-scoped "open-loop prepaid cards" segment ~US$295m in 2025 → ~US$970m by 2034 at ~14% CAGR (per IMARC, as of 2025) — figures come from paid/secondary reports, so treat as order-of-magnitude, not verified. RBA data cited via Novatti: ~A$954m loaded onto ~11.3m prepaid cards issued in Australia [UNSOURCED — RBA primary figure not verified here]. Structure: closed-loop (retailer/branded) cards dominate (~62% share, 2025), so the addressable **open-loop issuing** slice iGoDirect just entered is smaller and more contested. Why now: iGoDirect was awarded a **Visa Principal Member issuing licence in Australia in 2024**, letting it issue prepaid Visa cards directly (no third-party BIN sponsor) and launch a reloadable corporate prepaid Visa card. The Duncan appointment as "CEO of Cards & Payments" is the org-level follow-through on that licence — a signal it intends to scale the direct-issuing business as a standalone P&L, not just run rewards programs. (analysis)

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: gross/total load volume (RBA-style "amount loaded"), stored-value float, program count, take rate on load/interchange, breakage on closed-loop. Key AU/NZ players and basis of competition:
- **EML Payments (ASX:EML)** — historically the dominant AU prepaid supplier (salary-packaging, mall/gift cards); competes on scale + regulatory standing.
- **Novatti (ASX:NVT)** — card issuing / BIN sponsorship / closed-loop & white-label reloadable for AU/NZ; competes on infrastructure/embedded-finance.
- **Blackhawk Network** — gift/incentive cards, partners with Heritage Bank (which issues ~40% of AU prepaid cards); competes on retail distribution network.
- **Heritage/People's Choice** — issuing bank rails behind many programs.
Recent internal moves: [[EML Payments appoints Adam Olding as Global CEO]] (2026-03) and [[EML Payments appoints Stuart Will as CFO]] (2025-12) — EML has been rebuilding its C-suite, i.e. the incumbent is in a leadership-reset/turnaround phase; [[Klarna deepens Blackhawk Network gift-card partnership]] (2025-11) shows gift-card distribution deals still forming; [[Vivi Money launches AI financial platform with Visa in Australia]] (2026-03) shows new Visa-partnered entrants crowding AU cards. iGoDirect's position: **niche/catching-up** — 25+ years in rewards/promotions gives it a distribution + corporate-client moat (switching costs on incentive programs), but on issuing scale it is a new direct issuer well behind EML. Moat is intangible (brand/client relationships in rewards), not scale. (analysis)

**Risk flags.**
1. **Geographic + scale concentration.** iGoDirect is AU/NZ-only and sub-scale in direct issuing vs EML/Heritage; a single-market prepaid business carries interchange/regulatory-cap and client-concentration risk — one large corporate-rewards client leaving materially dents load volume. (Why: prepaid economics are volume-driven; without scale the fixed cost of scheme membership + compliance weighs on margin.)
2. **Regulatory/scheme dependence.** Being a Visa Principal Member means direct exposure to Visa scheme rules, capital/float safeguarding and RBA/ASIC prepaid & stored-value regulation; the incumbent EML's history of regulatory scrutiny (Irish/EU program-manager issues that hammered its share price) is the cautionary precedent for AU prepaid issuers. (Why: a single regulatory action on a stored-value program can freeze new-load growth.)
3. **Execution risk on the pivot.** Standing up a dedicated Cards & Payments P&L under a new CEO, moving from third-party-issued rewards to direct issuing, is a build-out — announced, not yet proven at scale; the news is a leadership hire, not disclosed volume/revenue traction. (Why: de-PR — "expansion" and "innovation" language, silent on load volume, active card count and unit economics.)

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Incremental, not a re-rating: a founder-led private AU prepaid provider is verticalizing (rewards → direct Visa issuing) to capture more of the payments value chain it previously rented from BIN sponsors. Falsifiable thesis: if iGoDirect converts its Visa licence into disclosed reloadable-card load volume and corporate-issuing wins over the next 12–18 months, it becomes a genuine EML/Novatti competitor in AU corporate prepaid; trigger to watch — announced program wins, load-volume/GDV disclosure, or any additional scheme (Mastercard) licence. What breaks the thesis: no volume disclosure + continued sector de-rating (EML-style), meaning the licence stays a capability, not a business. (analysis)
