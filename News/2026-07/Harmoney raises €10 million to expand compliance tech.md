## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **Round type:** strategic *minority* investment (not a priced Series A/B; "minority" + "evergreen PE"
  signals a growth-equity stake, not control, and no disclosed valuation). €10M ≈ $11M.
- **Investor:** Smile Sail (Belgium/EU software+AI evergreen fund). Sole named investor.
- **What Harmoney actually is (disambiguation):** modular orchestration software for
  onboarding/compliance/risk across the full client + third-party lifecycle — KYC, AML, MiFID, PEP,
  UBO, DORA, ESG, TPRM. It positions itself as "Counterparty Risk Management (CRM) orchestration",
  a platform layer that connects customers, compliance teams, third-party data providers and core
  systems — i.e. workflow/orchestration, not primarily a screening data provider.
- **Traction (as claimed, not independently verified):** 70+ financial institutions across 7 countries;
  named blue-chip logos **Belfius, Baloise, Ayvens**; "monitors millions of counterparties daily".
- **De-PR:** the July "push beyond traditional KYC" line is marketing polish on the same May story;
  no new metric, no ARR, no growth rate disclosed. "AI-driven product innovation" is unquantified.

## [1] Competitors / peers (corpus + market)
- **Orchestration / workflow peers (closest):** [[Fenergo appoints three senior product leadership executives]]
  (Fenergo — the incumbent CLM/onboarding-orchestration heavyweight for banks; Harmoney is the smaller
  EU challenger with the same "one platform for the full lifecycle" thesis).
- **AML/screening data + monitoring:** [[Sumsub taps ComplyAdvantage to expand AML screening]] and
  [[ComplyAdvantage and Google Cloud address India data rules]] (ComplyAdvantage = screening/data);
  [[Napier AI selected for FCA Supercharged Sandbox]] (Napier = transaction monitoring). These are
  data/detection engines Harmoney would *orchestrate*, not head-to-head — Harmoney's pitch is the
  connective layer above them.
- **AI financial-crime detection:** [[Quantifind raises round for AI financial-crime detection software]].
- **Position:** Harmoney is a regional (Benelux-led) mid-market challenger. Ahead on "unified
  orchestration" narrative; behind Fenergo on scale and ComplyAdvantage/Napier on data/detection depth.

## [2] Company history / fit
Founded 2016 (Ghent). Structural driver: EU regulatory tailwind — **AMLR** (single rulebook, applies
10 Jul 2027), **AMLA** (operational since Jul 2025, writing CDD/UBO/monitoring standards), **DORA**
(enforceable Jan 2025, pushes accountability into third-party chains). This pushes buyers from siloed
point tools toward continuous, orchestrated CRM — exactly Harmoney's wedge. Taking evergreen PE (patient
capital, no forced exit clock) fits a B2B-SaaS-selling-to-regulated-banks profile with long sales cycles.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Not novel as a category (CLM/onboarding orchestration exists — Fenergo, Encompass, others). The real
question is *durability of the orchestration layer*: if the value is "connect the data providers +
compliance teams", the margin risk is disintermediation — data vendors (ComplyAdvantage) or core-banking
incumbents could bundle orchestration and squeeze the middle layer. Value-add is real only if switching
costs (system-of-record status, deep workflow embedding at 70+ FIs) hold. Traction is self-reported;
no ARR/growth/retention disclosed → treat "market leader" claims (from the investor quote) as PR.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Plan: deepen existing clients, scale GTM internationally, more AI. Sentiment is favorable due to the
AMLR/AMLA/DORA regulatory demand wave through 2027. Risks: (a) €10M minority is modest vs Fenergo-scale
incumbents and well-funded ComplyAdvantage/Napier; (b) "orchestration" middle-layer commoditization;
(c) unproven that regional Benelux logos translate to pan-EU wins. Counterintuitive second-order:
regulatory harmonization (single AMLR rulebook) *lowers* the fragmentation that a per-jurisdiction
orchestrator monetizes — long-run it could compress, not expand, the orchestration premium (analysis).

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Harmoney (Ghent, founded 2016) is a European regtech selling modular Counterparty Risk Management / compliance-orchestration SaaS (KYC, AML, MiFID, PEP, UBO, DORA, ESG, TPRM) to 70+ financial institutions across 7 countries, incl. Belfius, Baloise, Ayvens. Market size for AML software specifically: estimates diverge widely — ~$3.84bn in 2025 rising to ~$10.74bn by 2035 at ~10.8% CAGR (per Precedence Research); other trackers cite ~$1.36bn 2026 base at 8.6% CAGR up to double-digit CAGRs of 14–18% (per Business Research Insights / Allied Market Research / Azilen) — i.e. TAM figure is model-dependent, treat as "AML software ~single-digit-$bn, low-teens CAGR" not a hard number. Transaction monitoring is ~51% of the AML-software segment (per Business Research Insights, as of 2025). **Why now:** an EU regulatory step-up is the real driver — the single AML Rulebook / AMLR applies from 10 Jul 2027, AMLA (the EU AML authority) has been operational since Jul 2025 and is writing technical standards, and DORA is enforceable since Jan 2025 extending accountability into third-party supply chains. This pushes buyers from periodic siloed point-tools toward continuous, orchestrated coverage — the exact positioning Harmoney is selling into (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs for compliance SaaS: ARR / ACV, NRR (land-and-expand across modules), customer count and logo quality, false-positive reduction. **Structure:** fragmented, three layers — (1) legacy incumbents (NICE Actimize, SAS, Oracle, Fiserv, FICO); (2) scaled VC/PE-backed challengers (ComplyAdvantage, Napier AI, Hawk, Feedzai); (3) niche orchestration players like Harmoney. Basis of competition = data/detection quality + workflow orchestration + regulatory coverage, not price. **Recent peer moves:** Napier AI took majority growth investment from Marlin Equity Partners (Feb 2025) and was selected for the FCA Supercharged Sandbox ([[Napier AI selected for FCA Supercharged Sandbox]], Nov 2025); Hawk raised a $56M Series C (Apr 2025); Sumsub integrated ComplyAdvantage screening ([[Sumsub taps ComplyAdvantage to expand AML screening]], Mar 2026); Feedzai raised $75M at a ~$2bn valuation (Oct 2025). **Position:** Harmoney is a small niche / regional player (Benelux-anchored, ~70 clients) versus these globally-scaled peers — described by its own investor as "emerging European market leader in Counterparty Risk Management," a narrower category than horizontal AML (de-PR: that is a self-serving framing, not a market-share fact). Moat is switching-cost / system-of-record stickiness once embedded in onboarding workflows (analysis); no scale or network-effect moat evident at this size.

**Comps & multiples.** This July note is a later digest re-mention of the SAME €10m round announced 30 May 2026 — see [[Harmoney raises €10 million for AI compliance platform]]. It is a strategic MINORITY investment from Smile Sail (an evergreen PE fund, ~€275m vehicle, group AUM approaching €1bn); NO valuation, revenue, ARR or stake % disclosed → Harmoney's own multiple = **no data / [UNSOURCED]**. - Feedzai — ~$2bn valuation on ~$31M ARR (2024) ⇒ EV/ARR ≈ $2,000M / $31M ≈ **65x** (fraud + AML; extreme, reflects platform scale + hype, not directly comparable to a €10m minority round).
- ComplyAdvantage — ~$27.3M revenue (2024), ~$158–171M total raised; last round Feb 2025; post-money not disclosed → multiple = no data.
- Napier AI — ~£18.9M revenue (FY2023); Marlin majority buy-in Feb 2025, terms undisclosed → multiple = no data.
- Hawk — $56M Series C (Apr 2025), ~$83M total raised; valuation not disclosed → multiple = no data.
Distribution not computed — only one peer (Feedzai) has both numbers publicly; qualitative comparison only. In-line/rich flag: N/A for Harmoney (no valuation).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Scale / concentration.** ~70 clients in 7 countries, Benelux-anchored; a €10m minority round is small vs peers' $50M+ rounds. Sub-scale players in a fragmented market are acquisition targets or get out-invested by ComplyAdvantage/Hawk/Napier as those scale GTM into the EU AMLR wave — execution/distribution risk is the core threat (second-order: losing the land-grab window into AMLR-2027).
2. **Category-definition risk.** "Counterparty Risk Management orchestration" is Harmoney's own framing; if buyers instead consolidate onto incumbents' or scaled challengers' broader platforms, the niche orchestration layer gets disintermediated / bundled away (margin captured up the stack).
3. **Regulatory-timing dependence.** The thesis leans heavily on AMLR (Jul 2027), AMLA and DORA driving spend; any delay, softening of technical standards, or slower bank budget cycles directly compresses the demand tailwind.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Not a re-rating event — a small strategic top-up that keeps a regional niche player in the AMLR land-grab, not a signal of consolidation or a new valuation benchmark (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: the EU AMLR/AMLA/DORA cycle drives a multi-year compliance-orchestration upgrade that favors integrated platforms over point tools. **Watch / trigger:** whether Harmoney lands a priced growth round with a disclosed valuation, expands materially beyond Benelux, OR gets acquired by an incumbent — any of which would confirm (or, if it stalls pre-2027, break) the "orchestration wins" thesis.
