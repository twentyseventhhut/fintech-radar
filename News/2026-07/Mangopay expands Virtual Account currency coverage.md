Mangopay expands Virtual Account currency coverage
Paula Albu
13 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Mangopay has expanded its Virtual Accounts offering, extending currency coverage from seven to 24 currencies. The update is designed to allow enterprise platforms to collect funds across a wider range of currencies while maintaining dedicated wallet and account separation for each currency.
Addressing operational complexity in multi-currency collections
According to Mangopay, the proprietary architecture underpinning the update is intended to simplify reconciliation and improve visibility over incoming payments. As platforms expand into new markets, managing collections across multiple currencies typically requires additional banking relationships, settlement processes, and reconciliation workflows. The expanded currency coverage is intended to give platforms a more consistent structure for collecting and managing funds as they scale internationally.
A company official at Mangopay said that entering new markets often introduces operational complexity for fast-growing platforms and marketplaces, as businesses must manage additional banking arrangements, account details, settlement processes, and reconciliation workflows across finance, product, and operations teams. The official said the Virtual Account currency expansion is designed to reduce this complexity by allowing platforms to collect funds in more currencies through a consistent account setup, with the aim of improving visibility over fund movement and simplifying reconciliation across markets.
Currency coverage
The expanded Virtual Accounts offering now supports business-to-business collections across 24 currencies, including pound sterling (GBP), Swedish krona (SEK), Japanese yen (JPY), US dollar (USD), and Polish złoty (PLN), among others. Mangopay has published the full list of supported currencies on its website.
Implications for platform growth
The expansion reflects a broader trend among payments infrastructure providers to support platforms and marketplaces operating across an increasing number of currencies and jurisdictions. By consolidating currency collection under a single account architecture, providers such as Mangopay aim to reduce the operational burden associated with managing multiple banking relationships as platforms expand into new international markets.

# Context-enrichment: Mangopay expands Virtual Account currency coverage
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Mangopay expanded its **Virtual Accounts** product (dedicated per-currency virtual IBANs for platforms/marketplaces to collect funds) from **7 to 24 currencies** for B2B collections. New set: AED, AUD, CAD, CHF, CNH, CZK, DKK, EUR, GBP, HKD, HUF, ILS, JPY, MXN, NOK, NZD, PLN, RON, SAR, SEK, SGD, TRY, USD, ZAR. Announced ~9–13 Jul 2026 (fintech.global dated 9 Jul; The Paypers retelling 13 Jul).
- **De-PR'd:** this is a **currency-list extension of an existing product**, not a new capability. The architecture is unchanged: 1 wallet = 1 currency, 1 Virtual Account = 1 wallet → so a Virtual Account still only ever holds a **single** currency; there is no "one account receives all 24" (per Mangopay docs). The PR framing of "consistent account setup / simplified reconciliation" is the standard virtual-IBAN pitch — real but incremental.
- **Why framed this way (→ what it reveals):** headline-anchored to "7 → 24" (>3x) because the absolute value-add is modest — adding currencies to a live rail. The dependency on the underlying **LU (Luxembourg) IBAN structure** is the tell: currency reach is gated by Mangopay's own banking/licence footprint (Crédit Mutuel Arkéa heritage, Lux base), which is why it ships in currency batches over years (7 currencies added back in 2014; USD/CAD later) rather than all at once. → The real question is not "is multi-currency new" (it isn't) but **"how deep does local collection go"** — a virtual IBAN in a currency ≠ genuine local rails/local receiving in every one of those 24 markets. PR is silent on which currencies get true local clearing vs FX-into-an-IBAN.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Crowded, commoditizing layer. Direct/adjacent:
- **Stripe (Connect)** — dominant for consumer/gig marketplaces (checkout, split payouts); Mangopay/Adyen compete on enterprise/regional depth.
- **Adyen** — enterprise acquiring + platform payouts; broader own-licence footprint. (see [[Adyen and Starling Bank offer Tap to Pay for UK SMEs]])
- **Airwallex** — multi-currency accounts + 150+ payout corridors, crossed $1B annualized revenue (see [[Airwallex crosses $1B annualized revenue]]); arguably the sharpest peer on multi-currency collection breadth.
- **BaaS/virtual-IBAN infra:** OpenPayd (see [[Currency.com selects OpenPayd for multi-currency infrastructure]], [[VALR adds multi-currency fiat capabilities via OpenPayd]]), Currencycloud, Banking Circle, Clear Junction (see [[Clear Junction launches multi-currency SWIFT remittance service]]), TransferMate (see [[QNB integrates TransferMate for multi-currency collections]]).
- **Position:** parity-to-catching-up. 24 currencies is competitive but not leading vs Airwallex/Banking Circle breadth. **Why the landscape is this way:** virtual IBANs + multi-currency collection have become **table stakes** for platform-payments infra; differentiation has migrated up-stack to reconciliation UX, marketplace payout/KYC orchestration, and vertical depth. Second-order: as currency lists converge across vendors, the moat is **local receiving rails + regulated wallet architecture**, not the count.

## [2] Company history / fit
Luxembourg-based marketplace/embedded-payments infra (founded 2013). Majority-owned by **Advent International** (2023 acquisition, incl. €75M primary capital; Crédit Mutuel Arkéa minority). Pre-acquisition disclosed scale: 2,500+ clients (Vinted, Chrono24, Leboncoin, Rakuten, Wallapop, Malt), ~€25B cumulative pay-ins since 2013, ~+50% volume CAGR. Recent corpus trajectory: marketplace wallet wins ([[Debenhams taps Mangopay for marketplace wallet payments]]), private-markets ([[Caption selects Mangopay for private markets payments]]), and a product-org build-out under new CPO ([[Mangopay names Andy Wiggan Chief Product Officer]]) targeting larger platforms + crypto.
- **Why it acts this way:** post-Advent mandate is to become a global category leader beyond Europe → needs to serve platforms scaling into more markets → so it steadily widens currency/geo coverage to defend and up-sell its enterprise base. This release fits the CPO-era "expanded embedded finance / bigger platforms" roadmap. It is a **retention/expansion feature**, consistent with strategy, not a strategic pivot.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **New?** Only incrementally — same product, +17 currencies. Multi-currency virtual accounts existed at Mangopay (USD/CAD added earlier) and across all peers.
- **Traction:** **announced, no adoption metrics** — zero named launch clients, no volume/uptake figures for the new currencies. Classic "capability shipped, not proven."
- **Value-add depth:** genuine for existing enterprise clients expanding internationally (fewer local banking relationships, per-currency reconciliation) — but the economics favor Mangopay: it deepens lock-in and captures **FX + collection fees** across more corridors. **Who captures the margin:** the wallet/IBAN operator monetizes FX spread and per-account fees; the platform gets convenience. Caveat: value hinges on **true local collection** in each currency — unconfirmed which of the 24 are local rails vs FX-into-a-Lux-IBAN. If mostly the latter, the reconciliation/visibility benefit is real but the "avoid local banking relationships" claim is overstated.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Expect further currency/geo batches and up-stack reconciliation/embedded-finance features under the Advent growth plan; likely paired with client-collection case studies later. Backdrop: payments-infra vendors are all racing to blanket platforms' multi-currency needs — a commoditization race. **Counterintuitive second-order:** more currencies at every vendor **erodes** currency count as a differentiator and pushes competition to price (FX spread) and orchestration/vertical depth — so a "coverage" announcement may signal Mangopay is defending parity rather than pulling ahead. Regulatory backdrop (EU PSD/IBAN-discrimination rules, local licensing) constrains how "local" each currency truly is.

## Red-team / challenge questions

1. **Is this actually new?** No — it is a currency-list extension (7→24) of a live product; multi-currency virtual accounts already existed at Mangopay (USD/CAD added earlier) and at every peer. **Verdict: incremental.**
2. **Which of the 24 currencies get TRUE local receiving rails vs FX-into-a-Lux IBAN?** Open — PR silent; docs tie coverage to the "LU IBAN structure," suggesting many are not native local rails. This materially affects the "avoid local banking relationships" claim.
3. **Any named launch clients or uptake numbers for the new currencies?** No — announcement only, zero adoption metrics. Traction unproven.
4. **Does one account hold multiple currencies?** No — 1 wallet = 1 currency = 1 Virtual Account (per docs). "Consistent account setup" is many single-currency accounts, not a unified multi-currency account.
5. **How does this compare to Airwallex/Banking Circle currency breadth?** Roughly parity-to-behind; Airwallex offers 150+ payout corridors. 24 is competitive, not leading.
6. **What does Mangopay monetize here?** FX spread + per-account/collection fees across more corridors; deepens enterprise lock-in. The margin sits with the IBAN/wallet operator, not the platform.
7. **Is currency count even a durable differentiator?** No — commoditizing; peers converge, competition shifts to FX pricing + reconciliation/orchestration UX and vertical depth.
8. **Why now?** Fits post-Advent (2023) "global category leader beyond Europe" mandate and the new-CPO product build-out; a retention/expansion feature, not a pivot.
9. **What's Mangopay's real scale today?** Pre-Advent figures only (2,500+ clients, ~€25B cumulative pay-ins, ~+50% CAGR); no fresh 2025–26 volume disclosure. Scale claims are dated.
10. **Regulatory constraint on "local" collection?** EU IBAN-discrimination / PSD rules and per-currency licensing limit how genuinely local each currency is — unaddressed in PR.
11. **Is The Paypers item a fresh event or a retelling?** Retelling of the ~9 Jul fintech.global announcement; same facts/figures, different source. Event itself is a single announcement.
12. **Does any prior corpus note cover THIS same expansion?** No — prior Mangopay notes are Debenhams (marketplace wallet), Caption (private markets), CPO appointment. This specific 7→24 currency expansion is not previously covered → fresh (as a distinct, if minor, event).
13. **Second-order risk if mostly FX-into-IBAN?** Reconciliation/visibility benefit real, but "consolidate away local banking" is overstated → could invite pushback from platforms needing true in-market accounts.
14. **Who needs whom more?** Existing enterprise platforms scaling internationally benefit from staying on Mangopay rather than adding vendors; Mangopay needs coverage parity to avoid losing them to Airwallex/Adyen. Mutual, mild.

Importance: 2/5 — Real, on-strategy product update for an established, Advent-backed marketplace-payments infra player, but incremental (currency-list extension of a live product), commoditized capability, no adoption/volume metrics, and single-source retelling. Not novel, not proven; matters mainly as evidence of Mangopay's steady coverage build-out.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Mangopay sits in embedded / marketplace-payments infrastructure: wallet-based multi-party fund flows plus multi-currency Virtual Accounts (dedicated per-currency IBANs/wallets) for platforms collecting cross-border. Market size: embedded payments ~$39bn in 2025 growing at a ~35% CAGR toward the early-2030s per Grand View Research (via grandviewresearch.com; wide estimate range across houses, so treat as order-of-magnitude, not a precise TAM). Structure: fragmented and layered — global acquirers/orchestrators (Adyen, Stripe) at the top, cross-border/multi-currency-account specialists (Airwallex, OpenPayd, Currencycloud/Visa) in the middle, and marketplace-specialists (Mangopay) targeting complex multi-seller payout flows. Entry barriers are regulatory (e-money/payment licensing per currency+jurisdiction) and integration/switching-cost-based rather than pure capital. Why now: platforms scaling internationally hit operational drag — extra banking relationships, settlement and reconciliation per currency — so consolidating collection under one account architecture (this news: 7 → 24 currencies) is the current competitive battleground (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: TPV/transaction volume, take rate, FX margin, number of platform customers. Mangopay's own disclosed metrics: >€110bn cumulative transactions processed and >250m end users (per Mangopay corporate site, as of 2024–25); it does not disclose annual net revenue publicly since 2022 (then ~€30m net revenue on ~€13bn volume) → current revenue/take-rate `[UNSOURCED]`. Peers and basis of competition (product depth × currency breadth × distribution):
- **Adyen for Platforms** — platforms net revenue €55.5m, +63% YoY; 177,000 platform customers (up from 96,000) per PYMNTS/Adyen 2025; competes on unified acquiring + embedded finance (issuing volume 8x YoY).
- **Stripe Connect** — API-first, developer distribution; the default global marketplace-payout rail.
- **Airwallex** — the closest multi-currency-account comp; $1.3bn annualized revenue (+74% YoY) and $287bn annualized volume as of Mar-2026 (per Fortune/company).
- **OpenPayd / Currencycloud (Visa)** — virtual-IBAN / multi-currency-account infrastructure; OpenPayd added NL virtual IBANs Nov-2025 ([[OpenPayd adds virtual IBANs]]).
Recent Mangopay moves in-base: Debenhams marketplace wallet ([[Debenhams taps Mangopay for marketplace wallet payments]], Jul-2025), Caption private-markets ([[Caption selects Mangopay for private markets payments]], Feb-2026). Position: **niche specialist** — strong in EU marketplace wallet/payout complexity (Vinted, Rakuten, LeBoncoin, Wallapop client base) but a currency-breadth laggard catching up (24 vs Airwallex/Adyen's broader coverage) (analysis). Moat: switching costs from deep wallet/payout integration + EU marketplace concentration, not scale (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** External: Airwallex last round $11bn post-money (Series H, Jun-2026, per CNBC/BusinessWire) on $1.3bn annualized revenue → round-valuation/revenue ≈ $11bn / $1.3bn = **8.5x** (private round multiple, not market cap; growth ~74% YoY partly justifies it). Adyen for Platforms is a segment inside listed Adyen, not separately valued → segment multiple = no data. Mangopay: last known valuation event is Advent's 2022 majority acquisition (Advent injected €75m primary capital, per Silicon Luxembourg); no post-2022 valuation or current revenue publicly verifiable → **Mangopay EV/Revenue = no data / `[UNSOURCED]`**. Distribution not computed (only one clean comparable multiple); qualitative read: Airwallex's 8.5x on hyper-growth sets the private-market benchmark; Mangopay is not directly comparable in scale/growth.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Competitive intensity / catch-up on currency breadth** — moving 7 → 24 currencies is table-stakes catch-up; Airwallex (60+ currencies) and Adyen out-scale on coverage and are pushing into embedded finance (issuing, accounts). Mangopay risks being out-featured in the exact dimension it just announced (analysis).
2. **Regulatory / licensing across currencies** — each new collection currency/jurisdiction adds e-money/payment-licensing, safeguarding and reconciliation obligations; expansion speed is gated by license coverage, and the PR is silent on which currencies are self-licensed vs partner-bank-dependent (de-PR: dependence on someone else's rails is unstated).
3. **Margin / commoditization** — multi-currency virtual accounts are becoming a commodity (OpenPayd, Airwallex, Currencycloud all offer them); differentiation and FX-margin economics compress as coverage converges, and take rate is undisclosed → margin trajectory `[UNSOURCED]`.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** For the sector this is incremental catch-up, not a re-rating — it confirms that currency breadth + reconciliation simplicity is the new competitive floor in marketplace-payments infra (analysis). Watch: whether Mangopay converts breadth into named enterprise wins/volume disclosures (like the Debenhams/Caption pattern) — that would validate the thesis; continued silence on revenue/take-rate or churn of marquee EU marketplaces to Adyen/Stripe/Airwallex would break it (analysis).
