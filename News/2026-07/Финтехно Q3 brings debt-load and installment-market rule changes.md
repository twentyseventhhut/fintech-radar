# Context-enrichment: Финтехно — Q3 brings debt-load and installment-market rule changes
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
This is a **Финтехно roundup** (2026-07-01) bundling THREE distinct Russian regulatory changes that bite from **1 July 2026 (start of Q3)** — not one event, and NOT the 400k installment-cap bill. The de-PR'd substance of each:

- **(1) Debt-load (ПДН) income methodology.** From **1 July 2026** the Bank of Russia changes how *undocumented* self-declared income is treated in the ПДН (показатель долговой нагрузки / DSTI) calculation: a **10% discount (haircut)** is applied — only **90%** of income declared in the application without documents counts — AND that figure is **capped at the regional per-capita income (Rosstat)**. From **1 July 2027** the CBR plans to drop the simplified no-documents route entirely and count **only officially documented income**. (ria.ru 2026-06-30, tass via smart-lab, investfuture 2026-07-01)
  - **Why structured this way / what it reveals:** this is a **transitional two-step tightening**. Borrowers (esp. self-employed / ИП / gray-income) currently inflate declared income to pass ПДН thresholds; the haircut + Rosstat cap is a 2026 stopgap, and the 2027 "documented-only" rule is the real hammer. It closes the biggest ПДН-gaming loophole — undocumented income — which structurally shrinks the addressable pool of high-ПДН borrowers.

- **(2) Macroprudential limits (МПЛ) for Q3 2026.** Decided **29 April 2026**, applied **1 July 2026**. Hard filters on risky retail issuance, near-prohibitive for the most over-loaded clients:
  - **Credit cards / lines / overdrafts:** share of issuance to borrowers with **ПДН > 80% = 0%** (effectively banned); **ПДН > 50% capped at 10%**.
  - **New-build mortgages:** risky-loan limit cut **7% → 5%**; **ready housing 20% → 15%**; **consumer loans secured by real estate 25% → 18%**.
  - **Non-target auto loans (car-collateral):** ПДН>50% **20%→18%**, ПДН>80% **5%→3%**; target auto loans held (25% / 5%).
  Affected segments: ИЖС, mortgage, non-target real-estate-collateral loans, unsecured consumer credit. (cbr.ru macroprudential_limits; rg.ru 2026-07-01; consultant.ru hotdocs/93818)
  - **Why:** the CBR is pacing down closed-loop over-indebtedness quarter-by-quarter; the "ПДН>80% = 0% on cards" is the sharpest single number — it converts a soft limit into a de-facto ban for the most stretched borrowers.

- **(3) New MFO base standard.** From **1 July 2026** a new CBR-approved **base standard for protecting financial-service recipients of microfinance orgs (МФО)** applies — **mandatory for ALL MFOs** regardless of SRO membership. It bans **pre-checked consent** for add-on services not offered simultaneously with the loan, requires full disclosure + a clear right to refuse, and bars **misleading/complex wording (dark patterns)**. Enforcement is hard: fines, prescriptions, up to **removal from the register**. (rg.ru 2026-07-01 "Банк России усилил защиту клиентов МФО"; rapsi)
  - **Why:** cleans the MFO customer journey of imposed services and dark patterns — a consumer-protection layer parallel to the quantitative ПДН/МПЛ tightening.

**The aggregated text is truncated** ("МФО больше не…"), but the three pillars are confirmed from primary/press sources.

## [1] Competitors / peers (regulatory context)
**Domestic subjects:** all banks + MFOs (ПДН + МПЛ), all MFOs (base standard). This is system-wide, not company-specific.
**International regulatory peers (dated):** UK FCA BNPL/Consumer-Duty from July 2026 — [[FCA confirms BNPL protections under Consumer Duty from July 2026]], [[FCA sets out BNPL oversight proposals]]; Central Bank of Oman BNPL rules 2026-03 — [[Central Bank of Oman introduces BNPL regulation]]; US scale marker — [[BNPL providers originated nearly $160 billion of US credit in 2025]].
**Position:** Russia's **quantitative DSTI/MPL toolkit is stricter than most Western BNPL regimes**, which lean on conduct/affordability rules rather than hard issuance-share caps. The "ПДН>80% = 0%" ban has no direct Western analogue in consumer cards.
**Why the landscape is this way (analysis):** Western regulators (FCA, US AGs) fight BNPL *conduct*; the CBR runs a **countercyclical capital/macroprudential machine** — it caps the *quantity* of risky flow, not just disclosure. Second-order: this compresses the high-ПДН consumer-credit funnel across ALL lenders simultaneously, so growth decelerates system-wide, not per-firm.

## [2] Company history / fit (the regulator's trajectory)
Fits a **consistent 2025–2026 CBR tightening arc** visible in the corpus: [[Russia's central bank calls MAX transaction approval premature]] (2026-03, payment-rail caution), [[Финтехно Russian central bank maps hybrid payment-instrument usage]] (2026-06), and the parallel installment-market regime [[Russian government backs installment-market bill with delay]] (2026-07, the 400k-cap bill — a DIFFERENT change). **Why the CBR acts this way:** Russian household credit ran hot; the regulator's response is a **layered squeeze** — (a) ПДН income realism, (b) МПЛ quantity caps, (c) MFO conduct — each closing a different leakage (income-gaming, high-risk volume, dark-pattern add-ons). The stated goal: cut закредитованность (over-indebtedness) and просрочка (delinquency).

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**What is genuinely new:** all three changes are **live/enacted from 1 July 2026** (МПЛ decided 29 Apr 2026; ПДН methodology and MFO standard effective 1 Jul 2026) — this is **real, not announced**. The one forward-looking item is the **1 July 2027 "documented-income-only"** step, which is announced. **As a roundup, its own novelty is aggregation**, not a scoop.
**Who captures the effect / second-order (analysis):** the ПДН income haircut + 2027 documented-only rule structurally **excludes gray-income borrowers** — hits MFOs and subprime consumer lenders hardest (their book skews to undocumented income); the "ПДН>80% = 0%" card ban prices out the most stretched clients entirely. Net: the pool shifts toward **documented-income, lower-ПДН borrowers** — good for asset quality, a drag on volume. The MFO base standard raises compliance cost, pressuring small MFOs.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Confirmed effective 1 Jul 2026:** ПДН 10% haircut + Rosstat cap; Q3 МПЛ (cards ПДН>80%=0%, mortgage/collateral cuts); MFO base standard. **Confirmed announced for 1 Jul 2027:** documented-income-only ПДН.
- **CBR framing:** reduce over-indebtedness, delinquency, and risky retail volume; consumer protection in MFO channel.
- **Market read:** credit-card issuance already fell sharply in 2025 (press cites ~-43%) under prior ПДН/МПЛ tightening + high key rate — Q3 rules push further the same direction. (analysis)
- **Counterintuitive 2nd-order effect (analysis):** squeezing documented/regulated credit may **push marginal borrowers toward the (separately-tightening) installment/BNPL channel** or informal lending — the CBR is closing loopholes in parallel (installment bill 1205970-8) precisely to prevent that arbitrage. The two Финтехно picks are thus **complementary halves of one tightening program**, not duplicates.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: Russian unsecured/retail lending + microfinance (MFO) + installment/BNPL (рассрочка), governed by the Bank of Russia's macroprudential-limit (МПЛ) regime keyed to the DSTI/ПДН ratio (показатель долговой нагрузки). Structure — bank-dominated: MFO outstandings are <4% of total consumer loans, with banks (Sber, T-Bank, Alfa, Sovcombank, OTP, Home Credit-type) holding the bulk; entry barriers are regulatory (licensing, capital, МПЛ quotas) not technological. Drivers/"why now" (all as-of/effective 1 Jul 2026): (1) ЦБ set stricter Q3-2026 МПЛ — for credit cards the share of issuance to borrowers with ПДН>80% must be zero and ПДН>50% capped at 10%; standard consumer loans ~18% (ПДН>50%) / 3% (ПДН>80%); new-build mortgage share at ПДН>80% cut to 5% (from 7%), secondary mortgage to 15% (from 20%) — per Bank of Russia / Consultant Plus / RG, 1 Jul 2026. (2) Income-verification tightening: a 10% discount applied to undocumented self-declared income (capped at Rosstat regional mean), and from 1 Jul 2027 ЦБ intends to count only officially confirmed income — a structural squeeze on thin-file borrowers. (3) MFO conduct standard from 1 Jul 2026 (no rollover — new microloans barred until prior repaid; removal of dark patterns / imposed add-ons); rides on the 100%-of-principal cap on total interest+fees effective 1 Apr 2026 (down from 130%). (4) Installment/BNPL now statutorily regulated under FZ-283-FZ (signed 31 Jul 2025, effective 1 Apr 2026): single price across payment methods, min operator capital ₽5m, mandatory BKI reporting for debts >₽50k, no paid add-ons without written consent.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: portfolio growth/contraction, NPL/просрочка share, ПДН mix of new issuance, cost of risk, share of issuance breaching МПЛ buckets. Recent dated context: unsecured consumer-loan portfolio contracted 4.6% YoY as of 1 Jan 2026, with NPL share at 13.0% vs 9.0% a year earlier (per Bank of Russia). MFO segment still growing but tiny (<4% of consumer credit); ЦБ notes borrowers migrating to MFO as bank access shrinks (32% of MFO clients in 2024 cited ease-of-access, up from 20% in 2023) — a disintermediation/regulatory-arbitrage vector the regulator is now closing. This note has no single protagonist company — it is a regulatory roundup — so positioning/moat analysis is N/A; the "players" are the banks and MFOs subject to the quotas, plus emerging BNPL operators (installment services now inside the perimeter).

**Comps & multiples.** No deal/valuation/round/metrics in the pick → trading multiples not applicable ("no data"). Internal comps (in-base regulatory precedent, via semsearch): [[Russia's central bank calls MAX transaction approval premature]] (ЦБ regulatory posture, 2026-03); [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]] (RU lending, 2026-02); adjacent cross-border macroprudential DSR precedent [[MTN MoMo Zambia partners Indo Zambia Bank on POS payments]] (DSR/affordability enforcement) and online-lending tightening [[Philippines SEC proposes stricter online lending platform rules]], [[RBI raises MSE collateral-free loan limit to ₹20 lakh]] — useful only as a directional "regulators are squeezing high-DSTI retail credit" pattern, not as valuation comps.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Illegal/black-market lending displacement** — as МПЛ + income-verification + rate caps push thin-file and high-ПДН borrowers out of the regulated bank/MFO perimeter, demand doesn't vanish; second-order effect is migration to unregulated/illegal lenders (a risk the Moscow Times/analysts flagged for RU as conditions tighten), which is bad for credit quality visibility and consumer protection.
2. **Volume/revenue compression for lenders** — a zero-quota on ПДН>80% credit cards plus shrinking mortgage buckets mechanically caps origination in the most profitable high-yield segments; on an already-contracting portfolio (-4.6% YoY) with rising NPLs (13.0%), this compresses net interest income and pressures MFO/consumer-lender unit economics.
3. **BNPL/installment margin & model risk (FZ-283-FZ)** — single-price-across-payment-methods + BKI reporting + no-add-on rules strip the hidden-fee and merchant-subsidy economics that fund "0%" рассрочка; sub-₽5m-capital operators are pushed out, and the added ПДН/BKI footprint makes BNPL count against borrowers' debt load — potentially shrinking the fastest-growing installment channel just as it is formalized.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is a coordinated de-risking of RU retail credit — МПЛ quotas, honest-income rules, rate caps and BNPL formalization together — that structurally shifts growth away from high-ПДН unsecured/card lending toward documented-income prime borrowers, and closes the MFO/BNPL regulatory-arbitrage escape valves one by one. Falsifiable thesis: over H2-2026 expect further portfolio contraction and continued NPL drift in unsecured consumer credit, with the tell being whether MFO growth and black-market indicators pick up displaced demand; the thesis breaks if origination volumes recover despite the caps (implying banks re-mix into prime without material top-line loss). Trigger to watch: ЦБ's next quarterly МПЛ decision (Q4-2026) and the 1 Jul 2027 official-income-only rule.
