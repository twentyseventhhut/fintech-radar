## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 2026-07-03 Gresham (formerly LSE:GHT, "Gresham Technologies") named **Spiros Giannaros** as CEO, succeeding **Mark Hepsworth**, who moves to a board role. This is a routine private-equity portfolio-company leadership swap, not an operational/product event.

De-PR'd specifics:
- Giannaros had already been an **Operating Advisor to Gresham and its owner STG (Symphony Technology Group) since February 2026** — so this is an *internal promotion of an existing insider*, not an external star hire. The "helped shape AI product/operational roadmap" framing means he was effectively running strategy for ~5 months before the title.
- Hepsworth's tenure as CEO was short: he was made CEO only in **November 2025** (moving up from Chair; he had been CEO of Alveo, the data-management firm STG merged into Gresham). So Gresham has had **three CEO configurations inside ~8 months** (pre-2025 leadership → Hepsworth Nov 2025 → Giannaros Jul 2026). **+ Why:** rapid churn at the top is the tell — STG is bolting together acquisitions (Alveo, then S&P Global's EDM/thinkFolio carve-out closed **Jan 2026**) and rotating in operators who fit the *next* phase (integration + AI/data platform), not running a stable franchise.

**Prompt hypothesis corrected:** Giannaros was **not** Torstone Technology CEO. His background is **Charles River Development / State Street Alpha (President & CEO), and IHS Markit (EDM & thinkFolio)** — directly relevant to Gresham's new EDM footprint. (Torstone was a red herring.)

**Open oddity (flag, not resolved):** secondary sources also report Giannaros was appointed CEO of **Confluence Technologies (Clearlake-backed) in October 2025**. A move from Confluence CEO to STG advisor (Feb 2026) to Gresham CEO (Jul 2026) inside ~9 months is unusual; sources don't cleanly reconcile it. Treat as open.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Gresham/Clareti competes in reconciliation, data integrity and enterprise data management (EDM): peers include **Duco, SmartStream (TLM), FIS (incl. the former Torstone post-trade platform), Broadridge, GoldenSource, and NeoXam**. On the pure EDM side, Gresham's Jan-2026 purchase of S&P Global's EDM + thinkFolio puts it head-to-head with **GoldenSource and NeoXam**. Position: consolidating challenger under PE, buying scale rather than winning it organically. **+ Why:** the segment is mature, sticky and fragmented — the winning play is roll-up/consolidation for multiple expansion, which is exactly STG's model.

## [2] Company history / fit
Trajectory: LSE-listed Gresham Technologies → **taken private by STG for ~£146.7m, completed July 2024, delisted** → merged with **Alveo** (STG's 2023 buy) under the Gresham brand → **acquired S&P Global's EDM/thinkFolio, closed Jan 2026** (Barings financed). Giannaros's EDM/Charles River pedigree fits the pivot from "Clareti reconciliation vendor" to "enterprise data + reconciliation platform." **+ Why:** commodity-ish reconciliation revenue → STG needs a larger, data-led platform story to justify a software multiple on exit → hence the EDM carve-out and a CEO who has actually sold/run EDM.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Novelty of *this* item: essentially zero. It is a CEO appointment at a private company — no product, customer, or financial disclosure. Real value-add, if any, is in what Giannaros signals about direction (AI + data platform integration of Alveo + EDM). Traction claims quoted are about the *acquired EDM asset* (30 of top 60 global asset managers, >$12trn AUM supported), not about anything Giannaros has yet done. **+ Why:** the durable value sits in the sticky EDM install base and Clareti reconciliation contracts; the CEO change doesn't move that either way.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Expect a Giannaros-led push on integrating EDM/thinkFolio + Alveo + Clareti into one data-automation stack with an "AI" wrapper, positioning for an eventual STG exit (secondary sale or, less likely, re-IPO). Risks: integration drag across three acquired platforms, leadership churn, and undisclosed economics of the S&P carve-out. **+ Why (second-order):** the story to watch is not the CEO but whether STG can turn a reconciliation tool + two data acquisitions into a coherent, multiple-worthy platform before exit — the CEO hire is a means to that, low-signal on its own.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Reconciliation / data-integrity software for capital markets sits inside back-office post-trade automation. Market sizing (attribute, treat as vendor estimates): the global reconciliation-software market was ~$2.3bn in 2025 with a claimed ~15% CAGR (per Precedence/Coherent-type reports, via web, as of 2025); capital-markets back-office automation is cited at ~12% CAGR to 2030 — both are secondary/analyst figures, not audited, so treat as directional, not a hard TAM. Structure: fragmented mid-cap vendor layer (Gresham, Duco, SmartStream, EZOPS, Fund Recs) below a few large incumbents (FIS/Data Integrity Manager ex-IntelliMatch, Broadridge, SS&C). Barriers = deep integration into bank plumbing + high switching costs once a recon platform is wired to books-and-records → sticky, high-margin recurring revenue. "Why now": (i) regulatory/data-quality pressure (T+1 settlement, reporting), (ii) an AI/ML re-tooling cycle in matching/exception management, (iii) PE consolidation of sub-scale listed vendors — Gresham itself was taken private.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: recurring-revenue mix, ARR net retention (NRR), and gross margin. Gresham's last public disclosures (FY2023, per company results via web): group revenue ~£49m (+1%), Clareti platform revenue £37.8m (+6%), Clareti recurring revenue £29.6m (+8%), Clareti ARR net retention 103% cc — solid stickiness, not hyper-growth. Key players & basis of competition: SmartStream TLM (high-volume, multi-product, large institutions — scale/breadth); Duco (cloud-native, fast time-to-onboard new recon types — agility); FIS Data Integrity Manager + a new FIS managed-recon service (bundling + managed-service distribution); Broadridge/SS&C (platform breadth). Recent moves: FIS launched an "Optimized Reconciliation" managed service (2025, per web) — incumbent pushing managed services downstream; Gresham acquired Markit EDM (enterprise data management / thinkFolio-adjacent portfolio-management assets) in early 2026, moving up-stack from recon into enterprise data management. Position: Gresham is a niche/challenger specialist — ahead on data-integrity focus, catching up on scale vs FIS/Broadridge. Moat = switching costs + intangibles (Clareti embedded in bank data flows) `(analysis)`.

**Comps & multiples.** Anchor = Gresham's own STG take-private (Alliance Bidco/STG), announced Apr 2024: 163.75p/share, £146.7m equity on a fully-diluted basis, EV ≈ £141.9m, at a ~26.9% premium (per FinTech Futures / PE-Insights / Investegate, via web). EV/Revenue arithmetic on FY2023 group revenue: £141.9m / £49m ≈ **2.9x**; on Clareti-only revenue: £141.9m / £37.8m ≈ **3.8x**. That is a low-single-digit software multiple — cheap end of the range, consistent with a low-liquidity LSE small-cap sold to PE (CityAM framed it as "no liquidity, no chance"), NOT a growth re-rating. Internal comps (same PE owner / same subsector), as `[[wikilink]]`:
- [[S&P Global completes sale of EDM and thinkFolio to STG]] — STG bought the very EDM asset Gresham later acquired; same-owner data-management pedigree (Jan 2026). Deal value: no data.
- [[Envestnet sells data provider Yodlee to STG]] — STG buying data-access/analytics assets (Jun 2025); pattern, not a multiple.
- [[TradingHub raises strategic funding from Nordic Capital]] — PE into capital-markets analytics/regtech (Mar 2026); valuation: no data.
Peer public multiples for Duco/SmartStream/EZOPS: private, last-round terms not disclosed → **no data**. Distribution not computed (only one clean valuation figure); qualitative read: Gresham changed hands cheap-to-in-line for a slow-growth recurring-revenue vendor.

**Risk flags.**
1. Incumbent disintermediation / margin capture — FIS's managed-recon service and Broadridge/SS&C bundling can undercut a standalone specialist on distribution; second-order: pricing pressure on Clareti renewals.
2. Integration/execution risk — a new CEO (Giannaros) plus back-to-back acquisitions (Markit EDM) plus the earlier STG plan to merge Gresham with Alveo = a multi-asset integration under PE ownership; execution slip could stall the ~1% top-line into decline.
3. Slow organic growth vs a PE cost/exit clock — FY2023 was ~flat revenue with 103% NRR; STG needs multiple expansion or M&A-driven scale to hit a return, raising the risk of cost-out over R&D and of another CEO/strategy churn (Giannaros is the second leadership change in the window).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` The Giannaros hire is a "build the platform for an exit" signal, not just a caretaker move — he ran Markit EDM (the asset Gresham just bought) and was already STG's operating advisor, so the thesis is consolidate recon + enterprise-data-management + AI into a larger data-integrity platform, then sell/roll up. Watch: whether Gresham + Alveo + EDM are formally merged into one platform and whether AI-matching becomes a paid module. Falsifiable break: if organic Clareti recurring revenue growth stays low-single-digit through 2026-2027, the roll-up-to-exit thesis is failing and it's just cost synergies.
