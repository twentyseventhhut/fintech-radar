[Финтехно] Банковский рынок Центральной Азии крайне неравномерен — и это очень заметно на визуализации десяти крупнейших банков четырёх стран: Казахстан, Узбекистан, Кыргызстан и Таджикистан. # Context-enrichment: Финтехно — Central Asia's ten largest banks ranked across four countries
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**Freshness verdict: FRESH (weak) — but this is republished evergreen data, not a new event.** The four-country asset-share visualization (Kazakhstan 57.8% / Uzbekistan 32.7% / Kyrgyzstan 6.9% / Tajikistan 2.5%) is a recurring infographic tracing to a Kursiv Media data-viz (Nurasyl Abrazakuly, ~Jan 2026) built on year-end-2025 central-bank data; a near-identical Russian write-up ran on bizmedia.kz on 2026-07-17. No distinct prior note in our corpus covers *this ranking*, so it is not a strict duplicate — but treat the 2026-07-16 date as a republication, not a fresh data release. Marked fresh only because no earlier corpus note reprints it.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
A ranking/infographic of the 10 largest banks across Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, with country asset-share headline claims. Component data independently checks out (approx.):
- **Country banking-sector assets (latest official):** KZ ~KZT 70.8trn ≈ **$135–140bn** (+15% y/y, end-2025); UZ UZS 924.8trn ≈ **$73.5bn** (+20.2% y/y, 1 Jan 2026); KGZ ~KGS 1,211bn ≈ **$13.9bn** (end-2025); TJK ~**$4.8bn**. Four-country total ≈ **$227–232bn**.
- On that base the claims hold: KZ ≈ 60% (source 57.8% — FX/date-sensitive, **[open]**); UZ ≈32% (source 32.7% ✓); KGZ ≈6% (6.9% ✓); TJK ≈2.1% (2.5% ✓). **KZ alone ($140bn) > other three combined ($92bn) — TRUE.** KZ+UZ ≈91.6% vs claimed 90.5% — directionally TRUE.
- **Halyk "~18%":** this is a *regional* share ($39bn / $227bn ≈17%), NOT its ~30% Kazakhstan-only share (Halyk KZT 20,410bn ≈ $39bn, Sep-2025). Comparison holds: **Halyk alone > Kyrgyz + Tajik systems combined** ($39bn vs ~$19bn) — TRUE.
- **"Halyk+Kaspi+BCC ≈ $73.7bn":** confirmed (~$39bn + ~$20bn + ~$15bn = ~$74bn), and this Kazakh trio ≈ **all of Uzbekistan's banking sector** ($73.5bn) — the single most striking, defensible claim.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the piece mixes measurement dates (KZ Sep-2025, UZ Jan-2026, KGZ/TJK 2025) and, in the $73.7bn trio, mixes Kaspi *Bank* vs Kaspi.kz *group* — minor but it means the exact percentages shouldn't be read to the decimal. The real signal is structural, not a "news event": one oil-scale, mostly-private market (KZ) dwarfs three others.

## [1] Competitors / peers — the actual regional top 10
Overwhelmingly Kazakh + Uzbek: **Halyk (KZ ~$39bn) · Kaspi.kz (KZ ~$20–21bn, Nasdaq: KSPI) · Bank CenterCredit (KZ ~$15bn) · NBU (UZ ~$12bn, state) · ForteBank (KZ ~$10–11bn) · SQB/Uzpromstroybank (UZ ~$8bn, state) · Agrobank (UZ ~$8bn, state) · Otbasy (KZ ~$7–8bn, state housing) · Asaka (UZ ~$6–7bn, state) · Kapitalbank (UZ ~$4.3bn, #1 private UZ bank).** Just outside: Ipoteka (OTP-owned), Xalq, and [[TBC Uzbekistan]]-type digital players — fast-growing but not yet top-10 by assets.
**Correction to the source's candidate list:** it flags TBC Uzbekistan but by assets **Kapitalbank, ForteBank and Otbasy rank ahead of it.** Kazakhstan plausibly holds 5 of the regional top 10.
**Why this way:** Kazakhstan's private, systemically-important banks (Halyk, Kaspi, BCC, Forte) reflect a mature commercial market; Uzbekistan's list is state-dominated (NBU/SQB/Agrobank/Asaka), the structural reason its sector "looks less mature" at equal asset size.

## [2] Sector history / fit
- **Ownership is the story:** UZ still ~62–65% state-owned; KZ predominantly private (Otbasy the main state bank). Banking Reform Strategy 2020–2025 aimed to lift UZ private-bank share toward 60% by end-2025 — it lagged (non-state ~32% end-2023). Landmark: OTP Bank acquired Ipoteka (Dec 2022, ~$324m). SQB/Asaka privatizations repeatedly postponed.
- **Cross-border 2025 integration:** Halyk took 49% of Uzbek payments firm Click ($176.4m); Click shareholders took 49% of Halyk's Uzbek sub Tengebank ($60.76m) — a genuine KZ→UZ push.
- Concentration everywhere: KGZ top-5 = 52.6% of assets; TJK top-5 = 75% of deposits.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Low novelty as a *news item* — evergreen data-viz. Value is analytical, not event-driven. What is genuinely notable: (1) three Kazakh banks ≈ all of Uzbekistan's sector; (2) Halyk alone > KGZ+TJK combined; (3) **Kaspi.kz ≠ "just a bank"** — Nasdaq-listed, ~$15–16bn market cap, FY2025 revenue KZT ~4.04trn (+59% y/y), ~75% operating margin (2024) — priced as a fintech platform, not a lender; it expanded via Kaspi.kz/Rabobank Türkiye ([[Kaspi.kz completes acquisition of Rabobank in Türkiye]]). Compare the Uzbek digital wave: [[Uzum plans $250-300M pre-IPO round ahead of 2027 listing]].

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Uzbekistan privatization + bank IPOs** are the region's biggest catalyst; "Uzbekistan-2030" (Dec 2025) plans only 4 state banks by decade-end. Whether SQB/Asaka finally sell is the key watch-item (repeatedly delayed).
- **Foreign entry accelerating:** OTP (Ipoteka), TBC (Georgia), Uzum (Tencent/VR Capital), Halyk (Tengebank/Click) — bets on UZ growth + young demographics. See also fintech access plays like [[VEON and Mastercard partner to widen financial services access]] and [[Binance Pay partners Alatau City Bank for Kazakhstan POS]].
- **KGZ/TJK:** high growth off tiny base (KGZ assets +28.6% in 4M 2026) — won't move the concentration picture. **KZ headwind:** 2025 profits dipped on higher taxes/reserves; asset growth slowing (15% vs 19.7% in 2024).
**Why the market goes this way:** the concentration is oil-scale + private-ownership legacy, not a temporary skew; second-order — the interesting flows are *into* Uzbekistan (privatization, foreign capital), so the "gap" narrative is really "who captures Uzbek convergence."

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Central Asia banking is a **hyper-concentrated, national-champion market** — the note's own de-PR'd point: on assets Kazakhstan holds ~57.8%, Uzbekistan ~32.7%, Kyrgyzstan ~6.9%, Tajikistan ~2.5%, so KZ+UZ = ~90.5% of the four-country pool and Kazakhstan alone > the other three combined. Within that, one bank — **Halyk** — controls ~18% of pooled assets, larger than the Kyrgyz + Tajik banking systems together; Halyk + Kaspi + Bank CenterCredit ≈ **$73.7bn** assets ≈ a third of the visualization (all figures per the note's Финтехно infographic, 2026-07-16). Structure is **consolidated at the top, state-heavy below**: Uzbekistan's ~9 state-owned commercial banks still hold ~62% of financial-system assets (World Bank FSAP, 2025); Kazakhstan is bank-dominated (~78% of financial-sector assets) with 21 banks but Halyk-led concentration since the 2018 Kazkommertsbank merger (World Bank; Edison, 2026). Barriers = capital + license scarcity + incumbent deposit franchises. **"Why now" / drivers:** (1) rapid formalization of cash economies — Uzbek card issuance roughly doubled to ~70m and the Fast Payment System processed >$10.2bn in one month (2025); (2) the super-app model (Kaspi in KZ, Uzum in UZ) converting payments rails into commerce+lending flywheels; (3) macro tailwind — Uzbekistan ~6.5% GDP growth, the region's growth leader (ADB, 2025), plus **remittances to Uzbekistan +27% to $18.9bn in 2025** (kun.uz, Jan 2026) feeding deposit and consumption growth.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs for these franchises: **total assets / market share, ROAE, cost-to-income, net interest margin, and (for the digital champions) MAU, cards issued, TPV**. Key players by country: **Kazakhstan** — Halyk (largest, ~18% of pooled assets; ~26.8% cost-to-income, best-in-class efficiency), Kaspi.kz (super-app, Nasdaq-listed), Bank CenterCredit (BCC, ~32.7% ROAE); **Uzbekistan** — state banks (~62% share) plus disruptors Hamkorbank (~39.5% ROAE per S&P/invesclub, 2025) and super-app Uzum; **Kyrgyzstan/Tajikistan** — small, fragmented, historically served by foreign (incl. Kazakh) subsidiaries. Basis of competition is shifting from **scale/deposit franchise** (incumbents) to **distribution + product via super-apps** (Kaspi, Uzum). Notable regional structural move: Kazakh Halyk has been **exiting peripheral markets** — sold banks in Tajikistan (2022), Russia (2022) and Kyrgyzstan (2024) (Edison, 2026) — i.e. retrenching to its dominant home base rather than pan-regional expansion. Recent peer moves: [[Kaspi.kz completes acquisition of Rabobank in Türkiye]] (2026-07-15, license-led Türkiye entry); [[Uzum plans $250-300M pre-IPO round ahead of 2027 listing]] (2026-06). Protagonist of this note is the **sector aggregate**, so "position" = the market is *ahead* on concentration/profitability (40%-ish ROAEs cited region-wide) but *behind* on diversification and cross-border scale.

**Comps & multiples.** These are the region's benchmark franchises; internal base already holds enriched valuation data:
- **Kaspi.kz (Nasdaq: KSPI)** — Q1 2026: revenue KZT1,080.6bn (+31% YoY), net income **KZT252bn ($526m), −1% YoY**; adj. EBITDA KZT368bn (+9%); total assets KZT11.1tn; profit margin **23% (down from 31%)** (Kaspi 1Q26 results / StockTitan, 2026-04). Prior enrichment on [[Kaspi.kz completes acquisition of Rabobank in Türkiye]] put market cap **~$16.5bn** (2026-06) at trailing **P/E ~7.2x** → implied TTM net income ≈ $16.5bn / 7.2 ≈ **~$2.3bn** `(analysis)`. That ~7x is **cheap vs global fintech/EM-neobank peers** (Simply Wall St peer avg ~19.9x) — a Kazakhstan-sovereign/EM/FX discount, not weak growth.
- **Hamkorbank (Uzbekistan)** — ~39.5% ROAE at **~1.8x P/E, ~0.61x P/B** (S&P / invesclub, 2025): even cheaper, illustrating the frontier-market discount on high-ROE Central Asian banks.
- **Internal round comp:** [[Uzum plans $250-300M pre-IPO round ahead of 2027 listing]] — Uzbek super-app at **$2.3bn (Mar 2026)**, 2025 revenue ~$691m, net income ~$176m (~25% margin) — a private-mark reference for the "super-app as bank" leg.

Distribution not computed (mixed listed vs private, mixed bank vs super-app metrics) → **qualitative: Central Asian bank/fintech franchises trade at deep single-digit P/Es despite 25–40% ROAEs** — cheap on multiples, but the cheapness *is* the risk premium.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Extreme geographic/sovereign concentration** — ~90.5% of pooled assets sit in just Kazakhstan + Uzbekistan; any single-country shock (Kazakh tenge devaluation — analysts saw ~525.8/USD end-2025, tenge tied to ruble/oil; Uzbek soum) hits the whole "regional" thesis. Second-order: a "Central Asia" allocation is really a levered KZ+UZ macro bet.
2. **Single-institution / national-champion concentration** — one bank (Halyk) = ~18% of the four-country pool; top-3 KZ banks ≈ a third. Systemic-importance and related-party/legacy-problem-asset risk flagged by the World Bank FSAP; a stumble at one champion is a systemic event.
3. **FX / dollarization drag** — deposits ~30% dollarized in KZ, and UZ loans ~43% / deposits ~30% dollarized (IMF/World Bank). High dollarization blunts monetary policy and exposes bank balance sheets to currency swings — a live risk given the tenge's external-shock sensitivity.
4. **Credit-cycle / formalization risk** — the growth engine (consumer/unsecured lending into newly-formalized, thin-file borrowers; cf. Uzum's ~$400m unsecured book) has not been through a full default cycle; today's 25–40% ROAEs may not be through-cycle.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This ranking reframes "Central Asia banking" as **two markets (KZ, UZ) plus a rounding error (KG, TJ)**, with the investable story concentrated in a handful of national champions trading at deep EM discounts (~7x Kaspi, ~1.8x Hamkorbank) against 25–40% ROAEs. Falsifiable thesis: the discount narrows only if (a) a credible cross-border/IPO event de-risks the region (Uzum's 2027 listing is the nearest catalyst) or (b) FX/sovereign stability improves; it *widens* on any tenge/soum devaluation or a credit-book default cycle. Watch: Uzum pre-IPO pricing and Kaspi's Türkiye traction as the two swing factors for regional re-rating.
