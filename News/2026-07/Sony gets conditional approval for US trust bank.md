## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** On 2026-07-09 the U.S. OCC granted **preliminary conditional approval** for Sony Bank (online arm of Sony Financial Group) to establish a de novo national trust bank — **Connectia Trust, National Association** — dedicated to USD-backed stablecoin issuance, reserve management, digital-asset custody, and fiduciary asset management. Connectia will be capitalized with **~$40M**, 100%-owned by Sony Bank, targeting **2027** commercial launch. Approval is conditional and non-final: it still requires OCC operational/capital/compliance sign-off AND clearance from Japanese regulators before stablecoin activity can begin.

**[1] Why it matters.** A federal trust charter lets Sony bring the **entire stablecoin lifecycle in-house under a single federal regulator** — issuance, redemption, reserves, custody — instead of leaning on a third party's state trust charter + a patchwork of 50-state money-transmitter licenses. It also makes Sony one of the first *foreign (Japanese) banking groups* to win a U.S. stablecoin trust charter, extending the OCC's post-GENIUS-Act chartering wave beyond native crypto firms.

**[2] Prior art / internal thread.**
- [[Sony Bank plans USD stablecoin by fiscal 2026]] (2025-12) — the original Nikkei-sourced plan: Sony applied for a U.S. banking license in Oct 2025 and planned a subsidiary to issue a USD stablecoin for PlayStation/anime/streaming payments. THIS note is the concrete regulatory milestone that plan reached.
- [[Coinbase faces banker pushback on trust bank charter bid]] (2025-11) — ICBA + Bank Policy Institute lobbied the OCC to reject crypto trust charters (Coinbase, Ripple, Circle, Paxos) on safety-and-soundness grounds; shows the political headwind Sony's charter also faces.
- [[Walmart, crypto firms and fintechs chase US bank charters]] (2025-11) — broader land-rush for national trust charters (Ripple, Coinbase, Wise, Walmart, Amazon).
- [[SoFi launches SoFiUSD, first stablecoin from a US national bank]] (2026-05) — a bank-issued USD stablecoin already live in the U.S., i.e. the model Sony is chasing.

**[3] Prior OCC approvals (external, dated).** The OCC conditionally approved **five** national trust charters on ~2025-12-12 (First National Digital Currency Bank / Circle, Ripple National Trust, BitGo, Fidelity Digital Assets, Paxos), then Bridge (Stripe) ~2026-02-12, Protego (early Feb), and Crypto.com (2026-02-23). Sony now joins this cohort. Framework is the **GENIUS Act** (signed July 2025): 1:1 reserves in cash/insured deposits/short-term Treasuries/qualifying MMFs, plus audit + sanctions rules.

**[4] Key facts / unknowns.** Tech backbone: Sony Bank partnered with **Bastion Platforms** (announced Dec 2025) for issuance/redemption/reserve/custody. Legal counsel: Sidley Austin. Open questions: token name/ticker, whether the coin will be usable across PlayStation/entertainment at launch, and the timing/conditions of Japanese regulatory sign-off.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
Red-team (second-order):

1. **Is this final or just conditional?** Conditional/preliminary only — final OCC sign-off + Japanese regulator clearance still pending; launch not until 2027. So "gets approval" overstates it. (answered)
2. **Is this a genuinely new event vs the Dec-2025 plan?** Yes — Dec note was an intention/application; this is the actual OCC conditional approval with a named entity (Connectia Trust), $40M capital, 2027 target. Fresh milestone, not a reprint. (answered)
3. **How big is $40M capital, really?** Modest — peer crypto trust charters carry comparable capital; adequate for a reserve-and-custody trust but not a full-service bank. Signals a focused, low-balance-sheet-risk model. (answered)
4. **Does the charter face the same banker lobby opposition as Coinbase/Ripple/Circle?** Almost certainly — ICBA/BPI have opposed the whole cohort on safety-and-soundness grounds; a foreign parent may draw extra scrutiny. Conditions likely reflect that. (answered)
5. **Why a bank-owned trust vs Circle/Ripple's crypto-native route?** Sony already holds a Japanese banking license and can leverage a regulated parent + Bastion tech — lower reputational/AML friction than a pure exchange. (answered)
6. **Legal/political durability?** OCC's stablecoin-charter authority is legally contested (Duke FinReg blog argues it's ultra vires); a court or new Comptroller could unwind the framework before 2027 launch. Real tail risk. (open)
7. **What's the actual demand?** Thesis is captive PlayStation/anime/streaming spend replacing card rails to cut fees — but no disclosed volume commitment; adoption unproven. (open)
8. **Does the entertainment use-case need a US charter at all?** Global entertainment payments could route through existing issuers; the charter mainly buys regulatory control and a moat, not a unique capability. (open)
9. **Competitive crowding.** By 2027 the US bank-issued stablecoin space includes SoFiUSD (live), Circle, Ripple RLUSD, Paxos, Bridge/Stripe, plus KlarnaUSD/Cash App — Sony is a late entrant without a distribution edge outside its own ecosystem. (answered)
10. **Reserve economics.** Revenue = float yield on reserves; at low balances and 2027 rate uncertainty, standalone economics are thin — value is strategic (ecosystem lock-in), not P&L. (answered)
11. **Japan-side risk.** FSA sign-off is a hard dependency and Japan's stablecoin rules differ; a mismatch could delay or reshape the token. (open)
12. **Bastion dependency.** Tech/custody outsourced to Bastion Platforms — concentration/operational risk if that partnership falters. (open)
13. **Is "single federal regulator vs patchwork" framing accurate?** Yes for issuance/custody, but MTL/state consumer rules can still bite at the point of consumer use depending on structure. (partially open)

Importance: 3/5 — a notable, well-sourced regulatory milestone: first Japanese banking group into the US stablecoin-trust cohort, concretizing the Dec-2025 plan. But it's conditional, 2027-dated, small-capital, and enters a crowded field, so near-term market impact is limited.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector.** US bank-chartered USD stablecoins under the GENIUS Act (signed July 2025). The OCC's national trust charter has become the preferred federal path for issuing/holding stablecoin reserves, replacing the state-trust + 50-state MTL patchwork. Sony's Connectia Trust is the newest entrant and among the first from a **foreign (Japanese) banking group**.

**In-base comparables.**
- [[SoFi launches SoFiUSD, first stablecoin from a US national bank]] (2026-05) — bank-issued USD stablecoin already live; the closest live analog to Sony's model.
- [[Sony Bank plans USD stablecoin by fiscal 2026]] (2025-12) — same story's origin; entertainment-ecosystem payments thesis.
- [[Coinbase faces banker pushback on trust bank charter bid]] (2025-11) — the ICBA/BPI opposition Sony's charter must also survive.
- [[Klarna launches KlarnaUSD stablecoin on Tempo blockchain]], [[SoFi launches SoFiUSD...]], [[Ripple's RLUSD stablecoin wins UAE regulatory approval]] — crowded issuer field (Circle USDC, Paxos, Ripple RLUSD, Bridge/Stripe, Cash App, Klarna).
- OCC trust-charter cohort (external): Circle/First National Digital Currency Bank, Ripple National Trust, BitGo, Fidelity, Paxos (all ~Dec 2025); Bridge/Protego/Crypto.com (Feb 2026).

**Competitive positioning.** Sony's edge is a **captive distribution ecosystem** (PlayStation, streaming, anime) plus an already-regulated banking parent and the Bastion Platforms tech stack — differentiating it from crypto-native issuers that lack native consumer demand. Weakness: late entry (2027), modest $40M capital, no full-service banking ambition, and no distribution advantage outside its own products.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Conditional / non-final approval** — final OCC sign-off plus Japanese (FSA) clearance still required; nothing operational before 2027, so any "launch" headline risk is far off and slippage-prone.
2. **Legal/regulatory durability of the OCC charter framework itself** — the OCC's authority to charter stablecoin trust banks is legally contested and politically opposed (ICBA, Bank Policy Institute); a court ruling or leadership change could unwind it.
3. **Crowded, low-margin market** — by 2027 the field includes multiple live bank/issuer stablecoins; reserve-float economics are thin at small balances, making the value strategic (ecosystem lock-in) rather than P&L-accretive, with unproven end-user demand.
