Paysafe connects to Primer to streamline card payments
Sinziana Albu
01 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Paysafe has partnered with Primer to offer card payment processing to online merchants using Primer's platform globally.
Paysafe, a global payments platform, has connected to Primer, a provider of unified payments infrastructure, enabling online merchants that use Primer to access dedicated card payment processing. The integration was completed through Primer for Partners, a no-code offering that Primer launched in December 2025.
Integration and capabilities
Through Primer for Partners, Paysafe's own technology team managed the integration into the Primer platform without requiring additional coding support from Primer. The addition of Paysafe extends the transactional capabilities of Primer's platform, which allows merchants to route transactions to the payment service providers of their choice. According to the companies, Paysafe processes credit card and debit card transactions within seconds, drawing on more than 30 years of experience in card approval optimisation.
Paysafe operates in more than 130 countries and said it tailored its integration with Primer to serve international merchants across sectors, including iGaming, social gaming, financial trading, ecommerce, and travel. Paysafe is already live on the Primer platform, processing payments for merchants operating across North America, Europe, and Australasia.
In addition, the partnership adds to Primer's roster of payment service providers available through its orchestration platform, which is designed to give merchants a choice of routing options rather than dependence on a single processor. Primer for Partners, launched in December 2025, is intended to let payment providers build and manage their own integrations without requiring Primer's development resources. Going forward, the same no-code framework will allow Paysafe's team to expand its offering on the platform. Planned additions include Paysafe's payment methods, such as the Skrill and Neteller digital wallets, along with the PaysafeCard and PaysafeCash eCash solutions.
 Rob Gatto , Chief Revenue Officer at Paysafe, said the partnership allows the company to streamline the payment journey for Primer's merchant partners and their customers, and that Paysafe intends to grow its presence in the payment orchestration space.
 Gabriel Le Roux , CEO and co-founder of Primer, said Paysafe built its own integration into the Primer for Partners solution and is already processing payments for Primer's merchants. He added that access to Paysafe through Primer gives merchants additional choice, broader coverage, and faster access to a payment provider with an established record in card performance.

## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS: FRESH** — but pattern-repeat, not landmark. This is a genuinely NEW technical integration (Primer, via the Dec-2025 "Primer for Partners" no-code tooling), distinct from Paysafe's prior orchestrator tie-ups. It is NOT a duplicate of [[Paysafe and Pay.com launch strategic partnership]] (Jan 2026) or [[Paysafe announces partnership with Spreedly]] (Feb 2026) — different partner, different deal. HOWEVER, it is the THIRD near-identical orchestrator deal in six months, so the marginal news value is low. No prior Paysafe-on-Primer announcement exists; prior Primer PSP roster (Stripe, Adyen, JPM — see [[Primer raises $100 million Series C for payments orchestration]]) never listed Paysafe.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On ~1 Jul 2026 Paysafe (NYSE: PSFE) went **live** on Primer's orchestration platform as a **card acquirer only**, in North America, Europe and Australasia. The integration was self-built by Paysafe's own engineers via **Primer for Partners** — a no-code partner-self-integration product Primer launched Dec 2025 — requiring no Primer dev resources. Verticals targeted: iGaming, social gaming, financial trading, ecommerce, travel.
- **De-PR'd:** The valuable part is NOT shipped. Paysafe's real differentiators — the **Skrill/Neteller wallets, PaysafeCard/PaysafeCash eCash** — are only "planned additions," not live. What's live is commodity card acquiring, the most substitutable layer. → Why framed this way: Primer needs a marquee external case study to validate "for Partners" (this is arguably as much a *Primer* product-proof story as a Paysafe distribution win); Paysafe gets PR cadence and cheap shelf-space. → Second order: announcing cards-only now, APMs "later," means the release is timed to product-marketing needs, not to a completed capability.
- **Silent on:** take-rate/revenue split, named live merchants, volume routed through Primer, fraud/chargeback liability (material in iGaming/trading), APM go-live timeline. The joint PR is the only evidence of "live in region X"; no third-party transaction proof.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Orchestration is now crowded. Independents: **Spreedly** (acquired Dodgeball fraud-orchestration Sep 2025; ~1/3 new business via partnerships), **Gr4vy** (~$27M funding; Worldline partnership Nov 2025; agentic-payments push), **Cellulant** (Africa/Tingg — no 2025-26 orchestration move surfaced, *open*), **Pay.com**. PSP-native orchestration is the bigger threat: **Stripe Orchestration + Vault-and-Forward** and **Checkout.com** (bought ProcessOut) both do multi-acquirer routing, blurring the PSP/orchestrator line. Primer's position: independent, enterprise-grade, AI-forward (see [[Primer unveils Companion AI agent for payments teams]]), fresh off a **$100M Series C** (May 2026, Sofina/Peak XV; total funding €146.6M).
- **+Why:** Orchestration bifurcates PSPs. It **commoditizes** undifferentiated card acquiring (merchants swap acquirers via config) but **empowers** PSPs with a genuine niche edge. Paysafe is buying shelf-space on the empowering side — its moat is high-risk verticals + proprietary wallets, not generic card rails.

## [2] Company history / fit
Paysafe is running a deliberate **multi-orchestrator distribution strategy in H1 2026**: Pay.com (Jan), Spreedly (Feb), Primer (Jul) — all "acquirer-first, wallets-later," all with CRO Rob Gatto quoted. ~130 countries, ~$167B annual volume (2025, per Primer's release). Plus a "Pay with Crypto" stablecoin-funding push for US iGaming.
- **+Why:** Paysafe is a **mid-tier acquirer** whose edge (iGaming/trading verticals, Skrill/Neteller) global whales (Adyen/Stripe) under-serve. Direct enterprise integrations are expensive; instead it plants itself on every orchestrator where multi-PSP demand already aggregates — "be where the routing happens." Structural logic: commodity acquiring take-rate pressure → distribute cheaply + lead with differentiated APMs.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Genuinely new *integration* and a real product-validation of Primer-for-Partners; but low novelty as an *event* — the third clone of the same deal. **Traction claims are thin:** "live in NA/Europe/Australasia" but zero named merchants, zero Primer-attributable volume ($167B is Paysafe's total, not routed-via-Primer), and the differentiating APMs unshipped.
- **+Who captures the margin:** In pure card routing, the orchestrator (Primer) captures the optimization/routing margin and can pit acquirers against each other → Paysafe risks being an interchangeable card option. Paysafe only escapes commoditization if/when Skrill/Neteller go live at scale — which is exactly the unshipped part. Until then the value-add is asserted, not demonstrated.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Next: Paysafe to add Skrill, Neteller, PaysafeCard, PaysafeCash via the same no-code framework — the real test. Market: orchestration TAM estimates diverge 2-3x (~$6.1B by 2030 @ ~24% CAGR per Grand View/R&M; up to ~$18B by 2034 elsewhere) — treat as soft analyst estimates, not audited.
- **+Why / counterintuitive:** As orchestration matures, being on *every* platform is table stakes, not advantage — the differentiation collapses back onto the underlying PSP's verticals/APMs/auth-rates. So Paysafe's ubiquity across orchestrators is defensive positioning, not a moat. The bigger structural risk for Primer itself: PSP-native routing (Stripe/Checkout.com) could disintermediate independent orchestrators for mainstream merchants, leaving them the high-risk/long-tail — coincidentally where Paysafe is strong (so Primer + Paysafe may be mutually reliant in the niches).

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Payment orchestration — a routing/abstraction layer that lets a merchant reach many PSPs/acquirers/APMs through one integration — is a small but fast-growing sub-vertical. Estimates diverge widely by definition: ~$2–3bn in 2025/2026 sizing with CAGRs clustered ~13–26% into the early 2030s (per multiple market-research firms — Grand View, Mordor, SkyQuest, Vantage — via WebSearch, as of Jul 2026; treat as indicative, not precise). Structure: fragmented and still forming — independent orchestrators (Primer, Spreedly/[[Spreedly acquires Dodgeball for fraud orchestration]], Praxis, Pay.com, BR-DGE) compete alongside incumbent-owned stacks (ACI Worldwide, and PSP-native "orchestration lite"). Entry barrier is the integration network, not capital: value accrues to whoever has the most acquirers/APMs pre-wired plus routing intelligence. Why now: (1) merchants want to cut dependence on a single processor for auth-rate and cost optimisation; (2) "no-code partner" self-integration (Primer for Partners, launched Dec 2025) collapses the onboarding cost of adding a new PSP, accelerating network build-out.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: number of pre-integrated PSPs/acquirers/APMs, auth/approval-rate uplift, routing coverage (geos), take-rate on routed volume. Basis of competition = distribution (breadth of pre-wired providers) and product (routing/AI intelligence), not price. Two distinct roles here: Primer is the **orchestrator** (the routing layer); Paysafe is a **supplier** (an acquirer/PSP that plugs into orchestrators). Recent moves show Paysafe running a deliberate multi-orchestrator distribution play as a *supplier*: joined Pay.com (Jan 2026, [[Paysafe and Pay.com launch strategic partnership]]), Spreedly (Feb 2026, [[Paysafe announces partnership with Spreedly]]), and now Primer (Jul 2026) — each as an acquirer option, self-built via no-code partner tooling. Paysafe's position: a scale supplier (130+ countries, 30+ yrs card-optimisation, iGaming/trading/travel niches) that is *joining* rather than *building* orchestration — catching-up as an orchestrator itself, but well-positioned as a preferred acquirer. Primer's position: independent orchestrator, freshly capitalised (see comps), moat = its growing integration network + AI routing (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.**
- **Primer (protagonist's counterparty):** Series C $100m, May 2026, led by Sofina; $170m raised to date; last *disclosed* valuation $425m at the 2021 Series B $50m round. 2026 post-money **not disclosed** → valuation multiple = no data / `[UNSOURCED]` (private, no revenue disclosed). (via BusinessWire / TechCrunch / Paypers)
- **Paysafe (NYSE: PSFE, public):** FY2025 revenue $1,701.4m, adj. EBITDA $428.8m; Q1 2026 revenue $442.7m with net loss $36.5m; TPV $31.2bn (+13% YoY). Market cap ~$336–497m across dates (Jun 2026). EV/Revenue is well below 1x on that market cap — arithmetic: even at the high end ~$497m equity / $1,701m rev = **~0.29x P/S**, i.e. deep-value / distressed multiple vs the sub-vertical (net-loss-making, cheap on revenue). Note market cap ≠ EV (Paysafe carries net debt) → clean EV/EBITDA `[UNSOURCED]` without a free balance-sheet pull. (per SEC 6-K / stockanalysis, via WebSearch)
- **Internal comps:** [[Primer raises $100 million Series C for payments orchestration]], [[Spreedly acquires Dodgeball for fraud orchestration]] (orchestrators consolidating fraud into the stack), [[This Week in Fintech payment intelligence redefines orchestration]], [[Paysafe and Pay.com launch strategic partnership]].
- Distribution not computed (private orchestrators disclose no revenue; only one public comp). Qualitative: orchestrators are venture-priced growth assets; Paysafe is a cheap, low-growth public processor — different animals, not directly comparable.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disintermediation of the processor by the orchestration layer.** As a supplier, Paysafe becomes one interchangeable acquirer in Primer's routing menu; the orchestrator owns the merchant relationship and routing logic, so Paysafe competes on price/auth-rate every transaction. Second-order: margin and pricing power migrate up the stack to whoever controls routing — Paysafe's own stated ambition to "grow in orchestration" is a response to exactly this squeeze.
2. **Announced ≠ material economics.** "Live and processing" is stated, but no volume, take-rate, fraud-liability or exclusivity is disclosed; the same pattern repeated across Pay.com/Spreedly/Primer suggests broad, non-exclusive distribution — each individually likely small relative to $31.2bn TPV. Watch what's *silent*: economics and exclusivity.
3. **Vertical/regulatory concentration.** Paysafe's edge sits in iGaming/social-gaming/financial-trading — higher-risk, licence-sensitive verticals; orchestrator distribution amplifies exposure to any regulatory tightening in gambling/trading payments.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** For the sector this is more consolidation of *supply* onto the orchestration layer — the no-code partner model (Primer for Partners) is turning "add a PSP" into a self-serve act, which should accelerate orchestrator network effects and re-rate independent orchestrators over PSPs (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if orchestration keeps commoditising acquirers, Paysafe-type processors face structural take-rate compression unless they climb into orchestration themselves. Trigger to watch: whether Paysafe's promised follow-on (Skrill/Neteller wallets, PaysafeCard/PaysafeCash) goes live on Primer — APM breadth, not card acquiring, is where a supplier can stay differentiated. Thesis breaks if orchestrators stay merchant-side-only and acquirers retain pricing power via superior auth rates.
