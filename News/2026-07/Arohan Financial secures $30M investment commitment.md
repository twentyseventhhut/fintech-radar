## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Arohan is an NBFC-MFI (microfinance) focused on eastern/north-eastern India, ~2m low-income (mostly female) borrowers across 800+ branches; FY25 revenue INR 1,581cr, PAT INR 110cr (per PitchBook/Tracxn/ipocentral, via WebSearch, as of 2026). The Indian NBFC-MFI sector is mid-downcycle, not growth: aggregate AUM fell ~19% YoY to ~INR 1.4 lakh cr as of Jun-2025, and CareEdge/ICRA model only ~4% AUM growth for FY26 (per CareEdge NBFC-MFI report, via WebSearch). Structure: fragmented, capital- and regulation-heavy (RBI-registered NBFC-MFI, priority-sector-linked funding); value sits with lenders who can hold asset quality and access cheap wholesale/DFI funding. Why now: this is a repair-cycle raise, not expansion — sector went through a 2024-25 over-leverage/asset-quality blow-up, and Arohan itself was under RBI curbs that were only recently lifted "satisfied" with revamped systems and fair-pricing commitment (via WebSearch). The stated use of proceeds (grow rural/semi-urban lending in north/east India) is standard for a DFI-style senior-debt line; the $30m closely matches the IFC/FMO debt pattern for Arohan (IFC proposed ~INR 1,300m / ~$20m senior debt; FMO/IFU/Nuveen/Piramal are existing backers) — treat as debt/DFI commitment, not equity, `(analysis)`.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: AUM growth, GNPA/GS3 (asset quality), credit cost / ATA, and cost of funds. Sector-wide GNPA was 4.9% (Jun-2025) and credit cost spiked to ~9.0% of average total assets in FY25 (from ~2.6% FY24) — NBFC-MFIs posted losses in FY25 and 9M-FY26 (per CareEdge, via WebSearch). Key listed peers: CreditAccess Grameen (largest), Spandana Sphoorty, Fusion Finance; adjacent small-finance banks Ujjivan/Jana also compete for the same rural borrower. Basis of competition: cost of funds + distribution reach + collections/underwriting discipline (not price — RBI caps pricing). Arohan's position: catching-up / mid-tier niche — regionally concentrated in the poorest, lowest-inclusion states, which is both its moat (deep rural distribution, incumbency) and its main risk (geographic concentration). No disclosed CAC/LTV → `[UNSOURCED]`.

**Comps & multiples.** External: CreditAccess Grameen trades at P/B ~2.65 and P/E ~26.75 (as of May-2026, per MoneyWorks4Me/marketsmojo, via WebSearch) — the quality/scale benchmark; Arohan's targeted ~INR 1,500cr IPO (2026, DRHP by Mar) on FY25 PAT of INR 110cr implies a demanded P/E ~13.6x (`1,500 / 110 = 13.6x`) — a discount to CreditAccess, consistent with smaller scale, regional concentration and a fresher regulatory track record `(analysis)`. This $30m round carries no equity valuation, so no EV/Revenue or market-cap multiple is computable → "no data" on the deal's own multiple. Internal comps (base): [[Crif High Mark India microfinance portfolio falls 16.5%]] (sector portfolio -16.5% to INR 3.45 lakh cr), [[AU, Ujjivan and Jana small finance banks report lower Q2 profits]] (peer earnings pressure), [[Optimo Capital raises Rs 150 crore for SME finance]] (adjacent India non-bank credit funding). Distribution not computed (only one clean listed comp) — qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
1. Asset-quality / credit-cost cycle — sector GNPA ~4.9% and ~9% credit costs drove sector losses; a debt raise adds leverage into a still-negative-outlook segment (CRISIL negative), so growing the book now risks amplifying, not smoothing, delinquency.
2. Geographic concentration — east/north-east India single-region exposure means one regional shock (floods, political farm-loan-waiver contagion, local over-indebtedness) hits the whole book; the raise deepens rather than diversifies this.
3. Regulatory overhang — RBI curbs were only recently lifted; renewed pricing/conduct scrutiny is a live tail risk, and it directly gates the planned 2026 IPO.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a balance-sheet-repair/pre-IPO funding step, not a sector re-rating — a solvent mid-tier MFI accessing wholesale/DFI capital signals the funding tap is reopening selectively for cleaned-up names. Falsifiable thesis: if Arohan's next reported GNPA/credit-cost trends down toward CreditAccess levels, the ~INR 1,500cr IPO prices near its ~13.6x implied P/E; trigger = DRHP filing (targeted by Mar-2026). What breaks it: another quarter of rising MFI delinquency or fresh RBI action would push the IPO out and re-widen the discount.
