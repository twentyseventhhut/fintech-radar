## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
### [0] TL;DR
Peru's **BanBif** (Banco Interamericano de Finanzas, the country's ~5th-largest bank by loans) has selected **Finastra Trade Innovation** plus **Finastra Corporate Channels** to overhaul its trade-finance stack. Announced **1 July 2026**; delivered with local implementation partner **TCMpartners**. The move **rips out incumbent Surecomp** (IBSnet / allNETT), which BanBif had run since ~2007 — i.e. this is a competitive displacement, not a greenfield win. Same Finastra product pairing that Al Rayan Bank went live with in 2025 (see [[Al Rayan Bank goes live with Finastra for corporate banking]]).

### [1] Dated timeline
- **~2007**: BanBif adopts **Surecomp** front-to-back trade-finance solution (IBSnet + allNETT); Surecomp publishes a case study touting BanBif "at the forefront of Peruvian trade finance."
- **09-Dec-2024**: BanBif reportedly acquired by **Banco de Crédito del Perú (BCP / Credicorp)** — historical majority owner was **Grupo Fierro** (87.3%) with **IFC** (12.67%). Ownership change is material context for a tech-refresh decision. *(unverified against a second source — flag.)*
- **2025-08**: **Al Rayan Bank** goes live on the identical Finastra Corporate Channels + Trade Innovation combo in 4 months — useful comparator ([[Al Rayan Bank goes live with Finastra for corporate banking]]).
- **2026-06-19**: Finastra agrees to **offload its Universal Banking (core banking) division to Pollen Street Capital** ([[Finastra offloads Universal Banking division to Pollen Street Capital]]); also **Phoenix core sold to CORA Group** ([[Finastra sells Phoenix bank core to CORA Group]]). Finastra says it will "concentrate fully on payments and lending."
- **2026-07-01**: Finastra + BanBif announce the trade-finance modernization (PR Newswire, Finextra, Trade Finance Global, prnewswire.co.uk).
- **2026-07-06**: Story enters our feed via FinTech Futures digest.

### [2] What exactly was picked
- **Finastra Trade Innovation** — back-office trade-finance processing engine (letters of credit, documentary collections, international guarantees; ISO 20022 MX / SWIFT MT-MX migration).
- **Finastra Corporate Channels** — modular corporate digital-banking front end unifying trade, supply-chain finance, cash management, treasury; API-enabled.
- **Implementation partner**: TCMpartners (local Peru presence). NOT the same integrator as Al Rayan (that was IBC Solutions).
- Stated benefits: faster LC / documentary-collection turnaround, better visibility, more automation, stronger compliance controls. **Adoption status = selection / project kickoff, NOT go-live.** No live date disclosed.

### [3] Competitive / strategic frame
- **Displaced incumbent = Surecomp** (IBSnet, allNETT) — the primary competitive story here. Other trade-finance software rivals: **CGI Trade360**, **Intellect (iGTB)**, **Newgen**, **China Systems**, plus Finastra's own legacy Trade Innovation base.
- Fits a broader 2026 trade-finance-digitization wave in our DB: [[HSBC launches TradeCash digital trade finance solution]], [[Citi and CredAble launch platform to digitize trade finance]], [[Drip Capital partners YES Bank on MSME trade finance]].
- **Strategic tension worth flagging**: Trade Innovation / Corporate Channels have historically sat inside Finastra's **Universal Banking** lending portfolio — the very division Finastra agreed to sell to Pollen Street on 2026-06-19. Whether this BanBif contract lands with retained-Finastra ("payments and lending") or migrates to Pollen Street's new UB entity is unclear and is the single biggest execution risk for BanBif. (Public sources do not resolve where Trade Innovation ultimately reports post-carve-out.)

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Trade-finance software is a small, slow-growing niche inside bank tech: estimates cluster around $4–9bn in 2024–25 with mid-single-digit CAGRs (e.g. ~3.1% 2026–35 per 360ResearchReports; ~3.5% 2026–33 per SkyQuest; a separate "digital trade finance" cut at 6.4% per Market.us — figures are secondary market-report estimates, treat as directional, not audited). Structure: consolidated at the top (Finastra, Oracle, Intellect/iGTB, CGI Trade360, China Systems) plus specialist challengers (Surecomp, Komgo, Contour) — per openpr/Datos-Insights citations. Value sits in the bank's back-office document engine (LCs, documentary collections, guarantees) with high switching costs. Why now: BanBif is replacing systems described as "nearly two-decade-old" (per Trade Finance Global, 2026-07) — a refresh cycle, not greenfield; the driver is LatAm cross-border trade digitization + push for faster LC/collection turnaround. De-PR: this is a software licence + implementation win (Trade Innovation for internal processing, Corporate Channels for a client portal), delivered with local partner TCMpartners — "adopted/announced," not yet evidenced as live at scale.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs (bank-tech): licence + recurring maintenance/SaaS ARR, implementation revenue, install-base retention (switching costs). Key players and basis of competition: incumbents Finastra (Trade Innovation), Oracle, Intellect iGTB (TradeXpress, GenAI analytics 2025), CGI (Trade360 cloud-native), China Systems; challengers Surecomp (TRADIFINANCE 6.0, API-first, ESG modules), Komgo, Contour — competition is on product depth, cloud-nativeness, and local delivery, not price. Recent peer moves: Standard Chartered completed an ICC-Swift digital bank guarantee via Komgo ([[Standard Chartered completes ICC-Swift digital bank guarantee via Komgo]], 2026-01); Citi + CredAble launched a platform to digitize trade finance ([[Citi and CredAble launch platform to digitize trade finance]], 2026-01). Finastra's position here: incumbent defending/expanding install base in LatAm via a local implementer — but the parent is being actively broken up (see risk flags), so this is an install-base retention win in a division Finastra is choosing to keep, against a backdrop of divestitures. **Risk flags.**
1. **Parent-in-breakup / continuity risk.** Vista is dismantling Finastra piece-by-piece: TCM to Apax (close H1 2026), Phoenix core to CORA and Universal Banking to Pollen Street (both 2026-06), US mid-tier unit shopped via PJT. Trade finance is a division Finastra is keeping *for now*, but a client signing in a business whose owner is a serial divestor faces roadmap/ownership-continuity uncertainty — second-order: BanBif's ~two-decade replacement bet is on a vendor whose corporate shape is unsettled.
2. **Delivery-partner dependence + single-client signal.** Implementation runs through local partner TCMpartners; execution and post-go-live support hinge on that third party, not Finastra directly — go-live/turnaround claims are announced, not yet demonstrated (integration risk on a legacy-replacement in a mid-size market).
3. **Commoditization / margin pressure.** Trade-finance software is low-growth (~3–4% CAGR) and crowded (Oracle, iGTB, CGI, Surecomp, Komgo, Contour); pricing power is limited, so wins like this defend share more than they expand economics — the value is in stickiness, not growth.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** For the sector: a marginal incumbent-retention data point, not a re-rating — Finastra is defending its LatAm trade-finance install base while shedding other divisions, signalling trade/transaction-banking is a "keep" asset in the Vista carve-up `(analysis)`. Falsifiable thesis: if Finastra keeps *winning/renewing* LatAm trade-finance banks through 2026 while divesting cores, trade finance is being groomed as a standalone or retained crown-jewel; it would be wrong if the trade-finance unit itself appears in a future divestiture headline (trigger to watch: any PJT/Evercore/Arma mandate touching trade finance).
