# Context-enrichment: ClearScore profits double as users surpass 25 million
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**FRESHNESS VERDICT: FRESH.** This is the FY2025 reporting period (year ended 31 Dec 2025), a genuinely NEW result vs the prior [[ClearScore triples profits to GBP18.8M for 2024]] note (which covered FY2024, published Sep 2025). New period, new figures — not a re-report. NOTE the metric switch (see [0]): the £18.8m headline for FY2024 was a statutory/PBT-style profit; the FY2025 headline anchors to £17.1m **adjusted EBITDA** (+101%). So the "doubling" is on a different profit line than the prior year's number — an apples-to-oranges framing to watch.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
ClearScore Group reported FY2025 (year ended 31 Dec 2025): group revenue **+37% to £144.7m**; **adjusted EBITDA +101% to £17.1m**; total group users **surpassed 25m globally**. Drivers cited: operational efficiency, AI investment, data innovation, plus the Aro Finance (Jan 2025) and Acre Platforms (Jan 2026) acquisitions.
- **Why the framing matters:** the headline "profits double" rests on **adjusted EBITDA**, not the statutory profit metric that produced the £18.8m FY2024 figure ([[ClearScore triples profits to GBP18.8M for 2024]]). £17.1m adj. EBITDA in FY2025 sitting *below* the £18.8m FY2024 profit number strongly implies the two are different lines — the "doubling" is off a lower/adjusted base. Anti-PR flag: the growth headline is real but the base is chosen to flatter.
- Revenue growth (+37%) is the harder, more durable signal than the EBITDA doubling. That is the number to trust.
- Secondary/derived revenue splits circulating (ClearScore Plus subscription ~£48m, Data-as-a-Service ~£45m, ~65% gross margin, 160+ lenders, ~£12bn facilitated) appear in analyst/business-model write-ups, NOT in the primary PR — treat as **unverified estimates**, not company disclosure.

## [1] Competitors / peers
UK credit-score-plus-marketplace space: [[Experian overhauls credit scoring, adds rental payments]] and [[Experian launches Experian Loans app inside ChatGPT]] (Experian — the incumbent bureau with a D2C arm), plus Credit Karma (Intuit), TotallyMoney, and MoneySuperMarket (aggregator-led). ClearScore's edge is a free-to-user, permissioned-data marketplace with a strong UK D2C brand (~16-18m UK users).
- **Position:** ahead of pure aggregators on data depth (owns the user relationship + open-banking data via D•One); behind Experian/Equifax on being an actual bureau. ClearScore rents credit data and monetizes distribution + its own permissioned data layer.
- **Second-order:** Experian is attacking the same lead-gen margin pool from the bureau side (rental data, ChatGPT loan app) — the structural question is whether the *distribution* layer (ClearScore) or the *data* layer (bureaus) captures the referral margin as lending goes agentic (cf. [[ClearScore launches Agentic Credit Broking Protocol standard]]).

## [2] Company history / fit
Trajectory: FY2024 £89m revenue / £18.8m profit ([[ClearScore triples profits to GBP18.8M for 2024]]) → FY2025 £144.7m revenue / £17.1m adj EBITDA. 2025 was its 10th year. Deliberate roll-up beyond core credit scoring: Aro Finance (Jan 2025, secured/B2B2C "ClearScore Everywhere"), D•One (open-banking data), and Acre Platforms (Jan 2026, mortgages — [[ClearScore acquires Acre Platforms to enter mortgages]] / [[ClearScore accelerates mortgage push with Acre acquisition]]). Distribution partnerships e.g. [[Monzo partners ClearScore to simplify debt consolidation]], [[ClearScore and Oakbrook partner on debt consolidation loans]].
- **Why:** a lead-gen take-rate on unsecured credit is a commodity, cyclical margin. To earn a software/platform multiple ahead of the London listing CEO Basini has flagged ([[ClearScore CEO eyes London listing as signature achievement]]), it must diversify into mortgages, embedded finance and DaaS — hence the acquisition cadence and the revenue mix push.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Not a new product — this is a results print. Real traction: revenue +37% (not just announcements), 25m users, verticalization into mortgages/secured/open-banking. Value-add is genuine on the top line; the profit-doubling claim is softer (adjusted metric).
- **Who captures the margin:** ClearScore owns the consumer relationship and permissioned data, so it captures distribution margin lenders would otherwise pay bureaus/aggregators. Risk: as agentic/AI credit broking commoditizes routing, the durable moat is the *permissioned data asset* (D•One), not the marketplace UI — the UI can be disintermediated by an LLM front-end (Experian's ChatGPT loans app is the warning shot).

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Continued international scaling (South Africa, Australia, New Zealand, Canada) and mortgage rollout via Acre into those markets. CEO has signalled a potential London IPO ([[ClearScore CEO eyes London listing as signature achievement]]); these clean, growing results are IPO-runway grooming.
- **Why / second-order:** a profitable, growing, diversified fintech is exactly the LSE-listing candidate the UK market wants. But the reliance on cyclical UK unsecured-credit lead-gen means a rate/credit downturn would hit the marketplace directly; the DaaS + subscription mix is the hedge, and how much of the £144.7m is durable (subscription/data) vs transactional (lead-gen) is the key un-disclosed number.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** ClearScore sits in UK credit-marketplace / financial-comparison lead-gen: a free consumer credit-score/monitoring app monetised via commission and lead-gen fees from lenders on matched products (credit cards, loans, car finance, now mortgages). No clean single TAM survives sourcing — general "credit-score tracking service" reports (Research & Markets) exist but are paywalled/unverified → market size = no data; don't invent. What is dated & sourced: group revenue +37% to £144.7m and pre-tax/full-year profit +101% (more than doubled), adjusted EBITDA £17.1m, on 25m users and 2.2m product sales in FY ended Dec-2025 (per ffnews/PRNewswire, as of 2026-07). Structure: fragmented at the consumer-front (ClearScore, Credit Karma UK, Experian, TotallyMoney, MoneySuperMarket) but sitting downstream of a consolidated 3-bureau oligopoly (Equifax — ClearScore's data supplier — Experian, TransUnion). Value is captured in the *matching layer* (lead-gen commission), not the score itself, which is a free loss-leader. Drivers / "why now": (1) open-banking + affordability-data lets the marketplace pre-qualify and lift lender conversion (higher commission per lead); (2) an AI/agentic shift — ClearScore's own "Agentic Credit Broking Protocol" ([[ClearScore launches Agentic Credit Broking Protocol standard]], 2026-04) and Experian's ChatGPT credit apps ([[Experian and OpenAI launch UK credit score app in ChatGPT]]) — points to distribution moving to agent-to-agent broking. Why it matters (second-order): if credit journeys move inside third-party agents, the front-end app that owns the *protocol/rails* keeps the commission even as its own UI is disintermediated.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: users/MAU, product sales (conversions), revenue-per-user, lead-gen take/commission per approved product. Key players & basis of competition: Credit Karma (UK, TransUnion data; parent Intuit's Credit Karma unit did ~$2.3bn global revenue FY2025, +32% — a far bigger, better-capitalised rival), Experian (bureau + consumer app, aggressively AI/ChatGPT-distributed — see 14+ in-base Experian moves incl. [[Experian overhauls credit scoring, adds rental payments]]), TotallyMoney (acquired by Intelligent Lending Jan-2025; CEO exited post-buyout — [[Alastair Douglas resigns as TotallyMoney CEO after buyout]]), MoneySuperMarket, NerdWallet (US, adjacent). Competition is on distribution + conversion, not price (consumer product is free). Recent moves: ClearScore acquired Aro Finance (B2B2C, "ClearScore Everywhere" embedded credit) early-2025 and Acre Platforms for mortgages (Jan-2026, [[ClearScore accelerates mortgage push with Acre acquisition]]); Monzo partnership on debt consolidation (2026-07, [[Monzo partners ClearScore to simplify debt consolidation]]). Protagonist's position: UK leader among the pure-play score-marketplaces, catching up vs Credit Karma at group scale but expanding TAM into mortgages/embedded. Moat (analysis): scale of matched-panel + Equifax data integration + emerging agentic-protocol standard = distribution/network moat, not a data-ownership moat (data is licensed from a bureau).

**Comps & multiples.** No public equity price for ClearScore (private) → its own multiple = no data. Reference points:
- **NerdWallet (NRDS)** — closest listed lead-gen comp: mkt cap ~$511m / FY2025 rev $836.6m = **P/S ≈ 0.61x** (as of 2026-05-18). Cheap, reflecting cyclical lead-gen + thin GAAP margins.
- **Intuit / Credit Karma** — Credit Karma unit rev ~$2.3bn FY2025 (+32%); no standalone multiple (embedded in Intuit) → EV/Rev [UNSOURCED].
- **ClearScore last valuation marker:** management floated a £1.5bn–£2.5bn IPO range ([[ClearScore CEO eyes London listing as signature achievement]], 2025-09). Sanity-check vs FY2025 rev £144.7m → implied **P/S ≈ 10.4x–17.3x** (£1.5–2.5bn / £144.7m). That is *far* above NerdWallet's 0.61x. Partly justified by growth (rev +37%, profit +101% vs NerdWallet +22%) and profitability, but a >15x gap to the only listed pure-play comp is a stretch — treat the IPO band as an aspirational founder-cited figure, not a market clearing price. Distribution not fully computed (only 1 clean listed comp + 1 private marker) → qualitative: ClearScore looks **rich vs the listed comp, defensible only on a premium-growth narrative**.
- Internal comps: [[ClearScore triples profits to GBP18.8M for 2024]] (rev £89.7m FY2024 → £144.7m FY2025 = +61% two-yr trajectory context), [[ClearScore acquires Acre Platforms to enter mortgages]].

**Risk flags.**
1. **Credit-cycle / affordability sensitivity.** Revenue is lender lead-gen commission — if UK lenders tighten and cut acquisition spend in a downturn, conversions and commission fall together; a single-country (UK) consumer-credit exposure amplifies this. The "improved efficiency/AI" narrative doesn't remove the cyclicality of the underlying lending demand.
2. **Data dependence on Equifax (disintermediation).** ClearScore licenses its core credit data from a bureau it doesn't own; the same bureaus (Experian) run rival consumer apps and are pushing AI/ChatGPT distribution — the supplier is also the competitor and controls the input cost/access.
3. **Agentic disintermediation of the front-end.** If credit journeys migrate into third-party AI agents, the app UI (its user-acquisition asset, "25m users") loses value unless ClearScore's own protocol becomes the adopted standard — a live bet, not a moat yet; "25m users" is a vanity metric if agents own the interaction.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) The doubling of profit + the mortgage/embedded expansion is a re-rating setup for a London IPO — watch for float confirmation and whether the £1.5–2.5bn band survives contact with public investors given NerdWallet trades at ~0.6x sales. Falsifiable thesis: ClearScore can hold a premium multiple only if it proves the agentic-broking protocol gets external adoption (lenders/other apps) — trigger to watch: first non-ClearScore adopter of the Agentic Credit Broking Protocol. Thesis breaks if UK lender marketing budgets contract (cycle) or Credit Karma/Experian out-distribute it via ChatGPT before the IPO.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Verdict (headline read).** BEAT / strong growth-and-profit print. FY2025 (year ended 31 Dec 2025, ClearScore Group) · revenue £144.7m (+37% YoY) · adjusted EBITDA £17.1m (+101% YoY — the "profit double" of the headline) · 25m+ global users · driver: marketplace scale + Aro Finance consolidation + AI-led efficiency. No public consensus exists (private, UK-filed) → assessed vs prior year, not vs Street.

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Revenue £144.7m, +37% YoY → implied FY2024 group revenue ~£105.6m (analysis; base not separately disclosed in the release).
- Adjusted EBITDA £17.1m, +101% YoY → implied FY2024 adj EBITDA ~£8.5m (analysis). This +101% is what the news frames as "profits double"; note it is **adjusted EBITDA**, not statutory pre-tax profit — the release does not disclose a group statutory profit figure [UNSOURCED].
- Users: 25m+ globally (milestone), up from ~24m cited earlier in the year (analysis).
- Product sales: 2.2m (credit cards, unsecured personal loans, car finance) via the core marketplace, plus 71 exclusive offers.
- Adjusted EBITDA margin ~11.8% (£17.1m/£144.7m) vs ~8.1% implied prior year (analysis) — operating leverage, not just top-line.

**By segment / driver.** Revenue growth is diversification-led, moving beyond credit scoring into embedded finance and mortgage tech. The UK standalone entity (Clear Score Technology Ltd) reported separately earlier: FY2024 UK revenue £89.7m (+17%), operating profit £18.8m (tripled from FY23 £6.4m) — see [[ClearScore triples profits to GBP18.8M for 2024]]. The group figure (£144.7m) captures international markets (South Africa, Australia, Canada) plus the Jan-2025 Aro Finance acquisition. Aro/D2C-specific contribution is **not broken out** in the release [UNSOURCED] — a key gap: we cannot separate organic marketplace growth from the Aro consolidation effect on the +37%.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public consensus (private company, Companies House filer) — beat/miss is qualitative vs prior year: revenue +37% and adj EBITDA +101% both accelerate against the UK entity's +17% revenue trend, consistent with acquisition-boosted group scaling. Trajectory across the corpus: [[ClearScore triples profits to GBP18.8M for 2024]] (FY24 UK print) → [[ClearScore accelerates mortgage push with Acre acquisition]] / [[ClearScore acquires Acre Platforms to enter mortgages]] (Jan 2026 M&A) → this FY25 group print. Direction: accelerating and diversifying.

**Guidance / forward.** No formal numeric guidance (private company). Forward signposts from the release: Acre Platforms acquisition (Jan 2026, mortgage distribution), the world-first Agentic Credit Broking Protocol (see [[ClearScore launches Agentic Credit Broking Protocol standard]]), a consumer ChatGPT app, and the group "close to £150m annual revenue" run-rate. Management tone: confident, growth-and-AI-forward; CEO has flagged a potential London listing (see [[ClearScore CEO eyes London listing as signature achievement]]) — this print (accelerating revenue + doubling EBITDA + 25m users) reads as a deliberate pre-IPO scaling narrative.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. *Credit-cycle sensitivity (core risk).* Revenue is lead-gen/marketplace commission on credit card, loan and car-finance placements → directly geared to lender risk appetite. Fact: 2.2m product sales drive revenue → why: monetisation depends on lenders funding offers → why it matters: a UK credit tightening (rising defaults, lenders pulling prime offers) compresses conversion and take-rate → second-order: EBITDA margin (just built to ~11.8% via leverage) would de-lever fast, and the doubling is not annuity-like. The +101% is not a floor.
2. *Adjusted-vs-statutory silence.* The headline "profit double" is **adjusted EBITDA**, not statutory profit; the group release stays quiet on statutory PBT and on acquisition/integration costs for Aro and Acre → de-PR flag: "adjusted" doubling can coexist with a much smaller (or acquisition-cost-dented) statutory number [UNSOURCED].
3. *Aro contribution not isolated.* +37% revenue blends organic marketplace growth with the first full year of Aro Finance → without the split, organic momentum is unverifiable; a large Aro portion would mean core-marketplace growth is slower than the headline implies (analysis).
4. *Positive: operating leverage is real.* EBITDA (+101%) growing ~2.7x faster than revenue (+37%) shows genuine cost discipline / AI-driven efficiency, not just volume — a durable pillar if the credit cycle holds.
