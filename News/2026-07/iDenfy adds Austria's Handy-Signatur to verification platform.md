iDenfy adds Austria's Handy-Signatur to verification platform
Sinziana Albu
14 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
iDenfy has integrated Austria's Handy-Signatur eID into its identity verification platform, adding a non-document verification option.
The addition allows businesses to verify Austrian users through a credential already used across public and private sector services, without requiring a physical document at the point of onboarding.
According to the official press release, the credential is applied across government services, banking platforms, and private sector applications in the German-speaking region. Within iDenfy's system, users confirm their identity through a mobile authentication flow using a passcode or a mobile SMS one-time password. The verification then draws on qualified-signature-backed identity data rather than information extracted from a scanned document.
Handy-Signatur is issued by the Austrian government and built on the OIDC standard. Each successful authentication returns a fixed set of attributes, including given name and family name, sourced from the government record rather than a document photograph. Because the credential is government-issued, it operates within the legal framework governing Austria's electronic identity infrastructure. Furthermore, iDenfy noted that Handy-Signatur certificates are valid for a maximum of five years; if not renewed before expiry, users must reapply in person at a registration authority to regain access to digital services.
Regulatory context
The integration also aligns with the EU's eIDAS 2.0 framework (Regulation (EU) 2024/1183), which requires all 27 member states to make at least one EU Digital Identity Wallet available by 31 December 2026, and mandates that banks, payment institutions, and e-money issuers accept digital identity credentials as a valid authentication mechanism under strong customer authentication (SCA). As a state-managed identity backed by a qualified signature, Handy-Signatur falls within this framework.
It was also said that the credential has underpinned Austria's identity infrastructure for more than a decade, adding that clients serving that market can now verify users through a method they already use, without requiring them to switch systems or produce a document.
iDenfy clients can enable the Handy-Signatur workflow through dashboard settings, without additional coding or integration work. The system can route users automatically to an electronic ID verification method, including Handy-Signatur or other supported non-document options, when document capture fails or is unavailable, for instance, due to image quality or lighting conditions. According to iDenfy, this scenario is a recurring cause of session drop-off during onboarding. The combined document and non-document verification flow is available to all iDenfy clients at no additional cost.

## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] TL;DR.** On 2026-07-14 iDenfy — a Lithuanian KYC/KYB/AML identity-verification vendor — added Austria's **Handy-Signatur** national eID as a *non-document* (electronic ID) verification option on its platform. Austrian users confirm identity via a mobile flow (passcode / SMS OTP), and iDenfy draws government-record identity attributes (given name, family name) backed by a qualified electronic signature, instead of scanning a physical document. Clients enable it via dashboard settings, no coding, at no extra cost; it can auto-route as a fallback when document capture fails (bad lighting/image quality), a common cause of onboarding drop-off. Part of iDenfy's steady country-by-country eID rollout.

**[1] What iDenfy is / the credential.** iDenfy (Kaunas/Vilnius, Lithuania) sells document + biometric-selfie identity verification, KYB business verification, AML screening and reusable/eID-based verification on pay-as-you-go pricing, with a human-in-the-loop review model. Handy-Signatur is Austria's mobile qualified electronic signature / eID, issued by the Austrian state (operated via A-Trust), built on the **OIDC** standard, in place >10 years. IMPORTANT CAVEAT: Handy-Signatur has been formally **superseded by "ID Austria"** — parallel operation of the two ended **2023-12-05**, and ID Austria is now required for e-government services; however existing Handy-Signatur certificates (~4M holders, out of ~9.2M population) remain valid for up to 5 years and can still be used for qualified signing. So iDenfy is integrating a *legacy-but-still-live* credential, not the current-issue Austrian eID brand.

**[4] So-what / positioning.** Incremental, low-drama product news: one more country in iDenfy's eID coverage map, timed to the eIDAS-2 acceptance mandate. Reduces onboarding friction/drop-off for Austrian-facing fintech/banking clients and strengthens iDenfy's EU-wide "document + non-document" story vs Veriff/Sumsub/Signicat. No funding, no financials, no exclusivity. Main analytical wrinkle: the integrated credential is the *legacy* Handy-Signatur (superseded by ID Austria in 2023), so real forward value depends on iDenfy also supporting ID Austria and, ultimately, the EUDI Wallet.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: RegTech / identity verification (IDV) — specifically **non-document eID verification** built on national qualified-electronic-signature (QES) / eID schemes. iDenfy's move plugs Austria's mobile eID into its KYC platform as a document-free onboarding route, sitting at the intersection of two pools: the broad IDV/KYC market (~$14–16bn 2025–26, ~11% CAGR per Mordor Intelligence, as of 2026, per the sibling [[1Kosmos extends Microsoft Entra Verified ID partnership]] note) and the narrower **e-signature / QES** market (Europe digital-signature revenue ~$1.34bn in 2024, claimed CAGR ~40–43% to 2030–33 per Grand View Research — treat the high CAGR as promotional/`[UNSOURCED]`, single-source). Why now: (1) **eIDAS 2.0 (Reg. (EU) 2024/1183)** requires every EU state to offer an **EU Digital Identity (EUDI) Wallet by 31 Dec 2026** and mandates banks/PIs/EMIs to accept digital-ID credentials under SCA — this seeds demand for QES-backed, government-issued identity rails exactly like Handy-Signatur / ID Austria; (2) deepfake/synthetic-identity fraud surge is pushing IDV away from commodity document-scan toward government-record-backed attributes (no document photo to spoof); (3) onboarding drop-off — the PR itself pitches eID as the fallback when document capture fails (lighting/image quality), a recurring conversion killer. Structurally the eID-connector layer is a **breadth/coverage game**: value accrues to whoever wires up the most trusted national schemes so multinational clients get one integration.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: pass/completion rate, coverage (# of countries / eID schemes / ID types), fraud-catch, assurance certifications (eIDAS, NIST IAL2/AAL2, ISO/IEC 30107-3 PAD), price-per-verification. Basis of competition here = **eID scheme coverage + distribution**, not certification depth. iDenfy (Kaunas, Lithuania; founded 2017; ~49 staff; 2,000+ ID types across 200 jurisdictions per company/getlatka) is a **breadth-and-price** player systematically bolting on national eIDs — a clear internal pattern: [[iDenfy adds Sweden's BankID verification]] (2025-07), [[iDenfy adds Smart-ID to identity verification for Baltic markets]] (2026-06), now Handy-Signatur (2026-07). Direct eID/QES-connector competitors (external, not in base as protagonists): **Signicat** (documents ID Austria natively), **eID Easy**, **Autenti**, **itsme** (which just bought Dutch iDIN — [[itsme acquires Dutch identification service iDIN]]), plus broad IDV peers Veriff, Sumsub, Onfido/Entrust, Jumio, Persona, Socure. A-Trust is the underlying QES issuer for Handy-Signatur/ID Austria (supplier, not competitor). Position (analysis): iDenfy is a **sub-scale, capital-light coverage aggregator** — not competing on assurance certifications or brand, but on "one API, many national eIDs, low/no incremental cost" (the Handy-Signatur workflow is enabled via dashboard toggle at no extra charge). Moat = accumulated integration breadth + low switching cost for existing clients; shallow and replicable.

**Comps & multiples.** Protagonist is **PRIVATE and largely revenue-funded (not VC-backed)** → EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E = no reliable data. Third-party estimates only (all `[UNSOURCED]`/directional): getlatka pegs iDenfy at ~**$5.4M ARR** and a ~**$16.2M valuation** (2025 est., self-report-derived, treat skeptically); Crunchbase shows a small ~$1.1M round (Mar 2025) but the company states its roadmap is funded by revenue, not venture capital. This makes iDenfy **1–2 orders of magnitude smaller** than external IDV leaders — Persona ~$2.0bn, Socure ~$4.5bn, ID.me ~$2.0bn ([[ID.me hits $2 billion valuation after raising $340 million]]), Veriff ~$1.5bn — and even below recently-funded infra startups like Didit ($7.5M raise, [[Didit raises $7.5 million for identity fraud infrastructure]]) in raised capital. Internal comps (base): [[1Kosmos extends Microsoft Entra Verified ID partnership]] (same-week identity sibling), [[itsme acquires Dutch identification service iDIN]], [[Wultra raises €6.8M Series A for digital identity platform]]. Distribution/spread not computable — no comparable public revenue denominators; qualitative read: iDenfy is a **profitable niche micro-cap-scale specialist**, not a valuation outlier, competing below the funded-scale players by being cheap and broad.

**Risk flags.** (1) **Handy-Signatur is a deprecated brand.** Austria officially **replaced Handy-Signatur with "ID Austria" on 2023-12-05**; no new Handy-Signaturen have been issued since, and eGovernment access already requires ID Austria (per Austrian Bundeskanzleramt / A-Trust / WKO). iDenfy's PR markets a **legacy/sunsetting credential** as a fresh capability; existing certificates still work for QES and the underlying A-Trust rails/attributes are the same, so the integration is technically live — but the "for more than a decade" framing masks that the go-forward scheme is ID Austria/EUDI Wallet, and the note's own "certificates valid max 5 years, else reapply in person" caveat compounds the runway question. **This is the headline flag.** (2) **Incremental, no-economics product PR.** No customer names, no volumes, no revenue attached; it is a dashboard-toggle feature shipped "at no additional cost" — i.e. a coverage checkbox, not a booked deal or market-moving event. Fits iDenfy's cadence of near-monthly eID/feature announcements (Sweden, Smart-ID, KYB, criminal-check, etc.), each individually low-signal. (3) **Single promotional source.** One vendor-issued item via The Paypers (also FF News, Financial IT, SecurityInformed — all press-release re-runs); no independent adoption validation; the "4M active users / 9.2M population" reach figures are vendor/aggregator claims. (4) **Commoditisation / scheme-owner risk.** eID connectors are replicable and the strategic endpoint is the EUDI Wallet (mandated end-2026), which could standardise/disintermediate per-scheme connector work; specialist eID vendors (Signicat, itsme, eID Easy) already cover ID Austria. (5) **No market impact.** iDenfy is private and tiny; zero read-through to any listed name.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) Nothing at the trade level — this is a coverage-expansion datapoint, not an inflection. The useful signal is thematic: pre-EUDI-Wallet, IDV vendors are racing to wire up national eID/QES schemes as document-free onboarding rails, and breadth-players like iDenfy monetise the long tail of national methods that scaled players deprioritise. Falsifiable thesis: as the EUDI Wallet goes live (end-2026 → acceptance 2027), per-scheme connectors like Handy-Signatur become transitional — expect iDenfy's announcement cadence to shift from "added national eID X" toward "added EUDI Wallet / member-state wallet support." Trigger to watch: an iDenfy note about **ID Austria** or **EUDI Wallet** support (confirms migration off legacy branding); named enterprise/logo wins or disclosed ARR (would upgrade from feature-PR to traction); continued legacy-scheme feature PRs with no adoption metrics → thesis of channel/coverage-checkbox stagnation holds.
