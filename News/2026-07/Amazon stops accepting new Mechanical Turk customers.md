Amazon will stop accepting new customers for Mechanical Turk
These may be the last days of Amazon’s Mechanical Turk.
An announcement on the Mechanical Turk website says that on July 30, 2026, the crowdsourcing service will close to new customers. Amazon Web Services says the decision was made after “careful consideration,” adding, “Existing customers can continue to use the service as normal. AWS continues to invest in security and availability improvements for Mechanical Turk, but we do not plan to introduce new features.”
In other words, Amazon isn’t completely pulling the plug, but the service is very much on life support.
First launched in 2005, Mechanical Turk was a marketplace where people were paid tiny amounts to perform simple tasks that resisted full automation — things like completing CAPTCHA challenges or identifying the basic sentiment in a sentence.
In its heyday, the service was at the center of debates around the ethics of crowdsourced labor , and it even played a small role in the early stages of the Facebook-Cambridge Analytica scandal. 
Beginning in 2018, Amazon began billing it as a way for companies to annotate data to train neural networks as part of its SageMaker AI service.
Mechanical Turk has also been described as the hidden enabler for companies taking a fake-it-till-you-make-it approach to AI, where products marketed as AI are actually powered by the Mechanical Turk workforce — all the more fitting since the original Mechanical Turk was itself a hoax, with a hidden human chess player pretending to be a chess-playing machine.
Over time, the relationship between Mechanical Turk and AI models grew even more complicated. In a snake-eating-its-own-tail irony , a 2023 analysis found that between 33% and 46% of workers on the platform were using large language models to complete their tasks, raising questions about the reliability of data annotated on the platform and also about whether humans needed to be in the loop at all.
This week, after Amazon’s decision became public, one Reddit user suggested the platform died “years ago,” with workers and researchers abandoning it due to bots and fraud. The user predicted , “Someone at Amazon is going to decide keeping the Mturk servers running is a waste of time and resources and pull the plug entirely.”

# Context-enrichment: Amazon stops accepting new Mechanical Turk customers
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS VERDICT: fresh.** No prior note in the corpus covers the MTurk wind-down (grep across `News/` returns only this note for "Mechanical Turk"). The event is a genuine new development (a July 30, 2026 new-customer freeze on a 20-year-old service), not a reprint. Fintech relevance is thin, however — this is an AI/gig-economy story that touches fintech only via the "gig-worker micropayments" and AWS-cloud angles.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- An announcement on the Mechanical Turk (MTurk) website states that on **July 30, 2026**, the crowdsourcing marketplace will **close to NEW customers**. This is a **new-customer freeze, NOT a full shutdown** — verified against multiple sources (TechCrunch, The Register, SiliconANGLE, MLQ). Existing customers "can continue to use the service as normal."
- AWS language (de-PR'd): decision made after "careful consideration"; it will keep investing in "security and availability" but **will not introduce new features**. AWS added "Amazon SageMaker AI – Mechanical Turk" to its **"Services in Maintenance"** list. → In plain terms: this is a **soft sunset / life-support**, the standard AWS deprecation choreography (freeze new sign-ups → maintenance mode → no roadmap → eventual retirement). No full-shutdown date has been given.
- **Why structured this way:** freezing new customers rather than pulling the plug lets AWS stop investing while avoiding breaching live enterprise workloads and contracts. It quietly caps the liability and lets attrition do the work. The absence of a shutdown date is deliberate — it minimizes migration panic among remaining SageMaker-tied customers.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **The market moved past MTurk's "cheap, anonymous, high-volume" model toward expert, high-quality human feedback.** Key players (dated): **Scale AI** (~$14B valuation; **Meta bought a ~49% stake for ~$14.3B in June 2025**, triggering a client exodus — Google, OpenAI, xAI, Microsoft sought alternatives; founder Alexandr Wang left for Meta; interim CEO Jason Droege; ~14% workforce cut July 2025). **Surge AI** (bootstrapped, founded 2020, >$1B annual revenue, profitable — "quality-first" skilled/well-paid humans). **Mercor** ($100M at $2B early 2025, talk of up to ~$10B late 2025) and Turing/Micro1 — "expertise sourcing" model: who produces the data matters more than volume.
- Amazon's own competing/replacement product: **SageMaker Ground Truth / Ground Truth Plus** — turnkey labeling with an expert or private workforce, automated labeling to cut cost. MTurk was actually one of three workforce options inside Ground Truth; Amazon is steering demand to the managed product, not the raw marketplace.
- **Position:** MTurk is a legacy laggard being retired while the category booms (see below). The item's real signal is not "Amazon exits" but "the cheap-crowdwork tier is obsolete; value migrated to curated expert labor."

## [2] Company history / fit
- MTurk launched **2005** as "artificial artificial intelligence" — micro-tasks (CAPTCHAs, sentiment tagging) humans do that machines couldn't. Named after the 18th-century chess "automaton" hoax (a hidden human) — an irony that fits its "fake-it-till-you-make-it AI" reputation.
- From **2018** Amazon rebranded it as a data-annotation tool for training neural nets under SageMaker.
- Fits AWS's broader pattern this cycle of consolidating around managed AI infra and pricing power — cf. [[Amazon raises AWS GPU reservation prices about 20% amid chip shortage]] and [[AWS in talks to sell Trainium AI chip racks to outside companies]]. Retiring a low-margin, reputationally messy marketplace to concentrate on higher-margin managed AI services is consistent.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty of the event: low.** This is a deprecation of a service widely seen as already dead. A 2023 analysis found **33–46% of MTurk workers were themselves using LLMs** to complete tasks — a snake-eating-its-tail collapse of data reliability. Reddit workers say the platform "died years ago" amid bots and fraud.
- **The value migrated up-market:** RLHF and eval work reward skilled reviewers rating model helpfulness/safety, not anonymous volume. That is precisely the tier MTurk could not serve, and where Scale/Surge/Mercor captured the margin. Related corpus signal on the "who produces the data / eval" premium: [[Arena AI leaderboard becomes a $100M business]].
- **AI data-labeling market (external):** ~**$2.32B in 2026**, CAGR ~23% to ~$6.53B by 2031 (Mordor); MTurk is being retired into a growing market — confirming the problem was model/quality, not demand.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Path: new-customer freeze (Jul 30, 2026) → maintenance mode → likely eventual full shutdown (no date). Existing workloads should migrate to Ground Truth or third-party vendors.
- Sentiment: treated as an obituary for a symbolic Web-2.0 institution, not a market-mover. Second-order: (analysis) LLMs both **automated away** the low-end tasks AND **poisoned** the crowd data (workers using LLMs), so AI killed MTurk from both ends — demand-side (automation) and supply-side (data-quality collapse). The winners are curated-expert labor platforms, several of which are now entangled with the very frontier labs they serve (Meta/Scale), raising a fresh concentration/conflict question that MTurk's neutral-marketplace model never had.
- **Fintech angle (thin):** MTurk was also an early rail for micro-payments to a global gig workforce; its wind-down is a footnote in the gig-payments story rather than a fintech event per se.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** MTurk sits in the "human data" layer of AI: crowdwork → data-labeling/annotation → RLHF/expert-data. This layer is small in absolute terms but growing fast: AI data-labeling ~$2.3bn in 2026 → ~$6.5bn by 2031 at ~23% CAGR (per Mordor Intelligence); a broader "data annotation" cut is put at ~$4.6bn 2026 → ~$38bn 2035 at ~26.5% CAGR (per Business Research Insights). "Why now": the value in the stack has migrated from cheap, generic microtasks (MTurk's model since 2005) to (a) ML-assisted labeling that removes humans from the loop, and (b) scarce *expert* human feedback for frontier RLHF. Note the de-PR: AWS is not just closing MTurk to new customers on 2026-07-30 — it is closing **SageMaker Ground Truth and Amazon Augmented AI (A2I) the same day** and moving MTurk to "Services in Maintenance" (per The Register / SiliconANGLE, 2026-07-03/05). So this is a broader AWS retreat from first-party human-labeling infrastructure, not a one-product cleanup.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: revenue run-rate, gross margin (labor-heavy, ~50–60% at Scale per press estimates), client concentration (5–12 frontier labs), and quality/expertise premium (RLHF/expert rates ≫ microtask rates). Basis of competition has shifted from **price/volume** (MTurk, Appen) to **quality/expertise + secure pipelines** (Scale, Surge, Mercor). Recent moves: Meta took a **49% stake in Scale AI for ~$14.3bn (~$29bn implied), 2026-06** (Forbes/Reuters); **Surge AI** ~$1bn+ revenue, bootstrapped, reportedly raising toward a **$15–25bn** valuation (Forbes/Teahose); **Mercor** in talks for **$500M at ~$20bn, 2026-07-09**, ~$2bn annualized revenue (June), paying ~30k expert contractors ~$85+/hr (Forbes/Bloomberg). Amazon/MTurk's position: **niche → exiting** — the original commodity-microtask incumbent, now on life-support, ceding the field.

**Comps & multiples.** No clean public multiples for the pure-plays (all private; enterprise labs don't disclose annotation P&L) → most multiples = "no data" / `[UNSOURCED]`. Directional round-valuation/revenue arithmetic (post-money valuation, NOT market cap; revenue figures are press estimates, not audited):
- Scale AI: ~$29bn / ~$2bn 2025E rev ≈ **~14.5x** (rev est. per stockanalysis/Forbes) `[UNSOURCED rev]`
- Surge AI: ~$15–25bn / ~$1bn rev ≈ **~15–25x** `[UNSOURCED rev]`
- Mercor: ~$20bn / ~$2bn annualized (June) ≈ **~10x** `[UNSOURCED rev]`
- Public cautionary comp — **Appen (ASX:APX)**: ~$4.3bn peak (2020) → sub-$130m today; FY2025 revenue ~$198m (-12.8% YoY), net loss ~$22m (per stockanalysis/Kalkine). Same commodity-labeling model as MTurk; 80% revenue in 5 clients (MSFT/Apple/Meta/Google/Amazon); Google pulled an ~$83m contract in 2024.
Distribution not computed (only 3 private comps, all `[UNSOURCED]` revenue). Qualitative: the private premium-tier names carry rich double-digit rev multiples justified by triple-digit growth; the **commodity tier (Appen, MTurk) has been re-rated to ~zero** — the outlier that matters here. No internal in-base comps: grep over `News/` surfaced no data-labeling/RLHF pure-plays (closest adjacent: [[General Intuition raises $320M to train AI agents on video games]] — training-data, not labeling).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Commoditization / disintermediation of the microtask model.** MTurk's death confirms that generic human-labeling has near-zero moat: ML-assisted labeling (Ground Truth cut annotation cost up to ~70%) and, ironically, LLMs doing the labeling (2023 EPFL study: 33–46% of MTurk workers used LLMs) hollow out the base. Second-order: even premium labelers face a shrinking window as models self-improve.
2. **Extreme client concentration.** The whole human-data layer sells to a dozen frontier labs (OpenAI/Anthropic/Google/Meta/Microsoft). Appen shows the failure mode — one client (Google, ~$83m) exit crushed revenue. Meta's Scale stake already triggered client defections; concentration cuts both ways.
3. **Data-quality / "fake-it" reflexivity.** LLMs contaminating human-labeled data undermines the product's core value proposition (reliable ground truth) and invites in-house or synthetic-data substitution.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** MTurk's wind-down is a **tombstone for the commodity crowdwork model, not a signal about the AI-data market**, which is booming one tier up (expert RLHF: Scale/Surge/Mercor). The bifurcation thesis (analysis): commodity labeling → $0; scarce-expert human data → richly bid, but on a clock set by how fast frontier models can self-supervise. **Immaterial to Amazon's P&L** — MTurk/Ground Truth are rounding errors against AWS (Q1-2026 results, per IR); the read-through is strategic (AWS deprioritizing first-party labeling infra) rather than financial. Falsifiable trigger to watch: whether AWS steers customers to a partner (e.g., Scale) or exits human-labeling entirely; and whether Mercor/Surge revenue keeps doubling into 2027 or plateaus as synthetic/self-play data scales — a plateau would break the premium-tier bull case.
