## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)

On **6 Jul 2026** Thought Machine announced its **FY2025** results (fiscal year ended Dec 2025): total revenue **surpassed $100m, up 57% YoY**; **ARR crossed $100m in Q2 2026** (ARR growth cited ~59%); **68 banks signed in total, 18 of them tier-one**; and **positive free cash flow in H2 2025**, with a claimed "clear path to sustained operating profitability." Primary source: TM newsroom, "Tier 1 Market Dominance Drives Thought Machine Revenue Past $100m Milestone" (thoughtmachine.net/press-releases/100m-milestone), syndicated to Finextra same day.

**De-PR'd:** three things the headline elides. (1) **Signed ≠ live.** "68 banks / 18 tier-one" is a **cumulative signings count, not go-lives.** The verifiable production references are far fewer (Mox/Standard Chartered, Isybank/Intesa, Zopa, Shawbrook, Atom; Bpifrance/SEB on Vault Payments). Marquee names that get over-read: JPMorgan (US retail, signed/in-progress 2021, no confirmed full go-live), Lloyds (probably live, no dated go-live PR), Curve (signed only). Note: **Chase UK is NOT a TM client — it runs on 10x Banking** (TM's JPMorgan deal is US-only). (2) **"FCF-positive in H2 2025" is a half-year, cash-flow metric — not statutory profit.** Prior audited losses were heavy (£69–71m in 2024, ~£63m in 2023 on ~flat ~£47.6m GBP revenue), and TheNextWeb's same-day coverage (single-source) puts the residual 2025 loss at **~£12m** — close to but not at bottom-line breakeven. **No calendar breakeven date is disclosed.** (3) **Currency framing flatters.** The $100m milestone is stated in **USD**; Companies House filings are **GBP** (~£47.6m FY2024). The "+57%" USD growth and the GBP statutory base are not the same denominator — anchoring the milestone to USD maximizes the headline.

**Why structured this way:** TM has repeatedly deferred its IPO (now pushed to ≥2028 per TNW). Crossing the psychological $100m revenue + $100m ARR + FCF-positive thresholds is the **investor-narrative bridge** it needs to sustain private-market confidence after a **down-ish 2025 top-up (£44.8m, existing investors only, no lead, no valuation disclosed)** and losses that had *widened* through 2024. The release is engineered to reframe the story from "burning cash, IPO slipping" to "at scale, self-funding, tier-one dominance."

## [1] Competitors / peers

Cloud-native core-banking SaaS is the battleground:
- **10x Banking** — the most credible cloud-native tier-one rival; runs **Chase UK** and Westpac; won Co-operative Bank NZ. Directly took the deal (Chase UK) that TM is sometimes credited with.
- **Engine by Starling** — the 2025-26 momentum story; battle-tested inside Starling; landed **Tangerine (Canada, 10-yr, Nov 2025)** — its first client bigger than Starling itself — plus AMP Bank GO, Salt, SBS NZ. See [[Tangerine signs with Engine by Starling]].
- **Temenos** — scale incumbent (>950 banks), retrofitting a legacy suite to SaaS on AWS; see [[VPBank moves to cloud-native core banking with Temenos, Red Hat]] and [[Temenos launches embedded AI agents across core and digital banking]]. Cloud-native-*by-origin* credentials contested — that's exactly TM's wedge.
- **Mambu** — weakened (co-founder CEO departed, layoffs) → an easier displacement target (secondary-sourced).
- **Tuum** ([[Tuum secures strategic partnership extension with Multitude Bank...]]), **Pismo (Visa-owned)** — Visa distribution wildcard, **Finxact/Fiserv**, **SAP Fioneer** round out the field.

**Position:** TM is a **leader among cloud-native challengers** (Gartner MQ leader, Retail Core Banking 2025) but **not the category leader by installed base** (Temenos) and is being outrun on marquee *go-lives* by 10x (Chase UK) and Engine (Tangerine). Its differentiator vs Temenos is genuine no-legacy architecture + smart-contract product engine; vs 10x/Engine the fight is deal cadence and reference go-lives, where TM's "signed vs live" gap is its exposure.

**Why the lay of the land is this way (2nd order):** Core-banking replacement cycles are 5–10 years and switching costs are enormous, so **each tier-one go-live is worth years of ARR and is a durable moat** — but that same stickiness means the market rewards *live* references, not signings. TM's headline optimizes the metric (signings) that is *cheapest to grow*, while the metric that *actually compounds* (production tier-one go-lives) is where rivals are landing blows.

## [2] Company history / fit

Founded 2014 by **Paul Taylor** (ex-Google text-to-speech; still CEO 2026). Funding: Series A $25m (2018, Lloyds-led) → Series B $125m (2020, Molten/Draper Esprit + Eurazeo) → **Series C $200m at >$1bn (Nov 2021, led by Nyca Partners** — *not Nium*; new investors ING, JPMorgan, Standard Chartered Ventures) → **Series D $160m at $2.7bn (May 2022, led by Temasek** — its first TM investment). Total priced ~$510m; **latest disclosed valuation is still $2.7bn (May 2022)** — the 2025 raise disclosed no mark, so $2.7bn should **not** be presented as "current." Prior corpus: [[Thought Machine lands GBP 45M funding as losses widen]], [[Shawbrook Bank goes live on Thought Machine's Vault Core]], [[Thought Machine signs General Bank of Canada for Vault Core]], [[Thought Machine powers Zopa Bank's new Biscuit account]], [[Fintech unicorns lukewarm on Reeves' stamp duty holiday]] (TM flagged UK-IPO conditions as insufficient).

**Why the company acts this way:** TM raised at a peak-2022 valuation ($2.7bn) then spent three years with revenue ~flat in GBP and losses widening — a classic **"grew into a valuation" pressure**. Long enterprise sales cycles mean signings precede revenue by years, so it needs a self-funding narrative to avoid a down round or a bad-market IPO. Reaching FCF-positive + $100m ARR is the structural move that lets it **wait for a better IPO window (≥2028)** rather than raise dilutively — hence both the metric framing and the SI partnerships (DXC, HCLTech) that offload implementation cost.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction

**What's genuinely new here:** the *inflection*, not a product. The real signal is **FCF-positive in H2 2025** plus **ARR crossing $100m** — the first hard evidence the model can self-fund. That is materially more credible than a signings count. Vault Core's value-add (smart-contract product engine, real-time, cloud-agnostic, no legacy code) is real and differentiated vs Temenos; but it's not new in 2026.

**Traction, honestly:** durable **ARR >$100m growing ~59%** is the durable-revenue base (multi-year SaaS/subscription licences, 3–5yr terms) — high-quality, sticky revenue that deserves a software multiple. The soft spot is **conversion of signings to live**: only ~5–7 verifiable production references against 68 signings. **Who captures the margin:** TM captures core-platform ARR, but hands implementation economics to SIs (DXC, HCLTech) — good for scaling FCF, but it means the *services margin and the deepest client relationship* partly sit with the integrator, and the "agentic/autonomous banking" story is **HCLTech's platform framing, not a TM-branded product**. Don't read agentic AI as a TM revenue line yet.

**Why it's real / what breaks it:** real because switching costs make live ARR near-annuity. It breaks if (a) signings keep outrunning go-lives (revenue stalls as bookings age), or (b) 10x/Engine keep taking the tier-one *live* wins that define the reference market.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment

Next: **IPO pushed to ≥2028** (per TNW); continued push on tier-one signings → go-lives; deepening SI channel (DXC/HCLTech) and Vault Payments; possible fresh raise (TNW single-sources a ~£30m/$41m tier-one-bank raise — unverified). Market backdrop: core-banking software ~**$13.3–13.8bn in 2025, ~10% CAGR** (Precedence/Grand View; narrower "cloud-native" figures diverge ~10x by scope — treat as directional).

**Why the market goes this way (2nd order):** cloud core-banking migration is a decade-long secular shift with a limited number of tier-one prizes, so the winners are decided by **who accumulates live tier-one references now**, because those become the proof points that win the *next* cohort. Counterintuitively, TM's $2.7bn-2022 valuation is a **fragility, not a strength**: a public listing forces a mark-to-market, and with only ~£47.6m GBP statutory revenue behind the USD-$100m headline, the IPO delay to 2028 is as much about *avoiding a valuation reset* as about market timing. The central question is therefore **not "is TM at scale?" (it is, on ARR) but "can it convert its signings backlog into live tier-one deployments faster than 10x and Engine — before its 2022 valuation forces a reckoning?"**

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Cloud-native core banking is the SaaS layer replacing legacy mainframe cores (Temenos T24-era, FIS, Fiserv). Cloud core-banking-platform TAM ~$1.6bn in 2025 → ~$1.9bn 2026, ~21% CAGR to ~$11bn by 2035 (per market.us, as of 2025; secondary vendor report, treat as directional). SaaS/hosted took ~58% of the broader core-banking market; neobanks/fintechs ~47% of end-demand (market.us, 2025). Structure: fragmented on the challenger side (Thought Machine, Mambu, 10x, Tuum, Thredd) vs. entrenched incumbents (Temenos, FIS, Jack Henry, Fiserv) still holding the tier-1 installed base. Barriers are high — mission-critical migration risk, multi-year sales cycles, regulatory scrutiny — which is also the moat once a bank commits. Why now: a visible cohort of tier-1 migrations is finally moving (TM: 18 tier-1s, >10% of the largest institutions), plus an AI-in-core wave (Temenos embedded AI agents, 2026-05; [[HCLTech and Thought Machine partner on AI-driven banking]] 2025-09).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: ARR, ARR growth, NRR, gross margin, FCF, and — for core banking specifically — # of live tier-1 banks (proof the platform survives real migrations). Key players: incumbents Temenos/FIS/Fiserv/Jack Henry (price/installed-base/distribution); cloud-native challengers Thought Machine, Mambu, 10x Banking, Tuum (product/architecture). Recent moves: [[Temenos acquires Swiss wealth platform additiv]] (2026-06, moving up-stack into wealth); [[10x Banking marks 100th release with version 10.0]] (2026-03); Mambu raised ~$100m in 2025 (per market.us); TM signed [[Thought Machine signs Shawbrook Bank as core banking customer]] (2025-09) and [[Thought Machine signs General Bank of Canada for Vault Core]] (2025-11). TM's position: ahead of the cloud-native peers on tier-1 penetration and on the profitability curve (positive FCF H2 2025, ARR crossing $100m). Moat = switching costs + intangibles (from-scratch, no-legacy-code Vault Core codebase); `(analysis)`.

**Comps & multiples.** No public market cap for TM (private). Comparability caveat: Temenos is a mature ~$1bn-rev listed incumbent, TM a hyper-growth private challenger — different scale/stage, so read as a bracket, not a like-for-like.
- **Temenos (SWX:TEMN)** — TTM revenue ~$1.08bn, market cap ~$5.87bn (as of ~May 2026). EV/Revenue ≈ $5.87bn / $1.08bn ≈ **5.4x** (using mkt cap as EV proxy; net debt not verified → treat as ~P/S). EV/EBITDA ~14.5x (valueinvesting.io, 2025-11). In-line for a mature, low-growth core-banking vendor.
- **Mambu (private)** — last round $5.5bn post-money (Series E, Dec 2021); revenue ~$128.6m (2024, getlatka). $5.5bn / $128.6m ≈ **43x** — a stale 2021-vintage mark, almost certainly no longer supported.
- **Thought Machine (private)** — last round $2.7bn post-money (Series D, May 2022; Temasek-led). $2.7bn / >$100m FY2025 rev ≈ **~27x** on the last round. But investor Molten Ventures marked TM down ~40% in Dec 2024 (UKTN) → implied ~$1.6bn → ~$1.6bn / $100m ≈ **~16x**. Both are private-round marks, NOT market value; the £45m raise in Oct 2025 (Tech.eu) suggests recent pricing may sit below the 2022 peak.
- Internal comps: [[Temenos acquires Swiss wealth platform additiv]], [[10x Banking marks 100th release with version 10.0]], [[Thought Machine signs Shawbrook Bank as core banking customer]].
- Distribution not computed (only 1 verifiable public multiple). Rich/cheap: even the marked-down ~16x sits above Temenos's ~5x, but TM grows 57% vs. Temenos low-single-digits — a growth-justified premium `(analysis)`, not an automatic red flag.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Profitability vs. the marketing.** "Positive FCF in H2 2025" and ">$100m ARR" are strong, but the audited FY2024 accounts (Companies House, filed 2025-09-30) show turnover only £47.6m and pretax losses ~£69–71m; the jump to $100m+ revenue at +57% is a Companies-House-filing claim not yet independently confirmed in audited FY2025 accounts. De-PR: ARR crossing $100m is dated Q2 2026, revenue is FY2025 — different periods, don't conflate.

3. **Migration/concentration & execution.** Revenue leans on a small number of large multi-year tier-1 migrations (management explicitly credits "multi-year commitments for several tier-1 migrations" for the ARR jump); a single delayed or churned tier-1 go-live materially swings ARR, and core-banking migrations are the highest-risk projects in banking IT.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` TM crossing $100m rev/ARR with positive FCF is the first credible signal that a cloud-native core vendor can reach incumbent-relevant scale AND profitability — supportive of a re-rating for the challenger cohort ahead of a 2028 IPO. Falsifiable thesis: if audited FY2025 accounts confirm >$100m revenue and losses narrowing toward breakeven, TM prices its next round/IPO above the 2022 $2.7bn. What breaks it: FY2025 filing shows the $100m is heavily bookings/ARR-flattered vs. GAAP revenue, or a flagship tier-1 migration slips — either would re-open the discount-to-2022 gap. Trigger to watch: the audited FY2025 Companies House filing (expected ~2026 H2).

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Verdict (growth read).** GROWTH INFLECTION (private, no consensus) · FY2025 revenue $100m+ (+57% YoY) · ARR crossed $100m in Q2 2026 · positive free cash flow in H2 2025 — the first structural break from a company that had been ~flat and deeply loss-making. No formal "beat/miss" possible: Thought Machine is private, its own audited FY2025 accounts are not yet filed, and the FY2025 numbers come from a self-reported milestone announcement, not a filing.

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Revenue FY2025: **$100m+**, **+57% YoY** (self-reported milestone; note text and press). This is a sharp acceleration — the last *audited* year (FY2024) was essentially flat.
- ARR: **$100m+**, crossed in **Q2 2026** (i.e. after the FY2025 close) — the recurring base now roughly equals reported annual revenue, a healthy sign for a core-banking SaaS.
- Free cash flow: **positive in H2 2025** (self-reported) — vs a company that was burning heavily; operating losses reportedly reduced from ~£70m toward ~£12m per secondary press (analysis: label as press-reported, not filed).
- Clients: **68 banks** signed globally, incl. **18 tier-one** institutions.

**Prior-year baseline (from the IR primary source — audited FY2024 accounts, filed 2025-09-30).**
The latest *filing* on record is the audited FY2024 group accounts (Companies House, Thought Machine Group Limited, co. 11114277). It shows a company that was NOT growing before this inflection:
- Turnover FY2024 ≈ **£47.6m** vs FY2023 ≈ **£47.8m** — essentially flat / -0.4% (secondary reads of the filing).
- Pre-tax loss FY2024 ≈ **£69.3m** vs FY2023 ≈ **£62.7m** — loss *widened* ~£6.6m (one secondary source, City AM, reads the loss as £71.2m, +20.6%; the exact figure differs by source, so treat the precise loss as [UNSOURCED at 1-decimal precision] pending the actual PDF).
- Headcount cut to ~**518–523** (FY2024) from ~**552** (FY2023) — cost discipline preceded the growth story.
Note the currency/framing gap: the audited base is in **GBP (~£47.6m)**; the FY2025 milestone is quoted in **USD ($100m+)**. Even allowing for FX (~£47.6m ≈ $60m at ~1.27), a jump to $100m+ is a genuine step-change, not just a currency re-cut — but the two are not like-for-like disclosures, so the "+57%" is the company's own USD-basis figure, not a filing-derived GBP figure.

**By driver.** Growth is enterprise/tier-one land-and-expand: 18 of the world's largest banks (>10% of the tier-one market per company framing), US as the largest market, UK ~15% of revenue, plus Australia and LatAm. The ARR≈revenue convergence suggests the mix is shifting from one-off implementation toward recurring platform fees — the key thesis line for a Vault-style infrastructure bet.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public consensus exists (private co) → assessment is qualitative vs the audited prior year: revenue ~flat (FY2023→FY2024) then **+57%** (FY2024→FY2025), and cash flow flipping from heavy burn to **positive FCF in H2 2025**. That is the material change. Prior corpus reports on this company: none found in News/ (no `[[wikilink]]` to a past earnings note).

**Guidance / forward.** No formal guidance (private). Management reframed the story away from valuation toward commercial traction — founder Paul Taylor: "We are trying to put less emphasis on valuation and more emphasis on commercial success." **IPO pushed to 2028 at the earliest** (previously floated sooner). Latest capital: a **£30m ($41m)** round from a tier-one bank that is also a client (reported mid-2026); the prior disclosed valuation was **$2.7bn** (2022 Series D), with a July 2025 **~£44.8m** round from existing investors — no new valuation disclosed.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. **Real inflection, but self-reported.** +57% revenue and positive H2-2025 FCF would validate the platform thesis — *if* they survive the audit. The last *filed* year was flat and loss-widening; there is a ~12–18 month lag before FY2025 audited accounts confirm the milestone. Second-order: treat "$100m+/+57%/FCF-positive" as management-marketed until Companies House shows it.
2. **Currency & basis inconsistency (de-PR).** The growth headline is USD self-reported; the audited base is GBP. The company is silent on an exact FY2025 GBP turnover and on the audited FY2025 loss — the two figures that would let you check the "+57%" and the FCF claim. Who's silent on what: they tout revenue and FCF, not the bottom-line loss for FY2025.
3. **IPO slip to 2028 = capital dependence.** Positive FCF in *one half* is not sustained profitability; the £30m/£41m tier-one round signals they still need external capital and a strategic-client anchor. Second-order: valuation held flat since 2022 ($2.7bn) — the "commercial success over valuation" reframing is a tell that the mark is under pressure.
4. **Concentration.** 18 tier-one banks driving the step-up means revenue is enterprise-concentrated; a single large-bank deferral or churn would swing the growth rate materially.
