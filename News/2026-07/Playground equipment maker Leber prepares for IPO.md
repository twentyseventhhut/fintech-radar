## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[Mergers.ru] 🛝 Производитель игрового и спортивного оборудования «Лебер» готовится к IPO. Как сообщала пресс-служба компании, в сентябре 2025 года по итогам первого потока программы pre-IPO/IPO «Сколково» «Лебер» получил главную награду «Лучшая готовность к IPO». В апреле 2026 года «Инновационный центр «Лебер» получил официальный статус резидента «Сколково», открывающий доступ к налоговым преференциям и инструментам поддержки. По прогнозам, завершение этого инвестиционного цикла приведет к снижению долговой нагрузки к концу 2026 года.

# Context-enrichment: Playground equipment maker Leber prepares for IPO
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
The digest ("Лебер готовится к IPO") reads as a fresh deal signal. De-PR'd, it is **not a filed or announced transaction — it is a "preparing/considering" narrative** built on two soft 2025 signals plus a 2025 financials footnote:
- **Sep 2025:** «Лебер» won "Best IPO readiness" in the first cohort of Skolkovo's pre-IPO/IPO program (a *readiness* award, pitched by exec director Artur Gladyshev, not a mandate). (leber.ru, 2025-09-23)
- **Aug 2025:** press reported the company "задумалась о выходе на IPO" and was recruiting finance staff "for IPO preparation"; the company declined comment. (abn.agency, 2025-08-11)
- **Apr 2026:** «Инновационный центр Лебер» got official Skolkovo-resident status → tax preferences. A support-programme milestone, not an equity event.
- **2025 accounts (ООО «Лебер»):** revenue **RUB 2.22bn**, net profit **RUB 36.6mn** — a sharp fall vs 2024, which the company attributes to "large-scale investment" and promises deleveraging by end-2026.

What is confirmed to be **open / undisclosed:** IPO timing (no half-year, no year committed), valuation, placement size, free float %, primary vs secondary, price range, bookrunners, and — critically — **whether the LLC has been converted to a joint-stock company (АО/ПАО), a hard prerequisite to list.** Still ООО. Treat as an **early-stage trial balloon**, not a live deal.

**Why framed this way / what it reveals (red-team):** the story is anchored to the flattering 2025 *readiness award* and a self-serving deleveraging promise, because the underlying numbers are moving the wrong way. Note the reporting inconsistency: the digest cites **2025 revenue RUB 2.22bn / net profit RUB 36.6mn**, but registry data (RBC Companies) shows **2024 revenue RUB 2.74bn / net profit RUB 206.5mn** and 2023 ~RUB 2.47bn / ~RUB 467mn. So on either dataset the trajectory is the same: **revenue roughly flat-to-down and profit collapsing (~56% in 2024, and by the digest's own 2025 figure profit is down ~82% to RUB 36.6mn).** Margin compression is precisely the story an issuer would want scrubbed before pricing — the "investment cycle" explanation is management framing, unaudited. A company times an IPO pitch to its *strongest* window; pitching into collapsing profit and a weak tape suggests fund-raising need (deleveraging) more than opportunistic value-capture.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Sector peers (product):** small architectural-forms / playground makers — KSIL, Ksil, Wonderland, Arbero (which **Leber acquired a controlling stake in, late Jul 2025** — a pre-IPO roll-up move). None are listed; the industry is fragmented and municipal-tender-driven.
- **Capital-markets peers (the relevant comp set — recent Russian small-cap listings):** the 2025 MOEX/SPB cohort — **JetLend** (crowdlending, SPB Exchange, IPO 2025-03-26 at RUB 60, ~RUB 6-6.5bn cap, faded post-listing), **GloraX** (developer), **Basis Group**, **DOM.RF** (state, RUB 31.7bn — the outlier). This is Leber's true peer set by *size and market*, and it is a cautionary one: **only 7 of 19 issuers that IPO'd in 2024-2025 traded above offer.**
- **Position:** Leber would be a **micro/small-cap** at ~RUB 2.2-2.7bn revenue — smaller than most of the above, in a niche with no listed comp to anchor a multiple.

**Why the lay of the land is this way / second-order:** Russia's equity market is a **sanctions-walled garden** — foreign institutions are effectively locked out, so pricing rests on **domestic retail + local institutions**. That makes valuations thin and story-driven, and it is why a Skolkovo "readiness" brand and a consumer-recognisable "playgrounds" story matter more than they would in a deep market. But thin demand also means small deals underperform (see JetLend), so the same walled garden that lets a micro-cap *attempt* a listing is what makes the listing likely to trade poorly.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Group traces to **2010** (Leber Group, founder Artem Sorokin); operating ООО «Лебер» registered **2018**. Own plant in **Mytishchi** (Moscow region), ~150 staff; exports to UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kazakhstan, Mongolia, Armenia etc. Claims 10,000+ installations, "Russia's largest" playground/МАФ maker. (leber.group; RBC Companies; Habr, all accessed 2026-07-17)
- Multi-owner LLC cap table (Sorokin + Bagdasaryan, Lichagin, Afanasyev, Mkrtumov via ООО «Лебер Групп»).
- **Founder's "~RUB 3bn revenue" claim is a rounded promotional figure** vs audited ~RUB 2.2-2.74bn.

**Why the company acts this way:** a mid-sized, capex-heavy, budget-dependent manufacturer with **falling margins and (per its own words) elevated debt** has a structural reason to seek equity: a pre-IPO/IPO raise deleverages the balance sheet without adding interest cost — attractive precisely because the CBR key rate is still **14.25%** (2026-06-19), making debt expensive. The Arbero acquisition + Skolkovo residency (tax breaks) + IPO-readiness pitch are a coherent "dress-for-sale" sequence. The pressure driving the story is **funding cost**, not a growth land-grab.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What is genuinely new here:** essentially nothing yet. The only 2026-dated event is Skolkovo-resident status (Apr 2026); the IPO itself is unchanged "considering" language from 2025. No new terms, no filing, no PAO conversion evidenced.
- **Traction of the *IPO*, not the business:** zero — no mandate, no size, no date.
- **Business traction:** real but deteriorating — profitable, exporting, but revenue flat and profit down sharply.

**Why the value-add is thin / who captures the margin (deeper):** playgrounds/благоустройство revenue flows through the federal **"Comfortable Urban Environment"** program and municipal budgets under **44-FZ/223-FZ tenders**. That means the *customer is the state*, demand is **cyclical and politically set**, and pricing power sits with procurement rules, not the manufacturer — a commodity-metalwork take-rate business, not a software-multiple story. For equity investors that caps the achievable multiple and adds budget-cycle/concentration risk. The "innovation centre / Skolkovo" wrapper is an attempt to reframe a metal-fabrication municipal supplier as a tech/growth name; nothing in the numbers supports a growth re-rating.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Market backdrop (in-corpus):** MOEX itself says **15-20 companies are in high readiness for IPO** ([[Moscow Exchange sees 15-20 companies ready for IPO]], 2026-07-13) — Leber is one of a crowd, not a standout, and does not appear on the named 2026 shortlists. Sentiment is cautious after a weak 2025 (~4 IPOs, ~RUB 33bn total).
- **Regulatory headwind — the decisive one:** the Bank of Russia's proposed **RUB 3bn minimum placement / free-float floor for tier 1-2 listings** ([[Bank of Russia proposes 3bn ruble minimum for IPO listing tiers]], 2026-07-06, effective ~2027): for a company with capital under RUB 30bn, free float must be ≥10% **and not less than RUB 3bn**. A RUB 3bn free float at 10% implies a **~RUB 30bn valuation** — implausible for a ~RUB 2.2bn-revenue, thin-margin manufacturer. Leber would therefore likely be pushed to a **lower listing tier** or a **pre-IPO / private placement** rather than a quality-tier IPO. This rule, landing exactly as Leber "prepares," materially reduces the odds of a real tier-1/2 MOEX IPO.
- **Demand pipe (in-corpus):** domestic pre-IPO vehicles are active and paying out ([[VIM Investments Pre-IPO funds pay investors 9.2bn rubles]]) — the likelier realistic route for a name this size is a **qualified-investor pre-IPO ЗПИФ**, not a public float.

**Why the market goes this way / counterintuitive second-order:** the same regulator push for "quality" liquidity that is meant to protect retail investors is what **prices Leber out of a real IPO** — the walled-garden retail demand that makes tiny listings *possible* is exactly what the CBR is now trying to fence off. Net: a low-probability, low-importance "preparing" story whose most probable outcome is *not* a MOEX IPO on the implied timeline.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Two layers intersect: the Russian primary-equity market (the listing) and RU playground/urban-equipment manufacturing (the business). (i) **The sector is government-demand-driven and mid-sized.** Per Minstroy figures cited in RU trade press, the annual Russian children's-playground market exceeds **~RUB 30bn**, with **>10,000 new objects** commissioned per year — pulled largely by state/municipal budgets under the "comfortable urban environment" (**«Формирование комфортной городской среды»**) landscaping/благоустройство programs and by private residential developers landscaping new housing estates (per kindermaf/DR Group/Minstroy-linked coverage, 2025-26). So the demand base is **procurement- and budget-cycle-dependent**, concentrated in large cities and new-build districts. (ii) **The RU IPO window it's listing into is hostile.** The MOEX index (IMOEX) sat near a ~2022 low (~2010 on 2026-07-17); the CBR key rate is **14.25%** (cut 25bp on 19 Jun 2026; next meeting 24 Jul) — equity still competes with ~15-18% risk-free deposits, and consensus is that a real IPO revival needs the rate down to ~12-13%, expected only H2 2026 (per CBR/Moscow Times; see [[Moscow Exchange sees 15-20 companies ready for IPO]]). 2025 delivered only **3 IPOs** (DOM.RF RUB 31.7bn, GloraX RUB 2.1bn, Basis RUB 3bn); 2026 YTD two small deals (B2B-RTS RUB 2.43bn, Incab RUB 2.04bn), both **below the RUB 3bn quality-tier floor** the CBR now proposes (see [[Bank of Russia proposes 3bn ruble minimum for IPO listing tiers]]). Why now (analysis): Leber won Skolkovo's "best IPO readiness" award (Sep 2025) and became a Skolkovo resident (Apr 2026) for tax preferences — this is a **readiness/pre-IPO-track signal, not a priced deal**; timing is aspirational and rate-path-contingent.

**Competitive landscape.** For the *issuer* the relevant KPIs are placement size, free float and post-IPO liquidity; for the *company* they are revenue scale, margin and order-book/state-contract mix. Sector players: Leber positions as a leading RU playground/sports-equipment maker; competition is other domestic MAF/dvorovoe-oborudovanie plants plus imports, competing on **price, certification/safety (GOST) and proximity to municipal procurement**, not technology or network effects. Recent company moves (per Leber/vc.ru 2025 case): ~1,000 playgrounds installed in 2025, expansion into export (cited: Latin America, Saudi Arabia), new product series slated for 2026. Position (analysis): a **small-cap niche champion** in a fragmented, tender-driven sector; its "moat" is a strategic/regulatory one (import-substitution, GOST-certified state-supplier status), which is real but commoditizable — cable-IPO parallel in [[Mergers.ru Incab Holding raises 2 billion rubles in IPO]]. It carries **no disclosed IPO size, valuation or float** — so it sits at the "ready" end of MOEX's 15-20 pipeline, most of which historically does not price (~15-20% conversion; see the MOEX-pipeline note).

**Comps & multiples.** No equity multiple is computable for Leber — **no IPO size, valuation or float has been disclosed** (WebSearch found only the award/residency and the FY financials), so EV/EBITDA, P/E, P/S = **no data**. The load-bearing arithmetic is instead the **financial trajectory**, which is a red flag: per the note's FY2025 RAS print, ООО «Лебер» revenue **RUB 2.22bn, net profit RUB 36.6m**; against FY2024 (subsidiary ООО «Лебер» revenue **RUB 2.7bn**, net profit **RUB 206.5m**; Leber Group profit ~RUB 201.9m, per ABN Aug 2025) that is **revenue ~-18%** (`2.22 / 2.70 − 1 = −17.8%`) and **net profit ~-82%** (`36.6 / 206.5 − 1 = −82.3%`), with net margin collapsing from ~7.6% to ~1.6%. Management attributes the drop to a heavy investment cycle (infrastructure, new products, new markets) expected to reduce leverage by end-2026 — plausible but self-reported `(analysis)`.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Deteriorating financials into a hostile window.** FY2025 revenue -18% / net profit -82% (margin 7.6%→1.6%) means Leber would market a **down year** into a 14.25%-rate, near-2022-low index — pricing pressure and a discount-to-clear are the base case; the "investment cycle" explanation is unverified and shifts the equity story to a forward de-leveraging bet.
2. **Customer/demand concentration on state budgets.** The sector is procurement- and благоустройство-program-dependent; a squeeze on municipal/regional budgets or a slower "comfortable urban environment" cycle hits order flow directly, and state buyers cap pricing power — cyclical, policy-exposed revenue with a commoditizable product.
3. **"Ready" ≠ "priced" + tier-3/liquidity risk.** No size/valuation/float disclosed; at ~RUB 2bn scale Leber likely misses the proposed RUB 3bn quality-tier floor (tier-3, like Incab), implying thin free float, low liquidity and index exclusion — and historically most of MOEX's "ready" pipeline slips rather than lists.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) Leber is a **pipeline-readiness data point, not a deal** — a small industrial in a state-demand sector queuing for a window that keeps slipping. Falsifiable thesis: Leber does **not** price a full MOEX IPO in 2026 absent a sustained CBR cut to ~12-13%; more likely it stays on the Skolkovo pre-IPO track or does a small tier-3 placement. Triggers to watch: the **24 Jul CBR decision** and H2 rate path; whether Leber discloses an actual size/valuation/free-float and FY2026 shows the promised de-leveraging; and the after-market of the next small RU listing (2025-26 cohort averaged negative in month one). Bull confirmation: a sub-13% rate plus a disclosed, credibly-priced deal with a recovering margin. Not a fintech event — judge as a RU capital-markets / consumer-industrial signal.
