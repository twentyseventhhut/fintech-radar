NOTO partners with Equifax UK on real-time AML screening
Sinziana Albu
01 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
NOTO and Equifax UK have partnered to launch Automated Watchlist Monitoring, a real-time anti-money laundering screening solution for the UK market.
The offering is built on NOTO 360™, NOTO's screening engine, and is designed to help Equifax clients manage compliance obligations across the customer lifecycle amid tightening UK regulatory requirements.
The partnership responds to a shifting UK regulatory and geopolitical environment. The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) set out changes to the treatment of domestic politically exposed persons (PEPs) in its Finalised Guidance FG25/3, published in July 2025, requiring firms to recalibrate their approach to this category. This has coincided with strengthened expectations from the Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation (OFSI) regarding sanctions screening for 2026 and beyond. Regulators, including the FCA and the US Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC), have increased expectations around continuous watchlist screening. In addition, the scope and complexity of global watchlists has also grown in recent years, adding pressure on firms to screen in real time, including at the transaction level.
AWM is intended for high-throughput environments and provides watchlist screening for individuals and entities with response times under 100 milliseconds, without limits on transaction volume. The underlying technology applies a matching algorithm to screen against sanctions lists, PEP registers, and adverse media sources, and can monitor changes to a customer's risk profile on an ongoing basis.
The solution includes configurable case management and thresholds by category, such as sanctions, PEPs, and adverse media, alongside a policy editor. It covers the customer journey from account opening through to ongoing monitoring, alerting, and transaction screening. AWM also maintains an audit trail of screenings and matches to support due diligence and transparency requirements. The service is integrated via a REST API and is also available in batch, drawing on Equifax's proprietary data sources.
Industry context
 Ivan Stefanov , CEO and co-founder of NOTO, said the Equifax partnership follows the company's recognition by Chartis as a 'market disruptor', and that the collaboration is intended to help Equifax clients streamline their compliance operations.
 Matt Jones , VP of Partnerships and European Growth at Equifax, said organisations can face difficulties with traditional batch remediation processes, including missed changes in customer risk profiles and higher operational costs. He described the AWM launch as a milestone for Equifax UK's AML roadmap, adding that the partnership is intended to broaden the AML and fraud technology available to Equifax customers, with a specific focus on watchlist screening.
Furthermore, the launch adds to a wider trend among compliance technology providers toward continuous, real-time screening rather than periodic batch processing, reflecting regulatory expectations that firms monitor risk on an ongoing basis throughout the customer relationship.

# Context-enrichment: NOTO partners with Equifax UK on real-time AML screening
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5. Freshness: fresh._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
NOTO (a Sofia-based, bootstrapped financial-crime SaaS vendor) and **Equifax UK** announced (~30 Jun 2026, PR Newswire; retold by The Paypers 01 Jul) a distribution/OEM partnership to launch **Automated Watchlist Monitoring (AWM)** — a real-time AML watchlist-screening service for the UK, built on NOTO's existing **NOTO 360** engine. Disclosed specs: sub-100ms response, no transaction-volume limit, screening of individuals + entities against sanctions lists, PEP registers and adverse media, ongoing risk-profile monitoring, configurable case management / thresholds / policy editor, audit trail, REST API + batch, drawing on Equifax's proprietary data.

**De-PR'd reality:** this is a **new product wrapper, not new technology** — AWM rides an already-marketed NOTO engine. It is **announced, not confirmed GA/live**: no go-live date, **no named launch client, no pricing, no exclusivity, no committed volumes** — all the substance that would prove materiality is deferred. The "<100ms" figure is **vendor self-reported, not independently benchmarked** (though consistent with NOTO's prior "double-digit-ms / ~65bn tx/yr" claims). **Why framed this way:** it is a channel announcement — Equifax lends enterprise distribution + proprietary data; NOTO lends a ready low-latency engine. Neither side commits to numbers because none are yet earned. The PR anchors to regulation (FG25/3, OFSI) to manufacture urgency around a routine OEM tie-up.
→ Why it matters: the central question is not "is real-time AML valuable" (it is) but **"does this specific deal have adoption"** — and on that, everything is silent.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Real-time / transaction-level screening is a crowded field: **ComplyAdvantage** (real-time API, ML entity resolution), **Feedzai** (sub-second, payment-rail scale — see [[Feedzai partners Neterium to launch transaction screening product]] and [[Feedzai and Neterium integrate screening into RiskOps platform]], Feb 2026, a directly analogous "engine specialist + platform" tie-up), **Napier AI**, **Neterium**, **Flagright** (real-time AML in lending — [[Fig selects Flagright for transaction monitoring and AML in Canada]]), plus list/data incumbents LexisNexis Risk, Dow Jones Risk, NICE Actimize, SymphonyAI, Ripjar, Quantexa. UK peers on the bureau/AML axis: [[SmartSearch and Plannr partner on AML compliance]] (triple-bureau AML). Also [[IDenfy launches AML screening tech]] (Oct 2025, real-time sanctions/PEP/adverse-media).

**Position:** NOTO is **catching up / at parity**, not ahead. Sub-100ms watchlist scoring is already claimed by Feedzai, DataVisor and others. **Why the landscape looks this way:** the Feedzai-Neterium pairing shows the winning pattern is "screening engine + distribution platform" — exactly what NOTO-Equifax replicates, but ~4 months later and with a smaller, unfunded engine partner. NOTO's real differentiator is the **unified fraud+AML platform + low/no-code config**, not raw latency. Second-order: latency has commoditized, so the moat shifts to data breadth (Equifax's contribution) and false-positive quality — where NOTO is unproven at UK-bank scale.

## [2] Company history / fit
NOTO: founded **2017, Sofia, Bulgaria** (legal entity historically "Notolytix"), by financial-crime professionals; CEO/co-founder **Ivan Stefanov** (ex risk background). **~69 employees** (self-reported, Sep 2025), **bootstrapped / no external funding raised**, strongest traction historically in **Latin America and Europe** — **no named marquee clients found**. Recently named a **"market disruptor" in Chartis FCC50 2026** — note this is the **emerging-challenger tier, not a leader ranking**, and NOTO's debut appearance.

Equifax UK already sells "Watchlist Check / AML Profile" (sanctions + PEPs, 5m+ profiles) but on an unstated (likely batch/periodic) processing model. **Why each acts this way:** NOTO, a small unfunded vendor, needs enterprise distribution to reach UK banks it cannot sell to directly → Equifax's brand + data is the channel. Equifax needs a **real-time/continuous layer** to defend its bureau franchise against pKYC-native challengers → buying (OEM) beats building a low-latency engine. The fit is logical; the risk is asymmetric — a bootstrapped 69-person Bulgarian vendor will face steep enterprise vendor-risk/financial-solidity scrutiny inside UK banks.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**What's genuinely new:** first announcement of the Equifax-branded AWM product for the UK. **What's NOT new:** the underlying NOTO 360 engine, and the "real-time vs batch" pitch (industry-wide since 2024; Feedzai-Neterium did the same shape in Feb 2026). **Traction: none disclosed** — no launch clients, volumes, or GA date. This is announcement, not adoption.

**Why the value-add is thin (for now):** the margin in this stack is captured by **whoever owns the data + the bank relationship — i.e. Equifax**, not the engine vendor. NOTO risks being a commoditized "latency component" behind Equifax's brand, capturing little pricing power (the classic OEM-supplier trap). The FG25/3 tailwind (softer domestic-PEP EDD → fewer false positives) plays to NOTO's low-false-positive pitch, but that same guidance helps every screening vendor equally — it is not NOTO-specific value. Value-add becomes real only if named UK banks adopt at volume; until then it is a co-marketing story.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Structural tailwinds are real and dated: **FCA FG25/3** (published 07 Jul 2025, revised 15 Jul 2025) recalibrating domestic-PEP treatment; **OFSI 2026** enforcement escalation (updated penalties guidance 09 Feb 2026; OFSI Strategy 2026-29 on 15 Apr 2026; max civil penalty doubling toward £2m/100% of breach value); broad shift to **perpetual KYC (pKYC) / continuous monitoring** over batch. These raise the cost of screening gaps → genuine demand tailwind for real-time tools.

**Why the market goes this way:** regulators moved from "controls present" to "controls effective," which forces continuous screening. **Counterintuitive second-order effect:** the same tailwind lifts every competitor, so a broad rising tide **compresses differentiation** — the winners will be those with distribution + data (bureaus like Equifax, scaled platforms like Feedzai), which structurally **favors Equifax over NOTO** in value capture. Risk to NOTO: vendor-risk hurdles at UK banks given its size/financials, plus no exclusivity disclosed (Equifax could swap engines).

**Freshness verdict: FRESH.** No prior NOTO note in corpus; no existing note covers this Equifax-AML deal (nearest analogues are the distinct Feedzai/Neterium tie-ups). Genuine new development, not a reprint.
