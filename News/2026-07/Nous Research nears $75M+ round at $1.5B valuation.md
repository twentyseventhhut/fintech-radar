Hermes agent maker Nous Research in talks for new funding at $1.5B valuation
Nous Research, the startup behind the open-source Hermes agent, is finalizing a new round of funding led by Robot Ventures, with significant participation from USV and other prominent investors at a $1.5 billion valuation, according to three sources with knowledge of the deal. The company is raising at least $75 million, and fielded a high level of interest from investors, according to the people. 
Nous Research declined to comment. USV and Robot Ventures didn’t respond to our request for comment.
The company was founded in 2023 by Jeffrey Quesnelle, Karan Malhotra, Ryan Teknium, Shivani Mitra. Before this round, it had raised a total of $70 million in funding from investors including Paradigm, Robot Ventures, North Island Ventures, OSS Capital, and Balaji Srinivasan, according to Crunchbase.
Weeks after Openclaw’s agent went viral, Nous Research released its own competitor called Hermes. OpenClaw is an agent that runs locally on a PC and can perform tasks on behalf of the user. One key difference is that Hermes shipped with built-in “skills,” such as web search, coding and image understanding. Furthermore, it was designed to automatically learn from people’s usage and build more skills without manual intervention. Additionally, the startup has released language models focused on coding and math .
Just like Openclaw, users can automate tasks with Hermes and chat with these agents or receive messages from them in apps like Telegram and Discord. These tools have become increasingly popular as they allow users to run their AI agents remotely and around the clock.
Open-source and widely adopted, Hermes has amassed a massive following on GitHub, boasting roughly 214,000 stars and nearly 40,000 forks. Developers can run Hermes on a desktop or on a virtual private server. 
But Nous Research also offers a cloud-hosted version , which some users may find to be more user-friendly, avoiding any setting up on their own machines. The hosted version is available via various paid tiers ranging from $20-$200 a month.
Sources say the new funding will help to expand Hermes’ products and business model further.

# Context-enrichment: Nous Research nears $75M+ round at $1.5B valuation
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**Fintech relevance caveat:** This is an AI-lab funding story, crypto-adjacent only via the Solana-based Psyche training network and Robot Ventures / USV being crypto-native backers. It is NOT a payments/banking/fintech-core event. It earns a place in a fintech radar as a *crypto x AI capital-flows* signal, not as fintech infrastructure. Keep the framing tight.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **Reported, not closed.** Per three sources cited by TechCrunch (13 Jul 2026), Nous Research (Austin) is *finalizing* / *in talks for* a round of **at least $75M**, led by **Robot Ventures**, with "significant participation" from **Union Square Ventures (USV)** and others, at a **$1.5B valuation**. Nous, USV and Robot Ventures all declined / did not comment → this is a leak, terms and close are unconfirmed. Treat $75M and $1.5B as reported floor/target, not signed figures.
- **The real anchor: a valuation step-up.** The last round (Series A, **led by crypto VC Paradigm, April 2025**) was struck at a **$1B valuation** — and that $1B was the **SAFT-implied fully-diluted (token) valuation**, i.e. priced off token warrants, not a clean equity round (Fortune/The Defiant). So the "$1.5B" is a ~50% mark-up in ~15 months, and its comparability to the $1B is muddied because the prior number was token-implied. **Why it matters:** the headline "$1.5B" may again be a token-linked / SAFT construct rather than pure equity — the sources don't say. Do not treat it as a clean equity valuation.
- **Prior funding total is stated inconsistently.** TechCrunch: total **$70M** before this round, founded **2023** by Quesnelle, Malhotra, Teknium, Mitra. Other trackers (Fortune, ICO Analytics, Gate): founded **2022**, ~**$65M** across two rounds (Distributed Global ~$15M pre-A + Paradigm $50M A). **Flag: founding year and cumulative total conflict across sources; TechCrunch is the primary here but the discrepancy is unresolved (open).**
- **What the money is for (vague):** "expand Hermes' products and business model further." No revenue disclosed, no unit economics — pure growth-capital framing.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Direct product rival: OpenClaw.** TechCrunch frames Hermes explicitly as a fast-follow competitor released "weeks after OpenClaw's agent went viral." Hermes' differentiation (per Nous): built-in skills (web search, coding, image understanding) and auto-learning new skills from usage. **Why:** Nous is *reacting* to OpenClaw's category, not defining it — the moat claim is "open-source + skills," which is easy to copy.
- **Decentralized-AI-training peers (internal corpus):**
  - [[Prime Intellect raises $130M Series A for enterprise AI agents]] — 2026-07-10, $130M Series A (importance 4). Same thesis (train-your-own agents without frontier labs), *bigger* round, enterprise-focused. Prime Intellect is arguably ahead on capital and enterprise positioning.
  - [[Venice AI becomes unicorn with $65M Series A]] — 2026-07-01, $65M Series A, unicorn, crypto-adjacent (Erik Voorhees), **already profitable, ~$70M ARR**. Contrast: Venice has disclosed traction/revenue; Nous has disclosed none. That gap is the story.
- **Crypto-VC pattern:** Paradigm (prior), now Robot Ventures + USV (crypto-native funds) — the round is being bid up inside the crypto-AI capital pool, which was flagged in the corpus as a theme: [[Haun Ventures raises $1 billion fund for crypto and AI]] (2026-05). **Why the lay of the land is this way:** crypto VCs are seeking "crypto x AI" narratives with token upside; a decentralized-training lab with a token roadmap is exactly the asset they can underwrite via SAFTs.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Trajectory:** founded 2022–23 (crypto-native founders who chose to prove AI-research credibility first, then layer blockchain). Seed ~$15–20M (Distributed Global, North Island Ventures, Delphi, Together AI, Solana co-founder Raj Gokal) → **Paradigm $50M Series A, Apr-2025, $1B SAFT valuation** → now $75M+ at $1.5B.
- **Product stack:** Hermes (open-source fine-tuned LLMs, ~33M Hugging Face downloads; DeepHermes-3 with toggleable reasoning, Feb-2025) + Hermes Agent (the newly viral product, ~214k GitHub stars, ~40k forks) + **Psyche Network** (decentralized GPU training on Solana, launched Jan-2025). Hosted tiers $20–$200/mo = the only visible revenue line.
- **Why the company acts this way:** open-source distribution (huge GitHub/HF footprint) builds mindshare cheaply but doesn't monetize; the token/Psyche layer is the intended value-capture mechanism. **Second order:** raising equity-ish capital *now*, ahead of any token issuance, lets insiders set a $1.5B mark that a future NOUS token can be anchored to — the round partly exists to price the token.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Genuinely notable:** the *distribution* (214k stars, ~33M HF downloads) is real and large — not a PR number. Open-weights adoption is the strongest fact in the story.
- **But traction ≠ revenue.** No ARR, no paying-user count, no GMV. The only monetization shown is $20–$200/mo hosted tiers. Contrast Venice AI's disclosed $70M ARR → Nous is being valued at 1.5x Venice's valuation on *undisclosed* revenue. **Who captures the margin:** open-source Hermes can be self-hosted for free, so Nous only monetizes the convenience (cloud tier) and, prospectively, the token. If the token never ships cleanly (as of Apr-2026 no official token existed; unauthorized "NOUS" pairs already trade on Solana DEXs — a reputational/regulatory hazard), the equity value-capture is thin.
- **Novelty vs OpenClaw:** incremental (built-in skills, auto-learning). Not a category-defining delta.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Sentiment:** hot. "High level of interest from investors" (leaked), oversubscribed framing, crypto-AI narrative at a peak (Haun's $1B crypto+AI fund, a16z crypto's "mainstream moment"). Step-up from $1B→$1.5B in ~15 months.
- **Regulatory backdrop:** an unissued token with unauthorized lookalike tokens trading on Solana DEXs is a live securities/consumer-protection risk if/when NOUS launches under US rules.
- **Counterintuitive second-order:** heavy crypto-VC concentration + token-implied valuations make the mark *fragile*, not safe — a $1.5B SAFT number can reprice fast with token-market sentiment, unlike a revenue-backed equity mark. The durable question is not "is the round big" but **"is any of this valuation backed by cash flows, or is it a token-narrative mark that Venice AI's profitability quietly exposes as thin?"**

## Market Research

**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: decentralized / open-source AI (agents + open-weight LLMs), a crypto-adjacent slice of AI-infra. No credible free TAM figure for "decentralized AI" specifically — no data; but the structural driver is measurable: open-weight models now account for ~1/3 of all inference token volume, and the best open-weight models (Qwen, DeepSeek V4, Kimi) sit within tens of Elo points of the closed frontier at ~10–50x lower cost/token (per OpenRouter/arena data via cryptobriefing/tensorlabbet, as of Apr 2026). Structure: fragmented, capital-light at the app layer (agents) but capital-heavy at the training layer (GPU fleets) — Nous straddles both. Entry barriers are mainly distribution/community (GitHub adoption) and a hard research moat on distributed training. "Why now": (1) enterprise flight from closed labs after the Anthropic/Fable "sudden shut-off" episode ([[Prime Intellect raises $130M Series A for enterprise AI agents]], [[Linas Newsletter JPMorgan, Goldman pull Claude from employee toolkits]]) is a live tailwind for "own your own model"; (2) the OpenClaw → Hermes local-agent wave; (3) crypto-AI capital rotation.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs (this is pre-revenue-disclosure, so watch): GitHub stars/forks & MAU (adoption proxy), open-weight token share, cloud/hosted ARR, and — for the crypto-native peers — token FDV. Basis of competition = research (distributed-training efficiency) + community/distribution, not price. Nous's assets: Hermes agent open-source with ~214k GitHub stars / ~40k forks; hosted tiers $20–$200/mo; Psyche network on Solana using in-house DisTrO optimizer (distributed training over the internet). Peers & recent moves: **Prime Intellect** — $130M Series A at $1B, ~$100M annualized RR, customers Ramp/Zapier (2026-07-08, [[Prime Intellect raises $130M Series A for enterprise AI agents]]); **Ollama** — $65M Series B, ~8.9M MAU, 176k stars (2026-07-09, [[Open source AI tool Ollama raises $65M, nears 9M users]]); **General Intuition** — $320M at $2.3B on gameplay-trained agents (2026-06-25, [[General Intuition raises $320M to train AI agents on video games]]); **Gensyn** — decentralized training, $AI token TGE Apr 2026, FDV ~$482–552M; **Pluralis** — $7.6M seed (Mar 2025, USV+CoinFund) at the "protocol learning" frontier. **Risk flags.**
1. **Valuation vs. visible traction (rich).** $1.5bn with zero disclosed revenue, while the nearest same-week comp (Prime Intellect) sits at $1.0bn WITH ~$100M RR. The uplift is priced on GitHub adoption + AI-hype rotation, not economics — re-rating risk if the hosted business ($20–200/mo tiers) doesn't convert stars into ARR. Second-order: an open-source flagship (Hermes, 40k forks) is the hardest thing to monetize — forks self-host for free.
2. **Token-vs-equity ambiguity / crypto overhang.** Nous is repeatedly framed with a "$1bn token valuation" (Paradigm round) yet has NOT officially issued a token; unofficial "NOUS" pairs already trade on Solana DEXs. A future TGE could dilute equity holders, and the Gensyn precedent (Apr-2026 pump-then-sharp-correction, FDV ~$0.5bn) shows how violently these marks reprice. Governance/liability of who captures value (equity vs token vs DAO) is unsettled. Second-order: an equity round at $1.5bn sitting above a crypto token structure creates conflicting claims on the same upside.
3. **Thin, contestable moat + platform dependence.** Distribution moat (community) is real but not defensible against Ollama/OpenClaw/frontier labs shipping the same agent UX; Psyche's distributed-training edge depends on Solana rails and on DisTrO staying ahead. Disintermediation risk if a frontier lab open-weights a comparable agent stack.
