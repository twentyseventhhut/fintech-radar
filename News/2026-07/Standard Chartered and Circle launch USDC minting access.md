Standard Chartered and Circle launch USDC minting access
Sinziana Albu
03 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Standard Chartered has partnered with Circle to give institutional clients integrated access to USDC minting and redemption.
The launch positions Standard Chartered as the first Global Systemically Important Bank (G-SIB) licensed to offer institutional clients this type of integrated access through a single onboarding and service process, without requiring clients to hold separate accounts with Circle.
The new offering connects fiat banking, digital asset infrastructure, and public blockchain networks within a single, bank-led structure. According to Standard Chartered, this allows institutions to move value across traditional and digital financial systems with greater speed and transparency. The capability is designed to support use cases, including on-chain settlement, treasury operations, and liquidity management, while also providing infrastructure intended to support payment-related applications in the future.
Through the process of embedding USDC access within its institutional offering, Standard Chartered combines banking, custody, and digital asset services under one structure, applying the risk management, compliance, and governance processes used across its wider institutional business.
Initial rollout in the DIFC
The capability is initially available to eligible clients through Standard Chartered's operations in the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC). The bank described this as the first phase of a broader global stablecoin proposition, with plans to expand into additional markets subject to regulatory approval and market readiness.
Moreover, the DIFC launch adds to the United Arab Emirates' position as a jurisdiction with an established regulatory framework for digital asset activity.
Industry context
The launch reflects continued demand from financial institutions and corporations for regulated stablecoin infrastructure that can support payments, treasury management, settlement, liquidity management, and broader participation in digital asset markets. Stablecoins such as USDC have increasingly been positioned by banks and payment providers as tools for faster cross-border value transfer, with regulatory clarity in jurisdictions such as the UAE and the EU contributing to institutional adoption.
 Roberto Hoornweg , Chief Executive Officer, Corporate and Investment Banking, Standard Chartered, said the launch extends the trust and governance standards used in traditional markets into digital assets, with the aim of supporting broader institutional participation in the sector through established regulatory frameworks and controls.
 Kash Razzaghi , Chief Commercial Officer, Circle, said the integration allows institutions to access Circle's regulated stablecoin infrastructure through Standard Chartered's banking platform, enabling use of USDC across payments, settlement, and treasury operations while maintaining existing compliance and risk management requirements.
The launch forms part of Standard Chartered's wider digital assets strategy, which spans banking, markets, securities services, custody, and digital market infrastructure. The bank has framed the initiative as part of its ongoing approach to stablecoins and digital assets within a regulated structure, with further geographic expansion expected as licensing and market conditions allow.

# Context-enrichment: Standard Chartered and Circle launch USDC minting access
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **2026-07-02** (The Paypers dated it 07-03): Standard Chartered announced institutional clients can **mint and redeem USDC through the bank** via a single onboarding — no separate Circle account needed. Rollout is **only** through StanChart's **DIFC (Dubai)** operations, framed as "phase one" of a broader global stablecoin proposition. Exact claim wording: "the first **G-SIB** licensed to offer institutional clients integrated access to USDC minting and redemption through a **single onboarding and service experience**." (SC/Circle press releases.)
- **De-PR'd**: this is an **activation/productization of a multi-year relationship, not a new tie-up.** StanChart has held cash backing USDC reserves and facilitated local USDC minting (via Zodia Markets, Singapore) since **2023**, and was named a **Circle Payments Network (CPN) design partner** on **2025-04-21**. The 2026 news re-packages that relationship into a client-facing bank service.
- **Why worded this way**: the "first G-SIB / integrated / single-onboarding" tag is a **defensive marketing hedge** — because **BNY announced essentially the same institutional USDC mint/redeem capability three days earlier (2026-06-29)**. StanChart's "first" survives only as literally phrased ("integrated single onboarding"), not as substantive priority. Ledger Insights reads the "no direct Circle account" model as the start of **correspondent banking for stablecoins** (plausible, unconfirmed).
- **Traction = none disclosed.** No client names, no volumes, no fees, no first-transaction evidence — announcement-grade only.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **BNY (BNY Mellon), 2026-06-29** — 3 days earlier — enabled institutions to custody, mint AND redeem USDC on its Digital Asset Custody platform; USDC the first stablecoin on it. Direct functional overlap; BNY led with "custody," StanChart with "integrated onboarding."
- **CPN bank cohort (2025-04-21)**: Santander, Deutsche Bank, Société Générale, Standard Chartered — banks helping build Circle's network, i.e. this space is already crowded with G-SIBs.
- **[[ClearBank to join Circle Payments Network for USDC and EURC]]** (2025-10) — a UK bank joining CPN for USDC/EURC; same "bank as stablecoin rail" thesis.
- **[[BBVA and Santander embrace stablecoins in crypto push]]** (2025-06) — BBVA added USDC to its Swiss crypto-asset service; European banks distributing USDC.
- **[[Fireblocks and Circle collaborate to speed stablecoin adoption]]** (2025-09) — infra layer (custody/tokenization) enabling banks to plug into USDC.
- **JPMorgan / Kinexys** — JPMD deposit token (not a stablecoin) on Base, heading to Canton — a competing *bank-issued tokenized dollar* model. **Société Générale** — EURCV bank stablecoin.
- **UAE domestic peers**: AE Coin (dirham stablecoin, final CBUAE license Dec 2024, live by Jan 2026), FAB DDSC dirham stablecoin, RAKBank (initial approval Jan 2026), and **Circle's own ADGM permission (Dec 2025)**. USDC (USD) is complementary to these dirham coins, not directly competing.
- **Position**: parity/catching-up, not ahead — the differentiation is narrow packaging over an already-live capability.

## [2] Company history / fit
- StanChart runs the **broadest bank digital-asset stack**: Zodia Custody (moving to full acquisition, ~Aug 2026 close; regulated custody folds into SC), Zodia Markets, tokenization (Libeara), a GSR stake, SC Ventures.
- Trajectory: **2023** USDC reserves + Singapore local minting → **2025-04** CPN design partner → **2026-07** DIFC client-facing mint/redeem. A logical, incremental step.
- **Why StanChart acts this way (analysis)**: as an emerging-markets/cross-border-focused G-SIB, it monetizes by being the **regulated on/off-ramp bridge** between fiat rails and public-chain USDC in corridors (Middle East, Asia, Africa) where it already has correspondent relationships. Owning the onboarding lets it capture treasury/settlement flows without ceding the client relationship to Circle — the same logic behind acquiring all of Zodia Custody.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new**: a G-SIB embedding USDC mint/redeem inside its institutional bank service under one KYC/compliance umbrella, in a MiCA-adjacent regulated jurisdiction (DIFC/DFSA). The **UX/compliance packaging** is the value-add, not the underlying capability (which StanChart already had internally since 2023 and which BNY shipped days earlier).
- **Traction**: **zero disclosed** — no clients, volumes, fees. Market reaction muted (CRCL reportedly ~+3.8% on the day). Treat as "framed/live-claimed," not "adopted."
- **Who captures the margin (analysis)**: economics undisclosed. If StanChart is effectively a **Circle distributor/correspondent**, the fee split and reserve-yield economics matter — USDC reserve income accrues to Circle, so the bank likely earns onboarding/transaction/treasury fees, not float. This is the key open question on durability.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- StanChart says expansion beyond DIFC is "subject to regulatory approval and market readiness" — **no dates**.
- **Regulatory backdrop**: US GENIUS Act (2025) legitimized bank stablecoin activity; DFSA's updated crypto-token rules took effect 2026-01-12; UAE Federal Decree Law No. 6/2025 gives crypto firms until **Sept 2026** to license. Tailwind is real.
- **Second-order (analysis)**: the wave of near-simultaneous G-SIB USDC integrations (BNY, StanChart) signals stablecoin mint/redeem is **commoditizing into a standard bank service** — good for Circle's distribution, but it compresses any single bank's differentiation to relationships/corridors, and raises the counter-question: does the bank capture margin, or become a low-fee pipe while Circle keeps the reserve yield?
- USDC context: ~$77B in circulation (Q1'26, #2 behind USDT ~$184B); Circle Q1'26 revenue $694M, on-chain volume $21.5T (+263% YoY). Circle IPO'd on NYSE (CRCL) 2025-06-05.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Stablecoin sector total supply is ~$311–320bn as of Apr–May 2026 (per DefiLlama/MacroMicro, via bitcoinfoundation.org, as of late Apr 2026), of which USDT ~$189.6bn (~59% dominance) and USDC ~$77.6bn — the two together >95% of outstanding (per DefiLlama, via bitrue.com, as of 29 Apr 2026). 21Shares forecasts >$1trn by end-2026 (via Forbes, 08 Apr 2026) — a forecast, not a fact `(hypothesis)`. Structure: highly consolidated at the issuer layer (Tether + Circle ~4/5 of the market), fragmented at the distribution layer where banks now compete. Entry barrier is shifting from tech to *regulatory license + bank balance sheet*: GENIUS Act (US, final OCC/Treasury rules targeted Jul 2026, enforcement Jan 2027) and MiCA (EU) turn a compliance moat into the gating asset. Why now: regulatory clarity in UAE/DIFC, EU (MiCA) and the US lets G-SIBs distribute a third-party stablecoin without issuer-level risk — Standard Chartered is framed as the *first G-SIB* to offer integrated USDC mint/redeem via single onboarding (per SC/Circle pressroom, 02 Jul 2026).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: USDC in circulation (EoP), on-chain transaction volume, reserve income, RLDC margin (revenue-less-distribution-costs), and Circle's on-platform share (higher share = less reserve income leaks to Coinbase). Basis of competition at the distribution layer is *balance-sheet trust + regulatory reach + client rails*, not price. Key moves: JPMorgan running deposit tokens via Kinexys, first major bank live on a public chain (Base) in early 2026; Citigroup + Coinbase institutional stablecoin partnership; BitGo launched institutional native minting ([[BitGo launches BitGo Mint for institutional stablecoin minting]]); BBVA/Santander added USDC custody/trading in 2025 ([[BBVA and Santander embrace stablecoins in crypto push]]) (all via Forbes/Cryptopolitan, Apr 2026). Protagonist positioning: Standard Chartered = niche-first-mover among G-SIBs (DIFC-only, phase one) `(analysis)`; Circle = the issuer being distributed, extending USDC's institutional reach without adding a new balance sheet. Circle's moat: regulatory licenses (MiCA/French, GENIUS-ready reserves) + issuer network effects; the SC deal deepens distribution but the *economics of the bank channel are undisclosed* — who earns the reserve yield spread is not stated.

**Comps & multiples.** Circle (CRCL) is the only pure-play public comp; the SC deal carries no valuation. CRCL market cap ~$16.2–16.6bn at ~$65.9/sh, ~248.6m shares, 01–02 Jul 2026 (per stockanalysis.com/Yahoo). Q1 2026 total revenue and reserve income $694m, +20% YoY (per TIKR / company, Q1 2026). Annualizing Q1 revenue: P/S ≈ $16.4bn / ($694m × 4 = $2.78bn) = **~5.9x** `(analysis — annualization, not reported LTM)`. Reserve income $652.5m (94.0% of revenue) — i.e. earnings are rate-sensitive, not fee-driven. Net income $55m, −15% YoY (rising opex). USDC circulation $77bn EoP Q1, +28% YoY; on-chain volume $21.5T, +263% YoY. Internal comp on issuance momentum: [[Circle mints $1 billion USDC in 24 hours amid institutional demand]] (10 Apr 2026) and [[Circle's USDC outpaces USDT growth for second year]] (07 Jan 2026). Verdict: ~5.9x P/S is not extreme for a name growing revenue +20% but is stretched given net income *fell* — the multiple prices a rate + volume story, not current margin. EV/EBITDA, forward: `[UNSOURCED]` (no free clean figure). Standard Chartered comps: not run (giant diversified bank, this line item is immaterial to its multiple) — "no data".

**Risk flags.** (1) *Rate dependence* — 94% of Circle revenue is reserve (interest) income; a rate-cut cycle compresses the whole USDC economics stack that SC is plugging into, and net income already fell −15% YoY despite circulation growth. (2) *Undisclosed channel economics + Coinbase leakage* — the SC/Circle split is silent; separately Coinbase takes ~50% of the remaining reserve payment base on USDC it holds (per 10-Q), so distribution partners' share is structurally squeezed, and a bank-led channel adds another mouth to feed. (3) *Regulatory/single-jurisdiction concentration* — live only in DIFC; expansion is "subject to regulatory approval," and GENIUS/MiCA divergence (e.g. MiCA's ~60% EU-bank-deposit reserve rule vs GENIUS T-bill flexibility) could fragment the "global" proposition. (4) *Disintermediation risk to Circle* — as G-SIBs (JPMorgan/Citi) push *own* deposit tokens, banks distributing USDC today could issue tomorrow, capturing the reserve spread themselves.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` The deal marks the distribution layer, not issuance, as the new battleground: whoever owns the institutional onboarding captures the client, and Circle is renting SC's balance sheet-trust rather than building it. Falsifiable thesis: if SC-type bank channels *raise* Circle's on-platform share (less reserve income to Coinbase), RLDC margin improves — watch Circle's on-platform-share and distribution-cost lines in Q2/Q3 2026 earnings. What breaks it: G-SIBs launching *own* stablecoins/deposit tokens instead of distributing USDC (Kinexys trajectory) — that turns today's partners into tomorrow's competitors.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Verdict (headline read).** MIXED / EPS beat, revenue miss · Q1 2026 (ended 2026-03-31, reported 2026-05-11) total revenue and reserve income $694M (+20% YoY; vs public consensus ~$715M, i.e. ~-$21M / -2.9% miss) · GAAP diluted EPS $0.23 (net income $55.3M) beating Zacks consensus by ~40% · USDC in circulation $77.0B (+28% YoY), onchain volume $21.5T (+263% YoY) · full-year 2026 guidance reaffirmed (unchanged). Beat on the bottom line, miss on the top line; net income down YoY on higher opex. This is the company's LATEST reported period — the Standard Chartered/USDC minting item itself is a partnership announcement, not a results event.

**Key figures (Q1'26 vs Q1'25, from Circle's own 10-Q / Q1'26 press release / 8-K).**
- Total revenue and reserve income: $694M (10-Q: "grew 20% to $694 million").
- Reserve income: $653M / $652.5M, +17.0% YoY, primarily from +39% growth in average USDC in circulation, partially offset by a 66 bps decline in the reserve return rate (3.5% vs 4.2% prior year). Reserve income = ~94% of total revenue.
- Other revenue: $42M, up $21M YoY (roughly doubled).
- Distribution, transaction and other costs: $407M (10-Q shows distribution and transaction costs $405M), +17% YoY, on increased distribution payments.
- Operating expenses: $242M, +76% YoY, driven by higher compensation from post-IPO stock-based compensation.
- Revenue less distribution cost (RLDC) margin: 41.4%, +1.5pp YoY (+1.3pp QoQ).
- Net income from continuing operations: -15% YoY; net income attributable to common stockholders $55.3M (10-Q: $55,253k vs $64,791k prior year, -14.7%). GAAP diluted EPS $0.23.
- Adjusted EBITDA: $151M, +24% YoY.

**By segment / driver.** Growth is overwhelmingly reserve-income-driven (~94% of the top line), which is a function of USDC balances × the reserve return rate — and the rate fell 66 bps YoY (3.5%), so revenue grew slower (+20%) than average circulation (+39%). Other revenue nearly doubled ($21M → $42M), a small but faster-growing non-rate-sensitive line. The standout operating metric is onchain transaction volume at $21.5T (+263% YoY) — velocity/adoption running far ahead of the balance sheet. The margin drag is opex (+76% YoY, mostly SBC), which is why net income fell despite 20% revenue growth.

**vs expectations / prior period.** Revenue $694M missed public (Zacks) consensus of ~$715M by ~$21M / ~-2.9%; EPS $0.23 beat the Zacks estimate by ~40%. The revenue miss is rate-driven (lower reserve return rate), not volume — circulation and transaction volume both grew strongly; the EPS beat reflects cost/tax lines below the revenue line. YoY net income is DOWN (-14.7%) on post-IPO SBC. Against the corpus trend: this follows a rough patch for the stock — see [[This Week in Fintech Circle shares fall most ever on stablecoin move]] and [[Circle shares fall over 20% on Clarity Act stablecoin yield threat]] (April 2026), both flagging the yield/rate-sensitivity risk that this print confirms in the reserve return rate decline.

**Guidance / forward.** Management AFFIRMED (unchanged) prior full-year 2026 guidance on key KPIs: USDC in circulation ~40% multi-year through-cycle CAGR; Other Revenue FY26 $150-170M; RLDC margin FY26 38-40%; Adjusted OpEx FY26 $570-585M. Explicitly excludes future financial impacts of the ARC Token presale, Arc incentive programs, and future Arc revenue. No numeric revenue guide raised or cut. Tone (CEO Jeremy Allaire, CFO Jeremy Fox-Geen): confident, emphasizing USDC becoming "the most widely used digital asset onchain," AI/economic-operating-system convergence, and the $222M ARC token presale (~$3B network valuation, backers include a16z, BlackRock, ICE, and Standard Chartered Ventures). Note who's quieter: the reserve return rate decline is acknowledged but not dwelt on, and the 76% opex jump is framed as post-IPO SBC rather than run-rate cost growth.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. Rate sensitivity is the core thesis risk and it is now visible in results: reserve return rate 3.5% (down 66 bps YoY) meant revenue (+20%) badly lagged average circulation (+39%). Fact → the top line is a function of Fed rates, not just adoption → any further rate cuts compress revenue even if USDC keeps growing → second-order: strengthens the strategic logic of distribution deals like the Standard Chartered/USDC minting partnership (grow circulation and non-rate "other revenue" to dilute rate dependence).
2. Opex +76% YoY (mostly SBC) drove net income DOWN -14.7% despite 20% revenue growth. Why it matters: the market rewards Circle as a growth story, but GAAP profitability is going backwards on comp → watch whether SBC normalizes or whether this is structural cost growth.
3. Volume decoupling from balances: onchain transaction volume +263% ($21.5T) vs circulation +28%. Adoption/velocity is real and accelerating, but Circle largely monetizes balances (reserves), not transaction velocity — so this growth doesn't yet convert to revenue. The Arc/ARC token and CPN (payment network) build-out is the attempt to monetize that flow; guidance explicitly excludes it, so upside is unquantified.
4. Guidance reaffirmed, not raised — despite an EPS beat. A "hold" rather than a "raise" signals management sees rate headwinds offsetting adoption tailwinds through FY26.
