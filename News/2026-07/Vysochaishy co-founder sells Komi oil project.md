 Сооснователь «Высочайшего» продает нефтяной проект в Коми 
 Сооснователь «Высочайшего» продает нефтяной проект в Коми 
Один из основателей золотодобытчика «Высочайший» (GV Gold) Сергей Докучаев и экс-замминистра энергетики Валерий Гарипов ищут покупателей на компании с лицензиями на нефтяные участки в Коми. Объявление о продаже опубликовано на площадке Nedraru в июле.
Господа Докучаев и Гарипов владеют по 50% в ООО «Нефтегеосервис» и ООО НК «Севергеофизика», которым принадлежат лицензии на Ермоловский и Висовский лицензионные участки соответственно. Прогнозные ресурсы по категории D0 (подготовленные) — 7,36 млн тонн и 11,13 млн тонн нефти. Срок действия лицензий — до конца 2028 года. Стоимость долей в двух компаниях — $30 млн, «Нефтегеосервис» оценен в $13 млн, НК «Севергеофизика» — в $17 млн.
Представитель продавцов сообщил “ Коммерсанту ”, что доли в компаниях продаются в целях привлечения инвестора для реализации проекта в связи с высокой стоимостью банковского финансирования. С учетом ресурсной базы и инфраструктурной доступности объекты перспективны для быстрого ввода в эксплуатацию, добавили там.
Как сказано в объявлении о продаже, по участкам проходит железная дорога, ЛЭП, нефтепровод «Транснефти», круглогодичные и сезонные дороги, в 50–70 км в Ухте есть НПЗ ЛУКОЙЛа. У Ермоловского участка высокая изученность сейсморазведки, пробурено пять поисковых скважин. У Висовского изученность высокая, но неравномерная, в 2009 году работы были приостановлены из-за отсутствия финансирования, пробурено семь скважин.
В 2006 году доли в «Нефтегеосервисе» и НК «Севергеофизика» приобрела польская Petrolinvest Рышарда Краузе, которого в 2008 году Forbes включил в рейтинг миллиардеров Восточной Европы с состоянием $1,3 млрд. Но до начала добычи дело так и не дошло. Впоследствии компания вышла из этих активов. Petrolinvest также вела разведку на нефтяных месторождениях в Казахстане, но бизнес в этой стране тоже оказался неуспешным. В 2021 году Petrolinvest подала заявление о банкротстве.
По последним данным, на 2022 год Сергей Докучаев владел около 20% «Высочайшего», который входит в топ-10 золотодобывающих компаний в России. Объединяет семь фабрик с общей мощностью до 10,5 млн тонн руды в год, доказанные и вероятные (P&P) запасы по JORC — 5,3 млн унций. Объем производства в 2025 году — 241 тыс. тонн унций. Согласно СПАРК, в «Нефтегеосервисе» и НК «Севергеофизика» господин Докучаев получил по 10% в 2009 году, а в 2023 году увеличил свои пакеты до 50%.
Руководитель проектов Strategy Partners Анна Васильева предупреждает, что ряд факторов может осложнить продажу.
Главный из них — высокая стоимость заемного финансирования: при текущих ставках инвестиции в разведку становятся невыгодными, что продавец называет причиной выхода из проекта, указывает эксперт. Кроме того, продолжает она, ресурсы оценены лишь по прогнозной категории D0, что подразумевает высокую геологическую неопределенность и необходимость дополнительных вложений в сейсморазведку и бурение разведочных скважин.
Олег Жирнов, партнер направления инвестиций и рынков капитала Kept, говорит, что на рынке присутствует ряд схожих активов для продажи. Но, подчеркивает аналитик, учитывая высокий уровень ставок и неопределенность на мировом рынке нефти, инвесторам интересны в основном активы с понятной геологией, высоким подтверждением запасов, относительной простотой добычи и доступом к логистике и сбыту.

# Context-enrichment: Vysochaishy co-founder sells Komi oil project
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Sergey Dokuchaev — a co-founder and ~20% shareholder of gold miner GV Gold («Высочайший», top-10 RU gold producer) and chairman of Lanta-Bank — together with ex-deputy energy minister **Valery Garipov** has put his **Komi oil exploration assets up for sale**, via a listing on the Nedra.ru marketplace in July 2026. The two men each hold 50% of **OOO «Нефтегеосервис»** (Ermolovsky licence area) and **OOO NK «Севергеофизика»** (Visovsky area). Asking price: **$30M total** ($13M Neftegeoservice + $17M Severgeofizika). Licences run to end-2028.
- **What this really is (de-PR):** This is **not a company M&A / not a GV Gold deal** — it is a personal-portfolio, side-asset disposal of two **exploration-stage, pre-production** licence vehicles. The "resources" are **7.36 Mt + 11.13 Mt category D0** — the *least-certain* Russian resource class (traps merely "prepared for drilling," not confirmed reserves). No production, no confirmed (C-category) reserves, no cash flow. So $30M is an **asking price for optionality on undrilled geology**, not a valuation of a producing field.
- **Why framed this way / why sell now:** The sellers' stated reason — "attracting an investor because bank financing is too expensive" — is the honest core. At current CBR rates, funding exploration capex on D0 geology is uneconomic; the seller is effectively **transferring drilling risk + capex burden** rather than monetizing value. The "sale to attract an investor" wording is a soft way of saying *they don't want to fund the exploration themselves.* Independent experts (Strategy Partners' Vasilieva, Kept's Zhirnov) flagged exactly this: high rates + D0 uncertainty + a market already full of similar assets → hard sale.
- **History flag:** These same licences were owned in 2006 by Poland's **Petrolinvest (Ryszard Krauze)**, who never reached production and exited; Petrolinvest went bankrupt in 2021. Dokuchaev got 10% each in 2009 and raised to 50% in 2023. So the assets have **already failed to reach production under one prior owner** — a ~20-year dormant exploration story.

## [1] Competitors / peers (RU asset-reshuffle corpus)
Not a fintech/tech deal — it sits in the **Russian resource/real-asset reshuffling** flow this vault tracks via Mergers.ru:
- [[Bashkiria sells part of Bashneft stake for 14.8bn rubles]] — regional oil-stake disposal (to Rosneft, with buyback) — closest oil & gas comp, but that's a *producing* asset vs. this exploration option.
- [[VTB to sell former Otkritie office to Wildberries-Russ]], [[Belaya Skala buys 47.2bn ruble asset portfolio]] — RU illiquid-asset disposals into domestic buyers under a closed, sanction-era market.
- [[Pharmacy chain 36.6 buys pharma retailer Trika]], [[Pharmasyntez acquires 70% of dialysis-solution maker Rester]] — same Mergers.ru domestic-consolidation stream.
- **Second-order:** Kept's Zhirnov notes several *similar exploration assets are already on the market* → this is a **buyer's market for undrilled Russian geology**; only assets with confirmed reserves + easy logistics + offtake are moving. Dokuchaev's asset has decent logistics (rail, Transneft pipeline, ЛУКОЙЛ Ukhta refinery 50–70 km) but the geology is unproven → likely a **discount / slow sale**, if it clears at all.

## [2] Company history / fit
GV Gold itself has a **stalled-liquidity backstory** relevant to *why a co-founder is trimming a side asset now*: the 2021 Moscow IPO ($500M target, ~91–113bn ₽ valuation) was **pulled**; the follow-on **UGMK acquisition (2021–22) collapsed on price**; BlackRock funds held ~18% pre-sanctions. Post-2022 the founders (Dokuchaev/Opaleva/Tikhonov ~20% each) sit on an **illiquid, exit-blocked** gold stake. (analysis) Against that backdrop, offloading a non-core, cash-burning Komi exploration option is a rational **personal de-risking / liquidity** move, not a strategic pivot. The oil assets were never operationally integrated with GV Gold — purely Dokuchaev's private venture.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Low novelty, no traction.** Nothing new was created or launched — a **20-year-dormant, twice-shelved exploration licence** (Visovsky work halted in 2009 for lack of funding; 12 exploration wells total across both areas) is simply being re-listed. No confirmed reserves, no production, no signed buyer, no price agreed — only a $30M ask.
- **Where value could be (analysis):** infrastructure adjacency (rail/pipeline/refinery) is the one real plus — it lowers a future developer's build-out cost. But value capture depends entirely on someone else funding seismic + appraisal drilling to convert D0 → bookable reserves, at a time when that capex is the *exact thing* rate levels make uneconomic. So the value-add is **contingent and unfunded**, not realized.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Base case (analysis): **slow, discounted, or unsuccessful sale.** Both cited experts are skeptical (rate environment, D0 uncertainty, crowded market for similar assets, global oil-price uncertainty). A buyer, if any, is likely a domestic player able to carry exploration risk cheaply or with strategic logistics interest (an Ukhta-region operator / ЛУКОЙЛ-adjacent). Regulatory clock: licences expire end-2028 — a buyer must move fast or risk relicensing. **No sanctions angle** on the asset itself; the broader context is sanction-era capital immobility pushing RU HNW owners to shed non-core, capital-hungry side assets.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is Russian small/mid upstream E&P M&A, not fintech — two Komi Republic exploration LLCs (Neftegeoservis / Ermolovsky block, Severgeofizika / Visovsky block, Timan-Pechora province) put up for sale by their private owners for a combined $30M. Sector size: Russia's oil & gas market is ~$46.9bn (2025) rising to ~$48.4bn (2026) (per Mordor Intelligence, unverified secondary — treat as order-of-magnitude, not audited). Structure: highly consolidated at the top (Rosneft, Lukoil, Gazprom Neft, Surgutneftegas dominate production), but a long tail of sub-scale exploration licenses held by private individuals — this asset sits in that fragmented tail. Barriers: capital-intensive (seismic + exploration drilling), long license/permit cycle, and now sanctions cut off Western tech and foreign buyers. Why now (per the note itself, confirmed): the seller cites the high cost of bank financing at current CBR rates as the reason to exit — exploration capex no longer pencils out, so the owners seek an equity investor rather than continue self-funding. Macro overlay (analysis): Urals fell from ~$68/bbl (Jan-25) to ~$54 (Oct-25) with discounts widening to ~30% after Nov-25 US sanctions on Rosneft/Lukoil (per CREA/Kpler, via web) — a weak price deck compounds the case against funding greenfield exploration.

**Competitive landscape.** KPIs for an asset like this: (1) resource confidence category, (2) $ per tonne / per barrel in-ground, (3) infrastructure access / netback. Basis of competition among sellers of such assets is geological certainty + logistics, not price. Per the note, Oleg Zhirnov (Kept) states there are already several similar assets on the market — i.e. this is a buyer's market for unproven upstream licenses; investors favour assets with "clear geology, high reserve confirmation, ease of production, and access to logistics and offtake." Protagonist's position: niche / disadvantaged. Positives cited: rail, power line, Transneft oil pipeline and roads cross the blocks, with a Lukoil refinery 50–70 km away in Ukhta — genuinely strong infrastructure. Negative: resources are only D0 (prognostic/"prepared") — the softest resource category, implying high geological uncertainty and further seismic + appraisal-drilling spend before any commercial C1/C2 booking. Visovsky work was suspended in 2009 for lack of financing; licenses expire end-2028, a tight runway to prove up and develop. History flag: Poland's Petrolinvest (Ryszard Krauze) owned these same stakes from 2006, never reached production, exited, and filed for bankruptcy in 2021 — the assets have a track record of failing to convert.

**Comps & multiples.** No public EV/EBITDA or P/E — this is a pre-revenue exploration asset, so the only meaningful metric is in-ground $/resource. Arithmetic (analysis): combined D0 resource = 7.36 + 11.13 = 18.49 mln t; ask $30M → ~$1.62 per tonne. In barrels (~7.33 bbl/t): ~$0.24/bbl of D0 prognostic oil. Per-asset: Neftegeoservis $13M / 7.36 Mt = $1.77/t; Severgeofizika $17M / 11.13 Mt = $1.53/t (the larger, cheaper-per-tonne block, consistent with its "high but uneven" seismic coverage). Sanity: $/bbl in the low tens of cents is extremely low vs producing-asset transaction metrics (typically several $/bbl of 2P), but that is *expected* for undrilled-to-commerciality D0 resource with 100% appraisal risk — cheap headline, but the discount reflects risk, not a bargain. Internal comps (in-base): [[Bashkiria sells part of Bashneft stake for 14.8bn rubles]] — Russian oil-asset M&A, but a large producing/state name (Rosneft buyer, republic seller with buyback) — not comparable on multiples, only as a "Russian oil M&A stays alive under sanctions" data point; [[Shishkarev drops plan to buy 49% of Delo Group from Rosatom]] and [[MTS sells 49.9% stake in its tower company]] — non-oil, but same 2026 pattern of Russian private/state owners reshuffling infra stakes under high-rate, capital-constrained conditions. No comparable small-upstream deal in the base → distribution not computed; qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Resource-confidence risk (geological).** D0 is the softest category — no commercial flows demonstrated; a buyer inherits full appraisal risk (seismic + exploration wells) before any reserves book, and Visovsky drilling has sat idle since 2009. Second-order: the $30M ask is really a call option on future drilling success, not asset value in the ground.
2. **License-clock + financing squeeze.** Licenses lapse end-2028 while high CBR rates make exploration debt uneconomic (the seller's own stated exit reason). Second-order: any buyer needs equity, not debt, and must prove-up fast or risk license loss — narrows the buyer pool to well-capitalised strategics.
3. **Disclosure / negative-precedent gap.** Valuation is a seller's asking price on a marketplace (Nedra.ru), not a closed transaction or independent audit; and prior owner Petrolinvest held the identical assets, never produced, and went bankrupt — a documented failure to convert that the sale framing omits.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) A test of whether sanctions-era Russia still has a functioning market for sub-scale greenfield upstream: at low oil prices and ~20%+ funding costs, unproven exploration licenses are structurally the hardest thing to sell (Kept notes several are already listed). Falsifiable thesis: this closes only at a steep discount to the $30M ask, or not at all before end-2028, unless a Lukoil-type strategic with local Ukhta infrastructure absorbs it as a bolt-on. Trigger to watch: identity of any buyer (strategic vs financial) and the closing price vs ask; a financial buyer at/near $30M would falsify the "buyer's market / distressed" read.
