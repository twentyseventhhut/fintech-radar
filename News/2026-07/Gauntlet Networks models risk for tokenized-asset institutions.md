## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** The note frames Gauntlet as the risk-modelling layer under **institutions using tokenized assets, stablecoins and DeFi** — i.e. the RWA-tokenization + institutional-DeFi-risk-infrastructure stack. Sizing (measurement varies by methodology, treat as directional): on-chain **RWA (ex-stablecoins) reached ~$26–31bn** by early/mid-2026 — ~$26bn (Mar 2026, defiprime) to ~$31bn (Jul 2026, rwa.xyz, +400% since early-2025, ~960k holders across 167 platforms); Treasuries ~$10bn and private credit ~$8bn are the two largest buckets, with six categories now each >$1bn (per rwa.xyz). Long-run forecasts are promotional and wide (BCG/Ripple $18.9tn by 2033; Standard Chartered $30tn by 2034) — cite as aspiration, not base case. Structure: **two-layer** — a thin, immutable lending/settlement base (Morpho Blue, isolated per-asset markets) with a discretionary **risk/curation layer on top** (Gauntlet, Steakhouse, Block Analitica, Bitwise); value in the risk layer accrues to model credibility + institutional distribution, not to the rails. Why now: post-GENIUS/CLARITY US regulatory clarity + TradFi entrants (Apollo, Ondo, Circle, SBI) pushing tokenized Treasuries/credit on-chain as collateral, which creates demand for a professional underwriting layer these institutions can't build in-house.

**Competitive landscape.** KPIs: assets-under-curation / vault TVL, performance-fee take, and — the one that matters most for a "risk" brand — **drawdown / bad-debt track record**. Gauntlet earns primarily **performance fees (% of vault yield)** plus bespoke B2B mandates (fee model is analyst-described, company undisclosed). Basis of competition = risk-model credibility, not price. Gauntlet's tokenized-asset footprint: **60+ vaults across 6+ chains** (Ethereum, Base, Katana, Arbitrum…), curating **~$1.2bn on Morpho** while underwriting **RWA allocations from Apollo and Ondo** (per Morpho, Jun 2026) — the clearest evidence it is positioned specifically for the *tokenized-asset-as-collateral* use case, not just crypto-native yield. Direct risk-management rival **Chaos Labs**: **$75m raised total** (Seed $20m Feb-2023: Galaxy/Coinbase Ventures/PayPal Ventures/Lightspeed/Bessemer; **Series A $55m Aug-2024 led by Haun Ventures**), post-money valuation not disclosed; distribution via Kraken DeFi Earn (Jan-2026). Curation rivals **Steakhouse Financial** (largest on Morpho, ~53% of Morpho stablecoin TVL, bootstrapped), Block Analitica, Bitwise, Re7. Position: **co-leader** of the curation layer; moat `(analysis)` = 2018-vintage simulation IP + an institutional roster (Apollo, Ondo, Coinbase, Circle) that raises switching costs for RWA underwriting — though vault curation itself is increasingly commoditised.

**Comps & multiples.** Series C: **$125m, SBI Holdings sole investor, closed Jun-2026** — ~5x the prior round; **post-money valuation NOT disclosed** (Chitra declined) → no multiple computable, and no verified revenue/AUM denominator exists (the ">$1.5bn supplied assets" / "$42–48bn monitored" figures are self-reported marketing, not audited). Prior mark: **Series B $23.8m at $1.0bn (2022-03, Ribbit/Paradigm/Polychain)**; cumulative raised ~$169m. Internal anchors for the rails/RWA it depends on:
- [[Morpho raises $175M for onchain credit infrastructure]] — ~$2.0bn valuation (2026-06); the base layer Gauntlet curates on.
- [[Kraken in talks to buy 15% Aave stake at $385M]] — Aave marked ~$385M (2026-06).
- [[Blockworks raises Series A extension at $192 million valuation]] — on-chain capital-markets data infra, $192M (2026-05).
- Chaos Labs $55m Series A (2024) — valuation no data.
Verdict: **not computable / no data** — do not infer a multiple; headline round size ≠ valuation and undisclosed = possibly flat/down vs the 2022 $1bn mark.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Platform concentration on Morpho.** The tokenized-asset business runs largely through one host protocol (~$1.2–1.4bn of TVL on Morpho); Gauntlet's revenue and its RWA-underwriting relationships ride one protocol's health and one governance regime — the same single-host dependency that ended its Aave mandate.
2. **Client-relationship fragility (hard precedent).** Gauntlet **terminated its multi-year Aave "Risk Steward" mandate in Feb-2024** over DAO dysfunction and fee compression, then defected to Morpho within a week — direct evidence that risk mandates are low-switching-cost and price-competitive, undercutting the "sticky institutional infrastructure" framing.
3. **Underwriting-loss / fiduciary exposure on tokenized assets.** As curator it takes **discretionary risk on RWA collateral (Apollo/Ondo private credit, Treasuries) for institutional capital**; a single bad-debt or depeg event on a curated vault impairs both AUM and the entire "risk manager" brand at once — and tokenized private credit carries off-chain default/valuation risk the on-chain model can't fully observe. The "non-custodial" framing may not insulate against fiduciary/regulatory questions after a loss.
4. **TVL cyclicality + strategic-investor concentration.** Performance-fee revenue scales with vault TVL/yield in a cyclical market; separately, a $125m round from a **single strategic (SBI, deeply tied to Ripple/XRP/RLUSD)** risks narrowing Gauntlet's neutral risk-manager positioning toward SBI/XRPL ecosystems and may signal generalist crypto VCs passed.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` Institutional capital (SBI) funding a **dedicated risk/underwriting layer for tokenized assets** validates that "who underwrites the RWA collateral" is becoming an investable slice of on-chain finance, distinct from the rails and the assets themselves. Falsifiable thesis: the curation/underwriting layer consolidates into 2–3 credible names (Gauntlet, Steakhouse, Chaos) capturing outsized fee share as tokenized Treasuries/credit scale as DeFi collateral. Trigger to watch: whether Gauntlet ships the promised JPY/MXN stablecoin vaults and diversifies host protocols beyond Morpho. Thesis breaks if a curator loss event on tokenized RWA collateral — or an Aave-style fee-compression defection — commoditises the layer.
