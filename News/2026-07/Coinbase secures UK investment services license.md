# Context-enrichment: Coinbase secures UK investment services license
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 7 Jul 2026 Coinbase's UK head Keith Grose announced that Coinbase's UK entity has been granted **investment-services authorisation by the FCA** — the UK's post-Brexit equivalent of a MiFID investment-firm permission. Coinbase itself titled the blog "Coinbase Obtains MiFID License in the United Kingdom". This sits **on top of** two permissions Coinbase already held in the UK: an **e-money licence** and a **crypto-asset (AML) registration** (the FCA registration secured Feb 2025). Coinbase frames the stack as making it "the most comprehensively regulated crypto player" in Britain.

What it unlocks (de-PR'd, and note the retail/institutional split):
- **Retail:** for the first time, UK users will be able to **trade equities (stocks)** on Coinbase. This is the genuinely new, consequential change for everyday users.
- **Institutional / advanced traders:** access to **perpetual futures across crypto, equities and commodities** (leveraged/synthetic exposure).
- **Caveat the PR skips:** the FCA still **bars the sale of crypto derivatives to UK retail** investors — so crypto perps remain institutional-only in the UK. For retail, equities are effectively the only new product. (analysis)

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** This is an *authorisation*, i.e. a permission to offer products — not a product launch. No live user numbers, no launch date for retail equities, no AUM. It is a regulatory milestone, not adoption. The reason Coinbase went the investment-firm route rather than waiting is timing: the FCA's **dedicated crypto regime takes legal effect ~Oct 2027** (with the authorisation window opening late 2026). By securing an *existing* MiFID-style permission now, Coinbase can sell regulated **equities and derivatives ~15+ months before** the bespoke crypto regime arrives — building product breadth and market share while rivals are still preparing crypto-regime applications. The equities/TradFi wrapper is regulatorily *available now*; the crypto wrapper is not yet.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Kraken** — launched **tokenised equity perpetual futures for non-US traders** (2025); also listed a tokenised GBP stablecoin from BCP in the UK (Nov 2025). Kraken/Payward built a CFTC-licensed derivatives stack (Bitnomial acquisition, 2026). Kraken is the closest "everything exchange" rival on equities-via-crypto-rails.
- **Binance** — offers equity perpetual contracts globally, but is on the back foot in Europe/UK (MiCA licence miss, Greek withdrawal, user losses per Jul-2026 corpus notes; also faces a £150m UK investor suit). Regulatory weakness = opening for Coinbase in the UK. (analysis)
- **Bybit** — relaunched in the UK (Dec 2025) but explicitly **not FCA-authorised**; operates spot-only via Archax's financial-promotions approval. This is the key contrast: Coinbase is now *directly authorised*, whereas Bybit (and previously Coinbase itself, and OKX) accessed the UK market *through Archax without their own authorisation*.
- **Revolut / eToro** — the real retail-equities competition. eToro already offers UK stock/ETF trading + Cash ISA; Revolut offers stocks and is testing UK stablecoins in the FCA sandbox. These incumbents already have millions of UK retail investing users. Coinbase enters equities as a **challenger, catching up** on TradFi breadth — its edge is the crypto-native base and unified UX, not a first-mover position in UK stock trading.

**Position:** *ahead* of pure-crypto rivals on UK regulatory depth (Bybit/Binance/OKX); *catching up* to established UK equity brokers (eToro/Revolut/Trading 212).

**Second-order:** the moat is regulatory permission + a unified multi-asset app, not the equities product itself (stock trading is commoditised in the UK). The question is whether Coinbase can convert its ~UK crypto base into equity users, i.e. cross-sell, before eToro/Revolut add deeper crypto. (analysis)

## [2] Company history / fit
Coinbase's UK trajectory: **FCA crypto registration (Feb 2025)** → **DEX trading + savings via ClearBank (Nov 2025, 3.75% AER, FSCS-protected)** → **crypto-backed lending via Morpho + fiat-backed tGBP stablecoin + DEX (Apr 2026)** → **investment-services authorisation (Jul 2026)**. See [[Coinbase launches UK savings account via ClearBank]], [[Coinbase launches crypto-backed lending for UK users]], [[Coinbase launches fiat-backed stablecoin tGBP in UK]], [[Bybit relaunches UK crypto platform after two-year gap]] (Archax route contrast), [[Coinbase CEO warns on UK stablecoin regulatory risks]].

Globally this fits the **"Everything Exchange"** strategy: US 24/5 stock trading + crypto/stock perps (via its MiFID entity, launched to 26 EU countries Mar 2026 — see [[Coinbase launches 24 7 stock perpetual futures]]), Kalshi prediction markets, Australia AFSL expansion (perps + equities — see [[Coinbase plans Australia expansion after securing licence]]). The UK authorisation is the same playbook applied to a major market.

**Why Coinbase acts this way:** structural. Coinbase's revenue is dominated by volatile crypto **transaction take-rate** — it posted a steep Q1-2026 loss with falling revenue (see [[Coinbase posts steep Q1 2026 loss as revenue falls]]) and cut ~700 jobs. To earn a durable, diversified revenue base (and a software/multi-asset-brokerage multiple rather than a crypto-cyclical one), it must expand beyond spot crypto into equities, derivatives, payments and savings. The UK — one of the largest, most sophisticated financial markets, ~8% (5.5m) crypto-owning adults — is a priority beachhead.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**What is genuinely new:** direct FCA authorisation for equities/derivatives in the UK — a real regulatory upgrade from the prior Archax-gateway/promotions-only footing. Retail equities on Coinbase in the UK is a new capability.

**What is NOT new:** the *products themselves*. Coinbase already runs US 24/5 stock trading and crypto/stock **perps in the US and 26 EU countries** under its MiFID entity (Mar 2026). So this is **geographic extension of an existing product suite into the UK under a UK permission**, not a new product line.

**Traction check:** none yet — this is an authorisation. No UK retail-equities launch date, no user/volume figures were disclosed. "Permission to offer" ≠ "live and adopted." The value-add is real but **prospective**.

**Who captures the margin:** commission-free equities is a loss-leader; the economics come from payment-for-order-flow-style spreads, FX, derivatives fees (institutional perps), staking/lending, and cross-sell into Coinbase One subscription. The durable question is whether Coinbase can build a **fee-diversified UK book** vs. remaining a crypto-transaction shop with a stock tab. (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Expect a phased rollout: institutional crypto/equity/commodity perps first, retail equities to follow (mirroring the Australia sequencing). Watch for an actual retail-equities go-live date and pricing.

**Regulatory backdrop:** the FCA is warming to crypto (crypto regime effective ~Oct 2027; stablecoin sandbox cohorts; property-status reforms) after the restrictive Oct-2023 promotions rules that pushed Bybit/OKX out. Coinbase is positioning to be the best-regulated incumbent when the bespoke regime lands — a **regulatory land-grab** ahead of competitors. Note the tension: Brian Armstrong has publicly criticised proposed UK stablecoin holding limits ([[Coinbase CEO warns on UK stablecoin regulatory risks]]), so Coinbase is simultaneously courting and lobbying the FCA.

**Second-order:** if Coinbase converts UK crypto users into a multi-asset investing base ahead of the 2027 regime, it entrenches before rivals; if retail equities stalls (as a me-too product against eToro/Revolut), the authorisation is a defensive checkbox rather than a growth driver. The weight of this item rests on execution/adoption still to come.

## Market Research

**Sector & drivers.** The event is a UK **MiFID-style investment-services authorisation** for Coinbase's UK entity — permitting equities (retail) and crypto/equity/commodity **perpetual futures** (institutional/advanced only) — *not* a licence under the future UK crypto regime. Two distinct UK regulatory tracks matter: (1) the AML-only **FCA cryptoasset register** (Coinbase already holds this, plus a UK e-money licence), and (2) the FCA's new comprehensive cryptoasset regime — final rules published **30 June 2026**, authorisation window **30 Sep 2026 – 28 Feb 2027**, mandatory from **25 Oct 2027** (per FCA, fca.org.uk). By taking a *securities* (MiFID) permission now, Coinbase can offer regulated stocks/derivatives ~15 months before the crypto rulebook binds — a deliberate "front-run the perimeter" move. Structural barrier: the FCA still **bans retail crypto derivatives** (in force since 2021), so crypto perps here are institutional/advanced-only; retail gets equities. "Why now": the UK is repositioning as a global crypto hub while Coinbase pursues its "everything exchange" strategy (crypto + equities + derivatives + stablecoin savings/borrowing on one platform). (coindesk.com; fca.org.uk, 30 Jun 2026)

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs for an exchange: trading volume & **spot/derivatives market share**, transaction take-rate, MTUs, subscription-and-services (S&S) revenue mix. Basis of competition here = **breadth of regulated product** (crypto → equities → derivatives) + distribution + balance sheet, not price. Key UK peers and their permissions:
- **Kraken** — FCA-registered cryptoasset firm **+ FCA e-money licence + FCA Investment Firm** (via subsidiary Crypto Facilities, whose MTF already runs crypto derivatives for professional clients); MiFID derivatives passporting via **CySEC (Cyprus)**; tokenised-equity push with Nasdaq/Deutsche Börse. Kraken is arguably *ahead* on the regulated-derivatives axis in the UK. (kraken.com; readwrite.com)
- **Revolut** — **Revolut Trading Ltd** secured FCA approval (May 2026) to expand UK investment services; holds FCA e-money authorisation. Direct competitor on the retail equities + crypto super-app axis. (fintech.global, 14 May 2026)
- **Gemini, Bitstamp** — FCA cryptoasset-registered for crypto; no comparable UK MiFID equities/derivatives build surfaced.

Protagonist's position: **catching up on regulated derivatives** (Kraken's Crypto Facilities MTF predates this), but Coinbase's edge is a US-listed balance sheet, brand, and the widest single-platform product stack. Moat = **regulatory-licence stack + scale** (analysis); the equities+perps addition raises switching costs for a multi-asset UK user.
