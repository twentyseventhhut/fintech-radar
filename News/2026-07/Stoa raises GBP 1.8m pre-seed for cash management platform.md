## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** London-based fintech **Stoa** raised **£1.8m (~$2.4m / ~€2.1m) in pre-seed funding** to scale its cash-management platform, which turns idle consumer and SME deposits into *upfront* lifestyle/business perks rather than traditional interest. Announced 2026-07-06. Source: [Connecting the Dots](https://www.connectingthedotsinfin.tech/r/6672b635); confirmed by [Finextra](https://www.finextra.com/newsarticle/48048/uk-savings-fintech-stoa-raises-18-million), [UKTN](https://www.uktech.news/fintech/stoa-raises-1-8m-to-scale-cash-management-platform-20260706), [Tech.eu](https://tech.eu/2026/07/06/stoa-secures-24m-for-cash-rewards-platform/), [FinTech Global](https://fintech.global/2026/07/06/stoa-lands-2-4m-to-reward-savers-with-upfront-perks/), [Finsmes](https://www.finsmes.com/2026/07/stoa-raises-2-4m-in-pre-seed-funding.html).

**[1] Who / round.** Co-led by **Bespokeist Partners** and **Ingenii Capital**; participation from **Force Over Mass Capital** and **Fuel Ventures**, plus fintech angels including **Suneel Hargunani** (ex-Citi) and **Rachel Sestini** (Shaw Gibbs partner, Canopy Capital co-founder). Founders: **Mike Saraswat (CEO)** and **Sam Goodenough (CTO)**; company founded 2022. Team pulled from Octopus Investments, Starling Bank, Vodafone, Oracle, Nationwide, Lloyds, Three UK. **John Mountain** (ex-CEO/co-founder of Starling Bank) joined as advisor. Sources: [Tech.eu](https://tech.eu/2026/07/06/stoa-secures-24m-for-cash-rewards-platform/), [The Fintech Times](https://thefintechtimes.com/uk-startup-stoa-launches-to-turn-savings-into-lifestyle-rewards-like-netflix-and-flights/).

**[2] Product / model.** Customers place funds into fixed-term **"Stoa Pots"** and receive **upfront rewards/perks from partner brands** (150+ benefits across 20 categories — Airbnb, Apple, Netflix, Strava, Waitrose, airlines) instead of, or alongside, interest. Deposits are held with **regulated banking partners** and eligible funds are **FSCS-protected**. Model combines behavioural finance + embedded-banking infra + merchant partnerships, connecting banks, brands and customers. Live in UK for consumers and businesses.

**[3] Why it matters / TAM.** Stoa cites **£600bn+** in low-/non-interest-bearing UK consumer accounts and **£250bn+** in UK SME cash reserves; US expansion (SME-only) targets a claimed **"$1 trillion idle cash opportunity across 35m US businesses."** The upfront-perks framing is a differentiated wedge into a crowded "yield-on-cash / cash-management" space — trading deferred interest for immediate merchant value (funded presumably by rate spread + merchant/affiliate economics).

**[4] Disambiguation.** This "Stoa" is the **UK consumer/SME cash-rewards fintech** — distinct from other companies named Stoa (e.g. the crypto/DeFi "Stoa Network", and various property/edtech ventures). Confirmed via multiple UK fintech trade outlets tied to the 2026-07-06 pre-seed.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
### Sector context — cash / treasury management & yield-on-cash

The "put idle cash to work" theme spans two adjacent markets: (1) **consumer/SME cash-rewards & savings** (Stoa's wedge), and (2) **corporate treasury / cash management** (money-market funds, T-bill access, forecasting). Stoa is unusual in swapping *deferred interest* for *upfront merchant perks* — a behavioural-finance twist that monetises the rate spread plus merchant/affiliate economics rather than passing yield through as APY.

**In-base comparables:**
- [[U.S. Bank launches AI-driven cash forecasting tool]] — incumbent-bank corporate-treasury forecasting; different segment (large corporates) but same "optimise idle cash" thesis.
- [[IG partners Upvest to expand European investment offering]] — embedded-investment infrastructure, adjacent to Stoa's embedded-banking/partner model.
- [[Two European fintech deals raise €9.8 million in a week]] — European early-stage funding backdrop; £1.8m pre-seed is small even by that bar.

**External competitor landscape (WebSearch, Jul 2026):**
- **TreasurySpring** — fixed-term fund (FTF) platform giving corporates/SMEs T-bill & bank-deposit access; a pure-yield UK comp to Stoa's SME ambition (but yield-, not perks-based).
- **Mercury Vault / Mercury Treasury** — US SME cash mgmt with expanded FDIC insurance + money-market fund investing (Vanguard/Morgan Stanley). Directly relevant to Stoa's planned US SME push.
- **Brex, Rho, HighRadius, GTreasury, Tesorio, Centime, Nomentia** — broader treasury-management/forecasting software; adjacent, not head-to-head on the perks model.
- Consumer side: UK neobank/savings apps (Chip, Chase UK, Trading 212 cash ISAs, Raisin marketplace) all compete for the same £600bn of idle deposits — but on **rate**, which is exactly the axis Stoa is trying to avoid competing on.

Sources: [TreasurySpring](https://treasuryspring.com/), [Brex treasury software](https://www.brex.com/spend-trends/business-banking/treasury-management-software), [Rho](https://www.rho.co/blog/treasury-management-software), [HighRadius](https://www.highradius.com/resources/Blog/best-treasury-management-system/).

### Risk flags (R = 6)

1. **Unit economics unproven.** Upfront perks must be funded from the deposit rate spread + merchant subsidies; if perk value > net spread, the model bleeds. No disclosed take-rate or per-pot economics.
2. **Not a bank / deposit-holder.** Relies on regulated banking partners for FSCS-protected deposits; Stoa itself is an intermediary — margin and control sit partly with partner banks.
3. **Rate-cycle sensitivity.** The whole value prop depends on a positive spread on deposits; a falling-rate environment compresses the pool that funds perks.
4. **Tiny cheque / early stage.** £1.8m pre-seed, company only founded 2022, product just launched — long runway/execution risk before Series A; US expansion is aspirational ("early partnership discussions").
5. **Crowded, incumbent-heavy market.** Competes for idle deposits against neobanks, savings marketplaces (Raisin), and (for SMEs) TreasurySpring/Mercury — several with more capital and existing distribution.
6. **Regulatory/consumer-duty exposure.** UK FCA Consumer Duty scrutiny of "rewards vs. forgone interest" framing — customers may earn *less* than a plain high-yield account; disclosure/suitability risk. Merchant-perk marketing also invites promotional-financial-product rules.

**Verdict:** Differentiated behavioural wedge with a clear large TAM narrative, but pre-seed scale, unproven unit economics, and a rate-dependent model make this a watch-list early-stage bet, not yet a category threat.
