1Kosmos extends Microsoft Entra Verified ID partnership
Sinziana Albu
14 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
1Kosmos has extended its partnership with Microsoft to become an official Entra Verified ID services and solution partner.
The arrangement builds on an existing relationship between the two companies and allows organisations to use verifiable credentials issued through the 1Kosmos platform for remote onboarding and identity verification.
The 1Kosmos platform, integrated with Microsoft Entra Verified ID, is listed on the Microsoft Security Store. The platform carries out identity proofing by verifying government-issued identification documents against a real-time biometric selfie with liveness detection, a process intended to establish digital identities with a defined level of assurance. According to the companies, the approach is designed to address risks such as hiring fraud, synthetic identities, and unauthorised account creation during remote onboarding.
Platform capabilities
The joint offering allows organisations to issue, hold, and verify digital identity credentials. Capabilities described by the companies include:
Verifiable credentials and decentralised identity: privacy-preserving digital identities supported by a decentralised private ledger for tamper-evident data and audit trails.
Remote identity proofing: verification of government-issued identification against biometric data, certified to the ISO/IEC 30107-3 and NIST IAL2/AAL2 standards.
Entra Verified ID integration: identity issuance and verification functioning as an External Authentication Method for Entra ID.
Fraud mitigation: binding of verified identity to individual users through biometric data, aimed at addressing identity theft, synthetic identities, and account takeovers.
Passwordless authentication: phishing-resistant access extended to environments beyond native Entra ID support, including Mac, Linux, VPNs, privileged access management systems, and legacy systems.
Password reset and account recovery: biometric-based self-service options for Entra ID and on-premises Active Directory accounts, intended to reduce the workload on IT service desks.
Passwordless access for restricted environments: extended to kiosks and shared workstations using 1Kosmos 1Key biometric security keys.
 Mike Engle , Chief Strategy Officer for 1Kosmos, said that the partner status reflects the company's focus on identity solutions for employee onboarding, describing the collaboration as a standards-based approach to remote onboarding and digital trust. In addition, Ankur Patel, Partner Group Product Manager at Microsoft, said that partner ecosystems allow customers flexibility in choosing identity solutions, noting that 1Kosmos joins an expanding group of partners supporting identity verification and account recovery.
Context
The partnership sits within a broader market focus on remote identity verification, as organisations manage onboarding processes conducted outside physical offices. Standards such as ISO/IEC 30107-3, which addresses biometric presentation attack detection, and the NIST IAL2/AAL2 identity and authentication assurance levels, are used as reference points for evaluating the strength of remote identity proofing and authentication mechanisms. Integration with Entra Verified ID as an External Authentication Method positions the offering within Microsoft's existing identity infrastructure, allowing organisations already using Entra ID to extend verification and passwordless authentication to additional use cases and environments.

## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
### [0] What happened
1Kosmos announced (PR 2026-07-09; picked up by The Paypers 2026-07-14) that it has **extended its existing partnership with Microsoft** to become an official **Entra Verified ID services and solution partner**. The 1Kosmos platform, integrated with Microsoft Entra Verified ID, is now listed on the **Microsoft Security Store**. The extension lets organisations use **verifiable credentials (VCs)** issued via 1Kosmos for remote onboarding and identity verification, acting as an **External Authentication Method (EAM)** for Entra ID.

### [1] What 1Kosmos is
US identity vendor (BlockID / 1Key platform) combining: document-based identity proofing (government ID vs. live biometric selfie + liveness), **passwordless / FIDO2 authentication**, a **decentralised private ledger** for tamper-evident credential storage, and reusable digital identity wallets. Certified to **ISO/IEC 30107-3** (presentation-attack detection) and **NIST IAL2/AAL2**; holds **FedRAMP High** + Kantara, and won a ~$194.5M Login.gov contract. Raised a **$57M Series B (2025-08)**, total funding ~$72M ([[Identity firm 1Kosmos raises $57 million Series B]]).

### [2] What the "extension" actually adds
Not a brand-new relationship. Prior milestones: BlockID native support for Entra ID (Feb 2025), Azure Marketplace listing, and Entra ID integration as an EAM. This step layers **Verified ID (verifiable-credential issuance/verification)** on top and adds the **Microsoft Security Store** listing. Capabilities cited: VC/decentralised identity, remote identity proofing, fraud mitigation (synthetic identity, account takeover, hiring fraud), passwordless auth extended to Mac/Linux/VPN/PAM/legacy, and biometric password reset/account recovery.

### [3] Market & why it matters
Decentralised-identity / verifiable-credential market ~$2.5–4.9B (2025), projected ~$7.4B (2026), CAGR >50%. Tailwinds: **eIDAS 2.0 / EUDI Wallet** (every EU state must offer a wallet by end-2026; acceptance mandated 2027), rising remote-hire fraud and synthetic/deepfake identity risk. Entra Verified ID is Microsoft's W3C-VC issuance/verification service (did:web); its partner ecosystem is expanding — **authID** achieved Entra Verified ID IDV Partner certification just a week earlier (2026-07-02), so 1Kosmos joins a competitive channel, not an exclusive one.

### [4] Competitive landscape
Identity-verification field: **iDenfy, Veriff, Onfido, Jumio, Sumsub, ID.me, Persona**; workforce/IAM-adjacent: **Okta, Ping Identity** (Ping acquiring Keyless for biometric auth — [[Ping Identity to acquire Keyless for biometric authentication]]); onboarding/KYB: **Trulioo** ([[Trulioo launches credit-decisioning capability for onboarding]]). 1Kosmos differentiates on decentralised wallet + biometrics + FedRAMP High and was rated highest for **workforce use cases** in Gartner's 2025 Critical Capabilities for IDV, but has small overall IDV market share (~0.24%). Related internal: [[iDenfy adds Austria's Handy-Signatur to verification platform]], [[The Paypers banking-grade FIDO authentication closes trust gap]].

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: digital identity / IAM — specifically reusable, IAL2-grade identity proofing + verifiable credentials + passwordless authentication. Sizing splits into two adjacent pools: the broad identity-verification market ~$14.2bn in 2025 → ~$15.8bn in 2026, ~11% CAGR to ~$26.8bn by 2031 (per Mordor Intelligence, as of 2026); and the much smaller but faster decentralised/reusable-identity segment, ~$3bn in 2025 with claimed CAGRs of 50–70% (per GMInsights / secondary reports, as of 2026 — treat these decentralised-identity CAGRs as promotional/`[UNSOURCED]`, ranges disagree wildly $2.6–4.9bn base). Structure: fragmented, with pure-play IDV vendors (Persona, Veriff, Socure, Jumio, Sumsub, Onfido), decentralised/credential specialists (1Kosmos, Dock), and platform incumbents (Microsoft Entra, Okta, Ping, IDEMIA/Thales) competing across a value chain where the durable margin sits in the assurance layer (certified proofing + credential issuance), not commodity doc-scan. Why now: (1) NIST SP 800-63-4 (final 2025) mandates presentation-attack detection and AI-generated-content checks for remote IAL2 — a regulatory tailwind for certified vendors; (2) eIDAS 2.0 requires every EU state to offer an EU Digital Identity Wallet by end-2026, seeding verifiable-credential demand; (3) deepfake/synthetic-identity fraud surge — Sumsub reported attacks up ~180% ([[Sumsub report identity fraud rate falls but attacks surge 180%]]); one bank logged 8,065 deepfake attempts / $347m losses in 8 months (per Biometric Update, Jul 2026). World Bank cites 30–50% onboarding-cost cuts from reusable credentials vs doc-scan (per World Bank, via Security Boulevard, 2026).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: pass/completion rate, fraud-catch (false-accept/reject), assurance certifications (NIST IAL2/AAL2, ISO/IEC 30107-3 PAD, FedRAMP), ARR/NRR. Basis of competition: certifications + distribution (marketplace/channel) more than price. 1Kosmos differentiator = one of few full-service Credential Service Providers holding BOTH FedRAMP High and NIST 800-63-3 certification, plus decentralised-ledger credential issuance — a narrower, assurance-first niche vs high-volume consumer IDV players. Recent peer/ecosystem moves: Ping Identity to acquire Keyless for biometric auth ([[Ping Identity to acquire Keyless for biometric authentication]], Nov 2025); Incode acquired AuthenticID ([[Mexico's Incode acquires AuthenticID for identity verification]], Sep 2025); Persona pushing an "agentic-AI identity layer" thesis (Apr 2025). Position (analysis): niche/catching-up on scale, but well-placed on the certified-reusable-credential wedge; the news itself (Entra Verified ID services partner + Security Store listing) is a distribution moat play — riding Microsoft's installed base rather than head-on IDV competition. Moat = intangibles (certifications, patents) + partner-ecosystem lock-in; switching costs modest.

**Comps & multiples.** Protagonist is PRIVATE, revenue undisclosed → EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E all "no data". Round marker (not market cap): 1Kosmos Series B $57m (incl. $10m Bridge Bank credit line) led by Forgepoint + Oquirrh Ventures, Aug 2025; total funding >$72m (prior $15m Series A); no post-money disclosed → valuation `[UNSOURCED]`; company claims 3x revenue growth (self-reported, unverified). External private peers (last-round valuations, NOT market caps): Persona $2.0bn ($200m Series D, Apr 2025); Socure ~$4.5bn; Veriff ~$1.5bn (Series C, Jan 2022, since profitable 2025). Internal comps (base): [[Ping Identity to acquire Keyless for biometric authentication]], [[Mexico's Incode acquires AuthenticID for identity verification]], [[Socure acquires BNPL data provider Qlarifi]]. Distribution not computed — no comparable revenue denominators disclosed; qualitative read: at >$72m raised and a claimed 3x growth, 1Kosmos is ~1–2 orders of magnitude smaller than the $1.5–4.5bn IDV leaders — a sub-scale specialist, not a valuation outlier. In-line vs stage.

**Risk flags.** (1) Rails dependence: the entire deal value hinges on Microsoft Entra Verified ID as the distribution channel — 1Kosmos supplies the proofing layer, but Microsoft owns the customer and could in-source or favour other Verified-ID partners (Ankur Patel explicitly framed it as an "expanding group of partners"), capping pricing power and making the relationship non-exclusive. (2) Sub-scale vs well-capitalised peers: $57m Series B is small next to Persona ($2bn) / Socure ($4.5bn); in a fragmented, certification-and-distribution-driven market, under-capitalisation risks being out-marketed or consolidated. (3) "Announced vs adopted" gap: press describes capabilities and a partner-status upgrade, not customer/revenue commitments; the note is silent on deal economics, seat volumes, or fraud-liability allocation — de-PR'd, this is a channel/marketplace listing, not proven enterprise traction.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) The Entra Verified ID partner ecosystem is becoming the battleground where reusable-credential vendors fight for enterprise distribution — expect more "Verified ID partner" announcements as eIDAS 2.0 (end-2026) and NIST 800-63-4 force IAL2-grade proofing into the mainstream. Falsifiable thesis: 1Kosmos's FedRAMP High + NIST certifications convert into named US-federal/regulated-enterprise wins via the Microsoft channel within ~12 months. Trigger to watch: disclosed logo/ARR wins or a Series C at a stepped-up valuation → thesis confirmed; continued capability-only PR with no adoption metrics → thesis wrong / channel-dependent stagnation.
