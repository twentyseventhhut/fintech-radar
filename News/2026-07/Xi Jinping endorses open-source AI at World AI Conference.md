# Xi Jinping endorses open-source AI at World AI Conference

> [!info] 2026-07-18 · 1 упоминаний · 0 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: MTS

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[MTS] Xi Jinping speaks at the 2026 World AI Conference in Shanghai. The speech from China’s paramount leader explicitly endorses open-source, accessible AI; global governance; and AI control and risk, and says China will make sure that “this fine steed of AI gallops with both speed and stability”. Xi announced the establishment of the World AI Cooperation Organization, made up of 29 mostly developing countries including Russia, Brazil, Pakistan, and Vietnam. WAICO members make up 36% of the Earth’s population, ~30% of GDP, and ~15% of frontier AI compute.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **2026-07-17**, Xi Jinping delivered the **first-ever Chinese-president keynote** in WAIC's 9-year history (Reuters/Xinhua/CGTN full text). De-PR'd:
- The speech is a **policy/positioning statement**, not a deliverable: it endorses open-source AI as "a public good," rejects "monopolisation" of AI by any one country ("not a solo performance by any single country but a symphony of global cooperation"), and objects to the "overstretching" of national-security concerns (i.e. US export controls — NPR framed the speech explicitly as a response to US curbs squeezing China's chip access).
- **Concrete commitments (same as the WAICO note):** over 5 years, 5,000 AI training/seminar slots for developing nations; AI-application cooperation centers with ASEAN, the Arab League, the African Union; an AI-powered meteorological warning system for 30 countries.
- WAICO framed as "a milestone in the history of world AI development," answering Global-South calls for a voice in AI governance.
- **+ Why this framing / what it reveals:** the open-source endorsement is not idealism — it is China's **strategic weapon**. With US export controls choking China's access to frontier compute (see [[Nvidia halves list of Asia buyers over China chip export curbs]], [[FT More firms pick Chinese AI models to cut costs]]), open-weighting Chinese models (DeepSeek, Qwen, Kimi, GLM) turns a supply constraint into a distribution advantage: it seeds Chinese models as the default in the Global South and undercuts the metered-API business US labs monetize. The speech is the head-of-state ratification of the open-weight-as-soft-power play the corpus already documents on the supply side.

## [1] Competitors / peers (governance blocs)
Fully covered in [[China establishes WAICO international AI organization]]: the US runs the competing **Pax Silica** hardware/supply-chain track (~24–35 states + Taiwan, Dec 2025) plus the US AI Action Plan's explicit aim to "counter Chinese influence in international governance bodies"; the EU exports rules via the binding AI Act (fully enforceable 2 Aug 2026); the Western cluster (OECD/GPAI/G7 Hiroshima) is values-gated, whereas WAICO drops the human-rights gate. Position: China is **first-mover on a formal intergovernmental AI body**, but behind on frontier-model access/compute. Nothing in the target note changes this landscape versus the WAICO note.

## [2] Company/actor history / fit
State-actor, not a company. Trajectory (per WAICO note): Global AI Governance Initiative (2023-10) → WAICO founding agreement (2026-07-16, Wang Yi signs, Guterres attends) → Xi keynote (2026-07-17). The Xi speech is the **capstone signalling event** of that sequence, consistent with China's sovereignty+development framing since 2023. No new trajectory data.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is AI-policy / open-source-model geopolitics, not a company deal — no round, TAM or trading metric attaches to Xi's speech, so valuation multiples = "no data". The market layer is the **open-weight model ecosystem** the speech is designed to endorse and export. Dated facts: on **2026-07-17** Xi gave his first-ever WAIC keynote, calling on countries to "encourage open source, openness, collaboration and sharing," pledged **5,000 AI-training slots** for developing nations over five years and Mazu weather-AI to 30 countries, and blessed the day-old **WAICO** (29 states) as "a milestone" (per CGTN full text / SCMP / Al Jazeera, 2026-07-17 — see the governance mechanics in [[China establishes WAICO international AI organization]]). The endorsement rides a real usage shift: **Chinese open-weight models ran ~61% of tokens on OpenRouter by May 2026, 4 of the top-5 most-used models were Chinese, and DeepSeek+Qwen went from ~1% to ~15% of global market share in a year** (per OpenRouter / trendingtopics / codercops, 2026). Structure: the *model layer is commoditizing* (open weights are non-exclusive, switching cost collapses to a config change) while barriers move to compute/GPU access and — critically — **standards + diplomatic reach**, a network-effects, winner-take-most game. Why now (two-sided): (1) US export controls on H100/H20 pushed China to **weaponize open source** — releasing weights crowdsources optimization onto cheaper hardware and makes Chinese models "the default foundation for developers across the Global South" (per RAND PEA4686 / USCC "Two Loops" / CSIS, 2026); (2) the world is mid-fragmentation — **Washington's AI Opportunity Statement has ~35 signatories vs WAICO's 29, with "relatively limited overlap"** (per BCG / Modern Diplomacy, 2026), so both blocs race to set the default before it hardens.

**Competitive landscape.** The KPIs here are not $-KPIs: **token/usage share, benchmark rank, $/1M-token, download/ecosystem lock-in, and signatory/standards adoption**. Two competing "products": (1) **US closed-weight** — OpenAI/Anthropic/Google, monetized proprietary frontier APIs, paired with export controls (the order forcing Anthropic to cut foreign access to Fable 5, which itself vacated the top closed-coding slot abroad — see [[New low-cost Chinese AI model rivals Anthropic and OpenAI]]); (2) **China open-weight** — DeepSeek, Alibaba Qwen, Moonshot/Kimi, Zhipu/GLM, MiniMax, competing on **price + open weights + state backing**, not a durable capability lead. Recent dated moves: **Qwen passed ~1bn cumulative Hugging Face downloads, overtaking Llama** (2026); **Kimi K3** shipped 2026-07-16 as the largest open-weight model from China ([[Moonshot AI releases frontier open model Kimi K3]]); **GLM-5.2** beat GPT-5.5 on SWE-bench Pro at ~1/6 the cost ([[New low-cost Chinese AI model rivals Anthropic and OpenAI]]); **DeepSeek** hit a ~$500M run-rate on a 2027-IPO track ([[DeepSeek annualized revenue nears $500 million]]); and the demand side is live — Apple was *required* to embed Qwen to ship AI in China ([[Alibaba's Qwen to power Apple Intelligence in China]]). China's position: **ahead on open-weight distribution and Global-South institutional form (WAICO), behind on frontier-model capability, compute and revenue.** The moat is network effects + geopolitical alignment; it is thin on capability (the open-weight crown rotates monthly across DeepSeek/Qwen/Kimi/GLM) and on enforcement `(analysis)`. Closed labs still hold a **~3-8pt lead on reasoning-heavy benchmarks** (per digitalapplied / deepinfra, Q2 2026) — the gap has closed on code, not on hardest reasoning.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation/round/deal attaches to a speech → EV/Revenue, P/E, price-per-user all "no data"; distribution not computed, qualitative only. Internal comps for the *open-vs-closed economics* theme (the read-through that matters), as `[[wikilink]]`:
- **DeepSeek** — ~$71-74B mark on ~$500M run-rate ≈ **~140x P/ARR** ([[DeepSeek annualized revenue nears $500 million]]) — the "open-weight national champion" priced on optionality, not economics.
- **Moonshot/Kimi** — reported ~$31.5B round on ~$300M ARR ≈ **~105x** ([[Moonshot AI releases frontier open model Kimi K3]]).
- **Zhipu/Z.ai (public HKEX)** — ~$6.55bn IPO valuation; revenue not cleanly disclosed → "no data" ([[New low-cost Chinese AI model rivals Anthropic and OpenAI]]).
- **US closed benchmark:** OpenAI ~$852bn / ~$25bn run-rate ≈ **~34x**; Anthropic ~$965bn / ~$47bn ≈ **~21x** — an order of magnitude larger in dollars *and* revenue, on far *lower* multiples than the Chinese open-weight cohort.
- **Price arithmetic (the lever the speech promotes):** DeepSeek V4 Flash **$0.14/$0.28** per 1M in/out vs Claude Opus 4.8 **$5.00/$25.00** → **>50-90x cheaper on output**; that price collapse (frontier-class output down ~94-99% since GPT-4) is what makes "open + free" exportable to the Global South. **Flag:** the Chinese open-weight cohort carries *richer* revenue multiples (~105-140x P/ARR) than the US closed leaders (~21-34x) despite ~1/50th the revenue — the marks price geopolitical-champion optionality, not unit economics `(analysis)`.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Open-source as a strategic-dependency trap (the real geopolitical risk).** "Free" Global-South adoption of Chinese open weights embeds them in partner governance/industrial/military stacks (per USCC "Two Loops" / CSIS, 2026); second-order — it creates a soft-power lock-in and standards gravity well that a values-gate-free WAICO can then formalize, the actual competitive threat to the US bloc, not the speech itself.
2. **Standards/regulatory fragmentation → compliance tax.** ~35-signatory US bloc vs 29-state WAICO with "limited overlap" means multinational fintechs/banks face divergent AI-governance regimes; the fixed regtech cost advantages incumbents with compliance teams and disadvantages the Global-South players WAICO claims to include — the opposite of the stated goal (see [[China establishes WAICO international AI organization]]).
3. **Data-sovereignty / procurement blocker for regulated Western finance.** A China-origin model — even open-weight — carries sanctions, NDAA-exclusion and model-governance scrutiny; routing customer/transaction data through Chinese endpoints is a hard compliance blocker, so the cost saving is largely unavailable to the most regulated fintech buyers regardless of Xi's "openness" framing.
4. **Rhetoric-vs-reality gap (de-PR).** "Endorses open source" is a *speech*, not policy: China's own labs increasingly price like closed models (Kimi K3 at $3/$15 ≈ Claude Sonnet, no longer a deep discount — [[Moonshot AI releases frontier open model Kimi K3]]), and DeepSeek is on a capex-heavy self-hosting/IPO track. "Open" is a distribution weapon, not a business model — flag who's silent on the economics.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` The trade isn't in the speech; it's in what it accelerates: a one-way **ratchet down the price floor for "good-enough" agentic inference**, capping US closed-lab pricing power on commodity tasks, plus a re-rating of **cross-border AI-compliance/regtech** and **sovereign-AI** infra as structural verticals as the two blocs diverge. Falsifiable thesis: within 12 months either a named regulated financial institution runs a Chinese open-weight model (or self-hosted derivative) in production **and** WAICO members adopt a shared AI-in-finance rulebook (bullish — the open-weight-standards flywheel is real), or it stays a communiqué + benchmark blitz (then it's pure geopolitics, no fintech signal). What breaks it: the blocs converging on shared technical standards, or Chinese open-weight benchmark leads failing independent verification / a US restriction on deploying Chinese weights — either restores incumbent pricing power. Trigger to watch: WAICO's first concrete standard/mutual-recognition regime, and whether Chinese open-weight OpenRouter share holds above ~45-60%.
