# Context-enrichment: The Paypers — Ravelin Global Fraud Trends 2026 key takeaways
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS VERDICT: FRESH (barely).** This is a genuinely DISTINCT Ravelin report from the [[Ravelin report finds 44% of enterprise merchants adopt agentic commerce]] note (2026-06-09). That June note covered Ravelin's standalone *Agentic Commerce & Fraud Report 2026* (the "44% already integrating" figure). THIS note covers Ravelin's annual flagship *Global Fraud Trends 2026* (ecommerce) report — headline stat "average enterprise loses USD 11.4M/year to fraud." Different report, different landing page, different headline. BUT the two share the SAME underlying survey base (n=1,504 fraud/payments pros, 10 countries, fielded by Qualtrics Jan 2026) — Ravelin marketing-slices one fieldwork into multiple "reports." So it's fresh-as-a-distinct-asset but low-novelty (a vendor survey PR, no market-moving event).

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
The Paypers published a "key takeaways" editorial write-up of Ravelin's **Global Fraud Trends 2026** report (report itself published ~2026-06-17; The Paypers piece ~2026-07-13). This is journalism-about-a-vendor-survey, not a corporate event. De-PR'd substance:
- **Headline loss:** avg enterprise ~**USD 11.4M/year** to fraud, +**7.5% vs 2025**. (Self-reported survey estimate, not measured/audited loss — treat as directional.)
- **First-party ("friendly") fraud:** **43%** of merchants say customer fraud rose YoY — the "blurring line between consumer and criminal" theme.
- **AI-attributed loss:** **66%** attribute ≥5% of losses to AI attacks. (Perception, not forensics — merchants can't reliably attribute a chargeback to "AI".)
- **SCA/3DS:** exemption use up to **78%** (from 72%); 3DS reframed as conversion-supporting, not just a fraud gate.
- **Payment-method risk ranking:** more merchants now rank **Google Wallet / Apple Pay** in their top-3 most-fraudulent methods; **PayPal** ranked 3rd riskiest. (Likely volume-mix bias: more volume → more absolute fraud, not necessarily higher per-txn rate — analysis.)
- **Agentic commerce fraud** flagged as "will definitely take off."
- **→ Why this way:** methodology = survey of 1,504 enterprise merchants (>$50M rev / >450 staff) across UK/US/CA/FR/DE/IT/ES/AU/BR/MX. Vendor-sponsored fraud surveys anchor on a scary rising-loss number ("+7.5%", "$11.4M") to build TAM for the vendor's own product. The 6-month lag (Jan fieldwork → June/July publish) is material for a fast-moving agentic topic.
- **→ What it reveals:** the ONE genuinely useful, harder-to-PR datapoint is the narrative shift — fraud is *stabilizing in frequency but rising in cost*, and the biggest threat is now **the customer** (first-party fraud), not the external criminal. That reframes fraud-ops from "block bad actors" to "adjudicate ambiguous good-customer behavior."

## [1] Competitors / peers
Ravelin competes in hosted transaction-fraud scoring vs **Sift, Signifyd, Forter, Riskified** (data-network depth is the moat). Comparable 2026 fraud-trend reports:
- **Adyen "Fraud's Identity Crisis" 2026:** traditional fraud losses **-20%**, but first-party fraud now the **most-reported type (44%)**. ⚠️ Note: this is a *second, unrelated "44%"* — do NOT conflate with Ravelin's agentic "44%". Adyen corroborates Ravelin's "first-party fraud is now the #1 problem" thesis.
- **Sumsub / Experian / LexisNexis:** deepfakes, AI-driven identity fraud as 2026 top threats; LexisNexis: US merchants lose **$4.61 per $1** of actual fraud.
- Corpus peers on the same fraud-tooling theme: [[SEON and Domaine launch Shopify-native fraud prevention app]], [[Chargeflow raises $35M for chargeback automation]] (first-party/chargeback), [[GrailPay raises $10.5M Series A for agentic payments risk decisioning]] (agentic-payments risk).
- **→ Why the landscape:** every fraud vendor is publishing a 2026 report converging on the same two themes (AI attackers + first-party fraud). Consensus = commoditized narrative; the report has little proprietary signal. Position: **parity** — Ravelin is neither ahead nor behind; it's competing on brand/thought-leadership, not a differentiated finding.

## [2] Company history / fit
Ravelin (UK fraud-detection vendor) fits its pattern: it runs an annual enterprise fraud survey and increasingly leans into the **agentic-commerce fraud** angle (see the June [[Ravelin report finds 44% of enterprise merchants adopt agentic commerce]] asset from the same fieldwork). **→ Why:** a fraud vendor's growth depends on merchants believing fraud is rising AND evolving (new vectors it uniquely detects). Publishing multiple sliced reports off one survey maximizes press cycles and positions Ravelin as the agentic-fraud category owner ahead of Sift/Forter — a land-grab for the emerging "AI-agent checkout" risk category.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Low novelty. This is a **restatement/re-slice** of the same Jan-2026 Qualtrics fieldwork already partially surfaced in June. No new event, no product, no adoption metric — it's survey PR. **Real value-add (analysis):** the durable insight is the first-party-fraud reframe (corroborated independently by Adyen), which genuinely shifts fraud-ops from perimeter-blocking to customer-adjudication — the margin/value in fraud tooling moves toward vendors that can score *ambiguous good customers*, not just block bad ones. The "$11.4M / +7.5%" numbers are self-reported and vendor-incentivized — treat as soft. No traction data attaches to this note.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Industry-wide context (independent of Ravelin): Juniper — global ecommerce fraud losses ~**$48B in 2025 → $131B by 2030** (+133%, friendly-fraud-driven); Nasdaq Verafin — illicit financial activity **$4.4T in 2025**, fraud/scam losses **$579B**. **→ Tension to flag:** Ravelin's "fraud is stabilizing" framing sits *at odds* with Juniper's +133% growth curve — either Ravelin's enterprise-only sample understates the trend, or "stabilizing frequency, rising cost" reconciles them. **→ Second-order:** as agentic checkout scales, fraud liability and SCA/3DS rules for AI-agent transactions are unresolved — the real 2026 question is not "will agentic fraud rise" but "who owns liability when an AI agent transacts" (issuer / merchant / agent platform / rails), which this report does not answer.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Ecommerce fraud detection & prevention (FDP), a subvertical of risk-tech. Market size: analyst estimates diverge widely — Grand View Research pegs the broader FDP market at ~$40.4bn in 2026 (18.1% CAGR to 2033); Fortune Business Insights ~$67bn (17.5% CAGR); Mordor ~$70bn (19.6% CAGR) (per each firm, via search, as of 2026) — treat the absolute number as "no data / wide range", but the direction (mid-to-high-teens % CAGR) is consistent across sources. Drivers ("why now"): (1) generative-AI lowers attacker cost — Ravelin's own survey says 64% of merchants saw AI-enabled fraud/abuse in the past year and 66% attribute ≥5% of losses to AI (per Ravelin GFT 2026, via africabusiness.com/thepaypers, 2026); (2) agentic commerce moving from novelty to production ([[Ravelin report finds 44% of enterprise merchants adopt agentic commerce]]) creating new automated-purchase attack surface. Structure: fragmented — the top-10 vendors reportedly account for a low-single-digit share of total revenue; competition on product/ML accuracy and chargeback-guarantee economics, not price alone. Report headline figure: average ecommerce enterprise lost ~$11.4m to fraud over the past year, +7.5% YoY (per Ravelin GFT 2026, via thepaypers, 2026).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: fraud-loss rate (% of GMV), chargeback/approval rates, false-positive (good-order rejection) rate, guarantee coverage. Key players & basis of competition:
- **Guarantee model** (absorb chargeback liability, price as % of GMV): Signifyd, Riskified, Forter, ClearSale, NoFraud.
- **Platform / trust-&-safety**: Sift (broader than payment fraud).
- **Behavioral analytics / issuer-side**: Featurespace (ARIC), now inside Visa.
- **Ravelin**: ML-native merchant-side fraud + payments optimization; positioned as ease-of-integration / analytics.
Recent moves: Visa completed the Featurespace acquisition Dec 2024 (~$925m est., undisclosed); Worldpay agreed to acquire Ravelin Feb 4 2025, closed Feb 21 2025 for $151.8m cash (Worldpay is GTCR-owned since 2023) (per Worldpay/BusinessWire, 2025). Protagonist's position: niche, mid-scale merchant-fraud specialist now embedded in an acquirer's stack — no longer independent; the "report" is now Worldpay-owned marketing. Moat `(analysis)`: proprietary transaction graph + distribution via Worldpay's acquiring rails; weaker standalone brand vs Forter/Signifyd at the enterprise top end.

Revenue-multiple arithmetic beyond Riskified P/S = "no data" (private peers don't disclose revenue). Flag: the RSKD ~2.0x P/S vs stale $1.3–3bn private marks shows a public/private valuation gap — private marks are pre-2023 and likely unrealizable today.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Report-marketing / conflict-of-interest bias** — the Global Fraud Trends 2026 report is Ravelin's (now Worldpay's) own survey; every "fraud is rising / AI threat is here" figure sells fraud software. Treat findings as vendor-sponsored data, not neutral research.
2. **Sample representativeness & self-report** — survey of merchants (2025 GFT surveyed ~1,900; agentic report ~1,500); loss figures ($11.4m avg, +7.5%; "66% saw more fraud") are self-reported perceptions, not audited losses, and skew toward Ravelin's own enterprise customer base → likely upward bias.
3. **Disintermediation of the protagonist** — post-Worldpay, Ravelin's independence and multi-acquirer neutrality erode; merchants on rival acquirers may hesitate, and Visa+Featurespace / issuer-side AI can capture fraud-scoring value upstream of the merchant layer.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` The AI-vs-AI fraud arms race is consolidating FDP into payment giants (Visa–Featurespace, Worldpay–Ravelin), squeezing standalone guarantee vendors (Riskified de-rated, private marks stale) between issuer-side AI above and acquirer-bundled fraud below. Falsifiable thesis: independent merchant-fraud pure-plays lose pricing power as acquirers bundle fraud into processing. Watch/trigger: another guarantee-vendor take-private or acquisition, or Worldpay/Visa cross-selling fraud "free" with acquiring — would confirm bundling; sustained independent-vendor revenue growth would break it.
