# Context-enrichment: Feathery raises $30M to power AI-driven RIA operations
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**Freshness: fresh.** No prior `company/feathery` note exists in the corpus; this is the first appearance and a genuine new event (Series A close, announced 2026-07-14). It is NOT a duplicate. The most relevant priors are direct comps — advisor-ops AI rounds: [[Jump raises $80M Series B for AI advisor platform]] (2026-02), [[Zocks raises $45M Series B for advisor automation]] (2026-01), [[Wealth.com raises $65M Series B for AI wealth platform]] (2026-04), [[Conquest Planning raises $80M Series B led by Goldman Sachs]] (2025-06), [[Altruist adds AI tax planning features to Hazel platform]] (2026-02).

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Feathery (founded 2021, SF; co-founders Peter Dun ex-Robinhood / Zack Khan) announced its **Series A led by Portage Ventures** on 2026-07-14, bringing **cumulative funding to "$30M total"** — participants Index Ventures, Bain Capital Ventures, plus strategic insurers Allstate Strategic Ventures and Erie Strategic Ventures, and Clocktower Ventures.
- **The "$30M" is total-funding-to-date, not a fresh $30M cheque.** Feathery's own blog, finsmes ("$30M in Total"), InvestmentNews and WealthManagement all use "in total funding." Backing out the ~$4–4.5M 2021 seed, the net-new Series A is undisclosed and materially smaller (~$25M implied — my inference, not stated). **Valuation not disclosed.**
- **Why framed this way:** headlining the cumulative "$30M" and the "AI operating & decisioning system" language inflates a no-code forms/intake engine into a wealthtech-multiple narrative. The note's aggregated text ("draw on data patterns across its client base") is the aspirational *pitch* layer, described in future terms — not a shown, live, differentiated AI product. Attribution of the quote to "Khan" (co-founder Zack Khan), not CEO Dun.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Feathery straddles two lanes — **forms/onboarding intake** (its legacy) and **advisor-ops automation** (its repositioning):
- **AI-native advisor ops (better-branded, further along):** Jump — $80M Series B 2026 (Insight), ~$105M total; Zocks — $45M Series B 2026 (Lightspeed/QED); Powder — $5M seed, GenAI onboarding co-pilot, closest to Feathery's paperwork angle.
- **Incumbent RIA/BD back-office:** Docupace (PE-owned, account-opening/onboarding suite — Feathery's truest non-AI analog); Advisor360°.
- **Adjacent planning/wealth AI:** Conquest ($80M Series B, Portage also a backer — same investor); Wealth.com ($65M Series B 2026).
- **Systems of record Feathery integrates INTO (not vs):** Orion (Denali AI), Envestnet/Tamarac, Addepar, Altruist (Hazel), Wealthbox.
- **Position:** parity on capital in the ops lane but **behind Jump/Zocks on AI-native brand**; **ahead on data-intake plumbing** where notetaker-first rivals are weak. **Why the gap:** Jump/Zocks won the "AI notetaker → agentic OS" wedge first (2025 rounds), owning the advisor-facing surface; Feathery's differentiated wedge is the **dual insurance + wealth vertical** and account-opening/transitions workflow — a narrower, plumbing-shaped niche that earns less narrative premium.

## [2] Company history / fit
Trajectory: developer no-code **form builder** (2021) → profitability + 7-figure ARR (Mar 2024, per Empact) → vertical repositioning into financial-services workflow (~2025) → Series A (2026-07). **Why it acts this way:** a horizontal forms tool is a commodity (competes with Typeform/Nintex/DocuSign) that earns a low multiple; verticalizing into regulated financial-services onboarding (KYC/KYB, account opening) with strategic insurer capital lets it claim a defensible workflow-automation multiple. The pivot fits its actual strength (structured data intake) — it is expansion, not a bolt-on AI veneer, but the "decisioning" layer is still mostly pitch.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Live with real clients (confirmed by reporting), unaudited vendor metrics:** wealth — Sequoia Financial (~$32B AUM), Allworth, Mission Wealth; insurance — Tokio Marine, Hiscox, Banner Life, Baldwin/Hilb/Hylant. Claims: 300+ firms, "tens of millions of monthly submissions," "$2B AUM moved Q1 2026" (this is onboarding *throughput*, NOT assets Feathery manages — it manages $0 AUM), 45% faster account-opening at Sequoia (single-customer), ~1/3 of Barron's Top 100 RIAs (2025).
- **Why the value-add is thin at the "AI" layer:** the load-bearing product is best-in-class intake + no-code workflows; the "data network across clients → decisioning" is aspirational. **Margin capture:** Feathery is **middleware** between advisors and systems of record (custodians, Orion/Envestnet/Addepar) — it books SaaS/workflow fees but is structurally **disintermediable** by those incumbents, several of whom (Altruist Hazel, Orion Denali, Advisor360, Wealthbox AI) are building the same onboarding/ops automation natively. Insurance (Allstate/Erie strategics) may be the more defensible margin pool than RIA.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Capital earmarked to "expand products that draw on data patterns across its client base" — i.e. build out the decisioning/insights layer and deepen both verticals. Sentiment: trade press (InvestmentNews, WealthManagement, finsmes, fintech.global) reprinted the announcement **with no skeptical framing or independent verification**. **Why the market goes this way:** 2025–26 advisor-tech funding is concentrating on "AI ops for RIAs" (Jump, Zocks, Wealth.com, Conquest); a mid-size vertical intake player raising into that tailwind is expected, not remarkable. **Second-order risk:** the same incumbents Feathery integrates into are its most likely acquirers *and* its most likely disintermediators — its defensibility depends on staying the neutral cross-custodian intake layer before Orion/Altruist/Envestnet close that gap natively.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: US wealthtech / RIA back-office automation — specifically the "data layer" for advisor transitions, client onboarding and document/workflow processing (Feathery brands itself an "AI operating & decisioning system for financial services," per Feathery blog, 2026-07). Market: AI-in-wealth-management tooling projected to grow from ~$1.8bn (2025) to ~$6bn by 2035 (per wealthmanagement.com, 2026 AI-trends piece; secondary citation, unattributed source methodology — treat as directional, not a hard TAM). Structure: highly fragmented, point-solution layer sitting between custodians, CRMs and financial-planning tools; barriers are data-integration breadth and trust/compliance, not capital. Why now: (i) advisor mobility is structurally high — Cerulli estimates ~9% of advisors (~$3.1tn AUM) changed firms last year (per fintech.global, 2026-05), so transition/onboarding is a recurring pain with measurable cost; (ii) GenAI moved from pilot to production in back/middle office, where ROI is most directly measurable against baseline manual costs — form-based entry is being replaced by conversational/context-aware ingestion (analysis, drivers per wealthtech sources 2026).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs run on: number of firms/seats, AUM moved through the platform, transition cycle-time (months→weeks/days), and integration coverage (# custodians/CRMs). Feathery discloses: 300+ firms across wealth + insurance (clients incl. Sequoia Financial, Allworth, Mission Wealth) and >$2bn AUM transitioned in Q1 2026 (per InvestmentNews / fintech.global, 2026-07). Key players & basis of competition: Dispatch (launched "Advisor Transitions" 2026-05 — the most direct head-to-head on the transitions use case; competes on data-orchestration breadth); AdvisorEngine and Advisor360° (broader wealth OS, migration/onboarding modules — compete on being the incumbent platform of record); and adjacent AI-notetaker vendors (Jump, Zocks, Zeplyn — Jump+Zocks alone raised >$170m combined) that are expanding downstream into CRM/back-office. Basis of competition = distribution + data-integration depth, not price. Protagonist position: catching up / co-leader in a still-forming category — well-capitalised for the stage but NOT the clear share leader; Dispatch is a near-identical competitor and better-funded peers are converging. Moat (analysis): weak-to-emerging — switching costs once a firm's data pipes run through it, plus a cross-client "data pattern" network-effect claim, but that claim is asserted, not evidenced [UNSOURCED on any retention/NRR metric].

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation disclosed for Feathery → **EV/Rev, P/S = no data** (round valuation not published; do not estimate). Round structure flag: the headline "$30M raise" is **$30M cumulative total funding, not a $30M new round** — it includes a $4.5m seed from 2021; the fresh Series A tranche size is not separately disclosed (per finsmes.com, 2026-07). Investors: Portage, Bain Capital Ventures, Index Ventures, plus strategics Allstate/Erie Strategic Ventures, Clocktower. Internal comps (private, funding-stage — valuations undisclosed, so qualitative only; distribution not computed): [[MDOTM raises $27M growth round for AI wealth management]] ($27m growth, AI wealth), [[Hadrius raises $27M for agentic compliance infrastructure]] ($27m, adjacent AI-native fin-services workflow, 500+ institutions), [[WealthReach raises $1 million seed for RIA growth platform]] ($1m seed, RIA growth), [[Macro Technologies launches AI decision engine for asset managers]] (AI decisioning, asset managers). Read: Feathery's $30m total sits mid-pack for the cohort; strategic-insurer backing (Allstate/Erie) is the differentiated signal, consistent with its insurance + wealth dual focus. In-line, not a stand-out on size — no re-rating readable without a valuation.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Headline inflation / thin fresh capital (de-PR).** "$30M" is cumulative (incl. 2021 $4.5m seed), not a $30m new injection — the actual Series A runway is smaller than the headline implies; a founder framing choice worth discounting when judging staying power vs peers that raised $27m in single rounds.
2. **Direct disintermediation by a near-identical peer.** Dispatch shipped an "Advisor Transitions" product in May 2026 targeting the exact same ingest→cleanse→reconcile transition workflow; with notetaker vendors (Jump/Zocks) also pushing into the back office, Feathery risks being squeezed between a better-funded transitions specialist and platform incumbents (AdvisorEngine/Advisor360°) — margin/positioning captured by a neighbouring stack layer.
3. **Unproven durability of the moat.** The core "data patterns across the client base" value-prop is asserted but retention/NRR/switching evidence is undisclosed [UNSOURCED]; if the offering is really a transitions point-tool, it is a one-time event (advisor moves), not recurring back-office spend — usage could be lumpy and churn-prone once a migration completes.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is a *new-entry / category-forming* signal, not a re-rating: the "advisor transitions + onboarding data layer" is crystallising into a fundable niche, and strategic insurers backing Feathery hint the buyer set extends beyond RIAs into insurance ops. Falsifiable thesis: the category consolidates fast and Feathery either becomes an acquisition target for a wealth-OS incumbent or gets out-scaled by Dispatch within ~12–18 months. Watch/trigger: (i) whether Feathery converts the $2bn-AUM-moved metric into recurring/expansion revenue vs one-off transitions, and (ii) any competitive raise or M&A by Dispatch — that would be the tell that the window to lead is closing.
