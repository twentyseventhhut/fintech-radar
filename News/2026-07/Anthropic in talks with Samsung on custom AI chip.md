Anthropic is discussing a new custom chip with Samsung
Back in April, Reuters reported that Anthropic was toying with the idea of producing its own AI chips as a means of responding to chip shortages. Now, it would appear that the company is getting serious about this idea.
On Thursday, The Information reported that Anthropic was in contact with Samsung to explore a collaboration around the pending chip. However, Anthropic hasn’t yet decided what the chip will be used for, how it will fit into the server, or how powerful it will be, according to the report.
When reached for comment, Anthropic told TechCrunch that a diversified hardware stack that includes chips from Google, Amazon, and Nvidia will continue to be pivotal to its compute strategy. On the topic of a potential Samsung partnership, the company said it had nothing further to add. 
A number of AI companies have sought to develop custom chips — both as a way to create unique hardware for specific compute tasks and to gain a certain amount of independence from Nvidia, which continues to be the undisputed leader of the chip industry.
Anthropic’s announcement may also be a response to one made last week by its key competitor, OpenAI, which has teamed up with Broadcom to announce its own custom-built inference processor, dubbed “Jalapeño.” OpenAI says that the chip is more efficient, demonstrating better performance-per-watt, than other competitor chips. Amazon and Google both offer custom-built TPUs as part of their cloud offering.
Samsung is already embedded in the AI industry, and acts as a major partner of Nvidia, producing chips that the company needs to train or run its AI models. In turn, Samsung uses Nvidia’s software to manufacture its chips. The duo are working on an AI chip factory in South Korea. Samsung has also discussed partnering with Google on its chip-making efforts.

# Context-enrichment: Anthropic in talks with Samsung on custom AI chip
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 2026-07-02 **The Information** reported that Anthropic is **in talks with Samsung's foundry** to manufacture a **custom AI chip**, reportedly targeting Samsung's **2nm process node and advanced packaging**. TechCrunch/Bloomberg/Reuters relayed it the next day.

De-PR'd reality — this is **very early-stage exploration, not a deal**:
- Per the report, Anthropic **hasn't decided what the chip will do, how it fits into the server, or how powerful it will be** — i.e. no spec, no tape-out, no volume, no date.
- Anthropic's own comment to TechCrunch was deliberately non-committal: it stressed a **"diversified hardware stack of Google, Amazon and Nvidia chips"** remains "pivotal," and had "nothing further to add" on Samsung. That is a company **not confirming** a chip program.
- This is the third step in a public breadcrumb trail, not a bolt from the blue: **April 2026** Reuters reported Anthropic was "toying" with designing its own chips amid the shortage (no team, no commitment) → **May 2026** Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron invested in Anthropic's ~$65B Series H (post-money ~$965B) as strategic infrastructure partners → **July 2026** the Samsung foundry talks surface. The investment "paved the way" (Korea JoongAng Daily) for exactly this kind of tie-up.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the news is really a *sourcing/optionality* move dressed by the press as a "chip play." Anthropic is compute-constrained and Nvidia-dependent; a Samsung relationship is (a) a hedge against the chip shortage and (b) a natural cash-out of the strategic equity Samsung just bought in. Framing it beside OpenAI's Jalapeño makes it look like a symmetric "OpenAI has a chip, now Anthropic does too" story — but OpenAI has a **named, taped-out, benchmarked** chip; Anthropic has **talks about a process node**. The two are a generation apart in maturity. (analysis)

## [1] Competitors / peers
Custom-silicon race, with maturity levels (the real story is who is *live* vs *exploring*):
- **OpenAI + Broadcom "Jalapeño"** (2026-06-24): first custom inference ASIC, reticle-sized, **9-month design-to-tape-out**, engineering samples already running GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark in the lab at target freq/power; deployment at gigawatt scale from 2026; Microsoft to buy ~40% of the initial run. This is Anthropic's direct comp and is **~1 year ahead in execution**.
- **Google TPU** and **Amazon Trainium** — the incumbents Anthropic already *rents*: Anthropic runs on Google TPUs (~1M-chip / 1GW+ 2026 deal announced 2025-10-23; then the **3.5GW Google+Broadcom deal from 2027**, [[This Week in Fintech Anthropic long-read on how AI trains AI]] context) and uses Trainium via AWS ([[AWS in talks to sell Trainium AI chip racks to outside companies]]). Google and Amazon now **sell** their TPUs/Trainium externally, eroding the "only Nvidia" framing.
- **ByteDance custom CPUs** ([[ByteDance develops custom CPUs to power AI infrastructure]]) and Chinese labs on domestic silicon ([[China orders tech giants to halt Nvidia AI chip purchases]]) — same "de-Nvidia" driver, different (geopolitical) cause.
- **Samsung's own position:** it had been building an ARM-based inference NPU for **OpenAI**, but those talks **stalled in early June 2026** (Korean media cite "strategic differences"). Anthropic slots into freed-up 2nm foundry capacity.

**Position:** Anthropic is **catching up / late** on owned silicon and **ahead** on rented custom silicon (its TPU book is the largest of any lab). **Second-order:** the moat isn't "having a chip" — Google/Amazon/Broadcom already commoditize custom inference ASICs — it's **fab capacity and HBM allocation**, which is why the *investment-then-foundry* structure with Samsung matters more than the chip design itself. (analysis)

## [2] Company history / fit
Trajectory: Anthropic went from a model lab to the **most valuable private AI company** (~$965B post-money, May 2026, surpassing OpenAI's ~$730B), on the back of tripled revenue and enormous compute commitments (Google TPU 1M-chip deal 2025-10; 3.5GW Google+Broadcom from 2027; Trainium via AWS). It has also pushed into fintech verticals ([[Anthropic launches Claude for Financial Services]], [[Intuit and Anthropic partner on custom AI financial agents]]).

**Why Anthropic acts this way (structural logic):** its cost base is **compute**, sourced almost entirely from three vendors (Google, Amazon, Nvidia) whose chips it doesn't control. At $965B and growing inference demand, **every point of performance-per-watt and every gigawatt of secured supply is existential**. Designing a chip is less about a hardware business and more about (1) supply security against the shortage and (2) bargaining leverage vs Nvidia/Google on price. Hiring an ex-OpenAI chip veteran (per Korea Herald/TechTimes) signals it's staffing up, not just musing. (analysis) The move fits, but it's a **~2027+ payoff at best** given it has no design yet.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Novelty is low-to-moderate and unproven.** What's genuinely new: a *named foundry partner (Samsung)* and a *process target (2nm)*, which is one notch more concrete than April's "toying." What's NOT new: the *idea* of an Anthropic chip (April), custom inference silicon as a category (OpenAI, Google, Amazon, ByteDance all preceded it), or the Samsung relationship (Series H investor since May).

**Traction: essentially zero.** No spec, no tape-out, no benchmark, no customer, no date. Contrast with Jalapeño (samples in lab) — Anthropic is at the **"talks" stage**, i.e. an announcement, not a product. Per the skill's anti-PR gate, this is "exploring," firmly not "live."

**Who captures the margin / who's silent:** silent on cost, timeline, volume, exclusivity, and whether the chip is for training or inference. The real value-add — **fab and HBM allocation** — is captured by **Samsung (foundry) + memory makers (SK Hynix/Micron)**, not by Anthropic; Anthropic gets supply security and Nvidia leverage. If the chip never tapes out, Anthropic still wins the *option value* of a Samsung supply lane. (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Watch for: (a) a **confirmed design win / tape-out date** (the real signal); (b) whether it's **training or inference** silicon; (c) Samsung foundry 2nm yield (Samsung has lagged TSMC on advanced-node yields — execution risk). Ironically, the report landed **as semiconductor stocks fell** on fears that lab-designed chips erode merchant-silicon demand (Nvidia).

**Why the market goes this way / second-order:** the hyperscaler/lab **vertical-integration wave** (OpenAI-Broadcom, Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Meta MTIA, now Anthropic-Samsung) structurally **pressures Nvidia's inference share** — but counter-intuitively **deepens dependence on the two chokepoints Nvidia doesn't own: leading-edge foundry (TSMC/Samsung) and HBM (SK Hynix/Micron/Samsung)**. So "everyone builds their own chip" doesn't democratize compute; it **concentrates power in Samsung/TSMC + the HBM oligopoly** — which is precisely why those three memory names invested in Anthropic. The fragility: Anthropic is betting supply security on Samsung's *weaker* foundry, a hedge that could underdeliver. (analysis/hypothesis)

## Challenge / red-team (second-order)

1. **Is this a deal or just talks?** — Just talks. The Information reports exploratory contact; Anthropic has no spec, no tape-out, no timeline, and declined to confirm. This is "exploring," not "building." (answered)

2. **Is it genuinely new vs the April Reuters "own chips" report?** — Marginally. April = "toying, no team, no design." July = a *named foundry (Samsung)* + *process target (2nm)* + a hired ex-OpenAI chip veteran. It's the next breadcrumb, so fresh, but incremental — not a program launch. (answered)

3. **Why Samsung specifically, and why now?** — Samsung invested in Anthropic's May Series H; the equity "paved the way." Samsung also just had its OpenAI ARM-NPU talks stall (~early June 2026), freeing 2nm capacity. This is capacity + relationship, not a chosen design. (answered)

4. **Training or inference chip?** — Open. Report says undecided. Matters hugely: inference (like Jalapeño) is where owned silicon pays off on performance-per-watt at scale; training would be a far harder, riskier bet. (open)

5. **How far behind OpenAI is Anthropic?** — ~1 year in execution. Jalapeño went design→tape-out in 9 months and has lab samples running GPT-5.3; Anthropic is pre-spec. If Anthropic taped out on a similar clock it's a late-2027 story at best. (answered)

6. **Does Anthropic even need its own chip given its TPU book?** — Arguably no, near-term. It has ~1M Google TPUs / 1GW+ (2026) + 3.5GW Google+Broadcom (2027) + Trainium via AWS. A Samsung chip is optionality/leverage/supply-security, not a compute necessity. This caps importance. (answered)

7. **Can Samsung's 2nm foundry actually deliver?** — Open/risky. Samsung has trailed TSMC on advanced-node yields; betting supply security on the weaker foundry is a hedge that could underdeliver. (open)

8. **Who captures the margin?** — Samsung (foundry) + SK Hynix/Micron/Samsung (HBM). Anthropic captures option value + Nvidia bargaining leverage, not a hardware-business margin. The memory oligopoly is the structural winner. (answered/analysis)

9. **What is Anthropic silent about?** — Cost, volume, timeline, exclusivity, train-vs-inference, and whether it will actually build vs just buy more merchant chips (April Reuters flagged it might just buy). Silence = low commitment. (answered)

10. **Is this a response to OpenAI/Jalapeño (narrative) or independent strategy?** — Both. The idea predates Jalapeño (April), but the July timing and press framing ride OpenAI's June 24 announcement. Partly reactive PR symmetry. (answered/analysis)

11. **Second-order effect on Nvidia?** — Every lab-designed inference chip chips at Nvidia's inference share, but *deepens* dependence on foundry + HBM chokepoints Nvidia doesn't own. Semiconductor stocks fell on the news — the market read it as merchant-silicon erosion. (answered)

12. **Does the Series H investment create a conflict / soft lock-in?** — Plausibly. Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron as both investors and suppliers aligns incentives but risks Anthropic overpaying for supply security or being steered to Samsung's fab regardless of best yield. (open/analysis)

13. **Could the chip never ship and this still be a "win"?** — Yes. Even as pure optionality, a Samsung 2nm lane hedges the chip shortage and pressures Nvidia/Google on price. Downside is bounded; that's why it's rational even at low ship-probability. (answered/analysis)

14. **What's the real signal to watch?** — A confirmed tape-out date + train/inference designation + Samsung 2nm yield data. Until then this is a sourcing headline, not a product. (open)

15. **Does this change Anthropic's competitive position materially today?** — No. Zero traction, no product, ~1yr behind OpenAI's actual chip. It's a directional signal about vertical integration, not a present-tense advantage. (answered)

**Importance: 3/5** — Genuinely fresh incremental step (named foundry + 2nm + a hire) and a strategically meaningful signal of AI-lab vertical integration and Nvidia de-risking, tied to Anthropic's ~$965B scale and its Samsung Series H investor. BUT: only "talks," zero traction, no spec/tape-out/date, ~1 year behind OpenAI's live Jalapeño, and possibly just optionality on top of an already-massive TPU book. Real weight is as a supply-chain/leverage story, not a product — hence a middle 3, not higher.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Custom AI silicon (in-house inference/training ASICs designed by labs/hyperscalers, fabbed by a merchant foundry). *Size/growth:* Broadcom — the dominant design-services house for these ASICs — booked AI revenue of $8.4bn in Q1 FY2026 (+106% YoY) and guided ~$10.7bn for Q2 (+140% YoY), with "clear line of sight" to >$100bn annual AI-chip revenue by late-2027/early-2028 (per Broadcom/Hock Tan, via FinancialContent, Mar 2026). *Structure:* the design layer is highly concentrated — Broadcom holds ~70%+ of the custom AI-accelerator design-services market, Marvell the main #2 (~$11bn AI-ASIC revenue guided for 2026) (per Tom's Hardware / Bloomberg Intelligence, May 2026). The *foundry* layer is even more concentrated: TSMC ~70% share ($122.5bn 2025 revenue) vs Samsung a distant #2 at ~6.5–7.2% ($12.6bn, -3.9% YoY) (per TrendForce/Semiecosystem, Q1 2026). *Why now:* labs want (a) independence from Nvidia's pricing/allocation and (b) a workload-tuned inference part — OpenAI's Broadcom-built "Jalapeño" (unveiled 24 Jun 2026, "substantially better perf/watt", deploying end-2026 with Microsoft) is the direct precedent Anthropic is answering, one week later (per OpenAI/TechCrunch).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: perf/watt (TCO per inference token), fab process node, advanced-packaging (CoWoS-class) capacity, time-to-tape-out. *Players & basis of competition:* Nvidia (merchant GPU, still "undisputed leader", competes on ecosystem/CUDA); in-house/custom camp — Google TPU, AWS Trainium/Inferentia, Meta MTIA, OpenAI+Broadcom (Jalapeño), now Anthropic+Samsung. Foundry/packaging: TSMC (default) vs Samsung (challenger, courting labs to fill 2nm). *Recent moves:* OpenAI–Broadcom Jalapeño announced 24 Jun 2026; Anthropic–Samsung talks reported 2 Jul 2026 (The Information), centered on Samsung's **2nm node + advanced packaging**; Anthropic hired Clive Chan, an early member of OpenAI's silicon team (per The Information/TechTimes). Note Anthropic already has 5GW of next-gen Google/Broadcom **TPU** capacity contracted and up-to-5GW AWS Trainium (per Anthropic Series-H, May 2026). *Position:* **catching up / early-stage** — Anthropic has not decided what the chip does, how powerful, or how it fits the server (per The Information); a diversified stack (Google/Amazon/Nvidia) remains the stated strategy. Moat if executed: workload-tuned perf/watt + supply diversification `(analysis)`; no silicon moat exists yet.

**Comps & multiples.** Protagonist is private, no chip P&L — chip multiples = no data. Anchors: Anthropic Series-H (May 2026) raised **$65bn at $965bn post-money** (round valuation, not market cap), now most valuable private AI co, ahead of OpenAI's ~$730bn pre-money (per Anthropic/TechCrunch). Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron were strategic investors in that round — the chip talks are a downstream of that capital relationship, not arm's-length. *Merchant enablers (public, verifiable):* Broadcom AI run-rate ~$10.7bn/qtr guided → ~$43bn annualized; its top-3 AI-ASIC customers (Google, **Anthropic**, Meta) ≈70% of AI revenue (per Broadcom, Mar 2026) — i.e. Anthropic is *already a major Broadcom custom-silicon customer*, which makes a parallel Samsung chip a second-source/diversification bet, not a green-field entry. Internal comps from base: [[OpenAI agreed to buy ten gigawatts of computer chips from Broadcom|Seven European fintech deals raise EUR 95 million in a week]] (OpenAI–Broadcom 10GW, cited $350–500bn), [[AWS in talks to sell Trainium AI chip racks to outside companies]], [[ByteDance develops custom CPUs to power AI infrastructure]], [[China orders tech giants to halt Nvidia AI chip purchases]] (Alibaba/Baidu in-house), [[Baseten reportedly raising at $400 million valuation for AI model infrastructure]] (inference-cloud, $400m). Distribution not computed — mixed models/stages, qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Foundry execution / yield risk on Samsung 2nm.** Samsung's foundry share fell YoY (~6.5%) while TSMC extended its lead; its 2nm yields are unproven at scale — a lab betting a first-ever chip on the challenger node risks slip/yield miss vs OpenAI's TSMC-class path. Second-order: schedule slip pushes Anthropic further behind on inference TCO.
2. **Vaporware / optionality, not commitment.** Anthropic confirms nothing beyond "diversified stack" and hasn't defined the chip's purpose/power/form-factor; "in talks" ≠ tape-out. De-PR: the announcement reads partly as a competitive response to OpenAI's Jalapeño one week prior. Risk of over-reading a signaling move.
3. **Related-party / concentration entanglement.** Samsung is both a Series-H investor and the mooted fab; Anthropic is simultaneously a top-3 Broadcom ASIC customer and holds 5GW Google-TPU capacity — spreading across Broadcom, Google, AWS, Samsung raises coordination/dis-synergy risk and dilutes any single-node volume needed for foundry economics to work.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` If real, this is Samsung using lab equity + captive demand to buy its way back into leading-edge foundry relevance against TSMC — a foundry-share re-rating story more than an Anthropic story. Falsifiable thesis: a signed 2nm tape-out/wafer commitment surfaces within ~12 months → watch for it; the inverse — talks lapse and Anthropic stays on Broadcom/Google TPU — would confirm this was signaling. Trigger to watch: any disclosed volume/node commitment, or Broadcom flagging Anthropic order softness.
