# GoTyme Bank surpasses 10 million users in Philippines

## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** GoTyme Bank surpassed **10 million registered users in the Philippines** (2026-07-10), just over three years (~3.25 yrs) after its 2022 launch — beating its original five-year target and hitting its mid-2026 goal on schedule. Customer deposits now stand at ~**US$861M ≈ P53 billion**. Cash-in/out is enabled at thousands of Robinsons and 7-Eleven locations plus ~600 self-service kiosks/ATMs. GoTyme is a JV between the Gokongwei group (Robinsons Retail / JG Summit) and the Tyme Group (backed by Patrice Motsepe's African Rainbow Capital). Sources: [newsbytes.ph](https://newsbytes.ph/2026/07/10/gotyme-bank-reports-10-million-users-after-3-years/), [bilyonaryo.com](https://bilyonaryo.com/2026/07/10/gokongwei-backed-gotyme-hits-10-million-users-in-just-over-three-years-deposits-top-p53-billion/money/), [Mindanao Times](https://www.mindanaotimes.com.ph/gotyme-bank-cements-position-as-a-trusted-bank-with-10m-users-and-p53b-in-deposits/).

**[1] Internal timeline (prior notes).** Clean, non-duplicative PH-user progression in our DB:
- [[GoTyme Bank reaches 6.5 million clients and P30 billion deposits]] (2025-07) — 6.5M users, P30B deposits.
- [[GoTyme surpasses 7 million users]] (2025-08) — 7M (up from 5M a year prior); CEO Nate Clarke targeting ~9M within the year.
- [[GoTyme reaches 7.8 million customers in the Philippines]] (2025-11) — 7.8M, ~600 kiosks/ATMs, 1,000+ ambassadors.
- [[GoTyme receives fresh $13.9 million capital injection]] (2026-07) — fresh US$13.9M injection, narrowing net loss, rising net interest income.
So this note (10M PH users, P53B deposits) is the **next milestone up the same curve**: +2.2M PH users since Nov 2025, deposits ~P30B→P53B in ~12 months.

**[2] Grounding / verification.** External sources corroborate 10M users + P53B deposits mid-2026, consistent with an intra-year path of 8M (2025), 9M (Mar 2026), 10M (Jul 2026). Deposit figures tie out: P43B (end-2025, [BusinessWorld](https://www.bworldonline.com/banking-finance/2026/02/20/731639/gotyme-bank-deposits-hit-p43-billion-in-2025/)) → P53B (Jul 2026); loans ~P6B, targeting ≥50% loan-book growth in 2026. Note's US$861M matches ~P53B at prevailing FX. GoTyme's Visa debit card recorded the highest active usage among PH Visa debit cards in 2025.

**[3] Why it matters / competitive frame.** GoTyme and **Maya** are the two scale leaders among BSP-licensed digital banks (six full licenses: Maya, Tonik, GoTyme, UNO, OFBank, UnionDigital). Peer state 2026: **Maya** — ~10.7M bank customers (end-2025), deposits P68B→**P76B** in Q1-2026 (+73% YoY), and the only PH digital bank already **profitable** (Group net income ~P1.7B FY2025), in The Asian Banker's global top-20 ([newsbytes](https://newsbytes.ph/2026/05/16/maya-deposits-jump-73-to-p76-billion-in-q1/)). **[[Tonik becomes first profitable standalone digital bank]]** (2026-05) — smaller but reached standalone profitability, high time-deposit rates (up to 8%). GoTyme leads on **users** but trails Maya on **deposits and profitability**, and is guiding to profitability only by **2027** (pushed from 2026). Its differentiator is the phygital retail-network moat (Robinsons/7-Eleven/kiosks).

**[4] Second-order read.** The 10M milestone is a scale marker, not an earnings event — GoTyme is still loss-making and just took a US$13.9M top-up. The strategic race is deposits + monetization (loans, cards, investing via DragonFi, crypto), not raw sign-ups. Distinct from the group's [[GoTyme Bank eyes $15 billion listing as customers top 21 million]] (2026-05), which is the **global TymeGroup** count (SA + PH) and an IPO-valuation story — a different milestone, not a duplicate of this PH-only figure.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
**Red-team / second-order questions**

1. **Are "10M users" registered accounts or active users?** — Almost certainly cumulative *registered* users, not MAU. GoTyme's own prior framing cites "top-four in monthly active app users" separately, implying MAU << 10M. Headline overstates engaged base.
2. **How does 10M users vs only P53B deposits read?** — ~P5,300 avg deposit/user; low ARPU, indicates a large tail of dormant/low-balance accounts. Maya has comparable customers but higher deposits (P76B), i.e. better monetization per user.
3. **Is this a duplicate of the May-2026 "21 million / $15B listing" note?** — No. That is the **global TymeGroup** count (South Africa + Philippines) and an IPO story (region/africa). This is PH-only registered users. Different milestone → **fresh**.
4. **Does it duplicate any earlier PH-user note?** — No; prior PH milestones were 6.5M, 7M, 7.8M, 9M (external). 10M is the next step up. Fresh.
5. **Profitability — 2026 or 2027?** — Timeline slipped: earlier guidance said 2026, now firmly **2027**. Still loss-making; the US$13.9M July injection underscores continued cash burn. Open risk to the growth narrative.
6. **Is deposit growth real or rate-driven (hot money)?** — Partly promo-driven; PH digital banks compete on 4–15% headline rates. If GoTyme trails Maya/Tonik on rates, deposits may be lower-cost but could also stall. Open.
7. **Loan-book credit quality?** — Loans ~doubled (P3B→P6B) targeting +50% in 2026 off a low base; unsecured consumer lending in PH carries elevated NPL risk. NPL/coverage not disclosed here. Open.
8. **FX consistency of US$861M vs P53B?** — Ties out at ~P57–61/US$; internally consistent. No red flag.
9. **Sustainability of the phygital cost base?** — 600+ kiosks, 1,000+ ambassadors, Robinsons/7-Eleven partnerships are opex-heavy; management claims opex grew <15% while gross profit doubled. Plausible but partner-fee-dependent moat. Open.
10. **Competitive threat from Maya's profitability + scale?** — Maya is ahead on deposits, profit, and global recognition; GoTyme's edge is users + retail distribution. Two-horse race at the top; GoTyme not clearly winning. Open.
11. **Regulatory / license cap risk?** — BSP digital-bank license moratorium; six incumbents. Favorable (limits new entrants), no adverse action noted. Low risk.
12. **Is 10M a hard milestone or a marketing round number?** — Corroborated across multiple PH outlets citing the bank's own mid-year update; credible but self-reported (no audited third-party count). Mild caveat.
13. **Does it move markets / is it investable?** — GoTyme is private (JG Summit / Robinsons Retail / Tyme JV); TymeGroup mulling a ~$15B listing. Indirect read-through to JG Summit-linked names, not a direct trade. Open.
14. **Data recency vs our other July note?** — Consistent with [[GoTyme receives fresh $13.9 million capital injection]] (2026-07-08); same period, complementary. Not conflicting.
15. **What's the genuinely new signal?** — First PH-specific breach of the 10M-user mark + P53B deposits, ahead of the original 5-year plan. Incremental milestone on a well-tracked curve, not a strategy shift.

**Importance: 3/5** — A clean, credible scale milestone for the #1-by-users PH digital bank, and the next step on a curve we already track (fresh, non-duplicate). But it is a registered-user vanity metric, GoTyme remains loss-making (profitability pushed to 2027), lags Maya on deposits/profit, and is a private, non-directly-investable name — notable but not thesis-changing.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
*(Adds a sector/valuation layer on top of [0]–[4]; does not restate the timeline/competitive frame already there.)*

**Sector & drivers.** Philippine digital banking is a **6-licence oligopoly by regulation** (Maya, Tonik, GoTyme, UNO, OFBank, UnionDigital; BSP licence moratorium in force) — the competitive set is fixed, so this is a share-consolidation game, not an open land-grab. The adjacent e-wallet layer is an order of magnitude larger (GCash / Maya wallet users in the tens of millions) — that is wallet reach, NOT bank-deposit TAM, so wallet user-counts must not be read as digital-bank balance-sheet size. Sector "why now": the cohort has crossed from *user-acquisition* into a *deposit-monetization + profitability* filter — Maya is already profitable (Group net income ~P1.7bn FY2025) while GoTyme has pushed breakeven guidance to **2027**. Total PH digital-bank deposit-pool figure: **no data** (no aggregate BSP print sourced this run) — do not estimate. Barriers: capital (recurring parent infusions), the BSP licence cap, and distribution — GoTyme's is the Gokongwei retail phygital network (Robinsons / 7-Eleven / ~600 kiosks).

**Sector KPIs & GoTyme's operational multiples.** The KPIs the segment now runs on are deposit-per-user, loan-to-deposit (monetization), NIM, and cost-to-income — not raw sign-ups. On the two figures in this note:
- **Deposit-per-user ≈ US$861m / 10m ≈ $86** (≈ P53bn / 10m ≈ **P5,300/user**) — a broad but shallow book; the arithmetic confirms the "registered-not-active" caveat, i.e. a long tail of low-balance accounts.
- **Loan-to-deposit ≈ P6bn / P53bn ≈ 11%** (loans ~P6bn per [0]–[2]; deposits P53bn) — very low; GoTyme funds a large cheap deposit base it has barely converted to interest-earning assets, which structurally caps NIM until the loan book scales.
These are operational, not trading, multiples — appropriate here because GoTyme is private.

**Comps & multiples.** GoTyme is a private JV (Gokongwei/JG Summit + Tyme) → **no listed market cap / public revenue → EV/Revenue, P/E, EV/EBITDA: no data**; a trading multiple is not computable. Peer read on the metric the sector prices (deposits + profit):
- **Maya (closest comp, profitable):** deposits **~P76bn (Q1-2026, +73% YoY)** vs GoTyme's P53bn; ~10.7m bank customers; already profitable (~P1.7bn FY2025). On the KPI that matters, Maya leads GoTyme on deposits and is the only cohort member in the black. Any ~$15bn valuation chatter attaches to the **TymeGroup** listing (global 21m-customer count, SA+PH), NOT the PH unit → `[UNSOURCED]` as a PH-unit valuation; see [[GoTyme Bank eyes $15 billion listing as customers top 21 million]].
- **Tonik (comp):** smaller, lending-led, reached **standalone** profitability with high (up to ~8%) time-deposit rates — see [[Tonik becomes first profitable standalone digital bank]]. Confirms a monetized-lending path GoTyme hasn't yet walked.
- **Internal GoTyme deposit/user curve (in-base):** [[GoTyme Bank reaches 6.5 million clients and P30 billion deposits]] (2025-07, 6.5m / P30bn) → [[GoTyme surpasses 7 million users]] (2025-08) → [[GoTyme reaches 7.8 million customers in the Philippines]] (2025-11) → this note (10m / P53bn, Jul-2026). Deposit CAGR is strong (P30bn→P53bn in ~12m ≈ **+77%**) but loan-to-deposit stayed low, so growth is deposit-gathering, not yet net-interest.
- Verdict: **no arithmetic trading multiple computable**; qualitative — GoTyme leads on users but is *behind Maya on deposits and profitability* and *behind Tonik on monetized lending*. Distribution not computed (no ≥3 comparable valuations).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Under-monetized balance sheet.** Deposit-per-user ~$86 and loan-to-deposit ~11% (vs Maya's larger, profitable book) mean a large cheap deposit base with a thin earning-asset side — NIM stays capped until loans scale; if GoTyme accelerates lending to close the gap, credit-cost risk rises in an unseasoned unsecured book (NPLs undisclosed here).
2. **CICO dependence on partner retail.** The 7-Eleven / Robinsons cash network is the moat *and* a single point of dependence — pricing/exclusivity/availability changes at the partners hit the core differentiator directly (margin captured by the rails owner). Second-order: as digital payments displace cash, today's CICO edge decays.
3. **Profitability lag vs a rising bar.** Maya (profitable) and Tonik (standalone-profitable) have already cleared the bar GoTyme now guides to only in 2027; the recent US$13.9m parent top-up underscores continued burn, so the unit is not self-funding and stays dependent on shareholder support.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` The milestone re-anchors the PH race from *who has the most users* to *who monetizes deposits* — on that axis GoTyme is not clearly winning despite leading on sign-ups. Falsifiable thesis: 10m users is a vanity metric unless loan-to-deposit and deposit-per-user climb over the next 2–4 quarters; the 2027 breakeven holds only if the loan book scales without an NPL spike. Triggers to watch: next loan-book / NPL print, deposit-per-user trend, and Maya's/UnionDigital's deposit figures for relative standing. Not directly investable (private); read-through is indirect via JG Summit-linked names and the TymeGroup IPO track.
