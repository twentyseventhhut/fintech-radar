 Бизнес «Родных полей» продан за 11,7 млрд рублей 
Покупателем 100% долей в ООО «Родные поля» стало АО «Тиара Холдинг», следует из протокола в ГИС «Торги». Сумма сделки составила 11,7 млрд руб. «Тиара Холдинг» стал единственным претендентом, подавшим заявку на участие в торгах, уточняет " Коммерсант ".
Зернотрейдер «Родные поля» ранее был известен как ТД «Риф» и считался одним из крупнейших экспортеров зерна в России. Его владельцем был Петр Ходыкин. Но в 2024 году компания поменяла название, а в 2025-м была обращена в доход государства по иску Генпрокуратуры. Ведомство указало, что господин Ходыкин также имел гражданство Сент-Китс и Невис (Британия) и как иностранный резидент не получил разрешение правительства РФ на участие в стратегическом предприятии. Предприниматель оспаривал это в суде, но вернуть бизнес ему не удалось.
АО «Тиара Холдинг» зарегистрировано в апреле 2025 года, учредители компании не раскрываются, гендиректор — Дмитрий Шевчук. По данным СПАРК, у «Тиара Холдинга» общая электронная почта с ООО «Маслоналивной терминал» (МТ) и ООО «Зерновой терминал» (ЗТ), которые занимаются перевалкой масла и зерна в порту Оля в Астраханской области. Согласно ЕГРЮЛ, компании МТ и ЗТ были выделены из АО «Морской торговый порт Оля», бенефициаром которого считается Джамалдин Пашаев. Текущие собственники МТ и ЗТ скрыты.
В протоколе собрания участников от сентября 2025 года владельцем ООО ЗТ названо ООО «Арион Холдингс», у которого были общие проекты с «Деметра-Холдингом», объединяющим активы в области экспортной логистики и торговли зерном. В начале 2025 года «Арион Холдингс» и «Деметра-Холдинг» почти одновременно получили доли в судоходной компании «Астрол».
Именно «Деметра-Холдинг» два собеседника издания на аграрном рынке называют конечным покупателем «Родных полей». Хотя в самой компании информацию не подтверждают. «Деметра-Холдинг» не принимал участия в аукционе и не комментирует действия других участников зернового рынка, заявил представитель группы.
Сейчас «Родные поля» не ведут торговлю зерном, говорят два собеседника издания на аграрном рынке. Хотя инфраструктура, по их словам, поддерживается в рабочем состоянии.
Портфель включает одиннадцать судов класса «река—море», вагонный парк из 1,64 тыс. вагонов, включая более 700 инновационных, а также портовый терминал в Азове мощностью 3,5 млн тонн в год. Выручка ООО «Родные поля» в 2025 году снизилась в 42,5 раза год к году, до 2,32 млрд руб., чистый убыток составил 6,39 млрд руб. против прибыли в размере 5,55 млрд руб. годом ранее.
Инвестиционный директор BGP Capital Юрий Левицкий объясняет, что фактически продан был уже не трейдерский бизнес, а имущественный комплекс. Актив он называет потенциально интересным: построить сопоставимый терминал с нуля дольше и дороже, чем купить готовый. Исходя из этого, господин Левицкий полагает, что круг конечных интересантов портфеля изначально был ограничен. Это могут быть вертикально интегрированный агрохолдинг или экспортер, которому есть чем загрузить терминал и флот, рассуждает он. Другим возможным вариантом эксперт считает покупку «Родных полей» портово-логистическим оператором южного направления, заинтересованным в инфраструктурных объектах. Нельзя исключать и участие структур, связанных с госбанками или госкомпаниями, добавляет Юрий Левицкий.
Директор «Совэкона» Андрей Сизов отмечает, что терминал «Родных полей» — мелководный. Напрямую вести с него поставки можно преимущественно в Турцию, торговля с другими странами предполагает перевалку и прямую зависимость от глубоководных портов, таких как Тамань. Существующая конъюнктура рынка, по его словам, также не предполагает значимых вложений. Экспортный потенциал зернового рынка сокращается из-за падения урожая, и доходы от эксплуатации терминалов постепенно будут снижаться, считает господин Сизов.

# Context-enrichment: Rodnye Polya (ex-TD Rif) grain-trading business sold for RUB 11.7bn to Tiara Holding
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5. This is a Russian agribusiness / grain-export-infrastructure M&A story — NOT a fintech/agentic-commerce story. The stage [1] `industry/agentic-commerce` tag is wrong; the correct read is agri commodity trading + port/logistics infrastructure, with a state-asset-privatization and sovereign-wealth angle. Weight to fintech radar is low._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- Rosimushchestvo (Russian Federal Property Agency) sold 100% of OOO "Rodnye Polya" (formerly Trading House "RIF", once Russia's largest grain exporter) via a GIS "Torgi" state auction. Winning bid: RUB 11.7bn — the **starting price**, i.e. no competitive uplift. Reporting (Vedomosti, Kommersant) notes it was sold **on the third attempt**, and **Tiara Holding was the sole bidder**. So "sold for 11.7bn" is a floor-price clearance of a distressed state asset, not price discovery.
- What was actually sold is **not a trading business but a property/infrastructure complex** (per BGP Capital's Yuri Levitsky, cited in source): the company **no longer trades grain**; infrastructure is kept "in working order". Assets: Azov port terminal (3.5 Mt/yr, **shallow-water** — Sovecon's Sizov notes it can ship mainly to Turkey directly, otherwise needs transshipment via deepwater ports like Taman), 11 river-sea class vessels, ~1,640 railcars (700+ "innovative"), plus land plots in Moscow, Rostov-on-Don and Rostov region.
- Financials confirm the distress: 2025 revenue RUB 2.32bn, **down 42.5x YoY**; net loss RUB 6.39bn vs RUB 5.55bn profit a year earlier. In 2023 it shipped ~11.3 Mt (~16% of Russia's grain exports) — the collapse is the direct result of losing the business.
- **Why structured this way / what it reveals:** This is the endgame of a **state expropriation → resale** chain. TD RIF's owner Petr Khodykin lost the asset in 2024–25 when the Prosecutor General turned it to state income (pretext: Khodykin also held St Kitts & Nevis citizenship and, as a foreign resident, lacked government approval to control a "strategic enterprise"; his court challenge failed). Practically, RIF had earlier been squeezed out via **withheld phytosanitary export documents** and a slashed H2 export quota (external reporting). So the RUB 11.7bn is the price the state extracts on the way out of an asset it took — and the sole-bidder, third-attempt, floor-price structure tells you the buyer pool was **pre-narrowed** to a strategic insider, not an open market.
- **Who the buyer really is:** AO "Tiara Holding" was registered April 2025, founders undisclosed, CEO Dmitry Shevchuk — a shell with no track record. SPARK shows it shares an email with OOO "Maslonalivnoy Terminal" and OOO "Zernovoy Terminal" (oil/grain transshipment at Olya port, Astrakhan), which were spun out of Morskoy Torgovy Port Olya (beneficiary reportedly Jamaldin Pashaev). Zernovoy Terminal's Sept-2025 owner was OOO "Arion Holdings", which had joint projects with **Demetra-Holding** (both took stakes in shipping co "Astrol" in early 2025). Two agri-market sources name **Demetra-Holding** as the ultimate buyer; Demetra denies participating in the auction. Tiara is thus almost certainly a **nominee/SPV** fronting for a larger strategic group.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Demetra-Holding** — Russia's largest vertically integrated grain export-logistics group (trading + infrastructure). Notably, a large stake is held by **Oman's sovereign wealth fund** (external reporting) — the finance/sovereign-capital angle. If Demetra is the true buyer, this consolidates yet another terminal + fleet under the dominant player.
- Structural context (external): ~**80% of Azov-Black Sea basin grain exports are now concentrated in ~5 companies**, vs ~15 companies for the same 80% in 2020. RIF's forced exit and this resale are a piece of that consolidation.
- Corpus peers (same beat, same source Mergers.ru, same weeks): [[Gorizont Industries seeks to buy Louis Dreyfus Russian assets]] (foreign grain trader's Russian assets being absorbed by a Russian buyer — same "de-foreignization + consolidation" pattern), [[Rosatom decides to buy out Shishkarev's stake in Delo Group]] (state/quasi-state absorption of transport-logistics infrastructure). Broader Russian state-driven asset transfers this month: [[Russia transfers Akzo Nobel local assets to state management]].
- **Position:** For the acquiring group, this is bolt-on consolidation (a cheap, ready-built terminal + fleet). Levitsky's point — building a comparable terminal from scratch is slower and costlier than buying one — is the real logic; the buyer pool was inherently small (a vertically integrated agroholding/exporter that can load the terminal and fleet, a southern port-logistics operator, or state-bank/state-company-linked structures).

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Rodnye Polya = ex-TD RIF:** for nearly a decade Russia's #1 grain exporter (11.3 Mt / ~16% share in 2023), owner Petr Khodykin. 2024: renamed Rodnye Polya. 2024–25: prosecutorial nationalization → state income. 2025: no longer trading; revenue collapses 42.5x; 2026: auctioned off.
- **Why it played out this way (analysis):** The asset's value migrated from the *trading franchise* (destroyed by regulatory/quota pressure) to the *physical infrastructure* (terminal, ships, railcars). The state extracted the trading flow first, then monetized the shell's hard assets. A distressed, single-bidder, floor-price sale is the logical exit for a politically taken asset whose going-concern value was already zeroed out.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty:** Low. No new business, product, or market — this is a change of nominal ownership of a dormant infrastructure complex. The "value-add" is purely the physical assets (terminal capacity, fleet, railcars, land), not any operating franchise.
- **Where the margin/value sits (analysis):** The real value is control of **Azov export capacity + river-sea fleet** feeding the deepwater ports. But the terminal is **shallow-water** (Turkey-direct only; otherwise dependent on Taman transshipment), and Russia's exportable surplus is **shrinking** (weaker harvest), so terminal economics are structurally softening (Sovecon). The margin accrues to whoever already has grain volume and downstream deepwater access to load this fleet — i.e. a big vertically integrated group (Demetra), not a standalone operator. A shell like Tiara has no volume, reinforcing the nominee read.
- **Fintech/finance relevance:** Marginal. The only finance-radar hooks are (a) sovereign-wealth capital (Oman fund behind Demetra) quietly consolidating a strategic Russian export chain, and (b) a state-privatization-of-expropriated-assets mechanism. Neither is a payments/banking/fintech development. Importance to a fintech digest: low.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** Watch for confirmation of the ultimate beneficiary (Demetra / Arion / Pashaev / Olya-port structures) once ownership filters through EGRYUL/SPARK. Expect the terminal + fleet to be plugged into the buyer's existing export logistics rather than a RIF-style trading revival.
- **Sentiment (external + analyst):** Analysts frame the buyer pool as pre-narrowed and the price as a floor, not a premium — consistent with a managed transfer. Broader backdrop is bearish for terminal returns: shrinking exportable surplus, Azov-basin shipping disruptions (Don-Azov channel halts reported July 2026), and ongoing consolidation (~5 firms = ~80% of basin exports).
- **Second-order (analysis):** Continued concentration of Russian grain exports into a handful of state-adjacent / sovereign-backed players raises single-point-of-control and sanctions/geopolitical exposure over the export chain — relevant to trade-finance and commodity-financing risk, but only tangentially to fintech.

### Red-team / challenge questions

1. **Is this a fintech story at all?** No. It's Russian agri-commodity + port/logistics infrastructure M&A, executed as a state-asset auction. The `industry/agentic-commerce` tag from stage [1] is wrong. Correct framing: agribusiness / grain-export infrastructure, with a state-privatization + sovereign-capital angle. → weighs down radar importance.

2. **Is RUB 11.7bn a real valuation or a floor-price clearance?** It's the **starting price**, sole bidder, third auction attempt (Vedomosti). No price discovery — this reflects a managed transfer, not a market-cleared valuation. Treat the number as an administrative floor.

3. **Who actually bought it?** Officially AO "Tiara Holding" (registered Apr-2025, undisclosed founders, CEO Shevchuk). Two agri-market sources name **Demetra-Holding** as the ultimate buyer via a nominee chain (shared email with Olya-port terminal cos → Arion Holdings → Demetra joint projects). Demetra denies bidding. Verdict: almost certainly an SPV fronting a strategic group. **Open** on formal beneficiary until EGRYUL confirms.

4. **Trading business or physical assets?** Physical assets only — the company no longer trades grain (BGP Capital). Sold: Azov terminal (3.5 Mt/yr), 11 river-sea vessels, ~1,640 railcars, land. The trading franchise was already destroyed.

5. **Why did the franchise collapse?** Regulatory expropriation: withheld phytosanitary export docs + slashed H2 quota (World Grain), then prosecutorial nationalization of TD RIF in 2024–25 (owner Khodykin's foreign residency / "strategic enterprise" pretext). Revenue fell 42.5x to RUB 2.32bn in 2025; net loss RUB 6.39bn.

6. **How strategic is the terminal really?** Limited — it's **shallow-water**, so direct exports mainly to Turkey; other destinations need transshipment via deepwater (Taman) (Sovecon). Value is as bolt-on capacity for a group with existing volume + deepwater access, not standalone.

7. **Is there a finance/capital angle worth flagging?** Yes, one: Demetra-Holding is partly owned by **Oman's sovereign wealth fund** (Moscow Times) — sovereign capital quietly consolidating a strategic Russian export chain. This is the only genuinely finance-adjacent hook.

8. **Does this deal move any fintech/payments/banking metric?** No. No payments, banking, lending, or digital-finance dimension. Only tangential trade-finance/commodity-financing risk from export-chain concentration.

9. **Is it a new event or a re-report?** Fresh — this is a distinct M&A closing (the RIF/Rodnye Polya auction result, ~9–10 July 2026). Not covered by any prior corpus note. Siblings [[Gorizont Industries seeks to buy Louis Dreyfus Russian assets]] and [[Rosatom decides to buy out Shishkarev's stake in Delo Group]] are different deals on the same beat, not duplicates.

10. **What's the second-order market effect?** Further concentration of Russian grain exports (~5 firms ≈ 80% of Azov-Black Sea basin, vs ~15 in 2020) — raises single-point-of-control and geopolitical/sanctions exposure over the export chain. Bearish terminal economics (shrinking surplus, Don-Azov shipping disruptions).

11. **Why sole bidder / third attempt?** Signals a pre-narrowed buyer pool (a managed transfer to a strategic insider), not distressed lack of interest per se. First two auctions failing then a floor-price sale to one bidder is the classic pattern for a politically taken asset.

12. **What does the buyer gain economically?** A ready-built terminal + fleet cheaper/faster than greenfield (Levitsky). But only accretive if the buyer has grain volume and downstream deepwater access to load it — which a shell cannot, reinforcing the nominee thesis.

13. **What is silent / unconfirmed?** True beneficial owner; whether Demetra denial is technical (nominee bid) vs substantive; financing source of the RUB 11.7bn; future operating plan for the dormant infrastructure. All **open**.

Importance: 2/5 — Notable Russian agri/logistics M&A with a real state-expropriation-and-resale narrative and a sovereign-wealth (Oman/Demetra) consolidation angle, but essentially zero fintech/finance-radar relevance: no payments, banking, lending, or digital-finance dimension. It is a well-reported, fresh event of sector interest, hence 2 rather than 1; the mis-tag toward agentic-commerce should be disregarded.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian grain export/trading is a strategically-controlled commodity-logistics sector, not a fintech vertical — finance relevance is via state-forced M&A, infrastructure valuation and sanctions/ownership overhang. Structure: highly consolidated post-2022 — the top-4 traders control ~75% of exports from Russia's Black Sea terminals, up from ~45% six years earlier (per AJOT/Reuters). Russia's share of world wheat trade is ~22% in 2024/25, down from ~28% in 2023/24 (per industry sources via zerno.ru / eзерно.ру). "Why now": (1) state expropriation as a consolidation tool — Rodnye Polya (ex-TD Rif, owner Pyotr Khodykin) was nationalised in 2025 by the Prosecutor General over the owner's St. Kitts & Nevis citizenship / foreign-resident status without strategic-enterprise clearance, then re-sold via Rosimushchestvo auction (third attempt — per Vedomosti); (2) shrinking export potential — Sovecon's Andrei Sizov notes falling harvest and thinning terminal economics; (3) the Azov terminal is shallow-water, so direct shipments go mainly to Turkey, with other routes dependent on deep-water ports (Taman) — capping standalone value.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: export-quota share, terminal throughput (mt/yr), transshipment volume, deep- vs shallow-water access. Key players: Demetra-Holding (largest wheat exporter, >50% of Russian wheat per S&P; 45% held via Southern Sea Investment for Oman's sovereign fund since VTB's 2023 exit; owns Novorossiysk grain terminal + 50% of Taman complex), Aston, UGC — the incumbent oligopoly. Rodnye Polya no longer trades grain; only its infrastructure (terminal + fleet + railcars) is maintained. Recent moves: sources (Kommersant/Vedomosti, 2026-07-09/10) name Demetra-Holding as the likely ultimate buyer behind Tiara Holding (registered Apr-2025, undisclosed founders, shared email with Astrakhan port-Olya oil/grain terminals; links via Arion Holdings to Demetra's Astrol shipping co) — Demetra publicly denies bidding. Protagonist's position: a distressed asset-complex being absorbed into an already-dominant player (analysis) — moat is scarce port/rail intangibles + license-adjacency, not a trading franchise.

**Comps & multiples.** The BGP Capital director (Yuri Levitsky) frames it explicitly: what was sold is a property/infrastructure complex, not a trading business — buyer universe was narrow (vertically-integrated agroholding/exporter or a southern port-logistics operator). Deal: RUB 11.7bn (~$144m external est., per The Moscow Times). Asset base: Azov terminal 3.5mt/yr + 11 river-sea vessels + 1,640 railcars (>700 innovative). Crude arithmetic: RUB 11.7bn / 3.5mt = ~RUB 3,340/t of annual terminal capacity — but this ignores the fleet/railcars, so it overstates the terminal-only figure; treat as a blended asset price, not a per-tonne comp. Earnings multiples NOT computable on a going-concern basis: FY2025 revenue collapsed 42.5x YoY to RUB 2.32bn with a RUB 6.39bn net LOSS (vs RUB 5.55bn profit FY2024) — negative earnings, so EV/EBITDA and P/E = no data; a revenue multiple (RUB 11.7bn / RUB 2.32bn ≈ 5.0x) is meaningless given trading has stopped. Internal comps (state-forced / infrastructure auctions in RU, same corpus month): [[Belaya Skala buys 47.2bn ruble asset portfolio]] (Raven Russia warehouse portfolio auctioned at RUB 47.2bn vs RUB 92.5bn start / RUB 46.3bn floor — same Rosimushchestvo-style forced-sale-below-appraisal pattern, single credible bidder dynamic) and [[Rosatom decides to buy out Shishkarev's stake in Delo Group]] (strategic transport/logistics consolidation into a state-linked owner). Both corroborate the read that RU strategic-asset deals clear near reserve price with a lone qualified bidder. Verdict: price sits at a distressed/reserve-price level for the asset complex; not comparable to going-concern grain-trader valuations.

**Risk flags.**
1. Single-bidder / opaque-beneficiary execution risk — Tiara Holding was the only bidder, is a year-old shell with undisclosed owners; if Demetra is the true buyer, effective further concentration of an already >50%-share exporter raises antitrust/political-reversibility risk (the asset was itself created by an expropriation that could set precedent).
2. Valuation-basis risk — priced as an idle infrastructure complex, not a cash-flowing trader; FY2025 net loss RUB 6.39bn and near-zero revenue mean the buyer pays for optionality (terminal/fleet/rail) whose economics Sovecon says are deteriorating with a shrinking export base — downside if throughput can't be filled.
3. Sanctions / ownership overhang — a shallow-water terminal skewed to Turkey, foreign (Oman-fund) exposure at the likely parent, and a title derived from state seizure over a foreign-citizen owner all leave the asset legally and geopolitically encumbered; secondary transferability and Western counterparty access are constrained.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is consolidation, not a re-rating: a distressed strategic asset moves from expropriation into (probably) the dominant, state-and-Oman-linked exporter, tightening an already oligopolistic top-4 structure. Watch for: SPARK/EGRUL beneficiary disclosure confirming or denying the Demetra link, and any FAS antitrust review — confirmation of Demetra as owner would be the trigger validating the "state-orchestrated grain-export concentration" thesis; a genuinely independent operator loading the terminal/fleet would falsify it.
