## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
[0] TL;DR
Stillman Bank — an independent northern-Illinois community bank founded in 1882, ~6 branches, retail/business/wealth/farm clients — has selected Quinte Financial Technologies' flagship **CaseHUB** platform to modernize and automate its **Regulation E** dispute operations. CaseHUB will centralize fraud, payment-dispute and complaint case management, automating the dispute lifecycle (including Reg E reporting/timelines) and integrating directly into the bank's existing **Jack Henry Banno** digital-banking stack. This is a vendor-selection / partnership item announced 2026-07-15 (BusinessWire), covered by FinTech Futures 2026-07-17.

[1] What happened
- Quinte announced Stillman Bank as a new CaseHUB customer to "strengthen and modernize" its Reg E dispute operations.
- Use case: manage high-risk operational cases — fraud, payment disputes, complaints — for retail, business and wealth-management clients from a single interface.
- Capabilities cited: case tracking, workflow automation incl. Regulation E reporting, access to transaction history/documentation, policy-controlled chargeback handling aligned to Visa/Mastercard rules.
- Deployment note: integration into the bank's Jack Henry Banno digital-banking platform.
- No deal size, contract length or go-live date disclosed.

[2] Who / what is involved
- **Quinte Financial Technologies** — NYC-based (founded 2019), cloud-based enterprise case-management vendor targeting community banks, credit unions, CUSOs and fintechs. Debuted at FinovateSpring 2025 (demoed Advanced Dispute Manager). CaseHUB is its flagship, positioned around AI-supported workflow decisioning; markets claims of up to ~40% reduction in handling time and auto-resolution of low-value disputes (sub-$25 cases ≈ 40% of volume). Other announced partners: Mountain America Credit Union, OrboGraph (check-fraud). No disclosed institutional funding to date (was raising as of 2025).
- **Stillman Bank** — small independent community bank; a representative regional design win rather than a marquee logo.
- **CaseHUB** — enterprise case-management / dispute + fraud + complaint platform; enhanced edition relaunched May 2026 (expanded configurability).

[3] Why it matters / context
- Fits the broader **dispute-automation / regtech** wave: Datos Insights pegged U.S. disputed charges at ~$11B, expected to rise ~40% by 2026, pushing issuers toward automation to meet Reg E (debit/ACH) and Reg Z (credit) timelines while cutting operational losses.
- Competitive field is crowded: **Quavo (QFD)**, **Pega Smart Dispute** (gen-AI/agentic), **Chargebacks911**, and merchant-side players like Chargeflow. Quinte's edge is the community-bank / credit-union segment plus tight core-platform (Jack Henry) integration — a segment larger vendors under-serve.
- Related internal coverage: [[Chargeflow raises $35M for chargeback automation]] (merchant-side chargeback automation, adjacent theme).

[4] Open questions / watch-items
- Deal economics and contract scope undisclosed; single small-bank win — low standalone signal, meaningful mainly as a proof point in Quinte's community-bank land-grab.
- Whether Quinte can convert Finovate visibility + Jack Henry integration into a repeatable channel vs. Quavo/Pega.
- Reg E automation carries compliance risk (provisional-credit timelines, error-resolution) — execution/audit outcomes worth watching.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Stillman Bank (independent community bank, northern Illinois) adopting Quinte's CaseHUB sits in **issuer-side dispute & case-management software** for banks/credit unions — distinct from merchant-side chargeback tools. Sector size: global chargeback-management software ~**$2.18bn–$3.0bn in 2026**, growing to ~$8.7–9.3bn by 2034–35 at **~13.5–15% CAGR** (per BusinessResearchInsights / IntelMarketResearch / Market.us, as of 2026 — vendor reports, treat as directional). North America ~39% share. Structure: **fragmented and bifurcated** — merchant-side (Chargebacks911, Verifi, Midigator, Signifyd, Chargeflow) vs. issuer/FI-side (Quavo, Pega, Quinte). Barriers are regulatory-knowledge and compliance depth, not capital: Reg E / Reg Z deadline tracking plus Visa/Mastercard/NACHA/Zelle network rules embedded in workflow. **Why now:** (1) rising fraud/dispute volumes push manual FI operations past capacity; (2) AI decisioning now clears low-complexity Reg E claims — Quavo cites 20–25% auto-resolution, CaseHUB claims up to 40% handling-time cut; (3) community banks/CUs, historically on manual or fragmented tooling, are the greenfield the sector is now selling into.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: auto-resolution rate on eligible disputes, handling-time reduction, disputes processed/yr, Reg E cycle-time compliance. Key issuer-side players + basis of competition (compliance coverage × automation depth × FI channel fit):
- **Quavo** — category leader for issuers; automates >12.5M consumer disputes/yr; **$300M growth investment from Spectrum Equity, Jul/Aug 2025** (total raised ~$306M). ARIA/AutoPay-AutoDeny decisioning; covers Reg E/Z + Visa/MC/NACHA.
- **Pega / legacy case-management** — broad, incumbent, not dispute-native.
- **Quinte (CaseHUB)** — the protagonist's vendor; positions as unified policy-controlled case system across fraud + disputes + complaints, low/no-code configurability; ADM covers ACH, POS, ATM, checks, wire, Zelle.
Recent moves: Quinte launched CaseHUB and shipped a **May 2026 platform enhancement** (modernized UI, expanded configurability, governed automation). **Position:** Quinte = **niche challenger** to Quavo in the FI segment; the Stillman win is a small community-bank logo, not a scale proof point. Moat = switching costs (embedded compliance workflows, regulatory timelines) + intangibles (rule libraries) `(analysis)`.

**Comps & multiples.** No public multiples — all key names are private; EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E = **no data**.
- **Quavo:** $300M Spectrum Equity investment (2025) — growth-equity round, **valuation undisclosed** `[UNSOURCED]`; scale anchor at 12.5M disputes/yr.
- **Quinte / Stillman:** deal terms undisclosed — no ticket size, no ARR (community-bank SaaS, likely low-five-to-six-figure ACV `(hypothesis)`, not sourced).
- Internal comps (merchant-side, adjacent not identical): [[Chargeflow raises $35M for chargeback automation]] — $35M Series A (incl. $10M debt), Nov 2025, ~$49M total, 15,000 merchant clients, ~80% friendly-fraud win rate; [[Chargebacks911 partners cside to boost fraud prevention]] (merchant-side incumbent).
- Distribution not computed (only one directly comparable issuer-side data point). Qualitative: capital is concentrating on the **issuer-side** ($300M into Quavo) — order of magnitude above merchant-side Series A rounds ($35M), signalling where investors see the durable, compliance-moated opportunity.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Displacement / catch-up risk** — Quinte is a niche challenger to a newly capitalized ($300M) Quavo; a single community-bank win doesn't de-risk competition for larger FIs, and Quavo's war chest can undercut on price/coverage.
2. **AI-automation overclaim vs. compliance liability** — "up to 40% handling-time cut" / auto-decisioning must survive Reg E/Z audit; mis-automated denials create regulatory and reputational exposure that lands on the bank, not the vendor.
3. **Small-account concentration / TAM ceiling** — community-bank ACVs are thin; a book built on sub-scale banks/CUs caps revenue per logo and raises churn/support cost per dollar `(analysis)`.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` FI-side dispute automation is the **re-rating leg** of a market historically framed around merchant chargebacks — Quavo's $300M raise sets the pace and Quinte is fighting for the long tail of community banks/CUs. Falsifiable thesis: if Quinte converts the Stillman-type wins into multi-bank/CUSO or core-integration deals, it's a credible #2; **trigger to watch** — a named CUSO/core-banking (Fiserv/Jack Henry/FIS) distribution partnership or a funding round. Thesis breaks if new logos stay one-off community banks with no platform/channel leverage.
