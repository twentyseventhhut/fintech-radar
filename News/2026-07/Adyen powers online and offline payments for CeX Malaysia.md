# Context-enrichment: Adyen powers online and offline payments for CeX Malaysia
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**Freshness verdict: FRESH.** New single-market retail win (CeX Malaysia, ~11 stores). Not a re-run of any prior note. Adyen merchant-win notes in the corpus cover different merchants — Starbucks EU [[Starbucks taps Adyen for payments across 940+ European stores]], Cathay Pacific [[Cathay Pacific expands global payments partnership with Adyen]], Uber [[Adyen and Uber expand partnership and launch Uber Kiosks]], Careem Pay [[Careem Pay partners with Adyen for remittance scaling]] — so no duplicate. Low importance, not stale.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- Adyen rolled out its **Unified Commerce** platform for **CeX Malaysia** — CeX's Malaysian e-commerce site (my.webuy.com) plus its **~11 physical stores** (mixed corporate-owned + franchise; master franchisee Nuovolife Sdn Bhd). Coverage clusters ~**July 1, 2026**; rollout reportedly began ~**July 2025** (exact date open). Payment methods supported: DuitNow, GrabPay, Visa, Mastercard, JCB, reconciled into one standardized format. (Confirmed: Retail Tech Innovation Hub, itbrief.asia.)
- **De-PR'd:** this is a small, single-country retail acquiring/omnichannel win — 11 stores, one market. The PR frame ("centralizes payment processing and reporting… streamline operations across its franchise network") is real but modest: the concrete problem solved was that CeX franchisees previously held **separate contracts with different local financial institutions**, forcing manual reconciliation. Adyen replaced that patchwork with a single platform + terminal-fleet management + single-view reporting, integrated to CeX's bespoke POS.
- **Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the value is operational consolidation for a franchise network, not a novel capability. No GMV, volume, or contract value disclosed → materiality is undisclosed and, given 11 stores, presumptively immaterial to Adyen (FY2025 processed volume €1,394B). The story is a *reference/showcase* for Unified Commerce in SEA franchise retail, not a financial event.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Malaysia acquiring/gateway market is crowded and local-incumbent-led** in mid-market/SMB: iPay88 (NTT Data), Fiuu (ex-Razer Merchant Services/MOLPay, holds a Mastercard acquiring licence), eGHL/GHL, senangPay, Billplz, Curlec (by Razorpay). Global/regional: Stripe, Checkout.com, 2C2P (Ant International), Airwallex, PayPal. (Sources: xenber.com, thereportcubes.com.) **Fiserv** did not surface in Malaysia-specific listings → its SEA retail-acquiring footprint here is open/unconfirmed.
- **Position:** Adyen competes **up-market** (enterprise/omnichannel single-platform), not pure local SMB acquiring. Local single-channel gateways struggle to give a multi-store retailer one cross-channel reporting view — that gap is exactly what CeX bought. (Analysis; no per-vendor market-share stat found.)
- **Why the landscape is this way (2nd order):** the Malaysian gateway market is small (~US$182M in 2024 → ~US$357M by 2030, ~11.75% CAGR), so global players like Adyen don't chase SMB volume here; they win *omnichannel/multi-country* retailers where their single-platform + reporting moat pays off. CeX (11 stores today, part of a ~645-store global chain) is a foothold/land-and-expand play, not a volume grab.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Fits Adyen's clear 2025–2026 pattern of **unified-commerce retail land-grabs**: Starbucks 943 EU stores in 7 weeks [[Starbucks taps Adyen for payments across 940+ European stores]], Uber/Uber Kiosks [[Adyen and Uber expand partnership and launch Uber Kiosks]], Cathay Pacific direct acquiring [[Cathay Pacific expands global payments partnership with Adyen]], adidas [[Adyen expands global Adyen Giving partnership with adidas]]. Also broader platform push: Talon.One loyalty M&A (€750M) [[Adyen agrees to acquire loyalty platform Talon.One for €750 million]], Adyen Agentic [[Adyen launches Adyen Agentic for AI commerce]], SAP Unified Payment [[Adyen and SAP launch SAP Unified Payment for Commerce Cloud]].
- Adyen has been **live in Malaysia since local acquiring launched July 6, 2020** (Adyen Malaysia Sdn Bhd; KL office), with Singapore as its APAC/SEA hub (MAS Major Payment Institution licence). So this is Adyen exercising an existing local footprint, not entering a new market.
- **Why Adyen acts this way (structural):** payments processing is a commodity take-rate business → Adyen needs the *software multiple* that comes from being the single omnichannel + reporting + (increasingly) loyalty/agentic layer. Every franchise/multi-store retailer it consolidates is a data + reporting lock-in that a single-channel local gateway can't replicate. APAC volume grew +25% in FY2025 (led by Japan/Australia); SEA franchise retail is the next fill-in.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty: low.** Unified Commerce is an existing Adyen product; DuitNow/GrabPay support is standard for Malaysia. Nothing technically new here — it's a deployment.
- **Traction: real but tiny and unquantified.** It's a *live* rollout (not "will explore"), which counts — but across only ~11 stores with **no disclosed GMV/volume**. Contrast Starbucks (943 stores) where traction was concrete.
- **Who captures the margin:** Adyen captures the acquiring take-rate + reporting stickiness; CeX gets opex savings (less manual reconciliation) and franchise-network standardization. The durable value to Adyen is the *reference* and the option to expand as CeX scales stores in SEA — the transactional revenue today is negligible.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** watch for store-count expansion (CeX Malaysia was CeX's first SEA location; land-and-expand into more SEA markets is the upside). Adyen's SEA/Japan momentum is the backdrop; Talon.One + Orb + Agentic integrations [[Adyen reshuffles leadership to drive Talon.One and Orb integrations]] are the bigger strategic story.
- **Sentiment:** neutral. This is routine merchant-win PR; not a stock-moving or thesis-changing event. Adyen FY2025: net revenue €2,364M, EBITDA €1,246M (53% margin), POS volume €311B (+34%).
- **Risk/what's silent:** no economics, no exclusivity, no ticket size disclosed; whether CeX uses Adyen outside Malaysia is unconfirmed (appears Malaysia-only). Franchise churn and CeX's own retail health (second-hand electronics is cyclical) are unstated risks.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Unified commerce = one platform/one contract processing a merchant's in-store, online and in-app payments with centralized reconciliation — the pitch behind CeX Malaysia's move to "centralize payment processing and reporting" across a franchise network. SEA is the "why now": Google/Temasek/Bain projected regional digital-payment TPV to pass $1trn by 2025, up from ~$600bn in 2021 (per GTB e-Conomy, via Statista, as of 2025); Malaysia e-commerce is projected at ~$23.5bn by 2027 at ~16% CAGR with >80% of buyers on e-wallets/cards/instant transfer (per Sellercraft outlook, 2025). Structure: acquiring is fragmented by geography and value-chain layer (gateway → acquirer → scheme → processor); Adyen's edge is owning the full stack on one ledger, which is the switching-cost/data moat. Driver: omnichannel retail (click-and-collect, inventory sync, one profile across channels) is pulling merchants off stitched-together POS + PSP stacks toward single-vendor unified commerce (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: processed/point-of-sale volume (TPV), take rate (net revenue / volume, bps), and net-revenue growth. Adyen reported (IR, H2 2025 shareholder letter, 2026-02-12): H2 2025 net revenue €1,270.7mn, +17% YoY / +21% constant-currency; EBITDA €702.1mn, margin 55%; FY2025 processed volume €1,394.3bn, +8% YoY (+21% ex one single large-volume customer); point-of-sale volume €173.1bn, +26% YoY; FY2025 EBITDA margin 53% vs 50% in 2024. Players & basis of competition: Adyen (single-stack unified commerce, enterprise), Stripe (developer-first, mostly digital), and European incumbents Worldline / Nexi / Ingenico competing on distribution and terminal fleets. Recent peer moves in the base: [[Worldline launches One Commerce in the UK]] (2026-02-23), [[Ingenico and Visa collaborate on unified commerce solutions]] (2026-03-19), [[Euronet acquires PaynoPain to expand omnichannel payments]] (2026-04-15), [[Airwallex unveils POS Payments for omnichannel transactions]] (2025-12-12), [[Adyen and SAP launch SAP Unified Payment for Commerce Cloud]] (2026-05-15). Position: Adyen is a leader in enterprise unified commerce (POS volume +26% YoY outpacing total +8% signals in-person share gains) (analysis); moat = switching costs + single-ledger data. CeX Malaysia itself is a small franchise win, not a material revenue event — de-PR: no ticket size, volume or economics disclosed.

**Comps & multiples.** Public-market multiples for Adyen diverge sharply across free sources, so treat as a range, not a point. Per stockanalysis/multiples.vc/Yahoo (as of Jun 2026): market cap ≈ $28.6–30bn (≈ €24.8bn); EV/EBITDA ≈ 11.2–13.0x; forward P/E ≈ 22x; EV/Revenue variously quoted 6.0x (on gross/processed-revenue basis) up to ~18x (on net-revenue basis) — the 3x spread reflects gross-vs-net revenue definitions, so a single clean EV/Rev is `[UNSOURCED]`. Sanity check on net revenue: FY2025 net revenue ≈ €2.49bn (H1 €1,221.2mn + H2 €1,270.7mn, IR) → against ≈ €25bn cap that is ~10x P/net-sales, well inside hyper-growth range and arguably justified by 20%+ CC growth + 53% EBITDA margin (analysis). Peers trade at a steep discount: Worldline ~0.6x EV/Revenue / ~3.5x EV/EBITDA and Nexi ~1.1x revenue (per multiples.vc/Digital Transactions, 2026) — reflecting stalled growth, not comparability; Adyen's premium is a growth-quality re-rating, not an outlier to flag. Stripe (private) marked ~$159bn at a Feb-2026 tender on ~$5.8bn 2025 net revenue (per press) — richer than Adyen but not listed. Distribution not computed: only 2 clean listed comps and both are structurally impaired.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Take rate / net-vs-gross compression.** POS and unified-commerce growth is volume-led; Adyen does not break out take rate in bps for this segment, and net revenue (+17%) trailed the implied volume mix — margin at scale depends on take rate holding, which is `[UNSOURCED]` at segment level. Second-order: if enterprise pricing keeps stepping down with volume tiers, EV/net-revenue premium is harder to defend.
2. **Long-tail SME/franchise wins are dilutive to the enterprise story.** Deals like CeX Malaysia are cheap to announce but immaterial; concentration risk cuts the other way — a "previously referenced single large-volume customer" already distorts FY2025 volume growth (8% reported vs 21% ex-customer), so Adyen's headline volume is sensitive to a handful of accounts.
3. **Regional disintermediation by local rails.** In SEA/Malaysia, e-wallets, QR and instant-transfer schemes carry >80% of consumer payments; if merchants route more volume to low-cost domestic rails, the acquirer's margin on that flow shrinks — value migrates down the stack.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Unified commerce is where the acquiring premium is being defended: whoever owns the in-store + online ledger on one contract keeps switching costs high and take-rate visibility (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: Adyen's POS volume keeps compounding >20% YoY and outgrows total processed volume through 2026, evidencing enterprise unified-commerce share gains. Trigger/what breaks it: watch the next business updates (Q1 2026 was 2026-05-06) — if segment net-revenue growth decelerates below CC-guided 20–22% or POS growth converges to total-volume growth, the omnichannel moat thesis weakens.

*Earnings layer on Adyen (IR-covered): the CeX Malaysia item is a customer/product announcement, not a print. Reviewed against Adyen's own latest filing — Q1 2026 Business Update, published 2026-05-06 (period ending 2026-03-31). Adyen reports profit/EBITDA only in H1/H2 financials, so the Q1 update carries net revenue + operating metrics only (unaudited); no EBITDA/margin/EPS this period.*

**Verdict (headline read).** IN-LINE (mild deceleration). Q1 2026 net revenue €620.8m, +16% YoY reported / +20% constant-currency (cc) — explicitly "in line with our expectations." Processed volume €382.0bn, +21% YoY. Growth "broad based" and resilient; no EBITDA/EPS this quarter. Financial objectives reaffirmed. Read is solid-but-cooling: reported growth 22%→16% YoY vs the year-ago quarter, though cc 20% sits at the bottom of the FY 20–22% cc guide. FX is the main reported-vs-cc gap (~4pts). Relevant to the CeX Malaysia story: in-person/Unified Commerce is the strongest reported segment.

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Net revenue €620.8m, +16% YoY (+20% cc) — vs Q1 2025 €534.7m, +22% YoY (+21% cc). Reported growth decelerated ~6pts; cc slowed 21%→20%.
- Processed volume €382.0bn, +21% YoY — vs Q1 2025 €314.8bn, +6% YoY (+25% ex single large-volume customer). The single-large-customer drag has now lapped, so the headline PV number normalized.
- EBITDA / EBITDA margin / net income / EPS: not disclosed in a Q1 Business Update — "no data" this period (next full P&L: H1 2026 financials, ~Aug 2026).
- Take rate: not reported as a standalone line in the Q1 update — "no data" (last disclosed at FY: for reference, H2 2025 shareholder letter framed processed volume €745.3bn +12% YoY / +19% ex single customer).

**By segment / driver.**
- **Digital** — net revenue €349.6m, +9% YoY (+13% cc); PV +15% YoY. Slowest segment; driven by share-of-wallet gains with largest content & subscriptions customers. (Prior year Q1 2025 Digital was +13% YoY, so ~4pts slower reported.)
- **Unified Commerce (in-person / POS / omnichannel — the CeX-relevant segment)** — net revenue €196.2m, +24% YoY (+28% cc); PV +26% YoY. Resilience in core luxury and small-format retail, plus strength in hospitality and food & beverage. 588 customers processing across multiple regions; 474 processing across channels "at scale." Transacting Unified Commerce terminals reached 453k, +85k YoY (vs 368k, +89k YoY a year ago). This is the fastest reported net-revenue segment ex-Platforms; note deceleration vs Q1 2025's +31% YoY, and Adyen flagged "a less pronounced shift in volumes between Digital and Unified Commerce" than prior quarters. A CeX-type online-plus-offline unified-commerce win is directly in this growth engine.
- **Platforms** — net revenue €75.0m, +35% YoY (+40% cc); PV +38% YoY. 34 customers processing over €1bn annually; 264k platform business customers (vs 177k YoY); platform transacting terminals 315k, +94k YoY. Fastest-growing segment but decelerating off a smaller base (Q1 2025 was +63% YoY).

**vs expectations / prior period.** Company states Q1 in line with its own expectations. No pre-earnings preview item on this print in the corpus and no public consensus retrieved in-pipeline, so beat/miss is assessed qualitatively vs prior periods, exact Street number [UNSOURCED]: reported net-revenue growth decelerated to +16% YoY (from +22% in Q1 2025 and low-20s% run-rate through 2025); cc +20% is at the low end of the reaffirmed FY guide. Neutral-to-slightly-soft, not a clean beat. `[[Adyen N.V. quarterly_results 2026-05-06]]` · trend vs `[[Adyen N.V. shareholder_letter 2026-02-12]]` (H2 2025 PV +12% YoY / +19% ex-customer).

**Guidance / forward.** Reaffirmed ("financial objectives remain the same"): 2026 net revenue growth 20–22% YoY cc; capex ≤5% of net revenue; 550–650 net new FTEs in 2026 (88 added this period). EBITDA margin objective re-framed to a longer horizon: "increase EBITDA margin to above 55% by 2028; 2026 EBITDA margin broadly in line with 2025" — a shift from the earlier "above 50% in 2026" wording seen in prior updates, i.e. the near-term 2026 margin bar is now "flat vs 2025" rather than a step-up, with the >50% having effectively been met and the new anchor pushed to >55% by 2028. Management tone (CFO Ethan Tandowsky): confident, "disciplined execution," resilient across an uncertain macro. Post-period M&A: definitive agreement (Apr 23, 2026) to acquire Talon.One for €750m, closing H2 2026 — a promotions/loyalty bolt-on for real-time in-transaction pricing across online and in-store (thematically adjacent to unified-commerce deals like CeX).

**Thesis-flags.**
1. Reported net-revenue growth decelerated to +16% YoY (22%→16%) and cc landed at the low end (20% of the 20–22% guide) → fact: growth is cooling; why: FX headwind (~4pts) plus a maturing base and the single-large-customer lap; why it matters: leaves little cushion under the FY guide; second-order: any macro/FX slip could push 2026 below the guided range, pressuring the growth premium in the multiple.
2. Near-term margin bar reset from "50%+ in 2026" to "broadly in line with 2025," with the aspirational anchor moved to >55% by 2028 → why it matters: 2026 is now an investment/flat-margin year (Talon.One integration, continued hiring, Intelligent Money Movement build), so the operating-leverage story is deferred, not delivered, this year.
3. Unified Commerce (the CeX-relevant in-person/omnichannel engine) at +24% YoY (+28% cc), 453k terminals (+85k YoY), 474 at-scale cross-channel customers → why it matters: in-person is still the clearest structural share-gain vector and validates the online-plus-offline land-grab that CeX Malaysia represents; watch that terminal net-adds (+85k) came in slightly below the prior-year pace (+89k) and UC revenue growth is off its 2025 peak — deceleration to monitor, not yet a break.
4. Talon.One acquisition (€750m, H2 2026 close) → first sizeable inorganic move; adds loyalty/promotions into the transaction flow but introduces integration and capital-allocation risk against a "flat 2026 margin" backdrop.
