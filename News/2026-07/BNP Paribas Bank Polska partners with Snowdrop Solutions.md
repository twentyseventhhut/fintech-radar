BNP Paribas Bank Polska partners with Snowdrop Solutions
Sinziana Albu
07 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
BNP Paribas Bank Polska has partnered with Snowdrop Solutions to display merchant logos in its GOmobile banking app.
Users of GOmobile, the mobile banking application of BNP Paribas Bank Polska, can now see merchant logos alongside their card transactions. The feature replaces technical merchant identifiers with recognisable names and logos, and is intended to make transaction records easier to interpret while also supporting fraud prevention efforts.
The functionality has been built using the MRS API, a transaction data enrichment tool provided by Snowdrop Solutions. The API processes raw merchant data and matches it with verified merchant names and logos, replacing the often unclear identifiers that typically appear in bank statements and transaction histories.
According to the bank, the update forms part of its Accelerate 2030 strategy, structured around three areas: expansion of app functionality, streamlining of spending management, and improvements to the overall user experience. Volodymyr Radin , Vice President of the Management Board of Bank BNP Paribas, said the changes are intended to make daily use of the GOmobile app clearer and more transparent for its customer base.
Security considerations
Beyond usability, the bank has framed the update as a security measure. Fraud attempts involving web addresses and company names that closely resemble those of legitimate businesses have reportedly increased, and the bank stated that showing verified merchant logos gives customers an additional way to confirm the authenticity of a transaction. This is presented as complementary to the behavioural protection mechanisms the bank has previously introduced, with the stated aim of maintaining security controls that operate without adding friction to everyday banking activity.
 Ken Hart , CEO and Founder at Snowdrop Solutions, said the partnership reflects how transaction enrichment data can be applied to improve customer-facing banking services, and that the company expects to continue supporting the bank's digital product development.
Rollout and availability
The bank noted that transaction information has been an area it has sought to improve for some time, and it positions the new feature as addressing a longstanding gap in how transaction data is presented to customers.
In addition, merchant logos appear automatically once the app is updated, with no additional configuration required from users. The updated version of GOmobile is available for download via Google Play and the App Store, and existing users will receive the update through their device's standard app update mechanism.
The partnership adds to a broader trend among European banks of using third-party data enrichment providers to improve the clarity of transaction data shown to retail banking customers, an area that has become more closely linked to both customer experience and fraud prevention strategies.

## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** BNP Paribas Bank Polska integrated Snowdrop Solutions' MRS API (Merchant Reconciliation System / "Merchant Recognition Service") into its GOmobile retail banking app so that card transactions now display verified merchant names and logos instead of raw technical descriptors. The rollout is automatic on app update (Google Play / App Store), with no user configuration. The bank frames it under its **Accelerate 2030** strategy (three axes: app-functionality expansion, spending-management, UX), and pitches it as both a UX and an anti-fraud measure — verified logos help customers spot look-alike / impersonation scams. Sources: The Paypers (07 Jul 2026, in-note), [ffnews](https://ffnews.com/news/bank-bnp-paribas-partners-with-snowdrop-solutions-to-launch-enhanced-transaction-transparency), [MojeBankowanie](https://mojebankowanie.pl/informacje-prasowe/informacje-prasowe-bankowosc/przejrzystosc-w-aplikacji-bankowej-nowe-partnerstwo-banku-bnp-paribas-z-snowdrop-solutions), [PRNews.pl](https://prnews.pl/logo-sprzedawcy-w-historii-transakcji-w-gomobile-492559).

**[1] Who / what is Snowdrop.** UK (London) company founded **2013 by Ken Hart** (CEO), originally a geolocation/maps business that pivoted into one of the first commercial transaction-enrichment platforms. Bootstrapped — **no VC funding** (Crunchbase/Tracxn); notably NOT "Mastercard-backed" despite that being a common framing. Ecosystem partner: **Google Cloud / Google Maps Premier Partner** and **Visa Fintech Connect** partner; its MRS API is Mastercard-mandate (**AN4569**) compliant. Core capability beyond logos: **geolocation** of card transactions (map view), merchant name, category, address, eco/sustainability insights. Prior clients: Banco Sabadell (real-time geolocation, Jan 2025), Banco BPM, Chocolate Finance, Wirex, GoHenry, Ruuky, Rocker, Ample. Sources: [Snowdrop About](https://snowdropsolutions.com/about-us/), [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/snowdrop-solutions).

**[2] Why it matters / regulatory driver.** Since **14 Oct 2023**, Mastercard requires all European issuers to supply enriched payment data to cardholders on request (mandate AN4569); a parallel expectation exists across issuers generally. This turns "clean transaction data" from a nice-to-have into a compliance + fraud-prevention + call-centre-cost lever. Enrichment vendors cite ~30–40% reductions in "what is this charge?" support volume/disputes. So this is a mandate-tailwind partnership, not a bespoke innovation.

**[3] Deal shape.** Vendor-integration / product launch, **not** an investment or M&A. No disclosed financials, transaction volumes, or contract length. BNP Paribas Bank Polska (~4.5m clients; part of BNP Paribas group, majority owner) is a mid-large Polish universal bank; GOmobile is its flagship app. This is a single-market (Poland) deployment, though Snowdrop's group relationships (Sabadell, BPM) suggest cross-BNP potential is unstated but plausible.

### Red-team Q&A

1. **Is Snowdrop "Mastercard-backed"?** No (open). Common framing, but Snowdrop is bootstrapped, no VC, and is a **Google Cloud/Maps Premier Partner** and **Visa Fintech Connect** partner. It is Mastercard-*mandate-compliant* (AN4569), not Mastercard-owned/funded. Correcting a likely misconception.
2. **Is this an investment or M&A?** No — it's a vendor integration / product launch. No money changes ownership.
3. **Any disclosed financials, contract term, or volumes?** No. Zero deal economics; not material to BNP Paribas group P&L.
4. **What actually shipped?** Merchant logos + names in GOmobile transaction history via MRS API. Confirmed by The Paypers, ffnews, MojeBankowanie, PRNews.
5. **Does the geolocation (map) feature ship here too?** Unclear (open). Snowdrop's core USP is transaction geolocation on a map (Sabadell), but the BNP Polska coverage emphasises *logos/names*; map view not explicitly confirmed for GOmobile.
6. **Is the fraud-prevention claim substantiated?** Bank-asserted, plausible mechanism (verified logos vs. look-alike scams) but no metrics. Treat as marketing.
7. **How novel is it?** Low. Snowdrop already runs Sabadell, BPM, Chocolate Finance, Wirex, GoHenry; this is another deployment on the Mastercard-mandate wave.
8. **Is it a duplicate of prior notes?** No — different bank (BNP Paribas Bank Polska) and date; distinct from [[Banco BPM launches transaction enrichment with Snowdrop]] and [[Chocolate Finance taps Snowdrop for clearer spending overview]].
9. **What's the real driver?** Mastercard AN4569 mandate (live Oct 2023) + BNP's Accelerate 2030 UX push. Compliance-adjacent, not blue-sky innovation.
10. **Who's the competitive threat to Snowdrop?** Spade (best-funded, US), Bud (UK, AI), aggregators (Plaid/Tink/MX) bundling enrichment. Snowdrop's edge = European/geo merchant graph; risk = small/unfunded.
11. **Cross-BNP expansion potential?** Unstated (open). Single-market Poland deal; no signal of group-wide rollout, though vendor has other European banks.
12. **Signal value of this note?** Mainly a datapoint on (a) Snowdrop's European bank traction and (b) enrichment shifting to table-stakes/compliance. Not a standalone strategic story.
13. **Source quality?** Trade press + bank PR (The Paypers, ffnews, MojeBankowanie/PRNews) + vendor site. Adequate; no independent/critical coverage.

**Importance: 2/5** — Legitimate, well-sourced, on-trend, but low materiality: a single-market vendor UX feature with no disclosed economics, incremental for a repeat vendor, riding a known regulatory tailwind. Useful as sector-tracking colour on transaction enrichment and Snowdrop's European traction, not as a needle-mover.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
### Sector: transaction-data enrichment / merchant intelligence

**What the category does.** Turns raw, cryptic card/ACH/wire descriptors ("SQ *ABC 8005551212") into verified merchant name, logo, category (beyond MCC), geolocation, and increasingly eco/carbon data. Value props: (1) UX/clarity in banking apps, (2) fraud prevention & fewer disputes/first-party "friendly fraud", (3) 30–40% cut in "unknown charge" support calls, (4) foundation-layer data for AI/agentic banking and rewards attribution.

**Regulatory tailwind.** Mastercard mandate **AN4569** (live 14 Oct 2023) obliges European issuers to provide enriched payment data on request. This is the primary demand driver for the current European wave (Sabadell, BPM, now BNP Paribas Polska) and structurally favours vendors with a clean pan-European merchant graph. Enrichment is shifting from differentiator to table-stakes/compliance.

**Comps (WebSearch-sourced):**
- **Snowdrop Solutions** (the vendor here): London, founded 2013 by Ken Hart, **bootstrapped / no VC** (Crunchbase, [Tracxn](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/snowdropsolutions)). Differentiator = **geolocation** heritage (Google Maps Premier Partner) + merchant logos + eco insights. Visa Fintech Connect partner. Clients: Sabadell, BPM, Wirex, GoHenry, Chocolate Finance, Ruuky, Rocker, Ample. Positioning: Europe-first, geo-strong, mid-market/challenger + universal banks. NOT Mastercard-owned/backed (common misconception).
- **Spade** (US): best-funded pure-play; **$40m Series B Mar 2026** (Oak HC/FT led, a16z, Flourish, YC). 99.9% US/Canada coverage, <40ms P99, 1.9bn tx/day peak, 470% YoY growth. Clients Stripe, Bilt, Mercury. US/Canada-centric — limited direct overlap with Snowdrop's Europe focus. [[Spade raises $40m Series B for transaction data platform]].
- **Bud Financial** (UK): AI open-banking + enrichment; ~$80m Series B (2022); tens of billions of tx; pivoting to AI/MCP for banking ([[Bud Financial launches MCP server for AI banking]]).
- **Ntropy** (US/NY): API enrichment/classification; ~$14m raised (Tracxn); smaller.
- **MX / Plaid Enrich / Tink Insights / Zafin / Lune**: aggregator-bundled or adjacent enrichment. Aggregators bundle enrichment as a feature; pure-plays (Snowdrop, Spade) compete on merchant-graph quality/coverage. See [[Zafin introduces transaction enrichment to unlock loyalty]], [[UAB and Lune partner for AI-powered personalised banking]].

**Competitive read.** Two-tier market: US pure-plays racing on AI/data-platform scale (Spade) and speed; European pure-plays (Snowdrop) winning on local merchant coverage, geolocation, and mandate compliance. Aggregators commoditise the low end. Snowdrop's moat is a European/geo merchant graph and a reference book of live bank deployments — but it is small and unfunded relative to Spade/Bud, and enrichment is increasingly bundled by aggregators.

### Risk flags (5)
1. **Commoditisation / mandate = table-stakes.** Once AN4569 makes enrichment mandatory, it becomes a compliance checkbox with margin pressure; differentiation shrinks to coverage/accuracy/latency. (Sector risk.)
2. **Scale & funding gap.** Snowdrop is bootstrapped and small vs. VC-backed Spade ($40m)/Bud ($80m); limited resources to defend merchant-graph quality as AI-native rivals accelerate.
3. **Aggregator squeeze.** Plaid/Tink/MX/Bud bundle enrichment into broader data platforms; banks already buying aggregation may not pay for a standalone vendor.
4. **Unverified marketing claims.** Fraud-prevention and UX benefits are bank/vendor-asserted; no independent metrics, no disclosed accuracy/coverage for the Polish merchant set, no deal financials.
5. **Single-market, thin deal disclosure.** Poland-only launch, no contract value/term/volumes; low materiality to BNP Paribas group. "Logos in an app" is incremental, not strategic.

**Bottom line:** legitimate, on-trend European enrichment deployment riding the Mastercard-mandate wave; low individual materiality, useful as a datapoint on Snowdrop's European bank traction and the enrichment-as-compliance shift.
