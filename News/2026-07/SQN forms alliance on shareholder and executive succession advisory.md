# Context-enrichment: SQN × Drayton, Glendower & Mokhobo — executive-search alliance for the Africa market
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS: fresh** (single-source, no prior corpus note on either firm or on a Russia→Africa exec-search corridor; not a duplicate). Genuinely new tie-up, but low weight — announcement-stage, no economics, single Russian-language source (Mergers.ru).

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
A lightweight **cross-border referral/cooperation partnership between two boutique executive-search firms** — NOT M&A, NOT a JV, NOT equity. The title's "shareholder and executive succession advisory" framing is misleading: this is **leadership/board search**, not proxy-solicitation or shareholder-activism advisory.
- **SQN** — Moscow management-consulting boutique advising investors, shareholders and CEOs on succession, CEO/CFO search, board-of-directors formation and employer brand; built around **Yaroslav Glazunov** (ex-head of Spencer Stuart and Korn Ferry in Russia, ex-LetterOne). Part of KFR Group; merged mid-2025 with employer-brand agency makelove. (SQN's registered legal-entity name — open.)
- **Drayton, Glendower & Mokhobo** (draytonglendower.com) — Johannesburg boutique exec-search/board-advisory, founded 2016 by **Keamogetse "Moula" Mokhobo-Amegashie** (ex-Spencer Stuart partner for sub-Saharan Africa, 12 yrs); Level-1 B-BBEE, exclusive IIC Partners member for South Africa since Dec 2023. Owned footprint = SA + Ghana (+ claimed EU); "western/eastern Africa" breadth rests on the IIC network, not owned offices.
- **Stated purpose:** supply executive/board talent for (a) Russian companies already operating in Africa and (b) firms weighing African entry.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the most plausible origin is *personal, not strategic* — **both principals are ex-Spencer Stuart office heads** (Glazunov = Russia, Mokhobo = sub-Saharan Africa). This is an old-colleagues network monetizing the post-2022 Russia→Africa business shift with near-zero capital outlay. It surfaced on a Russian M&A outlet (Mergers.ru) because SQN's PR machine feeds that channel — but there is **no Drayton-side or English-language corroboration**, so treat as "announced, not demonstrably live."

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Africa exec-search (Drayton's arena):** Amrop, Odgers Berndtson, Signium Africa, Heidrick & Struggles, Spencer Stuart, Egon Zehnder, Jack Hammer, plus IIC Partners / AESC networks. Drayton competes as a local boutique borrowing global reach via IIC (~40 offices).
- **Russian exec-search (SQN's arena):** Ward Howell, Magnum Hunt, M-Choice, Selecty — plus the shrunken local arms of the global majors that pulled back after 2022.
- **The alliance's niche = the Russia↔Africa executive-talent corridor.** Competition here is thin *precisely because* Western majors avoid Russia — that vacated lane is the whole value proposition. **+ Second-order:** the moat is regulatory/geopolitical (sanctions keep incumbents out), not capability-based — which means it is as fragile as it is defensible: a sanctions-driven advantage evaporates if the geopolitics reverse, and it repels Western-facing African clients.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **SQN:** Glazunov-brand consultancy (author of *Anti-Titanic*; founded "CEO Academy" 2017). Services span the investment cycle — pre-deal management diligence, post-deal integration/succession, pre-exit board & CEO work. July 2025 makelove merger pitched as "Russia's first management-plus-HR-consulting ecosystem" (synergy claims in "billions of rubles" — promotional, single-sourced, discount).
- **Drayton, Glendower & Mokhobo:** 2016-founded, ~8–10 staff; board search/evaluation, C-suite search, assessment, interim, coaching. Credibility rests on Mokhobo's Spencer Stuart pedigree + IIC membership.
- **Fit:** coherent and complementary — Russian demand-side relationships (SQN) meet on-the-ground African search + governance credibility (Drayton). **+ Why now:** sanctions are structurally redirecting Russian corporates toward Africa; a Russia-based advisor needs a credible local African partner, and a Western-lineage African boutique gains inbound Russian-capital referrals with no reputational cost *inside Russia*.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty: moderate.** Cross-border referral alliances are routine (that is literally what IIC/AESC networks do). The only novel element is the **explicit Russia→Africa framing** at a politically hot moment.
- **Value-add: real but narrow.** A credible African search partner is genuinely scarce for Russian firms now that the majors avoid Russia; Drayton gets a referral channel into Russian capital.
- **Traction: thin / unproven.** No disclosed mandates, clients, economics, exclusivity, headcount or revenue targets. No Drayton-side or English announcement. **Reads as announcement-stage.** **+ Who captures the margin:** in a referral alliance the fee-splitting party who *owns the client relationship* captures the value — here that is SQN (holds the Russian corporate relationships), leaving Drayton as fulfillment; so this likely tilts to SQN's benefit unless mandates flow the other way.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Catalyst:** the **3rd Russia–Africa Summit, Moscow, 28–29 Oct 2026** — a natural PR/marketing showcase; expect follow-on announcements around it.
- **Macro tailwind:** sanctions redirecting Russian firms to Africa; active state courting (Lavrov, SPIEF Russia–Africa dialogue).
- **Sentiment:** cautiously plausible as a niche, opportunistic, low-capital referral play on a real macro trend. **+ Counterintuitive second-order:** the Russia tie is simultaneously the alliance's *reason to exist* and its *ceiling* — sanctions/reputational exposure will deter Western-facing African clients from a Russia-linked search chain, so the addressable market is confined to Russia-inbound flows and can't broaden into mainstream African corporate search. Risk skews to it staying a marketing banner rather than a revenue line.
