restructuring charges, accrued in Q2 2026.

## Market Research (sector layer)

_Note: this pick (2026-07-02, "dozens at Bay Area HQ") is the WARN/local-detail tail of the SAME ~10% RIF filed 2026-06-16 (8-K, Item 2.05, ~290 of ~2,900 FTE, $20m severance + $8m SBC). See [[Robinhood to cut 10% of workforce in restructuring]] for the full [0]–[4] enrichment. Below is the sector/comps layer ONLY, not a re-tell._

**Sector & drivers.** US retail brokerage / trading-app subvertical. Structure: a **barbell** — low-cost incumbents (Schwab post-TD Ameritrade, Fidelity, IBKR) holding the mass-affluent/AUM pool vs. app-native, transaction-skewed disruptors (Robinhood, Coinbase, eToro). Barriers are regulatory (broker-dealer, FINRA, crypto/prediction-market licensing) + scale in order-flow/NII economics, not technology — hence a crowded challenger tier. Two live drivers, both dated: (1) a **tech/fintech "efficiency" layoff wave** through mid-2026 — Robinhood −10% (Jun 16), GitLab −14%/~350 + exit of 22 countries (Jun 2, subsequent event), Coinbase ~700/−14% "AI-driven" (May 5, [[Coinbase cuts about 700 jobs in AI-driven restructuring]]), eToro −7% (Jan 15, [[eToro announces 7% workforce reduction]]), IronFX −150 (Mar 16, [[IronFX cuts 150 jobs in Cyprus]]), PrimaryBid −40% (Jan 6, [[PrimaryBid cuts 40% of staff amid GBP 18m loss]]); (2) **revenue-mix migration** from cyclical transaction flow to durable NII + subscriptions (Robinhood Gold 4.3m subs +36% y/y, banking >$2bn deposits). "Why now": these cuts land on **record volumes and profit**, not a downturn — cost discipline is being used for operating leverage / margin defense, a different regime from the 2022 crash-driven cuts. (analysis)

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: **funded/investment accounts, net deposits (platform-asset growth), ARPU, transaction take vs. NII split, adj. EBITDA margin.** Robinhood Q1 2026: 27.4m funded customers (+6%), Total Platform Assets $307bn (+39%), ARPU $157 (+8%), adj. EBITDA $534m (+14%), ~50% adj. EBITDA margin. Basis of competition: incumbents compete on **price + AUM/advice + trust**; app-natives on **product velocity + engagement + new instruments** (options, crypto, prediction markets, tokenization). Recent peer moves: Morgan Stanley low-fee crypto pilot on E*TRADE (May 6, [[Morgan Stanley pilots low-fee crypto trading on E-TRADE]]); Ant/AlipayHK adds zero-commission stock trading (May 5, [[Ant Group adds stock trading to AlipayHK]]) — incumbents/super-apps encroaching on the app-native turf. Robinhood's position: **ahead among app-native active traders**, catching up in durable AUM/banking vs. incumbents. Moat: **engagement + product-velocity + brand switching costs** among younger cohorts (intangible), NOT scale-in-AUM yet (Schwab/Fidelity dwarf it). (analysis)

**Risk flags.**
1. **Transaction-cycle concentration.** FY25 transaction revenue ($2,628m) is ~59% of net revenues and grew +60% off cyclical flow (options, crypto, prediction markets); crypto already fell −47% y/y in Q1 2026. A rich ~22x P/S prices in continued peak-cycle volumes — a mean-reversion in flow re-rates the multiple down and would retro-turn the "proactive lean" into 2022-style forced cuts. (why: the same 8-K that shows "strength" hard-codes dependence on record — i.e. potentially peak — volumes.)
2. **Regulatory overhang on the new growth legs.** Prediction markets (Bernstein: ~$150m→~$586m revenue 2026) and crypto/tokenization face live legal/regulatory contest; a hit to these legs strikes exactly the revenue Robinhood is being valued for. (why: growth premium concentrated in the least-settled, most-contestable products.)

(why: second-order reputational cost even when cash impact is trivial.)

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Not a re-rating on its own — the layoff is a **symptom of a strategic pivot** from cyclical transaction flow to durable NII/subscription/banking revenue, executed on peak volumes for operating leverage. Falsifiable thesis: if durable revenue (Gold subs, NII, banking) keeps out-growing transaction flow over the next 2–4 quarters, the "lean-at-the-peak" discipline holds and defends the premium multiple; **trigger to watch — Q2/Q3 2026 prints on the transaction-vs-NII split and prediction-market/crypto volumes.** What breaks it: a downturn in transaction flow forcing a *fifth* RIF, which would collapse the "position of strength" framing and the ~22x P/S. (analysis)

> [!note] Earnings layer added by [2] earnings-reviewer. Primary source: Robinhood's own SEC filings (Q1 2026 8-K/Ex-99.1 filed 2026-04-28 + Form 10-Q filed 2026-04-29, period ended 2026-03-31). This is the freshest reported quarter as of 2026-07-04 (the layoffs are a June/July 2026 event; Q2 2026 has not yet been reported). Numbers quoted are as-reported.

**Verdict (headline read): MISS on the top line, IN-LINE on the bottom line.** Total net revenues $1.07bn (+15% YoY) *missed* public consensus of ~$1.14bn by roughly -$70m / -6%, dragged by a 47% YoY collapse in crypto revenue to $134m off the prior-year trading boom; GAAP diluted EPS $0.38 (+3% YoY) landed just shy of the ~$0.39 consensus (-~2%). Management *raised* the 2026 adjusted-opex + SBC outlook by $100m (to $2.70bn–$2.83bn) to fund Trump Account infrastructure. So the print is a revenue miss cushioned by cost discipline — directly the backdrop against which the Bay Area layoffs read.

**Key figures (Q1 2026 vs Q1 2025, from the filing):**
- Total net revenues: **$1,067m** vs $927m, **+15% YoY**.
  - Transaction-based revenues: **$623m** vs $583m, **+7% YoY** — led by other/event-contracts revenue $147m (+320% YoY) and equities (+46%); crypto revenue **~$134m, -47% YoY** (the swing factor behind the top-line miss).
  - Net interest revenues: **$359m** vs $290m, **+24% YoY**.
  - Other revenues: **$85m** vs $54m, **+57% YoY** (~+$31m), mostly Robinhood Gold subscription growth.
- Net income attributable to Robinhood: **$346m per the press release (+3% YoY)** / $350m per the 10-Q narrative (+4%) — small basis/rounding difference between the two Robinhood documents; use $346m as the headline earnings figure.
- GAAP diluted EPS: **$0.38, +3% YoY**.
- Operating metrics: Net Deposits **$18bn** (22% annualized growth); Gold Subscribers **4.3m, +36% YoY** (record); Net Deposits and Gold both accelerating even as crypto trading normalized.

**Opex / cost lens (the layoffs-relevant part):**
- **Total operating expenses $656m, +18% YoY** — opex grew *faster* than the 15% revenue line, i.e. modest negative operating-cost leverage this quarter.
- **Adjusted Operating Expenses + SBC $607m, +14% YoY.**
- Disclosed line items (Q1 2025 → Q1 2026, $m): brokerage & transaction 50 → 60; technology & development 214 → 241; operations 31 → 38. Marketing, general & administrative, and provision-for-credit-losses lines are in the filing but not fully re-quoted here — [UNSOURCED] at the exact-dollar level in this note.
- **Guidance action — RAISED:** 2026 adjusted-opex + SBC outlook lifted by ~$100m to **$2.70bn–$2.83bn** (from ~$2.6bn–$2.725bn), explicitly to build/support the Trump Accounts UI (see [[Robinhood and BNY tapped to build Trump Accounts app]]).

**Why the layoffs fit the numbers (why-ladder / de-PR):**
- Fact: the SEC filing behind the layoffs frames the cuts as staying "lean and disciplined" and accelerating "product velocity." → Why: Q1 showed opex +18% outrunning revenue +15%, *and* management simultaneously chose to add $100m of spend on a new product (Trump Accounts). → Why it matters: to fund new-product investment without letting total opex balloon past the $2.70–2.83bn envelope, Robinhood is re-allocating — cutting "dozens" at the Bay Area HQ to free budget for higher-velocity bets. → Second-order effect: the layoff is a *mix shift within a raised opex ceiling*, not a cost-cut to a shrinking business; revenue is still +15% and non-crypto lines (equities, event contracts, net interest, Gold) are strong. This is offense-flavored trimming, consistent with the guidance *raise*, not defensive retrenchment.
- De-PR flag: the top-line "miss" is almost entirely a crypto base-effect (crypto -47%), not a franchise problem — transaction ex-crypto, NII and subscriptions all grew double/triple digits. Management leans on those; the number they are quieter about is that opex leverage went the wrong way this quarter (+18% opex on +15% revenue), which is precisely why headcount discipline is being messaged now.

**Internal trend (corpus):** related Robinhood context in the base — [[Robinhood and BNY tapped to build Trump Accounts app]] (the specific driver of the opex-guidance raise), [[Robinhood pivots to super app for family finance]], [[Robinhood debuts $695 Platinum card to rival Amex]] (the "product velocity" the layoffs claim to accelerate).

**Thesis-flags:**
1. Crypto is now a swing factor, not a growth engine (-47% YoY) — quarterly revenue will stay volatile on crypto trading cycles; the miss vs consensus was a crypto-comp miss, not structural.
2. Negative near-term operating leverage (opex +18% > revenue +15%) + a $100m guidance *raise* = the layoffs are the pressure-release valve to hold the opex ceiling; watch Q2 2026 for whether adjusted-opex + SBC growth decelerates below revenue growth.
3. Non-crypto engine is healthy and diversifying (Gold subs +36% to 4.3m record, NII +24%, event contracts +320%) — supports the "lean but investing" narrative rather than a distressed cut.
