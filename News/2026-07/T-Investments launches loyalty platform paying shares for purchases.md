Сервис «Т-Инвестиции» запустил платформу для программ лояльности эмитентов
Сервис «Т-Инвестии», брокерское направление в экосистеме «Т-Банка», объявил о запуске первой в России платформы для программ лояльности компаний-эмитентов, которая мотивирует их клиентов становиться акционерами, а акционеров — покупателями товаров и услуг. 
Таким образом, эмитенты решают сразу две задачи: привлекают новых инвесторов и увеличивают продажи своих товаров и услуг среди клиентов. 
Условия программ лояльности могут включать разные механики , адаптируясь под бизнес-задачи эмитента: например, дарение его акций, привилегии по бонусной карте или повышенный кэшбэк при покупках товаров и услуг компании. 
Как инвестор становится клиентом эмитента: инвестор покупает ценные бумаги эмитента в приложении «Т-Инвестиции»; «Т-Инвестиции» автоматически отслеживают сделку и присылают инвестору промокод для получения привилегий и бонусов от эмитента; инвестор активирует промокод и использует бонусы при покупке товаров или услуг эмитента (например, карту лояльности со специальными условиями).
Как клиент эмитента становится акционером: клиент в обычной жизни совершает покупки товаров или услуг компании-эмитента; «Т-Инвестиции» направляют ему SMS со ссылкой на подарочные акции эмитента; клиент принимает подарок — акции зачисляются на его брокерский счет в «Т-Инвестициях».
Весь процесс происходит бесшовно для клиентов и акционеров компании. 
Преимущества для компаний-эмитентов: расширение базы инвесторов ; новые точки контакта с текущими акционерами; рост основного бизнеса — например, оборотов, среднего чека и частоты покупок в розничных магазинах; возврат «спящих» клиентов и работа с оттоком покупателей; рост узнаваемости бренда.
Преимущества для покупателей: возможность стать акционером любимого бренда, совершая привычные покупки; знакомство с инвестиционным рынком без финансовых затрат.
Преимущества для инвесторов: эксклюзивные привилегии за владение ценными бумагами эмитента; оптимизация повседневных расходов.
Первыми среди российских эмитентов платформу Т-Инвестиций использовали три компании: ПАО «Фикс Прайс» , ПАО «Каршеринг Руссия» (бренд « Делимобиль »), ПАО «МГКЛ» .
Конкретные условия программы лояльности определяются с каждым эмитентом индивидуально. 
 Антон Мальков , управляющий директор по рынкам капитала « Т-Банка », сказал: «На российском фондовом рынке растет роль розничного инвестора и компаниям важно выстраивать с ним более регулярное взаимодействие. Решение «Т-Инвестиций» позволяет не только достигать этой цели, но и расширять базу инвесторов. Россияне становятся акционерами любимого бренда, совершая привычные покупки в обычной жизни. Реализация подобных программ будет способствовать привлечению новых участников на фондовый рынок и развитию финансовой грамотности и культуры долгосрочного инвестирования в России».
Для запуска программы лояльности компания может оставить заявку на сайте «Т-Банка» или связаться с персональным менеджером, который поможет подобрать индивидуальные условия и инструменты продвижения.
Подключение эмитента к платформе «Т-Инвестиций» занимает от двух недель и не требует сложной интеграции. При реализации партнерских кампаний «Т-Инвестиции» также отслеживают, чтобы рыночные факторы торгов ценными бумагами эмитента (цена, спрос, предложение, объемы) не искажались.

# Context-enrichment: T-Investments launches loyalty platform paying shares for purchases
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 2026-06-30 T-Investments (the brokerage arm of the T-Bank / T-Technologies ecosystem) announced a **B2B platform that lets listed issuers run loyalty programs whose reward is the issuer's own equity or share-linked perks**. Two flows: (a) an investor who buys an issuer's shares in the T-Investments app is auto-detected and sent a promo code for that issuer's discounts/loyalty-card perks; (b) a retail customer who buys the issuer's goods gets an SMS offering **gift shares** credited to a T-Investments brokerage account. Mechanics per issuer are bespoke — "gift shares, bonus-card privileges, or higher cashback." First three issuers: **Fix Price, Karsharing Rossiya (Delimobil), MGKL**. Onboarding "from two weeks, no complex integration"; T-Investments says it monitors that campaigns don't distort the issuer's share price/volume.

**Why framed this way / what it reveals (red-team):** The PR headline "first in Russia" is doing heavy lifting. It is true only for the **B2B platform/tooling** framing — issuer-run **shareholder-perk programs already exist in Russia** (Delimobil, Novabev, Sber, VTB, and T-Technologies' *own* shareholder loyalty program with free subscriptions/cashback were all live by early 2026, per a March-2026 T-Invest community post). So the novelty is packaging/self-serve, not the concept. No numbers disclosed: no reward rates, no gift-share budget per issuer, no user counts, no take-rate T-Investments charges issuers. It is an **announcement with three launch partners, not disclosed adoption**. The "we monitor that trading factors aren't distorted" line quietly flags the real risk (issuers paying customers in their own thinly-traded stock).

## [1] Competitors / peers
The "spend → get fractional shares" model is well-trodden abroad, with a **poor traction record**: **Bumped** (US) built exactly this — a platform for brands to reward customers with fractional shares of the brand's stock; it was acquired by **Bakkt in Feb 2023 for ~$0.58M** (a near-total write-off of its venture value) and effectively wound down. **Stash Stock-Back** (debit-card rewards in fractional shares) persists but as a retention gimmick, not a category. **Bunq** and **Revolut** offer fractional shares but not as a purchase-linked reward. Inside Russia, the closest peers are issuers' own shareholder-perk schemes (Sber, VTB, Novabev, Delimobil) — T-Investments' angle is being the **broker-as-platform** aggregating this across many issuers.

**Why the landscape is this way / second order:** The recurring failure mode (Bumped) is that **stock rewards are a customer-acquisition subsidy, not a durable loyalty currency** — merchants dislike variable, market-priced liabilities, and users treat tiny fractional grants as noise. T-Investments' edge over Bumped is structural: it is a **broker with an existing 40M+-account retail base and the trade-detection rails**, so distribution and custody are solved — the exact things a standalone startup lacked. That improves odds but doesn't fix the core issuer economics.

## [2] Company history / fit
Fits T-Bank's stated strategy cleanly. In the same week (early July 2026) T-Bank publicly argued that **standalone banking apps will lose relevance** and finance will live inside platform/contextual scenarios (see [[Финтехно T-Bank sees standalone banking apps losing relevance]]), and is pursuing embedded ecosystems (Avto.ru, multibanking — see [[T-Bank multibanking pilot reaches 700k users]]). A broker-run engagement platform that ties **everyday purchases to brokerage-account funding** is a natural extension: it grows T-Investments' funded-account base while giving issuers a growth tool.

**Why the company acts this way:** T-Investments monetizes **funded brokerage accounts and order flow**, so any mechanism that converts ecosystem shoppers into account holders (gift shares land in a *T-Investments* account) directly feeds its core funnel. The platform is less "a favor to issuers" and more **T-Investments buying new brokerage relationships using issuers' equity budgets** — a clever alignment where the issuer pays the customer-acquisition cost.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Genuinely new element: a **self-serve, broker-hosted B2B platform** (vs. each issuer building bespoke perks) with automated trade-detection → promo-code and purchase → gift-share flows, live with **three named launch issuers**. Everything else — stock-as-reward, shareholder perks — is prior art both globally and in Russia.

**Why value-add is thin, deeper:** No unit economics disclosed. The margin question: who pays? Gift shares and elevated cashback are **funded by the issuer**, T-Investments captures new funded accounts + likely a platform fee — so T-Investments is the structural winner regardless of whether issuers see ROI. For issuers, the value hinges on unproven claims (returning "dormant" customers, higher average check); the Bumped precedent says **conversion is weak and grants get treated as noise**. Traction to date = announcement + 3 partners; **no adoption metrics**, so importance stays low.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Backdrop is favorable: Russian retail-investor penetration is at record highs — **~41M brokerage accounts on MOEX by end-2025, ~5M opened in 2025** — and MOEX has pushed shareholder-value themes (MVBI index). More issuers (esp. consumer-facing PAOs) may sign on as a cheap "engage the retail float" tool. Risks: (1) **share-price distortion / manipulation optics** — paying customers in thinly-traded stock invites regulatory scrutiny (note Delimobil itself was flagged by the CBR over alleged manipulation in April 2026); (2) low durable ROI for issuers → churn of partners; (3) it deepens T-Bank's ecosystem lock-in, which regulators are watching.

**Why the market may go this way / counterintuitive:** The program's success metric for T-Investments (funded accounts) and for issuers (sales uplift) **diverge** — T-Investments can "win" (more accounts) even if issuers see no sales lift, which makes the platform's headline growth a **poor proxy for real value creation**. The genuinely watch-worthy second-order effect is regulatory: rewarding purchases with an issuer's own equity blurs marketing and market-making, and in a market where a launch partner (Delimobil) is already under manipulation scrutiny, that is the real fragility, not the loyalty mechanics.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian retail-brokerage subvertical (industry/wealth + trading). T-Investments (brokerage arm of T-Bank / T-Technologies, ticker TCSG.ME) is Russia's largest broker by active clients on the Moscow Exchange, a leadership position it has held since ~2019 when it added 800k+ retail investors in one year (per Tinkoff Group IR, 2019–2021). Parent T-Technologies had 54.9m customers and clients' funds/AUM of RUB 6.4tn as of Q1 2026, +23% YoY (per Aton/Alfa citing Q1-2026 IFRS, as of 2026-05-21). Structure: the Russian brokerage/wealth stack is concentrated among ecosystem players (T-Bank, Sber, VTB) that bundle brokerage into super-apps; entry barriers are distribution/licensing + the captive banking base, not technology. Why now: post-2022 the retail investor is the dominant marginal buyer on MOEX (foreign flows gone), so issuers court retail directly — T-Bank's own MD of capital markets (Anton Malkov) frames the launch as "the growing role of the retail investor" needing "more regular interaction." This is an engagement/CAC play riding that shift, not a new revenue line disclosed as such.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: active brokerage accounts, AUM / clients' funds, and (for the platform itself) issuer take / attach rate — none disclosed for this product. Key players in RU brokerage: T-Investments (leader by active clients), Sber (largest by registered accounts / banking base), VTB, Finam, BCS; new entrant Ozon plans to launch "Ozon Investments" in autumn 2026 with MOEX access ([[Ozon to launch brokerage arm Ozon Investments in autumn 2026]]). Basis of competition: distribution + product breadth + fees, increasingly engagement/loyalty. Recent move (2026-06-30): T-Investments launched what it calls Russia's first issuer-loyalty platform — customers earn gift shares for ordinary purchases (SMS → accept → shares credited to broker account), and investors who buy an issuer's stock get promo-codes/perks. First three issuers: PJSC Fix Price, PJSC Carsharing Russia (Delimobil, MCX:DELI), PJSC MGKL. Protagonist position: ahead (analysis) — it monetizes its dual role as both the largest retail broker AND a consumer bank, a two-sided moat (broker rails + banking base) that a pure broker (Finam) or a pure retailer can't easily replicate; onboarding an issuer takes "from two weeks."

**Comps & multiples.** No deal/valuation/round attaches to this product launch — it is a platform feature, so trading-comp arithmetic on the pick itself is "no data." Parent-level anchor (supporting, not a product multiple): T-Technologies Q1 2026 operating net profit RUB 46.5bn, +40% YoY; ROE 26.7%, with a full-year guide of ROE ≥30% and net-profit growth ≥20% (per The Moscow Times / Aton, as of 2026-05-21; IR press release https://cdn.tbank.ru/static/documents/8268ed24-6654-4f23-9fd1-154a67b55c7d.pdf). Internal comps (grep, semsearch notes-db absent): [[Ozon to launch brokerage arm Ozon Investments in autumn 2026]] (competing new entrant, same month); [[T-Bank multibanking pilot reaches 700k users]] (same distribution/engagement flywheel). Global precedent for the mechanic (not a valuation comp): US startup Bumped ran fractional-share loyalty; a Columbia Business School study of its two-year pilot (Target, McDonald's, Kroger, Lowe's, Costco) found weekly spend at rewarded brands rose ~40% and shifted visits away from competitors (per Forbes/RetailWire) — evidence the shares-for-purchases lever measurably lifts spend, though Bumped never reached scale (unit economics unproven). Verdict: qualitative comparison only, distribution not computed.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Undisclosed economics / who pays** — the note is silent on take rate, who funds the gift shares (issuer vs T-Investments), and cost per acquired shareholder; a share-giveaway CAC can be expensive and the P&L contribution is unquantified. Second-order: if issuers bear the cost, adoption beyond the three launch names (Fix Price/Delimobil/MGKL) is the real test.
2. **Regulatory/market-integrity risk** — gifting shares to drive purchases and buying-to-get-perks can distort trading (price/volume/demand); T-Investments itself says it monitors so "market factors aren't distorted," an implicit admission the CBR could scrutinize this as inducement or market-conduct risk.
3. **Small, gimmick-scale launch vs execution** — only 3 issuers, all small/mid-cap (Delimobil is loss-adjacent: FY2024 IFRS net profit ~RUB 8m); the feature may stay a marketing novelty rather than a durable engagement moat if larger blue-chips don't join.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This blurs the line between a loyalty program and a brokerage funnel: T-Investments turns its consumer base into an issuer-marketing channel, deepening the super-app engagement moat vs Sber/VTB and pre-empting Ozon's autumn brokerage launch. Falsifiable thesis: if ≥10 issuers (incl. one large-cap) sign within ~12 months and T-Technologies cites incremental active-account or AUM growth from it, it's a genuine flywheel; trigger to watch — issuer count and any CBR guidance on share-linked loyalty. What breaks it: it stays at ~3 small issuers with no disclosed engagement lift = marketing gimmick.
