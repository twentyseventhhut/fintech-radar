Agentic winners will be determined by Trust not Tech
Published: June 11, 2026
Alexandra Rusu
11 Jun 2026 / 5 Min Read
 Bringing the voices of merchants and consumers from across five European countries together for the first time anywhere, new research from inclusive global payments platform, Ecommpay, reveals a significant gap between the promise of agentic commerce and consumer readiness. 
 The white paper, Before They Check Out , identifies that although AI is becoming part of consumer life, shoppers are not ready to hand over full buying power to a digital agent. It also draws attention to the critical needs of vulnerable consumers that can’t be ignored as agentic commerce evolves. 
 The findings suggest that the biggest barrier to agentic commerce adoption is not consumer awareness or merchant interest; it’s trust at the point of payment.  
 The payments ecosystem – from merchants to PSPs - needs to focus on nurturing consumer trust if it is to benefit from the commercial opportunities of agentic commerce. Currently some merchants are building agentic commerce into their own platforms where there is more control on the checkout experience. But the big question will come when merchants allow AI platforms to offer a checkout facility.  
 More than any single technical development, adoption will depend on merchants trusting the solution: clear authority, visible controls, secure payment flows and reliable fallback routes. Already holding a strong position of trust, payment service providers (PSPs) are ready to support identity proofing, transaction mandates, authentication, fraud prevention and dispute management.  
 If you’re already considering the role of agents in the customer experience, this research provides valuable insight into consumer expectations.

# Context-enrichment: The Paypers — "Agentic winners decided by trust, not tech"
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
The Paypers ran a **thought-leadership / sponsored-insight** piece (dated 11 Jun 2026) that is essentially a write-up of **Ecommpay's own white paper "Before They Check Out."** Ecommpay is a **PSP/acquirer** (founded 2012, Riga/UK-FCA authorised) — so this is not neutral research, it is a **PSP arguing that PSPs should own the "trust layer"** of agentic commerce, i.e. the conclusion conveniently expands the author's own TAM.
The survey (~1,750 consumers across UK/FR/DE/ES/IT) reports: only **13.9%** would let an AI agent complete purchases independently; **50.1%** would not share card details with an agent; **44.3%** would cap agent spend at €100 (only ~6% allow >€500); **55.9%** already use AI for search. Thesis: the binding barrier to agentic commerce is **trust at the point of payment**, not awareness or merchant interest; PSPs are "ready" to supply identity proofing, mandates, authentication, fraud prevention, dispute management.
**+ Why structured this way / what it reveals:** trust is the one bottleneck a PSP can *package and sell*. But the harder, genuinely-unsolved problem is **liability / dispute-rights allocation** when an agent is prompt-injected or buys the wrong thing — a legal + network-rule question PSPs cannot unilaterally solve. Reframing a legal gap as a trust-service opportunity is the PR move. Ecommpay's *own* data even undercuts the claim: consumers blame **AI companies (39%) far more than payment providers (24%)** for agent fraud — so consumers do not instinctively see the PSP as the trust anchor.

## [1] Competitors / peers
The stack, and who actually holds leverage:
- **AI platforms / demand:** OpenAI (ChatGPT + Stripe **ACP/Instant Checkout**, ~4% take-rate on Shopify txns), Google (**AP2**, 60+ partners, donated to FIDO), Perplexity, Amazon. Own **intent/discovery — the scarcest asset.**
- **Card networks / rails + mandates + tokens:** Visa **Intelligent Commerce / Trusted Agent Protocol (TAP) / Payment Passkeys / Intelligent Commerce Connect**; Mastercard **Agent Pay / Agentic Tokens**. Control tokenisation + SCA binding.
- **PSPs/acquirers (Ecommpay's claim):** Stripe, Adyen, PayPal, Worldpay, Ecommpay — the **squeezed middle**, most at risk of commoditisation.
**+ Why the lay of the land is this way:** the middle layer is exactly the layer being disintermediated — which is *why* a PSP publishes a paper declaring that middle the crown jewel. Related corpus notes: [[Visa expands agentic commerce access for merchants in Europe]], [[CaixaBank completes first agentic payment with Visa]], [[Worldline, ING and Visa complete live agentic payment pilot]], [[Thredd joins Visa Agentic Ready Programme starting with Zilch]], [[Cross River expands Stripe partnership for agentic commerce]]. All of them independently reach the same second-order conclusion: **value accrues to the network's token/identity primitive, not to the issuer/processor/PSP relay.**

## [2] Company history / fit
Ecommpay = inclusive PSP/direct acquirer, ~2M txns/day claimed; monetises 3-D Secure, tokenisation, fraud tooling and dispute management — precisely the "trust layer" services the paper says agentic commerce needs. Same week it also shipped a self-serve SMB payments platform ([[Ecommpay launches self-serve payments platform for UK small businesses]]). **+ Why it acts this way:** a commodity acquiring take-rate pushes Ecommpay to differentiate on *compliance/identity as a product* — a white paper positioning PSPs as the agentic trust anchor is brand marketing for that pivot, not disinterested research.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Novelty of the *event*: low — it is a vendor survey/commentary, not a launch or deal. Novelty of the *content*: the 5-country consumer dataset is a distinct artifact (not duplicated elsewhere in the corpus), and the survey numbers are usable colour on consumer readiness. Corroborated by independent surveys: **ACI — six in ten UK consumers would abandon an AI shopping agent after one mistake** (28 Jun 2026); Gartner/Forrester (2026) both find consumers want AI shopping *help*, not autonomous *purchasing*.
**+ Where the value-add / margin really sits:** genuine live traction lives one layer up — Visa Intelligent Commerce went live in Europe (2 Jul 2026, 30+ issuers, merchants lastminute.com/Frasers); Mastercard Agent Pay live in HK/Thailand (Mar–Apr 2026); ChatGPT+Etsy/Shopify live. Autonomous-agent GMV in 2026 is still a rounding error vs the $1–5T-by-2030 forecasts (Bain/McKinsey). The one **real** leg of Ecommpay's thesis is regulatory: **PSD3/PSR keeps SCA liability with the delegating PSP** — so PSPs do retain a durable authentication role, but that is a compliance obligation more than a moat.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Standards fragmentation persists (ACP vs AP2 vs TAP vs UCP/x402); Visa's "support all four" hedge signals no winner yet. **Liability is the open frontier:** networks say "standard tokenised rules — issuer liable, chargebacks intact," but manipulated-intent/prompt-injection cases don't map cleanly to "fraud"; Visa's risk team flagged a large H1-2026 spike in dark-web chatter about hijacking delegated agent credentials. **+ Counterintuitive second-order effect:** the "big question" the piece raises — merchants ceding checkout to AI platforms — is framed as a *trust* problem, but it is really an *economics/control* fight (data, margin, dispute exposure, plus paying the platform's ~4% cut). The trust framing conveniently obscures that it is a business-model concession, not a technology gap.

## Red-team / challenge questions
1. **Is Ecommpay a neutral researcher?** No — CONFIRMED conflict. It is a PSP/acquirer; the "PSPs = trust layer" conclusion directly expands its own TAM. Read as positioning.
2. **Is trust really the #1 bottleneck?** Partly PR. Consumer hesitancy is real and cross-validated, but the *harder* constraint is liability/dispute-rights allocation — a legal + network-rule problem PSPs cannot solve alone.
3. **Do consumers actually see PSPs as the trust anchor?** No — Ecommpay's own data: 39% blame AI companies for agent fraud vs 24% payment providers. Trust anchors to the platform/brand, not the invisible PSP.
4. **Who bears liability when an agent is prompt-injected or buys the wrong thing?** OPEN. Networks say "standard tokenised rules, issuer liable, chargebacks intact," but manipulated-intent cases don't map to "fraud." No case law as of mid-2026.
5. **Are the survey stats trustworthy?** Directional only — single-vendor ~1,750-respondent panel across 5 countries; methodology not independently verifiable. Cross-check against ACI/Gartner/Forrester (which agree directionally).
6. **Is this note a duplicate of the Visa/Mastercard/CaixaBank agentic notes?** No — those are live-transaction *events*; this is a distinct consumer-survey commentary. Not stale, but adjacent/overlapping in theme.
7. **Is agentic commerce live at scale?** No. Live = controlled pilots (Visa EU 2 Jul 2026; MC HK/Thailand; ChatGPT+Etsy/Shopify). The big $ numbers are 2028–2030 forecasts, not GMV.
8. **Where does the margin actually accrue?** Demand/intent (AI platforms; OpenAI's ~4% take-rate is the clearest signal) and the network token/mandate layer (Visa/MC). PSPs/checkout are the squeezed middle. (hypothesis, corpus-consistent)
9. **Does regulation genuinely favour PSPs?** Partly — the one real leg. PSD3/PSR keeps SCA liability with the delegating PSP, giving a durable authentication role. But that is a compliance obligation as much as a moat.
10. **Is the "merchants ceding checkout to AI platforms" question a trust problem?** No — it's an economics/control fight (data, margin, dispute exposure, platform take-rate). The trust framing obscures the business-model concession.
11. **Any quantified vulnerable-consumer finding to cite?** No — the piece gestures at vulnerable consumers but the accessible summaries carry no specific stat. Treat any such claim as unverified.
12. **What is the durable takeaway for a reader?** Consumer readiness is the current gating factor for *adoption UX*, but the *value-capture and liability* battles are being decided a layer above the PSP — so "trust, not tech" is half-right and self-servingly framed.
13. **Author/date reliability?** The 11 Jun 2026 / Alexandra Rusu byline could not be independently re-confirmed (a later recap is bylined differently); the white paper and thesis are confirmed. Minor — flag only.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Agentic commerce = AI agents that discover, cart and *pay* on a consumer's behalf. Forecasts are wide and mostly top-down: McKinsey pegs $3–5tn of orchestrated retail spend globally by 2030 (per McKinsey QuantumBlack, Oct 2025, via digitalcommerce360); the credible bottom-up band is far lower — Morgan Stanley (Dec 2025) sees agentic capturing 10–20% of US e-commerce by 2030 ($190bn base / $385bn bull), J.P. Morgan up to 25%, eMarketer just $144bn by 2029. Structure: value chain splits into (i) model labs / agent surfaces (OpenAI, Google, Perplexity), (ii) discovery+checkout protocols (OpenAI–Stripe **ACP**, Google/Shopify **UCP**), (iii) authorization/trust layer (Google **AP2** signed mandates, Visa **Trusted Agent Protocol**, Mastercard **Agent Pay** tokens), and (iv) PSPs/merchants at the point of settlement. Barriers are network-effect and regulatory, not technical — which is precisely the Ecommpay/Paypers thesis: the constraint is *trust at the point of payment*, not capability. Why now: the rails shipped in H2-2025 (AP2 Sep 2025 with 60+ partners; ACP Sep 2025; Visa Trusted Agent Sep 2025), so 2026 is the adoption test.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: agent-attributed GMV, authorization/approval rate, dispute rate, tokenized-transaction share. Positioning basis is *who owns the consent record*: networks (Visa/Mastercard) bind a tokenized credential to agent+merchant+consent scope; protocols (AP2/ACP) standardize the mandate and checkout; PSPs sit closest to identity proofing, mandates, auth, fraud and dispute management — the exact functions Ecommpay claims. The Paypers/Ecommpay pick is thought-leadership positioning PSPs as the "natural trust layer," grounded in a survey of 1,750+ consumers across UK/FR/DE/ES/IT. Recent moves: Visa launched **Intelligent Commerce Connect** — one integration spanning four protocols (Trusted Agent, Machine Payments, ACP, UCP); Mastercard shipped **Agent Pay for Machines** (Jun 2026); both joined AP2 (Sep 2025). Protagonist position: Ecommpay is a *niche* PSP staking a trust-narrative flag, not a rails owner — `(analysis)` the moat here accrues to whoever holds the signed consent record, and today that's the networks and the AP2/ACP spec, not any single PSP.

De-PR flag — the strongest external counter to the "trust is the only gate" framing: OpenAI **quietly killed ChatGPT Instant Checkout in Mar 2026** after ~5 months of near-zero sales (ACP the spec survives; the consumer surface did not). So the barrier so far has been demand/utility, not merely trust plumbing.

**Comps & multiples.** Covered networks (IR-grounded, not comps to Ecommpay which is private, no data): Visa Q2 FY2026 (ended 31 Mar 2026) revenue $11.23bn, +17% y/y; payments volume $3.7tn (+9% cc); 66.1bn processed txns; ~50% of transactions tokenized (per Visa IR / investor.visa.com). Mastercard Q2-2026 revenue $8.40bn, +15.8% y/y; EPS $4.60; ~40% of switched transactions tokenized as of Dec, targeting 100% of e-commerce by 2030 (per Mastercard IR, via Meyka). Trading multiples not computed — no free real-time EV/market-cap here → `[UNSOURCED]`. Internal comps: [[McKinsey projects agentic commerce could drive $5 trillion by 2030]], [[PayPal integrates Mastercard Agent Pay and comes to ChatGPT]], [[Checkout.com adopts Agentic Commerce Protocol]], [[EMVCo to develop specifications for agentic payments]], [[Mastercard and Majid Al Futtaim launch agentic payments in UAE]]. Distribution not computed — qualitative only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Unproven consumer adoption / hype vs live GMV.** Only ~14% of US consumers trust AI to place orders (vs 65% to compare prices); Instant Checkout's near-zero-sales shutdown shows announced rails ≠ real GMV. Second-order: the $3–5tn TAM is aspirational; the bottom-up 10–25%-of-e-commerce band is the number to underwrite.
2. **Liability / dispute gap.** No jurisdiction has agent-specific purchasing law; agent-initiated txns show ~2.4x the dispute rate of comparable CNP, skewed to "did-not-authorise"/"not-as-described" not fraud. CFPB's Jan-2026 advisory keeps consumer recourse intact, narrowed only where the mandate is well-scoped — so unbounded mandates leave issuers/merchants holding the bag. Second-order: caps who will actually delegate spend.
3. **Standards fragmentation.** ACP vs AP2 vs UCP vs MPP/x402 are complementary-in-theory but multi-integration-in-practice; a niche PSP betting on being *the* trust layer risks being disintermediated by whichever network/spec owns the signed consent record.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` The value-capture question is *who holds the mandate*, not who checks out — and that favors the networks (tokenized consent) and the AP2/ACP standard bodies over standalone PSP trust-narratives. Falsifiable thesis: if agent-attributed GMV and approval rates don't visibly inflect through 2026–27 (watch merchant-side ACP/UCP live checkout volumes and any successor to Instant Checkout), agentic commerce stays a rails-in-search-of-demand story regardless of how good the trust layer gets. Trigger to watch: the first PSP/network to disclose real agent-GMV and dispute economics (today everyone is silent on both).
