### [0] What happened

Sberbank reported H1 2026 (Jan–Jun) net profit under Russian accounting standards (RPBU/RAS) of **RUB 995.3bn, +20.4% YoY**, with **ROE ≈ 23.0%**. SberBank Online monthly active users (MAU) reached **84.7m**. The print was disclosed via Sberbank's own press service around 2026-07-09 and re-reported in the "News & Trends by Sber" channel on 2026-07-13.

### [1] Dated timeline (RAS / RPBU monthly cadence)

- **2026-02-26** — FY2025 results (record): net profit ≈ **RUB 1.7tn (+8.4% YoY)**, ROE 22.7%; board plans **50% dividend payout (~RUB 850bn)**. ([Moscow Times](https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2026/02/26/sberbank-reports-record-22bln-profit-in-2025-a92055))
- **Q1 2026** — strong start: **ROE 24.4%** vs a 22.5% target; IFRS Q1 net profit +16.5% YoY. ([Investing.com slides](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/sberbank-q1-2026-slides-24-roe-drives-profit-growth-amid-slowdown-93CH-4644579))
- **5M 2026** — net profit under RAS **+21.1% YoY**. ([AKM](https://www.akm.ru/eng/news/sberbank-s-net-profit-for-5-months-of-2026-according-to-ras-increased-by-21-1/))
- **June 2026 (month)** — net profit **RUB 168.1bn, +17% YoY**, ROE 22.4%; monthly **NII +27.4% YoY to RUB 321.3bn**. ([fomag 6M26 RSBU](https://fomag.ru/news/sber-finansovye-rezultaty-6m26-rsbu/))
- **2026-07-09 / 07-13** — **H1 2026: RUB 995.3bn, +20.4% YoY, ROE ~23.0%**; MAU 84.7m. ([Moscow Times brief](https://ru.themoscowtimes.com/2026/07/09/brief-chistaya-pribyl-sberbanka-za-yanv-iyun-vyrosla-na-204-do-9953-mlrd-r-a200367))

### [2] Prior-period comparison (the base)

- **H1 2025 (RAS): RUB 826.6bn, +7.5% YoY, ROE 22.2%**; H1 2025 net fee & commission income −1.4% YoY to RUB 348.8bn (high prior-year base + shift to subscription model). ([AKM](https://www.akm.ru/eng/news/sberbank-s-net-profit-under-ras-increased-by-7-5-in-6-months/), [MarketScreener](https://www.marketscreener.com/quote/stock/SBERBANK-OF-RUSSIA-6494829/news/Russia-s-Sberbank-reports-H1-net-profit-of-826-6-bln-roubles-50466260/))
- RUB 826.6bn × 1.204 ≈ **RUB 995bn** — the +20.4% figure ties out cleanly against the H1 2025 RAS base.
- Growth acceleration story: H1 2025 grew only **+7.5%**, whereas H1 2026 is up **+20.4%** — a marked reacceleration, consistent with strong NII growth (June NII +27.4%) as the rate environment supported margins.

13. **Any one-off items inflating H1?** — Not flagged in the print; H1 2025 fee softness was attributed to a one-off high base — need to confirm no comparable distortion in 2026. Open.

**Importance: 3/5** — Sberbank is Russia's systemically dominant bank and the single best real-time gauge of Russian retail/corporate banking health; a +20.4% H1 profit acceleration (from +7.5% a year earlier) with ROE ~23% is a genuinely strong, macro-relevant signal. Capped below 4 because it is a routine monthly-cadence RAS disclosure (not the higher-signal IFRS group print), largely priced/anticipated after 5M (+21.1%) and June data, and its investability is sanctions-constrained for a global fintech audience.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian banking is a state-anchored oligopoly: Sber alone controls roughly a third of sector assets, and the top-5 (Sber, VTB, Gazprombank, Alfa, T-Bank) dominate — high barriers via capital, regulation and deposit-network effects. The defining macro driver is the rate cycle: the Bank of Russia cut its key rate 25bp to 14.25% on 19 June 2026 — the 9th consecutive cut in an easing cycle down from a two-decade high — with annual inflation ~5.6% (per CBR, as of mid-June 2026) and the next decision due 24 July 2026 (per cbr.ru). "Why now": falling but still very high rates keep asset yields elevated while funding costs re-price down, which is exactly what shows up in Sber's H1 result — RAS net interest income +25.3% y/y to RUB 1,800.9bn, versus fee income essentially flat at +1.7% to RUB 354.6bn (per Interfax/Vedomosti, 09 Jul 2026). Second-order effect: the profit engine is spread, not fees — margin expansion that structurally compresses as the CBR eases further into 2026-2027 (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Bank KPIs that matter here: ROE, net interest margin (NIM), cost/income (C/I), and cost of risk. Sber's H1 2026 RAS: ROE ~23%, C/I 25.7% (opex +15.0% to RUB 588.6bn), retail loan book RUB 19.8tn (+1.6%), SberBank Online MAU 84.7m — best-in-class efficiency and scale. Peers, recent dated moves: VTB Q1 2026 net profit RUB 132.6bn (-6.2% y/y), 4M RUB 162.8bn, ROE 17.5%, FY target only RUB 600-650bn (per Global Banking & Finance / tatar-inform, 2026) — i.e. a bank whose earnings are shrinking while Sber's grow. T-Technologies (T-Bank) FY2025 operating net profit RUB 174.4bn (+43%), ROE 29.1% (per intellinews/AKM) with 2026 guidance of 20%+ profit growth — higher ROE and faster growth but ~1/6 of Sber's scale, a digital-retail niche disruptor. Alfa-Bank and the top-5 compete mainly on distribution and digital product velocity, not price. Sber's position: clear franchise leader (scale + lowest C/I + deposit gravity); moat = deposit network effects + scale + its GigaChat/tech stack (analysis). T-Bank out-earns it per unit of equity; VTB is the laggard.

**Comps & multiples.** Sber: mkt cap ~RUB 6.28tn at ~RUB 290.8/share; reported EPS ~RUB 78.7 (FY2026 basis) → **P/E = 290.8 / 78.7 ≈ 3.7x** (per financemarker/investmint, 2026); dividend ~RUB 37.6/sh, yield ~12.9%, register close 20 Jul 2026, 50% payout policy. Internal corpus comps (RU banking/fintech, H1 2026 context): [[VTB takes large Ozon share stake as collateral]], [[VTB sets five conditions to scale Open API across market]], [[Russia's central bank rejects Sber, Alfa, T-Bank payment system project]], [[Bank of Russia signals no rate cut just to ease business]]. Cross-peer P/E for VTB and T-Technologies = no data (not verified from a free source) → distribution not computed, qualitative only. Read: at ~3.7x earnings and ~13% dividend yield, Sber screens cheap on an absolute basis, but this is the standard sanctions/geopolitical discount on Russian equities, not a mispricing — cheapness is structural, not an opportunity for most global capital (analysis; [UNSOURCED] on a clean peer multiple set).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Rate-cycle reversal of the profit driver.** H1 growth is powered by NII (+25.3%) while fees are flat (+1.7%); as the CBR continues easing from 14.25%, NIM compresses and the +20% profit trajectory is hard to sustain into 2026-2027 (per CBR trajectory; analysis).
2. **RAS vs IFRS / disclosure gap.** The RUB 995.3bn is unaudited RAS (parent-only), not consolidated IFRS — RAS flatters the standalone bank and omits group items; cost of risk and asset quality under a slowing economy are the silent variables here.
3. **Geopolitical/sanctions concentration.** Single-country, sanctioned issuer: earnings are strong but the equity is uninvestable for most non-Russian capital and exposed to macro/policy shocks and any renewed rate-hike scenario (fuel-price/fiscal risks flagged around the 24 Jul CBR meeting).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Not a re-rating — a confirmation that Sber remains the sector's cash machine even as the rate cushion thins; the read-across is that Russian bank earnings peak with the rate cycle and the easing path is the key swing factor (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if the CBR keeps cutting, watch H2 NII growth decelerate sharply and fee income stay soft — if H2 NII growth stays >20% y/y despite lower rates, the thesis (margin-driven, cycle-dependent) is wrong. Trigger: the 24 Jul 2026 CBR decision and Sber's H1 IFRS release. (analysis)

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Verdict (headline read).** BEAT / STRONG · net profit RUB 995.3bn (+20.4% YoY) · ROE 23.0% · driven by net interest income +25.3% YoY on a widening margin + flat cost of risk (1.5%) + operating leverage (C/I 25.7%) · no formal guidance in the release (management reaffirms the trajectory toward RUB ~2tn FY net profit). These are RAS (РСБУ, bank-only) results, not IFRS Group — the note headline says "по РПБУ". No public street consensus is published for Sber's monthly RAS prints, so beat/miss is qualitative vs the prior-year period and the run-rate.

**Key figures (with growth, 6M 2026, RAS bank-only).**
- Net profit: RUB 995.3bn (+20.4% YoY). June alone RUB 168.1bn (+17.0% YoY).
- ROE: 23.0% (6M); June 22.4%.
- Net interest income: RUB 1,800.9bn (+25.3% YoY; June +27.4% YoY) — the main profit engine, margin expansion as funding costs ease.
- Net fee & commission income: RUB 354.6bn (+1.7% YoY; +1.2% adjusted for methodology change). Weak — the soft spot of the print (June rebounded to +14.7% YoY).
- Operating expenses: RUB 588.6bn (+15.0% YoY); cost/income 25.7% (improved) — costs still rising double-digit but revenue outpaces them → positive operating leverage.
- Provisions & FV loan revaluation: RUB 361.9bn (6M); cost of risk 1.5% ex-FX, flat YoY.
- Capital adequacy N1.0: 14.6% (vs 14.1% a year ago) — cushion built, supports the ~50% payout dividend case.

**By segment / driver.** Growth is almost entirely a NII story (+25.3%): the spread between asset yields and a normalising cost of funds is widening as the CBR rate-cut cycle feeds through. Fees are near-flat (+1.7% 6M) — the drag; not scaling with the balance sheet, though June's +14.7% hints at a base-effect recovery. On the balance sheet: retail loans RUB 19.8tn (+5.7% YTD), mortgages RUB 13.2tn (+6.8% YTD) reaccelerating on subsidised programmes; corporate loans RUB 31.1tn (+2.1% YTD ex-FX) — retail is outrunning corporate. Funding: retail deposits RUB 34.2tn (+3.7% YTD), corporate funds RUB 12.7tn (+3.0% YTD) — a stable, deposit-funded book.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public consensus exists for Sber's monthly RAS results (labelled: no data / [UNSOURCED] for a "street" number) → assessed vs prior year and run-rate. Momentum is accelerating: 5M 2026 net profit was +21.1% YoY (RUB ~827bn) and 6M lands at +20.4% — high-teens/low-20s growth sustained through H1. Annualising the RUB 995bn H1 run-rate points at ~RUB 2tn FY2026 net profit, above FY2025's reported result and consistent with the "path to 2 trillion" framing in the Russian press. No prior Sber earnings note exists in the corpus for a `[[wikilink]]` trajectory comparison; the closest IR anchor is FY2025 IFRS (the IR `latest_result`), which is a different standard and not directly comparable to these RAS bank-only figures.

**Guidance / forward.** No formal quantitative guidance in the RAS release (monthly RAS prints never carry one). Independent read (analysis): at the H1 run-rate and with cost of risk contained at 1.5% and N1.0 at 14.6%, FY2026 net profit ~RUB 2tn and ROE in the low-20s look achievable barring a macro/rate shock; capital build supports a continued ~50% payout. What management leaves out of a RAS print: no IFRS Group ROE, no NIM disclosure, no forward cost-of-risk range — watch the H1 2026 IFRS Group release (expected late July, cf. the 2025-07-29 6M IFRS cadence in ir_latest) for the harder numbers.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. NII +25.3% is the whole story → margin expansion on falling funding costs. Why it matters: it is rate-cycle-driven, so it is partly a tailwind that fades if the CBR pauses — quality of the beat is good now but not all structural. Second-order: peak-margin risk into 2027.
2. Fee income +1.7% (near-flat) → the franchise's non-interest engine is stalling while the balance sheet grows. Why it matters: revenue mix is leaning harder on rate-sensitive NII, reducing diversification. Bear-case flag; watch whether the June +14.7% rebound sticks.
3. Cost of risk flat at 1.5% + N1.0 up to 14.6% → asset quality holding and capital accreting despite retail loan growth reaccelerating (+5.7% YTD). Why it matters: confirms the "high-ROE, well-capitalised, dividend-paying" bull pillar; underpins the ~RUB 2tn/50%-payout case. Second-order: room to keep growing the book without a capital raise.
4. Opex +15.0% YoY with C/I still improving to 25.7% → operating leverage intact, but cost growth is double-digit (AI/tech + wage inflation) — if NII decelerates, the C/I improvement reverses fast.
