Volt launches stablecoin checkout for Profee transfers
Sinziana Albu
09 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Volt has launched a stablecoin checkout for Profee, letting customers top up their Profee wallets using USDC ahead of cross-border money transfers.
Customers can now fund their Profee wallet and in-app balance directly from stablecoin balances held in their own crypto wallets, with Volt handling acceptance, on-chain confirmation and settlement to Profee in fiat currency. Once the wallet is funded, the fiat balance can be used for cross-border transfers and virtual card top-ups through Profee.
Previously, Profee customers holding crypto balances had to convert them into fiat and move the funds into a bank account before topping up their Profee wallet. The new checkout removes that step, allowing customers to fund their wallet directly from wallets including MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, Phantom, and Trust. Volt manages the acceptance flow in full: wallet connection, on-chain confirmation, exchange rates, and fiat settlement to Profee. In addition, KYC, AML, and transaction monitoring controls are applied in the same way as for Profee's existing payment methods, and the company's ledger, treasury, and reconciliation systems remain unchanged.
Volt already provides A2A payment infrastructure for merchants across more than 30 markets, and the stablecoin pay-in feature runs on the same platform. Profee, which is authorised by the Central Bank of Cyprus, operates across more than 90 corridors. The two companies first partnered in May 2025 on bank-account transfers before extending the relationship to stablecoin acceptance.
Target users and market context
The feature is aimed at customers who already hold balances in stablecoins rather than in bank accounts, including freelancers paid in USDC and diaspora workers who use crypto wallets for everyday transactions. For Profee, the integration is also intended to support expansion into markets with volatile local currencies, where some customers treat dollar-denominated stablecoins as a store of value. The USDC top-up is live for eligible customers in the EU.
The launch follows regulatory developments affecting stablecoins in the US and the EU. In the US, the GENIUS Act has established a federal framework for payment stablecoins, and the Digital Asset Market CLARITY Act cleared the Senate Banking Committee on 14 May 2026, before being placed on the Senate Legislative Calendar for potential full Senate consideration. In the EU, the stablecoin provisions under the Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) have applied since 30 June 2024, with the regulation fully applicable from 30 December 2024.
Volt co-founder and chief executive officer Steffen Vollert said the company aims to let regulated merchants accept stablecoins and fiat through the same infrastructure, citing interest from merchants in remittances, retail, travel, and automotive sectors. Moreover, Profee general manager Artemis Rossides said the stablecoin checkout extends the existing partnership with Volt without altering Profee's regulated operating model.

### [0] TL;DR
On **2026-07-09** **Volt** went live with a **"stablecoin checkout"** for **Profee**, letting Profee customers top up their wallet / in-app balance with **USDC** held in their own crypto wallets (MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, Phantom, Trust). Volt handles wallet connection, on-chain confirmation, FX and **fiat settlement to Profee**; the funded fiat balance is then used for Profee's cross-border transfers and virtual-card top-ups. Live for eligible EU customers. This removes the prior manual step (convert crypto→fiat→bank→top-up). It is a **live deployment** of Volt's BVNK-powered stablecoin pay-in rail, applied to a remittance use case.

### [1] Who / What
- **Volt** — London-based real-time, rail-agnostic (A2A / "Pay by Bank") money-movement platform / payments gateway; A2A infra for merchants across **30+ markets**. CEO/co-founder **Steffen Vollert**. Stablecoin acceptance runs on the same platform via its **BVNK** partnership (BVNK = enterprise stablecoin infra: acceptance, on-chain settlement, custody, compliance).
- **Profee** — cross-border money-transfer / remittance service; brand of Sibilla Solutions LTD; **authorised by the Central Bank of Cyprus** (note text), also FCA-registered; operates across **90+ corridors**, send from ~13 EU currencies into 70+ receive currencies. GM **Artemis Rossides**.
- **Feature** — USDC pay-in as a funding method for a regulated EMI wallet. KYC/AML/txn-monitoring applied as for existing methods; Profee's ledger/treasury/reconciliation unchanged (bolt-on, not a rearchitecture).

### [2] Timeline (dated)
- **May 2025** — Volt + Profee first partner on **bank-account (A2A) transfers**.
- **2025-06-27** — Volt powers real-time top-ups/payouts for e-wallet **paylado** (same wallet-funding pattern, via A2A) — [[E-wallet paylado chooses Volt for real-time top-ups and payouts]].
- **Dec 2025** — **Volt + BVNK** partnership announced to add stablecoin (USDC/EURC) acceptance at checkout — [[Volt taps BVNK to add stablecoin acceptance to checkout]].
- **2026-02-12** — Volt goes live with "Pay with Stablecoins" checkout across its network — [[Volt integrates stablecoin acceptance into payments network]].
- **2026-06-30 / 2024-12-30** — EU **MiCA** stablecoin provisions applicable; US **GENIUS Act** framework + **CLARITY Act** (cleared Senate Banking Committee 2026-05-14).
- **2026-07-09** — **Volt stablecoin checkout for Profee goes live** (this note).
- **2026-07-10** — Circle receives OCC approval for national trust bank (adjacent stablecoin-legitimacy milestone).

### [3] Why it matters
- Extends Volt's stablecoin pay-in rail from generic merchant checkout into **remittances** — one of the highest-fit stablecoin use cases (the ~$900B remittance market is the front line of the stablecoin-vs-Western-Union fight, per Fortune Jan 2026).
- **Target users**: freelancers/gig workers paid in USDC and diaspora workers who already hold crypto — funds transfers directly without an off-ramp. Also positions Profee for **markets with volatile local currencies** where USDC is a store of value.
- Reinforces the "**one integration, stablecoin + fiat**" thesis Volt is selling to remittance/retail/travel/automotive merchants.

### Red-team Q&A

1. **Is this a new product or a re-skin of existing Volt/BVNK rail?** — Mostly a **deployment**: same stablecoin pay-in stack Volt built with BVNK (Dec 2025 / live Feb 2026), now wired to a specific remittance partner. Novelty = the remittance/wallet-funding use case, not the tech. Lowers the "importance" ceiling.

2. **Is it live or an announcement/pilot?** — **Live** for eligible EU customers (USDC top-up). Confirmed by note + Finextra + Volt newsroom. Not vaporware.

3. **How big is Profee?** — Mid-size remittance EMI: 90+ corridors, CBC-authorised, FCA-registered, ~€100k/yr per-user cap. Not a top-tier player (not Wise/Remitly/Western Union scale). Limits reach.

4. **Which stablecoins / chains?** — USDC only (per note). Wallets: MetaMask, Coinbase, Phantom, Trust (so multi-chain incl. Solana via Phantom). No USDT/EURC/PYUSD mentioned for this integration.

5. **Regulatory conflict?** — Discrepancy: note says "Central Bank of Cyprus"; external shows CBC + FCA. Consistent (cross-border EMI holds multiple licences). MiCA/GENIUS framing is context, not a specific approval for this feature. No red flag.

6. **Does it change Profee's economics/risk model?** — Explicitly **no**: ledger/treasury/reconciliation unchanged; KYC/AML same as existing methods. Volt absorbs on-chain acceptance + FX + fiat settlement. Bolt-on.

7. **Is the "removes conversion step" claim material?** — Yes qualitatively (UX for crypto-holding senders), but no volume/adoption metrics disclosed. Benefit is real but unquantified.

8. **Financial terms?** — None disclosed (no round, no fee, no volume). Product partnership, not a funding/M&A event.

9. **Competitive threat / differentiation?** — Crowded: Western Union, MoneyGram, Ripple/RLUSD, Circle+partners, Stripe, PayPal PYUSD all chasing stablecoin remittances. Volt's edge = single A2A+stablecoin checkout integration. This deal is incremental proof, not a moat.

10. **Is USDC-in-remittance a durable trend or hype?** — Durable: stablecoins ~$46T volume in 2025, remittances a named killer app (Fortune, Bessemer, Ripple). Tailwind is real; this is one small instance of it.

11. **Any duplicate of prior Volt notes in the vault?** — Related but **not duplicate**: prior notes cover Volt+BVNK partnership (Dec 2025) and network-wide stablecoin launch (Feb 2026); this is a *specific named partner (Profee) going live*. Distinct event, distinct date.

12. **Freshness vs the Feb 2026 Volt stablecoin note?** — Fresh: new counterparty + new vertical (remittances) + new date (2026-07-09). Not a rehash of the same announcement.

13. **Reach of "EU only, eligible customers"?** — Narrow initial scope; limits near-term impact. Expansion (volatile-currency markets) is stated intent, not yet delivered.

14. **Second-source corroboration?** — Yes: The Paypers (primary), Finextra, Volt newsroom. Well-sourced, not single-source.

15. **What would make this a 4-5?** — Disclosed volumes, exclusivity, a tier-1 remittance partner, or a new licence. None present.

**Verdict:** Real, live, well-sourced, on a durable trend — but incremental (known rail + mid-size partner + EU-only + no financials/metrics).

**Importance: 2/5** — Legitimate, dated, corroborated product go-live that reinforces the stablecoin-remittance thesis and Volt's A2A+stablecoin positioning, but it is an incremental deployment of an existing rail with a mid-size partner, narrow initial scope, and no disclosed economics.

# Market Research: Volt launches stablecoin checkout for Profee transfers

_As of 2026-07-15. Sector context + in-base comps + risk flags. Skeptical read; no invented figures._

## What this is (positioning)
Volt is a rail-agnostic, real-time A2A ("Pay by Bank") payment platform (30+ markets). This launch extends its **stablecoin pay-in** rail into a remittance use-case: Profee (Cyprus EMI, authorised by Central Bank of Cyprus, 90+ corridors) customers top up their fiat wallet directly from self-custody stablecoin wallets (MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, Phantom, Trust) using **USDC**. Volt handles wallet connection, on-chain confirmation, FX and **fiat settlement to Profee**; Profee's ledger/treasury/reconciliation and KYC/AML are unchanged. Live for eligible EU customers only. Partnership builds on a May 2025 A2A/bank-transfer integration.

The strategic thesis: Volt becomes a single checkout layer where regulated merchants accept **both fiat (A2A) and stablecoins** through one integration. Volt cites merchant interest in remittances, retail, travel, automotive. Target end-users: freelancers/gig workers paid in USDC and diaspora workers holding crypto as a day-to-day account, plus users in volatile-currency markets who treat USD stablecoins as a store of value.

## Sector context
- **Stablecoins in cross-border/remittances is the hottest adoption vector.** Cross-border payments are consistently cited as the #1 institutional stablecoin use-case (Fireblocks 2026: ~90% of surveyed institutions taking action). Industry commentary (Triple-A's Eric Barbier, in-base note "stablecoins could replace 25% of cross-border payments") frames a large TAM. Corridor economics (e.g. Philippines ~0.5–1.5% all-in vs. legacy remittance fees) are the pull. Caveat: vendor/marketing sources — treat "85% of digital remittances via stablecoins" type stats as directional, not audited.
- **Regulatory tailwind, but this is the actual moat/gate.** EU MiCA stablecoin provisions apply since 2024-06-30 (fully applicable 2024-12-30); USDC (Circle) is MiCA-compliant, which is why Volt/Profee can offer it in the EU today. US GENIUS Act sets a federal payment-stablecoin framework; the Digital Asset Market CLARITY Act cleared Senate Banking Committee 2026-05-14. Regulation is enabling here, not a headwind.
- **The A2A + stablecoin convergence is now a competitive pattern, not unique.** Volt's own stablecoin acceptance (launched Feb 2026) presents "Pay by Crypto Wallet" alongside "Pay by Bank," supporting USDC and EURC — the same converged-checkout play now applied to a remittance partner.

## In-base comparables (grep over News/2026-07)
Direct / adjacent competitors and analogues already in the base:
- **RedotPay ↔ OpenPayd** (2026-07-01) — stablecoin payment infrastructure for a consumer crypto-card player; same "abstract fiat↔stablecoin movement" thesis.
- **Velocity — $38M Series A** (2026-07-15) — embedding stablecoins into consumer payment back-ends / global banking network. Direct infra competitor being funded.
- **Depa Finance ↔ Modern Treasury** (2026-07-09) — stablecoin-native cross-border rails + Global USD Accounts; competing orchestration layer.
- **Visa + M-Pesa stablecoin pilot in DRC** (2026-07-02) — stablecoins settling wallet top-ups behind the scenes; scaled incumbent doing the same "invisible stablecoin rail under a fiat wallet" model Volt/Profee use.
- **Sunfintech ↔ Flutterwave** (2026-07-15), **Flutterwave ↔ Circle Ventures** — emerging-market cross-border/remittance rails, USDC-aligned.
- **Thunes / Triple-A** (podcast note 2026-07-03) — payout-network incumbents supporting stablecoin funding; the payout side of the same corridor.
- **A2A/open-banking peers**: GoCardless+Sage Pay by Bank, Yapily, Nopan — Volt's core A2A turf; shows the "Pay by Bank" base into which stablecoin pay-in is bolted.

Read: Volt is **not first and not alone**. Its differentiation is bundling A2A + stablecoin pay-in for regulated EMIs under one integration, with fiat settlement so the partner's regulated model is untouched — a genuinely useful de-risking, but a crowded lane (Visa, Circle-backed players, and funded infra startups all present).

## Risk flags
1. **Attribution / dependency risk — Volt's stablecoin acceptance is powered by BVNK, not built in-house.** The note says "Volt manages the acceptance flow in full," but external sources (PYMNTS, Volt newsroom, Financial IT, Feb 2026) show Volt's stablecoin pay-in runs via a **BVNK partnership**. So the on-chain acceptance/settlement leg is a third-party dependency; the "full control" framing is a simplification. Concentration/vendor risk sits under the headline.
2. **Stablecoin demand momentum is softening.** In-base note "Stablecoin market cap shrinks $10B since May" (2026-07-14): ~$7.7B June contraction, largest single-month dollar drop since the 2022 Terra-Luna collapse (~3%). Not a crisis, but the launch lands into a cooling-liquidity backdrop — adoption stats used to justify TAM are pro-cyclical.
3. **Single stablecoin, single region, single partner, thin disclosure.** USDC only, EU only, one EMI (Profee), one channel/source (The Paypers), n_mentions=1. No volumes, no fee take-rate, no user counts disclosed — impossible to size impact. Reads as a product-launch PR datapoint, not a proven revenue line.
4. **Depeg / FX / settlement risk passed to the flow.** Model converts self-custody USDC → fiat at Volt-set exchange rates before Profee settlement. A USDC depeg or on-chain-confirmation delay directly hits the top-up value; users bear FX/timing basis. Fine in calm markets, stress-untested.
5. **Regulatory whipsaw is the key single-point sensitivity.** The entire EU offering rests on USDC's MiCA compliance and Profee's Cyprus EMI licence. Any MiCA re-interpretation of stablecoin pay-ins, or corridor-specific AML tightening, could pull availability. US CLARITY Act is still only calendared, not law — US expansion is not yet unlocked.
6. **Both counterparties are private, no financials.** Volt and Profee are private; no valuation, funding-runway, or transaction-economics data to ground durability. Competitor Velocity just raised $38M for a near-identical thesis — capital intensity of the lane is rising.

## Bottom line
Credible, well-timed extension of Volt's A2A franchise into stablecoin remittances with a real regulated partner and a clean UX-de-friction story (removes the manual crypto→fiat→bank→top-up hop). But it is a crowded, cooling-liquidity lane; the acceptance rail leans on BVNK (third party); and disclosure is too thin to size. Treat as a directional signal of the A2A+stablecoin convergence theme, not a standalone value event.
