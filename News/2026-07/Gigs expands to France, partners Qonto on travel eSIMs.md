# Context-enrichment: Gigs expands to France, partners Qonto on travel eSIMs
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Gigs — the "OS for embedded telecom" / MVNE — announced its **France market entry**, launching with **Qonto** as anchor partner. Qonto becomes "the first French fintech to embed travel eSIMs directly into its product," letting its ~600k SME customers discover, activate and manage travel connectivity inside the Qonto app (install in <1 min, 5G/4G LTE in 180+ countries per Gigs' France PR). Admins can assign eSIMs to travelling employees "as simply as they manage payment cards."

- **This is a launch (live), not just an MoU** — the eSIM feature is described as available in-app. Good.
- **The "first French fintech" claim is the marketing hook** — narrowly true (national-first framing), but it is the Nth iteration of a pattern Gigs has already run globally many times over. Two de-PR points:
  - Gigs is selling the SAME embedded-travel-eSIM SKU it has shipped repeatedly (N26, Nubank Ultravioleta, PicPay, ShopBack, Wealthsimple, Motorola, a LATAM airline). Qonto is a new logo + a new geography for Gigs, not a new product capability.
  - The justifying stat (**"42% of Qonto customers have ≥1 employee travelling for business," top destinations US/UK/Morocco**) is a demand-sizing figure from the vendor PR, not a disclosed take-up/attach metric. No pricing, no revenue split, no exclusivity, no target attach disclosed.
- **Why framed this way:** Gigs needs a marquee French anchor to open the France go-to-market; Qonto gets a cheap, on-brand "perk" to bolt onto its SME super-app narrative ahead of its banking-licence build-out. Both sides benefit from the "first in France" superlative more than the economics justify.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Two distinct competitive frames:
- **Gigs' rivals (embedded-telecom / MVNE):** Airwallex-powered Circles (telco embedded finance, 70+ countries — [[Circles integrates Airwallex embedded finance for telcos]]), 1Global, Telgea, Firsty ([[Belfius partners Firsty.app for global mobile banking access]]), plus travel-eSIM aggregators (Holafly, GigSky, Airalo behind the scenes). Gigs is the category leader by fintech logo density (Klarna, Revolut Mobile, Nubank, OnePay, Cash App, Sezzle, Lendable) — a genuine "Stripe-for-telecom" position (Speedinvest thesis).
- **Qonto's SME/travel-perk peers:** Every neobank now ships travel eSIM as a feature: N26 (Italy [[N26 launches Travel eSIM mobile plans in Italy]], Spain [[N26 launches eSIM mobile plans in Spain]]), Revolut, Monzo ([[Monzo opens waitlist for eSIM mobile plans with loyalty discounts]]), YouTrip, Brubank, Nubank Ultravioleta. On the SME-specific side, **Tide already shipped Gigs-powered business phone numbers** ([[Tide launches Business Phone Number plans powered by Gigs]], Mar 2026) — and Tide competes head-on with Qonto in France (Tide launched France Sept 2025). So Gigs is arming both sides of the FR SME-banking fight.
- **Position:** Qonto is a **fast-follower**, not a pioneer — it is catching up to a saturated neobank feature, differentiating only on the SME/employee-assignment angle (which Tide reached first via the same vendor).
- **Second-order:** the "first French fintech" superlative is fragile precisely because the underlying capability is commoditised by Gigs itself. The eSIM perk is a table-stakes retention feature, not a moat.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Qonto:** French B2B neobank, profitable, ~600k SME customers, applied for a French banking licence in 2025 ([[Qonto applies for French banking licence]]), building own card processor, expanding product surface aggressively — Upvest MMFs ([[Qonto partners with Upvest to offer businesses money market funds]]), Acasi accounting acquisition ([[Qonto acquires Acasi to add certified accounting in-house]]), 4% remuneration, AI agents across 600k customers ([[Qonto deploys two AI agents across 600,000 customers]]), Southern-Europe/CEE hiring. Target: 2m customers by 2030.
  - **Why this move fits:** Qonto's whole strategy is to **own more of the SME's operating stack** and lift primary-account share (78%). A travel eSIM is a low-cost, high-visibility engagement/perk add-on that reinforces "run your whole business in Qonto" — consistent with owning the value chain (same logic as the banking-licence push, just at the perk layer). It is peripheral to the P&L but on-strategy for stickiness.
- **Gigs:** founded 2020 (Frank/Bauer), $73M Series B led by Ribbit (Dec 2024; Google Gradient/YC/Speedinvest — NOT a16z), 850M+ combined distribution reach, claims 25x growth with fintech partners. France/Qonto is a logo-and-geography expansion, directly funded by the "expand geographical footprint" Series B mandate.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Genuinely new?** No — the capability is old for Gigs. New = (a) France as a Gigs market, (b) Qonto as a Gigs logo, (c) national-first SME framing. That's incremental, not novel.
- **Traction?** Only demand-sizing (42% of customers travel; 600k base) — **no disclosed attach/activation numbers.** Prior comparable launches (N26, Nubank) also went uncorroborated on take-up. Treat as an announced launch with unknown adoption.
- **Who captures the margin?** In embedded telecom the **MVNE/OS layer (Gigs) captures the durable, recurring, multi-tenant margin**; the fintech distributor (Qonto) gets a thin resale/perk margin and mostly non-monetary retention value. This is the key second-order point: the recurring economics accrue to Gigs, not Qonto. Qonto's win is engagement/brand, not revenue — the eSIM is a cost-of-retention line, not a profit line.
- **What breaks it:** commoditisation. If every SME neobank ships the same Gigs-powered eSIM (Tide already has), the feature stops differentiating and becomes a price/UX race — pushing value further toward Gigs and toward whoever owns the customer relationship.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Trend:** fintech-as-telecom / "fintech's mobile moment" is now mainstream — 2025-26 saw N26, Revolut Mobile, Monzo, Klarna, Cash App, Nubank NuCel, OnePay, Sezzle, Zip, Lendable all enter. eSIM/MVNO has shifted from novelty to expected neobank feature. Gigs is the arms dealer riding all of it.
- **Qonto next:** expect the eSIM to be folded into premium SME tiers and its Southern-Europe/CEE expansion; watch whether Qonto ever discloses attach. The bigger Qonto story remains the banking licence + up-market push, not eSIMs.
- **Counterintuitive second-order:** the more neobanks pile in, the more the value concentrates in Gigs (single supplier to competing distributors) — the distributors commoditise each other's perk while Gigs' multi-tenant recurring revenue compounds. For Qonto specifically, this is a small, defensible-enough engagement feature but **not a needle-mover on economics or competitive position** vs Tide (which had it first via the same vendor).
- **Risks/silence:** no pricing, margins, exclusivity, or attach targets disclosed; "first in France" is a marketing frame, not a moat.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Two stacked markets. (1) Travel eSIM / embedded connectivity: the eSIM-for-travel market was ~$6.8bn in 2025, projected to ~$28.4bn by 2034 at ~17.2% CAGR (per intelmarketresearch/dataintelo secondary reports, as of 2025 — treat as directional, not audited). Kaleido Intelligence pegs retail travel-eSIM spend at ~$3.3bn in 2025 (+165% vs 2023). (2) SME neobank value-added services — Qonto's core: EU B2B business accounts. Structure: connectivity is fragmenting away from carriers toward "connectivity-as-a-service" middleware (Gigs, 1Global) that lets any app spin up an MVNO; value is migrating from the carrier to the distribution surface (the fintech app) that owns the customer and the "why now" moment (crossing a border). Barrier to entry for the fintech is low (integrate an SDK); the moat sits with whoever owns the SME relationship. Why now: roaming remains a real, recurring pain (US carriers ~$10/day; ~$70 for a EU week before one byte), and super-app perk bundling is the current playbook to lift engagement/retention and justify premium tiers (per Gigs/fintechmagazine, 2025-26).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: for Gigs — number of live embedded deployments, activated eSIMs, recurring platform revenue; for Qonto — customers (600k), revenue, subscription+commission mix, deposit interest income, banking-license progress. Two distinct competitive planes:
- *Embedded-connectivity rails (Gigs' plane):* Gigs vs 1Global/1GLOBAL (powers Revolut roaming — 200k signups in weeks per 1Global case study), plus MVNE incumbents. Gigs already powers Revolut, Nubank, Klarna, Tide, Cash App, PicPay, LATAM Airlines, Walmart One Pay (per gigs.com/llms.gigs.com, 2025-26). Basis of competition: breadth of carrier coverage + developer experience ("Stripe for telecom" framing). Gigs' position: leader/ahead in the fintech-distribution niche `(analysis)`; moat = intangible integrations + multi-carrier network + switching costs once embedded.
- *Consumer travel-eSIM brands (the demand Qonto's SMEs would otherwise buy direct):* Airalo (190+ countries), Holafly (>$500m cumulative revenue, ~$200m in 2024, 15m+ eSIMs sold, 150+ destinations — per Holafly PR, Sep 2025), Ubigi. These are the disintermediation threat: SMEs can already buy Airalo/Holafly without Qonto.
- *Qonto's position:* "first French fintech to embed travel eSIMs" — catching-up vs global peers, not a first-mover globally. Nubank shipped a travel eSIM in 2024 (Ultravioleta perk; enhanced Jul 2025); Revolut runs eSIM + full mobile plans; Tide (Gigs-powered) launched Business Phone Numbers in Mar 2026 [[Tide launches Business Phone Number plans powered by Gigs]]. Qonto's edge is distribution into its 600k EU SME base, not novelty.

**Comps & multiples.** IR-grounded (Qonto, PRIMARY):
- Qonto is private — no public market cap; multiples are illustrative on last-round value, not market cap. Last disclosed valuation: €4.4bn (Sifted, via 2024 accounts) — down from the $5bn/2022 raise. 2024 revenue €448.7m (+44% YoY vs €311.6m 2023); of which subscriptions+commissions €261.3m (+35%), deposit-interest income €136m (+24.5%). Net profit €144m 2024 (2nd profitable year, doubling €71m in 2023) [[Qonto doubles profit to EUR144m in second profitable year]]. 600,000+ customers across 8 EU markets, banking-license filed with ACPR Jul 2025 (per qonto.com IR PR 2025-07-03, https://qonto.com/docs/pdf/03072025_cilicense_pressrelease_global.pdf; also TechCrunch 2025-07-02).

Rich on an absolute basis but the name is profitable (~32% net margin: €144m/€448.7m) and growing 44%, so revenue-multiple sits in a defensible band for a profitable EU fintech; not an outlier-flag on growth-adjusted view. P/E on €4.4bn/€144m = **~30.6x** — in-line-to-rich for a 44%-grower.
- Gigs (protagonist rail): Series B $73m led by Ribbit Capital (Dec 2024), >$100m raised total (Speedinvest, Gradient Ventures, YC). **No disclosed post-money valuation** — `[UNSOURCED]`; no revenue disclosed → EV/Revenue **no data**.

Distribution not computed for the rail layer (fewer than 3 comparable disclosed figures) — qualitative only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Third-party rail dependence + thin economics.** Qonto sits atop Gigs (which sits atop carriers). The eSIM is a retention perk, not a margin engine — Qonto discloses no take-rate or revenue on connectivity, and the release is silent on economics. Second-order: value/margin is captured downstack (Gigs, carriers); Qonto risks paying for engagement without a P&L contribution.
2. **Disintermediation / low differentiation.** Airalo, Holafly, Nubank, Revolut, Tide already offer embedded/consumer travel eSIMs; "first French fintech" is a narrow claim. Second-order: as embedding becomes table-stakes (Gigs powers most peers), it stops being a moat and becomes a cost of parity — churn protection at best, not acquisition.
3. **Announced vs adopted, and core-execution distraction.** Metric disclosed is 600k *eligible* customers, not activations/attach rate `[UNSOURCED]`. Qonto's value driver is the ACPR banking license + deposit monetization, not eSIMs; connectivity perks are peripheral to the credit/deposit thesis. Second-order: attention/roadmap risk if perks divert from the license and lending build-out.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Embedded connectivity is crossing from novelty to expected feature in EU SME neobanking — this is a parity move, not a re-rating `(analysis)`. Falsifiable thesis: if Gigs converts its fintech-distribution lead into pricing power, the re-rating accrues to the rail (Gigs), not the banks embedding it; watch for a Gigs up-round with a disclosed valuation/revenue as the trigger. Thesis breaks if Qonto/peers disclose that connectivity meaningfully lifts attach/retention or ARPU — which would flip eSIMs from cost-of-parity to a genuine engagement lever. Near-term watch item for Qonto's own thesis remains the ACPR license decision, not eSIMs.
