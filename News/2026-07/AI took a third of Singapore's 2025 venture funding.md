# AI took a third of Singapore's 2025 venture funding

Singapore-based AI startups raised about S$1.8 billion ($1.4 billion) in 2025, accounting for nearly a third of the country’s total venture funding value as investors poured more capital into artificial intelligence and deeptech bets.
 The recently released Singapore Venture Funding Landscape Report 2025 by EY-Parthenon, in partnership with Enterprise Singapore, showed that total venture funding in Singapore in 2025 hit S$5.9 billion ($4.6 billion).  
 At the forefront was investments towards AI-led opportunities, as AI startups accounted for 42.8% of the 472 deal volume in 2025. Singapore’s AI deal value grew 28% year-on-year, while the sector’s share of the nation’s total deal value nearly doubled to 30% in 2025.   
 Among the notable deals are Supabase, which raised over S$127 million in a Series E round to scale its developer platform; and Surfin Meta Digital Technologies , which secured over S$33 million to support its expansion into new markets. 
 This, according to EnterpriseSG, reflected “strong investor conviction and capital concentration” into AI-led opportunities. 
The rise in AI bets comes against the backdrop of overall declining deal value in Singapore, with the nation seeing a 34% decline in 2025. The deal value has been in decline for years now, reflecting overall sentiment in the region.
 
 “Even as we navigate evolving global economic conditions, Singapore’s position as a globally connected hub continues to attract founders and investors. The strong foundations of our ecosystem have allowed us to weather challenges and continue to build capacity in priority emerging areas such as AI and deep tech,” said Emily Liew, Assistant Managing Director, Innovation, EnterpriseSG.  
 Deeptech investments also expanded during the year, with its share of deal value hitting 24.7% in 2025, rising from 11.1% three years prior, while share of deal volume increased from 11.4% to 19.3% over the same period. 
 Amid rising AI and deeptech investments, Singapore remained primarily a fintech hub, with the sector still getting the biggest share of venture funding during the year. Fintech deal value rose 34% year- on-year to over S$2.1 billion in 2025. With this, fintech accounted for 36% of total fundraise value during the year. 
 In total, Singapore accounted for 74% of fintech funding in the region last year. 
 “This is underpinned by Singapore’s position as ASEAN’s financial hub, supported by a deep fintech ecosystem and proactive regulatory frameworks that enable faster market entry and de-risk early commercialisation,” EnterpriseSG said.  
 Overall, EnterpriseSG said the numbers reflect the resilience of its startup ecosystem, noting that the nation retained fourth  position globally in the latest StartupBlink Global Startup Ecosystem Index (GSEI). Singapore climbed from tenth in the rankings  in 2021 to fourth in 2025 and holding the position in 2026.  
 “Our continued investments,  including the recent S$37 billion under Research, Innovation and Enterprise (RIE) 2030 and the S$1 billion for the Startup SG Equity scheme, reaffirm our commitment to foster an innovation-led economy through strong partnerships, talent development, and deepening research capabilities,” Liew said.

# Context-enrichment: AI took a third of Singapore's 2025 venture funding
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
EY-Parthenon + Enterprise Singapore released the **Singapore Venture Funding Landscape Report 2025** (reported by DealStreetAsia, published ~2 Jul 2026). Hard numbers:
- **Total Singapore VC in 2025: S$5.9B ($4.6B)** — down **~34% YoY** (from ~$7.0B in 2024). Deal value has fallen for years.
- **AI startups: ~S$1.8B ($1.4B)**, ≈30% of total deal value (share **nearly doubled** YoY), **42.8% of the 472 deals** by volume; AI deal value **+28% YoY**.
- **Fintech is still the single biggest sector: S$2.1B deal value (+34% YoY), 36% of total.** Singapore = **74% of all SEA fintech funding**.
- **Deeptech: 24.7% of deal value** (up from 11.1% three years prior; volume share 11.4%→19.3%).
- Named deals: **Supabase Series E >S$127M**; **Surfin Meta Digital Technologies >S$33M**.

**+ Why framed this way:** The headline ("AI took a third") is a *share-of-a-shrinking-pie* story dressed as a growth story. Total funding fell 34%, so AI's rising share (30%) partly reflects the **denominator collapsing**, not just AI booming — though AI's absolute +28% is real. EnterpriseSG's phrase "strong investor conviction and capital concentration" is the honest tell: capital is **concentrating into fewer, later, AI/deeptech bets** while early-stage and other sectors contract. Note the framing conflates two figures: fintech is *still* #1 by value (36%), yet the headline leads with AI (30%) — the "AI narrative" is being foregrounded over the fintech reality.

## [1] Competitors / peers (comparable ecosystems & reports)
- **Global backdrop:** SVB/BCG data (see [[AI-enabled fintech took 30% of 2025 venture capital investment]]) says AI = 58% of *global* VC in 2025 and AI-fintech = 30% globally. Singapore's ~30% AI share is thus roughly *in line with the global AI-fintech skew* — Singapore is riding, not leading, the global concentration. (Same "30%" number, entirely different scope — do not confuse.)
- **Regional peers:** Per EY-Parthenon's own city ranking, Singapore ($4.6B, 472 deals) sits **4th globally by deal value behind Silicon Valley ($162B), New York ($27B), London ($14.7B), Beijing ($10.3B), Shanghai ($8.6B)** — an order-of-magnitude gap to the top tier; Singapore is a hub *within* Asia, not a global capital magnet.
- **APAC funding is at a decade low** ([[KPMG: Asia-Pacific fintech funding falls to a decade low]]) — Singapore's decline is the regional norm, not a Singapore-specific failure.

**+ Why:** The concentration is **sectoral, not name-specific** — it is a beta bet on the AI theme, mirrored globally. The multiple gap to Silicon Valley reflects capital depth and exit markets, not deal quality; it closes only if SEA gets liquid public/M&A exit paths for tech.

## [2] Company / ecosystem history & fit
- Singapore's VC value has declined **for several consecutive years**; 2025's $4.6B continues that. Yet Singapore **climbed StartupBlink GSEI from 10th (2021) → 4th (2025), holding in 2026**.
- State capital underpins this: **S$37B RIE 2030** and **S$1B Startup SG Equity**.
- Late-stage share rose to **33.3% of volume (from 26.9% in 2024)**; early-stage contracted — a maturing/de-risking ecosystem.

**+ Why the govt-narrative posture:** EnterpriseSG must tell a "resilience" story because the raw trend (‑34%) is negative. The structural logic is real though: Singapore leans on **regulatory speed + ASEAN financial-hub status** to keep the fintech crown (74% of SEA fintech) even as total dollars shrink — it competes on *institutional moat*, not deal volume.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
This is a **research-report data drop**, not a company event — its "novelty" is the *first full-year 2025 read* on Singapore's ecosystem.
- **Real signal (traction, not announcement):** absolute AI +28% YoY and deeptech share 11%→24.7% are genuine reallocations of live capital, backed by named priced rounds (Supabase, Surfin Meta).
- **Weak signal:** "AI took a third" is partly a denominator artifact; the more durable fact is **fintech still #1 (36%) + Singapore = 74% of SEA fintech**.

**+ Who captures the margin:** The report shows *where money went*, not *what returned*. Capital concentration into AI/deeptech + late stage means **fewer, bigger bets** — this makes the ecosystem's headline dependent on a handful of names (a Supabase down-round would visibly dent the aggregate). Concentration reads as strength but is **fragility** at the index level.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Sentiment: cautious. Investors "demand more from startups"; early-stage funding is contracting regionally; APAC at a decade low.
- Structural drivers: continued **flight to AI/deeptech quality + late stage**; Singapore defends the fintech hub via regulation and ASEAN gateway role.
- Risks: (a) if the global AI VC wave cools, Singapore's ~30% AI share unwinds fastest; (b) thin exit markets cap the multiple vs global peers; (c) concentration = index fragility.
- **Counterintuitive second-order effect:** the "AI took a third" headline signals *momentum* but is really a symptom of a **shrinking, concentrating market** — resilience of *share* masks decline of *scale*.

## Top challenge / red-team questions

1. **Denominator effect:** AI's share "nearly doubled to 30%" — how much is AI *growing* vs the *total pie shrinking 34%*? AI absolute is +28% YoY, total is ‑34%, so a large part of the share gain is the denominator collapsing. **(answered: both, but share gain is inflated by decline).**
2. **AI vs fintech headline conflict:** Fintech is still #1 by value (36% vs AI's 30%). Why does the headline lead with AI? Narrative preference for the AI story over the fintech reality. **(answered).**
3. **Definition overlap:** Are "AI", "deeptech", and "fintech" mutually exclusive buckets, or does an AI-fintech startup get double-counted across the 30%/36%/24.7% figures? Report methodology unclear. **(open — likely overlapping categories).**
4. **Concentration fragility:** How dependent is the S$1.8B AI total on 1–2 mega-rounds (e.g. Supabase S$127M)? If concentrated, the aggregate is one down-round from a visible dent. **(open — likely top-heavy).**
5. **Is Supabase even "Singaporean"?** Supabase's $10.5B valuation / $500M raise is a US-narrative company; how much of its round counts to Singapore? Domicile vs operations matters for the "Singapore AI" claim. **(open).**
6. **Returns vs deployment:** The report measures *money in*, not DPI/exits. Is any of this concentration actually returning capital? No exit data given. **(open — no exit data).**
7. **74% of SEA fintech — real strength or small pond?** Singapore's 74% SEA fintech share sits atop a *declining* SEA total; is dominance of a shrinking market meaningful? Contextual with [[KPMG: Asia-Pacific fintech funding falls to a decade low]]. **(answered: share strong, scale weak).**
8. **State capital crutch:** How much of the "resilience" depends on S$37B RIE 2030 / S$1B Startup SG Equity vs private conviction? EnterpriseSG is both funder and narrator — conflict of framing. **(open).**
9. **Exit-market gap:** Singapore is 4th by deal value but an order of magnitude below Silicon Valley ($4.6B vs $162B). Does the ecosystem have exit paths to justify late-stage concentration? Thin IPO/M&A markets are the ceiling. **(answered: exits are the binding constraint).**
10. **Early-stage contraction:** Late-stage rose to 33.3% of volume; early-stage shrank. Does starving early-stage today hollow out the 2028 pipeline? Second-order risk to future funding. **(open — structural risk).**
11. **YoY base gaming:** Is the "+28% AI / +34% fintech" measured off a depressed 2024 base? Growth rates flatter against a weak comparator. **(open).**
12. **Global correlation:** Singapore's ~30% AI share ≈ global AI-fintech 30% (SVB/BCG). Is Singapore leading or just importing the global AI beta? Riding, not leading. **(answered).**
13. **What breaks the thesis:** If global AI VC cools in 2026, which SEA hub's headline unwinds fastest? Singapore's, given its ~30% AI dependency. **(answered — analysis).**
14. **Enterprise-software collapse:** Enterprise SW/data infra fell $2.2B→$1.0B (‑53%). Is AI *cannibalizing* enterprise-SW budgets, i.e. reclassification rather than net-new? Possible relabeling of the same bets. **(open — plausible reclassification).**
15. **Report independence:** EY-Parthenon (paid advisory) + EnterpriseSG (govt promoter) co-author — how much is the framing marketing for Singapore Inc.? Treat "resilience" claims skeptically. **(answered: framing bias present).**

**Importance: 3/5** — Credible, well-sourced full-year data drop from an authoritative source (EY-Parthenon/EnterpriseSG) with real deal-level detail and clear signals (AI +28%, deeptech share doubling, fintech still #1, Singapore = 74% of SEA fintech). But it is a *macro research report*, not a company/deal event, the headline "third" is partly a denominator artifact of a market down 34%, and it lacks exit/returns data. Useful ecosystem context for the digest, not a market-moving item.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is a market-report story: EY-Parthenon's *Singapore Venture Funding Landscape Report 2025* (with Enterprise Singapore), so figures are report-derived, not company disclosures — treat methodology-dependent. Size/mix (per EY-Parthenon via DealStreetAsia, EnterpriseSG PDF, caproasia, TNGlobal, all Jul 2026): total Singapore VC deal value 2025 = S$5.9bn ($4.6bn) across 472 deals; deal value **fell 34% YoY** (and deal volume ~35%), the continuation of a multi-year contraction, not a one-off. AI is the concentration story: AI deal value **+28% YoY**, its share of total value **~doubled to 30%** (S$1.8bn/$1.4bn), and AI was 42.8% of deal *volume*. Deep-tech share of value hit 24.7% (from 11.1% three years prior). Yet fintech is still #1 by value: **S$2.1bn, +34% YoY, 36% of total** — i.e. fintech and AI overlap and both grew while the overall pie shrank. Structure: highly concentrated at the top of the funnel — capital rotated into AI/deep-tech mega-rounds while early/seed and non-AI sectors bore the decline (EnterpriseSG's own phrase: "capital concentration"). Why now: global AI capital wave + Singapore's regulatory/hub positioning (MAS, RIE 2030 S$37bn, Startup SG Equity S$1bn) pulling a shrinking regional pool into one city.

**Competitive landscape.** KPIs for a VC-landscape story: deal value, deal volume, sector share of value, YoY growth, median/avg round size. Singapore's peers are the *other SEA hubs* it out-draws, not company competitors. Regional concentration: Singapore took the lion's share of a falling regional pool — the note cites **74%** of SEA fintech funding, but independent Tracxn/fintechnews data for 9M-2025 put Singapore at **84–87%** of a ~US$835m ASEAN fintech total (down ~36%) — different windows/samples (full-year vs 9M, EY vs Tracxn), so read "dominant share, exact figure source-dependent." Recent moves the note names: **Supabase** Series E >S$127m (dev platform); **Surfin Meta Digital Technologies** >S$33m — both AI/software, illustrating where the money went. Corroborating regional frame (DealStreetAsia, Fortune, Jul 2025–2026): SEA equity deal volume ~461, lowest in 6+ years, funding down ~80% from 2022 peak; SEA deep-tech +19% to ~$1.0bn even as volume fell — same "up in value, down in count" concentration pattern regionally. Position: Singapore = ahead / regional gateway; moat = regulatory/institutional (MAS, sovereign capital, hub status), i.e. intangible + network, not product (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** No single-company valuation here — this is aggregate market data, so **EV/Revenue, P/E etc. = not applicable / no data** (nothing to compute). Internal comps from base (same SEA/Singapore funding-report thread):

**Risk flags.**
1. **Methodology / single-source risk.** Every headline number is one report (EY-Parthenon + EnterpriseSG, a state agency with an incentive to frame "resilience"). "30% AI share," "42.8% of deals," and "74% of SEA fintech" are definition-sensitive (what counts as AI vs deep-tech vs fintech overlap; deal-value coverage; disclosed-only rounds). Independent data (Tracxn 84–87%) diverges — so the *level* is soft even if the *direction* is well-corroborated.
2. **Concentration masquerading as health.** A 34% funding decline dressed as an "AI boom": growth is in a handful of large AI/deep-tech rounds while the broad early-stage base contracts. Second-order — headline "AI took a third" can hide a thinning seed pipeline that feeds future rounds.
3. **AI-cycle / mega-round dependence.** The +28% AI value hangs on cycle-sensitive capital chasing a global AI narrative (globally ~10 firms took ~80% of AI funding). If the AI capital wave cools, Singapore's "third" reverses fast — the diversification into AI is also a new correlation risk.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** For the sector: Singapore is consolidating as SEA's *funding hub for a shrinking pie* — AI/deep-tech re-rating the mix while the region de-rates overall (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if 2026 shows AI's share holding ≥30% *and* total deal value stabilizing/rising, it's a genuine ecosystem shift; if AI share holds only because total value keeps falling (denominator effect), it's concentration, not growth. Watch: 2026 seed/early-stage deal *count* (the leading indicator) and whether fintech-AI overlap keeps fintech at #1 or AI-native names displace it.
