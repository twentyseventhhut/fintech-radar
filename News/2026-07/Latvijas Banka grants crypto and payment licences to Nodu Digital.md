Latvijas Banka issues crypto and payment licences to Nodu Digital
Sinziana Albu
10 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Latvijas Banka has granted Nodu Digital SIA a licence for crypto-asset services and a licence to operate as a payment institution.
The crypto-asset service licence allows Nodu Digital SIA to exchange crypto-assets for funds and to provide crypto-asset transfer services on behalf of clients. In addition, the payment institution licence permits the company to execute payments, including transfers to payment accounts.
Nodu Digital SIA is the tenth company to receive a licence from Latvijas Banka under the Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA). MiCA entered into force in late 2024, establishing a unified legal framework for crypto-asset service providers across the EU and introducing a mandatory authorisation requirement for firms operating in the sector. Once a provider is authorised in one EU member state, it can offer its services throughout the bloc through a cross-border notification mechanism, without needing to obtain separate licences in each jurisdiction.
The dual licensing of Nodu Digital SIA, covering both crypto-asset services and payment institution activities, reflects a broader pattern among firms seeking to combine crypto and fiat payment functionality under a single regulatory umbrella. This approach allows a company to offer clients both crypto-to-fiat conversion and conventional payment execution without relying on separate licensed partners for each function.
Licensing process and support tools
Latvijas Banka has published licensing guides that outline the documentation required for applications and the review deadlines companies can expect during the process. The central bank has also released an overview of the classification of crypto-assets under MiCA, intended to help companies determine which of their assets fall within the scope of the regulation and which are subject to other legal frameworks.
In addition, in order to support applicants ahead of formal submission, Latvijas Banka offers pre-licensing consultations, during which its experts review the applicable legal requirements with companies and carry out an initial assessment of regulatory compliance. According to Latvijas Banka, this stage helps companies prepare more complete applications and contributes to a smoother licensing process overall. Requests for these consultations can be submitted through the central bank's website.
Context for the EU crypto sector
The steady increase in MiCA-licensed firms passing through Latvijas Banka points to continued interest in Latvia as a licensing jurisdiction for crypto-asset businesses within the EU. As more providers complete authorisation, the cross-border notification mechanism under MiCA is expected to play a growing role in determining how crypto-asset services expand across member states, since a single authorisation can support pan-EU operations. For payments and fintech firms monitoring the sector, the number and pace of licence approvals in individual jurisdictions offer one indicator of how implementation of the MiCA framework is progressing across the EU.

# Context-enrichment: Latvijas Banka grants crypto and payment licences to Nodu Digital
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 8 July 2026 the supervision committee of **Latvijas Banka** (Bank of Latvia) approved **two** licences for **Nodu Digital SIA**: (1) a **MiCA CASP licence** authorising it to *exchange crypto-assets for funds* and to *provide crypto-asset transfer services on behalf of clients*, and (2) a **payment-institution (PI) licence** covering payment execution / money remittance (transfers to payment accounts). Nodu is the **10th company** authorised under MiCA by Latvijas Banka since MiCA came into force at end-2024 (source: The Paypers, Finextra).

De-PR read: this is a **narrow-scope CASP**, not a full exchange. The two named CASP services (crypto-to-fiat exchange + transfer-on-behalf-of-clients) are exactly the pieces a **stablecoin off-ramp** needs — not custody, not trading venue, not portfolio management. The dual CASP+PI stack is the point: it lets Nodu run the whole flow **fiat-in → stablecoin transfer → fiat-out** under one roof, so end clients "never touch crypto." → Why structured this way: Nodu is B2B2B/B2B2C infra (analysis, per its own model), so it needs the PI leg to move fiat and the CASP leg to legally touch the stablecoin — a single partner story is its whole selling proposition vs stitching together licensed partners.

The PR frame ("Latvia's strategy to win the European crypto race") overstates the individual event: this is one small pre-seed startup getting a licence it needs to operate. The genuinely notable fact is the **dual authorisation**, which is still relatively rare — Latvia's precedent case was **Paybis Europe** (also CASP+PI). Vagueness flag: no client, volume, or go-live figures accompany the grant; a licence is permission, not traction.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Bridge** (Stripe-owned): secured **MiCA + EMI across all 27 EU states** (2026-07-06) — see [[Bridge secures MiCA and EMI authorisations across EU]]. This is Nodu's explicitly-named US benchmark; Bridge now has EU regulatory cover at scale and Stripe distribution.
- **Zerohash**: US stablecoin/crypto infra, the other name Nodu benchmarks against (Mastercard was reportedly in talks to buy it — [[Mastercard in talks to buy crypto firm Zerohash]]).
- **Ripple**: full MiCA CASP via Luxembourg CSSF, EEA-wide ([[Ripple secures full MiCA CASP authorisation in Europe]]) — a heavyweight going the same passporting route.
- Latvia dual-licence precedent: **Paybis Europe** (CASP+PI, same Latvijas Banka template). Other Latvia MiCA grants: Neverless SIA, AlphaRoute SIA.
- EMI/PI passporting peers in-corpus: [[Standard Chartered granted MiCA and EMI licences in Luxembourg]], [[Paytm secures Luxembourg payment licence for Europe expansion]], [[Pay10 secures Hungary EMI licence to launch EU operations]].

Position: Nodu is **catching up on paper** to Bridge/Ripple but is a **fraction of the size** ($1.45M pre-seed vs Stripe/Ripple balance sheets). → Why the landscape is this way: MiCA turned "compliance" into the moat — the licence is table-stakes, so the real contest is distribution (Bridge = Stripe; Ripple = its own institutional book) not the paper itself. A licence gives Nodu the right to compete, not a win.

## [2] Company history / fit
Nodu Digital was founded **2025**, London-HQ with **Latvian roots/office**, by **Alex Novozhenov (CEO), Vladislav Nikolayev (CTO) and Daria Dubinina** — the team behind **Crassula**, a decade-old European fintech platform (150+ clients, "hundreds of billions of euros" processed, per TFN/tech.eu). Trajectory: **$1.45M pre-seed led by Digital Space Ventures** (early backer of Revolut, PaySend) announced 2025-12-19 — [[Nodu raises $1.45M to upgrade European payment rails]]. Product proof-point: **Paysend integration** (Mar 2026) for stablecoin payouts into 43 currencies / 62 countries — [[Paysend and Nodu enable stablecoin payouts to banks and wallets]].

Fit: tight and logical. The Dec-2025 raise thesis was explicitly "MiCA-ready European alternative to US-focused Zerohash/Bridge." → Why the company acts this way: you cannot sell "regulated European stablecoin rails" without the actual EU licence — so this grant is the **operational unlock** of the funding thesis, not a pivot. The founders' Crassula base (40+ existing clients reportedly carried over) is the intended distribution wedge.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Novelty is **modest and regulatory, not technical**. Off-ramps into 100+ countries, CASP+PI dual licences, and passporting all exist already (Paybis, Bridge, Ripple). What Nodu adds is a **native-EU, MiCA-first infra option** for banks/fintechs that don't want to build compliance in-house or route through US providers.

Real value-add test: → the margin in stablecoin rails sits with whoever owns the **fiat on/off-ramp + compliance layer** at scale. Nodu is trying to own exactly that layer in Europe — but (analysis) the on-ramp/off-ramp is increasingly commoditised (Paysend, OpenPayd, Bridge all offer it), so the durable moat is **distribution + the licence + Crassula's client base**, not the tech. Traction to date is **thin and disclosed only as capability**: one funding round, one named partnership (Paysend), 40+ legacy clients (unverified as live on Nodu rails), zero volume/GMV figures. This is a **"licensed and ready," not "at scale"** situation.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Next: Nodu can now **passport** the CASP + PI across the EU/EEA via MiCA's cross-border notification, targeting EU banks digitalising and seeking stablecoin revenue. Watch for named bank/fintech clients and any volume disclosure — those, not the licence, will validate the thesis.

Market backdrop is favourable and well-documented in-corpus: MiCA-compliant euro stablecoins are surging ([[MiCA-compliant euro stablecoins grow 128% ahead of deadline]]) while USDT is being pushed out of Europe ([[Tether abandons Europe as MiCA ban wipes USDT from exchanges]], [[Revolut to delist USDT for European users under MiCA]]) — a gap a compliant euro-stablecoin infra layer can fill. Latvia is deliberately positioning as the **most accessible EU CASP jurisdiction** (lowest €2,500 application fee, fastest time-to-market, 30+ firms in pipeline), competing with **Lithuania** (larger EMI/PI ecosystem, 200+ EMIs, but a shrinking crypto sector post-MiCA). → Counterintuitive second-order effect: the "race" narrative benefits **Latvijas Banka as a brand** more than any single licensee — for Nodu the licence removes a blocker but leaves it a sub-$2M startup competing against Stripe-scale (Bridge) and Ripple-scale balance sheets. Risk: commoditisation of off-ramps + well-capitalised incumbents passporting the same way could squeeze a pre-seed player before it scales.

## Top challenge / red-team questions

1. **Is this really new, or a re-run of a prior Nodu note?** Fresh. Prior corpus notes cover the **funding** ([[Nodu raises $1.45M to upgrade European payment rails]], Dec 2025) and the **Paysend partnership** ([[Paysend and Nodu enable stablecoin payouts to banks and wallets]], Mar 2026) — neither covers the **Latvijas Banka licence grant** (8 Jul 2026). New regulatory event, not a reprint.

2. **What exactly was granted?** Two licences: a MiCA **CASP** (crypto-to-fiat exchange + transfer-on-behalf-of-clients) and a **payment-institution** licence (payment execution / money remittance). *Not* a full exchange, custody, or trading licence. Answered.

3. **How rare is a dual CASP+PI grant?** Uncommon but not unique — Latvijas Banka's precedent was **Paybis Europe** (same template). So Nodu is following an established Latvian dual-licence path, not breaking new ground. Answered.

4. **Is Nodu the "10th MiCA licensee in Latvia" a meaningful milestone?** Only as an indicator of Latvia's pace; it says nothing about Nodu's own quality or scale. Marketing anchor. Answered.

5. **Licensed vs live — is there any traction?** No volume/GMV/named-bank disclosure with the grant. Only prior capability signals: Paysend integration (43 currencies/62 countries) and reportedly 40+ carried-over Crassula clients (unverified as live on Nodu). **Licensed ≠ at scale.** Open on real usage.

6. **Who captures the margin in this stack?** The fiat on/off-ramp + compliance layer — which Nodu targets — but it is increasingly commoditised (Paysend, OpenPayd, Bridge). Durable moat is distribution + licence + Crassula base, not tech. (analysis)

7. **How does Nodu compare to Bridge?** Bridge (Stripe-owned) has MiCA+EMI across all 27 EU states and Stripe distribution ([[Bridge secures MiCA and EMI authorisations across EU]]); Nodu is a $1.45M pre-seed. Paper parity, vast resource/distribution gap. Answered.

8. **Does the passporting claim hold?** Yes — MiCA CASP + PI can be notified cross-border across EU/EEA without per-country relicensing. Standard mechanism, same as Ripple ([[Ripple secures full MiCA CASP authorisation in Europe]]). Answered.

9. **Why Latvia and not Lithuania?** Latvia = fastest/cheapest CASP entry (€2,500 fee, first-mover, 30+ in pipeline); Lithuania = bigger EMI ecosystem (200+ EMIs) but a shrinking crypto sector post-MiCA. Nodu chose Latvia for speed/cost + its Latvian roots. Answered.

10. **Is the "Latvia wins the European crypto race" framing credible?** Partly — Latvia is genuinely the most accessible CASP jurisdiction, but the narrative benefits Latvijas Banka's brand more than any single small licensee. Marketing overreach for this specific story. (analysis)

11. **What's the market tailwind?** Strong: euro-stablecoin supply +128% ([[MiCA-compliant euro stablecoins grow 128% ahead of deadline]]) while USDT exits Europe ([[Tether abandons Europe as MiCA ban wipes USDT from exchanges]]). Compliant euro rails have a real gap to fill. Answered.

12. **Biggest downside trigger?** Commoditisation of off-ramps + well-capitalised incumbents (Bridge, Ripple) passporting identical licences could squeeze a sub-$2M pre-seed before it reaches scale. Open.

13. **Who is silent about what?** No client names, no volumes, no fee economics, no runway disclosure. All the metrics that would prove the thesis are absent. Open.

14. **Does the founding team de-risk it?** Somewhat — ex-Crassula founders (decade-old platform, 150+ clients) bring credibility and a warm client base, the strongest single positive here. Answered.

15. **What changes the central question?** From "did Nodu get licensed" (yes, expected) to **"can a pre-seed infra startup convert a commodity licence + a legacy client base into durable volume before Stripe-scale rivals passport the same rails?"** That is unproven. (analysis)

**Importance: 3/5** — A genuine, fresh regulatory milestone that operationally unlocks Nodu's funded thesis and is a clean data-point on Latvia's MiCA hub push (10th licensee, dual CASP+PI). But it is a licence, not traction: the company is a small pre-seed player, the off-ramp niche is commoditising, and heavyweight rivals (Bridge/Stripe, Ripple) hold the same paper with far more distribution. Above baseline for the dual-licence + credible ex-Crassula team + strong euro-stablecoin tailwind; capped below 4 by the absence of any volume/adoption evidence and the small scale.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** EU crypto-asset services under MiCA (in force since late 2024; hard transitional cut-off passed 1 July 2026 — no MiCA CASP = no legal EU service). As of 9 Jul 2026, ~280 firms hold a CASP licence across 25 EU/EEA states, all passportable bloc-wide (per casptracker.eu/helmsadvisory via WebSearch, as of 2026-07-09). Structure is fragmented and mid-consolidating: the 1 July cut-off plus €0.5–1m per-licence compliance cost is squeezing small players toward larger, better-capitalised hands (per Sumsub/Zyphe, WebSearch). Value in the crypto-payments stack is captured by whoever holds BOTH the crypto rail and the fiat rail; Nodu's dual grant (CASP + payment institution) removes reliance on a licensed fiat partner. "Why now": the transitional window closed 1 July 2026, so the current burst of grants is firms racing to keep EU market access, not greenfield demand.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: number/pace of authorisations per NCA, licence scope (exchange/transfer/custody vs full), and passporting reach; for the issuer specifically — transaction volume and crypto-to-fiat throughput (not disclosed → `[UNSOURCED]`). Jurisdiction league (WebSearch, 2026): Germany 57 · France 31 · Netherlands 28 · Malta 22 · Cyprus 20 · Ireland 12 · Spain 12 · **Lithuania 6** · Latvia small but fast-growing. Latvia's pitch is speed + pre-licensing consultation and a "gateway to EU" narrative; Malta hosts crypto-natives (OKX, Crypto.com, Gemini), Lithuania is the EMI+CASP volume entry point, Germany the institutional leader. Recent peer moves: Coinbase secured EU MiCA licence (Jun 2026); Ripple preliminary CASP in Luxembourg (Jun 2026); OpenPayd, Flowdesk, Triple-A, WhiteBIT, Januar all MiCA-authorised (2026); Latvia's Kanga Exchange (18 Jun 2026) and Backpack EU (May 2026). Nodu is the **10th** Latvijas Banka MiCA licensee since end-2024 (note + Finextra). Position: niche new entrant — one of ~280 CASPs, no disclosed scale, competing on the dual-licence combo rather than brand or volume `(analysis)`. Moat is thin: the licence itself is the intangible barrier, but ~280 peers hold the same passport, so it is not differentiating `(analysis)`.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation, round or financials disclosed for Nodu → multiples = **no data** / `[UNSOURCED]` (private, licence-grant event, not a raise). Internal comps (same MiCA-licence-grant event type, EU): [[Coinbase secures MiCA licence to serve EU crypto customers]], [[Ripple receives preliminary MiCA CASP licence in Luxembourg]], [[OpenPayd lands MiCA approval for EU crypto services]], [[Flowdesk Europe gets AMF CASP authorization under MiCA]], [[Triple-A secures MiCA CASP licence from France's AMF]], [[AMINA secures MiCA license in Austria]], [[WhiteBIT EU secures MiCA license from Austria]]. Best size-matched comp: [[Januar secures MiCA licence and raises €1M]] — a small issuer that paired the licence with only a €1m raise, illustrating the modest capitalisation of new MiCA entrants. Distribution not computed (no comparable disclosed valuations for these licence-grant events) → qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Small-issuer viability / compliance burden.** €0.5–1m licensing cost plus ongoing AML/CFT, Travel Rule (zero-threshold CASP-to-CASP), complaint-handling and supervisory reporting fall hardest on sub-scale firms without large compliance teams — a licence grant is entry, not survival (per Sumsub/Zyphe, WebSearch).
2. **Passporting value is overstated in practice.** A single authorisation notionally serves the bloc, but the moment a firm serves multiple member states it inherits multiple host supervisors, complaint channels and higher proof-of-model burden; "one licence, whole EU" is a legal claim, not an operational one (per VoveID/AMLBot, WebSearch). Second-order: Nodu's pan-EU reach may cost far more than the headline suggests.
3. **Commoditised licence, crowded field.** ~280 CASPs already passport EU-wide and Latvijas Banka has 15 more in active + 29 in pre-licensing (44 live projects); the licence confers legality, not a competitive moat, so differentiation must come from product/distribution Nodu has not disclosed.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a routine capacity add to the EU crypto-rails supply, not a re-rating — the signal is Latvia's pace (10 licences, 44 projects in the pipeline) confirming a small-jurisdiction land-grab for post-1-July CASP flow versus Germany/Malta/Lithuania. Falsifiable thesis: MiCA drives consolidation, so most sub-scale 2026 licensees (Nodu among them) get acquired or wound down within ~18–24 months rather than scaling independently. Trigger to watch: whether Nodu discloses volume/a raise or gets absorbed by a larger acquirer — silence on economics (ticket size, fiat-partner banking, target clients) is itself the tell. Thesis breaks if small dual-licence issuers show durable standalone volume growth.
