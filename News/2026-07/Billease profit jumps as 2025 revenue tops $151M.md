# Context-enrichment: Billease profit jumps as 2025 revenue tops $151M
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
BillEase (brand of **First Digital Finance Corporation / FDFC**, Manila) published its **FY2025 audited results** (year ended 31 Dec 2025), first surfaced in trade press ~14–16 Jul 2026:
- **Revenue US$151.2M, +>80%** (FY2023 was $55–57M → roughly tripled over two years).
- **Net profit US$13.6M (~P840M)** — DealStreetAsia frames it as profit "doubling" vs FY2024 (implied ~$6.8M).
- **Gross loan book US$212.1M, +77%** (implied ~$120M base end-2024).
- **Total assets US$233.5M**; total equity **$109.4M** (paid-in $82.9M + retained earnings $26.6M); equity/assets ~47%.
- **ROA ~6.8%** (self-reported); 100k+ new customers/month.

**+ Why this framing / what it reveals.** Two de-PR points:
1. **"Profit jumps" is growth, not a turnaround.** The loss-to-profit swing already happened in **FY2023** (first-ever profit, $5.95M). So this is a maturing, compounding lender — genuinely notable in a sector where most peers still burn cash — but the "jumps" verb flatters an incremental (if strong) trend.
2. **"Funded expansion through operating profits, not new external capital" is half-true.** On the *equity* side it checks out: retained earnings rose $12.8M→$26.6M (≈ the $13.6M net profit) ⇒ no dividends, no new equity in 2025 (last equity was Series C, Sept 2024). BUT growing a loan book 77% to $212M against only ~$109M equity **necessarily leaned on external debt** — Billease raised a **~P5B (~$90M) syndicated corporate-notes facility (Security Bank Capital, May 2025)** plus prior debt lines. So: self-funded on equity/opex — yes; self-funded overall — **no**. The PR anchors to the flattering "no new capital" frame while omitting the debt engine that actually funds a lending book. (analysis)

Source caveat: figures are **company-reported ("audited")** relayed by trade press (Fintech News PH, Back End News, MegaBites, Mindanao Times, DealStreetAsia). FDFC is private; no independent auditor report or SEC-filed PDF located online.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Market:** Philippines BNPL ~US$3.2B (2025) → ~US$5.4B by 2030.
- **PH digital-lending / BNPL peers:** [[Atome Philippines secures $81M loan from Asia United Bank]] and [[Atome secures $345M debt facility for SE Asia BNPL]] (Advance Intelligence Group — debt-fuelled, historically loss-making), Cashalo, UnaCash, Salmon ($88M raise 2025), [[Tonik appoints former TNEX CEO Bryan Carroll to senior role]] (neobank), Home Credit PH, GCash/GGives, Shopee SPayLater, [[PayRex raises $2M Series A from Kollab]] (PH payments adjacency). Regional: Kredivo, Akulaku.
- **Position:** With **$151M revenue + $212M loan book + sustained profit**, Billease is among the **largest and one of the very few profitable** pure-play PH digital lenders. Contrast is the story: Atome scales via giant syndicated *debt* facilities ($345M, $81M) with unproven profitability; Billease grows its book self-funded on the equity side and prints a $13.6M profit.

**+ Why the lay of the land is this way (second-order).** SEA BNPL's default model is "buy growth with cheap debt/equity, defer profitability." Billease diverged: disciplined underwriting + reinvested profit → a **stronger equity buffer (47%)** than debt-heavy peers. That is exactly what lets it pivot to a **deposit-funded bank** (see [4]) — cheaper cost of funds — whereas debt-reliant peers stay hostage to wholesale funding markets. The real differentiator isn't the revenue number; it's the **funding-cost trajectory**. (analysis)

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Founded 2017** (FDFC est. 2015); CEO/co-founder **Georg Steiger** (ex-McKinsey) + ex-colleagues. SEC-registered financing company + BSP Operator of Payment System.
- **Products:** BNPL/checkout installments, cash loans, "Balikbayad." ~1.3M+ users, 10,000+ merchants (2023 figures).
- **Trajectory:** FY2023 first profit ($5.95M, ~47% ROE) → FY2025 $13.6M. Loan portfolio >P10B, targeting P15B in 2026.
- **Fit:** logical — a profitable consumer lender monetizing its balance sheet, now vertically integrating into banking.

**+ Why the company acts this way.** A financing-company license caps the model: it lends off equity + wholesale debt, the most expensive funding. To keep compounding without diluting or over-levering, Billease **bought a rural bank (>90% of Rural Bank of Sta. Maria, Nov 2025)** and is injecting P1B — the structural driver is **converting to deposit funding** to lower cost of capital and unlock a durable margin. Profitability isn't the endgame; it's the collateral that finances the bank pivot. (analysis)

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Fresh disclosure?** Yes — FY2025 audited numbers first reported mid-July 2026 (DealStreetAsia ran a "profit doubles" teaser 20 May 2026). New reporting period, not a reprint.
- **Real traction (numbers, not announcements):** $151M revenue, $212M book, $13.6M profit, 100k+ new users/month, retained-earnings self-funding — all hard figures, corroborated across ≥5 outlets and internally consistent (RE math ≈ net profit).
- **Strategic novelty:** the **rural-bank acquisition → digital-bank build** (P500M injected June 2026, P1B committed) is the genuinely new development beyond "good year."

**+ Why the value-add is real (deeper).** In BNPL/lending, margin is captured by whoever funds the loan cheapest and underwrites best. Billease's profitability proves underwriting; the bank charter attacks the funding side. **Who captures the margin:** today, wholesale lenders (Security Bank et al.) take a spread on Billease's debt; post-charter, Billease captures that spread itself via deposits. That's the durable, multiple-supporting move — not the headline revenue. What breaks it: SEC rate caps (see [4]) compress the asset yield the whole model rests on. (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** digital-bank rollout (deposits/savings) via the rural-bank charter; loan book P10B→P15B (2026).
- **Sentiment:** credibly positive — 3 straight profitable years, ROA ~6.8%, 47% equity/assets, clear cost-of-funds strategy.
- **Risks / who's silent:** (1) **SEC MC 14/2025** (issued 10 Dec 2025) cut the effective monthly cap to ~12% on small loans ≤P10k with a 100%-of-principal total-cost cap, effective **1 Apr 2026** — a direct margin headwind for small-ticket lending, and Billease is silent on the FY2026 impact. (2) **Asset quality:** +77% book growth into a consumer-credit cycle, yet **NPLs undisclosed** — "no loosening of standards" is unverified. (3) Bank-buildout execution + regulatory risk. (4) Private company, no independent financials.

**+ Why the market goes this way (counterintuitive).** Rapid loan growth reads as strength, but +77% is precisely when hidden credit risk accumulates — the profit could be a **timing artifact** (revenue booked before the cohort's losses mature). The bull case (bank charter, cheap funding) and the bear case (undisclosed NPLs + rate-cap margin squeeze) hinge on the **same unpublished number**: portfolio credit quality. That is the central question, not the revenue milestone. (analysis)

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Billease sits in Philippine consumer finance / BNPL — a subvertical inside the broader SEA alternative-lending pool. Sizing is noisy: the PH "FinTech Lending & BNPL Apps" market is put at ~US$1.6bn, with PH BNPL projected to reach ~US$5.37bn by 2030 (per KenResearch / secondary trade press, as of 2026) — treat as directional, not audited. Structure: fragmented and multi-model — e-wallet super-apps (GCash, Maya) that bolt on credit, pure BNPL/consumer-lenders (Billease, Atome, Home Credit PH, Kredivo), and digital/rural banks (Tonik, Salmon, UNO). Entry barriers are rising on two axes: capital (loan books are balance-sheet-heavy, so funding cost is the moat) and regulation (lending-company + eventual bank licensing). Why now: (1) low card penetration + a young, mobile-first, underbanked base drives structural BNPL adoption `(analysis)`; (2) the regulatory perimeter is tightening — BSP Circular 1133 caps small loans (≤₱10k) at 6%/mo nominal, and in Nov-2025 the SEC proposed extending caps to unsecured loans up to ₱20k / ≤6-mo tenor (per SEC/Conventus Law) — a real headwind for high-APR short-ticket lenders.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: gross loan book, revenue growth, ROA, monthly new-customer adds, and funding cost/mix. Key players & basis of competition — competition is on distribution (super-app reach) and cost of funds, not price alone. Recent dated moves: Atome (Advance Intelligence Group) closed a US$345M upsized debt facility (Jan-2026), up from US$200M in 2024, and posts >US$500M annualized net revenue / >US$6bn GMV (2025); Maya Bank + Atome struck a US$48M PH loan-channeling deal (Sep-2025) [[Maya Bank and Atome partner on $48M Philippine lending]]; Salmon Bank net income +83% to US$2.6M in H1-2025 [[Salmon Bank net income jumps 83% in first half of 2025]]; Salmon raised US$88M (2025) and a US$50M Nordic bond (Sep-2025). Billease's position: mid-scale but distinctive — it self-funded 77% loan-book growth to US$212.1M "through operating profits instead of raising new external capital," and is pushing into banking (₱1bn capital into Rural Bank of Sta. Maria, Ilocos Sur, committed by end-2026 per Philstar/Manila Bulletin). Moat `(analysis)`: profitable self-funding lowers reliance on external debt cost — a differentiator vs debt-hungry peers — but scale is well below Atome. CAC payback / LTV-CAC / NRR not disclosed → `[UNSOURCED]`.

**Comps & multiples.** Billease FY2025 (audited, per fintechnews.ph / Mindanao Times): revenue US$151.2M (+>80%), net profit US$13.6M, gross loan book US$212.1M (+77%), total assets US$233.5M, ROA ~6.8%, >100k new customers/mo. Arithmetic: net margin = US$13.6M / US$151.2M = **9.0%**; revenue/loan-book = US$151.2M / US$212.1M = **0.71x** (implies a very high effective yield/APR on the book — consistent with short-ticket BNPL/consumer-lending economics, worth watching against the rate-cap regime). No market cap / round valuation is public for Billease → EV/Revenue and P/E = **no data**. External peers: Atome parent AIG last priced at ~US$2bn (Series D, 2021) — a round valuation, not market cap, and stale; Kredivo Group has raised ~US$904M (no fresh 2025 valuation) — `[UNSOURCED]`. With only one clean, comparable public figure set (Billease's own) and no peer market caps, a multiple distribution is **not computed → qualitative comparison only**. Internal comps as scale anchors: [[Salmon Bank net income jumps 83% in first half of 2025]] (H1-25 NI US$2.6M — Billease is materially more profitable in absolute terms), [[Maya Bank and Atome partner on $48M Philippine lending]], [[Philippine fintech Salmon secures $88M in funding]], [[Tonik raises $12M pre-Series C for Philippine bank]].

**Risk flags.**
- **Regulatory margin compression (primary).** The 0.71x revenue/loan-book ratio implies a rich effective yield; the Nov-2025 SEC proposal to cap unsecured loans ≤₱20k directly threatens the high-APR short-ticket segment BNPL lenders live on — a second-order hit to net margin and ROA precisely as Billease scales.
- **Credit/vintage risk masked by growth.** A loan book growing 77% in one year mechanically dilutes the visible NPL ratio (young vintages haven't seasoned); the audited P&L is silent on provisioning/coverage — profit quality is unproven through a full credit cycle. `(analysis)`
- **Bank-buildout execution + funding.** Committing ₱1bn into a rural-bank arm consumes the same operating profit that funds loan growth; if self-funding can't cover both, Billease is pushed back toward external debt at a higher cost of funds — eroding the very moat that differentiates it from Atome/Salmon.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` Billease reframes the PH BNPL story from "burn-to-grow" to "profitable, self-funded consolidator moving up-stack into banking" — if durable, it's a re-rating template for the subvertical and a live consolidation signal (BNPL → licensed bank). Falsifiable thesis: profitable self-funding holds through the banking build-out. It breaks if (a) SEC rate caps land on the ₱20k tier and compress yield, or (b) seasoning NPLs force provisioning that turns the P&L. Trigger to watch: first post-rate-cap quarterly + rural-bank NPL disclosure in FY2026.

Third consecutive profitable year — genuinely rare for a BNPL/digital lender. No forward guidance in the release; a qualitative ₱15bn loan-book target was floated separately (Nov-2025).

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Revenue: US$151.2M, +>80% YoY (PHP ₱9.3bn, +>80%). Implied 2024 base ≈ US$83–84M (analysis). Trajectory: US$57M in 2023 → ~US$83M 2024 → US$151M 2025, i.e. re-acceleration.
- Net profit: US$13.6M (PHP ₱840M), up YoY (2024 net income not separately disclosed in this release — [UNSOURCED] exact prior figure). Net margin ~9.0% (13.6/151.2).
- ROA: ~6.8% — high for a consumer lender; signals strong asset yield / low funding drag.
- Gross loan book: US$212.1M, +77.0% YoY (consumer loan portfolio ≈ PHP ₱11.9bn, +>75%).
- Total assets: US$233.5M. Total equity (31-Dec-2025): US$109.4M = US$82.9M paid-in + US$26.6M retained earnings.
- Customer growth: >100,000 new customers/month; growth attributed by CEO Georg Steiger to repeat users + strong acquisition.

**By segment / driver.** Growth is credit-led: revenue +>80% vs loan book +77% — revenue outran the book, implying stable-to-rising take-rate / yield (mix shift into interest-bearing products, consistent with the FY2024 narrative of "expansion of interest-bearing products"). Not a pure BNPL float story anymore — this is an interest-earning consumer-lending P&L. Self-funded expansion (sales network, marketing, merchant partnerships) from operating profit is the standout: no equity dilution, no new debt round cited for 2025.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public consensus exists (private, unrated) → beat/miss N/A; assess vs prior years only. Trajectory US$57M (2023) → ~US$83M (2024) → US$151.2M (2025) shows YoY growth re-accelerating from ~45% to >80%. Net income scaled from a small 2023 base to US$13.6M in 2025 while staying positive throughout — profitability is sustained, not a one-off. No prior Billease note in the corpus to `[[wikilink]]`.

**Guidance / forward.** No formal FY2026 guidance in the results release (private company — none expected). Directional forward markers, all separately sourced (not in this print): (1) targeting ~PHP ₱15bn gross loan book post rural-bank acquisition (BusinessWorld, Nov-2025); (2) ₱1bn total capital commitment into banking arm Rural Bank of Sta. Maria–Ilocos Sur (RBSM) — ₱500M infused, ₱500M committed by YE2026 (Jun-2026). Management tone: confident, growth-forward. What they leave out: no explicit disclosure of NPL / provisioning / cost-of-risk metrics in the release — for a lender growing the book 77%, credit quality and reserve coverage are the unaddressed line, and "funded from operating profit" says nothing about asset-quality trajectory.
