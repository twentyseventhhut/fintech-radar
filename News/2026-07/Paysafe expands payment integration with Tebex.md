Paysafe expands payment integration with Tebex
Sinziana Albu
09 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Paysafe and Tebex have expanded their partnership to integrate the Paysafe Gateway into Tebex Checkout.
Tebex has integrated the Paysafe Gateway into its platform, giving video game merchants access to card payments alongside a range of alternative payment methods (APMs) through a single connection. Tebex Checkout has offered Paysafe's prepaid eCash product, PaysafeCard, since 2016, and the new integration with Paysafe API is expected to extend that relationship to the company’s broader payment portfolio, including credit and debit card processing, with transactions handled by Paysafe.
The Gateway also links Tebex to Paysafe's branded APMs. Tebex Checkout has gone live with Paysafe's Openbucks solution, which allows gamers in the US to pay using third-party gift cards purchased in-store at more than 67.000 locations, or via Openbucks' own Obucks digital card, sold online through authorised resellers.
Payment optionality for global gaming markets
Tebex operates as an extension of gaming studios, managing payments, compliance and support on their behalf. The company said that adding Openbucks and other Paysafe-powered payment options helps studios reach players across more markets, offering payment methods aligned with regional preferences.
The video gaming sector has increasingly relied on APMs to accommodate players who lack access to traditional card payments or prefer cash-based or prepaid alternatives, particularly younger audiences and players in specific regional markets. Gift card-based payment schemes such as Openbucks address this segment in the US market, where cash-to-digital conversion remains a relevant payment channel for gaming purchases.
 Zak Cutler , President of Global Gaming at Paysafe, said the expanded partnership connects Tebex Checkout to a wide range of payment options, including Openbucks, with the aim of simplifying gaming monetisation for studios operating in a competitive market.
 Liam Wiltshire , Vice President and General Manager of Tebex, said payments form part of how studios build relationships with players, and that the expanded Paysafe partnership gives Tebex additional choice and flexibility at checkout, intended to reduce friction and support studios' growth plans.
Market context
The expanded integration reflects a broader trend among payment providers to consolidate multiple payment rails, both card-based and alternative, into single API connections for platform partners. For gaming-specific monetisation platforms such as Tebex, offering a wider range of payment methods at checkout is positioned as a factor in improving transaction completion rates across different regional player bases.
Paysafe has expanded its gaming-sector partnerships in recent years, building on its existing PaysafeCard relationship with Tebex established in 2016. The company has not disclosed financial terms of the expanded partnership.

# Context-enrichment: Paysafe expands payment integration with Tebex
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **07 Jul 2026** Paysafe (NYSE: PSFE) announced it has **strengthened its existing partnership with Tebex** by integrating the **Paysafe Gateway** into **Tebex Checkout** (businesswire PR; retold by The Paypers 09 Jul, and by fintech.global / iBSi / StockTitan / Morningstar). The Paypers headline in-corpus ("expands payment integration") is a re-title of the same PR.

De-PR'd substance:
- Tebex Checkout has carried Paysafe's prepaid eCash product **PaysafeCard since 2016**. The new step routes Tebex through the **Paysafe API/Gateway**, adding **card processing (credit/debit) + a range of APMs** via a single connection — i.e. Paysafe moves from being ONE APM in Tebex to being a **full acquiring/gateway rail** for card + branded APMs.
- One concrete "live" deliverable: Tebex Checkout has **gone live with Openbucks** (Paysafe-owned cash/gift-card scheme) — US gamers pay with third-party gift cards bought at **67,000+ in-store locations**, or the Obucks digital card via authorised resellers. This is the only capability explicitly stated as **live** rather than "coming".
- **Financial terms: not disclosed.** No GMV, no volume commitment, no exclusivity, no take-rate.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the PR anchors to the flattering **"since 2016"** base to frame a 9-year relationship as momentum, but the actual net-new increment is narrow: (a) card + broader APMs through one API (plumbing consolidation, not a new product), and (b) Openbucks going live in the US. Paysafe is the **payments supplier**; Tebex remains the **merchant-of-record (MoR)** that owns the studio relationship, tax, compliance and disputes. So Paysafe is winning a **rail slot inside someone else's checkout**, not a strategic platform position. That framing is the whole red-team here.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Gaming-monetization / MoR layer (Tebex's world): **Xsolla** (leading gaming MoR), **Coda Payments, Paddle, Appcharge, Aghanim, FastSpring**, and **Stripe** at the raw-processing layer. Tebex (owned by **Overwolf** since 2022, operating since 2011) has processed **$1.5B+** to date across game servers (Hypixel, FiveM) and studios — a large niche, but small vs Xsolla's breadth.
Payment-rail layer (Paysafe's peers competing to be the rail inside these platforms): **Adyen** (treats iGaming as just-another-MCC, processor not MoR; Q1 2026 platform revenue +28% citing gaming/travel wins), **Stripe, Checkout.com, Nuvei/Worldpay**. Paysafe's differentiator is its **gaming-native APM stack** — PaysafeCard, Openbucks, Skrill/Neteller — that generalist processors (Adyen/Stripe) don't own.

**Why the lay of the land is this way / second-order:** the value in gaming payments is bifurcating. The **MoR layer** (Tebex/Xsolla) captures the durable, defensible margin because it owns compliance, tax, chargebacks and the studio relationship; the **rail layer** (Paysafe/Adyen) is comparatively commoditized and interchangeable. Paysafe's edge is real but **defensive**: its APMs (cash-in-store, prepaid, gift cards) are exactly what a card-only stack can't replicate, so it's hard to fully displace — but that edge buys it a **supplier** seat, not the platform economics. Paysafe is roughly at **parity/catch-up** here: it's plugging into a platform, same posture as its June-2026 Primer tie-up ([[Paysafe joins Primer platform to streamline card payments]]).

## [2] Company history / fit
Paysafe's 2025-26 pattern is a **string of distribution deals into third-party gaming/payment platforms**, each individually small:
- 2025-11 [[Paysafe enables Pix payments for Boosteroid in Brazil]] (gaming APM, LatAm)
- 2026-04 [[Paysafe launches Pay with Crypto for US iGaming market]] (MoonPay-powered stablecoin deposits)
- 2026-02 [[Paysafe announces partnership with Spreedly]] and [[Paysafe powers FuturePay's Latin American expansion]]
- 2026-07 [[Paysafe joins Primer platform to streamline card payments]] (orchestration plug-in) — the closest structural twin to this Tebex deal.

**Why the company acts this way:** the financial backdrop forces it. Paysafe posted a **$87.7M Q3-2025 net loss** ([[Paysafe posts $87.7 million Q3 net loss on tax charge]], largely an $81.2M US deferred-tax valuation charge), carries a heavy debt load, and management is explicitly **prioritising deleveraging** while pivoting to merchant solutions and digital wallets ([[Paysafe launches PaysafeWallet digital wallet]]). With a commoditizing take-rate and a de-rated equity, Paysafe needs **incremental volume at near-zero incremental cost** — bolting its rail into existing platforms (Tebex, Primer, Spreedly) is exactly that: capital-light volume via other people's checkouts. Gaming (~$167B annualized group volume) is its strongest vertical, so leaning in there is logical, if unglamorous.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Genuinely new?** Only incrementally. The relationship is 9 years old; what's new is the **Gateway/API upgrade (card + APMs in one connection)** and **Openbucks going live** in the US. It is NOT a new product, a new company relationship, or a re-report — so it's **fresh** but low-magnitude.
**Traction:** essentially none disclosed. No merchant count, no volume, no conversion uplift — only the qualitative claim that more APMs "improve completion rates". Tebex's $1.5B cumulative is *Tebex's* number, not attributable Paysafe volume. Openbucks-live is the one verifiable adoption fact.

**Why the value-add is real-but-thin / who captures the margin:** the value-add is real at the player level — cash/gift-card rails materially lift conversion for the **unbanked / under-18 / regional cash-preferring** gaming segment that card-only checkouts lose. That's a legitimate Paysafe moat. BUT in the stack the **margin is captured by Tebex** (the MoR keeps the spread, owns the customer); Paysafe earns a **processing/interchange-plus fee on the slice it powers**. So the correct central question is not "is Tebex a good win?" but **"how much incremental, durable volume does Paysafe actually pull through vs. the card processors already in Tebex, and at what net take-rate after Tebex's cut?"** — and the PR is silent on all of it. Absent numbers, this reads as one distribution tile in a broad tiling strategy, not a needle-mover.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Expect the **same playbook to continue**: more APM go-lives inside Tebex (branded APMs beyond Openbucks), and more platform plug-ins (post-Primer). The structural driver is real — gaming/creator monetization is shifting to **MoR platforms + broad APM menus**, and cash-to-digital conversion remains a live channel in the US and emerging markets. Regulatory backdrop: iGaming APMs and prepaid/gift-card rails carry AML/KYC and responsible-gambling scrutiny, which is partly *why* studios outsource to an MoR (Tebex) rather than Paysafe directly.

**Why the market goes this way / counterintuitive second-order:** the more gaming payments consolidate onto MoR platforms, the more Paysafe's role **thins to interchangeable supplier** — a wave of "partnership" announcements can mask **structural disintermediation**, where the platform (Tebex/Xsolla), not the rail, sets terms and captures the multiple. For a deleveraging, loss-carrying Paysafe, a steady drip of capital-light gaming volume is exactly the right survival move — but it is unlikely to re-rate the equity on its own. Sentiment: neutral; this is execution-on-strategy, not a catalyst.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Gaming payments is a fast-growing niche of acquiring. Per Xsolla's Q2 2025 State of Play (via Xsolla newsroom), mobile gaming alone is projected at ~$126bn in 2025; a third-party report (Dataintelo, unverifiable secondary — treat as directional) sizes the "gaming payments" market at ~$38.6bn in 2025 growing ~11% CAGR. Structure: highly fragmented on the payment side — general acquirers (Adyen, Stripe, Worldpay), gaming-specialists (Xsolla, Paysafe, Nuvei), and wallets/APMs (Skrill, Neteller, PaysafeCard) all compete; barriers are regulatory (iGaming/gambling licensing, prepaid/e-money) and network (local APM coverage). Why now: studios increasingly need alternative payment methods (prepaid, cash-to-digital, gift cards) to monetise younger and under-banked players who lack cards — the exact wedge behind Paysafe's Openbucks/PaysafeCard and the Tebex Checkout integration (per note, 09 Jul 2026). Second-order effect: payment optionality is being repositioned from a cost center to a conversion lever (transaction-completion rates), so distribution to monetisation platforms like Tebex matters more than raw rails.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: TPV/volume, take rate, per-transaction margin, and APM/local-method coverage (attach). Key players & basis of competition: Adyen and Stripe compete on single-API breadth + scale; Xsolla competes on gaming-native tooling (700+ developers, 200+ markets per Xsolla) and in Aug 2025 partnered Adyen to launch its own PSP/Merchant-of-Record product; Nuvei (Advent-owned) on gaming + regulated-gambling reach; Paysafe on its prepaid eCash (PaysafeCard) and gift-card rails (Openbucks, 67,000+ US retail locations per note). Recent moves: Xsolla×Adyen PSP (Aug 2025); Paysafe's own 2026 cadence — Pay with Crypto for US iGaming ([[Paysafe launches Pay with Crypto for US iGaming market]]), PaysafeWallet ([[Paysafe launches PaysafeWallet digital wallet]]), Spreedly and Primer orchestration links ([[Paysafe announces partnership with Spreedly]], [[Paysafe joins Primer platform to streamline card payments]]). Protagonist's position: niche/defensive incumbent in gaming APMs — moat is the installed prepaid/gift-card acceptance network and the pre-2016 PaysafeCard relationship with Tebex, now extended to full gateway/card processing (analysis). It is catching up, not leading, on developer-facing gateway breadth vs Xsolla/Adyen.

**Comps & multiples.** Paysafe (NYSE:PSFE), as of ~08 Jul 2026: price ~$7.75, market cap ~$398m, EV ~$2.67bn (net debt ~$2.2bn dominates the cap structure), FY2025 revenue ~$1.70–1.71bn (Merchant Solutions $905m + Digital Wallets $815m). Multiples: EV/Revenue = $2.67bn / ~$1.74bn ttm = ~1.53x; EV/EBITDA ~7.94x (per stockanalysis). Peers: Adyen (AMS:ADYEN) — EV/Rev ~6.0x, EV/EBITDA ~11.2x on 53% FY25 EBITDA margin (companiesmarketcap/multiples.vc + Adyen H2-25); Nuvei — no live multiple, taken private by Advent Nov 2024 at ~$6.3bn EV [UNSOURCED as a current multiple]; Xsolla — private, no data. Read: Paysafe trades at a deep discount to Adyen (1.53x vs 6.0x EV/Rev) — cheap on revenue, but justified: low single-digit organic growth (Merchant +5%, Wallets +6% FY25), a highly levered balance sheet (net debt >5x its equity value), and a $87.7m Q3-25 net loss driven by a $81.2m US deferred-tax valuation-allowance charge ([[Paysafe posts $87.7 million Q3 net loss on tax charge]]). In-line-to-cheap for its growth; not a mispricing. Distribution not computed (only 2 live comparable multiples).

**Risk flags.**
1. Leverage/equity thinness — net debt ~$2.2bn against ~$398m equity value; almost all enterprise value is debt, so operating stumbles hit equity holders disproportionately. Refinancing/rate risk is the dominant variable, not this partnership.
2. Disintermediation & margin-layer risk — as orchestrators (Spreedly, Primer) and gaming PSPs (Xsolla×Adyen) let studios pick rails à la carte, Paysafe risks being a commoditised method behind someone else's checkout; economics/take rate of the Tebex deal are undisclosed (who keeps the spread is unclear).
3. Regulatory concentration in gaming/iGaming — gambling and prepaid/e-money licensing across jurisdictions is a live compliance exposure; APM gaming volumes are sensitive to regional gambling-rule changes.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) Incremental, not re-rating: the Tebex expansion deepens Paysafe's gaming-APM distribution but doesn't alter the leverage-driven equity thesis. Falsifiable test — watch Merchant Solutions organic growth and take-rate trend in 2026 quarterly prints: if gaming-APM wins lift organic growth into double digits and de-lever, the ~1.5x EV/Rev discount to peers could compress; if growth stays mid-single-digit while debt service bites, the discount is deserved and persists. Trigger to watch: any disclosure of Tebex/Openbucks volume or take rate, and 2026 debt refinancing terms.
