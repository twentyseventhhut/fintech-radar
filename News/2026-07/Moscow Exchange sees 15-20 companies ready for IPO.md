## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened (one line).** At a 2026-07-13 event Moscow Exchange (Мосбиржа) said 15–20 companies are in a "high degree of readiness" for IPO; the quote is attributed to Boris Blokhin (senior managing director for sales & business development), with MOEX head Viktor Zhidkov adding some are "at a more advanced stage" while others "have work to do" ([Vedomosti](https://www.vedomosti.ru/investments/news/2026/07/13/1213152-gotovimi-k-ipo), [Kommersant](https://www.kommersant.ru/doc/8674190)).

**[1] Who/what/numbers.**
- Named candidates cited as high-readiness: Evraz, alco-retailer VinLab («Винлаб»), and subsidiaries of MTS and Rostelecom (e.g. Solar/«Солар» cybersecurity, RTK-DPC/«РТК-ЦОД»); «Сибур» and «Солар» described as "technically ready." SIBUR is the single most-anticipated issuer — a 2% float could raise ~RUB 40–60bn ([Kommersant](https://www.kommersant.ru/doc/8250929), [Forbes](https://www.forbes.ru/investicii/551218-glava-rostelekoma-dopustil-ipo-solara-v-pervoj-polovine-2026-goda)).
- ~250 companies list a possible public-market exit in their strategies (the far end of the funnel; 15–20 is the near-term subset).
- Corroboration: the Ministry of Finance separately said ~20 companies are ready and expects ~14 IPO deals in 2026 ([RBC](https://www.rbc.ru/quote/news/article/69956e739a7947113cf8b006)).

**[2] Internal corpus (grep fallback; semsearch down).**
- [[Bank of Russia proposes 3bn ruble minimum for IPO listing tiers]] (2026-07-06) — CBR's RUB 3bn-minimum + free-float reform is the quality gate this "readiness" pipeline must clear; its own [4] flags brokers projecting ~20 IPOs / RUB 50–100bn in 2026. Most-adjacent prior note.
- [[Mergers.ru Incab Holding raises 2 billion rubles in IPO]] (2026-06-24) — a real, small (~RUB 2bn, tier-3) 2026 MOEX IPO; the thin reality against which "15–20 ready" is measured.
- [[Mergers.ru SPB Exchange to run first 2026 SPO with NFK-sberezheniy]] (2026-06-24) — venue/primary-market context; confirms how sparse actual RU new-issue flow is.
- Demand-side: [[VIM Investments Pre-IPO funds pay investors 9.2bn rubles]] (2026-07-02), [[Russian pre-IPO platform Rounds shuts down]] (2026-06-22) — pre-IPO funnel feeding the pipeline is active but fragile.

**[3] Market conditions (why "ready" ≠ "will list").**
- 2025 was weak: only ~9 issuers / 4 real placements, mostly late-year; IPO proceeds <RUB 33bn (total IPO+SPO ~RUB 125bn, of which two large names = 88%). Biggest 2025 IPO = DOM.RF (~RUB 25bn Nov); GloraX (~RUB 2.1bn) and Basis/«Базис» (~RUB 3bn) followed ([bosfera](https://bosfera.ru/press-release/obem-ipo-i-spo-v-2025-godu-prevysil-125-mlrd-rubley), [CBR](https://www.cbr.ru/press/event/?id=28429)).
- Rates: CBR cut to 14.25% on 2026-06-19; next meeting 2026-07-24. Consensus is IPO revival needs the key rate down to ~12–13% to beat deposits; expected mainly H2 2026 ([CBR](https://www.cbr.ru/press/pr/?file=19062026_133000key.htm), [ABN](https://abn.agency/2026/02/13/rynok-ipo-v-rossii-v-2026-godu-ozhidaet-ozhivlenie-vo-vtoroj-polovine-goda-na-fone-snizheniya-klyuchevoj-stavki/)).
- MOEX index (IMOEX) hit a ~late-2022 low ~2117 on 2026-07-09, pressured by fuel-price inflation / higher-for-longer expectations ([Lenta](https://lenta.ru/news/2026/07/07/indeks-mosbirzhi-opustilsya/)) — a hostile issuance window right now.
- 2026 volume forecasts range widely: cautious RUB 60–120bn (Forbes) up to RUB 300bn IPO+SPO (Vedomosti, [link](https://www.vedomosti.ru/investments/articles/2025/12/18/1164233-obem-ipo-i-spo-mozhet-dostich-300-mlrd-rublei)).

**[4] So what.** This is a pipeline/sentiment signal from the exchange, not a deal. "15–20 ready" is a supply-side readiness claim contingent on a rate-cut-driven demand recovery (H2 2026) and on clearing CBR's tightening listing rules. History (2025: many "ready", few placed) argues for discounting the headline. Watch: the 2026-07-24 CBR decision and rate path, SIBUR/Solar timing, and whether any named candidate actually files.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
**Red-team (second-order):**

1. Is "15–20 ready" a firm count or a soft sales pitch by the exchange? — SOFT. It comes from MOEX's sales/business-development chief; the exchange earns fees on listings, so it has incentive to signal a deep pipeline. Zhidkov's own caveat ("others have work to do") undercuts firmness.
2. How does "15–20 ready" reconcile with ~250 companies citing IPO in strategies? — 250 is the aspirational funnel; 15–20 is the near-term high-readiness subset. Consistent, not contradictory.
3. Does "ready" mean "will list in 2026"? — NO. MinFin expects only ~14 deals in 2026; 2025 had many "ready" names but only ~4 real placements. Readiness ≠ execution.
4. Is the current window even open? — WEAK. IMOEX at a ~2022 low (~2117, 2026-07-09) and key rate 14.25% make issuance unattractive; consensus needs ~12–13% for revival, expected H2 2026.
5. Are the named candidates confirmed or press speculation? — MIXED. Solar (Rostelecom) has been explicitly guided by Rostelecom for H1 2026; SIBUR is widely reported; Evraz/VinLab are more "cited as ready" than officially announced. Treat names as indicative.
6. Could this be double-counting SPOs/pre-IPO as IPOs? — Possibly. The exchange sometimes blends IPO+SPO in pipeline talk; the 15–20 figure appears IPO-specific per the quote, but downstream volume forecasts (RUB 60–300bn) mix both.
7. Is this a duplicate of the CBR-3bn note or prior MOEX items? — NO. Internal grep found no prior standalone note asserting the "15–20 ready" count. Closest is [[Bank of Russia proposes 3bn ruble minimum for IPO listing tiers]] (different event: regulation, not exchange pipeline).
8. Does the SIBUR "RUB 40–60bn at 2% float" math hold? — Implies ~RUB 2–3tn valuation, plausible for SIBUR. But 2% is a tiny free float — signals a small placement, weak liquidity, not a blockbuster.
9. Geopolitical/sanctions risk to the pipeline? — Open. Evraz especially carries cross-border/sanctions complexity; "ready" operationally may not equal "clearable to list."
10. Does CBR's new RUB 3bn minimum thin the pipeline? — Likely yes for small names (effective ~2027); could push tier-3 candidates to rush or drop out, but most named high-readiness firms (SIBUR, Solar, Evraz) clear the bar easily.
11. Is the 2025 baseline being spun optimistically? — Note that 2025 IPO+SPO volume rose ~22.6% YoY even as issuer count fell — concentration (2 names = 88%) flatters the total. A "recovery" narrative should not lean on that.
12. What's the catalyst that converts readiness to deals? — The 2026-07-24 CBR decision and a sustained cut trajectory to ~12–13%; without it, "15–20 ready" likely persists as backlog into 2027.
13. Could the source (Mergers.ru/preqveca) have garbled the attribution/number? — Low risk; cross-checked against Vedomosti and Kommersant which independently report 15–20 and Blokhin/Zhidkov.

**Importance: 3/5** — A credible, well-attributed, fresh pipeline/sentiment signal from the exchange with concrete named candidates (SIBUR, Solar) and hard 2025 baseline, useful for tracking RU capital-markets reopening. But it is a supply-side readiness claim, not a deal, and history plus a hostile rate/index backdrop mean it should be discounted; not market-moving on its own.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is a Russian primary-equity-market pipeline story: MOEX CEO says **15–20 companies are in "high readiness"** for IPO, out of **~250 companies** that have flagged a possible public-market exit in their strategies (per Vedomosti/Kommersant, 13 Jul 2026). Read this against the actual base rate: the RU primary market has been **starved of size and back-loaded**. 2024 was the peak with **13 IPOs + 4 SPOs**; 2025 collapsed to just **3 IPOs + 3 SPOs**, all non-resource (per MOEX FY2025 release; PREQVECA), with H2 volume small (e.g. **DOM.RF raised ~RUB 31.7 bln**). YTD-Oct 2025 total across IPO+SPO was only **~RUB 50–60 bln (~$0.5–0.6 bln)** (per wealthorbitcenter, Oct 2025). Why now / why the "15–20" number is aspirational: (1) the **CBR key rate is 14.25%** (cut 25bp on 19 Jun 2026, ninth consecutive cut; next meeting 24 Jul) (per CBR/Moscow Times, Jun 2026) — falling but still punitively high, so risk capital and retail demand are constrained; (2) the same "15–20 ready" framing was used for 2025 and delivered only 3 — the ratio of "ready" to "actually priced" is historically ~1-in-4 to 1-in-5; (3) **pre-IPO placements** (shares to a limited circle of qualified/wealthy investors, avg account ≥RUB 1 mln, multi-year lock to eventual listing) are now the preferred on-ramp — a sign the *public* window is still narrow. Net: the announcement is a **pipeline-optimism data point**, not evidence of a live wave; the swing variable is the rate path.

**Competitive landscape.** For the exchange operator (MOEX) the KPIs are **listing count / primary-market volume, ADV and trading fees, and net interest income on client balances**; for the *pipeline* the KPI is **conversion of "ready" candidates into priced deals**. Key named 2026 candidates (per T-Bank/Vedomosti/smart-lab, Dec 2025–Jul 2026): **Solar** (Rostelecom cybersecurity sub), **VK Tech** (VK cloud/SaaS sub), **SIBUR** (petrochemicals, ~2% float on the "Eastern"/SPB venue), **Medscan**, **Sutochno.ru**. Important contradiction to flag: **MinFin said no state-participation IPOs are planned for 2026** (Vedomosti, 9 Jun 2026) — which cuts against earlier talk of DOM.RF-style state placements (RZD, Rosatom, Rostec, VEB, Pochta) filling the pipeline. So the credible 2026 list skews **private tech/industrial**, not state champions. Basis of competition for issuers: they compete for a **shrunken retail-investor wallet** (retail participation in RU IPOs fell ~4.5x YoY per CBR's Mar-2026 review) — this is a demand-constrained, not supply-constrained, market. Protagonist position: MOEX is the **monopoly domestic venue** (SPB Exchange a distant #2, running its first 2026 SPO — see [[Mergers.ru SPB Exchange to run first 2026 SPO with NFK-sberezheniy]]); its moat is regulatory/infrastructure, but its primary-market *revenue* depends on a wave that keeps slipping.

**Comps & multiples.** No public-company multiple is computable here (the note is a pipeline statement, not a deal with a price/valuation), so this is a **conversion/precedent comparison**, not a trading-comp table.

*Precedent arithmetic (conversion, verifiable):* 2025 delivered **3 IPOs vs the ~15–20 "ready" claimed a year earlier ≈ ~15–20% hit rate**. Applying that same ratio to today's "15–20 ready" implies a **realistic 2026 outcome of ~3–5 priced IPOs**, not 15–20 — unless the rate path breaks sharply lower. External benchmark: CBR/MOEX have a stated ambition of **20+ listings/year**, so 2026 "success" would be closing even half that. No EV/EBITDA or P/E is applicable (no deal, no price) — **no data** by design.

**Risk flags.**
1. **"Ready" ≠ "priced" — high conversion risk.** The 15–20 figure is a *pipeline* count; the 2025 precedent (3 of ~15–20) implies most slip. Second-order: repeated slippage erodes the credibility of MOEX's pipeline guidance and delays the fee recovery for the exchange.
2. **Rate-dependence.** At a 14.25% key rate, equity competes with ~15–18% risk-free deposits/bonds; the wave only fires if CBR keeps cutting through H2 2026. If the 24 Jul meeting holds or inflation re-accelerates, the window stays shut — the whole thesis is a bet on the rate path, not on issuer readiness.
3. **Thin, deteriorating demand base + state pullback.** Retail IPO participation fell ~4.5x YoY and only 7 of 19 recent RU IPOs stayed above offer (CBR Mar-2026); MinFin has ruled out state-participation IPOs for 2026, removing the large anchor deals that could have carried volume. Small private issuers (Incab-type) must clear into a demand-constrained, retail-dependent market — pricing pressure and weak after-market are the base case.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is **pipeline optimism from the venue that benefits from a wave** — treat as a soft leading indicator, not a commitment. Falsifiable thesis: **2026 will NOT deliver 15–20 IPOs; the realistic outcome is low-single-to-mid-single digits** unless CBR cuts materially in H2. Triggers to watch: the **24 Jul CBR decision** and the H2 rate trajectory; whether marquee private names (**VK Tech, Solar, SIBUR**) actually price vs. re-route into **pre-IPO**; and the after-market of whatever lists next (the RU 2025 cohort averaged negative in the first month). Bull confirmation would be a sub-13% key rate plus ≥2 tech listings clearing above offer; the base case is a slow, deleveraging-driven, small-cap primary market with pre-IPO absorbing the demand the public market can't. Not a fintech event — judge as a RU capital-markets signal.
