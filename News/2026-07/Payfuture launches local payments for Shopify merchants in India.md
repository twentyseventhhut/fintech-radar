Payfuture launches local payments integration for Shopify merchants in India
Sinziana Albu
07 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Payfuture has integrated with Shopify to give merchants access to local payment methods in India, including UPI and NetBanking.
The move is aimed at merchants seeking to reach consumers in India's ecommerce market without building separate local payment infrastructure.
Through the Payfuture app, Shopify merchants can now offer Unified Payments Interface (UPI) and NetBanking to customers in India. Both methods are widely used across the country, reflecting a broader shift towards mobile-first and account-based payment instruments rather than card transactions. In addition, for merchants operating internationally, the integration is intended to remove the need to establish individual connections with local payment rails, banks, or acquiring partners in order to accept these methods.
Market context
According to the official press release, India's online retail sector has been projected to reach a value of between USD 150 billion and USD 170 billion by 2027. Growth in the market has been linked to widespread mobile-first consumer behaviour and continued reliance on domestic payment systems such as UPI, which has become one of the primary methods for digital transactions in the country. Similar dynamics have also been observed elsewhere in South Asia, where local payment preferences continue to shape how international merchants approach market entry.
As competition among ecommerce platforms operating in the region increases, the ability to support local payment preferences, alongside compliance with regional regulatory requirements, has become a factor that merchants weigh when deciding where and how to scale their operations. Platforms and payment providers that can offer this kind of localisation are positioned to support merchants seeking longer-term access to the market, rather than short-term or test-stage entry.
 Manpreet Haer , CEO and Co-Founder of Payfuture said that the Shopify integration forms part of Payfuture's wider approach to providing local payment connectivity through established ecommerce platforms. The company has said it will continue to invest in regional infrastructure to support merchants expanding into India and other high-growth markets. It was also noted that the aim of the integration is to allow international merchants to operate within the Indian ecommerce ecosystem while maintaining compliance and operational consistency.
The launch adds to a series of local payment integrations Payfuture has pursued with global commerce platforms, as part of its broader positioning as a payments provider focused on emerging and high-growth regions.

## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** On 07 Jul 2026 Payfuture announced a Shopify integration that lets Shopify merchants offer India's two dominant account-based rails — UPI (Unified Payments Interface) and NetBanking — to Indian shoppers through a single connection to the Payfuture app, without building their own local acquiring/banking stack. Positioned squarely at international ("cross-border") merchants selling into India who want to accept domestic methods without setting up local payment rails, bank relationships, or acquiring partners. Source: [The Paypers](https://newsletter.thepaypers.com/), primary press release on [payfuture.net newsroom](https://www.payfuture.net/newsroom/payfuture-shopify-integration/) (mirrored via GlobeNewswire on [Manila Times](https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/07/07/tmt-newswire/globenewswire/payfuture-launches-local-payments-integration-for-merchants-on-shopify-in-india/2379744)).

**[1] Who is Payfuture.** Emerging-markets payments infrastructure company; founded 2019, HQ London; CEO/co-founder Manpreet Haer. Operates a regulated Electronic Money Institution (Payfuture FS MT Ltd) under the Malta FSA. Notably **bootstrapped — no external venture funding** ([The Fintech Times](https://thefintechtimes.com/new-faces-big-ambitions-payfuture-next-chapter-in-emerging-markets/), [Forbes 2022](https://www.forbes.com/sites/davidprosser/2022/09/13/exploiting-the-potential-of-emerging-markets-with-payfuture/)). Its pitch is a single integration for local payment acceptance and cross-border fund movement across Africa, Asia and the Middle East. Recent leadership additions: Praful Morar (deputy CEO), Sanda Laicena (group CLO).

**[2] Why it matters.** India online retail is projected at USD 150–170bn by 2027 (per the press release); UPI is ~64% of India payment-gateway volume and processes >14bn transactions/month, ~half of all real-time payments globally. Card penetration at checkout is comparatively low, so an international merchant that only accepts cards leaves most of the addressable Indian buyer base uncovered. The integration is a distribution play: reach Shopify's merchant base (see [[Shopify merchants clear $100 billion in Q1 GMV]]) with a plug-in that removes the regulatory/technical burden of accepting UPI/NetBanking cross-border.

**[3] How it fits Payfuture's arc.** This extends a run of platform/partner distribution deals: the June 2026 [[Payfuture and Atlantic Partners Asia partner for cross-border payment access]] tie-up (single-integration access to Payfuture's local rails for APA's clients) and prior emerging-markets rails deals (e.g. Profee). The Shopify launch is the same "one integration → many local methods" model, now embedded directly in a global commerce platform rather than sold via a partner.

**[4] Adjacent signals.** Same-day The Paypers batch also carried [[PhotonPay adds Apple Pay and Google Pay to global checkout]] and a UPI-goes-to-Greece item — both reinforcing the theme of local-method / wallet localisation for cross-border checkout. A structurally similar prior story: [[payabl. launches payments integration for Shopify merchants]] (Nov 2025, EU-focused). ## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
**Red-team questions**

1. *Is this a genuine product launch or a marketing/wire item?* — Mostly a distribution/marketing announcement. It is a real product (a Shopify app enabling UPI/NetBanking), but the press release is thin on numbers (no merchant count, no TPV, no pricing). Multiple hits are syndicated GlobeNewswire copies (Manila Times, salestechstar, logistics-trade sites) of one release. **Answered — light substance.**
2. *Is Payfuture actually licensed to move INR/UPI cross-border under RBI rules?* — **OPEN / key risk.** Accepting UPI + NetBanking from Indian buyers for foreign merchants implicates RBI's Payment Aggregator – Cross Border (PA-CB) regime. Payfuture's disclosed regulated entity is a Malta EMI; the release does not state an RBI PA-CB authorisation or a licensed local partner. Cross-border UPI acceptance typically routes through an RBI-authorised PA-CB. Unconfirmed whether Payfuture holds/partners for this.
3. *Does the merchant still need a local Indian entity?* — Under PA-CB rules, foreign merchants can be onboarded by a licensed PA-CB without a separate Indian entity, but CDD/KYC and FEMA settlement obligations sit with the licensed aggregator. So Payfuture's "no separate connections" pitch only holds if it (or a partner) carries the PA-CB licence. **Answered conditionally.**
4. *How does this differ from Shopify's own payment options or Shopify Payments?* — Shopify Payments and existing India PSPs (Razorpay, Cashfree, PayU) already serve domestic Indian merchants. Payfuture's angle is specifically the international merchant who is not an Indian resident entity — a narrower niche. **Answered.**
5. *Is the addressable niche large?* — Uncertain. Most India ecommerce is served by domestic merchants on domestic PSPs. Cross-border-into-India inbound retail is a real but smaller slice; the USD 150–170bn figure is the whole online-retail TAM, not Payfuture's serviceable share. **Overstated framing.**
6. *Competitive moat?* — Weak. UPI/NetBanking connectivity is increasingly commoditised; PA-CB licensees (EximPe et al.), Razorpay, Cashfree and orchestration layers (Juspay) all offer this. Moat is breadth of emerging-market coverage + platform distribution, not India uniqueness. **Open.**
7. *Economics for Payfuture?* — Undisclosed. UPI carries zero-MDR (no merchant discount rate) for many transaction types in India, compressing PSP revenue; cross-border FX/settlement spreads are the more likely monetisation. **Open — margin question.**
8. *Is "single integration" real or aspirational?* — Plausible (it's a Shopify app), but no independent verification of go-live, method coverage limits, or settlement currencies. **Open.**
9. *Any Shopify endorsement / is this an official Shopify partnership?* — The release frames Payfuture as delivering via the Shopify app ecosystem; no evidence of a Shopify co-marketing or exclusive deal. Likely a standard Shopify App Store integration. **Answered — not a strategic Shopify deal.**
10. *Funding/scale credibility?* — Payfuture is bootstrapped (no VC), profitable historically (2021 gross profit ~$11m per Forbes), which cuts both ways: disciplined but limited balance sheet to out-invest funded India incumbents. **Answered.**
11. *Duplicate of the June APA note?* — No. Distinct event (Shopify-India integration vs APA cross-border partnership), same strategic thread. **Answered.**
12. *Settlement/FX and repatriation risk for merchants?* — Not addressed in release; FEMA and settlement-currency mechanics matter to international merchants and are unstated. **Open.**
13. *Fraud/chargeback handling for UPI/account-based methods?* — UPI is push-based (low chargeback) but has its own dispute/refund flows; release silent on fraud tooling. **Open.**

**Importance: 2/5** — A modest, marketing-led product/distribution announcement from a bootstrapped, non-listed emerging-markets PSP. Real and on-trend (cross-border local-method localisation, UPI's rise), and a reasonable read on where checkout localisation is heading, but low signal: no financials, narrow serviceable niche, unresolved RBI PA-CB licensing question, commoditised capability, and no independent verification beyond one syndicated press release. Worth logging as a data point in the "cross-border-into-India local payments" theme; not market-moving.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector: India online-payments acceptance + cross-border-into-India checkout**

**Market size / structure.** India payment-gateway market ~USD 2.07bn (2025) → est. USD 2.31bn (2026), forecast USD 4.01bn by 2031 at ~11.7% CAGR ([Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/india-payment-gateway-market)). UPI dominates: ~64% of gateway volume in 2025 and >14bn transactions/month (~half of global real-time payments); NetBanking and cards fill the rest, with cards structurally losing share to account-based rails. India online-retail TAM projected at USD 150–170bn by 2027 (Payfuture press release). BNPL is the fastest-growing method (~11.8% CAGR). Zero-MDR mandates on much of UPI compress fee-based PSP margins — a structural headwind for anyone monetising UPI acceptance.

**Where Payfuture sits.** Not a domestic Indian PSP. It is a cross-border / emerging-markets acceptance layer targeting *international* merchants selling into India (and other high-growth markets). The Shopify launch is a distribution wedge into that niche. Competitive strength = breadth of emerging-market local-method coverage + platform embedding, not India-specific depth.

**Competitors / comps.**
- **Razorpay** — market leader, ~55% of India online gateway share, ~USD 180bn annual TPV, FY25 revenue ₹3,783cr (+65%); received RBI PA-CB (cross-border) licence Jan 2026; pre-IPO. See [[Razorpay scales back offline payments ahead of IPO]]. ([coinlaw.io](https://coinlaw.io/razorpay-statistics/))
- **PayU (India)** — RBI-approved online/offline/cross-border; H1 FY26 revenue ~USD 397m. See [[PayU India clocks $397 Mn revenue in H1 FY26]], [[PayU secures RBI approval for online, offline and cross-border payments]].
- **Cashfree** — differentiates on T+0 instant settlement; strong SMB/cash-flow positioning.
- **Juspay** — API-first orchestration/router layer (tokenisation, routing) used by incumbents and banks; turned profitable FY25 (₹62cr net profit). See [[Juspay turns profitable with ₹62 crore net profit in FY2025]].
- **PA-CB niche specialists** (e.g. EximPe) — directly address the "global brand accepts UPI/cards/netbanking into India via one integration, no local entity" use case that overlaps Payfuture's pitch. ([eximpe.com PA-CB](https://eximpe.com/pa-cb-license))
- **Cross-border-into-India regulatory frame:** RBI PA-CB regime (Master Directions; PwC/Lexology summaries) governs foreign-merchant onboarding, CDD/KYC and FEMA settlement. Foreign merchants need no separate Indian entity, but a *licensed* PA-CB must carry the compliance — Payfuture's RBI PA-CB status/partner is unstated. ([PwC India](https://www.pwc.in/industries/financial-services/fintech/payments/cross-border-payment-aggregators-regulations-and-business-use-cases.html))

**Ecosystem context — Shopify (merchant platform, not the payments subject).** Payfuture is the payments partner; Shopify is the checkout surface. Shopify Q1 2026 (qtr end 31 Mar): GMV USD 100.7bn (+35% YoY), revenue USD 3.17bn (+34%), FCF margin 15%, with European GMV +48% and broad-based international growth ([Shopify PR](https://www.shopify.com/investors/press-releases/shopify-delivers-again-merchants-clear-100-billion-q1-gmv)). Millions of merchants in 175+ countries. Scale/global-merchant mix is why a local-method plug-in has distribution value — but this is platform context; do not overweight (Payfuture captures only whatever cross-border-into-India flow it wins, a small slice).

**Risk flags (5).**
1. **Regulatory/licensing (highest):** cross-border UPI/NetBanking acceptance triggers RBI PA-CB obligations; Payfuture's disclosed entity is a Malta EMI — RBI PA-CB authorisation or licensed local partner unconfirmed.
2. **Commoditisation / thin moat:** UPI+NetBanking connectivity offered by Razorpay, Cashfree, PayU, PA-CB specialists; little India-specific differentiation.
3. **Margin compression:** zero-MDR on UPI limits acceptance-fee revenue; monetisation likely leans on FX/settlement spreads, undisclosed.
4. **Narrow serviceable market:** most India ecommerce is domestic-merchant/domestic-PSP; cross-border-inbound retail is a smaller slice than the USD 150–170bn headline TAM implies.
5. **Disclosure/verification:** single syndicated press release, no merchant count, TPV, pricing, or go-live metrics; bootstrapped balance sheet limits ability to out-invest funded incumbents.
