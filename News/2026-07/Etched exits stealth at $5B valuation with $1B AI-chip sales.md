Nvidia competitor Etched hits $5B valuation, $1B in sales for AI chip
Nvidia AI chip competitor Etched issued a progress report on Tuesday, after TSMC successfully manufactured its chip earlier this year. The startup says it has already booked $1 billion in contract orders for its product: full systems powered by those chips.
Etched is currently in the process of testing that first product with customers. It calls these systems “frontier inference clusters,” bundles that include the chips along with custom-designed racks and software, all built to help frontier models run inference faster, more cheaply, and with better power efficiency than rivals, Etched claims. (Inference is what happens after a user submits a prompt — it’s currently the biggest bottleneck, and the biggest cost center for AI companies trying to serve customers at scale, which is exactly why investors are paying attention to anyone promising to solve it.)
Etched, founded in 2022, also revealed that it has now raised a total of $800 million to date. The most recent tranche was an unannounced $500 million round closed in December at a $5 billion post-money valuation, the company said.
The startup has attracted a notable group of investors, too, including VentureTech Alliance, Jane Street, Hudson River Trading, Two Sigma, Ribbit Capital, and Stripes, who led the $500 million round. It has also secured angel investment from AI heavyweights including Andrej Karpathy, Geoffrey Hinton, Fei-Fei Li, Arthur Mensch, and Scott Wu. The cap table also includes billionaires Stanley Druckenmiller and Peter Thiel.
Although the startup’s press release frames Tuesday’s announcement as Etched “coming out of stealth,” co-founders — CEO Gavin Uberti (pictured above) and president Robert Wachen — have actually been talking to TechCrunch about their chip plans since 2024. Both dropped out of Harvard and became Thiel fellows to found Etched, as Uberti told TechCrunch at the time.
By 2024, Etched was already on investors’ radar, having raised more than $125 million. But on Patrick O’Shaughnessy’s “ Invest Like the Best ” podcast, the founders said that back in 2023, they struggled to get investors interested — even with a 30-page memo arguing that AI would eventually need specialized chips, not just general-purpose GPUs. Every major investor they pitched passed. The company was reportedly operating month-to-month, close to running out of cash, in those early days.
Today’s funding environment looks like a different planet by comparison. Investors are chasing everything AI-related, especially chip technology that speeds up inference. Competitor Cerebras had the first breakout IPO of the year, while AI chip maker Groq just raised $650 million . Hyperscalers Amazon, Google, and Microsoft all build their own in-house AI chips. Even OpenAI just announced its first custom chip , built by Broadcom.

# Context-enrichment: Etched exits stealth at $5B valuation with $1B AI-chip sales
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**Fintech-relevance framing (honest):** This is a general **AI-infrastructure / semiconductor** story, NOT squarely fintech. Its fintech tangency is one step removed: the cap table is dominated by **quant-trading houses acting as VCs** — Jane Street, Hudson River Trading, Jump Trading, Two Sigma — plus fintech VC Ribbit Capital. So the "fintech angle" is *who is funding it* (trading firms treating AI-inference silicon as a strategic/financial bet), not what the product does. Kept in-corpus as an AI-infra data point (cf. [[AWS in talks to sell Trainium AI chip racks to outside companies]], [[Baseten reportedly raising at $400 million valuation for AI model infrastructure]]).

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Etched (San Jose; founded 2022; CEO Gavin Uberti, president Robert Wachen — Harvard dropouts / Thiel Fellows) issued a "coming out of stealth" progress report on ~2026-06-30. De-PR'd facts:
- **Money is real:** $800M raised to date; most recent tranche a **$500M round led by Stripes, closed Dec-2025, $5B post-money** (previously unannounced). Investors confirmed across TechCrunch + PR: VentureTech Alliance (TSMC-linked strategic, *not* lead — PR lists it first, which misleads), Jane Street, HRT, Jump, Two Sigma, Ribbit, Stripes; angels Karpathy, Hinton, Fei-Fei Li, Mensch, Scott Wu, Druckenmiller, Thiel.
- **"$1B in sales" is BOOKINGS, not revenue.** PR wording: ">$1B in signed customer contracts"; TechCrunch: "booked $1B in contract orders." The product ("frontier inference clusters" = Sohu chips + custom racks + software) is **in customer testing, not shipped** ("first racks ship this summer"). **Zero customer names; no disclosure of binding vs LOI, deposit size, or refundability.**
- **"Exiting stealth" is spin.** Etched has been public since **June 2024** (Sohu unveil + $120M Series A led by Primary + Positive Sum) and raising since 2023.
- **Silicon claim:** first-pass ("A0") success on TSMC N4P "earlier this year" (2026). First-party only.

**→ Why framed this way:** anchoring to "$1B sales" + "out of stealth" manufactures a fresh-blockbuster narrative for a 4-year-old company whose real news is a quiet Dec-2025 round now being marketed. The bookings-as-sales conflation is the core PR move — it converts backlog on an unshipped product into an implied revenue run-rate.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Inference-silicon is crowded and hot. **Nvidia** (Blackwell/Rubin) owns inference by default. **Groq** (LPU) reportedly raising **$650M** (~2026) after a reported ~$20B Nvidia licensing deal. **Cerebras** IPO'd in 2026 ("first breakout IPO of the year"). **SambaNova, d-Matrix**, and hyperscaler in-house silicon — **AWS Trainium/Inferentia** (now being sold externally, cf. [[AWS in talks to sell Trainium AI chip racks to outside companies]]), **Google TPU, Microsoft Maia** — plus **OpenAI+Broadcom** custom chip (cf. [[Seven European fintech deals raise EUR 95 million in a week]] re the Broadcom order). Serving-layer peers like Baseten monetize inference in software ([[Baseten reportedly raising at $400 million valuation for AI model infrastructure]]).
**Position:** Etched is a late, unshipped entrant differentiated by an extreme bet — a **transformer-ONLY ASIC** (attention/QKV/softmax/FFN hardwired). **→ Why it matters:** everyone else keeps flexibility (GPU/reconfigurable/CGRA); Etched trades flexibility for claimed throughput. That is the whole thesis and the whole risk.

## [2] Company history / fit
2022 founded → 2023 near-death "month-to-month," every major investor passed on a 30-page "transformers need custom silicon" memo → 2024 Thiel Fellows + $120M Series A + Sohu unveil (claimed 500k+ tok/s on Llama-70B, "one server replaces 160 H100s") → Dec-2025 $500M at $5B → 2026 taped-out silicon + $1B bookings. **→ Why act this way now:** with TSMC having produced first silicon and a serving-focused inference market at peak hype, timing a loud "stealth exit" maximizes the marketing value of an existing round and pre-sells racks before shipping — de-risking demand narrative ahead of production.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Genuinely novel:** a fully transformer-specialized ASIC is a real architectural bet, not a me-too GPU. **BUT traction is announced, not proven:**
- **All performance numbers (500k tok/s, 20× H100, 160-H100 replacement) are first-party; NO independent benchmarks exist.**
- **Nothing is deployed/live** — product is in testing; $1B is backlog on an unshipped system.
**→ Who captures margin / what breaks it (analysis):** value-add is real *only if* two things hold — (a) the throughput/$/watt edge survives independent benchmarking against Blackwell/Rubin at ship time, and (b) transformers remain the dominant inference architecture. A transformer-only ASIC has hard **architecture lock-in**: it is worthless for diffusion, SSMs/Mamba, or any post-transformer paradigm. That is a structural, not incremental, risk — one architecture shift strands the silicon.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Near-term: first racks "ship this summer" (2026); watch for (i) independent benchmarks, (ii) named customers converting bookings → shipped revenue, (iii) yield on N4P. Sentiment is euphoric — "inference will eat the AI industry" (Baseten/StrictlyVC framing), quant firms and Thiel names on the cap table signal conviction (and financial-engineering appetite). **→ Counterintuitive second-order effect:** the marquee cap table and $1B "sales" headline make Etched look de-risked, but concentration on a single fixed architecture + unbenchmarked claims + zero shipments means the downside is binary. If Nvidia Rubin closes the inference gap or the model architecture drifts, a $5B mark built on backlog compresses fast. The real question is not "is the chip fast" but **"will it still be the right chip when it ships, and will bookings become revenue."**

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** AI inference silicon, transformer-ASIC subvertical. Sector context: inference (not training) is now the majority of AI compute — "two-thirds of all AI compute" per TrendForce/analyst citations via Introl, 2026 — and the biggest cost center for model serving, which is the "why now" for specialized silicon. Market size: AI-inference silicon ~$150bn by 2026 vs ~$40bn in 2023, and total AI accelerator market projected at ~$604bn by 2033 (per Bloomberg Intelligence, via Introl blog, as of 2026) — treat as a secondary/analyst citation, directional not audited. Structure: heavily consolidated at the top — Nvidia still holds ~70–80% of AI accelerators by revenue (per Silicon Analysts, 2026); in inference specifically ~60–75%. The design/co-design layer is even more concentrated: Broadcom + Marvell reportedly ~95% of custom-ASIC co-design (per Oplexa/Introl, 2026). Key driver: the ASIC-vs-GPU divergence — custom ASICs projected ~44.6% CAGR vs ~16.1% for merchant GPUs through 2033 (TrendForce, via TechTimes 2026-05-26). Entry barriers are extreme: capital (Etched has raised $800m and is still only *testing* first silicon), TSMC leading-edge allocation (Sohu on 4nm N4P), and software/compiler ecosystem vs Nvidia CUDA.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: tokens/sec/$ (perf-per-dollar), tokens/sec/watt (power efficiency), design-win backlog / contracted revenue, and time-to-working-silicon. Etched's differentiator is a *transformer-only* ASIC (Sohu) — it hard-wires the transformer architecture, trading generality for claimed throughput (Etched claims ~500k tok/s on Llama-70B for an 8-chip server — vendor claim, unverified). Basis of competition: perf/$ and perf/watt vs Nvidia's incumbency + software moat. Players split three ways: (1) merchant challengers — Groq, Cerebras, SambaNova; (2) hyperscaler in-house — Google TPU v7, AWS Trainium 3, Microsoft Maia, Meta MTIA, plus OpenAI's Broadcom-built chip; (3) Nvidia itself. Recent dated peer moves: Cerebras IPO'd May 2026, pricing 30m shares at $185, raising $5.55bn at ~$56bn fully-diluted (CNBC/TechTimes, 2026-05). Groq confirmed a $650m raise (2026-06-22, TechCrunch), pivoting toward an inference "neocloud"; last disclosed valuation $6.9bn (Sep round); notably, Nvidia struck a reported ~$20bn tech-licensing/"not-acqui-hire" with Groq in Dec 2025. AWS in talks to sell Trainium racks externally (2026-06). Etched's position (analysis): far behind on revenue realization (Cerebras recognized $510m 2025 revenue; Etched has $0 recognized, only $1bn *contracted*), niche on architecture bet, but early on the "dedicated inference ASIC" thesis. Moat = intangibles/architecture specialization (analysis); the risk is that specialization is also its fragility (see risks).

**Comps & multiples.** Etched: $5bn post-money (Dec 2025 round, led by Stripes) — this is a *round valuation, not market cap*. Revenue recognized = $0 (product in customer testing); $1bn is *contracted/booked*, not shipped or recognized, so no clean EV/Revenue is computable → **no data** on a trailing multiple. On *contracted* backlog: $5bn / $1bn = 5.0x price-to-booked-backlog (analysis; backlog is not revenue — cancellable, undelivered). External comps: Cerebras — ~$56bn FD market cap on $510m 2025 revenue ⇒ ~110x P/S (public, both numbers sourced: TechTimes/CNBC 2026-05) — extreme, and heavily caveated by 86% revenue concentration in UAE-linked entities. Groq — $6.9bn last round, revenue undisclosed ⇒ multiple = **no data**. Internal comps (base): [[Baseten reportedly raising at $400 million valuation for AI model infrastructure]] (inference software, 2026-06), [[AWS in talks to sell Trainium AI chip racks to outside companies]] (2026-06), [[Tencent and Alibaba in talks to invest in DeepSeek above $20B]] (2026-04). Distribution not computed (only 1 comparable P/S figure). Flag: at 5.0x price-to-*booked-backlog* with zero recognized revenue and untested-at-scale silicon, valuation is **rich and momentum-driven** — but this is normal for pre-revenue frontier-silicon in the current cycle; the flag is that it is unsupported by shipped economics, not merely "above range."

**Risk flags.**
1. **Execution / silicon-not-yet-shipped.** $1bn is *contracted*, product is in *testing*; the $5bn mark prices success that hasn't happened. If Sohu misses perf/power claims or slips vs Nvidia's next node, contracts (likely cancellable) and the valuation both compress.
2. **Architecture-specialization fragility (second-order).** A transformer-*only* ASIC monetizes one architecture. If frontier models shift (SSMs/Mamba, MoE routing changes, diffusion-LM, new attention variants), a hard-wired transformer chip risks stranding — the opposite of a GPU's generality. This is the structural counterpart to its speed advantage.
3. **Disintermediation by hyperscalers + Nvidia.** The buyers of inference silicon (Google/AWS/Microsoft/Meta/OpenAI) are also building in-house ASICs, and Nvidia's ~$20bn Groq deal shows it will license/absorb credible challengers. Merchant inference-ASIC TAM may be squeezed between captive hyperscaler silicon above and Nvidia's software+licensing moat below — margin captured by other stack layers.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Etched is a marker of the 2026 inference-ASIC re-rating: capital is flowing to pre-revenue silicon on the thesis that inference cost is the binding constraint (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if a dedicated transformer-ASIC can beat GPU perf/$ at scale in production, expect follow-on rounds and possibly an M&A/licensing bid (à la Groq–Nvidia) within 12 months. What breaks it: independent third-party benchmarks showing Sohu underperforms on real frontier models, or a mainstream architecture shift away from vanilla transformers — either would invalidate the specialization bet and the mark. Fintech relevance is indirect (AI-infra), but the investor angle is direct: the cap table is thick with quant/prop-trading capital (Jane Street, HRT, Two Sigma, Jump) and fintech VCs (Ribbit, Stripes) — a signal of how financial-infra investors are positioning around AI-compute economics.
