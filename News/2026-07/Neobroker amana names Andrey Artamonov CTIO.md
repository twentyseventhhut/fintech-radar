## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** On 2026-07-06, Dubai-based MENA neobroker **amana** appointed **Andrey Artamonov** as **Chief Technology and Information Officer (CTIO)**. His mandate spans software development, corporate IT, business intelligence, cybersecurity, cloud infrastructure and platform operations. Stated goals: strengthen amana's tech platform, support growth, and scale as it expands into new markets. This is a leadership/engineering-org story, not a funding or M&A event.

**[1] Who is Artamonov.** ~20 years in trading tech. **17 years at Devexperts** (a well-known trading-platform vendor), including **9 years as CTO**, where he led a 700+ engineering org building trading/brokerage/wealth systems used by brokers, broker-dealers and banks across the US, Europe, Middle East and East Asia. Most recently founded **TripleA Digital**, a Lisbon-based automation/AI consultancy for financial workflows. Key nuance: amana already runs Devexperts' **DXtrade** platform — so this is a **vendor-side → broker-side** move, i.e. hiring the person who used to build the platform amana buys.

**[2] Who is amana.** Dubai-headquartered broker; the trading/investing **app relaunched Sept 2022**, growing from ~320k (early 2024) to **500k+ users** by 2026. Offers 6,800+ assets: 1,200+ MENA stocks, US equities (commission-free), FX, metals, ETFs, and 450+ cryptocurrencies (marketed as the largest crypto lineup among MENA brokers, Mar 2025). Legacy roots are a forex/CFD business; positioning has shifted toward physical stocks and a younger retail demographic. Multi-licensed: **DFSA, FCA, CySEC, CMA (Jordan), LFSA, FSC**. Regulatory press describes it as "the MENA region's leading neobroker" (self/PR framing — treat as marketing).

**[3] Why it matters.** Signals amana is investing in in-house technical depth (rather than pure vendor reliance) to differentiate as MENA retail brokerage crowds up. A CTO-of-a-major-vendor hire is a credible senior signal. But: a C-suite tech appointment is a low-magnitude event on its own — no capital, no metrics, no product launch disclosed.

**[4] Prior notes / comps in DB.**
- [[Sarwa crosses $1 billion in client assets under management]] — UAE (Abu Dhabi) rival, first homegrown UAE fintech to $1bn client AUM (May 2026); wealth + trading.
- [[Wio Invest surpasses $1bn in assets under administration]] — ADQ-owned UAE platform, $1bn AUA in <2 years (Jul 2025); direct scale comp.
- [[CredibleX raises $15 million Series A led by Mubadala]] — UAE fintech capital-formation comp (embedded lending, different vertical).
- Robinhood entering the region (applied for DFSA license, hiring for Dubai) tightens competition for amana.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector: MENA retail neobrokerage.** GCC/MENA retail investing is a fast-growing, increasingly crowded field. Drivers: young, digitally-native population; commission-free US + local-stock access; crypto demand; regulators (DFSA, SCA, CMA-Egypt/Jordan) issuing broker/wealth licenses. GCC fintech projected ~15% CAGR through 2030 (per Sarwa/Fast Company framing — vendor-adjacent, treat as directional not audited).

**Competitive map (amana's peers):**
- **Sarwa** (UAE/Abu Dhabi): crossed **$1bn client AUM** (May 2026), first homegrown UAE fintech to do so; wealth + trading. See [[Sarwa crosses $1 billion in client assets under management]].
- **Wio Invest** (UAE, ADQ-owned): **$1bn AUA** in <2 years (Jul 2025), $4bn+ YTD order volume; sovereign-backed = deep pockets. See [[Wio Invest surpasses $1bn in assets under administration]].
- **Thndr** (Egypt→Saudi): **4M+ users**, raised **$15.7M** (total ~$37.8M) led by Prosus Ventures; largest user base by count in the region.
- **baraka** (UAE): stocks/bonds/options/gold, GCC-focused, beginner tier.
- **eToro / Robinhood**: global entrants. Robinhood **applied for a DFSA license** and is staffing a Dubai expansion — a direct future threat to amana on its home turf.

**Where amana sits.** amana leads on breadth of instruments (6,800+ assets, 450+ crypto — largest MENA crypto lineup) and 500k+ users, but has NOT publicly disclosed AUM/AUA — the metric peers (Sarwa, Wio) compete on. User count ≠ assets; amana's forex/CFD heritage skews toward higher-churn, lower-AUM active traders vs. Sarwa/Wio's wealth-building base. amana's edge is technical/product breadth; this hire reinforces that positioning.

**Read on the hire (market lens).** Bringing in a Devexperts ex-CTO (the vendor whose DXtrade amana already uses) is a bet on **in-house engineering depth** to reduce vendor dependence and differentiate on platform quality/AI. Credible senior signal. But it's a personnel move: no capital, no metric, no product. Impact is narrative/strategic, not financial.

**Risk flags (R = 4):**
1. **No disclosed scale metric** — amana touts 500k users but no AUM/AUA; "leading neobroker" is self-PR. Hard to verify competitive standing vs. Sarwa/Wio's audited-ish $1bn milestones.
2. **Crowding / big-tech entry** — Robinhood (DFSA-applied) and eToro entering MENA compress margins and talent competition; amana's commission-free model has thin unit economics.
3. **Forex/CFD legacy & regulatory exposure** — legacy CFD business carries higher regulatory and reputational risk (WikiFX-type scrutiny appears in searches); multi-jurisdiction licensing (DFSA/FCA/CySEC/CMA/LFSA/FSC) is compliance-heavy.
4. **Execution risk on the hire** — vendor-side → broker-side transition and building a large in-house org is non-trivial; a single C-suite appointment doesn't de-risk scaling. Key-person / integration risk.
