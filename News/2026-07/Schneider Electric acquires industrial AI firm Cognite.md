Rueil-Malmaison, France — June 30th, 2026 — Schneider Electric , a global leader in energy management and automation, today announces that it has entered into a definitive agreement to acquire 100% of Cognite Holding B.V. (“Cognite”), a leading provider of industrial data and AI software, in an all cash transaction valued at $3.1 billion.
 Strengthening Schneider Electric’s leadership in industrial AI 
Industrial AI is shifting from supporting analytics to executing operations, from describing what is happening in industrial infrastructure to deciding and acting on it. Capturing this shift requires more than models: it requires a unified, contextualized foundation of industrial data on which AI can be trusted to operate at scale.
Cognite brings that foundation. Its cloud native platform combines a unified industrial data model with agentic AI capabilities, enabling customers to operationalize AI directly within plant operations, asset management and engineering workflows.
Olivier Blum, Schneider Electric CEO, commented: “Cognite has built something rare, a truly industrial grade AI platform that turns the complexity of operational data into a competitive advantage. This acquisition strengthens AVEVA, Schneider Electric’s wholly owned industrial software company, in the highest‑growth segments of the market and positions Schneider Electric at the centre of the next phase of industrial intelligence.
I have been extremely impressed by the world-class technology team and am convinced their unique AI expertise will be a catalyst in advancing intelligence across Schneider Electric’s portfolio.
At Schneider Electric, we have always believed the energy transition demands intelligence, intelligence demands data, and unlocking its full value requires AI.
By bringing Cognite into Schneider Electric and AVEVA, we unite the world's most comprehensive energy management and automation infrastructure with the software and AI capabilities to make it natively intelligent. Together, we go beyond connecting systems. We give them the ability to think, adapt, and act. This is what industrial intelligence looks like at scale.”
 A leading industrial AI platform with strong growth momentum 
Cognite was founded in 2017 and today employs more than 800 people in the Americas, Europe, Middle East and Asia-Pacific, specializing in cloud‑native data and AI platforms. Its technology enables the integration and contextualization of complex industrial data through a unified data model and knowledge graph, supporting advanced analytics and AI‑driven applications for asset‑intensive industries.
In 2025, the annual revenue exceeded $170 million, marked by 36% growth in ARR bookings and a rapid adoption of the Atlas AI platform. ( Cognite’s Moonshot and AI Drive Record-Breaking Year )
The transaction complements the capabilities of AVEVA’s CONNECT industrial intelligence platform, reinforcing it as a leader for comprehensive industrial intelligence solutions spanning design, build, operation and optimization.
 Compelling strategic fit with AVEVA 
Cognite's scalable, open architecture combined with the capabilities of CONNECT means that analytics and industrial AI can ingest industrial data across the asset lifecycle and throughout customers' existing data ecosystems and investments. The combination extends CONNECT with enterprise-wide data contextualization capabilities and leading-edge agentic AI.
Cognite’s core technologies will act as a powerful data and AI enabler within CONNECT:
 Industrial Data Foundation : Cognite’s Data Fusion and knowledge graph enable the integration, modelling and contextualization of engineering, operational and enterprise data at scale.
 AI Platform : Atlas AI introduces advanced modelling capabilities, generative and agentic AI, enabling automation of industrial workflows combined with accelerated and improved decision‑making.
Together, Cognite and AVEVA will form a unified platform built for the next phase of intelligence for industrial AI.
 Transaction terms and next steps 
Under the terms of the agreement, Schneider Electric will acquire 100% of Cognite’s share capital in an all-cash transaction. The completion of the transaction remains subject to customary closing conditions, including the receipt of required regulatory approvals. The transaction is expected to be completed in the coming quarters. Upon completion, Cognite will be integrated with AVEVA and will be fully consolidated and financially reported within Schneider Electric’s Industrial Automation business.

# Context-enrichment: Schneider Electric acquires industrial AI firm Cognite
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

**FRESHNESS: fresh.** No prior corpus note covers Cognite, AVEVA/OSIsoft, or Schneider industrial-AI M&A. Internal `sem search` returned only weakly-related unrelated M&A (Cube/Acin RegTech, CMS/Securens); grep for "cognite|aveva|osisoft" found no matching notes. This is a genuinely new, large deal — not a re-run.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Schneider Electric signed a definitive agreement (announced **2026-06-30**, Rueil-Malmaison; analyst call **2026-07-01**) to acquire **100% of Cognite Holding B.V. for $3.1B, all cash**. Cognite folds into **AVEVA** (Schneider's wholly-owned industrial-software arm) and is consolidated into Schneider's Industrial Automation segment. Close "in coming quarters," subject to regulatory approvals.
- **What is actually being bought:** two assets — (1) **Cognite Data Fusion** (a knowledge-graph / unified industrial data model that contextualizes sensor + ERP + engineering + maintenance data) and (2) **Atlas AI** (generative/agentic layer on top). The de-PR'd thesis is a **data-layer land grab**, not a model play: Schneider is paying for the contextualized industrial data foundation that AI must sit on, to bolt onto AVEVA's CONNECT platform.
- **Numbers (real):** 2025 revenue **>$170M**, **+36% ARR bookings growth**, ~800 employees. → $3.1B / $170M ≈ **~18x revenue** — a steep multiple for industrial software (typically 5–10x), i.e. Schneider is paying an AI-premium.
- **Why structured this way:** all-cash + fold-into-AVEVA (not standalone) signals Schneider wants integration/consolidation, not a financial bet. The PR anchors to ARR-bookings growth (36%) rather than absolute revenue or profit — a flattering forward-looking frame that sidesteps whether Cognite is profitable (undisclosed → likely not, given the growth-stage profile).

## [1] Competitors / peers
Industrial-AI / DataOps landscape splits three ways: **AI-native industrial data platforms** (Cognite, C3.ai, SymphonyAI, Sight Machine); **OT-rooted automation incumbents embedding AI** (AVEVA itself, AspenTech, GE Vernova, Rockwell, Honeywell, Siemens Xcelerator); and **domain specialists** (Augury, Seeq, Braincube). Verdantix/analyst quadrants place **Cognite among the Leaders** (with ABB, AVEVA, C3.ai, GE Vernova, IBM, Seeq, SymphonyAI).
- **Position:** by buying Cognite, Schneider/AVEVA absorbs one of the strongest AI-native data-model players and pulls it out of the competitive field — a defensive + offensive move vs Siemens (Xcelerator) and Palantir (which spans industrial + supply chain at a higher altitude). **Palantir Foundry** is the sharpest strategic comp/threat at the data-contextualization layer.
- **Why the landscape is this way (2nd order):** AVEVA already owns **PI System (ex-OSIsoft)** — the de-facto real-time industrial data historian. Cognite's knowledge-graph contextualization is the layer *above* the historian. So the deal stitches "raw time-series capture (PI) → contextualized data model (Cognite) → agentic AI (Atlas)" into one stack. The competitive question shifts from "who has the best AI model" to **"who controls the contextualized industrial data graph"** — because whoever owns that graph owns the moat AI apps must rent.

## [2] Company history / fit
Cognite founded **2016/2017** in Oslo, spun out of / majority-owned by Norwegian industrial conglomerate **Aker ASA** (~50%+ post-2021). Backers: **Accel** (Series A), **TCV** ($150M Series B in **2021** at a **~$1.6B valuation** = unicorn, 9.4% stake), **Aramco Overseas** (7.4%, bought Aker BP's stake in Feb 2022). → **$3.1B exit is ~2x the 2021 mark** over ~5 years, a solid but not explosive return for a late-2021-vintage entry.
- **Why Schneider acts this way:** Schneider is an energy-management + automation hardware/commodity player seeking a **software multiple**. AVEVA (fully re-acquired by Schneider in 2023) is the vehicle. Cognite adds the highest-growth, AI-native segment to AVEVA — classic hardware-incumbent-buys-software-multiple logic. Structural pressure: industrial AI is moving from "describing" to "deciding/acting" (agentic), and Schneider can't let a Palantir/Siemens own the data layer under its own installed base.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Real value-add is the **data-contextualization moat**, and it's more real than typical PR: Cognite has **paying industrial clients and >$170M revenue** (traction, not vaporware), and the knowledge-graph + PI-historian combination is genuinely hard to replicate. Atlas AI (agentic) is newer and less proven — "rapid adoption" is asserted, not quantified with named live agentic deployments (→ **open**).
- **Who captures the margin (2nd order):** the durable value is the **data foundation** (Data Fusion + PI), not the AI models — models commoditize, the contextualized graph does not. Schneider is betting the industrial-AI race is **won at the data layer**, so it's buying the layer that captures margin regardless of which LLM/agent wins. Risk: if open standards or hyperscalers (Azure/AWS industrial data services) commoditize contextualization, the 18x multiple compresses.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Integration into AVEVA CONNECT; regulatory approvals pending; close in "coming quarters." Sentiment **mixed-to-skeptical on price**: Schneider **stock fell up to 2.9%** in Paris on announcement. Bernstein (Leslie): valuation "invites scrutiny" but strategically aligned. JPMorgan (Buller): Schneider deals draw initial price-skepticism but historically hold up strategically. ARC Advisory frames it as reinforcing the "data foundation for industrial AI."
- **Why the market goes this way (2nd order):** the ~18x multiple prices in agentic-AI upside that isn't yet proven at scale → execution/integration risk is the swing factor. Counterintuitive effect: pulling Cognite out of the neutral-vendor field may **cost it non-Schneider industrial customers** (rivals won't standardize on a Schneider-owned platform), so consolidation could shrink Cognite's TAM even as it deepens Schneider's — the classic captive-vs-neutral tradeoff.

## Red-team / challenge questions

1. **~18x revenue for an unprofitable growth-stage firm — justified?** $3.1B / $170M ≈ 18x, vs 5–10x for industrial software. Justified only if agentic AI (Atlas) scales; otherwise pure AI-premium overpay. Profitability undisclosed → **open, lean overpay**.
2. **Only ~2x the 2021 $1.6B mark in ~5 years — is that actually a great exit?** For Aker/TCV a modest return; for Schneider it means it paid up in a hot market. Reframes the deal as Schneider-driven strategic necessity, not a bidding war. **(analysis)**
3. **Is Atlas AI (agentic) live at scale or announced?** PR says "rapid adoption," no named live agentic deployments or agent-count/GMV metrics. **Open — treat as early.**
4. **Does owning Cognite shrink its TAM?** Rivals (Siemens, ABB, Honeywell) won't standardize on a Schneider-owned data platform → captive-vs-neutral tradeoff could cost non-Schneider revenue. **Key downside trigger.**
5. **What does AVEVA's PI System (ex-OSIsoft) already do that Cognite duplicates vs complements?** PI = time-series historian; Cognite = contextualization graph above it. Complementary, but overlap risk in "unified data model" marketing. **Complements (analysis).**
6. **Who is the real competitive threat the deal defends against — Palantir or Siemens?** Palantir Foundry at the contextualization/agentic layer is the sharpest; Siemens Xcelerator on installed-base breadth. Both. **(analysis)**
7. **Regulatory risk?** All-cash, cross-border (Norway/NL/France), industrial data. EU/other approvals pending; concentration in industrial-AI-analytics could draw scrutiny. **Open.**
8. **How much is durable ARR vs project/transactional revenue?** 36% *bookings* growth cited, not net revenue retention or churn. Durability of the $170M base = the multiple's foundation. **Open.**
9. **Does the margin sit in data or models?** Thesis: data foundation captures margin, models commoditize. If hyperscalers commoditize industrial contextualization, 18x compresses. **Core hypothesis.**
10. **Aramco (7.4%) and Aker exiting — signal?** Strategic/state-linked holders cashing at 18x = they see this as a peak price. **(analysis, mild negative signal.)**
11. **Integration risk into AVEVA CONNECT?** Schneider's track record (JPMorgan notes initial skepticism then strategic hold-up) vs actual technical merge of two data models. **Open — execution swing factor.**
12. **Is "agentic AI executing operations" real or aspirational?** The whole thesis rests on AI moving from describe → decide → act in plants. Safety/liability/regulatory barriers to autonomous industrial action are severe. **Largely aspirational near-term.**
13. **Why fold into AVEVA rather than run standalone?** Signals consolidation/cost logic over independent scaling — and that Schneider values the asset for its stack, not as a growth platform. **(analysis.)**
14. **Stock down 2.9% — durable or noise?** Reflects price-skepticism; recovers only if agentic traction materializes. **Sentiment risk, not thesis-breaking.**
15. **Freshness check:** any prior corpus note on Cognite/AVEVA/OSIsoft? **No** — confirmed fresh via sem search + grep.

**Importance: 4/5** — a $3.1B all-cash acquisition of a Leaders-quadrant industrial-AI firm at a headline ~18x multiple, reshaping the industrial DataOps competitive map (Schneider/AVEVA vs Siemens/Palantir) and validating the "AI-won-at-the-data-layer" thesis. Not a 5 because it's fintech-adjacent (industrial, not core financial services), close is pending, and the agentic-AI value driver is still largely announced rather than proven at scale.

**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: industrial AI / DataOps + operational-technology (OT) software for asset-intensive industries (energy, manufacturing, infrastructure). Sizing (third-party market reports — treat as directional, not audited): industrial-AI software est. ~$23.5bn in 2026 → ~$53.0bn by 2031 at ~17.6% CAGR (per Mordor Intelligence, as of 2026); the broader industrial-automation software market ~$43.9bn in 2026 growing ~7.5% CAGR (same source). Enterprise Manufacturing Intelligence — the closest adjacency to Cognite's data-contextualization layer — is the fastest sub-segment at ~22.6% CAGR from ~$5.0bn (2026) (Mordor, 2026). Structure: consolidating — a handful of OT incumbents (Schneider/AVEVA, Emerson/AspenTech, Siemens, Honeywell, Rockwell, ABB) are buying the AI/data layer rather than building it, because the moat is proprietary access to plant/asset data + domain models, not the LLMs themselves. Barriers: deep OT integration, safety/reliability certification, and installed-base lock-in — high switching costs once a data model is embedded in operations. Why now: the note's own PR frames the shift "from analytics to executing operations" (descriptive → agentic/decisioning AI); the arms race among industrial majors to own the "industrial data foundation" is the catalyst (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: ARR / ARR-bookings growth, NRR, and gross-retention (OT software runs on multi-year subscriptions with high stickiness). Cognite disclosed 2025 revenue >$170M and ARR-bookings growth of 36% (per Schneider/Cognite PR); NRR, gross margin, and profitability are [UNSOURCED] — the PR is silent, a de-PR flag. Key players & basis of competition (product/data-access + installed base, not price): Emerson/AspenTech (agentic "aspenONE"/Enterprise AI platform launched May 2026), Siemens (Xcelerator), Honeywell, Rockwell (partnered w/ external AI), Palantir (horizontal, pushing into industrials). Recent moves: Emerson closed its full AspenTech buy-in at $265/sh (~$16.8bn EV, tender expired Mar 2025) and launched an industrial-AI platform on top (May 2026) — the direct strategic template for this deal. Protagonist's position: Cognite is a well-regarded pure-play "industrial DataOps"/contextualization specialist (~800 staff, founded 2017, Oslo) — a niche leader on the data-foundation layer, now catching the incumbents' distribution via AVEVA's CONNECT. Moat (analysis): intangibles (knowledge-graph/data-model) + switching costs once embedded; distribution moat is borrowed from AVEVA post-close, not organic.

**Comps & multiples.** Deal implied multiple — Cognite EV/Revenue = `$3.1bn / ~$0.17bn ≈ 18.2x` (2025 revenue >$170M; exact denominator undisclosed, so treat as ceiling of ~18x). Peers:
- **AspenTech / Emerson (Mar 2025):** EV/Revenue = `$16.8bn / $1.13bn (FY2024) ≈ 14.9x`; growth ~8% (per stockanalysis.com; Emerson/AspenTech PR). Comparable model & geo, larger scale + far slower growth.
- **AVEVA / Schneider (2023):** whole-business value ~$10.8bn (per Businessweekly/MergerSight); revenue not cleanly disclosed here → EV/Rev = no data. Same acquirer, prior template.
- **Emerson (public, diversified industrial):** ~5.2x EV/Rev, ~19.1x EV/EBITDA (per stockanalysis.com) — parent-conglomerate multiple, NOT a software comp; included only to show the software layer trades ~3x the hardware layer.
- **Internal comps (base, weak match — no industrial-AI/DataOps deals):** closest by shape are AI-into-vertical M&A: [[Cube, a London‑based RegTech firm, acquired operational‑risk AI provider Acin]], [[Docupace names new CEO and acquires InvestEdge]] — both undisclosed multiples, different verticals; qualitative only.
Distribution not computed (only 1 clean comparable multiple). **Flag: rich but growth-justified.** Cognite's ~18x vs AspenTech's ~15x is a premium, but Cognite grows ARR-bookings ~36% vs AspenTech ~8% — on a growth-adjusted basis the premium is defensible for a scarce pure-play; it is NOT cheap and leaves little margin for execution slippage. Clean EV/EBITDA / NTM / consensus → [UNSOURCED] (Cognite private, profitability undisclosed).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Valuation/execution.** ~18x revenue on a business whose margins and NRR are undisclosed — the thesis rests entirely on sustained 30%+ ARR growth continuing post-close; any deceleration re-rates the price paid (second-order: goodwill/impairment risk on Schneider's balance sheet).
2. **Integration/disintermediation into AVEVA.** Value depends on stitching Cognite's data layer into AVEVA CONNECT without losing Cognite's open, vendor-neutral appeal; over-tying it to Schneider hardware could push neutral customers toward Palantir/Emerson (margin/logo attrition).
3. **Regulatory close risk + competitive response.** Deal is subject to "customary regulatory approvals" over "coming quarters" (per PR) — not closed; and Emerson/Siemens now have a clear signal to counter-consolidate the remaining independent industrial-AI targets, inflating future prices.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is consolidation, not a new entry: the OT majors are racing to own the industrial-data-foundation layer, and after AspenTech and now Cognite the pool of independent, scaled pure-plays is nearly exhausted — expect the next moves to be either smaller tuck-ins or richer multiples (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if Cognite sustains ~30%+ ARR-bookings growth inside AVEVA through FY2027, ~18x looks cheap in hindsight; the thesis breaks if post-close disclosure shows growth decelerating toward AspenTech's high-single-digits or if neutral-customer churn appears — watch Schneider's Industrial Automation segment ARR commentary at the next results as the trigger.
