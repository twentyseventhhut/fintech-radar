## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**What happened.** On 2026-07-10 the OCC granted Circle *final* approval to establish **First National Digital Currency Bank, N.A.**, operating as **Circle National Trust** — a national trust bank under direct federal OCC oversight. This is the last step of a ~12-month regulatory process, not a fresh filing. (Circle press release; Coindesk; American Banker, all 2026-07-10.)

**Timeline (final ≠ conditional).**
- 2025-06-30 — Circle files its national trust charter application with the OCC.
- 2025-12-12 — OCC issues **conditional** approval to Circle (alongside Ripple, BitGo, Fidelity Digital Assets, Paxos — five firms in one announcement, OCC NR-2025-125).
- 2026-07-10 — OCC grants **final** approval; Circle may now organize and open the bank. This is the milestone in this note.

So this note is the *final* approval — a genuinely distinct, later milestone from the December 2025 conditional nod. It is the point at which Circle can actually stand up the entity, not merely a planned intent.

**What a national trust charter enables for Circle.**
- Federally regulated **digital-asset custody** on a fiduciary basis — initially custody for Circle and its affiliates (i.e., custody of the USDC reserve assets themselves), later potentially a limited set of institutional customers (banks, regulated derivatives firms).
- Direct single federal regulator (OCC) instead of a 50-state money-transmitter patchwork — the same 50-state-relief logic that drives Coinbase, Ripple, Paxos and Nubank charter bids (see [[Nubank receives conditional OCC approval for US bank]], whose charter similarly bundles "digital asset custody").
- A trust charter is deliberately narrow: **no FDIC insurance, no deposit-taking, no lending, no fractional reserve**. Circle explicitly is not becoming a full-service bank. Reserve *management* (as opposed to custody) is framed as a *future* capability, not day-one.

**Regulatory backdrop — GENIUS Act.** The GENIUS Act (signed July 2025) is the enabling context: it mandates 1:1 high-quality-liquid-asset reserves (Treasuries ≤93-day maturity), reserve segregation, and a federal-or-state charter for "permitted payment stablecoin issuers." Issuers above $10B outstanding must sit under the federal regime (or get a waiver). USDC is well over that threshold, so a federal footprint is effectively table-stakes for Circle; the trust bank is how Circle institutionalizes reserve custody under that regime. An OCC-supervised trust bank holding the reserves is a credibility and compliance asset, not just a growth option.

**Competitive landscape (custody / crypto trust charters).**
- **Anchorage Digital** — the incumbent: the *only* digital-asset firm operating under a full OCC national trust charter to date (granted 2021). Circle's approval erodes Anchorage's uniqueness.
- **Coinbase** — received *conditional* OCC trust approval in April 2026 (Coinbase National Trust Co.); faced organized banker pushback (ICBA + Bank Policy Institute) that also targeted Circle, Ripple and Paxos — see [[Coinbase faces banker pushback on trust bank charter bid]]. Coinbase custodies ~$376B institutional AUC.
- **Paxos, Ripple, BitGo, Fidelity Digital Assets** — conditionally approved 2025-12-12 alongside Circle; Bridge and Crypto.com conditional in Feb 2026. So a crowded field of ~11 filers in ~83 days; Circle is the **first to convert conditional → final**, a genuine first-mover signal among the 2025 cohort.

**Why the "what it actually means" framing matters (the analytical mention).** The value is less about a new product and more about (1) removing 50-state MTL friction, (2) putting USDC reserve custody under a federal prudential regulator (de-risking the reserve-transparency narrative that has dogged stablecoins), and (3) positioning ahead of GENIUS Act enforcement. It is a moat-deepening / de-risking move rather than a revenue event.

**Prior Circle context in the DB:** [[Circle warns EU stablecoin rules risk dual licensing by March 2026]] (MiCA-side regulatory pressure), [[Circle launches Arc public testnet]] and [[Circle explores native token for Arc as Q3 profit surges]] (infrastructure/vertical-integration ambitions), [[Coinbase faces banker pushback on trust bank charter bid]] (the pushback that named Circle), [[Nubank receives conditional OCC approval for US bank]] (parallel OCC-charter-with-custody path).

**Skeptical caveats.**
- "Final approval" ≠ open for business: Circle must still organize the entity and satisfy any pre-opening conditions before it operates.
- Custody is initially for Circle/affiliates only; the third-party institutional-custody business is demand-contingent and competes directly with Anchorage/Coinbase/BitGo.
- No FDIC insurance and no lending — narrower than headlines implying "Circle is now a bank."
- Reserve *management* under the charter is future/aspirational, not granted-and-live.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Total stablecoin market cap ~$315bn across 382 tokens (per DefiLlama/StableCoin.com, as of Jun 21, 2026); USDC on-chain ~$74.9bn (~23.8% share) vs USDT ~$186bn (~59%) — a duopoly at issuance, top-5 hold ~88.6%. Structure: concentrated in issuance (reserve float economics favor scale), fragmenting downstream in custody/distribution (exchanges, wallets, banks). Barriers: capital + reserves + regulation. Why now — the GENIUS Act (enacted Jul 18, 2025) created the first US federal payment-stablecoin framework; OCC national trust charters are now the compliance vehicle. Second-order effect: a federal charter converts a regulatory cost into a distribution moat — banks that would not custody with a state-chartered NYDFS entity can transact with an OCC-supervised one.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: (a) stablecoin in circulation (float), (b) reserve return rate × float = reserve income, (c) on-chain transaction volume. Key players in the charter/custody race — Circle, Paxos, Ripple, BitGo, Anchorage. Basis of competition: regulatory standing + distribution reach, not price (USDC/USDT are par instruments). Recent moves: Paxos converted its NYDFS charter to an OCC national trust on 2026-12-12 [[Paxos pursues national trust bank charter from OCC]]; the OCC conditionally approved five national-trust applicants (incl. Circle, Ripple, Paxos, BitGo) in Dec 2025 [[Walmart, crypto firms and fintechs chase US bank charters]]; Anchorage had its OCC consent order lifted (2025-09) [[Anchorage Digital has OCC consent order lifted]] and raised at $4.2bn from Tether (2026-02) [[Tether invests $100M in Anchorage Digital at $4.2B value]]. Circle's position: co-leader in regulated USD stablecoins, and — as a public company (NYSE: CRCL) — the most transparent issuer. Circle applied 2025-06-30, got conditional approval Dec 2025, final approval 2026-07-10 (announced as "First National Digital Currency Bank, N.A.", operating as Circle National Trust). Moat: brand + federal charter + the only listed pure-play issuer (analysis); note the charter initially only custodies for Circle and affiliates, with reserve management and third-party institutional custody as *future* capabilities — announced, not yet live.

**Comps & multiples.** CRCL market cap ~$16.44bn at ~$66.64 (Jul 12, 2026), down sharply from a 52-wk high of $262.97 — a ~75% de-rate from post-IPO peak. Circle IR actuals (Q1'26, filed 2026-05-11, 10-Q crcl-20260331): total revenue + reserve income $694m (+20% YoY); reserve income $653m (+17%, driven by +39% avg USDC float partly offset by −66bps reserve return rate); net income cont. ops $55m (−15% YoY); USDC in circulation $77.0bn; on-chain volume $21.5tn (+263%); Adj EBITDA $151m. FY25: revenue+reserve income $2.7bn (+64%), Adj EBITDA $582m (drive_url: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1876042/000187604226000148/final_05x11q1epr30.htm).
- P/S (annualized Q1'26): $16.44bn / ($694m × 4 = $2.78bn) ≈ **5.9x** — mid-range for a fintech but elevated for a business whose revenue is ~94% reserve income (interest-rate-levered, not recurring SaaS).
- P/E (annualized Q1'26 net income): $16.44bn / ($55m × 4 = $220m) ≈ **75x** — rich; above the ~10–50x sanity range, but net income is compressed by opex, so revenue multiple is the fairer lens.
- Private peers (round valuations, NOT market caps): Anchorage $4.2bn (Feb 2026); Paxos last known ≥$500m raised, no fresh mark [UNSOURCED for current valuation]. Not directly comparable — custodians/infra vs an issuer with reserve float — so distribution not computed; qualitative only.
Flag: CRCL screens *not cheap* on P/S given rate-dependent revenue; the collapse from $263 to $67 already prices in reserve-rate normalization.

**Risk flags.** (1) Rate dependence — 94% of revenue is reserve income; the −66bps reserve-return-rate decline already cut margin despite +39% float growth; further Fed cuts compress revenue independent of USDC adoption. (2) Charter is narrow and prospective — custody launches for Circle/affiliates only; third-party institutional custody and reserve management are "future capabilities," so the revenue impact is a promise, not a P&L line; execution/timing risk. (3) Competitive crowding — Paxos already converted (Dec 2025), Ripple/BitGo/Anchorage all chartered or lifted; a federal charter is becoming table-stakes, not a differentiator, eroding the moat this news implies.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** The charter shifts USDC's reserve/custody plumbing from a NYDFS-state footing to direct OCC oversight, which lowers the bar for regulated banks and derivatives orgs to custody USDC — a distribution unlock, not a revenue event yet (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if the charter is real leverage, watch for named third-party institutional custody clients and any reserve-management insourcing (which would let Circle capture more of the float spread) within 2–3 quarters; absence of both by FY26 year-end would confirm the approval is compliance hygiene, not a growth catalyst.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Circle — latest reported quarter: Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-11).** Verdict vs. consensus: **mixed — EPS beat, revenue slight miss.** Diluted EPS of $0.21 beat the Zacks consensus by ~40%; total revenue of $694M missed the Street by ~2.9%.

**Reported figures (Q1 2026, YoY):**
- Total revenue & reserve income: **$694M, +20% YoY** (vs. prior-year Q1'25).
- Reserve income: **$653M, +17% YoY** — driven by +39% YoY growth in *average* USDC in circulation, partly offset by a 66 bps decline in the reserve return rate (yield fell to ~3.5%).
- Other revenue: **$42M** (+$21M YoY).
- Net income (continuing ops, GAAP): **$55M, −15% YoY** — reserve-income growth was more than offset by higher stock-based comp and investment in product/distribution.
- Adjusted EBITDA: **$151M, +24% YoY.**
- Total distribution, transaction & other costs: **$407M, +17% YoY.**
- GAAP operating expenses: **$242M, +76% YoY**; adjusted opex: **$136M, +32% YoY.**

**USDC / network metrics:**
- USDC in circulation (period-end): **$77.0B, +28% YoY.**
- USDC onchain transaction volume: **$21.5T, +263% YoY**; USDC ~63% of stablecoin transaction volume.
- CPN annualized transaction volume: ~$8.3B (as of 2026-03-31).

**Guidance:** Circle left full-year 2026 guidance **unchanged / affirmed** at Q1; no new quantitative FY26 range was disclosed. Notably, current guidance excludes any impact from the ARC token presale, ARC incentive programs, or future ARC-related revenue — to be addressed on a later call.

**Thesis flags — how the OCC national trust charter affects the reserve-income model:**
- The reserve-income line is the earnings engine (~94% of revenue) and is **rate-sensitive**: the return rate already fell 66 bps YoY to ~3.5%; further SOFR/Fed cuts would compress the dominant revenue component before ARC and CPN scale. Growth in the quarter came from *volume* (+39% avg USDC), not rate.
- The **Circle National Trust** federal charter brings USDC reserve custody and digital-asset custody under direct OCC oversight, in-housing custody/settlement rather than relying solely on third-party bank/custodian partners. This can lower counterparty/operational risk on the reserve pool, deepen the regulatory moat (aligns with GENIUS-Act-style federal stablecoin oversight), and support institutional adoption — reinforcing the volume lever that is now the primary driver of reserve income.
- Watch: net income is declining YoY on rising opex (GAAP opex +76%) even as revenue and adj. EBITDA grow; profitability hinges on new non-reserve revenue (CPN, ARC, other +$21M) scaling as reserve yields normalize lower.
