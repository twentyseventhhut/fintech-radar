Qliro becomes PPRO's exclusive Nordic BNPL partner
Sinziana Albu
01 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Qliro has signed an exclusive agreement with PPRO to become its sole Nordic BNPL provider for merchants and payment service providers.
PPRO connects merchants and PSPs to local payment methods through a single integration, allowing businesses to offer region-specific payment options without building individual connections to each provider. Through the new agreement, PPRO's global merchant network gains access to Qliro's BNPL service, a payment method the companies describe as widely used among Nordic consumers.
BNPL has become a standard component of the checkout mix in several markets, with merchants offering it typically reporting higher conversion rates and larger order values. According to the companies, adoption of BNPL in the Nordic region is among the highest globally, making dedicated regional coverage relevant for merchants seeking to serve this consumer base. One characteristic highlighted by the companies is that Qliro returns consumers to the originating merchant after purchase, rather than routing them through a separate platform.
Addition to PPRO's Buy Now Pay Local portfolio
Furthermore, for PPRO, Qliro is added to its Buy Now Pay Local solution, which provides access to a portfolio of BNPL providers considered locally preferred across different European markets. The addition positions Qliro as the designated Nordic option within that portfolio.
For Qliro, the agreement provides a route to global merchants that sell to Nordic consumers without requiring individual commercial relationships with each one. Qliro has operated in Sweden, Norway, Finland, and Denmark for more than ten years, offering a BNPL and consumer app service in the region.
The companies frame the partnership as connecting existing merchant volume through PPRO's network with Qliro's established Nordic consumer base. Qliro has stated an ambition to become the market leader in the Nordic region within three to five years, and the PPRO agreement is positioned as part of that broader strategy. Financial terms of the agreement were not disclosed.

# Context-enrichment: Qliro becomes PPRO's exclusive Nordic BNPL partner
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 30 Jun–01 Jul 2026 Qliro AB (Swedish listed BNPL + consumer-app lender) signed an **exclusive** agreement to become PPRO's **sole Nordic BNPL provider** for the merchants and PSPs that plug into PPRO's single-integration local-payments platform. Concretely: Qliro is slotted into PPRO's "Buy Now Pay Local" portfolio as the *designated Nordic option* across SE/NO/FI/DK. **Financial terms undisclosed; no committed volume, no go-live merchant named, no revenue guidance tied to it.**

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** This is a *distribution* deal, not a product or capital event. PPRO's value prop is "one integration → many local methods"; it curates ONE locally-preferred BNPL per market to avoid channel conflict and simplify the merchant checkout. So "exclusive" here is PPRO's standard portfolio design (one Nordic slot), not Qliro out-muscling rivals in a contested auction — the exclusivity is *within PPRO's rail*, not exclusivity over Nordic merchants generally. The de-PR'd read: Qliro rents PPRO's global merchant funnel; PPRO fills a gap (it had SE/NO/FI/DK uncovered) in a portfolio it launched only 8 months ago. The "Qliro returns the consumer to the originating merchant" line is a jab at the redirect-heavy checkout UX of larger BNPLs — real but marginal.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Direct template: PPRO + Scalapay (03 Feb 2026)** — same structure, Southern Europe. See [[Scalapay connects with PPRO to expand BNPL in Europe]]. Qliro is the Nordic analogue of that deal.
- **PPRO's portfolio build-out:** [[PPRO launches Buy Now Pay Local BNPL solution in Europe]] (Nov 2025, the parent product) and [[PPRO adds BLIK Pay Later for BNPL in Poland]] (Dec 2025). This is deal #3+ in a systematic one-per-market curation.
- **The elephant — Klarna:** Nordic BNPL is Klarna's home turf; it holds the strongest Swedish share via deep retailer penetration and is aggressively expanding *distribution* itself — [[Klarna to become available through Swedbank Pay in the Nordics]] (Apr 2026), [[Klarna becomes payment option in Vipps and MobilePay apps]] (Nov 2025), [[Klarna integrates Vipps MobilePay across Nordics]] (Jun 2025). Klarna also does the PSP/aggregator dance ([[PayU expands Klarna partnership in Romania]]).
- **Walley** rounds out the Nordic instalment trio (home-improvement/lifestyle merchants).

**Why the landscape is this way / second-order:** Both Klarna and Qliro are now racing to embed via *aggregators and wallets* rather than direct merchant integration — the growth lever has shifted from "sign the merchant" to "sit inside the rail the merchant already uses". PPRO gives Qliro reach it can't win one-by-one; but Klarna is doing the same at larger scale (Swedbank Pay, Vipps/MobilePay reach far more Nordic checkouts than PPRO's cross-border merchant base). So Qliro's exclusivity inside PPRO is a *niche* win — global merchants selling *into* the Nordics — not a challenge to Klarna's domestic base.

## [2] Company history / fit
Qliro has run BNPL + a consumer app across SE/NO/FI/DK for 10+ years; merchants include CDON, Nelly. It has been modernising its stack — [[Qliro taps Vilja to power next-gen payments expansion]] (core-banking migration, Sep 2025) — and stated Q1 2026 metrics (per web): TPV +38%, new merchants ~+150%, returned to profitability. Stated ambition: **Nordic market leader in 3–5 years**.

**Why the company acts this way:** As a sub-scale challenger against Klarna, Qliro can't out-spend on domestic retailer BD, so it (a) rebuilds the platform (Vilja) to lower unit cost and (b) rents third-party distribution (PPRO) to add merchant volume cheaply. The PPRO tie-up is the *cheapest incremental TPV* available and fits the "leader in 3–5 yrs" narrative it needs for its listed-equity story.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Low novelty.** The mechanism (curated local BNPL inside PPRO) is 8 months old and already executed with Scalapay/BLIK. What's *new* is only that the Nordic slot is now filled by Qliro. **Traction: zero disclosed** — no merchants live, no volume commitment, no economics. Qliro's cited growth (+38% TPV) is company-wide and predates/independent of this deal.

**Why the value-add is thin, one level deeper:** In this stack PPRO captures the merchant relationship and the integration margin; Qliro captures the credit/BNPL take-rate on the incremental volume that flows through. The real question is *how much volume PPRO's Nordic-facing cross-border merchants actually generate* — likely a small tail vs Qliro's domestic book. Exclusivity protects Qliro's slot but caps upside to whatever PPRO's funnel delivers. No disintermediation, no new capability — a channel add.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Watch for a named merchant go-live and any volume disclosure in Qliro's next interim report (Q2/Q3 2026); absent that, treat as PR. Sweden BNPL is projected to ~$8.7bn by 2031 (Yahoo/GlobalData) with Klarna leading and banks/PSPs (Adyen, Stripe) embedding instalments — i.e. margins compress as BNPL becomes a checkout commodity.

**Why the market goes this way / second-order:** As BNPL commoditizes into a default checkout tile, the winners are whoever owns *distribution and consumer default*, not the lender. Klarna's wallet/PSP embedding (Vipps, Swedbank Pay) is a stronger version of exactly Qliro's PPRO move. Counterintuitive risk: chasing "market leader" via rented rails can *lower* Qliro's blended margin (aggregator revenue-share + thinner cross-border volume) even as TPV headline grows — volume vanity vs unit-economics reality.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** BNPL sits inside the local-payment-method (LPM) aggregation layer of European payments. Market size: Europe BNPL ~$191.3bn in 2025, growing +12.4% (per ResearchAndMarkets, via BusinessWire, 2025-02-28); Sweden BNPL alone ~$6.13bn in 2026, +9.3% YoY, and forecast +7.3% CAGR 2026–31 down from a 14.9% CAGR in 2022–25 (per market reports, via GlobeNewswire 2026-02-03 / Statista) — i.e. the Nordic pool is large, mature and now decelerating. Structure: two distinct layers here — (1) BNPL *lenders* (Klarna, Qliro), which is regionally fragmented behind one dominant player, and (2) LPM *aggregators* (PPRO), a consolidated middleware layer that resells everyone's rails through one integration. Barriers differ: BNPL needs balance-sheet/credit licensing and consumer brand; aggregation needs breadth of connections and merchant/PSP distribution (network effects). Why now: BNPL is a "standard component of the checkout mix" with higher conversion/AOV (note), and Nordic adoption is among the highest globally — so a global aggregator adding a *dedicated* Nordic BNPL closes a real coverage gap rather than chasing greenfield growth.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: for BNPL — TPV/GMV, take rate, credit-loss ratio, conversion uplift; for aggregators — number of LPMs/markets, merchant count, TPV routed. Key players & basis of competition: Klarna is the reference point — ~35% global BNPL share and the most-downloaded BNPL app in both Sweden and Norway in 2025 (per ECDB/Statista), competing on brand + retailer penetration; Qliro is the domestic challenger (Sweden/Norway/Finland/Denmark, >10yrs); Vipps MobilePay and Swedbank Pay are increasingly *distributing* Klarna in the Nordics (see [[Klarna to become available through Swedbank Pay in the Nordics]], [[Klarna becomes payment option in Vipps and MobilePay apps]]) — meaning Klarna is winning the wallet/PSP shelf-space war. On the aggregator side PPRO competes on breadth (85+ markets, 2m merchants — PPRO ESG 2024, drive_url below) vs peers like dLocal/EBANX (emerging-markets tilt). Protagonist positioning: **Qliro is catching up, not ahead** — it explicitly targets Nordic market leadership "within three to five years" (note), which concedes it is *not* leader today. The PPRO deal is a distribution moat play: exclusivity as PPRO's sole Nordic BNPL gives Qliro switching-cost lock-in on inbound cross-border merchant volume without per-merchant sales (analysis). Qliro's own moat is thin (no network effect vs Klarna's brand); the moat it is renting is PPRO's.

**Comps & multiples.**
- **Qliro AB (STO:QLIRO)** — public, so a real market multiple exists. Market cap ~SEK 527–665m (range across sources, stockanalysis.com, mid-2026); net revenue 2025 SEK 304.9m (+4.67%) / LTM ~SEK 287m. P/S = 527–665m / ~295m ≈ **1.8x–2.3x**. In-line/cheap for a returning-to-profit fintech; the low multiple reflects low-single-digit reported revenue growth despite +38% TPV / +41% BNPL growth in Q1 2026 — the market is pricing execution risk and credit losses, not hyper-growth. Q1 2026: net rev +19%, operating profit SEK 0.2m (from −15.1m), first quarterly profit (Inderes / MFN, 2026).
- **PPRO** — private; last disclosed valuation **>$1bn** (unicorn, from 2021 $180m round, ppro.com), plus a €85m/2024 tranche (PayPal Ventures, Eurazeo, J.P. Morgan, Citi Ventures, BlackRock — PPRO/PayPal 2024-03-13, drive_url below). No TPV/revenue disclosed publicly → **EV/Revenue = no data**; treat >$1bn as a round valuation, not a market cap.
- Klarna: post-IPO reference peer but not a clean comp (bank licence, US exposure, ~35% share) — qualitative only.
- Distribution not computed (only one clean public multiple, Qliro); qualitative comparison used.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Klarna disintermediation / share concentration.** Klarna already dominates Nordic BNPL and is being embedded *inside* the wallets and PSP rails (Vipps MobilePay, Swedbank Pay) that Qliro also needs — so Qliro wins PPRO's cross-border merchants while potentially losing the domestic wallet shelf. The exclusivity is defensive.
2. **Dependence on someone else's rails + unknown economics.** Both companies are silent on financial terms, fraud liability and take-rate split (note: "terms not disclosed"). Qliro's incremental volume rides entirely on PPRO's merchant network — a channel it does not control, and PPRO can (by design) swap partners in other regions.
3. **Decelerating, mature market + credit risk.** Nordic/Swedish BNPL growth is slowing (CAGR stepping down to ~7%), and Qliro's reported revenue growth (+4.67% FY25) lags its TPV growth — margin/credit-loss pressure is the constraint, and a leadership push into a slowing market is capital-intensive.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a *distribution* deal, not a re-rating catalyst — it widens Qliro's funnel to global merchants selling into the Nordics without changing the competitive reality that Klarna owns the consumer. Falsifiable thesis (analysis): if the PPRO channel is material, watch Qliro's TPV/merchant-count adds and cross-border mix in the next 2–3 interim reports; the thesis is wrong if TPV growth stays high but *reported net revenue* stays low-single-digit — that would show the PPRO volume is low-take-rate pass-through, not accretive. Trigger to watch: any disclosure of the take-rate split, or Klarna signing a competing aggregator.
