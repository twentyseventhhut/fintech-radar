Nopan and DIAS partner to expand IRIS Commerce internationally
Sinziana Albu
02 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Nopan has partnered with DIAS to extend IRIS Commerce, Greece's account payment method, to international payment service providers and merchants.
IRIS Commerce allows consumers to pay directly from their bank accounts through their banking applications rather than using cards. According to the official press release, adoption of the payment method has increased since its introduction, with ecommerce transaction volumes rising by more than 70% in 2025 alone and increasing 16-fold since 2020. IRIS Commerce is currently accepted by all e-shops in Greece and is available to the majority of web and mobile banking users in the country.
Details of the partnership
Under the agreement, Nopan will integrate IRIS Commerce into its platform, allowing payment service providers and merchants based outside Greece to offer the payment method to their customers. Nopan positions the move within its broader activity of enabling account and wallet payment methods for providers and merchants across Europe, adding IRIS Commerce to methods it already supports in other markets. For DIAS, the partnership is intended to extend the reach of IRIS Commerce beyond Greece as adoption of the payment method continues to grow domestically.
 Konstantin Surkov , co-founder and CEO of Nopan, said consumers increasingly expect to pay using local payment methods they already know, and that the company's role is to help payment service providers and merchants bring those methods to market. Furthermore, Stavroula Kampouridou , CEO of DIAS, said the partnership would allow more businesses to offer Greek consumers a payment experience described as fast, secure, and familiar, supporting the continued growth of digital commerce in Greece.
Market context
The partnership reflects a broader trend of payment service providers and merchants seeking to support A2A payment methods that are already established with local consumers, alongside card-based options. IRIS Commerce's growth in Greece, spanning both ecommerce acceptance and banking app availability, is presented by the companies as an example of this shift toward domestic account payment schemes gaining scale.
The agreement adds IRIS Commerce to Nopan's portfolio of European account and wallet payment methods, which the company has stated it intends to continue expanding across the region.

# Context-enrichment: Nopan and DIAS partner to expand IRIS Commerce internationally
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS / DUPLICATE VERDICT: FRESH** — but low-weight. This is a *distinct* event from the sibling pick "IRIS Payments connects Greece to European EuroPA network" and from the corpus note [[Greece joins EuroPA through IRIS Payments]]. Those are **scheme-level P2P interlinking** (Greek users pay/send cross-border via their own bank apps, alliance of Bizum/Bancomat/MB WAY). This Nopan–DIAS deal is a **commercial distribution integration**: Nopan puts IRIS Commerce (ecommerce checkout / merchant acceptance) into its PSP-facing platform so **non-Greek merchants can accept IRIS**. Opposite direction, different use case (merchant checkout vs P2P transfers), no cross-mention in either press release. Not a duplicate. (Announced 02 Jul 2026, The Paypers.)

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Nopan and DIAS (Greece's interbank clearing operator, which runs IRIS) signed a partnership under which Nopan will integrate **IRIS Commerce** — Greece's account-to-account (A2A) bank-app payment method for online checkout — into Nopan's platform, so PSPs and merchants **based outside Greece** can offer IRIS to (chiefly Greek) shoppers. It adds IRIS to Nopan's existing catalogue of European account/wallet local payment methods (LPMs).
- **Announced, NOT live.** The text gives quotes and domestic stats but **no launch date, no live foreign merchants, no processed-volume figures** for the Nopan-IRIS integration itself. "Will integrate / allowing" is intent language.
- **The headline growth numbers are IRIS's DOMESTIC adoption, not anything Nopan delivered:** ecommerce volumes +70% in 2025, 16x since 2020, "accepted by all Greek e-shops." **Why this framing matters:** the PR borrows IRIS's genuine domestic scale to dress up a small, unproven cross-border integration. The impressive stats and the deal are decoupled.
- **Number check:** IRIS's overall 2025 growth is independently corroborated (~122M instant txns, €10.9bn, ~72.8% YoY, ~4.2M users, ~70k e-shops; mandatory for Greek businesses since 1 Dec 2025 — see [[IRIS Payments becomes mandatory for Greek businesses]]). The *ecommerce-specific* +70% is plausible (tracks the ~73% overall) but DIAS-sourced; **"16x since 2020" is unverified** by any third party.

## [1] Competitors / peers
A2A / local-payment-method enablement in Europe is **crowded and consolidating**:
- **Rails/schemes** (the actual methods): EuroPA, EPI **Wero**, Bizum, MB WAY, Bancomat, Blik, IRIS.
- **A2A / open-banking aggregators & enablers**: Volt (80+ markets, one API), Trustly (Pay-by-Bank leader), Brite, Token.io, Vyne, Yapily/Tink, Kevin.eu, Banked, Noda — **Noda's UK arm (NaudaPay) is winding down (late 2025)**, a marker the segment is shaking out.
- **Nopan's position:** it does not claim to be another rail; it positions as the **merchant-side performance/enablement layer on top of** schemes (Wero, IRIS…) — tokenization, MIT/recurring on A2A, conversion optimization. Closest overlap is Volt/Brite (aggregators). By any available traction measure Nopan is a **small challenger**, catching up, not a leader.
- **Why the landscape is this way (analysis):** the EU Instant Payments Regulation makes SEPA Instant free/ubiquitous → A2A becomes cheap → the value migrates from "moving the money" (commoditized rail) to "getting the local method accepted and converting at checkout" (the enablement layer). That is precisely the slice Nopan is chasing — but it is also where Volt/Trustly already sit, so distribution reach, not concept, decides the winner.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Nopan:** Paris-based, licensed **Payment Institution authorized by DNB** (passportable EU-wide); founded by ex-Netflix/Visa payments people, CEO **Konstantin Surkov**. Founding year, funding, valuation and headcount are **not public / unverified** — treat as **early-stage but operational**.
- **2026 momentum is a string of partnership announcements, not disclosed volumes:** EPI/Wero Principal Member (17 Jun 2026), Xsolla wallet payments, now DIAS/IRIS. **Why it acts this way (analysis):** a young enabler with no rail of its own must accumulate scheme partnerships to build a credible "one integration, many LPMs" catalogue — logo-collection is the growth mechanic, but each logo is only worth its live volume, which here is undisclosed.
- **Fit for DIAS:** logical. DIAS has maxed domestic penetration (IRIS is now mandatory), so the only growth vector is **outbound reach** — letting Greek diaspora / travelling Greeks pay non-Greek merchants with IRIS. Nopan gives it distribution without DIAS building its own international sales channel.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new:** IRIS Commerce becoming addressable by non-Greek PSPs. Prior IRIS news was domestic (limits, mandate) or scheme-level P2P (EuroPA). Merchant-acceptance-outside-Greece is a new vector.
- **But value-add is unproven:** zero live merchants, zero volume disclosed. The addressable demand is narrow — foreign merchants only care about accepting IRIS to serve **Greek** customers (a ~4M-user, single-country base). This is a **long-tail LPM add**, not a mass-market European rail.
- **Who captures the margin (analysis):** IRIS is chargeback-free and cheaper than cards, so merchants save on interchange; DIAS earns scheme/clearing economics; Nopan earns an integration/orchestration fee. With no interchange to skim and thin A2A economics, Nopan's margin depends on volume it has not yet shown. The multiple such a business earns rests on becoming the default LPM catalogue for enterprise merchants — commoditizable if Volt/Trustly/Wero-native routes cover the same methods.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** watch for an actual go-live date and named foreign merchants; absent those, this stays an announcement.
- **Regulatory backdrop:** EU **Instant Payments Regulation** — euro-area PSPs must **receive** SEPA Instant since **9 Jan 2025** and **send** + Verification of Payee since **9 Oct 2025** (note: not the "Oct 2026" sometimes cited) — plus mandatory IRIS acceptance in Greece, is the structural tailwind making A2A ubiquitous. Broader sovereignty push (EuroPA, Wero, EPI) is the macro theme — see [[EuroPA and EPI prepare to expand cross-border payments in Europe]] and [[payabl. joins European Payments Initiative to expand Wero]].
- **Sentiment / second-order (analysis):** the real momentum in Greek/European A2A is the **scheme interlinking** (IRIS↔EuroPA, Wero) — a sovereignty play backed by regulation and central banks. A private enabler bolting a single-country LPM onto its catalogue is a footnote to that story unless it proves enterprise-merchant volume. **The central question is not "is IRIS growing" (it clearly is, domestically) but "does anyone outside Greece actually need to accept IRIS, and can Nopan win that thin demand against incumbents" — and on that, the release is silent.**

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** European account-to-account (A2A) / instant payments, subvertical: local A2A payment-method enablement for PSPs & merchants. Sizing: A2A is projected to rise from ~24% to nearly 40% of European ecommerce by 2035 (per Celent, via Worldline "6 trends to watch in 2026"); Europe payments digital-wallet/A2A rails cited compounding ~17.7% CAGR (per Mordor Intelligence, as of 2026). Domestic proof-point behind this deal: DIAS reported 122.1m instant-payment transactions in Greece in 2025, +72.8% YoY, worth €10.9bn (+70% YoY); IRIS Commerce ecommerce volumes +70.8% YoY and 16x vs 2020, across 70,000 e-shops and 1.2m POS (per athens-times / protothema, as of Jun 2026 — consistent with the note's ">70% in 2025 / 16-fold since 2020"). Structure: fragmented — a patchwork of national A2A schemes (IRIS in Greece, BLIK Poland, Bizum Spain, Satispay Italy, Wero DE/FR/BE), now consolidating cross-border under EuroPA (as of 30 Jun 2026, Phase One links ~57.3m citizens across 5 countries; targeting 173.1m across 18 countries by year-end, per tovima). Value in the chain shifts from card interchange toward bank-rail A2A; entry barriers are regulatory (PSP licensing, SCA) and network effects (bank-app reach). Drivers / "why now": (1) SEPA Instant Payments Regulation mandatory since 2025 + PSD3/PSR trilogue deal (Nov 2025, adoption ~mid-2026) widening A2A use cases (per Norton Rose Fulbright); (2) "payment sovereignty" — Europe reducing dependence on transatlantic card rails (Visa/Mastercard), a theme the note's own source page headlines. Second-order effect: every domestic scheme now needs an international on-ramp to be usable by non-domestic merchants — exactly the gap Nopan fills.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: for A2A enablers/orchestrators — authorization/conversion rate, number of methods & markets covered, per-transaction cost vs cards, merchant count. Key players & basis of competition: (a) A2A infrastructure / pay-by-bank aggregators — Trustly, Volt (80+ markets, single API), Token.io (open-banking rails, backers incl. HSBC, Mastercard); (b) card-acquirer incumbents adding A2A (Worldline, Nexi — Nexi's XPay already lists IRIS in Greece); (c) scheme operators themselves (DIAS/IRIS, EPI/Wero). Competition is on breadth of local methods × conversion, not price alone. Recent moves: Nopan became a Principal Member of EPI to accelerate Wero enterprise-merchant acceptance (2025–26); Xsolla–Nopan wallet-payments partnership (Europe); ACI Worldwide powering Rabobank's Wero (Jun 2026); EuroPA–EPI sovereign-network collaboration (2025). Protagonist's position: niche but well-placed — Nopan (founded by ex-Netflix payment leaders, CEO Konstantin Surkov) is a focused A2A/wallet-only PSP-orchestration layer that already supports BLIK, Bizum, Satispay, Wero; adding IRIS Commerce extends coverage. Moat `(analysis)`: switching costs / integration depth once embedded in a merchant's checkout, plus a growing method-catalog network effect — modest vs Trustly/Volt scale, so "catching up / specialist," not incumbent.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation/round/metrics are disclosed for Nopan or DIAS in this deal, and Nopan is private with no public round → multiples "no data" / `[UNSOURCED]`. Peer capital-raised anchors (private, mark as funding raised, NOT market cap): Volt — ~$86m+ raised to date (Series B $60m led by IVP, Jun 2023); Token.io — Series C $40m (2022), plus strategic investment from HSBC. These size the funded-competitor set but do not yield a comparable multiple for Nopan → distribution not computed, qualitative comparison only. Internal comps (corpus): [[Greece joins EuroPA through IRIS Payments]], [[EuroPA and EPI partner for pan-European payments sovereignty]], [[Pan-European payment providers sign sovereign network MoU]], [[BoursoBank integrates Wero into its payment solutions]], [[ACI Worldwide powers Rabobank's Wero instant payments]]. In-line/rich flag: not computable (no Nopan financials).

**Risk flags.**
- Announced vs live: the release describes integration/expansion intent; actual international IRIS Commerce acceptance, live merchant volumes, fees and fraud-liability terms are undisclosed — de-PR gap (economics + ticket size are silent). Why: reach outside Greece depends on non-Greek consumers holding Greek bank apps, so cross-border utility may lag the domestic growth story unless routed via EuroPA interoperability.
- Disintermediation / rails dependence: Nopan is a thin enabling layer sitting on someone else's rails (DIAS/IRIS, EPI/Wero, bank APIs). If schemes build direct international acceptance or EuroPA + acquirers (Nexi already lists IRIS) offer it natively, margin can be captured up/down-stack. Why: enabler economics compress when the underlying network commoditizes access.
- Concentration / execution: as a small specialist competing against far larger-funded Trustly/Volt/Token.io and card incumbents adding A2A, Nopan's growth hinges on continued single-method deal wins; slower merchant adoption of any one local method drags the whole catalog thesis. Why: no disclosed scale buffer if a marquee method underperforms.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a small-but-signal data point in the A2A-sovereignty land-grab: national schemes are racing to become internationally reachable, and specialist enablers (Nopan) are the connective tissue before EuroPA/PSD3 fully commoditize cross-border A2A. Falsifiable thesis: Nopan's "many-local-methods" model out-converts generic pay-by-bank in home markets and it consolidates into a top European A2A-orchestration name. Trigger/what to watch: live IRIS Commerce cross-border volumes, additional Principal-Member/scheme wins, and whether EuroPA interoperability makes per-method integrators redundant — that last one would break the thesis.
