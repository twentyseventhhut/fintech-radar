## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
### [0] What happened
Revolut has surpassed **120 branded ATMs in Spain**, ~one year after launching its first machines there (launch announced June 2025). The terminals bring Revolut's "100% digital" experience into the physical world: fee-free cash withdrawals for its customers, the same competitive in-app FX rates, plus physical-card dispensing (source: Connecting the Dots in Fintech, 2026-07-15).

### [1] Why it matters
- **Digital bank going physical, on purpose.** Spain is Revolut's *first* market for its own-brand ATM network — a deliberate "phygital" bet. Spain remains one of Europe's most cash-reliant economies (cash ~60% of point-of-sale payments), so owning cash-out infrastructure removes a friction that kept some users tied to incumbent banks.
- **Cheap distribution + brand.** ATMs double as billboards and card-issuance points in high-footfall locations, lowering CAC in a market where Revolut already has ~5M customers and is targeting 10M ([[Revolut targets 10 million customers in Spain by next year]]).
- **On-plan execution.** June 2025 plan: 50 ATMs in Madrid/Barcelona first, then 150 more across Valencia, Málaga etc., up to **200 nationwide** at a cadence of 3–5 machines/week. 120 at the one-year mark is roughly mid-trajectory toward that 200 target.

### [2] Internal grounding (prior notes)
- [[Revolut integrates Catalan across ATMs, web, and app]] (2026-04) — same ATM network; "up to 200 machines nationwide" plan; Barcelona as Southern-Europe tech hub. Closest prior ATM reference, but stated a *plan*, not a count reached.
- [[Revolut to open first physical store in Barcelona]] (2026-05 / 2026-04) — parallel physical-retail push; ATMs + a permanent store = coordinated phygital strategy.
- [[Revolut targets 10 million customers in Spain by next year]] (2026-05) and [[Revolut grows Spain deposits 16.3% to EUR 4.64 billion]] (2026-06) — Spain is a flagship, fast-scaling market (deposits EUR 4.64B); ATMs support deposit/relationship depth.
- [[Neobanks claim 27% of Spain's retail market as Revolut surges]] (2025-10) — competitive backdrop: neobank share gains that physical presence is meant to defend/extend.

### [3] External timeline
- **Jun 2025** — Revolut launches its first ATMs in Spain (Madrid + Barcelona), first in the world; sleek 32" touchscreen, unlimited free withdrawals, physical card dispensing; plan for up to 200 by 2026 at 3–5/week (Revolut newsroom; Disruption Banking; The Fintech Times; Finance Magnates; Catalan News).
- **2026** — Spain remains the pilot; expansion to Portugal, Italy, Germany flagged for 2026+.
- **Jul 15, 2026** — 120-ATM one-year milestone announced (Connecting the Dots in Fintech).
- **Regulatory frame:** Revolut operates in Spain under its EEA banking licence (Bank of Lithuania / ECB, credit institution; also under Bank of Spain oversight). ATMs do **not** require a separate Spanish banking licence — they run off the existing EEA passport. (Separate ongoing licence pushes — UK full licence secured 2026-03, French licence in progress — are unrelated to the ATM rollout.)

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
### Sector context — neobank omnichannel & Spanish cash access

Revolut's ATM push inverts the classic neobank playbook: instead of pure app-only, it grafts a branded physical layer onto a 100%-digital core. The Spanish ATM product (launched ~June 2025) offers fee-free cash withdrawal, competitive in-app FX rates, and — distinctively — physical card dispensing directly from the terminal, cutting the card-delivery friction that slows onboarding. Original plan: 50 machines in Madrid/Barcelona scaling toward ~200 across Valencia, Málaga and other capitals by 2026, with Spain as the pilot before Germany, Italy and Portugal (source: [Revolut news](https://www.revolut.com/news/revolut_launches_its_first_atms_in_spain_sleek_design_unlimited_free_withdrawals_for_customers_and_physical_cards_dispensing/); [Finance Magnates](https://www.financemagnates.com/fintech/revolut-to-install-first-atms-in-spain-plans-to-reach-200-by-2026/)). Crossing 120 ATMs after one year is on-trajectory but modestly behind the aspirational 200.

The strategic logic ties to the wider Revolut "become the primary bank" thesis, visible in-base: it just rebranded to "Revolut Bank" in Ireland to capture salary deposits ([[Revolut rebrands as Revolut Bank in Ireland]]) and is preparing a 2027 US bank launch ([[Revolut plans to launch US bank in 2027]]). Physical cash access is a credibility/primacy lever, not a revenue center.

Spain is a rational first market. Structurally, Spain has hollowed out physical banking: branches fell ~58% (45,707→19,015) and ATMs ~23% (61,714→47,639) between 2008–2021, leaving ~560k people in ~2,930 municipalities without local banking access (Banco de España / CECA 2025 monitoring). Incumbents' retreat creates white space where a fee-free, sleekly-branded Revolut ATM doubles as physical-world advertising and a differentiator versus app-only rivals (N26, Bunq) that offer no proprietary cash rail.

### In-base comps

- [[Revolut rebrands as Revolut Bank in Ireland]] (2026-07-15) — same "primary-bank/deposit-capture" strategy; ATMs and rebrand are two facets of the same push for legitimacy/primacy.
- [[Revolut plans to launch US bank in 2027]] (2026-07-14) — global banking-charter ambition; physical presence is part of the same maturation arc.
- [[Revolut strengthens AML and fraud controls in Lithuania]] (2026-07-10) — flags rising regulatory scrutiny of Revolut's EU banking entity; relevant to physical cash handling (KYC/AML at ATM).

### IR grounding (PRIMARY — Revolut FY2025 Annual Report, filed 2026-04-29)

Consolidated Annual Report 2025 ([drive/url](https://assets.revolut.com/pdf/annualreport2025.pdf), Revolut Group Holdings Ltd, filed 2026-04-29):
- Revenue up ~46% to ~£4.5bn (~€5.2bn / ~$6bn press framing); profit before tax up ~57% to ~£1.7bn (~38% PBT margin); record net profit ~$2.3bn.
- Retail customers +30% to ~68.3m (75m+ by mid-2026); business customers +33% to ~767k; internal target ~100m customers by mid-2027.
- Spain called out as a top market: the FY25 report states Revolut "redefined the ATM in Spain" (free cash withdrawal / card collection) and that ~38% of newly opened bank accounts in Spain are Revolut accounts; roughly 1-in-3 new accounts in Spain/France/Italy are Revolut (source: [Revolut Annual Report 2025](https://www.revolut.com/annual-report-2025/); [Revolut FY25 press](https://www.revolut.com/en-US/news/revolut_reports_record_profit_of_2_3bn_for_2025_as_revenue_surges_to_6bn/)).

IR read: the ATM expansion is corroborated by Revolut's own filed narrative and sits on a highly profitable, fast-growing base — this is discretionary land-grab spend, not a margin threat. Note the milestone is a press/marketing announcement, not an audited disclosure; the "120" figure and its ROI are unverified beyond company communication.

### Risk flags

1. **Execution vs. target / marketing-milestone risk.** 120 ATMs after one year trails the ~200 aspiration; the figure is a self-reported PR count with no unit economics, utilization, or cost disclosed. Treat as a vanity metric until reflected in filings.
2. **Structurally negative unit economics of owned ATMs.** ATMs carry high fixed cost (hardware, cash-in-transit, real estate, maintenance) for a product Revolut gives away fee-free. Justifiable only as customer-acquisition/primacy spend, not as a standalone P&L line — dilutive if scaled aggressively across Germany/Italy/Portugal.
3. **Regulatory/AML exposure on physical cash.** Cash withdrawal and card dispensing raise KYC/AML and cash-handling obligations; the concurrent [[Revolut strengthens AML and fraud controls in Lithuania]] item signals heightened EU scrutiny of Revolut's banking entity — physical cash rails add attack surface (money-mule, cash-out fraud).
4. **Competitive moat is shallow / easily contested.** The ATM network is a marketing differentiator, not a defensible moat; Spanish incumbents (via CaixaBank/Santander shared networks, Correos Cash, mobile branches) already run far denser cash coverage, and app-only rivals can partner with third-party ATM networks (e.g. Allpoint/Euronet) at lower capex to neutralize the "free cash" claim.
5. **Cash secular decline undercuts the thesis.** Building physical cash infrastructure in a market where cash usage is structurally falling risks stranded capex; the durable payoff is brand visibility, not withdrawal volume.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Earnings backdrop — Revolut FY2025 results (own filing, primary).** The 120-ATM Spain milestone lands against a company reporting record results. Per Revolut Group Holdings Ltd's Annual Report 2025 (consolidated FS for year ended 31 Dec 2025; published 29 Apr 2026, [filing](https://assets.revolut.com/pdf/annualreport2025.pdf)):

- **Revenue:** $6.0bn (£4.5bn), +46% YoY vs $4.0bn (£3.1bn) in 2024.
- **Profit before tax:** $2.3bn (£1.7bn), +57% YoY; PBT margin 38% (vs 35% in 2024).
- **Net profit:** $1.7bn (£1.3bn), up from $1.0bn (£790m) in 2024 — a record.
- **Retail customers:** 68.3m at year-end, +30% YoY (16m new retail customers added globally). Business customers 767K, +33%.
- **Customer balances:** $67.5bn (£50.2bn), +66% YoY. Lending portfolio +120% to $2.9bn (£2.2bn).
- **Diversification:** 11 product lines each exceeded ~$135m (£100m) in revenue.

**Spain / Western Europe context (reported).** Spain is cited in the annual report as one of Revolut's strongest Western European markets — Revolut reports that 38% of newly opened accounts in Spain are Revolut accounts, and lists France/Italy/Spain among its fastest-growing markets where it is increasingly used as a primary account rather than a travel card. The ATM program itself is called out in the filing: Revolut "redefined the ATM" in 2025, letting Spanish customers withdraw cash or collect a card for free, "with more countries coming soon." Regional investment underpins the physical push — new Madrid/Barcelona offices and plans to hire up to 800 employees in Spain by 2028, plus a Western Europe HQ in Paris.

**Guidance / ambitions.** Reiterated target of 100m customers by mid-2027 (reported "1 million new customers every 17 days"; 75m+ retail as of report date). U.S. national bank charter filed March 2026; licensed bank in 30+ of 40 markets.

**Thesis flags.** The 120-ATM Spain expansion is a small, capital-light physical-layer add-on to a fast-scaling, now solidly profitable neobank — it operationalizes the "digital-first, physical where useful" positioning the filing highlights and reinforces Spain as a lead Western Europe market. It is a strategic/brand signal (free cash withdrawals, app-grade FX at terminals), not a financially material line item; no ATM-specific revenue is disclosed. Watch for rollout to additional countries ("more coming soon") as the tell on whether physical terminals become a repeatable expansion motion.

*Sources: Revolut Annual Report 2025 (own filing, PBT/revenue/customers); Revolut FY2025 results release ([revolut.com](https://www.revolut.com/en-US/news/revolut_reports_record_profit_of_2_3bn_for_2025_as_revenue_surges_to_6bn/)); Annual Report 2025 Spain/Western Europe disclosures ([revolut.com/annual-report-2025](https://www.revolut.com/annual-report-2025/)).*
