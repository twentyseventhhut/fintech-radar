# Context-enrichment: RTP Global leads $30M Series B in AI platform Attention
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Attention (attention.com, New York, founded 2021 by CEO Anis Bennaceur and CTO Matthias Wickenburg) raised a **$30M Series B, led by RTP Global** (Leonid Boguslavsky's fund), with returning investors Aglaé Ventures, Eniac, Alven and new investor Linea, plus a cohort of "customer-angels". The round was announced via PR on **2026-06-24** (PRNewswire/GlobeNewswire); the Russian-venture channel picked it up 2026-07-08, so the corpus date lags the actual event by two weeks.
- **What Attention actually is (de-PR'd):** a sales/revenue-AI SaaS. It sits on top of sales calls and CRM: transcribes conversations, drafts and sends follow-ups, auto-fills the CRM ("system of record"), and runs "the next play" / coaching. Marketing frames it as "the AI system that RUNS revenue teams — not just records them", i.e. it is positioning against pure conversation-intelligence incumbents (Gong records; Attention claims it acts).
- **Traction claims (PR, unverified):** ARR "up 4x YoY"; ">20M agent actions/month" since launching agents; ">500 customers" (named: Abridge, Scale, Lovable, Preply, Engine, BambooHR). One headline customer stat: Abridge cites "5x coaching efficiency" alongside "4x sales-org growth" — a customer-supplied, self-reported figure, not audited.
- **Base-anchoring caveat:** "ARR up 4x" is given without an absolute number. Third-party getlatka lists ~$11.6M ARR for 2024 (unverified, likely self-report). "4x agent actions" and "4x ARR" are the kind of multiples that flatter off a small 2024 base ($14M Series A was Oct 2024 — this is a fast follow-on ~20 months later).
- **Why structured this way:** the round is stacked with **existing insiders re-upping** (Alven led the Series A; Eniac/Aglaé returning) plus a NEW lead (RTP Global) to set price and signal an "up-market/enterprise" push. Customer-angels are a cheap credibility/lock-in device. The narrative pivot from "recording" to "autonomous agents" is the fund-raising hook of the 2026 cycle, not a proven product state.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Direct space = **revenue intelligence / conversation intelligence / agentic sales**:
- **Gong** — conversation-intelligence incumbent (records/transcribes/coaches), added forecasting; deep coaching moat.
- **Clari** — pipeline/forecasting for CROs; post-Salesloft merger, enterprise forecast roll-up.
- **Oliv AI, Sybill** — emerging, lead on agentic CRM auto-fill with cheap per-seat pricing (~$19/user/mo).
- Combined Gong+Clari stacks for 200-rep teams clear ~$500/user/mo — the "stack tax" Attention pitches to displace.
- **Position:** Attention is a **small, fast-growing challenger** betting the whole story on the "agentic execution gap" — the industry consensus (per 2026 comparison writeups) is that neither Gong nor Clari has achieved truly autonomous CRM/forecast execution. That gap is real but everyone is racing into it (Gong/Clari both ship "AI agents"; Oliv/Sybill undercut on price). Attention is not clearly ahead — it is one of several claiming the same wedge.
- **Internal corpus has NO Gong/Clari/revenue-intelligence raise notes** — this is a first-of-kind entry for the sales-AI vertical in this fintech corpus, which itself signals it is off-core.

## [2] Company history / fit
- 2021 founded; **Oct 2024 — $14M Series A** led by Alven (Eniac, 645 Ventures, Aglaé, Frst, Liquid2). **Jun 2026 — $30M Series B** led by RTP Global. Fast, insider-heavy trajectory.
- **Fit with THIS (fintech) corpus is weak.** Attention is a sales/revenue-ops AI tool, not a fintech: no payments rails, no lending, no banking/regulated money movement. Its only fintech adjacency is that its customers include revenue teams (some fintechs) and that finance-ops-AI is an ambient theme — cf. Attention is further from money-movement than any of these.
- **Why it's in the corpus at all:** the item entered via "Русский венчур" purely because **RTP Global / Leonid Boguslavsky** (a Russian-origin investor) led it — the news hook is the INVESTOR, not the fintech relevance. RTP Global also appears in the corpus on fintech deals: [[Tazapay raises $36m Series B led by Circle Ventures]] and [[Stable Money raises INR 173 crore Series B]] (RTP-backed).

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new:** not much technologically — call transcription + CRM auto-fill + follow-up drafting is a crowded 2024–2026 category. The claimed novelty is **degree of autonomy** ("agents that act", 20M actions/mo) rather than assist. That is a real product direction but the metric ("agent actions") is vanity-prone: an "action" can be a low-value auto-draft.
- **Traction that is real vs announced:** the durable signal is **4x YoY ARR + a priced up-round led by a new outside investor** — that is genuine demand, not just a press release. The weak signals are the unaudited customer testimonials (Abridge "5x/4x") and the absolute-ARR omission.
- **Who captures the margin (analysis):** Attention is a thin application layer sitting on (a) the LLM providers below it and (b) the CRM (Salesforce/HubSpot) that owns the system of record. If Salesforce/HubSpot or Gong/Clari ship equivalent native agents, Attention's wedge commoditizes fast. Its defensibility rests on workflow lock-in and proprietary sales-outcome data, not on model IP — a fragile moat (hypothesis).

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Stated plan:** roll out an expanded "agentic AI suite" and push **up-market into enterprise** revenue orgs. That is the standard Series B → enterprise motion; the risk is colliding head-on with Gong/Clari's enterprise install base and procurement.
- **Sentiment:** the 2026 revenue-intelligence market is consolidating (Clari+Salesloft) and repricing around "who delivers autonomous agents". Capital is flowing to the agentic-execution wedge, but it is contested — Attention's $30M is modest next to incumbents' war chests.
- **Second-order (analysis):** an insider-heavy round with a single new lead can mask soft external demand; the enterprise push exposes Attention to longer sales cycles and to platform (CRM/LLM) disintermediation. Upside case: it becomes the "action layer" incumbents underbuilt. Downside trigger: Salesforce/HubSpot/Gong native agents close the gap → Attention re-rates as a feature, not a platform.
- **Fintech relevance for OUR corpus: LOW.** Off-core; kept only for the RTP Global investor thread. Not a payments/banking/regulatory event.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: AI revenue-intelligence / sales-ops automation, moving from *recording* (conversation capture, CRM enrichment, forecasting) toward *acting* (agentic follow-ups, CRM updates, next-play execution). Market size: revenue-intelligence estimated at ~$3.8bn in 2024 growing ~12% CAGR (per Custom Market Insights, via custommarketinsights.com — third-party market report, treat as indicative, not audited). The bigger tailwind is agentic AI: IDC forecasts enterprise AI spend ~$307bn in 2026 with industry/vertical solutions growing ~36% CAGR (per IDC Worldwide AI Spending Guide, via secondary citations — [UNSOURCED] primary). Why now: the category is re-basing from "AI that records the call" to "AI that runs the play" — Attention's own PR positions explicitly against Gong ("not just records them"), i.e. the pitch is displacement of first-gen conversation-intelligence, not a greenfield.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: ARR growth, NRR, seats vs. usage/outcome-based pricing, agent-actions volume. Incumbents: **Gong** (~$500M ARR run-rate 2025-26, ~4k customers, ~2.2k staff; secondaries at ~$4.5bn end-2025 vs. $7.25bn 2021 primary — a down-round signal per Calcalist/GetLatka); **Clari + Salesloft** (merged Dec 2025, ~$760M combined pre-merger funding, 5,000+ orgs, forecasting-led); **Chorus** (bought by ZoomInfo ~$575M all-cash). Basis of competition: data + integration depth + workflow SOPs, NOT model quality (models are a commodity input). Recent moves: Clari-Salesloft merger closed fall/Dec 2025; Salesloft prior bolt-on of Drift. Protagonist position: **niche challenger, catching up** — Attention reports ARR +4x YoY and >20M agent-actions/month, but off a tiny base vs. Gong's $500M ARR. Moat `(analysis)`: closed-loop "action → outcome" attribution (it acts, so it can tie results to its own work) — a switching-cost/workflow moat if it holds, but unproven at enterprise scale.

**Comps & multiples.** Attention: $30M Series B, led by RTP Global (closed 2026-06-24); Aglaé, Eniac, Alven, Linea + customer angels. Post-money **not disclosed** → EV/Revenue = no data. ARR is undisclosed in $ (only "+4x YoY") → all revenue multiples = no data. Peer references (round valuations, not market caps): Gong ~$4.5bn secondary (2025) / $7.25bn primary (2021); Salesloft $1.1bn (Series E); Chorus $575M (acquisition). On $500M ARR Gong's ~$4.5bn secondary implies ~9x EV/Revenue = `$4.5bn / $0.5bn = 9.0x` — a de-rated marker for the category vs. 2021's ~22x on ~$330M. Distribution not computed for Attention (no disclosed valuation/revenue) → qualitative only. Internal comps: base is essentially empty for this vertical — `grep` surfaced no in-base revenue-intelligence deals (nearest tag hits are unrelated: an agentic customer-service raise and a sustainability-fintech deal). No usable `[[wikilink]]` comps.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Commoditization / wrapper risk** — value must sit in data + integrations + codified workflow, not the LLM; if OpenAI/Anthropic-native agents or the CRM vendors (Salesforce) absorb "draft follow-up + update CRM," the app layer is disintermediated. "Agent-washing" (Gartner) also muddies differentiation.
2. **Incumbent gravity + consolidation** — Gong ($500M ARR) and Clari+Salesloft (5,000+ orgs, incumbent distribution) can bundle agentic features into installed bases; a $30M Series B challenger competes on distribution it doesn't have. Category multiples are de-rating (Gong secondary ~$4.5bn vs. $7.25bn primary), pressuring exit math.
3. **Disclosure gap** — headline metrics are ratios/volumes ("+4x ARR", "20M agent-actions/mo") with no absolute ARR, NRR, customer count, or gross retention. Vanity-metric risk; can't test whether growth justifies (undisclosed) valuation.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a *new-entry bet on a category shift*, not a re-rating: capital is chasing "agentic execution" over "passive recording," and RTP is buying the thesis that action-attribution beats analytics. Falsifiable thesis: Attention wins if outcome/usage-based pricing + closed-loop attribution drive enterprise NRR >120% and it lands lighthouse enterprise logos before Clari-Salesloft/Gong ship comparable agents. What breaks it: incumbents bundle agentic follow-ups at zero marginal price, or ARR growth is off a base so small it never reaches escape velocity. Trigger to watch: first disclosed absolute ARR / named enterprise logos, and whether the next round is an up-round vs. the de-rating category tape.
