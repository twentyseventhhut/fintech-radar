Yapily brings bank account verification to Google Cloud in Europe
Sinziana Albu
07 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Yapily has extended its bank account verification service to Google Cloud customers across eleven European markets.
Yapily, an Open Banking infrastructure provider, has expanded its collaboration with Google Cloud, enabling business customers in Germany, France, the Netherlands, Spain, Portugal, Lithuania, Italy, Belgium, Austria, Ireland, and Sweden to verify their bank accounts during onboarding using Open Banking connectivity. The rollout builds on a bank account verification service Yapily began providing to Google in October 2025, extending the capability specifically to Google Cloud's customer base in the region.
Addressing the limits of manual verification
Confirming that a business owns the bank account it claims to hold has traditionally relied on manual processes: requesting supporting documents, waiting for them to arrive, and cross-referencing the details by hand. For customers, this approach has often meant delays and repeated document requests at the point of onboarding. In addition, for providers, it has translated into higher operational overheads and an optimized risk of applications being abandoned when the process takes too long, an outcome that can affect conversion in competitive markets.
Under the expanded arrangement, bank account verification is now embedded directly into the Google Cloud onboarding process across the eleven markets listed. Verification is carried out using bank-sourced data, with results returned within minutes rather than after a manual document review. The integration also consolidates what would otherwise be separate verification processes into a single connection with Yapily, giving Google Cloud one integration point covering all eleven markets rather than country-by-country arrangements.
Open Banking's expanding role in enterprise onboarding
According to the official press release, bank account verification is regarded as one of the more established applications of Open Banking, an area that has grown alongside the broader ecosystem. In the UK, Open Banking payments have reached 36 million transactions per month, according to Juniper Research, with over 17.5 million active user connections recorded by Open Banking Limited. Across the wider European market, user numbers surpassed 63 million as of 2024, according to Statista, while Open Banking now accounts for around 17% of total e-commerce transaction value on the continent, per data cited by The Payments Association.
Yapily connects to both business and consumer accounts at more than 2,000 banks across 19 markets in the UK and Europe, supporting use cases including Pay by Bank, Recurring Pay by Bank, data enrichment, transaction categorisation, and account verification. The company has stated that it has reached profitability, grown revenue, and added enterprise customers, including Google, Adyen, Ant Group, Intuit QuickBooks, Pleo, and Allica Bank.
Commenting on the expansion, Stefano Vaccino , founder and CEO of Yapily, said deploying verification at this scale across European markets reflects the current shape of enterprise Open Banking infrastructure.
The extension of the Google Cloud partnership indicates continued demand among large technology platforms for Open Banking-based identity and verification tools as an alternative to document-based onboarding checks.

**[0] What happened (2026-07-07).** Yapily, a London-based Open Banking infrastructure provider (founded 2017 by Stefano Vaccino), extended its bank-account-verification service to **Google Cloud** business customers across **11 European markets**: Germany, France, the Netherlands, Spain, Portugal, Lithuania, Italy, Belgium, Austria, Ireland and Sweden. Businesses can now verify ownership of their bank account during Google Cloud onboarding via Open Banking connectivity (bank-sourced data, results in minutes), replacing manual document requests. Google gets a single Yapily integration covering all 11 markets rather than country-by-country. Source: The Paypers / Open Banking Expo / FStech (07 Jul 2026).

**[1] Timeline / continuity (skeptic-check: this is an EXPANSION, not a new deal).**
- **Oct 2025** — Yapily first began supplying bank-account verification to Google (initial deployment for business customers). This July 2026 story is an extension of that existing relationship to Google Cloud's customer base specifically. So "new partnership" framing would be wrong; it is a rollout/scale-up.
- **Jul 2026** — coverage broadened to the 11-market list embedded in Google Cloud onboarding.

**[2] Company status (verify the vendor's self-claims).** Yapily states it has "reached profitability, grown revenue, and added enterprise customers." Named enterprise clients: Google, Adyen, Ant Group, Intuit QuickBooks, Pleo, Allica Bank. Third-party data points (treat as estimates, not audited): getLatka cites ~$13.4M ARR for 2025; Tech.eu (Feb 2025) reported the Adyen deal and that losses were "significantly reduced" — i.e. profitability is a recent/tight claim, not a long-established one. Total funding is reported around $69-71M (Series B $51M led by Sapphire Ventures in the 2021 era, plus $5.4M and $13M earlier rounds); the older "95% of Europe by year-end" and $51M-raise language predates this story and should NOT be presented as current news. Yapily says it connects to 2,000+ banks across 19 markets in the UK and Europe.

**[3] Why it matters / market context.** Bank-account verification is one of the more mature, lower-risk Open Banking use cases (vs. payments), which is why a hyperscaler like Google adopts it for KYB-style onboarding. Cited ecosystem stats (from the release, so vendor-selected): UK Open Banking payments ~36M tx/month (Juniper), 17.5M+ active connections (OBL); European Open Banking users >63M as of 2024 (Statista); ~17% of European e-commerce transaction value (The Payments Association). The signal: large tech platforms are substituting Open-Banking identity/verification for document-based onboarding checks.

**[4] Competitive landscape.** Direct Open Banking / verification competitors: **Tink** (owned by Visa), **TrueLayer** (raised undisclosed round Feb 2026), **Plaid** (US-centric, expanding in EU), plus GoCardless, Bud, Salt Edge, Kevin, Volt, Fiserv/Finicity. Yapily's differentiator here is (a) an existing, expanding hyperscaler relationship and (b) single-integration multi-market coverage. Winning a Google mandate is a credibility/distribution win but the underlying verification capability is not uniquely defensible — any of Tink/TrueLayer could serve the same use case. Note Tink's Visa ownership and Plaid's scale make them structurally stronger balance-sheet competitors.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
**Red-team questions**

1. Is this actually new? No — it extends a service Yapily began supplying to Google in **October 2025**. The novelty is only the 11-market Google Cloud rollout. Any "new partnership" framing overstates it.
2. Is it "Google Cloud" or "Google"? The Oct 2025 launch was framed as Google (business customers); this story specifies Google Cloud's customer base. Worth not conflating the two entities in the digest.
3. Is Yapily embedded in GCP Marketplace? The source says verification is embedded in the Google Cloud onboarding process — NOT clearly that it is a GCP Marketplace listing. Don't claim Marketplace distribution without confirmation.
4. Deal size / economics? None disclosed. No contract value, no volume commitment, no revenue impact. This is a capability announcement, not a financial one.
5. "Reached profitability" — audited? No. It's a vendor self-claim; Tech.eu (Feb 2025) framed it as losses "significantly reduced." Present as claimed, not fact.
6. Revenue figures? Only third-party estimates (~$13.4M ARR, getLatka) — not company-confirmed. Do not state as authoritative.
7. Is exclusivity implied? No evidence Google uses Yapily exclusively; Google could use multiple providers per region. Assume non-exclusive.
8. The "2,000+ banks / 19 markets" stat — Yapily's own; unverified independently. Fine to attribute, not to assert.
9. Ecosystem stats (36M tx, 63M users, 17%) are vendor-curated from the press release and mix UK-only and EU-wide/2024 data — cherry-picked; don't generalize.
10. Competitive threat: could Tink (Visa) or TrueLayer displace Yapily on this? Yes technically; the moat is the relationship, not the tech. Adoption by Google is a signal, not a lock-in.
11. Regulatory exposure? Open Banking verification depends on PSD2/PSD3 and national API quality; account coverage varies by country — the "single integration, 11 markets" pitch masks uneven underlying bank connectivity.
12. Adoption evidence? None — no customer counts, verification volumes, or conversion-uplift figures given. Claims about "reducing onboarding drop-off" are unquantified.
13. Is the source primary? The note is from The Paypers reprinting a press release; original is Yapily's own blog + release. Single-source, PR-origin.
14. Could "eleven markets" shrink or change? List is specific but rollout timing per market isn't detailed — treat as announced, not necessarily all live day one.

**Importance: 2/5** — Incremental expansion of an existing Oct-2025 Yapily–Google verification deal to 11 EU markets. Credible distribution/credibility win for Yapily and a data point on hyperscalers adopting Open Banking for KYB onboarding, but no deal economics, no exclusivity, PR-sourced, and the capability is commoditized among Tink/TrueLayer/Plaid. Notable for Open-Banking watchers, low broad-market significance.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** European open banking is a mid-sized, still-maturing infrastructure market. Sizing figures vary widely by vendor (Europe open banking ~$14.3bn in 2025, per Transpire Insight/Fortune Business Insights — treat with caution, these secondary reports are unaudited and cover a broad "open banking" definition incl. payments + data). More concrete adoption anchors from the note itself: European open banking users surpassed 63m as of 2024 (Statista, via press release); open banking ~17% of European e-commerce transaction value (The Payments Association, cited); UK OB payments reached 36m tx/month with 17.5m active connections (Juniper Research / Open Banking Ltd). Structure: fragmented on the supply side (Plaid, Tink/Visa, TrueLayer, GoCardless, Yapily, Qwist, plus bank-owned APIs) but consolidating — Visa bought Tink (2021), Mastercard bought Aiia, and account-verification/KYB is one of the more commoditised OB use cases. "Why now": distribution has shifted to hyperscaler marketplaces — the news is Yapily embedding directly into Google Cloud's onboarding across 11 EU markets, i.e. a channel play, not a new product. Bank account verification is explicitly framed (per PR) as "one of the more established applications of Open Banking."

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: bank coverage/reach (Yapily: 2,000+ banks, 19 UK/EU markets), enterprise logo count, revenue growth, and (for infra) gross margin / path-to-profit. Key players & basis of competition: Plaid (US-anchored, data-access scale), Tink (Visa-owned, pan-EU aggregation), TrueLayer (payments-API/A2A focus), GoCardless (Direct Debit + OB), Qwist, plus in-house bank APIs — competition is on coverage breadth, reliability/uptime, single-integration convenience, and increasingly distribution partnerships. Recent peer moves: TrueLayer acquired Dutch consumer-credit fintech in3 (2026-05) and raised an undisclosed Series E (2026-02); Coinbase+Tink brought Pay by Bank to Germany (2025-11); PayPoint acquired AperiData for OB (2026-06). Yapily's position: mid-tier by scale but well-positioned on distribution/enterprise logos (Google, Adyen, Ant Group, Intuit QuickBooks, Pleo, Allica Bank) and states it has reached profitability. Moat `(analysis)`: primarily switching costs (single integration covering 11–19 markets) + the Google Cloud channel as a distribution moat; weak network effects vs Plaid/Tink scale.

**Comps & multiples.** Yapily is private, not in IR coverage — external figures below are third-party/last-round and mixed-vintage; treat as directional.
- **Yapily** — 2025 revenue ~$13.4M (getlatka); last disclosed valuation $40.3M (getlatka/PitchBook), total funding ~$70.8M over 5 rounds incl. $51M Series B. If both figures are taken at face value: EV/Rev ≈ `$40.3M / $13.4M ≈ 3.0x` — but the $40.3M "valuation" tag is stale/inconsistent with a $51M Series B, so this multiple is unreliable → treat as `[UNSOURCED]` rather than a real comp.
- **TrueLayer** — valuation ~$750M (Oct 2024, Tracxn); revenue not publicly verifiable → EV/Rev = no data.
- **Tink** — acquired by Visa for €1.8bn (2021); revenue at deal not cleanly public → historical M&A anchor, not a live multiple.
- **Plaid** — $8bn valuation (Feb 2026 tender), 2024 revenue $390M, ~$430M ARR 2025 → EV/Rev ≈ `$8bn / $390M ≈ 20.5x` (US-scale, not directly comparable to Yapily's EU niche).
Distribution not computed (fewer than 3 clean same-model figures; vintages differ 2021–2026). Qualitative read: OB-infra names trade on growth+coverage, not earnings; Plaid's ~20x reflects US scale/ARR, not applicable to Yapily. **Risk flags.**
1. **Client/channel concentration & disintermediation.** The story's value hinges on Google (both a customer since Oct 2025 and now the distribution rail). Deep reliance on one hyperscaler's marketplace means Google captures the customer relationship; Google could switch providers or build in-house, and Yapily's economics/take on the deal are undisclosed — classic "someone else's rails" risk.
2. **Commoditisation & margin pressure.** Account verification is explicitly "established/mature" OB — low differentiation vs Plaid/Tink/TrueLayer; competition on coverage and price compresses infra margins. Yapily is sub-scale (~$13M rev, ~140 staff) against a Visa-backed Tink and an $8bn Plaid.
3. **Regulatory/standards dependence.** Coverage depends on PSD2/PSD3 API quality and bank cooperation across 11–19 markets; API breakage or PSD3/FIDA shifts directly hit reliability, the core KPI. Profitability claim is self-reported and unaudited.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a distribution re-rating, not a product shift: OB-infra is moving from direct B2B sales to being resold inside hyperscaler onboarding stacks — verification-as-a-feature bundled by AWS/Google/Azure. Falsifiable thesis: if hyperscaler-channel deals become the primary growth vector for OB-infra, sub-scale independents (Yapily) either get acquired (Tink/Aiia pattern) or squeezed as clouds standardise verification. Trigger to watch: a competing hyperscaler naming a different OB provider (e.g. AWS + Plaid/TrueLayer), or Yapily disclosing audited FY profitability/next round — either would confirm or break the "profitable independent" story.
