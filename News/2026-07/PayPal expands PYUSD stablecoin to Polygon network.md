# PayPal expands PYUSD stablecoin to Polygon network

> [!info] 2026-07-10 · 1 упоминаний · 0 источника(ов) с текстом

[Connecting the Dots in Fintech] 🇺🇸 PayPal is expanding PYUSD to the Polygon network, enabling native issuance of its stablecoin and integration with Polygon's Open Money Stack. The move is designed to simplify global business payments and support broader adoption of PYUSD for cross-border settlement.

## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** On 2026-07-09 PayPal announced native issuance of its PYUSD stablecoin on the Polygon PoS network, via issuer Paxos, integrated into Polygon's "Open Money Stack." Native issuance removes the need for bridged token versions; businesses already on Polygon can access PYUSD through the wallets, fiat on/off-ramps and compliance tools they already use, moving regulated dollars across borders and cashing out to local currency through a single integration. Paxos issues PYUSD under an OCC-supervised national trust charter. ([The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/407719/paypal-seeks-increase-pyusd-stablecoin-native-issuance-on-polygon), [American Banker](https://www.americanbanker.com/payments/news/paypal-adds-pyusd-to-polygon), [CryptoTimes](https://www.cryptotimes.io/2026/07/09/paypal-usd-launches-natively-on-polygon-for-global-payments/))

**[1] Why it matters.** This is a distribution play, not a new product: PYUSD is a small stablecoin (~$2.8–2.9B supply, #3 behind USDT ~$143B and USDC ~$78B) that PayPal is pushing toward B2B cross-border payments. Polygon settled >$2.6T in cumulative stablecoin volume and ~$3B/day, so native PYUSD gains access to an active payments network. For PayPal, stablecoins/PYUSD are a strategic lever under new CEO Enrique Lores as branded checkout — its core, slowest-growing engine — stalls.

**[2] Prior art / timeline.** PYUSD launched 2023 on Ethereum, later Solana. Earlier in 2026 PayPal expanded PYUSD access to ~70 markets, incl. Africa (see [[PayPal expands dollar stablecoin to seventy markets]]). This Polygon move is the next chain/rail expansion. PayPal is also broadening PYUSD as banks prepare a shared bank-consortium stablecoin ([Yahoo/American Banker](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/crypto/articles/paypal-boosts-stablecoin-banks-prepare-195603383.html)).

**[3] Internal connections.**
- [[PayPal expands dollar stablecoin to seventy markets]] — the 70-market PYUSD expansion (2026-05), same PYUSD growth thrust, different event.
- [[Cash App rolls out stablecoin and new features]] — competing fintech stablecoin push.
- [[JPMorgan launches JPMD deposit token on Base]] / [[Worldline partners with Fipto for stablecoin rails]] — bank/PSP stablecoin-rail competition.

**[4] Open questions.** Actual PYUSD supply/volume issued on Polygon (undisclosed); whether B2B cross-border adoption materializes vs. announcement; fee/revenue model for PayPal; competitive pressure from a coming bank-consortium stablecoin.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector.** Stablecoin payments are the hot 2026 fintech theme: aggregate stablecoin supply is approaching ~$420B, and stablecoin transaction volume hit a record ~$1.79T in June 2026 (Visa data). PYUSD processed ~$2.42B and ranked #3 by that measure. PayPal's Polygon move fits the industry pivot from crypto-trading use to B2B/cross-border settlement rails.

**PYUSD scale (external, dated).** ~$2.84B market cap / ~2.9B tokens circulating for the week ending 2026-07-05; peaked near $4.1B in March 2026, contracted since. ~680% YoY supply growth (from a small base). Still an order of magnitude below USDC (~$78B, Q1 2026) and USDT (~$143B). ([DeFiLlama](https://defillama.com/stablecoin/paypal-usd), [Spark](https://www.spark.money/research/stablecoin-supply-420-billion-growth))

**In-base comps.**
- [[PayPal expands dollar stablecoin to seventy markets]] — geographic PYUSD expansion; this is the chain-rail complement.
- [[JPMorgan launches JPMD deposit token on Base]], [[Cross River launches unified stablecoin payment platform]], [[Worldline partners with Fipto for stablecoin rails]] — bank/PSP stablecoin-rail competition.
- [[Cash App rolls out stablecoin and new features]] — consumer-fintech stablecoin competition.

**IR grounding (PRIMARY — PayPal is public, EDGAR-covered).** PayPal's latest reported period is Q1 2026 (results 2026-05-05, 8-K + earnings release, [SEC 8-K 2026-05-05](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1633917/000163391726000065/pypl1q-26earningsrelease.htm); 10-Q [pypl-20260331](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1633917/000163391726000067/pypl-20260331.htm)). Reported figures: **TPV $464B (+11% spot / +8% FXN); revenue $8.35B (+7.2%); non-GAAP EPS $1.34.** Branded checkout TPV only +2% FXN — the strategic weak spot the stablecoin/PYUSD push is meant to complement, not fix. PYUSD-specific supply/market-cap is NOT disclosed in PayPal's financials (issuer Paxos holds reserves); PayPal does not book PYUSD float as its own revenue.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Materiality risk** — PYUSD is small and its float economics accrue mainly to Paxos; near-term PayPal revenue impact is immaterial. This is optionality, not a growth driver.
2. **Competitive/share risk** — PYUSD is a distant #3; USDC/USDT dominate and a bank-consortium stablecoin is in the works, capping PYUSD's B2B share.
3. **Execution/adoption risk** — announcement vs. sustained B2B cross-border volume; no Polygon-specific PYUSD volume disclosed; core branded-checkout stall (+2% FXN) is the real thesis overhang, not stablecoins.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Latest reported period: Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-05).** Source: PayPal 8-K / earnings release ([SEC 8-K 2026-05-05](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1633917/000163391726000065/pypl1q-26earningsrelease.htm)), Form 10-Q ([pypl-20260331](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1633917/000163391726000067/pypl-20260331.htm)).

**Reported figures (vs prior / consensus).**
- Revenue **$8.35B, +7.2% YoY** — beat consensus by ~3.8%.
- Non-GAAP EPS **$1.34** vs ~$1.27 est — **beat** (~+5.6%).
- Transaction revenue $7.50B (+7%); Other Value-Added Services $852M (+10%).
- **TPV $464B, +11% spot / +8% FXN.**
- **Branded checkout TPV +2% FXN** (up from +1% in Q4 2025) — still the slowest-growing major segment (~29% of TPV); the key thesis flag.
- Venmo TPV +14% YoY (6th straight double-digit quarter); Pay with Venmo TPV +34%; BNPL TPV +23%.
(Sources: [Investing.com Q1 slides](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/paypal-q1-2026-slides-beats-estimates-but-guidance-weighs-on-stock-93CH-4676997), [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/earnings/2026/paypal-shares-sink-amid-online-branded-checkout-headwinds/))

**Verdict: BEAT on Q1 revenue & EPS**, but stock fell as guidance/branded-checkout headwinds dominated.

**Guidance / strategy context.** New CEO Enrique Lores (effective 2026-03-01) is running a turnaround: FY2026 branded-checkout guide only "slightly positive to low-single-digit" TPV growth, with targeted investments creating ~3 pts of transaction-margin headwind; non-GAAP operating margin compressing ~229 bps as PayPal reinvests in checkout, pricing and rewards. Management pulled its prior multiyear outlook in favor of annual guidance during the reset. ([Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4546453-paypal-outlines-2026-focus-on-branded-checkout-execution-and-expects-closer-to-50-percent), [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/earnings/2026/paypal-shares-sink-amid-online-branded-checkout-headwinds/))

**Thesis link to this pick.** The PYUSD-on-Polygon expansion is strategic optionality (B2B cross-border stablecoin rails) layered on top of a business whose core branded-checkout engine is stalling (+2% FXN). It does not move current-period financials; PYUSD supply/float is held by issuer Paxos and is not a disclosed PayPal revenue line. Watch Q2 2026 print (due late July/early Aug) for whether checkout reinvestment shows traction.
