# Agave Raises $15M Series A to Bring AI to Construction Financials

 Business Wire 

Tue, July 7, 2026 at 10:00 AM EDT 5 min read

_Backed by Accel, Agave streamlines financial workflows on top of contractors' existing systems, during critical moment as 41% of industry workforce retires in the next five years_

**SAN FRANCISCO, July 07, 2026**--(BUSINESS WIRE)--Agave, the AI platform for construction financials, today announced it raised a $15M Series A funding round from Accel, who led Agave's Seed round, with continued participation from YCombinator. The round brings Agave's total funding to over $20 million since its origin in late 2021. Founded by Tom Reno, John Zucchi, and Pooria Azimi, Agave will use the new capital to expand its team and scale its growing suite of products, which already help hundreds of contractors boost their team's productivity by automating accounting and back-office work.

This raise comes on the heels of exceptional growth for Agave. The company has been profitable for over two years, tripled its revenue year-over-year, and works with more than 500 general and specialty contractors from $5 million to more than $5 billion in annual revenue. Its customers span commercial, civil, and residential construction and trades including electrical, roofing, and concrete, like Evans General Contractors, Stacy Witbeck, Brinkman Construction, Satterfield & Pontikes, and RW Dake. Today, Agave is used across more than 80,000 construction projects in the U.S. and Canada, representing more than $100 billion in project volume. Agave customers report saving more than 60 hours per month on tasks like data entry, financial reporting and cost reconciliation across systems.

Controlling finances is critical to maintaining profit margins on construction projects, which typically span years, require unexpected changes mid-project, and involve dozens of otherwise unrelated companies. The accounting behind it differs significantly from standard business rules. Every project runs as its own profit center, costs are tracked by job, phase, and cost code, and scope shifts mid-build through change orders. Generic accounting systems and AI tools do not support these nuances, and the systems that do are decades old, on-prem or hosted on remote servers, and built without modern APIs.

"AI adoption in construction won't come from replacing existing systems, but from enabling AI to work seamlessly with them," said Tom Reno, cofounder and CEO of Agave. "Agave helps contractors unlock the value of AI on top of the tools that already run their business, turning siloed data and manual processes into streamlined workflows that allow contractors to take on more jobs at higher margins, without painful system migrations or training an army of new employees."

Story Continues

Agave spent four years forging partnerships with the major construction platforms and building proprietary, two-way connections with more than 14 ERP and project management systems. These two-way connections enable Agave to access data and automate complete workflows end to end, where other tools with simpler connections can manage only isolated pieces.

Agave's products reflect this focus on integration and augmentation over replacement. They give construction field teams real-time financial data from ERPs inside project management systems like Procore and Autodesk Forma Build, and automatically code, reconcile, and import invoices and credit-card transactions to ERPs. Agave helps onboard new vendors by comparing long insurance documents against predefined requirements before approving them to do work and receive payments. Users can also build their own AI agents, automating business-specific workflows that pre-built tools can't support. Customers report over 80% reduction in time spent across thousands of tasks per month.

Agave helps the industry confront a looming challenge: 41% of the construction workforce will retire in the next five years, and only 10% of the entire workforce is under 25 years old. The experienced staff who manage current processes are exiting faster than younger workers are entering. Agave's solutions are becoming increasingly essential to the future of construction, helping companies codify institutional knowledge, automate work, and augment teams with tools that boost their productivity.

"Most energy in AI goes into building new software. The bigger opportunity is deploying AI to operate on systems companies already depend on, helping enterprises get more out of what they have today," said Vas Natarajan, Partner at Accel. "Agave does this for construction, going straight at the messy, domain-specific problems most teams avoid. It's a rare combination of experienced team and market need, and they're the ones to capitalize on the opportunity."

"We've more than doubled in size over the past few years, and Agave's been a big reason my team can keep up without adding headcount," said Ken Pattridge, CFO of RW Dake Construction. "Agave keeps our Project Management and ERP data in sync automatically, so the numbers from the field, like change orders, show up in our accounting system without anyone rekeying them. And the AI does in minutes what would otherwise take us days. I've had it turn a master job report into tailored financials for each of our 47 PMs, and with a few more prompts, scan months of activity across hundreds of credit cards to catch expenses coded incorrectly that we should've billed to clients. That kind of analysis used to take days of manual work to pull together, so it often never got done. I don't know how we'd keep up without Agave now."

**About Agave**

Agave is the AI platform for construction financials, built to work seamlessly with the systems contractors already use. It serves general and specialty contractors, connecting more than 14 ERP and project management systems across cloud, hosted, and on-prem environments. Agave's products include cross-system data integration and AI tools for AP invoice automation, expenses management, vendor compliance, analytics and agent building. Profitable and backed by Accel and YCombinator, Agave is used by more than 500 contractors across North America today.

# Context-enrichment: Agave raises $15M Series A to automate construction finance workflows
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **Deal:** Agave (San Francisco, founded late 2021, YC W22) raised a **$15M Series A led by Accel** (who also led its ~$2.9M 2023 seed), with continued YC participation. Total funding now **$20M+**. Announced 2026-07-07 (Business Wire).
- **What it actually is (minus PR):** Agave is a **two-way data-integration layer** for construction software — it reads/writes across **14+ ERP and PM systems** (Procore, Autodesk Build/Forma, Sage Intacct/100C/300 CRE, Viewpoint Vista/Spectrum, Foundation, CMiC, Deltek ComputerEase, HCSS, QuickBooks). Its own site cites **33 connected systems / 950 API endpoints**. On top of those rails it now deploys **AI for AP-invoice coding/reconciliation, expense management, vendor-compliance (insurance-doc checking), analytics, and user-built agents.**
- **Traction (verifiable, from the release):** 500+ contractors ($5M–$5B revenue); **80,000+ projects**, **$100B+ project volume** across US/Canada; customers save "60+ hrs/month" and "80% reduction" on tasks. **Profitable 2+ years, revenue tripled YoY.** ~75 employees (May 2026).
- **Why structured this way / what it reveals:** The company **repositioned from "unified construction API" (its 2023 seed framing) to "AI platform for construction financials"** — same integration moat, new AI surface. The traction metrics (profitable, tripled revenue) are real substance, not vanity; a $15M A on a profitable 500-customer base is a **modest, growth-not-survival raise**, consistent with the "profitable for 2 years" claim. The 41%-retirement-in-5-years stat is the PR hook (labor-shortage narrative) — directionally real but marketing-framed.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Construction fintech is crowded and the corpus tracks it densely. Distinguish by **what layer each owns:**
- **Billing/pay-app workflow:** [[Siteline]] (~$18M seed+A; subcontractor pay-apps, lien waivers), [[Handle closes $27 million Series B for construction finance]] (liens/waivers/payments/receivables, $27M Series B, May 2026), Flashtract/Trimble Pay (acquired by Trimble 2024).
- **Working-capital / early-pay:** [[Billd raises $7.3 million for construction subcontractor early-pay]] (supplier upfront + 120-day terms), [[Earlytrade raises $25M for construction payments AI]] (agentic AP marketplace, June 2026), Speedchain ([[Speedchain raises $111M for construction finance platform]]), Pillar.
- **Design/estimating AI (adjacent, not fintech):** [[Higharc raises funding for AI homebuilding software]] ($95M Series C, June 2026 — homebuilder design, not financials).
- **Financial-ops / AP for contractors (direct):** Adaptive (job costing, progress billing, retention), Briq, Built Technologies.
- **Position:** Agave is **not** primarily a payments or lending play — it's the **integration/data plumbing** underneath. Its closest analogue is **Adaptive** (AP + financial ops) but Agave's edge is breadth of two-way connectors. **Why this matters (analysis):** Siteline/Handle/Earlytrade own a *workflow* (billing, liens, early-pay) but must still integrate with legacy ERPs; Agave sells the *pipes*. Whoever owns the integration layer can commoditize the workflow apps above it — OR be relegated to a "Plaid for construction" middleware with thin margins. That tension is the central question (see [3]).

## [2] Company history / fit
- **2021:** Founded (Tom Reno, John Zucchi, Pooria Azimi). **YC W22.** **Aug 2023:** ~$2.9M seed led by Accel; angels from Plaid, PlanGrid, Fieldwire, EquipmentShare, Honest Buildings + Autodesk/Procore execs (TechCrunch, 2023-08-30). Framing then: **"unified API"** — one integration to reach all construction software.
- **2023→2026 evolution:** Spent ~3 years building **two-way** connectors (harder than read-only API aggregation), then layered AI financial-ops products on top. **2026-07:** $15M A, reframed as "AI platform for construction financials."
- **Why it acts this way (analysis):** A pure integration-API business (Merge/Finch/Plaid analogue) has a **hard ceiling** — connectors are a cost center and commoditize. By moving *up-stack* into AP automation / expense / compliance built on its own rails, Agave converts a middleware moat into **application revenue with a software multiple**, capturing more of each contractor's spend than a per-API-call fee would. Same logic Accel-partner Vas Natarajan's quote hints at: "deploy AI on systems companies already depend on."

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Genuinely new?** The *integration moat* is not new (it's the 2021–2023 thesis). What's new is (a) the **AI financial-ops product suite** on top and (b) the funding to scale it. The **two-way** (read AND write) connections to legacy on-prem/hosted ERPs are the durable, hard-to-replicate asset — most rivals do read-only or single-system.
- **Traction is real, not announced:** profitable 2+ yrs, 500+ paying contractors, $100B project volume, tripled revenue. This is unusually credible for a Series A (most peers are pre-profit).
- **Who captures the margin (analysis):** Two paths. (i) **Platform path** — Agave becomes the mandatory data layer; workflow apps (Siteline, Handle) build ON Agave → Agave captures infra margin. (ii) **Application path** — Agave's own AP/expense apps win and it deprioritizes being neutral plumbing → competes directly with Adaptive/Built. **The risk:** if Procore/Autodesk build native two-way sync or acquire a connector rival, Agave's plumbing commoditizes; if it goes full-app, it loses neutrality and third-party partners. **What underpins the multiple:** the switching cost of 14+ live ERP write-integrations per customer — genuinely sticky, since re-keying across systems is the exact pain it removes.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Plans:** expand team, scale product suite (AP, expenses, compliance, agent-builder). Labor-shortage tailwind (41% of workforce retiring in 5 yrs) is the demand narrative — codifying institutional knowledge as automation.
- **Sentiment:** Construction fintech saw heavy 2025–2026 funding (Handle $27M, Earlytrade $25M, Higharc $95M, Speedchain $111M). Capital is flowing; Agave's profitability differentiates it from cash-burning peers.
- **Second-order (analysis):** As GenAI makes it trivial to build *point* automation on top of any single ERP, the defensible layer shifts to **cross-system reconciliation** — exactly Agave's ground. But the same GenAI lowers the barrier for Procore/Autodesk to offer native AI, so Agave's window to entrench as the neutral layer is finite. A $15M raise (modest vs peers' $25–111M) suggests **disciplined, profit-backed scaling** rather than a land-grab — a strength if the category consolidates, a risk if a rival out-raises and out-markets the "integration layer" position.

**Importance: 3/5** — Substantive, credible traction (profitable, 500+ customers, $100B volume) and a genuinely defensible two-way integration moat make this more than a routine seed-stage announcement. ## Market Research

**Sector & drivers.** Construction fintech (lien-management + automated payment platforms) sized at ~$3.1bn in 2026, projected to ~$7.4bn by 2034 at ~11.4% CAGR (per marketintelo.com report, as of 2026; secondary/unverified vendor research — treat as indicative, not audited). The broader AP-automation market is ~$6.9bn in 2026 growing ~12.4% (per Mordor Intelligence, via search, 2026). Structure: highly fragmented — value is captured across the stack by ERPs (Sage, Vista, Foundation), project-management systems (Procore, Autodesk), and a long tail of point solutions for billing, liens, AP, and expense. Barriers are integration depth (two-way ERP connectors, many on-prem/decades-old with no modern APIs) rather than capital. Why now: (1) construction accounting is genuinely non-standard — every project is its own P&L, job/phase/cost-code tracking, retainage, change orders, lien rights — which generic AI/accounting tools don't model, leaving a defensible vertical wedge; (2) a labor cliff — Agave cites 41% of the construction workforce retiring within 5 years and only 10% under 25 (per Agave PR, Business Wire, 2026-07-07; company-sourced, unverified) — pushing automation of institutional knowledge. `(analysis)`

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: ARR growth, gross retention, number of contractors/seats, project volume processed, and integration coverage (# of ERP/PM systems connected). Key players & basis of competition:
- **Integration/automation layer (Agave's lane):** Agave (14+ two-way ERP/PM connectors, 500+ contractors, 80k+ projects, >$100bn project volume, profitable, revenue tripled YoY — all company-sourced), **Adaptive** ($19M Series A Jul-2024 led by Emergence w/ a16z; $26.4M total; QuickBooks-centric AP/AR/payments, per PRNewswire), **Rabbet**, **Constrafor**.
- **Billing/pay-app & liens:** **Siteline** (~$18M seed+A; ~$4.7M ARR per getlatka, unverified), **Flashtract** (acq. by Trimble 2024, now Trimble Pay; ~$16.3M raised pre-deal), **Handle** ([[Handle closes $27 million Series B for construction finance]]), **GCPay**, **Levelset** (acq. by Procore for $500M).
- **Working-capital/early-pay:** **Billd** ([[Billd raises $7.3 million for construction subcontractor early-pay]]), **Earlytrade** ([[Earlytrade raises $25M for construction payments AI]]), **Speedchain** ([[Speedchain raises $111M for construction finance platform]]), **Pillar** ([[Construction finance startup Pillar closes EUR3.2M]]).
- **Incumbent gravity:** Procore and Autodesk own the workflow surface Agave plugs into (Levelset/PlanGrid/Textura absorbed by Procore/Autodesk/Oracle) — basis of competition is distribution + platform ownership.
Recent moves: Adaptive Series A (Jul-2024); Handle Series B (2026-05); Earlytrade $25M + agentic-AI pivot (2026-06); Agave Series A (2026-07). Agave's position: a **niche integration/augmentation** play — deliberately not replacing ERPs but sitting on top of them; moat is the multi-year two-way connector library (switching cost + hard-to-replicate integration intangibles), reinforced by profitability. Ahead of most peers on integration breadth; behind Procore/Autodesk on distribution. `(analysis)`

**Risk flags.**
1. **Platform disintermediation / dependence on others' rails.** Agave's entire value is a middleware layer atop Procore, Autodesk, and 14+ ERPs. Those incumbents own the customer relationship and can build native two-way sync or acquire a connector rival (precedent: Procore↔Levelset $500M, Autodesk↔PlanGrid $900M) — collapsing Agave's wedge. Second-order: margin and pricing power sit with whoever owns the workflow surface.
2. **Crowded, well-funded vertical.** Adaptive (a16z/Emergence), Handle (WEX strategic), Earlytrade, Speedchain and the Procore/Autodesk suites all target overlapping AP/finance-automation budgets; differentiation is integration depth, which competitors can narrow over time. Risk of feature commoditization compressing the "60+ hours saved" ROI story.
3. **Unverifiable, company-sourced traction.** Headline stats ("$100bn project volume", "80% time reduction", "profitable 2 years", "tripled revenue") are all PR-sourced with no absolute revenue or valuation disclosed — impossible to size or benchmark; the labor-cliff "41% retiring" TAM narrative is also self-cited.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` Agave reinforces the thesis that construction fintech's durable value is the **integration/AI-augmentation layer over legacy ERPs**, not another point tool — a sub-vertical now seeing steady $15–27M rounds (Adaptive, Handle, Earlytrade, Agave within ~12 months), suggesting an emerging category, not a re-rating. Falsifiable thesis: Agave stays independent and defensible as long as Procore/Autodesk keep buying (not building) connectors and ERPs remain API-poor. Watch/trigger that would break it: a Procore or Autodesk native "universal ERP sync + AI AP" launch, or an acquisition of Agave/Adaptive/Rabbet by a platform incumbent — either signals the middleware window is closing.
