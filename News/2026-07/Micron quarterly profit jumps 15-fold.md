# Context-enrichment: Micron quarterly profit jumps 15-fold
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

**Timeline note:** The FT headline is dated 2026-07-06 in our digest, but the underlying event — Micron's **fiscal Q3 2026** results (quarter ended ~late May 2026) — was actually reported on **2026-06-24/25**. So the FT item is a ~11-day-late re-report of an already-public reporting period. However, the Q3 results themselves are NOT covered in any prior corpus note → treated as **fresh** for our DB (new reporting period), while flagged as a lagged re-tell of a June event.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **Fiscal Q3 2026 (ended ~May 2026):** revenue **$41.4bn**, up ~4.4x YoY from **$9.3bn** a year ago. GAAP net income **$28.2bn / $24.46 EPS** vs $1.89bn / $1.68 a year ago → the literal **~15-fold profit jump** in the headline. Non-GAAP EPS **$25.11** vs ~$20.49 consensus (beat ~22%).
- **Gross margin ~85%** — a level that now rivals/exceeds Nvidia and Meta. This is the real story: memory, historically a low-margin cyclical commodity, is temporarily earning software-like margins because HBM supply is sold out.
- **Data-center revenue ~$25bn** in the quarter; enterprise SSD ~$5bn (~20% of DC). Record free cash flow.
- **HBM booked out:** entire **calendar-2026 HBM supply** (price + volume) locked, including HBM4; HBM3E/HBM4 booked through **calendar 2027**, demand extending into 2028.
- **Guidance (FQ4 2026):** revenue **~$50.0bn ±$1.0bn** (record), EPS **~$31.00 ±$1.00**, FCF expected **>$30bn**. Stock +~15% after-hours to ~$1,213, valuing MU ~**$1.16tn**.
- **De-PR:** the 15x figure anchors to a depressed year-ago base ($1.89bn net income during the 2024-25 memory trough) — a genuine cyclical inflection, but the multiple is flattered by the low base. **Why it matters:** the durable question is not "how big was the jump" but "how long does an 85% gross margin in a commodity survive before capacity normalizes pricing" (→ [4]).

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **HBM market share (2025 baseline):** SK Hynix leader ~**62%**, Micron ~**21%**, Samsung ~**17%**. In HBM specifically Micron has overtaken Samsung; the 2026 battle pivots to **HBM4**.
- **SK Hynix** — completed HBM4 development, claims +40% power efficiency, 10 Gbps; the primary AI-memory supplier to OpenAI's stack alongside Samsung. **Micron's HBM4** claims >11 Gbps, ramping ~2Q26 (calendar).
- **Samsung** — trailing in HBM qualification/yields, the swing supplier whose recovery would add supply.
- **Position:** Micron is #2-3 in HBM but riding the same tide; its margin print now matches the AI-infrastructure leaders. **Second-order:** the ~85% margin is **sectoral (memory supercycle), not name-specific** — all three vendors are sold out, so the gap vs Micron's historical margins is a cycle artifact that closes when coordinated capacity (Micron capex hiked to ~$20bn) lands in 2027-28.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Micron is the only US-based DRAM/HBM maker; historically a deeply cyclical commodity player (year-ago quarter: near-trough $9.3bn rev / $1.89bn NI). See [[Wall Street eyes memory maker Micron as next Nvidia]] (2026-06-28) — the market re-rated MU as an "AI pick" ahead of these results.
- The **[[Micron and Anthropic announce memory partnership]]** (2026-06-23) fits: Micron designing memory with Claude in mind + Anthropic taking equity, positioning Micron as Anthropic's main memory partner (mirroring Samsung/SK Hynix ↔ OpenAI). **Why:** a commodity supplier locking a hyperscaler/lab as an anchor demand-and-design partner de-risks the next capex cycle and defends the multiple.
- **Structural logic:** commodity take-rate + brutal cyclicality → Micron needs both (a) HBM's temporary scarcity premium and (b) strategic customer lock-ins to justify a >$1tn valuation.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new (to our corpus):** the actual Q3 hard numbers — $41.4bn rev, ~85% GM, $50bn FQ4 guide — none of which appear in prior notes (which were pre-earnings commentary + a partnership).
- **Traction, not announcement:** this is realized revenue/cash, not a roadmap. Calendar-2026 HBM sold out is a booked backlog, not a projection.
- **Who captures the margin:** in the AI stack, memory was the classic margin-loser; the supercycle has temporarily moved pricing power to the three memory oligopolists. **What breaks it:** the same oligopoly is adding capacity (Micron $20bn capex) — the margin is underpinned by scarcity, and scarcity is self-correcting in memory.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **FQ4 guide $50bn / $31 EPS** implies continued acceleration; sell-side unanimously bullish into the print, HBM4 the next catalyst.
- **Sentiment:** MU re-rated toward a "next Nvidia" narrative; ~$1.16tn cap. **Counterintuitive second-order:** peak margins + peak multiple in a historically cyclical name = maximum fragility, not safety. The 15x/85%-GM print marks how good it is now, which historically is where memory cycles are most exposed to a 2027-28 pricing normalization once coordinated capacity lands.
- **Risks:** coordinated capacity additions (all three vendors) normalizing HBM pricing; Samsung yield recovery adding supply; AI-capex digestion; the guidance itself assumes uninterrupted hyperscaler demand.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Memory (DRAM / HBM / NAND) inside AI-infra. Sizing: BofA pegs the 2026 HBM market at ~$54.6bn, +58% YoY, and forecasts global DRAM revenue +51% YoY on ASPs +33% — framed as a "supercycle" (per BofA, via eeNews Europe / Silicon Analysts, as of 2026). HBM TAM projected >$100bn by 2027 (per Micron, Q3 FY26 remarks). Structure: consolidated oligopoly — SK Hynix ~50-55%, Samsung ~35-40%, Micron ~5-10% HBM share (per Silicon Analysts, 2026); high barriers (capex-heavy fabs, advanced-packaging/CoWoS bottleneck, multi-year process leads). Why now: HBM for AI accelerators consumes ~3x the wafer capacity per GB of standard DRAM (per TrendForce, via Fortune 2026-02), so makers reallocate away from consumer/enterprise DRAM, tightening the whole DRAM market; TrendForce sees conventional DRAM contract prices +55-60% in Q1 2026. Micron exited consumer memory to concentrate on enterprise/AI (per Fortune, 2026).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: DRAM/HBM bit-shipments & ASP (revenue-per-bit), HBM qualification with accelerator vendors (Nvidia), gross margin, capacity sold-out %. Players & basis of competition: SK Hynix (HBM leader, 2026 HBM output sold out; ~$5.2bn HBM to Nvidia in Q1 2026), Samsung (scale + logic/foundry), Micron (fast follower, HBM3E/HBM4). Basis = process/packaging leadership + customer qualification, not price. Recent moves: Micron and SK Hynix both crossed $1tn market cap in May 2026 (Micron first, per 247WallSt / Yahoo); Micron reported record Q3 FY26 (late June 2026) — revenue $41.46bn (+346% YoY), GAAP net income $28.24bn, DRAM ~77% of revenue (per Micron IR / CNBC). The FT-headlined "profit jumps 15-fold" reflects this earnings blowout. Micron says its entire 2026 HBM supply — incl. next-gen HBM4 — is already priced and volume-committed; signed 16 Strategic Customer Agreements (take-or-pay, price bands) covering up to half of future revenue (per Micron Q3 FY26 remarks). Position: **niche-but-scaling** #3 in HBM, but a full participant in the DRAM tightness; moat = scale + advanced-packaging know-how + newly durable multi-year contracts (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** No in-base comps — the news corpus is fintech/payments-only; nearest tag `industry/infrastructure` yields no memory/semi peers (grep over News/ returns only payments-context "Samsung"/"NVIDIA" hits). External:
- Micron (MU): market cap ~$1.095tn; trailing P/E ~46; forward P/E ~9 (per Benzinga / 24-7 WallSt, 2026). Forward P/E 9 vs trailing 46 = market pricing a huge FY26 earnings jump; PEG ~0.07 → growth barely priced in.
- SK Hynix: >$1tn market cap; record Q1 2026 revenue ₩52.6tn (~$35.5bn); forward PEG ~0.08.
- Samsung: semis operating profit ₩53.7tn (~$36.1bn) in Q1 2026.
- 2026 YTD stock: Samsung +149%, SK Hynix +215%, Micron +245% (through late May, per Benzinga).
Flag: **in-line / cheap on forward earnings** — a forward P/E of ~9 is well below the ~10-50x sanity band and is the market signalling it does not believe peak-cycle earnings are sustainable (classic memory-cycle discount), NOT a mispricing to arbitrage. Trailing P/E ~46 is a base-effect artifact of a prior trough quarter.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Cyclicality / 2027 glut** — memory is the textbook commodity cyclical; SK Hynix already cut HBM capex on internal 2027-oversupply modelling, and TechInsights forecasts an industry downturn by 2027. Why: capex-heavy, inelastic supply meets elastic demand; a capacity wave landing as AI orders slow compresses margins fast (fixed-cost under-absorption). The forward P/E of ~9 is the market pricing exactly this. 
2. **Customer / demand concentration** — HBM economics ride on a handful of AI-accelerator buyers (Nvidia dominant). Any AI-capex air-pocket hits HBM ASP and volumes disproportionately; Micron's take-or-pay SCAs mitigate but don't eliminate.
3. **Competitive share risk** — Micron is #3 (~5-10% HBM); if SK Hynix/Samsung out-execute on HBM4 qualification, Micron's upside compresses first when tightness eases (disintermediation within the oligopoly).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a mid-cycle **re-rating** of memory from commodity to "AI-infra strategic input" — take-or-pay contracts and sold-out capacity give unusual earnings visibility (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: pricing power holds through FY27 IF HBM stays supply-constrained; trigger to watch = HBM/DRAM contract-price direction into H1 2027 and any capex re-acceleration by the three makers — a return to raw-capacity expansion would be the tell that the glut clock has started, breaking the "durable pricing" thesis.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Verdict (headline read).** BEAT (blowout) · FQ3 FY2026 (qtr ended ~May 2026, reported 24 Jun 2026) revenue $41.46bn (+346% YoY; vs guidance $33.5bn → +24% / +$7.96bn, vs public consensus ~$35.7bn → +$5.77bn / +16%) · non-GAAP EPS $25.11 (vs consensus ~$20.49 → +$4.62) · GAAP EPS $24.67 · driver = AI-driven DRAM/HBM price + volume, gross margin at a record 84.9% · Q4 guidance introduced at ~$50bn, ~15–22% above Street. The FT "profit up 15-fold" refers to net income: $28.24bn vs $1.89bn in FQ3 FY2025 (~15x). Shares +~13–14% after-hours.

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Revenue $41.46bn (+346% YoY, +74% QoQ vs $23.86bn; FQ3 FY2025 was $9.30bn).
- GAAP net income $28.24bn (~15x vs $1.89bn YoY); GAAP EPS $24.67. Non-GAAP net income $28.86bn; non-GAAP EPS $25.11.
- Operating income $33.7bn, operating margin 81.2%.
- Non-GAAP gross margin 84.9% — a record (guidance beat was margin-led: pricing + mix, not just volume).
- Operating cash flow $25.39bn (vs $11.90bn QoQ and $4.61bn YoY).

**By segment / driver.**
- DRAM: record $31.3bn, 76% of revenue — the growth engine; HBM inside DRAM is the AI lever.
- NAND: record $9.9bn (+361% YoY), 24% of revenue.
- HBM4 (on 1-beta DRAM) in high-volume shipments to the lead customer's platform; qual samples shipped to multiple end-customers → forward datacenter visibility. The 346% surge is squarely AI-memory-cycle driven (price + HBM mix), i.e. a supply-tight up-cycle, not a one-off.

**vs expectations / prior period.** Beat on both lines: revenue +$5.77bn / +16% vs public consensus (~$35.7bn, as of late Jun 2026); non-GAAP EPS +$4.62 vs ~$20.49. Also beat the company's own $33.5bn guide by 24% — the guidance itself was a bar and it was cleared decisively. YoY momentum is extreme (revenue 4.5x, net income ~15x) off a depressed FQ3 FY2025 base ($9.30bn rev / $1.89bn NI), so the multiple is amplified by an easy comp — but the +74% QoQ acceleration shows the up-cycle is live, not just a low-base optic. No prior Micron earnings note in the corpus to `[[wikilink]]`.

**Guidance / forward.** Q4 FY2026 revenue guided to ~$50bn (introduced/raised trajectory), ~15–22% above Street forward — management is guiding UP into the print, the strongest possible signal of confidence in HBM demand and pricing durability. Plausibility supported by HBM4 already in high-volume shipment and multi-customer qual pipeline; key assumption is that DRAM/HBM pricing holds (memory is cyclical — the risk is not this quarter but the eventual roll-over). Management tone: confident, leaning into AI-memory contracts.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. Guidance raised into a blowout (Q4 ~$50bn, +$8.5bn QoQ) → fact: sequential acceleration continues; why: HBM supply is sold out at rising prices; why it matters: earnings power is re-rating, not mean-reverting yet; second-order: pulls up the whole HBM/memory complex (SK Hynix, Samsung) and DRAM-exposed names.
2. Record 84.9% gross / 81.2% operating margin → the beat is margin-led (pricing/mix), the highest-quality kind of beat; de-PR check: management is NOT silent on margin — they lead with it, which is credible given DRAM tightness.
3. HBM4 in volume + multi-customer qual → de-risks the FY2027 datacenter mix and reduces single-customer concentration risk.
4. Cyclicality is the one unspoken risk: figures this parabolic on a ~15x YoY comp are a cycle top-risk flag — sustainable through Q4 on guidance, but the eventual pricing roll-over is the thesis watch-item, not any current-quarter softness.
