Hercle secures MiCA and EMI licences in Italy
Sinziana Albu
02 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Hercle has secured an EMI licence from the Bank of Italy and a MiCA CASP authorisation from CONSOB, enabling regulated stablecoin settlement.
The two authorisations arrive as MiCA's transitional period ended on 1 July 2026, after which full authorisation became mandatory for providers serving clients within the EU. With both licences in place, Hercle is among a limited number of providers permitted to combine regulated electronic-money and payment services with crypto-asset services across the European market under a single framework.
End-to-end settlement capability
The EMI licence permits Hercle to hold and safeguard client funds, issue electronic money, and execute payments. The MiCA CASP authorisation covers the custody, exchange, and transfer of crypto-assets, including stablecoins. Combined, the two licences allow Hercle to manage a cross-border payment transaction from start to finish: receiving a fiat deposit, converting it into stablecoins, and paying out in local currency, with one regulated entity responsible for the full transaction rather than multiple intermediaries.
At the same time, according to the company, with the new authorisations, this activity is now conducted under a regulated structure across the EU, a development that may support further growth as the market for cross-border payment and stablecoin infrastructure moves toward increased regulatory oversight. The company is set to continue to focus on meeting the needs, preferences, and demands of clients and users in an ever-evolving market, while prioritising the process of remaining compliant with the regulatory requirements and laws of the industry as well.
 Gabriele Sabbatini , CEO and co-founder of Hercle, said that holding both the EMI and MiCA authorisations allows the company to offer regulatory certainty alongside faster settlement, reducing multi-day settlement processes to a shorter timeframe. It was also added that clients now work with a single regulated counterparty rather than managing multiple intermediaries across the payment chain.
Hercle stated that the authorisations will support its planned expansion into South America and Africa, alongside continued development of its API infrastructure and institutional product offering.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Hercle S.r.l. (Milan, founded 2019, ~30 staff) obtained **two separate Italian authorisations**: a **MiCA CASP authorisation from CONSOB** (with Banca d'Italia's opinion) and an **EMI / IMEL licence from Banca d'Italia** (Art. 114-quinquies TUB). Both are worded as **GRANTED, not applied-for**. Timing is the point: MiCA's transition window closed and Italy's transitional VASP regime ended **30 Jun 2026**, so a cluster of Italian firms was authorised at end-June/1-Jul. Hercle is one of **only ~8 Italian CASPs** at that cut-off (alongside CheckSig, Conio, RIV-Digital, Olliv, CryptoSmart, Hodlie, Young Platform).
→ **Why structured this way:** the "dual licence" framing is the real substance. Under MiCA Title IV a **euro e-money token (EMT) can only be issued by a bank or an authorised EMI** — so CASP+EMI is the minimum legal stack to run an end-to-end fiat→stablecoin→fiat cross-border payment inside one regulated entity (receive fiat deposit → issue/hold e-money → convert to stablecoin → custody/transfer/exchange → pay out local currency). That vertical integration, not liquidity depth, is the story.
→ **What's under-verified (skeptical flag):** the **CASP is multi-source confirmed** (BeBeez, Cointelegraph, Crypto Economy). The **EMI rests on Hercle PR + one Italian outlet (MilanoFinanza)** and is absent from crypto-trade coverage; exact EMI grant date is **open** and should be checked against Banca d'Italia's IMEL register. PR throughput figures ($20B+ cumulative volume, 200+ clients, <5min settlement) are **self-reported and independently unverified** (Fortune flagged this in Oct 2025). No Hercle-issued stablecoin has actually been announced — the EMI so far is a legal gateway, not a live product.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Prior art is dense. Early MiCA CASPs: Boerse Stuttgart Digital (BaFin, first EU-wide, Jan 2025), Bitpanda (Jan 2025), OKX/Crypto.com (Malta, Jan 2025), Bitstamp (Lux, May 2025), Kraken (Ireland, Jun 2025), Coinbase (Lux, ~Jun 2026), B2C2 (Lux, first OTC LP, May 2026), Ripple (Lux, preliminary, Jun 2026). **Both CASP + EMI/e-money** is rarer but not unique: **SG-Forge** (AMF CASP + EMI, issues EURCV/USDCV), **Ripple** (EU EMI + preliminary CASP), **Circle** (ACPR EMI for EURC). Peer combos in-corpus: [[Zodia Custody secures Luxembourg license to expand EU stablecoin services]] pairs a Payment-Institution licence with a MiCA CASP.
→ **Position:** Hercle is plausibly the **first Italian firm with both CASP + EMI**, but a **niche player by scale**. Wintermute clears ~$15B/day; Hercle cites $20B *cumulative*. Flow Traders, Bitpanda (~€371M rev), Talos, Boerse Stuttgart are 1–2 orders of magnitude larger. Hercle competes on **compliance + cross-border fiat/stablecoin rails**, not deepest-pool liquidity.

## [2] Company history / fit
Founded 2019 (Milan; Swiss entity Hercle SA, Lugano). Leadership: Gabriele Sabbatini (CEO/co-founder), Arturo Schembri (CFO), Marco Levarato (CTO). Product: institutional single-dealer OTC desk (HercleX), liquidity aggregation across 35+ venues, plus a growing stablecoin-based B2B cross-border payments line. Funding: €1.7M seed (PranaVentures, Kairos) → **29 Oct 2025: $60M total = $10M equity (F-Prime Capital lead; Fulgur, Exponential Science) + $50M credit facility**. Ran under Italy's transitional VASP regime (OAM register PSV32).
→ **Why the company acts this way:** OTC/settlement is a thin-margin, pass-through business. The regulatory stack is Hercle's differentiation lever for its size — it converts a commodity execution desk into a "single regulated counterparty" for cross-border stablecoin payments, which is what the announced **expansion into South America and Africa** and API/institutional roadmap are built on.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Genuinely new: the **combined CASP+EMI** legally enables in-house end-to-end fiat↔stablecoin settlement with **one regulated entity** replacing a chain of intermediaries, and (per the CEO) collapsing multi-day settlement to sub-day. That is a real structural claim, not just PR.
→ **Why it's real / where it breaks (2nd order):** the value-add hinges on (a) the EMI actually being usable to hold client fiat / issue an EMT — currently a legal capability, no live stablecoin; and (b) volume. The **least-verified part is exactly the traction** ($20B, 200+ clients). If those are low-margin pass-through flows, the regulatory moat matters more than throughput. Margin in this stack is captured by whoever holds the client relationship AND the fiat rails — the dual licence is precisely a bid to capture both rather than remain a pure execution utility.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Regulatory backdrop is the driver: MiCA CASP grandfathering had a **hard EU outer limit of 1 Jul 2026** (no extensions); Italy's VASP transition ended 30 Jun 2026 (Law Decree 95/2025) — hence the end-June approval cluster. In Italy, **CONSOB supervises CASPs, Banca d'Italia supervises EMI/EMT issuers**. Hercle plans expansion into South America and Africa plus API/institutional build-out.
→ **Why the market goes this way (counterintuitive):** the transition deadline is a forcing function that **thins the field** — only authorised entities may serve EU clients, so a mid-size firm with the full stack can punch above its scale during the shakeout. Risk: dual-licence maintenance and EMT-issuance capital/reserve requirements are heavy for a firm on a $10M equity base; the moat could invert into a cost drag if volumes don't materialise, and SG-Forge/Ripple hold the same combo at far greater scale.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Regulated EU crypto/stablecoin infrastructure. "Why now": MiCA's transitional (grandfathering) regime ended 1 July 2026 — after that date serving EU clients without a CASP authorisation is no longer covered by transitional arrangements (per Elliptic/Elvinger Hoss, as of Jul 2026). This is the structural catalyst behind the whole current licensing wave. Market is still early/fragmented: ~210 CASPs full-authorised across 23 member states as of May 2026, up from ~60 in early 2026 (per CCN/Sumsub secondary counts, as of 2026) — a pre-deadline scramble. Italy specifically was a laggard: it approached the deadline with zero MiCA CASP licences and only ~8 authorised around/after 30 Jun 2026 (Consob/Bank of Italy, via SpazioCrypto, Jun–Jul 2026); Hercle is one of them. Value in this chain accrues to whoever holds BOTH the money-side licence (EMI/PI) and the crypto-side licence (CASP), because that is the entry barrier — capital + dual-regulator approval (Consob for CASP conduct, Bank of Italy for EMI/custody-settlement under D.Lgs. 129/2024). Second-order: dual-licence holders can internalise the fiat↔stablecoin↔fiat leg and capture margin that today leaks to multiple intermediaries.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: transactions processed / settlement volume, settlement speed, currency & asset coverage, institutional client count, take rate `[UNSOURCED]`. Hercle (founded 2019, Milan; institutional infra bridging stablecoins + cross-border settlement) discloses (via BusinessWire/SiliconCanals, Oct 2025): 200+ institutional clients (banks, brokers, PSPs, corporate treasuries), >$20bn cumulative transactions, 30 digital assets / 25 currencies, ~90% of transactions <5 min, electronic OTC desk "HercleX", and a Talos liquidity integration. Basis of competition = regulatory coverage + settlement speed + liquidity depth, not price. Peers pursuing the same dual-licence "single framework" play, with dates: Fipto (France, dual PI+CASP, Jan 2026, self-styled "Europe's first dual-licensed stablecoin PI"); Ripple (EU EMI + preliminary MiCA CASP, "single integration" collect/exchange/pay-out); Standard Chartered (MiCA + EMI, Luxembourg, per the same Paypers issue as Hercle); Zodia Custody (Lux Payment Institution alongside MiCA CASP, Jun 2026); BVNK (Malta CASP, Feb 2026); Triple-A (France CASP, Jun 2026). Position: Hercle is a **niche institutional/settlement specialist**, not a scale leader vs Coinbase/Crypto.com/Nuvei; its edge is being one of the few Italy-domiciled dual (EMI+CASP) holders (analysis). Moat = intangible (dual regulatory licence) + local bank integrations for fiat rails — real but replicable given ~210 CASPs EU-wide, so switching costs/network effects are thin (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation in the note; comparison is qualitative — **distribution not computed**. Hercle's last disclosed financing is $60M (Oct 2025): $10M equity led by F-Prime (Fulgur Ventures, Exponential Science) + a $50M credit line (BusinessWire, Oct 2025); no post-money disclosed → EV/Revenue, P/S = **no data** / `[UNSOURCED]`. Internal comps (same MiCA+EMI/dual-licence theme, base): [[Stablecoin player Fipto achieves dual authorisation status]] (dual PI+CASP, France); [[Zodia Custody secures Luxembourg license to expand EU stablecoin services]] (PI + CASP); [[BVNK secures MiCA licence in Malta]]; [[Triple-A secures MiCA CASP licence from France's AMF]]; [[Nuvei secures EU CASP licence under MiCA]]; [[Crypto.com receives limited financial institutions licence in Europe]]; [[Coinbase secures MiCA license in Luxembourg]]; [[dtcpay gets green light for Luxembourg EMI licence]]. None carry a public multiple → no trading-comp arithmetic possible; qualitative only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **"Limited number of providers" claim is soft.** The note (via company PR) frames Hercle as among a "limited number" combining EMI + CASP; but Fipto, Ripple, Zodia and Standard Chartered already hold comparable dual licences — the position is early-mover-in-Italy, not a durable EU moat. Why: if the licence is the moat and ~210 CASPs exist, differentiation must come from volume/liquidity, which Hercle does not lead.
2. **Regulatory/jurisdiction concentration.** Licences are Italy-issued (Consob + Bank of Italy); MiCA passporting should extend reach, but home-NCA supervision, EMT/PSD2 interplay (EBA flagged CASPs moving EMTs may need separate PI/EMI cover) and post-deadline scrutiny concentrate execution risk on one regulator. Why: a compliance stumble at the home NCA jeopardises the whole passport.
3. **Expansion + concentration risk.** Growth thesis leans on South America / Africa expansion and API/institutional product build-out (note) — new geographies with their own licensing, FX and AML regimes, funded partly by a $50M credit line (leverage). Why: capital-intensive geographic expansion against thin disclosed equity raises execution/funding risk if volumes don't scale.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is a licence-driven land-grab, not a re-rating event: the 1 Jul 2026 deadline just converted regulatory optionality into a scarcity asset, and Italy going from ~0 to ~8 CASPs means the domestic settlement rail is up for grabs. Falsifiable thesis: Hercle can turn its dual EMI+CASP status + $20bn track record into share of EU cross-border stablecoin settlement. What breaks it: if larger dual-licensed players (Ripple, Standard Chartered, Fipto) undercut on liquidity/price, or if EMT/PSD2 rule-tightening raises Hercle's compliance cost faster than volume grows. Watch/trigger: next disclosed volume figure, a priced funding round (would finally allow a multiple), and whether the South America/Africa launch actually goes live vs "announced".
