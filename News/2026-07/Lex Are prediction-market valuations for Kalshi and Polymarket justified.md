### [0] What / when
FT **Lex** opinion column (~2026-07-14) asking whether prediction-market valuations for **Kalshi** and **Polymarket** are justified. Not a news break — a **valuation-scrutiny lens** on the prediction-market funding boom. Digest fragments captured: "_Should prediction markets get $40B valuations?_" and "_Why Kalshi at $2B is worth twice as much as Polymarket_" (the "$2B" phrasing is a stale/garbled artefact — see [4]; the live comparison is a **~20x vs ~15x revenue-multiple gap** favouring Kalshi).

### [1] The valuation picture Lex is scrutinising
**Kalshi ladder:** $2bn (Jun-2025) → $11bn (Dec-2025 Series E) → **$22bn (Series F, Mar/May-2026, Coatue-led, $1bn raised)** → **~$40bn target in talks** (reported Jun-2026, *not closed*). ~91% US prediction-market share. ~$2bn annualized revenue (Sacra). $40bn/$2bn ≈ **~20x revenue**.
**Polymarket ladder:** ~$1bn (mid-2025) → **~$8bn (ICE, $2bn total: $1bn Oct-2025 + $600m 27-Mar-2026)** → **~$15bn target** (talks, Apr-2026). Annualized revenue **topped $1bn** (confirmed to CNBC 26-Jun-2026). $15bn/$1bn ≈ **~15x**; ICE mark ≈ ~8x; PitchBook third-party mark only ~$10.7bn (~10–11x).
Detail: internal notes [[Kalshi builds prediction markets for AI compute pricing]], [[Analysis Why Intercontinental Exchange invested $2B in Polymarket]], [[Polymarket annualized revenue tops $1 billion]], [[Zuckerberg eyed Kalshi buy before building Meta's Arena]], [[Kalshi hires CME's Udesh Jha as risk chief]], [[Polymarket raises $2B from ICE at $9B valuation]].

### [2] The Lex thesis (reconstructed — column paywalled)
- Prediction markets are being **"priced as exchange infrastructure before proving they are more than a very busy scoreboard."** A $40bn business needs **ordinary weeks to matter**, not just elections / NBA playoffs / World Cup spikes.
- The multi-tens-of-billions gap between the two prices a **premium on Kalshi's CFTC federal standing** (a DCM, "messy legal fight inside the US system") over Polymarket's cleaner-but-offshore/crypto product.
- Kalshi's ~20x multiple is stretched vs Polymarket's ~15x; the premium rests on **regulatory moat + ~91% share, not current economics**.
- Benchmark: **DraftKings/Flutter ~3–5x sales** — the 15–20x holds only if these are "exchanges," not "regulated gambling."

### [3] Sustainability / red flags Lex leans on
- **Revenue is high-beta, not annuity.** Polymarket only began charging fees Q1-2026 ($0 in 2025); its ">$1bn" is a ~6-week-old run-rate flattered by US-app launch + FIFA World Cup (May fees actual **$28.07m** ×12 ≈ $337m; Sacra ~$375m). Kalshi's ~$2bn is likewise a peak run-rate.
- **Sports concentration = legal fragility:** ~90% of Kalshi volume (Kentucky AG: 89%, >$23bn in 2025); ~85–90% of Polymarket fee volume. This is the slice exposed to gambling-reclassification.
- **Fee-revenue lead is real:** Kalshi $137.86m vs Polymarket $28.07m in May-2026 (~5:1); Jun volume $21.1bn vs $9.7bn.
- **Regulatory overhang:** 12+ states allege unlicensed sports betting; CFTC published a proposed prediction-markets rule (10-Jun-2026, 45-day comment). Favourable now, but a future CFTC chair or adverse sports-contract ruling is an existential tail risk. Polymarket's planned token adds airdrop-farming stickiness doubts.

### [4] Synthesis
Lex is the **skeptic's frame** over facts our corpus already logged: both duopolists carry ~15–20x-revenue private marks on **unaudited, seasonally-flattered run-rates** where ~90% of volume is sports. The bull case = regulatory moat + share justify an "exchange" multiple; the bear case (Lex) = strip out election/World-Cup cyclicality and the CFTC-standing premium and you are paying exchange-infrastructure prices for a business that still looks like a high-turnover sports casino. The "$2B twice-as-much" digest line is stale (Kalshi's Jun-2025 mark); the honest comparison is Kalshi's **~20x vs Polymarket's ~15x** per-dollar-of-revenue premium, plus Kalshi's ~5:1 fee lead.

Sources: CNBC (26-Jun-2026, Polymarket $1bn); Bloomberg/TechCrunch/Kalshi (Series F $22bn); ICE IR + CoinDesk (ICE $2bn/$600m); CoinDesk/Benzinga (Kalshi $40bn target); Investing.com, Startup Fortune, 99Bitcoins (Lex thesis, multiples, sports %); PitchBook via NPR (Kalshi $22bn / Polymarket $10.7bn).

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**What this note is.** A Lex (FT) commentary posing two valuation questions — *"Should prediction markets get $40bn valuations?"* and *"Why Kalshi at ~$2bn [revenue] is worth twice as much as Polymarket."* Title-only stub (2 mentions, no primary-source text). It is a **valuation-justification opinion piece**, not a new event/number — so the job here is to stress-test the two claims against the vault's own comps and external data, not to log a development.

**Sector & drivers.** Prediction / event-contract markets — a US-centred hyper-growth trading subvertical that re-rated from a grey market into CFTC-blessed infrastructure. Combined Kalshi+Polymarket monthly notional went from <$5bn (Sep-2025) to ~$24-30bn (Apr-2026 peak ~$29.8bn); full-year 2025 industry volume >$63bn (TRM Labs / Pew, 2026). Structure: a **duopoly** with Kalshi holding ~91% of US prediction-market volume (BofA, Apr-2026, [[Kalshi holds 91% of US prediction market, BofA data shows]]). "Why now / why $40bn": on 2026-01-29 CFTC Chair Selig withdrew the Biden-era rule that would have banned political/sports event contracts and ordered "clear standards" rulemaking — the regulatory green light that let ICE underwrite Polymarket ($2bn, [[Polymarket raises $2B from ICE at $9B valuation]]) and lets Kalshi price like exchange infrastructure. Revenue model = **taker-fee take-rate on volume** (Kalshi ~1.1% uniform, both-sided; Polymarket lower, category-tiered, maker-rebated, large fee-free geopolitics book) — i.e. revenue is a rake on gambling-adjacent, event-elastic volume, NOT recurring SaaS.

**Claim 1 — "Should prediction markets get $40bn valuations?" (froth test).**
- Kalshi is raising at **~$40bn** (Jun-2026, could close Q3, [[Kalshi in talks to raise at $40 billion valuation]]), up from ~$22bn (May-2026, Coatue/Sequoia/a16z/Morgan Stanley) and ~$11bn (Nov-2025) — a **~20x valuation move in ~12 months** off a ~$2bn base (2025-06). At a ~$2bn annualized run-rate that is **~20x EV/Rev**; on Sacra's newer ~$3bn Jun-2026 estimate (up from $735m FY2025) it is **~13x**.
- **Anchor comp (the acquirer/exchange class):** public exchange-data operators trade at exchange-infra multiples, not consumer-fintech ones — **CME ~17.6x EV/Rev, ~24.7x EV/EBITDA; ICE similar scale** (Yahoo Finance / GuruFocus, 2026, external). So a ~13-20x forward multiple for the #1 CFTC-regulated venue is **rich but NOT obviously absurd IF you accept the "these become the next CME/ICE" framing** — which is precisely the bull case Kalshi is selling. The froth is in the *denominator quality*, not the multiple headline.
- **Why the number is soft:** the run-rate is a seasonally-flattered extrapolation — World Cup + US-launch peak, unaudited, not TTM. Kalshi did ~$260m realized revenue on ~$22.9bn volume in 2025 (~1.14% take); the "$2-3bn" annualizes a tournament peak month (May fees $137.86m; Jun volume ~$31.5bn, +87% MoM). Post-tournament mean-reversion (as political volume reverted after the 2024 election) would expose the multiple as peak-cycle. Verdict: **$40bn is defensible only on the exchange-infrastructure thesis holding AND the run-rate proving durable — two unsettled bets, so "justified" is conditional, not established.** (analysis)

**Claim 2 — "Kalshi worth ~2x Polymarket" (the gap test).**
- On the comparable metric the gap is real: **Kalshi out-earns Polymarket ~5:1 on May fees ($137.86m vs $28.07m)** and ~3x on June volume (~$31.5bn vs ~$10.3bn), with a ~$2bn+ run-rate vs Polymarket's ~$1bn+ ([[Polymarket annualized revenue tops $1 billion]], [[Kalshi passes $2 billion annualised revenue, in early IPO talks]]). So a ~2x *revenue* premium understates Kalshi's actual fee lead.
- BUT the *valuation* gap is **wider than the revenue gap**: ~$40bn vs ~$15bn (Polymarket, [[Polymarket in talks to raise at 15B valuation]]) ≈ **~2.7x**, vs a ~2x revenue-run-rate gap (or ~5x on realized fees). The delta beyond revenue is a **regulatory-standing premium**: Kalshi is a CFTC-registered DCM with USD-native rails and ~91% US share; Polymarket is offshore-USDC-native carrying a crypto-ties discount (Fortune, 2026-04-21) and only recently re-entered the US. So Lex's "worth twice as much" is directionally right but the *reason* is mostly **regulatory moat + monetization maturity**, not just the revenue ratio. (analysis)

**Comps & multiples (private rounds = valuations, not market caps).**
- Kalshi: ~$40bn / ~$2bn run-rate = **~20x** (or ~13x on ~$3bn Sacra est).
- Polymarket: ~$15bn / ~$1bn run-rate = **~15x**.
- Both ~13-20x sit **in-line with each other and near exchange-infra comps (CME ~17.6x EV/Rev), but on unaudited, event-peak denominators** — true trailing multiples are materially higher. For a consumer/gambling-adjacent frame, DraftKings trades ~3-5x sales; the ~4-5x premium is priced on CFTC-derivative status + growth, not durable economics.
- Kalshi valuation ladder: $2bn (Jun-2025) → $11bn (Nov-2025) → $22bn (May-2026) → ~$40bn talks (Jun-2026). ICE-specific multiples [UNSOURCED] here.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Regulatory/legal reclassification (category-central).** ~65-90% of both venues' fee revenue is *sports* — the segment most exposed to "unregulated sportsbook, not a derivatives market" reclassification. >12 states suing Kalshi; a Massachusetts court already issued a preliminary injunction rejecting Kalshi's CFTC-preemption argument as "overly broad"; Minnesota's felony ban effective 2026-08-01; mixed federal outcomes (favorable 3rd-Circuit CEA-preemption read, but unsettled). The CFTC blessing underpinning the $40bn is a preliminary/administrative posture and CFTC composition is politically reversible. An adverse ruling hits the exact revenue that both the ~$40bn and ~$15bn rounds capitalize.
2. **Valuation froth / run-rate quality.** ~13-20x on an unaudited, World-Cup-peak run-rate that only exists because fees launched recently (Polymarket Q1-2026; Kalshi ramped 2025-26). A post-tournament volume cliff (watch Aug-Sep-2026 monthly notional) mean-reverts the denominator and re-rates both marks — the multiples are peak-cycle, not steady-state.
3. **Event-driven volume cyclicality + concentration.** Revenue is a rake on episodic sports/election catalysts, not annuity flow; WSJ found 0.1% of Polymarket accounts capture 67% of profits while 99.9% net lose ([[WSJ finds 0.1% of Polymarket accounts capture 67% of profits]]) — a thin whale cohort whose activity is the most novelty-elastic. Losing-retail churn after the World Cup directly compresses the fee base.
4. **Moat compression from incumbents.** Cboe, CME (FanDuel Predicts), Nasdaq, Robinhood, Novig (CFTC-approved Jun-2026) all racing in post-CFTC-clarity — potentially compressing take-rates and eroding the duopoly premium both valuations assume.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) The Lex piece is a **valuation-skepticism prompt, not a development** — it adds no new number the vault lacks. Net read: the *relative* gap (Kalshi >> Polymarket) is well-supported by fees, volume, and regulatory standing; the *absolute* level (~$40bn / ~$15bn, ~13-20x) is **conditionally justified** — it prices these as future CME/ICE-class data-and-exchange infrastructure, a thesis that is falsifiable on two triggers: (a) a post-World-Cup volume/revenue cliff in Q3-2026 prints, or (b) an adverse CFTC/court ruling on sports contracts. Either would expose the multiples as peak-cycle venture bets that mis-priced the regulatory outcome; absent either, the exchange-infra re-rate holds and the raises clear.
