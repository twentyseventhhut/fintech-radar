## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** Costas Michael, founding CEO of Revolut's digital assets unit in Cyprus (Revolut Digital Assets Europe), is stepping down after ~4 years, per a Finance Magnates exclusive (2026-07). He led Revolut's crypto operations across the EEA and oversaw its CASP and MiCA licensing. He will remain an adviser to the board. Successor not yet named publicly; his next move undisclosed (he declined to comment).

**[1] Who / entity.** Michael set up Revolut's Cyprus branch, secured the first Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) licence in Cyprus, and later the MiCA licence (CySEC licence No. 001/2025). Prior background: MD of XTB's Cyprus division for 4+ years, and earlier traditional financial-services roles. Cyprus is the regulated hub for Revolut's EEA crypto passporting.

**[2] Why it matters / strategy.** The Cyprus MiCA licence underpins Revolut's entire EEA crypto stack: the retail Crypto offering, the standalone exchange **Revolut X** (launched Nov 2024, 30 EEA countries, ~200 tokens, desktop), and the planned **"Crypto 2.0"** platform (280+ tokens, zero-fee staking up to ~22% APY, 1:1 stablecoin-USD conversion). EU lawyers say the MiCA licence also positions Revolut to legally issue its own stablecoin, with a 2026 launch called "plausible" (would require activating its Lithuanian EMI + EBA supervision if significant). Losing the founding architect of this licence during MiCA-transition crunch is a continuity risk — see the parallel [[Revolut to delist USDT for European users under MiCA]] MiCA-driven change.

**[3] Broader Revolut context — leadership churn.** This is one of several 2026 senior exits, signalling a generational transition ahead of an IPO:
- [[Revolut co-founder Vlad Yatsenko steps down as CTO]] — CTO → non-exec director from 2026-07-01; Donato Lucia (8-yr insider) succeeds as VP Technology.
- [[Revolut Trading UK CEO steps down amid wealth push]] (2026-07) — separate UK trading/wealth leadership change.
- Head of expansion Dmytro Strelchuk left in April 2026 after 8 years.
Valuation backdrop: $75bn secondary share sale completed 2025-11-24 (up 67% from $45bn) with Coatue, Greenoaks, Dragoneer, a16z, Nvidia's venture arm; a further secondary at >$100bn planned for H2 2026; IPO target up to $200bn (~2 yrs out). Full UK banking licence granted March 2026. See [[Revolut plans secondary share sale at over $100B valuation]] and [[Revolut's $75 billion valuation a stepping stone to a trillion, says Fuse boss]].

**[4] Open questions / what to watch.** (a) Who replaces Michael as CASP/Digital Assets CEO — internal vs external, and whether the vacancy delays Crypto 2.0 / stablecoin plans. (b) Whether the departure is voluntary or part of a deliberate reshuffle (Finance Magnates framed the CTO exit as a broader "reshuffle"). (c) Regulatory continuity: keeping key-personnel requirements satisfied under CySEC/MiCA during a CASP leadership change. (d) His "adviser to the board" status — how substantive vs ceremonial.

_Sources: Finance Magnates / TradingView (2026-07); BrokerChooser; CoinDesk (2025-10-23, MiCA licence); Crunchbase/PitchBook (Nov 2025 $75bn); TechCrunch/CoinDesk (Apr 2026 $200bn IPO target); Bloomberg (Jun 2026 Yatsenko)._

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Neobank-embedded crypto exchanges sit inside the broader retail crypto-trading market, which in Europe is being reshaped by MiCA (uniform CASP licensing; transition deadline 30 Jun 2026, per multiple reports). Structure is consolidating around licensed operators: Coinbase (public), Kraken ($20bn private round Jan 2026, incl. $200m from Citadel Securities [[Kraken raises $800m at $20bn valuation]]), Bitpanda (targeting €4–5bn / up to ~$5.5bn Frankfurt IPO H1-2026 [[Bitpanda gears up for Frankfurt IPO in H1 2026]]), plus neobank challengers (Revolut X). Why now: (1) MiCA is the entry barrier and the moat — a pan-EEA CASP + MiCA licence is capital-and-compliance heavy, so distribution-rich neobanks that clear it gain a durable edge; (2) revenue is highly cyclical/volume-linked — Coinbase Q4-2025 revenue fell 20% to $1.8bn with a $667m net loss as prices/activity dropped [[Coinbase posts $667 million net loss as Q4 revenue falls 20%]], showing the beta of pure-play trading revenue.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: trading volume, take rate (fee %), assets/balances on platform. Basis of competition: fee/price + distribution. Fees — Revolut retail ~2.5%, Revolut X far cheaper after a ~96% fee cut in Mar-2025 (per Forbes); Coinbase pro ~0.4–0.5% taker, Kraken ~0.16–0.26% (per traders' comparison sites, as of 2026). Revolut's edge is distribution, not price at the app layer: 68.3m retail customers (FY2025) vs Bitpanda ~7m, giving a built-in funnel to Revolut X. Recent moves: Revolut secured a Cyprus MiCA/CASP licence (the unit Michael built), integrated Polygon processing $690m to date (Nov-2025 [[Revolut integrates Polygon, processing $690M to date]]), and is delisting USDT for EEA users under MiCA [[Revolut to delist USDT for European users under MiCA]]. Position: catching up as a challenger to Coinbase/Kraken on trading depth/liquidity, but ahead on distribution and multi-product embedding (analysis). Moat = distribution + regulatory licences (network/scale), not trading UX.

**Comps & multiples.** IR-grounded protagonist figures below (Wealth = crypto/exchange proxy). Peer valuations:
- Kraken: $20bn private round (Jan-2026); revenue not publicly split → EV/Rev = no data.
- Bitpanda: €4–5bn (~$4.7–5.5bn) targeted IPO valuation; ~7m users → price/user ~$670–790 (analysis, round valuation not market cap).
- Coinbase: public; Q4-2025 revenue $1.8bn (annualized run-rate volatile), net loss $667m → EV/EBITDA not meaningful this quarter.
- Revolut group: $75bn secondary valuation (Nov-2025, up 67% from $45bn); on FY2025 revenue $6.0bn → P/S ≈ $75bn / $6.0bn = 12.5x (rich vs a bank, in-line for hyper-growth fintech). Crypto is a slice, not the driver.
No 3+ comparable revenue multiples for the crypto units specifically → distribution not computed; qualitative comparison only. Internal comps: [[Kraken raises $800m at $20bn valuation]], [[Bitpanda gears up for Frankfurt IPO in H1 2026]], [[Coinbase posts $667 million net loss as Q4 revenue falls 20%]].

**IR-grounded figures (Revolut Group Holdings Ltd, Annual Report 2025, FY ended 31-Dec-2025):** Group revenue $6.0bn / £4.5bn, +46% YoY (2024: $4.0bn). Profit before tax $2.3bn / £1.7bn, +57%; net profit $1.7bn; PBT margin 38%. Retail customers 68.3m (+30%). The **Wealth** division — which houses the Revolut X crypto exchange — grew revenue **+298% to $647m in FY2024** (from $158m in 2023), per Revolut disclosure via DL News; this made crypto the fastest-growing line and the strategic context for the Cyprus unit Michael led. (Source: assets.revolut.com/pdf/annualreport2025.pdf)

**Risk flags.**
1. Key-person / execution transition: the founding CEO who secured the Cyprus CASP + MiCA licence is leaving to advisory status — regulatory relationships and licence-maintenance continuity carry key-person risk right as MiCA enforcement tightens (why: the licence is the moat; disruption during EEA rollout could slow the fastest-growing revenue line).
2. Revenue cyclicality/beta: Wealth's +298% jump rode a crypto up-cycle; Coinbase's -20% quarter shows how fast trading revenue can reverse — the segment's growth is not annuity-like (why: valuation credit for crypto growth can de-rate on a market turn).
3. Competitive squeeze on fees: Revolut X undercut itself 96% to compete with Kraken/Coinbase pricing — a race-to-the-bottom on take rate compresses the very margin that made Wealth attractive (why: distribution advantage may not fully offset thinner per-trade economics).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** A leadership handoff at a $647m, +298% unit is a watch-item, not a thesis-breaker: with the licence secured and distribution intact, near-term continuity is likely (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if Revolut X keeps compounding EEA volume post-transition, Revolut re-rates as a top-3 European crypto venue by users, pressuring Bitpanda's IPO narrative. Trigger/what-breaks-it: watch the FY2026 Wealth revenue print and any MiCA/CASP compliance friction in Cyprus — a stall or licence hiccup within 2–3 quarters would falsify it.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Note:** This is a leadership change (crypto-unit founding CEO Costas Michael steps down), not an earnings event. Revolut is private but publishes annual accounts. Figures below are from Revolut Group Holdings Ltd — Annual Report 2025 (consolidated FY ended 31 Dec 2025), the latest reported financials, provided as financial context.

**Source:** Revolut Group Holdings Ltd — Annual Report 2025, published 2026-04-29. drive_url: https://assets.revolut.com/pdf/annualreport2025.pdf (IR-covered, `ir_latest.json` key `revolut`).

**Latest reported annual figures (FY2025):**
- **Group revenue: £4.5bn, +46% YoY.** Fifth consecutive year of net profit.
- **Profit before tax: £1.7bn, +57% YoY.**
- **Net profit margin: ~29%.**
- **Revenue mix:** fee-based revenue = 76% of turnover (+4.2pp YoY), inverting the interest-heavy model of traditional banks. Largest single stream is card payments (22.2%), then interest income (21.6%), subscriptions (15.7%).
- **Wealth services (equities + crypto trading): 14.7% of revenue** — the segment relevant to this crypto-unit leadership change. FX services = 13.4%. 11 product lines exceeded £100m revenue in 2025.

**Customer growth:** added ~16m users in 2025; publicly reported surpassing 70m customers globally by mid-2026 (from ~50m), targeting 100m by mid-2027.

**Context for the leadership change:** Costas Michael was founding CEO of Revolut's Cyprus-based digital-assets unit and led its CASP/MiCA licensing across the EEA; he steps into a board-adviser role. Crypto sits within the wealth-services segment (14.7% of FY2025 revenue) — no standalone crypto revenue is broken out in the reported figures. Revolut has recently been building out its crypto leadership (e.g. hiring ex-Coinbase risk chief Michael Schroeder), so this is a transition rather than a retreat.

*(No standalone crypto-unit P&L is disclosed; the 14.7% wealth-services figure — equities plus crypto combined — is the closest reported proxy.)*
