OpenAI President Greg Brockman will continue to oversee the company's products after Fidji Simo officially stepped down from her role.
Simo, who served as the company's product and business chief, left her position due to chronic illness. 
"I am deeply grateful for all Fidji has done for OpenAI and to advance our mission," Brockman wrote in a post on X. 
There's a new second-in-command at OpenAI . 
 Greg Brockman , the company's president, is officially responsible for OpenAI's most important and profitable projects after Fidji Simo stepped down from her role on Thursday due to chronic illness.
Simo, a former Meta executive and ex-CEO of Instacart , served as OpenAI's product and business chief for about a year, focusing the company's road map and helping it scale. Simo, who was diagnosed with postural orthostatic tachycardia syndrome, or POTS, in 2019, took a medical leave in April, and said Thursday that she would transition to a position as a part-time advisor . 
Brockman, an OpenAI co-founder, took over product responsibilities during Simo's absence, and will continue to lead those efforts, according to a source familiar with the company's plans who asked not to be named due to confidentiality. Brockman will oversee OpenAI's ChatGPT product business, as well as its go-to-market teams, enterprise teams and compute initiatives, the person said.  
"I am deeply grateful for all Fidji has done for OpenAI and to advance our mission, and for the opportunity to have worked alongside her for the past few years," Brockman wrote in a post on X on Friday.
Reporting directly to CEO Sam Altman , Brockman is under pressure to bring in revenue and justify OpenAI's $852 billion valuation , especially as the company gears up for what's expected to be a historic IPO. OpenAI confidentially filed its prospectus with regulators in June, but the company hasn't disclosed when it plans to debut and is reportedly delaying until next year. 
OpenAI is also facing increasingly stiff competition from rivals, including Anthropic, Google and Elon Musk's SpaceX , along with a host of cheaper open-weight models primarily out of China. 
ChatGPT's market share fell below 50% for the first time in March, according to a report from Sensor Tower , and OpenAI has been aggressively touting its AI coding assistant, Codex , in an effort to win over more users.

Sarah Friar, OpenAI's finance chief, and Jason Kwon, the company's strategy chief, will report to Altman. The company doesn't plan to hire anyone to replace Simo, the person familiar said. 
Brockman co-founded OpenAI alongside Altman and a group of others, including Musk, in 2015. He and Altman have been close allies, and when Altman was briefly ousted from his role as CEO in 2023, Brockman quit the company in solidarity. Both men rejoined OpenAI days later. 
"Greg and I are partners in running this company," Altman wrote in a blog post at the time. "We have never quite figured out how to communicate that on the org chart, but we will." 
They were also both at the center of a high-profile legal brawl earlier this year. Musk sued Brockman, Altman and OpenAI, alleging they went back on commitments they made to keep the the AI lab a nonprofit. 
In federal court in Oakland, California, in May, Brockman testified about the startup's early years and pushed back on Musk's account of events. He was grilled about his personal financial ambitions, his understanding of OpenAI's structure and Musk's involvement at the company. 
Musk ultimately lost the case after an advisory jury said he waited too long to sue, a verdict that was immediately adopted by a federal judge. 
"I think the tech we are developing is transformative," Brockman said from the witness stand. "This is going to be the most important technological shift in human history."

## Market Research

**Sector & drivers.** Frontier AI labs — the org/commercialization layer, not model research. Size (revenue, not TAM): OpenAI's annualized run-rate is ~$25bn mid-2026 (~$2bn/mo), up from $21.4bn end-2025 and $3.7bn FY2024 — per Sacra/Tech Insider via web, as of mid-2026. Mix reported as ~$17bn ChatGPT subs, ~$6.5bn API, ~$1.5bn Sora/licensing; enterprise is >40% of revenue and "on track to parity with consumer by end-2026" (unverified single-source, treat as directional). Structure: oligopolistic and consolidating — a handful of capital-intensive labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, xAI) plus cheap Chinese open-weight models compressing the low end. Barriers = compute capital + capital-markets access, not IP per se. Why now: the move is timed to a confidential SEC filing (June 2026) ahead of a prospective IPO reportedly targeting >$1tn; consolidating product+enterprise+GTM+compute under one owner is a "get the P&L IPO-ready" signal. Second-order: whoever controls compute controls unit economics — bundling compute with GTM under Brockman means COGS and revenue now answer to the same person, which is what an S-1 narrative needs.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: revenue run-rate, enterprise/consumer mix, paying seats, and gross margin (compute cost per token). Key players & basis of competition: OpenAI (consumer distribution via ChatGPT), Anthropic (enterprise/coding, Claude), Google DeepMind (distribution + owned TPU compute), xAI (capital + X distribution), Chinese open-weight models (price). Competition is shifting from raw capability to distribution + price + enterprise trust. Recent dated moves: ChatGPT consumer share fell below 50% for the first time in March 2026 (Sensor Tower, via note) — the first hard sign of consumer erosion; OpenAI is pushing Codex to defend the coding segment where Anthropic leads; talent is flowing OUT to Anthropic (John Schulman, DeepMind's John Jumper — see internal comps). OpenAI's position: still #1 by consumer reach and run-rate, but catching up on enterprise/coding and losing the "most valuable lab" crown — Anthropic reached a ~$965bn post-money (May 2026) vs OpenAI's $852bn (March 2026). Moat: consumer brand + distribution (network/scale), NOT switching costs — enterprise buyers are demonstrably multi-homing.

**Comps & multiples.** Private labs, so these are round valuations (post-money), not market caps; run-rates are self-reported and un-audited — treat P/S as an order-of-magnitude sanity check, not a clean multiple.
- OpenAI: $852bn post-money (Mar 2026) / ~$25bn run-rate = **~34x** revenue.
- Anthropic: ~$965bn post-money (May 2026) / ~$47bn reported run-rate = **~21x** revenue. (An earlier $350bn term sheet was signed Jan 2026 — re-rated ~2.75x in ~4 months.)
- xAI: ~$230bn+ round (early 2026) / revenue not disclosed → **no data** on multiple.
Distribution not computed (only 2 comparable P/S figures). Flag: at ~34x revenue OpenAI is RICHER per dollar of revenue than Anthropic's ~21x despite Anthropic reporting the faster run-rate and higher valuation — i.e. OpenAI is being asked to grow into its price. Above the ~0.5–20x EV/Rev software range, but that is normal for hyper-growth; the flag is relative, not absolute — OpenAI must out-execute Anthropic on revenue to hold the multiple. **Risk flags.**
1. **Key-person / execution concentration.** Product + enterprise + GTM + compute now all report through one co-founder, with no plan to backfill Simo — a single point of failure right before an IPO, and Brockman is an engineer/president, not a proven consumer-product or enterprise-sales operator. Second-order: if he stumbles, there's no No.2 bench left.
2. **Leadership churn as a governance signal.** ~12+ senior exits in 2025 plus an April 2026 triple exit (Weil, Peebles, Narayanan); of 11 co-founders only Altman and Brockman remain. Consolidation may be less "strategic clarity" than "no one left to delegate to" — a diligence red flag for IPO buyers and a talent-retention risk as Anthropic actively hires the departures.
3. **Valuation must be grown into.** ~34x run-rate revenue while consumer share has already cracked below 50% and enterprise is contested by Anthropic — any revenue miss vs the IPO narrative de-rates hard; the IPO is reportedly already delayed toward 2027.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This reads as OpenAI shifting from a research-led to an execution-/revenue-led org ahead of a listing — the leadership has been almost entirely reconstituted around enterprise monetization. Falsifiable thesis: OpenAI's premium multiple vs Anthropic is unjustified unless run-rate growth stays ahead of Anthropic's. Trigger to watch: next disclosed run-rate + enterprise mix and the S-1 timing; the thesis breaks (OpenAI re-rates back up) if Brockman ships an enterprise/GTM inflection and consumer share stabilizes above 50%. Watch also whether the compute-under-GTM bundling actually improves disclosed gross margin.
