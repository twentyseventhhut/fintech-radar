Authologic enables Google Wallet ID verification for businesses
Sinziana Albu
06 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Authologic has enabled businesses to access Google Wallet identity credentials for KYC/AML verification.
The move extends the reach of Authologic's existing verification infrastructure to a wallet already used by consumers to store government, bank, and Google-issued credentials.
Google Wallet allows users to store verified identity credentials issued by governments, banks, or Google itself directly on their phones. Through Authologic's OmniID platform, businesses can now accept these credentials, including Google ID Pass, a digital passport available in several countries, among them the US, the UK, Brazil, Singapore, and Taiwan. When a business requests verification, users can share only the specific attributes required, such as a name or age confirmation, directly from their device, without submitting a full document.
OmniID connects businesses to a range of identity verification methods beyond Google Wallet, including government eIDs, EU Digital Identity (EUDI) wallets, mobile driving licences, bank-based verification, and biometric checks. In addition, the platform selects the verification method it determines most suitable based on a user's location and device, and provides fallback options for individuals who do not yet hold a digital credential.
 Jarek Sygitowicz , co-founder and chief strategy officer at Authologic, said the company's aim has been to make credentials already held in Google Wallet accessible to businesses running KYC/AML processes without requiring separate integrations for each wallet or credential type, describing the collaboration with Google as a step toward that goal.
Regulatory and market context
The announcement coincides with a broader shift in how identity credentials are issued and verified. Under the EU's eIDAS 2.0 regulation, member states are required to make digital wallets available to citizens by December 2026. In the US, twenty-one states currently issue mobile driving licences, with additional states expected to introduce them this year.
As governments and financial institutions issue more credentials directly to personal devices, the proportion of users able to verify their identity digitally is expected to increase. Authologic said this trend should reduce reliance on document uploads and manual review, processes that continue to contribute to the cost of KYC compliance for businesses operating across multiple jurisdictions.

# Context-enrichment: Authologic enables Google Wallet ID verification for businesses
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**FRESHNESS: fresh.** No prior Authologic note exists in the corpus (`grep -rli authologic News/` → none). This is first coverage of Authologic and of this specific Google-Wallet-into-OmniID integration (announced/reported ~2026-07-03 to 2026-07-06). Not a re-report. Related corpus notes exist on the same digital-ID-orchestration wave — [[Wultra raises €6.8M Series A for digital identity platform]], [[Didit raises $7.5 million for identity fraud infrastructure]], [[Barclays and Lloyds join UK banking digital ID plan]], [[Banca Transilvania and BPC deliver Romania's EU digital identity payment]] — but none cover this event.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **Authologic** (British-Polish, Warsaw HQ, founded 2020 by Krzysztof Klimczak, Jarosław "Jarek" Sygitowicz, Marek Rogoziński; YC-backed) added **Google Wallet credentials — including Google ID Pass — as an accepted verification source inside its OmniID orchestration platform**, so businesses running KYC/AML can now accept a Google-Wallet-held credential without building a bespoke integration. Reported by The Paypers on 2026-07-06; framed by outlets (PYMNTS, FF News, Biometric Update) as a "Google–Authologic digital identity partnership."
- **De-PR'd delta:** this is an **orchestration/relying-party integration**, not Authologic issuing IDs and not Google endorsing Authologic as sole partner. Authologic adds one more "rail" (Google Wallet) to an existing menu — government eIDs, EUDI wallets, mobile driving licences (mDLs), bank-based verification, biometrics — behind a single API. The genuinely new part is that a widely-distributed consumer wallet (Google) can now feed a regulated KYC flow via Authologic's hub with **selective disclosure** (share only name / age, not the full document).
- **Why structured this way:** Authologic's whole thesis is "don't integrate every wallet/credential yourself — integrate our hub once." Google Wallet is the highest-leverage single credential to add because of device reach. The PR anchors to Google's brand to signal legitimacy; the substance is that Google's **ID Pass** (digital passport) only reached scale in 2026 (US → UK, then Brazil/Singapore/Taiwan on 2026-04-25), so there is finally a credential worth orchestrating. **Announced-and-live for the integration; but the underlying credential base is still thin/nascent** — the value depends on how many consumers actually hold Google ID Pass.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Reusable-ID / orchestration peers:** Yoti (consumer reusable ID wallet + age verification), Onfido (now Entrust; document+biometric at enterprise scale), Persona (developer-configurable building blocks), Veriff (document/biometric + reusable profiles), Trulioo, iDenfy ([[iDenfy adds Smart-ID to identity verification for Baltic markets]]), Didit ([[Didit raises $7.5 million for identity fraud infrastructure]]), Wultra ([[Wultra raises €6.8M Series A for digital identity platform]]).
- **Position:** Authologic is **ahead specifically on the "eID/wallet aggregation hub" framing** (claims 40+ eID systems integrated) rather than on document-scan volume, where Onfido/Veriff dominate. The competitive question is not "who scans a passport best" but **"who becomes the orchestration layer between many wallets and many relying parties."**
- **+ Second-order dynamics:** The incumbents built moats on **document upload + liveness** — a stack that eIDAS 2.0 / mDL / Google-Apple wallets are structurally **commoditizing** (why upload a document when the phone holds a verified credential?). This is why Onfido sold to Entrust and why Persona/Veriff are adding "reusable" layers. Authologic is betting the value migrates from "capture the document" to "route the credential" (analysis). Risk: the wallet issuers (Google, Apple, EUDI member states) could expose relying-party APIs directly and **disintermediate the hub** — the same disintermediation pattern flagged for orchestration players elsewhere in the corpus.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Trajectory: founded 2020; earlier round ~€7.5–7.6M (OpenOcean); **$8.2M Series A led by OpenOcean (Oct 2024)** with Y Combinator, Peak Capital, SMOK VC. Named clients cited historically: Santander Leasing, eToro, LV Bet. Product evolution: from "automate KYC via government e-IDs" → OmniID = wallet-agnostic orchestration.
- **Fit:** logical and on-strategy. Authologic's entire positioning ("global eID hub / next evolution of KYC") makes adding Google Wallet the obvious next move the moment Google's ID Pass reached multiple countries in 2026. **Structural driver:** a hub's value is a function of coverage; each new credential/wallet added compounds the "integrate once" pitch — so Authologic is incentivized to be first to plug in every new wallet (Apple, Samsung, EUDI, Google) as a **land-grab for the orchestration position** before incumbents or the wallet issuers occupy it.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Really new:** the ability for a relying-party business to accept a **Google-Wallet credential (incl. Google ID Pass) for KYC/AML through a single third-party API with selective attribute disclosure.** That specific plumbing is new.
- **What's PR vs substance:** "partnership with Google" overstates — this reads as Authologic implementing against Google's wallet/credential interfaces (relying-party integration), not a bilateral commercial deal with Google as investor/sponsor. **No traction numbers disclosed:** no client count using the Google path, no volume, no conversion-lift figure. So value-add is **plausible but unproven** — a capability announcement, not adoption.
- **+ Who captures the margin (analysis):** In this stack, the **credential issuer** (Google, a government, a bank) holds the trust anchor; the **relying party** (the business) pays for verification; Authologic sits in the middle as the router. Margin accrues to the router **only if** integrating wallets stays hard enough that businesses won't do it themselves and issuers don't offer direct relying-party SDKs. Google offering its own age/identity verification APIs (it already ships age/identity verification features in Wallet) is the direct threat to that middle position. The real question shifts from "does Authologic support Google Wallet?" to **"will orchestration remain a paid layer, or does Google/EUDI make it a free commodity?"**

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Regulatory tailwind (the core driver):** eIDAS 2.0 requires EU member states to offer EUDI wallets (note text says by Dec 2026; the corpus [[Banca Transilvania and BPC deliver Romania's EU digital identity payment]] cites the eIDAS 2 mandate for banks to accept EUDIW for KYC/SCA by 2027; EWC target 80% of EU citizens with a state ID wallet by 2030). In the US, ~21 states issue mDLs. As device-held verified credentials proliferate, document-upload KYC declines — the tailwind Didit's and Wultra's rounds also ride.
- **Sentiment:** industry press (Biometric Update: "wallet interoperability takes shape as Google's influence grows") treats this as part of a 2026 inflection where wallets become the primary KYC surface and **orchestration/interoperability** is the strategic prize.
- **+ Counterintuitive second-order:** more credential standards and wallets (EUDI + Apple + Google + Samsung + mDLs) **increase** fragmentation short-term, which is bullish for aggregators like Authologic — but the same standardization drive (eIDAS interoperability, ISO mDL) that creates the wallets could later **collapse** the integration complexity that justifies a paid hub. Concentration around Google's wallet is double-edged: it gives Authologic a big credential to route, but strengthens exactly the platform most able to disintermediate it.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Identity verification (IDV) is a ~$14–17bn market in 2026 depending on definition and growing at a low-teens CAGR: per Mordor Intelligence, $14.19bn (2025) → $15.78bn (2026) at ~11.2% CAGR to 2031; per Coherent Market Insights, ~$16.05bn (2026) at ~16.5% CAGR (both secondary-research figures, as of mid-2026, treat as indicative not audited). The subvertical Authologic sits in — reusable/wallet-based ID and eID orchestration — is a fast-growing slice riding a regulatory catalyst: under eIDAS 2.0 all 27 EU member states must offer an EU Digital Identity (EUDI) wallet by Dec 2026 (target 80% adoption by 2030; per EU Commission / eIDAS readiness trackers). Parallel "why now" on the device side: Google Wallet added digital ID Pass / passport support across the US, UK, Brazil, Singapore, Taiwan and is extending to Spain, Italy, France, Ireland, Estonia by end-summer 2026 (per 9to5Google, Biometric Update, Jun–Apr 2026); Apple Wallet mobile driver's licences are live in ~13–14 US states + Puerto Rico with more pending (per MacRumors, May 2026). Structure: fragmented — dozens of document/biometric IDV vendors plus government eID rails; value is migrating up-stack from "capture-a-selfie-and-a-document" toward orchestration layers that route across wallets/eIDs. Barriers: regulatory accreditation, per-jurisdiction integrations, and fraud/liability track record rather than capital.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: verifications processed (volume), pass/completion rate, fraud catch rate, and coverage (countries × credential types). Key players split into (a) document+biometric IDV incumbents/disruptors — Onfido (acquired by Entrust, Apr 2024), Jumio, Veriff, Sumsub, Persona, Yoti, iDenfy, Fourthline, Trulioo, Vouched; and (b) the government/eID orchestration niche where Authologic competes — connecting businesses to EUDI wallets, government eIDs, mDLs, bank-ID and now Google Wallet via one integration. Basis of competition here is distribution/coverage and integration breadth, not just algorithm accuracy. Recent peer moves: Persona raised $200M at a $2bn valuation (May 2025, per SiliconANGLE); Veriff ~$1.5bn valuation / ~$110M ARR (2025); Trulioo launched credit-decisioning for onboarding and iDenfy partnered Axiology on DLT onboarding (both in-base, late 2025/early 2026). Authologic's position: niche/early — a specialist eID orchestration layer, not a scaled IDV incumbent. Its moat is (analysis) integration breadth + being an early Google Wallet business-side acceptance partner; it is a thin switching-cost/distribution moat, not a data or scale moat.

**Comps & multiples.** Authologic is private, YC/OpenOcean/Peak-backed, ~$8.79M raised over 4 rounds (per Tracxn/PitchBook via web) — no disclosed valuation or revenue, so **no multiple can be computed for the protagonist**. Peer reference points (round valuations, NOT market caps): Persona $2bn post-money (2025); Veriff ~$1.5bn (2025). Revenue multiples: Veriff ~$1.5bn / ~$110M ARR = ~13.6x ARR (round valuation basis, hyper-growth — in line for a fast-growing private IDV name, not an outlier flag); Persona valuation-to-revenue not computable (revenue undisclosed) → `[UNSOURCED]`. Distribution not computed (only 1–2 comparable figures). Internal comps in base: [[Entrust 2026 Identity Fraud Report spans one billion verifications (2)]], [[Fleur de Roos promoted to Chief Operating Officer at Fourthline]], [[Axiology partners iDenfy to streamline DLT capital markets onboarding]], [[Trulioo launches credit-decisioning capability for onboarding]], [[Veriffreports that about 4% of verification attempts in 2025 were fraudulent, and]] — none carry an Authologic-comparable valuation, so comparison stays qualitative: Authologic is 2–3 orders of magnitude smaller by funding than the unicorn IDV cohort.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Platform dependence / disintermediation.** Authologic's OmniID reach hinges on Google (and, by extension, Apple/government) issuing and opening wallet credentials. If Google offers businesses direct acceptance APIs — as it is already doing with wallet ID features — the orchestration middleman can be squeezed; margin and the relationship both sit one layer above Authologic's stack.
2. **Regulatory/standards execution risk.** The eIDAS 2.0 Dec-2026 deadline is real but technical specs remain incomplete and rollout is uneven across member states; a wallet-orchestration thesis is exposed to slippage in actual credential availability vs. announced coverage.
3. **De-PR gap — "enabled" ≠ adopted at scale.** The announcement is a capability launch; no business-adoption volume, pricing, or fraud-liability split is disclosed. Selective-disclosure attribute-sharing shifts, but does not clearly assign, fraud liability between wallet issuer, Authologic and the relying business.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is an early sign the IDV value chain is bifurcating: credential issuance concentrates in a few wallet platforms (Google/Apple/state eID), while a thin orchestration/acceptance layer competes to be the single business-side integration. Falsifiable thesis: reusable wallet credentials will compress the per-check price of document+selfie IDV over 2026–2028, pressuring capture-centric incumbents (Jumio/Veriff-style) unless they pivot to orchestration. Trigger to watch: whether Google ships a direct business-acceptance API (bypassing partners) and whether EUDI wallets actually go live across major member states by Dec 2026 — either the platform going direct, or the wallets not shipping, would break the thesis.
