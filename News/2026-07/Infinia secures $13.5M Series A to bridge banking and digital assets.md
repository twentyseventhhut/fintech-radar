## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** Infinia — a Uruguay-based (Montevideo) money-movement and account-infrastructure startup — raised a **$13.5M Series A led by Bain Capital and Variant Fund**, with participation from Y Combinator and Lattice. Capital goes to regional regulatory licences and expansion across LatAm, Africa and Asia. The digest framed the company as "global"; sources place its HQ in Uruguay.

**[1] What it actually does.** Compliance-first infrastructure bridging **stablecoins with local payment rails / A2A networks** in emerging markets: a unified API for cross-border payouts, collections and multi-currency virtual accounts that handles local regulatory guardrails natively. Named clients include **Stripe, Bridge and Despegar**. Founders Ianai Urwicz (CEO) and Alejandro Rettig; Ianai was on the founding team of **[[Kueski partners with dLocal to expand BNPL across Mexico|dLocal]]**, scaling it $0→$100M ARR — strong founder-market fit in emerging-market payments.

**[2] Why it matters.** Sits squarely in the 2026 stablecoin-rails wave: same thesis as [[M1X Global builds blockchain rails for sovereign financial instruments]] (blockchain financial infra) and the dLocal + Circle/BVNK stablecoin-payout partnerships already in-base ([[dLocal integrates Circle Payments Network for stablecoin payouts]], [[dLocal and Félix launch stablecoin WhatsApp remittances in LatAm]]). Emerging-market cross-border payments is exactly where stablecoins have live product-market fit; Infinia is picking up ex-dLocal DNA to attack it.

**[3] Prior art / competition.** Direct comparables: **Bridge (Stripe-owned)**, BVNK, and dLocal itself — Infinia counts Bridge and Stripe as *clients*, positioning as infra-below-the-infra rather than a head-on rival. Related in-base: [[Visa, Stripe, 140 firms launch Open USD stablecoin]], [[KakaoPay develops super wallet for stablecoins and tokenized assets]]. Regulatory tailwind from the MiCA licence rush ([[Sygnum secures MiCA CASP licence in Liechtenstein]], [[FalconX receives EU MiCA authorization]]).

**[4] Open questions.** Revenue/volume undisclosed (private, Series A). "Across LatAm, Africa, Asia" is expansion *intent*, not live coverage. Valuation not disclosed. Reliance on stablecoin regulatory clarity in each target market.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector.** Emerging-market cross-border payments infrastructure using stablecoins as settlement rails — the highest-conviction real-world stablecoin use case. 2026 has seen heavy activity: MiCA-driven licensing rush in Europe and a parallel EM build-out (dLocal + Circle/Félix stablecoin payouts, BVNK, Bridge/Stripe). Infinia's wedge is a compliance-first unified API connecting local A2A rails with stablecoin efficiency for payouts/collections/virtual accounts.

**In-base comps.**
- [[M1X Global builds blockchain rails for sovereign financial instruments]] — blockchain financial infra, seed $5.5M (Paradigm); adjacent, institutional-facing.
- [[dLocal integrates Circle Payments Network for stablecoin payouts]] and [[dLocal and Félix launch stablecoin WhatsApp remittances in LatAm]] — the incumbent Infinia's founders came from; both customer-benchmark and competitor.
- [[Visa, Stripe, 140 firms launch Open USD stablecoin]] — Stripe (an Infinia client) advancing its own stablecoin stack; coopetition signal.
- [[KakaoPay develops super wallet for stablecoins and tokenized assets]] — demand-side pull for stablecoin/local-rail bridges in Asia.

**Investors / validation.** Bain Capital (generalist tier-1) + Variant Fund (crypto-native) co-lead, plus Y Combinator (S23 cohort, prior $500K) and Lattice — a strong dual signal (traditional VC + crypto conviction).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Regulatory/licensing dependency** — expansion thesis hinges on securing EMI/CASP-type licences country-by-country across LatAm, Africa, Asia; slow or denied approvals directly cap growth.
2. **Coopetition with Stripe/Bridge** — named clients are also potential competitors who could internalise the local-rails layer, squeezing Infinia's position.
3. **Undisclosed traction & thin differentiation** — no revenue/volume/valuation figures; "compliance-first unified API" is a crowded pitch (BVNK, dLocal, Bridge), and EM payout margins are structurally thin and FX-exposed.

**IR grounding.** Not IR-covered (not in ir_latest.json); private Series A — no filings. Skipped.
