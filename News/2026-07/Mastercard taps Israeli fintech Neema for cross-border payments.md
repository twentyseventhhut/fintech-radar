## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** Mastercard signed an agreement to use Israeli cross-border fintech **Neema**'s technology infrastructure to power **Mastercard Move** inbound transfers to Israel — enabling businesses and individuals abroad to send money to Israel in real time (announced 2026-07-10, Connecting the Dots in Fintech). This positions Neema as an infrastructure/rails partner for Mastercard's disbursement network into an Israel corridor.

**[1] Who / what is Neema.** Neema (founded 2015, CEO & co-founder Moshe Kimhi) is a global cross-border payments platform for banks, fintechs and financial institutions, offering payout across 120+ destinations via a high-availability API. It reaches card rails (Visa, Mastercard, UnionPay, Elcart, Korti Milli, Humo, Uzcard) plus wallets. Its stack: [[Neema unveils dynamic routing engine for cross-border FX]] (May–Jun 2025, patent-pending Dynamic Routing® cutting payout cost up to 24%, +18% success rate), [[Neema unveils global Push-to-Card capability]] (Jan 2026, bank-less card payouts), [[Neema unlocks direct payment route to one billion Alipay wallets]] (Mar 2026, China corridor), and a Request-to-Pay collection tool (Dec 2025).

**[2] Why it matters.** For Mastercard, this extends **Mastercard Move** (its cross-border disbursement platform — ~200 countries, 150+ currencies, and per Mastercard growing from ~10bn toward ~17bn endpoints; transaction growth >35% in Q4 2025) via a specialist partner for the Israel inbound corridor rather than building it in-house. For Neema it is a marquee validation — moving from being a Mastercard *card* consumer to being *infrastructure inside Mastercard's own product*.

**[3] Pattern / prior art.** Mastercard has repeatedly partnered with regional/specialist fintechs to extend Move into corridors: [[Felix and Mastercard accelerate US-to-LatAm remittances]] (Nov 2025), [[FNB and Mastercard launch Globba cross-border payments]] (Nov 2025), [[Mastercard and Thunes bring stablecoin payouts to mainstream]], [[Mastercard and JD.com partner on cross-border payments]] (May 2026). The Neema deal fits the same corridor-by-corridor Move expansion playbook.

**[4] Open threads.** Deal is announced, not confirmed live at scale; no disclosed volume, corridor count, exclusivity, or economics. Whether "infrastructure" means white-labeled rails or a routing integration is unstated. Neema is private (no financials).

## Челлендж / ред-тим

1. **Is this genuinely new or a re-report of an older Neema–Mastercard link?** Neema already issued Mastercard-scheme payouts (Push-to-Card, Jan 2026). This deal is different: Neema as *infrastructure inside Mastercard Move* for Israel-inbound, not Neema using Mastercard rails. New — fresh.
2. **Is the deal live or just announced?** Announced only; no go-live date or volume. Open.
3. **What corridor/scope?** Stated as inbound-to-Israel real-time transfers; whether it later expands is unknown. Open.
4. **Exclusivity?** Not disclosed; Mastercard runs many parallel Move partners (Felix, FNB/Globba, Thunes). Likely non-exclusive.
5. **Economics for Neema?** Undisclosed. Revenue-share/per-transaction implied but no figures. Open.
6. **How material to Mastercard?** Immaterial to MA financials (one corridor); strategic Move-footprint story, not a revenue event. Answered.
7. **How material to Neema?** Highly material — marquee reference customer that could pull in other banks/fintechs. Answered.
8. **Competitive threat displaced?** Neema competes with Nium, TerraPay, Thunes, Wise Platform for the underlying rails; Mastercard picking Neema for Israel is a corridor win vs those. Answered.
9. **Regulatory/geopolitical risk?** Israel-inbound corridor exposed to regional instability and sanctions-screening complexity; also FX-control-light vs the China corridor. Partial.
10. **Does Mastercard's own "17bn endpoints" claim make Neema redundant?** No — Move's reach is aggregated via exactly such local partners; Neema supplies the last-mile Israel rail. Answered.
11. **Concentration risk for Neema?** Being a Mastercard supplier is a double-edged marquee — dependency risk if Mastercard later in-sources. Open.
12. **Any figures to verify?** No hard numbers in the announcement; MA Move endpoint counts (~10–17bn) come from MA marketing, treat as directional. Flagged.
13. **Duplicate of a prior note?** No prior note covers a Mastercard-Move-uses-Neema deal. Fresh.

Importance: 3/5 — a credible, strategically clean partnership (marquee validation for a private Israeli fintech; incremental corridor for Mastercard Move), but no disclosed volume/economics and immaterial to Mastercard financials, so mid-tier newsworthiness.
