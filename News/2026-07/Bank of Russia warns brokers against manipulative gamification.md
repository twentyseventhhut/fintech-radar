Геймификация  – это включение в процесс взаимодействия с потребителем элементов игры, 

снижающих критичность восприятия потребителями существенной информации о финансовых 

продуктах 4.

В нефинансовой сфере геймификация воспринимается скорее  как  приятное дополнение 

к основному сервису, не несущее рисков финансовых потерь. Это может быть выдача 

коллекционных игрушек за определенную сумму чека, накопление «звездочек»  для  получения 

бесплатного кофе  или  прохождение игр ради повышенного кешбэка. 

Сама по себе геймификация не является ни однозначно позитивным, ни заведомо негативным 

явлением – ее итоговое влияние на потребителя определяется совокупностью контекста, 

решаемых бизнесом задач и конкретных применяемых инструментов. В одних случаях 

игровые механики действительно способны помочь пользователю быстрее освоить сложный 

продукт, сформировать полезные привычки, например регулярно откладывать сбережения, 

или  просто сделать рутинные операции более увлекательными  без  какого -либо ущерба  для  его 

благосостояния. Такая этичная геймификация органично встраивается в канву потребительского 

опыта и служит интересам и клиента, и бизнеса. 

Однако игры  и игровые элементы иногда могут включать практики,  не  всегда отвечающие 

интересам конечного потребителя. Такие практики условно можно отнести  к «темным паттернам». 

«Темные паттерны»  – это маркетинговые и иные приемы (в том числе особенности дизайна 

клиентских интерфейсов и усложнения клиентского пути  для  совершения потребителем 

определенных действий  или  ознакомления с определенной информацией), подталкивающие 

потребителя к принятию определенного решения,  как  правило, выгодного  для  финансовой 

организации и невыгодного  для  потребителя, в процессе предоставления финансового продукта 

(дополнительной услуги). Например, когда финансовая организация намеренно скрывает важную 

информацию о самом финансовом продукте (дополнительной услуге), чтобы ввести потребителя 

в заблуждение и предоставить ему неподходящий финансовый продукт (дополнительную услугу) 5.

«Темные паттерны» можно разделить на две категории: маркетинговые 6 и психологические 7.

Банк России в ходе надзорной деятельности выявляет рост использования  ПУРЦБиКИ 

в дистанционных каналах различных способов  вовлечения  граждан в инвестиционную 

деятельность , в том числе  «темных паттернов» .

# Context-enrichment: Bank of Russia warns brokers against manipulative gamification
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **14 Jul 2026** the Bank of Russia (Consumer Protection Service) published a **consultation report** — доклад для общественных консультаций — "О геймификации, наджинге и иных практиках вовлечения потребителей на рынке инвестиционных услуг". Comments due **31 Aug 2026**.

**This is NOT a rule — it is a discussion paper.** The de-PR'd reality: the CBR is floating the idea of moving gamification from *soft law* (methodological recommendations № 22-МР of 27.12.2024 and № 1-МР of 20.01.2025 — both non-binding) toward a proper regulatory base. No ban is in force, no draft legal instrument exists, no effective date, no cost-benefit analysis. The headline framing "warns brokers" overstates it: the report explicitly calls gamification "neither unambiguously positive nor inherently negative" and dedicates a section to *legitimate* educational nudging. → Why structured as a доклад: the CBR self-admits the area currently has only recommendation-level cover; a consultation is the procedural first step before it can harden anything — so the news is **agenda-setting, not enforcement**.

The §3 proposals (8 options, all for discussion): (1) define formal criteria of "gamification"; (2) restrict high-risk types — betting/raffles/lotteries, nudges to buy *specific* instruments, tournaments that hide fees/losses/risk-segmentation (survivorship bias); (3) regulate risk-hiding interface design and promos rewarding suitability-test passing / specific-instrument trading / IPO participation / margin / turnover-or-deposit targets; (4) educational gamification must push only toward learning, not trades; (5) mandatory pre-compliance review of gamified content by brokers; (6) **assess treating push/SMS/email nudges as investment recommendations** (highest-impact lever — would trigger suitability duties); (7) define liability measures; (8) give clients a **right to opt out of gamification** (a "no gamification" checkbox). → Why item 6 matters most: recharacterizing marketing pushes as *individual investment recommendations* (borrowed from ESMA) would force suitability checks on the nudge engine and could gut nudge-driven monetization — but it is the least-developed ("assess feasibility").

De-PR'd scale that explains the "why now" (Moscow Exchange 2025 data, cited in the PDF): **40.1M private investors** (+5M in 2025), **76M accounts** (+11.7M), retail = **70.7% of equity volume**, 31.3% bonds, 56.5% derivatives; SPB Exchange 1.86M active accounts. CBR confirms it *receives complaints* about gamification (pop-ups/contests distracting from investing; push used to advertise games not market news) but publishes **no complaint count**.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Two axes: (a) the domestic brokers whose models are in scope; (b) the international regulators the CBR is copying.

**Domestic (in scope):** The report mostly anonymizes but footnotes name **Альфа-Инвестиции / Альфа-Турнир** (Alfa itself calls gamification an operating-income driver; tournament losses cited at 82–110% / ₽600k–3M) and **РИЧ — Russian Investment Championship** (Т-Банк's tournament; "участники РИЧ в октябре совершили сделки на 1,8 трлн руб."). Sber/SberInvestor, ВТБ Мои Инвестиции, БКС, Финам run comparable mechanics (tournaments, подарочные акции, coin games, streaks) but are **not named** — whether they are targeted is open.

**International comparators (the CBR is a fast-follower, not an originator):**
- **IOSCO** Digital Engagement Practices final report FR/07/2025 (19 May 2025) — CBR reproduces its 5 DEP examples.
- **ESMA** retail-investor-protection final report — source of the "push = individual recommendation" principle.
- **US SEC Investor Advisory Committee** DEP recommendations; **FINRA Rule 2111** (suitability); SEC DEP request-for-comment (Aug 2021, post-GameStop).
- **FCA (UK):** Nov 2022 gamification report; 2024 Occasional Paper 66 (push notifications +11% trades, prize draws +12% risky trades; ~9,000 consumers; hits low-literacy/women/18–34 hardest), under Consumer Duty (in force 31 Jul 2023).
- **AMF (France, 2023)** gamification/copy-trading experiment; **Robinhood** removed confetti Mar 2021; **Zerodha** "Kill Switch" as positive prior art.

→ Why the lay of the land is this way: the global regulatory consensus (IOSCO→ESMA→FCA→SEC) crystallized in 2021–2025; the CBR is importing a finished template rather than breaking ground. Second-order: because it is a follower, the *ideas* are battle-tested but the *domestic political friction* (NAUFOR, large brokers) is the real variable, not the substance.

## [2] Company history / fit
CBR retail-investor-protection trajectory: suitability-testing law (306-ФЗ, 31.07.2020) → **mandatory testing of non-qualified investors from 01.10.2021** → market-manipulation findings in **trading contests (2021–2022)**, cited in this very report → public criticism of broker gamification (Oct 2024) → **methodological recs № 22-МР (27.12.2024)** first defining "dark patterns"/"gamification" (non-binding) → № 1-МР (20.01.2025) → **qualified-investor threshold hikes** (asset test → ₽12M from 28.07.2025, → ₽24M from 01.01.2026; brokers opposed) → **this consultation (14.07.2026)**. Parallel 2026 threads: proposed ethics code for investment finfluencers, crypto-access testing.

→ Why the CBR acts this way: it wants the 40M-investor base to keep growing *and* to be protected — a structural tension. The qual-investor hikes push users toward "qualified" status to unlock risk; gamification that rewards passing tests exploits exactly that gate → the report is the natural sequel to 22-МР, closing the loop from "we defined dark patterns" to "we may now regulate them." This fits logically; it is not opportunistic.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Substance is real but incremental.** Genuinely new *for Russia*: (a) first signal to potentially *hard-regulate* gamification vs merely recommend; (b) a concrete **opt-out right**; (c) the **push-as-recommendation** idea. None is novel globally — all imported. **Traction = zero by construction**: it is a consultation, so there is no adoption, no enforcement, no draft, no timeline. The only hard "traction" is that the CBR already has *contest-manipulation enforcement cases on file* (2021–2022), giving it precedent to escalate.

→ Why the value-add is contingent: the paper's teeth depend entirely on which of the 8 options survives consultation. Opt-out checkbox + tournament-disclosure are cheap and likely; "push = recommendation" is disruptive and contested; everything else is soft. The margin-capturing insight: brokers' *client-acquisition and re-engagement* economics (подарочные акции are often fractional/off-quote and may not even cover the investor's own fees — the report flags this survivorship bias) are what is actually at stake, not trading fees per se. If pushes become regulated recommendations, the CBR effectively taxes the acquisition funnel of Russia's largest brokers.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Comment window to **31 Aug 2026**. Expect pushback from **НАУФОР** (self-regulator, historically resists tightening — it opposed the qual-investor hikes) and from Т-Банк (РИЧ) and Alfa (Alfa-Turnir) whose monetization leans on tournaments. As of 14 Jul, no NAUFOR/broker statement on this specific report was found (open). Realistic path: consultation → possible new Указание / Base Standard amendment or hardened recs in **2027+**; opt-out + tournament-disclosure most likely near-term; "push = recommendation" most contested. Watch for the CBR naming/fining a specific broker to set precedent (it has the contest cases to do so).

→ Why the market goes this way / counterintuitive second-order effect: consultation papers usually land soft, but the *combination* of a 40M-investor base, named large brokers, and an already-existing enforcement track record makes this more than boilerplate. Counterintuitively, the biggest brokers may *prefer* a hardened rule with a clear opt-out over the current ambiguity — a bright-line reduces reputational/complaint risk and locks in a level playing field, while the smaller, tournament-dependent players bear the cost.

**Sector & drivers.** This is a Russian retail-brokerage / investor-protection regulatory event, not a company deal — no valuation/round/metrics attach; comps are precedent-based, not trading multiples. Market size (from the CBR paper text, sourced to Moscow Exchange 2025 data): **40.1mn private investors** (+5mn in 2025), **76mn brokerage accounts** opened (+11.7mn), retail = **70.7%** of equity trading volume, **31.3%** of bonds, **56.5%** of the derivatives market; SPB Exchange counted 1.86mn active accounts (as of 2025, per CBR paper). Retail inflows hit a record **RUB 2.5trn** in 2025 (per CBR, via the paper). Structure: the "40mn investors" headline is deeply hollow — only **~5.5mn accounts hold >RUB 10k**, and ~1mn qualified investors control **77%** of retail assets and **70%** of the 2.5trn inflow (per Finance Magnates, as of 2026). That gap is the "why now": the account base was inflated by bank/broker acquisition funnels (free shares, gamified onboarding) rather than genuine investing — which is precisely the behavior the CBR paper targets. Barriers to entry in RU brokerage are regulatory (broker license + CBR supervision) and distribution (bank-ecosystem app reach); the value chain is captured by the large bank-brokers (Sber, T-Bank/Tinkoff, VTB, Alfa) whose apps embed the games. Driver/headwind: this is a **consultation paper** (comments to **31 Aug 2026**), i.e. pre-rulemaking — announced intent to set gamification criteria, cap high-risk mechanics, regulate push/SMS/email nudges, and set accountability, NOT a live rule (de-PR: nothing is binding yet).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs the RU retail-broker race runs on: **funded accounts** (not gross accounts), **trading activity / turnover**, and **customer-acquisition via app engagement** — exactly the metrics gamification juices, which is why the CBR flags a conflict (broker revenue rises with churn/frequency vs. client interest). Key players are the bank-ecosystem brokers who own the mobile funnel; app "games, tournaments, marketing-quests, raffles" cited in the paper are their acquisition/retention tools. Protagonist (CBR) position: proactive rule-setter converging with global norms (ahead vs. its own prior light-touch stance), consistent with its broader 2026 consumer-protection push [[Russia tightens counter-sanction limits on retail non-resident clients]].
