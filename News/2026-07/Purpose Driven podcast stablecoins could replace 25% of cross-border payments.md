# Context-enrichment: Purpose Driven podcast — "stablecoins could replace 25% of cross-border payments" (Triple-A's Eric Barbier)
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
A **podcast appearance**, not an event. On the "Purpose Driven" podcast (host Michel Breeuwer), **Eric Barbier, founder & CEO of Triple-A**, argues stablecoins could take **25% of cross-border payments** ([episode](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wKzKsHiLsj8); circulated 2026-07-03 via Connecting the Dots / StrictlyVC). There is **no deal, product, or number of Triple-A's own** — it is a thesis interview.

**The "25%" is a headline, not a forecast.** Two problems:
- **No stated denominator.** Total cross-border flows are **~$190tn (2023) → ~$290tn by 2030** (McKinsey 2025 Global Payments Report). 25% of $190tn ≈ **$47tn/yr** — larger than the *entire* adjusted annual stablecoin transaction volume today (~$11tn, Visa/Allium). So "25% of all cross-border" is arithmetically absurd near-term; the figure can only make sense against a **narrow B2B / EM-remittance sub-segment**, which the title conveniently omits (→ this is the load-bearing ambiguity).
- **His own spoken number is lower.** Summaries of the same conversation quote Barbier at **"10–20% over the next two-to-three years"** — i.e. the "25%" is the marketing wrapper on a "10–20% by ~2028–29" claim. Even that is aggressive: stablecoins are **<1% of cross-border payment volume today** (FXC Intelligence).

**Why framed this way:** Barbier sells stablecoin payment rails; the claim is directly self-serving (anti-PR flag). His pedigree (Mobile 365→SAP; TransferTo→Thunes/DT One, ~$400M exit) buys him a hearing, but the number is a vendor talking point, not a modeled projection.

## [1] Competitors / peers
This is a **thesis** shared across the whole stablecoin-payments cohort, not a Triple-A differentiator. The real corridor traction sits with:
- **Bridge (Stripe), Conduit (~$10bn annualized), Orbital (~$12bn annualized)** — the largest B2B stablecoin cross-border processors (FXC).
- **Mastercard Move + Thunes** (near-real-time stablecoin payouts, see [[Mastercard and Thunes bring stablecoin payouts to mainstream]]); **TerraPay/Cobre** LatAm corridors ([[Cobre and TerraPay partner for cross-border payments]]); **Circle Payments Network, Ripple/RLUSD**.
- Triple-A's own edge is **regulatory coverage (US/EU/SG) + merchant accept/payout**, not volume leadership — it is a mid-size PSP, not a rail-setter. **+ Why:** the margin in cross-border stablecoins accrues to whoever controls **liquidity/on-off-ramp and the corridor**, not the merchant front-end; Triple-A risks being a distributor on top of Bridge/Fireblocks-class rails.

## [2] Company history / fit
Triple-A (founded 2017, Singapore; **$10M Series A Oct 2023** led by Peak XV + Shorooq + 1982 Ventures) is a regulated crypto/stablecoin payment institution — accept + payout so merchants never hold crypto. Clients cited: Grab, Farfetch, Razer. It fits Barbier's remittance-inefficiency thesis logically (his Thunes/DT One lineage is literally cross-border money movement). **+ Why he evangelizes:** a mid-size PSP needs category tailwind and mindshare; loud, quotable forecasts are cheap distribution. See prior Triple-A notes: [[Triple-A's Eric Barbier on stablecoins as cross-border rail]] (March 2026, Stablecoin Stories — the earlier, softer version of this exact thesis), [[Triple-A strengthens cross-border payment infrastructure with Mastercard Move]], [[Triple-A secures MiCA CASP licence from France's AMF]].

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Novelty of the claim: ~zero.** Barbier made the same "stablecoins are the best cross-border rail" argument in **March 2026** ([[Triple-A's Eric Barbier on stablecoins as cross-border rail]]); this is a re-airing with a sharper (unfounded) percentage. **Real underlying traction (the substance):**
- B2B is the genuine bright spot: **~$226bn ≈ 60% of stablecoin payment volume is B2B**; B2B stablecoin payments rose from **<$100M/month (early 2023) to >$6bn/month (mid-2025)** — ~60× (Artemis). Corpus corroborates: [[Stablecoin payments jump 70% since the GENIUS Act]] ($10bn+ in Aug for real payments, B2B two-thirds, ~$250k avg transfer).
- But absolute base is tiny vs the TAM, and stablecoins remain **<1% of cross-border** (FXC). **+ Why the 25% breaks:** getting to 25% of any broad cross-border denominator requires >25× real-usage growth against bank/treasury preference for insured deposits, FX on/off-ramp spreads in exotic corridors, and MiCA's squeeze on USD stablecoins in the EU.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
**Third-party forecasts don't back 25%.** Bullish houses forecast **stablecoin market cap, not payment share**: Standard Chartered ~**$2tn by 2028**; Citi base **~$1.9tn / bull $4tn by 2030**. Skeptics: **JPMorgan** rejects a $1tn-by-2028 market; **BIS 2025 AER** says stablecoins fail "singleness/elasticity/integrity" and stay small. Tailwind = **US GENIUS Act** (signed 18 Jul 2025, 1:1 reserves); headwind = **EU MiCA** restricting non-euro (USD) stablecoin usage (transition ends 1 Jul 2026). **+ Second-order:** the honest read is directional (B2B corridors are genuinely inflecting) but the "25% of cross-border" number is a marketing figure with **no independent analyst corroboration**; realistic near-term share is **low-single-digit %**, with B2B/EM corridors the wedge over 3–5 years.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Cross-border payment *flows* are ~$208tn (2025, per FXC Intelligence), but the addressable *revenue pool* is only ~$625bn (FXC Intelligence, 2025) — flows are huge, monetization thin; B2B is the largest slice (~72.8% of revenue share, Grand View Research, 2025). Stablecoins remain a rounding error inside that base: total stablecoin market cap ~$290–315bn (mid-2026, DefiLlama/StableCoin.com), of which USDT ~$184bn and USDC ~$73–77bn (~83% combined). Cross-border **B2B** stablecoin payments were only ~$13.4bn in 2026, with Juniper Research projecting ~$5tn by 2035 (via CoinDesk, 2026-04-27) — a forecast, not a run-rate. "Why now": the US GENIUS Act (2025) set 1:1 reserve/liquid-asset backing rules, giving regulated issuers a compliance moat and pulling banks (JPMorgan Kinexys, Japan/Italy bank consortia) onto the rails `(analysis)`. Barbier's own "25% of cross-border" is a *founder forward claim*, not a measured share — de-PR'd, current stablecoin penetration of cross-border value is low-single-digit at best `(analysis)`.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: settlement volume (TPV), take rate on FX/settlement, on/off-ramp coverage (corridors, licences), reserve-return dependence for issuers. Layers: (1) **issuers** — Circle (USDC), Tether (USDT); (2) **rails/orchestration** — Triple-A, Bridge (Stripe), BVNK, Cross River, Thunes/Mastercard Move, Ripple; (3) **incumbents defending** — Swift (ISO 20022 cutover), correspondent banks, Wise. Recent moves: [[Mastercard and Thunes bring stablecoin payouts to mainstream]] (2025-11); [[Cross River launches unified stablecoin payment platform]] (2025-11); [[Coinbase pulls out of $2B BVNK acquisition]] (2025-11); Triple-A–Neo treasury partnership (2026-06-23, per triple-a.io). Triple-A's position: **niche orchestrator/PI** — licensed in US/EU/Singapore, >1,000 enterprise customers, merchant acceptance + auto-conversion to local FX (per triple-a.io); not an issuer, so it captures a thin take rate on top of someone else's stablecoin and rails `(analysis)`. Moat = licences + corridor coverage (switching cost is low; no network effect vs the issuers).

**Comps & multiples.** Triple-A is private, no disclosed valuation/volume → **no data** on its multiple. Best public proxy is the issuer layer: Circle (CRCL) — Q1'26 revenue + reserve income $694m (+20% YoY), reserve income $653m, USDC circulation $77.0bn (+28% YoY), on-chain volume $21.5tn (+263% YoY) (Circle IR, Q1 2026; IR slug `circle` present). Circle's economics are ~94% reserve (interest-rate) income, NOT payment take-rate — so it is a *rates* proxy, not a clean comp for a payments orchestrator `(analysis)`. Internal comps: [[Coinbase pulls out of $2B BVNK acquisition]] anchors a stablecoin-rails asset at a ~$2bn ask (deal collapsed → mark as indicative, not cleared). EV/Revenue arithmetic not computable for Triple-A (both numerator and denominator undisclosed) → **[UNSOURCED]**. Qualitative: issuer layer is where value pools today (Circle at scale); the orchestration layer Triple-A sits in is fragmented and thin-margin — distribution not computed, qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disintermediation / thin-margin squeeze** — Triple-A adds a layer on top of Circle/Tether + card networks (Mastercard Move, Thunes); as issuers and networks push direct payout (Mastercard–Thunes, 2025-11), the orchestrator's take rate is the first to be competed away. Second-order: no pricing power without an issuer or a proprietary corridor.
2. **The 25% claim is unfalsifiable PR** — current cross-border stablecoin B2B (~$13.4bn) vs the flow base (~$208tn) implies well under 1% penetration; a jump to 25% would need a ~1,000x re-rating of adoption. Treat as marketing, not a forecast; risk that the note's headline anchors an inflated TAM.
3. **Issuer/reserve fragility feeds through** — S&P cut USDT to "weak" (2025-11, BTC in reserves, disclosure gaps); any de-peg or reserve scare on the underlying stablecoin hits every orchestrator's settlement instantly. Regulatory divergence (GENIUS Act vs EU MiCA vs Asia) also fragments corridors.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Reads as a **new-entry/land-grab** story, not a re-rating: stablecoins are genuinely eating specific corridors (LATAM, Asia B2B), but the value accrues to *issuers* (Circle-type reserve economics) and *networks*, not to thin orchestration PIs like Triple-A `(analysis)`. Falsifiable thesis: if regulated issuers/networks launch native cross-border payout at scale, standalone orchestrators get squeezed to sub-scale niches. Trigger to watch: whether Triple-A discloses TPV/take-rate (silence on economics is telling) and whether a network (Mastercard/Visa) or issuer (Circle Arc) absorbs the orchestration layer.
