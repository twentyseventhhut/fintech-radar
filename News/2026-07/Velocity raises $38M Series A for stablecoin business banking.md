# Context-enrichment: Velocity raises $38M Series A for stablecoin business banking
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**Identity confirmed (disambiguation).** This is Velocity of London ([velocity.xyz](https://www.velocity.xyz/)), an enterprise stablecoin treasury/settlement infrastructure startup founded 2025 — NOT Velocity Global, Velocity Black (Amex), Velocity Ledger, or Velocity Frequent Flyer. Came out of stealth May 2025 with a $10M pre-seed; the $38M Series A was announced 2026-07-14 (Fortune "exclusive").

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **$38M Series A**, announced **2026-07-14**. **Leads: Dragonfly + FirstMark Capital.** Participating: Coinbase Ventures, Capital One Ventures, QED, Activant (pre-seed lead, following on), Ripple, Wintermute Ventures.
- **Valuation: NOT disclosed** — CEO declined. Any implied number is unverified. Cumulative raised ≈ **$50M** ($10M pre-seed 2025 + $38M now); the digest's "help businesses tap stablecoin growth" is PR framing.
- **Use of funds:** expand the global banking/payments network; obtain licenses for **Africa and LatAm**; build custody; develop **yield-generating stablecoin products**; deepen compliance. Note the crypto-incumbent-heavy cap table (Coinbase, Ripple, market-maker Wintermute) — strategic/distribution money, not neutral capital.
- **Live vs announced:** platform is live in **US, parts of Europe, Australia** with a working USDC API, SOC 2 Type I, ISO 27001. Africa/LatAm, custody, and yield are **roadmap, not live**.
- **→ Why structured this way:** a ~14-month-old firm raising $38M with **zero disclosed volume/client metrics** and an undisclosed valuation is being funded on **team pedigree + sector momentum**, not proven scale. The Fortune "exclusive" drop + crypto-native leads signal a narrative-first raise. **This is the anti-PR core:** substance = credible team and rails; the "business banking" tag oversells a still-thin, mostly-USD product.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Velocity targets CFO/treasury teams (treasury + settlement orchestration), placing it next to BVNK, Conduit, Modern Treasury — not the card-issuers.
- **BVNK** — enterprise B2B/cross-border treasury; ~$20bn+ processing volume ([[BVNK surpasses $20bn in processing volume]]); being acquired by **Mastercard for up to $1.8bn** ([[Mastercard to acquire stablecoin firm BVNK for up to $1.8bn]]). The dominant, bank-backed incumbent Velocity must out-execute.
- **Conduit** — cross-border B2B payouts, 15+ currencies/100+ countries, backed by **Dragonfly** (Velocity's own lead) and Circle. Same lead investor in an adjacent peer → possible portfolio steering/consolidation angle.
- **Rain** — enterprise stablecoin cards/infra, $250M Series C at $1.95B ([[Rain raises $250M Series C at $1.95B valuation]]); far larger and better-capitalized.
- **Checker** — small stablecoin payment-network raise ([[Checker raises $8 million for stablecoin payment network]]) — shows how commoditized the "stablecoin payment network" pitch is at the low end.
- **Daya** — African stablecoin cross-border ([[Daya raises $2.4M pre-seed for African stablecoin cross-border payments]]) — an incumbent already in Velocity's stated Africa expansion target.
- **→ Why this landscape / second-order:** the segment is crowded and consolidating upward (Mastercard buying BVNK and Zerohash, [[Mastercard to acquire crypto firm Zerohash for ~$2 billion]]). The scarce asset is **distribution + a bank/network parent**, not tech. Velocity's differentiation ("hold, move, settle in one platform") is positioning, not a moat. The real question shifts from "is stablecoin treasury a market" (yes) to **"can a sub-scale independent survive between Mastercard-owned BVNK and a $1.95B Rain, or is it an acqui-target?"**

## [2] Company history / fit
- Founded **2025, London**. Co-founders at pre-seed: **Tom Greenwood** (founder of Volt real-time payments; IFX) and **Eric Queathem** (CEO; ~9yrs Worldpay incl. its crypto/global-payouts division; ex-McKinsey). Series A coverage foregrounds **Queathem only** — Greenwood's current involvement is **open**.
- **Pre-seed:** $10M / £7.42M, 2025-05-28, led by Activant (Fuel, Triton, Fabric, Commerce, Digital Space, Preface participating). Product ("Stablecoin Payment Account") consistent from day one — **no pivot**.
- **→ Why the company acts this way:** the founders come from payments/FX rails where take-rates are commodity; stablecoin settlement lets them collapse prefunding and claim a software-multiple story. Structural logic is coherent; execution/traction is the gap.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's actually new:** marginal. The pitch (stablecoin settlement + local banking rails + compliance + custody + yield for treasury teams) is the **BVNK/Conduit playbook**. Claimed novelty is packaging breadth and eliminating prefunding.
- **Traction:** **none disclosed** — no clients named, no TPV/GMV, no corridors, no revenue. Only USDC confirmed. Live-in-3-regions + SOC 2/ISO are table stakes, not traction.
- **→ Why value-add is thin, deeper:** in this stack the **margin is captured by whoever owns the bank/network endpoint** (Visa/Mastercard, or a chartered parent). An independent orchestrator risks being disintermediated as BVNK (Mastercard) and Bridge (Stripe) bundle the same capability into owned distribution. Velocity's yield ambition also sits in a **regulatory grey zone** (GENIUS restricts issuer yield). Value-add is real only if it locks in enterprise integrations before the giants commoditize the layer.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** licenses for Africa/LatAm; custody build; yield products; deeper banking network.
- **Sentiment:** frothy/positive — Fortune exclusive, crypto-heavy cap table, "stablecoins going mainstream in business finance."
- **Regulatory backdrop:** UK is a **near-term headwind** — BoE policy statement + draft Code (2026-06-22), FCA core rules finalized (2026-06-29/30, 1% stablecoin capital vs MiCA's 2%), £40B per-coin issuance ceiling; authorization window 2026-09-30→2027-02-28, **full regime effective 2027-10-25**. So regulated GBP stablecoins won't be live until 2027 → Velocity must lean on **USD/USDC and non-UK corridors** now. US **GENIUS Act** and EU **MiCA** are the frameworks actually in play today.
- **→ Why / counterintuitive second-order:** the crypto-incumbent cap table (Coinbase, Ripple, Wintermute) is a double edge — it buys distribution but also flags Velocity as a likely **eventual acquisition target** rather than a standalone winner. Capital + strategic backers concentrating in the segment makes a sub-scale name **fragile-to-consolidation, not safe**.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: stablecoin-based business banking / treasury & settlement for cross-border B2B payments. Market sizing (attribute, treat as vendor/analyst estimates, not audited): cross-border B2B stablecoin transaction value ~$13.4bn in 2026, projected to $5tn by 2035 (per Juniper Research, via CoinDesk, 2026-04-27); B2B is cited as ~60% of "real" stablecoin payment activity, reaching ~$226bn annualized in 2025 (per openfx/reap secondary compilations, 2026 — unverified primary, treat as directional). Structure: fragmented and pre-consolidation — a stack of on/off-ramp orchestrators (Bridge, BVNK), API rails (Stripe, Modern Treasury), and now treasury/banking front-ends (Velocity, Flex). Value is migrating up-stack from raw ramps toward CFO-facing treasury tooling; entry barriers are regulatory (licenses per corridor) and banking-network access, less so tech. Drivers + "why now": (1) GENIUS Act (US federal stablecoin framework, signed July 2025) plus EU/Singapore/UAE/Japan regimes give corporates legal cover to hold/settle in stablecoins; (2) incumbent validation — Mastercard agreed to acquire BVNK for up to $1.8bn (2026), signaling the rails are being absorbed into card networks. `(analysis)` The "why now" is regulatory de-risking, not new tech: the primitive existed; policy made it board-approvable.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs (Velocity discloses none of these — all `[UNSOURCED]` for the company): annualized settlement/payment volume (TPV), take rate on settlement, number of live corridors, prefunding capital freed. Key players & basis of competition: BVNK (~$30bn annualized volume in 2025, 2.3x YoY, per Forrester/press) and Bridge (Stripe-owned) compete on breadth of ramps and corridors; Airwallex competes on 200+-country local-payment connectivity and is now adding tokenized products; Flex ($70M Series B1, 2026-07-14, "business banking on stablecoin rails," 32 currencies / 170 countries) is the closest direct peer and raised the *same day* as Velocity. Recent moves: Mastercard–BVNK deal (2026); Airwallex reversing its stablecoin skepticism (2026); Coinbase's aborted $2bn BVNK bid ([[Coinbase pulls out of $2B BVNK acquisition]], 2025-11). Protagonist position: `(analysis)` late/niche entrant — founded May 2025, ~$50m total raised vs peers at $30bn+ volume and $1.8bn exits. Differentiator claimed: CFO/treasury-team targeting (not crypto-native businesses) and removing prefunding, led by ex-Worldpay exec Eric Queathem (9 yrs, corporate strategy/M&A/payouts). Moat is thin and mostly team/distribution intangible `(analysis)`; no disclosed network effect or switching cost yet.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disintermediation / late entrant into a consolidating stack.** BVNK is being bought by Mastercard and Bridge by Stripe; the rails are being absorbed by networks with distribution Velocity lacks. Second-order: a sub-scale treasury front-end risks being squeezed between card-network-owned rails below and banks' own tokenized products above.
2. **Regulatory & licensing dependency across corridors.** Velocity's stated growth (Africa, LatAm) is gated on obtaining licenses per market; each is a capital/time cost and a single point of failure. Second-order: expansion timeline is exogenous to product execution — slips are hard to control.
3. **Investor-set / geo ambiguity + zero disclosed traction.** Round is US-VC-led (Dragonfly, FirstMark, Capital One Ventures, QED) with a Fortune "US exclusive" framing, while the note tags region/uk — actual HQ/regulatory home is unclear from the corpus (flag, not a claim). No TPV, corridor count, or revenue disclosed, so the raise cannot be underwritten on metrics — it is a team/thesis bet `(analysis)`.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` Two same-day raises (Velocity $38M, Flex $70M) plus the Mastercard–BVNK deal mark the sub-vertical shifting from ramp-building to CFO-facing treasury banking — a land-grab for enterprise treasury relationships before networks lock them in. Falsifiable thesis: Velocity re-rates up only if it publishes real corridor/TPV traction within ~12 months; absent that, the more likely outcome is acqui-hire by a network/bank rather than independent scale. Trigger to watch: first disclosed volume metric or a corridor license grant (Africa/LatAm) — its absence is the tell.
