NOBA Bank, Tieto Banktech partner to improve payment security
Paula Albu
07 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
NOBA Bank Group has partnered with Tieto Banktech to implement Verification of Payee (VoP) functionality across its brands, Bank Norwegian and Nordax Bank, as part of its fraud and payments framework. Finland is planned as the first market for the rollout, with the underlying infrastructure designed to support deployment across all markets where NOBA operates.
Responding to rising payment fraud
The implementation comes as payment fraud continues to increase across Europe. According to a joint 2025 report by the European Banking Authority (EBA) and the European Central Bank (ECB), the total value of payment fraud in Europe rose from EUR 3.5 billion in 2023 to EUR 4.2 billion in 2024, with new fraud types continuing to emerge. The two companies said the rollout is intended to strengthen NOBA's existing fraud and payments framework in response to this trend.
Technical scope and deployment model
The implementation is planned to include both European Payments Council (EPC) Verification of Payee and Nordic Verification of Payee (NVOP), allowing NOBA Group to address Euro-denominated payments as well as payments across Nordic markets. The solution will be delivered as a software-as-a-service (SaaS) offering hosted on AWS public cloud infrastructure, which the companies said is intended to support a scalable rollout across European markets.
A company official at Bank Norwegian said the implementation is intended to build on the bank's existing security position without requiring a trade-off between security, convenience, and scalability, adding that the aim is to maintain the payment experience customers expect while adding an additional layer of protection consistently across markets. A company official at Tieto Banktech said the partnership is intended to help NOBA Group continue strengthening its defences against evolving fraud risks while maintaining a seamless payment experience for customers, drawing on the company's cloud-based Verification of Payee solution.
Supporting consistency across markets and brands
For NOBA Group, which operates across several European markets, the implementation is intended to support a more consistent payment experience across its different brands and geographies. Rather than implementing Verification of Payee on a country-by-country basis, the platform is designed to provide a configurable and scalable foundation that can be adapted across brands and markets as the rollout expands beyond Finland.

# Context-enrichment: NOBA Bank and Tieto Banktech partner to boost payment security
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **2026-07-02** (Tieto press release; The Paypers wrote it up 2026-07-07), **NOBA Bank Group** signed **Tieto Banktech** (the banking software arm of Finland's **TietoEVRY**) to implement **Verification of Payee (VoP)** across NOBA's brands **Bank Norwegian** and **Nordax Bank**. De-PR'd, this is a **vendor software procurement**, not a strategic alliance: NOBA is buying a **SaaS VoP module** hosted on **AWS public cloud** to satisfy a **regulatory obligation**. The solution covers both **EPC VoP** (Euro / SEPA) and **Nordic VoP (NVOP)** (Nordic-currency rails). **Finland is the first rollout market**, with the platform "designed to" extend to all NOBA markets.

**Why now / what it reveals**: VoP is **mandated**, not optional. Under the EU **Instant Payments Regulation**, the **EPC VoP scheme was mandatory for euro-area PSPs from 9 October 2025**; **non-euro-area EEA PSPs (Norway, Sweden) have until 9 July 2027**. NOBA operates deposit/credit products in the euro area (incl. Finland — hence "first market") plus the Nordics, so it needs **both** EPC VoP and NVOP. Choosing a **single SaaS vendor** to deliver "configurable, scalable, cross-brand" VoP is the de-PR'd point: NOBA is **outsourcing a compliance commodity** rather than building per-country, avoiding fragmented implementations across Bank Norwegian / Nordax / multiple geographies. No contract value or duration disclosed.

**Named execs (thin)**: Andreas S. Pedersen (Head of Tech/Projects/Product, Bank Norwegian); Sathish Padmanathan (Head of Global Payments, Tieto Banktech). Quotes are generic ("best possible experience", "state-of-the-art"). No metrics.

## [1] Competitors / peers (VoP / payment-security vendors)
VoP is a **crowded, deadline-driven vendor market** created by the Oct-2025 mandate:
- **SurePay** (NL) — the original name-check / VoP incumbent, pan-EU.
- **Form3, Bottomline, LSEG/Refinitiv, Moody's, iPiD, Senzing** — VoP / IBAN-name-check routing.
- **EBA CLEARING (RVM)** and **Swift** — VoP directory / routing infrastructure that vendors plug into.
- **Tieto/TietoEVRY** — competes as a **Nordic-native core-banking + payments SaaS** vendor bundling VoP into its transaction-banking platform.
Tieto's angle vs pure VoP specialists: **incumbency in Nordic core/card/payments** (it already runs card issuing + instant-payments rails for Nordic banks) and **NVOP** coverage, which the SEPA-only specialists don't natively address `(analysis)`.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **NOBA Bank Group** = the rebrand of the merged **Nordax Bank + Bank Norwegian** (merger completed ~2021–22); Nordic specialist/niche bank, HQ Stockholm, backed by **Nordic Capital + Sampo**. **IPO'd on Nasdaq Stockholm in Sep 2025 at ~$3.7bn (SEK 35bn)** — see [[Digital bank NOBA sets Sweden IPO at $3.7B valuation]]. Operates Bank Norwegian, Nordax, Svensk Hypotekspension; deposits/cards also in DE/ES/NL/IE. A **public** niche bank now under scrutiny to show clean compliance + low credit losses.
- **Tieto Banktech / TietoEVRY** = large Finnish/Nordic IT-services + banking-software group; **market-leading financial-SaaS provider in the Nordics**. Already visible in this corpus signing Nordic banks to its platform: [[Cultura Bank partners with Tieto Banktech on core banking platform]] (2025-12, full end-to-end core banking incl. financial-crime tooling). Also won a **Nordic Tier-1 card-issuing/payments contract (Dec 2023)**. VoP is a natural add-on module to that stack.

**Fit**: NOBA needs multi-brand, multi-market, dual-scheme (EPC+NVOP) VoP fast and cheaply; Tieto already sells exactly that as SaaS to Nordic banks. Low-friction, obvious pairing.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Low novelty.** This is a **compliance-driven vendor selection** for a **standardised, regulator-mandated** feature — VoP is being bought by ~3,000 euro-area PSPs on the same clock. Genuine specifics: (a) **dual EPC VoP + NVOP** in one SaaS (moderately notable — covers both Euro and Nordic rails); (b) **AWS public-cloud SaaS** delivery (cloud-first for a regulated bank); (c) **Finland-first, cross-brand** rollout design. **Traction is nil/unproven**: no go-live date, no transaction volumes, no fraud-reduction metrics, no contract value. Everything is "planned"/"designed to."

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Watch for a **Finland go-live** and any **fraud-reduction / false-positive** numbers (none yet). Broader arc: VoP is **table-stakes fraud control** layered on top of tougher EU rules — cf. [[EU agrees new rules on online payment fraud protection]] (Nov 2025, shifting APP-fraud liability onto PSPs). Sentiment: neutral/routine vendor-win PR. **Second-order**: as a newly-public bank, NOBA showing proactive, cross-market fraud tooling is mild reputational/regulatory hygiene; for Tieto it's another Nordic reference logo to sell VoP against SurePay et al.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Segment = **payment-fraud prevention / Verification of Payee (VoP) tooling** for banks, at the intersection of RegTech and payments infrastructure. **Why now is regulatory, not commercial**: under the EU **Instant Payments Regulation (IPR)**, the European Payments Council **VoP scheme became mandatory for euro-area PSPs on 9 October 2025** (~3,000 banks/PSPs in scope); **non-euro-area EEA PSPs (incl. Norway, Sweden) have until 9 July 2027**. This is a **hard-deadline compliance market** — demand is mandated, so vendors compete on integration speed, scheme coverage, and cost, not on creating demand. Layered on top: the EU's tougher **PSD3/PSR** and the Nov-2025 political agreement shifting **APP-fraud liability onto PSPs** ([[EU agrees new rules on online payment fraud protection]]) raise the cost of *not* having robust name-check + transaction-freeze tooling. Backdrop cited in-note: EBA/ECB report total EU payment fraud rose **€3.5bn (2023) → €4.2bn (2024)**.

**Competitive landscape.** VoP/name-check vendors: **SurePay** (NL incumbent, pan-EU IBAN-name check), **Form3**, **Bottomline**, **LSEG**, **Moody's**, **iPiD**, plus routing/directory infra from **EBA CLEARING (RVM)** and **Swift**. **Tieto Banktech / TietoEVRY** competes as a **Nordic-native core-banking + payments SaaS** vendor that bundles VoP into an existing transaction-banking stack — differentiators: (1) **dual EPC VoP + Nordic VoP (NVOP)** coverage (SEPA-only specialists don't natively do NVOP); (2) **incumbency** in Nordic core banking, card issuing (won a Nordic **Tier-1 card-issuing/payments** contract Dec 2023) and instant payments; (3) recent Nordic reference logos — [[Cultura Bank partners with Tieto Banktech on core banking platform]] (2025-12, full core + financial-crime tooling) and now NOBA. Basis of competition = scheme coverage + speed-to-comply + cloud delivery, not price alone. Tieto positions as "market-leading financial-SaaS provider in the Nordics" (vendor claim, unverified market-share figure).

**Comps & context (deal terms undisclosed).** No contract value, term, or go-live disclosed → **no multiples computable**. Qualitatively this is a **small, routine module sale**, not a platform migration. Adjacent internal comps for scale/context:
- [[Cultura Bank partners with Tieto Banktech on core banking platform]] (2025-12) — *broader* Tieto win (full end-to-end core banking incl. anti-financial-crime), so a higher-value engagement than a VoP-only module; useful as the "same vendor, bigger scope" reference.
- [[Digital bank NOBA sets Sweden IPO at $3.7B valuation]] (2025-09) — the customer: a **listed** (~SEK 35bn / $3.7bn) Nordic niche bank, Nordic Capital + Sampo backed; explains the compliance-hygiene motive.
- [[BNP Paribas Bank Polska partners with Snowdrop Solutions]] (2026-07) and other Paypers "Fraud & Fincrime" items (LexisNexis/Promon, Experian Identity Connect, Authologic/Google Wallet) — same week's cluster confirming a **fraud/identity vendor-procurement wave** across European banks.
- External: SurePay et al. serve the same VoP demand — no public per-deal pricing, so **qualitative: routine, mandated, no over/under-pay signal**.

**Risk flags (3).**
1. **Compliance-commodity, low-differentiation.** VoP is scheme-standardised and mandated; margins and stickiness are thin, and NOBA can switch schemes/vendors at moderate cost. For Tieto the moat is bundling into its core stack, not VoP itself — a soft moat.
2. **Efficacy gap vs the headline fraud problem.** VoP curbs IBAN-name **misdirection** and some APP fraud, but **socially-engineered APP scams** (victim authorises payment to a correctly-named mule) largely defeat name-check. Marketing this as answering the €4.2bn fraud surge oversells VoP's actual coverage — necessary-not-sufficient. `(analysis)`
3. **Third-party / cloud-concentration (DORA) exposure.** Delivering a regulated bank's fraud control as **SaaS on AWS public cloud** concentrates operational + outsourcing risk; under EU **DORA**, critical-ICT-provider dependence invites supervisory scrutiny, and an AWS/Tieto outage would degrade payment-integrity checks across NOBA's brands.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Confirms the **mandated-VoP procurement wave** (Oct-2025 euro-area deadline; Jul-2027 for Nordics) is now flowing to **mid-cap listed Nordic banks**, and that **incumbent core-banking vendors (Tieto) are capturing VoP by bundling** rather than losing it to pure-play specialists (SurePay). Falsifiable triggers to watch: (a) a disclosed **Finland go-live + fraud-reduction / false-positive metrics** would upgrade this from PR to proof; (b) if NOBA later expands the Tieto relationship to core banking (as Cultura did), the "land-and-expand" thesis validates; (c) if SurePay/Form3 win comparable Nordic banks, Tieto's NVOP+incumbency edge is weaker than claimed.
