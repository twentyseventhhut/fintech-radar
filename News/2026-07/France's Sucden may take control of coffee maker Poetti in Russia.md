 Французская Sucden могла получить контроль над бизнесом производителя кофе Poetti 
Компания «Милфудс» договорилась о стратегическом партнерстве со структурой Sucden в России ООО СДС, следует из письма контрагентам (копия есть у “ Коммерсанта ”). Соглашение не будет эксклюзивным и направлено на получение доступа к расширенному ассортименту зеленого кофе. Изменения в структуре управления «Милфудс» не предполагаются. Другие параметры соглашения в компании не привели.
Частью сделки, вероятно, стала передача в залог подконтрольному структурам Sucden ООО «Агросервис» доли в «Милфудс» единственного текущего владельца бизнеса Викаса Сои. Соответствующий договор был заключен в июне 2026 года, следует из данных СПАРК. Это обеспечение по товарному кредиту, пояснили в Sucden. «Милфудс», по словам представителя компании, закупает у нее кофе, регулярно увеличивая объемы. На это требуется финансирование.
Sucden — крупная французская продовольственная компания, специализируется на поставках сахара, кофе, какао. В России компания работает с 1991 года и известна как производитель сахара. Sucden принадлежат четыре завода в Липецкой, Пензенской областях и Краснодарском крае. За год они могут выпускать 800 тыс. тонн белого сахара и большой объем побочных продуктов. В управлении компании 260 тыс. га сельхозземель. Ранее в Sucden официально заявляли о намерении сохранить российские активы.
ООО «Милфудс» существует с 2008 года. В 2022 году компания выкупила российские активы финского производителя кофе Pauling. На его мощностях в Твери запустили линейку Poetti. Викас Сои ранее был главой российской Milagro Beverage Company (бренды кофе Milagro, D’Arte, Belagio). Активы этой компании, вероятно, вошли в периметр «Милфудс». Сейчас ей принадлежат права на официальный сайт и бренд Milagro.
До прихода в кофейный бизнес господин Сои работал в холдинге «Мегаполис» Игоря Кесаева и Сергея Кациева. Этих предпринимателей на рынке называли конечными владельцами «Милфудс», но официально информация не подтверждалась. Выручка ООО «Милфудс», согласно СПАРК, в 2025 году составила 9,46 млрд руб., увеличившись на 93,9% год к году. Чистая прибыль была 427,9 млн руб. против убытка годом ранее.
В Sucden говорят, что планов входить в уставный капитал «Милфудс» нет. Хотя один из источников на аграрном рынке убежден, что фактически речь идет о завуалированной покупке. Другой собеседник издания знает, что «Милфудс» прицельно искала покупателя на бизнес. Инвестбанкир Илья Шумов оценивает бизнес «Милфудс» в 2,5–3 млрд руб.
Илья Шумов замечает, что подобная сделка могла бы быть для Sucden частью сценария вертикальной интеграции. Он замечает, что после 2022 года структуры французской компании уже заключали в России сделки купли-продажи. В 2024 году они выкупили ООО «Агро Эко Мокшан» у турецкого агрохолдинга Arbel Group. Консультант практики оценки бизнеса и активов Neo Елизавета Варова объясняет, что бизнес по обжарке кофе гарантирует Sucden в России устойчивый сбыт и позволит заработать на марже готового продукта.
Передачу доли в залог госпожа Варова называет получением контроля без формальной покупки. Это может быть промежуточным этапом в большой сделке по приобретению: схема гарантирует, что актив не перейдет конкурентам. Хотя инвестиционный директор BGP Capital Юрий Левицкий замечает, что бизнес «Милфудс» растет и требует оборотного капитала. Когда банковское финансирование дорогое, кредитором вполне может выступить поставщик. В таких случаях, по словам эксперта, обычно предполагается, что объем займа сопоставим с масштабом заемщика. Исходя из оценки господина Левицкого, размер бизнеса «Милфудс» мог составить 2–3 млрд руб.
Старший партнер Lidings Андрей Зеленин называет передачу в залог доли в бизнесе в рамках товарного кредита необычной схемой. Традиционно все ограничивается банковскими гарантиями, иногда требуется в поручительство собственника. Младший партнер «Ляпунов Терехин и партнеры» Анастасия Ляпунова объясняет, что эта модель слишком сложна для оформления обычной отсрочки платежа. Советник практики корпоративного права МКА «Аронов и партнеры» Антон Новосельцев замечает, что в договоре может быть отдельно зафиксировано право Sucden оставить долю за собой по цене независимого оценщика или передать ее третьему лицу без проведения торгов.

# Context-enrichment: France's Sucden may take control of coffee maker Poetti (Milfoods) in Russia
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
De-PR'd: there is **no announced acquisition**. What actually exists (per Kommersant, 2026-07-15, sourced from a "letter to counterparties," not an official release):
1. A **non-exclusive "strategic partnership"** between Russian coffee roaster **Milfoods LLC** (owns the **Poetti** brand; ex-Paulig Tver plant, bought 2022) and **OOO SDS**, the Russian arm of French soft-commodities trader **Sucden** (Sucres et Denrées), to give Milfoods access to a wider **green-coffee** range. No management change planned.
2. In **June 2026**, Milfoods' sole owner **Vikas Soi pledged his stake in Milfoods** to Sucden-controlled **OOO Agroservis**. Sucden calls this **collateral for a commodity/trade credit** financing Milfoods' rising coffee purchases; it says it has **no plans to enter Milfoods' charter capital**.
3. The "control"/"veiled purchase" framing comes from **unnamed market sources**, not from Sucden or Milfoods. Milfoods 2025 revenue **9.46bn RUB (+93.9% YoY)**, net profit **427.9m RUB** (prior-year loss); banker Ilya Shumov values it at **2.5–3bn RUB**.

**Why structured this way (the core red-team):** the deal is deliberately built as *trade credit + equity pledge* rather than a share purchase. → An **un-enforced pledge** does not change the shareholder register, so it plausibly sits **below Russia's inbound foreign-investment approval trigger** for "unfriendly"-country investors (France is on Russia's unfriendly list). → It hands Sucden **economic control-leverage without a formal M&A** that would require the Government Commission, an independent appraisal, and disclosure. → The "letter to counterparties" (not a press release) surfacing the story is consistent with a party that does **not** want this framed as an acquisition. The honest read: **de facto control is a plausible interpretation of a 100%-equity security interest, but formally this is a financing arrangement, not a confirmed takeover.**

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Structural peer inside the corpus:** [[VTB takes large Ozon share stake as collateral]] — same mechanism (a large equity stake taken as *collateral* rather than an outright purchase), showing the pledge-as-quasi-control pattern is a recurring Russian-market structure, not a Sucden novelty.
- **Russian consumer/agri M&A comps (2026):** [[Ekto Prime fund becomes owner of Top Lubricants plant]], [[Mother and Child Group buys Ilyinskaya Hospital for 8.5bn rubles]], [[Neofarm acquires Astrakhan pharmacy chain Na Zdorovye]], [[Mergers.ru Sveza acquires Pharmaceutical Packaging Center]] — the domestic consolidation backdrop; almost all are Russian-buyer deals. A **French buyer increasing exposure is the outlier** here.
- Coffee-roasting peers in Russia (post-exit landscape): the segment was reshaped when foreign roasters left in 2022 (Paulig sold to Soi; the Poetti brand was launched *on* those ex-Paulig assets). Position: Sucden would be **catching a downstream asset** cheaply via financing rather than competing head-on.

**Why the lay of the land is this way:** post-2022 exits stripped foreign roasters out; local players (Milfoods) picked up the plants at distressed terms. → A green-coffee *trader* (Sucden) is a natural upstream partner/creditor for a cash-hungry local roaster. → Second order: the control-via-collateral route is the *dominant* deal grammar in this market right now (cf. VTB/Ozon) because outright transfers are legally/politically expensive on both the exit (35%+ tax, 60% discount) and inbound (commission approval) sides.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Sucden in Russia since 1991**; four sugar plants (Lipetsk x2, Penza, Krasnodar), ~800k t white sugar/yr, ~250k ha farmland (source states ~260k). Publicly on record (via Russian-unit CEO Etienne Pelletier, Feb 2026) as **intending to stay** — unusual post-2022.
- **Prior inbound Russian M&A: 2024 purchase of OOO "Agro Eko Mokshan"** (grain/elevator) from Turkish Arbel Group — confirms a consistent "keep buying Russian agri-assets" posture. The pledgee "Agroservis" traces to Sucden's earlier TRIO Group sugar acquisition.
- **Milfoods:** founded 2008; 2022 bought Paulig's Russian assets (brands/recipes excluded), launched Poetti at Tver; Soi previously ran Milagro Beverage Company and worked at Megapolis (Kesayev/Katsiev).

**Why the company acts this way:** Sucden is a **commodity trader on thin take-rates** on green coffee. → Financing/controlling a fast-growing roaster (+94% revenue) captures **downstream roasting + branding margin** and **locks in an offtake buyer** for its green-coffee flow — textbook vertical integration for a trader trying to move up the value chain. → It does this via *credit* not *equity* to keep the Russian-asset footprint growing without tripping approval/appraisal machinery.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new:** the July 2026 disclosure itself (a June-2026 pledge). No prior public Sucden/Milfoods tie-up was found before this — so as an *event* it is **fresh**.
- **What's NOT new:** the *mechanism* (equity-pledge-for-credit as quasi-control) is not novel — see VTB/Ozon in-corpus. And "control" is **not confirmed** — Sucden denies charter-capital entry; only unnamed sources call it a veiled buy. So there is **no traction/closure to report**: no completed transfer, no register change, no approval on record.
- **Fintech relevance: LOW/marginal.** The only financial-technology/structuring hook is **structured trade finance** — a commodity-credit facility collateralized by 100% of the borrower's equity. This is secured-lending/structured-trade-finance, not payments/digital-banking/embedded finance. Better fit for M&A / commodities / Russia-sanctions coverage than for a fintech feed.

**Why the value-add is thin (for a fintech reader):** the "innovation" is a *legal wrapper* (pledge routing around approval), not a product, platform, or rail. → Who captures margin: Sucden captures downstream roasting margin + supply lock-in; Milfoods gets working capital when bank financing is expensive. → What breaks it: if Sucden ever **enforces** the pledge, the transfer *would* likely trigger Government Commission review — the structure's whole benefit evaporates at the moment of formal control.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Likely path (hypothesis): the pledge is a **placeholder/option** ensuring the asset can't go to a competitor while Sucden decides whether to convert to formal ownership. Lawyers quoted (Lidings' Zelenin, Aronov & Partners' Novoseltsev) call the equity-pledge-for-trade-credit structure **"unusual"** and note the contract may embed a right for Sucden to keep the stake at an appraiser's price or on-sell it without auction — i.e. an embedded call option.
- **Regulatory backdrop:** inbound deals by "unfriendly"-country investors need Government Commission approval; the un-enforced pledge appears designed to defer that trigger. Exit-side rules (60% discount, 35%+ budget contribution, Presidential sign-off >50bn RUB) don't bind Sucden directly since it is *adding*, not exiting, exposure.
- **Risks:** sanctions/reputational (Sucden's continued Russian presence is already contested by advocacy outlets — treat war-support allegations as unverified/contested); enforcement risk on the pledge; and the deal being re-characterized by regulators as a disguised acquisition.

**Why the market goes this way / second-order:** when both exit and inbound formal transfers are expensive, **collateralized-credit control becomes the equilibrium deal structure** for foreigners who want Russian exposure. → Counterintuitive effect: a French firm *deepening* Russian exposure while peers flee raises its **idiosyncratic sanctions/reputational tail-risk** even as it lowers its transaction/approval cost — cheaper deal, more fragile franchise.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Relevance caveat.** This is a commodities/consumer FMCG M&A story (French sugar-and-soft-commodities trader Sucden edging toward control of Russian coffee roaster Milfoods, maker of the Poetti/Milagro brands). Direct fintech angle is minimal; the read below stays tight and focuses on the deal structure, the Russian foreign-transaction regime, and the sanctions/capital-controls risk that is the transferable signal for a cross-border-into-Russia deal.

**Sector & drivers.** Russia's coffee market is ~$4.3bn in 2025, forecast to ~$5.1bn by 2030 (~3.1% CAGR); ground-coffee retail runs ~70–85k tonnes/yr in 2026 (per StrategyHelix / IndexBox, via web, as of 2026). Milfoods 2025 revenue was RUB 9.46bn, +93.9% YoY, with RUB 427.9m net profit vs a prior-year loss (per SPARK, via Kommersant/Mergers.ru, 2026-07-15) — i.e. a fast-growing roaster in a low-single-digit-growth market, gaining share off a base built on the 2022 buyout of Finnish Paulig's Russian (Tver) assets. Structure: post-2022 the Russian FMCG/agri space is dominated by the *exit* wave — 80 foreign firms left in 2025, ~547 fully exited since 2022; exit deals were just ~23 in 2025 (~8% of M&A volume) and roughly halving yearly (per KSE/Kyiv Post, Russia Post, via web). Why now: Sucden is the rare *stayer that buys* — it publicly reaffirmed intent to keep its Russian sugar assets and in 2024 bought Agro Eko Mokshan from Turkey's Arbel Group; the Milfoods move fits a vertical-integration logic (green-coffee supply → roasting margin → captive offtake) (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** KPIs for a roaster: roasting/private-label volume, green-bean sourcing cost, gross margin on finished product, retail brand share. Sucden's edge is upstream: it is a major global green-coffee/sugar/cocoa trader (~800k t/yr Russian sugar capacity, four plants, ~250k ha land, 1991-onward presence) — supplying Milfoods green coffee and taking Soi's Milfoods stake in pledge to Sucden-linked OOO Agroservis (June 2026) secures offtake and blocks rivals. Basis of competition here is supply security + capital, not brand marketing. Milfoods is a niche-but-scaling domestic roaster (Poetti, Milagro, D'Arte, Belagio) whose owner Vikas Soi was reportedly seeking a buyer; two market sources call the "strategic partnership / товарный кредит" framing a disguised acquisition — Sucden denies plans to enter Milfoods' charter capital.

**Comps & multiples.** Milfoods valued at RUB 2.5–3bn (investment banker Ilya Shumov) / RUB 2–3bn (Yuri Levitsky, BGP Capital), via Kommersant. On RUB 9.46bn 2025 revenue that is EV/Revenue ≈ 3.0bn / 9.46bn ≈ 0.3x — a low-single-digit-tenths multiple, i.e. valued as a thin-margin private-label roaster, not a branded FMCG asset (net margin ~4.5% = 427.9m/9.46bn). No listed pure-play RU coffee comp to cross-check; treat as qualitative. Internal comps (recent RU control-change M&A, base): [[Mergers.ru Sveza acquires Pharmaceutical Packaging Center]], [[Neofarm acquires Astrakhan pharmacy chain Na Zdorovye]], [[Ekto Prime fund becomes owner of Top Lubricants plant]], [[VTB to sell former Otkritie office to Wildberries-Russ]] — all domestic-buyer consolidation; a Western buyer *adding* Russian exposure (this deal) is the outlier vs the corpus and the market.

**Risk flags.**
- *Sanctions / reputational exposure (French/EU buyer).* Increasing Russian footprint post-2022 cuts against EU sentiment; Sucden already faces NGO/press scrutiny over its Russian operations. A French acquirer deepening Russian assets carries headline and secondary-sanctions risk that could impair its core global trading franchise — a second-order cost far larger than the RUB 2–3bn asset.
- *Government-commission / approval regime.* A formal acquisition of a Russian business by an "unfriendly"-jurisdiction party needs Government Commission approval and, above RUB 50bn asset value, presidential consent; exit/transfer rules now impose a ≥60% mandatory discount and ≥35% budget contribution on *sellers* (per Cleary/Morgan Lewis/EY, via web, 2024 rules) — the pledge-not-purchase structure is plausibly designed to sidestep this regime, but that same structure invites regulatory recharacterization risk.
- *Capital controls / profit repatriation.* Even if Sucden captures the roasting margin, moving rouble profit out to France is constrained by counter-sanction limits on non-resident/unfriendly capital flows (cf. [[Russia tightens counter-sanction limits on retail non-resident clients]]); returns may be trapped onshore.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) Signal, not a re-rating: against an exit-dominated market, a Western trader is *consolidating* a Russian consumer asset via a debt-collateral back door — a template for staying-firms to build control "without formal purchase" and dodge the exit-discount/contribution regime. Watch for (trigger) an actual equity transfer / Government-Commission filing, or a Sucden call/put on the pledged stake being disclosed — that would confirm the "disguised acquisition" thesis; continued denial + no filing would falsify it and keep it a mere trade-finance pledge.
