# Context-enrichment: ConnectOne rolls out nCino AI agents across commercial lending
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5. Freshness: FRESH (distinct US bank + distinct product layer — the Agentic Operating System — vs the prior [[DNB goes live on nCino platform for corporate lending]] Banking-Advisor go-live; both are vendor-PR nodes in the same nCino AI-agent campaign)._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **Vendor-sourced customer story, dated 14 Jul 2026** (nCino PR / GlobeNewswire; StockTitan, Yahoo, Manila Times rewrites; FinTech Futures item 16 Jul is the retelling in this note). Single-source = **nCino's own PR**; no independent ConnectOne-IR confirmation located.
- **What is actually LIVE:** **nCino Banking Advisor** (the conversational-AI layer) in production, PLUS **two custom AI agents built on nCino's Agentic Operating System (AOS)**, together comprising **16 distinct "skills"**, targeting high-friction commercial-lending tasks. "Additional capabilities rolling out over the coming months" = roadmap, not live.
- **Metrics disclosed (this is the differentiator vs prior nCino notes):** active users **+41% in 10 weeks**; document/credit-policy search **~20 min → ~30 sec (97.5% reduction)**; headline **~60% task-time reduction** on the targeted lending tasks; ConnectOne's stated **goal = 50% frontline-efficiency** uplift. → Why flag: these are **vendor-supplied, self-reported operational metrics** for narrow, cherry-picked tasks (document search), not audited loan-cycle or economic outcomes (no ACV, no loan-volume, no cost-save $). Adoption (+41% users) is real signal; the 60%/97.5% figures are best-case task micro-benchmarks, not portfolio-level productivity.
- **Why structured this way:** the PR foregrounds a *US community/commercial bank building its own agents* on nCino's AOS — precisely because nCino's growth narrative now needs to show that the AOS is a **platform others build on**, not just a feature nCino ships. ConnectOne is the proof-of-concept logo for "customer-built agents," which is a stronger monetisation story than "we shipped an assistant."

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Agentic-banking / AI-agent-in-workflow vendors:** **Backbase** (bought **Kasisto** Jun 2026 to add agentic banking — [[Backbase acquires Kasisto to advance agentic banking]]); **Uptiq** ($25M Series B Feb 2026, AI agents that automate lending/wealth workflows — [[Uptiq raises $25M Series B for bank AI agents]]); **Lama AI** ($12M Series A, AI agents in banking); Temenos (NVIDIA GenAI), Salesforce Agentforce (nCino is Salesforce-native, so partly adjacent/partly overlapping).
- **Core/LOS incumbents whose lending workflow this augments:** Finastra, Temenos, FIS/Fiserv, MeridianLink.
- **Position:** nCino is **ahead on the "bank-buildable agent platform for commercial lending" specifically** — the AOS + Digital Partners (five role-based agents: Executive, Analyst, Service, Processor, Client) is a more productised agentic story than Uptiq/Lama (smaller, funding-stage) or Backbase (just acquired the capability, integration pending). → Why: nCino already owns the commercial-lending system-of-record for ~2,700 institutions, so it can drop agents *on top of the data/workflow it already holds* — a structural distribution advantage rivals lack. The real contest is not features but **who owns the underlying lending data the agents act on**.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **ConnectOne Bancorp (NASDAQ: CNOB):** NJ-based **commercial bank focused on small-to-middle-market businesses**; post-merger with **The First of Long Island** (completed **2 Jun 2025**) it runs **~$14B assets / ~$11B loans / ~$11B deposits** — a top-5 community bank on Long Island. A digitally-forward community bank is exactly the kind of buyer that adopts agentic tooling to punch above its headcount. → Why ConnectOne acts this way: a ~$14B commercial bank cannot out-hire tier-1 competitors, so **per-banker productivity (the stated 50% goal) is the lever** to defend commercial-lending margins against larger rivals — the AI-agent adoption is a cost/efficiency play, not a growth-tech vanity project.
- **nCino (NASDAQ: NCNO):** growth thesis = **AI (Banking Advisor → Digital Partners → AOS) + expansion**. AOS launched 2026 as the layer letting banks build on top of native Banking Advisor with their own data/workflows, "governed within the same trust infrastructure" (auditable/explainable). ConnectOne is the flagship **US** customer-built-agent reference — complementary to [[DNB goes live on nCino platform for corporate lending]] as the **European** Banking-Advisor go-live. → Why nCino leads with these logos within ~2 weeks of each other: it is a coordinated proof campaign to convert the "agentic AI" narrative into pipeline for a **decelerating (~8–9% FY27 guide) SaaS name**.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Genuinely newer than the DNB note in one respect:** ConnectOne is building **custom agents on the AOS** (platform extensibility), not just consuming nCino's shipped assistant — that is the more defensible, higher-monetisation motion. And unlike most nCino PRs, this one carries **adoption + task metrics** (+41% users/10wk).
- **But value-add is only partly proven:** metrics are vendor-reported, task-level, best-case (document search), not loan-economics; "16 skills / two agents" is early; the transformative claims ("future of commercial lending", 50% efficiency) are aspirational. No disclosed $ save, no loan-volume, no error/quality data, no independent audit of the "auditable/explainable" governance claim. → Why margin capture matters: nCino's durable win is if banks build enough on the AOS that switching cost rises (data + custom agents + workflows locked in) → the AOS becomes the **agent-orchestration + data layer** for commercial lending. The risk is commoditisation — Salesforce Agentforce, Uptiq, and general LLM tooling can also "add agents to bank workflows"; nCino's moat is the **incumbent lending data/system-of-record**, not the agents themselves.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** ConnectOne "additional capabilities over coming months"; nCino broader **Digital Partners** rollout (5 role-based agents, 4 via Banking Advisor). Sector tailwind: bank AI-agent adoption accelerating (Backbase/Kasisto, Uptiq, Lama AI, DNB — all 2026).
- **NCNO context:** decelerating SaaS (FY27 total-rev guide ~$639–646M, ~8–9% growth; subscription ~12% YoY); trades ~2.9x EV/Rev (cheap vs Temenos ~4.6x) — **no AI premium priced in**. → Why the market shrugs at individual logos: announcement/adoption-grade wins (even with +41% user metrics) don't rerate a decelerating name; the market wants the AOS/agent story to show up as **accelerating subscription ACV**. Second-order: nCino's real bet is that owning the commercial-lending data layer lets it monetise agents that competitors can't match — but if agentic capability commoditises faster than switching costs rise, nCino stays a ~3x-revenue LOS with an AI wrapper, not a rerated platform.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** ConnectOne's rollout sits in **cloud/agentic banking software for commercial lending** — a subvertical of the ~$8.35bn commercial-loan-software market (2026 est., per GMInsights), with the narrower corporate-lending-platform segment pegged at ~$4.4bn in 2026 growing to ~$11.0bn by 2030 (CAGR ~24.5%, per MarketsandMarkets). Structure: **consolidating, vendor-led**. A handful of platform incumbents (nCino, Finastra, Jack Henry, Q2) sit atop the loan-origination stack; barriers are high — deep core-banking integration, regulatory/audit requirements, and switching costs once a bank's lending workflow runs on one OS. "Why now": the 2025–26 shift from GenAI copilots to **agentic** systems (auditable, role-based agents that execute multi-step tasks, not just draft text) is the sector's live catalyst — nCino, Jack Henry (Google Cloud Gemini agents, Jun 2026), Q2 (Q2 Code, Apr 2026) all repositioned within ~90 days of each other.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: **ACV/subscription ARR, net revenue retention (NRR), Rule-of-40**. Key players & basis of competition: **nCino (NCNO)** — de-facto standard for US commercial-lending origination, competing on depth/embeddedness of workflow; **Finastra** — launched cloud-native LaserPro commercial-lending tool (Feb 2026); **Jack Henry** — core-banking scale (~7,400 US community banks/CUs), competing on distribution + Google Cloud agentic security (Jun–Jul 2026); **Q2 Holdings** — digital-banking front end. New entrants attack the same wedge with fresh capital: **Uptiq** ($25M Series B, Feb 2026 — bank AI agents), **Lama AI** ($12M Series A, Jun 2026), and consolidation via **Finova/Cubit Labs** (Jul 2026). Protagonist position: nCino is **ahead** on agentic-in-lending — its Agentic Operating System (AOS) lets banks deploy native Banking Advisor agents then build custom ones under one trust/audit layer. ConnectOne reports concrete traction (document search 20min → ~30s, active users +41% in 10 weeks; nCino claims Analyst Digital Partner cuts relationship-review effort 60–70%), which is a real reference deploy, not just an announcement. Moat: **switching costs + data/integration intangibles** (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** nCino (NCNO), FY2026 (ended Jan 2026): revenue **$594.8m (+10% YoY)**, subscription **$523.1m (+12%)**, ACV **$602.4m (+17%)**, subscription NRR **110%**, GAAP net income **$5.2m** (turned profitable from −$37.9m). Market cap ~**$1.7–2.0bn**, EV(TTM) ~**$1.9bn**.
- **EV/Revenue = $1.9bn / $0.595bn ≈ 3.2x** (a secondary source cites 3.5–3.6x on TTM/forward rev — in-line).
- Peer read (per ainvest secondary cite): **Jack Henry ~5x**, **Fiserv ~6x** revenue vs nCino ~3.5x — nCino trades at a **discount** despite a higher growth profile (~13% subscription CAGR FY24–26 vs Jack Henry ~3%). P/E not computed (single-year GAAP profitability just reached; EV/EBITDA on non-GAAP op income $129.4m ≈ 14.7x, but GAAP-basis `[UNSOURCED]`). Verdict: **cheap-to-in-line vs peers on revenue, justified-or-cheap given growth** — the discount reflects smaller scale + only-recent GAAP profitability, not overvaluation.
- Internal comps: [[Finastra launches cloud-native commercial lending tool]] (direct product rival), [[Uptiq raises $25M Series B for bank AI agents]] and [[Lama AI raises $12 million Series A for AI agents in banking]] (private agentic entrants), [[nCino launches Integration Gateway for financial institutions]] (nCino platform history), [[Finova acquires AI lendtech start-up Cubit Labs]] (lendtech M&A). Private-round valuations not disclosed → "no data".

**Risk flags.**
1. **Single-reference-deploy signal risk.** ConnectOne is one bank; the 60–70% / 97.5% efficiency figures are **vendor/customer-supplied**, not independently audited — early-adopter results may not generalize across nCino's base, and no economics (contract size, per-seat pricing) disclosed. Second-order: PR traction ≠ durable ACV expansion.
2. **Agentic commoditization / margin capture.** Jack Henry (Google Gemini) and Q2 are racing the same agentic wedge within a quarter; if the LLM/agent layer standardizes on hyperscaler stacks, value can migrate to Google/foundation-model providers, compressing nCino's differentiation to workflow-only.
3. **Execution/trust risk in regulated lending.** Agents that touch credit decisions carry model-risk, explainability and examiner scrutiny; a governance failure at any reference bank would chill community-bank adoption sector-wide.
4. **Concentration on community-bank tech budgets.** nCino's NRR (110%) depends on cash-strapped small banks funding AI upgrades through a rate-sensitive cycle; a lending-volume downturn pressures the very budgets that fund these deployments.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is an early **agentic re-rating catalyst** for lending software, not a valuation event yet (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if nCino converts agentic references into **accelerating ACV/NRR** over FY2027, its ~3.2–3.6x EV/Rev discount to Jack Henry/Fiserv should narrow. Trigger to watch: next NCNO print (Q2/Q3 FY27) for ACV growth re-acceleration and any disclosed AOS attach/monetization; thesis breaks if ACV growth decelerates below the mid-teens while peers ship comparable agents.
