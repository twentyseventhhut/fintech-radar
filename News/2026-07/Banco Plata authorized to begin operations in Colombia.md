## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** On 2026-07-09, Colombia's Superintendencia Financiera (SFC) authorized Plata (the Mexican digital bank, "Banco Plata") to begin operations in Colombia — not as a full bank, but under the regulatory figure of a *compañía de financiamiento* (financing company), which lets it take deposits and originate credit. This completes the licensing process; Plata now enters final technical readiness (infrastructure integration, security testing, connection with allies) before launching products "in coming weeks." Colombia is Plata's **second market after Mexico** and its first international expansion. ([La República](https://www.larepublica.co/finanzas/plata-recibio-la-autorizacion-de-funcionamiento-por-parte-de-la-superfinanciera-4433103), [Semana](https://www.semana.com/economia/empresas/articulo/plata-sa-recibe-autorizacion-para-operar-en-colombia/202620/), [El CEO](https://elceo.com/negocios/banco-plata-llega-a-colombia-como-compania-de-financiamiento-podra-captar-recursos-y-otorgar-creditos/))

**[1] Who is Plata (correcting the prompt's "Ualá backing").** No Ualá link — Plata is an **independent** Mexican digital bank founded April 2023 by ex-Tinkoff executives (CEO Neri Tollardo, Danil Anisimov, Alexander Bro). Backers include Kora (lead), Moore Strategic Ventures, TelevisaUnivision, plus the families of Oleg Tinkov and Michael Calvey (Baring Vostok) as investors/advisors. Ualá is in fact a **competitor**, not a backer. ([citybiz](https://www.citybiz.co/article/723215/surprise-backers-of-mexicos-fintech-unicorn-plata-revealed/), [TechFundingNews](https://techfundingnews.com/mexico-plata-raises-250m-bank-licence-valuation/))

**[2] Prior art / internal thread.** Plata is heavily covered in-base: [[Banco Plata wins full banking licence in Mexico]] (2026-02, CNBV full bank licence), [[Banco Plata secures $300M financing from Oaktree and Macquarie]] (2026-06), [[Banco Plata to deploy 300 smart ATMs across Mexico]] (2026-07), [[Banco Plata adds cash deposits across OXXO stores via tapi]] and [[Belvo and Banco Plata launch open finance credit in Mexico]] (both 2026-05). This Colombia authorization is a distinct, new event (new jurisdiction), not a reprint of any of the above.

**[3] Scale / funding.** Plata raised a ~$405M Series C in April 2026 at a **~$5B valuation** (up from $250M Series B / $3.1B in Oct 2025 and $160M Series A / $1.5B in Mar 2025); ~$1B+ raised to date. It reached >1M users and ~10% of new Mexican credit-card issuance within ~18 months. Colombia expansion is said to be backed by ~$2B in capital + international financing. ([Crowdfund Insider](https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2026/04/274693-latin-americas-digital-bank-plata-secures-405m-in-series-c-funding-reaching-5b-valuation/), [Ventureburn](https://ventureburn.com/plata-raises-405m-hits-5b-valuation-in-fintech-boom/))

**[4] Why it matters.** Colombia is a crowded neobank market — Nequi (~24M users), DaviPlata, MOVii, Nu Colombia (Nubank, >3M in region), Lulo Bank (Gilinski, ~650k), Pibank (Grupo Pichincha), Ualá, RappiPay — all SFC-supervised with Fogafín deposit insurance. Plata enters as a well-capitalized, credit-first challenger transplanting its Mexican origination/risk stack, but as a *financing company* (not full bank) it competes in a category already contested by Nequi's own financing-company license. ([Fintech News America](https://fintechnews.am/fintech-colombia/54903/nequi-daviplata-and-movii-dominate-colombias-neobanking-market-but-new-challengers-emerge/))
