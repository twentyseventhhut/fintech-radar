## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Coatue Management (Philippe Laffont) published its **"Public Markets Update"** deck + replay on **2026-05-06** (coatue.com, slug `public-markets-update-2026-05-06`). Linas repackaged it (~mid-May / re-surfaced Jun 21 and again Jul 12). **The public page carries zero dollar figures** — every number is secondary, from paywalled newsletter recaps (Linas/Burnett Substacks, globaldatacenterhub) of a gated deck; mainstream press did not cover the May 6 deck.
- **"$12 trillion" is a FUNDING-CAPACITY envelope (2026–2031), NOT committed capex:** ~$6T cumulative hyperscaler EBITDA + ~$4T debt/leverage capacity + ~$2T sovereign/private-credit/neoclouds. → **Why framed this way:** set deliberately *above* expected spend (~$3–7.6T peer consensus) so Coatue can argue "the money exists → not a bubble." A rhetorical device, not a forecast. Treating "$12T" as capex overstates the build-out ~2–4x.
- Confirmed 2026E figures (secondary): 4 hyperscalers ~$680B capex vs ~$66B combined FCF (MSFT $39B, GOOG $29B, META $5B, AMZN −$7B); semi FCF ~$525B (Nvidia $184B, Samsung, SK Hynix); tokens ~16T→35T; OpenAI+Anthropic combined ARR ~$55B.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **The framework (verbatim-confirmed, 2 sources): "sellers of scarcity" vs "buyers of scarcity."** Sellers (chips/memory/storage/optics/power: Nvidia, Broadcom, TSMC, Micron, SK Hynix, GE Vernova) book margin now, ~+107% YTD; buyers (MSFT, GOOG, AMZN, META, Oracle) monetize slowly over 5–7yr contracts, ~+4% YTD. Coatue flags **only Nvidia + Broadcom as structural** moats; rest cyclical.
- **"Most extreme winner/loser split in tech history" is FALSE by Coatue's own chart:** top/bottom quintile spread 2026 ~85pt (+51%/−33%) is *narrower* than 2009 (~95pt) and 2021 (~102pt). Coatue's own line: "not seen this since 2009." The superlative is the newsletter's embellishment.
- **Estimate peers (spend, not capacity):** Goldman ~$7.6T (2026–31), McKinsey ~$5.2T by 2030, Morgan Stanley ~$2.9–3T through 2028, Dell'Oro $1.7T by 2030 — all far below $12T and a different metric.
- **Same "1999 hardware-vs-spenders divide" call independently made by JPMorgan** ([[JPMorgan warns AI trade echoes dot-com-era bubble]], 2026-06-18): SOX +87% YTD, Mag-7 ETF −7% from peak, Microsoft −18% YTD / worst month since 2000. → Coatue's "sellers vs buyers" and JPMorgan's "1999 divergence" are the **same observation, opposite valence** (Coatue: buyers re-rate later; JPMorgan: dot-com warning).

## [2] Company history / fit
Coatue runs this genre yearly and escalates the trillion-dollar framing: **2024 EMW** "$1T AI TAM / knowledge-work TAM"; **2025 EMW "The Great Separation"** (same winner/loser thesis, named Nvidia/Broadcom/Oracle/OpenAI); **Oct 2025** "AI is not a bubble" deck. May 2026's "$12T" is the next rung. → **Why:** as a concentrated tech-long fund, Coatue is **talking its own book** — read as positioned, not neutral. Track-record blemish: Laffont **sold ~72–77% of Coatue's Nvidia stake 2023–24**, right on the thesis but missing much of the single best stock; now explicitly picks-and-shovels (TSMC, Lam Research, Applied Materials "over Nvidia," CNBC 2026-06-23).

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Not novel: an escalation of prior decks; the "$12T" and quintile %s are single-sourced to paywalled posts (UNCONFIRMED at primary level). Genuine value = the **"sellers vs buyers of scarcity"** lens, which reframes the debate from "bubble?" to "**who captures the AI margin first**" — toll-takers now, hyperscalers on a lag, software disrupted. Falsifiable. **What breaks it:** a demand air-pocket reverses the cyclical sellers (ex-Nvidia/Broadcom) hardest and the $12T "capacity" never converts to spend → capital concentration makes the winners fragile, not safe. There is nothing to "adopt" — it's a deck's view.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Named bear counterweights, all live: **Sequoia/Cahn "$600B question"** (>$600B/yr revenue needed to break even); **Goldman/Covello** "too much spend, too little benefit"; **Bain** ~$800B revenue shortfall by 2030; **Michael Burry** ~$176B understated GPU depreciation 2026–28 (SOXX puts, early Jul 2026); **MIT** 95% of enterprise GenAI pilots no P&L impact; circular Nvidia↔OpenAI↔Oracle financing (Nvidia *paused* its ~$100B OpenAI deal Jan–Feb 2026). **Coatue's blind spot:** the deck does not engage the depreciation/ROIC critique. Watch hyperscaler Q2/Q3-2026 capex guidance and FCF inflection as the tell.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** The pick is a public-markets research report from Coatue (crossover hedge fund / VC), so the "sector" is public-markets AI exposure and the hyperscaler capex cycle it is priced on — not a company deal. Facts: Coatue projects **$12T of cumulative AI capex 2026–2031**, funded by ~$1T/yr hyperscaler EBITDA growing ~10% (~$6T), +3x debt/EBITDA leverage (~$4T), + sovereigns/private-credit/neoclouds (~$2T) (per Coatue May 2026 update, via linas.substack.com / globaldatacenterhub, as of 2026-05-06). 2026 hyperscaler capex alone is guided at **~$725bn, +77% YoY** — Amazon ~$200bn, Alphabet ~$175–185bn, Meta ~$125–145bn, Microsoft >$120bn — vs ~$410bn in 2025 and ~$226bn in 2024 (per company guidance, via Tom's Hardware / Yahoo Finance, Feb 2026). Structure: highly **consolidated demand** (5–6 hyperscalers) feeding a **single-supplier chokepoint** — Nvidia captures ~90% of AI GPU spend (via multiple analysts, 2026). Why now: capex has crossed from cash-funded to debt-funded and markets have started to reprice the winner/loser split.

**Competitive landscape.** KPIs the report runs on: capex $ and capex-to-sales intensity, EBITDA coverage, ARR/ARR-growth for AI-native names, and the "sell vs buy scarcity" position. Coatue's core framework: **markets reward "sellers of scarcity" (chips/power/turbines — book full margin now) and punish "buyers of scarcity" (hyperscalers deploying capex today for delayed, multi-year returns)** (via linas.substack.com / thevccorner). Independent corroboration: [[JPMorgan warns AI trade echoes dot-com-era bubble]] (2026-06-18) flags the same 1999-style split — chip/memory stocks soaring while spenders like Microsoft and Meta lag. AI-native traction cited: OpenAI + Anthropic combined **~$55bn ARR by Apr-2026, up from $3.4bn early-2024**, adding ~$10bn/month (via Coatue, secondary). Coatue's positioning is "still early, not a bubble" (18-chart deck) — an interested party (it is long the AI complex), so treat as a bull-side view, not neutral (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** No single traded security here — a report, not a deal — so no clean EV/Revenue arithmetic; the relevant "multiple" is **capex intensity (capex/sales)**: AI complex ~34% in 2026, projected ~37% by 2028, vs ~32% dot-com peak in 2000 — i.e. capex intensity now runs *hotter* than the dot-com peak (via secondary analyst compilations, 2026). Sequoia's Cahn's benchmark: the industry must earn **~$3T** to justify deployed chips/DCs; current annual capex-vs-AI-revenue gap ~$600bn (via TechCrunch 2026-07-09 / Forbes). Internal comps as precedent for the capex/demand-concentration thesis: [[Amazon raises AWS GPU reservation prices about 20% amid chip shortage]] (scarcity pricing, seller-side), [[JPMorgan warns AI trade echoes dot-com-era bubble]] (repricing), [[McKinsey projects agentic commerce could drive $5 trillion by 2030]] (demand-side TAM claim). Distribution not computed — heterogeneous, qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Circular / concentrated demand.** ~$2.1T cloud revenue backlog with roughly half tied to two FCF-negative counterparties (OpenAI, Anthropic); a $1T+ capex cycle underwritten by ~$1T of demand from two cash-burning parties → if either slows funding, the buyer-side capex thesis unwinds fast.
2. **Debt-funded capex + depreciation drag.** Big-five capex now exceeds cash flow after buybacks/dividends, pushing them to debt markets; assets take 18–36 months to earn returns while depreciation hits now → margin/ROIC risk if adoption or pricing disappoints.
3. **Interested-source / de-PR risk.** The report is Coatue's own bull-side marketing of a book it is long; its "early, not a bubble" conclusion is DATA to weigh against the $3T/$600bn-gap skeptics, not a neutral verdict — treat as a positioned view.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** If Coatue's frame holds, the near-term trade stays with **sellers of scarcity** (Nvidia, power/turbine names) and the re-rating risk sits on **buyers** (hyperscalers) until contracted compute revenue arrives — a rotation, not a crash (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: watch whether the capex-vs-revenue gap **narrows** through 2026–2027 earnings (OpenAI/Anthropic ARR run-rate vs backlog draws); if the gap keeps widening and debt issuance accelerates, the "not a bubble" call breaks and the seller-side lead compresses too. Trigger: next hyperscaler capex guides + any OpenAI/Anthropic funding/ARR miss.
