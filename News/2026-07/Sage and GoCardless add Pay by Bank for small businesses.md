Sage, GoCardless add Pay by Bank for small businesses
Paula Albu
14 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
GoCardless has expanded its partnership with Sage to integrate Pay by Bank, its open banking payment feature, directly within Sage Business Cloud Accounting. The integration adds instant one-off account-to-account payments alongside GoCardless' existing Direct Debit capabilities.
Extending an existing partnership
The launch builds on a multi-year relationship between the two companies. With the expansion, Sage users across the UK and Ireland can offer their customers a card-free method to pay invoices instantly online. By embedding a Pay by Bank option directly within Sage invoices, small businesses gain a method for collecting one-off charges that fall outside regular Direct Debit schedules, such as upfront setup fees, additional services, or account top-ups.
Because Pay by Bank payments move directly between accounts rather than relying on card details, the companies said the integration is intended to reduce the risk of failed payments caused by expired or lost card information. GoCardless and Sage said the approach can reduce single transaction costs by an average of 54% compared with traditional card networks, while also lowering the likelihood of failed payment attempts.
The integration is also designed to reduce administrative work associated with manual bookkeeping. Once a customer pays an invoice through Pay by Bank, the payment status syncs automatically with the Sage dashboard to reconcile the outstanding balance, removing the need for manual data entry or matching transactions against bank statements.
A company official at GoCardless said the partnership addresses a common challenge for small businesses managing tight margins, where high card fees and delayed payments place pressure on available resources. The official said combining automated recurring collections with instant open banking payments is intended to help businesses protect cash flow while reducing card-related costs.
A company official at Sage said the integration is intended to reduce the administrative burden associated with invoicing and reconciliation, allowing businesses to spend less time on manual admin. The official said bringing Pay by Bank natively into Sage Business Cloud Accounting closes the gap between invoice creation and real-time bookkeeping, giving businesses clearer visibility into their cash flow.
Broader context
The integration reflects continued growth in the use of open banking-based payment methods among small and mid-sized businesses in the UK and Ireland, as providers look to combine payment collection with accounting software to reduce administrative overhead and transaction costs traditionally associated with card payments.

# Context-enrichment: Sage and GoCardless add Pay by Bank for small businesses
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
GoCardless embedded its open-banking **Pay by Bank** (one-off account-to-account payment initiation) inside **Sage Business Cloud Accounting**, so SMBs in the **UK and Ireland** can add a "pay this invoice by bank" button alongside GoCardless' existing Direct Debit. Payment status auto-syncs back to Sage to reconcile the invoice. Announced 14 Jul 2026 (The Paypers); framed as live ("Sage users… can offer").

**De-PR'd reality:** the headline verb is "**expand**", not "launch" — and that is the whole story. This is the **third layer of an 8-year-old relationship**, not a new partnership:
- **2017** — GoCardless first powered **Direct Debit** inside Sage.
- **Apr 2024** — partnership *expanded* to add Sage Intacct + open-banking access (US/CA/UK/FR).
- **14 Jul 2026** — the open-banking capability is now productized as **one-off Pay by Bank for invoices**, UK/IE only.

**Why structured this way:** Direct Debit only handles *scheduled recurring* pulls; it cannot capture one-off, out-of-schedule charges (setup fees, top-ups, ad-hoc services). Pay by Bank fills exactly that gap — an A2A push for the invoice-level one-off. So this is a **feature-completion move within an existing rail**, not a strategic pivot. The disclosed number ("~54% cheaper per transaction vs card networks") is a GoCardless talking point (open item: who bears the fee, exact bps — not disclosed; fraud/liability terms — not addressed).

## [1] Competitors / peers
Accounting-suite embedded A2A payments is a **parity race**, and Sage is arguably the last of the big three to close it:
- **Intuit QuickBooks (UK)** — the sharpest direct comp: native **QuickBooks Payments** with **Pay by Bank + Pay by Card**, and it *publishes pricing* (Pay by Bank 1% + VAT, capped £4; card 1.2% + 20p). Sage/GoCardless disclosed no economics.
- **Xero** — open-banking bill/invoice pay via **Crezco**, plus **acquired Melio (US bill-pay) Dec 2025** to deepen embedded payments.
- Infra A2A players usable for invoicing: Crezco, Volt, Banked, Token, TrueLayer, Yapily, Tink.

**Why the lay of the land is this way:** the accounting software owns the invoice, the reconciliation and the SMB relationship — the highest-value point to inject a payment. GoCardless supplies the rail; Sage owns the distribution. The second-order dynamic: this is not Sage/GoCardless leading, it is **catching up to QuickBooks' already-live pay-by-bank** — parity, not differentiation.

## [2] Company history / fit
GoCardless has spent 2024–26 pushing hard from Direct Debit into open-banking/A2A: Nordigen + **Nuapay (€32.75m, closed Sep 2024)**, UKPI participation, **first recurring VRP with Jellyfish Energy (Mar 2026)** [[GoCardless delivers first VRP payment with Jellyfish Energy]], and **Recurring Pay by Bank on UKPI rails (Jun 2026)** [[GoCardless launches Recurring Pay by Bank on UKPI rails]]. It also runs a partnerships/embed playbook — cf. the near-identical **Sequence** tie-up two weeks earlier [[GoCardless and Sequence partner to embed Direct Debit into billing]]. Financially it is stretched: FY24 rev £132m (+41%), targeting first adjusted-profit year FY26 after **cutting ~90 jobs (Apr 2026)** [[GoCardless cuts 90 jobs targeting profitability]].

**Why the company acts this way:** GoCardless is being **acquired by Mollie for ~€1.05–1.1bn (Dec 2025, closing mid-2026)** [[Mollie to acquire GoCardless for about €1.05B]]. On the eve of that close it needs to show its A2A/open-banking growth story is real and distributed. Landing Pay by Bank inside Sage's ~1m+ SMB base is exactly the kind of distribution proof point that supports the acquisition narrative. For Sage, the structural logic is simpler: embedded payments monetize the invoice flow and are now table stakes vs Xero/QuickBooks.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**What is genuinely new:** *one-off* Pay by Bank inside Sage Accounting for UK/IE — a specific product surface that did not exist before. What is **NOT new:** the Sage-GoCardless relationship (2017), open-banking-in-Sage (2024), or GoCardless "Pay by Bank" as a concept — recurring Pay by Bank was covered a month earlier [[GoCardless launches Recurring Pay by Bank on UKPI rails]].

**Traction:** none disclosed for this integration — no live SMB count, no volume. GoCardless-wide open-banking traction is ~35–37k businesses since 2021, still small vs its DD base. So this is an **announcement, not adoption**.

**Why the value-add is real but thin (1–2 levels deeper):** the real value is reconciliation automation — invoice→payment→auto-match closes a manual bookkeeping loop. But **who captures the margin?** GoCardless (soon Mollie) takes the acceptance economics; Sage takes distribution + stickiness. The SMB gets a ~54% cheaper one-off vs card — genuine but only on out-of-schedule charges, a slice of total volume. The multiple case rests on card→A2A migration; what breaks it is bank coverage/UX friction and the fact that cards still = 84% of UK spend.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
UK open-banking payments hit 351m in 2025 (+57% YoY); commercial **VRP/UKPI scheme went live 2 Jun 2026** [[UK Payments Initiative launches new open banking scheme]], with Wave 2 (general e-commerce) expected H2 2026. Expect Sage to extend from one-off Pay by Bank into **recurring/VRP** once UKPI coverage broadens — that would be the more material follow-on.

**Why the market goes this way + counterintuitive second-order effect:** the National Payment Vision and UKPI are politically pushing A2A as a card-duopoly alternative, so accounting suites embedding it is the natural distribution channel. But the counterintuitive point: this deal makes **Mollie** (post-close) the beneficiary of Sage's SMB distribution — a Sage×GoCardless headline is really a **Mollie embedded-A2A land-grab** dressed as a small-business feature. Risk: none of the economics or adoption are proven, and QuickBooks already got there with published pricing.

## Top challenge / red-team questions

1. **Is this actually new, or a rebrand of the 2024 expansion?** — Mostly not new. The Sage×GoCardless open-banking access was announced Apr 2024; only the *one-off Pay by Bank invoice surface* is genuinely new. The headline verb "expand" confirms incremental status. **Fresh but low-weight.**
2. **Announced or live?** — Framed as live ("can now offer"), but no live SMB count, volume, or launch cohort disclosed. Treat as GA-claim without adoption proof. **Open.**
3. **What is the true delta vs existing Direct Debit?** — One-off, out-of-schedule A2A push (setup fees, top-ups) that DD cannot capture. That is the entire functional gain — narrow.
4. **Who pays the fee and what is the bps?** — Not disclosed. Only "~54% cheaper per transaction vs card networks" (a GoCardless claim). QuickBooks by contrast publishes 1%+VAT capped £4. **Open — a red flag on economics.**
5. **Fraud / liability?** — Not addressed in the release. A2A push payments have different (generally lower) fraud/chargeback exposure than cards, but terms unstated. **Open.**
6. **How does this compare to QuickBooks/Xero?** — Parity at best; QuickBooks already has native Pay by Bank with published pricing; Xero has Crezco + Melio. Sage is closing a gap, not opening one.
7. **What share of SMB invoice volume is even addressable?** — Only one-off/out-of-schedule charges; recurring stays on DD; cards still 84% of UK spend. Small slice.
8. **Who captures the margin in the stack?** — GoCardless/Mollie takes acceptance economics; Sage takes distribution/stickiness; SMB gets marginal cost saving on a subset of payments.
9. **How does the Mollie acquisition change the read?** — Materially. Post-close (~mid-2026) this is Mollie gaining embedded-A2A distribution across Sage's ~1m+ SMBs. The real strategic actor is Mollie, not "GoCardless." (analysis)
10. **Is GoCardless's A2A push proven?** — ~35–37k businesses on open-banking payments since 2021 vs a much larger DD base; first recurring VRP only Mar 2026. Early, not scaled.
11. **Why announce now?** — On the eve of the Mollie close and while targeting first FY26 adjusted profit after 90 layoffs — a distribution proof point that supports the acquisition narrative. (hypothesis)
12. **What's the natural follow-on?** — Extending Sage to recurring/VRP once UKPI coverage broadens (Wave 2, H2 2026). That would be the materially bigger event.
13. **Any exclusivity?** — Not stated. Sage could still add other rails; GoCardless still embeds elsewhere (cf. Sequence tie-up two weeks prior). **Open.**
14. **Duplicate risk?** — Distinct from the June "Recurring Pay by Bank on UKPI rails" (that = GoCardless product launch; this = Sage integration of one-off Pay by Bank). Not a duplicate, but same theme/timeframe → low incremental weight.
15. **What would move importance up?** — Disclosed volumes/pricing, a recurring/VRP extension, or explicit tie to the Mollie combined entity's SMB strategy.

**Importance: 2/5** — Incremental feature-completion within an 8-year partnership; UK/IE-only, one-off charges, no disclosed economics or adoption, and it merely reaches parity with QuickBooks/Xero. Matters mainly as a data point in the UK card→A2A migration and as pre-close distribution proof for the Mollie–GoCardless deal. Fresh (a distinct new product surface) but light.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: UK/IE open-banking A2A ("Pay by Bank") embedded in SMB accounting software. The A2A wave is structural, not a one-off launch: UK open-banking payments grew ~53% y/y, with ~33m Pay by Bank transactions in Nov-2025 and monthly volumes past 37m (per Open Banking Expo / Payments Association, 2026). Globally, Juniper projects A2A transactions rising from 60bn (2024) to 186bn (2029), and A2A's share of non-cash volume from 16% (2023) to 22% (2028) as cards slip from 57% to 50%. "Why now": the UK National Payment Vision + the newly-live UK Payments Initiative (UKPI) A2A scheme (Q1-2026) give recurring open-banking payments a formal rail — the first credible alternative to the Visa/Mastercard near-duopoly that GoCardless itself pegs at 84% of UK retail spend and £1.5bn in annual card fees (per GoCardless, [[GoCardless launches Recurring Pay by Bank on UKPI rails]], Jun-2026). This launch cements that thesis at the SMB/invoicing layer, not just enterprise.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: TPV/payment volume, take rate, payment-success/failure rate, attach rate of A2A onto existing Direct Debit base. Two camps (per Sifted): (1) productisation — own consumer-facing checkout — GoCardless, TrueLayer, Volt; (2) infrastructure/API — Yapily, Tink (Visa-owned), Salt Edge. Basis of competition = distribution + coverage + success rate, not price alone. Recent peer moves: TrueLayer processes ~50% of UK Pay by Bank volume, >2x its nearest rival, ~20m+ users (per openbankingtracker/Sifted, 2026) — it, not GoCardless, leads in raw one-off checkout volume. Visa completed its first UK A2A VRP in Nov-2025 ([[Visa completes first A2A variable recurring payment in UK]]); Devengo raised €2m for SEPA A2A ([[Devengo raises €2M for A2A payments in Europe]]). GoCardless, TrueLayer, Yapily, Plaid and Acquired are all UKPI founding members — the scheme is shared plumbing, so differentiation shifts to distribution. GoCardless's position: **niche-leader in recurring/DD, catching-up in one-off Pay-by-Bank.** Its moat is its 15-year Direct-Debit base (its "bank guess" feature claims sight of 80% of UK payers) + embedded distribution through accounting/billing partners — Sage here, and [[GoCardless and Sequence partner to embed Direct Debit into billing]] (Jun-2026). The Sage tie-up converts DD switching costs into an A2A on-ramp: intelligent routing falls back to DD where open banking isn't available (per note + Recurring Pay by Bank note) — a real distribution edge (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** IR-primary (GoCardless is IR-covered, slug `gocardless`):
- **FY25 (reported Apr-2026):** Group revenue **£160.9m**, +22% y/y (FY24 £132.3m); payments processed **£79.2bn**, ~2x y/y (boosted by the Nuapay acquisition, closed Sep-2024); net losses -27% (after -55% in FY24); **first adjusted-EBITDA-positive quarter** in Q4 FY25, guiding to first adjusted-EBITDA-positive full year in FY26 (per GoCardless IR "grows 22% in FY25", 2026-04-13, https://gocardless.com/blog/gocardless-grows-in-fy-25; first profit quarter 2025-10-08, https://gocardless.com/blog/gocardless-achieves-profitability). Restructuring: ~90 roles cut ([[GoCardless cuts 90 jobs targeting profitability]], Apr-2026).
- **Valuation anchor:** Mollie agreed to acquire GoCardless for **€1.05–1.1bn** (Dec-2025, [[Mollie to acquire GoCardless for about €1.05B]], IR: https://gocardless.com/blog/mollie-to-acquire-gocardless), completion expected mid-2026. That is DOWN from a ~$2.1bn 2022 round (reports pointed to ~$1.5bn pre-deal) — a marked down-round.
- **Arithmetic — EV/Revenue on the take-out:** €1.05bn ≈ £0.90bn (at ~£0.855/€) / FY25 rev £160.9m ≈ **5.6x revenue** on the deal price; on the 2022 $2.1bn (~£1.65bn) mark it was materially richer. Take rate implied: £160.9m rev / £79.2bn TPV ≈ **0.20%** — thin, consistent with DD/A2A economics vs card acquiring. EV/EBITDA = **no data** (only one positive quarter; no clean annual EBITDA disclosed) [UNSOURCED]. Peer public multiples not computed — TrueLayer/Yapily/Volt are private, last-round figures not verified here → "distribution not computed, qualitative comparison."
- Flag: at ~5.6x on the deal, in-line-to-cheap for a 22%-grower that just turned the profitability corner — the down-round from 2022 reflects the 2021-22 multiple reset, not a broken business.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Take-rate / margin compression from own thesis.** A2A's pitch is "-54% vs cards" per this note — GoCardless is actively cannibalising the very card-fee spread that funds richer take rates; at ~0.20% blended take rate, volume must scale hugely to offset. Second-order: profitability now leans on cost discipline (the 90 cuts) more than pricing power.
2. **Distribution beats it in one-off checkout.** TrueLayer already runs ~50% of UK Pay by Bank; GoCardless enters one-off A2A as a follower. If the SMB race is won on checkout ubiquity (Amazon/eBay going live in 2026), GoCardless's edge is only where an accounting/DD relationship pre-exists — narrower than card rails.
3. **Ownership/integration overhang.** Post-Mollie (close ~mid-2026), roadmap and independence are uncertain; a €3.5bn combined group serving 350k+ SMEs could re-prioritise. Partner exclusivity with Sage is not disclosed (de-PR: economics, fraud liability and ticket-size caps are all silent in the announcement).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) Not a re-rating event — it's a distribution land-grab: A2A moves from "enterprise/utility bills" into the SMB invoice, where accounting software is the choke point. Falsifiable thesis: embedded-in-accounting A2A becomes GoCardless's defensible lane vs checkout-led TrueLayer. Trigger to watch: FY26 disclosure of A2A/Pay-by-Bank volume as a share of the £79.2bn TPV, and whether the Sage integration + UKPI rails lift blended take rate or merely defend the DD base. Thesis breaks if GoCardless keeps reporting card-fee-alternative wins without volume mix shifting, i.e. announcement-heavy, adoption-light.

Gross profit, cash and margin are **not disclosed** publicly → "no data".

**Verdict (financial backdrop).** GROWTH-DECELERATING BUT PROFITABILITY-INFLECTING — FY25 revenue **£160.9m (+22% YoY)** vs +38% in FY24 (a clear slowdown), but the story is the profit inflection: **first adjusted-EBITDA-positive quarter** in Q4 FY25, net losses **-27%**, and a stated FY26 target of a first full **EBITDA-positive year**. Volume **doubled to £79.2bn** (Nuapay-inflated; ~+33% underlying). Backdrop is supportive for the Sage open-banking expansion: A2A/Pay-by-Bank is the growth-and-margin engine GoCardless is leaning into.

**Key figures (FY25, year ended 30 Jun 2025; vs FY24).**
- Revenue (Group turnover): **£160.9m, +22% YoY** (from £132.3m). UK/European operations **£155.5m, +18% YoY**. Deceleration vs **+38%** in FY24 — a thesis flag, though partly base-effect and post-restructure.
- Payments processed: **£79.2bn**, roughly **2x** prior year — but inflated by the **Nuapay** acquisition (closed Sep 2024); **underlying volume ~+33%** ("nearly a third") ex-M&A. This is the honest growth read, not the headline 2x.
- Profitability: **net losses -27% YoY** (on top of -55% in FY24); **first adjusted-EBITDA-positive quarter** in Q4 FY25. Gross profit / margin / operating profit / cash — **not disclosed** (private accounts; "no data").
- Customers / reach: **over 100,000 businesses** on-platform (collect + send via Outbound Payments, launched to become end-to-end). Combined Mollie footprint cited at **350,000+** businesses.
- Open-banking / Pay-by-Bank mix: **not separately quantified** in FY25 results ("no data" on discrete A2A revenue) — but strategically front-and-centre (Recurring Pay by Bank launched Mar 2025; Protect+ fraud tool Oct 2025; the Sage deal here extends it to one-off invoice A2A).

**By driver.** Growth driven by new customer/partner signings, renewals/expansions, and the **Nuapay** acquisition (open-banking capability + volume). Net-loss reduction reflects the completed **restructure** (including ~90 role cuts) — margin improvement is cost-led as much as revenue-led, a point management is quieter on.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public consensus exists (private company) → assess vs prior year only. Revenue growth **decelerated (+22% vs +38%)** — a genuine slowdown [confirmed, gocardless.com FY25 release]. Loss trajectory **improved** (-27% after -55%). This is the second consecutive year the company narrows the gap to profitability, consistent with the FY24 note trajectory ([[GoCardless revenues up 38 percent in FY24]] if present in base; else FY24 release). Prior quarter: first **EBITDA-positive quarter** already flagged Q4 FY25 (10-08-2025 release "GoCardless achieves profitability").

**Guidance / forward.** Management **introduced** a forward target: **first EBITDA-positive year on an adjusted basis in FY26** — a concrete, near-term profitability milestone (not a vague "path to"). Plausible given the demonstrated Q4 FY25 crossover and completed cost restructure; risk is that "adjusted" EBITDA excludes share-comp/one-offs, so cash breakeven may lag. Revenue guidance: none given.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. **Pending Mollie acquisition (€1.1bn / £920m, announced 11-Dec-2025, ~90% stock, expected close mid-2026).** Fact → GoCardless is being absorbed into Mollie (combined ~€1.95bn valuation, 350,000+ businesses). Why it matters → the Sage Pay-by-Bank tie-up here becomes part of a **larger card+bank single-platform** proposition post-close; ownership/roadmap continuity is a real integration risk for Sage. Second-order: A2A distribution scales via Mollie's merchant base, but GoCardless-standalone strategy questions become Mollie's. This valuation (~5.7x FY25 revenue) sits well below the 2022 ~$2.1bn peak — a **down-round exit**, the key skeptical read.
2. **Growth deceleration to +22%** (from +38%), with headline volume flattered 2x by Nuapay — underlying ~+33%. Why it matters → the open-banking/Pay-by-Bank land-grab (Sage, etc.) is partly about **reaccelerating** organic volume; watch whether A2A mix reverses the top-line slowdown.
3. **Profit inflection is real but cost-led.** First EBITDA-positive quarter + -27% losses came alongside restructuring/headcount cuts.
