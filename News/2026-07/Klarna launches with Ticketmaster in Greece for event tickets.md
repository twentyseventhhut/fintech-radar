Klarna launches with Ticketmaster in Greece for event tickets
Sinziana Albu
13 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
Klarna has launched a partnership with Ticketmaster in Greece, enabling instalment payments for event tickets.
Through the partnership, consumers purchasing tickets via Ticketmaster in Greece can now split the cost of their purchases at checkout, covering categories such as live music, sports, and entertainment events.
The integration allows Ticketmaster customers in Greece to use Klarna's payment options when buying tickets online, rather than paying the full amount upfront. This positions Klarna within a sector that has traditionally relied on single upfront card or bank payments, and reflects the broader expansion of instalment-based payment methods into new categories of consumer spending beyond retail and e-commerce.
For the live events sector, ticket prices can represent a significant one-off cost for consumers, particularly for higher-value events such as concerts, festivals, or major sporting fixtures. Through the process of offering the option to divide payments, the partnership is aimed at making ticket purchases more manageable for consumers, potentially widening access to events that might otherwise require a larger upfront outlay.
Strategic relevance for Klarna and Ticketmaster
The move extends Klarna's presence in Greece into a new vertical, following the company's broader push across Europe to embed its payment options into diverse merchant categories. For Ticketmaster, the addition of instalment payments represents a shift in the checkout experience offered to Greek customers, aligning it with practices already adopted in some other markets where buy now, pay later options have been introduced for ticketing purchases.
At the same time, the partnership also illustrates the continued convergence between payments technology and the entertainment and live events industry, an area where digital ticketing platforms increasingly compete not only on catalogue and availability but also on payment flexibility. As BNPL services expand into sectors such as ticketing, travel, and subscriptions, providers are seeking to differentiate through vertical-specific integrations rather than generic retail applications alone.
Neither company has disclosed the financial terms of the arrangement or specific volume expectations for the Greek market. The integration applies to Ticketmaster's operations in Greece, with no indication given of plans to extend the arrangement to other markets at this stage.

### [0] TL;DR
On 2026-07-13 Klarna launched a partnership with **Ticketmaster in Greece**, enabling **pay-in-instalments** for event tickets (live music, sports, entertainment) at online checkout. Consumers can split ticket cost instead of paying the full amount upfront. It is a single-country merchant go-live: no financial terms, no volume targets, and no stated plan to extend to other markets. Strategically it deepens Klarna's Greek footprint (Klarna Greece launched consumer BNPL in 2026) into a new vertical — ticketing/experiences — and fits the broader BNPL push beyond retail into travel, ticketing and subscriptions.

For consumers it eases access to higher-priced events; for Ticketmaster Greece it is a conversion/basket lever. No systemic or competitive shift.

### Sector context — BNPL, and its push into ticketing/experiences

The Klarna–Ticketmaster Greece tie-up (instalments for live music, sports, entertainment at checkout) is a small, unpriced country-level rollout, but it sits inside two structural trends worth tracking:

- **BNPL is now a mass-scale credit category.** US BNPL providers originated ~$160bn of consumer credit in 2025, with pay-in-4 ~half of volume (Federal Reserve FEDS Note, Jun 2026 — in-base: [[BNPL providers originated nearly $160 billion of US credit in 2025]]). BNPL's vertical expansion beyond retail e-commerce into ticketing, travel and subscriptions is the differentiation play cited in the source text.
- **Ticketing BNPL is not new for Klarna.** Ticketmaster already runs Klarna Pay-in-3 in the UK and Klarna/Affirm at US checkout (help.ticketmaster.co.uk; klarna.com/us/store/Ticketmaster). Greece is thus a geographic extension of an existing merchant relationship, not a new product. Low novelty; incremental GMV.

### Klarna's European merchant-expansion pattern (in-base comps)

Greece fits a dense, ongoing European "embed-into-local-checkout" campaign visible across the base this month:
- [[Klarna partners with SIBS to add MB WAY in Portugal]] — local rail integration, same "combine global brand + local payment habits" playbook.
- [[Klarna secures €900M financing facility in Germany]] — €900M facility funding up to €5bn of German Fair Financing loans over 2yrs; capital-efficiency angle.
- [[Klarna plans new BNPL risk transfer to fuel US growth]] — ~SEK 5bn (~$516M) SRT to free capital for expansion.
- US build-out: [[Klarna applies for US banking license in Utah]] (Utah ILC, FDIC-insured), plus June FDIC-insured savings (3%+ APY) and "US fastest-growing market." Direction of travel = deposit-funded, own-balance-sheet bank, not pure merchant BNPL.

Adjacent BNPL activity in-base shows a crowded, consolidating category: [[Qliro becomes PPRO's exclusive Nordic BNPL partner]], [[Tabby launches Tabby Cash cashback account beyond BNPL]], [[Malaysia's AhaPay expands BNPL with DirectD partnership]], [[Dutch retailer Bol wins payment provider license for BNPL]], [[Instalments fintech Float goes live in the UK]].

### IR grounding — Klarna's own reported results (PRIMARY)

Klarna is public (NYSE: KLAR; 20-F filed 2026-02-26). Latest reported quarter **Q1 2026 (released 2026-05-14)** — figures cross-checked against the earnings release and multiple wires:

- **Revenue ~$1.0bn, +44% YoY**; transaction-margin dollars $389M (+44%).

- **Fair Financing GMV +138% YoY** — longer-tenor interest-bearing credit is the fastest-growing (and highest credit-risk) line; the German €900M facility and ticket instalments feed exactly this segment.
- 119M active consumers (+21%), 1M+ merchants (+49%). Q2'26 guide: GMV $35.5–36.5bn, rev $960M–1,000M, adj op income $30–50M.

**IR read-through:** growth is real (GMV +33%, rev +44%) and profitability has flipped positive, but margins are thin (net income ~$1M on ~$1bn revenue). The Greece ticketing deal is immaterial to the P&L on its own; it matters only as one tile in the merchant/GMV-density mosaic that drives the top line.

### Risk flags

1. **Regulatory tightening is imminent and directly on-vertical (highest-signal).** The UK FCA's binding BNPL / "deferred payment credit" regime came into force **15 July 2026** — two days AFTER this pick — bringing authorization, mandatory affordability/creditworthiness checks and disclosure duties. The **EU Second Consumer Credit Directive (CCD II)** applies from **20 Nov 2026** and, unlike CCD I, captures interest-free, sub-3-month and small (<€200) credit — i.e. exactly the pay-in-3 / pay-in-30 ticketing product. Higher compliance cost, checkout friction and slower onboarding across Europe (incl. Greece) follow. (FCA; EUR-Lex CCD II.)
2. **AI credit-scoring classified high-risk under EU AI Act** — [[Финтехно: EU deems AI credit scoring high-risk]] — raises the compliance bar for the automated affordability decisioning underpinning instant instalment approval.
3. **Live litigation / affordability backlash.** [[Klarna faces class action lawsuit in the Netherlands]]: Stichting Massaschade & Consument alleges BNPL breaches Dutch consumer-credit rules; potential liabilities >€500M. A precedent here would ripple across EU markets.
4. **Credit-risk concentration in the fastest-growing line.** Fair Financing +138% YoY is longer-tenor interest-bearing lending; instalment ticketing (higher-ticket, discretionary, one-off spend) skews toward this. Provisions are stable today (0.55% of GMV) but a consumer downturn or looser underwriting to chase GMV would pressure this first — hence the parallel SRT / off-balance-sheet risk-transfer moves.
5. **Deal materiality / disclosure gap.** No financial terms, no volume expectations, single-country, single-merchant, no stated multi-market rollout. Positive optics, negligible standalone P&L impact — do not over-index on it as a growth catalyst.
6. **Thin-margin profitability.** Net income only ~$1M in Q1'26; the just-achieved GAAP breakeven is fragile and sensitive to credit-loss upticks or compliance-cost step-ups from (1)–(2).

**Bottom line:** Low-novelty geographic extension of an existing Ticketmaster relationship; immaterial on its own but consistent with Klarna's European merchant-density strategy. The real watch-item is regulatory — FCA (15 Jul 2026) and EU CCD II (20 Nov 2026) both land on the interest-free instalment product this deal represents, just as Klarna's profitability turned only marginally positive.

**Klarna (KLAR) — Q1 2026 results as backdrop to the Greece/Ticketmaster expansion**

Klarna is IR-covered; figures below are Klarna's own reported numbers. Latest reported quarter: **Q1 2026** (earnings release dated 2026-05-14). This Greece/Ticketmaster launch (2026-07-13) has no disclosed financial terms, so results frame it as context for Klarna's European/vertical-expansion trajectory rather than a modeled driver.

**Reported figures (Q1 2026, YoY):**
- **GMV: $33.7B, +33% YoY** (Q1'25 $25.3B). U.S. GMV +39%; ex-U.S. GMV +31% — European/international growth remains strong and is the backdrop for pushing into new merchant verticals like ticketing.
- **Revenue: $1.0B, +44% YoY** (Q1'25 $694M). **Take rate 2.97% of GMV** (up from 2.74%) — revenue growing faster than GMV, i.e. monetization improving.
- **Transaction margin dollars: $389M, +44% YoY** (Q1'25 $271M).
- **Provision for credit losses: $186M, 0.55% of GMV** (vs 0.54% in Q1'25) — credit performance stable/benign, aided by seasonal strength and maturation of the Fair Financing portfolio.
- **Net income: $1M** (vs net loss of $99M in Q1'25); operating income $17M (vs $(89)M). **Adjusted operating profit: $68M** (vs $3M).
- **Active consumers: 119M, +21%**; **merchants: 1M+, +49%** (Q1'25 671K) — the merchant-network expansion (new verticals such as live-events ticketing) is directly visible in the count.

**Guidance / outlook:**
- Full-year 2026 outlook reiterated: **GMV >$155B**, revenue >2.8% of GMV, transaction margin >1.04% of GMV, adjusted operating profit >6.9% of revenues.
- Q2 2026 guidance: GMV $35.5B–$36.5B, revenue $960M–$1.0B, transaction margin dollars $375M–$395M, adjusted operating income $30M–$50M.

**Beat/miss:** Q1 2026 was reported by multiple outlets as a **beat** (revenue and GMV ahead of estimates), with the company swinging to a small net profit vs a prior-year loss.

**Thesis flags for the Greece/Ticketmaster launch:**
- Consistent with Klarna's stated strategy of embedding instalment payments in diverse merchant categories across Europe and differentiating via vertical-specific integrations (ticketing/live events, beyond retail/e-commerce). The +49% YoY merchant growth is the quantitative expression of this land-grab.
- Take-rate expansion (2.97%) and benign credit losses (0.55% of GMV) give Klarna room to add lower-friction, higher-value ticketing checkouts without straining unit economics — though live-events instalments (large, discretionary one-off purchases) bear watching on future loss rates.
- Financially immaterial in isolation: no disclosed terms or Greek volume expectations, single-market (Greece only), one vertical. Directional support for the European-expansion thesis rather than a numbers-moving event.
