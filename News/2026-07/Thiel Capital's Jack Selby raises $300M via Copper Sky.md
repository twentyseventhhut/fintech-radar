Thiel Capital’s Jack Selby nabs stakes in hot startups like Etched through Arizona connections
Nvidia competitor Etched announced this week that TSMC had manufactured its first chip earlier this year. While the four-year-old startup valued at $5 billion is getting ready to ship systems powered by that chip to customers later this summer, scaling production may prove challenging. Like other chip designers, Etched must compete for limited capacity at TSMC’s Taiwan factories.
Copper Sky Capital, one of Etched’s early investors, is hopeful that the chipmaker will find a solution to its manufacturing constraints by eventually producing chips at Arizona’s TSMC facility. When the four-year-old VC firm invested in Etched’s $120 million Series A two years ago, founder Jack Selby secured an allocation in part by promising to help the startup eventually reshore its chip fabrication to Arizona.
Selby, a former PayPal exec and longtime managing director to Peter Thiel’s family office, Thiel Capital, founded Phoenix-based Copper Sky in 2021 (formerly known as AZ-VC). The firm’s first $115 million fund focused primarily on startups based in Arizona and the Southwest. Selby’s thesis was that most coastal startups, particularly those based in California, Massachusetts, and New York, are grossly overpriced compared to companies popping up in his region. However, Selby saw an opportunity to bridge the gap in the other direction by helping California-based hardware startups move their production to Arizona.
Selby credits Copper Sky’s investment in Etched — an otherwise hard-to-access startup — to his influential role in Arizona’s economy. As a board member of the Arizona Commerce Authority , Selby is deeply involved in recruiting out-of-state businesses to set up manufacturing operations in the region.
“When Copper Sky invested with Etched, the company clearly understood our connectivity to the Arizona semiconductor industry, and in particular the local TSMC GIGAFAB,” Selby told TechCrunch.
While Copper Sky has recently expanded its focus beyond the Southwest to include nontraditional venture hubs nationwide, Selby said that the firm is also interested in backing hardware companies, including in the defense sector, that can set up manufacturing operations in Arizona. 
The firm is expected to soon have more capital to invest in those higher-priced coastal companies, and those throughout the United States. Copper Sky is currently raising a $300 million second fund, according to a regulatory filing .

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
**Reconciled reality of the numbers — the headline is misleading.** Copper Sky Capital (Phoenix; formerly AZ-VC) is *raising* a **$300M second fund** — this is a **target from a regulatory filing (Form D-style)**, NOT capital already closed. There is **no confirmed final close**, and no source cites a **$135M** figure (that number does not appear in TechCrunch, Bloomberg, PRNewswire, or the firm's own materials — it appears to be a conflation; the closest real figure is Fund I's **$115M**). So the accurate framing is: **"Copper Sky is *seeking* up to $300M for Fund II"**, not "raised $300M." The note's title "raises $300M" overstates it — Fund II is open and being marketed to family offices (Bloomberg, Feb 2026: "Peter Thiel's money manager seeks $300M from global family offices" — Middle East, Europe, central US).

- **Fund I:** $115M, closed, Arizona/Southwest-focused.
- **Fund II:** $300M **target**, launched ~Sept 2025, still raising as of Jul 2026.
- **Etched:** Copper Sky was an early investor in Etched's **$120M Series A ~2 years ago** (2024). Etched now valued ~$5B; TSMC manufactured its first chip; Selby's angle is reshoring fab to Arizona's TSMC GIGAFAB.
- **Why framed this way:** the TechCrunch story is really a *profile* of Selby's Arizona-connection deal-sourcing edge (board of the Arizona Commerce Authority → allocation access to hard-to-enter rounds like Etched). The "$300M second fund" is a one-line kicker, not a closed-round announcement. The PR value = "Thiel money-man + hot AI-chip name + Arizona reshoring narrative." ## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Asset class is US regional / non-coastal emerging-manager venture (Fund II, target $300M vs debut $115M — a ~2.6x step-up). Structure: fundraising for emerging/first-and-second-time managers is one of the most inhospitable environments in a decade — per PitchBook/NVCA data cited across 2026 industry coverage, first-time fund activity has collapsed toward decade lows as LPs concentrate capital in re-ups with established GPs ("flight to quality"), while family offices remain the most accessible pool for smaller managers (as of 2026, via Pipelineroad / PE International). "Why now": the entry thesis is a cross-regional valuation arbitrage — Selby claims an "average 70% valuation discount vs coastal companies with similar financial metrics" (per PRNewswire AZ-VC II launch, Sep 2025; this is the GP's own marketing claim, not independently verified). Fund II widens the mandate beyond the Southwest to "nontraditional venture hubs nationwide" plus defense/hardware reshoring to Arizona (per TechCrunch, 2026-07-02). Second-order effect: broadening the mandate dilutes the original geographic-arbitrage differentiation just as the fund triples in size — the "non-coastal discount" pitch and the plan to chase "higher-priced coastal companies" are in tension.

**Competitive landscape.** Emerging-manager VC KPIs: fund size / step-up ratio, DPI/TVPI track record, GP re-up rate, LP concentration. Copper Sky's differentiation is not financial multiples but *access via influence* — Selby sits on the Arizona Commerce Authority board and used that position (plus a promise to help reshore chip fab to Arizona's TSMC GIGAFAB) to win an allocation in Etched's 2024 $120M Series A, an otherwise hard-to-access deal (per TechCrunch). Basis of competition among regional emerging managers is distribution/access + local network, not price or product. Moat: the Arizona Commerce Authority seat and Thiel/PayPal-mafia network are genuine intangible/relationship moats `(analysis)` — but they are person-dependent (tied to Selby individually) and non-durable if his public role changes. Position vs peers: niche, access-driven, catching up on scale (Fund II would move it from micro toward lower-mid-market emerging manager).

**Comps & multiples.** No trading multiples apply — this is a private GP raising a blind-pool fund, not a company with revenue/EV. What is checkable is the anchor mark that underpins the Fund II pitch: Etched entry at the $120M Series A (Jun 2024, led by Positive Sum + Primary Venture Partners) → last round ~$500M at a **$5.0bn** post-money (Jan 2026, per Bloomberg/TechCrunch). Etched Series A post-money was not disclosed publicly `[UNSOURCED]`, so a precise entry→now multiple can't be computed; on a rough basis the 2024-vintage $5bn mark is a very large paper markup but the realized DPI is "no data" (unrealized, illiquid). Internal comps (recent emerging-manager / growth-fund closes in base, as context on size — not direct peers): [[Secha Capital reaches second close on $40M SME growth fund]], [[Mubadala-backed AAF closes $55M early-stage hybrid fund]], [[Alive Ventures closes $55 million Latin America fund]], [[Khwarizmi Ventures launches second fund with $70 million first close]]. All are $30–70M funds — Copper Sky's $300M target is an order of magnitude larger, so the raise is ambitious relative to the emerging-manager closes the base is seeing in 2026 (flag: large step-up into a hostile LP climate).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Key-person / access dependence.** The entire edge (Etched allocation, deal access) is tied to Selby's personal Thiel network and his Arizona Commerce Authority seat; loss of the public role or of Selby erodes the moat. Also a potential conflict-of-interest optic: a state economic-development board member steering reshoring deals into his own fund's portfolio.
2. **Fundraising / step-up risk.** ~2.6x jump ($115M→$300M) into a 2026 market where LPs are pulling back from emerging managers and re-upping with established names; the $300M is a *target from a regulatory filing*, not a close — "raising," not "raised" (de-PR).
3. **Thesis drift + single-name concentration.** Broadening from "70% non-coastal discount" to chasing higher-priced coastal/defense hardware undercuts the original arbitrage; and the marketing leans heavily on one hot mark (Etched), whose own scaling depends on scarce TSMC capacity — a paper markup, not realized DPI.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a test of whether relationship/policy-access differentiation can carry an emerging manager to lower-mid-market scale ($300M) in a flight-to-quality LP environment — most peers in-base are closing at $30–70M. Falsifiable thesis: if Copper Sky closes at or near $300M, it signals LPs will still fund emerging managers whose edge is *access* (not track record) and validates the Arizona-reshoring angle as a fundable narrative. What breaks it: a close well short of target, a markdown on Etched, or Selby exiting the Commerce Authority role. Trigger to watch: the actual Fund II final-close size/date and Etched's ability to secure TSMC Arizona capacity.
