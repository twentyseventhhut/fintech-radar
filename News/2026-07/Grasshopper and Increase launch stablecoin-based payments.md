Grasshopper and Increase launch stablecoin-based payments
Paula Albu
02 Jul 2026 / 5 Min Read
US-based Grasshopper Bank has launched a stablecoin-based payments capability developed in partnership with Increase, a banking infrastructure provider for technology companies. The capability allows fintechs to move funds internationally using blockchain rails, aiming to combine faster settlement with an API-based user experience.
Addressing delays in cross-border payments
International payments typically take longer to settle than domestic transactions due to differing bank operating hours, processing cutoffs, correspondent banking networks, and batch processing cycles. While stablecoins can offer a faster settlement alternative, converting fiat funds onto blockchain rails has historically taken several days. The new capability is designed to reduce that friction by allowing fintechs to convert fiat funds held in a Grasshopper Bank account into USDC, issued through Circle's regulated affiliates, via an Increase API. Transactions can then be tracked end-to-end, with funds settling on supported blockchains on a continuous basis.
A company official at Increase said stablecoin payments have traditionally been constrained by the sequence of ACH payments and wire transfers required before a blockchain transaction could occur, and that the partnership with Grasshopper is intended to make the process more direct by allowing customers to move funds on- and off-chain from accounts they already hold.
How the capability works
The offering is integrated natively into Increase's banking infrastructure, enabling fintechs to access stablecoin payments across multiple blockchains. Transactions can be initiated in US dollars and converted into stablecoins for on-chain settlement or initiated on-chain with funds landing in a US dollar account. The structure is intended to give fintechs flexibility in how they route international payments, depending on whether a transaction originates in fiat currency or on a blockchain network.
Positioning within the payments landscape
A company official at Grasshopper Bank described stablecoins as a new mechanism for cross-border money movement, noting that the bank intends to continue operating within existing regulatory and risk management frameworks as it expands its stablecoin offering. Grasshopper Bank said the partnership positions it among an early group of US banks to bring stablecoin capability to market as part of a broader payments strategy, targeting fintechs seeking alternative routes for international money movement.
The launch reflects a wider trend of banks and infrastructure providers integrating stablecoin rails into traditional banking systems, as regulated stablecoin issuance and blockchain settlement continue to be explored as complements to existing cross-border payment methods.

# Context-enrichment: Grasshopper and Increase launch stablecoin-based payments
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5. Freshness: fresh._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 2026-07-01/02 Grasshopper Bank (US digital SMB/commercial bank) and Increase (banking-infrastructure/BaaS provider for tech companies) announced a stablecoin-payments capability: a fintech holding fiat in a Grasshopper account can convert USD → USDC (issued via Circle's regulated affiliates) through an **Increase API**, then settle on supported blockchains 24/7 (or the reverse — on-chain → USD account). End-to-end tracking; multi-chain; bidirectional (fiat-in or on-chain-in).
- **De-PR'd:** This is a **launch of a capability/API**, not a live-traction disclosure. No named client fintechs, no volume, no fee/spread economics, no list of supported chains, no go-live vs "available" distinction beyond the press language. The "delays in cross-border payments" framing (bank hours, correspondent chains, ACH→wire sequencing before an on-chain tx) is the standard stablecoin-rails pitch used by every peer below.
- **Why structured this way (analysis):** The value is in **collapsing the ACH/wire pre-funding sequence** — Increase's own official frames the delta as "customers move funds on/off-chain from accounts they already hold" (i.e., no separate treasury hop). That is a real UX/settlement-time delta *if* Increase's direct core does the fiat leg and Circle does the mint. But it is Circle USDC underneath — Grasshopper/Increase are **distribution + a bank wrapper**, not issuers. The economics (who keeps the reserve yield, the FX/conversion spread) are silent.
- **Second order (analysis):** Grasshopper is **being acquired by Enova ($369M, announced Dec 2025, expected to close H2 2026 pending OCC/Fed approval)** — see [[Enova to acquire Grasshopper Bank for $369M]]. So this launch lands *during a pending change of control*. Reads as feature-shipping / roadmap-continuity (stablecoins were flagged in Grasshopper's H2-2025 innovation plan) rather than a standalone strategic bet; durability of the product past the Enova close is an open question.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Bank/BaaS + Circle stablecoin rails is now a crowded lane, almost all built on the **same USDC/Circle substrate**:
- **FIS + Circle** (2025-07) — USDC money-movement for FI customers → [[FIS partners with Circle for bank stablecoin payments]].
- **Visa** launched USDC settlement for US banks (2025-12) → [[Visa launches USDC stablecoin settlement for US banks]].
- **Nium + Circle** (2026-05) — USDC settlement wired into global payouts (Circle Payments Network) → [[Nium and Circle connect USDC settlement with global payouts]].
- **Circle + Finastra** (2025-08/09) — USDC into GPP's $5T/day cross-border network → [[Circle and Finastra weave USDC settlement into bank payments]] / [[Circle partners with Finastra on cross-border stablecoin payments]].
- **Banking Circle** (EU) — stablecoin settlement/clearing after CASP license (2026-04/05) → [[Banking Circle launches stablecoin settlement after CASP license]].
- **Bridge (Stripe, ~$1.1B acq)** — API-first stablecoin issuance/orchestration; **BVNK** ($30B+ 2025 flows); **Column, Lead Bank** — the direct BaaS-for-stablecoin comps.
- **Position:** catching-up / parity, not ahead. Grasshopper's own claim is "among the **first US banks**" to bring stablecoin capability — a defensible but modest badge given FIS/Visa/Finastra already shipped bank-facing USDC rails 6–12 months earlier.
- **Why the landscape looks this way (analysis):** Post-GENIUS-Act regulatory clarity + Circle's aggressive FI distribution (CPN) turned "stablecoin rails" into a **checkbox product** banks/BaaS bolt on via Circle, not a moat. Differentiation is collapsing to distribution (which fintechs you already hold deposits for) and the fiat on/off-ramp UX — which is exactly where Increase's direct-Fed-core is the actual asset here, not Grasshopper.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Grasshopper:** founded 2016, digital commercial bank for SMB/startups/VC/BaaS; ~$160M raised; $46.6M raise Aug 2025 (Patriot Financial Partners) → [[Grasshopper raises $46.6 million for small-business bank]]; also AI/BaaS moves (Narmi MCP, Lendio SBA). Now under Enova acquisition. Stablecoins were pre-flagged in its H2-2025 innovation plan, so this fits the roadmap.
- **Increase:** founded 2021 by Darragh Buckley (Stripe's first employee), YC S20, **self-funded**, ~<20 people, profitable in 2025, connects directly to the Fed, processing >$100B/yr; monthly volume tripled and deposits 4x'd. Prior corpus: push-to-card add (2026-05) → [[Increase adds push-to-card transfers to payments platform]]; Buckley's bank stake → [[Stripe's first employee acquires stake in Twin City Bank]].
- **Why they act this way (analysis):** Increase monetizes as pure API infra — every new rail (RTP, push-to-card, now stablecoin) widens the API surface it rents to fintechs; stablecoin is a natural next primitive. Grasshopper supplies the **chartered-bank + FDIC account** that holds the fiat leg. The pairing is logical: Increase = rail/core, Grasshopper = the bank of record. But Increase's own separate bank ownership (Twin City) hints it may not need Grasshopper long-term.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Really new?** No new primitive. USD↔USDC via bank account + API already exists (FIS, Nium, Bridge, Banking Circle). The **incremental** value-add is Increase eliminating the ACH→wire pre-funding hop by doing the fiat leg on its own direct core — a genuine but narrow latency/UX improvement.
- **Traction:** **None disclosed.** No clients, no volume, no chains named. Announcement-stage; treat as "launched capability," not adoption. (Per skill: framed ≠ live.)
- **Who captures the margin (analysis):** **Circle** keeps the USDC reserve yield (the fat part). Grasshopper/Increase capture a thin conversion/API fee + deposit float on the fiat leg. So this is a **commoditized distribution layer on someone else's mint** — the multiple-bearing asset (reserve economics) sits at Circle, not here. That caps strategic upside and is why importance is low.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Regulatory tailwind: GENIUS Act + bank-led USDC minting momentum (Circle + Standard Chartered bank-led minting, 2026-07) is pulling more chartered banks into issuance/settlement. Sentiment: stablecoin B2B/cross-border volume large and growing ($33T total stablecoin volume 2025, +72% YoY per industry press — includes non-payment flows; treat as directional).
- **Risks / open:** Enova close could re-prioritize or absorb the product; no proof of fintech uptake; competition from Stripe/Bridge and Circle's own CPN could disintermediate a small-bank wrapper. **Counterintuitive second order (analysis):** the more banks ship near-identical Circle-based rails, the *less* any single launch matters — value concentrates further into Circle (the mint) and into whoever owns the fiat on/off-ramp UX at scale, not into "the Nth US bank to offer USDC."

## Red-team / challenge questions

1. **Is any of this live?** No client fintechs, no volume, no go-live date beyond "launched" — is this a capability announcement or actual production adoption? → **Open; treat as announcement-stage.**
2. **What is the actual settlement-time delta** vs a plain FIS/Nium/Circle USDC rail? Increase's claim is "no ACH→wire pre-funding hop." How many hours/days does that save in practice? → **Open; unquantified.**
3. **Who keeps the reserve yield** on the USDC? → Almost certainly **Circle** (the issuer). Grasshopper/Increase get a thin conversion fee + fiat float. This caps the strategic value.
4. **What FX/conversion spread** do fintechs pay to cross USD↔USDC↔local? → **Silent.** The real cross-border economics live here and are undisclosed.
5. **Which blockchains are supported?** "Supported blockchains" is unnamed → chain coverage (Solana/Base/Ethereum/Tron?) is unknown and material for cost/speed.
6. **Does the Enova acquisition ($369M, close H2 2026) survive/absorb this product?** → **Open.** Change-of-control risk over the launch's durability.
7. **Why does Grasshopper matter if Increase already owns a bank (Twin City)?** → Possible that Increase uses Grasshopper opportunistically; the bank-of-record could be swapped, weakening Grasshopper's positioning.
8. **Is "among the first US banks" defensible** when FIS (2025-07), Visa (2025-12) and Finastra (2025-08) already shipped bank-facing USDC rails? → Marketing; it's parity/catch-up, not first-mover.
9. **What's the fraud/AML liability split** on on/off-ramp and who owns travel-rule/OFAC screening on-chain? → **Silent.**
10. **Is there any exclusivity** between Grasshopper and Increase, or is Increase offering the same API to other banks? → **Open;** likely non-exclusive given Increase's infra model, which commoditizes Grasshopper's role.
11. **What durable revenue does this add** to Increase (API/usage) vs Grasshopper (deposits/float)? → For a small bank mid-acquisition, likely immaterial to the $369M valuation.
12. **Does this deepen dependence on Circle** (single-issuer USDC concentration)? → Yes; no multi-issuer/EURC/USDT optionality mentioned → concentration risk.
13. **Counterfactual:** if a fintech already uses Bridge/Stripe or Circle CPN directly, why route through a small acquiring bank? → Weak switching incentive absent a clear price/latency edge.
14. **Does regulatory clarity (GENIUS Act) help incumbents or the small players?** → Likely accelerates big-bank and Circle-direct issuance, squeezing small-bank wrappers → second-order negative for this positioning.
15. **What breaks the thesis?** → Circle disintermediating via CPN + Stripe/Bridge scale → a Nth-me-too USDC rail from a bank being acquired has little standalone weight.

**Importance: 2/5** — Real but narrow UX/settlement improvement (removing the ACH→wire pre-funding hop) on top of a fully commoditized USDC/Circle rail. No disclosed traction (clients/volume/chains/economics), it is parity/catch-up vs FIS/Visa/Nium/Finastra, the margin-bearing asset sits at Circle, and the launching bank is mid-acquisition by Enova. Notable mainly as another data point in the "banks bolt on Circle USDC rails" trend, not as a standalone mover.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** US bank-issued stablecoin cross-border payments, post-GENIUS Act (signed Jul 2025). Sizing: stablecoin *payment* volume (ex-trading/bot flows) hit ~$390bn in 2025, ~2x YoY, of which B2B ~$226bn, +733% YoY (per McKinsey/Artemis, via cnbc.com/whitesight, as of 2025). Forward: 21Shares forecasts total stablecoin market >$1tn by end-2026, and industry estimates put stablecoins at 5–10% of cross-border by 2030 = $2.1–4.2tn (per fintechweekly/purdue citations — forecasts, treat as directional, not verified TAM). Structure — fragmented and layered: issuer (Circle/USDC here), chartered bank fiat on/off-ramp (Grasshopper), and API/orchestration infra (Increase). Value increasingly captured by whoever owns the direct fiat↔chain conversion inside a regulated account, collapsing the old ACH→wire→bridge sequence. Barriers: bank charter + OCC oversight, Circle affiliate access, network integrations. **Why now:** GENIUS Act gave banks a legal path to stablecoin rails; OCC conditionally chartered Circle/Paxos + others (Dec 2025); JPMorgan Kinexys live for institutions early-2026. Regulatory clarity + faster-settlement demand from SMB/fintech cross-border is the catalyst.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: cross-border TPV on-chain, fiat↔USDC conversion volume, settlement speed/uptime (24/7), API attach rate; economics (spread/FX/conversion fee) undisclosed here → `[UNSOURCED]`. Basis of competition = distribution (which banks/fintechs plug in) + regulatory posture, less price. Key players & recent dated moves: Visa launched USDC settlement for US banks with Cross River + Lead Bank (Dec 2025); Circle + Finastra/Mastercard wove USDC into bank cross-border rails (Aug–Sep 2025); Brex added USDC pay-in/out (Oct 2025); Banking Circle launched EU stablecoin settlement post-CASP (Apr–May 2026); Stripe/Bridge, Column, Modern Treasury on the infra layer. Grasshopper's position: **niche / fast-follower**, not first — it markets itself as "among the first US banks," but Cross River and Lead Bank were already live on Visa's rail in Dec 2025. Moat is thin and mostly borrowed: the differentiation (native fiat↔USDC inside an existing account) sits in Increase's API and Circle's issuance, not in Grasshopper. Grasshopper's own asset: a national charter + fintech/BaaS distribution ($3bn deposits, $1.19bn BaaS deposits, $40bn+ tx volume in 2025, per grasshopper.bank press) (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** No round/valuation/revenue attached to this launch → deal multiples not computable ("no data"). Reference points: Increase (private, banking-core API for tech cos; funding/valuation not disclosed in reviewed sources → `[UNSOURCED]`). Nearest priced infra comp — **Stripe acquired Bridge for $1.1bn** (closed Feb 2025), a ~5.5x step-up on Bridge's Aug-2024 $200m Series A post-money = `$1.1bn / $200m = 5.5x` in ~2 months (revenue-multiple not disclosable → the 5.5x is markup-on-prior-round, NOT EV/Rev). Grasshopper itself: **Enova to acquire for $369m** (announced ~end-2025, expected close late 2026; per finovate) — a bank-level, not stablecoin, valuation. Internal comps (base): [[Visa launches USDC stablecoin settlement for US banks]], [[Brex launches stablecoin payment platform supporting Circle USDC]], [[Circle partners with Finastra on cross-border stablecoin payments]], [[Banking Circle launches stablecoin settlement after CASP license]], [[Visa, Bridge, Pine Labs and Wirex expand stablecoin card initiatives]]. Verdict: qualitative comparison only, distribution not computed (no ≥3 comparable revenue/EV figures).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Pending acquisition / integration overhang.** Grasshopper is being acquired by Enova ($369m, close expected late 2026). A brand-new, thin-staffed stablecoin line launched into an ownership transition risks deprioritization or strategy reset under a consumer-credit-focused acquirer — execution and continuity risk.
2. **Disintermediation — value sits above the bank.** The economic differentiation (native on/off-ramp, multi-chain routing) lives in Increase's API and Circle's issuance. Grasshopper supplies the charter and a fiat account; if Increase/Circle plug the same stack into other chartered banks, Grasshopper is a substitutable rail with limited pricing power.
3. **Late-mover + regulatory/AML exposure.** "Among the first" is de-PR'd to fast-follower (Cross River/Lead Bank live Dec 2025). Cross-border on-chain flows for fintechs raise sanctions/AML/travel-rule liability; PR is silent on who owns fraud/compliance liability, FX spread, and ticket size — the undisclosed economics are the tell.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Confirms the GENIUS-Act pattern: chartered banks renting stablecoin capability from infra providers (Increase, Circle) rather than building it — a distribution land-grab, not a re-rating (analysis). Watch whether the value accrues to the infra layer (Increase/Bridge/Column) or the charter holders; thesis breaks if banks in-source issuance/on-ramp (via own OCC stablecoin charters) and cut the middleware. Trigger to watch: post-Enova-close roadmap for this product, and whether Grasshopper discloses any TPV/economics.
