 В «Сбере» спрогнозировали всплеск сделок M&A на российском рынке 
В «Сбере» прогнозируют усиление активности сделок по слиянию и поглощению (M&A, Mergers and Acquisitions) осенью 2026 года. Этот процесс позволит экономике и фондовому рынку начать выход из фазы замедления. Таким прогнозом поделился с «Р БК Инвестициями » в кулуарах Финансового конгресса Банка России главный аналитик «Сбера» Михаил Матовников.
«Активы, в наибольшей степени затронутые падением спроса и финансовыми трудностями, будут находить нового собственника и так или иначе избавляться от избыточного долга. Это позволит экономике начать перераспределение ресурсов — финансовых, трудовых и производственных, чтобы использовать их более эффективно. Это будут самые ранние индикаторы того, что экономика вошла в фазу реструктуризации, — значит, появились люди, деньги и понимание, что делать с этими активами», — отметил эксперт. По его мнению, период восстановления займет около года.
Главный индикатор выхода экономики из замедления, по словам эксперта, рынок увидит уже в процессе восстановления. Им станет резкое замедление инвестиционного спада — годовое падение инвестиций вместо текущих 14% составит 2–3%.
В данный момент, как отметил Матовников, Россия переживает первый в новейшей истории страны циклический кризис — период, когда производственные мощности превышают потребности экономики. Это результат переинвестиций в предыдущий период, считает эксперт. Из такого кризиса, по словам Матовникова, компании, как правило, выходят через смену собственника без закрытия бизнеса, реже — через банкротство. В результате в выигрыше от высоких ставок останутся компании с ресурсом для приобретения конкурентов, уверен аналитик.

# Context-enrichment: Sber forecasts surge in Russian M&A activity this autumn
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5. Freshness: fresh._

**FRESHNESS / DUPLICATE VERDICT: FRESH.** No prior note in the corpus carries this specific Sber M&A macro-forecast. The closest internal items are *individual* Russian deals ([[Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka]], [[VTB to sell former Otkritie office to Wildberries-Russ]], [[2GIS founder Sysoev on investors and the sale to Sber]], [[Russia weighs selling up to 5% of NSPK to single investor]]) and one foreign exit ([[Citigroup to sell remaining Russian operations at $1.2bn loss]]) — none is Sber's cyclical-restructuring thesis. This is a fresh piece of *commentary* (not a deal), so weight is modest.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Mikhail Matovnikov, chief analyst / senior managing director at Sber, told **RBC Investments** on the sidelines of the **Bank of Russia Financial Congress** (held 1 July 2026) that Russian M&A activity will **intensify in autumn 2026**. His mechanism is explicitly *domestic and cyclical*, not sanctions-driven:
- Russia is in its "first cyclical crisis in modern history" — over-investment in the prior cycle left production capacity above demand.
- Distressed assets (hit by falling demand + high rates) will change owners and shed excess debt; firms typically exit such a crisis *via ownership change without shutting the business* (bankruptcy is rarer).
- This reallocation of capital/labour/production is the "earliest indicator" the economy has entered a **restructuring phase**; recovery ~1 year.
- His headline recovery signal: the investment slump easing from a ~14% annual drop to 2–3%.
- Winners: firms with **spare capital to buy weakened competitors** — i.e., high rates *concentrate* assets toward the strong.

**Why framed this way / what it reveals:** This is the *optimistic wrapper on a pessimistic base*. Sber (both as Russia's dominant lender and as an M&A financier via SberInvestments/CIB) has an interest in reframing a corporate-distress cycle — rising loan-restructuring requests, deteriorating borrower health — as a *healthy* reallocation with an M&A upside it can bankroll and advise on. Note the same Matovnikov warned in April 2026 of a "wave of non-payments" and accumulating banking-system risk (abn.agency, 14 Apr 2026). So "M&A surge" = the deal-flow *symptom* of a distressed-debt/restructuring cycle, not a bull market. (analysis)

## [1] Competitors / peers (as forecasters, and as deal market)
- **The forecast itself** is consensus among Russian houses, not a Sber scoop: T-Bank/Raptor Capital, Forbes.ru, Kommersant and legal advisers (Kept, AK&M) all flag *rising 2026 M&A on the far side of peak rates*. So Sber is echoing, not leading (parity, arguably lagging the published-research crowd).
- **Actual market direction contradicts the "surge" tone so far:** Q1 2026 Russian M&A was **$5.3bn, −28% YoY** (Kommersant); Jan–Sep 2025 was **$25.9bn, −23% YoY**, deals 288 vs 321. The surge is a *forecast*, and the base is falling.
- **Foreign-exit channel is nearly dead**, not a driver: AK&M counts ~**23 exit deals in full-2025 (≈8% of volume)**, down from 111 (2023) and 63 (2024); 9M-2025 exit value was $642.8m (2.5% of total). Mandatory ≥60% discount + 35% "voluntary" budget contribution + Government Commission approval have throttled it (Russia Post/KSE).

**Why the lay of the land is this way (2nd order):** The popular Western frame — "M&A surge = foreigners fleeing" — is *wrong for this story*. The foreign-exit wave already happened and is exhausted; the *next* Russian M&A leg is **intra-Russian distress consolidation**. That shifts the central question from "who's leaving Russia" to "which domestic balance sheets survive high rates and absorb the rest." (analysis)

## [2] Company history / fit
Sber sits on both sides of this thesis: (a) largest corporate lender → first to see restructuring requests and cost-of-risk rise; (b) an active *acquirer/consolidator* itself — recent [[2GIS founder Sysoev on investors and the sale to Sber]] shows Sber absorbing tech assets, and it anchors an ecosystem (SberInvestments/CIB advisory). So a "buyers with capital win" forecast is also self-interested: Sber *is* one of the best-capitalised buyers. The fit is logical: a lender facing a distressed-credit cycle would rather see borrowers *sold and recapitalised* than default. (analysis)

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Low novelty as a *claim* (consensus). The value-add is Matovnikov's **specific, falsifiable indicator** — investment-slump deceleration from ~14% to 2–3% as the turn signal — which is more useful than a generic "deals will rise." Traction = **zero** here: this is a forecast, no deal count, no pipeline figure, no timeline beyond "autumn / ~1 year." Treat as directional commentary, not evidence. Who captures the value in a distress-M&A cycle: the *financier/advisor* (Sber CIB) and the *capital-rich acquirer* — the seller realises little (fire-sale, debt overhang). (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Macro driver:** the Bank of Russia rate-cut path. Sber has repeatedly revised its 2026 key-rate call (from ~12% toward ~13% end-2026); cheaper money is the precondition for the autumn deal pickup. If cuts stall, the "surge" slips.
- **Counterintuitive 2nd-order effect:** high rates + this forecast mean asset *concentration*, not broad recovery — the strong buy the weak. That makes the surviving names *systemically larger and more fragile*, and it hollows out competition. A "recovery via M&A" narrative can coexist with a genuinely stressed real economy (14% investment drop, rising bad debts).
- **Risk to the thesis:** the raw data (Q1-26 −28%, foreign exits ≈0) is *falling*, so the "surge" is unproven; also government/antitrust approval and the same red tape that killed foreign exits could slow domestic distressed sales.

## Market Research

**Sector & drivers.** Russian M&A market (deal advisory / capital markets, ties to banking). Market SIZE contracted, not surged: per AK&M, 2025 total value fell **24.6% to $41.12bn** (from $54.52bn in 2024); underlying volume ex-mega-deals $26.55bn, -8.1% YoY (per AK&M, via akm.ru, for FY2025). The *driver mix has rotated*: foreign-exit deals — the 2022–24 growth engine — have collapsed to a "trickle": **23 exit deals in 2025, ~8% of volume** (vs 63 in 2024, 111 in 2023), with experts (Maxim Tolkachev, DRT) projecting exits "will tend toward zero" (per Russia Post, via russiapost.info, mid-2026). Barriers to exit are structural: Government Commission approval + mandatory ≥60% discount + 35%-of-value "voluntary" budget contribution (per Russia Post) — plus a rising share of forced seizures/nationalizations (per KSE Institute). Sber's *own* thesis (Matovnikov, main analyst, at CBR Financial Congress) is different and internally consistent: an autumn-2026 surge driven by **domestic distressed consolidation** — over-invested, over-indebted firms in a "cyclical crisis" changing owners without closing, with high-rate survivors (cash-rich acquirers) buying weaker rivals. "Why now": the rate cycle is turning — CBR cut to **14.25% on 19 Jun 2026** (25bp, smaller than the 50bp consensus), 4th cut in a path down from 16.50% (Oct 2025), inflation 5.6% as of 15 Jun 2026 (per CBR/Moscow Times). Cheaper (but still-high) money + a year of demand shock = the classic setup for distressed M&A.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: deal count & aggregate value (AK&M threshold $1m), deal-advisory league-table share, financing (acquisition lending) volume. Basis of competition among Russian advisors post-Western-exit: balance-sheet (ability to *finance* the buyer), state proximity, and deal origination. Sber's position: **ahead** — it is simultaneously the dominant acquisition lender, an equity/DCM underwriter (Sber CIB), and the largest bank by assets, so it captures both the advisory fee and the financing spread on the same consolidation wave `(analysis)`. Note the tell: AK&M flags the *commercial* deal market shrinking amid rising *state-owned* transactions — i.e. the "surge" Sber forecasts is partly a re-allocation toward state/quasi-state buyers, a segment where Sber (majority state-controlled) is structurally advantaged. Recent macro comps: Citi completed its Russia exit at a $1.2bn loss (Jan 2026, sold AO Citibank to Renaissance Capital) — a late data point confirming the exit wave is ending, not starting.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation multiple applies — this is a market-forecast commentary, not a deal with a price. In-base comps for the Russia M&A/exit theme: [[Citigroup to sell remaining Russian operations at $1.2bn loss]] and [[Citi board approves sale of AO Citibank to Renaissance Capital]] (foreign-exit endgame); [[Global fintech investment rebounds to $116 billion in 2025, KPMG says]] (global M&A/exit-activity backdrop). Sber standalone-financials sanity check (IR): profitable, high-ROE base to fund acquisition lending — see IR block. Peer advisor multiples: no data (Russian advisors/CIB units are not separately listed).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Thesis-vs-data gap** — Sber forecasts a *surge* while independent trackers (AK&M -24.6%; DRT "toward zero" on exits) show a *shrinking* market; the surge is a directional hypothesis on domestic distress, unquantified. If the CBR rate path stalls (Jun cut undershot; CBR warned fiscal risks may require a *higher* rate path), distressed sellers hold and the consolidation wave slips — the whole "autumn 2026" timing breaks.
2. **Composition risk / state capture** — the incremental volume skews to state-owned and forced-seizure transactions (AK&M, KSE), so a headline deal-count "surge" can mask a *commercial* market still contracting; fee economics for genuine advisory may not follow the count.
3. **Source is an interested party** — the forecaster (Sber) is the largest beneficiary (advisory + acquisition financing + state proximity); treat the "surge" call as talking-its-own-book, not neutral analysis `(analysis)`.

**IR grounding (Sber).** Sber is IR-covered and sits on a strong, high-return balance sheet — the capacity that underpins the "survivors buy weaker rivals" thesis. Latest reported: Summary Consolidated IFRS Financial Statements **12M 2025 / FY2025** (published 2026-02-26). Disclosed profitability profile (annual report highlights): ROE 17.0% (also a 10.3% figure disclosed alongside, ratios per report), net interest margin 5.9%, cost-to-income 30.3%, net interest income RUB 3,000bn, net fee & commission income RUB 843bn, cost of risk 98bp. Read: a profitable, capital-generative franchise able to *finance* acquisitions on both sides (advisory + acquisition lending), so it is a direct beneficiary of the consolidation wave it forecasts — reinforcing risk flag #3 (interested-party call). Latest FY2025 statements: https://www.sberbank.com/common/img/uploaded/files/info/reporting_q4_8a5ewlbo_2025_en.pdf · FY2024 annual report (highlights above): https://drive.google.com/file/d/11RQ_7D9_QI8dPMWK_MNYA2WyMuhRJNtA/view

**What this changes (idea-lens).** If the thesis holds, the next Russian M&A leg is **domestic distressed consolidation, financed by the survivors' balance sheets** — a re-rating of cash-rich acquirers and the banks that fund them, not a re-run of the foreign-exit discount grab. Falsifiable trigger: watch Q4-2026 AK&M deal-count/value and CBR's next rate decisions — an autumn pickup in *commercial* (non-state) deals + continued rate cuts confirms Matovnikov; a stalled rate path or flat commercial deal-count refutes it `(analysis)`.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
> [!note] This story is market outlook/commentary (Sber's chief analyst M. Matovnikov forecasting a Russian M&A surge in autumn 2026), not an earnings report. No results are disclosed in the news itself. Sber's own latest reported results (FY2025 IFRS, published 2026-02-26) are used below only as financial backdrop for the CIB/advisory franchise that would benefit from such a surge.

**Verdict (headline read on Sber's latest print — FY2025 IFRS). BEAT / IN-LINE** · Net profit RUB 1.7tn (+7.9% YoY) with ROE 22.7% — a record profit delivered through a domestic economic slowdown; net interest income powered the beat while fee income slipped. Not a "miss": profit still grew and hit management's ~22–23% ROE ambition. This scale and profitability are what underpin Sber's CIB advisory franchise — the largest domestic balance sheet and deal-financing capacity in Russia — which is precisely the business that stands to gain if the forecast M&A wave (distressed-asset ownership changes, deleveraging) materialises this autumn.

**Key figures (FY2025 IFRS, primary source = Sber's own filing).**
- Profit for the year attributable to shareholders: **RUB 1,707.4bn** vs **RUB 1,581.6bn** in 2024 = **+8.0% YoY** (filing). Group net profit reported at **RUB 1.7tn, +7.9% YoY** (press).
- **ROE 22.7%** for 2025 (down from ~24% run-rates in 2024 but still elite for the environment; 9M/Q3 2025 ROE was 23.7%).
- **Net interest income RUB ~3.55tn, +18.5% YoY** — the growth engine; Q4 2025 NII +19.9% YoY to RUB 988.2bn. High rates lifted NII despite a slowing economy.
- **Net fee & commission income RUB 833.7bn, −1.1% YoY** — a decline; Q4 −5.2% YoY to ~RUB 219bn. The soft spot.
- Segment detail (filing): B2B net interest income — corporate customers RUB 610.2bn (2024: 617.4), individuals RUB 581.6bn (2024: 441.2), securities RUB 35.5bn (2024: 11.1).

**By segment / driver.** Growth is NII-led (rate-driven, +18.5%) offsetting a small fee-income contraction (−1.1%). Individuals NII inside B2B jumped to RUB 581.6bn from RUB 441.2bn, while corporate NII was roughly flat (RUB 610.2bn vs 617.4bn) — consistent with a domestic slowdown weighting on corporate demand, the very backdrop Matovnikov cites for the coming restructuring/M&A cycle. ROE compression (to 22.7%) is the honest read: still world-class, but margin/return normalising as the cycle turns.

**Thesis-flags.**
- **NII vs fee mix (fact → why → matters → second-order).** NII +18.5% carried the year while fees fell −1.1% → Sber's earnings are currently rate-dependent → if rates fall in a recovery, NII tailwind fades → a rising M&A/advisory fee cycle (the story's thesis) is the natural offset, monetising Sber's dominant CIB position.
- **ROE normalising (22.7% vs ~24%).** Returns are stepping down as the cycle turns → signals the "slowdown" Matovnikov describes → a restructuring/M&A wave that reallocates capital is the mechanism Sber expects to reignite growth; Sber is positioned as a net beneficiary (financing + advising acquirers "with resources to buy competitors").
- **Scale = franchise moat.** RUB 1.7tn profit / 22.7% ROE = the largest domestic capital base → gives Sber the balance sheet to underwrite and finance the very deals it forecasts → advisory + lending double-dip if the autumn 2026 surge lands.
