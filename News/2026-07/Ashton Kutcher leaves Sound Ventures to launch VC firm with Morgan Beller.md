Ashton Kutcher leaving Sound Ventures to launch new VC firm with Morgan Beller
Ashton Kutcher is stepping away from Sound Ventures — the firm he co-founded with Guy Oseary 11 years ago — to start a separate VC fund, the Wall Street Journal reported . The actor and investor’s new firm is being co-founded with Morgan Beller, who until recently was a general partner at seed-focused VC outfit NFX and previously co-led cryptocurrency project Libra at Meta. Beller also spent nearly three years as a partner at Andreessen Horowitz.
TechCrunch had separately heard that Kutcher was preparing to leave; the WSJ’s report confirms it and adds new detail on his plans with Beller. The name of the new firm hasn’t been made public yet. 
Kutcher’s exit doesn’t appear to be a sign of trouble at Sound Ventures — investors often leave firms that are underperforming, but that’s not the case here. The firm, which has backed companies like Brex and Gusto, was also an early investor in OpenAI, Anthropic, and Fei-Fei Li’s World Labs.
The split is also notable for what it signals about where AI money is heading next: Sound built its reputation on concentrated, high-conviction bets in category-leading AI labs, while Kutcher’s new fund appears to be chasing the layer underneath those companies — the infrastructure and energy that power them.
“He and his fund consistently make it onto [my] rankings of top unicorn investors. An interesting case!” Stanford finance professor Ilya Strebulaev, who tracks top-performing VCs, wrote on X .
The actor has known OpenAI’s Sam Altman since Altman founded Loopt — years before the launch of the ChatGPT maker.
Kutcher’s departure was partly due to different views on which startup stages to target for investments, with Sound leaning toward backing companies that are already more established, rather than betting on very early-stage startups, according to WSJ.
Kutcher and Beller are focused on making early-stage investments in AI infrastructure, energy, and deep tech startups — startups built around hard science and engineering breakthroughs rather than software alone.
Despite leaving Sound Ventures, Kutcher will continue to serve as an adviser to the firm. Meanwhile, Oseary and Sound general partner Effie Epstein will advise Kutcher and Beller’s new firm.

## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**What happened.** On 2026-07-01, TechCrunch (confirming a Wall Street Journal report) reported that Ashton Kutcher is leaving Sound Ventures — the firm he co-founded with Guy Oseary 11 years ago (in 2015) — to launch a separate, unnamed early-stage VC firm. His co-founder is Morgan Beller, who until recently was a general partner at seed-focused NFX, previously co-created Meta's Libra/Diem cryptocurrency (leading Novi wallet strategy), and spent nearly three years as a partner at Andreessen Horowitz.

**The thesis.** Kutcher and Beller are targeting early-stage bets in AI infrastructure, energy, and deep tech — "hard science and engineering breakthroughs rather than software alone." A Kutcher spokesperson told the WSJ the firm "will be focused on investing in a post-AGI world." This is a deliberate move down the stack: rather than backing the category-leading model labs (Sound's playbook), the new fund chases the compute/energy layer underneath them.

**Why the split.** Reporting stresses this is not a distress exit. The divergence was over stage: Sound has drifted toward more established, later-stage companies, while Kutcher wants to return to the earliest stage. Ties remain amicable — Kutcher stays on as a Sound adviser, and Oseary plus Sound GP Effie Epstein will advise the new firm. Stanford's Ilya Strebulaev noted Kutcher's fund "consistently make[s] it onto rankings of top unicorn investors."

**Sound Ventures context.** Founded 2015, >$1B AUM. It backed Airbnb, Uber, Brex, Gusto, GitLab, Affirm and Flexport, and — critically — was an early investor in OpenAI, Anthropic, Stability AI and Fei-Fei Li's World Labs. In May 2023 it closed a ~$240M AI Fund built for extreme concentration: ~6 companies at $35–50M each, at the foundation-model layer. Kutcher's decade-long relationship with Sam Altman (dating to Altman's Loopt days) seeded that access.

**Morgan Beller profile.** Cornell statistics grad; a16z deal-team partner; corp-dev/strategy at Medium; joined Facebook in 2017 where she co-created Libra (with David Marcus and Kevin Weil). Libra was renamed Diem, its assets sold to Silvergate in 2022 and written off in 2023. She joined NFX (a ~$1.5B seed fund) as GP in 2020, leading crypto investments, and has now stepped down to co-found this firm. She gives the new fund a rare blend of crypto-founder credibility and large-platform strategic experience.

**Prior related notes (internal):**
- [[Haun Ventures raises $1 billion fund for crypto and AI]] — the largest recent crypto/AI raise; a benchmark for the fundraising environment Kutcher/Beller enter.
- [[CMT Digital closes $136 million fourth crypto venture fund]] — veteran crypto VC ($600M+ AUM) still raising into 2025-26.
- [[Silicon Road Ventures launches India agentic-AI fund, hits first close]] — another "spin-up new fund around AI thesis" data point (2026-05).
- [[SMBC plans $300 million fund for US fintech and AI]] — corporate-backed AI/fintech fund formation, same broad wave.

**Open questions / unknowns.** The new firm's name and fund size are both undisclosed. No first-close, LP roster, or check-size has been reported. "Post-AGI" energy/infrastructure investing is a thesis with no track record yet; the differentiation from Sound's own AI mandate is stated but unproven.

Sources: TechCrunch (2026-07-01), WSJ (via TechCrunch/TNW), TheNextWeb, Forbes, Variety/Fortune (2023 Sound $240M AI fund).

14. Single-source origin (WSJ) with company confirmation but no primary fund docs. How much should be treated as confirmed vs. reported intent?

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** The pick sits at the intersection of two things: (a) the *venture-firm-formation* market (emerging/solo-GP fund launches) and (b) the *AI-infrastructure + energy + deep-tech* thesis those funds are chasing. On (b), spend is the driver: US hyperscaler AI-infrastructure capex is projected at ~$650bn in 2026, with data-centre capex alone ~$602bn, +36% YoY (per Forbes/Intellectia citations, via web, as of Jan 2026) — this is the "layer underneath" the labs that Kutcher's new fund says it is targeting (energy, compute, hard-science). On (a), the fund-formation environment is *tight for first-time managers*: first-time fund closings fell to 34 in 2024, -50% vs 66 in 2022, and average time-to-final-close across VC is ~15 months (Carta/Sifted, via web, as of 2026); LPs now underwrite solo GPs on "deep sector expertise + proprietary deal flow + prior operating track record," not on a thesis alone. Why now: Sound was built on concentrated, late-ish, high-conviction bets in the *labs* (OpenAI, Anthropic, World Labs); Kutcher's stated reason for leaving is a *stage* disagreement — he wants very-early bets, Sound leans established (WSJ via TechCrunch). So this is a spin-out to chase the picks-and-shovels layer at seed, exactly as capex into that layer inflects.

**Competitive landscape.** KPIs the fund-formation "sector" runs on: fund size / final close vs target, DPI/TVPI track record, LP conversion rate, and named deal access. Key players / comps this quarter show the crowd Kutcher is entering: **Deep33** ($100m first close — quantum, AI-infra, energy, robotics), **Ruya Ventures** (solo GP Rick Hao, €43m/~$50m first fund, oversubscribed, deep-tech/energy/semis), **Duration Ventures** (ex-Lightspeed/GC, targeting $375m debut enterprise-AI), plus in-corpus [[Air Street Capital closes third fund at $232m for AI]], [[Duration Ventures targets $375 million debut enterprise-AI fund]], [[Ashish Dave launches Sanskrit Capital targeting $84-120 million fund]]. Basis of competition = distribution/access, not capital — the layer is capital-heavy but the differentiator is proprietary deal flow into scarce founders. Kutcher's position: **ahead on brand/access, unproven on the new mandate**. Moat = intangibles/network (Altman relationship pre-ChatGPT; Beller ex-a16z/NFX/Meta-Libra brings seed-stage deal flow and crypto/infra credibility) — genuine, but it is *personal* moat, not firm moat, and it is being rebuilt from zero LP dollars. Note Sound (Oseary) and Kutcher will cross-advise — an amicable split, not a blow-up.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation/round/metrics attach to this pick — the new firm's **fund size, name, and LP base are all undisclosed** — so trading multiples do **not apply** ("no data"). What is anchorable is the *reference asset*, Sound Ventures: >$1bn AUM; its 2023 AI Fund closed oversubscribed at ~$240m, later topped up to ~$265.8m (BusinessWire/TechCrunch/VCJ, via web). Peer first-fund sizes for a "rich/cheap" read on what a credible debut looks like here: Deep33 $100m (first close), Ruya ~$50m (final close), Duration $375m (target), Sanskrit ~$84–120m (target), Air Street $232m (Fund III, not a debut). Read: a Kutcher+Beller debut landing anywhere in the ~$100–375m band would be **in-line to rich** for a first-time vehicle purely on brand; a top-decile *track record* argument (Strebulaev's "top unicorn investor" ranking) is a marketing input, not a verified DPI — treat as `[UNSOURCED]` until returns/attribution are disclosed. Median/distribution not computed (comps are heterogeneous by stage/geo/vintage) — qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **First-time-fund + undisclosed-mandate execution risk.** New firm, new stage (very-early vs Sound's later-stage comfort zone), new partnership, no announced fund size or LPs. Second-order: brand raises the money, but seed AI-infra/energy underwriting is a *different muscle* than late-stage lab bets; a mispriced mandate shows up only in ~vintage-year DPI years later.
2. **Capital-intensity / disintermediation of the "energy + infra" thesis.** The layer Kutcher is chasing (data-centre power, hardware, deep tech) is precisely where hyperscaler/sovereign/PE-and-infrastructure balance sheets ($650bn capex) dominate. Second-order: a seed VC risks being disintermediated on the biggest cheques and squeezed into ancillary bets, with returns captured by whoever owns the megawatts and the fabs, not the early equity.
3. **Key-person / personal-moat concentration.** The moat is two people's networks (Kutcher's brand + Beller's a16z/NFX/Meta pedigree), not an institutional franchise. Second-order: LP underwriting and deal access both hinge on those individuals — the same single-decision-maker concentration LPs are flagged as wary of in the 2026 solo/emerging-manager climate.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a *new entry*, not a re-rating: a marquee spin-out signals that smart brand-name money now sees the alpha migrating from AI *labs/apps* down to *infra + energy + deep tech* at seed. Falsifiable thesis: if the AI-infra layer is the next return driver, expect more lab-era investors to launch or pivot debut funds toward compute/energy/hardware over the next 12–18 months. Trigger/catalyst to watch: disclosure of the fund's **name, size, and anchor LPs** — a large, fast, oversubscribed close would confirm LP appetite for the picks-and-shovels thesis; a slow/small close (or a return to software-adjacent bets) would falsify it and suggest the celebrity brand doesn't transfer to a capital-heavy, hard-science mandate.
