# Context-enrichment: PayPal joins the European Payments Council (EPC)
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**BLUF:** Genuinely new (official PayPal press release **2 Jul 2026**; our note is a ~4-day-late secondary pickup), but low-intensity: a **membership / seat-at-the-table** move, not a governance win or a product launch. PayPal is **not the first fintech in** — Stripe joined the EPC on **1 Jan 2025**. The load-bearing subtext, unstated in the PR, is the collision with **Wero/EPI** and the EU's payment-sovereignty push: PayPal, a Luxembourg-licensed EU bank now subject to the Instant Payments Regulation, is buying compliance proximity and interoperability insurance inside a rulebook body dominated by the very European banks sponsoring its main wallet rival.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- PayPal joined the **European Payments Council** — a Brussels industry body (NOT an EU regulator) that writes/maintains the SEPA **scheme rulebooks**: SCT (credit transfer), **SCT Inst** (instant, <10s), **SRTP** (Request-to-Pay), **SPAA** (payment-account access / premium open banking). ~83 member orgs, ~30-seat Board, historically bank-controlled.
- PayPal says it will "contribute to discussions on interoperability, instant payments, fraud prevention, resilience and consumer payment experiences." That is a **working-group input** description, not a mandate.
- **De-PR'd:** "help shape payment standards" is doing heavy lifting. EPC has membership tiers; the associate/participation tier lets you sit in working groups but does NOT confer General Assembly voting. **PayPal's exact tier / voting rights are undisclosed** — the single biggest determinant of how much "shaping" is real vs optics. (open)
- No new product. The official release does **not** mention PYUSD, Venmo, Xoom, Wero or the digital euro. This is positioning.
- **Why framed this way (analysis):** membership is administrative and near-zero-cost, but yields high regulatory optics in an EU actively promoting home-grown rails. The PR anchors to "shaping the future of European payments" precisely because there is no concrete deliverable to point to.

## [1] Competitors / peers (EPC + Wero split)
- **Stripe** — EPC member since **1 Jan 2025**; the fintech precedent (PayPal is the 2nd high-profile non-bank, ~18 months behind). Tink participates in the SPAA scheme.
- **EPI / Wero** — the bank-backed wallet built explicitly as a **sovereign alternative to Visa, Mastercard AND PayPal**. Launched 2 Jul 2024; ~**43–46M** registered users and **€7.5bn+** transfers in year one; live P2P in DE/FR/BE, expanding to LU/NL, e-commerce in DE (end-2025) and **POS planned for 2026**. EPI is itself an EPC member and the EPC promotes Wero. So **PayPal now sits at the same table as its main European competitor's sponsor** (cf. [[Wero e-commerce goes live in Germany]], [[Nuvei and EPI launch Wero payments for European merchants]]).
- **Visa/Mastercard** — the incumbents Europe wants to disintermediate; EPC membership status not confirmed (open). Their competitive lens with PayPal is live in the UK (cf. [[FCA opens competition probe into PayPal, Mastercard and Visa]]).
- **Why the landscape is this way (analysis):** the EU's IPR + digital-euro track is engineering a shift from card rails to A2A instant rails. Whoever authors those rulebooks shapes the next decade of European retail payments; a foreign wallet not in the room risks being standardised toward the edge.

## [2] Company history / fit
- PayPal-Europe runs on a **Luxembourg banking/EMI licence**, so it is itself bound by the **Instant Payments Regulation** (receive SCT Inst by 9 Jan 2025; send + free **Verification of Payee** by 9 Oct 2025 for euro-area PSPs). Joining the EPC plugs it directly into the VoP / instant-payments working groups it must comply with — the most concrete, non-PR rationale.
- Fits a broader 2026 European-defence + turnaround posture under new CEO Enrique Lores: it comes amid Germany-market softness ([[PayPal cites Germany growth slowdown on Q4 2025 earnings call]]) and a stablecoin push ([[PayPal expands dollar stablecoin to seventy markets]]).
- **Why PayPal acts this way (analysis):** its European wallet franchise is structurally threatened by Wero's 2026 e-commerce/POS expansion. A commodity-checkout incumbent facing a subsidised sovereign rival protects itself cheaply by getting inside the standards process rather than fighting the rulebook from outside.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty: low.** A membership approval, not a capability. Stripe already set the fintech precedent; the process (Nominating/Governance committee → General Assembly approval) is routine.
- **Value-add (real but diffuse):** (1) compliance proximity on SCT Inst/VoP; (2) interoperability insurance so PayPal stays "the wallet and commerce layer regardless of underlying rail" (Europe CEO Sean Byrne's framing = ride whatever rail wins, incl. Wero rails); (3) influence over **SPAA** / pay-by-bank rulebooks relevant to embedding PayPal in A2A flows.
- **Traction: none to report.** No users, volume or product added. Influence won't surface until rulebook cycles play out and depends on the (undisclosed) membership tier.
- **Where the margin sits (analysis):** on A2A instant rails the scheme owners (EPC rulebooks) and the wallet layer (Wero vs PayPal) capture different value. PayPal joining EPC is a bid to keep the wallet layer plugged into rails it doesn't own — coopetition: cooperate on standards, compete on the wallet. It does not resolve who captures consumer relationship/margin as cards give way to instant.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Watch for: PayPal's actual working-group participation and any tie to **VoP** delivery, plus positioning near the **digital euro** (ECB to invite PSP interest in 2026; pilot H2 2027 — ECB-run, NOT EPC-run, so any linkage is adjacent, not conferred). (open)
- The material European contest is **Wero's 2026 e-commerce + POS rollout**, not this seat. Analyst-adjacent commentary (PPI Group) explicitly frames Wero as a share-taker from PayPal.
- **Coverage quality:** near-verbatim reprints of the PR; no independent critical analyst take found. Treat the "430M-user bet" framing as inferential.
- **Second-order (analysis):** the EU sovereignty agenda cuts against foreign incumbents; PayPal being "part of the conversation" hedges reputational/regulatory risk of being framed as the incumbent to displace — but a seat cannot substitute for a competitive European instant/A2A product.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** European instant/A2A payments, plus scheme governance. SEPA Instant became a hard mandate: per the EU Instant Payments Regulation (adopted 13 Mar 2024), eurozone PSPs must *receive* instant euro transfers since 9 Jan 2025 and *send* them (plus mandatory Verification-of-Payee) since 9 Oct 2025, priced no higher than standard transfers (per ECB / European Payments Council, as of 2025). Adoption is inflecting: instant credit transfers were 23% of euro-area credit-transfer *count* but only 7% of *value* in H1 2025 (per ECB payments statistics, H1 2025); third-party forecasts see instant reaching ~50% of credit transfers in 2026 (per Finextra/industry commentary — treat as (analysis)). Why now: the send-side mandate + VoP just went live (Oct 2025), so scheme rules (fraud, VoP, interoperability) are actively being rewritten in 2025–26 — the exact window in which a seat at the EPC table has the most influence. Structure: SEPA rails are a regulated, cooperative utility (EPC sets scheme rulebooks); value at the edges (wallets, acquiring, fraud) is fragmented and competitive, with regulation as the primary entry barrier.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: TPV/volume, take rate, transaction margin, and (for a scheme) *rulebook influence*. PayPal reports as a global PSP/wallet, not an EPC-native: Q1-26 TPV $464bn (+11% spot / +8% FXN), revenue $8.4bn (+7%), transaction margin dollars +3%, 430m+ active accounts (per PayPal Q1-26 release, 2026-05-05; account count per PayPal EPC press release, 2026-07-02). Basis of competition on European rails is standards + fraud + interoperability, not price. Recent EPC moves: **Revolut, Wise and Bits joined the EPC (2026-01-12)** — see [[Revolut, Wise and Bits join the European Payments Council]]; the Wero/EPI camp is scaling a rival A2A wallet — [[Mollie joins EPI Company to accelerate Wero]], [[Nuvei and EPI launch Wero payments for European merchants]], [[Wero launches in Dutch online stores in October 2026]]. PayPal's position: *catching up on governance* — it joins the EPC roughly six months after Revolut/Wise, entering as a licensed European bank (its own framing). Moat: network of 430m accounts + EU banking licence + fraud data — but note the seat itself is not proprietary; every large PSP can get one.

**Comps & multiples.** Public payments peers (market data as of ~11–29 Jun 2026, via stockanalysis/GuruFocus/companiesmarketcap):
- **PayPal (PYPL):** mkt cap ~$38.9bn; EV/Revenue ~1.08x; trailing P/E ~8.3x; EV/EBITDA ~5.3x.
- **Adyen:** mkt cap ~$28.6bn; EV/Revenue ~6.0x; P/E ~28x; EV/EBITDA ~15.7x.
- **Block (XYZ):** mkt cap ~$44.3bn; P/E trailing ~52x / fwd ~16x; EV/EBITDA ~26.6x.
- **Wise:** mkt cap ~$11.9bn; P/S ~4.7x; P/E ~23x; EV/EBITDA ~19.8x.
PayPal is clearly the **cheap outlier** — EV/Rev ~1.1x vs Adyen ~6.0x and Wise ~4.7x P/S; P/E ~8x vs peers 23–52x. Arithmetic sanity: PayPal EV/Rev ≈ (EV ~$35–36bn) / (LTM rev ~$32–33bn) ≈ ~1.1x; consistent with its low-single-digit revenue growth (~7%) and mature-wallet profile vs Adyen/Wise growth. So the discount is a *maturity/growth* discount, not a mispricing — no comp-based over-valuation flag. Median/distribution not formally computed (mixed models: wallet vs acquirer vs FX). Internal valuation comps for this specific EPC event: none (governance membership carries no deal value) — no data.

**Risk flags.**
1. *Marginal-influence / me-too risk (why it matters):* PayPal joins after Revolut, Wise and Bits (Jan 2026); an EPC seat is non-exclusive, so the announcement is largely positioning — the second-order effect is optics-heavy, with no P&L impact and no disclosed economics.
2. *Rival rail (Wero/EPI) disintermediation:* the EPI/Wero A2A wallet, backed by European banks and merchants (Mollie, Nuvei), is the strategic threat to PayPal's checkout in Europe; helping shape SEPA-instant standards does not neutralise a competing consumer wallet gaining merchant coverage.
3. *Regulatory cost tail:* the same mandate PayPal now helps write (instant + VoP + fraud-liability rules) raises compliance/fraud-liability costs across the sector; as an EU-licensed bank PayPal is exposed to whatever fraud-liability allocation the EPC lands on — a cost it cannot fully pass through under IPR pricing-parity rules.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** For the sector: this is fintech/wallet representation in European scheme governance consolidating (Revolut, Wise, Bits, now PayPal) — a shift from bank-only rule-setting toward wallet/fintech input, especially on fraud and VoP (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if PayPal's EPC membership is strategically material, watch for PayPal actually *offering* SEPA-instant send/receive natively (not just influencing rules) within ~12 months; absent a live instant-payments product tie-in, treat the membership as PR positioning, not a re-rating catalyst. Trigger to watch: PayPal integrating Wero-style A2A or SEPA-instant into its European checkout.

> [!note] Earnings review — added by [2]. This note is a regulatory/standards item (PayPal joining the European Payments Council), NOT an earnings report. **Verdict (headline read).** BEAT on the print, but the market treated it as a de-facto MISS on quality. Net revenues **$8.35bn (+7% YoY)** vs public consensus ~$8.05bn (beat +~$0.30bn / +~3.8%); non-GAAP EPS **$1.34** vs consensus ~$1.27 (beat +$0.07 / +5.5%). But **GAAP diluted EPS $1.21 (-6% YoY)** and **non-GAAP operating margin 18.4% vs 20.7% a year ago (-~229bps)** — margin compression is the story, not the top-line beat. New CEO **reaffirmed** (did not raise) FY2026 guidance. The stock fell ~8% on the print. Read: top-line beat, margin/quality miss.

**Key figures (with growth, as reported):**
- Net revenues **$8.35bn, +7% YoY** (spot). (release; web-corroborated)
- Total Payment Volume (TPV) **~$464bn, +11% YoY** (one aggregator cites $463.95bn merchant volume +11%; cf. Q4'25 TPV $475.1bn +9%) — volume growth **accelerated** vs Q4.
- Transaction margin dollars **~$3.81bn, +3% YoY** — the core "quality of revenue" line grew far slower than revenue (+7%) or volume (+11%): the classic PayPal divergence (volume up, unit economics up much less).
- GAAP diluted EPS **$1.21, -6% YoY**; GAAP net income **~$1.113bn, -14% YoY** — hit by ~$0.08 negative from the strategic-investment/crypto portfolio.
- Non-GAAP diluted EPS **$1.34, +~5.5% YoY** (adj. beat).
- Non-GAAP operating margin **18.4% vs 20.7% Q1'25 (-~229bps)** — headline thesis-flag.
- Payment transactions / active accounts: not cleanly re-disclosed in the sources pulled; for reference Q4'25 was active accounts **439m (+1.1% YoY)**, transactions 6.8bn (+2%) — active-account growth has been ~1%, i.e. essentially flat [figures beyond the above marked no-data for Q1'26].

**By segment / driver.** Growth is volume-led (TPV +11%) but earnings-quality-poor: transaction margin dollars only +3%, so revenue mix / lower-margin PSP-style volume and rising costs are diluting profitability. GAAP was further dragged by an ~$0.08 mark on the strategic-investment/crypto book (a non-operational, volatile item — de-PR: this flatters the "adjusted" narrative and is why GAAP EPS fell while non-GAAP rose).

**vs expectations / prior period.** Beat on both headline lines vs **public consensus** (revenue ~$8.05bn, EPS ~$1.27, as of the 2026-05-05 print). But sequentially/thesis-wise this **decelerates on quality**: revenue +7% while transaction margin dollars only +3% and non-GAAP op-margin down ~229bps YoY. Trajectory vs prior corpus prints: [[PayPal cites Germany growth slowdown on Q4 2025 earnings call]] (Q4'25: revenue +4%, adj EPS $1.23 +3%, TPV +9%, active accounts 439m +1.1%) and [[In addition to the ChatGPT move, PayPal released its Q3 2025 earnings]] — the pattern of "solid volume, thin transaction-margin growth, ~1% account growth" persists into Q1'26; revenue growth reaccelerated (+7% vs +4%) but margin compressed harder.

**Guidance / forward.** **Reaffirmed, not raised.** FY2026 non-GAAP EPS guided **down low-single-digits to slightly positive** vs FY2025's $5.31 base; transaction margin dollars guided **roughly flat / slight decline (ex-interest on customer balances)**; non-transaction opex **~+3%**. Management tone (new CEO): cautious, cited a "complex and dynamic operating environment," framed the year as a turnaround/reset rather than a growth re-acceleration. De-PR: reaffirming (not raising) after a top-line beat + guiding EPS flat-to-down = management is not confident the beat is sustainable; the silence is on when transaction-margin growth re-accelerates.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. **Margin compression (-~229bps non-GAAP op-margin YoY)** → PayPal is buying volume at the expense of unit economics → transaction margin dollars +3% vs revenue +7% → second-order: FY EPS guided flat-to-down despite the beat; buybacks (~$1.5bn, ~34m shares in Q1) are doing the per-share heavy lifting, not operating growth.
2. **Volume vs monetization gap** (TPV +11% / transaction margin $ +3%) → mix shift toward lower-take-rate volume → matters because the whole bull case is "re-monetize the network"; still not visibly working.
3. **~1% active-account growth** (Q4'25 base) → the network is mature/stagnant on users → growth must come from ARPU/branded-checkout monetization, which ties directly to why the EPC/SEPA standards seat (this note's actual topic) matters: European instant-payments/interoperability positioning is a lever to defend and re-monetize branded checkout in a key region.
4. **New CEO reset + guidance only reaffirmed** → turnaround, not inflection → stock -8% on the print signals the market wants raised guidance, not a beat-and-hold.
