Singapore-based fintech firm Ant International is exploring stablecoin license applications in multiple global jurisdictions, according to an executive’s statement on July 9.

Kelvin Li, head of platform tech at Ant International, shared the plans during the Reuters Next Conference in Singapore.

He clarified the company is not focusing on cryptocurrency transactions but wants to improve global payments through stablecoins.

“We believe stablecoins are an important means that will enable us to provide global payments in a much more efficient way and bring much better customer experience,” he stated.

Ant International is the overseas arm of Chinese fintech giant Ant Group.

## 🧠 Food for thought

### 1️⃣ Stablecoin market is experiencing explosive growth amid regulatory clarity

The stablecoin market has expanded dramatically from just $5 billion in January 2020 to approximately $255 billion in 2025, demonstrating sustained institutional interest beyond crypto speculation 1.

Annual transaction volume for stablecoins is projected to reach $15.6 trillion in 2024, reflecting their increasing adoption for both trading and cross-border payments 2.

This growth coincides with major regulatory developments, including the U.S. Senate’s passage of the GENIUS Act in June 2025, which creates a comprehensive framework for stablecoin issuers with clear reserve requirements and consumer protections 3.

For companies like Ant International, this regulatory clarity reduces operational uncertainty while creating new compliance standards they must navigate as they enter the space.

The market is attracting both established financial institutions and technology companies that recognize stablecoins’ potential to reduce friction in global payments, particularly as regulators establish clearer guidelines.

### 2️⃣ Intense competition for stablecoin licenses reflects strategic importance

Ant International isn’t alone in its stablecoin ambitions, with over 40 companies currently preparing applications for Hong Kong’s stablecoin licensing system that launches August 1, 2025 4.

Major Chinese financial institutions and internet giants are among the applicants, creating a highly competitive landscape where only a few firms are expected to meet the stringent technical and regulatory requirements 4.

This competition extends globally, with Ripple recently applying for a U.S. national bank charter to support its RLUSD stablecoin, demonstrating how companies are pursuing various regulatory approaches to establish positions in this space 5.

The high barriers to entry reflect the complexities involved in stablecoin operations, which require sophisticated technical infrastructure and compliance capabilities that few organizations currently possess.

For Ant International, which already has significant payment infrastructure through its parent company, stablecoins represent a natural evolution of its financial services that could enhance its competitive position in global markets.

### 3️⃣ Stablecoins are evolving from crypto trading tools to mainstream payment infrastructure

While stablecoins initially gained popularity primarily as tools for crypto traders to move between tokens, they’re increasingly being recognized for their potential to transform traditional payment systems 6.

The ability to facilitate faster settlement times and lower transaction costs compared to traditional payment networks makes stablecoins particularly valuable for cross-border transactions, which aligns with Ant International’s stated focus on global payments 7.

This shift is evident in regions with economic instability, such as Argentina, where inflation exceeded 100% in 2022, leading to a significant increase in digital wallet downloads as citizens sought stability through dollar-pegged digital assets 1.

Companies exploring stablecoins are divided into two strategic groups: those focused on cross-border payments and those interested in issuing stablecoins directly, showing the market’s diversification beyond cryptocurrency applications 4.

For financial technology companies like Ant International, stablecoins represent an opportunity to reduce dependency on correspondent banking networks and potentially deliver more efficient payment services to underserved markets.
