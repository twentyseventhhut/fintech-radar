# ZaloPay partners Singapore Tourism Board on QR payments

> [!info] 2025-07-06 · 1 упоминаний · 1 источника(ов) с текстом

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[This Week in Fintech] ZaloPay, a Vietnamese mobile wallet provider, partnered with the Singapore Tourism Board (STB) to enable QR code payments for Vietnamese tourists in Singapore.

Vietnam-based digital payment app Zalopay has partnered with the Singapore Tourism Board (STB) to improve travel convenience for Vietnamese tourists visiting Singapore.

This is STB’s first collaboration with a Vietnamese payment platform. As part of the partnership, Zalopay has expanded its “Global QR Scanning” feature.

This allows users to make payments directly in Singapore through STB’s partner network, removing the need for currency exchange or international credit cards.

Exchange rates and transaction fees are clearly displayed within the app.

The app also provides travel services, including flight and hotel bookings, and payment options at restaurants and entertainment venues in Singapore.

Users can access local deals and exclusive benefits designed for Vietnamese tourists through collaborations with Singapore-based partners.

Zalopay and STB plan to promote Singapore as a travel destination for Vietnamese travelers through marketing campaigns.

## 🧠 Food for thought

### 1️⃣ Southeast Asia’s fintech sector is reshaping cross-border tourism experiences

The Zalopay-STB partnership exemplifies a broader regional trend where fintech solutions are transforming international travel experiences through seamless payments.

Asia-Pacific’s fintech market is projected to reach US$171.32 billion by 2025 and nearly $360 billion by 2030, with digital payments serving as a primary growth driver 1.

Digital payment adoption is particularly accelerating in tourism, with platforms like Zalopay addressing key traveler pain points such as currency exchange, transaction fees, and payment accessibility, which traditionally complicated international trips.

The timing is strategic as research shows two-thirds of global consumers traveled internationally last year, creating substantial demand for cross-border payment solutions that eliminate friction 2.

This partnership represents the growing “digital payments as a service” model in tourism, where financial technology directly enhances destination appeal beyond just facilitating transactions.

### 2️⃣ Vietnam-Singapore relationship evolves from traditional diplomacy to digital economic collaboration

While diplomatic relations between Vietnam and Singapore date back to 1973, this partnership signals a shift toward technology-driven economic collaboration.

Singapore has invested over $40 billion in Vietnam, making it one of Vietnam’s largest foreign investors, while bilateral trade reached around US$8 billion in 2022 3.

The tourism sector specifically has become a key connector, with Vietnam ranking among Singapore’s top 15 source markets with over 393,000 Vietnamese visitors in 2024 alone.

This evolution aligns with the broader ASEAN strategy to develop regional payment connectivity initiatives that enhance economic integration beyond traditional trade agreements 4.

The partnership demonstrates how economic diplomacy in Southeast Asia increasingly focuses on digital infrastructure development rather than just physical trade, creating new avenues for bilateral engagement.

### 3️⃣ Mobile payment providers are competing for the valuable outbound traveler segment

Zalopay’s international expansion strategy targets Vietnam’s growing outbound travel market, particularly young urban travelers seeking digital-first experiences.

Research indicates that payment choice is becoming a primary driver of travel business success, with customers increasingly selecting travel providers based on available payment options 5.

The inclusion of travel services (flights, hotels, restaurants) within Zalopay’s ecosystem follows the comprehensive “travel super app” model that has proven successful in other Asian markets.

This approach addresses the documented shift in consumer preference toward digital wallets for travel transactions, with contactless payments doubling in popularity at some international destinations over just two years 6.

For mobile payment providers, capturing the international traveler segment is particularly valuable as these users typically have higher spending power and transaction frequencies compared to domestic-only customers.
