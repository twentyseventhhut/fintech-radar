Revolut, a United Kingdom-based fintech company, has partnered with Ant International to enable its customers to send money to China in Chinese Yuan (CNY) using Alipay.

The partnership aims to provide compliant and efficient cross-border remittance services for Revolut users.

This service is available globally, with a focus on overseas Chinese users.

With the new feature, Revolut users can transfer money by providing the recipient’s Alipay ID, name, and address.

Transfers are processed instantly, with fees varying by customer plan: Standard plan users are charged 0.15% (minimum SG$5.90), Premium plan users 0.12% (minimum SG$4.72), while Metal plan users can transfer for free.

Revolut also offers other international money transfer services, including Instant Card Transfers, bank transfers, mobile wallet transfers, and peer-to-peer transfers.

Launched in 2004, Alipay is the most widely used payment app in China and is part of Ant International’s global remittance solutions.

## 🧠 Food for thought

### 1️⃣ China’s strategic position in global remittance flows

China represents a massive remittance opportunity as the second-largest recipient globally after India, with approximately $48 billion in inflows in 2024 according to the article 1.

This position is supported by China’s estimated 35 million migrants worldwide, with the USA being the largest source of remittances due to its approximately 4 million Chinese-Americans primarily located in California and New York 1.

Chinese migrants in the USA tend to be better educated with higher average incomes compared to other migrant groups, contributing to the substantial remittance volumes 1.

Singapore identifies China as its leading remittance destination according to the article’s cited 2023 report, making this partnership strategically important for Revolut’s expansion in Asia.

The partnership taps into a well-established remittance corridor that has significant economic impact, as remittances often serve as crucial financial lifelines for recipient families.

### 2️⃣ Digital transformation reshaping the global remittance landscape

This partnership reflects the broader shift toward digital-first remittance solutions, with digital remittances projected to exceed $300 billion globally 2.

The traditional remittance industry has historically been dominated by banks and money transfer operators like Western Union, which charge average fees of 10.9%, significantly higher than Revolut’s tiered fee structure starting at 0.15% 2.

Digital-first fintech companies consistently outperform traditional providers in customer satisfaction, with 16 of 18 reviewed companies scoring above 4.0 out of 5 on Trustpilot, indicating strong consumer preference for digital solutions 3.

The integration with Alipay, China’s most widely used payment app, allows Revolut to leverage an existing digital infrastructure rather than building its own network in a challenging market.

This partnership represents how fintech companies are increasingly unbundling traditional banking services, with the international money transfer market projected to reach $1.33 trillion by 2032 as fintechs continue gaining market share from traditional banks 4.

### 3️⃣ Competitive remittance industry driving innovation and cost reduction

The remittance market is becoming increasingly consolidated, with approximately 20 companies expected to dominate 90% of the U.S. outbound remittance market by 2025, driving both innovation and competitive pricing 5.

Revolut’s tiered fee structure (from free for Metal customers to 0.15% for Standard customers) represents the broader industry trend of reducing fees to gain market share, a stark contrast to the global average remittance cost of approximately 7% 2.

Western Union maintains the most extensive network in China among traditional providers, but Revolut’s digital approach through Alipay partnership offers a potentially more cost-effective and convenient alternative 1.

This partnership comes amid Revolut’s broader reduction in international money transfer fees across all customer tiers, demonstrating how competition is benefiting consumers through lower costs.

The focus on instant processing for China-bound transfers aligns with consumer expectations for faster settlement times, a key differentiation point in the competitive remittance marketplace.
