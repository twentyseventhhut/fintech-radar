# Western Union Eyes Stablecoin Integration as GENIUS Act Spurs Industry Shift

Amara Khatri[](https://twitter.com/cryptodailyuk)

 Published **10 months ago** on **July 22, 2025**

**Global remittance powerhouse Western Union is exploring the rollout of stablecoin services within its digital wallet infrastructure, signaling growing legacy interest in crypto following the passage of the GENIUS Act.**

## Legacy Remittance Giant Enters Stablecoin Race

Western Union is preparing to integrate stablecoins into its digital wallet ecosystem, signaling a strategic move by the 175-year-old remittance firm to modernize cross-border payments. CEO Devin McGranahan confirmed that the company is actively exploring stablecoin partnerships for on- and off-ramp services, allowing users to buy and sell crypto assets within a regulated framework.

The move comes amid growing optimism around stablecoins in traditional finance, driven by recent legislative developments and a push for faster, more cost-effective payment solutions.

## GENIUS Act Triggers Industry-Wide Momentum

Western Union’s stablecoin exploration follows the enactment of the U.S. GENIUS Act, a sweeping federal law establishing regulatory clarity for stablecoins. Signed into law by President Donald Trump, the legislation mandates full backing of stablecoins by liquid reserves such as U.S. dollars, along with annual audits for major issuers exceeding $50 billion in market cap. It also outlines issuance rules for foreign entities operating in the U.S. market.

Industry players view this legal clarity as a green light to accelerate pilot programs and adoption timelines. Regulatory certainty, combined with growing demand for always-on settlement systems, has set the stage for legacy institutions like Western Union to adapt.

## Faster Payments, Lower Costs, and Global Reach

McGranahan highlighted stablecoins’ potential to drive three core improvements: instant cross-border transfers, seamless conversion between digital and fiat currencies, and a more reliable store of value for users in volatile economies. He emphasized that the innovation aligns with Western Union’s long-standing mission to evolve with the financial landscape.

The company is already piloting settlement innovations in regions such as Africa and South America and working with infrastructure partners to streamline foreign exchange conversion. These efforts aim to reduce friction in the remittance process, a space where stablecoins are increasingly competitive.

## Stablecoins Push Legacy Firms Toward Innovation

The appeal of stablecoins lies in their ability to reduce global remittance fees, which still average around 6.6%, according to OwlTing CEO Darren Wang. With near-instant settlement and round-the-clock availability, stablecoins offer a viable path to meeting the UN’s target of sub-3% transfer costs by eliminating intermediaries and minimizing FX markups.

Analysts predict a sharp rise in stablecoin adoption by 2026 as regulatory frameworks like the GENIUS Act in the U.S. and Europe’s MiCA mature, allowing for deeper integration into mainstream financial workflows.

Stablecoins are an "opportunity, not a threat," for international money transfer company Western Union, according to CEO Devin McGranahan.
McGranahan said in an interview with Bloomberg on Monday that anything allowing users to send money faster and cheaper across borders is going to be good for Western Union and its customers.
Stablecoins are crypto tokens pegged to the value of a traditional financial asset , such as a fiat currency, with the U.S. dollar being the most prevalent.
Allowing users to send value denominated in U.S. dollars across borders using blockchain technology, making transfers faster, cheaper and more efficient than traditional methods, would appear to be a disruptor of Western Union's business case.
"Last I checked, you couldn't spend stablecoin if you wanted to buy a Coca Cola, so converting stablecoins into fiat currencies, particularly in harder to convert currencies, is an opportunity for us," McGranahan said. 
For such users, a stablecoin may represent a store of value, he added.
Stablecoins have formed the backbone of new cryptocurrency regulation in the U.S., with the first significant crypto bill, known as the GENIUS act, becoming law last week. 
The GENIUS act creates a definition for stablecoin payments, with the Federal Reserve and the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency overseeing their issuance by banks and non-banks respectively.
