Token Asia Korea, which operates the global fintech platform "Toxia," announced on the 15th that it has signed a strategic partnership with the fruit dessert franchise "Dalcom Wangatanghuru" and opened its first Wangatanghuru store in Jakarta, Indonesia.
Indonesia is the world's fourth-largest population with a population of 280 million, and K-content, K-fashion, and K-dessert are becoming mainstream consumption trends centered on digitalized young people.
Tokshia is a digital platform that modernizes Indonesia's unique mutual aid financial culture, innovating social finance based on mobile and growing rapidly in the local market. Through this collaboration, Token Asia Korea applied not only financial infrastructure but also a "retail intelligence" module for K-franchise operation.
It provides customized coupon issuance, real-time CRM, purchase analysis, and automation marketing to enable simultaneous customer acquisition, maintenance, and sales improvement. On the day of its opening, Dalmat Wangatanghuru secured more than 1,000 new members through the "Toxia App Subscription Promotion," proving digital synergy between the two companies.
Dalkwangatanghuru plans to open additional stores in five major Indonesian cities by the first half of 2026. It is also preparing to enter ASEAN, including Thailand, Vietnam, and the Philippines. Toxia continues to develop customized retail solutions that reflect local laws and consumer characteristics.
"This entry into Indonesia is thanks to consulting by Token Asia Korea, which has strengths in global strategies and digital infrastructure," said Chung Chul-hoon, CEO of Dalmat Wangatanghuru. "Toxia is a CRM-centered retail tech platform that provides real-time insights to store operators by precisely analyzing customers' consumption patterns and preferences," said Jang Jae-hoon, CEO of Token Asia Korea. "We will support more Korean brands that want to enter Indonesia to settle in the Southeast Asian market based on digital."
