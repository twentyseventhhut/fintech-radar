Trading platform eToro has activated its Capital Markets Services (CMS) license from the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS).

This enables the company to enter the Singapore market.

With this license, eToro can offer eligible retail investors in Singapore access to stocks from over 20 global stock exchanges.

It also includes exchange-traded funds and derivatives on its platform.

This expansion is part of eToro’s strategy to increase its presence in the Asia-Pacific region.

## 🧠 Food for thought

### 1️⃣ Singapore’s strategic fintech hub status drives platform expansion

eToro’s entry into Singapore aligns with the city-state’s explosive fintech growth trajectory, as the market is projected to expand from $42.77 billion in 2025 to $69.64 billion by 2030, representing a 10.24% CAGR1.

This expansion comes amid a broader surge in Singapore’s fintech funding, which reached $543 million in Q2 2025 alone, a 30% increase from the previous year, demonstrating strong investor confidence despite global economic uncertainties2.

Singapore’s Monetary Authority (MAS) has created a particularly welcoming environment for fintech innovation, committing S$150 million over three years through its Financial Sector Technology and Innovation Scheme (FSTI 3.0)3.

With over 1,400 fintech companies already operating in Singapore, accounting for nearly 50% of Southeast Asia’s fintech funding, eToro enters a mature ecosystem that serves as a gateway to the broader Asia-Pacific region4.

### 2️⃣ Regulatory frameworks balance innovation with consumer protection

eToro’s successful acquisition of a Capital Markets Services license demonstrates Singapore’s dual approach to regulation, encouraging innovation while maintaining strict oversight to protect consumers.

The MAS has established regulatory sandboxes specifically designed to allow fintech companies to test innovations in a controlled environment before full market deployment5.

Singapore has recently intensified its focus on consumer protection in digital payments and investment platforms, with particular attention to combating scams and ensuring proper disclosure to retail investors6.

This regulatory framework has been critical to establishing Singapore’s reputation as a trustworthy financial center, with the MAS actively refining regulations for digital token service providers and wealth management platforms, areas directly relevant to eToro’s business model7.

### 3️⃣ Social investing platforms face fierce competition in wealth tech

eToro enters a competitive landscape dominated by rapidly growing local wealth management platforms like Endowus, which achieved an astonishing 1345.26% growth rate with $6.58 million in revenue in 20238.

The wealth management sector in Singapore has seen particularly strong innovation, with platforms increasingly leveraging AI-driven solutions and offering fractional investments in previously inaccessible asset classes4.

Local players like ADDX have raised significant funding ($140 million) while focusing on democratizing access to private equity and alternative investments through tokenization, a trend that aligns with MAS’s emphasis on tokenization as a key development in Singapore’s fintech sector49.

eToro’s social investing approach, which allows users to view and copy other investors’ portfolios, offers a differentiated value proposition in a market where personalized financial experiences are becoming increasingly important3.
