# Fintech firm Kiwi launches interest-back EMI on UPI ahead of Diwali: How it works

## Kiwi introduces India’s first Interest-Back EMI on UPI, letting users split high-value purchases into instalments and earn cashback on interest, ahead of Diwali.

ADVERTISEMENT

ByAnshul[](https://twitter.com/anshul91_m "https://twitter.com/anshul91_m")October 3, 2025, 5:16:11 PM IST (Published)

2 Min Read

[](https://www.google.com/preferences/source?q=cnbctv18.com "CNBCTV18 on Google")[](https://api.whatsapp.com/send?text=Fintech%20firm%20Kiwi%20launches%20interest-back%20EMI%20on%20UPI%20ahead%20of%20Diwali%3A%20How%20it%20works%20https%3A%2F%2Fwww.cnbctv18.com%2Fpersonal-finance%2Ffintech-firm-kiwi-launches-interest-back-emi-upi-ahead-of-diwali-how-it-works-19701975.htm "Fintech firm Kiwi launches interest-back EMI on UPI ahead of Diwali: How it works")

Fintech startup Kiwi has introduced India’s first interest-back EMI on UPI, allowing consumers to convert high-value purchases into instalments while receiving a portion or full interest refund as cashback. The feature comes ahead of the Diwali season, a period when festive spending across categories such as electronics, gold, home appliances, and travel typically rises.

 In 2024, India’s festive spending crossed ₹3.5 lakh crore, with e-commerce contributing nearly ₹90,000 crore during the Diwali week.

 More than half of these high-ticket purchases were made using EMI or pay-later options.

 Traditional EMI plans, while helping consumers manage affordability, often involve additional interest costs. Kiwi’s interest-back EMI aims to address this by providing cashback on the interest paid. Under the scheme, a 3-month EMI offers 100% interest cashback, while 6- and 9-month EMIs provide 50% cashback.

 The service is available at any UPI-enabled merchant. Users can scan a QR code or make an online payment, choose “Pay with EMI” through the Kiwi app, authenticate via UPI PIN, and receive instant cashback. The feature does not require a physical card and is designed to provide transparency and a mobile-first experience.

 By bringing EMIs onto UPI, Kiwi expands access to structured credit beyond traditional credit card users.

 UPI currently reaches over 40 million merchants across India, but has largely been used for low-value transactions.

 The launch aims to make high-ticket purchases more accessible and to provide an alternative to credit card-based EMIs.

 Kiwi has previously issued over 2 lakh RuPay Credit Cards and is among the first fintechs to integrate Credit on UPI, a feature introduced by NPCI, with bank partnerships.

New Delhi, Oct 3 (PTI) Fintech firm Kiwi, a pioneer in credit on UPI, on Friday announced the launch of interest-back EMI on UPI to cash in on the upcoming Diwali festive buying.      Consumers can now split big-ticket purchases into easy instalments and earn back the interest paid as cashback, making festive shopping more affordable and rewarding, Kiwi said in a statement.      Kiwi's interest-back EMI offers a refund of the interest paid -- partially or fully -- as instant cashback, making purchases significantly more consumer-friendly, it said.      With this feature, a three-month EMI comes with 100 per cent cashback on interest, making it effectively interest-free, while six and nine-month EMIs offer 50 per cent cashback on interest, it said.      "Customers can pay at any UPI merchant by simply scanning a QR or making an online payment, select 'Pay with EMI' on the Kiwi app, view charges upfront, authenticate with their UPI PIN, and instantly receive cashback on the interest portion. With interest-back EMIs, we are ensuring that consumers can celebrate without financial strain."      With this launch, he said, UPI steps into big-ticket affordability, an area once dominated by credit cards.
