# Pesalink, Cellulant partner to power instant bank-to-bank merchant payments

By: CORRESPONDENT| 1st Oct 2025 | 2 min read Listen

**NAIROBI, Kenya, Oct 1 – Integrated Payment Services Limited (IPSL), operators of Pesalink, has partnered with pan-African payments firm Cellulant to expand instant customer-to-business (C2B) payments in Kenya.**

The integration links Pesalink’s network of more than 80 financial institutions—including 39 commercial banks, SACCOs, and fintechs—with Cellulant’s Tingg platform, enabling customers to pay merchants directly from their bank accounts.

The firms say the partnership addresses persistent challenges in digital payments such as delays, reconciliation errors, and limited options while promoting interoperability across Kenya’s financial system.

At checkout, customers can now pay up to Sh999,999 per transaction, with higher daily limits and instant confirmation features to reduce errors. Each transfer carries a unique reference number, ensuring funds are correctly matched to merchants and invoices.

Businesses on Cellulant’s platform benefit from faster settlements, fewer disputes, and more accurate reconciliation—an important shift for sectors such as travel and airlines, where high-value transactions are common.

“Pesalink was built to simplify life through instant, affordable payments for everyone. Our partnership with Cellulant directly addresses merchant pain points in reconciliation while giving customers a seamless experience,” said Plounne Oyunge, Chief Growth Officer at Pesalink.

Cellulant, whose Tingg platform processes over 4.5 million transactions daily across Africa, said the collaboration will improve trust and efficiency in digital payments. “Every time we remove friction from money movement, businesses grow faster and communities thrive,” said Michael Muriuki, VP of Group Innovation and Software Engineering at Cellulant.

Kenya’s digital payments market is projected to hit $9.36 billion (Sh1.4 trillion) in 2025, with mobile money accounting for $5.85 billion (Sh913 billion).

Pesalink currently processes Sh4 billion daily, with monthly volumes exceeding Sh110 billion—reflecting 41 percent year-on-year growth.

Cellulant and Pesalink Partner to Enable Instant Bank-to-Merchant Payments in Kenya

 
 F
 
 

 
 
 
 

 
 
 
 

 
 
 
 

 
 E
 
 
Through this partnership, millions of consumers will now be able to make direct payments from their bank accounts to merchants, eliminating the need to move funds between mobile wallets, bank accounts, and other platforms before completing transactions.
 Cellulant,   a leading African payments technology company, has entered into a partnership with Pesalink   to simplify and accelerate digital payments across Kenya. The collaboration will integrate Cellulant’s robust payment platform, Tingg, with Pesalink’s extensive network, which connects more than 80 financial institutions including 39 commercial banks, SACCOs, and fintechs.
Through this partnership, millions of consumers will now be able to make direct payments from their bank accounts to merchants, eliminating the need to move funds between mobile wallets, bank accounts, and other platforms before completing transactions. The service is designed to make payments seamless, fast, and reliable for both customers and businesses.
One of the major benefits of the integration is instant settlement of transactions, which means businesses no longer need to worry about lost or delayed transfers. Each payment will also carry a unique identifier, allowing for easier tracking and reconciliation. By removing the fragmentation caused by different payment platforms, the partnership makes it possible for customers to pay in a single step directly from their bank.
The collaboration between Cellulant and Pesalink is expected to boost Kenya’s digital economy by enhancing efficiency and transparency in financial transactions. For merchants, this marks an end to frequent payment delays and reconciliation issues, while for consumers, it represents a new level of convenience and confidence in digital payments.
