Payconiq International, Buckaroo and EPI announce strategic agreement to clear the path for Wero's arrival in Luxembourg
October 12, 2025
 Share this article 
 
 
 
 Brussels, Luxembourg, Utrecht — October 13th, 2025 - Payconiq International, Buckaroo and the European Payments Initiative (EPI) today announced a strategic agreement to bring Wero to Luxembourg. Subject to regulatory review, Payconiq’s direct merchant portfolio will transfer to Buckaroo in early 2026. Buckaroo will then enable Wero acceptance for these merchants once the standalone Wero app goes live in Luxembourg, targeted for mid‑2026. 
This collaboration ensures that consumers and merchants in Luxembourg will soon benefit from one of the most advanced, feature-rich payment systems in Europe, designed to make everyday transactions faster, easier, and more secure. As part of this agreement, Buckaroo will take over Payconiq’s direct merchant portfolio in Luxembourg, ensuring a smooth transition to Wero and setting the stage for a future-proof seamless payment experience. 
 
 Accelerating the adoption of Wero 
As a result of the strategic partnership between EPI, Payconiq International and Buckaroo, Luxembourg merchants will gain access to state-of-the-art payment technology and expertise, ensuring they stay ahead in an increasingly digital economy. This partnership will further accelerate widespread adoption of Wero in the Benelux.
 
 What this means for consumers 
Instant, seamless payments across Europe.
Top-tier security and privacy, fully aligned with European standards.
A simple, feature-rich experience in one intuitive Wero app.
 
 What this means for merchants 
Access to a unified European payment platform, reducing complexity and costs.
Faster settlement and improved cash flow through instant payments.
Advanced tools to stay competitive in a digital-first economy.
 
Under the agreement, merchants will be legally transferred to Buckaroo in early 2026 - pending regulatory review. Buckaroo will then migrate the merchant base from Payconiq to Wero, providing merchant acceptance of Wero, once the Wero standalone app is launched in Luxembourg by mid 2026.
This step reinforces Luxembourg’s role as a flagship market for European payment innovation and supports the broader vision of a connected, competitive, sovereign and secure digital economy in Europe.
 “This agreement reflects our commitment to an orderly and responsible migration from  Payconiq to Wero on the Luxembourg market. By preparing the transfer of our merchants to Buckaroo, we are ensuring continuity and setting the stage for their future on the Wero platform. Our teams remain fully engaged to guarantee a smooth and transparent transition.” James King, Authorised Manager of Payconiq International 
 “Taking over Payconiq’s Luxembourg merchant portfolio strengthens our European footprint and positions us at the forefront of cross-border payment innovation. This is more than a technical migration—it’s a strategic move toward harmonised, future-proof payment services. We look forward to working with Payconiq and Wero to deliver a secure and merchant-friendly transition.” Paul Scholten, CEO of Buckaroo: “With the transition of Payconiq’s merchant portfolio to Buckaroo as the new merchant service provider, Wero will be available to all merchants in Luxembourg as a payment solution. We are excited to begin serving the Luxembourg market.” Martina Weimert, CEO of EPI Company 
About Buckaroo
Since its founding in 2005, the Utrecht-based Buckaroo has grown into one of the leading Payment Service Providers for online and in-store payment solutions in the Netherlands and Belgium. Buckaroo serves over 47,000 large business and SME customers and processes more than 1 billion transactions annually. The fintech also has a large in-house network of fixed and mobile payment terminals. Buckaroo is owned by Keensight Capital, a leading European Growth Buyout firm investing in fast-growing, profitable companies across Technology and Healthcare. 
More information:  www.buckaroo.eu  
About EPI
 EPI (or European Payments Initiative) is supported by 16 European banks and payment service providers. They have joined forces with a common goal: to offer a unified mobile payment service, to all European companies and citizens, Wero . EPI intends to enable European consumers and merchants and to carry out all types of retail transactions simply, via a resolutely sovereign digital wallet.
Find out more at epicompany.eu 
 About Wero
Based on instant account-to-account (A2A) payments, Wero further streamlines payments in Europe by eliminating intermediaries in the payment chain and the associated additional costs. Wero already supports payments between individuals, and will shortly unveil payments to professionals (P2Pro) and those for online and mobile purchases. Point-of-sale payments will also be offered, as well as value-added services such as merchant loyalty programs and recurring subscription management.
Find out more at wero-wallet.eu .
