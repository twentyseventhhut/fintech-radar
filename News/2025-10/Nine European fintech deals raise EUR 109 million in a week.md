BlackFin Tech Weekly - October 6th, 2025
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity
Hello FinTech Friends,
 Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry! Over the last week, there were nine fintech deals in Europe , raising a total of €109 million , including four transactions in the UK, two in Germany, one in Portugal, one in Spain and one in France. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Feedzai , a Portugal-based payments platform providing AML (Anti , KYC and transaction fraud detection, has raised €63.8 million in a Series E round led by Lince Capital and Explorer Investments . 
 Paid , a UK-based platform enabling businesses to sell online and manage payments and logistics, has raised €17.9 million in a Seed round led by Lightspeed . 
 Umony , a UK-based AI-driven compliance solutions to monitor, achieve and ensure regulatory adherence in communications, has raised €12.8 million in a Series A round led by Notion Capital . 
 Let’s dive in! 
 Feedzai 
 Feedzai , headquartered in Portugal, provides AML (Anti-Money Laundering), KYC, and transaction fraud detection solutions for financial institutions. The company has raised €63.8 million in a Series E round led by Lince Capital and Explorer Investments , with participation from Oxy Capital and Buenavista Equity Partners . The new funding will support Feedzai’s international expansion and further enhance its fraud prevention technology.

 Paid 
 Paid , based in the United Kingdom, offers a platform that enables businesses to sell online and manage payments and logistics. The startup has secured €17.9 million in a Seed round led by Lightspeed Venture Partners , with participation from EQT Ventures and Fuse . The fresh capital will be used to accelerate product development and commercial growth. 
 Read more 
 Umony 
 Umony , also based in the United Kingdom, develops AI-driven compliance solutions to monitor, archive, and ensure regulatory adherence in corporate communications. The company has raised €12.8 million in a Series A round led by Notion Capital , with participation from Crane Venture Partners and Seedcamp . The funds will be used to expand Umony’s market presence across Europe and strengthen its compliance technology.

 In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 Finary , a France-based B2C investing app that centralizes, analyzes, and optimizes personal wealth management, has announced the acquisition of Affluent , a France-based fintech providing digital tools for independent financial advisors (CGPs). The deal expands Finary’s capabilities by integrating Affluent’s expertise in financial planning and manual transaction tracking, reinforcing its ambition to build an all-in-one wealth management platform for investors. Affluent’s core team, with strong product and tech backgrounds from Qonto and asset management, will join Finary to lead product development and enhance the web experience, while continuing to advance innovation within the platform.

 Carta , a US-based global ownership management platform helping companies, investors, and employees manage equity and ownership, has announced the acquisition of Accelex , a UK-based provider of data acquisition, analytics, and reporting solutions for alternative investors and asset servicers. The deal strengthens Carta’s capabilities by integrating AI-powered data automation and portfolio analytics, enhancing transparency and efficiency for institutional allocators. Accelex’s solutions will be incorporated into Carta’s platform to expand its reporting and data offerings.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 Lloyds Banking Group and Schroders plan to unwind their Schroders Personal Wealth joint venture after missed growth targets and frequent leadership changes. Lloyds is set to take full control by acquiring Schroders 49.9% stake. SPW launched in 2019 with £13 billion of assets and had £15.7 billion and about 300 advisers at the end of 2024, far short of ambitions to reach £25 billion in five years and become a top three UK planner. Taking control would let Lloyds sell a wider range of products to more than 3 million mass affluent customers and accelerate the strategy of chief executive Charlie Nunn to grow fee based wealth and insurance income that is less sensitive to interest rates. SPW will keep running money into Schroders funds, while Schroders concentrates on higher end wealth and tighter links with its asset management arm. Both companies declined to comment

 Revolut has launched high yield savings accounts in the US paying 4 percent to standard users and 5.5 percent to Metal tier subscribers. The move builds on its new secured credit card and supports its ambition to surpass 100 million global customers. The accounts are offered through Cross River Bank as Revolut continues to weigh whether to apply for a US banking license or acquire one. Executives hold regular meetings with regulators but say no formal application is in progress. The savings rates sharply outpace rivals including Capital One at 3.5 percent, Goldman Sachs’ Marcus at 3.65 percent and Cash App at 3.75 percent, strengthening Revolut’s competitive edge. The company’s US tiers range from a free standard account to premium at $9.99 and Metal at $16.99 monthly, which offer extra perks. The launch comes as US deregulation under President Donald Trump boosts expectations for looser bank merger rules, creating a more favorable landscape for fintech challengers

 London has fallen out of the top 20 IPO markets for the first time in decades, ranking 23rd after deal volume plunged 69 percent to $248 million this year, the weakest since the 1980s. Its largest IPO, MHA Plc’s £98 million listing, highlighted the absence of major Wall Street banks, with only small local firms handling transactions. Competitors like Singapore and Mexico surged ahead, raising $1.44 billion and $460 million respectively. The city has lost major listings to rivals, including Verisure to Stockholm and Unilever’s Magnum spin-off to Amsterdam, while private equity buyers like KKR and Bain have been taking undervalued firms private. Some upcoming IPOs like Visma , Loveholidays and Navoi Mining may boost the pipeline, but many large UK companies, including Wise and AstraZeneca , are shifting primary listings to New York, reinforcing fears of a long-term structural decline

 The ECB has selected Italian firms Almaviva and Fabrick to build the official app and infrastructure for the Digital Euro, aiming to accelerate preparation even before formal legislative approval. They will develop mobile apps across smartphones, tablets, and smartwatches, plus an open interoperable infrastructure with SDKs and APIs so European payment service providers can integrate. The joint venture is initially four years (extendable to ten), and the contract is valued around €153 million. Despite lacking formal approval from the European Parliament and member states, the ECB is pushing ahead with design and development. Launch is tentatively targeted by mid-2029, with key regulatory decisions expected by mid-2026 regarding limits on consumer holdings and other design features.
