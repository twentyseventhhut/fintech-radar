# NTT Data study projects Mexico's payment digitalization growth

> [!info] 2025-10-26 · 1 упоминаний · 2 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: This Week in Fintech

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[This Week in Fintech] According to the “Payment Methods Study Mexico 2025” by NTT DATA, Mexico’s payment ecosystem is projected to process over 4.5 billion annual transactions via SPEI by 2025, and the country’s Banking-as-a-Service (BaaS) market is forecast at US$800 million for the same year. These numbers signal that Mexico is at a strategic inflection point for payment digitalization, offering substantial opportunities for Latin American fintechs in payment infrastructure, embedded finance, API integrations, and expansion of digital financial services.

In the digital era, where convenience and speed are essential, payment methods in Mexican e-commerce have experienced an amazing evolution in recent years. This evolution has not only helped consumers but has also changed the way companies operate and compete in the market.
A decade ago, credit and debit cards were virtually the only payment methods accepted online. Today, however, Mexican consumers enjoy a wide range of options, including digital wallets, bank transfers, payment via QR codes at convenience stores, buy-now-pay-later payment methods, and even cryptocurrencies.
Along this same line, from STP, a business fintech that provides customized services to more than 1,500 companies in Mexico, including 95% of the fintechs operating in the country, we have seen that more and more companies are looking to automate their payments and collections by transfers such as SPEI, CoDi, and Dimo because it does not cost them to generate this transaction, so they can reach more customers through these systems.
For example, CoDi for Business helps to generate QR codes and send customized billing messages per transaction in a 100% automated way through API integration with the company's system/ERP.
The growth of SPEI in recent years in the country has been relevant due to the benefits it provides regarding the digitalization of businesses; in 2020, the number of these transactions totaled 1,248 million. In 2021, SPEI registered 2,028 million transactions (62% growth versus 2020), and in 2022 they reached 2,834 million transactions (40% growth versus 2020).
Likewise, the newest digital payment tool, Dinero Movil (Dimo) from the Bank of Mexico (Banxico), which allows users to make transfers in real-time to other account holders based solely on their cellphone number, is shaping up to be a method that reinforces financial inclusion in the country.
It highlights the impressive progress in the adoption of real-time transfers in Mexico. Before 2018, this payment method was rare throughout Latin America, but with tools such as SPEI and CoDi, transfers have gained significant traction, especially in the travel vertical, according to Ebanx's Beyond the Borders (2022-2023) study.
Security and Trust in the Digital Age
Security has become a major concern for both consumers and businesses in e-commerce. Fortunately, technological advances have made it possible to implement robust security measures, such as two-factor authentication and end-to-end encryption, that protect online transactions.
Consumer confidence is essential to the continued growth of e-commerce in Mexico. Advances in payment method security are a key factor in gaining and maintaining that trust. Companies that embrace these technologies not only protect their customers but also enhance their reputation and credibility.
The Role of Fintechs 
According to the Fintech Mexico Annual Report NTT Data 2023, trust between consumers and e-commerce has grown, supporting the security offered by the fintech sector compared to traditional banking. This has led to increased investment and the birth of new fintechs with more complete value propositions.
In addition, a Mastercard report revealed that a high percentage of consumers, especially millennials and Gen Z, have fintech tools associated with their primary bank accounts, strengthening the interconnection between these two worlds.
In this regard, the Mexican Association of Online Sales (AMVO), states that transfers have developed at the pace of growth of e-commerce, due to its characteristics, especially after the pandemic.
With this payment method, transactions are immediate; if the customer is paying for a product or service, he will no longer have to wait 24 or 48 hours for his payment to be confirmed by the merchant, the payment is made directly, 24 hours a day, seven days a week.
Innovation and Competition
Increasing competition in Mexico's e-commerce market has driven innovation in payment methods. Companies are constantly looking for ways to differentiate themselves and attract new customers. This has led to the development of loyalty programs, discounts, and special offers linked to specific payment methods.
In addition, the rise of cryptocurrencies is beginning to change the way some companies accept online payments, although it is still in its early stages.
In summary, Mexico's e-commerce payment methods are undergoing a transformation that benefits both consumers and businesses. The diversification of payment options, improved security, and constant innovation are driving the growth of the online market in the country.
The evolution continues in this field and promises an even more convenient and personalized online shopping experience for all Mexicans, backed by the trust and security provided by fintech.
