## HSBC said the move marks a significant expansion of its existing lending programme for India’s startup ecosystem. The bank already has a substantial balance sheet allocation for fund financing across venture capital and domestic private equity funds, and now plans to broaden this offering through the Innovation Banking platform.

By Asmita PantOctober 9, 2025, 11:57:43 AM IST (Published)

HSBC on Thursday (October 9) announced the launch of HSBC Innovation Banking in India, a dedicated platform aimed at supporting the country’s fast-growing technology and venture ecosystem.

As part of the launch, the bank plans to allocate $1 billion in non-dilutive debt capital to Indian startups across various stages of growth.

The new initiative will provide banking and financing solutions tailored to entrepreneurs—from seed to IPO—as well as their investors. The funding programme is designed to help startups scale their businesses without diluting equity, enabling founders and investors to retain greater control.

HSBC said the move marks a significant expansion of its existing lending programme for India’s startup ecosystem. The bank already has a substantial balance sheet allocation for fund financing across venture capital and domestic private equity funds, and now plans to broaden this offering through the Innovation Banking platform.

“By expanding Innovation Banking into India, we are signalling our intent to support entrepreneurial ambition globally,” said David Sabow, Global Head of HSBC Innovation Banking. “Our $1 billion allocation is a clear sign of our intention to support start-ups on their path to greater growth through funding and accessing the benefits of our global expertise.”

Ajay Sharma, Head of Banking, HSBC India, said, “Innovation is reimagining the future of the Indian economy and shaping new global possibilities. With the launch of HSBC Innovation Banking in India, we are deepening our support for the vibrant startup ecosystem, where we have a proven track record of partnering with clients on their growth journeys.”

Dilip Gopinath has been appointed as Head of HSBC Innovation Banking, India.

With nearly two decades of experience in financial services, Gopinath will lead a dedicated team focused on providing bespoke financial solutions to startups and tech-driven businesses.

HSBC Innovation Banking operates globally across key innovation hubs, including the US, UK, Australia, New Zealand, Israel, Continental Europe, Hong Kong, mainland China, and now India.

Highlighting India’s position as a “tech and talent hub” and one of the world’s most dynamic startup ecosystems, the bank cited estimates that by 2030, Indian startups could contribute $1 trillion to the domestic economy and generate 50 million new jobs.
