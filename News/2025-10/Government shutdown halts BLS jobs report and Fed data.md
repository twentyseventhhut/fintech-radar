# Government shutdown halts BLS jobs report and Fed data

> [!info] 2025-10-28 · 1 упоминаний · 1 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: This Week in Fintech

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[This Week in Fintech] Of the many things that stop when the government shuts down, so does data reporting. The Bureau of Labor Statistics is shut down which means there is no Jobs Report coming out. The Jobs Report is the Fed’s most important indicator of labor market health and inflation. It includes nonfarm payrolls, unemployment rate, wage growth. The Consumer Price Index is also not reported, which is a more direct measure of inflation. Other federal government data points will also not be collected, however Fed research continues as is. Another obstacle faced by the Fed is loss of access to private jobs data from ADP. There is financial market data available, but it’s not a 1 to 1 replacement for key data indicators that are missing due to the shutdown.

A line of people attending a jobs fair in Sacramento, California, earlier this month. Photo: David Paul Morris/Bloomberg via Getty Images

The Bureau of Labor Statistics will not release the October jobs report, citing the impact from the government shutdown, the agency said on Wednesday.

**Why it matters:** The lack of data will leave a key gap in economic policymakers' official understanding of how the struggling labor market fared this fall.

**Zoom in:**The agency's closure during the record-long shutdown prevented officials from collecting all of the key data needed during the month.

*   It comes at a vulnerable moment for the economy, when data like the unemployment rate, which is based on surveying American households, can be particularly affected.

**Yes, but:**The BLS said it will release the November jobs report on Tuesday, Dec. 16 — 11 days later than planned. That report _will_ include payroll employment data for October, the key indicator of job growth.

*   However, the household survey — on which the unemployment rate, labor force participation and similar measures are based — was not conducted during the shutdown, and therefore no October readings will be published.
*   "The household survey is not able to be retroactively collected," BLS said on its website.

**Of note:**The Federal Reserve's policy committee will meet Dec. 9-10 and appears divided on whether to cut interest rates for the third time this year.

*   With the delayed data release, it will not have the official October and November payrolls data in hand as it makes that call.

**What's next:**Thursday morning, the agency will release September jobs data that was collected before the shutdown.

*   Analysts surveyed by Bloomberg expect the report will show 55,000 jobs added that month and the unemployment rate unchanged at 4.3%.
