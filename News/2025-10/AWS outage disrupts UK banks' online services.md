**Disruption affecting Amazon Web Services led to a spike in reported outages across a wide range of internet services, including Halifax, HMRC, and Lloyds Bank, as ITV News Business Editor Joel Hills reports**

* * *

Many of the world’s biggest apps went down on Monday, including Canva, Snapchat, and Duolingo, due to an apparent issue affecting Amazon Web Services.

A huge number of internet services were affected by the outage, including banks, government websites, messaging services, and social media.

Downdetector, a website that tracks complaints about websites and web pages not operating properly, showed that several UK banks were experiencing issues, including Barclays, Lloyds and the Bank of Scotland, according to Downdetector.

More than 2,681 reports at Amazon Web Services (AWS) and 500 reports of issues for the HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) were reported on Downdectector by 9 am UK time on Monday.

Other services that showed a spike in reported outages included Slack, Ring, Vodafone, and EE.

Reported outages on Downdetector appeared to spike between 9am and 10am for most services, and then decline through Monday morning, with AWS stating at 10.27am it had seen “significant signs of recovery”.

Amazon Web Services (AWS) powers the infrastructure behind many websites, which is why the impact is so widespread.

Customers also reported an increase in outage reports at UK banks, including Lloyds, Halifax, and Bank of Scotland, with 6,925 Downdetector outage reports at Lloyds at 9.31am.

An error message posted on Halifax’s website read: “Sorry, we’re unable to process your request at the moment. We’re currently having some technical problems.”

In an update, posted at 9:26 am by Amazon confirmed "significant error rates" affecting its services.

"During this time, customers may be unable to create or update Support Cases," the statement said.

“Engineers were immediately engaged and are actively working on both mitigating the issue and fully understanding the root cause.

Graphic design platform, Canva was one of the organisation's whose webpage failed to load.  Credit: Canva website

A further update posted at 10.01am said the "potential root cause" had been identified.

"We recommend customers continue to retry any failed requests," it added.

The organisation has said it will continue to provide updates.

In an update at 11:35, Amazon said that most of its operations were "succeeding normally" but that it was continuing to work towards "full resolution".

"Underlying DNS issue has been fully mitigated," the organisation added.
