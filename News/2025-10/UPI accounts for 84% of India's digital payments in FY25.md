# UPI accounts for 84% of India's digital payments in FY25

UPI accounted for 84% of total transactions processed in India’s retail payment systems in FY25, according to the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) Annual Report 2024-2025 . In terms of volume, UPI surged 41.7% to touch 185.9 billion transactions in FY25 from 131.1 billion transactions in FY24. The value of UPI transactions rose 30.3% to Rs 260.6 lakh crore during the same period.
Meanwhile, the RBI emphasised its goal of UPI expansion to 20 countries by 2028-29, alongside increasing the footprint of UPI and RuPay cards. Currently, UPI is live across seven countries. In that regard, the apex bank referenced India’s participation in Project Nexus , an initiative for cross-border payments via UPI with 4 ASEAN countries. 
How did the overall payment landscape fare? 
The total payment and settlement systems recorded a 34.8% growth in transaction volumes in 2024-25, down from the 44% growth recorded in the previous fiscal year. However, the value of transactions grew by 17.3% in FY25 from 15.8% in FY24.
The apex bank attributed this value growth to an increase in Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) payments, which rose 12% in volume terms and 17.8% in value terms. As of March 2025, RTGS services are available across 1.73 lakh IFSCs of 250 member banks, while National Electronic Funds Transfer (NEFT) is available through 1.74 lakh IFSCs of 236 member banks. 
Meanwhile, the volume of total digital payments grew by 35% in FY25 from 44.3% in FY24. In value terms, total digital payments grew by 17.9% in this fiscal year from 16.4% in 2023-24. Besides this, the volume and value of retail transactions increased by 34.9% and 16.1%, respectively, in 2024-25.
Further, the number of point of sale (PoS) terminals grew by 24.7% to 1.1 crore while UPI Quick Response (QR) codes increased by 91.5% to 65.8 crore in 2024-25. 
 Issuance of new licenses: During the year, the RBI approved 26 online payment aggregators (PAs), five Payment Aggregators-Cross Border (PA-CB), 11 non-bank Prepaid Payment Instrument (PPI) issuers, one Trade Receivables and Discounting System (TReDS) entity, and one white label ATM (WLA) operator.
Fraud prevalence in 2024-25 
In total, FY25 saw 23,953 instances of fraud, a decrease from 36,060 in the previous year. However, the amount defrauded rose to Rs 36,014 crore in 2024-25 from Rs 12,230 crore in 2023-24. This discrepancy occurs due to the removal of fraud classification in 122 cases, amounting to Rs 18,674 crore in the previous fiscal years, and subsequent fresh reporting as per a Supreme Court judgement.
According to the report, 56.5% of frauds have occurred in the category of digital payments (card/internet). FY25 recorded 13,516 frauds in card transactions, valued at Rs 520 crore. Comparatively, in FY24, India saw 29,082 instances of fraud amounting to Rs 1,457 crore.
Efforts to combat fraud
The Central Payments Fraud Information Registry (CPFIR) has been extended to 49 scheduled Urban Cooperative Banks (UCBs), all local area banks, 43 regional rural banks (RRBs), 71 district central co-operative banks (DCCBs), and 234 non-scheduled UCBs as of March 2025. For context, this system is a web-based payment-related fraud reporting solution launched in 2020. 
Additionally, the RBI claimed to engage with overseas supervisors to “adopt global best practices in operational resilience and cyber security” to prepare against digital fraud and IT-based risks.
