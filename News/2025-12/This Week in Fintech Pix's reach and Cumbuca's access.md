Fewer Brazilians are using physical cash for payments as the country embraces what Google calls the “Cashless Era,” marked by a decisive shift from paper currency to digital transactions, according to the study Payments in Transformation: From Cash to Code. 
 A key driver behind this transition has been the expansion of banking access. The movement started in 2011 with the end of the Visa-Mastercard duopoly, which opened the payment market to new players. In Brazil, the trend accelerated with the introduction of Pix, the Central Bank’s universal digital payments system, and the pandemic-era emergency aid program, which led many people to open bank accounts to receive government benefits. 
 Since then, cash usage has plummeted, dropping from 43% of respondents in 2019 to just 6% in 2024. Cash, once the dominant payment method in Brazil, has been overtaken by Pix, which accounted for 47% of total financial transactions by the end of 2024. 
 Pix has been used by 93% of Brazilian adults, with 62% naming it their most frequent payment method. “Consumers already see it as the most convenient option. Pix has the fastest adoption and growth rate,” said Thais Melendez, Google’s head of strategic insights. 
 According to the survey, 41% of respondents cited security as the main reason for using Pix, followed by convenience (37%), zero fees (36%), and discounts on purchases (33%). 
 Regarding how Pix is used, 76% have used it for direct transfers, 75% for QR code payments, and 37% for contactless transactions. 
 Additionally, 22% reported using Pix in installments—a feature officially launching in September via Brazil’s Central Bank but already offered by some financial institutions. “This indicates strong demand for Pix installments, which could evolve into a microcredit alternative,” Ms. Melendez said. 
 The main appeal of Pix installments lies in its flexibility, since it doesn’t affect credit card limits, and in the discounts offered by merchants. Convenience and security are also key factors, especially for online shopping where card details aren’t required. Pix is also seen as a financial inclusion tool, benefiting those without access to traditional credit. 
 Pix vs. credit cards 
 Despite Pix’s rapid rise, 58% of respondents said their credit card usage has increased over the past five years. Google’s annual study, running since 2019, shows credit cards remain the second most popular payment method after Pix. 
 Some 62% of respondents said they own at least one credit card—a figure rising to 79% among wealthier Brazilians (class A). On average, respondents hold two cards. 
 The primary reasons for using credit cards include perks like airport lounge access (20%), especially among class A consumers; higher credit limits (20%), particularly among classes C and D; and ease of use (17%), most common among young adults aged 18 to 24. 
 In qualitative comparisons, credit cards only outperform Pix in loyalty programs. Pix is rated superior in security, convenience, speed, and acceptance. 
 Respondents reported using credit cards mainly for high-value online purchases like travel, electronics, and furniture. Pix dominates in subscriptions, delivery apps, peer-to-peer transfers, marketplaces, and clothing stores. 
 In physical retail, credit cards are preferred for apparel, electronics, and furniture, while Pix leads in everyday transactions like utility bills, restaurants, entertainment, and services. “It’s about usage occasions and consumer needs. In some situations, Pix is simply more practical,” said Gustavo Pena, Google Brazil’s industry director. 
 Payment terminals and merchant trends 
 Businesses are increasingly seeking integrated financial services, and when choosing a payment terminal brand, additional services like checking accounts and insurance are deciding factors, according to the survey. Among business respondents, 80% valued bundled services when selecting a terminal provider. 
 While 93% considered insurance essential, along with loyalty programs (92%) and cash flow management tools (90%), actual adoption remains low—just 22% use insurance, 20% participate in loyalty programs, and 19% use financial management tools. 
 Companies typically use two payment terminals and are familiar with at least seven brands. Among businesses with terminals, 70% use standard models, 47% have devices supporting contactless payments, and 44% use QR code readers for Pix. 
 Among companies without terminals, 25% accept payments via QR code and another 25% use “tap on phone” technology, which transforms NFC-enabled smartphones into payment devices. 
 The main reasons for adopting “tap on phone” include convenience (63%), mobility (55%), and speed (45%). Barriers include lack of technology (33%), concerns about phone loss or theft (33%), and lack of knowledge about how it works (27%), although 73% of businesses are aware of the feature. 
 When selecting a terminal brand, key factors are settlement speed (35%), transaction fees (26%), and security (25%). Still, 28% cited fees as a barrier and 21% found them difficult to understand.
