Albania Grants Preliminary License to Digital-Only JET Bank
The Supervisory Council of the Bank of Albania has granted preliminary approval for JET Bank to operate in the country, marking a significant step towards its full banking license.
Under the council’s decision, JET Bank is now authorized to pursue a license to conduct banking and financial activities in the Republic of Albania. The move positions the bank to become the country’s first fully digital financial institution, combining advanced technology with a simple, secure, and customer-focused model. The bank aims to introduce a new approach to financial services, distinct from traditional banking methods.
According to Albanian banking law, the licensing process for both domestic banks and foreign bank branches involves two stages: preliminary approval and final license issuance. Following the preliminary approval, JET Bank has up to 12 months to meet all legal and regulatory requirements, including capital deposits, hiring staff, establishing operational and technical infrastructure, and securing premises to conduct banking activities.
JET Bank becomes the second institution to receive preliminary approval to enter the Albanian banking market, following Turkey’s Ziraat Bank.
The Albanian banking sector has been undergoing a period of consolidation since 2015, reducing the number of commercial banks from 16 to 11 through acquisitions and mergers, with the exception of the Bank of Credit of Albania, which exited via liquidation. The last major consolidation occurred in 2022, when Alpha Bank Albania was acquired by OTP Bank Albania.
However, after less than a decade of consolidation, the sector appears poised for expansion. Rapid growth in recent years, along with improved profitability, has made the market attractive to new entrants. With preliminary approvals for JET Bank and Ziraat Bank, the total number of commercial banks in Albania is expected to rise to 13 in the coming year.
