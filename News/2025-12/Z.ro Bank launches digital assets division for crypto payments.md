O Z.ro Bank acaba de criar o Z.ro Digital Assets, nova unidade de negócios do banco responsável por processar transferências e pagamentos internacionais com criptoativos. A nova vertical nasce um mês depois de o Banco Central regular a prestação de serviços de ativos digitais, inserindo alguns deles, como as stablecoins, no mercado de câmbio .
A unidade integrará tecnologia de processamento própria e contará com uma base operacional na Suíça para custódia e movimentação de criptomoedas .
Com o movimento, a instituição financeira movimentará fundos de clientes para fora dos países de atuação do banco, que já tem forte presença na América Latina. A unidade criará rotas de pagamento entre a América Latina e regiões como Europa, Estados Unidos e Ásia.
A meta do Z.ro Bank é processar US$ 20 bilhões em transações com o exterior até 2030. O Z.ro Digital Assets vai operar no mesmo braço do Z.ro International, área do grupo dedicada às transações financeiras entre países da América Latina.
A nova unidade será comandada por Sérgio Massa, ex-diretor de operações do banco. Ele terá a missão de liderar essas transações com o exterior de forma eficiente, previsível e com menor custo.

"No crossborder, cada minuto custa dinheiro. O que fizemos foi usar tecnologia para encurtar caminhos, reduzir intermediários e entregar previsibilidade. A América Latina precisa de rotas internacionais que funcionem de forma simples e segura. E é exatamente isso que estamos oferecendo", diz Massa.
A aposta do banco na agilidade de processamento está em sua infraestrutura, que é própria. Com isso, a Z.ro Digital Assets não dependerá de parceiros externos para acessar as stablecoins —criptomoedas atreladas ao valor de algum ativo físico de referência, como o dólar .

Z.ro Bank announces Sergio Massa as CEO of the Z.ro Digital Assets vertical.
 Betting The 15.12.25 
 By: Magno Jose
 -A 
 + A 
Z.ro Bank, a techfin specializing in financial solutions for large companies, announces the arrival of Sérgio Massa as CEO of Z.ro Digital Assets, a new vertical dedicated to the international transfer and dispersion of funds. This area is launched with the goal of processing US$20 billion in cross-border payments by 2030, driven by a fully proprietary crypto infrastructure and a new operational hub in Switzerland.
With over 15 years of experience in technology, operations, and international business, including nearly a decade in the crypto ecosystem, Massa assumes responsibility for leading all cross-border operations at Z.ro Bank. His focus will be on increasing the efficiency of moving funds outside the bank's operating countries, reducing costs, shortening paths, and offering greater predictability for global clients.
“This new phase of Z.ro Digital Assets is a response to the complexity of international payments. We will simplify routes, eliminate intermediaries, and create a safer, faster, and more predictable experience for companies operating outside the region,” says the executive.
The structure led by Massa will operate in synergy with Z.ro International, the vertical responsible for local collection in Latin America. Together, the operations form a single chain, prepared to scale international routes with less friction and lower costs than the traditional banking system. The executive's arrival coincides with the global expansion of Z.ro Bank, in a market expected to exceed US$1 trillion by 2030. In Latin America, the bank is already expanding in Argentina, Chile, and Peru, with entry into Mexico planned for the coming months.
 About Z.ro Bank 
Z.ro Bank is a fintech company born in Porto Digital in Recife and accelerated by Endeavor, Itaú, and the IDB. It operates as a payment institution authorized by the Central Bank and, in the last two years, has processed more than R$ 45 billion in transactions. It is recognized by the market as a Pix specialist and, during some months of 2023, processed more than 5% of all Pix transactions from individuals to businesses in Brazil. It was also recognized and awarded by Exame magazine as the third fastest-growing company in terms of Net Revenue in the country. Recently, it launched the first "developer-first" bank, with all APIs open, so that large companies can plug in and shape the interfaces to their liking, customizing flows, dashboards, and reports to improve the efficiency of their financial management.
