BlackFin Tech Weekly - December 16th, 2025
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity
Hello FinTech Friends,
 Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry! Over the last week, there were four fintech deals in Europe , raising a total of €64.5 million , including two transactions in UK, one in Germany and one in Switzerland. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Real Finance , a Switzerland-based virtual machine-compatible Layer 1 blockchain designed to integrate real-world assets (RWAs) and provide institutional-grade tokenization infrastructure, has raised €24.7 million in a Venture round led by Nimbus Capital . 
 LI.FI , a Germany-based non-custodial cross-chain liquidity protocol and developer toolkit enabling on-chain swaps and transfers through a single integration, has raised €24.7 million in a Series A extension led by Multicoin Capital and CoinFund . 
 Yonda Tax , a UK-based tax automation platform that handles the calculation, filing, and payment of VAT, Sales Tax, and GST across jurisdictions, has raised €12.8 million in a Venture round led by Kennet Partners . Let’s dive in! Real Finance 
 Real Finance , headquartered in Switzerland , is building a Virtual Machine-compatible Layer 1 blockchain designed to seamlessly integrate real-world financial assets (RWAs) and provide institutional-grade infrastructure for tokenization. The company has raised €24.7 million in a Venture round led by Nimbus Capital , with participation from Magnus Capital and Frekaz Group . The fresh funds will be used to accelerate development of the “institutional rails” for tokenized assets and support Real Finance’s near-term objective of tokenizing €500 million in real-world assets. Read more LI.FI 
 LI.FI , headquartered in Berlin , develops a non-custodial protocol and developer toolkit that enables applications to execute on-chain swaps and cross-chain transfers through a single integration point. The company has raised €24.7 million in a Series A extension led by Multicoin Capital and CoinFund , bringing total funding around €44 million. The fresh capital will be used to scale operations and hire and to accelerate product development, including infrastructure for AI agents and stablecoins and the planned launch of an open intent & solver marketplace (Q1 2026). Read more Yonda Tax 
 Yonda Tax , headquartered in London , provides a tax automation platform that manages the calculation, filing, and payment of VAT, Sales Tax, and GST across multiple countries, helping businesses handle indirect tax compliance end-to-end. The company has raised €12.8 million in a Venture round led by Kennet Partners , with participation from NYO Capital and Portfolio Ventures . The new capital will be used to enhance platform features and functionality and to support expansion into new industries and additional tax jurisdictions, as the company continues to serve 350+ clients globally on a predictable fixed-fee model. Read more In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 WTW , a UK-based global advisory, broking, and solutions company, has announced the acquisition of Cushon , a UK-based fintech specializing in workplace pensions, savings, and financial wellbeing. The transaction strengthens WTW ’s wealth management offering by integrating a digital-first pensions and savings platform focused on employee engagement and long-term financial wellbeing. The deal is currently pending, and financial terms have not been disclosed. Read more 
 Mollie , a Netherlands-based online payment processor and gateway, has announced the acquisition of GoCardless , a UK-based recurring payments platform. Valued at approximately €1.1bn, the transaction aims to create a leading European payments champion by combining Mollie ’s broad merchant acquiring capabilities with GoCardless ’ expertise in account-to-account and recurring payments. The deal is currently pending and would significantly expand Mollie ’s footprint across the European payment’s ecosystem.  Dutch payments group Mollie has agreed to acquire UK fintech GoCardless for about €1.05bn in a largely share-based deal aimed at creating a new European payments champion valued at roughly €3bn. Backed by investors including Blackstone and EQT , Amsterdam-based Mollie will add GoCardless’s bank payments capabilities to its existing card and financial services offering, with the combined group serving more than 350,000 small businesses and enterprises. Talks began over the summer and the transaction is expected to close by mid-2026. Executives said the businesses were highly complementary and that the deal was focused on product expansion rather than cost-cutting, despite a sharp reset in fintech valuations since the pandemic. Mollie was valued at €6.5bn in a 2021 funding round, while GoCardless was last valued at $2.1bn in early 2022, with major backers including Balderton Capital , Accel and BlackRock . Read more 
 Automated digital wealth manager Wealthfront said it raised $486m after pricing its U.S. initial public offering at $14 a share, the top of its marketed range, valuing the company at about $2bn. The Palo Alto-based group sold 34.6m shares in the offering, capping a resilient year for U.S. IPO activity despite tariff concerns and a prolonged government shutdown earlier in 2025, and adding to a strong run of fintech listings alongside firms such as Chime and Klarna . Founded in 2008 by Andy Rachleff and Dan Carroll , Wealthfront offers automated investment, cash management and lending tools and has incorporated artificial intelligence into its financial planning software. The company will list on Nasdaq under the ticker “WLTH”, with Goldman Sachs , J.P. Morgan and Citigroup acting as underwriters. Read more · 
 Starling Bank is exploring the acquisition of another UK lender as it seeks to put its £12bn of customer deposits to more profitable use by expanding into higher-return areas such as corporate lending. Founded in 2014, Starling has struggled to grow lending in line with deposits and believes an acquisition would be faster than organic expansion. The bank has previously been linked to takeover talks, including a reported approach from Shawbrook , and has also faced regulatory scrutiny over past lending and controls, though it says these have since been strengthened. Alongside M&A, Starling is pursuing growth through its Engine banking software platform and is considering longer-term expansion into Europe and the US, potentially including a US IPO. Read more 
 Payments group Airwallex has raised $330m in a funding round that values the business at $8bn, as it opens a San Francisco base and steps up its push into Silicon Valley. The Singapore-based fintech has diluted the stake of long-time shareholder Tencent , which now holds significantly less than 10 per cent, to make room for US investors including T Rowe Price and Robinhood Ventures . Chief executive Jack Zhang said the fundraising provided external validation as Airwallex gears up for a potential IPO in the next three to four years, with the company planning to double its US workforce to more than 400 staff and hire artificial intelligence engineers to strengthen its technology. Airwallex , founded in Melbourne in 2015 and now headquartered in Singapore, rejected recent claims that it allowed US customer data to be accessed from China, saying it has strict controls in line with US law.
