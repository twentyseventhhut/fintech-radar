Private equity firms Permira and Warburg Pincus have agreed to acquire Clearwater Analytics, a Boise, Idaho-based investment and accounting software maker, for $8.4 billion (including assumed debt).

**The big picture:**It's a boomerang buyout for Permira and Warburg Pincus, which both invested in Clearwater in 2020 and helped take it public one year later.

*   Each firm still holds more than a 2% stake in the company, per S&P Capital IQ, with Warburg Pincus partner Cary Davis remaining on its board of directors.

**Zoom in:** Francisco Partners and Temasek also are participating on the buyside.

*   Clearwater shareholders will receive $24.55 per share in cash, a 47% premium over where shares traded before mid-November reports of a possible deal.
*   Activist investor Starboard Value last week disclosed around a 5% position in Clearwater, calling it undervalued.

**Go deeper,**via Reuters:"The company operates a single, multi-tenant cloud platform that aggregates portfolio data and performs complex accounting and analytics in one place."
