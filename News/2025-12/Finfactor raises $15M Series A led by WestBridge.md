 Finfactor, the parent company of Finvu Account Aggregator, has raised $15 million in its Series A round led by WestBridge Capital, with participation from existing investors Varanium Capital, DMI Sparkle Fund and IIFL Fintech Fund. 
 The Pune based company had earlier raised $2.5 million in July 2022 from Varanium Nexgen Fund, IIFL, DMI Sparkle Fund and others. 
 According to the company, the new capital will be used to expand its product suite for financial institutions, enhance its analytics capabilities and advance its goal of becoming a full stack technology provider for banks and BFSI clients. 
 Co-founded by Manoj Alandkar and Munish Bhatia, Finvu Account Aggregator enables secure data exchange for banks, insurers, stockbrokers, investment advisors and other financial institutions, and claims to have served more than 50 million consumers. 
 Finfactor offers data analytics and artificial intelligence driven solutions such as a multi AA gateway, bank statement analyser, loan monitoring and collections tools and wealth management capabilities. It serves more than 150 BFSI clients including HDFC Bank, Axis Bank, Motilal Oswal, Canara Bank and CRED, supported by a team of more than 120 professionals. 
 WestBridge recently led the $40 million Series B round for credit infrastructure fintech FinBox. It has also backed enterprise AI platform UnifyApps, edtech firm SpeakX, and took part in a secondary transaction in Rapido to facilitate an exit for Swiggy.

# Finvu AA Operator Finfactor Raises $15M Series A

**Finfactor**has raised US$15 million in Series A funding, led by **WestBridge Capital**, with participation from Varanium Capital, DMI Sparkle Fund, and IIFL Fintech Fund.

The India-based fintech operates **Finvu AA**, an RBI-licensed account aggregator, and plans to utilize the funds for product development, analytics expansion and technology upgrades for banks and financial institutions.

Founded by **Manoj Alandkar** and **Munish Bhatia**, Finvu AA is among India’s earliest licensed AAs and has enabled data-sharing for **over 50 million users**, while serving more than 150 banks and financial organizations, including **HDFC Bank**, **Axis Bank**, **Motilal Oswal**, **Canara Bank**, and **CRED**. The company currently employs around 120 people.

**Key Highlights**

*   _Finfactor raises US$15 million to scale Finvu AA’s technology and analytics_
*   _Finvu AA supports 50M+ users and 150+ financial institutions across India_
*   _India’s AA ecosystem spans 780+ institutions, creating strong demand for compliance and integration tools_

Industry-level metrics show that India’s **Account Aggregator (AA) system** enabled Rs.1.67 lakh crore across 18.9 million loans in FY25 and facilitated 4.77 crore financial services. However, public data does not break down Finvu AA’s user base, consent volumes or market share among licensed AAs.

Similarly, there is no visibility into Finvu’s transaction velocity, FIUs on its platform, or per-provider usage on the **AA dashboard**, making it difficult for investors to interpret the US$15 million raise as an indicator of market leadership or baseline growth.With 780+ financial institutions in the AA network, each must implement RBI-defined API specifications and obtain optional certification through Sahamati-empanelled auditors, driving demand for integration and compliance tooling.

As AA adoption expands across business loans, secured loans, wealth management and insurance, FIUs increasingly require consent UX design, analytics dashboards and risk-model integrations—opportunities for third-party vendors.
