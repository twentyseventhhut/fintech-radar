 Capital Small Finance Bank (CSFB) has reported robust financial results for the second quarter, showcasing steady growth across key business metrics. The bank also unveiled ambitious expansion plans, aiming to double its loan book to ₹16,000 crores by FY29.
Financial Highlights
CSFB demonstrated strong performance in Q2:
Gross advances rose 17.7% year-on-year to ₹7,907.00 crore
Deposits increased 19.8% year-on-year to ₹9,317.00 crore
Profit After Tax (PAT) grew 4.6% year-on-year to ₹35.00 crore
Return on Assets (RoA) improved to 1.3% from 1.2% in the previous quarter
The bank maintained a healthy asset quality with Gross Non-Performing Assets (GNPA) at 2.70% and Net Non-Performing Assets (NNPA) at 1.38%, showing a slight improvement from the previous quarter.
Key Financial Metrics
Growth Strategy and Future Outlook
Mr. Sarvjit Singh Samra, Managing Director & CEO of Capital Small Finance Bank, commented on the results: "The quarter ended September 30 was marked by healthy credit offtake and stable margins, reflecting the strength of our core operating model."
The bank has outlined an ambitious growth strategy, targeting to:
Double its loan book to ₹16,000+ crores by FY29
Expand its branch network to 300+ locations by FY29
Improve profitability metrics, aiming for a Return on Assets (RoA) of 1.6%+ by FY29
Expansion Plans
CSFB is focusing on expanding its presence beyond its core markets:
Entering new states like Uttar Pradesh and Gujarat
Intensifying penetration in current markets (Haryana, Punjab, Rajasthan & Delhi)
Targeting 30%+ branches outside Punjab by 2029 (currently at 17.5%)
Business Focus
The bank continues to emphasize:
Organic loan book growth in MSME, Mortgages, and Agriculture segments
Maintaining a secured lending portfolio (currently at 99.2% secured loans)
Elevating its retail-focused liability franchise
Strengthening operational efficiency to enhance profitability
Capital Small Finance Bank's strong quarterly results and ambitious growth plans reflect its commitment to expanding its presence in the small finance banking sector while maintaining robust financial health and asset quality.
