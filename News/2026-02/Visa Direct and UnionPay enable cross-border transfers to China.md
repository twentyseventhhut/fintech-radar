SAN FRANCISCO & DOHA, Qatar--(BUSINESS WIRE)--At Web Summit Qatar, Visa (NYSE:V) and UnionPay International (UPI) announced an agreement to enable cross-border money movement into Chinese Mainland through Visa Direct. Once fully rolled out, clients will be able to send cross-border remittances and business-to-consumer payouts to more than 95 percent of UnionPay International debit cardholders in Chinese Mainland, through a single connection.

> This milestone showcases Visa’s commitment to opening global corridors and extending reliable money movement capabilities to people and businesses worldwide.
> 
> Share

By connecting Visa Direct’s global money movement network to UPI’s MoneyExpress platform, Visa will provide a more seamless, secure, and transparent way for consumers and businesses worldwide to send money into Chinese Mainland, one of the world’s largest remittance destinations. This milestone showcases Visa’s commitment to opening global corridors and extending reliable money movement capabilities to people and businesses worldwide.

“Global business now moves at internet speed, but money hasn’t always kept pace,” said Vira Platonova, Global Head of Visa Direct. “By expanding Visa Direct’s reach through UnionPay International, we’re shrinking the world again – not through tests or theory, but through real, critical infrastructure operating at massive scale, speed and reliability.”

The connection will support a broad set of cross‑border use cases, including creator and freelancer payouts, contractor disbursements, reimbursements, and family remittances. As platforms, marketplaces and employers operate without borders, these payments and payouts increasingly require the ability to reliably reach people using global scale and real‑time delivery.

“Cross‑border remittances are a key livelihood link connecting personal life, economic activity, and financial needs across borders, and are an important part of UnionPay’s international strategy,” said Larry Wang, CEO of UnionPay International. "This cooperation with Visa represents a precise alignment of the two sides’ strengths and is also an active response to the digitalization and convenience trends in global cross‑border remittances. It will allow more overseas users to enjoy low‑cost, high‑efficiency, and trustworthy cross‑border remittance services, while delivering business growth to partners at home and abroad and supporting the development of a broader, more efficient global cross‑border remittance service ecosystem."

Visa Direct continues to scale as a foundational layer of the global economy – helping clients unlock new markets, improve liquidity, and deliver faster, more predictable payment experiences worldwide. The expanded Visa Direct connection to UnionPay International is expected to be available starting in the first half of 2026.

“Whether it’s a creator getting paid, a contractor receiving earnings or a family sending support across borders, reach and reassurance are everything,” added Platonova. “Expanding Visa Direct into one of the world’s largest, most complex markets will bring that trust to life at scale, where reliability and reach matter most.”

Visa Direct and UnionPay International Plan Real-Time Cross-Border Tr6ansfers Into Chinese Mainland

 
 Rosalia Mazza 
 
 
 
Tuesday, February 3rd 2026 03:06 PM
 
 

 Visa Direct and UnionPay International have announced a new cross-border payment connection that will allow real-time money transfers to most UnionPay debit cardholders in Chinese Mainland, with rollout expected in 2026.

 A New Link in One of the World’s Largest Remittance Corridors 
At Web Summit Qatar, Visa Inc. and UnionPay International revealed a plan to connect their money movement systems, opening a new channel for cross-border payments into Chinese Mainland. The agreement will link Visa Direct’s global payout network with UnionPay International’s MoneyExpress platform, enabling senders to reach most UnionPay debit cardholders in the market through a single technical connection.
The companies said the service is designed to support real-time transfers once the rollout is complete. Availability is expected in the first half of 2026. When fully implemented, the connection would give payment providers, marketplaces, and financial institutions access to more than 95 percent of UnionPay International debit cards in Chinese Mainland.
This move targets one of the world’s most active remittance destinations. Money flows into Chinese Mainland from overseas workers, family members, and international businesses remain a central part of global payment activity. The new link aims to reduce processing delays and simplify payout operations across borders.
 
 How the Network Connection Works 
Visa Direct operates as a card-based money movement service that allows funds to be sent directly to eligible cards in near real time. UnionPay International’s MoneyExpress platform supports inbound cross-border payments to UnionPay-issued cards in Chinese Mainland.
By linking the two systems, senders using Visa Direct will be able to route funds directly into the UnionPay network without building separate integrations. The technical bridge is designed to handle transaction routing, settlement coordination, and compliance checks required for international transfers.
The companies described the project as infrastructure-level integration rather than a consumer-facing app launch. Payment firms and corporate users will access the service through existing Visa Direct channels, while recipients in Chinese Mainland will receive funds directly on their UnionPay debit cards.
 
 Expanding Use Cases for Global Payments 
The partnership targets a range of payment scenarios that continue to grow in volume. These include freelance and creator payouts, contractor compensation, business-to-consumer disbursements, reimbursement payments, and family remittances.
Digital platforms increasingly rely on fast settlement to support cross-border workforces and global user bases. Delays of several days can disrupt cash flow for individuals who depend on frequent payouts. Real-time or near real-time transfers address this issue by shortening the time between payment initiation and receipt.
The new connection also supports businesses that manage international supplier networks or operate regional hubs outside Chinese Mainland. By enabling direct card payouts, firms can avoid some of the complexity tied to traditional bank transfer systems.
 
 The Strategic Importance of Chinese Mainland Access 
Chinese Mainland represents one of the most complex payment environments in the world. Regulatory requirements, settlement processes, and domestic network structures differ from those in many other markets. For international payment providers, building access to local cardholders often requires separate technical and regulatory arrangements.
UnionPay International operates the international arm of China’s domestic card network and provides access to UnionPay-branded cards issued abroad and within Chinese Mainland. By working with UnionPay International, Visa gains a route into a market that has historically been difficult for external payment systems to enter at scale.
For UnionPay International, the link with Visa Direct extends inbound payment reach from senders in regions where Visa has a strong issuing footprint. The result is a two-way expansion of network coverage.
 
 Infrastructure Over Marketing 
The announcement at Web Summit Qatar emphasized operational readiness rather than consumer promotion. Both companies framed the agreement as a foundation for broader money movement services rather than a short-term product launch.
Visa executives described the project as part of a longer-term effort to modernize cross-border payments by building direct network connections instead of relying on layered correspondent banking models. UnionPay International leaders described the link as aligned with demand for digital payment tools that support lower-cost international transfers.
Although the companies used public remarks to outline the partnership, the focus remained on network connectivity, processing capability, and system integration.
 
 What This Means for the Fintech Sector 
The deal reflects a broader shift across the fintech sector toward infrastructure partnerships. Payment platforms increasingly depend on shared networks to reach new markets without duplicating compliance and settlement systems.
Real-time cross-border payments remain a central challenge for fintech providers. While domestic instant payment systems have expanded rapidly, international transfers often face delays due to currency conversion, regulatory checks, and settlement batching.
Direct network connections reduce these friction points by allowing funds to move through fewer intermediaries. For fintech firms building payout services, this type of integration can lower operational complexity and speed up deployment.
The Visa-UnionPay connection also signals rising demand for payout-focused services rather than only merchant acquiring. Platforms that serve creators, gig workers, and digital marketplaces require fast outbound payment options to remain competitive.
 
 Regulatory and Operational Considerations 
Cross-border money movement into Chinese Mainland involves regulatory oversight from multiple jurisdictions. Payment systems must comply with anti-money laundering rules, data protection standards, and local settlement requirements.
The companies said testing and phased deployment will take place before full rollout. This period allows transaction routing, compliance workflows, and system resilience to be validated under live conditions.
Although real-time processing is the goal, regulatory approvals and operational readiness will determine the pace of expansion. Market observers note that similar integrations often begin with limited corridors before expanding to broader coverage.
 
 A Broader Trend Toward Network Interoperability 
The agreement follows a growing pattern among global payment networks to pursue interoperability rather than isolation. Card networks, real-time payment systems, and digital wallet platforms increasingly seek direct links that allow money to move across ecosystems.
For Visa, Visa Direct has become a central part of its strategy to extend beyond card purchases into disbursements and account-to-card payments. For UnionPay International, international partnerships remain a key method for expanding acceptance and inbound transfer capability.
This trend reflects changing consumer behavior. Users now expect international payments to operate with the same speed and reliability as domestic transfers. Meeting that expectation requires cooperation among payment networks that once operated largely in parallel.
 
 Market Impact and Competitive Context 
Competition in cross-border payments has intensified. Traditional banks continue to modernize correspondent networks, while specialized remittance firms promote digital-first transfer models. Technology companies and wallet providers also push into international payout services.
By linking two major card networks, Visa and UnionPay International position themselves as infrastructure providers for third-party platforms rather than only consumer brands. This approach allows marketplaces, payroll providers, and financial apps to plug into existing rails rather than building custom solutions.
Analysts note that scale remains the main advantage of large networks. Coverage across issuing banks, merchants, and cardholders creates network effects that smaller payment providers struggle to replicate.
 
 Timeline and Next Steps 
The companies expect the service to become available in the first half of 2026. During this period, technical integration, regulatory coordination, and partner onboarding will take place.
Once active, the system will allow payment providers using Visa Direct to send funds directly to UnionPay debit cards in Chinese Mainland. Expansion beyond initial corridors may follow depending on demand and operational performance.
No specific transaction limits or pricing details were disclosed at the time of the announcement. These elements are expected to be finalized closer to launch.
 
 Why This Matters for Global Money Movement 
Cross-border payments remain one of the slowest segments of financial infrastructure modernization. While domestic real-time systems have become common in many countries, international transfers still rely heavily on batch processing and multi-bank settlement paths.
Direct network connections offer a different model. They reduce the number of steps required to move funds across borders and provide greater transparency on transaction status.
For individuals sending money to family members, faster settlement improves access to funds. For businesses paying contractors or customers, shorter settlement cycles improve liquidity management.
The Visa-UnionPay initiative reflects growing recognition that global money movement requires shared infrastructure rather than isolated platforms.
 
 Looking Ahead 
The planned connection between Visa Direct and UnionPay International represents another step toward real-time international payments becoming standard rather than exceptional. Whether this model expands to additional corridors and networks will depend on performance, regulatory alignment, and market adoption.
For now, the agreement signals that major payment networks are investing in infrastructure that supports faster, more direct money movement across borders. If successful, the integration could influence how future cross-border payment systems are designed and deployed.
As global commerce becomes increasingly digital, the ability to move money quickly and reliably remains a central requirement. The next phase will test whether large-scale network partnerships can deliver that promise at sustained volume.
