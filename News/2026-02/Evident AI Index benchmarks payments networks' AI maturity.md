PayPal has been named the global leader in AI talent in the 2026 Evident AI Index for Payments, ranking first worldwide for AI Talent and second for Innovation across the payments industry.
Artificial intelligence has long been central to PayPal’s commitment to trust and innovation. For years, AI has powered the company’s industry-leading fraud prevention, risk management, and the personalized experiences delivered to millions of consumers and merchants worldwide. PayPal’s early investments in AI have positioned the company to lead in the era of agentic commerce – extending trust, security, and seamless access across our global network of consumers and merchants.
 
“As commerce becomes increasingly intelligent and agentic, PayPal is building the infrastructure that will enable safe, open, and trusted AI-driven experiences at global scale,” said Srini Venkatesan, Chief Technology Officer at PayPal. “Our world-class AI talent, combined with sustained investment in our platforms and research, enables us to move rapidly from concept to scaled deployment – delivering real impact for our customers.”
The Evident AI Index is an independent benchmark that evaluates AI maturity across the financial services sector, analyzing millions of public data points across four key categories: Talent, Innovation, Leadership, and Transparency. In their inaugural AI Index for payments, PayPal placed first in Talent and second in Innovation, underscoring its ability to attract and develop top AI expertise while translating emerging technologies into production at scale.
“We are committed to leading with responsible, merchant-first innovation that empowers businesses to compete and thrive in an AI-driven economy,” said Michelle Gill, GM of Small Business and Financial Services at PayPal. “Evident’s independent analysis highlights PayPal’s strength in translating AI innovation into measurable results for merchants.”
This latest recognition adds to a growing list of accolades highlighting PayPal’s leadership at the intersection of artificial intelligence and financial services, reinforcing the company’s position as a trusted innovator shaping the future of global commerce.

 ABOUT THE EVIDENT AI INDEX 
At Evident, we believe transparency is critical to harnessing the value of AI for commercial—and societal—gain. That's why we built the Evident AI Index. 
Recognized as the most trusted benchmark of its kind by global media and leading financial institutions, the Evident AI Index provides senior AI leaders with comprehensive, independent insights into how AI is being deployed across financial services.
In February 2026, we launched the Evident AI Index for Payments , ranking 12 major processors and networks on their AI maturity across four pillars: Transparency, Leadership, Innovation, and Talent.
Read on to explore the key findings, or download the full report.

 KEY FINDINGS 
1
LEADERS DEMONSTRATE CONSISTENT INVESTMENT ACROSS CRITICAL AI ENABLERS
Visa, Mastercard, and PayPal lead Evident’s inaugural ranking of AI maturity within the payments sector. The leaders demonstrate a high degree of consistency across each pillar of the Index – outpacing their peers in three of the four areas of assessment: Talent, Innovation, and Transparency. 

2
GREATER AI MATURITY DRIVES USE CASE DEPLOYMENT
Over the past two years, the 12 payment companies assessed documented nearly 100 AI use cases in press releases or investor relations materials. Payment companies with higher Index scores drove a larger share of the use cases found, demonstrating how sustained investment in AI enablers are accelerating use case deployment.

3
EVIDENCE OF ROI IS EMERGING, BUT ONLY AT LEADING COMPANIES
 Nearly a third of disclosed AI use cases specify reported outcomes , including efficiency gains, risk reduction, and revenue uplift. Firms in the top half of the Index account for nearly 80% of these disclosures , further underscoring the link between AI maturity and the ability to scale use cases with demonstrable ROI.



4
NO COMPREHENSIVE ROI REPORTED ACROSS GROUP-WIDE AI ACTIVITIES (YET)
 No payments company has disclosed realized or projected ROI across all enterprise or group-wide AI activities (yet). This represents a missed opportunity to demonstrate how AI investment is already delivering firmwide benefits. We see this happening at pace in adjacent sectors, where over 1-in-5 banks already do so.

5
CARD NETWORKS ACCOUNT FOR ALMOST HALF OF THE TALENT STACK
 The three major card networks account for nearly half (48%) of the talent stack employed by companies in the Index. American Express has the most AI Developers, owing to its role as both card issuer and payment network. PayPal boasts 2.9x as much AI talent volume compared to other processors, owing to the breadth and depth of AI specialization within its diverse product portfolio.

6
AI RESEARCH IS THRIVING, BUT CONCENTRATED IN THE HANDS OF A FEW
While three quarters of payment companies have published AI research over the last three years, this output remains highly concentrated. Visa, PayPal, and Mastercard account for 85% of published work – signaling continued investment in capability-building that define and advance the “cutting edge” of AI innovation.

7
C-SUITE DRIVES AWARENESS OF AI STRATEGY AND EVOLVING PRIORITIES
 Only half of payment companies call out AI as a critical enabler of business objectives in strategic documents or investor relations relations materials. In the absence of more comprehensive AI:ROI reporting, the industry remains more reliant on CEOs and senior leaders to message major milestones with regards to AI strategy.

8
ONLY 1-IN-3 PUBLISH RESPONSIBLE AI PRINCIPLES
Although half of payment companies explicitly allude to Responsible AI (RAI) principles in strategy documents or external communications, only a third openly publish these tenets . Among these, Mastercard and PayPal are the only companies to go the extra mile in demonstrating active operational implementation.
