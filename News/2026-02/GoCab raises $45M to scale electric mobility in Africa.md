# GoCab bags $45m mix of debt and equity funding to accelerate financial inclusion in Africa

GoCab will use the funds to bolster its drive-to-own electric vehicle fleet, expand its presence in "new high-growth cities across and outside the continent", and integrate AI solutions into its operations.

Ivorian mobility fintech GoCab has landed a $45 million financing round made up of $15 million in equity and $30 million in debt, to expand its fleet of electric vehicles (EVs) and footprint in "new high-growth cities across and outside the continent", according to a company statement.

GoCab, created in 2024 by Azamat Sultan and Gendrick Ketchemen, offers drivers and delivery couriers across five different countries in Africa access to EVs with drive-to-own financing options. The gig workers can use the vehicles to earn income, which can then be used to pay off the EV over time.

Commenting on the new injection of capital, Sultan says: "This round allows us to scale responsibly expanding access to fair, ethical financing while accelerating the transition to electric mobility, lowering carbon emissions, and building a more inclusive and sustainable future in close alignment with our investors."

The GoCab announcement also adds that the funds will facilitate the integration of AI solutions into the fintech's credit scoring, fleet optimisation, and risk management operations.

The $45 million raise was led by E3 Capital and JANNGO Capital, with KawiSafi Ventures and Cur8 Capital also participating.

London-based Cur8 Capital is also participating in GoCab's $60 million Shariah-compliant debt facility, which is currently being structured. Alongside other investors, it has committed more than $30 million in debt funding.
