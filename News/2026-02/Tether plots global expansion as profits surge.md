Stablecoin giant Tether invests $50 million in sleep technology startup Eight Sleep
The firm behind the $183 billion USDT stablecoin is expanding beyond its crypto roots to longevity and artificial intelligence.
Tether has invested $50 million in sleep technology startup Eight Sleep at a $1.5 billion valuation, expanding its reach beyond stablecoins.
Eight Sleep will use the funding to develop new AI-driven health features powered by Tether’s QVAC architecture, which processes data on devices rather than in the cloud.
The deal continues Tether’s push to deploy its more than $10 billion in one-year profits into ventures across energy, payments, artificial intelligence and health technology.
Tether, the crypto firm behind the most popular stablecoin USDT USDT $ 0.9993 , has invested $50 million in sleep technology startup Eight Sleep at a $1.5 billion valuation, according to a Wednesday press release and data from Crunchbase .
With the funding, Eight Sleep plans to develop new AI health features using Tether’s QVAC architecture, a computing framework designed to process data at the device level rather than relying fully on cloud systems.
Eight Sleep builds sensor-equipped sleep systems that track biometrics such as heart rate and temperature during the night. Its flagship "Pod" product adjusts mattress temperature and generates sleep insights based on real-time physiological data.
"We believe advanced personalized AI is the perfect pathway to understand and expand human potential," Paolo Ardoino, CEO of Tether, said in a statement.
The investment is the latest example of Tether pushing beyond stablecoins and crypto infrastructure. The firm is best known for its $183 billion USDT stablecoin, which is popular as a savings and payments tool across emerging markets with limited access to U.S. dollars. Tether reported more than $10 billion in net profits through 2025 and has increasingly channeled those earnings into venture investments across energy, payments, artificial intelligence, and health technology.
The deal follows Tether’s recent launch of QVAC Health, a platform that aggregates personal health data from wearables and other sources while keeping the information encrypted and under the user's control.

In a recent profile by Fortune , Tether , the issuer of the world’s dominant stablecoin USDT, emerges as a fast-growing global entity with around $187 billion in assets under management. The digital assets focused company is eyeing significant expansion into the US market, bolstered by partnerships with established financial players like Cantor Fitzgerald . However, the spotlight falls on CEO Paolo Ardoino ‘s stark warnings about societal decay in the West, painting a picture of impending cultural and economic turmoil.
 Ardoino envisions a future where centralized systems falter, urging a shift toward decentralized technologies to mitigate collapse.
This latest perspective underscores Tether’s strategic pivot beyond mere cryptocurrency, positioning it as a builder of resilient, peer-to-peer infrastructures.
Yet, this narrative isn’t solely one of potentially negative developments.
Tether’s evolution demonstrates adaptability, transforming potential vulnerabilities into strengths through broad diversification.
By branching into commodities, advanced tech, and financial tools, the firm is fortifying its position against global uncertainties.
A key pillar of this strategy is its deepening involvement in gold .
Tether has allocated 10% to 15% of its investment portfolio to physical gold, acquiring around 27 tons in the fourth quarter of 2025 alone.
This move not only hedges against fiat currency volatility and geopolitical risks but also enhances its gold-backed token, XAUt, which now represents a significant portion of the global gold-stablecoin market.
Innovations like Scudo, a fractional unit for everyday gold transactions, further democratize access, making precious metals practical for payments amid soaring gold prices.
Tether’s foray into artificial intelligence and data sectors adds another layer of resilience.
The company has committed over $1 billion to AI robotics firm Neura and invested heavily in decentralized computing and data infrastructure.
These ventures align with Ardoino’s vision of peer-to-peer alternatives to traditional cloud services, potentially disrupting centralized giants.
In fintech, Tether explores renewable energy , bitcoin mining, and even agricultural tech, creating a multifaceted portfolio that generates diverse revenue streams.
Projections suggest 2026 profits could top $10 billion, fueled by these expansions.
Central to Tether’s ecosystem integration is its ties with web3 participants.
A standout relationship is with Anchorage Digital , a federally regulated crypto bank.
Tether recently invested $100 million in equity, valuing Anchorage at $4.2 billion and deepening their collaboration.
Anchorage issues Tether’s new U.S.-compliant stablecoin, USA₮, enabling institutional adoption under strict regulatory frameworks.
This partnership bridges offshore crypto with U.S. banking , addressing past opacity concerns.
Beyond Anchorage, Tether has poured $775 million into social media platform Rumble and $150 million into Gold.com , fostering tokenized assets and physical gold access.
Collaborations extend to royalty firms like Elemental Altus and Metalla, securing gold -linked cash flows outside traditional finance.
While critics highlight Tether’s history of regulatory scrutiny and money-laundering allegations, its transparency improvements—via regular attestations—and strategic shifts signal maturity.
Ardoino’s forecasts may seem a bit alarming, but they also appear to propel proactive diversification, making Tether more adaptable.
As stablecoins integrate into global finance, Tether ‘s balanced approach—blending caution with diversification into gold and AI focused initiatives—could redefine resilience in an unpredictable environment.
