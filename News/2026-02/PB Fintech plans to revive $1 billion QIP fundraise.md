# PB Fintech plans to revive $1 billion QIP fundraise

> [!info] 2026-02-08 · 1 упоминаний · 1 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: This Week in Fintech

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[This Week in Fintech] PB Fintechplanned to revive a $1 billion fundraise through qualified institutional placement after consulting shareholders and assessing market conditions.

PB Fintech, which runs the insurance marketplace Policybazaar, has withdrawn its plans to raise fresh funds via a Qualified Institutional Placement (QIP). In a regulatory filing , the insurtech company said its board meeting, scheduled for February 5, 2026, to discuss a potential QIP, had been “cancelled”.
 1. What was the QIP for? In a separate regulatory disclosure last week, PB Fintech said it was considering a QIP to pursue inorganic growth opportunities in domestic and international markets through strategic investments, acquisitions, or partnerships.
During the post-earnings call for Q3 FY26 , Yashish Dahiya, co-founder and Group CEO of PB Fintech, said that Policybazaar already has a 93% market share in insurance distribution in India. He said markets like the US and Europe are highly profitable but lack innovation, and that Policybazaar can take its India playbook to international markets.

 “If you look at certain markets. The US is, I think, 50 times bigger than the Indian market; Europe is 15 times bigger than the Indian market. We’ve been observing those markets for the last 20 years very, very closely. They are very profit-rich markets, but they have almost zero innovation. Almost zero. And, of course, they are crying for innovation. So, I do think we will add a lot of value. If we did not think so, we wouldn’t want to expand globally,” Dahiya said. 

Alok Bansal, Executive Vice Chairman at PB Fintech, highlighted that over the past few years, the company has moved deeper into the value chain through new initiatives such as PB Healthcare and PB Pay. “Those learnings potentially can be taken to other markets, whether it’s Southeast Asia, whether it’s in the Middle East, whether it’s in Europe,” Bansal said.
However, the announcement of global expansion triggered a stock sell-off, with investors dumping over 3.6 million shares of PB Fintech on February 3, 2026, against an average daily volume of 1.87 lakh in the past month, as per BSE data . The rout in the stock continued until February 5, 2026. On the same day, the insurtech company announced that its QIP plan had been shelved.
 2. Brokerages raise concerns over PB Fintech’s global ambitions: While the management defended its global expansion strategy on the earnings call, brokerages flagged several risks in the company’s QIP plans, according to local media reports.

 “When a company is growing its India revenues at over 40%, commands more than 90% market share in its core line of business, and accounts for close to 25% of the term and retail health insurance market, the investment narrative is usually built around deepening domestic dominance rather than geographic expansion,” Macquarie reportedly said . 

The brokerage went on to call PB Fintech’s potential capital raise for overseas expansion “unrelated diversification”, while also raising concerns about investor anxiety over the previous healthcare foray.
It is important to note that PB Fintech ventured into the healthcare segment last year with the incorporation of a new wholly owned subsidiary, PB Healthcare Services. At the time, Motilal Oswal said that the hospital venture is a “long-cycle investment”.
In its latest note, brokerage firm JM Financial also expressed concerns about the company’s international expansion plans, noting that management bandwidth is already heavily focused on the healthcare business. It added that funding overseas expansion at this stage could introduce volatility into the company’s operations.

 “With the company already sitting on an Rs 5,000 crore+ cash pile, the requirement of a QIP suggests a large acquisition, potentially resulting in 5-6% dilution. While management suggested the acquisition would be EPS-accretive, it would need to be at a significant valuation discount as Indian markets are unlikely to ascribe PB’s current trading multiple for the international entity,” JM Financial said. 
 
 
 
 
 
 

 3. PB Pay Gets Payment Aggregator Licence: In another regulatory filing last week, PB Fintech said the Reserve Bank of India has granted Certificate of Authorisation to its wholly owned subsidiary PB Pay, to operate as a payment aggregator (PA). However, it did not mention whether it will function as an online, offline or cross-border PA. For context, PB Pay was incorporated in March 2024 .
Over the past few months, a slew of fintech companies have secured PA licences from the RBI, particularly for facilitating cross-border payment transactions. This includes Juspay , Cashfree , Amazon Pay, BillDesk, BriskPe, PayU , and Razorpay, among others.
 4. PB Fintech eyes insurance MGA tag: During the post-earnings call, Dahiya also hinted at the possibility that PB Fintech could operate as managing general agent (MGA) once the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) notifies final guidelines.
MGAs, as a concept, did not exist in India. In December last year, the Centre passed the Sabka Bima Sabki Raksha (Amendment of Insurance Laws) Bill, 2025, which recognised MGAs as insurance intermediaries for the first time.
Sarbvir Singh, Joint Group CEO at PB Fintech, said the MGA model fits neatly into the insurtech company’s business model, noting that it would allow the firm to make decisions on both underwriting and claims.
On the financial front, PB Fintech’s consolidated net profit surged 165% year-on-year (YoY) to Rs 189 crore in Q3 FY26, while operating revenue jumped 37% YoY to Rs 1,771 crore.
