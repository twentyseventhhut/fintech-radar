Tensions between banks and crypto firms have intensified over this issue in recent months. Banking trade groups have pushed back against the GENIUS Act, a stablecoin framework that cleared Congress over the summer, despite the law prohibiting stablecoin issuers from paying interest directly to holders.
The legislation leaves room for third-party platforms, including exchanges like Coinbase, to offer rewards. Bank groups argue that a lack of clear limits could siphon deposits away, particularly hurting community banks, while some in the crypto industry accuse banks of trying to stifle competition.
The Blockchain Association will participate in the meeting, according to CEO Summer Mersinger. She stated that Congress has a clear opportunity to deliver durable, bipartisan rules that protect consumers, foster responsible innovation, and ensure the United States remains a global leader in next-generation financial and internet technology.
Washington renewed its push last year to pass legislation regulating the crypto industry at large. Over the summer, the House Financial Services Committee, House Agriculture Committee, and full House passed the CLARITY Act, which garnered Democratic support.
However, negotiations in the Senate have wobbled over the past few months. Two key committees, the Senate Agriculture Committee and Senate Banking Committee, need to amend and vote on their respective bills before they can go to the full Senate floor for a vote.
The Senate Banking Committee's hearing was pulled at the last minute earlier this month after Coinbase withdrew its support, citing concerns around tokenized equities and stablecoin rewards treatment. The Senate Agriculture Committee is now set to hold its hearing on Thursday, but the bill text put forward last week lacked Democratic support.
The White House has been pushing for quick passage of a crypto market structure bill. Last week, Patrick Witt, executive director of the President's Council of Advisors for Digital Assets, said that the U.S. needs to pass the bill promptly before it loses momentum under the current crypto-friendly administration, stating there will be a crypto market structure bill and that it is a question of when, not if.
