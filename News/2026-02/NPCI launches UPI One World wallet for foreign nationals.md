NPCI Launches 'UPI One World' Wallet for Foreign Delegates

 NPCI rolls out ‘UPI One World’ wallet for international visitors at India AI Impact Summit 2026. 

 Enables real-time payments across India without a local bank account or mobile number. 

 Wallet supports international cards, simplifies cashless transactions, and showcases India’s digital payment system. 

 The National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) has introduced its ‘ UPI One World’ wallet service for international delegates attending the India AI Impact Summit 2026 at Bharat Mandapam, New Delhi, from February 16-20.

 This pilot initiative allows visitors from over 40 countries to make real-time Person-to-Merchant (P2M) transactions across India without needing a local bank account or Indian mobile number.

 Authorized issuers at New Delhi International Airport and the NPCI Pavilion at the summit enable travelers to load the wallet using international debit or credit cards. Payments can be made by scanning standard UPI QR codes, with a per-transaction limit of Rs 25,000 and a monthly cap of Rs 50,000. Remaining balances at the end of the visit can be refunded to the original payment source in line with foreign exchange regulations .

 Sohini Rajola, Executive Director - Growth at NPCI, said, “UPI is the world’s largest real-time payment system. By extending this service to foreign guests, we provide a convenient way to experience India’s digital payments ecosystem and Made-in-India technology”.

 The launch highlights India’s growing digital infrastructure , with UPI processing 21.7 billion transactions in January 2026. NPCI aims to simplify cross-border payments, reduce the need for cash, and demonstrate India’s fintech leadership to global policymakers and technology leaders.
