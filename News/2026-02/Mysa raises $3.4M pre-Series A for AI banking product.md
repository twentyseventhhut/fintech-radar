## Indian fintech startup Mysa raises $3.4m pre-series A

Share

Add TIA as a preferred source

Indian fintech startup Mysa has raised US$3.4 million in a pre-series A funding round co-led by Blume Ventures and Piper Serica, bringing its total funding to US$6.2 million.

The company plans to use the new capital to enhance its AI features and banking products, including procurement, expense management, and corporate credit cards.

Based in Bengaluru, Mysa offers automation tools that integrate with legacy ERPs and banks to improve financial workflows for mid-sized companies.

Since launching last year, Mysa has processed over 1,500 crore rupee (US$163 million) in annualized transactions and connected with more than 15 banks, including Axis and HDFC.

The startup aims to serve over 1 million Indian mid-sized firms in the next five years, focusing on embedded financial services and automation.
