Former Nutmeg CEO leaves after JPMorgan rebrand

Matt Gatrell, a top executive at the digital wealth manager now known as JPMorgan Personal Investing, has stood down as a director

By Justin Cash

Monday, 16 February 2026 at 11:19

Share

Resize

The Wall Street giant is trying to bolster its Chase retail brand in the UK after a major acquisition Photo: Mike Kemp/Getty Images

Former Nutmeg chief executive officer Matt Gatrell has stepped down after JPMorgan’s takeover and rebrand of the digital wealth manager.

JPMorgan bought Nutmeg, one of the UK’s best-known robo-advisers, for £700m in 2021. In November last year, it axed the Nutmeg brand, launching JPMorgan Personal Investing in a bid to capture more of the UK wealth market alongside its nascent Chase banking offering in the country.
