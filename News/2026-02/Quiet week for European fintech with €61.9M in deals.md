BlackFin Tech Weekly — February 24th, 2026
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity.
Hello FinTech Friends,
Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry!
 Last week was somewhat quiet for European fintech. There were four deals in Europe, totaling €61.9m in disclosed funding, including two transactions in the UK, one in the Netherlands, and one in Germany. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 mea Platform , a UK-based AI-native insurance technology company automating operations across carriers, brokers and MGAs, has raised €42.4m in a growth equity investment led by Scottish Equity Partners (SEP) . 
 Stacks , a Netherlands-based finance automation platform helping teams automate the month-end close, has raised €19.5m in a Series A round led by Lightspeed . 
 Ubyx , a UK-based infrastructure provider enabling stablecoin payments between issuers and banks, has received an undisclosed strategic investment in a Venture Round led by AB Xelerate . 
 Let’s dive! 
 mea Platform 
 mea Platform , a UK-based AI-native insurance technology company automating operations across carriers, brokers and MGAs, has raised €42.4m in a growth equity investment led by Scottish Equity Partners (SEP) . Founded in 2021, mea is deployed across 21 countries and has processed over 400bn in gross written premium through its platform, delivering automation and measurable operational impact for customers globally. The company has been bootstrapped to date and is now in its fourth consecutive year of profitable growth; the new funding will support accelerated product development, deeper customer engagement, and continued expansion across (re)insurance operations.

 Stacks 
 Stacks , a Netherlands-based finance automation platform, has raised €19.5m in a Series A round led by Lightspeed , with participation from General Catalyst , EQT Ventures & Growth , and s16vc . Stacks is building an AI-native parallel ledger designed for autonomous agents to run the month-end close end-to-end, turning close workflows into real-time financial intelligence; the company says it already supports 30+ enterprise customers and is rolling out a reporting and analysis suite, including AI Flux Analysis. The funding will be used to accelerate product development and scale enterprise adoption as Stacks pushes toward an AI-native CFO toolchain. 
 Read more 
 Ubyx 
 Ubyx , a London, and New York City-based provider of clearing and acceptance infrastructure for tokenised deposits and regulated stablecoins, has received an undisclosed strategic investment in a venture round from AB Xelerate (Lead), Arab Bank’s fintech accelerator. The proceeds will support the build-out of a shared network enabling regulated financial institutions to issue, accept, and redeem regulated digital money at par value across multiple blockchains and jurisdictions, while maintaining regulatory and compliance standards, positioning Ubyx as an interoperability layer for production-grade stablecoin and tokenised deposit settlement.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 ClearBank is accelerating its European growth with the opening of a French branch, responding to strong demand for cloud-native agency banking and real-time payments across the continent’s fast-growing fintech ecosystem. To support expansion, ClearBank is restructuring its commercial organisation into three regional hubs: Northern Europe, Eastern Europe and Southern/Western Europe, enabling sharper market focus and deeper local engagement. The clearing bank has secured traction with over 30 clients across European markets since obtaining its EU banking licence, with its API-driven platform positioned to support fintechs and financial institutions.

 Swedish fintech giant Klarna reported a net loss of $26 million for Q4 2025, reversing a $40 million profit from the prior year and missing analyst forecasts of a $9.8 million loss, sending shares down 23% to a record low. CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski attributed the loss to the timing mismatch of fast growth, where costs are recognised upfront while revenue follows later. Quarterly revenue crossed $1 billion for the first time, up 38% year-on-year, slightly beating expectations. Klarna continues to lean on AI adoption to reduce headcount while raising average employee compensation by 60% since 2022, with Siemiatkowski noting future roles will focus on human relationships with merchants and consumers.

 eBay has partnered with open banking provider TrueLayer to launch Pay by Bank at checkout for UK buyers, enabling instant account-to-account payments authorised directly through customers’ banking apps. The integration removes card details and manual entry, with transactions authenticated using bank-grade security. Buyer credentials are neither shared with nor stored by eBay , while TrueLayer manages secure payment processing. eBay Ventures also made a strategic investment in TrueLayer , signalling deeper alignment as the marketplace diversifies payments alongside cards, BNPL, and wallets.

 Britain’s biggest banks will hold their first formal meeting to establish a national payments alternative to Visa and Mastercard , amid concerns that geopolitical tensions could disrupt US-owned infrastructure. The initiative, chaired by Barclays UK CEO Vim Maru , will bring together major lenders including Lloyds , NatWest , Nationwide and Santander UK to fund a new entity called DeliveryCo. Currently, 95% of UK card transactions run on Visa or Mastercard rails. The Bank of England will develop infrastructure blueprints, with a target go-live around 2030. The move mirrors EU efforts toward payment sovereignty following concerns that reliance on US networks poses systemic risk.
