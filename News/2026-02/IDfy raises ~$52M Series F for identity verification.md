## Indian online identity verification firm IDfy nets $53m series F

Share

Add TIA as a preferred source

Trust and regtech platformIDfy raised 476 crore rupees (US$53 million) in a series F funding round, which included both primary and secondary transactions.

The funding was led by Neo Asset Management’s Neo Secondaries Fund, with participation from existing investors such as Blume Ventures, Analog Capital, Elev8, IndiaMART, and Kae Capital.

The primary capital will be used for acquisitions, international expansion, and product development, while the secondary portion provides liquidity for early investors and employees.

Founded in 2011, IDfy offers services like digital onboarding, risk mitigation, and fraud detection, serving over 500 enterprise clients across more than 10 sectors in seven countries.

The company has raised over US$120 million to date, including a US$27 million round earlier involving Elev8, KB Investment, and Tenacity.

_🔗 Source: Entrackr_

## 🧠 Food for thought

Implications, context, and why it matters.

### IDfy’s FY25 profit looks stronger than the funding news implies

*   FY25 results look better than the funding update implies.
*   The update cites a Rs 1.6 crore net profit, while filings reviewed by Entrackr put FY25 net profit at Rs 7.8 crore 1.
*   FY24 ended with a Rs 8.8 crore net loss 1.
*   The numbers land as India’s data-privacy rules take shape under the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, which the company and investors describe as supportive for digital trust and compliance platforms 2.

### The TrustStack pitch moves IDfy beyond one-off enterprise tools

*   More enterprise teams want one integrated setup instead of stitching together several single-purpose compliance products.
*   Investors are backing the “TrustStack” approach, which brings digital onboarding, risk controls, and privacy governance into one product 2.
*   IDfy says the platform includes 150+ application programming interfaces (APIs) plus no-code workflows (tools that let business users build processes without writing software), covering KYC/KYB (Know Your Customer/Know Your Business) and background verification 34.
