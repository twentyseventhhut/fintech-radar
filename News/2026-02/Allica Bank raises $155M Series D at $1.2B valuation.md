# Allica Bank achieves unicorn status with $155m Series D

Allica, now valued at $1.2 billion, plans to leverage its funding to enhance its proprietary technology stack and embark on its first expansion beyond the UK.

[](https://www.fintechfutures.com/author/cameron-emanuel-burns)

Allica Bank, a UK-based digital business bank, has hit unicorn status following the completion of a $155 million Series D funding round.

The round, which values the company at $1.2 billion, was supported by Ventura Capital, GLG, and Sona AM, alongside existing investors TCV and Blue Owl, who have supported Allica since 2022 and 2021, respectively.

The digital challenger reveals that the "majority of the round consists of common equity, alongside a portion of new additional Tier 1 equity capital".

Founded in 2019, Allica Bank focuses on serving established businesses with 5-250 employees, offering business banking and savings accounts alongside lending solutions such as commercial mortgages, asset finance, and bridging finance. Additionally, the bank provides personal savings accounts for individual customers.

Since launching its lending services in 2020, Allica, led by CEO Richard Davies, claims to have lent £4 billion and secured £5 billion in customer deposits. Now serving over 30,000 small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs), representing around a reported 5% of the market, the bank is targeting 10% of the SMB finance market by 2028.

Related:ICYMI fintech funding round-up: Blnk, Trustap, Finovox, and more

Recent developments for Allica include its selection as one of the first banks and building societies to join the UK government's new scale-up initiative and its entry into the embedded finance market through the acquisition of London-based fintech Kriya in October 2025.

According to a company statement, the Series D funding will support Allica's "continued lending growth" while enabling deeper investment in its proprietary technology stack, including the use of AI to enhance SMB lending solutions. Moreover, the cash injection will fuel Allica's expansion beyond the UK for the first time.

To find out more about Allica's future plans, watch our recent video interview with CEO Richard Davies at FinovateEurope earlier this month.
