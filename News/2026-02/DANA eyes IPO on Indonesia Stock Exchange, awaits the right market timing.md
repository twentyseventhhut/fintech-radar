DANA eyes IPO on Indonesia Stock Exchange, awaits right market timing
 
 
 
 

 Published on 23/02/2026 GMT+7 
 

 Reading time 3 minutes 
 

 Author: Julian Isaac 
 

 Editor: Imanuddin Razak 
 
Published on 23/02/2026 GMT+7 Reading time 3 minutes Julian Isaac Imanuddin Razak
Digital wallet operator PT Espay Debit Indonesia Koe, known under the brand DANA Indonesia, is reportedly preparing for an initial public offering (IPO) on the Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX).
The company is said to be seriously finalizing preparations for a public listing. According to a report by Ajaib Sekuritas, DANA has been closely studying the IPO experiences of other digital companies that went public earlier, including PT Bukalapak.com (BUKA) and PT GoTo Gojek Tokopedia (GOTO).
DANA CEO and Co-founder Vince Iswara indicated that the IPO plan would proceed once DANA’s full range of products is considered fully optimized. The company is also drawing lessons from other digital platforms’ listing processes to better anticipate capital market dynamics and challenges.
DANA’s Communications Director, Olavina Harahap, confirmed that an IPO remains part of the company’s long-term strategy, although timing remains flexible.
“IPO is currently still part of our long-term strategy and will be executed at the right time. Of course, it will be adjusted to market and company conditions,” Olavina told Katadata on Friday, February 20, 2026.
She added that DANA’s current priority is to ensure optimal user experience and service quality, while continuing to enhance security and user convenience.
“Our main objectiveis that users can maximize our services and ultimately help improve their financial well-being,” she said.
 Major investors 
In its early years, DANA received financial backing from PT Elang Sejahtera Mandiri (EAM), part of the Emtek Group, alongside China’s Alibaba Group. Emtek has since divested its stake in the company.
In 2022, DANA announced strategic investments from conglomerate Sinar Mas and e-commerce giant Lazada Group. Through its subsidiary PT Dian Swastatika Sentosa, Sinar Mas invested US$25 million (around Rp350 billion) in DANA. Dian Swastatika operates across sectors including power generation, coal, gold, chemicals and internet services.
On the technology side, DANA has partnered with Ant International, which manages Alipay+, forming a core foundation of DANA’s digital payment system since its launch.
 Digital payments journey 
Although DANA was officially launched in 2018, its founder Vince Iswara has been active in digital payments since 2008. A graduate of Waseda University’s MBA program in Strategic Management (class of 2002), Iswara founded digital payment firm INDOMOG in 2008 to facilitate transactions for gamers.
He led INDOMOG until 2015 before becoming Director and Country Lead of Alipay Indonesia from 2015 to 2017. A year later, DANA was established, leveraging technological collaboration with Alipay to tap into Indonesia’s growing electronic money market.
With strong investor backing, technological partnerships and a rapidly expanding digital economy, DANA is preparing for a potential debut on Indonesia’s stock exchange.
