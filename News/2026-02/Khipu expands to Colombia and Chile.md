# Khipu expands to Colombia and Chile

[Connecting the Dots in Fintech] 🌎 Peruvian FinTech Khipu expands to Colombia and Chile. The company aims to address a critical operational challenge for businesses across Latin America. This move signals Khipu's ambition to become a regional leader in the rapidly evolving financial technology sector.

[This Week in Fintech] 🇵🇪Khipu, the Peruvian expense management startup, outlined its 2026 expansion plans, targeting 250 corporate clients and entry into Chile and Colombia. The company focuses on automating expense reporting for mid- and large-sized enterprises seeking to reduce inefficiencies from manual processes.

Khipu, Fintech peruana de rendición de gastos, se expandirá hacia Colombia y Chile este año
El 2025 fue un año de consolidación para la startup peruana Khipu , que continúa escalando sus servicios de simplificación de procesos financieros. Gabriel Proaño Noel, CEO & cofounder de la firma, remarca que la startup, que registró su primera ronda de financiamiento por US$150.000 el año anterior, busca ser un referente en su sector a nivel regional, con la mirada puesta en la expansión regional y global.
“Comenzamos con las pequeñas empresas, pero entendemos que nuestro producto va para las medianas y grandes. Son estas las que tienen la necesidad y verán el ahorro”, precisa a Día1 el ejecutivo.
 Rendición de gastos: solución a un problema 
 Khipu ofrece en el mercado local el producto de rendición de gastos a diversas compañías ; un aspecto que la Fintech ha identificado como un “punto de dolor” y un “problema operativo” en el sector corporativo, al ser un proceso que implica excesividad de carga manual.
Según la startup, de hecho, las compañías asumen sobrecostos de entre 5% y 10% de su facturación anual para resolver ‘inhouse’ la rendición de cuentas.
“En todo este camino hay muchos huecos y distintas personas de una compañía gastan tiempo en actividades no relacionadas a hacer crecer la empresa, sino en cumplir un proceso interno que es súper burocrático”, explica Proaño. “Si bien el servicio que otorgamos puede verse como algo sencillo o no tan necesario, realmente estamos ayudando a toda la empresa, desde el mismo contador hasta el mismo ejecutivo comercial. Es ahí donde está el valor”, añade.
Dentro de los objetivos para sus operaciones este año, Khipu apunta a llegar a 250 clientes y a escalar el modelo orgánico de operaciones que tienen a la fecha.“Nos hemos concentrado el año pasado en desarrollar mucho con los clientes para entender las necesidades del mercado. Con la meta de llegar a los 250 clientes, apuntamos a concretar los US$100 mil mensuales de facturación para diciembre”, indica el ejecutivo. 
 Plan de Expansión 
El plan de expansión de Khipu para este 2026 también apunta a Chile y Colombia; dos mercados en los que, según Proaño, están dadas las condiciones para posicionarse y competir con otros ‘players’ del mercado.
“Va a ser primero Chile y luego Colombia. Nos toca a nosotros ser el referente de este sector en el Perú . El ecosistema peruano de startups está en crecimiento, pero ha sido bien lento en comparación a nuestros vecinos. Queremos marcar una diferencia aquí. Y creo, como te comento, que hay interés tanto del mercado como de las empresas y el ecosistema”, agrega.
 Khipu busca simplificar la rendición de gastos de las empresas; un proceso por el que las compañías asumen sobrecostos de entre 5% y 10% de su facturación anual.
La propuesta de la startup, cabe resaltar, combina la digitalización de la información de facturas de todo tipo a través de la inteligencia artificial y del reconocimiento óptico de caracteres (OCR), con el objetivo de agilizar los procesos de registro de documentos contables.
Según Proaño, de avanzar con los hitos previstos para los primeros seis meses del año, la firma buscaría abrir una nueva ronda de financiamiento de entre US$500.000 y US$1 millón durante el segundo semestre. 
“Tenemos una mirada global. Sabemos que lo que ofrecemos es una necesidad a nivel de Latinoamérica, y eso es lo que estamos buscando”, puntualiza.

 In the last decade, the Fintech ecosystem in Latin America has experienced unprecedented growth. In particular, this story has its roots in Chile, where one of the first actors of this revolution was born: Khipu .
Founded by Roberto Opazo and Emilio Davis , Khipu celebrates its tenth anniversary , marking an important resistance and pivot in the technological financial world of the southernmost country in the world.

From the very beginning, Khipu stood out for its approach. “ We started as a solution that automated the generation of transfers,” recalls Opazo. in an initiative that was born out of a personal need, when Opazo, being a course delegate at his children’s school, was looking for an efficient way to collect fees.
Over time, what started as a solution to an everyday problem, quickly morphed into a platform that challenged (and certainly upset) industry giants like Transbank.
He was once invited to a meeting with the acquiring company – which had a monopoly period – in which Opazo felt intimidated: “There were 12 lawyers and I would tell you that was bullying,” he recalls.
Fortunately, that show of force didn’t go any further, “nothing like that happened because our legal preparation had been well done and therefore we never had a legal problem.”

 Skepticism of the new 
Khpu’s journey was not without its challenges. In its early years, the company faced skepticism and resistance from the banking sector and potential customers. “There was a perception that banks were in total control and that a fintech like ours would not be able to operate without their permission,” says Opazo.
However, there were others who gave them good feedback and urged them to continue. With that, Khipu not only managed to operate, but also drove a change in the mindset of the sector, proving that collaboration between banks and Fintechs could be mutually beneficial.
One of the most significant milestones in Khipu’s history was the addition of Cinemark as its first major customer. “It was a pivotal moment,” Opazo says. “Cinemark gave us the opportunity to prove that our solution was not only viable, but also superior in many ways, especially in mobile adaptability.” 
 The Next Evolution: Open Finance 
Over the past few years, Khipu embraced the concept of Open Finance , evolving from a payments platform to an end-to-end solution that encompasses data, payments, and more.
“Our vision has always been to open up the financial world to make it more accessible and equitable” Roberto Opazo, Executive Director of Khipu
While they have ambitious plans for Latam, they try to keep their feet firmly on the ground. “At the moment, Khipu is in a stage of consolidation and expansion. We are focused on strengthening our presence in Chile and bringing our solution to more countries in Latin America,” says its co-founder.
In this sense, Opazo clarifies that “Argentina elected us. It happened to us that a couple of entrepreneurs from Argentina arrived super motivated with the idea of marketing Khipu in Argentina and ended up saying ‘yes, you get excited’,” he recalls.
That’s where a question was born that accompanies them when they face the possibility of other markets.” Does this country require this service? And we think so. There’s a space for us. It’s not that we think we’re going to be the new monopoly in that country. There are different price-quality combinations, and we offer one. And certain types of businesses find us very convenient.” explains the Avonni Award winner.

 And the next level? 
With the start of this second decade, Khipu has a number of challenges to stay in an ever-growing ecosystem of solutions. Faced with the question of whether the startup can continue to grow for another 10 years, the answer from its co-founder is certain: yes
“Without a doubt, Kipu does, but led by me or by Emilio and me? I don’t think so. I believe that at some point within the next five years, Kipu should move to a new stage in its life cycle and in that new stage aspire to be a much larger company without its founding partners, but acquiring a degree of maturity that makes it permanent over time.” Roberto Opazo, co-founder of Khipu
Only time will tell.
