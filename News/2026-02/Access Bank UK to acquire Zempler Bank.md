# SME-focused Zempler Bank set to be acquired by The Access Bank UK

"The transaction has now received regulatory approval and is expected to complete in the coming weeks," the two companies say in a joint statement sent to FinTech Futures.

The Access Bank UK,the UK subsidiary of Nigeria's Access Bank, has reached an agreement to acquire London-based challenger Zempler Bank.

In a joint statement sent to FinTech Futures, both Zempler and The Access Bank UK confirmed: "The Access Bank UK Limited has agreed to acquire a 100% shareholding in Zempler Bank Limited. The transaction has now received regulatory approval and is expected to complete in the coming weeks."

The financial details of the deal have not been disclosed, but City AM reports that the deal will see The Access Bank UK pay approximately £3.45 per Zempler Bank share.

Founded in 2005, Zempler initially provided prepaid payment services for consumers before shifting its focus to small businesses and start-ups. It now offers products such as current accounts, overdrafts, and credit cards for SMEs, along with business tools for spending insights, accounting, invoice generation, cashback, and saving pots.

Related:Barclays to buy UK money app GoHenry

The lender, originally known as CashPlus, has been led by CEO Rich Wagner since its inception and landed its full banking licence in 2021. It claims to have served over 650,000 businesses and employs more than 350 staff.

The Access Bank UK, which specialises in asset management, commercial banking, and trade finance for UK-based clients, has yet to announce its plans for Zempler following the acquisition.

According to its website, the UK subsidiary states that its objective is to "grow the international business of the Access Bank Group".The Lagos-based parent company, which manages $28.8 billion in assets, operates over 700 branches across Africa, with additional locations in Dubai, Paris, Hong Kong, and London.
