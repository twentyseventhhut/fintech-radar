## SG fintech startup Lyte raises $4.2m

Share

Add TIA as a preferred source

Singapore-based fintech platform Lyte has raised SG$5.3 million (US$4.2 million) in funding, with SG$5 million (US$3.9 million) contributed by Lim Chap Huat, co-founder and chairman of Soilbuild Group.

The remaining amount came from Ho Hin Wah, CIO of Genedant Capital, and Great Noble International II Limited.

Lyte offers financial tools aimed at freelancers, solopreneurs, and sales workers.

_🔗 Source: Alternatives.pe_

## 🧠 Food for thought

Implications, context, and why it matters.

### Lyte’s payments-advance business is financed through a separate private debt fund

*   The SGD 5.3 million is an equity investment in Singapore-based fintech platform LYTE, while its payments-advance line uses a different funding setup.
*   LYTE’s payments-advance activity runs on the Azure-Lyte Fund, a private debt vehicle that provides financing to the LYTE group of companies 1.
*   The setup connects with the lead investment from Lim Chap Huat, co-founder and chairman of Soilbuild Group, since LytePay began with exclusive service agreements with Singapore’s five largest property agencies 1.
*   The product “Advance” lets commission-based workers, including real estate agents, receive commissions in as fast as one business day, though timing can vary by agency 2.

### This approach may steadier income for freelancers

*   By reducing income swings, LYTE’s approach could support longer-term freelance work.
*   It turns uneven commission timing into a steadier stream, which reduces a common strain of self-employment.
*   LYTE has advanced over SGD 200 million since 2018 across 20,000 transactions, and it describes a playbook for expansion into other freelance sectors 1.
*   For investors, the Azure-Lyte Fund targets a return of over 6% per annum 1.
