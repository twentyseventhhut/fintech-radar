SAN FRANCISCO, CA — The San Francisco Giants and Airwallex , a leading global financial platform for modern businesses, today announced a multi-year partnership, bringing together two organizations with a shared commitment to innovation and the city of San Francisco. As part of the agreement, Airwallex will become the Giants’ official jersey patch partner, debuting across Giants uniforms this season, beginning with Spring Training. Airwallex will also serve as the Giants’ Official Financial Software Partner.
Through this partnership, the Giants and Airwallex will highlight the role of global connection — both in business and in sport. Baseball has long been a game that brings people, cultures, and generations together, and the Giants continue to support Major League Baseball’s ongoing efforts to grow the game internationally. In parallel, Airwallex enables companies, customers, and partners to operate internationally through modern financial tools including global payment processing and spend management.
The San Francisco Giants are tapping Airwallex to bridge the gap between their local roots and international reach, ensuring their financial infrastructure is fast, borderless, and efficient. By integrating a suite of Airwallex products—including corporate cards, expense management, and payment processing—the Giants are streamlining everything from executive travel and expense spend to high-value transactions like suite sales and Giants Enterprises events.
Founded in Australia in 2015, Airwallex has expanded rapidly in the U.S. — and in the Bay Area, in particular. To anchor their growth, Airwallex recently established its global headquarters in San Francisco with plans to double its San Francisco headcount over the next three years.
“This partnership evolved in a shared commitment to San Francisco, a passion for innovation, and a vision to connect people on both a local and global scale,” said Larry Baer, President and CEO of the San Francisco Giants. “Airwallex’s long-term loyalty to the city makes them a natural jersey patch partner, and we’re thrilled to welcome them to the Giants family.”
“San Francisco is our home in the U.S., and the Giants are one of the city’s most iconic institutions,” said Jack Zhang, Co-Founder and CEO of Airwallex. “We’ve been building here quietly for years, but this partnership marks our bold commitment to the city’s future. Featuring Airwallex on the Giants’ jersey is a meaningful way to further establish our presence here, connect with the community, and share our story with a wider audience.”
Additional partnership details regarding product integrations, activations, programming, and community initiatives will be announced over time.

 About San Francisco Giants 
 One of the oldest teams in Major League Baseball, the 142-year-old franchise moved to San Francisco from New York in 1958. After playing a total of 42 years in Seals Stadium and Candlestick Park, the team moved to the privately constructed, downtown ballpark on the corner of 3rd and King in 2000. The organization is widely recognized for its innovative business practices and baseball excellence having been named in the past decade the Sports Organization of the Year by Street & Smith’s Sports Business Journal, Organization of the Year by Baseball America and ESPN’s Sports Humanitarian Team of the Year. Oracle Park is also the only ballpark in the country to have earned Silver, Gold and Platinum LEED certification for an existing building. 
 Since opening its gates, Oracle Park has become internationally renowned as a premier venue in the world of both sports and entertainment. On the diamond, more than 66 million spectators have witnessed countless magical moments, including three World Series Championships (2010, 2012 & 2014), the raising of four National League Pennants and eight playoff appearances. The ballpark has also hosted some of music’s biggest acts, including Dead & Company, Lady Gaga, Beyoncé & Jay-Z, Ed Sheeran, the Rolling Stones, the Eagles, Bruce Springsteen and the E-Street Band, Green Day and Billy Joel. 
 About Airwallex 
 Airwallex is a leading global financial platform for modern businesses. We are building the future of global banking for a borderless, real-time, intelligent economy. 
 More than 200,000 companies worldwide – from startups to public enterprises – use Airwallex to manage their global banking and financial operations, or to build and monetize their own financial products using Airwallex infrastructure. 
 Founded in Melbourne in 2015, Airwallex holds 80 licenses across North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia-Pacific, forming one of the most comprehensive financial infrastructures in the world. This regulated backbone powers Airwallex products at global scale, including: payment acceptance, billing, global accounts, corporate cards, and spend management. 
 The company is co-headquartered in San Francisco and Singapore with over 2,000 employees across 26 offices. Learn more at www.airwallex.com .

SCOTTSDALE -- The Giants will have a new manager in 2026. They'll also have a new patch on their jerseys.

The organization announced Wednesday that Airwallex signed a multi-year agreement to be its official jersey patch partner. Founded in Australia a decade ago and now based in San Francisco, Airwallex is a global financial platform.

"This partnership evolved in a shared commitment to San Francisco, a passion for innovation, and a vision to connect people on both a local and global scale," Giants president and CEO Larry Baer said in a press release. "Airwallex's long-term loyalty to the city makes them a natural jersey patch partner, and we're thrilled to welcome them to the Giants family."

Airwallex is the third company to be represented on Giants jerseys since patches were added in 2023. They originally partnered with Cruise, but the autonomous vehicle company had its permits suspended shortly after the splashy announcement. The Giants then switched to Chevy and wore black and white patches representing the company.

The Airwallex patches are also black with white lettering and have the company's logo on them.
