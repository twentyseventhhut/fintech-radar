BlackFin Tech Weekly — February 17th, 2026
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity.
Hello FinTech Friends,
Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry!
 Over the last week, there were twelve fintech deals in Europe, raising a total of €141.1 million , including four transactions in the UK, three in Switzerland, two in Ireland, one in France, one in Germany and one in Sweden. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Lassie , a Stockholm -based prevention-first pet insurance platform combining AI-driven automation with proactive pet care, has raised $75m in a Series C round led by Balderton Capital and Felix Capital . 
 Circit , an Ireland -based confirmation and financial data verification platform building an independent verification layer for modern audit, has secured $22m in growth equity funding in a round led by Ten Coves Capital . 
 Tembo , a UK-based digital savings and mortgage platform helping first-time buyers onto the property ladder, has raised more than £15m in a funding round led by Gresham House Ventures . 
 Let’s dive! 
 Lassie 
 Lassie , a Stockholm -based prevention-first pet insurance platform combining insurance with proactive pet care through an AI-powered app, has raised $75m in a Series C round. The round was led by Balderton Capital and Felix Capital , with participation from Inventure , Passion Capital and Stena Sessan . The funding will support Lassie’s expansion across Europe, further investment in AI-driven claims automation and preventive health capabilities, and the development of a broader pet care ecosystem beyond traditional insurance.

 Circit 
 Circit , an Ireland -based confirmation and financial data verification platform, has secured $22m in growth equity funding to scale its independent verification network and strengthen its AI-enabled audit infrastructure. The round was led by Ten Coves Capital , with participation from existing investors Aquiline and MiddleGame Ventures . The proceeds will be used to invest in product innovation, expand AI-powered workflows and bank API connectivity, and accelerate go-to-market efforts, particularly in the US, as the company positions itself as the independent verification layer underpinning modern, technology-driven audit processes.

 Tembo 
 Tembo , a UK -based digital savings and mortgage platform focused on helping first-time buyers access home ownership, has secured more than £15m in a new funding round . The round was led by Gresham House Ventures , with participation from McPike Family Office — the largest shareholder in Starling Bank — alongside existing investors including Aviva Investors , Goodwater Capital , Love Ventures , Ascension and British Business Bank . The funding will support the expansion of Tembo’s savings and mortgage products as it aims to simplify affordability challenges and broaden access to home ownership in the UK.

 In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 NatWest Group has agreed to acquire UK wealth manager Evelyn Partners in an all-cash deal valued at €3.1b, adding ~€80bn of assets under management and ~2,400 employees. The transaction will be integrated into NatWest’s Private Banking & Wealth Management division, and is intended to accelerate NatWest’s push into higher-margin, fee-based wealth and financial planning for affluent customers. NatWest expects ~€115m of annual cost synergies; the deal will be funded from existing resources and is subject to customary regulatory approvals, with closing targeted for summer 2026. 
 Read more 
 Admiral Group has reached an agreement to acquire Flock , a UK-based digital commercial fleet insurer, valuing Flock’s equity at €92m. The deal deepens Admiral’s move into commercial motor by bringing in Flock’s AI-driven, telemetry-based underwriting platform, which uses proprietary risk models trained on hundreds of millions of miles of driving data to reward safer fleets with better pricing and real-time safety insights. Flock will become Admiral’s telemetry fleet insurance proposition, with its team and technology integrated into Admiral’s fleet offering; Flock CEO Ed Leon Klinger will join Admiral Pioneer’s leadership team, following a partnership between Admiral Pioneer and Flock that began in 2024. The transaction is expected to complete in Q2 2026, funded through existing resources and credit facilities, and remains subject to regulatory approval.

 Model ML has acquired Captide (terms undisclosed) to strengthen its AI workflow platform with a dedicated financial disclosure data layer that converts complex corporate filings into LLM-ready, citable structured data. Captide’s API ingests sources such as SEC filings and earnings calls, and already spans 2.5m+ documents supporting 1bn embeddings, enabling Model ML’s AI agents to more reliably retrieve, reason over, and cite underlying source material in regulated financial workflows. The acquisition follows Model ML’s recent €63m fundraise and builds on earlier expansion steps, as the company pushes toward an end-to-end “AI operating system” for finance with stronger traceability and compliance.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 The UK’s Financial Conduct Authority confirmed that new rules for Buy Now Pay Later lenders will take effect from 15 July 2026, bringing the sector under FCA regulation for the first time. Under the new framework, lenders must provide clear upfront details on payments, conduct affordability checks, and offer support to consumers in difficulty. Borrowers will also gain access to the Financial Ombudsman Service for dispute resolution. Firms can register for the temporary permissions regime between 15 May and 1 July 2026, with six months to apply for full FCA authorisation. An estimated 20% of UK consumers used BNPL in the year to May 2024, with market growth exceeding £13 billion.

 Martina Weimert , CEO of the European Payments Initiative (EPI) , urged European businesses to “urgently” adopt payment schemes like Wero to reduce dependence on Visa and Mastercard . Wero has gained nearly 49 million customers since its 2024 launch, backed by Deutsche Bank and BNP Paribas . The push intensifies amid rising US-Europe tensions under President Trump , including tariff threats. Visa and Mastercard dominate the EU card market, while 13 member states lack national alternatives. The ECB is developing ways to settle blockchain payments in central bank euros to compete with US dollar stablecoins like USDC and USDT . The digital euro CBDC project aims to achieve similar sovereignty goals.

 Adyen has launched Personalize , a new product within its Adyen Uplift payment optimisation suite that adapts checkout experiences based on individual shopper behaviour using dynamic identification technology. The feature recognises returning customers and adjusts payment method displays, security protocols, and checkout flows in real time. Early adoption data shows businesses can improve conversion rates by up to 6% and reduce transaction costs by up to 3% using Personalize . Adyen Uplift , launched in January 2025, has helped over 6,500 businesses reduce payment costs by 9.4% and decrease false positive transaction blocks by 42% on average.

 Goldman Sachs has partnered with Anthropic to develop autonomous AI agents for internal operations, with embedded Anthropic engineers working at the bank for six months on Claude -based solutions. The agents, launching soon, will handle trade accounting and client onboarding/vetting. CIO Marco Argenti describes them as “digital co-workers” for complex, process-intensive tasks, with unexpected success beyond initial coding applications. CEO David Solomon has outlined a multiyear plan to reorganise the bank around generative AI, with the focus on “injecting capacity” to improve client experience rather than replacing jobs.
