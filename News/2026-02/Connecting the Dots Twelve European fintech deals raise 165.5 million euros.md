BlackFin Tech Weekly — February 9th, 2026
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity
Hello FinTech Friends,
Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry!
 Over the last week, there were twelve fintech deals in Europe, raising a total of €165.5 million , including seven transactions in the UK, two in Sweden, one in the Netherlands, one in Denmark and one in Lithuania. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Artificial , a UK-based insurtech using AI to streamline underwriting and specialty insurance placement for brokers and carriers, has raised €38.2m in a Series B round led by CommerzVentures . 
 Duna , a Netherlands-based regtech offering an AI-native business identity and onboarding/KYC platform for regulated companies, has raised € 30m in a Series A round led by CapitalG . 
 Bound , a UK-based investment banking platform providing self-serve FX hedging and currency management for SMEs, has raised €20.8m in a Series A round led by AlbionVC . 
 Let’s dive! 
 Artificial 
 Artificial , headquartered in the UK , is an AI-powered platform streamlining underwriting and specialty insurance placement for brokers and carriers. The company has raised €38.2m in a Series B round led by CommerzVentures , with participation from Move Capital Fund I , alongside existing investors 6 Degrees Capital , Augmentum Fintech , Force Over Mass , and TrueSight Ventures . The proceeds will be used to grow the team, invest further in the technology, and expand into new markets as the company supports larger brokers and carriers modernizing their operations.

 Duna 
 Duna , headquartered in the Netherlands , is an AI-native business identity platform for regulated companies, providing onboarding and KYC workflows and aiming to build a “digital passport” for every business with shareable identity and one-click onboarding. The company has raised €30m in a Series A round led by CapitalG , with continued participation from Index Ventures . The proceeds will be used to double down on R&D and expand enterprise and AI capabilities as the company scales traction with large financial institutions and platforms. 
 Read more 
 Bound 
 Bound , headquartered in the UK , is a self-serve FX hedging and currency management platform helping SMEs automate currency risk management, protect margins, and manage international cash flows. The company has raised €20.8m in a Series A round led by AlbionVC , with participation from Notion , Album VC , 1818 VC , Moonfire , Gohub Ventures and Valar . The proceeds will be used to grow the team, reach new customers, and accelerate product development (more automation and smarter workflows) as the company scales its platform across additional use cases and verticals.

 In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 Zurich Insurance Group has agreed in principle with Beazley on the key financial terms of a potential all-cash acquisition, valuing the specialist insurer at up to approximately £8 billion. The revised proposal offers total consideration of up to 1,335 pence per share, comprising 1,310 pence in cash plus permitted FY2025 dividends of up to 25 pence. Following earlier rejected bids, Beazley’s board has indicated it would be inclined to recommend the offer, subject to final terms and documentation, while Zurich is expected to begin confirmatory due diligence. If completed, the transaction would create a leading global specialty insurance platform with around $15 billion in gross written premiums and a strong Lloyd’s of London presence.

 Arcesium has acquired Stockholm-based portfolio and order management systems provider Limina , creating a unified front-to-back investment platform for asset managers, hedge funds and asset owners. The transaction integrates Limina’s cloud-native P/OMS capabilities with Arcesium’s middle- and back-office solutions, aiming to eliminate fragmented legacy systems and deliver a single, data-driven operating model across the investment lifecycle. The full Limina team will join Arcesium , with CEO Kristoffer Fürst taking on a senior product role, as the group expands its front-office offering and strengthens its European footprint. The acquisition supports Arcesium’s global growth strategy and follows recent international expansion in Europe and Asia.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 Chase has launched Chase Protect , its first UK insurance product priced at £12.50/month, bundling worldwide travel insurance, mobile phone protection, and UK breakdown cover into the Chase app. The product is delivered through Collinson Insurance (travel), Assurant (mobile), and the AA (breakdown), with customers managing the full policy lifecycle including claims within the app. The launch advances Chase ‘s UK super-app ambitions, competing directly with traditional packaged bank accounts. Chase plans to continue to layer more services onto its current account offering.

 BBVA has joined Qivalis , a consortium of 12 major European banks including BNP Paribas , ING , and UniCredit , building a regulated euro-pegged stablecoin under the EU’s MiCA framework. Headquartered in Amsterdam , Qivalis is seeking an EMI licence from the Dutch central bank with a commercial launch targeted for H2 2026 to enable faster cross-border payments and on-chain settlement. The initiative targets a stablecoin market dominated by USD-pegged tokens, where euro-denominated tokens hold less than $1bn market cap vs. over $300bn globally.

 PayPal has appointed Enrique Lores , former HP Inc. CEO and PayPal board chair since July 2024, as its new President and CEO effective March 1, 2026, replacing Alex Chriss after 2.5 years. The board cited a pace of execution “not in line with expectations.” Q4 results missed consensus, with revenue of $8.67bn (+4% YoY) and EPS of $1.23 vs. analyst estimates of $1.29. PayPal also projected full-year 2026 adjusted profit to decline in the low-single digits, surprising investors who expected growth. Shares dropped as much as 19% intraday, the largest decline in over four years. CFO Jamie Miller will serve as interim CEO until Lores formally takes over.

 Ripple received full authorisation as an Electronic Money Institution from Luxembourg’s CSSF on 2 February 2026, allowing it to accelerate the expansion of Ripple Payments across the EU via passporting rights under single market rules. The licence follows Ripple ’s January 2026 EMI and cryptoasset registration from the UK’s FCA , marking a rapid build-out of its regulated European footprint. Ripple Payments currently reaches over 90% of daily FX markets with $95bn+ in processed volume. Beyond payments, Ripple recently closed a $1.25bn acquisition of prime broker Hidden Road (clearing $3trn annually) and secured a $500m strategic investment at a $40bn valuation in November 2025.
