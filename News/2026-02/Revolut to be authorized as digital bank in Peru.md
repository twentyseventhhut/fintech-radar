Digital bank Revolut has reached a significant milestone in its Latin American expansion, securing an Organisation Authorisation from Peru’s Superintendency of Banking, Insurance and AFP (SBS). Announced on 1 April 2026, this approval represents a vital early phase in the company’s effort to establish a fully regulated digital bank in the South American country.
With this license, Revolut is positioned to become Peru’s first entirely digital bank , marking a major boost for the nation’s growing fintech sector.
The Organisation Authorisation enables Revolut to officially register as a banking entity within Peru.
It serves as the foundational step before the firm advances to a comprehensive supervisory review, which will determine whether it receives the Functional Authorisation needed for full-scale operations.
Once that final clearance arrives, the company plans to introduce a broad array of digital banking tools tailored to local customers.
Revolut, which serves more than 70 million users worldwide, views this development as confirmation of its readiness to deliver innovative financial services.
The authorization highlights the effectiveness of Peru’s regulatory framework and the capabilities of the firm’s local leadership.
By combining its advanced global technology platform with region-specific knowledge, Revolut aims to provide more competitive, transparent, and customer-focused financial solutions that address the needs of Peru’s increasingly tech-oriented population.
This latest progress strengthens Revolut’s strategic push across Latin America. Peru now joins Brazil, Mexico , Colombia, and Argentina as one of the company’s core markets in the region.
The expansion supports Revolut’s wider objective of entering 100 countries globally, reflecting its commitment to building a truly international financial ecosystem.
In the coming months, Revolut will focus on growing its Peruvian workforce and building the necessary local infrastructure.
These efforts will ensure that its upcoming product lineup aligns closely with the country’s economic realities and customer preferences.
The planned services are designed to give users greater visibility and control over their money, while promoting higher standards of transparency.
 Julien Labrot , CEO of Revolut Peru, welcomed the news:
 “Securing our Organisation License is a testament to the efficient regulatory environment in Peru and our team’s dedication to meeting the highest compliance standards. The entry of a major digital player like Revolut is expected to intensify competition in Peru’s financial services industry and drive greater inclusion.” 
By offering modern, mobile-first banking options, the company could help modernise payments and enable more consumers to manage their finances effectively in an evolving digital economy.
As it prepares for full launch, Revolut continues to invest in local talent and systems.
This methodical approach underscores the firm’s long-term vision for sustainable growth in Peru and across Latin America .
For consumers, the development signals the arrival of fresh, high-value alternatives in a market hungry for innovation. With regulatory hurdles gradually clearing, Revolut’s Peruvian strategy is set to open new opportunities for both the company and the millions of potential users it hopes to serve.
