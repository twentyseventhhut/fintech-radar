# Banca Sella takes full control of fintech Hype for €85m

Banca Sella is buying out partner Illimity Bank's 50% stake to gain complete control of the 1.9 million-customer digital banking platform.

Italy's Banca Sella has agreed to acquire full control of Milan-based fintech Hype for €85 million, subject to Bank of Italy approval.

Originally founded under Banca Sella in 2015, Hype is an electronic money institution with 1.9 million customers. The platform offers payments, cryptocurrency services, savings, credit, and insurance through a mobile app. In 2020, Hype became a joint venture when Italian neobank Illimity acquired a 50% stake, while Banca Sella purchased a 10% share in Illimity.

This latest transaction involves Banca Sella and its parent company, Banca Sella Holding, purchasing Illimity's 50% stake in Hype alongside the remaining 50% held by Banca Sella Holding, following procedures outlined in the joint venture's shareholder agreements.

Banca Sella expects the acquisition to strengthen its digital capabilities for younger customers by optimising technology investments and accelerating AI developments.

Hype will continue to operate as an "independent and distinct" entity, according to a statement from Banca Sella, and will be able to "rapidly expand its range of offerings and leverage the bank's branch network to meet its customers' evolving needs, while maintaining its distinctive user experience".
