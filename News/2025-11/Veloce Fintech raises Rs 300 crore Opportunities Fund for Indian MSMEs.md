Veloce Fintech Unveils Rs 300 Cr Opportunities Fund To Back MSMEs, Startups
 
 # Veloce Fintech 
 # Lemon Group 
 # Nirav Jogani 
 Veloce Fintech, part of the Lemon Group, has introduced its second SEBI-registered Category-II Alternative Investment Fund under the Veloce Opportunities strategy, with a target corpus of Rs 300 crore, including a green-shoe option. The fund has already secured over Rs 100 crore in commitments from family offices, ultra-HNIs, and business groups, with the first close happening at Rs 90 crore within days of the launch. 
 Speaking with BW Disrupt, Nirav Jogani, Founder, said the fund will focus on private credit and debt opportunities for MSMEs, startups, and late-stage real estate projects that struggle to access timely bank or NBFC financing. “A substantial portion of the fund is going to be invested in private credit, private debt opportunities, because we see that a lot of MSMEs, a lot of startups, a lot of late-stage real estate projects, they don't really get funding from the banks or NBFCs. We invest in non-convertible debentures of these companies and provide the last mile of the funding, which is not a very long-term sort of funding; it is one to two years kind of funding,” he said. 
 Second Fund Builds On Earlier Learnings Jogani added that this is Veloce’s second fund, following the full deployment of its first Rs 200 crore fund. “The first fund we did, we had launched last year, and in about one year, we used that fund. It was a 200 crore fund, comprising a 100 crore fund and a 100 crore green shoe option. So total 200 crores we raised is fully deployed and that is already in action,” he said. 
 He noted that while the overall investment thesis remains unchanged, the new fund structure incorporates greater flexibility. “Out of some of the learnings of the first fund, we have slightly built in more flexibility into our PPM for the second fund to enable the fund manager to take appropriate calls while investing,” he added. 
 Private Credit Emerging As Key Financing Channel On the growing relevance of private credit, Jogani said traditional banking often takes too long to disburse funds, leaving many MSMEs and developers constrained. “Traditional banking channel takes a lot of time before the actual funds get dispersed. Many times, that time is not available for the MSMEs, for the real estate companies. What we do is, in a very short time period, maybe a week or 10 days, we can turn around, churn the numbers, do the credit assessment or the company assessment, even complete the due diligence, and we can give that money to them sooner,” he said. 
 HNIs, Global Investors Back The Fund Jogani said the second fund has seen strong participation from HNIs and a Mauritius-based feeder fund. “Mainly, currently, it is all HNIs; also in the second fund, we have a fund from Mauritius. So some of the global investors are investing through that fund. It is more like a feeder fund,” he said. 
 He added that institutional investors are yet to join as the firm is still young. “We don't have a lot of institutions as of now because we have a relatively shorter history; we are just over a year old now,” he said. 
 On the government’s recent RDI Fund and policy measures promoting innovation and private capital, Jogani said the policy direction is encouraging, though India still has a long way to go. “The policy and the thought process and the direction is probably very, very encouraging. Just that it is still early days and we have a very long way to go, a long way to travel if we are to really compete with the likes of the US or China,” he said. 
 Jogani pointed out that while India talks extensively about startups, the ecosystem for innovation and exits remains limited. “For a lot of investors who invest in alternative funds, especially focused on startups, they don't get easy exits. And therefore, the money does not churn. They get stuck up because of the lack of an ecosystem. We don't have a lot of secondary market,” he said, adding that restrictive policies around valuation and compliance have made secondary transactions difficult. 
 He added that “alternatives can grow only if it is a liquid market. People who invest can easily recoup their investment.” 
 Focus On MSME Value Creation Jogani said that while the startup sector attracts attention, the larger opportunity lies in enabling long-standing MSMEs to scale. “There are so many family-run companies, so many companies that have been in existence for 10, 20, 30 years. They have got stuck in the middle range. They are 100, 200, 300 crore turnover. They are not able to grow faster for want of capital resources, even systems processes. If we can help those companies grow, achieve size and scale, make them go public, I think we can create huge value in that spectrum,” he said.
