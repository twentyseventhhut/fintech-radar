# **Permissionless Building in Fintech**

There’s a strange paradox in African fintech: the more legitimate you become, the harder it gets to build.

When you start, you can move fast. You can test ideas, ship in a week, and talk directly to users. But once you get a license, once you become official, everything slows down. Suddenly, every new feature is a negotiation. You’re not just talking to users anymore, you’re talking to regulators, banks, and compliance officers. You begin to realize that in fintech, permission isn’t just about safety, it’s a gate that decides who gets to innovate.
