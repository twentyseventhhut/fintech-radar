Indonesia to expand digital payment network to Saudi Arabia, India
 JAKARTA, Oct. 30 (Xinhua) -- Indonesia plans to expand the use of its Quick Response Code Indonesian Standard (QRIS) digital payment system to Saudi Arabia and India, following its successful implementation in several Southeast Asian countries, Bank Indonesia said on Thursday.
 The QRIS system is currently available for cross-border transactions in Malaysia, Thailand, and Singapore, and is in the process of being introduced in Japan and South Korea.
 "We will continue to connect the QRIS to India, Saudi Arabia, and other countries. The QRIS is not only a symbol of our sovereignty, but also a demonstration of our competence to operate cross-border," Bank Indonesia Governor Perry Warjiyo said at the Indonesia Digital Economy Festival and the Indonesia Fintech Summit and Expo in Jakarta.
 According to the central bank, the total digital payment transactions in Indonesia reached 12.99 billion in the third quarter of 2025, representing a year-on-year growth of 38.08 percent. Transactions using QRIS alone surged 147.65 percent, reflecting a strong increase in both users and merchants adopting the system. ■

Bank Indonesia to Expand QRIS Use to India and Saudi Arabia
Reporter
 Ilona Estherina 
November 13, 2025 | 10:40 am
 TEMPO.CO ,  Jakarta  -  The Governor of Bank Indonesia (BI), Perry Warjiyo, announced BI's plan to broaden the reach of Quick Response Code Indonesian Standard ( QRIS ) services to India and Saudi Arabia. The expansion comes after the Qris collaboration between countries was established with Japan.
BI is also in the process of forging partnerships with South Korea and China. "We are also in the process with South Korea alongside Saudi Arabia and India," Perry said during a meeting with the House of Representatives Commission XI, on Thursday, November 13, 2025.
Perry did not provide detailed targets for the expansion plan. However, the service expansion is part of BI's payment system policy for QRIS usage domestically and internationally.
On August 25, BI announced that Indonesian visitors to Japan, including Osaka, can use QRIS. Transactions with this service can already be conducted at several tenants in Pavilion Indonesia in Osaka, Japan. Meanwhile, the QRIS service in China is expected to be usable by the end of 2025.
BI Deputy Governor Filianingsih Hendarta previously stated that QRIS could be used in South Korea next year. "Perhaps in the first half of next year, we can already use it in South Korea," she said at the National Fintech Month (BFN) opening event at Wisma Danantara, Jakarta, on Tuesday, November 11, 2025.
Filianingsih also stated that by September 2025, QRIS had reached 10.33 billion transactions, or 158 percent of the initial target. This digital payment system has reached 58 million users and 41 million merchants across Indonesia.
The majority, or 90 percent, of the merchants using it are micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs). Filianingsih stated that the service has also been used in other neighboring regional countries such as Malaysia, Thailand, and Singapore.
