# IRIS payments to become mandatory for Greek businesses

> [!info] 2025-11-19 · 1 упоминаний · 1 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: Connecting the Dots in Fintech

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[Connecting the Dots in Fintech] 🇬🇷 IRIS payments will be mandatory for business-to-consumer transactions starting next month. Authorities finalized technical specifications for integrating direct payments at points of sale, including electronic funds transfer terminals and data publishing services, under the principle of mandatory receipt issuance for all payments.

Starting December 1, 2025 , all businesses in Greece that are required to accept card payments will also be obliged to accept instant payments via IRIS when transacting with private individuals (consumers).
The Independent Authority for Public Revenue (AADE) is finalising the regulatory and technical framework to ensure seamless integration between cash registers, POS terminals, and the IRIS instant payment system.
Key Principle
“ Payment received (whether by card or IRIS) = mandatory issuance of receipt directly from the cash register ”
This rule aims to close tax loopholes and ensure every transaction is automatically recorded and reported to the tax authority in real time.
What’s Changing Technically?
AADE has updated the connection protocols (amending earlier decisions A.1098/2022 and A.1155/2023) and introduced two new decisions:
 A.1147/2025 and A.1160/2025 
These define exactly how businesses must accept IRIS instant payments at the point of sale through:
 Physical POS terminals (the familiar card machines), which will now also support IRIS QR codes or direct instant transfers.
 Electronic invoicing/document issuance providers (cloud-based systems used by many professionals and small businesses).
In practice, when a customer chooses to pay via IRIS (using their bank’s mobile app), the payment will be instantly confirmed, and the cash register will automatically issue and transmit the receipt to AADE – just like a normal card transaction.
Who Must Comply and By When?
All businesses already required to have a POS terminal (essentially all retail and most service providers) must be IRIS-ready by December 1, 2025 .
Technology providers (POS manufacturers, cash-register software companies, payment institutions) have been given specific compliance deadlines by AADE to roll out the necessary updates.
Why This Matters
Faster, cheaper payments for consumers (no card fees for the customer).
Instant settlement for merchants (money lands in the business account immediately, 24/7).
Full traceability for the tax authority, reducing cash and tax-evasion opportunities.
Greece is aligning with the EU’s Instant Payments Regulation, making IRIS mandatory in retail just as SEPA Instant is becoming compulsory across the Eurozone in 2025–2026.
Businesses should contact their POS/provider immediately to ensure their systems will be updated before the December 1 deadline. Non-compliance will likely result in the same fines as refusing card payments.
