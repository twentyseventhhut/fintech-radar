Polish digital payments system BLIK has introduced a pilot program that allows users to receive mobile money transfers in euros from European partner systems.

YOU MAY LIKE:  BLIK payment platform reshapes online shopping in Poland, eclipsing cards Business

BLIK carried out its first cross-border mobile transfer, from a Portuguese payment system to one of its Polish users, during this week’s Web Summit 2025 conference in Lisbon.

The demonstration marked the launch of the pilot phase of a project designed to integrate BLIK with other European mobile payment systems, the company said Wednesday.

The pilot phase will include several partner payment systems, starting with Portugal’s MB WAY and later including Spain’s Bizum, Italy’s Bancomat and Scandinavia’s Vipps.

Polish participants in the pilot program include Santander BP customers, with PKO BP bank users to follow.

Developed by a consortium of six Polish banks, BLIK was introduced in 2015 and now boasts 19.9 million users, according to Polish tech site Antyweb.For payments in stores or online, the system enables users to transfer funds directly through their mobile banking apps by entering a one-off, automatically generated six-digit code.

According to BLIK’s operator, Polski Standard Płatności, the mobile payment platform processed 207.3 billion złoty (€48.8 billion) of transactions in the first half of 2025, a 31% increase on the same period in 2024 – putting the service far ahead of cards in online sales.
