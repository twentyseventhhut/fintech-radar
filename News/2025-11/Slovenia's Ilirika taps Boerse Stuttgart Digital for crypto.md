As digital assets have become a permanent feature of the European financial landscape, increased attention has turned to the topic of regulation. MiCAR provisions for crypto service providers will be mandatory as of 2026. This step is intended to further tighten the unified and mandatory framework for digital assets. For financial institutions like ILIRIKA brokerage house plc. in Slovenia, it means that long-term market access will only be possible with reliable partners who fulfil regulatory requirements at the highest level. That is why Slovenia’s best-known broker is now opting for Boerse Stuttgart Digital’s infrastructure. 
Boerse Stuttgart Digital and ILIRIKA brokerage house plc. go into partnership
Amid this dynamic development, Boerse Stuttgart Digital and ILIRIKA brokerage house plc., one of Slovenia’s top brokerage companies, are taking a crucial step: a strategic partnership that will provide institutional clients in Slovenia and the entire EU with seamless access to cryptocurrencies. This is based on fully MiCAR-compliant solutions that combine security, transparency, and institutional standards.
The collaboration brings together Boerse Stuttgart Digital as Europe’s leading crypto infrastructure provider, and one of Slovenia’s most renowned brokerage houses – a partnership that combines regional expertise with European regulatory power. 
Crypto offerings are integrated directly into ILIRIKA plc.’s platform
The goal of the partnership is to expand MiCAR-compliant access to crypto services. To achieve this, ILIRIKA brokerage house plc. is integrating Boerse Stuttgart Digital’s institutional trading and custody solutions fully into its existing platform. As a result, clients benefit from a simple, secure, and regulated way to trade and store cryptocurrencies, with no additional technical hurdles. In fact, this is an offering that guarantees maximum convenience when accessing digital assets.
 “As Europe’s leading crypto infrastructure partner, our mission is to drive the mass adoption of cryptocurrencies by providing institutional-grade trading and custody solutions,” said Joaquín Sastre Ibáñez, Head of Institutional Business at Boerse Stuttgart Digital. He added that, “We are delighted to partner with ILIRIKA brokerage house plc., one of Slovenia’s leading brokerage companies, to broaden easy and reliable crypto access in Europe. Together, we offer their clients exposure to digital assets without compromising on trust, security, or compliance.” 
Two MiCAR pioneers join forces
ILIRIKA plc. and Boerse Stuttgart Digital are the first providers in their respective countries to receive a MiCAR license and implement it across Europe. As partners, they are able to expand their groundbreaking work into new business areas, creating financial products that build trust and reduce administrative effort for ILIRIKA brokerage house plc. clients to a minimum.
Institutional investors in particular will benefit from automated and transparent processes, adherence to European standards, and a modular platform that guarantees top-level, MiCAR-compliant expertise. 
Cryptocurrencies are increasingly popular in Slovenia
In the last few years, Slovenia has become a hotspot for digital assets.
 Eight percent of the population are already using cryptocurrencies in 2025.
Revenue in the sector is expected to reach around 7.2 million US dollars in 2025; the annual growth rate is over seven percent.
The capital, Ljubljana, was named the world’s crypto-friendliest city by the Crypto Cities Index – more than 150 crypto ATMs and a high level of acceptance in retail are clear proof of this distinction. The country ranks among the top players in Europe in terms of crypto assets per user.
However, from 2026, the government is planning a 25 percent tax on cryptocurrency gains. The debate around location appeal and innovation shows that regulatory clarity and tax requirements are increasingly important factors, not only for private users, but in particular for institutional market actors.
 “As the first MiCAR-licensed company in Slovenia, ILIRIKA plc. is raising the bar for secure and regulated access to cryptocurrencies. By partnering with Boerse Stuttgart Digital, we are integrating institutional trading and custody solutions directly into our platform. This will enable our clients to reliably trade and store cryptocurrencies,” said Igor Štemberger, President of the Management Board at ILIRIKA brokerage house plc. 
Powerful message for the European crypto market
The partnership between Boerse Stuttgart Digital and ILIRIKA brokerage house plc. is an important milestone for regulated access to digital assets in Slovenia and all over Europe. It combines Boerse Stuttgart Digital’s established, MiCAR-compliant crypto infrastructure with ILIRIKA plc.’s regional market competence, creating a secure and efficient foundation for institutional trade – free from technical complexity and regulatory uncertainty.
At the same time, Slovenia’s high level of crypto adoption confirms the strategic importance of this step. According to the ECB, the country has the highest percentage of crypto-asset owners in the euro area, at over 15 percent. Taking that solid foundation as a starting point, the partnership offers solutions that build trust, simplify processes and considerably reduce administrative effort for ILIRIKA brokerage house plc. clients. Both companies are making an important contribution to the continued development of an increasingly regulated European crypto infrastructure.

**Ilirika**, Slovenia’s leading brokerage firm, has partnered with **Boerse Stuttgart Digital**, Europe’s foremost crypto infrastructure provider, to deliver **MiCAR-compliant cryptocurrency trading and custody services** across Europe. The partnership represents a major milestone in advancing regulatory-grade crypto adoption within the European Union, aligning with the framework established by the EU’s **Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCAR)**.

Under the collaboration, Boerse Stuttgart Digital’s **institutional-grade brokerage and custody solutions** will be fully integrated into Ilirika’s digital investment platform. This will enable clients to buy, sell, and hold cryptocurrencies securely within a regulated environment, backed by robust risk management and bank-level safeguarding. The initiative aims to expand investor confidence and accelerate mainstream participation in digital assets across Slovenia and the wider European region.

“As Europe’s leading crypto infrastructure partner, our mission is to drive the mass adoption of cryptocurrencies by providing institutional-grade trading and custody solutions,” said **Joaquín Sastre Ibáñez**, Head of Institutional Business at Boerse Stuttgart Digital. “Partnering with Ilirika allows us to extend secure and compliant access to digital assets across Europe, ensuring users can trade and store crypto with full trust and transparency.”

### Takeaway

Boerse Stuttgart Digital’s partnership with Ilirika marks a major step toward regulated crypto adoption in Europe, embedding MiCAR-compliant trading and custody within traditional brokerage infrastructure.

## Ilirika Becomes Slovenia’s First MiCAR-Licensed Crypto Brokerage

Ilirika recently became the **first Slovenian company licensed under MiCAR**, positioning it at the forefront of Europe’s regulated crypto revolution. By partnering with Boerse Stuttgart Digital—the **first provider in Germany** to receive a MiCAR-compliant license—Ilirika strengthens its ability to offer customers a fully regulated gateway to digital assets within the European Economic Area.

“As the first firm in Slovenia licensed under MiCAR, Ilirika is raising the bar for secure, regulated access to digital assets,” said **Igor Štemberger**, President of the Management Board at Ilirika. “Embedding Boerse Stuttgart Digital’s infrastructure into our platform combines EU-level compliance with institutional-grade trading and custody. This ensures that our clients can engage with crypto confidently and responsibly.”

The move also reflects a growing trend among traditional financial institutions embracing **regulated crypto integration**. As demand from investors accelerates, partnerships between established brokerages and crypto infrastructure providers are becoming critical to offering secure, scalable, and compliant digital asset access under the EU’s new regulatory framework.

### Takeaway

Ilirika’s MiCAR authorization and partnership with Boerse Stuttgart Digital cement its role as a regional leader in bridging traditional finance and digital assets under unified EU regulation.

## Meeting Rising Demand for Regulated Crypto Solutions

Crypto adoption in Slovenia is growing at an **annual rate of 7%**, with nearly **50% growth across Eastern Europe**, reflecting a region-wide shift toward digital assets. This trend underscores the necessity for financial institutions to incorporate crypto products or risk falling behind competitors in a rapidly evolving market.

Through this partnership, Ilirika and Boerse Stuttgart Digital are addressing the increasing demand for **secure, transparent, and MiCAR-compliant solutions—providing institutions and retail investors with the infrastructure to access digital markets confidently. Their collaboration aligns with the EU’s ambition to harmonize crypto regulations, ensuring market stability and consumer protection across member states.**

The partnership also reinforces Boerse Stuttgart Digital’s position as Europe’s **premier regulated crypto infrastructure provider**, offering custody and trading solutions that meet the needs of both retail brokerages and institutional asset managers. Together with Ilirika’s established client base and regional expertise, the partnership sets a precedent for how traditional financial players can safely embrace the digital asset economy.

### Takeaway

The Ilirika–Boerse Stuttgart Digital alliance addresses Europe’s surging demand for compliant crypto infrastructure, blending regulation, trust, and technology to foster sustainable market growth.
