# Exclusive: LemFi rolls out savings accounts for UK immigrants

LemFi is moving to expand beyond remittances with new UK savings accounts offering 3.92% interest rates, targeting immigrants who face barriers accessing traditional financial services despite strong financial backgrounds.

[](https://www.fintechfutures.com/author/tyler-pathe)

LemFi has launched savings accounts in the UK as part of its expansion beyond money transfers into broader financial services for immigrants, FinTech Futures can exclusively reveal. 

The accounts offer a 3.92% interest rate, above the UK average of 2.27% for similar products. Deposits are held with ClearBank, which has provided LemFi with agency banking services since December 2023. The London-based fintech has been testing the product in private beta since August this year, reporting 7,000 pilot users.

Speaking with FinTech Futures, Ridwan Olalere, CEO and co-founder of LemFi, explains that the interest rate is tied to the Bank of England base rate, but will eventually move to a variable structure. "The priority is to offer reliable value so our customers can build savings with confidence," Olalere comments.

When asked how LemFi's latest product would build said confidence, Olalere says the savings accounts work alongside the company's flagship offering LemFi Credit, which recognises international credit histories and uses alternative assessment methods to fill gaps overlooked by traditional lenders.

"We’ve designed our products so that over time, customers can demonstrate financial stability in a way that supports access to credit," Olalere explains. "By bringing savings into LemFi, customers can build a richer financial profile through the platform they already use for everyday transactions and remittances."

The company previously introduced Send Now Pay Later services in October, as part of an active strategy to expand credit lines for customers. "You can expect to see more credit-related features roll out over the coming year," Olalere confirms with FinTech Futures.

LemFi, founded in 2021, serves over two million customers across Europe and North America for transfers to 30 emerging markets. The company has raised over $85 million in funding, including $53 million in a Series B round led by Highland Europe in January this year.

Other backers include Left Lane Capital, Palm Drive Capital, Y Combinator, Zrosk Investment Management, Global Founders Capital, and Olive Tree.
