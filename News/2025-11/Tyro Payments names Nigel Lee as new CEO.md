# Fintech and payments veteran new Tyro CEO

06 November 2025 6:43AM Ian Rogers 

Tyro Payments have named Nigel Lee as their next CEO.

Lee will replace Jon Davey from early January.

Lee “has deep payments expertise across issuing, acquiring, software and hardware solutions and has worked at companies including Ingenico, American Express, First Data Corporation, MoneyGram and DataMesh Group” Tyro said.

Most recently, he spent four years in the roles of Regional Managing Director Asia Pacific and Chief Customer Officer at Ingenico, where he worked with Tyro.

He has also been an Operating Partner at private equity asset management company, APIS Partners, specialising in fintech portfolio companies across Asia and Africa, and made investments in several payments businesses during his tenure.

He is a member of the executive steering committee for CapGemini’s World Payments Report and is an Advisory Board member of the Emerging Payments Association Asia.

[](https://www.bankingday.com/rd/256/778)

“He knows the Australian market well given the global reach of his experience and Asia Pacific leadership roles” Tyro Chair Fiona Pak Poy said.

Currently based in Singapore, Lee will relocate to Sydney.

Jon Davey announced his resignation as Tyro CEO In June, having served in the role for three years.

Davey has accepted the CEO role at a private equity backed business based in Melbourne, and is outside of the financial services sector.
