BlackFin Tech Weekly - November 17th, 2025
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity
Hello FinTech Friends,
 Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry! Over the last week, there were six fintech deals in Europe, raising a total of €110 million, including three transactions in the United Kingdom, one in France, one in the Netherlands and one in Italy. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Wonderful , a Netherlands-based enterprise-grade AI agents that handle voice, chat and email interactions with full integration, has raised €86 million in a Series A round led by Index Ventures . 
 Sunday , a France-based fintech developing a QR-code payment solution enabling faster checkout and increased turnover in restaurants, has secured €18 million in a Series B round led by DST Global Partners. 
 Adclear , a UK-based banktech providing asystem that optimizes advertising by detecting fraud and improving campaign performance, has raised €2.4 million in a Seed Round backed by Outward VC , AFG Partners & Tenity . 
Let’s dive in!
 Wonderful 
 Wonderful , headquartered in Amsterdam with its research & development operations in Israel, is a banktech company providing enterprise-grade AI agents that handle voice, chat, and email interactions with full integration into internal systems. The company focuses on enabling large organizations to deploy human-level AI agents across markets, languages, and regulatory environments to manage complex customer workflows end-to-end. Wonderful has raised €86 million in a Series A round led by Index Ventures , with participation from Insight Partners , IVP , Bessemer , and Vine Ventures . The funds will be used to further develop Wonderful ’s integrated AI agent platform, enhance its local deployments in key regulated markets, and accelerate its expansion across Europe and into additional international regions.

 Sunday 
 Sunday , headquartered in France, is a payments company that develops a QR-code payment solution enabling faster checkout and increased turnover in restaurants. The company allows guests to pay directly from their smartphones by scanning a QR code, helping restaurants shorten wait times, improve staff efficiency, and increase revenue per service. Sunday has raised €18 million in a Series B round led by DST Global Partners . The funds will be used to further enhance Sunday ’s payment platform, roll out new features for restaurant partners, and accelerate its expansion across existing and new European and international markets. 
 Read more 
 Adclear 
 Adclear , headquartered in the United Kingdom, is a regtech company providing a system that optimizes digital advertising by detecting fraud and improving campaign performance. The company helps advertisers and platforms reduce wasted spending, increase transparency across the ad supply chain, and drive higher returns from their media investments. Adclear has raised €2.4 million in a Seed round led by Outward VC , with participation from AFG Partners and Tenity . The funds will be used to further enhance Adclear’s fraud-detection and performance-analytics technology, deepen integration with leading advertising platforms, and accelerate its commercial expansion across Europe and other key markets.

 In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 Finsolutia , an Iberian player specializing in credit and real-estate asset management and servicing, has announced the acquisition of Hipoges , one of the leading independent servicers in Spain, Portugal, and Greece. The transaction significantly enhances Finsolutia’s operational scale and geographic reach while expanding its multi-asset servicing capabilities. The combined group strengthens its position within the European loan and real estate servicing ecosystem, offering an integrated platform able to manage a larger volume of NPLs, REOs, and structured assets.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 Robinhood plans to let retail customers buy shares in a new fund run by its Robinhood Ventures arm that will hold a concentrated portfolio of private artificial intelligence companies, at least five described as best in class, and may use borrowing to amplify returns. Chief executive Vlad Tenev says the aim is to give ordinary investors access to the rapid value creation in private AI. The closed end fund would sit within a broader push by asset managers to channel smaller investors into private markets, helped by relaxed rules on alternatives in retirement plans. Critics warn that an illiquid, complex private equity style vehicle could misfit Robinhood’s fast trading user base and trap capital if sentiment turns. The firm is already experiencing intense demand for riskier products, with revenues and event contract volumes both surging. Management argues the structure balances accessibility with prudent risk control.

 Charles Schwab agreed to buy private markets fintech Forge Global for about $600m in cash, paying $45 a share, with closing expected in the first half of 2026. Forge runs a marketplace where qualified investors trade shares of late-stage private companies and has handled more than 17 billion dollars of transactions since 2014. It also supplies private markets data and is building an interval fund structure with lower investment minimums to widen access. The deal will fold Forge into Schwab’s wealth and advisory franchise, bolstering a recently launched alternative investments platform that currently serves very affluent retail households. Schwab expects to offer more direct exposure to private equity style growth companies while improving liquidity and price transparency for founders, employees, and early backers. Forge’s largest shareholders, Motive Capital and Deutsche Börse , have committed their support.

 FE fundinfo has acquired UK based fintech Contengo to deepen analytics offered to financial advisers, though terms were not disclosed. Contengo , founded in 2020, specialises in wealth analytics, performance reporting, and data migration, using a data engine that pulls transactions from adviser platforms and back-office systems into a single control module for informed decision making. FE fundinfo will embed Contengo’s tools into a new command deck within its Nexus investment management platform, launched in November, to give advisers clearer fee visibility and automated data processing. The combined system is intended to streamline cost monitoring, run real time scenario modelling off live valuations and transaction histories, and deliver AI driven money weighted performance insights. Advisers will also be able to benchmark against simulated portfolios and evidence Consumer Duty compliance.

 Lloyds Banking Group analysed data from more than 30,000 staff accounts to assess financial resilience during pay talks with unions. The customer insights team compared spending patterns, saving rates and salary growth of the lowest paid employees with similar customers, concluding that staff had fared better through the cost-of-living crisis. Lloyds says information used in the exercise was anonymous and fully aggregated, and that such analysis complies with data rules. Union officials at Accord , Unite and Affinity raised privacy concerns and warned that the findings risked being used to justify a smaller pay rise for the lowest earners, who were offered £1,200 extra in both 2026 and 2027. Recognised unions nevertheless recommended the deal, worth 7% to 9% on average, while Affinity plans to complain to the Information Commissioners Office . Observers say the dispute highlights growing tensions around workplace data.
