New AAMVA verification tool from iDenfy plus directly into DMV records
Lithuania-based iDenfy has introduced a new verification product that plugs directly into the American Association of Motor Vehicle Administrators’ ( AAMVA ) Know Your Customer (KYC) verification platform. A release from the firm says the tool enables access to official DMV records in real-time, giving companies a new workflow option that “instantly verifies any U.S. citizen’s driver’s license” against registered state databases.
Domantas Ciulde, CEO of iDenfy says that, “AAMVA verification verifies that the user’s document is valid, not expired, not forged and actually belongs to them.” He believes that, when paired with a biometric check, the new AAMVA workflow is among the most effective approaches for U.S. driver’s license checks, automatically matching multiple data parameters against official motor vehicle records to make sure the submitted license is genuinely registered by a state authority.
The AAMVA Verification product can be integrated via dashboard or API, and is aimed at sectors like transportation that specifically require confirming the user’s age and ability to operate a vehicle.
Forty-one states in the U.S. are active members of AAMVA, which serves as a central authority for data standardization, policy alignment and information exchange among motor vehicle agencies. Like many organizations handling biometrics and sensitive personal information, the organization is facing the new threats of document and identity theft and deepfake frau d. The iDenfy integration with AAMVA equips the onboarding system with iDenfy’s AI-powered facial recognition and liveness detection identity verification tools, as measures to help combat the spike in fraud.
“Trust and accuracy are the foundation of secure digital onboarding,” Ciulde says. “By integrating AAMVA checks, we’re enabling our partners in the U.S. to validate identities using one of the most reliable sources available directly from motor vehicle agencies.”
Korean rental app Socar adopts mDL verification
 SoCar , a popular, app-based car-sharing service in South Korea, has introduced a mobile driver’s license ( mDL ) verification feature. The firm has typically required users to photograph their physical driver’s license and enter the license number manually. A release says the new system aims to “build a faster, simpler, contactless verification environment.”
Shin Seung-hwan, head of SOCAR’s common platform PM team, says that “since the release of the mobile driver’s license verification feature at the end of September, inquiries related to license registration have decreased by 53 percent, showing a clear service improvement effect.”
Shin says there are plans to expand integration beyond the government mobile ID to include mDL verification through private third-party apps.

RegTech provider **iDenfy** has unveiled a major upgrade to its identity verification platform with the launch of its **U.S. Driver’s License Check** solution, offering businesses instant access to the **American Association of Motor Vehicle Administrators (AAMVA)** database. The new integration enables real-time verification of U.S. driver’s licenses, enhancing Know Your Customer (KYC) workflows for industries where license validation is a regulatory or operational requirement.

The AAMVA-powered check allows companies in sectors such as **car rentals, ride-sharing, logistics, and insurance** to instantly confirm license authenticity during onboarding. The feature matches user data — including name, date of birth, license number, and expiration status — directly against official DMV records, ensuring that the license is genuine, valid, and unaltered.

“AAMVA verification confirms that the user’s document is valid, not expired, not forged, and actually belongs to them,” said **Domantas Ciulde**, CEO of iDenfy. “When integrated alongside biometric checks, it becomes one of the most effective defenses against deepfakes and AI-powered identity fraud — challenges that are growing rapidly across online services.”

### Takeaway

iDenfy’s AAMVA integration transforms license validation into a real-time, authoritative KYC step, enabling U.S. businesses to onboard users securely and instantly.

## Reducing Fraud Through Verified Data Access and AI-Powered KYC

With **identity theft and license fraud on the rise** — resulting in over **$12.4 billion in losses in 2024** according to the FTC — the new integration is designed to close a major verification gap in digital onboarding. The U.S. driver’s license remains one of the most commonly used forms of identification, yet inconsistencies across state-issued licenses have made it a target for forgery and fraud.

By connecting directly to AAMVA’s official records across **41 active member states**, iDenfy’s system automates authenticity checks and reduces reliance on visual inspection or static data comparison. The API-driven process ensures that businesses can confirm identity details in seconds — verifying licenses that are active, unexpired, and state-authorized before granting access or approving applications.

“Trust and accuracy are the foundation of secure digital onboarding,” added Ciulde. “By integrating AAMVA checks, we’re enabling our partners to validate identities using one of the most reliable sources available directly from motor vehicle agencies.”

### Takeaway

Direct AAMVA data access significantly enhances fraud prevention and ensures state-level validation, reducing errors and onboarding friction across high-risk industries.

## Expanding Automated KYC Capabilities Across Regulated Sectors

The AAMVA workflow adds to iDenfy’s growing suite of AI-powered KYC and anti-fraud solutions, which already include facial recognition, liveness detection, and support for over **3,000 identity documents** from more than 200 territories. The combined solution is fully API-compatible, allowing companies to integrate automated license checks into their onboarding flows via dashboard or back-end connection.

Beyond mobility and transportation, the system provides value across **insurance underwriting, background screening, and employer compliance** in logistics and delivery sectors. Each verification is securely logged for traceability and audit readiness, helping businesses meet regulatory reporting and data governance standards.

**Viktor Vostrikov**, CMO of iDenfy, emphasized the importance of authoritative verification in today’s digital economy: “AAMVA verification is another step forward in our mission to provide data-driven identity tools that go beyond document scanning. This integration connects directly to official data sources, meaning our clients don’t just verify — they confirm identity with institutional accuracy.”

### Takeaway

iDenfy’s AAMVA-based license verification expands its role as a RegTech leader, setting a new benchmark for accuracy and compliance in digital identity verification.
