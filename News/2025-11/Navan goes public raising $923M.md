Navan’s recent IPO generated substantial earnings for its early investors despite a sharp drop in its share price on debut.

Navan, an American company with a large Israeli R&D center and about 3,400 employees worldwide, sold 30 million new shares, raising US$750 million, while existing shareholders sold 6.9 million shares for US$173 million.

Co-founders Ariel Cohen and Ilan Twig each sold shares worth around US$25 million, and senior employees sold about US$15 million.

Oren Zeev, an early investor who operates Zeev Ventures from California, sold 200,000 shares for US$5 million and now holds a 16% stake valued at roughly US$800 million post-IPO.

Other major shareholders include Lightspeed Venture Partners with 21.4%, and Andreessen Horowitz with 11%.

Navan’s market value dropped by 20% on its first trading day.

## 🧠 Food for thought

Implications, context, and why it matters.

### Navan moved from filing to a completed Nasdaq debut

*   It filed to list on Nasdaq under “NAVN” with Goldman Sachs & Co. LLC and Citigroup as lead book-running managers (the banks coordinating the IPO) 1.
*   Trailing 12-month revenue reached $613 million, up 32% 2. Gross bookings (the total value of bookings handled) were $7.6 billion, up 34% 2.
*   Last private valuation was $9.2 billion 2. Backers include Andreessen Horowitz; Coatue; Goldman Sachs; and Lightspeed 2.
*   The IPO sold 30 million new shares to raise $750 million; existing holders sold 6.9 million for $173 million. The stock fell 20% on the first day.

### Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) and fintech vendors can plug into Navan’s partner network

*   Partner programs span Affiliate & Referral Partners; Professional Services; Accounting Partners; and Travel Suppliers, plus co-marketing/co-selling (joint marketing and sales) to grow the sales pipeline 3.
*   The Partner Console from Navan launched with integrations to NetSuite, QuickBooks, and Xero (popular accounting platforms) 4. That release sets a standard workflow for accounting software partners 4.
*   Connect by Navan pulls card feeds (transaction data from corporate cards) from more than 250 global banks 4. Coverage includes Citi; Citizens; Brex; and Rho, which opens doors for payment processors plus financial data providers 4.
*   Business-to-Business (B2B) software vendors that target finance teams can benefit from Navan’s 68% gross margin and public listing 2. That profile may support more partnerships into its enterprise base which includes Unilever, Adobe, and Geico 2.
