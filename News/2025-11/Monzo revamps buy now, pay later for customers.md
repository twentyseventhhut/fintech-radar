Monzo has introduced a significant change to its buy now, pay later scheme, Monzo Flex, for its 13 million customers. The digital bankis simplifying the use of its service by allowing customers to select the "pay later" option at checkout and extend their repayment period to six or twelve months, albeit with interest.

Customers can now utilise Monzo Flex at both online and in-store checkouts when using Apple Pay. Kunal Malani, Monzo's general manager, expressed his excitement about the new feature: "Our customers love using their Flex credit card with ApplePay and we're thrilled to be the first bank to bring this feature to Apple Pay users in the UK."

He added: "Now our customers are able to choose their monthly payments-right at checkout with Apple Pay when using their Monzo Flex credit card.

"This brings an even more convenient experience for our customers when shopping online."

To use this feature, customers must first add their Flex card to their Apple Wallet, reports Birmingham Live.

When shopping online or in apps, they simply tap the Apple Pay button at checkout. For in-store purchases, they double-click the side button on their iPhone.

They then select their Flex card to view their payment options. If Flex isn't set as their default card, they can navigate to Other Cards and Pay Later Options to select it.

Finally, they tap Pay in Full to check out using their Flex default setting, or tap Pay Later to choose a payment plan that suits them.
