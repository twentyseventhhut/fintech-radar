Cash Plus launches 750 million dirham IPO to expand network and boost growth 
The Moroccan financial group Cash Plus is launching an initial public offering (IPO) worth 750 million dirhams, with 400 million raised through a capital increase, at a subscription price set at 200 dirhams per share. This operation values the group's equity at approximately 4.5 billion dirhams.
The primary aim of this stock market listing is to finance the expansion of Cash Plus's network, which includes 4,900 sales points, 87% of which are franchised, with 77% located in urban areas.
For the year 2025, the group anticipates a net banking income of 878 million dirhams, marking a 15.7% increase compared to 2024, and a net income attributable to the group of 237 million dirhams, reflecting a 21% rise.
The subscription period will run from November 19 to 25, inclusive, with the initial listing scheduled for December 8. The placement syndicate includes the usual market players.

# Morocco: Cash Plus gets regulatory approval for MAD 750 Million IPO on the Casablanca Stock Exchange

 Also available: [](https://www.african-markets.com/fr/bourse/bvc/feu-vert-du-regulateur-pour-lintroduction-de-cash-plus-a-la-bourse-de-casablanca-une-ipo-de-750-millions-de-dirhams)Published: November 01, 2025 5:39 PM 

The Moroccan Capital Market Authority (AMMC) has granted its approval for the planned listing of Cash Plus on the Casablanca Stock Exchange, as of October 31, 2025, under reference VI/EM/036/2025. This marks the official launch of the company’s IPO process, positioning it among financial service providers operating in payment and money transfer services.

The IPO will be carried out through a dual operation: the issuance of 2 million new shares to increase the company’s capital, alongside the sale of 1.8 million existing shares. The offering price has been set at 200 Moroccan dirhams per share, bringing the total transaction value to 750 million dirhams.

The subscription period is scheduled from November 19 to 25, 2025, while the listing on the “Principal F” compartment of the Casablanca Stock Exchange is expected to begin on December 8, 2025.

This operation follows an Extraordinary General Meeting held on October 17, 2025, which approved the opening of the capital to the public. Cash Plus intends to use the proceeds to support its development strategy, particularly the expansion of its agency network and the acceleration of investments in digital solutions.

Founded in 2004, Cash Plus has become a key player in financial services accessibility in Morocco. With an extensive nationwide network, the company offers money transfer services, bill payments, foreign exchange and payment accounts, while also developing digital tools to make everyday financial services more accessible. Its model combines a dense territorial presence with an innovation-driven approach focused on financial inclusion.
