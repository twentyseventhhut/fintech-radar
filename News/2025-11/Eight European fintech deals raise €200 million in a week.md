BlackFin Tech Weekly - November 24th, 2025
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity
Hello FinTech Friends,
 Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry! Over the last week, there were eight fintech deals in Europe , raising a total of €200 million , including two transactions each in France and Denmark and one each in the UK, Spain, Norway and Germany. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Flatpay , a Denmark-based Payments company providing mobile point of sale payments system, has raised €147 million in a Venture Round led by AVP and Smash Capital . 
 Deblock , a France-based platform providing a non-custodial crypto and fiat wallet, has raised €30 million in a Series A led by Speedinvest . 
 Condukt , a UK-based Regtech solving business onboarding challenges by providing KYB services, has raised €8.6 million in a Venture Round backed by Lightspeed , MMC Ventures and Cocoa . 
 Let’s dive in! 
 Flatpay 
 Flatpay , based in Denmark, provides an integrated mobile point-of-sale solution combining payment terminals, POS software, and merchant analytics for SMEs. The company differentiates through transparent pricing, simplified onboarding, and a frictionless user experience, positioning itself as a challenger to incumbents like SumUp and Zettle. Flatpay has raised €147 million in a Venture Round led by AVP , with Smash Capital , Hedosophia , Seed Capital , and Dawn Capital participating. The funding will support geographic expansion across Northern Europe and strengthen the platform’s product stack, including analytics and value-added services for merchants.

 Deblock 
 Deblock , headquartered in France, offers a non-custodial crypto and fiat wallet designed to provide a secure, seamless bridge between traditional finance and Web3. The platform enables users to manage assets with enhanced security features (including MPC and seedless recovery) and focuses on delivering a consumer-friendly Web3 experience. The company has secured €30 million in a Series A round led by Speedinvest , joined by CommerzVentures , Latitude , Shapers , Headline , Chalfen Ventures , and Triton Capital . The new capital will accelerate product development, enhance compliance capabilities, and support Deblock’s expansion across Europe. 
 Read more 
 Condukt 
 Condukt , based in the UK, delivers a KYB platform that automates business verification by consolidating official registries, financial records, UBO data, and compliance checks into a single API-driven workflow. The company aims to help fintechs and financial institutions reduce onboarding friction while meeting increasingly strict AML and regulatory requirements. Condukt has raised €8.6 million in a Venture Round backed by Lightspeed , MMC Ventures , and Cocoa . The funding will enable the company to expand its international data coverage, develop advanced automation and AI-driven decisioning, and support broader commercial rollout.

 In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 Integral , a Germany-based provider of accounting, payroll and taxation services for SMEs backed by General Catalyst , Cherry Ventures and Puzzle Ventures , has announced the acquisition of cleverlohn , a Germany-based cloud payroll and HR platform offering digital employee data management, compliant filings and dedicated support. The transaction, currently pending, marks a significant step in strengthening Integral’s payroll offering and supports its ambition to build a leading European player in accounting, tax and payroll services for SMEs.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 Lloyds is buying digital wallets provider Curve in a transaction reported at about £120m, with completion targeted for the first half of 2026 subject to regulatory approval. Curve’s board concedes the sale price falls short of the ambitious valuation once hoped for and recognises the disappointment among many investors, yet argues the deal offers the best realistic outcome for creditors and long-suffering shareholders. The acquisition follows months of shareholder unrest at Curve , including an unsuccessful attempt to oust chair Lord Fink and founder-CEO Shachar Bialick and fierce criticism from leading investor IDC Ventures . For Lloyds , incorporating Curve Pay will add a modern wallet, letting 28m customers combine cards, earn richer rewards, access pay-later tools and avoid foreign-exchange fees, although the group insists the purchase will not materially affect earnings guidance.

 UBS chair Colm Kelleher has discussed with US Treasury secretary Scott Bessent what relocating the Swiss bank’s headquarters to the US might involve, as management explores contingency plans if Switzerland presses ahead with tougher capital rules. Proposed requirements would force UBS to hold an extra $26bn of capital, which it argues go well beyond demands on global peers and would curb competitiveness. The talks form part of a broader effort to pressure Bern to dilute reforms, introduced after UBS rescued Credit Suisse , intended to prevent another systemic collapse. While executives say they still prefer to remain based in Switzerland, they insist on assessing alternatives, and activist shareholder Cevian Capital has warned the new regime could make running a major international bank from the country “not viable”. By contrast, the Trump administration is loosening regulations, raising European concerns that US deregulation could distort competition and increase global financial risks.

 PayPal has renewed and expanded its partnership with investment firm KKR under a deal that will see KKR’s credit funds buy up to €65bn (about $75bn) of European buy-now-pay-later receivables, supported by a replenishing loan commitment of up to €6bn. The loans are originated in France, Germany, Italy, Spain and the UK, where KKR has already acquired most of PayPal’s existing BNPL book following an initial 2023 transaction. PayPal will keep responsibility for underwriting, servicing and all customer-facing activity while shifting the receivables off its balance sheet, reinforcing a “balance-sheet-light” model. KKR says the enlarged program reflects both the strength of PayPal’s growing European BNPL platform and the scale of its asset-based finance franchise, as demand for instalment payment options continues to rise across key European e-commerce markets and everyday spending categories in recent quarters.

 The global cryptocurrency market has lost about $1.2tn in value in six weeks as traders retreat from speculative assets amid concerns over lofty tech valuations and uncertainty about US interest-rate cuts. The combined market capitalisation of more than 18,000 tokens tracked has fallen roughly 25% since early October, while bitcoin has slid 28%, erasing its gains for the year. Analysts say heavily leveraged bets have magnified the downturn, with Donald Trump’s threat of “massive” tariffs on China on 10 October triggering liquidations of around $20bn of margin positions in a single day, leaving a lasting psychological scar dubbed “10/10”. Other major coins have dropped even more steeply, digital-asset investment products have suffered their biggest outflows since February, and some investors see the crypto slump feeding into weakness in high-growth US tech stocks as traders rotate into more defensive equity funds.
