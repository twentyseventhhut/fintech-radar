BlackFin Tech Weekly - November 10th, 2025
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity
Hello FinTech Friends,
 Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry! Over the last week, there were six fintech deals in Europe , raising a total of €40 million , including two transactions in Germany, two in Switzerland, one in Spain and one in the United Kingdom. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Future , a Switzerland-based digital asset investment firm that structures regulated vehicles providing institutional investors with compliant exposure to Bitcoin, has raised €30 million in a Venture Round led by Fulgur Ventures , Nakamoto , and TOBAM . 
 ReStabilise , a UK-based fintech developing an institutional-grade GBP and multi-currency stablecoin issuance platform, has secured €3.2 million in a Pre-Seed round led by Project A Ventures . 
 Maesn , a Germany-based banktech providing a Master API for developers to connect financial and payroll stacks, has raised €2.3 million in a Venture Round backed by Get.de , TSIC , and NRW Bank . 
Let’s dive in!
 Future 
 Future , headquartered in Switzerland, is a digital asset investment firm that designs and manages regulated vehicles offering institutional investors compliant exposure to Bitcoin. The company focuses on bridging traditional finance with the crypto ecosystem through transparent, governance-based structures that align with European regulatory frameworks. Future has raised €30 million in a Venture Round led by Fulgur Ventures , Nakamoto , and TOBAM . The funds will be used to expand Future ’s product suite of institutional-grade Bitcoin investment vehicles, strengthen its compliance and governance capabilities, and scale its presence across Europe to meet growing demand from institutional and wealth management clients.

 ReStabilise 
 ReStabilise , based in London, develops an institutional-grade platform for issuing and managing multi-currency stablecoins, including GBP and other major fiat currencies. The company enables regulated financial institutions and fintechs to create and manage stablecoin operations with full transparency and compliance, positioning itself as a key player in the evolving UK stablecoin framework. The firm has secured €3.2 million in a Pre-Seed round led by Project A Ventures . The proceeds will be used to accelerate product development, expand the technical and compliance teams, and pursue regulatory engagement with the UK Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) ahead of upcoming stablecoin regulations. ReStabilise also plans to pilot its platform with selected banks and payment providers by early 2025. 
 Read more 
 Maesn 
 Maesn , headquartered in Germany, offers a banktech API platform that allows developers to integrate financial and payroll data seamlessly into applications. The company’s “Master API” aggregates fragmented financial systems, enabling faster, compliant connectivity across European financial institutions. Maesn aims to simplify embedded finance for fintechs, neobanks, and enterprise clients. Maesn has raised €2.3 million in a Venture Round backed by Get.de , TSIC , and NRW Bank . The funds will be allocated to product scaling, developer onboarding, and new integrations with major European banking APIs under PSD2 and upcoming PSD3 standards. The company also plans to expand its operations into additional EU markets and strengthen partnerships with payroll providers to broaden its data coverage.

 In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week: 
 FundApps , a UK-based fintech providing a platform that automates compliance monitoring and reporting, has announced the acquisition of SteelEye , a UK-based integrated trade and communications surveillance platform. The deal enables FundApps to strengthen its regulatory technology suite by combining compliance monitoring with trade surveillance, thereby expanding its position in the RegTech ecosystem.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 A federal judge issued a temporary injunction blocking the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau’s Open Banking rule, pausing compliance deadlines. The lawsuit was filed by Forcht Bank , the Kentucky Bankers Association , and the Bank Policy Institute . The court said the groups are likely to prove the bureau exceeded its authority and that they face unrecoverable costs if forced to prepare now. The action coincides with new bureau leadership under the Trump administration. Finalized in 2024 under Biden , the rule sought to let consumers access and share data from bank accounts, cards, wallets and payment apps at no cost. Banking groups argue it imposes costly burdens without clear authority. The injunction remains while the agency reconsiders the rule, and supporters say it avoids spending on requirements that may be revised. The pause delays push on data portability and creates uncertainty for partnerships and API plans.

 Revolut introduced a feature letting users convert dollars to USDC or USDT at a one to one rate with no fees or spread. It supports six networks, including Ethereum , Solana and Tron . The rollout follows a MiCA license from Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission , allowing regulated crypto services across the European Economic Area. Revolut will absorb conversion spreads while pegs hold, aiming to remove friction between fiat money and crypto assets. For individuals and small firms, a monthly limit of about $578,630 per thirty days covers most transactions. The feature reduces foreign exchange bleed and wire transfer costs and reframes stablecoins as working capital tools rather than speculation. With Western Union , MoneyGram , and Zelle pursuing stablecoin settlement, Revolut gains positioning among regulated providers integrating blockchain for payments and treasury uses for SMEs.

 FundApps and SteelEye agreed to merge, creating one integrated RegTech platform that combines surveillance, monitoring, reporting and analytics. FTV Capital , an investor in FundApps , will provide additional equity to support integration. Leaders from both firms said the combined business will deliver a single platform across the compliance lifecycle, replacing the need to stitch multiple systems, and will use automation and data to increase transparency and control. Once combined, the company will serve more than 350 clients across 18 countries, generate nearly £50 million in annual recurring revenue, and operate from hubs in London, New York, Singapore, India and Portugal. The move marks consolidation in RegTech and signals rising demand for unified solutions among banks, asset managers, hedge funds and commodities firms. FTV Capital earlier appointed Brad Bernstein , Richard Earnshaw and Max Weber to the FundApps board.

 The European Central Bank plans readiness to issue a digital euro in 2029, with a pilot in 2027, yet the project faces strong resistance from lawmakers and banks. Fourteen lenders including Deutsche Bank , BNP Paribas and ING argue the retail design duplicates private services with no clear benefit and are backing Wero , a private service built to rival US providers. Spanish MEP Fernando Navarrete recommends a scaled back model focused on offline payments rather than real time online use. The plan needs new legislation because the ECB can issue only physical cash today. Eurozone finance ministers endorsed progress, while supporters cite a fall in cash use in stores from seventy two percent to fifty two percent in 2024 and the rise of US backed stablecoins. Industry groups warn of costs, citing a PwC estimate up to thirty billion euros, which the ECB disputes, saying under six billion. The parliamentary outcome remains open.
