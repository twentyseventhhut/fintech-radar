Paytm Q2 Profit Falls To 21 Cr After Gaming Impairment Hit
 # Paytm 
 # Profit 
 # Decline 
 # Gaming Business 
Paytm reported a net profit of Rs 21 crore in the September quarter, down 97 per cent from Rs 930 crore in the same period last year. The sharp decline was primarily due to a Rs 190 crore impairment loss on its investment in Paytm First Games, following the government's ban on real-money gaming platforms.
The exceptionally high profit in the year-ago quarter had also been boosted by the sale of Paytm’s ticketing business to Zomato, which had led to a one-time gain.
The company’s operating revenue rose 24 per cent year-on-year to Rs 2,061 crore in Q2 FY26, compared with Rs 1,659 crore a year earlier. Total expenses declined to Rs 2,062 crore from Rs 2,245 crore last year, with payment processing and employee benefits remaining the largest cost components.
Paytm said its customer acquisition cost dropped 42 per cent year-on-year, although it increased 16 per cent sequentially. The company has been expanding its on-ground sales presence in smaller towns to deepen merchant relationships. Spending on sales teams rose 39 per cent to Rs 297 crore.
The fintech reported an Ebitda margin of positive 7 per cent, improving from negative 24 per cent a year ago, signalling better operational efficiency. It also said it holds a cash balance of Rs 13,068 crore to support new business initiatives.
Total payment volume on the platform grew to Rs 5.7 lakh crore in the September quarter, up 5 per cent from Rs 5.4 lakh crore in the previous quarter. The merchant base using Paytm services increased 22 per cent year-on-year to 13.7 million. Monthly transacting users, which had declined after regulatory restrictions on Paytm Payments Bank, recovered to 75 million.
The company said it remains focused on driving merchant engagement and expanding financial services offerings across key growth markets.
