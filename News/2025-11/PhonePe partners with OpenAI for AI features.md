PhonePe Partners With OpenAI To Integrate AI Features
 # Technology 
 # Fintech 
 # Innovation 
 # Digital 
Indian fintech platform PhonePe has partnered with artificial intelligence firm OpenAI to integrate its AI features across PhonePe's ecosystem. The collaboration, announced on Thursday, will make OpenAI's ChatGPT discoverable within both the main PhonePe consumer app and the dedicated PhonePe for Business app. 
The alliance aims to expand the reach of advanced AI technology to a broad user base in India. The company stated that new use cases for AI are expected to emerge as adoption of these services grows across the country. 
Rahul Chari, founder, whole-time director, and chief technology officer at PhonePe, commented on the strategic nature of the deal. “This strategic alliance demonstrates that collaborations between innovative companies in this space can help expand the reach of cutting-edge technology to the broader population,” Chari said. 
For OpenAI, the partnership represents a significant step into the Indian market. The company highlighted PhonePe's extensive local reach and understanding as key reasons for the collaboration. 
“India is a global hub for innovation, and PhonePe’s deep understanding of the country’s fabric and its user base make them the ideal partner. This partnership will demonstrate the immense value of consumer AI across India, helping millions of users enhance their daily lives,” said Oliver Jay, head of international at OpenAI. 
PhonePe brings a substantial network to the partnership, with over 610 million registered users and more than 44 million merchants on its platform. 
This is not OpenAI's first foray into partnerships with Indian payment firms. Last month, Razorpay, the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI), and OpenAI collaborated to launch agentic payments on ChatGPT. 
That feature, currently in a pilot stage, allows users to shop and complete payments within a single chat interface. These agentic payments are supported by Razorpay's banking partners and are built on domestic Unified Payments Interface (UPI) innovations, aiming to facilitate seamless transactions for AI-native commerce. 
The series of collaborations signals a growing convergence of artificial intelligence and digital payments in India, creating new avenues for user engagement and commercial activity.
