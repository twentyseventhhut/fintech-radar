## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened (one line).** German neobank N26 reported its first-ever full-year net profit for FY2025: net income of ~€1.6M on revenue of >€500M (€501.6M, +13% YoY) and gross profit of €350.5M (+33%), a structural turning point after a €42M loss in 2024.

**[1] Why it matters.** N26 is Germany's most valuable fintech and a flagship continental neobank. Reaching sustained full-year profitability validates the model after years of losses, the BaFin growth cap, and a leadership upheaval. It puts N26 in the small club of profitable European neobanks (Revolut, Monzo, Starling) and improves its flexibility around a possible future IPO and any new capital raise.

**[2] Context / how we got here (dated timeline).**
- 2013 — N26 founded by Valentin Stalf & Maximilian Tayenthal.
- 2021 — BaFin imposes a growth cap (~50–60k new customers/month) over anti-money-laundering control gaps; €10M penalty later applied.
- 2023 — Net loss of €102.4M.
- 2024-05/06 — BaFin lifts the growth restriction (effective ~1 June 2024); monthly sign-ups more than double (see [[N26 CEO Valentin Stalf on rebuilding after regulatory setbacks]]). Q3 2024 = first profitable quarter (net operating income ~€2.8M); deposits pass €10B; ~4.8M revenue-relevant customers end-2024.
- 2025-06 — Fresh BaFin scrutiny over risk management; Netherlands mortgage restriction (see [[N26 faces new BaFin scrutiny over risk management]]).
- 2025 H2 — Investor pressure ousts founders; ex-Bundesbank exec Andreas Dombret nominated chair; Marcus Mosen moves to co-CEO (see [[Connecting the Dots Two European fintech deals raise €36 million]]).
- 2025-12 — N26 publicly targets first annual profit for 2025 (see [[N26 targets first annual profit in 2025]]); BaFin appoints a special monitor and tightens oversight (see [[BaFin places N26 under stricter oversight with special monitor]]).
- 2026-03 — FY2024 loss narrowed to €42M from €102.4M (see [[N26 narrows 2024 loss to €42 million]]).
- 2026-04 — Mike Dargan (ex-UBS) takes over as CEO (pending BaFin approval).
- 2026-06 — FY2025: first full-year net profit (€1.6M), revenue >€500M.

**[3] Who is affected.** N26 management (new CEO Dargan), backers (Allianz X, Tencent, Coatue, Insight, etc.), ~7M+ European customers, BaFin, and neobank peers as a profitability benchmark.

**[4] Key numbers (with sources).**
- Revenue FY2025: €501.6M, +13% YoY. ([Sifted](https://sifted.eu/articles/n26-hits-profitability))
- Gross profit: €350.5M, +33%. ([Sifted](https://sifted.eu/articles/n26-hits-profitability))
- Net income: ~€1.6M (vs −€42M in 2024; −€102.4M in 2023). ([Sifted](https://sifted.eu/articles/n26-hits-profitability))
- Customers: ~4.8M revenue-relevant end-2024 (FY2025 figure not cleanly disclosed). ([n26.com press](https://n26.com/en-eu/press/press-release/n26-group-reports-first-quarterly-profit-as-growth-in-customer-numbers-accelerates-strongly))
- Deposits: passed €10B in Q3 2024 (FY2025 figure not confirmed). ([Fintech Magazine](https://fintechmagazine.com/articles/n26-breaks-even-after-bafin-cap-removal-posts-q3-profit))
- 2026 guidance: profitable growth, "operating leverage and disciplined investment." ([Sifted](https://sifted.eu/articles/n26-hits-profitability))

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
Red-team of the FY2025 profitability claim. Sources: digest text + [Sifted](https://sifted.eu/articles/n26-hits-profitability).

1. **Is net profit real or marketing-thin?** Largely real but razor-thin: ~€1.6M net income on €501.6M revenue (~0.3% net margin). It is a genuine first full-year profit, but a single bad quarter could flip it back to a loss.

2. **Revenue growth of +13% — is that slowing?** Yes, materially. After the BaFin cap lifted, N26 guided ~40% growth for 2024; 13% in 2025 suggests sign-up momentum cooled sharply post-cap-removal. Open question on whether the post-cap rebound has already peaked.

3. **What drove the swing to profit — revenue or cost cuts?** Mostly margin/cost discipline: gross profit +33% vs revenue +13%, so unit economics and interest income (high-rate environment) did the heavy lifting, plus operating leverage. Profitability may be rate-sensitive.

4. **Interest-rate dependence?** Open/likely high. Neobank profitability in 2024–25 leaned on elevated ECB rates lifting net interest income on deposits. ECB cuts would pressure the thin net margin.

5. **Are the figures audited / IFRS / German GAAP?** Not specified in the digest. FY2024 (€42M loss) was reported under HGB-style statutory accounts; need to confirm basis for FY2025 before treating €1.6M as final.

6. **Does the profit include one-offs?** Unknown — open. Need to check for one-time items (e.g., provision releases, FX, deferred-tax effects) that could be flattering the bottom line.

7. **Customer count at end-2025?** Open. Last clean figure is ~4.8M revenue-relevant end-2024; FY2025 customer/deposit totals were not cleanly disclosed in coverage — a gap given they underpin the revenue story.

8. **Regulatory overhang still live?** Yes. A BaFin special monitor was appointed in Dec 2025 and a Netherlands mortgage restriction stands. Compliance remediation costs and reputational risk persist despite the profit headline.

9. **Leadership stability?** Founders ousted in H2 2025; new CEO Mike Dargan (ex-UBS) only took over ~April 2026. The FY2025 profit was delivered mid-transition; strategy continuity is unproven.

10. **How does N26 compare to peers?** Far behind in absolute terms: Revolut ~US$2.3B pre-tax profit / US$6B revenue (2025); Monzo ~£114M adjusted pre-tax. N26's €1.6M net profit is a milestone but tiny by comparison — "profitable but sub-scale."

11. **Is "leading European digital bank" defensible?** Partly marketing. By value/Germany footprint yes; by customers (~7M) and profit it trails Revolut (68M+) and UK peers. Treat the self-description with skepticism.

12. **Path to IPO?** Open. Profitability improves IPO optionality, but thin margins, regulatory monitor, and a new CEO make a near-term listing unlikely; no IPO timeline confirmed.

13. **Deposit/funding stability?** Open. Deposit base (>€10B at last disclosure) is the funding engine; no FY2025 deposit figure confirmed, and deposit beta in a falling-rate world is a watch item.

14. **Could the digest's "€500M / €1.6M" be conflating revenue-recognition definitions?** Low risk — multiple sources (Sifted, press) align on €501.6M revenue and ~€1.6M net income, so the headline numbers are corroborated.

15. **Sustainability into 2026?** Guidance says profitable growth via operating leverage, but with a 0.3% net margin and rate tailwinds fading, 2026 profitability is plausible but not assured.

Importance: 4/5 — A genuine structural milestone (first full-year profit) for Germany's most valuable fintech and a benchmark event for continental neobanks. Capped below 5 because the profit is razor-thin, growth is decelerating, regulatory overhang persists, and absolute scale lags UK/global peers.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector context.** European/UK neobanking entered a profitability-and-maturity phase in 2024–2026. After a decade of growth-over-profit, the leaders are now posting profits, helped by elevated interest rates lifting net interest income on deposits and by tighter cost discipline. N26's first full-year net profit (€1.6M on €501.6M revenue, FY2025) is the continental-Europe instance of this shift. The Fitch view that LatAm neobanks face a "considerable" path to profitability (see [[Nu, Revolut and other neobanks face slow path to profitability]]) contrasts with the now-profitable European cohort.

**In-base comparables (from the vault).**
- [[N26 narrows 2024 loss to €42 million]] — direct prior-year baseline: −€42M (2024) vs −€102.4M (2023); FY2025's +€1.6M completes the loss-to-profit arc.
- [[N26 targets first annual profit in 2025]] — management's Dec-2025 guidance, now confirmed delivered.
- [[BaFin places N26 under stricter oversight with special monitor]] / [[N26 faces new BaFin scrutiny over risk management]] — the live regulatory overhang that caps how clean this milestone is.
- [[N26 passes 275,000 customers in Belgium, eyes under-18s]] — growth-engine detail; also shows Revolut outscaling N26 ~4x in Belgium.
- [[N26 CEO Valentin Stalf on rebuilding after regulatory setbacks]] / [[Connecting the Dots Two European fintech deals raise €36 million]] — leadership transition context.

**Peer benchmark (external).**
- Revolut: FY2025 pre-tax profit ~US$2.3B, revenue ~US$6B (+46%), 68M+ customers.
- Monzo: FY2025 adjusted pre-tax profit ~£114M, 15M+ customers.
- Starling: deposits ~£12.1B, ~4.6M accounts, sustained profitability.
- N26: net income ~€1.6M, revenue €501.6M, ~4.8M (end-2024) customers.
Read: N26 is now profitable but an order of magnitude smaller in profit than UK peers — "profitable but sub-scale."

**Risk flags.**
1. **Thin-margin profit** — ~0.3% net margin; one weak quarter could reverse it.
2. **Decelerating growth** — revenue +13% vs ~40% guided for 2024; post-cap rebound appears to be fading.
3. **Regulatory overhang** — BaFin special monitor (Dec 2025) + NL mortgage restriction; remediation cost and reputational risk continue.
4. **Rate sensitivity** — profitability leans on high-rate net interest income; ECB cuts pressure the bottom line.
5. **Leadership transition risk** — founders ousted H2 2025; CEO Mike Dargan only in seat ~April 2026.
6. **Disclosure gaps** — FY2025 customer and deposit totals not cleanly published; accounting basis/one-offs for the €1.6M not confirmed.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Trigger:** type/earnings — N26's first full-year net profit (FY2025). Figures below are reported, not estimated; gaps flagged.

**Reported figures (FY2025, vs prior year).**
| Metric | FY2025 | FY2024 | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | €501.6M | ~€443M | +13% |
| Gross profit | €350.5M | ~€263M | +33% |
| Net income | ~€1.6M | −€42M | +€43.6M swing (loss→profit) |
| Net margin | ~0.3% | negative | — |

Prior history: net loss −€102.4M (2023) → −€42M (2024) → +€1.6M (2025). This is N26's **first full-year net profit** in its 12-year history; Q3 2024 was the first profitable quarter.

**Vs prior-year / consensus.**
- Vs prior year: clear improvement — loss-to-profit swing, gross profit growth (+33%) well ahead of revenue growth (+13%) signaling operating leverage and margin expansion.
- Vs guidance/consensus: management had publicly guided to "first annual profit in 2025" (Dec 2025) and a positive result that should grow in 2026 — so this is **in line with management's own stated target**. No external analyst consensus number exists (N26 is private), so "beat/miss" is judged against its own guidance: delivered as promised. Revenue growth (13%) undershot the ~40% pace seen post-cap-removal in 2024, so growth itself came in soft.

**Guidance (2026).** Profitable growth, sustained profitability via "operating leverage and disciplined investment" (CEO Mike Dargan). No specific revenue/profit target disclosed.

**Thesis flags.**
- POSITIVE: structural profitability milestone; margin (gross profit) scaling faster than revenue; validates the model post-cap and post-leadership shake-up.
- CAUTION: net profit razor-thin (€1.6M / ~0.3% margin) — not yet a margin of safety; revenue growth decelerating sharply; profit likely flattered by high-rate net interest income (rate-sensitive); FY2025 customer/deposit totals and accounting basis/one-offs not cleanly disclosed; BaFin special monitor still active.

**Verdict: IN-LINE (delivered on management's first-full-year-profit guidance), but a thin, low-quality beat on the bottom line with a soft top line.**
