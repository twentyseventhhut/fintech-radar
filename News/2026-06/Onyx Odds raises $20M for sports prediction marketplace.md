**_Investment Round Totaling $20M Comes Less Than a Year after Onyx Odds Comes out of Beta_**

NEW YORK--(BUSINESS WIRE)--**Onyx Odds**, the fastest-growing social sports prediction platform, announced today its Series A funding round totaling**$20M**, led by **Payward**, the parent company behind **Kraken**, **NinjaTrader**and **Bitnomial**. The round values Onyx Odds at $220 million less than one year after coming out of beta.

Onyx Odds enables users to engage with sports outcomes through exchange-traded instruments, offering a new, market-driven way to participate in sports markets. Founded in 2025, the company has quickly attracted a dedicated and growing community of users, underscoring its momentum across the broader prediction and data marketplace.

As part of the investment, Onyx Odds will integrate with Payward Services, the B2B infrastructure platform behind Kraken and Payward's wider product suite, to power its prediction markets offering on Payward's fully licensed US derivatives stack and embed crypto trading inside the Onyx app. The single integration gives Onyx the foundation to extend its lead in prediction markets without spending years assembling vendors, licenses and operational capabilities itself.

The raise follows a period of strong momentum for Onyx Odds, driven by user engagement and retention. With nearly a million users, industry-leading retention has made Onyx one of the largest prediction platforms within its first year, on less than $8M in venture funding prior to this round.

“This investment marks a major milestone for Onyx Odds and validates the strength of both our platform and our vision,” said **Leul Dadi**, founder and CEO of Onyx Odds. “Payward brings a deep expertise and allows us to scale more efficiently while continuing to deliver a best-in-class experience for our users.”

For Payward, the investment marks a significant step into prediction markets and reinforces its position as a multi-asset infrastructure platform, freeing partners to focus on product and user experience instead of stitching together a patchwork of vendors, licenses and capabilities.

“Prediction markets are one of the fastest-growing emerging categories in financial services,” said Mark Greenberg, Head of Payward Services. “Payward is the only US platform that brings a CFTC-registered FCM, a CFTC-designated DCM and a global crypto exchange under one roof, and makes the full stack available through Payward Services. Onyx is the kind of operator that knows what to do with that foundation, and we're investing to help them scale it.”

**ABOUT ONYX ODDS:**

Onyx Odds is a leading social sports prediction platform. The company was founded in 2025 and is headquartered in New York City.

**ABOUT PAYWARD:**

Payward Services is the B2B infrastructure platform built on 15 years of operating Kraken, one of the world's largest crypto platforms. Through a single integration, partners can access crypto and tokenized equity trading, fiat and stablecoin payments, yield, lending, prediction markets and derivatives. Fintechs, banks, brokerages, payment providers, exchanges, consumer tech platforms and asset managers can use Payward Services to offer digital assets to their clients without building the stack themselves.

Liquidity, custody, payments, compliance, risk and settlement are integrated by design, replacing fragmented multi-vendor stacks with a single regulated foundation. Partners build on the same infrastructure that powers Payward's family of products, deployed as modular services they can scale alongside their own.

For more information, visit https://www.payward.com/payward-services.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **The deal:** Onyx Odds, a NY startup, raised a **$20M Series A** at a **$220M post-money** valuation. Led by **Payward** (parent of Kraken, and now of NinjaTrader + Bitnomial); **Xfund** and **Breyer Capital** also in. Announced via BusinessWire 2026-06-17; covered by The Block/Axios/CNBC-adjacent press ~2026-06-24; surfaced in StrictlyVC 2026-06-25.
- **Prior funding:** "less than $8M" before this round → so $220M post on $20M new = the round itself re-rated the company ~3x+ over a small base in under a year.
- **The PR frame ("exchange-traded contracts tied to game outcomes" / "prediction marketplace"):** this is the misleading part and the core of the red-team. Independent product reviews (Trustpilot, Deadspin, Dimers, TheLines, win.gg as of 2026) describe Onyx Odds **today as a SWEEPSTAKES sportsbook** — owned by **Onyx Games LLC**, running on two virtual currencies (**Onyx Coins** for free play, **Onyx Cash** awarded as bonus and redeemable for prizes), operating in 40+ states under US sweepstakes law, **NOT** a CFTC-regulated event-contract exchange. There is **no Onyx-held CFTC DCM/FCM** — unlike Kalshi, Polymarket (US), Crypto.com (CDNA), Gemini Predictions, or Novig (CFTC DCM approved 2026-06-16, see [[Over $5 billion bet on World Cup across Polymarket and Kalshi]]).
- **Why structured this way:** the deal's actual mechanism is in the fine print — Onyx will **integrate with Payward Services** to "power its prediction markets offering on Payward's fully licensed US derivatives stack and embed crypto trading inside the Onyx app." So the substance is: **Payward provides the regulatory rails (CFTC FCM + DCM via Bitnomial) and crypto, Onyx provides the consumer brand/distribution.** The "exchange-traded" language describes a *future* migration onto Payward's stack, not what Onyx is now. The PR anchors to a flattering future state and a flattering base ("nearly a million users… within its first year, on less than $8M").
- **Who's silent about what:** no GMV/volume disclosed (vs Kalshi/Polymarket which quote billions); "nearly a million users" with "industry-leading retention" — unverified, and "users" of a free sweepstakes app ≠ funded traders. Nothing on revenue, take-rate, or whether the sweepstakes-to-CFTC-contract migration is approved/live. BBB/Trustpilot carry withdrawal-friction complaints.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **CFTC-regulated event-contract exchanges:** Kalshi (~$22bn val, ~57% share, ~$17.9bn monthly volume May 2026 — [[Kalshi raises $1 billion Series F at $22 billion valuation]], [[Kalshi and Polymarket eye roughly $20 billion valuations]]); Polymarket (~$7.1bn monthly, re-rated toward $10–20bn — [[Polymarket nears $200M raise at $1 billion valuation]]); Crypto.com / CDNA "OG" ([[Crypto.com launches OG prediction market platform]], [[Crypto.com and Underdog partner on sports prediction markets]]); Gemini Predictions ([[Gemini Predictions unveils event contracts for sporting events]], [[Gemini moves to enter prediction markets]]); Robinhood+Kalshi ([[Robinhood and Kalshi launch football prediction markets]]); **Novig** — the closest direct comp: sports-focused, CFTC DCM approved 2026-06-16, $75M Series B (Pantera) at **$500M**, >$8bn annualized volume.
- **Distribution-layer peers** (apps that don't own the exchange): Robinhood, Webull, Underdog — they ride Kalshi/Polymarket rails. **Onyx sits here** but with a sweepstakes legacy, not a brokerage license.
- **Position:** Onyx is **catching up / niche** — a consumer-brand challenger with no exchange license of its own, valued at **$220M vs Novig $500M and Kalshi $22bn**. The cheaper sticker reflects that it is earlier and unlicensed-on-its-own; the bet is brand + Payward rails close the gap.
- **Why the lay of the land is this way (2nd order):** the sector has bifurcated into **rail owners** (own the CFTC DCM, capture exchange economics) and **distributors** (own the user, pay for access). Onyx is trying to **buy its way from distributor to rail-rider** via Payward rather than build a DCM — cheaper and faster, but it means the durable margin (exchange fees) accrues to Payward, not Onyx. The valuation gap to Novig is partly a licensing gap and partly a "house book vs peer-to-peer exchange" model gap.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Founded **2025** (PR says 2025; StrictlyVC said "two-year-old" — likely founded 2024, out of beta <1yr ago; treat founding as 2024–25, exact year (hypothesis)). Bootstrapped on <$8M, grew a sweepstakes sports-prediction app to a claimed ~1M users, then took its first institutional round.
- **Fit for Onyx:** logical — a sweepstakes book has a regulatory ceiling (state-by-state sweepstakes scrutiny is rising) and weaker monetization than a real-money/event-contract exchange. Migrating onto Payward's CFTC stack is the path from "fun-coin app" to "regulated trading product" without spending "years assembling vendors, licenses and operational capabilities."
- **Fit for Payward:** this is the **consumer front-end for the regulated-rails strategy** Payward has been assembling all year — Bitnomial acquisition for the CFTC-licensed derivatives stack ([[Payward acquires Bitnomial for CFTC-licensed derivatives stack]], [[Payward completes acquisition of derivatives platform Bitnomial]]), Reap for up to $600M ([[Kraken parent Payward to acquire Reap for up to $600 million]]), Deutsche Börse's $200M investment ([[Deutsche Borse invests $200M in Kraken parent Payward]]), Payward's own raise at **$20bn ahead of an IPO** ([[Kraken parent Payward raises capital at $20 billion valuation]]). Onyx is a **small ($20M) strategic stake** that seeds demand for Payward Services and validates the B2B-infrastructure narrative pre-IPO.
- **Why Payward acts this way:** Kraken's core crypto take-rate is commoditizing; it needs a multi-asset infrastructure story (crypto + tokenized equities + payments + **prediction markets**) to justify a $20bn/IPO multiple. Funding a consumer prediction app it also supplies is **vertical-integration-by-minority-stake** — it books a partner logo without consolidating the consumer risk.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Real novelty: low.** Sweepstakes sportsbooks are a crowded, well-trodden category; the prediction-market wrapper is the 2025–26 hype overlay. The genuinely new element is the **Payward integration** — a single CFTC-licensed stack + embedded crypto behind a consumer app. But that capability is **Payward's**, not Onyx's, and it is **announced, not live** (the PR says Onyx "will integrate," no go-live date).
- **Traction: unverified.** ~1M users and "industry-leading retention" are PR claims with no GMV/volume/revenue. For prediction markets the meaningful metric is **notional volume** (sector hit ~$21–30bn/month in 2026 per TRM/Sportico); Onyx quotes none. A free-coin user base does not equate to funded-contract volume.
- **Who captures the margin (the central question):** if Onyx migrates onto Payward Services, **Payward captures exchange/clearing/custody/crypto fees** (the durable, regulated margin) while Onyx keeps front-end/marketing economics and brand. So Onyx risks being a **customer-acquisition skin on Payward's rails** rather than the entity that owns the trading economics — the same disintermediation risk Robinhood/Webull face vs Kalshi, but here the "exchange" is also your lead investor. The $220M value rests on **brand + retention + an option on regulated migration**, not on owned infrastructure.
- **What breaks it:** sweepstakes legal crackdown (several states are scrutinizing sweeps sportsbooks); failure/delay of the CFTC migration; or commoditization as every neobank plugs into Kalshi/Polymarket/Payward and "prediction markets" becomes a feature, not a destination.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Plans:** integrate with Payward Services; launch prediction markets on the CFTC stack; embed crypto trading in-app. Watch for a go-live date and first volume disclosure.
- **Sector sentiment: hot but politically/legally contested.** Volume scaled to **~$21bn/month (2026, per TRM)**; >$5bn traded on the World Cup alone ([[Over $5 billion bet on World Cup across Polymarket and Kalshi]]). But: **Minnesota became the first state to ban prediction markets** ([[Minnesota becomes first US state to ban prediction markets]]); **Wisconsin (and other states) sued Kalshi/Coinbase/Polymarket/Robinhood/Crypto.com**; CFTC issued a prediction-markets advisory and is mid-rulemaking (ANPR Mar 2026); WSJ found **0.1% of Polymarket accounts capture 67% of profits** ([[WSJ finds 0.1% of Polymarket accounts capture 67% of profits]]) — a "casino, not a market" critique. Major leagues (NFL/NBA) still holdouts on licensing.
- **Why the market goes this way (2nd order):** federal (CFTC) vs state-gaming jurisdiction is the whole ballgame. If CFTC preemption holds, sweepstakes books like Onyx have every incentive to **convert to CFTC event contracts** (escape state gambling law) — which is exactly why the Payward rails matter. Counterintuitive risk: a **flood of look-alike apps onto the same few licensed rails commoditizes the front-end**, so today's "land grab" valuations (incl. Onyx's) compress unless a brand builds genuine network liquidity. Capital/regulatory concentration around a handful of DCMs makes the *distributors* fragile, not safe.
