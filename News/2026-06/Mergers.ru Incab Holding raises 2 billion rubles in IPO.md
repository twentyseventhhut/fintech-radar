# Context-enrichment: Incab Holding raises RUB 2 bln in Moscow Exchange IPO
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

> Note: this is NOT a fintech round — it is an industrial (fiber-optic cable) IPO that landed in the
> feed via the capital-markets / Moscow-Exchange angle. Weight should be judged as a capital-markets
> data point about the Russian primary market, not as a fintech story.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- On **24 June 2026** PJSC **Incab Holding** (Инкаб Холдинг), Russia's largest fiber-optic cable
  manufacturer (Perm), began trading on the Moscow Exchange (MOEX) at **RUB 100/share**, completing
  a book-built IPO that ran **16–23 June 2026**.
- **Proceeds: ~RUB 2.04 bln** (the round figure of "2 bln rubles" in the headline). This figure
  includes ~1.8 mln shares reserved for post-listing price stabilization. A **~20.3% stake** was
  placed; **post-money market cap >RUB 10 bln** (pre-money ~8–8.8 bln at the 100–110 indicative range).
- **This was a primary (cash-in) deal, not a cash-out:** the controlling shareholder did **not** sell
  and retained control. Stated use of proceeds = **deleveraging — paying down debt to Sberbank**
  (group debt cited at ~RUB 4 bln). So the IPO covers roughly **half** the bank debt.
- Financials anchored to **H1 2025 IFRS, LTM basis**: revenue **RUB 5.9 bln**, EBITDA **RUB 1.03 bln**,
  EBITDA margin **18.3%**. Capacity up to **~100k km of cable/year**.
- **Why structured this way (analysis):** a small (~20%) free float + no founder cash-out + proceeds
  earmarked to repay Sber is the textbook "rate-relief / balance-sheet repair" listing, not a
  growth-capital or liquidity event. At Russia's high policy-rate backdrop, RUB 4 bln of bank debt is
  expensive; equity is being raised mainly to swap costly debt for permanent capital. The small float
  also keeps valuation pricing tight and control intact — typical for the thin 2025–26 RU primary market.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Sector peers (RU fiber/cable):** Incab is the **#1 domestic optical-cable maker**; rivals are other
  RU cable plants and, critically, **Chinese fiber/cable imports**. Context that matters: in early 2026
  **China raised optical-fiber prices to Russia ~4x**, and a **domestic fiber plant was halted** (reported
  after Ukrainian strikes) — both tighten supply and arguably help a domestic integrator like Incab on
  pricing/volume (analysis; the input cost squeeze cuts both ways on margin).
- **IPO peers (RU primary market 2026, by size/timing):** the relevant comp set is small recent MOEX
  listings — e.g. **B2B-RTS** (also flagged an MOEX IPO, ~11.5% offered). Median RU IPO size in the
  period is **~RUB 2.5 bln**, so Incab at ~RUB 2 bln is a **typical small-cap** deal, not a flagship.
- **Position:** dominant in its niche domestically; as an IPO it is **mid-of-pack/small** and arrives
  into a hostile primary market (see [4]). **Why (analysis):** the RU listing pipeline is starved of
  size — most issuers are small industrials/financials repairing balance sheets, not scaling.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Group converted to **PJSC ("Incab Holding") on 24 April 2026**, the standard pre-IPO corporate
  re-org, then listed ~two months later. Customer base reads as the RU infrastructure backbone:
  **Rostelecom, MTS, MegaFon, Rosneft, Gazprom** — telco + energy.
- **Fit (analysis):** the listing fits the company's logic — a mid-margin (18% EBITDA) industrial with
  heavy bank debt in a high-rate economy needs **permanent capital to cut interest expense**. The
  "import-substitution + AI/datacenter fiber demand" narrative (heavily used in RU press) is the growth
  hook; the actual cash use is **deleveraging**, which is more defensive than growth-oriented.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty: low.** This is a conventional small industrial IPO; nothing technologically or
  structurally new. The only "newness" is corporate (a private champion becomes public).
- **Real traction (the substance):** **completed** placement with live trading from 24 June — i.e.
  it actually priced and listed (in a market where many deals stall), raising the full ~RUB 2 bln.
  That is genuine, not a "plans to" announcement. EBITDA of RUB 1.03 bln and a real, named customer
  base are concrete.
- **Where value is captured (analysis):** at ~RUB 10 bln cap on RUB 1.03 bln EBITDA, the implied
  EV/EBITDA is roughly low-double-digit before adding net debt — not cheap for a cyclical, capital-
  intensive cable maker exposed to Chinese input costs. The durable value is the **domestic
  supply-security / import-substitution** position; the fragile part is **margin**, squeezed between
  rising Chinese fiber input prices and customer (telco/energy) bargaining power. Disintermediation
  risk is low (physical product), commoditization risk is real (cable is a commodity).

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Backdrop is poor for new listings:** per a March 2026 CBR review, **only 7 of 19** RU IPOs of the
  prior two years stayed above offer price; 2025 IPOs fell on average ~5.2% day-1 and ~8.7% within a
  month; **retail participation fell ~4.5x** YoY. Sberbank expects **at least 5 IPOs in 2026**, with
  recovery hinging on **rate cuts**.
- **Outlook (analysis):** Incab's after-market performance will likely track the **policy-rate path**
  more than its own fundamentals — a deleveraging-driven small-cap in a weak primary market. Second-order:
  because proceeds repay debt rather than fund expansion, the equity story depends on **rates falling
  AND fiber demand (datacenter/AI, 5G, import-substitution) holding** while Chinese input costs don't
  crush the 18% margin. Counterintuitively, the **strategic-asset / supply-security** framing may give
  it more downside protection (and possible state/anchor demand) than a typical small-cap consumer IPO.
- **Risks:** thin float = low liquidity/volatility; cyclical demand; FX/input-cost exposure to China;
  customer concentration in state-linked telco/energy.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Two distinct sectors meet here: the Russian primary equity market (the listing) and RU optical-fiber/cable manufacturing (the business). (i) **RU primary market 2026** is thin and back-loaded: 2025 delivered only **4 IPOs raising ~RUB 30.6 bln** vs an expected 15–20 (JetLend, DOM.RF, GloraX, Basis); 2026 forecasts run **up to ~20 deals / RUB 50–100 bln** (per Expert RA, via Investfunds/Vedomosti, Mar 2026), but consensus is the real wave only comes in **H2 2026 and only if the CBR cuts the key rate** — H1 was effectively paused. So Incab listed *into* a weak, pre-recovery window. (ii) **RU optical-fiber demand is structurally strong but import-dependent:** datacenter capacity expanding **~15–20%/yr**, RU consuming ~10.5% of global fiber output (~60 mln km) in 2025 (per ico-optics/United24, 2026); the cable market is projected **+5–7% nominal in 2026** (per IndexBox). Why now: Russia's only fiber plant (OVS, Mordovia) is **shut**, localization mandates were **postponed to 2028**, and Chinese fiber prices jumped **2.5–4x** (~$2.33→$5.80/km, early 2025→Jan 2026, with full prepayment demanded). This is the double edge — it lifts a domestic cable integrator's pricing/volume but **squeezes its input costs and ties up cash**.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: EBITDA margin, capacity utilization (Incab ~100k km/yr), and net-debt/EBITDA (the actual reason to list). Players: Incab is the **#1 domestic optical-cable maker**; the binding competitor is **Chinese fiber/cable imports** (RU fiber-cable still 60–75% imported per IndexBox), not other RU plants. Recent moves: indicative range **RUB 100–110** set 15 Jun 2026; book ran 16–23 Jun; priced at **RUB 100 — the bottom of the range** (soft demand); first trade **24 Jun 2026**; Incab also built a submarine-cable plant in Primorsky Krai (~RUB 600 mln, completed Jun 2026). Position: **dominant niche / small-cap listing.** Moat (analysis): import-substitution + supply-security with state-linked customers (Rostelecom, MTS, MegaFon, Rosneft, Gazprom) — a regulatory/strategic-asset moat, not a tech or network-effect one; commoditization risk is real.

**Comps & multiples.** *Internal comps:* the corpus has **no comparable Russian IPO** — `sem search` returned only unrelated EMEA/India/MENA listings ([[Connecting the Dots Four European fintech deals raise €31 million]], [[Cash Plus IPO draws 81,466 subscribers, 15.5% public float]], [[NSE board approves long-awaited IPO plan after SEBI nod]], [[GCash operator Mynt eyes $8 billion valuation in Philippine IPO]]) — none are cable/RU comparables, so no peer-multiple set is computable from the base. *External arithmetic (verifiable):* post-money cap ≈ **RUB 10 bln** (RUB 100 × ~100 mln shares implied by ~20.3% float on RUB 2.04 bln). On note's LTM EBITDA RUB 1.03 bln → **P/EBITDA ≈ 10 / 1.03 = ~9.7x**, and with ~RUB 4 bln gross debt **EV/EBITDA ≈ (10 + 4) / 1.03 = ~13.6x** (gross, pre-cash) — **rich** for a cyclical, capital-intensive commodity cable maker (sanity range EV/EBITDA ~8–25x; the rich read is the margin/cyclicality, not the absolute level). Caveat: company guides **EBITDA doubling to ~RUB 2.2 bln by end-2026** (per Finam/T-Investments coverage); on that *forward* number EV/EBITDA ≈ **~6.4x** — but that is unverified guidance `[UNSOURCED]` and a near-100% growth assumption, so the trailing ~13.6x is the defensible figure. Note also a financial-base inconsistency in sources: 2024 revenue cited as **RUB 6.08 bln** (EBITDA RUB 1.06 bln) and 2025 revenue **~RUB 5.0 bln** — i.e. revenue may have *fallen* YoY, which sits awkwardly with the doubling-EBITDA guidance. No public, free P/E available.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Priced at the bottom of the range (RUB 100) into a paused H1 primary market** — signals soft institutional demand; after-market is likely to track the **CBR rate path**, not fundamentals, and the broad RU IPO recovery itself is only forecast (back-loaded to H2). Second-order: poor debut risks reinforcing retail withdrawal from RU IPOs.
2. **Input-cost / FX squeeze on an 18% margin** — the same China fiber shock (+2.5–4x, full prepayment) that supports domestic demand inflates Incab's COGS and working capital; if it imports fiber, the margin (and thus the ~13.6x EV/EBITDA) is vulnerable, and the FY2024→2025 revenue dip suggests the demand tailwind is not yet flowing through.
3. **Partial deleveraging + thin float** — ~RUB 2 bln proceeds repay only ~half of ~RUB 4 bln Sber debt, so leverage stays material at a high policy rate; ~20% float with control retained means **structurally low liquidity / high volatility**, and customer concentration in state-linked telco/energy caps pricing power.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) Incab is a **minor confirming data point that the RU primary market is open-but-weak and deleveraging-driven**, not recovering — a low-end-priced small industrial swapping costly bank debt for equity, the textbook high-rate balance-sheet-repair listing. Falsifiable thesis: this is *not* the start of the 2026 IPO wave; the real test is **whether the forecast H2 wave actually fires on CBR rate cuts** and whether marquee names (Rostelecom, Evraz, VK Tech, MTS units) list. Trigger to watch: Incab's first-month after-market vs offer (RU 2025 cohort averaged ~–8.7% in a month) and the actual FY2026 EBITDA print vs the RUB 2.2 bln guidance — a miss breaks the cheap-forward-multiple case; a real rate-cut-driven H2 pipeline would be the bull confirmation. Not a fintech event — judge as capital-markets signal only.
