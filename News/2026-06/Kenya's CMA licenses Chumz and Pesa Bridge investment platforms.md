# Kenya's CMA licenses Chumz and Pesa Bridge investment platforms

The Capital Markets Authority of Kenya has licensed two fintech firms as Intermediary Service Platform Providers, clearing Moneto Ventures Limited and Pesa Bridge Limited to connect retail investors to regulated collective investment schemes through digital channels.
 • The approvals add to a growing roster of licensed ISPPs, now numbering seven, since the license category was created. 
 • The ISPP licence class was introduced under the 2023 CIS Regulations to bring fintech aggregators into direct regulatory oversight. 
 • Jipay Payment Solutions and Sycamore Capital's Cashlet app were licensed in September 2025, followed by Safaricom and Airtel Money in December 2025, and I&M Capital in February 2026. 
Moneto Ventures operates Chumz, a mobile savings and investment app that functions as a collective investment scheme intermediary platform, enabling investors to save and invest towards specific goals with a minimum of KSh 5.
The app was developed in partnership with Nabo Capital and allows users with a mobile money account to create individual or group financial goals, track progress and earn interest. Moneto Ventures was admitted to the CMA Regulatory Sandbox in May 2021 and became the sixth firm to exit the programme before receiving its full ISPP licence on 26 May 2026.
Pesa Bridge Limited operates a mobile-first digital infrastructure designed to bridge retail investors with licensed fund managers and custodians, with a focus on digitising access to and distribution of collective investment schemes across Kenya and the wider East African region.
Previously, such platforms operated through partnership arrangements with licensed intermediaries, leaving the CMA one step removed from the entity holding the customer relationship. The new framework requires aggregator platforms to hold their own CMA authorisation.
