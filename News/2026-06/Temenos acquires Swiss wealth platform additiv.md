# Temenos acquires wealth management fintech

Geneva-headquartered banking technology specialist **Temenos** has entered a definitive agreement to buy **additiv AG**, a Switzerland-based fintech company that provides a specialist platform to orchestrate financial services, for an undisclosed price. Aimed at strengthening the purchaser’s offering in wealth management, the roughly half cash, half equity deal is expected to close during Q3 2026. Financially, the acquisition is expected to be marginally accretive to annual recurring revenue (ARR) but neutral on EBIT, EPS, and free cash flow for FY26.

Founded in 1998, additiv’s technology helps banks and wealth managers to design and launch wealth propositions. Based in Zurich and with 10 offices around the world including in the UK, the company employs c. 200 personnel worldwide. The fintech has 30 clients globally and counts AXA, NatWest and RBS among its customers. additiv is described as having delivered strong double-digit ARR growth over the last three years.

The deal is framed around three strategic pillars: deepening Temenos' wealth management proposition, expanding AI-driven orchestration across complex customer journeys, and strengthening the "experience layer" on top of company's core banking platform. The absence of any declared purchase price multiple, however, makes it impossible to assess whether Temenos is paying a fair price for its latest acquisition.

The integration of additiv should, nonetheless, add to the momentum Temenos has been steadily building under its new leadership. Having raised its FY28 targets back in February 2026, the company opened FY26 with a positive set of numbers, posting ARR growth of 13% with product revenue growth of 14%. Profitability also moved in the right direction, with EBIT up 20% and with two percentage points of margin (see here). Progress in the key US market has, however, been slower of late. Addressing this challenge is the test facing Temenos as it pursues its objective of generating $1.23bn ARR (FY25: $860m) in three years’ time.
