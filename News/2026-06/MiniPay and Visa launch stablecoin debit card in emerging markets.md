MiniPay and Visa launch stablecoin debit card in emerging markets
Sinziana Albu
25 Jun 2026 / 5 Min Read
MiniPay has launched a Visa debit card powered by Gnosis Pay, connecting stablecoin users in emerging markets to global commerce.
The digital debit card allows eligible users across the European Economic Area, Africa, Latin America, and Southeast Asia to spend stablecoin balances at merchant locations in 46 countries, with no cryptocurrency knowledge required at the point of sale. Merchants receive payment in their local currency, and no crypto-specific setup is needed on their end.
The card is issued through Monavate's regulated card issuing platform and operates on Gnosis Pay's infrastructure, which connects MiniPay's stablecoin balances to Visa's global payment network. Gnosis Pay also serves as programme manager, overseeing the technical and compliance layer across multiple markets. Users can add the card to Apple Pay and Google Pay for contactless mobile payments. The card carries no monthly or annual fees, with transactions subject to a nominal foreign exchange fee.
Building on a growing stablecoin payments ecosystem
According to the official press release, the demand for faster, lower-friction ways to save, send, and spend has grown in emerging markets, where users are increasingly holding value in stablecoins but have faced limited access to global payment infrastructure. The MiniPay Card addresses that gap directly.
The launch follows the earlier introduction of Virtual Bank Accounts, a feature that allows users to receive money into their wallets in stablecoins. The card extends that capability, enabling users to spend those balances in person or online at more than 175 million merchant locations wherever Visa is accepted. MiniPay already integrates with local payment rails, including M-Pesa in Kenya, OPay in Nigeria, and Mercado Pago in Latin America.
 Friederike Ernst , founder of Gnosis, described the card programme as addressing a key gap in stablecoin usability: giving users in markets such as Nigeria or Kenya the ability to spend digital dollar holdings at checkout in another country, without any requirement for merchants to engage with cryptocurrency.
 Marek Olszewski , CEO of Celo Core and co-founder of the Celo blockchain on which MiniPay is built, said the launch demonstrates the blockchain's capacity to support real-world stablecoin adoption at scale, with Visa and Gnosis Pay now part of the Celo ecosystem.
In selected markets, the MiniPay Card will offer cashback in digital assets, including Tether Gold (XAUt0), USDT, and USDC.

# Context-enrichment: MiniPay and Visa launch stablecoin debit card in emerging markets
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 2026-06-23 Opera/MiniPay announced the **MiniPay Card**, a *digital* (virtual) Visa debit card that lets MiniPay's stablecoin wallet users spend balances at Visa merchants. The Paypers picked it up 2026-06-25. The actual stack, de-PR'd:
- **Visa is the network, not the builder.** The card is **issued via Monavate** (regulated card issuer / Visa principal), runs on **Gnosis Pay's** infrastructure, and **Gnosis Pay is the programme manager** (technical + compliance layer). "MiniPay and Visa launch…" is a brand framing; Visa supplies acceptance rails it already sells to 130+ stablecoin card programmes.
- **Scope is narrower than the merchant count implies.** Headline "175M+ merchants" = the global Visa estate, but spend is enabled for **eligible users in 46 countries** across EEA, Africa, LatAm, SE Asia — i.e. *acceptance* is global, *issuance/eligibility* is selective.
- **Fees:** no monthly/annual fee; a "nominal" FX fee (unquantified — see red-team). Cashback in **XAUt0 (Tether Gold), USDT, USDC** in *selected* markets only.
- **Funding asset is vague.** MiniPay holds USDT/USDC/cUSD; PR doesn't state which is debited or the on-chain settlement asset. Gnosis Pay's own card settles via EURe/GBPe/bridged USDC on Gnosis Chain through Monerium e-money rails — MiniPay is Celo-native, so a bridge/conversion step is implied but undocumented (analysis).
- **Why framed this way:** the press anchors on Visa + the 175M figure because the real differentiator (a 16M-wallet distribution base in emerging markets) is the asset, while the plumbing is commodity (Gnosis Pay/Monavate). The card extends an existing funnel: it follows MiniPay's **Virtual Bank Accounts** (receive in stablecoins) — receive → now spend.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Stablecoin-linked Visa cards are a **crowded 2026 category**, not a first:
- **Visa + Bridge (Stripe)** — expansion to **100+ countries** announced 2026-03-03 (live in 18), self-custody wallets (MetaMask, Phantom) [[Visa expands stablecoin reach across CEMEA with Yellow Card]] context. This is the most direct, larger-footprint rival.
- **Gnosis Pay's own card** — same infra MiniPay rents; self-custodial Visa, ~0.5% FX, GNO cashback.
- **Coinbase Card** (Visa, USDC/BTC rewards, 3% FX), **Rain** (~$3B annualized, Western Union remittances, $250M Series C Jan 2026 at ~$1.95B), **Reap** (~$6B annualized, Asia/ME corporate). **Quantoz** became a direct Visa principal Feb 2026 [[Quantoz partners Visa to make stablecoins spendable]].
- **Mastercard** runs a parallel 85+ firm crypto partner programme.
- **Position:** MiniPay is **catching up on product** (card is table-stakes by mid-2026) but **ahead on emerging-market distribution** — 16M wallets in Africa/LatAm/SEA that rivals (mostly crypto-native or developed-market) lack. Visa itself flagged emerging-market/cross-border stablecoins as the opportunity [[Visa Stablecoins Represent Opportunities in Emerging Markets, Cross-Border Money Movement]].

## [2] Company history / fit
- MiniPay launched **Sep 2023** in Nigeria (Opera + Celo + Mento), passed **1M** users early on, **8M wallets / 200M tx** by Jul 2025 [[MiniPay surpasses 8 million wallets and 200 million transactions]], **15M wallets** by May 2026 (+123% YoY) [[MiniPay reaches 15 million wallets on African adoption]], **~16M** at card launch.
- Opera–Celo partnership deepened Dec 2025 with a Q1 2026 roadmap to make MiniPay "a gateway to both traditional payment infrastructure and onchain utility" — the card is the explicit execution of that roadmap [[Opera and Celo extend partnership to scale stablecoin payments]]. The XAUt0 cashback ties to the Tether Gold integration noted there (~30k users in first weeks).
- Local rails already integrated: **M-Pesa (Kenya), OPay (Nigeria), Mercado Pago (LatAm)**, plus **Noah** for cross-border [[Noah partners Opera to bring stablecoins to MiniPay users]].
- **Why:** MiniPay's value is held-balance utility. A non-custodial wallet with no spend leg leaks users to cash-out via local off-ramps; the card closes the loop and monetizes via FX without touching banking rails directly.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Genuinely new here:** very little technologically — stablecoin→Visa cards exist widely (Gnosis Pay, Bridge, Coinbase). The novelty is **applying that to a mass emerging-market base at near-zero card fees** with no crypto knowledge at POS.
- **Real value-add:** distribution + UX, not rails. The defensible asset is the 16M wallets and local on/off-ramp integrations; Gnosis Pay/Monavate/Visa are rentable commodities.
- **Traction caveat:** this is a **launch, not adoption** — zero card activations, GMV, or active-card numbers disclosed. Wallet count ≠ card uptake; emerging-market debit conversion is unproven. "Digital/virtual" card (no physical issuance mentioned) further limits in-person reach in low-smartphone-penetration segments (analysis).
- **Margin question (why it matters):** in this stack, **who captures the margin?** Visa takes interchange/network fees, Monavate the issuing economics, Gnosis Pay the programme-manager cut. MiniPay/Opera monetize a "nominal FX fee" — a thin take-rate. So the card risks making MiniPay a **distribution front-end for Gnosis Pay/Visa rails** rather than the value capturer — the central question shifts from "did they launch a card" to "can a thin-FX wallet earn enough per spend to justify the funnel, vs. just keeping users in higher-margin remittance/on-ramp flows."

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Roadmap: extends Virtual Bank Accounts; cashback expansion; deeper Celo/Visa ecosystem integration. Expect physical card and more of the 65+ countries over time (plans, not committed).
- **Market backdrop:** Visa-issued crypto card spend reportedly +525% in 2025; Visa supports 130+ stablecoin card programmes in 40+ countries — strong tailwind, but also **commoditization**: by end-2026 a stablecoin card is undifferentiated, so winners are decided by distribution and unit economics, exactly where MiniPay is strong/weak respectively.
- **Risks:** (1) regulatory — stablecoin/e-money rules across 46 jurisdictions (MiCA in EEA, fragmented Africa/LatAm); (2) FX-fee transparency vs. local off-ramp competition; (3) fraud/chargeback liability on non-custodial balances (silent in PR); (4) Bridge/Stripe's 100-country push could out-scale MiniPay outside Africa.
- **Second-order:** the more these cards commoditize, the more **Visa/Mastercard win regardless of which wallet brands the card** — the card race entrenches the incumbent networks it was rhetorically meant to disrupt.

## Top challenge / red-team questions

1. **Is this actually "MiniPay and Visa," or MiniPay renting commodity rails?** Visa = acceptance network; the card is issued by **Monavate** and managed by **Gnosis Pay**. The Visa-co-brand framing inflates the story. (answered — analysis)
2. **Which stablecoin is actually debited, and what's the on-chain settlement asset?** PR lists USDT/USDC/cUSD held in-wallet but never states the spend/settlement asset; Gnosis Pay normally settles via EURe/GBPe/bridged USDC on Gnosis Chain — implies a Celo→Gnosis bridge step. **Open.**
3. **What is the "nominal FX fee" in numbers?** Unquantified in all sources; comparable cards run 0.5%–3%. Material for emerging-market users vs. local off-ramps. **Open.**
4. **Card activations / active cards / spend GMV?** None disclosed — this is a launch. 16M wallets ≠ card adoption. **Open.**
5. **Physical or virtual-only?** Sources say "digital" card + Apple/Google Pay; no physical issuance mentioned, which limits in-person reach where mobile-wallet acceptance is thin. **Open.**
6. **Who carries fraud/chargeback liability on non-custodial balances?** Silent in PR — a real gap for a self-custody product on a chargeback network. **Open.**
7. **Eligibility: which of the 46 countries are live now vs. "coming"?** "Eligible users in selected markets" — selection criteria and live list undisclosed. **Open.**
8. **How does this beat Visa+Bridge (Stripe), 100+ countries, March 2026?** Bridge has wider footprint and self-custody support; MiniPay's edge is African/EM distribution only. (answered — partial)
9. **Where does MiniPay capture margin if Visa/Monavate/Gnosis Pay each take a cut?** Only a thin FX fee remains — does card spend earn more than keeping users in remittance/on-ramp flows? **Open / central.**
10. **Is the cashback (XAUt0/USDT/USDC) a real incentive or a subsidy with a sunset?** "Selected markets," no rate or duration. Likely promotional. **Open.**
11. **Regulatory exposure across 46 jurisdictions (MiCA in EEA + fragmented Africa/LatAm/SEA)?** Compliance is Gnosis Pay's job per PR, but cross-border stablecoin-card legality is uneven. **Open.**
12. **Does the card cannibalize MiniPay's existing M-Pesa/OPay/Mercado Pago off-ramp revenue?** Could shift volume from local rails to Visa rails — net economics unclear. (analysis)
13. **What's the prior art and first announcement?** Celo–Opera Dec 2025 roadmap explicitly pre-announced "gateway to traditional payment infrastructure" — the card is execution of a 6-month-old plan, not a surprise. (answered)
14. **Does Visa's 130+ stablecoin-card programmes / +525% 2025 spend mean commoditization?** Yes — by mid-2026 a stablecoin card is table-stakes; winners decided by distribution + unit economics. (answered)
15. **Second-order: who really wins?** As cards commoditize, **Visa/Mastercard capture value regardless of wallet brand** — the "disrupt Visa" narrative entrenches Visa. (analysis)

**Importance: 3/5** — rationale: Solid, logically-consistent execution of a known roadmap by a genuinely large EM wallet (~16M), and the distribution base is a real differentiator. BUT: low technical novelty (stablecoin-Visa cards are a crowded 2026 category — Bridge, Gnosis Pay, Coinbase, Rain, Reap), zero adoption/GMV disclosed (launch not traction), MiniPay rents commodity rails and captures only a thin FX margin, and bigger-footprint rival (Visa+Bridge, 100+ countries) is already moving. Notable for EM stablecoin adoption and as a marker of category maturity, not a structural shift — hence 3, not higher.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.**
- **Size/growth (stablecoin cards):** crypto/stablecoin card spend reached **~$18bn annualized** by Jan 2026, up from ~$100m/month in early 2023 (~106% CAGR), now rivalling P2P stablecoin transfers (~$19bn) — per Artemis, via CoinDesk (2026-01-16). Independent corner: **Visa-issued** stablecoin-linked card spend specifically hit a **$3.5bn annualized run rate in Q4 FY2025, ~+460% YoY**; Visa total stablecoin *settlement* hit **$4.5bn annualized** by Feb 2026 — still a rounding error vs Visa's **$14.2tn** 2025 payments volume (`$4.5bn / $14.2tn ≈ 0.03%`) [[Visa stablecoin settlement reaches $4.5bn annualized run rate]]. Forward "~$30bn by end-2026" is an analyst projection — `(hypothesis)`, treat as directional.
- **EM demand (the actual thesis):** stablecoins solve a real inflation/dollar-access problem in MiniPay's footprint. Nigeria processed **~$26bn** stablecoin volume in 2024 (Chainalysis, via search); Argentina **~$34bn** (TRM, 2024) with 67% cross-border. Prior art for the exact MiniPay move exists — **Lemon Cash** already runs a Visa USDC debit card in Argentina. So the demand is sourced and real, but the *card* leg is not a new idea even within EM.
- **Structure:** the stablecoin-card stack is a **3-layer commodity chain** — (1) network (Visa/Mastercard), (2) program manager + issuer (Gnosis Pay/Monavate, Rain, Bridge), (3) consumer brand/distribution (MiniPay, Coinbase, Lemon). Value is migrating to layers (1) and (3); layer (2) is rentable. Cards dominate spend because they need **zero new merchant integration** (Artemis) — that is the barrier-of-entry inversion: incumbency of Visa acceptance, not crypto tech, is the moat.
- **Why now:** mass EM stablecoin held-balances + Visa actively reselling settlement rails to 130+ programmes = a land-grab window before the category fully commoditizes (which the note's [4] already calls for end-2026).

**Competitive landscape.**
- **Sector KPIs:** card spend GMV / annualized run-rate, active-card count, FX take-rate (the unit economics), and attach rate (cards ÷ wallets). **MiniPay disclosed none** of these at launch — `[UNSOURCED]`; only the 16M wallet base is given (denominator without numerator).
- **Players & basis of competition (distribution + footprint, not tech):**
  - **Visa+Bridge (Stripe)** — 100+ country plan (2026-03), self-custody wallets; widest footprint, the direct over-scaler [[Visa and Bridge expand stablecoin cards to 100+ countries]].
  - **Rain** — pure program-manager layer, **$250m Series C at $1.95bn post-money** (Jan 2026, led by Iconiq; ~$338m raised total), now adding Mastercard issuance (Fortune 2026-05-04). This is the closest *valued* comp to MiniPay's rented layer.
  - **Quantoz** — became a **direct Visa principal** (Feb 2026) [[Quantoz partners Visa to make stablecoins spendable]]; **Tangem+Paera** USDC Visa card (Nov 2025) [[Tangem launches USDC Visa card with Paera]]; Coinbase Card, Reap, Wirex/Pine Labs.
  - **Mastercard** runs a parallel 85+ partner crypto programme.
- **MiniPay's position:** **niche-leader on EM distribution, follower on product.** Catching up technically (card = table-stakes by mid-2026), genuinely ahead on a 16M-wallet Africa/LatAm/SEA base + local rails (M-Pesa/OPay/Mercado Pago) that crypto-native rivals lack. Moat = **distribution/network + on/off-ramp integrations (intangible)**, NOT the rails it rents.

**Comps & multiples.**
- **External — Rain** (closest comp, the layer MiniPay rents from Gnosis Pay): **$1.95bn post-money round valuation** (NOT a market cap), Jan 2026. No public revenue/run-rate → **revenue multiple = no data**. Useful only as a magnitude anchor: the *rented infra layer* alone is privately valued ~$2bn, which underscores how little of the value sits at MiniPay's thin-FX consumer layer.
- **Internal comps (corpus):** [[Visa and Bridge expand stablecoin cards to 100+ countries]] · [[Visa stablecoin settlement reaches $4.5bn annualized run rate]] · [[Quantoz partners Visa to make stablecoins spendable]] · [[Tangem launches USDC Visa card with Paera]]. None carry a public multiple.
- **MiniPay itself:** no round/valuation disclosed (Opera-owned). **No valuation, no GMV, no take-rate → multiples not computable.** Distribution not computed (<3 comparable figures); qualitative only. **FX-fee benchmark:** peers run **0.5% (Gnosis Pay) to 3% (Coinbase)**; MiniPay's "nominal FX fee" is **unquantified** [[note red-team Q3]] — the single most important undisclosed number, since FX is MiniPay's only revenue line.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Value capture is structurally thin.** Visa (interchange) + Monavate (issuing) + Gnosis Pay (program-manager cut) each take a slice; MiniPay keeps only a "nominal FX fee." Second-order: MiniPay risks being a *distribution front-end* for rails it doesn't own — and may cannibalize its own higher-margin M-Pesa/OPay off-ramp revenue (note [3]/Q12). With Rain's *infra layer* valued ~$2bn, the economics clearly favor the layer MiniPay rents, not the layer it operates.
2. **Launch ≠ adoption + scaling rival.** Zero card activations/GMV disclosed against 16M wallets; debit conversion in EM is unproven, and virtual-only issuance limits in-person reach where mobile-wallet acceptance is thin. Meanwhile Visa+Bridge's 100+ country push can out-scale MiniPay outside Africa before it converts its base.
3. **Multi-jurisdiction regulatory + fraud exposure.** 46 jurisdictions (MiCA in EEA + fragmented Africa/LatAm/SEA); chargeback/fraud liability on **non-custodial** balances is silent in the PR — a genuine gap, since a self-custody product on a chargeback network has no obvious party absorbing reversal risk. Project Syndicate (2026-02) flags monetary-sovereignty pushback in EMs, a regulatory tail risk to the whole EM-stablecoin-spend thesis.

**What this changes (idea-lens).**
This is a **category-maturity marker, not a structural shift** (analysis): as stablecoin cards commoditize toward ~$30bn 2026 run-rate, Visa/Mastercard capture value *regardless* of which wallet brands the card — the "disrupt Visa" framing entrenches Visa. Falsifiable thesis: **MiniPay's edge is distribution, so the only metric that validates it is attach rate × FX-margin per active card, not wallet count.** Trigger to watch: first disclosed active-card / GMV figure and the quantified FX fee; if attach stays low or FX is undercut by local off-ramps, the card is a feature, not a business. What breaks the thesis: MiniPay publishing strong card-spend GMV with a defensible take-rate, or becoming a Visa principal itself (removing the Gnosis Pay/Monavate margin leak).
