# Context-enrichment: Linas Newsletter — JPMorgan, Goldman pull Claude from employee toolkits
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS VERDICT: STALE (duplicate).** This Linas Newsletter item (2026-06-28) re-frames an event already enriched in the corpus as [[JPMorgan Chase blocks Anthropic AI access for Hong Kong staff]] (2026-06-19), which itself folded in Goldman's April 2026 block. The Linas piece adds a punchy "Wall Street banks are now America's AI border guards" framing plus unrelated bonus content (GLM-5.2 open-weights model, a "Claude Fable 5 Lite on Opus 4.8" jailbreak how-to), but the **core news — JPMorgan & Goldman removing Claude from employee toolkits — is the same event, no new terms or new period.** Treat as a commentary re-run, not a new development.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
The substance is unchanged from the prior note: both banks removed Anthropic's **Claude models from an internal drop-down of approved LLMs** — Goldman in **April 2026** (Bloomberg, ~2026-04-29) for its Hong Kong bankers, JPMorgan in **June 2026** (FT/American Banker, ~2026-06-18) likewise for Hong Kong staff. **This is geography-scoped (Greater China / Hong Kong), not a firm-wide ban.** The Linas headline ("pulled Claude models from its employees' toolkits") drops the Hong Kong qualifier, which inflates the scope — a marketing simplification to make it read as a sweeping Wall-Street-vs-Anthropic story.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the trigger is **contractual, not regulatory** — both banks applied a strict reading of Anthropic's usage policy, which bars use in Greater China (Hong Kong/Macau included). No SFC/HKMA directive ordered this; Goldman reportedly consulted Anthropic and reached the same conclusion. OpenAI (GPT) and Google (Gemini) stayed enabled in the same toolkits, which is the tell: this is a vendor-terms compliance action against one provider's policy, not a blanket "no Western AI in HK" stance. JPMorgan's HK staff continue on OpenAI models via the bank's own **LLM Suite** portal (230,000+ employees). The "AI border guards" framing is the Linas value-add — banks self-segregating AI access by geography — and that framing is genuinely the interesting second-order point, but the underlying event is old.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **OpenAI is the structural beneficiary.** Every Anthropic access-restriction episode in 2026 has been a subsidy to OpenAI: HK staff fall back to GPT in LLM Suite; OpenAI separately offered GPT-5.5 Cyber to nine UK banks that lost Anthropic's Mythos ([[OpenAI offers nine UK banks access to GPT-5.5 Cyber model]]). Pattern repeats — banks swap Claude → OpenAI rather than losing capability.
- **Google (Gemini)** also stays enabled, a secondary beneficiary.
- **Chinese alternatives in-region:** HSBC HK, Hang Seng, BOC HK etc. test Baidu/Alibaba Cloud GenAI in the HKMA sandbox — so the HK enterprise-AI market is bifurcating into "US models for US-bank-controlled workloads" vs "Chinese models for local players," with Anthropic structurally squeezed out of the Asian bank-enterprise segment.
- **Note (analysis):** the Linas "bonus" content on GLM-5.2 (a strong open-weights model) is itself a competitive data point — open Chinese models are the substitute local banks reach for, reinforcing the bifurcation thesis.

## [2] Company history / fit
Anthropic's enterprise push into financial services is real and recent: **Claude for Financial Services** (Jul 2025), **Goldman deploying Claude agents** for trade accounting & client onboarding ([[Goldman Sachs deploys Anthropic Claude AI agents]], Feb 2026, embedded engineers, 6-month build), LSEG/Intuit partnerships. The HK block is the **paradox in the corpus**: the same banks deepening US Claude deployments are cutting Claude in Asia. This fits a broader 2026 arc of Anthropic access friction — the US export-control directive on Fable 5/Mythos 5 ([[Trump walks back calling Anthropic a national security threat]]), the Pentagon blacklisting, and Anthropic's own aggressive account suspensions ([[Anthropic suspends 60+ Claude accounts, disrupting fintech Belo]]). Anthropic's safety-first / restrictive-policy DNA is simultaneously its enterprise selling point and the source of its access fragility.

- A sharper editorial frame ("AI border guards" — banks as de facto enforcers of US AI geo-policy). (analysis) This is a real second-order observation but it's commentary on a known fact, not a new fact.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Watch for contagion** to other Asian offices of Western banks — the contractual logic (Anthropic's unsupported-regions policy) applies wherever Greater-China exposure exists. (hypothesis)
- **Anthropic IPO backdrop:** confidential filing ~Jun 2026, ~$965bn target valuation, ~$47bn ARR (per StartupHub/Fortune, mid-June 2026). Asian enterprise-AI exclusion plus the US export-control overhang are real prospectus risk factors, though the *direct* revenue at stake from HK bank toolkits is almost certainly immaterial.

The June US export-control directive on Fable 5/Mythos 5 is a *separate* restriction track; the Linas "border guards" frame risks merging two distinct stories.

9. **Contagion risk?** Plausible to Singapore/Tokyo/Dubai given the same contractual logic, but unconfirmed — open. (hypothesis)

10. **Who benefits?** OpenAI (GPT fallback in LLM Suite; the recurring 2026 pattern), Google (Gemini), and Chinese models (GLM-5.2, Baidu/Alibaba) for local HK banks. Every Anthropic access episode is an OpenAI subsidy.
