IRIS Payments connects Greece with European EuroPA network
Sinziana Albu
30 Jun 2026 / 5 Min Read
DIAS S.A. has connected Greece's IRIS Payments service with the European Payments Alliance, known as EuroPA.
Starting June 30, 2026, IRIS Payments users in Greece can make instant cross-border money transfers using only a recipient's mobile phone number, without entering an IBAN. The connection was implemented by DIAS S.A., Greece's national interbank payment clearing institution, through its participation in the EuroPA initiative.
Cross-border reach across five countries
For decades, Europe’s fragmented landscape has stood in stark contrast with the dominance of the Visa and Mastercard duopoly. Now, with the rise of instant payments, regulatory momentum, and the ambition to strengthen European payment authority, a new chapter is unfolding.
According to an interview with The Paypers , a t the centre of this transformation stands EuroPA – the European Payments Alliance – a coalition of national payments providers working to connect Europe’s domestic champions into a single, interoperable framework. What began as a southern European initiative has, within two years, evolved into a continent-wide collaboration that now covers 10 countries and over 100 million users.
Through EuroPA, IRIS Payments users can send instant transfers to users of equivalent services in Spain, Portugal, Italy, and Andorra. The service is initially available through Eurobank, Piraeus Bank, and Viva.com, with additional Greek banks expected to join gradually. In addition, EuroPA links national A2A instant payment solutions, including MB WAY in Portugal, Bizum in Spain, and BANCOMAT Pay in Italy, with the aim of building a unified European payments ecosystem.
The launch marks the first phase of IRIS Payments' European interoperability for P2P transfers, connecting 57.3 million citizens across five countries. By the end of 2026, the network is expected to expand to more than 176 million citizens across 18 countries. In 2027, interoperability is expected to extend to online and physical retail payments.
Built on the SEPA Instant Credit Transfer scheme
The cross-border connection relies on the European SEPA Instant Credit Transfer, or SCT Inst, scheme, which governs the routing and clearing of transactions within seconds. Transactions are conducted using Strong Customer Authentication, in line with European security standards.
Stavroula Kampouridou, CEO of DIAS S.A., said the connection of IRIS Payments with EuroPA represents a milestone for the Greek payments ecosystem and for building a unified European payments experience. She noted that today's interoperability concerns P2P transfers, but described it as a first step, with business payments expected to follow so that European citizens can eventually pay across the continent as easily as sending a message from their phone. Kampouridou added that DIAS views the future of payments as instant, interoperable, and European, with initiatives such as EuroPA interconnecting national solutions to strengthen competition and payments sovereignty across the region.

# Context-enrichment: IRIS Payments connects Greece to European EuroPA network
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**FRESHNESS / DUPLICATE VERDICT: FRESH.** The corpus already holds [[Greece joins EuroPA through IRIS Payments]] (13/16 Jun 2025), but that was the **signing of a Letter of Intent** and a statement that DIAS would be "technically ready to support IRIS P2P transactions via EuroPA in the first half of 2026." This 30 Jun 2026 item is the **actual go-live of that P2P interoperability** — a genuine "plan → live" follow-up, not a reprint. Different date, different milestone (LoI/readiness vs live), new named banks (Eurobank, Piraeus, Viva.com) and hard figures (57.3M citizens live). So: **fresh**, but explicitly the delivery of the 2025 announcement — frame it as "the 2026 promise landed," not as brand-new news. Distinct from the sibling [[Nopan and DIAS partner to expand IRIS Commerce internationally]], which is merchant-side *acceptance* of IRIS Commerce abroad (opposite direction, ecommerce checkout, not P2P).

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
DIAS S.A. (Greece's national interbank clearing operator, runs IRIS) switched on **cross-border P2P** for IRIS Payments via the **EuroPA** alliance, effective **30 Jun 2026**. Greek users can now send instant transfers to users of equivalent A2A schemes in **Spain (Bizum), Portugal (MB WAY), Italy (BANCOMAT Pay), and Andorra** using only a **mobile phone number (no IBAN)**.
- **Live, but narrow.** Genuinely live (not "will explore"), yet the initial footprint is thin: **only 3 Greek banks** (Eurobank, Piraeus, Viva.com), "additional banks expected gradually." It is **P2P only** — retail/commerce is slated for **2027**. The rails are **SEPA Instant Credit Transfer (SCT Inst)** with **Strong Customer Authentication**; no new proprietary rail was built.
- **The headline reach numbers are potential, not usage:** "**57.3M citizens across five countries**" is the *addressable population* of the five markets, not active users; "**176M citizens across 18 countries by end-2026**" is a *target*. Verified against The Paypers / naftemporiki / athens-times — the figures are consistent across sources but all trace to the same DIAS press release. **Why this framing matters:** stating population reach rather than transacting users is the standard A2A-alliance PR move — it inflates a modest 3-bank P2P launch into a "continent-scale" headline. The real unit is "how many corridors carry real volume," which is undisclosed.
- **EuroPA's own scale claim** ("10 countries, 100M+ users within two years") is likewise alliance-level potential; independent sources put the operational EuroPA+EPI footprint at **~130M users / 13 countries** after the Feb 2026 MoU — so the "18 countries / 176M" end-2026 target is ambitious relative to where the network actually is today.

## [1] Competitors / peers
The relevant contest is **pan-European sovereign A2A networks**, not card schemes directly:
- **EPI / Wero** — the other pole. Bank-owned wallet on SCT Inst/TIPS; live P2P in DE/FR/BE, rolling into ecommerce (Belgium Mar 2026). On **2 Feb 2026** EuroPA and EPI signed an **MoU to interconnect via a central hub** (~130M users / 13 countries, ~72% of EU+Norway population), P2P in 2026, commerce/POS by 2027 — see [[EuroPA and EPI prepare to expand cross-border payments in Europe]] and [[EuroPA and EPI partner for pan-European payments sovereignty]]. **So EuroPA and Wero are converging into one interoperable network, not competing to the death.**
- **EuroPA members:** Bizum (ES, 30M+ users), MB WAY/SIBS (PT), BANCOMAT (IT), Vipps MobilePay (Nordics), BLIK (PL, euro-transfer pilot underway), DIAS/IRIS (GR).
- **The incumbents being displaced:** Visa/Mastercard — the whole initiative is explicitly a **payment-sovereignty** play to cut transatlantic dependence.
- **Item's position:** Greece is a **late but full joiner** — one of the smaller markets, going live P2P roughly in line with the alliance's 2026 P2P phase. Ahead of some (still-piloting) members on live cross-border P2P; behind Wero on the commerce leg.
- **Why the landscape is this way (analysis):** each national scheme is locally dominant but historically **trapped inside one country**. The EU Instant Payments Regulation makes SCT Inst free and ubiquitous, which turns the *rail* into a commodity → the only way for national champions to matter cross-border (and resist Visa/MC) is to **interlink via a shared hub**. Hence the cooperative EuroPA↔EPI hub rather than a winner-take-all fight: no single brand has to win; the user always sees their home brand.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **DIAS trajectory:** IRIS has gone from a domestic P2P/P2B tool to national infrastructure — **mandatory for Greek businesses since 1 Dec 2025** ([[IRIS Payments becomes mandatory for Greek businesses]]), with expanded transfer limits in early 2026 ([[IRIS expands instant mobile payment limits in Greece]]). 2025 domestic scale: ~122M instant txns, ~€10.9bn, ~73% YoY growth, ~4.2–4.6M users.
- **Why DIAS acts this way (analysis):** IRIS is **domestically maxed** (mandate = near-saturation). The only remaining growth vectors are (a) **outbound P2P via EuroPA** (this item) and (b) **outbound commerce via partners** (the Nopan deal). Both are the same structural logic — a saturated national scheme must go cross-border or stall. EuroPA gives DIAS continental reach without building bilateral links to every country.
- **Fit:** clean. Greece signed the LoI in Jun 2025 precisely to be "technically ready in H1 2026"; this is that timeline executed on schedule — a credibility point for DIAS.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new vs the 2025 note:** it's **live**, with real banks and SCA on SEPA Instant. The 2025 item was intent; this is delivery. That *is* the value-add — execution risk retired.
- **But traction is unproven:** no transaction counts, no active-user numbers for the cross-border corridors, only 3 Greek banks, P2P only. "57.3M reach" ≠ adoption.
- **Who captures the value (analysis):** on a free/commoditized SCT Inst rail there is **no interchange to skim**; DIAS earns scheme/clearing economics, the banks get a retention/engagement feature, and the *strategic* winner is **European payment sovereignty** (keeping cross-border euro flows off Visa/MC). The economic prize is not P2P (money-losing everywhere) but the **2027 commerce/POS leg**, where merchant acceptance and interchange-free A2A actually threaten card revenue. **So the real question is not "did Greece join P2P" (it did, on time) but "will the 2027 commerce phase convert this plumbing into card-displacing volume" — and that is unbuilt.**

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Roadmap:** more Greek banks onboard gradually; network target **176M / 18 countries by end-2026**; **online + physical retail payments in 2027**; EuroPA↔EPI hub interconnection (P2P 2026, commerce 2027).
- **Regulatory backdrop:** EU **Instant Payments Regulation** (SEPA Instant: receive since Jan 2025, send + Verification of Payee since Oct 2025) + **PSD3/PSR** (trilogue deal Nov 2025) are the structural tailwind; the ECB's **digital euro** looms as both complement and potential competitor to these private rails.
- **Risks / silences:** fraud-liability split on cross-border P2P is unstated; the aggressive "18 countries by end-2026" target sits against a network that is ~13 countries today; commerce (the value leg) is a 2027 promise; only 3 Greek banks live.
- **Sentiment / second-order (analysis):** the **counterintuitive point** is that the P2P go-live is *not* where the money is — it is a **trust-and-plumbing milestone** whose real purpose is to prove the hub works before 2027 commerce. For Visa/Mastercard the P2P leg is harmless; the 2027 A2A-at-checkout leg is the actual threat. This item's weight is therefore **strategic/directional** (sovereignty roadmap on track) rather than commercially material today.

## Red-team questions

1. **Is this a duplicate of the 2025 "Greece joins EuroPA" note?** No. The 2025 note = **LoI signed + "technically ready in H1 2026"** (intent/readiness). This = **actual P2P go-live on 30 Jun 2026** with named banks and live corridors. A real plan→live follow-up. **Fresh**, but must be framed as delivery of the 2025 promise, not brand-new news.

2. **Is it the same event as the Nopan/DIAS sibling?** No. Nopan = merchant-side *acceptance* of IRIS **Commerce** abroad (ecommerce checkout); this = **P2P** person-to-person transfers via EuroPA. Opposite direction, different use case, no cross-mention. Distinct.

3. **Live or announced?** Live P2P (30 Jun 2026), not "will explore." But narrow: only **3 Greek banks** (Eurobank, Piraeus, Viva.com), P2P only, commerce deferred to 2027.

4. **Are "57.3M citizens / 176M / 18 countries" users or population?** **Population reach / targets**, not active or transacting users. 57.3M = addressable population of 5 markets; 176M/18 = end-2026 *target*. No adoption/volume figures disclosed. All trace to one DIAS release.

5. **Does the 18-country / 176M end-2026 target reconcile with reality?** Tension: independent sources put operational EuroPA+EPI at **~130M users / 13 countries** after the Feb 2026 MoU. Jumping to 18/176M by end-2026 is ambitious. Open (target, unverified).

6. **What is the precise mechanism delta vs a plain SEPA transfer?** Phone-number addressing (no IBAN) + SCA on top of SCT Inst, routed through the EuroPA/EPI hub so each user keeps their home brand. The rail itself is commodity SEPA Instant — the novelty is the alias/routing layer, not settlement.

7. **Who captures the economics?** On free/commoditized SCT Inst there's **no interchange to skim**; P2P is money-losing everywhere. DIAS gets clearing economics, banks get engagement/retention. The real economic prize is the **2027 commerce/POS leg** — unbuilt.

8. **Is EuroPA competing with Wero/EPI or merging with it?** Merging. The **2 Feb 2026 EuroPA↔EPI MoU** interconnects both via a central hub (P2P 2026, commerce 2027). Not a death-match; a cooperative sovereignty network.

9. **Founding date of EuroPA — reliable?** Sources conflict (Nov 2024 vs Dec 2023 founding by Bancomat/Bizum/SIBS; operationally live since Mar 2025). Minor, flagged; doesn't affect this item's substance.

10. **Fraud-liability / dispute handling on cross-border P2P?** Silent. No mention of who bears fraud loss across schemes — a standard omission in these releases.

11. **Was Greece late or early?** Late-ish full joiner (smaller market), but delivered P2P **on the 2025-promised H1-2026 timeline** — a credibility/execution point for DIAS.

12. **Is domestic IRIS growth durable or mandate-driven?** Largely **mandate-driven** (mandatory for Greek businesses since 1 Dec 2025). Real usage, but policy-forced domestically; says little about voluntary cross-border P2P uptake.

13. **What would make this importance 4–5?** The **2027 commerce/POS leg going live** with real merchant volume, disclosed cross-border transaction counts, or many more banks/countries actually transacting — i.e., proof the plumbing carries card-displacing volume.

14. **What's the second-order point most miss?** The P2P go-live is **strategically directional, not commercially material** — its purpose is to prove the hub works before the 2027 commerce phase, which is the actual threat to Visa/Mastercard. P2P alone is harmless to the card duopoly.

15. **Any digital-euro overhang?** Yes — the ECB digital euro could both complement (shared rails/sovereignty) and partially cannibalize these private A2A wallets; unaddressed in the release. Open.

Importance: 3/5 — A real, on-schedule **go-live** (not just intent) of Greece's cross-border P2P via EuroPA, part of the credible pan-European payment-sovereignty story (EuroPA↔EPI hub, IPR/PSD3 tailwind), which lifts it above a routine partnership. But it is capped: P2P-only, just 3 Greek banks, reach figures are population not adoption, no volumes, and the value-creating commerce leg is a 2027 promise. Directional/strategic weight, not commercially material today. Explicitly the delivery of the 2025 [[Greece joins EuroPA through IRIS Payments]] LoI.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Sector: pan-European instant A2A (account-to-account) payments interlinking — connecting national mobile P2P champions into an interoperable, "sovereign" network as an alternative to the Visa/Mastercard duopoly. Structure: fragmented by national scheme (each country historically has its own domestic champion — Bizum/ES, MB WAY/PT, BANCOMAT Pay/IT, IRIS/GR, Vipps MobilePay/Nordics, Wero via EPI), now consolidating not by merger but via a central technical hub on shared European rails; each brand keeps its own users/merchants (per Euronews / europeanbusinessmagazine, via WebSearch, as of 2026). Barriers are high: regulation (SCT Inst mandate, SCA), multi-country bank distribution, and network effects — the reason interlinking rather than a greenfield scheme is the chosen path. Drivers/"why now": (1) EU regulatory push — the Instant Payments Regulation forcing SCT Inst reachability + EU "payment sovereignty" politics reducing dependence on US card networks; (2) live scale reached — the EuroPA+EPI combined footprint is ~130m users across 13 countries (~72% of EU+Norway) per europeanbusinessmagazine, and EuroPA alone now spans 10 countries / 100m+ users per the note. IRIS itself: 4.6m Greek users (+760k YoY), €10.9bn 2025 P2P value (+70%), 122.1m transactions in 2025 (+72.8% YoY) per athens-times/GreekReporter (via WebSearch). Phase-one cross-border reach 57.3m citizens/5 countries, targeting 173–176m/18 countries by end-2026, with e-com/POS in 2027.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: registered users, transaction count & value (TPV), interoperable-country reach, and — the eventual monetisation lever — merchant/POS acceptance and take rate (P2P is largely free, so economics only appear at the retail/e-com phase). Key players & basis of competition: this is a *coalition*, not a head-to-head — EuroPA members (Bizum ~28.8m users, ~90% of Spanish instant payments; MB WAY/SIBS; BANCOMAT Pay; Vipps MobilePay ~12m) interlinking with EPI/Wero (>50m registered Wero users, 1,100+ member institutions) under a Feb-2026 MoU. Competition is against the incumbent card rails (Visa/Mastercard) on price, sovereignty and UX (phone-number → no IBAN), not against fellow EuroPA members. Recent moves: EuroPA×EPI collaboration announced Jun-2025 → hub model Sep-2025 → MoU signed 3 Feb 2026 → Bancomat/EPI/SIBS retail interoperability demo Apr-2026 → ACI Worldwide selected to power EPI instant payments Jun-2026 → IRIS/Greece goes cross-border 30 Jun 2026. IRIS/DIAS position: a *late but fast* joiner — Greece connects in phase one via only 3 banks initially (Eurobank, Piraeus, Viva.com), so niche/catching-up on breadth but riding strong domestic adoption momentum. Moat = network effects + regulated clearing position of DIAS (national interbank clearer) `(analysis)`.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation/round/deal in the note — DIAS/IRIS is a bank-owned utility clearer, EuroPA/EPI are consortia; no market cap or post-money is disclosed. **EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E = no data** (no listed protagonist, no published financials). Price-per-user / TPV are the only quantitative anchors: IRIS €10.9bn TPV across 4.6m users = ~€2,370 P2P value per user in 2025 (arithmetic: €10.9bn / 4.6m); Bizum ~1bn annual transactions across ~28.8m users. Distribution not computed — heterogeneous, non-comparable entities; qualitative comparison only. Internal comps (base, `[[wikilink]]`): [[EuroPA and EPI partner for pan-European payments sovereignty]] (2025-06), [[Pan-European payment providers sign sovereign network MoU]] (2026-02), [[Bancomat, EPI and SIBS demonstrate cross-border payment interoperability]] (2026-04), [[ACI Worldwide and EPI to power instant payments in Europe]] (2026-06), [[Wero launches e-commerce payments in Belgium]] (2026-03). All confirm a consistent phased-rollout narrative, so IRIS/Greece reads as in-line execution of an established programme, not an outlier event.

**Risk flags.**
1. **No monetisation in the live phase.** Phase one is P2P only — typically free-to-user with near-zero take rate; the economics that would justify displacing card rails only appear in 2027 (e-com/POS). Second-order: the "Visa/Mastercard killer" thesis is unproven until retail acceptance ships, and merchant economics/fraud liability are conspicuously undisclosed.
2. **Coordination & governance risk across a multi-country consortium.** EuroPA+EPI is interlinking, not merging — dependent on every national scheme, regulator and shareholder bank staying aligned; ambitious end-2026 targets (176m/18 countries) hinge on schemes joining "gradually." Slippage or a defector re-fragments the network. Second-order: reach numbers are announced coverage, not adopted usage — de-PR gap between "connected" and "actively used cross-border."
3. **Dependence on someone else's rails/vendors.** The whole scheme rides SEPA SCT Inst and third-party processors (e.g. ACI Worldwide powering EPI); IRIS is one of 3 banks in Greece at launch. Concentration + tech-vendor dependence means margin/value can be captured by the infrastructure layer, and any SCT Inst / SCA operational issue hits all members at once.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** IRIS/Greece is an incremental *coverage* milestone that thickens the EuroPA network effect ahead of the decisive 2027 retail phase — worth watching as a proxy for whether the sovereignty coalition converts announced reach into real cross-border volume `(analysis)`. Falsifiable thesis: if by end-2026 EuroPA reports actual cross-border transaction volumes (not just "citizens reachable") and confirms the 2027 e-com/POS rails with defined merchant economics, the pan-European A2A bloc becomes a credible card-network challenger; trigger/catalyst = published cross-border TPV + a dated POS-acceptance launch. What breaks it = milestones stay "reach announced" with no volume disclosure, or a major scheme defects/delays. `(analysis)`
