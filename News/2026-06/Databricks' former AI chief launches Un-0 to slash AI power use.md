Databricks’ former AI chief thinks he can cut AI’s power bill by 1,000x
The drive to discover the next big thing in AI has funded some pretty ambitious projects — but one company is taking it as a chance to rebuild computing architecture from the ground up.
Led by Naveen Rao, formerly the head of AI at Databricks, Unconventional AI promises to make inference processing vastly more power efficient. The secret weapon: a new kind of oscillator-based computer architecture.
On Thursday, the company released its first model AI — called Un-0 — an image-generation system tool that shows for the first time how the company’s technology can replicate conventional AI systems. In an accompanying new paper, the company’s research team details how they built a fully functional image-generation model using a software simulation of the new architecture — one that performs just as well as state-of-the-art diffusion models.
“This is the ‘hello world’ of a new kind of computer,” Rao told TechCrunch. “Over the next year, you’re going to start seeing some pretty interesting news around this.”
The output from the new Un-0 model is similar to that of image-generation models like Stable Diffusion or OpenAI’s GPT Image 1. The impressive part is how it arrives at that performance. The model is built on an oscillator-based architecture that is completely different from the chips that power conventional computing and traditional LLMs. The advantages of the oscillator-based computing are complex, but Rao believes it will ultimately reduce power use by as much as 1,000 times.
Much of the infrastructure to get there is still being built. The current version of Un-0 runs on a software simulation of Unconventional’s oscillator chips, but the company plans to release schematics for an actual chip soon. From there, the plan is to build an entire inference stack from the ground up, with Unconventional AI eventually supplying compute capacity just like any other provider.
“We will build a new kind of system composed of our chips,” says Rao. “We will run AI models there, and we will have a network cable where prompts come in and inferences go out, but it’ll be done at 1/1000 of power.”
It’s a stunningly ambitious goal, particularly for a company that still counts less than 50 employees. But given the scale of the AI buildout and the anticipated cost of meeting the growing demand for inference, it may be one of the few efforts to meet the scale of the problem. As Rao sees it, the available supply of power will be one of the hard limits for AI in the years to come — and Unconventional is one of the few projects able to address it.
“AI scaling is hard because of energy. It’s going to be the fundamental limit in the next few years. You just can’t go past it. It’s going to be an energy-limited problem, at the end of the day,” he says.

# Context-enrichment: Databricks' former AI chief launches Un-0 to slash AI power use
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 2026-06-25, **Unconventional AI** (Unconventional, Inc.) — led by **Naveen Rao**, former head of AI at Databricks — released **Un-0**, an image-generation model said to match state-of-the-art diffusion models (Stable Diffusion, OpenAI GPT Image 1). The headline claim: an oscillator-based computing architecture that could cut AI inference power by **up to 1,000x**.

Critical de-PR distinctions the press frame blurs:
- **Un-0 is the model, not the company.** The company is Unconventional AI. The TechCrunch headline ("launches Un-0") obscures this.
- **No chip exists.** Un-0 runs on a **software *simulation*** of an oscillator chip that has not been fabricated. The company says it "plans to release schematics for an actual chip soon" — no tape-out date, no fab partner, no process node disclosed.
- **The 1,000x is an aspiration, not a result.** It is the design target for hardware that doesn't exist, never measured on silicon. The baseline ("conventional AI systems") is undefined — no watts, no tokens/joule, no named reference device (e.g. H100/B200). As stated it is effectively unfalsifiable.
- **What was actually demonstrated:** a research paper + a working image model on a simulated architecture. That is a genuine "hello world" (Rao's own term), not a shipping power-efficiency product.

→ Why framed this way: the strongest verifiable asset here is **founder reputation + capital**, not silicon. Anchoring coverage to a dramatic "1,000x" and a live-looking model (image output people can see) converts an early-research bet into a "breakthrough" narrative. The real news is that a credibly-backed team has reached a simulation milestone — orders of magnitude short of the claimed end state.

## [1] Competitors / peers
The AI-inference power/efficiency race is crowded, but Un-0 sits at the most radical, least-proven end:
- **Groq** — deterministic digital LPU, *shipping*; Nvidia struck a ~$20B deal for its tech/team (reported late 2025/early 2026).
- **Cerebras** — wafer-scale digital; **IPO'd ~May 2026**, real revenue (~$510M FY25). Opposite of Un-0's pre-product stage.
- **d-Matrix** — digital compute-in-memory ("Corsair" reportedly in production ~June 2026), memory-centric digital.
- **Etched** — transformer-hardwired ASIC ("Sohu").
- **EnCharge AI** — analog in-memory compute — closest "analog efficiency" peer.
- **Rain AI** — neuromorphic / brain-inspired — closest architectural cousin.

Un-0's differentiator: it discards **both** transistor logic **and** the von Neumann architecture for coupled-oscillator physics ("physics as a computational primitive"). Every near-term rival ships or nearly ships **digital** silicon; Un-0 has the least hardware to show.

→ Why the landscape is this way (analysis): the market has split between (a) pragmatic digital accelerators monetizing now and (b) physics-rethink moonshots. The economic pull is real — see internal [[Datacentres use 6% of UK and US electricity, says IDCA]] (AI energy use up 15% globally in two years) — but capital has overwhelmingly rewarded shippable digital. Un-0 being funded so richly pre-silicon is the exception, driven by name, not parity.

## [2] Company history / fit
Rao has a serial-hardware-exit record: **Nervana** → Intel (~$400M+, 2016); **MosaicML** → Databricks (~$1.3B, 2023), where he became AI chief. Exit from Databricks announced **2025-09-12** (moved to advisory; Databricks pledged to invest in the new venture). Unconventional raised a **$475M seed at a $4.5B valuation** (announced 2025-12-08/09) — ~2 months after founding, pre-product. Leads: a16z + Lightspeed; participants reportedly Lux, DCVC, Databricks, and Jeff Bezos; Rao added $10M of his own.

→ Why he acts this way (analysis): Rao's pattern is "build novel AI silicon, exit." The fit is logical — energy is becoming the binding constraint on AI scaling, and he has the track record + relationships to raise moonshot capital. The structural tell: the $4.5B valuation was *below* the ~$5B he reportedly sought — appetite exists but is not unlimited for an unbuilt chip.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Genuinely novel **approach** (oscillator-fabric compute), but **traction is near-zero**: no chip, no customers, no revenue, no commercial timeline, no independent benchmark of either model quality or the power claim — only the company's own paper and simulation. The "comparable to Stable Diffusion" claim is unverified externally.

→ Why the value-add is unproven, one level deeper (analysis): analog/neuromorphic compute has a long graveyard of architectures that look excellent in simulation and die in fabrication — noise, device variation, and integration at scale routinely erase paper gains. The margin question in the inference stack is "who controls the watts-per-token at production scale." Un-0 cannot capture that until silicon exists and is independently measured. Until then the durable asset is option value on a reputation, not a defensible efficiency product. Contrast with peers monetizing today (internal: [[Allbirds' new AI business CEO has a plan but no team]] notes rivals like inference clouds already "deploying compute clusters for customers").

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Near-term commitments are modest: release chip schematics "soon," then build a full inference stack and eventually sell compute capacity ("prompts in, inferences out, at 1/1000 power"). Sentiment is bifurcated — heavy capital + marquee press enthusiasm vs. skeptical trade coverage flagging "will it work?" The macro tailwind is real and well-documented internally: data-center electricity demand is a hard, rising constraint ([[Datacentres use 6% of UK and US electricity, says IDCA]]; [[Scala Data Centers secures $328 million for Chile expansion]]; [[Mastercard invests €250 million in three France data centres]]).

→ Counterintuitive second-order effect (analysis): the very scale of the energy problem makes a 1,000x story *easy to fund and hard to falsify* — investors buy lottery tickets against the binding constraint, which inflates valuations of pre-silicon bets. That concentration of capital on an unproven architecture is a fragility, not a validation: the next real signal is a working chip with third-party watts-per-token numbers, not another model demo. The central question shifts from "can it match diffusion models?" (it apparently can, in sim) to **"can the oscillator architecture survive fabrication and beat digital incumbents on measured efficiency before the capital/patience runs out?"**

**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: AI-inference silicon / energy-efficient compute. Market size: the inference-AI-chip market is put at ~$20.4bn in 2026 → ~$85.8bn by 2034, CAGR ~27.8% (per IntelMarketResearch, via web, as of 2026); inference is now ~two-thirds of all AI compute, up from ~half in 2025 and ~a third in 2023 (web, 2026) — i.e. the demand pool Un-0 targets (inference, not training) is the fastest-growing slice. The "why now" is the binding input: IEA projects data-center electricity ~945 TWh by 2030 (web, 2026); internal corpus confirms the constraint is live and rising ([[Datacentres use 6% of UK and US electricity, says IDCA]], AI energy up ~15% in two years). Structure: the accelerator layer is consolidating around Nvidia, with a fringe of well-funded challengers; value in the inference stack accrues to whoever controls watts-per-token at production scale. Entry barriers are extreme — capital, fab access, and (for non-digital designs) unproven device physics. Un-0 sits at the most capital-intensive, least-proven corner: a pre-silicon, oscillator-physics moonshot.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: tokens/joule (watts-per-token), throughput (tokens/sec), $/token at scale, utilization. Basis of competition for challengers: efficiency + price vs Nvidia GPUs, traded against flexibility. Key players & recent moves: **Cerebras** IPO'd 2026-05-14 (CBRS), FY25 revenue ~$510M (+76% YoY), day-1 mkt cap ~$95bn, since de-rated toward ~$50bn (web). **Groq** — Nvidia took its assets/team for ~$20B (signed 2025-12-24) at 2.9x its $6.9bn valuation; the remaining entity confirmed a fresh **$650M raise (2026-06-22)** and re-staffed (web). **d-Matrix** — raised **$275M at a ~$2bn valuation** (mid-2026), Microsoft-backed, digital compute-in-memory (web). **Etched** (transformer ASIC), **EnCharge** (analog in-memory), **Rain AI** (neuromorphic) round out the architectural fringe. Protagonist position: **furthest behind on hardware** — every named peer ships or nearly ships digital silicon; Un-0 has only a software simulation and a research paper. Even on the demo itself, trade coverage notes conventional generators remain stronger on absolute quality and parameter efficiency (SiliconANGLE, 2026-06-26). Moat: option value on Rao's reputation + IP, not a defensible product `(analysis)`.

**Comps & multiples.** Comparability caveat: Un-0/Unconventional is pre-revenue, so no revenue/EBITDA multiple is computable — the only anchor is round valuation. Peers (round/market valuations, not like-for-like multiples):
- **Cerebras**: ~$95bn day-1 mkt cap on ~$510M FY25 rev → EV/Rev ≈ $95bn / $0.51bn ≈ **186x** at peak; at de-rated ~$50bn ≈ **98x** (web). Extreme even for hyper-growth — but it has real, audited revenue; Un-0 has none.
- **Groq**: Nvidia paid ~$20B vs a $6.9bn last private valuation → **2.9x** the prior round (web).
- **d-Matrix**: **~$2bn** post-money, in production this year (web).
- **Unconventional AI** (subject): **$475M seed at $4.5B** post-money (2025-12), pre-product (note [2]). Implied: investors paid ~$4.5bn for zero revenue and no fabricated chip — i.e. a valuation **higher than d-Matrix, which already ships**, on far less proof.
Internal comps (corpus): [[Baseten reportedly raising at $400 million valuation for AI model infrastructure]] (inference-infra, June 2026 — note "inference will eat the AI industry"); [[AWS in talks to sell Trainium AI chip racks to outside companies]] (custom-silicon competition); [[Datacentres use 6% of UK and US electricity, says IDCA]] and [[Scala Data Centers secures $328 million for Chile expansion]] (the energy-constraint driver). Verdict: **rich vs the only ship-stage private comp (d-Matrix)** purely on founder pedigree; revenue multiples "no data" (pre-revenue).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Valuation-vs-proof mismatch.** $4.5bn pre-silicon exceeds shipping peer d-Matrix (~$2bn) — the price embeds a hardware breakthrough that doesn't exist; if fabrication erodes the gains (the historical fate of analog/neuromorphic designs), the round has no revenue floor to catch it.
2. **Sim-to-silicon execution risk.** The 1,000x is a design target measured only in simulation, with no named baseline device/workload — effectively unfalsifiable until tape-out, with no disclosed fab, node, or date. Second-order: each peer that ships digital efficiency narrows Un-0's window before capital/patience runs out.
3. **Disintermediation / incumbent gravity.** Nvidia's Groq absorption shows incumbents will buy out efficiency threats; a pre-product oscillator stack risks being lapped or made redundant by digital gains (d-Matrix, Trainium, Cerebras) before it reaches market.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a new-entry lottery ticket on the energy constraint, not a re-rating of the sector — the inference-efficiency race is being won today by digital silicon with revenue (Cerebras, d-Matrix) and consolidation (Nvidia/Groq). Falsifiable thesis: Un-0's value is reputational option-money, not product, until a fabricated chip posts **independent watts-per-token vs a named digital part (H100/B200)**; the trigger to watch is the promised chip schematics → tape-out → third-party benchmark. What breaks the bear case: an external lab reproducing material energy gains on real silicon. Absent that, the next "interesting news" is more likely another simulation demo than a shipping efficiency product.
