Worldline brings Click to Pay to recurring payments in Europe
Sinziana Albu
24 Jun 2026 / 5 Min Read
Worldline has become the first payment provider in Europe to enable Click to Pay for recurring payments via its Global Collect platform.
The capability is available on Global Collect, Worldline's cross-border payments platform, and is scheduled for general availability from 30 July 2026. The move is aimed at digital-first businesses seeking to scale subscription payments across multiple markets.
Checkout friction and payment failures remain persistent challenges for subscription businesses operating across markets. Click to Pay, built on EMVCo Secure Remote Commerce standards and supported by major card networks, allows consumers to register once and check out across participating merchants without manual card entry, passwords, or lengthy forms. According to figures cited by Mastercard, the technology can increase checkout conversion by up to 6%. Data from Paddle indicates that involuntary churn, typically triggered by expired or reissued cards, can account for up to 40% of total subscription churn.
In addition, through the process of extending Click to Pay to recurring and stored credential payments, Worldline aims to address both acquisition and retention challenges. At the point of the initial transaction, the system securely stores tokenised payment credentials. These tokens are then used for subsequent recurring billing cycles and are automatically updated when cards are renewed or replaced, removing interruptions that frequently result in failed payments and subscriber loss.
The functionality is directed at digital subscription models across sectors, including software as a service (SaaS), streaming, gaming, and digital memberships, where payment continuity directly affects revenue and profitability. The tokenised approach also supports stronger security and simplified compliance for merchants, as credential management operates within the EMVCo framework.
 Gertjan Dewaele , Head of Product and Technology at Worldline's Global Commerce division, noted that Global Collect is designed to help international merchants convert sign-ups into sustained revenue by reducing checkout friction and avoiding payment failures. He added that the outcome for subscription businesses is stronger revenue protection across global markets.
Consumer adoption and platform scope
With millions of users globally, Click to Pay has seen steady consumer uptake. Visa Consumer Research from 2025 found that 89% of consumers rated the method as equivalent to or better than other digital payment options, reflecting growing familiarity with the standard across markets.
For Worldline, the development extends the capabilities of Global Collect to cover cross-border subscription commerce from initial checkout through to recurring billing, automated token renewal, and compliance management.

# Context-enrichment: Worldline brings Click to Pay to recurring payments in Europe
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- Worldline (Euronext: WLN) announced on **2026-06-24** that it is the **"first payment provider in Europe to enable Click to Pay for recurring payments"** — the capability lives on **Global Collect**, its cross-border online-payments platform, and goes to **general availability 30 July 2026** (so as of the announcement it is *announced, not yet live* — a ~5-week gap).
- Mechanism (de-PR'd): at the *initial* checkout, Click to Pay (built on **EMVCo Secure Remote Commerce**, backed by Visa/Mastercard) stores **tokenised card credentials**; those network tokens are then reused for subsequent billing cycles and **auto-updated when a card is reissued/expires** (network token lifecycle management). Net: one capability = (a) first-checkout conversion + (b) account-updater for recurring rails. **This is two well-established primitives (CTP + network-token auto-update) packaged together for subscriptions — not a new protocol.**
- The cited numbers are all **third-party, not Worldline's own results**: "up to **6%** conversion uplift" (attributed to Mastercard), "involuntary churn up to **40%** of total subscription churn" (Paddle), "**89%** of consumers rate CTP equal/better" (Visa Consumer Research 2025). These are *vendor/network marketing figures*, not Worldline merchant outcomes — flag as PR-grade benchmarks, not traction.
- **Why framed this way:** the headline value is the word **"first"**. The underlying components (Click to Pay; network-token auto-billing-updater) already exist across the industry; Worldline is claiming a *narrow regional-first* on combining them on one cross-border platform. The framing leans on Mastercard/Paddle/Visa stats precisely because Worldline has **no own adoption numbers to show** (zero merchants, GMV, or live conversion deltas disclosed).

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Visa / Mastercard (the rail owners):** CTP and EMV network tokenisation *are* their products. Mastercard is publicly pushing "one-click by 2030 / phase out manual card entry" with auto-updating tokens for subscriptions (Mastercard, 2025); Mastercard + U.S. Bank expanded subscription management (Oct 2025). Worldline sits **on top of** their rails, not in competition with them — it is a distributor, which caps where it captures margin. ([[Visa scales Click to Pay for Revolut cardholders]], [[Visa expands Click to Pay across Asia Pacific]])
- **Issuer/acquirer CTP roll-outs:** Visa scaled CTP to Revolut cardholders (2026-06-17) [[Visa scales Click to Pay for Revolut cardholders]]; Visa expanded CTP across APAC with **2c2p, Adyen, AsiaPay, Worldpay** (2025-08-14) [[Visa expands Click to Pay across Asia Pacific]]; Revolut activated CTP on all UK cards (2026-03-30) **explicitly "amid low adoption challenge"** [[Revolut activates Click to Pay for all UK cards]]. So multiple PSPs already wire CTP into checkout — Worldline's differentiator is the *recurring/stored-credential* slice, not CTP itself.
- **Recurring-payment / failed-payment peers (the real competitive set):** Ecommpay upgraded subscriptions to cut failed recurring payments (2025-09) [[Ecommpay upgrades subscriptions to cut failed recurring payments]]; PPRO launched subscriptions for local payment methods (2025-06) [[PPRO launches subscription payments for local payment methods]]; Worldline's *own* Online EFTPOS "Repeat Pay" in NZ (2025-09) [[Worldline launches Online EFTPOS Repeat Pay in New Zealand]]; Adyen, Stripe (Billing + adaptive acceptance + network tokens), Recurly, Chargebee/Paddle all do dunning + token auto-update today. **Worldline is at parity-to-catching-up on the function; the only genuinely new claim is "CTP-as-the-rail for recurring, regional-first."**
- **Why the landscape looks this way (2nd order):** the scarce asset is *consumer enrolment* in CTP, not merchant plumbing. PSPs can all integrate it; none can force the 96% of UK shoppers who've never used CTP to use it (GlobalData 2025: only **4%** have ever used CTP). The competitive prize therefore sits with the *networks* (who own enrolment) — Worldline's "first" wins a marketing line, not a defensible moat.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Worldline is in the middle of a **deep restructuring** ("pruning program" / **North Star 2030**): FY2025 results and a string of divestments (MeTS, Worldline North America, Cetrel, PaymentIQ, India, NZ, ANZ-Worldline Australia) removing **€900M+ revenue / €200M+ EBITDA** in 2026; a **€500M capital raise** completed March 2026 (rights issue ~121% take-up). Q1 2026 (fully-pruned) revenue **€831m, -0.5% y/y**; **Merchant Services +1.6% y/y** (first growth since Q4 2024); Financial Services -7.4% (planned contract terminations).
- The product **fits the turnaround logic**: after shedding capital-heavy/low-margin lines, Worldline needs to show its remaining **Merchant Services / Global Commerce** engine can grow on *software-led* features (subscriptions, cross-border, value-add). Global Commerce revenue is still declining on eRetail churn + credit derisking, with growth only in Travel & Hospitality — so a low-cost, headline-grabbing "first in Europe" feature on Global Collect is exactly the kind of cheap re-rating signal a battered name reaches for.
- **Why the company acts this way:** acquiring is a **commodity take-rate** business under price pressure; Worldline's equity has been crushed ("broken European payments champion"). To defend a multiple it must move the story toward **software/value-add attach** on the merchant book. This launch is a (small) data point in that narrative, not a financial event. ([[Worldline and Klarna partner to enable flexible payments everywhere]], [[Worldline launches Online EFTPOS Repeat Pay in New Zealand]])

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new:** a *regional-first* claim — extending CTP from one-off checkout to the **full recurring/stored-credential lifecycle** on a single cross-border platform (Global Collect), with automatic token renewal across markets. The component tech (CTP, EMV network tokens, account-updater) is **not new**; the packaging + the "first in Europe for recurring" positioning is.
- **Traction = none yet.** GA is **30 July 2026**; the note carries **zero** live merchants, GMV/TPV, or Worldline-measured conversion/churn deltas. Every quantified benefit is borrowed from Mastercard/Paddle/Visa. Per the de-PR gate: **announced, not adopted.**
- **Where the value-add really is / who captures the margin (2nd order):** the durable benefit (token auto-update killing involuntary churn) is delivered by **network tokenisation owned by Visa/Mastercard**. Worldline is the *integration layer*. So the margin on the recurring-rail value largely accrues to the **networks**; Worldline captures only its acquiring/processing take-rate plus a thin value-add fee. The structural risk: CTP-for-recurring becomes a **commoditised checkbox** every PSP offers within 12–18 months (Visa/MC are mandating tokenisation broadly), eroding the "first" advantage. Worth flagging: the headline benefit is essentially **account-updater**, which most large PSPs already ship — the "CTP" wrapper mainly adds first-checkout enrolment, whose adoption is the weak link.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Near term:** GA 30 Jul 2026; watch for *named launch merchants* and any Worldline-disclosed conversion/churn metrics post-launch (their absence = it stayed a press line). Expect rival PSPs (Adyen, Stripe, Checkout.com, Nexi) and the networks to ship equivalent "CTP-for-subscriptions" framing quickly, neutralising the "first."
- **Sentiment:** Worldline equity sentiment is **distressed-turnaround** (capital raise, mass divestitures, FCF still negative €(80)–(70)M guided for 2026). This launch is *immaterial* to the financials and to the thesis — it's a narrative tile in North Star 2030, not a catalyst.
- **Why the market goes this way (counterintuitive 2nd order):** the harder Worldline leans on "first-in-Europe" feature PR, the more it signals that the **core take-rate business lacks organic growth** — feature flags are cheap, durable acquiring volume is not. The real re-rating question for WLN is **execution on the pruned cost base + stabilising Merchant Services volumes**, not CTP-for-recurring. For the *sector*, the structural drift is clear: static PANs → tokenised, auto-updating credentials become table stakes, compressing the differentiation any single PSP can claim.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.**
- Subvertical: **cross-border online acquiring / subscription (recurring) payments**, a slice of the European PSP/acquiring market. Structure is **fragmented and consolidating**: a few scale players (Adyen, Worldline, Nexi, Stripe, Checkout.com, Mollie) plus many niche subscription-billing specialists (Recurly, Chargebee, Paddle, Ecommpay, PPRO). Value in the chain is created by the **card networks** (Visa/Mastercard own CTP + network tokenisation), with PSPs/acquirers as the distribution/integration layer earning take-rate + value-add fees — so the *durable* margin on tokenised-credential value sits upstream, a structural cap on what a launch like this captures for Worldline (analysis).
- Drivers / "why now": (1) **regulatory + network push to kill static PANs** — Mastercard's "phase out manual card entry / one-click by 2030" with auto-updating tokens (Mastercard, 2025); EMVCo's 2026 agenda extends tokenisation/SRC to agentic payments. → tokenised, auto-updating credentials become *table stakes*, compressing per-PSP differentiation (second-order). (2) **Involuntary churn economics** for the subscription economy: up to **40%** of subscription churn from expired/reissued cards (Paddle, via Worldline PR) — a real, sizeable pain that account-updater rails address.
- Sector size: no clean free TAM figure for "European recurring CTP" — **no data**; adjacent signal: Worldline LTM revenue ~$5.3bn, Nexi ~$6.8bn (per stockanalysis/companiesmarketcap, as of early-2026).

**Competitive landscape.**
- Sector KPIs: **Merchant Sales Value / TPV, take-rate, acquiring volume growth, authorisation/conversion rate, involuntary-churn rate, CTP enrolment**.
- Key players & basis of competition (product + cross-border breadth + price): Adyen and Stripe lead on single-platform tech and adaptive acceptance; Worldline/Nexi compete on scale + bank distribution in Europe; Recurly/Chargebee/Paddle on billing logic. CTP itself is offered via Visa/Mastercard to *all* PSPs (Visa+2c2p/Adyen/AsiaPay/Worldpay in APAC, 2025-08 [[Visa expands Click to Pay across Asia Pacific]]; Visa→Revolut 2026-06 [[Visa scales Click to Pay for Revolut cardholders]]).
- Recent moves: Ecommpay cut failed recurring payments (2025-09 [[Ecommpay upgrades subscriptions to cut failed recurring payments]]); PPRO launched subscriptions for local methods (2025-06 [[PPRO launches subscription payments for local payment methods]]); Worldline's own Repeat Pay NZ (2025-09 [[Worldline launches Online EFTPOS Repeat Pay in New Zealand]]).
- Protagonist position: **parity-to-narrow-lead** — a *regional-first* claim on CTP-for-recurring on Global Collect; moat is thin (cross-border platform breadth only), since the underlying tech is network-owned and commoditising (analysis).

**Comps & multiples** (as of early-2026, public aggregators; mark "as of" approximate):
- **Worldline (WLN):** EV ~$3.4bn / LTM revenue ~$5.3bn → **EV/Revenue ≈ $3.4bn / $5.3bn ≈ 0.6x**; EV/EBITDA ≈ $3.4bn / $0.98bn ≈ **3.5x**. Deeply distressed.
- **Nexi:** EV/Revenue ≈ **0.8x** (EV-implied / ~$6.8bn rev); EV/EBITDA mid-single-digit (figures inconsistent across sources → treat as approximate).
- **Adyen (ADYEN.AS):** market cap ~$36bn, EV ~$24bn / LTM net revenue ~$3bn → **EV/Revenue ≈ 8.1x**; EV/EBITDA ≈ **15.2x**.
- Internal comps: Checkout.com **$12bn** valuation via buyback (2025-09 [[Checkout.com reaches $12B valuation via employee share buyback]]); Mollie→GoCardless **€1.05bn** (2025-12 [[Mollie to acquire GoCardless for about €1.05B]]); Worldline→Eurobank Greek merchant stake **€72m** (2026-06 [[Worldline buys remaining Eurobank Greek merchant stake for €72 million]]); BillDesk→Worldline India (2026-03 [[BillDesk acquires Worldline's India payments business]]).
- Flag: **massive intra-sector multiple gap** — Adyen ~8x EV/Rev vs Worldline ~0.6x is **~13x**. The gap is **growth- and quality-driven, not anomalous**: Adyen grows net revenue ~20%+ on one platform; Worldline is shrinking/restructuring with a fraud-allegation overhang [[Worldline shares plunge on fraud-concealment allegations]]. Worldline is **cheap-for-a-reason**, not a mispriced bargain (analysis). A feature like CTP-for-recurring does **not** move this — it's immaterial to the multiple.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Margin captured upstream:** the durable churn-killing value = Visa/Mastercard network tokenisation; Worldline earns only thin value-add → limited re-rating leverage from such launches (second-order: feature parity ≠ pricing power).
2. **Commoditisation / "first" decays fast:** Visa/MC mandating tokenisation broadly + peers (Adyen/Stripe/Checkout.com/Nexi) ship equivalents within ~12–18 months → the differentiator erodes (hypothesis).
