# Context-enrichment: Van Lanschot Kempen becomes Principal Member of EPI, adds Wero
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- 2026-06-23: Van Lanschot Kempen (VLK), the listed Dutch private bank / wealth manager (Euronext Amsterdam, newsroom press release), **became a Principal Member of EPI** and signed a partnership to bring **Wero** to its clients inside the VLK digital-banking environment. **Rollout planned for "late 2026"** — i.e. announced, NOT live.
- The only genuinely novel angle in the PR: VLK frames itself as the **first wealth manager to join EPI**. Everything else is template language ("sovereign European payments", "seamless cross-border").
- **What "Principal Member" actually means (de-PR'd):** EPI has a tiered structure — 16 founding **shareholders** (ABN Amro, ING, Rabobank, BNP Paribas, Deutsche Bank, Sparkassen, Worldline, Nexi, etc.) who own and govern EPI, vs. **Principal Members** (consumer-side PSPs that distribute the Wero wallet to their account holders) and **acceptor PSPs** (acquirers/processors enabling merchant acceptance). VLK is a **consumer-side Principal Member**, i.e. a distributor that will surface Wero to its own clients — it is NOT taking an ownership stake and does NOT govern EPI. The PR's "expanding network and influence" line conflates distribution with governance. **→ Why this matters:** a Principal-Member badge is a low-commitment, marketing-friendly way to "be part of European sovereignty" without the capital and obligations of a shareholder. The substance is "VLK will offer Wero as a feature," not "VLK helps build the rail."
- **Who's silent about what:** no client numbers, no economics (interchange-free A2A means little issuer revenue — the upside for VLK is retention/optics, not payments margin), no exact rollout date beyond "late 2026," no mention of whether VLK's affluent client base even wants a P2P/e-com wallet.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- This is now a **well-worn template**. Prior EPI "becomes Principal Member" / "joins to bring Wero" notes in the corpus: [[Airwallex becomes EPI Principal Member to bring Wero]] (2025-11, merchant/acquirer side), [[payabl. joins European Payments Initiative to expand Wero]] (2025-10, PSP/merchant side). Shareholder-tier expansions: [[EPI's Wero adds Erste and Raiffeisen as shareholders in Austria]] (2026-06). Acceptance/processing: [[ACI Worldwide and EPI to power instant payments in Europe]] (2026-06), [[Nexi implements Wero for eCommerce in Germany]] (2026-04).
- **Position:** VLK is a late, niche follower. The competitive "lay of the land": EPI is methodically signing up every category of participant (shareholder banks, neobanks, processors, acquirers, now a wealth manager). VLK is the first of the *wealth* category, but that is a small, low-volume segment — affluent private-bank clients are not the P2P-payments mass market Wero needs for network effects.
- **vs. cards/incumbents:** Wero's pitch is **A2A (instant SEPA, interchange-free)** vs. Visa/Mastercard interchange-bearing rails. For a private bank the payment-volume story is marginal; the real competitor for VLK's attention is simply "do nothing / keep using iDEAL." Since iDEAL is migrating into Wero anyway (NL, 2026–2027), VLK joining is partly a defensive must-do, not a bold bet.

## [2] Company history / fit
- VLK is a ~€100bn+ AuM Dutch private bank/wealth manager — not a retail payments player. It has historically outsourced/under-invested in everyday banking UX.
- **Why it acts this way (analysis):** In NL, **iDEAL is being phased into Wero (2026–2027)** with ABN Amro, ING, Rabobank driving the migration after DNB approval. Any Dutch bank serving consumers will *have* to support Wero regardless of enthusiasm. VLK dressing a compliance/migration necessity as a strategic "sovereignty" partnership is classic PR framing. The "first wealth manager" hook gives it a differentiation story at near-zero incremental cost.
- Fit is logical but minor: it lets VLK modernize its digital-banking environment and signal European-sovereignty alignment to clients, amid the broader 2026 Dutch-bank push (Rabobank/ING/ABN) to reduce reliance on US payment tech.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty:** low. The mechanism (a PSP becomes Principal Member and distributes Wero) is identical to Airwallex/payabl. The single new element is the **segment** (wealth/private banking) and the **named brand** (a listed Dutch bank lending credibility). No new capability, no new geography (NL already in scope via iDEAL migration).
- **Traction:** this is an **announcement**, not adoption — rollout "late 2026," zero VLK client numbers. Background Wero traction (external, for weight, not VLK's): EPI announced **>50–55m registered users** (Feb 2026) across BE/DE/FR; A2A live in DE/FR/BE, rolling out NL/LU; e-com acceptance live DE (Nov 2025), FR/BE (Jan 2026). So Wero itself has real momentum — but VLK adds essentially nothing to those numbers.
- **Who captures value (analysis):** the value accrues to **EPI/Wero** (one more distribution node, a wealth-segment logo) and to **VLK's optics/retention**, not to a new payments P&L. In a sovereignty narrative VLK is a beneficiary/distributor, not a margin-capturer.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Watch the **late-2026 rollout** actually shipping, and any VLK client-uptake figures (likely never disclosed — a red flag that volume is immaterial).
- Structural backdrop: the meaningful European-sovereignty events are the **iDEAL→Wero migration** (NL, through 2027), the **Digital Euro** progressing in the EU Parliament ([[Digital Euro clears key hurdle in EU parliament]], 2026-06), and shareholder-tier expansions (Austria). VLK's membership is a small confirmation of EPI's "collect-every-participant" land-grab, not a driver.
- **Second-order (analysis):** the very fact EPI now touts a *wealth manager* as news shows the easy, high-volume retail-bank sign-ups are largely done; growth is moving to long-tail niche distributors. That is mildly bullish for breadth but says little about depth/usage.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Wero is a pan-European account-to-account (A2A) wallet built by EPI (16 shareholder banks/PSPs) as a card-network alternative. Sizing the rail vs the incumbent it targets: per Mordor Intelligence (via mordorintelligence.com), the Europe payments market is ~$0.74tn in 2026 growing ~15% CAGR, with digital-wallet/A2A rails growing fastest (~17–18% CAGR), outpacing cards; the Europe credit-card market is ~$1tn growing only ~4% CAGR — i.e. A2A is the share-gainer, cards the slow incumbent. But scale asymmetry is the real story: Visa+Mastercard process ~$24tn globally and hold ~61% of eurozone card payments and "nearly all" cross-border (per Euronews 2026-05-01, europeanbusinessmagazine.com); Wero transferred only ~€5–7.5bn cumulatively in its first ~12 months (per Banking.Vision / Société Générale). So the A2A "threat" is structurally real but quantitatively tiny today. "Why now": EU payment-sovereignty push + the NL iDEAL→Wero migration (2026–2027) forcing every Dutch consumer bank onto Wero regardless of enthusiasm — which is what makes VLK's move defensive, not strategic.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs for an A2A wallet: registered users, active users, transaction count/value, e-com acceptance footprint, and (for a distributor like VLK) client uptake — none of which VLK discloses. Wero traction (external, for weight): EPI announced **>50m registered users** by Feb 2026 (note text), up from ~14m (Nov 2024); cumulative transactions >100m, ~€5–7.5bn transferred in year one (per Banking.Vision, Société Générale). NB: the corpus chunks (EPI press templates) show institutional members climbing 27 → 45 → "over 50" across 2025-10 → 2026-04 → 2026-06 — a measurable land-grab. Recent peer/category moves: N26 signed Wero (Dec 2025, H2-2026 go-live); Nexi e-com Germany (2026-04); ACI Worldwide processing deal (2026-06); Erste/Raiffeisen joined as Austrian *shareholders* (2026-06). VLK's position: **niche late follower** — first *wealth* manager, but a low-volume segment that adds a logo, not network density, to a >50m-user wallet. Basis of competition for VLK is not price/product but distribution/optics; no moat created (it is a distributor, not an owner of the rail).

**Comps & multiples.** This is a partnership/distribution event with **no deal value, round, or transferred economics → multiples N/A for the event itself**. For context only, the protagonist VLK trades at ~€2.6–3.0bn market cap on ~€196bn client assets (Q1-2026), P/E ~15–20x (range across stockanalysis.com / Simply Wall St / companiesmarketcap.com — `[UNSOURCED]` as a single clean figure) — a normal listed-wealth-manager rating, entirely unaffected by a near-zero-revenue Wero feature. Internal comps (same EPI-distribution template, as `[[wikilink]]`): [[Airwallex becomes EPI Principal Member to bring Wero]] (2025-11, acquirer side), [[payabl. joins European Payments Initiative to expand Wero]] (2025-10, PSP side), [[EPI's Wero adds Erste and Raiffeisen as shareholders in Austria]] (2026-06, shareholder tier), [[Nexi implements Wero for eCommerce in Germany]] (2026-04), [[ACI Worldwide and EPI to power instant payments in Europe]] (2026-06). Distribution not computed — these are non-priced partnerships; qualitative comparison only: VLK is the lowest-volume, most-symbolic of the set.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disintermediation / zero margin capture (analysis).** A2A is interchange-free, so the distributor (VLK) earns essentially no payments P&L; value accrues to EPI/Wero (a distribution node) while VLK gets only retention/optics. Second-order: if Wero ever monetises (e.g. e-com merchant fees), the economics sit with EPI and acquirers, not the consumer-side bank — VLK is a price-taker on someone else's rail.
2. **Dependence on others' rails + forced-hand (analysis).** Because NL iDEAL is migrating into Wero, VLK *must* support Wero; this is a regulatory/migration necessity dressed as strategy. The risk is mis-reading it as a competitive edge when every Dutch peer gets the same capability.
3. **Adoption undisclosed → likely immaterial.** No client numbers, no rollout date beyond "late 2026," and an affluent private-bank base that is not the P2P/e-com mass market Wero needs. Absence of any volume metric is itself the red flag that the contribution is negligible.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** For the sector, this is a breadth signal, not a depth one: EPI now touting a *wealth manager* implies the high-volume retail-bank sign-ups are largely done and growth is moving to long-tail niche distributors (analysis) — mildly bullish on coverage, silent on usage. Falsifiable thesis: Wero's challenge to cards is decided by **active-usage and e-com conversion in 2026**, not membership count; the trigger to watch is real transaction-value growth past the ~€7.5bn year-one base and the NL iDEAL migration completing — not which logo joins next. What would make VLK's move material: disclosed client uptake or a shareholder-tier upgrade — neither present.
