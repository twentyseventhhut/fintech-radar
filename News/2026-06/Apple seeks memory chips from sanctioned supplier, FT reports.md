## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)

The FT (cited "six people familiar," published ~2026-06-26) reports Apple is **lobbying the US Commerce Department for assurance that China's CXMT (ChangXin Memory Technologies) will NOT be added to the Entity List**, so Apple can safely source DRAM from it. Apple first approached Commerce "more than a month ago" (~May 2026). Apple declined to comment; the White House did not respond; CXMT did not comment.

**Premise correction (load-bearing):** The headline "sanctioned supplier" is imprecise. CXMT is on the **Pentagon's 1260H "Chinese military companies" list** (restored 2026-06-08), **NOT** the Commerce Entity List. Buying chips *from* a 1260H-listed firm is **not currently barred** by US export law — the Entity List restricts *exports TO* a listed firm, not *purchases FROM* it (BIS FAQ #142). So this is **prospective regulatory risk-mitigation**, not a current violation and not (yet) a signed supply deal. **+ Why framed this way:** Apple is buying political insurance before committing volume — it wants a guarantee the rug won't be pulled (an Entity-List designation would impose typically-refused export licensing on the relationship). The "what happened" is a *lobbying* move, not a procurement announcement.

CXMT makes **DRAM** (DDR4/DDR5, LPDDR5X); HBM3 is only on its end-2026 roadmap. It does **not** make NAND — that is the separate firm YMTC, also named as a NAND alternative in the same thread. The supplier here is CXMT for DRAM.

## [1] Competitors / peers

The big three control >95% of world DRAM: **Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron**. Apple is reportedly ~60% reliant on Samsung for DRAM (estimate; treat as approximate). **+ Why turn to a blacklisted Chinese maker:** (a) cost — Chinese quotes reportedly 10–15% below the incumbents; (b) supply security — Apple's DRAM coverage was unusually short, only into H1 2026; (c) leverage against the big three's hardball AI-era pricing. Analysts ("Micron has nothing to worry about," 24/7 Wall St) read CXMT as a **leverage play, not displacement** — it faces real EUV/process limits on Apple-grade LPDDR5X at volume. Compare prior corpus signals: the West fears China is *acquiring* advanced tooling ([[US fears ASML's top chip tool ended up in China]]) — here the flow is reversed, a US flagship *sourcing from* China.

## [2] Company history / fit

**Closest prior art (2022):** Apple explored buying NAND from **YMTC** for China-market iPhones, then dropped it after export controls and lawmaker security warnings — same playbook, killed by Washington politics. **Feb 2026:** reports Apple was evaluating CXMT (DRAM) + YMTC (NAND) for diversification/leverage. **~May 2026:** approached Commerce. **2026-06-25:** Apple raised prices on Mac, iPad, home devices and Vision Pro (the **iPhone was explicitly excluded** — correcting any "iPhone hike" framing). **~06-26:** FT story breaks, days after the hikes. **+ Why Apple acts this way:** it faces the worst memory shortage in its history (Tim Cook: "hundred-year flood"; "everything on the table") and is structurally short of DRAM — so it is willing to court political risk to secure supply and pressure incumbents. See [[AI memory-chip demand pushes Apple to raise iPhone prices]].

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction

**What's new:** a US flagship *publicly lobbying Washington for cover* to buy from a Chinese military-listed memory maker is an escalation vs the quiet 2022 YMTC retreat — this time Apple is actively pushing the government rather than backing off. **+ Traction is thin and contested:** no signed deal is confirmed; product/region scope and volumes are **open** and not firmly attributable to the FT report. The "iPhone 18 Pro BOM triples / $1,299 start" numbers circulating are **analyst speculation, not the FT** — Ming-Chi Kuo separately predicted flat iPhone 18 Pro pricing. **Who captures the margin:** if this is mostly leverage, the value-add accrues to Apple as a *negotiating signal* to Samsung/SK Hynix, not as real CXMT volume.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment

**Memory backdrop:** DRAM contract prices rose ~90% QoQ in Q1 2026; memory "quadrupled over ~three quarters" (attributed to Counterpoint) as makers diverted wafers to HBM for AI data centers; relief not expected until 2027–2028 (Intel CEO: "no relief until 2028"). See [[StrictlyVC AI data-center boom emerges as new inflation driver]]. **Political risk is the live variable:** Rep. John Moolenaar (chair, House Select Committee on the CCP) called partnering with CXMT "a grave mistake"; the US has so far *held off* blacklisting CXMT (BIS hasn't published Entity List updates since 2024). **+ Counterintuitive second-order effect:** Apple's lobbying may itself *raise* the salience of CXMT in Washington, increasing — not decreasing — the odds of an Entity-List designation. This connects to the broader 2026 squeeze on Chinese tech firms ([[Pentagon adds Alibaba and Baidu to Chinese military companies list]], [[China orders tech giants to halt Nvidia AI chip purchases]]).

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** DRAM is a ~$120bn+ market in a once-a-generation squeeze. Size: DRAM ~$121.8bn in 2025, ~$128bn 2026E (per Fortune Business Insights, ~7% CAGR; a competing report puts 2026 at $126bn growing ~16% CAGR to 2031 — wide spread, treat as order-of-magnitude). The structure is a tight **oligopoly**: Samsung ~38%, SK Hynix ~29% in Q1 2026 (per Counterpoint), with Micron the #3; the big three hold >95% of the market — barriers are capital (multi-bn fabs), process/EUV know-how and multi-year HBM contracts. **Why now:** makers are diverting wafers to HBM for AI data centers (HBM ~23% of DRAM wafers), structurally starving commodity DRAM. DRAM contract prices are forecast up ~70%+ in 2026 (PC DRAM ~+90–110% QoQ in early 2026); per Counterpoint, **20%+ output growth is needed before relief**, normalization not expected until 2027–2028 (Intel CEO cited in note: "no relief until 2028"). This is the live driver behind Apple's move — its DRAM coverage ran only into H1 2026 and it is structurally short (see [[AI memory-chip demand pushes Apple to raise iPhone prices]], [[StrictlyVC AI data-center boom emerges as new inflation driver]]).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: bit-shipment growth, blended ASP / contract-price trajectory, HBM mix (margin-accretive), wafer capacity (kwspm). Players: incumbents **Samsung / SK Hynix / Micron** compete on process node, HBM qualification and capacity; the disruptive new entrant is **CXMT**, now the world's #4 DRAM maker at ~7.7% share Q4 2025 (per SemiAnalysis/Counterpoint), scaling capacity from ~100k toward ~300k wafers/month by end-2026. Basis of competition: for the incumbents it is HBM qualification + node; for CXMT it is **price** (Chinese quotes reportedly 10–15% below incumbents per note) and Beijing-backed capital, not yet Apple-grade LPDDR5X at volume (EUV/process limits). Apple's position is that of a **price-taker/leverage-seeker**, not a peer — it is ~60% reliant on Samsung for DRAM (note, approximate) and is courting CXMT to pressure incumbent pricing rather than to displace them ("Micron has nothing to worry about" — 24/7 Wall St, via note). Recent moves: CXMT cleared for a STAR Market IPO (May 2026); Apple raised Mac/iPad/home/Vision Pro prices 2026-06-25 (iPhone excluded); FT report ~2026-06-26.

**Comps & multiples.** Memory incumbents trade **cheap on earnings but on near-peak cyclical numbers** — the classic memory-cycle trap (low headline P/E at the top). External (per multiples.vc / Benzinga / 24/7 Wall St, mid-2026):
- **Micron (MU):** market cap ~$1.16–1.37tn (CNBC: crossed $1tn May 2026); fwd P/E ~10–14x. Q3 FY26 revenue $41.46bn, Q4 guide ~$50bn → run-rate ~$180–200bn annualized. On ~$1.2tn cap that is **EV/Revenue ≈ $1.2tn / ~$180bn ≈ ~6.7x** (revenue is HBM-inflated and cyclical-peak; multiple flatters/deceives accordingly). PEG ~0.07 (the market is pricing a down-cycle).
- **SK Hynix:** EV/Revenue ~8.3x, EV/EBITDA ~10.7x, P/E ~14.4x (fwd P/E ~8x).
- **Samsung:** EV/Revenue ~4.0x, EV/EBITDA ~8.6x, P/E ~13.7x (conglomerate-diluted).
Distribution (3 comps, EV/EBITDA): ~8.6–10.7x — **in-line, low end of the ~8–25x sanity band**, consistent with cyclical-peak earnings the market refuses to capitalize. **CXMT (the supplier):** private/IPO-stage — STAR Market IPO seeking ~$4.3bn (RMB29.5bn); valuation estimates wide, ~$27bn up to ~$42bn (mark as **round/IPO valuation, not market cap**). No EV/EBITDA — earnings not publicly verifiable → "no data." Internal comps: [[Pentagon adds Alibaba and Baidu to Chinese military companies list]], [[US fears ASML's top chip tool ended up in China]], [[China orders tech giants to halt Nvidia AI chip purchases]], [[This Week in Fintech The cost of US tech export controls]].

**Risk flags.**
1. **Regulatory / export-control overhang (live).** CXMT is on the Pentagon 1260H list; an Entity-List designation would impose typically-refused export licensing on the relationship. Buying *from* a 1260H firm is legal today (BIS FAQ #142), so this is *prospective* — but Apple's own lobbying raises CXMT's Washington salience and may *increase* designation odds (second-order). Rep. Moolenaar already called partnering "a grave mistake."
2. **Supplier concentration both ways.** Apple ~60% on Samsung today (single-vendor dependence); diversifying to a Beijing-backed, capacity-immature supplier swaps one concentration risk for political/quality risk — CXMT can't yet deliver Apple-grade LPDDR5X at volume.
3. **Cyclical-peak comps mirage.** Incumbent "cheap" P/E (~8–14x) sits on once-in-a-generation peak earnings; if CXMT/YMTC capacity (300k wafers/mo by end-2026) plus the IPO build-out lands into a 2027–2028 supply normalization, the same multiples imply a sharp earnings down-leg — disintermediation by low-cost Chinese DRAM is the structural threat to incumbent margins.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** The memory squeeze is converting CXMT from a watched-but-marginal #4 into a strategic swing supplier — and Apple's lobbying signals demand-side desperation that legitimizes Chinese DRAM in Western BOMs (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: this is **leverage, not displacement** — if no signed CXMT volume deal materializes and incumbent contract pricing softens in H2 2026, the move was a negotiating leak. It breaks if (a) BIS adds CXMT to the Entity List (kills the option, re-tightens incumbent pricing power) or (b) Apple confirms real CXMT DRAM volume in shipping products — watch the BIS Entity List (no updates since 2024) and CXMT's STAR Market listing as the two catalysts.
