WeRize Raises $7 Million to Expand AI-Powered Financial Services
Team Startupcity
 Synopsis 
 WeRize has secured $7 million in a pre-Series C funding round led by Sony Innovation Fund. The fresh capital will help the fintech startup strengthen its AI capabilities and expand its portfolio of investment, credit, and lending products. 
Social distribution fintech platform WeRize has raised $7 million in a pre-Series C funding round led by Sony Innovation Fund , with participation from existing investor 3one4 Capital . With this investment, the company’s total equity funding since launch has crossed $28 million.
The latest round comes nearly three years after WeRize raised $15.5 million in June 2022 from British International Investment, Sony Innovation Fund, and existing backers. That round valued the company at $115 million.
The Bengaluru-based startup plans to use the fresh capital to strengthen its proprietary AI and technology platform . It will also expand into new financial products, including mutual funds, online bonds, secured co-branded credit cards, and housing loans.
Founded in 2019 by Vishal Chopra and Himanshu Gupta , WeRize focuses on bringing financial services to underserved consumers across India’s smaller towns and cities. The company offers customized credit, insurance, and savings products to people living in more than 4,000 Tier II, III, and IV towns.
WeRize operates a three-sided marketplace that connects consumers, local financial entrepreneurs, and financial institutions. Through its platform, over 19,000 trained financial micro-entrepreneurs help distribute products from banks, NBFCs, insurers, and mutual fund companies. The company currently serves customers in more than 5,000 towns and cities without relying on physical branches and supports six Indian languages.
A key differentiator for WeRize is its proprietary AI infrastructure. The platform is trained on more than 20 billion data points collected from over 4 million households across India. This AI layer helps manage multiple functions across the business, from lead generation and customer engagement to underwriting, risk assessment , and product design.
The company uses these insights to create financial products tailored to the needs of consumers in smaller cities, a segment that remains underserved by traditional financial institutions.
WeRize has also reported strong business growth. According to the company, it is currently operating at an annualized gross revenue run rate of $65 million and a net revenue run rate of $32 million. It also claims an EBITDA run rate of $15 million and an annualized pre-tax profit run rate of more than $7 million.

The startup expects its profits to grow two to three times over the next 12 months as it scales its offerings and deepens market penetration.
Its financial performance reflects this momentum. Regulatory filings show that WeRize’s revenue from operations increased 64% to Rs 236 crore in FY25 from Rs 144 crore in FY24. The company also doubled its net profit during the same period, building on the strong growth it achieved in the previous fiscal year.
The latest funding highlights continued investor confidence in fintech companies that are leveraging artificial intelligence to improve financial inclusion . With fresh capital, a growing product portfolio, and strong profitability metrics, WeRize is looking to strengthen its position as a leading fintech platform serving India’s underserved population.
