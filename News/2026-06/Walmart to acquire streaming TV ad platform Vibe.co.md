## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[StrictlyVC] Walmart agreed to acquire Vibe.co, a streaming TV ad platform that helps advertisers create and buy digital ads, as the retailer looks to bring more small and midsize marketers onto Walmart Connect and expand its push into commerce media. Terms were not disclosed. Vibe.co has raised a total of approximately $78.8 million and was last valued at $410 million in a September, 2025, round led by Hedosophia. Variety has more here.

ILLUSTRATION: VARIETY VIP+

Walmart is one of the nation’s more prominent advertisers, but, increasingly, it’s looking to become a bigger sell of advertising as well.

The retailing giant, which offers video-advertising inventory around its stores and via its Vizio TV-manufacturing unit, said Tuesday that it intends to acquire Vibe.co, a platform that allows advertisers to buy and create digital ads. Walmart sees the purchase as one way to facilitate more business from small and medium-sized marketers who might not typically buy commercials on a national media platform.

“Walmart Connect is focused on making commerce media more accessible, more measurable and easier to activate for advertisers of all sizes,” said Ryan Mayward, general manager and senior vice president, Walmart Connect U.S., in a prepared statement “Vibe.co has created a purpose-built platform that simplifies streaming TV advertising, and together, we can help more businesses connect with customers across streaming environments while measuring the impact of those campaigns through Walmart’s commerce capabilities.”

Walmart is just the latest national ad seller to make new efforts to attract local and regional advertisers. Many of these marketers are looking to bolster their customer base with national commercials, or to get more deeply into video advertising to boost sales.

The new deal builds on recent partnerships Walmart has struck Magnite, Yahoo DSP, and Google DV360.

Walmart on Tuesday offered some peek at its vision for selling advertising by unveiling some of its intentionsto roll out new video series to Vizio users that feature celebrity hosts and influencers talking about certain kinds of shopping occasions, such as early-summer patio and garden sales or interest in going back to school.

Following the close of the transaction, Arthur Querou and Frank Tetzlaff, co-founders of Vibe.co, will join Walmart Connect. The parties expect the transaction to close by the end of fiscal year 2027. Walmart said it does not expect the transaction to have any effect on recent projections tied to sales and operating income.

# Context-enrichment: Walmart to acquire streaming TV ad platform Vibe.co
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**Freshness: FRESH.** Internal semantic search surfaced no prior note on this Walmart–Vibe.co deal. The top internal hit "[[Vibe Retail and Mega Payments partner on independent-retail POS]]" is a DIFFERENT company (a retail point-of-sale vendor, not the CTV adtech firm Vibe.co). Genuinely new M&A event; not a reprint.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **June 23, 2026** Walmart signed a definitive agreement to acquire **Vibe.co**, a self-serve connected-TV (CTV) ad platform aimed at SMBs/mid-market. Close expected by end of Walmart **fiscal year 2027 (= Jan 31, 2027)**; subject to customary conditions. Founders **Arthur Querou** (CEO) and **Franck Tetzlaff** (CTO — note: "Franck," the note's "Frank" is a typo) join Walmart Connect.
- **Terms officially "not disclosed"** — but the WSJ reported the price at **~$1.4B (~$1.2B cash + ~$180M retention payments tied to key staff staying ~4 years)**; Digiday's sources put valuation "$1B+." So the de-PR'd reality: a ~$1.4B mostly-cash deal with founder lock-ups. The number is journalist-sourced, NOT company-confirmed (analysis: treat as reported).
- The note's figures **all check out**: Vibe raised ~$78.8M total; last valued **$410M** in a **Sept 2025 Series B ($50M) led by Hedosophia**.
- Walmart says the deal **will not change** its sales/operating-income guidance — signal it is strategic/long-dated, not a near-term revenue mover.
- **Why structured this way:** ~$180M of the price is retention, not purchase — Walmart is buying a *team + a book of ~10,000 SMB advertisers + tooling*, and needs the founders to stay. The "guidance-neutral" framing tells you the financial materiality is low; the value is strategic optionality (the SMB long tail + closed-loop attribution), not 2026 EBIT. The "terms not disclosed" silence is the anti-PR tell: a ~3.4x step-up in 9 months ($410M → $1.4B) is awkward to publish, so it isn't.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Self-serve CTV for SMBs is **prior art, not invention**:
- **Amazon** is the benchmark and ahead: **Sponsored TV is fully self-serve with no minimum**; Amazon DSP self-serve ~$10K min. Huge owned supply (Prime Video, Fire TV) + retail purchase data + the June-2025 exclusive **Amazon DSP–Roku** tie-up (~80M US CTV households). Amazon already has self-serve + retail data — the exact combo Walmart is buying toward. See [[Amazon Teams With Papa Johns... Alexa Ads Using Agentic AI]] (in the same source page) for Amazon's parallel push.
- **Roku** was the leading *independent* SMB self-serve CTV player — but **Fox agreed to acquire Roku (~$22B) on June 15, 2026**, eight days before this deal, removing it as a neutral independent. Notable consolidation backdrop.
- **The Trade Desk** (Ventura TV OS) — neutral CTV, but no retail-data asset. **MNTN/Magnite** chase the same SMB CTV growth.
- Retail-media peers building self-serve ad tools for SMBs: [[PayPal launches Ads Manager for small businesses]] (Oct 2025), [[Mastercard powers personalized advertising with Commerce Media]] (Oct 2025), [[PayPal partners with Rokt for post-transaction ads]]. Instacart+Trade Desk, Kroger+Roku, Target Roundel are mostly partnership-based, not owned end-to-end.
- **Position:** catching up on tooling, #2 behind Amazon on scale/time-in-market, but potentially leapfrogging the RMN pack via an **owned vertical stack** (Vizio supply + Vibe tooling + Walmart purchase data + attribution).
- **+ Second-order:** Amazon won the SMB CTV race because it married self-serve UX to first-party purchase data years earlier; Walmart's premium is *name/data-specific*, not sectoral — it can only justify a $1.4B tag because Walmart purchase data closes the attribution loop (~only 25% of CTV campaigns carry lower-funnel objectives today). The gap to Amazon closes only if Walmart's data attribution beats Amazon's on conversion, not just on price.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Walmart Connect** ad revenue: global ad business **$6.4B in FY2025, +37%** (Walmart Connect US +41% in Q4); ad+membership = **~1/3 of quarterly profit** (CFO Rainey). Yet ad revenue is only **~1% of $713B sales** vs Amazon's ~8% — huge headroom and the explicit reason for the aggression.
- Build path: **Vizio** (~$2.3B, closed Dec 3 2024) gave the TV OS + device/audience data + owned supply; open-ecosystem partnerships with **Magnite, Yahoo DSP, Google DV360** push Walmart data into third-party CTV buying. Vibe adds the **owned self-serve front door** Vizio lacked.
- **+ Why Walmart acts this way:** retail is a thin-margin commodity; advertising is high-margin and now ~1/3 of profit, so Walmart needs to convert its purchase-data exhaust and the SMB sellers already on its marketplace into recurring ad revenue. Vibe is the missing UI layer to onboard the long tail self-serve — turning the "ecom flywheel" into an ad flywheel. Compare its broader commerce-media land-grab: [[Walmart partners OpenAI for ChatGPT checkout]].

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new for Walmart:** an owned self-serve CTV buying layer + ~10,000 SMB advertisers + the team. The *capability* (self-serve CTV) is not new (Amazon/Roku/MNTN). This is **acqui-capability, not invention**.
- **Vibe traction (self-reported, Sept 2025, unaudited):** ~$100M ARR in <2 years, 10,000+ advertisers, ~120M households, 500+ apps. Founded Sept 2022, dual NYC + Paris HQ. Caveat: never independently verified; Digiday says it has "grown significantly" since.
- **+ Where the margin/value really sits:** the durable asset is the **closed-loop attribution** (Walmart purchase data + Vizio supply), not Vibe's SMB book per se. SMB ad revenue is high-churn and budget-sensitive — a $100M ARR on 10K SMBs may be far less sticky than enterprise (no NRR disclosed). The real moat is data, not the tool; the tool is replicable, the Walmart-Sam's-Club purchase graph is not. The central question shifts from "did Walmart buy growth?" to "can Walmart's attribution out-convert Amazon's to keep SMBs from churning back to Amazon's no-minimum Sponsored TV?"

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Market tailwind:** US CTV ad spend ~$37.95B in 2026 (+~14-15%), to pass linear TV by 2028. US retail media ~$58.8B (2025) → ~$69.3B (2026). The exact intersection — **retail-media-on-CTV — ~$5.0B (2025) → ~$10.3B (2028)**, +45.5% in 2025; ~1-in-5 CTV dollars projected to retail media by 2027. Fastest-growing, most defensible slice — Walmart's explicit target.
- Next: close by Jan 31 2027; integrate Vibe self-serve UI onto Vizio supply + Walmart data + Magnite/Yahoo/DV360 rails; watch SMB onboarding/retention and whether $100M ARR survives integration.
- **+ Counterintuitive second-order:** owning a DSP-like self-serve tool may *conflict* with Walmart's "neutral, open-ecosystem" partner positioning (Magnite/Yahoo/DV360) and with the 500+ third-party publishers Vibe buys from — some of whom have their own retail-media ambitions and may resist selling to a Walmart-owned buyer. Vertical integration buys control but risks ecosystem friction. The Roku→Fox deal also signals the *independent* CTV middle is being bought up on both sides — adtech is consolidating into a few vertically-integrated giants.

## Top challenge / red-team questions (second-order)

1. **Is this genuinely new capability or just buying a tool?** Mostly buying a tool + team + ~10K-advertiser book. The new part for Walmart is the *owned self-serve buying layer* it lacked; self-serve CTV itself is prior art (Amazon, Roku, MNTN). Verdict: acqui-capability, not invention.
2. **What's the real strategic value?** Closed-loop attribution + the SMB long tail: Vizio gave supply/data, Vibe gives the front door so SMBs self-buy CTV measured against Walmart purchases. Answered.
3. **Why pay ~3.4x the Sept-2025 valuation ($410M → ~$1.4B) in 9 months?** Strategic premium for fit + founder lock-up + denying the asset to rivals amid Roku→Fox consolidation; not a financial multiple. Partly open — no synergy/DCF figures disclosed.
4. **Is the ~$1.4B price real?** WSJ-sourced ($1.2B cash + ~$180M retention), NOT company-confirmed; Walmart/Vibe deliberately silent. Open (reported, not official).
5. **Is the $100M ARR / 10K-advertiser claim audited?** No — self-reported (Sept 2025), never independently verified. Open / key uncertainty.
6. **SMB churn / revenue durability?** SMB ad spend is high-churn and budget-sensitive; $100M ARR on 10K SMBs is likely less sticky than enterprise. No NRR/retention disclosed. Open / central risk.
7. **Can Walmart out-execute Amazon here?** Amazon already has no-minimum self-serve Sponsored TV + retail data + huge owned supply + the Roku DSP tie-up. Walmart is structurally behind on scale/time. Answered: catching up, #2 near-term at best.
8. **Does Roku→Fox (June 15) change the math?** Yes — removes the leading *independent* SMB-friendly CTV platform 8 days before this deal, raising the strategic value of owning an independent self-serve tool. Answered (contextual).
9. **Does owning a DSP-like tool conflict with the open-ecosystem partnerships (Magnite/Yahoo/DV360)?** Potential channel conflict with "neutral" positioning. Open.
10. **Will Vibe's 500+ third-party publishers keep selling to a Walmart-owned buyer** that competes with their own retail-media ambitions? Open / supply-side risk.
11. **Does "no guidance change" mean it's financially immaterial?** Yes near-term — implies strategic/long-dated, not a 2026/2027 revenue mover. Answered.
12. **Antitrust / regulatory risk?** ~$1.4B adds capability, not horizontal share; Vizio cleared. Low but non-zero given Walmart's data accumulation. Largely open (no filings reviewed).

14. **Where does the durable margin sit?** In the Walmart purchase graph + attribution, not the tool (replicable). Moat = data, not Vibe's UI. Answered (analysis).
15. **Who's silent about what?** Price (Walmart + Vibe — only WSJ has it); current/post-deal Vibe revenue; SMB retention metrics; separate Vizio vs Walmart-Connect-US revenue breakouts. Open.

Held below 4 because: it is acqui-capability not invention, financially guidance-neutral (immaterial near-term), price unconfirmed, traction self-reported and SMB-churn-exposed, and it leaves Walmart still well behind Amazon on scale. Adtech, not core fintech/payments, so peripheral to the radar's center of gravity.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Retail-media-on-CTV is the live wedge of two large, fast-growing pools. US CTV ad spend ~$37.95bn in 2026, +~14% YoY, set to pass primetime-linear at the upfront level for the first time in 2026 (CTV upfront ~$17.73bn vs linear ~$16.98bn) and overall by ~2028 (per eMarketer, as of Jun 2026). The intersection — retail media bought ON CTV — is the fastest slice: ~$4.46bn (2025) → ~$9.03bn (2029), +45.5% in 2025, ~1-in-5 CTV dollars to retail media by 2027 (eMarketer). Structure: consolidating, not fragmented — barriers are owned supply + first-party purchase data + closed-loop attribution (capital + data network effects), not just tooling, which is replicable. Why now: ad margin >> retail margin, and adtech is being vertically integrated on both sides — Fox→Roku (~$22bn, 2026-06-15) removed the leading *independent* SMB-friendly CTV platform eight days before this deal, raising the strategic value of owning a neutral self-serve front door (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: ad revenue growth %, take rate / ROAS, advertiser count and net revenue retention (SMB churn), and attribution close-rate (only ~25% of CTV campaigns carry lower-funnel objectives today — the gap Walmart's purchase data targets). Players & basis of competition: Amazon (no-minimum self-serve Sponsored TV + Prime Video/Fire TV owned supply + retail data + Roku-DSP tie-up) competes on scale + data and is the #1 benchmark; The Trade Desk (Ventura OS) competes as the neutral independent DSP but has no retail-data asset; MNTN competes on performance/measurement for SMBs; Magnite/Yahoo DSP/Google DV360 are Walmart's own open-ecosystem rails. Recent moves: Fox→Roku ~$22bn (2026-06-15); Walmart completed Vizio ~$2.3bn (2024-12-03) for owned TV OS + supply. Protagonist's position: clear #2 behind Amazon, catching up on tooling — Vibe supplies the owned self-serve buying layer Vizio lacked. Moat = the Walmart/Sam's-Club purchase graph + closed-loop attribution (intangibles + data network effects), NOT Vibe's UI, which any rival can rebuild (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** Deal vs the note's self-reported ARR: reported price ~$1.4bn (WSJ: ~$1.2bn cash + ~$180m retention; company-undisclosed) / ~$100m ARR (Vibe self-reported Sept-2025, unaudited) = **~14x EV/ARR** — rich, but on an unverified denominator, so treat as indicative not a true multiple. Last private round Sept-2025: $410m post-money (round valuation, not market cap) → a reported ~3.4x step-up in 9 months.
Public CTV-adtech comps (TTM, as of Jun 2026):
- The Trade Desk (TTD): EV ~$8.4bn / TTM rev ~$2.97bn = **2.83x EV/Rev**, ~15.5% growth (gurufocus/macrotrends). Profitable, neutral DSP — the cheap end vs its own historical median (~17.9x).
- MNTN (MNTN): TTM rev ~$299m, **~2.2x EV/Rev**, ~9.1x EV/EBITDA, ~35% growth; stock −65% since the May-2025 $16 IPO (stockanalysis/multiples.vc) — the closest public proxy for *self-serve SMB CTV*, and the market has de-rated it hard.
- Internal comps (same retail-media / commerce-media-for-SMB theme): [[Mastercard powers personalized advertising with Commerce Media]], [[PayPal launches Ads Manager for small businesses]], [[PayPal launches PayPal Ads ID advertising identifier]], [[Flipkart launches Advertise Now Pay Later for 24,000 sellers]].
Read: at ~14x on unaudited ARR, Vibe is priced 5-6x above where the public CTV-adtech tape trades (~2-3x EV/Rev) — justifiable only if you value the strategic fit/data-attribution, NOT the standalone economics. With only 2 truly comparable public names, distribution not computed; qualitative — peers cluster ~2-3x EV/Rev, the deal multiple is an outlier driven by control premium + denial-to-rivals, not growth alone (analysis).

**IR grounding (PRIMARY — actuals).** Walmart is covered. Per Walmart's Q1 FY27 earnings release (8-K filed 2026-05-21, quarter ended 2026-04-30), global advertising grew **+36%**, with **Walmart Connect (ex-Vizio) +44%**; ad/membership/marketplace mix is improving margins (https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/104169/000010416926000095/earningsreleasefy27q1.htm). Full-year context: FY2026 global advertising ~$6.4bn (reported +46%), still only ~1% of $713bn net sales vs Amazon's ~8% — the headroom that frames the buy. The ~$1.4bn price is journalist-sourced, NOT in any filing; Walmart says the deal does not change FY27 sales/operating-income guidance → financially immaterial near-term, strategic/long-dated.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Valuation vs comps unjustified by economics** — ~14x EV/ARR on *unaudited* SMB ARR while public CTV-adtech trades ~2-3x and the nearest proxy (MNTN) is down 65% post-IPO; if the $100m ARR is overstated or churns, the control premium has no growth backstop (second-order: a write-down risk Walmart can absorb given immateriality, but a signal on retail-media froth).
2. **SMB revenue durability / disintermediation by Amazon** — SMB ad budgets are high-churn and price-sensitive; Amazon's no-minimum Sponsored TV is a free exit. Walmart only retains the long tail if its attribution out-converts Amazon's, else the book leaks back (margin captured by the rival with bigger owned supply).
3. **Channel conflict with own open-ecosystem rails** — owning a DSP-like buyer can antagonize Magnite/Yahoo/DV360 partners and the 500+ third-party publishers Vibe buys from, several with their own retail-media ambitions who may resist selling to a Walmart-owned buyer (second-order: vertical integration buys control at the cost of neutrality).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a consolidation play, not a re-rating: the *independent* CTV middle is being bought from both ends (Fox→Roku on supply, Walmart→Vibe on the buy-side), pushing the sector toward 3-4 vertically-integrated retail-media-on-CTV giants where data-attribution, not tooling, is the moat. Falsifiable thesis (analysis): if Walmart's Q2-Q4 FY27 advertising growth holds ~40%+ as SMB onboarding via Vibe ramps, the deal validates the owned-stack model; trigger to watch — first post-close advertiser-count / Walmart-Connect disclosure and whether the self-reported $100m ARR survives integration. Thesis breaks if growth decelerates toward sector ~14% or Walmart goes silent on Vibe metrics post-close.

## Earnings Review

**Verdict (headline read).** IN-LINE on EPS, modest BEAT on revenue — and a clear BEAT on the line that matters for the Vibe.co thesis: advertising. Q1 FY27 (qtr ended 2026-04-30, reported 2026-05-21): total revenue **$177.75bn, +7.3% YoY** (+5.9% constant-currency), vs public consensus ~$174–175bn → +~1.7% beat. Adjusted EPS **$0.66, +8.2% YoY**, exactly in-line with consensus ($0.66). Global advertising **+37% YoY**, Walmart Connect (US, ex-VIZIO) **+44% YoY** — the highest-growth, highest-margin engine, accelerating into the deal. FY27 guidance reaffirmed (unchanged). Primary source: Walmart Q1 FY27 8-K earnings release (filed 2026-05-21, EDGAR acc. 000010416926000095).

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Total revenue **$177.75bn, +7.3% YoY** (+5.9% cc).
- Operating income (GAAP) **$7.67bn, +7.6% YoY**; adjusted op income +5.1% cc. Operating margin ~flat at **4.3%** (drag: ~$175m higher-than-expected fuel costs, ~250bps headwind to op-income growth).
- Gross margin **25.1%, +~20bps YoY** (vs 24.9%); mix help from higher-margin ad/membership/marketplace income.
- Adjusted EPS **$0.66, +8.2% YoY** (prior-year base $0.61).
- eCommerce **+26% YoY** (global, US and Sam's all ~23–27%); Walmart US comp sales **+4.1% ex-fuel**; Sam's Club US comp **+3.9% ex-fuel**; International net sales **+10.1% cc**.

**By segment / driver (advertising = the Vibe.co rationale).**
- **Global advertising +37% YoY**; **Walmart US advertising +36%**; **Walmart Connect (US proprietary platform, ex-VIZIO) +44%** — i.e. the owned-platform growth (the layer Vibe.co bolts onto) is running well above the segment total and above eCommerce (+26%) and total revenue (+7.3%). Marketplace-seller ad spend **+>50%** — the exact SMB/long-tail demand pool Vibe.co is meant to onboard self-serve.
- **Global membership income +17.4% YoY.** Management frames advertising + membership as **~1/3 of operating income** — the high-margin profit mix that justifies paying a strategic premium for ad tooling.
- This is the cleanest read on the deal: the buy targets the fastest, most profitable, accelerating part of the P&L, not a turnaround.

**vs expectations / prior period.** Revenue beat public consensus by ~+1.7% (~$174–175bn expected, as of 2026-05-21 per LSEG/StreetAccount aggregation via CNBC/TIKR). Adjusted EPS $0.66 = consensus $0.66 exactly → in-line, +8.2% YoY. Advertising trajectory vs prior quarters: FY26 full-year global advertising was ~$6.4bn (reported +46% FY); Q1 FY27 +37% global / +44% Connect shows growth moderating off a larger base but still ~5x company revenue growth — sustained, not a one-off. Internal corpus has no prior Walmart earnings note; related retail-media context: [[Walmart partners OpenAI for ChatGPT checkout]], [[PayPal launches Ads Manager for small businesses]], [[Mastercard powers personalized advertising with Commerce Media]], [[Flipkart launches Advertise Now Pay Later for 24,000 sellers]].

**Guidance / forward.** **Reaffirmed / unchanged.** FY27: constant-currency net sales **+3.5%–4.5%**, adjusted EPS **$2.75–$2.85**. Q2 FY27: adjusted EPS **$0.72–$0.74**. Note: the full-year adj-EPS range ($2.75–$2.85) sits *below* the pre-print Street ~$2.91 (LSEG) — guidance is the soft spot, not the quarter. Management explicitly stated the Vibe.co acquisition **does not change** FY27 sales/operating-income projections → financially immaterial near-term, consistent with a strategic/long-dated buy (close expected by FY27 year-end, 2027-01-31). Tone on advertising: confident, emphasized as a structural margin/mix story.

**Thesis-flags (does the ad trajectory justify the Vibe.co buy?).**
1. **YES on momentum.** Connect +44% / global ads +37% with marketplace-seller ad spend +>50% is the live demand signal: SMB/long-tail sellers are already spending, and Vibe.co supplies the self-serve CTV front door to capture more of it → why it matters: the deal is bolting tooling onto a demonstrably accelerating, owned-platform funnel, not betting on a new market. Second-order: even guidance-neutral, it raises the ceiling on the highest-margin profit pool (ads ≈ 1/3 of op income).
2. **Margin mix is the real prize, and it's working.** Gross margin +~20bps and ad/membership carrying op income against a 250bps fuel drag shows the high-margin mix is already cushioning thin retail economics → why it matters: it rationalizes paying a control premium (~$1.4bn reported, company-undisclosed) for ad capability rather than core retail.
3. **Growth is decelerating off a big base (flag).** +37% global now vs ~+46% FY26 — law of large numbers is biting; the bull case needs Vibe.co's SMB onboarding to re-accelerate Connect, which is unproven and won't show until post-close → second-order: if H2 FY27 ad growth drifts toward sector ~14%, the deal's strategic premium loses its growth backstop.
4. **Beat was top-line/ads, not bottom-line; guidance < Street.** EPS only in-line and FY adj-EPS guide below the ~$2.91 whisper → the print does not give management a financial cushion; the advertising flywheel is the offset they are leaning on, which is precisely why owning the SMB ad funnel (Vibe.co) is strategically load-bearing rather than optional.

Net: the ad-business trajectory **does** support the strategic rationale (fastest-growing, highest-margin, accelerating owned platform with visible SMB demand), even though the deal is guidance-neutral and ad growth is moderating off a high base.
