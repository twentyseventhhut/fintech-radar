For many US companies, Ramp is a strong solution for spend management and corporate cards. But as soon as your business starts operating internationally—paying contractors abroad, collecting revenue in different markets, or managing multiple currencies—you may need more than a US bank account.
Ramp gives you a US account. Limited gives you a global banking infrastructure.
While Ramp focuses primarily on helping businesses manage spending in the United States, Limited helps companies (US and non-US based) operate across borders with local accounts, global payments, and multi-currency capabilities built in.
What Limited Offers
With Limited, businesses can access:
US business accounts for domestic payments and collections.
MXN and BRL accounts for local operations in Mexico and Brazil.
Additional local currency accounts as your business expands into new markets.
Local payment rails that help you move money faster and reduce international transfer costs.
SWIFT payments for global transfers when local rails are not available.
Built for Companies That Operate Globally
If your business serves customers, employees, suppliers, or contractors across multiple countries, managing everything through a single USD account can create unnecessary friction.
Limited helps businesses:
Collect payments in local currencies.
Pay suppliers and contractors worldwide.
Reduce foreign exchange complexity.
Access local and international payment networks from one platform.
Manage global cash flow without maintaining multiple banking relationships.
Scale Beyond Borders
A US account is a great starting point. But for businesses operating internationally, it is only one piece of the puzzle.
With Limited, you get the flexibility to bank locally, move money globally, and manage your finances across multiple markets—all from a single platform.
