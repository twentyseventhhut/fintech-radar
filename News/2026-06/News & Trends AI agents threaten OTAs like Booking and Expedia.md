# Context-enrichment: AI agents threaten OTAs like Booking and Expedia
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
This is **commentary/market color**, not an event: the hospitality-optimist thesis that AI agents will become a cheaper alternative to OTAs (Booking.com, Expedia), whose high commissions the market resents, so intermediaries are "most at risk" of disintermediation. Cited proof-of-stress: BKNG "-15% YTD", EXPE "-7% YTD" 2026.

**De-PR'd / corrected:**
- **The figures are understated.** As of early July 2026, BKNG ~$184–185 vs a Jul-2025 ATH ~$230 — down roughly **-20% to -27% YTD** for most of 2026 (the "-15%" was true only briefly early in the year). EXPE ~$264–269 vs a **$300 ATH on 2026-01-08** — down roughly **-12%+ YTD**, not "-7%". So the drawdown is real but bigger than stated.
- **Mis-attribution is the core flaw.** Booking's 2026 weakness is driven mainly by a **Middle East / Strait of Hormuz shock and an April 28 guidance cut** (Q2 room-night growth guided to just +2–4%), **not** AI disintermediation. Pinning the stock move on AI agents is a narrative overlay.
- **The single strongest 2026 data point cuts against the thesis:** on **2026-03-05 OpenAI de-emphasized direct checkout in ChatGPT**, reverting to discovery + handing the transaction back to partner apps; OTA stocks *rallied* (EXPE +12%, BKNG +8%, TRIP +5%). Google (Nov 2025) said it has "no intention of becoming an OTA"; Project Mariner was folded into Gemini (May 2026); OpenAI Instant Checkout was retired after ~a dozen merchants ever shipped.

**Why the thesis is framed this way → why it matters:** OTAs are a pure intermediary take-rate business, so any new "answer layer" between the traveler and supply is existentially framed. But the current agent architecture (ChatGPT Apps, Google partner-routing, ACP/AP2) keeps the **merchant of record** as the OTA/supplier and routes *through* them → near-term this **adds** an intermediary (a new AI-search tax), it does not remove commissions. The central question shifts from "will agents kill OTAs?" to **"who captures the new margin layer — and is it just Google's performance-marketing game replayed, which Booking already wins?"**

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Agent platforms:** OpenAI (Operator, ChatGPT Apps w/ Booking + Expedia as first travel partners Oct 2025; Instant Checkout/ACP Sep 2025, retail-only, retired ~Mar 2026), Google (Gemini Agent Mode, Project Mariner → folded into Gemini May 2026; AP2 Sep 2025), Perplexity (native hotel booking via Selfbook+Tripadvisor Mar 2025, ~140k hotels; Comet browser free Oct 2025), Anthropic.
- **Infra / rails (see corpus):** Sabre+PayPal+Mindtrip "first end-to-end agentic travel" (Feb 2026) — see [[Sabre, PayPal and Mindtrip build agentic AI travel experience]]; Visa+AWS agentic commerce with **Expedia B2B** as a named partner (Dec 2025) — see [[Visa and AWS enable next-generation agentic commerce capabilities]]; Amadeus full-stack AI commerce + Max assistant (Jun 2026) — see [[Amadeus launches AI commerce solution and Max hospitality assistant]]; Visa Trusted Agent Protocol — see [[Fintech Wrap Up Visa Trusted Agent Protocol reference]]; Revolut Pay + AP2 targeting travel perks — see [[Revolut is preparing its payment platform for the rise of agentic commerce,]].
- **Position:** OTAs are **not being bypassed yet** — they are the supply/inventory layer every agent must route through. Payment standards (Mastercard Agent Pay Apr 2025, Google AP2 Sep 2025, Visa TAP Oct 2025) are plumbing, not demand rerouting.

**Why this lay of the land → second order:** The friction every agent platform hit — payments, refunds, cancellations, disruption liability, service, regulation — **is** the OTA moat. That is why OpenAI/Google retreated to routing-through, not owning. The moat is not "recommendation," which agents commoditize, but "transaction + trust + service," which they don't (yet).

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Booking Holdings:** working simultaneously with Google, OpenAI, Anthropic, Amazon; Priceline **"Penny"** now does end-to-end agentic booking, and Fogel cites a "noticeable uplift" in conversion. Fogel's frame: AI performance-marketing is "the model we already mastered with Google for 20 years."
- **Expedia Group:** Romie (2024), Trip Matching (+Instagram), Operator/Copilot integrations, Hotels.com agent, and **Open World B2B agentic APIs + MCP server** announced May 2026 ("live in coming months," i.e. mostly not-yet-live). Its **2025 10-K added "generative and agentic AI" as a named competitive threat for the first time**; CEO Ariane Gorin fears its brands becoming **"invisible pipes consumers never see."** ~2/3 of Expedia bookings originate direct (cited as insulation).

**Why they act this way:** commodity take-rate businesses need to avoid becoming the "dumb pipe," so both are racing to be **the agent's preferred supplier** and to defend direct/loyalty traffic — offense (be inside ChatGPT) plus defense (own the customer relationship).

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Novelty of the item: low — it is a re-statement of a well-worn thesis with a stock-price hook.** Genuine traction on *disintermediation* is unproven: **no credible public figure shows a meaningful share of OTA bookings sourced via third-party AI agents** (the oft-cited "AI handles >half of queries" is Expedia's **own customer-service bots**, not agent-sourced demand — a common conflation). 

**Where value/margin actually sits (deeper):** Under ACP/AP2 the OTA stays merchant of record and keeps the customer, while the AI platform takes a small fee → near-term this is a **new marketing/discovery tax**, not commission elimination. The **more substantive** near-term harm is **Google AI Overviews cutting organic CTR** (~-58% top-page, up to ~-65% on some travel keywords) → this raises the *free-funnel* cost even if agentic booking never disintermediates. That, not "agents replace OTAs," is the real margin threat.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Sentiment has **partly reversed**: TD Cowen called OpenAI's Mar-2026 checkout retreat a "stunning admission"; consensus increasingly frames AI-disintermediation of Expedia as **overstated** (EXPE ~2/3 direct-brand insulation). BKNG consensus ~Buy, ~$224 12-mo PT (≈+30% upside from ~$185).
- **Why the market may keep pressuring OTAs anyway:** the durable risk is funnel economics (AI Overviews) + a possible AI "tax" on discovery, plus macro (Hormuz). Counterintuitive second-order effect: the loudest "AI kills OTAs" narrative coincided with agent platforms **retreating** to routing-through — so the AI narrative is a **valuation overhang more than an operational one** right now.
- Risks / open: if an agent platform ever becomes merchant of record at scale (not the case in mid-2026), the economics flip.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Online travel is a large, structurally intermediated market: the three listed OTAs alone booked ~$120bn (Expedia FY2025 gross bookings, per Expedia Q4/FY2025 8-K) and Booking cleared far more — FY2025 revenue $26.9bn, +13% y/y, adj. EBITDA $9.9bn, +20% (per Booking Q4/FY2025 press release). The subvertical is **intermediation/aggregation**: OTAs create value by aggregating fragmented supply (hotels, airlines, rentals) and monetizing discovery + booking via a take rate. Structure is a **consolidated oligopoly at the top** (Booking, Expedia, Trip.com, Airbnb) sitting on a **fragmented long tail of suppliers** — classic aggregator economics. Barriers: brand trust, supply density, and above all **paid-search distribution** (Google) — a dependency, not a moat. "Why now": agentic commerce is going live in travel through 2025–2026 — Sabre+PayPal+Mindtrip end-to-end agentic travel (2026-02-13), Visa/AWS "Travel Booking Agent" blueprint (2025-12), and ChatGPT Agent Mode booking hotels in-interface — so the disintermediation risk moved from theory to demoable product (per Skift 2026-07-02; HBR 2026-01).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: **gross bookings, take rate (revenue/gross bookings), room nights, adj. EBITDA margin.** Booking take rate ~14.8% in Q4 2025 (Q1 seasonally lower at 10.3%); Expedia runs a lower blended take rate. Key players & basis of competition: Booking (scale + international hotels), Expedia (B2B/Vrbo, US-weighted), Trip.com (APAC, $8.19bn 2025 rev, ~$48bn mkt cap, +~60% y/y intl OTA bookings Q3 2025), Airbnb (rentals, saturating). Basis of competition is shifting from **traveler-facing distribution** to **being the trusted rail/inventory source for AI agents**: Trip.com explicitly positions as "trusted infrastructure for AI agents" via APIs/MCP; Google says it has "no intention of becoming an OTA" (Skift 2025-11-20) — i.e. wants to route, not intermediate. Protagonist position: Booking is **ahead** on scale/margins and is itself pushing AI (agentic trip-planning), but its moat (brand + supply) is real while its **distribution moat via Google is the exposed flank** (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** Public, verifiable (as of ~June 2026):
- **Booking (BKNG):** mkt cap ~$130–137bn; FY2025 rev $26.9bn → **P/S ≈ $130bn / $26.9bn ≈ 4.8x**; EV/EBITDA on FY2025 adj. EBITDA $9.9bn ≈ ~13x on equity value (EV not cleanly sourced free → treat as approximate `[UNSOURCED]` for net debt).
- **Expedia (EXPE):** mkt cap ~$27bn; FY2025 rev $14.7bn → **P/S ≈ $27bn / $14.7bn ≈ 1.8x**. Materially cheaper than Booking — consistent with lower margins/take rate and higher perceived disintermediation risk.
- **Trip.com (TCOM):** mkt cap ~$48bn; 2025 rev ~$8.19bn → **P/S ≈ 5.9x** — richest of the three, justified by highest growth (~60% intl bookings growth).
Distribution: P/S range ~1.8x–5.9x; growth-multiple correlation holds (Trip.com richest+fastest, Expedia cheapest+slowest). Booking **in-line to modestly premium** vs its growth. Internal comps (agentic-travel precedents): [[Sabre, PayPal and Mindtrip build agentic AI travel experience]], [[Visa and AWS enable next-generation agentic commerce capabilities]], [[Visa launches Intelligent Commerce Connect for agentic commerce]], [[Stripe launches Agentic Commerce Suite for AI agents]]. EV/EBITDA and P/E not cleanly computable from free sources → "no data" on precise EV.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disintermediation of the take rate.** If agents book direct via supplier APIs, the ~10–15% OTA commission is the exact margin an agent can compress; the P&L line most exposed is precisely the one that funds the business (second-order: less cash to bid on Google/marketing).
2. **Distribution dependence on Google.** OTAs' biggest cost is paid search; if Google's own agentic layer routes travel, the platform that OTAs pay could become the platform that bypasses them — a supplier-turned-competitor squeeze.
3. **Claim-vs-tape mismatch / de-PR flag.** The note cites BKNG −15% / EXPE −7% YTD as of 2026-06-30, but June/July 2026 market data shows **BKNG down ~19–27% YTD and EXPE down ~30% YTD** — the sell-off is deeper than the note states, and part of BKNG's drop is attributed to Middle East geopolitics, not purely AI (per TIKR, 2026). So the "AI killed the OTA" narrative is partly confounded by macro/geopolitics — don't over-attribute.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is an early **re-rating on a structural-threat narrative**, not yet a demonstrated revenue hit (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if agentic bookings were already leaking material volume, you'd see take rate and room-night growth roll over — but Booking's Q1 2026 revenue still grew +16% and take rate held, so the multiple compression is running *ahead* of the fundamentals. Watch trigger: first quarter where an OTA's **take rate or gross-bookings growth actually inflects down**, or a Google/OpenAI move from "route to OTA" to "book direct." Until then the bear case is priced faster than it's proven; the setup breaks if OTAs succeed at becoming the *trusted inventory rail* for agents rather than the party bypassed (analysis).
