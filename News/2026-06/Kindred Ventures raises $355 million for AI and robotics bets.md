Written by: 
 Steve Jang and Kanyi Maqubela 

 Share
 
 We are proud today to announce our new $355M set of funds, Kindred Ventures IV and Kindred Selector II. 
 When we announced our last set of funds in 2023 , we said, “We like the very beginning of cycles –– investing at the frontier.” 
 Most cycles start with researchers, hobbyists, and developer side projects. They are technical, private, and unlikely. Great early-cycle investing is different from how most venture capital is practiced lately. It requires research , diving into source material — white papers, posters at conferences, broken (though fascinating!) demos — instead of coverage , reviewing a large number of competitive pitches at the same time as the market. It requires patience , as often a theme area will take years to go from too-early to ready for velocity. And finally, it requires curiosity : the only way to persist in the pursuit of a budding thesis without immediate reward is to love the game for itself. 
 In the earliest days of Kindred Ventures, we had our first deep dive witnessing self-driving vehicles and eVTOL protos at Uber ATG. In 2019 and 2020, we made lead investments in primitive LLM-grounded hardware devices, GAN and video avatars, and computer vision-based consumer AI. Our curiosity in these early areas sparked a multi-year research endeavor to understand how the technology stack would change after 15 years of cloud and mobile. When generative AI started to accelerate, we had a running start and could keep pace: our 2022-2025 funds were our first AI-focused funds, and investments in Perplexity , Fal , Corgi , PlayAI , Quo , Inversion Space , Nuro ,  Rebellions , Creatify , Aalo Atomics , Mem0 and Causal Labs have both grown rapidly and taught us how AI will transform the whole world. Making their own AI transformation quickly, a previous cousin cohort of Kindred companies, Color , Heartbeat Health , Northflank , Opal Electronics , Tala , and Tonal have pointed and accelerated into AI vectors.  
 Today, we believe AGI is upon us, just not evenly distributed. It emerged some time in 2025, when code began writing itself . The acceleration can be felt in the velocity software and hardware companies are seeing across categories. As we all know now, the entire global economy — perhaps society at large — is all downstream of what is made possible by software and hardware in the hands of ingenious humans today.  It can be seen in the explosive demand for compute and new sources of energy. It can be witnessed in the dizzying orchestration of computer agents completing months of knowledge work in a few minutes. It can be felt in the research velocity in the sciences and the foundations of knowledge. It can be measured in the protein and peptide expressions in new drug discovery. It can be heard in the speed, fidelity, and sensitivity of AI voice and video in devices and on screens. And it can be perceived in the increasing sentience and dexterity of a humanoid robot and the hesitant probing right-hand turn of a robotaxi. It is not emerging from one source, but everywhere all at once. It feels like spring in the garden, where every flower suddenly blooms. So what is the beginning of this cycle, then? What is this frontier? 
 We believe it starts with novel intelligence : this is what consumers and enterprises alike are asking from our technology. Novel intelligence is AGI-capable models and harnesses which extend human capabilities not copy them. Today, AI looks at the data and ideas of the past. Tomorrow, it will help us be creative and make new discoveries about the future. This will happen in physical AI, where consumer robots both serve as companions and tools, where industrial robots transform and expand factories, datacenters, and manufacturing plants, and where autonomy changes how we move around the world (and beyond) safely at scale. In digital AI, where new operating systems for work will create magic for employees and even teammates where none exist, where new consumer experiences will connect us, work for us, comfort, and delight. We believe novel intelligence is the standard for new technology going forward, and we are looking to partner with companies that exist on that novel intelligence frontier. This frontier will redefine human-computer interaction and what is entirely possible. The form factors for this are hard to imagine today, but will be new hardware, new physical and biological architectures, and new social structures. If AI becomes more pervasive, our relationship with each other changes — we believe — for the better. 
 We also recognize that the world has to be reformatted to make novel intelligence truly forward-looking and work for everyone. We are investing in novel forms of compute, infrastructure, and energy that will power these tools. The capital and infrastructure build-out story is in its first chapter, and it will continue to unfold for a decade ahead. 
 If you are thinking about these topics the way we are, in early exploration of novel intelligence, in products, compute, world models, science, and in outer space and inner space, we would love to partner with you.
