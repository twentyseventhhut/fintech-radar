Investment technology platform Raise Financial Services has launched a new app called Millions, specifically targeting Generation Z and first-time investors. The app allows users to invest in mutual funds, stocks, systematic investment plans (SIPs), and initial public offerings (IPOs) while following a goal-based investment strategy.

The platform, regulated by the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI), currently does not impose any onboarding or brokerage fees for direct equity investments.

The minimum SIP amount is set at ₹500, while trading in equity, exchange-traded funds (ETFs), and margin trading incurs a fee of either ₹20 or 0.03%, whichever is lower. For equity futures and options (F&O), a flat fee of ₹20 per executed order is charged.

Millions aims to tap into a rapidly growing user base, as Gen Z accounted for 40% of all NSE-registered investors in FY25, a significant increase from 25% in FY20. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has also reported a decline in the median investor age from 38 to 33.

Furthermore, millennials and Gen Z together controlled half of all mutual fund assets in 2025, with their preference for online investment tools leading to platforms like Groww, Zerodha, and Upstox capturing 80% of retail equity investors.

Raise Financial Services is expanding its offerings beyond traditional broking to establish a comprehensive investment ecosystem. The company raised $120 million in its Series B funding round last October, achieving a valuation of over $1.2 billion.

Founded in 2021 by Pravin Jadhav, Alok Pandey, and Jay Prakash Gupta, Raise primarily serves users in Tier I and II cities with a suite of financial products, including the stock broking app Dhan and the algo-trading platform Stratzy.

Recent acquisitions, including GreenLife Insurance Broking and Filter Coffee, reflect its strategy to diversify into insurance distribution and media, respectively. Additionally, Raise is in discussions to acquire the wealthtech platform Infinyte Club for approximately $10 million.

## WeRize secures ₹28 million funding led by Sony Innovation Fund

Bengaluru-based fintech WeRize has raised $28 million in a funding round led by Sony Innovation Fund. The capital will enhance its AI capabilities and expand its financial product offerings.

## Highlights

*   WeRize raises $28 million led by Sony Innovation Fund
*   Funding will enhance AI and technology capabilities
*   Startup targets consumers in smaller cities and towns
*   Operates across over 5,000 cities in India

Bengaluru-based fintech WeRize has raised ₹28 million in a funding round led by Sony Innovation Fund, with participation from existing investor 3one4 Capital. The company plans to utilize the funds to strengthen its artificial intelligence (AI) and technology capabilities, while also expanding its product suite to include mutual funds, bonds, co-branded credit cards, and housing loans.

Founded in 2019 by former Lendingkart executives Vishal Chopra and Himanshu Gupta, WeRize focuses on consumers in smaller cities and towns. The startup distributes financial products through a network of local distributors, employing a model akin to social commerce platforms.

WeRize connects financial institutions, including banks, non-banking financial companies (NBFCs), mutual funds, and insurance providers, with customers and distributors through its technology platform.

The company operates across more than 5,000 cities and towns in India and offers services in six Indian languages. WeRize's AI-driven platform facilitates customer acquisition, assisted sales, underwriting, risk assessment, and product recommendations, leveraging data collected from over four million households across the country.

As of March 2026, WeRize reported an annualized gross revenue run rate of ₹65 million and a net revenue run rate of ₹32 million, with annualized earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization (EBITDA) of ₹15 million and a pre-tax profit run rate exceeding ₹7 million.

Vishal Chopra, Co-founder and CEO of WeRize, noted that approximately 300 million people in small-city India are earning, saving, and transacting, yet the financial system is still navigating how to effectively reach them with appropriate financial products and distribution methods.
