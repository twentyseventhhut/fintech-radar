[Linas Newsletter] The BIG News 🗞️ Two months after Capital One closed its $5.15 billion purchase of Brex, the second major fintech acquisition of 2026 has landed. Nuvei will now acquire Payoneer for $2.75 billion in all cash, creating a cross-border payments platform giant processing over $500 billion in annual volume on roughly $3 billion in revenue across 190 countries. Not too shabby!

[Linas Newsletter] But while Capital One bought Brex to own the CFO’s software stack, this deal is about owning the full cross-border money lifecycle, from payment acceptance to payout in Shenzhen or São Paulo. Thus, the real story isn’t the M&A itself. It’s who’s behind it, what they actually paid, and what a pending stablecoin bank charter could mean for the combined platform.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Nothing new on the deal itself. Linas Beliūnas (Substack) repackages the already-signed Nuvei→Payoneer take-private ($7.40/share cash, ≈$2.75bn equity value, signed 12 Jun 2026, announced 15–16 Jun) under a sharper editorial thesis: **the deal "completes Advent's quiet payments empire."** The framing's substance — already established in [[Nuvei to acquire Payoneer for $2.75 billion]]: Nuvei was taken private by Advent International in 2024 for ~$6.3bn ($34/share); Advent had earlier bought Paya; Nuvei now buys Payoneer in all-cash, debt-financed. So the protagonist is not really Nuvei but **Advent, assembling a pay-in (Nuvei acquiring) + pay-out (Payoneer payouts) closed loop inside one PE portfolio.**

**Why this framing and what it reveals.** Linas's value-add is re-centering the question from "is Nuvei+Payoneer good synergy?" to **"what is Advent building?"** — citing (Linas, unverified specifics) a "~2.2x China-discount multiple" on Payoneer and a "$15–20bn payments platform" PE roll-up ambition. Treat the 2.2x / $15–20bn as the newsletter's editorializing, not disclosed figures `[UNSOURCED]`. The de-PR'd core was already nailed in the prior note: the "44% premium" anchors to the pre-leak price (the stock had already jumped ~27–31% on the Reuters leak, so the premium to the trading price was ~10%); $7.40 is BELOW Payoneer's ~$10 SPAC IPO price (2021) — an orderly exit of a public story that didn't work, not an "unlock."

## [1] Competitors / peers
Unchanged from [[Nuvei to acquire Payoneer for $2.75 billion]]: combined entity ≈$3bn revenue, >$500bn volume, 2.4m customers, 190+ countries — still a challenger vs Stripe (~$91.5bn). Payoneer's cross-border/SMB-payout rivals: PayPal/Xoom, Wise, Airwallex; acquiring-side rivals to Nuvei: Adyen, Checkout.com, Worldpay/GPN. **The Advent lens adds one comp angle:** this is the PE roll-up pattern in payments (cf. activist/consolidation pressure in [[Elliott Management builds stake in Global Payments]]) — buy commoditizing take-rate assets at scale, on leverage, rather than build.

## [2] Company history / fit
Already covered. Advent: took Nuvei private 2024 (~$6.3bn, ~$3.2bn debt), bought Paya earlier, now Payoneer via Nuvei — a deliberate pay-in + pay-out stack assembly. Nuvei since 2024: M&A-led — direct acquiring Canada/Mexico ([[Nuvei launches direct acquiring operations in Mexico]]), stablecoin rails ([[Nuvei adopts stablecoin payment rails]]), EU MiCA/CASP licence ([[Nuvei secures EU CASP licence under MiCA]]). Payoneer: rate-sensitive earnings, Greater China ~34% of revenue, 6% layoffs ([[Payoneer cuts 6% of workforce amid profitability pressure]]), Trust Bank charter ([[Payoneer files for US National Trust Bank charter]]), Bridge stablecoins ([[Payoneer to launch stablecoin services powered by Bridge]]).

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**As an EVENT: no novelty — it is the third note on one deal.** As COMMENTARY, the only durable add is the "Advent owns both halves of the pipe" closed-loop thesis: FX spread + float yield + take-rate on BOTH legs stay in-house. That's a real structural point, but the prior note already flagged that much of Payoneer's "profit" is float/interest income (rate-dependent), and that card networks + stablecoin rails (Bridge/Stripe) pull margin out of cross-border. So the closed loop is a margin-retention argument that is itself threatened by disintermediation and rate normalization — exactly the prior note's [3] conclusion. No new traction data here.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Unchanged: regulatory gate is the binding constraint — close expected mid-2027 (outside date 12 Jun 2027 +3mo), multi-jurisdiction approvals given Greater China exposure; William Blair downgraded PAYO on the deal (upside capped at the $7.40 cash price). Newsletter sentiment is "PE masterclass / build-only era is over." Second-order: stacking two diluting take-rate businesses into one ~5.5x-levered entity makes the combination MORE fragile to a rate cut or China-flow shock, not safer — "scale" here is leverage, not a cushion.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: TPV/volume, take rate (bps), ARPU, interest-income mix. Players: Stripe (~$91.5bn last valuation `[UNSOURCED]` private), Wise, Airwallex, PayPal/Xoom on payout; Adyen, Checkout.com, Worldpay/GPN on acquiring. **What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is consolidation, not re-rating: Advent assembling a pay-in+pay-out platform to retain margin a commoditizing sector is bleeding to networks/stablecoins. Thesis breaks if a rate-cut cycle + China softness hit before synergies land.

_Earnings layer to value the take-private, anchored on Payoneer's OWN latest reported results (Q1 2026, filed 7 May 2026). This news item carries no earnings itself; figures below ground the deal price._

**Verdict (headline read).** Latest Payoneer print = **BEAT** · Q1 2026 (reported 7 May 2026, the most recent quarter, ~7 weeks before this item) · total revenue **$261.6m (+6% YoY)**, revenue ex-interest **$210.1m (+11% YoY)** · adj EBITDA **$69.4m** · driver: B2B/Checkout strength offsetting falling interest income · **2026 guidance RAISED**. The take-private at $7.40 / ~$2.75bn buys an accelerating ex-interest franchise at a depressed multiple because the headline is dragged by rate-sensitive interest income.

**Key figures (Q1 2026, Payoneer IR/8-K 7 May 2026).**
- Total revenue **$261.6m (+6% YoY)**; revenue **ex-interest $210.1m (+11% YoY)** — the gap is the falling interest-income contribution (~$51.5m of total, ~20%).
- Adj EBITDA **$69.4m**.
- Volume (TPV) **$22.8bn (+16% YoY)**; **B2B volume +44% YoY** (more than doubled the growth rate).
- ARPU **$513 (+17% YoY)**; ARPU ex-interest **+22% YoY** — 7th consecutive quarter of 20%+ ex-interest ARPU growth.
- SMB customer revenue **$189m (+12% YoY)**; B2B SMB revenue **+23%**, Checkout revenue **+46% YoY**.
- Take rate: B2B mix driving SMB take-rate expansion (exact bps not in summary; FY2025 ~120bps, Q4 ~111bps for trend).

**By segment / driver.** Growth engine = B2B + Checkout (the durable, payment-margin lines), accelerating. Drag = interest income (rate-sensitive float on client balances) declining YoY, which compresses the +11% ex-interest into +6% headline. This is the central de-PR point for valuing the deal: the asset's HIGH-quality growth (B2B +44% volume, Checkout +46%) is masked by its LOW-quality, rate-exposed interest layer.

**vs expectations / prior period.** Public reports frame Q1 2026 as a **beat** (revenue $261.6m, adj EBITDA $69.4m, stock climbed on the print, per Investing.com transcript / StockTitan). Exact consensus figures `[UNSOURCED]` (no paid feed). vs prior period: ex-interest growth +11% continues the multi-quarter B2B-led acceleration seen across [[Payoneer reports Q4 and full year 2025 results]] (FY2025 revenue $1,052.8m +8%; ex-interest $821m +14%) — momentum intact into 2026.

**Guidance / forward.** **RAISED** FY2026: revenue ex-interest **$900–940m** + interest income **~$200m** (≈$1.10–1.14bn total) · adj EBITDA **$285–295m** · company guides to **exit 2026 at a mid-teens** ex-interest growth rate (Seeking Alpha / PRNewswire, 7 May 2026). Management tone confident; emphasis on B2B and ARPU ex-interest. What they leave understated: the ~$200m baked-in interest income is a rate bet — a cut cycle pressures both EBITDA and the float-yield leg of the Nuvei closed-loop thesis.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. **Ex-interest acceleration (+11% Q1, mid-teens exit target)** → the deal buys a genuinely accelerating B2B/Checkout franchise, not a melting one → second-order: the ~2.2x EV/Revenue looks cheap on the durable engine once you strip the float.
2. **Interest income ~20% of revenue, declining** → headline growth (+6%) understates the business but the EBITDA is rate-exposed → at a rate cut, both Payoneer EBITDA and Nuvei's ~5.5x leverage math get worse together.
3. **B2B volume +44%, Checkout +46%** → the high-margin mix shift is real and on-trend → supports Nuvei's "own both halves of the pipe" rationale better than the FY2025 snapshot did.
4. **Greater China ~34% concentration** (from prior note) sits underneath these figures → the same growth is the same regulatory/flow risk gating a mid-2027 close.
