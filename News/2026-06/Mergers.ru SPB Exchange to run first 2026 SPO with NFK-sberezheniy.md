# Mergers.ru: SPB Exchange to run first 2026 SPO with NFK-sberezheniy

> [!info] 2026-06-24 · 1 упоминаний · 0 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: Mergers.ru

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[Mergers.ru] 🏦 СПБ биржа проведет первое с начала 2026 года SPO. Биржа планирует с 26 июня 2026 года допустить обыкновенные акции «НФК-сбережений». Бумаги будут размещены под тикером NFKS, стартовая цена составит 48,2 рубля за акцию, минимальный лот – 10 штук, то есть порог входа равен 482 рублям. Формирования книги заявок не предусмотрено – сделки заключаются напрямую через стакан в основную сессию с 10:00 до 18:50 мск. Акции включены в список ценных бумаг биржи с 9 июня, регистрационный номер выпуска 1-01-09351-P. В ходе SPO мажоритарный акционер компании «Пионер-лизинг» реализует пакет в 25% минус одна акция, а привлеченные средства передаст эмитенту на развитие бизнеса. «НФК-сбережения» – инвестиционная компания группы «Пионер-лизинг», основанная в 2003 году. Компания оказывает брокерские, дилерские и депозитарные услуги, специализируется на организации облигационных выпусков для малого и среднего бизнеса.

# Context-enrichment: SPB Exchange to run first 2026 SPO with NFK-sberezheniya
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
SPB Exchange (ПАО «СПБ Биржа», ticker SPBE) admitted ordinary shares of PAO "NFK-sberezheniya" — a small investment/broker company of the Pioneer-Leasing group, founded 2003 — to trading from 26 June 2026 under ticker **NFKS**. Start price 48.20 RUB, min lot 10 shares → entry threshold 482 RUB. Issue reg. number 1-01-09351-P; included in the securities list 9 June. **No order-book (book-building) process** — deals are struck directly in the main-session glass (10:00–18:50 MSK).
- The selling party is majority owner **Pioneer-Leasing**, offloading **25% minus one share**; the seller pledges to **transfer the proceeds to the issuer** for business development — i.e. this is structured as a "cash-in via the seller" SPO, not a normal secondary sale where cash goes to the seller (vedomosti.ru, interfax.ru pressrelease).
- **Buyback ladder (the key, under-emphasised detail):** Pioneer-Leasing offers to repurchase shares at **60 RUB after 1 year, 70 RUB after 2 years, 80 RUB after 3 years** — i.e. a guaranteed +24% in year 1 off the 48.20 entry. This is effectively a put option / structured-note dressed as equity, marketed to retail. (smart-lab.ru, interfax.ru)
- **Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the absence of book-building, the tiny ticket, the direct-glass mechanism and the buyback ladder all signal this is a **micro-cap retail placement, not an institutional SPO**. The "first SPO of 2026" framing is a venue/recency hook (PR), not a sign of market revival. The buyback guarantee is the real selling mechanism — it converts a risky illiquid micro-cap into a quasi-bond, which is exactly how Pioneer-Leasing already funds itself (it is a serial bond issuer). The "cash-in" label flatters what is the controlling owner selling down a quarter of the company.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Venue peers:** MOEX (Moscow Exchange) is the dominant equity venue; its **first IPO of 2026 was B2B-RTS** (PJSC B2B-RTS, Sovcombank-backed, ~11.5% offered incl. stabilisation). MOEX hosts the real institutional pipeline; SPB Exchange landing the "first 2026 SPO" of a micro-cap is a consolation, not parity.
- **Issuer peers:** NFK-sberezheniya is an independent (non-bank-owned) broker. Per the placement narrative, **no independent broker (outside banking structures) has done a listing in ~10 years** — so the comparison set is thin and old; this is novelty-by-scarcity, not strength.
- **Why the lay of the land is this way:** Russia's public-offering market is structurally thin post-2022 (2025: total public share offerings ₽125.2bn but only **9 issuers**, 4 first-timers; two large names dominated — Bank of Russia data). SPB Exchange, cut off from its former US/HK foreign-stock franchise by sanctions, is courting domestic micro-caps that MOEX largely ignores — a downmarket niche play.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **SPB Exchange:** formerly the foreign-stock gateway for Russian retail; OFAC-designated **Nov 2023**, freezing ~**$3bn** of Russians' foreign holdings. Pivoted to ruble instruments under CEO Yevgeny Serdyukov (appointed Nov 2023). Financials weak: **IFRS profit fell 41% to ₽0.63bn in 2025**, ₽122.4m RAS loss in Q1 2026. Recovery is via "neo-assets" — perpetual futures on foreign stocks (Tesla, Amazon, Netflix, AMD, Coinbase) and crypto indices launched Apr 2026 with T-Investments; neo-asset turnover hit a record ₽3.18bn (21 May 2026); Russian-securities turnover ₽180.1bn in Apr 2026 (1.6× YoY).
- **Why the company acts this way (analysis):** having lost its core franchise and with collapsed profit, SPB Exchange needs *any* primary-market flow to rebuild relevance and listing revenue. Hosting micro-cap SPOs/IPOs that MOEX won't prioritise is a low-cost way to differentiate and to claim primary-market activity. The "first SPO of 2026" PR is part of that relevance-rebuilding.
- **NFK-sberezheniya / Pioneer-Leasing:** a serial SME bond organiser (Pioneer-Leasing has 9+ bond series). NFK became a PAO (public JSC) shortly before listing. Tapping equity with a buyback ladder mirrors their bond-issuance DNA — they sell fixed-return paper to retail.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Genuinely new:** an independent (non-bank) broker doing a public equity placement is rare in Russia (~10-yr gap, per the placement). The cash-in-via-seller + buyback-ladder structure is unusual.
- **But traction is unproven:** no book-build, no demand evidence, micro ticket (482 RUB lot). The "value-add" to the market is symbolic. **Who captures value:** Pioneer-Leasing monetises a 25% stake at a price it itself backstops, NFK gets development capital, SPB Exchange gets a PR win and minor listing flow. Retail buyers get a quasi-bond whose downside protection depends entirely on **Pioneer-Leasing's solvency** — a leveraged SME-bond house. If the guarantor weakens, the "60 RUB put" is worthless.
- **Why the value-add is thin (analysis):** the buyback ladder shifts the real question from "is this an attractive equity story" to "is Pioneer-Leasing's credit good enough to honour the put?" That reframes it as a credit play, not an equity placement.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Watch: actual placed volume / liquidity in NFKS after 26 June (direct-glass, no book-build → likely thin); whether more micro-caps follow SPB Exchange's downmarket niche; SPB Exchange's neo-asset and (mid-2026) regulated-crypto roll-out (MOEX/SPB to launch under a framework finalised by 1 Jul 2026).
- **Risks/sentiment:** Russian primary market is selective and modest; MOEX IPO Index weak. SPB Exchange's recovery rests on neo-assets + crypto, not on micro-cap listings. **Second-order (analysis):** SPB Exchange leaning on micro-caps and synthetic foreign exposure signals it has *not* recovered its core business — it is rebuilding around regulatory-arbitrage niches (perpetual futures on US stocks for retail that can no longer hold them directly), which carries its own sanction/regulatory fragility.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian primary equity market is structurally thin and shrinking on the retail side. Per Bank of Russia (IPO/SPO 2025 review, via cbr.ru), total public share offerings rose to ₽125.2bn in 2025 (from ₽102.1bn in 2024) but issuer count fell from 19 to 9 (4 first-timers), and **the bulk was two large financial-sector names** (est. cap ₽300–400bn). Critically for this deal: **retail participants collapsed 4.5× to ~74,000 people**, and retail's share of IPO/SPO volume fell to 36% (from 40%), while institutions rose to 52% — i.e. the market is consolidating toward big, institution-backed deals, the opposite end from a ₽482-lot micro-cap (cbr.ru, as of 2025 review). Structure: highly consolidated/concentrated at the top, with a long thin tail that MOEX largely ignores — the niche SPB Exchange is courting. "Why now": SPB Exchange, OFAC-designated Nov 2023 (~$3bn of foreign holdings frozen, per note context), needs *any* primary-market flow to rebuild listing relevance after losing its foreign-stock franchise; its actual recovery vehicle is "neo-assets" — perpetual cash-settled futures on US equities (Tesla, Amazon) and BTC/ETH indices launched ~Apr 2026 (interfax.com, financemagnates.com), i.e. regulatory-arbitrage synthetic exposure, not micro-cap listings.

**Competitive landscape.** Venue KPIs: listing/primary-flow count, secondary turnover, and (for SPB) neo-asset turnover. Players: **MOEX** is the dominant equity venue and hosts the real institutional pipeline (note: B2B-RTS = first 2026 MOEX IPO); **SPB Exchange (SPBE)** is the sanctioned challenger pivoting downmarket. Basis of competition here is NOT price/product but *access to issuers MOEX won't prioritise* — a residual niche. Recent moves: SPB neo-asset turnover hit a record ₽3.18bn (21 May 2026, per note/vedomosti); the NFK-Sberezheniya admission (from 26 Jun 2026, ticker NFKS, 48.20 RUB start, no book-build) is the first SPO *on SPB Exchange* in 2026 — a venue-specific superlative, not market revival. Protagonist position: **niche / structurally weakened**, not "ahead." Moat is thin — venue network effects sit with MOEX; SPB's only differentiator is willingness to host paper others decline, which is not a durable moat (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** No equity trading multiples computable: NFK-Sberezheniya's total share count and implied market cap are not in the corpus or public results, so the absolute raise from "25% minus 1 share at 48.20 RUB" and any P/B / P/E = **no data**. For SPBE itself, IFRS profit is **contested**: note context says profit −41% to ₽0.63bn (2025); a TAdviser-fed web snapshot says net profit −11% to ₽6.2bn — figures conflict, so SPBE earnings multiples = `[UNSOURCED]` pending reconciliation. The economically correct comp set is **credit, not equity** (the buyback ladder reframes NFKS as a quasi-bond): guarantor **Pioneer-Leasing carries ACRA B(RU)** (downgraded, outlook revised to stable) on the issuer and **B+(RU)** on a bond series (acra-ratings.ru) — deep-speculative, with high leverage, negative operating cash flow, weak interest coverage and declining asset quality flagged by analysts (tbank.ru bond review, Jan 2026); it has kept servicing coupons (20th coupon on series 4 paid 19 Dec 2025). Distribution not computed (single relevant credit). Internal comps: the base has **zero** direct SPB Exchange / NFK notes (sem search returns only adjacent Russia-sanctions-pivot stories: [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]], [[Russia's A7 cross-border payments firm plans global expansion]]) — comparison set is empty, consistent with the note's red-team.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Guarantor credit risk (primary).** The +24%-yr1 buyback put is only as good as Pioneer-Leasing's solvency, and Pioneer-Leasing is rated **ACRA B(RU)/B+(RU)** — speculative, high-leverage, negative OCF. Second-order: this is a single-B credit play marketed as equity; a guarantor downgrade/distress makes the 60/70/80-RUB ladder worthless and turns retail "downside protection" into an unsecured claim on a subprime SME-bond house.
2. **Liquidity / price-discovery risk.** No book-building, direct-glass micro-cap into a market where retail IPO/SPO participation fell 4.5× in 2025 (cbr.ru) → likely thin, illiquid trading and no demand validation; exit before yr-1 may be at a steep discount with the put not yet in force.
3. **Venue-PR / relevance framing risk + marginal fintech relevance.** "First SPO of 2026" is a venue-specific recency hook from a sanctioned exchange that has *not* recovered its core business; the event's economic substance (a controlling owner selling down 25% with a self-backstopped put) is far smaller than the PR implies, and global-fintech relevance is marginal.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) Not a re-rating of Russian primary markets — it is SPB Exchange productising a *credit-wrapped micro-cap listing* franchise for issuers MOEX skips. Falsifiable thesis: if 26-Jun NFKS turnover/liquidity stays thin AND no second independent-broker listing follows within ~2 quarters, this is a one-off PR event, not a niche franchise. Watch as trigger/what-breaks-it: any ACRA/Expert RA action on Pioneer-Leasing, NFKS volume after 26 Jun, and the mid-2026 regulated-crypto roll-out (the real SPB recovery bet).
