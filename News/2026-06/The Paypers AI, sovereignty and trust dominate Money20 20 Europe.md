## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
This is a cluster of three The Paypers thought-leadership pieces recapping **Money20/20 Europe 2026** (RAI Amsterdam, **2–4 June 2026**; the show's 10th European edition, ~7,500 attendees, 2,300+ companies from 105+ countries, 450+ speakers across six stages). The note's three headlines — "Europe's fintech priorities shift as AI and sovereignty take centre stage," "Who Builds Trust in the Age of Agentic Commerce?" and "Agentic winners will be determined by Trust not Tech" — are commentary, not announcements. De-PR'd: the *substance* is a thematic read on three convergent threads — **agentic AI/commerce**, **European payment sovereignty**, and **trust as the binding constraint**.

The honest core (not the PR frame): the conference's actual signal is that **agentic commerce is hitting a deployment wall, not a launch moment**. Reporting from the show flagged that top code-driven agents neared ~95% transaction-completion in benchmarks, but the majority of autonomous models broke down on real-time compliance checks — i.e., agentic payments cannot scale safely without deterministic guardrails and verified-identity rails (analysis, citing show coverage). So "Trust not Tech" is less a slogan than a description of where the bottleneck actually sits: liability, intent-verification, and who is accountable for an agent's purchase.

**Why framed this way / what it reveals:** The Paypers is a payments trade publisher; the recap monetizes attention around the year's flagship EU event. The "sovereignty" framing is genuinely louder in 2026 because of geopolitical pressure on Europe to reduce dependence on US card networks and US-dollar stablecoins — but the publisher conflates *aspiration* (strategic autonomy panels) with *capability* (Wero still sub-scale vs Visa/MA). Read it as a sentiment barometer, not a set of facts.

## [1] Competitors / peers (the themes, with corpus anchors)
- **Agentic payments rails** are controlled by the card networks, which dominated this exact debate in the corpus: [[Mastercard and Visa enlist banks for agentic payment pilots]] (2026-02), [[Visa launches Intelligent Commerce Connect for agentic commerce]] (2026-04), [[Getnet and Mastercard run first live AI-agent payment in Mexico]] (2026-06), [[Fintech Wrap Up Mastercard agentic token framework reference]] (2026-03), [[DBS pilots Visa agentic commerce suite for payments]] (2026-02). Position: the *trust/identity* layer is the contested ground precisely because Visa/MA already own the rails.
- **European sovereignty / A2A rails:** EPI's **Wero** wallet "dominated infrastructure discussions" as the homegrown A2A alternative — corpus: [[ACI Worldwide and EPI to power instant payments in Europe]] (2026-06), [[EPI's Wero keeps adding vendors across the EU]] (2026-02), [[Wero launches e-commerce payments in Belgium]] (2026-03). Barriers are network effects (two-sided merchant/consumer) and bank coordination — Wero's moat is 16-bank backing, its risk is exactly that committee structure. "Why now": transatlantic tensions + EU sovereignty agenda + MiCA giving euro stablecoins a legal rail + the agentic-AI wave forcing a new "who authorizes the agent" question all converging at one event.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: for wallets/A2A — registered users, transaction volume/value, merchant acceptance, recurring-payment attach; for agentic payments — transaction-completion rate under edge-case/compliance routing; for stablecoins — circulating cap, issuer concentration. Recent dated moves: Worldline + Mastercard + ING demoed "Europe's first production-ready agentic payments flow" (Mastercard Agent Pay) at the event; top agent hit ~95% completion but most tested agents failed edge-case routing/real-time compliance (per The Fintech Times / Fintech Futures, June 2026) — i.e. agentic execution is announced, not yet trustworthy at scale. Positioning: on sovereignty, Wero is *catching up* — real scale (50m users) but still pre-POS, pre-recurring-at-scale, geographically partial (DE/FR/BE + LU/NL onboarding) vs Visa/Mastercard ubiquity; moat = bank consortium + EU policy tailwind `(analysis)`. On agentic, the card schemes (Mastercard/Visa) are *ahead* of any European-sovereign alternative — the "sovereign" agentic rail does not yet exist, which is the tension the recap surfaces but does not resolve.

**Risk flags.**
1. *Agentic trust gap (execution risk).* Most tested agents fail edge-case routing/real-time compliance; "production-ready" is a demo claim, not live unmonitored throughput — second-order: liability/fraud-loss allocation for an agent's bad transaction is unresolved, and the recap is silent on who eats it.
2. *Wero consortium/coordination risk + dependence on others' rails.* 16-bank governance slows execution (still pre-POS, partial geography); and it rides bank account-to-account rails it doesn't own end-to-end — sovereignty narrative outruns delivered ubiquity vs Visa/Mastercard.
3. *Stablecoin scale + concentration vs sovereignty goal.* Euro stablecoins are ~€450m (a rounding error) and ~41% concentrated in Circle's EURC — a *US-headquartered* issuer dominating the "European" euro-stablecoin layer directly undercuts the sovereignty thesis; ECB has flagged sovereign-bond/financial-stability spillovers as the asset class scales.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` The recap reframes the European payments contest from "cards vs A2A" to "who authorizes and stands behind the AI agent" — and on that axis the US schemes (Mastercard Agent Pay) are first to a working rail while no sovereign-European agentic equivalent exists. Falsifiable thesis: if Wero ships recurring payments + POS + a credible agentic-authorization layer across ≥5 countries within 12 months, the sovereignty push becomes real; if it does not (stuck in consortium coordination), Visa/Mastercard capture the agentic layer in Europe too and "sovereignty" stays a conference slogan. Trigger to watch: Wero Luxembourg/Netherlands live metrics (H2 2026) and any EU-sovereign agentic-payments standard vs Mastercard/Visa agent rails.
