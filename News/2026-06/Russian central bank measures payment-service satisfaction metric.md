Такие  способы обеспечивают необходимый уровень доверия потребителей.  Основная возрастная категория 

потребителей, ответивших на данный вопрос , –  граждане от 1 8 д о 24 лет и от 3 5 д о 44 лет. 

ТОЧКИ РОСТА 

При этом граждане недостаточно удовлетворены удобством оплаты  ЖКУ,  образовательных и государственных услуг :

• При оплате платежной картой необходимо вводить ее полные реквизиты, подтверждать операцию кодом. 

• В приложении банка не всегда можно получить ссылку на оплату, создать шаблон  для  быстрой оплаты. 

Граждане хотят оплачивать эти  услуги проще и удобнее, в один клик с возможностью гибкой настройки  под  свои 

потребности. 

Интересные наблюдения 

Региональные особенности проявились  в ответах  по  сценариям оплаты покупок  в магазинах  и оплаты ЖКУ: 

• На отдаленных  и малонаселенных территориях Российской Федерации использование безналичных 

платежных инструментов  при  покупках  в магазинах менее частотное,  что  связано  с географической 

спецификой данных регионов.  В Северо-Кавказском федеральном округе  при  совершении гражданами 

безналичной оплаты наиболее популярен платеж  по  QR-коду,  а не  платежной картой. 

• На выбор платежного инструмента  при  оплате ЖКУ повлияли особенности региональных систем приема 

платежей  за  ЖКУ. Так, оплата  через  приложение банка распространена  в Центральном, Северо-Западном 

и Южном федеральных округах, использование иных способов оплаты (в том числе наличными деньгами) – 

в Северо-Кавказском федеральном округе. УДОВЛЕТВОРЕННОСТЬ БИЗНЕСА 

В отличие от граждан представители бизнеса оценивают платежные услуги существенно сдержаннее. На уровень

РАСЧЕТЫ С КОНТРАГЕНТАМИ 

Банки предлагают комфортные условия  для  осуществления расчетов с контрагентами , в т ом

по банковским счетам, корпоративным картам. Также активно развивается  СБП , которая позволяет 

бизнесу осуществлять  мгновенные расчеты. 

ЗАРПЛАТНЫЕ ПРОЕКТЫ 

Уровень удовлетворенности бизнеса напрямую связан с уровнем автоматизации  процесса выплаты 

зарплаты, предоставлением банками зарплатным клиентам других продуктов и услуг по льготным 

ставкам, приоритетным рассмотрением их заявок на кредитование. 

Наиболее высоко оценены сценарии расчетов с контрагентами и сотрудниками  (выше общего уровня 

удовлетворенности бизнеса).

Это, в свою очередь, оказывает влияние на  уровень удовлетворенности бизнеса  по таким операциям .

ВЫВОДЫ 

• Уровень удовлетворенности граждан платежными услугами  относительно высокий, однако имеются 

точки роста. Приоритетными направлениями  для  дальнейшего анализа и работы в целом являются

платежным сценариям ниже общего уровня удовлетворенности платежными услугами у потребителей .

• Уровень удовлетворенности бизнеса относительно низкий. Приоритетным направлением  для  дальнейшего 

анализа и работы является расчетно-кассовое обслуживание .

ПЛАНЫ 

Полученные результаты оценки будут обсуждаться с банками и ассоциациями участников платежного рынка 

в целях выработки совместных мер по повышению качества платежных услуг .

Результаты оценки могут найти отражение в порядке раскрытия банками информации потребителям об условиях 

оказания платежных услуг в рамках работы  над  проектом федерального закона № 80192 2-8  «О внесении

закона «О потребительском кредите (займе)» .

В следующем году Банк России повторно проведет опрос граждан и бизнеса  для  мониторинга динамики уровня 

удовлетворенности потребителей платежных услуг.  Динамика данных позволит оценить, насколько действия 

регулятора и участников рынка сделали платежи проще и удобнее  для  граждан и бизнеса .

В последующих опросах потребителей платежных услуг также планируется наличие открытых вопросов 

для  сбора комментариев граждан и бизнеса к оценке удовлетворенности. Это поможет лучше понять ожидания 

пользователей и выявить зоны  для  дальнейшего развития. ПРИЛОЖЕНИЕ

# Context-enrichment: Bank of Russia publishes first-ever payment-service satisfaction metric (2025 results)
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**Freshness: FRESH.** First-ever publication (summer 2026) of a new annual CBR indicator that was pre-announced in Jan 2026. It is a SIBLING of, but NOT a duplicate of, [[Финтехно Russian central bank maps hybrid payment-instrument usage]] (story s9be4dcad), which covers a different CBR 2025 sociological report — "Отношение населения к различным средствам платежа" (cash vs cashless attitudes, file 62081). The target is file 62086, the satisfaction assessment. Same publication batch, distinct documents and figures.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- The Bank of Russia (CBR) published the results of its **first-ever assessment of consumer satisfaction with payment services**, for 2025. Survey ran **16 Feb – 23 Mar 2026**, all regions, **>10,000 citizens** plus a business sample. It will be repeated annually.
- Headline figures: **overall citizen satisfaction 71%**; **overall business satisfaction 40%**. By citizen scenario: transfers 82%, in-store 79%, online 73%, telecom 71%, government payments 68%, utilities (ЖКУ) 64%, tips/rental 64%, education 62%. By instrument: phone-number/SBP 84%, contactless 82%, biometrics 73%, QR 72%, e-wallet 71%, bank app 69%, card 67%. Business: settlements with counterparties 51%, payroll projects 43%, cash-settlement (РКО) 25%, cross-border 12%.
- **Why structured this way / what it reveals:** This is a regulator building a *supervisory KPI*, not a consumer-facing comparison. The note's own gloss is correct — **no per-bank differentiation** ("среднее по больнице"), so it cannot be used by consumers to choose a bank; its purpose is to let the CBR triangulate where the *system* underperforms (utilities, government, education for retail; РКО and cross-border for business) and prioritise regulation. The low business number (40% vs 71% retail) is the real signal: cross-border at **12%** and РКО at **25%** quantify the cost of sanctions/de-risking and opaque blocking practices — the CBR is effectively measuring the damage from its own AML/151-FZ blocking regime and geopolitics.
- **Methodology caveat (flag):** The Jan-2026 pre-announcement defined the *index* as a NET difference (% rating 9–10 minus % rating 0–6 on a 0–10 scale). The published report-card presents satisfaction as plain percentages (71%/40%). Whether "71%" is the net-difference index or a top-box share is not made explicit in the dumped text — a real ambiguity for anyone trying to track the series year-over-year. (analysis)

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Direct structural peer (corpus):** [[Colombia central bank survey shows electronic payments growth]] — Banco de la República runs an annual electronic-payments survey. Central-bank payment-behaviour/satisfaction surveys are a standard tool (also ECB SPACE, Bank of England, Riksbank, Fed Diary of Consumer Payment Choice). The CBR is *catching up* to a long-standing global practice, not innovating.
- **Private-sector analogue (corpus):** [[Monzo beats rivals in UK customer satisfaction survey]] and [[Revolut secures full UK banking licence]] both rely on YouGov-style *brand* satisfaction. The contrast is the point: private surveys rank named players for marketing; the CBR's is deliberately bank-agnostic and supervisory.
- **Why the lay of the land is this way:** In a deeply concentrated, increasingly state-shaped Russian payments market (SBP, Mir, NSPK), a regulator-run aggregate metric substitutes for the competitive consumer-choice signal that brand surveys provide elsewhere. Second-order: a state metric with no per-bank breakdown protects banks from public ranking while giving the CBR a private lever — it shifts accountability from "which bank is bad" to "which scenario the system must fix."

## [2] Company history / fit (CBR)
- Fits a clear CBR trajectory of formalising payment-market oversight in 2026: the paired **digitalization index** (15 indicators, 5 sub-indices) announced alongside satisfaction in Jan 2026; plus regulatory activism this period — [[Финтехно Russian central bank sets digital ruble payroll tariffs]], [[Russia's central bank calls MAX transaction approval premature]]. The CBR is simultaneously building rails (SBP, digital ruble) and now the *measurement apparatus* to justify and steer further intervention.
- **Why the CBR acts this way:** As both operator (NSPK/SBP/Mir) and regulator, it needs evidence to set priorities and to back the consumer-disclosure provisions of draft federal law **№ 801922-8** (amending the National Payment System law and the Consumer Credit law on how banks disclose payment-service terms). The metric becomes the empirical backbone for that legislation. (one level deeper: the survey isn't neutral research — it is being staged to feed a specific disclosure mandate.)

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty:** Genuinely new *as a CBR instrument* (first run), but methodologically ordinary — it replicates what other central banks have done for years. Value-add is in the *Russian-specific diagnostics*, not the technique.
- **Traction = zero yet:** This is a baseline reading, not an outcome. There is no time series, no proof any number moves, and explicitly no bank-level data. "Will be discussed with banks and associations" is intent, not action.
- **Where the real signal is:** The business sub-scores. Cross-border at 12% and РКО at 25% are the only figures here that would move a fintech thesis — they quantify (a) the durability of cross-border payment friction under sanctions and (b) merchant/SME frustration with opaque acquiring tariffs and account blocking. That is where the regulatory pressure (and potential product opportunity for alternative-rails/cross-border players) actually sits. (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- CBR will repeat the survey in 2027 (adding open-ended questions) to track dynamics; results feed discussions with banks/associations and the № 801922-8 disclosure project. Priority remediation areas named: government payments, utilities, education (retail) and РКО (business).
- **Why the market will go this way / second-order:** The metric is a soft-power lever. Once a baseline exists, the CBR can publicly point to laggard scenarios to justify mandates (one-click utility/education payment standards, acquiring-tariff transparency, clearer blocking criteria). Counterintuitive effect: by publishing an *aggregate* with no bank names, the CBR keeps the political cost low while preserving the option to regulate hard later — the absence of per-bank data is itself a strategic choice, not a limitation. For fintechs, the actionable read is the structurally low cross-border/РКО satisfaction, which signals continued regulatory attention to SME payment pain rather than any retail-rails change.

## Top challenge / red-team questions

1. **Is it really new?** Yes — first-ever publication of this CBR metric (pre-announced Jan 2026, results summer 2026). FRESH.
2. **Is it a duplicate of the hybrid-instrument note?** No. [[Финтехно Russian central bank maps hybrid payment-instrument usage]] (s9be4dcad) covers a *different* CBR 2025 report (cash vs cashless attitudes, file 62081). This is file 62086, satisfaction. Sibling, not reprint.
3. **Is "71%" the announced net-difference index (9–10 minus 0–6) or a top-box share?** OPEN — the pre-announcement defined a net index; the report-card shows plain %. The dumped text does not reconcile this. Material for tracking the series.
4. **Does the metric let consumers compare banks?** No — explicitly no per-bank differentiation. It is supervisory, not consumer-facing. By design.
5. **Is there any traction/outcome?** No — baseline only, no time series, no committed remediation, just "will be discussed with banks."
6. **What is the single most economically meaningful figure?** Business: cross-border 12% and РКО 25%. These quantify sanctions friction and acquiring/blocking opacity — the only thesis-moving numbers.
7. **Why is business satisfaction (40%) so far below retail (71%)?** Cross-border failure (geopolitics) + opaque tariffs + AML/151-FZ blocking with unclear criteria. The CBR is partly measuring damage from its own regime. (analysis)
8. **How representative is the citizen sample?** >10k, skewed to Central (27.7%) and Ural (23.7%) federal districts and to ages 35–54 (>50%). Self-selected online survey → likely over-represents digitally active users → upward bias on satisfaction. OPEN on weighting.
9. **Is the technique novel globally?** No — ECB SPACE, BoE, Riksbank, Fed, Banco de la República ([[Colombia central bank survey shows electronic payments growth]]) all run such surveys. CBR is catching up.
10. **What does the metric actually feed?** Draft federal law № 801922-8 (payment-service disclosure) + the paired digitalization index. It is staged to back specific legislation, not neutral research.
11. **Who is silent about what?** No bank names, no methodology weighting in the public text, no absolute index value vs the announced formula, no business sample size disclosed.
12. **Could this change a fintech/investment view?** Marginally — only via the structurally low SME cross-border/РКО scores signalling continued regulatory focus on SME payment pain. Retail numbers are confirmatory, not actionable.
13. **What-if it falls next year?** A drop would be a public lever for harder mandates (one-click utility/gov payments, acquiring-tariff transparency). The aggregate-only format keeps political cost low while preserving that option.
14. **Does the headline frame mislead?** Mildly — "71% satisfaction" sounds like a positive PR story; the real story is the 40% business / 12% cross-border weakness and the metric's role as a regulatory instrument.
15. **Second-order:** Publishing an aggregate with no bank breakdown is itself a strategic choice — shields banks from ranking, concentrates the lever with the CBR. Not a limitation, a design.

The only thesis-relevant content is the low business cross-border/РКО satisfaction, which merely confirms known sanctions/SME-friction dynamics. Informative, low weight.
