## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** On 2026-06-22, The Information reported that Kalshi has passed **$2bn in annualised revenue** (a run-rate, not audited full-year earnings), up roughly **3x since November 2025** (~$667m run-rate then). The surge was driven by NBA playoffs and FIFA World Cup betting volume. Kalshi has begun **early, informal IPO talks** with investment banks; a listing is unlikely before **late 2027 / early 2028**.

**[2] Trajectory (valuation, dated).** $2bn (Jun-2025) → $11bn (Dec-2025) → $22bn (May-2026 Series F, Coatue) → reportedly **targeting ~$40bn** in a new round that could close as soon as Q3-2026 (CoinDesk, 2026-06-24). Total raised ~$2.685bn across five rounds since Jun-2025.

**[3] Volume & operating metrics (dated, exact).** May-2026 monthly volume **$16.81bn** (+13.5% vs April); annualised trading volume rose from **$52bn → $178bn** in six months to early May; **institutional volume +800%** over that span. June-2026 monthly volume reported at ~$21.1bn (CoinDesk) vs Polymarket's ~$9.7bn. Sports contracts = up to **~90% of revenue/volume** (Congressional Research Service, year ending Feb-2026).

**[4] Regulatory backdrop.** Third Circuit (2026-04-06, *KalshiEX v. Flaherty*) held the Commodity Exchange Act likely preempts state gambling laws for sports event contracts on CFTC-registered DCMs, affirming Kalshi's preliminary injunction — but a Massachusetts state court (Jan-2026) ruled the other way, and Kalshi faces 19+ federal suits and a live CFTC rulemaking (comments due 2026-04-30). The dispute may reach the Supreme Court. This is the central swing factor for the IPO thesis.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
**Red-team questions**

1. "$2bn annualised revenue" is a **run-rate**, annualising a single peak month (NBA playoffs + World Cup). What is the actual trailing-twelve-month revenue, and how much is the headline inflated by seasonal sports peaks?
2. If sports = ~90% of volume/revenue, the entire IPO thesis rests on one regulatory outcome. How is the business valued if courts/CFTC restrict sports event contracts?
3. Revenue "tripled since November" — but November was itself elevated by the 2025 election cycle aftermath. Is the base period cherry-picked to maximise the multiple?
4. What is the **take rate** implied? $2bn revenue on $178bn annualised volume = ~1.1%; on June's ~$21bn/month ($250bn annualised) it's lower. Which volume base is the $2bn actually annualising, and is the take rate sustainable or promotional?
5. How much volume is wash/self-cross/incentivised trading (institutional market-maker rebates) vs genuine end-user demand?
6. The Third Circuit win is a *preliminary injunction*, not a final merits ruling, and a Massachusetts court ruled the opposite. What is the realistic probability the Supreme Court narrows CFTC preemption?
7. A ~$40bn target on $2bn run-rate revenue = ~20x revenue for a business that is largely retail sports gambling dressed as a derivatives exchange. Is this multiple defensible vs DraftKings (~3-5x sales)?
8. "Early IPO talks" with no listing before late-2027/2028 — is the leak timed to support the concurrent $40bn private round rather than signalling genuine IPO readiness?
9. Polymarket's volume is roughly half Kalshi's and it's raising at ~$15bn. Why the valuation premium for Kalshi beyond US regulatory status — is it durable?
10. What are the unit economics net of customer acquisition, market-maker incentives, and rising legal/lobbying spend (19+ federal lawsuits)? Is the run-rate revenue actually profitable?
11. Concentration risk: how much revenue is tied to a handful of mega-events (World Cup, NBA playoffs, elections)? What does an event-light quarter look like?
12. Is "annualised revenue" gross handle/notional, net commission, or gross gaming revenue? The figure's definition materially changes the multiple — has Kalshi disclosed which?
13. Reputational/political risk: Democrats are urging the CFTC to rein in prediction-market sports betting and insider-trading concerns. Could an IPO be derailed by a change in CFTC composition or the CLARITY Act?

**Importance: 4/5 — rationale.** A genuinely material datapoint: a ~3x revenue jump to a $2bn run-rate plus the first concrete IPO signal repositions prediction markets from a niche to plausible financial infrastructure, and it directly feeds an active ~$40bn fundraise. Marked down from 5 because the figure is an unaudited, seasonally-flattering run-rate and the entire thesis is hostage to one binary regulatory outcome (sports = ~90% of revenue), so the durable signal is weaker than the headline implies.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector context.** Prediction / event-contract markets have transitioned in 2025–26 from political-betting curiosity to a fast-scaling, partly-institutionalised asset class. Kalshi is the US-regulated leader (CFTC-registered DCM); Polymarket is the crypto-native rival. Growth has been propelled by sports event contracts (NBA, NFL, FIFA World Cup) and, increasingly, institutional liquidity providers (Susquehanna, Jump) treating contracts as hedging/trading instruments. The structural question is whether these are "exchanges" (high-multiple infrastructure) or "regulated sports gambling" (low-multiple consumer betting).

**Comparable set / benchmarks.**
- **Polymarket** — closest comp. ~$9.7bn June-2026 volume (vs Kalshi ~$21.1bn); reportedly raising at ~$15bn vs Kalshi's ~$40bn target. Discount partly attributed to crypto ties / weaker US regulatory standing.
- **Kalshi own trajectory** — $2bn (Jun-25) → $11bn (Dec-25) → $22bn (May-26) → ~$40bn target (Q3-26). ~20x revenue on $2bn run-rate.
- **DraftKings / FanDuel (Flutter)** — listed sports-betting comps trade ~3–5x sales; highlights how aggressive a ~20x prediction-market multiple is if the business is reclassified as gambling.
- **Exchange comps (CME, ICE, Coinbase)** — the bull case prices Kalshi against derivatives-exchange multiples; far from validated at this scale/regulatory certainty.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Regulatory binary (highest).** ~90% of revenue is sports contracts; outcome hinges on CFTC preemption surviving final merits + Supreme Court, against active state suits (MA ruling adverse, Jan-26) and a live CFTC rulemaking. A loss could gut the IPO thesis.
2. **Revenue quality / definition.** "$2bn annualised" is an unaudited run-rate annualising a seasonal peak; TTM revenue and the metric's definition (handle vs net commission vs GGR) are undisclosed.
3. **Valuation stretch.** ~20x run-rate revenue and an 8x valuation jump in <12 months invite a markdown if growth or regulation disappoints.
4. **Event concentration / seasonality.** Heavy dependence on a few mega-events (World Cup, NBA playoffs, elections) makes volume lumpy.
5. **Volume quality.** Institutional +800% growth may include incentivised/market-maker volume; unclear share of organic end-user demand.
6. **Political/headline risk.** Democratic pressure on the CFTC, insider-trading concerns, gaming-industry lobbying (CLARITY Act) could shift the framework before any IPO.
7. **Competitive/timing risk.** New CFTC-approved entrants (e.g., Novig) and Polymarket's US re-entry could compress take rates.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
_Note: Kalshi is a private company. These are reported operating metrics (revenue run-rate and trading volume), **not audited earnings**. No income statement, margins, or profitability were disclosed. Figures are journalist-sourced (The Information), not company financial statements._

**Reported figures (dated, exact).**
- **Annualised revenue run-rate:** ~**$2.0bn** (reported 2026-06-22). This is a run-rate (a peak month annualised), not trailing-twelve-month audited revenue.
- **Prior run-rate (Nov-2025):** ~**$667m** → implies ~**3x growth** in ~7 months.
- **Monthly trading volume:** **$16.81bn** in May-2026 (+13.5% MoM vs April); ~**$21.1bn** in June-2026 (CoinDesk).
- **Annualised trading volume:** **$52bn → $178bn** over six months to early May-2026.
- **Institutional volume:** **+800%** over six months to early May-2026.
- **Revenue mix:** sports contracts up to **~90%** of revenue/volume (CRS, year ending Feb-2026).

**Growth vs prior.** Revenue run-rate ~3x since Nov-2025; trading volume ~3.4x annualised over six months. Growth is real but front-loaded by seasonal sports catalysts (NBA playoffs, FIFA World Cup) and an institutional-volume ramp.

**Beat / miss / in-line.** **No consensus or guidance exists** (private, no formal earnings). Against the implicit narrative of rapid scaling, the print is **a beat on the growth narrative** — a faster-than-expected revenue ramp that materially supports the ~$40bn fundraise and IPO optimism.

**Thesis flags.**
- Run-rate ≠ TTM: headline likely flatters durable revenue; no margin/profitability disclosed.
- Implied take rate ~1.1% on $178bn annualised volume; sustainability and promotional component unknown.
- ~90% sports concentration means the revenue figure is only as durable as the regulatory outcome.
- Metric definition (handle vs net commission vs GGR) undisclosed — materially affects any valuation multiple.
