Tabby gets consumer and SME finance licences in Saudi Arabia
Claudia Pincovski
30 Jun 2026 / 4 Min Read
Tabby, the Saudi Arabia-based financial services app, has been granted consumer finance and SME finance licences by SAMA. The approvals allow the company to offer longer instalment plans on larger purchases, in addition to its existing four-instalment, interest-free model, and to provide working capital to small and medium-sized businesses.
The SME finance licence allows Tabby to provide working capital funding directly to retailers operating on its platform, supporting their growth alongside the consumer-facing payment options.
Extended payment plans now live across retail partners
The longer plans are already available across several of Tabby's retail partners, including Noon, Fitness Time, Almanea, IKEA, Almosafer, Almatar, and flynas. They apply to purchases above USD 533, with a limit of up to USD 13,333. The plans are being rolled out to an initial group of customers, with availability extending to all eligible users over the coming weeks.
The plans are structured to be Shariah-compliant, based on a Murabaha model in which the total cost is agreed upfront and remains fixed for the duration of the plan. There is no compounding interest and no late fees, consistent with the no-fee, no-interest structure of Tabby's existing four-instalment offering.
The extended plans are intended to support purchases in categories that typically involve higher costs than everyday retail, such as education, travel, used cars and short-term rentals. Merchants operating in these sectors can integrate Tabby at checkout to offer instalment-based payment over a longer period.
Regulatory background
The two licences extend Tabby's regulatory footprint in Saudi Arabia, following its graduation from SAMA's regulatory sandbox and its Buy Now, Pay Later licence, received in 2025. SAMA grants finance licences to companies that meet its requirements for compliance, security and consumer protection. The move aligns with the objectives of Saudi Vision 2030, which includes expanding access to fair and transparent financial services.
A company official said the new licences extend the flexibility Tabby already provides to a broader range of larger purchases, including education, home furnishings and travel, in response to demand from existing customers.
Tabby reports more than 25 million registered users and over 65,000 partner businesses across the GCC. The new licences are expected to support the company's expansion into higher-value payment categories within its largest market.

# Context-enrichment: Tabby wins consumer and SME finance licences in Saudi Arabia
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 29–30 Jun 2026 SAMA granted Tabby (Riyadh-HQ'd, MENA's most valuable fintech) TWO new finance licences in Saudi Arabia: a **consumer finance licence** and an **SME finance licence**. These sit ON TOP of the BNPL licence Tabby received in 2025 after graduating SAMA's sandbox — this is a genuine regulatory step-up, not a re-report of the 2025 BNPL grant.

De-PR'd substance:
- **Consumer finance (live, partly):** longer instalment plans (press cites up to ~12 monthly payments) on larger tickets — USD 533 floor, USD 13,333 ceiling (~SAR 2,000–50,000). Already live at Noon, Fitness Time, Almanea, IKEA, Almosafer, Almatar, flynas; "rolling out to all eligible users over coming weeks" → so **partial live, not full-population**. Structured as Shariah-compliant **Murabaha** (fixed cost agreed upfront, no compounding, no late fees).
- **SME finance (permission only, NOT live):** working-capital funding directly to retailers on Tabby's platform. **No launch date, no terms, no ticket sizes, no rate disclosed** → treat as a confirmed regulatory permission, not a shipping product. This is the single biggest gap the PR glosses over.
- Scale claims (unaudited PR figures): 25M+ registered users, 65,000+ partner businesses across GCC.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the classic BNPL→lender graduation. A pure BNPL "Pay-in-4" is a thin, interest-free product with a merchant-funded take rate; a consumer/SME **finance licence** lets Tabby (a) extend duration and ticket (higher-margin instalment credit, education/travel/used-cars) and (b) lend working capital to its own merchant base — turning platform data into a credit product. The Murabaha wrapper is not incidental: it is the regulatory/religious precondition for charging for duration in KSA without calling it "interest". The consumer half is shipped precisely because it is easy to bolt onto existing merchant rails; the SME half is announced-only because it needs capital, underwriting and balance-sheet mechanics Tabby hasn't detailed.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Tamara** (direct KSA BNPL rival) is the reference point and is AHEAD on the headline capability: SAMA licensed **Tamara Finance for consumer finance on 3 Mar 2025** — roughly 15 months before Tabby's consumer finance grant. Tamara also lined up up to **USD 2.4B** in Shariah-compliant financing (Goldman, Citi, Apollo) to fund exactly this kind of larger-ticket/credit expansion. So on the "become a regulated lender" axis, **Tabby is catching up, not leading.**
- **Internal corpus comps (SAMA lending track):** [[SAMA grants Rifd initial approval for consumer finance]] (Feb 2026) and the broader SAMA licensing wave — [[Ateon receives Saudi SAMA payment services license]], [[SAMA grants Drahim open-banking payment services license]], [[Saudi central bank begins licensing open banking fintechs]] — show SAMA is systematically formalising a fintech lending/payments perimeter under Vision 2030. Tabby's licences are one node in that programme, not a one-off favour.
- **Tabby's own adjacent licences:** [[Tabby secures Stored Value Facilities wallet license in UAE]] (Apr 2026, CBUAE SVF — accounts/cards) shows the SAME multi-licence, full-stack playbook running in parallel across two regulators.

**Why the lay of the land is this way / second-order:** BNPL economics (merchant-funded, interest-free, thin) don't support an IPO multiple on their own; both Tabby and Tamara are racing to convert a payments footprint into a regulated **credit balance sheet** because that is where durable, spread-based revenue lives. Tamara moved first on the consumer licence and pre-funded the balance sheet ($2.4B); Tabby's edge is scale/brand and a broader multi-market licence stack (KSA BNPL+consumer+SME, UAE SVF). The real contest is not "who has BNPL" but "who can underwrite duration credit profitably at scale under Murabaha" — and there Tamara's disclosed war-chest is a concrete lead Tabby has not yet matched in the public record.

## [2] Company history / fit
Trajectory: Series E **USD 160M at USD 3.3B** (Feb 2025, expand-beyond-BNPL + IPO framing) → secondary sale at **USD 4.5B** (Oct/Nov 2025, see [[Gulf Startup Tabby nabs $4.5 billion valuation in secondary sale]] / [[Tabby completes secondary share sale at $4.5B valuation]]) → KSA BNPL licence 2025 → UAE SVF wallet licence Apr 2026 → **KSA consumer + SME finance licences Jun 2026**. Saudi unit revenue reported at **USD 387M, +42%** ([[Tabby reports $387m revenue from Saudi unit, up 42%]]). IPO on Tadawul repeatedly flagged (banks hired), targeted around 2026.

**Why the company acts this way:** a commodity, interest-free take-rate caps the multiple. To justify a $4.5B mark and a Tadawul IPO, Tabby needs (a) higher-margin duration credit and (b) a B2B lending line (SME working capital) that monetises the merchant graph it already owns. The consumer + SME finance licences are the regulatory unlock for both. Fit is logical and consistent with the 18-month "beyond BNPL" narrative — this is Tabby doing exactly what it told Series E investors it would do, in its single largest market, right before an IPO.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Genuinely new for Tabby:** yes — first time it can offer regulated longer-duration consumer credit and SME working capital in KSA. Not new for the MARKET (Tamara had the consumer licence 15 months earlier; Rifd, others in the SAMA pipeline).
- **Traction — split verdict:** consumer side has REAL early traction (named live merchants: Noon, IKEA, flynas, etc.; concrete ticket band $533–$13,333) but only "initial group of customers", full rollout pending → live-but-partial. **SME side = zero disclosed traction** (announcement only).

**Why the value-add is real or not, deeper:** the durable margin shifts from merchant-funded MDR to **spread on duration credit + SME lending spread**. That is a real upgrade IF Tabby can fund and underwrite it. The unanswered question is **who provides the balance sheet**: Tamara pre-announced $2.4B; Tabby's June PR is silent on funding, cost of capital, loss rates and SME underwriting. Under Murabaha with "no late fees / no compounding", the economics depend entirely on the fixed upfront markup and on default control — with no downside penalty mechanism disclosed, the loss-absorption and collections model is the load-bearing unknown. So the licence is necessary but not sufficient: value accrues only if the capital, underwriting and Murabaha unit economics work — none of which is evidenced here.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** full consumer rollout "over coming weeks"; SME product launch date/terms TBD; IPO on Tadawul remains the strategic backdrop (this licence stack is IPO-story fuel — "regulated full-stack lender", not "BNPL app").
- **Regulatory backdrop:** aligned with Vision 2030 / National Fintech Strategy; SAMA is actively licensing a lending+payments perimeter (Rifd, Drahim, Ateon, open-banking cohort) → tailwind, but also more licensed competitors.
- **Risks / second-order (analysis):** (1) Consumer-credit expansion into KSA households at larger tickets raises credit-loss and (eventually) over-indebtedness/regulatory-scrutiny risk — the "no fees" model removes penalty revenue that normally cushions defaults. (2) Tamara's funded head start means Tabby may compete on a balance sheet it hasn't yet disclosed. (3) SME working-capital lending is a different, riskier underwriting discipline than consumer BNPL; announcing it pre-IPO is narrative-positive but execution-heavy. **Counterintuitive:** the licences make Tabby look bigger/safer for an IPO, but they also move it onto Tamara's turf and onto a capital-intensive lending model where the thin, capital-light BNPL edge no longer protects it.

Entry barrier is now primarily regulatory/capital: SAMA requires licensing, min. ~SAR 5m (~USD 1.33m) capital and Saudisation quotas. **Why now:** the story is not BNPL adoption but regulatory graduation — Tabby moved from SAMA's sandbox → BNPL permit (2025) → full consumer-finance + SME-finance licences (Jun 2026), letting it lend on tickets from ~USD 533 up to ~USD 13,333 (SAR 2,000–50,000) over up to 12 months, all Shariah-compliant Murabaha, no interest/late fees. **Risk flags.**
1. **Credit risk migrates onto Tabby's balance sheet.** Moving from 4-instalment interest-free BNPL to 12-month term lending on tickets up to ~USD 13,333 plus SME working capital shifts the model from short-duration merchant-funded credit to genuine consumer/SME lending — longer duration, higher loss-given-default, and untested underwriting on larger tickets. Second-order: funding cost and cost of risk become the binding constraint, not merchant sign-ups.
2. **Market concentration.** Saudi Arabia is explicitly Tabby's largest market and the locus of this expansion; regulatory, macro (oil-cycle/consumer-credit) or competitive shocks there hit disproportionately. The Shariah/no-late-fee structure also removes penalty-fee revenue, so unit economics lean entirely on merchant take and lending spread.
3. **"Announced vs live" gap on the SME product.** Consumer plans are already live at named partners (Noon, IKEA, flynas, etc.), but Tabby has disclosed no launch date, pricing or terms for SME working-capital lending — treat SME as a permission, not a running business, and note the company is silent on loss rates and SME underwriting.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a re-rating catalyst dressed as a licence: Tabby is converting a payments/BNPL wrapper into a regulated full-stack consumer + SME lender, which (if underwriting holds) justifies a lending-multiple rather than a payments-multiple and widens TAM into higher-value categories (education, travel, autos). Falsifiable thesis: if Tabby's next equity round prices above USD 4.5bn on the strength of lending income, the licence thesis is validated; it breaks if term-lending loss rates or SME defaults force provisioning that compresses the very spread the expansion was meant to capture. Watch for: SME product go-live + any disclosed cost-of-risk, and whether Tamara answers with its own consumer/SME finance licence.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Verdict (headline read).** Private, no consolidated earnings — but Tabby is IR-covered and has disclosed hard operating/financial metrics. This item is a **regulatory print, not an earnings print**: SAMA consumer-finance + SME-finance licences. Read it as **thesis-POSITIVE for lending economics and pre-IPO readiness** — it converts Tabby's KSA book from a 4-instalment interest-free product (float on merchant fees) into a licensed, longer-duration, higher-ticket Murabaha lender that can also fund merchants directly. No new financials in the news itself; the most recent disclosed financials are the audited FY2025 Saudi-unit result below.

**Key figures (all company/IR-disclosed, not estimated).**
- **KSA subsidiary FY2025 (first independently audited statements, y/e 31 Dec 2025):** net profit **$55m** on revenue **$378m** (headline framing: **$387m** revenue). Revenue **+42% YoY** from **$267m** (2024); net income **+82% YoY** from **$30m** — **second consecutive year of profitability**. [[Tabby reports $387m revenue from Saudi unit, up 42%]]
- **Registered users (GCC):** **>25m** as of this note (2026-06-30) — up from **>15m** at Series E (Feb 2025) and **>12m** in Apr 2024.
- **Partner businesses / sellers:** **>65,000** (2026-06) vs **>40,000** sellers (Feb 2025) and **>30,000** brands (Apr 2024).
- **Annualized sales volume (GMV proxy):** **>$10bn annualized** as disclosed at Series E (Feb 2025); no fresher GMV figure disclosed in IR since. [UNSOURCED for 2026].
- **Valuation trajectory:** Series E **$160m** at **$3.3bn** (12 Feb 2025, led by Blue Pool Capital & Hassana, with Wellington and STV) → secondary share sale implying **$4.5bn** (27 Oct 2025). No newer valuation disclosed.

**By driver.** The two SAMA licences act on the highest-margin part of the book. (1) *Consumer finance:* enables **extended instalment plans** on tickets from **~$533 up to $13,333** (Shariah-compliant Murabaha, fixed cost agreed upfront, no compounding interest, no late fees), live across Noon, IKEA, Fitness Time, Almanea, Almosafer, Almatar, flynas. This lifts average order value and duration well above the ~$50–100, 6-week BNPL norm, pushing Tabby up-market into education, travel, used cars and short-term rentals. (2) *SME finance:* lets Tabby extend **working-capital funding directly to platform retailers** — a new revenue line (merchant lending) that also deepens merchant lock-in. Both build on the 2025 BNPL licence and 2024 SAMA-sandbox graduation.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No earnings consensus exists (private). Momentum vs prior corpus: users roughly **doubled** in ~18 months (12m Apr-24 → 25m Jun-26); sellers **>2x** (30k → 65k); KSA-unit revenue **+42% YoY** with net margin ≈ **14–15%** ($55m/$378m). The trajectory is acceleration in scale with sustained, audited profitability — unusual for a BNPL name and central to the IPO case.

**Guidance / forward.** No formal guidance (private).
