# Context-enrichment: VTB to sell former Otkritie office to Wildberries-Russ
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
VTB signed a purchase-and-sale contract (disclosed ~26 Jun 2026 by First Deputy Chairman Dmitry Pyanov, closing by end-June) for the ~24,400 sq m building that was **FK Otkritie's former head office**, part of the **Vivaldi Plaza** class-A complex (Letnikovskaya St. 2, Zamoskvorechye, near Paveletsky station, Moscow). The building fell under VTB control after VTB absorbed Otkritie.
- **Buyer:** VTB did **not** officially name it. RBC's original reporting (via mergers.ru) names the merged **Wildberries + Russ company (ООО «РВБ» / RVB)**; Tatyana Kim (Bakalchuk) herself has said WB-Russ acquired the former Otkritie office via open tender. So buyer = RVB (RBC + Kim's own comment; VTB itself keeps it anonymous — a real gap, not confirmation).
- **Price:** **not disclosed.** VTB frames the deal as done "at book value, no significant P&L impact." A ~7.7 bn rub figure circulated as the *listed starting/auction price*, not a confirmed final consideration.
- **Purchase vs lease (open):** earlier May 2026 items said WB was "considering renting"; later reporting and Kim's statement say WB **bought** it. A sale is the stronger read.
- **Why structured this way:** VTB is running a broad June-2026 **non-core real-estate sell-off** (also Gals-Development developer >100 bn rub; Otkritie back-office on Verkhnyaya Radishchevskaya; RNKB and ex-Bank-of-Moscow offices), all "at book value," ahead of new CBR limits on banks' immobilized/non-core assets effective **1 Apr 2027**. Selling at book value with no gain flagged tells you the goal is **regulatory-capital relief and balance-sheet cleanup**, not a profit event — the "at book value" line is precisely the anti-PR tell (a bank does not brag about a real-estate gain here because there isn't one).

## [1] Competitors / peers
This is a Russian non-core-asset disposal, so peers are other banks shedding assets and other e-commerce players grabbing Moscow HQ space:
- **VTB itself** is disposing multiple ex-Otkritie / ex-Bank-of-Moscow properties in the same window (see [0]) — Otkritie's real estate is being liquidated across the group.
- **Foreign-bank exits** provide the western mirror: [[Citigroup to sell remaining Russian operations at $1.2bn loss]] and [[Citi board approves sale of AO Citibank to Renaissance Capital]] (Jan 2026) — the same "get non-core Russian balance sheet off the books" theme, but driven by sanctions/exit rather than CBR capital rules.
- **On the buyer side:** RVB has been assembling Moscow space aggressively — reportedly buying a Moscow-City skyscraper for HQ (Dec 2024) and bidding on Legion I (~46.7k sq m, 2025). The Vivaldi Plaza building fits a HQ-consolidation land-grab for a fast-growing combined team.
- **Second-order:** the transaction sits inside a tightening VTB↔RVB orbit (VTB reportedly buying into WB Bank / RVB fintech projects, disclosed May 2026; see [3]). The office sale is the least strategic node of that relationship — a convenient counterparty for a building VTB wants off its books.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **Aug 2017:** CBR put FK Otkritie (then Russia's largest private bank) into sanation — the country's largest-ever bank bailout (~456 bn rub recapitalization). 
- **Dec 2022:** CBR sold 100% of FK Otkritie to VTB for **340 bn rub** (cash + OFZ bonds). 
- **1 Jan 2025:** Otkritie ceased to exist as a legal entity, merged into BM-Bank within VTB group; Otkritie brand retired.
- The former Otkritie HQ became a redundant asset the moment the brand and legal entity were folded in. Selling it is the logical endpoint of that integration. **Why VTB acts now:** the CBR's 1-Apr-2027 immobilized-asset limits create a hard deadline to convert non-yielding property into capital headroom — structural regulatory pressure, not opportunism.
- On the buyer: Wildberries + Russ merger announced Jun 2024; JV ООО «РВБ» (Wildberries 65% / Russ Outdoor's Stinn 35%). RVB continues to expand (see [[Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka]], Jun 2026). A large central-Moscow HQ fits a company scaling e-commerce + advertising + embedded finance.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new (end-June 2026):** the *signed sale contract* and VTB's confirmation. Talks were reported May–June 2026 (WB interest, rent-vs-buy, 7.7 bn starting price); end-June is the first "contract signed" datapoint — a real "talks → deal" follow-up, hence **fresh**, but a confirmation rather than a surprise.
- **Value-add for a fintech/banking audience is modest:** a book-value building disposal with an undisclosed price and an unnamed (officially) buyer. No P&L, no capital number, no confirmed price. The durable signal is directional: VTB is cleaning non-core assets ahead of 2027 CBR rules, and the VTB–RVB relationship is deepening on multiple fronts (fintech stakes, and now real estate).
- **Who captures value:** RVB gets a large central HQ; VTB gets a marginal capital-relief tick. Neither is a needle-mover on its own.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Expect further VTB non-core disposals through 2026–early 2027 as the 1-Apr-2027 CBR immobilized-asset cap approaches — this building is one line item in a program (Gals-Development, RNKB, Verkhnyaya Radishchevskaya, etc.).
- Watch whether VTB ever discloses a price or the buyer officially; absent that, the RVB identity rests on RBC + Kim's own comments (an open point).
- The more consequential VTB↔RVB thread is the fintech partnership (WB Bank / RVB fintech), not this office.
- **Second-order:** a bank forced to dump property at book value into a tight domestic market signals that CBR capital rules — not asset appreciation — are driving Russian bank balance-sheet decisions; the "buyer is a fast-growing e-commerce champion" framing is a nicer story than the underlying "regulator-mandated cleanup."

## Do NOT conflate
- This is **NOT** [[VTB takes large Ozon share stake as collateral]] — that is a separate ~June 30–Jul 1, 2026 collateral/financing event where VTB became pledgeholder of an Ozon stake from AFK Sistema (~320 bn rub). Different company (Ozon, not Wildberries), different mechanism (pledge, not real-estate sale).

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is a Russian-bank capital/real-estate-optimisation story, not a growth-M&A one. VTB is disposing of a ~24,400 sq m office (former Otkritie HQ, part of Vivaldi Plaza on Letnikovskaya St. near Paveletsky Station), inherited when VTB absorbed Otkritie in 2022. Driver = capital pressure: Russian banks face a tightening CBR capital regime, and CEO Andrei Kostin has said VTB alone needs ~RUB 1.7tn (~$22bn) over five years to meet regulatory requirements; the CBR is specifically changing how capital is calculated for non-core assets (Kostin/Investing.com, 2026). CFO Dmitry Pyanov flagged a broader non-core disposal programme — including the Camellia Hotel in Sochi and BM Bank collateral (mostly commercial real estate) — targeting "several tens of billions of rubles" (AKM/RBC, 2026). "Why now": FY2025 IFRS net profit fell 8.9% to RUB 502.1bn, ROE dropped to 18.3% (from 22.9%), provisions rose ~10x to RUB 200.3bn, and the capital-adequacy ratio N20.0 stood at just 9.8% (base capital N20.1 = 6.4%) at 01-Jan-2026 — thin above minima (per RBC/Banki.ru, 25-26 Feb 2026, from VTB's own IFRS release). Selling a non-yielding, capital-consuming office directly relieves that constraint.

**De-PR / correction to the headline.** The note's title says VTB will "sell" the office "to Wildberries-Russ." More recent reporting (Kommersant, Vedomosti, RIA, 14-May-2026) reframes this: VTB plans to sell the whole Vivaldi Plaza business centre via **auction** with a starting price of ~RUB 7.7bn, and the united Wildberries+Russ company (RWB) stated it intends to **lease** (not buy) the former Otkritie space, for team expansion — RWB already occupies ~8,000 sq m in a nearby centre for Wildberries Bank. So "sale to Wildberries-Russ" is likely an early-consultant framing; treat buyer identity/structure as unconfirmed `(analysis)`.

**Competitive landscape.** Relevant KPIs here are capital ratios (N20.0/CET1-equivalent), ROE, and cost-of-risk, not payments metrics. VTB is Russia's #2 bank; the move sits within a wider VTB–RWB ecosystem tie-up (VTB has also reportedly taken a stake in Wildberries' digital-financial-assets/fintech arm; RWB's Wildberries Bank build-out), so the real-estate leg is one node in a bank-marketplace convergence, distinct from a simple property disposal. Basis of competition for the asset itself = price at open auction. Recent peer moves in bank real-estate rationalisation: Deutsche Bank taking over Revolut's Canary Wharf HQ [[Deutsche Bank to move into Revolut's Canary Wharf HQ]] (Dec-2025) — office footprint recycled between financial players. VTB's position: forced seller optimising a balance sheet under regulatory pressure, not an opportunistic one.

**Comps & multiples.** No trading multiples apply — this is a single-asset property transaction, not an equity deal. Price anchor: auction start ~RUB 7.7bn for ~24,400 sq m ≈ RUB ~316k/sq m (7.7bn / 24,400) as an implied floor `(analysis)`; final clearing price = no data (auction pending). Internal comps for the "sanctioned/Russian bank disposal & non-core exit" pattern: [[Citigroup to sell remaining Russian operations at $1.2bn loss]] (Jan-2026, forced Russia exit, sold at a loss), [[Nedbank sells Ecobank stake for $100 million]] (Sep-2025, non-core stake disposal). These are directional analogues on the "shed non-core / free capital" theme, not price comps for Moscow offices. Valuation flag: qualitative comparison only, distribution not computed.

**Risk flags.**
- **Headline/structure risk** — the deal may be a lease + open auction, not a bilateral sale to RWB; the note's stated buyer and transaction type are not yet confirmed, so the "who buys and at what price" is unresolved and could change the capital benefit.
- **Capital adequacy, thin** — N20.0 at 9.8% (base 6.4%) is only modestly above minima with buffers; a single office sale yields at most low tens of billions of RUB against a stated ~RUB 1.7tn five-year capital need — helpful at the margin, not a fix. Second-order: expect further, larger disposals, so this is a signal of ongoing capital stress, not strength.
- **Related-party optics** — a bank selling/leasing prime real estate into an ecosystem partner (RWB) it is simultaneously investing in raises pricing-independence questions; the open-auction route partly mitigates this, which is likely why VTB chose it.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` Read this as a marker of VTB's 2026 capital squeeze rather than ecosystem expansion: the tell to watch is the *pace and size* of the broader non-core disposal list (Sochi hotel, BM Bank collateral) and any CBR move on non-core-asset capital rules — if disposals accelerate, VTB is prioritising ratio repair over dividends. Falsifiable trigger: if the Vivaldi auction clears near/below the RUB 7.7bn floor and RWB only leases, the "strategic sale to a partner" thesis is wrong and this is plainly distressed balance-sheet cleanup. Sector-wide, expect more Russian-bank office/real-estate recycling as CBR capital tightening bites through 2026.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
> [!note] Earnings-review layer — grounding an asset-sale story in VTB's own filings. This is an M&A/real-estate story, **not an earnings release**, but VTB is IR-covered, so figures below are quoted verbatim from VTB's **latest own filing**: FY2025 IFRS results (published 25.02.2026) and the FY2025 analyst-call transcript (03.03.2026). Not consensus, not web.

**Verdict (headline read on the underlying issuer).** NO-RESULTS in the news itself — the Otkritie-office → Wildberries-Russ deal carries no price or P&L. On VTB's own last print: **IN-LINE / slight beat vs own target** — FY2025 net profit **RUB 502.1bn (−9% YoY)**, above management's own RUB 500bn target, "silver"/2nd-best ever; ROE **18.3% (−4.6pp)**; capital adequacy N20.0 **9.8%** — the thin ratio that makes non-core asset sales (like this office) load-bearing.

**Key figures — FY2025 IFRS (VTB own filing, exact).**
- Net profit **RUB 502.1bn**, **−9% YoY** (vs own target RUB 500bn — beat by RUB 2.1bn). Driver of the decline: avg key rate 19.2% in 2025 vs 17.5% in 2024. Positive income-tax line **+RUB 79bn** from deferred-tax-asset recognition flattered the result.
- **ROE 18.3% (−4.6pp)**.
- **NIM 1.4% (−30bp)**; management flags a Sep-2025 inflection back toward the 2%+ margin zone.
- Net fee income **RUB 307bn (+14.2%)** — the stable-income pillar (≈doubled in 5yr from RUB 150–160bn).
- **Cost of risk 1.1% (+80bp)**; corporate 0.7%, retail 1.9%. NPL 90+ round-tripped 3.5%→4.3%→back to 3.5% (partly via write-offs); IFRS-9 Stage-1 share fell 85%→83%, Stage-2 9.7%→10.4%, Stage-3 5.2%→6.7% — quiet quality deterioration.
- Opex **RUB 560bn (+17%; +12.5% ex-Pochta Bank)**; **CIR 47.3% (+2.7pp)** — missed own target; ~1pp of the CIR miss and >2pp of opex growth blamed on inability to merge BM-Bank alongside RNCB.

**Balance / the capital story that this sale feeds into (verbatim).**
- **Capital adequacy N20.0 = 9.8% at YE2025 (+70bp YoY)** — this is thin. 2025 minimum was 9.25%; from the **01.04.2026 reporting date the minimum rises to 10.0%**, and VTB's own plan is to reach **10.3%** in one quarter. Basel buffers are being restored stepwise through 2028.
- Credit maneuver: **retail loans −6.8% YoY to RUB 7.3trn**, corporate **+8% to RUB 17.2trn** (CIB/large caps, lower RWA weight); LDR 83.2%. VTB deliberately shrinks capital-heavy unsecured retail to defend the ratio.
- Record **dividends RUB 276bn** paid; capital shored via two subordinated loans, the largest SPO on the RU/EU market, and an RAS-profit audit — plus, for 1H2026: planned sale of ~RUB 300bn of retail loan book to third parties (frees ~RUB 50bn capital) and an NMA-amortization reform that could free ~RUB 100bn.

**How the Otkritie-office sale fits (this is the point).** In the FY2025 call, CFO Dmitry Pyanov was asked directly about non-core disposals and said VTB **"will continue getting rid of non-core assets"** — last year it sold many hotels (some above book value); 2026 marquee sales named: the **"Vesna" mall (from BM-Bank's balance), remaining Domodedovo-district land, possibly the Holiday Inn Sokolniki**. Crucially he noted these are expected **at ~book value and are NOT built into the 2026 profit target** — i.e. their role is capital/RWA relief, not earnings. The Vivaldi-Plaza / ex-Otkritie office (≈24.4k m², inherited via the Otkritie integration) is exactly this species of legacy real estate: selling it to Wildberries-Russ monetizes a non-core, capital-consuming asset to help VTB clear the 01.04.2026 step-up to a 10.0% minimum from a starting 9.8% — a one-off disposal plugging a thin-capital gap, not a recurring earnings driver. Second-order: with N20.0 barely above the new floor while management defends a 50% dividend payout, each such sale directly buys headroom for that payout and for the credit maneuver.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public consensus/street estimate is in the pipeline for this print, and the news carries no deal price → deal-level financials **[UNSOURCED]**. Against VTB's own guidance the print is a modest beat (RUB 502.1bn vs 500bn target). Trajectory over the corpus: 9M-2025 and prior-quarter calls (2025-10-29, 2025-08-05, 2025-05-14) already flagged the same credit-maneuver + capital-utilization theme, so this is continuation, not a break.

**Guidance / forward (VTB own, FY2025 call).** 2026 macro: avg key rate ~14% (exit 12%), GDP ~+1%, inflation 5–6%; corporate loans +8–9%, retail +7–8%. VTB will continue the credit maneuver, "may even sell retail portfolios," and continue non-core disposals — with a possible ЦБ mega-regulation from 01.04.2027 on mobilized/financial-subsidiary assets (Rosgosstrakh, NPFs, insurers) that could force further sales. Management tone: confident on profit-target delivery and 50% dividend commitment (final ratio decided ~mid-April 2026; AGM 30.06.2026), but candid/defensive on the capital math — the ratio is being managed to the wire.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. **Thin capital is the binding constraint, not profitability.** N20.0 9.8% vs a 10.0% minimum landing 01.04.2026 → why non-core real-estate sales (this office, "Vesna", hotels, land) are strategically material despite trivial P&L → each disposal is capital-relief plumbing that underwrites the 50% dividend.
2. **Earnings quality caveat.** RUB 502.1bn leaned on a +RUB 79bn deferred-tax-asset tax benefit and NPL write-offs masking Stage-2/3 migration → the "beat" is lower-quality than headline; more reason capital must come from the balance sheet (asset sales), not retained earnings.
3. **Credit maneuver = shrinking retail.** Retail loans −6.8% is a deliberate RWA reduction → NIM/volume pressure now (NIM 1.4%) traded for capital adequacy → asset disposals are the same trade in real-estate form.
4. **Dividend risk (second-order).** The 50% payout is management aspiration pending the shareholder/CBR decision by mid-April 2026; if capital falls short, disposals like this one are the swing factor that keeps the payout intact.
