Consumer crypto trading app Fomo has raised $75 million in a Series B round led by Index Ventures at a $550 million valuation as the startup looks to expand headcount and pursue potential acquisitions.

Union Square Ventures participated in the round alongside a group of angel investors, including Zynga co-founder Mark Pincus, Discord CEO Humam Sakhnini, and Eventbrite co-founder Kevin Hartz, according to a Monday announcement.

Fomo was founded in 2025 by Paul Erlanger, Se Yong Park, and Prashan Dharmasena, all veterans of the crypto trading platform dYdX. The app is designed to compress crypto onboarding to roughly 30 seconds, regardless of a user's familiarity with digital assets. It offers social features, including trader leaderboards and a trade feed, and supports more assets than Coinbase across multiple chains, without requiring users to manage bridges or gas fees, per the report.

The startup is non-custodial — meaning it does not hold customer funds — a structure it argues provides regulatory flexibility, though the company's co-founders have stressed they prioritize compliance.

Fomo listed perpetual futures contracts in June and is onboarding approximately 3,500 new users per day, Fortunereported. The company has 17 employees.

Index's participation carries weight beyond the check size. The firm previously backed stablecoin startup Bridge, which Stripe acquired for $1.1 billion in 2025, and is not a dedicated crypto investor — its portfolio spans companies including Figma and Scale AI.

An Index partner told Fortune the firm backed Fomo because it sees a structural market shift in consumer blockchain trading, not because of the crypto label alone, according to the report. Union Square Ventures has also previously invested in Polygon and crypto developer Matter Labs.

The Block reported on Fomo's $17 million Series A in November 2025, which was led by Benchmark and brought the company's total funding to $19 million at the time. The Series B brings total disclosed funding to roughly $94 million.

Fomo's co-founders have said their long-term ambition extends beyond cryptocurrency, with plans to serve as a gateway to tokenized stocks, derivatives, and other onchain assets as that market develops.

# Context-enrichment: Fomo raises $75M Series B led by Index Ventures
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Fomo — a ~13-month-old NYC consumer crypto trading app — raised a **$75M Series B at a $550M post-money valuation**, led by **Index Ventures**, with **Union Square Ventures (USV)** and angels Mark Pincus (Zynga), Humam Sakhnini (Discord CEO, appointed — not a founder), and Kevin Hartz (Eventbrite). Founded 2025 by ex-dYdX engineers **Paul Erlanger, Se Yong Park, Prashan Dharmasena**. Total disclosed funding "~$94M" (= $17M Series A led by Benchmark, Nov 2025, on $19M-then total + $75M now; the "$94M" rounds up an undisclosed seed). 17 employees.
- **De-PR caveats (the core of the red-team):** Nearly all 30+ articles trace to **one Fortune exclusive (Jun 22, 2026) + Fomo's own blog** — breadth of coverage is PR amplification, not independent corroboration. The headline traction numbers are **founder-sourced and unaudited**: "~3,500 new users/day", "625K joined", "$4B volume", "110M social interactions". → "Users" here = **gross signups**, not funded/retained accounts; no DAU, retention or funded-conversion disclosed. The company's own softer datapoint (~68K first-time Apple Pay buyers on ~$25M, ~$370 avg) implies **most claimed users never funded**. So the $550M mark (~7.3x the raise, on 17 people) is a **momentum/social-trend bet, not fundamentals**.
- **Why structured this way:** Fomo is **non-custodial** (doesn't hold funds) — framed as "regulatory flexibility". That posture is partly **regulatory arbitrage**: perps were reportedly listed via **Hyperliquid** and are **non-US** (per crypto.news/Decrypt; Fortune does not name Hyperliquid or say "non-US" — secondary-only). "More assets than Coinbase across chains" is a **founder marketing claim**, and cuts both ways: listing tokens regulated CEXs won't touch is a degen feature and a securities/fraud liability.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **[[Phantom outlines crypto super-app strategy]]** — the **closest analog and the real tell**: same plumbing (non-custodial wallet UI routing perps to **Hyperliquid via builder codes**). Phantom shipped perps Jul 2025 and is the largest Hyperliquid builder (>$20M builder fees, ~$39B volume, 137K traders). Fomo runs the **same model at smaller scale**; its only differentiation is the **social layer** (leaderboards, trade feed) + novice onboarding speed.
- **[[Coinbase launches 24 7 stock perpetual futures]]** / **[[Coinbase Derivatives will launch US Perpetual-Style Futures]]** — custodial, **CFTC-regulated** "everything exchange" (tokens/stocks/Kalshi event contracts) that *can* legally serve US-retail perps onshore. Fomo cannot (non-US perps).
- **[[Kraken rolls out 24 7 perpetuals for tokenized US stocks]]** / **[[Kraken launches Perps for crypto futures trading]]** — regulated CEX; launched US CFTC perps June 2026 onshore.
- **Funding-stage comps (all in corpus):** **[[Hyperliquid web3 'SuperApp' based raises $11.5 million Series A led by Pantera]]** (Based: perps + prediction markets + spending in one app), **[[Trading startup Liquid raises $18 million seed round]]**, **[[PopDex raises $30 million seed for perpetuals DEX]]**, **[[Ostium raises $20M Series A from General Catalyst and Jump Crypto]]**. The 2025-26 consumer-crypto/perps funding wave is crowded; Fomo's $550M mark is the richest of the cohort.
- **Position:** parity-to-behind on infrastructure (it owns none), ahead only on social UX. **Second-order:** the moat (social layer) is exactly what Coinbase/Robinhood can copy; the upstream venue (Hyperliquid) is US-geoblocked and faces CME/ICE calls for CFTC scrutiny.

## [2] Company history / fit
Series A ($17M, Benchmark, Nov 2025) → Series B ($75M, Index+USV, Jun 2026) in ~7 months. Founders are dYdX alumni but built **no exchange** — Fomo is an **interface across third-party protocols**, the opposite of dYdX's sovereign app-chain. **Why this shape:** founders chose the front-end/distribution layer (own the customer, social graph, onboarding) and outsource matching/settlement to Hyperliquid et al. → captures the relationship and the regulatory exposure, but **not** the exchange economics. Roadmap (equities, perpetuals, prediction markets, tokenized stocks) is the same "consumer onchain super-app" thesis as Based/Phantom.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Genuinely new: little. The value-add is **distribution + social UX** (feed, leaderboards, ~30-sec onboarding — also a founder claim, unaudited), not protocol innovation. **Why the value-add is thin:** Fomo is a **wrapper over Hyperliquid** — it inherits Hyperliquid's solvency/oracle/regulatory risk while owning the front-end legal exposure. **Who captures the margin:** the **revenue model is undisclosed**; architecture implies **builder/interface fees** routed through Hyperliquid — but no figure confirmed, so the durable take-rate is unknown. Traction is **gross signups, not funded users**; without retention/funded-conversion the $4B "volume" and 625K "users" don't underwrite the multiple. **Index angle:** its weight comes from backing **[[Bridge wins initial approval for national bank trust charter|Bridge]]** (Stripe, $1.1B, 2025) and being a generalist (Figma, Scale AI), framing this as a "structural consumer-blockchain shift" not a crypto bet — a credibility signal, but still a name/momentum endorsement.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Use of funds: headcount + potential acquisitions + asset-class expansion (equities/perps/prediction markets/tokenized stocks). Sentiment: positive (Decrypt: "big VCs still betting on consumer crypto"), but reflexively tied to the 2026 retail-crypto risk-on cycle. **Risks:** (1) **US-retail non-custodial perps unresolved** — the Apr 2026 SEC "Covered User Interface Provider" guidance addresses *securities* front-ends, NOT CFTC perp jurisdiction (TechTimes' "SEC cleared non-custodial wallets" framing conflates the two — misleading); (2) **counterparty concentration** on Hyperliquid; (3) **commoditization** — social wrapper copyable by Coinbase/Robinhood. **Why the market may still fund this:** capital is chasing the consumer-onchain distribution layer; **counterintuitive second-order:** the $550M momentum mark makes Fomo **more fragile, not safer** — it must grow into a fundamentals-light valuation in a sector where the regulated incumbents hold the onshore-perps and compliance advantage.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: consumer non-custodial crypto-trading front-ends routing perps to a third-party DEX (Hyperliquid). Sector size proxy — perp DEXs now hold ~13.5% of perpetual-futures open interest (up from <4% in early 2025), and Hyperliquid alone ran ~$180–190bn of 30-day volume in Apr-2026 with >70% perp-DEX share (per Yellow/DefiLlama citations, as of Apr 2026). For scale on the *interface* layer Fomo plays in: ~40% of Hyperliquid DAUs trade via third-party frontends and the builder-code ecosystem has paid out $40m+ cumulatively (per cryptobriefing/Hyperdash, 2026). Structure: a **two-sided dependency** — value at the matching/settlement layer accrues to Hyperliquid (the venue); front-ends like Fomo/Phantom capture only a **capped builder fee (0.1% max for perps)** on flow they route. Barriers at the interface layer are low (UI + distribution), high at the venue layer (liquidity network effects). Why now: the CFTC opened **onshore** US perps in 2026 (Kalshi BTCPERP approved May-2026; Kraken/Bitnomial launched CFTC-regulated US perps Jun-15-2026; Coinbase routing via Bermuda affiliate) on an **asset-by-asset** basis — pulling the regulated-perp market *onshore* and undercutting Fomo's offshore/non-custodial regulatory-arbitrage edge just as it scales.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: routed perp **volume**, **builder-fee take-rate** (capped 0.1%), funded-trader count, and fee-revenue/day. Players: incumbent front-end **Phantom** (the dominant non-custodial wrapper); regulated onshore venues **Coinbase / Kraken / Robinhood** (custodial, CFTC-path); the underlying venue **Hyperliquid**; and a crowded funding cohort (Based, PopDex, Liquid, Ostium, Dreamcash). Basis of competition: distribution + UX, not price (take-rate is protocol-capped and identical for builders). Recent moves: Phantom **$20.6m cumulative builder fees on ~$39bn volume, 137K traders, ~$100K/day** (per cryptobriefing/Hyperdash, 2026); Kraken onshore US perps **Jun-15-2026**; CME suing CFTC over the perp approvals (per The Defiant, 2026). Position: Fomo is **behind on infrastructure** (owns no venue) and **behind Phantom on the same model at ~10x smaller scale**, ahead only on the social layer (leaderboards/feed) — a UX moat, not a structural one *(analysis)*. The regulated incumbents hold the onshore-perp + compliance advantage Fomo cannot match.

**Comps & multiples.** Best external comp — **Phantom** (same non-custodial→Hyperliquid model, larger): Series C **$150m at $3.0bn** (Jan-2025, Sequoia/Paradigm), on **15m MAU / $25bn self-custody assets**. Price-per-MAU = `$3.0bn / 15m ≈ $200/MAU`. Fomo at **$550m** on founder-claimed **625K signups** (gross, unaudited) = `$550m / 625K ≈ $880/signup` — i.e. **~4.4x Phantom's price-per-user on a softer (signup, not MAU/funded) denominator** → reads **rich** vs the one clean comp, even before discounting unfunded signups. On revenue: Fomo discloses **no revenue**, so EV/Revenue = **no data**; if Fomo's economics mirror Phantom's builder take (~$20.6m / ~$39bn ≈ **0.05% effective**), its routed volume would have to reach Phantom's ~$39bn just to imply ~$20m of lifetime fees — context for a $550m mark. Internal comps (richest-of-cohort confirmed): [[Hyperliquid web3 'SuperApp' based raises $11.5 million Series A led by Pantera]] ($11.5m A), [[PopDex raises $30 million seed for perpetuals DEX]] ($30m seed), [[Trading startup Liquid raises $18 million seed round]] ($18m seed), [[Ostium raises $20M Series A from General Catalyst and Jump Crypto]] ($20m A), [[Tether invests in Hyperliquid frontend Dreamcash for USDT0 perps]]. Distribution not computed (rounds, not market caps; mixed stages) — qualitative: **Fomo's $550m is the richest mark of the consumer-perps cohort by a wide margin.** Sanity: a $550m private mark on a fundamentals-light, fee-capped wrapper is justifiable only on hyper-growth — but the growth metric (signups) is the wrong, unaudited denominator, so the growth-multiple link is **unverified [UNSOURCED]**.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Onshore-perp disintermediation (timing).** CFTC opened regulated US perps in 2026 (Kraken/Kalshi/Coinbase) — Fomo's core edge (offshore non-custodial arbitrage) erodes exactly as regulated incumbents serve US-retail perps onshore with compliance Fomo lacks. Second-order: the regulatory moat flips from asset to liability.
2. **Margin captured upstream + fee cap.** Builder fees are protocol-capped at 0.1% and the venue (Hyperliquid) owns liquidity and can re-price; Fomo captures a thin, non-durable slice of flow it doesn't control. Second-order: a $550m mark on a fee-capped wrapper with undisclosed revenue is hard to underwrite.
3. **Counterparty concentration on Hyperliquid.** Fomo inherits Hyperliquid solvency/oracle/regulatory risk (CME suing CFTC over perp approvals) while owning front-end legal exposure — single-venue dependence with no hedge disclosed.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** *(analysis)* Fomo's mark is a **late-cycle sentiment top-tick** for consumer-perps wrappers, not a re-rating on fundamentals: at ~4.4x Phantom's price-per-user on a weaker denominator, it prices in execution the metrics don't yet show. Falsifiable thesis: **the durable winners are the venue (Hyperliquid) and onshore-regulated incumbents (Coinbase/Kraken), not the social wrapper.** Trigger to watch — Fomo disclosing *funded-account* conversion and routed-volume/builder-fee run-rate (would validate or break the mark); what makes the thesis wrong — Fomo's social layer producing a measurable retention/CAC edge that incumbents can't fast-follow.
