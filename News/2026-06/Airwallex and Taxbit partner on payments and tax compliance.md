The partnership connects two parts of your compliance workflow that are often handled separately. Airwallex manages the payment infrastructure, accepting payments, holding multi-currency balances, and paying out to sellers across 200+ countries. Taxbit handles the tax side: collecting W-8/W-9 documentation at onboarding, normalizing tax-relevant payment data, and generating required tax reporting outputs including 1099s, 1042-S, CRS 2.0, and international reporting, based on the seller's jurisdiction and tax documentation.
The result is a connected workflow from seller onboarding to year-end tax reporting, with less manual handoff between systems.
High-growth marketplaces and platforms scaling globally face two things that break at once:
Managing multi-currency payouts across 200+ countries
Keeping pace with tax obligations including W-8/W-9, 1099s, CRS 2.0, and international tax forms, without adding manual, error-prone processes
As volume grows, many platforms end up stitching together payouts and tax reporting with manual, error-prone processes. Taxbit and Airwallex each solve one side of that equation, and together they reduce the gap between payment settlement and reporting obligations.
For US obligations, Taxbit supports W-8/W-9 collection, 1099 reporting, and 1042-S reporting.
For international obligations, the solution supports CRS 2.0 and DAC7/MRDP reporting for platforms and marketplaces, alongside broader international tax reporting workflows.
