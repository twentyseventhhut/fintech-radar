# Palestine Monetary Authority signs $50 million SME financing deal

The Palestine Monetary Authority and the National Bank signed a framework financing agreement worth $50 million aimed at expanding access to finance for micro, small, and medium-sized enterprises across Palestine, as part of a broader initiative funded by the European Union worth €400 million to support the Palestinian economy.
The agreement is part of a financing program with a total value of approximately $462 million, implemented by the European Commission through the European Investment Bank in cooperation with the Palestine Monetary Authority and participating local banks.
The program aims to improve Palestinian companies' access to finance and enhance economic stability amid difficult financial conditions.
The initiative also includes remaining technical assistance funding of €2.1 million out of €3.5 million allocated to support the financial system and initiatives for developing startups and institutions supporting the sector.
The importance of this agreement lies in the fact that small and medium-sized enterprises form the backbone of the Palestinian private sector, yet they suffer from limited access to finance.
This financing is expected to allow capital to be directed directly to the most needy companies, ensuring business continuity, preserving job opportunities, and strengthening local supply chains in an economic environment characterized by increasing uncertainty.
Part of the financing has been structured as subordinated loans to banks to strengthen the banking capital base and ensure that financing is allocated towards lending to small and medium-sized enterprises.
The initiative partners emphasize that providing reliable financing sources for these companies is essential for the continuation of economic activities and the resilience of private sector businesses in the face of financial and operational crises.
