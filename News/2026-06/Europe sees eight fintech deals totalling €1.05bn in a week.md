BlackFin Tech Weekly — June 8th, 2026
Every Week, we publish a short digest which sums up last week’s Fintech activity.
Hello FinTech Friends,
Welcome to another week of fintech insights. Let’s explore the news and trends shaping the industry!
 Over the last week, there were eight fintech deals in Europe, totaling €1.05bn in disclosed funding, including two transactions in the UK, two in Spain, and one each in Belgium, Germany, Switzerland, and Bulgaria. 
Congratulations to the three largest rounds announced last week:
 Kpler , a Belgium-based commodity and maritime trade intelligence provider, has raised €859.1m in a strategic growth equity investment from Sixth Street . 
 Factorial , a Spain-based AI workforce operations platform, has raised €128.9m in a Series D round led by General Catalyst . 
 Gradient Labs , a UK-based AI-native customer operations platform for financial services, has raised €22.3m in a Series A round led by Octopus Ventures and CommerzVentures . 
 Let’s dive! 
 Kpler 
 Kpler , a Belgium-based commodity and maritime trade intelligence provider, has raised €859.1m in a strategic growth equity investment from Sixth Street , with existing investor Insight Partners rolling part of its stake and Five Arrows exiting. The company provides data and analytics across commodities, maritime, and defence markets through its Kpler and MarineTraffic platforms, aggregating physical asset data to deliver real-time market intelligence. The round values the company at approximately €3.2bn . The funding will support expansion into adjacent markets and accelerated product development, as Kpler aims to become the definitive platform for real-time decision-making across global trade.

 Factorial 
 Factorial , a Spain-based AI workforce operations platform, has raised €128.9m in a Series D round led by General Catalyst , with participation from Atomico and Four Rivers . The company provides an all-in-one platform that automates HR, finance, and IT workflows, rebuilt around AI agents and now serving more than 16,000 businesses across over 90 countries. The round values the company at approximately €2.1bn . Alongside the equity round, General Catalyst committed up to a further €464m through its Customer Value Fund, bringing total capital committed to over €600m. The funding will support the company’s transition to an AI-powered workforce operations platform and expansion across Europe, as Factorial aims to deepen its presence in Germany. 
 Read more 
 Gradient Labs 
 Gradient Labs , a UK-based AI-native customer operations platform for financial services, has raised €22.3m in a Series A round led by Octopus Ventures and CommerzVentures , with follow-on participation from Redpoint Ventures and Exceptional Capital . The company builds specialist AI agents that automate regulated financial workflows such as customer support, lending operations, disputes, fraud handling, and KYC verification, now serving more than 32 million end users. The funding will support US expansion and the development of an operating system for autonomous bank operations, as Gradient Labs aims to help banks run their customer operations on autopilot.

In addition to this week’s fundraising activity, here is the European M&A activity for the week:
 Vertice , a UK-based AI procurement platform, has acquired Vendr , a US-based SaaS purchasing and pricing-intelligence platform. The acquisition combines Vendr’s software pricing dataset and negotiation team with Vertice’s AI procurement platform, creating what the company describes as the largest procurement intelligence dataset in the market, spanning more than €64bn in global indirect spend and over 250,000 negotiated contracts. The transaction supports Vertice’s strategy to make its autonomous negotiation agents smarter and lead the next phase of AI-driven procurement. 
 Read more 
 OpenFX , a US-based cross-border payments and FX infrastructure provider, has agreed to acquire Embed , a Netherlands-based embedded payments company. The acquisition brings Embed’s Dutch payment-institution and UK electronic-money licenses in-house which, combined with a MiCAR license, gives OpenFX a stablecoin payments regulatory stack across the EU and UK. The transaction, which remains subject to regulatory approval, supports OpenFX’s strategy to expand cross-border stablecoin settlement across Europe, alongside new offices in London and Amsterdam. 
 Read more 
 Kaiko , a France-based digital asset market data, analytics, and indices provider, has acquired Amberdata , a US-based institutional digital asset data provider specializing in derivatives analytics and onchain data. The acquisition, Kaiko’s fifth and following Cometh , combines the two firms’ market data and analytics businesses to create an institutional digital asset data platform spanning more than 200 exchanges, over 20 blockchains, and 20,000 digital assets. The transaction supports Kaiko’s strategy to become the definitive institutional data partner across centralized and decentralized markets. 
 Read more 
 Legora , a Sweden-based collaborative AI platform for lawyers, has acquired Cadastral , a US-based AI agent platform purpose-built for commercial real estate. The acquisition, Legora’s fourth of 2026, marks its entry into commercial real estate and anchors a new engineering hub in New York. The transaction supports Legora’s strategy to extend its agentic operating system into the industries that generate the most complex legal work, building on recent momentum that took the company past €86m in annual recurring revenue at a €4.8bn Series D valuation. 
 Read more 
 Penning , a Denmark-based MiCA-licensed digital asset service provider, has acquired the wealth-management business of Veli , a Lithuania-based crypto investment platform. The acquired unit, to be rebranded Penning Wealth, brings Veli’s regulated wealth business, its European client base, and a perpetual licence to its investment-platform software, while Veli UAB continues as a separate legal entity. The transaction supports Penning’s strategy to build a MiCA-compliant digital asset wealth platform focused on long-term, passive portfolios under its EU and EEA passporting. 
 Read more 
 Admiral Group , a UK-based financial services and insurance group, has completed the acquisition of Flock , a UK-based digital commercial fleet insurance provider, in a transaction valuing the company’s equity at €92.5m . The acquisition strengthens Admiral Group’s fleet insurance offering by integrating Flock’s telemetry-based technology platform and team into its motor proposition. The transaction supports Admiral Group’s strategy to broaden its insurance product offering and leverage data, technology, pricing, claims, and risk management capabilities to deliver digital fleet insurance at scale.

 And finally, we bring you four news stories that caught our eye last week: 
 Wise , the London-based fintech with 19mn users, saw its shares fall as much as 20% on Monday after confirming it was responding to questions from a Brussels prosecutor over alleged money laundering. The probe, reported by the Bureau of Investigative Journalism , involves €500mn in transactions tied to fraud, corruption and drug trafficking, and began in 2025 after Wise appeared in hundreds of criminal files. Wise said no specific findings had been shared and called it speculative to comment; shares later traded 8% lower, valuing it near €10.1bn. Rivals Monzo and Starling were previously fined for AML failings. 
 Read more 
 The BCG and FT Partners Global Fintech Report 2026 finds the world’s largest fintechs are now more profitable than ever, with 74% in profit and average EBITDA margins up 400 basis points to 20% in 2025. The sector drew roughly €50 billion in equity funding, up 53% year over year, while global revenues topped half a trillion dollars, growing 22% and now making up about 4% of the financial services pool. Maturity reflects regulation, rising M&A and neobank growth. BCG ’s Inderpreet Batra called the firms disciplined, while FT Partners ’ Steve McLaughlin said AI will divide winners from laggards. 
 Read more 
 SpaceX , Elon Musk ’s space company, will allocate up to a quarter of its €65 billion float to retail investors, far above the 5% to 10% large-cap IPOs usually reserve, the Financial Times reports. A SpaceX IPO site routes orders through brokers including E*Trade , Fidelity , Robinhood , Schwab , and SoFi , plus UK apps Revolut and eToro . Germany’s Trade Republic is also confirmed, with subscription in-app. Musk prefers retail over Wall Street giants; SoFi CEO Anthony Noto says weak retail uptake reflects a lack of issuer supply, not demand, with retail conditioned not to take part because they lack access. 
 Read more 
 JP Morgan , Citi , Bank of America , and Wells Fargo are reportedly launching a tokenised deposit system in 2027 to counter the stablecoin boom, The Wall Street Journal reported on 5 June 2026. The Clearing House will run the network, linking rails and digital assets so deposits settle instantly on a 24/7 blockchain. CEO David Watson called it “a big move for the banks,” with multinationals first. It targets programmable treasury, cross-border payments, and liquidity, spurred by Trump ’s GENIUS Act. In 2025, JP Morgan partnered with DBS after launching JPM Coin. The bank-owned network launches in H1 2027.
