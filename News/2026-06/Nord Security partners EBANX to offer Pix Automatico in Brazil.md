# Context-enrichment: Nord Security partners EBANX to offer Pix Automatico in Brazil
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**Verdict: FRESH but low-weight.** A NEW merchant case-study (Nord Security) with NEW figures, not a reprint of the prior [[EBANX enables Pix recurring payments for Hotmart]] note — different merchant, different metrics. But it is a **single-source EBANX press release** (GlobeNewswire/PR Newswire, 2026-06-24), syndicated verbatim across Manila Times, Yahoo Finance SG/UK, Malaysia Sun, MerchantsEye etc. No independent journalism. All performance numbers trace to EBANX; none are auditor- or regulator-confirmed.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- EBANX issued a results-PR: ~6 months after Nord Security integrated Pix Automático via EBANX (so live ~Dec 2025), recurring Pix is **28% of Nord's Brazil TPV** and adopted by **35% of its Brazil user base**; **>60% of its Pix Automático users are new subscribers**; MIT (merchant-initiated transaction) approval rate **+20 percentage points**, framed as "outperforming credit cards."
- De-PR'd: the headline "Nord Security partners EBANX to offer Pix Automático" is a **re-framing of a months-old integration as a fresh partnership**. The actual news is the adoption stat, not a new tie-up. "Outperforming credit cards" is specifically the +20pp MIT approval rate — a narrower claim than the paraphrase implies.
- **Why structured this way:** EBANX is mid-funnel in a customer-acquisition push for its Pix Automático rail. Publishing a marquee global merchant's success (Nord/NordVPN) is a sales asset, not disclosure. The "28%/6 months" anchor is flattering and uncheckable — Nord publishes no underlying numbers. Treat as **EBANX-claimed, uncorroborated.**

## [1] Competitors / peers
Pix Automático went live as a Central-Bank-mandated rail on **2025-06-16** (originally scheduled 2024-10-28, delayed). Every cross-border PSP rushed to wrap it:
- **PPRO** — Pix Automático day one, 2025-06-16 ([[PPRO adds Pix Automatico to its payments platform]]).
- **dLocal** — "SmartPix" tokenized Pix-on-file, 2025-08-05 ([[dLocal introduces recurring Pix payments]]) — positions BETWEEN card-on-file and the CBB rail.
- **Stripe** — Pix via EBANX (2025-08, [[Stripe enables Pix payments in Brazil via EBANX]]); Pix Automático mandates documented (exact GA date open).
- **Boku** — PI licence + Pix live 2025 ([[Boku launches regulated payments in Brazil after central bank clearance]]); Pix Automático on roadmap, not confirmed live.
- Adyen, Mercado Pago, PicPay — recurring-Pix present/expected; dated launches open.
- **Position:** EBANX is **at parity, not ahead** — recurring Pix is a commoditized rail wrapper available from ≥5 PSPs since mid-2025. The differentiation is merchant logos and approval-rate optimization, not the capability. **Why:** Pix Automático is a public Central Bank rail; no PSP owns it, so value migrates to distribution (merchant book) and conversion tooling (tokenization, retry logic), not the rail.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **EBANX** — founded 2012 Curitiba; cross-border PSP for ~15 emerging markets; merchants incl. Spotify, Uber, AliExpress, SHEIN; ~$460M raised (Series C $430M, Advent, June 2021); still private, IPO mooted 2022 but not executed. Has run this exact playbook before: [[EBANX enables Pix recurring payments for Hotmart]] (2025-09-24, claimed +32pp retention), [[EBANX integrates Yape for cross-border merchant payments]], plus the [[Brazil's Pix surpasses 5 billion transactions amid fraud challenges]] research-report PR. Pattern: EBANX manufactures dated, vendor-sourced "X% / Y-point uplift" stats to market each capability.
- **Nord Security** — Vilnius, 2012; NordVPN/NordPass/NordLayer; $1.6B valuation (2022, $100M) → ~$3B (2023-09-28, Warburg Pincus, $100M). **Fit is genuine:** subscription billing in a market where ~60M Brazilians lack credit cards — recurring Pix directly addresses card-light churn/acquisition. This is the rare PR where the strategic logic (card-light subscription billing) is real even if the numbers are unverifiable.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty: low.** Neither the rail, the EBANX product, nor the recurring-Pix concept is new; the only new element is one merchant's claimed result.
- **Traction: claimed, single-sourced.** 28% of TPV in 6 months is aggressive but not implausible for a card-light subscription merchant — directionally consistent with EBANX's own Hotmart claim (+32pp retention) and PagBrasil's Q1-2026 ecosystem growth (txns +182% QoQ, vendor-sourced). But there is **zero independent corroboration**; Nord disclosed no base numbers.
- **Why the value-add may be real:** the margin in this stack does NOT sit with the rail (free public infrastructure) — it sits with (a) the merchant's avoided card interchange/decline cost and (b) the PSP's conversion tooling. For a global subscription seller, replacing failed card auth (where ~60M consumers have no card at all) with an A2A debit is a real ARPU/retention lever. The risk: as every PSP commoditizes recurring Pix, EBANX's take-rate compresses and the "28% case study" becomes table-stakes rather than a moat.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Expect EBANX to keep publishing merchant case studies to lock distribution before competitors before take-rates compress; Beyond Borders 2026 (EBANX) claims Pix = 81% of business-to-merchant payments in Brazil at EBANX.
- **Risks / silences:** no fraud-liability, no economics/take-rate, no exclusivity, no churn-after-mandate disclosure (Pix Automático lets users cancel consent unilaterally — a structural churn risk unaddressed). Official CBB nationwide Pix Automático adoption totals remain unpublished/open.
- **Second-order:** the more EBANX advertises easy recurring-Pix wins, the faster the rail commoditizes across PSPs → the central question shifts from "does recurring Pix work?" (clearly yes) to "can EBANX defend a premium take-rate once every PSP offers the same public rail with similar conversion tooling?"

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: cross-border acquiring / alternative-payment-method (APM) orchestration in LATAM, with the news hook on Pix Automático (Brazil's instant-payment recurring rail, live since June 2025). The rail is scaling fast: per PagBrasil, Q4'25→Q1'26 Pix Automático transactions +182%, new users +181%, recurring users +177% (PagBrasil, 26-Jun-2026). EBANX's own IR projects Pix Automático transaction volume growing ~41%/month and ~USD 30bn in recurring payments cumulatively in its first two years (to ~Jun-2027) (EBANX IR product update, 16-Dec-2025; "Pix to approach 8bn monthly transactions" study, 13-May-2026). Structure: the acquiring/orchestration layer is fragmented and intermediated — Pix is free, low-cost public infrastructure built by the Banco Central, so the value EBANX captures is integration + global-merchant reach + fraud/approval optimization, NOT the rail itself. Why now: recurring billing was impossible on Pix until Automático shipped; that opens subscription monetization (SaaS, streaming, VPN/security like Nord) on a rail that previously only did one-off payments. Second-order: as Pix/A2A volume rises, blended take-rates on card-based processing compress — a structural headwind even as volumes grow (see dLocal evidence below).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: TPV, take rate (gross profit / TPV), approval/authorization rate, gross-profit mix by geography. Key players in LATAM cross-border APM: dLocal (NASDAQ: DLO, the closest listed pure-play peer), EBANX, plus PPRO, Boku, and global generalists Adyen/Stripe pushing into the region. Basis of competition: local-method coverage, approval rates, and global-merchant distribution rather than price (the rail is commoditized). Recent dated moves: PPRO added Pix Automático day-one of the BCB rollout (16-Jun-2025); Boku secured a BCB payment-institution licence (31-Jul-2025) and launched regulated Brazil payments (12-May-2026); EBANX itself enabled recurring Pix for Hotmart with a claimed +32pt retention lift (24-Sep-2025) and for Stripe's Pix acceptance via EBANX (12-Aug-2025). Protagonist position: EBANX is a scaled incumbent in LATAM APM — IR claims record +48% TPV growth in 2025 (Brazil TPV +51%), 65% of gross profit generated outside Brazil, 500+ global merchants (EBANX IR, 12-Mar-2026 & 28-May-2026, drive_url https://drive.google.com/file/d/16gAm9CXE52KQeyBmp47ELLGnN5VoPz00/view , https://drive.google.com/file/d/15Vt2KKHvldLX1nP9EcPjZTaJfpwqOoAc/view ). Moat (analysis): distribution + local-method breadth + accumulated approval-rate data (intangibles/scale), not network effects; switching costs for merchants are moderate. Nord Security is the merchant/reference customer, not a payments competitor.

**Comps & multiples.** Best listed comp is dLocal (same model: emerging-market cross-border APM, Pix-exposed). dLocal FY2025: revenue ~$1.21bn TTM, TPV ~$41bn (+60% YoY); market cap ~$3.87–4.04bn (May-2026) → P/S = $3.87bn / $1.21bn ≈ 3.2x (range 3.2–3.3x) (stockanalysis.com, macrotrends, Yahoo Finance, dLocal SEC 6-K FY2025). dLocal trades in-line/cheap for a 60%-growth payments name — the discount reflects take-rate compression (TPV +60–73% vs gross profit +40%, i.e. blended take rate falling as Pix/A2A mix rises). EBANX is private: last disclosed round ~$430m Series C (2021), unicorn since 2019; a secondary aggregator (getlatka) cites ~$1bn valuation and ~$360.9m ARR — treat as `[UNSOURCED]`/round-vintage, not a verified market value, so EBANX EV/Revenue = "no data". The "R$2.6B TPV" figure that surfaced in one secondary blog is inconsistent with EBANX's own +48% TPV-growth disclosure and is disregarded. Internal comps (corpus): [[EBANX enables Pix recurring payments for Hotmart]], [[Stripe enables Pix payments in Brazil via EBANX]], [[PPRO adds Pix Automatico to its payments platform]], [[Boku launches regulated payments in Brazil after central bank clearance]], [[Brazil's Pix surpasses 5 billion transactions amid fraud challenges]]. Distribution not computed — only one truly comparable listed name (dLocal); qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
1. Take-rate compression / disintermediation: Pix is free public rail; as A2A volume displaces cards, blended take rates fall (dLocal's gross profit grew ~40% vs TPV ~60–73%). EBANX captures integration value, not the rail — margin per unit of volume structurally erodes even as TPV grows.
2. Concentration / metric framing: the headline "28% of payment volume" and "outperforms credit cards" are EBANX-issued, single-merchant (Nord Security) figures, 6 months in — share of one merchant, not a verified sector or EBANX-portfolio metric, and approval-rate claims are unaudited. Silent on absolute volume, fees, and fraud liability.
3. Regulatory/fraud dependence: Pix Automático economics and rules are set by the Banco Central (e.g., mandatory-debit and installment rules in late-2025), and Pix is facing fraud scrutiny (corpus: "Pix surpasses 5bn transactions amid fraud challenges") — EBANX is a price-taker on rail rules it does not control.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is a new-entry/usage data point, not a re-rating: recurring Pix turns Brazil into a viable subscription-billing market for global digital merchants, and EBANX is positioning as the orchestration on-ramp. Falsifiable thesis: if Pix Automático recurring TPV approaches EBANX's projected ~USD 30bn cumulative by mid-2027 and is matched by EBANX gross-profit (not just TPV) growth, the orchestration layer holds value; what breaks the thesis — gross-profit growth lagging TPV (à la dLocal) would confirm the rail commoditizes EBANX's economics. Watch: EBANX's next TPV/gross-profit disclosure and dLocal's Brazil take-rate trend as the read-through.
