# Context-enrichment: Travel Wallet picks NH and KB Securities for 2027 Kosdaq IPO
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 2026-06-25 Travel Wallet ((주)트래블월렛), an 8-year-old Seoul travel-fintech, announced it had appointed **NH Investment & Securities and KB Securities as co-lead underwriters** for a planned **2027 Kosdaq IPO**, while "accelerating" its global digital wallet and B2B payments lines. The underwriter mandate is real (company statement, multiple Korean outlets same day) — but the rest is an equity-story frame, not a milestone. There is **no disclosed target valuation, no filing date, no listing date**.

The de-PR'd reality the IPO PR sits on top of:
- **Travel Wallet is not profitable.** Audited: FY2024 revenue ₩59.2bn / operating loss ₩30.2bn (loss roughly tripled YoY); FY2025 revenue ₩70.6bn (+19%) / operating loss still ₩12.3bn, net loss ₩5.6bn. The "흑자전환 / ₩100bn revenue 2025" narrative was a *target* it **missed** — what actually turned positive was Q4-2024 *contribution margin* (variable-cost-only), not operating profit.
- **Why framed this way (→ what it reveals):** Management blames the FY2024 loss on a ₩37.5bn FX translation loss (record won slide) plus ~₩15bn card-production cost. That deflection conveniently masks the structural story: the FX spread the whole business monetizes is being competed to zero by the banks (see [1]). The "2027 IPO + global wallet + stablecoin" packaging is doing narrative work disproportionate to shipped reality — it reframes a loss-making, single-market FX-card business as a global-infrastructure growth story ahead of a listing.
- Founded Oct 2017, CEO Kim Hyung-wook (ex-FX researcher / Samsung Asset Mgmt). Holds a **Visa Principal License** (direct issuer, first Korean firm in Visa Fintech Fast Track); product launched Feb 2021. ~106 staff, "pre-unicorn" (예비유니콘) designation Sept 2025.

## [1] Competitors / peers
The Korean travel-card market is a **margin price war**, and the standalone prepaid model is the collateral.
- **Trigger:** Toss Bank's "lifetime free FX" (평생 무료환전, Jan 2024) — permanent 0-fee conversion, drew >600k FX accounts in 3 weeks and forced the cascade.
- **Leader: Hana Travlog** — first mover (Jul 2022), 10M users (Dec 2025), ~53% of overseas debit/check-card spend, 58 currencies fee-free.
- **#2 Shinhan SOL Travel** (Feb 2024) — 3M cards by Mar 2026. Fast-followers: KB Travelers, Samsung iD Global, Woori Wibee (all 2024).
- **Wise** is effectively inbound-only in Korea (FX-control regulation) — not a direct domestic rival.
- **Why this is the central question (→ second order):** Travel Wallet built itself on the FX spread (free only up to ~$500/currency/month, then ~0.5–2.5%). Banks now give that away universally to lock in *deposit* customers — they can eat free-FX losses because they cross-subsidize from banking. A standalone prepaid player with no deposit/lending relationship **structurally cannot**. So the real question is not "can it IPO" but "what is the durable margin once the spread is zero" — and the answer is being decided against it. Cf. [[Kbank advances third IPO attempt in South Korea]]: even a deposit-funded Korean neobank had to cut valuation ~20% and try three times to list. A travel-card pure-play has a weaker hand.

## [2] Company history / fit
Trajectory: Series A ₩7.5bn (2020) → Series B ₩15.8bn @ ₩100bn post (2021) → Series C ₩19.7bn (2023) → Lightspeed *secondary* (~5.2%, no fresh capital, 2024) → Series D ~₩16.1bn @ **~₩370bn post** (Sep–Oct 2025). Cumulative primary ~₩59bn; press cites ~₩90bn cumulative (undisclosed bridges). Revenue scaled fast (₩2.7bn FY22 → ₩70.6bn FY25) on Korea's post-COVID outbound-travel boom; ~9.6M cumulative cards, ~₩9.8tn cumulative GMV, MAU 1.6M (self-reported PR, not audited).
- **Why it acts this way (→ structural pressure):** A commodity FX-spread take-rate being competed to zero cannot support a software multiple. So Travel Wallet *must* reposition as "global wallet + B2B rails infrastructure" to justify an IPO multiple — and it must time the listing to the still-elevated travel/GMV numbers before the spread fully erodes. The pivot is a survival move, not opportunistic expansion. Note the IPO is a ~1-year slip (CEO floated 2026 in 2024) — a soft slip, no withdrawn filing.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Live & real:** core card (scaling but loss-making); a genuine **B2B cloud issuing/settlement business (PaaS/BaaS with Visa, shipped May 2023)** that now powers *rivals* — Kakao Bank's 달러박스 routes all non-USD currencies through Travel Wallet; BC Card x Visa "내 외화 머니" runs on its rails. Turning channel competitors into distribution is the one genuinely clever, structurally interesting move. **But it is still tiny** — targeted at only ~10% of revenue (~₩10bn) by end-2025, with **no booked B2B revenue actually disclosed**. Japan wallet is live (Apr–May 2026) but small.
**Announced / vaporware-risk (NOT live):** US service ("within 2026," no date); cross-border remittance / "living finance"; **won stablecoin (Avalanche MOU only, no product, regulation-gated)**.
- **Where the margin goes (→ what underpins/breaks the multiple):** The honest value-add is the B2B rails — selling "don't build ₩5–10bn of issuing infra yourself." That *could* earn an infrastructure multiple. But Visa sits above it (Travel Wallet is a Visa Principal-License issuer — Visa captures network economics and is a single point of dependency), and below it the banks it serves can vertically integrate once the spread is theirs to give away. So Travel Wallet risks being a **thin rails-reseller squeezed between Visa and the banks** rather than the margin-capturing layer. The "global wallet" and "stablecoin" lines are equity-story garnish; treat as not confirmed.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
2027 Kosdaq listing; NH + KB confirmed leads; process began ~Apr 2026 (RFPs); K-GAAP→K-IFRS conversion needed before 예심. No valuation/date disclosed.
- **Climate — cautionary for fintech specifically (→ counterintuitive):** Korea's two marquee fintechs *fled* the domestic market — **Toss/Viva Republica dropped its Kospi IPO (Oct 2024) for a Nasdaq listing ~2026 (>$10B)** explicitly because Korea "would not fairly value a fintech"; **Dunamu/Upbit** is merging into Naver Financial eyeing Nasdaq (2031 backstop). The domestic cautionary tale: **Kakao Pay** is ~80% below its 2021 peak (cf. [[Kakao Pay links Japan's PayPay to offline payments in Korea]]). **K Bank** only listed (Mar 2026) after **three withdrawals and ~halving its valuation** ([[Kbank advances third IPO attempt in South Korea]]). Kosdaq itself is soft/volatile (~880–990 in 2026) while Kospi runs hot on AI/chip names.
- **Net:** A 2027 Kosdaq fintech IPO is feasible (window is open) but faces documented skepticism toward fintech valuations, a weak board, the awkward precedent that Korea's *best* fintechs chose not to list there, and — uniquely for Travel Wallet — a still-loss-making P&L whose core margin engine is being competed away. The choice of Kosdaq (not Kospi/Nasdaq like its peers) itself signals it is too small/unprofitable for the bigger boards (analysis).

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: Korean travel-FX / multi-currency prepaid cards, with a thin B2B card-issuing-as-a-service (PaaS) layer underneath. No clean Korea-specific travel-FX TAM is publicly verifiable — global forex-prepaid-card figures exist (per Market Research Future, ~$184bn 2025 → ~$764bn 2035, ~15.3% CAGR, via marketresearchfuture.com) but that is a secondary, paywalled-style market-report number covering a far broader scope, so treat as **directional only, not a Korea TAM** [UNSOURCED for Korea]. What is sourced and decisive is the demand pool: Korea's outbound-travel rebound (Japan/SE-Asia/Oceania load factors up through 2025, per Euromonitor coverage) is the single driver of GMV — i.e. the top line is **structurally a derivative of travel volume**, not a recurring software base. Structure: **rapidly consolidating around bank-owned super-apps, not fragmenting.** The value in the chain has migrated from the FX spread (now ~zero) to *deposit acquisition* — banks give free FX away as a customer-acquisition cost and recapture it in banking/lending, a cross-subsidy a standalone prepaid player cannot run. "Why now": Toss Bank's permanent free-FX (Jan 2024) drew >1M customers in 4 months (per Korea Times/Korea Herald), converting the spread from a revenue line into table stakes. Second-order effect: the only defensible place left for a pure-play is *infrastructure* (issuing rails) — which is exactly why Travel Wallet is repositioning ahead of the listing.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: cumulative GMV / FX-transaction volume, overseas debit-card payment **share**, MAU, and (for the B2B line) booked issuing/settlement revenue. Players & basis of competition — competition is on **price (free FX) + distribution (bank super-app + deposit base)**, not product:
- **Hana Travlog (leader):** crossed **10M users Dec 2025** (≈1M in <1yr at launch Jul 2022), **₩5.4tn cumulative FX**, top overseas-debit share for **34 consecutive months**, ~50% share (per Korea Times, koreaherald.com, asiae). This is the share-of-wallet benchmark Travel Wallet is measured against.
- **Toss Bank:** free-FX FX-account, >1M customers in 4 months (2024) — the price-war trigger.
- **Shinhan SOL Travel** (Feb 2024, ~3M cards by Mar 2026 per note); fast-followers KB Travelers / Samsung iD Global / Woori Wibee (all 2024).
- **Travel Wallet (protagonist):** 9.6M *cumulative* cards, ₩9.8tn *cumulative* GMV (self-reported, per Korea Times 2026-06-25), MAU 1.6M. Position: **niche / catching-up and structurally squeezed** — large cumulative card count but no deposit base, no comparable disclosed *active* FX-share, and a P&L still in operating loss. Moat (analysis): the *one* genuine asset is **switching-cost-ish B2B rails** (Visa Principal License + cloud issuing live since May 2023, powering Kakao Bank 달러박스 non-USD and BC Card×Visa). That is a real intangible/integration moat — but it sits *below* Visa (network economics) and *above* banks that can vertically integrate, i.e. a moat over a shrinking middle.

**Comps & multiples.** No IPO valuation is disclosed, so forward multiples = **no data**. Anchor points:
- Last private round: **~₩370bn post-money** (Series D, Sep–Oct 2025). On FY2025 revenue ₩70.6bn that is **₩370bn / ₩70.6bn ≈ 5.2x trailing P/S** — but on a **₩12.3bn operating-loss** business, so EV/EBITDA / P/E = **not computable (EBITDA-negative)**; judge on revenue multiple only. ~5x P/S for a loss-making, single-market, commodity-spread player is **rich vs. the de-rated Korean fintech tape** (see risk flags), not cheap.
- Internal comps (fintech-IPO climate, Korea): [[Toss plans domestic listing after US IPO]] — Toss valued ₩10–20tn but **chose Nasdaq first**, domestic listing only ~later; [[Naver, KakaoPay and Toss turn profitable, expand globally]] — the marquee names reached *profitability* before/around listing, which Travel Wallet has not; [[Kbank advances third IPO attempt in South Korea]] — a deposit-funded neobank still had to cut valuation ~20% over three tries; [[Kakao Pay links Japan's PayPay to offline payments in Korea]] — Kakao Pay ~−80% from 2021 peak. Direct travel-FX trading comps are unlisted (Hana/Shinhan/Toss are bank subsidiaries), so a peer multiple set **cannot be computed — qualitative comparison only**.
- Outlier flag: ~5x trailing P/S is inside the normal EV/Rev guide range, but for a business whose revenue growth (+19% FY25) is **decelerating into a price war that zeroes its core take-rate**, the multiple is **not obviously justified by growth** — flag as rich.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Margin disintermediation (core).** The FX spread Travel Wallet monetizes is being competed to ~0 by deposit-funded banks; without a deposit/lending cross-subsidy it has no structural answer — second-order: revenue can keep rising on travel volume while contribution margin erodes, so "growth" masks a deteriorating unit economy.
2. **Korean fintech valuation de-rating + soft Kosdaq.** Kosdaq sits ~900 vs Kospi ~9,000, was called "a graveyard for retail investors" (Seoul Economic Daily, 2026-06-25), fell -4.5% on "Black Friday" 5 Jun 2026 and saw a record Kospi drop 23 Jun — and even a *profitable* fintech ("Company K", 30% margins) trades **below half the fintech/software sector P/E** there. A loss-making travel-FX pure-play listing into this is mispriced-to-the-downside risk; the choice of Kosdaq (not Kospi/Nasdaq like Toss/Dunamu) itself signals it is too small/unprofitable for the better boards (analysis).
3. **Stacked dependency: Visa above, banks below.** Entire issuing model rests on a single Visa Principal License (no Mastercard/UnionPay diversification found); the B2B clients (Kakao Bank, BC Card) are also its retail competitors who can in-source once the spread is theirs to give — so the rails business is a **thin reseller squeezed at both ends**, and it is still only ~10% revenue target with **no booked B2B revenue disclosed**.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a **shakeout marker, not a re-rating**: the 2027 mandate confirms a standalone Korean travel-FX prepaid player is trying to escape a zero-margin core by re-labelling itself "global wallet + issuing infrastructure" before the spread fully erodes (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: *the IPO only re-rates if Travel Wallet discloses material, growing, audited **B2B issuing revenue** that decouples the top line from the dying FX spread.* Trigger to watch: the 예심/K-IFRS filing and any first disclosed B2B segment revenue; **what breaks the thesis** = B2B stays an undisclosed ~10% sliver and the listing leans on cumulative-GMV vanity metrics — in which case expect a Kospi/Nasdaq-peer-style valuation cut or a slip past 2027.
