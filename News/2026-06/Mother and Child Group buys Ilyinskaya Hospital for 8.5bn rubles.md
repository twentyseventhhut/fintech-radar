 ГК «Мать и дитя» выкупила «Ильинскую больницу» за 8,5 млрд рублей 
 ГК «Мать и дитя» (МКПАО «МД Медикал Груп»), один из лидеров на рынке частных медицинских услуг в России, объявляет о приобретении Ильинской больницы - крупного многопрофильного амбулаторно-госпитального центра в Московской области.
Ильинская больница - это многопрофильный госпиталь общей площадью 14 088 кв. м., открытый в 2019 году и расположенный в густонаселенном районе Московской области, вблизи Новорижского и Ильинского шоссе. В рамках сделки Компания приобретает в собственность здание госпиталя в элитном районе Московского региона, укомплектованного передовым медицинским оборудованием, и земельный участок размером 1,44 га под возможное строительство дополнительной очереди по цене, сопоставимой со строительством нового госпиталя аналогичного масштаба. При этом в состав Группы войдут мощности с подтвержденной клиентской базой, обеспеченной квалифицированным медицинским персоналом.
Стоимость сделки составит 8,5 млрд руб., включая 1,1 млрд руб. долга на балансе приобретаемого актива, с оплатой собственными и заемными денежными средствами. Сделка была одобрена ФАС и находится на регистрации.
Ильинская больница – одно из ведущих медицинских учреждений Москвы и Московской области, которое оказывает экстренную и плановую многопрофильную медицинскую помощь взрослым и детям - от первичной общей практики до сложной высокотехнологичной медицины. В Ильинской больнице собрана сильнейшая команда врачей – экспертов, признанных мировым медицинским сообществом. Ильинская больница обладает широкими возможностями лучевой, эндоскопической и лабораторной диагностики. Операционный блок Ильинской больницы – передовой высокотехнологичный комплекс, действующий согласно современным международным протоколам безопасности, оснащенный лучшей хирургической техникой, в т.ч. роботизированной системой Da Vicni, позволяющей выполнять малоинвазивные и органосохраняющие операции. Онкологический профиль Ильинской больницы позволяет оказывать пациентам услуги по направлениям хирургии и таргетной химиотерапии. Мощность Ильинской больницы составляет 276 тыс. посещений специалистов и 27 740 койко-дней в год.
Общая численность персонала Ильинской больницы – 662 человека, из них 236 врачей, многие из которых имеют ученые степени докторов и кандидатов медицинских наук.
Данное приобретение является стратегическим шагом, направленным на усиление позиций ГК Мать и дитя на рынке Московского региона, а также развитие госпитального формата оказания медицинской помощи, являющимися основными драйверами роста выручки.
Фокус на оказании многопрофильной медицинской помощи Ильинской больницы позволит существенно увеличить долю этого направления в Группе, а синергии в хирургии и онкологии - выйти на новый уровень стационарной медицинской помощи в Москве.
В рамках сделки Ильинская больница продолжит работу под историческим брендом, сохранив существующий уровень обслуживания и укрепив кадровый потенциал за счет интеграции ведущих специалистов и современных медицинских централизованных процессов.
Марк Курцер, генеральный директор ГК «МД Медикал», отметил:
«Эта сделка позволяет нам стать самым крупным коечным фондом в Российской Федерации и самым диверсифицированным коммерческим стационаром Московского региона, оказывающим медицинскую помощь от акушерства и неонатологии до онкологии и хирургии.
Ильинская больница – это сильный бренд, с превосходной репутацией и лояльными пациентами, с уникальной экспертизой в высокотехнологичной медицине. И нам крайне важно сохранить историческое название, а также фокус на оказании стационарных услуг, поэтому мы не планируем открывать родильное отделение на базе приобретаемого актива.
Развитие госпитального звена – на сегодня приоритет развития Группы, только в условиях мощностей такого масштаба можно оказывать медицинскую помощь широкого профиля от амбулаторного приема до высокотехнологичной медицины. И приобретая Ильинскую больницу, обеспеченную оборудованием, квалифицированным персоналом и клиентской базой, мы получаем крупный госпиталь здесь и сейчас, экономя на сроках строительства и выхода на плановую загрузку, по цене, сопоставимой со строительством нового стационара».
Ранее основным владельцем «Ильинской больницы», по данным " Коммерсанта ", был казахский предприниматель Тимур Кулибаев, зять бывшего президента страны Нурсултана Назарбаева.

# Context-enrichment: Mother and Child (MD Medical Group) buys Ilyinskaya Hospital for RUB 8.5bn
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**FRESHNESS / DUPLICATE VERDICT: FRESH.** No prior note in the corpus covers MD Medical Group / "Mother and Child" / Ilyinskaya (confirmed via `sem search` — top hits are unrelated healthcare-fintech items, scores <0.03 — and via `grep`, which returns only this note). This is a genuinely NEW M&A event (deal announced/closed 24-25 Jun 2026, FAS-approved, in registration). NOTE a near-collision: MDMG did a *different* RUB 8.5bn deal in May 2025 — the "Expert" (Эксперт) clinic network. That is a separate prior transaction with the same headline figure, NOT this Ilyinskaya deal. Do not conflate them.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- MD Medical Group (MKPAO "MD Medical Group", MOEX: MDMG, brand "Mother and Child", CEO Mark Kurtser) is acquiring 100% of AO "Ilyinskaya Hospital" and OOO "Klinik Management Group" — a 14,088 sq m multi-profile out-patient + in-patient hospital in Moscow Region (opened 2019, near Novorizhskoye/Ilyinskoye highways), plus the building, advanced equipment (incl. a da Vinci robotic surgery system) and a 1.44 ha land plot for a possible future build-out.
- **Price: RUB 8.5bn total, INCLUDING RUB 1.1bn of debt sitting on the target's balance sheet.** So enterprise value ~8.5bn; equity portion ~7.4bn. Paid with own + borrowed cash. **Per Kommersant/Vedomosti/smart-lab: MDMG drew a RUB 6bn Sberbank credit line, 4.5-year tenor; management guides ~4-year payback** (these financing/payback specifics are NOT in the company PR — they come from press, so treat payback as management guidance, not audited).
- Seller (per Kommersant): Kazakh businessman Timur Kulibaev, son-in-law of ex-president Nazarbayev — i.e. an exit by a foreign owner of a Russian asset, consistent with the broader 2022-26 wave of foreign-owner divestitures in Russia.
- **Why structured this way:** debt-included headline price flatters nothing — it is honest EV framing. The Sberbank loan (not equity dilution) shows MDMG levering a net-cash balance sheet to fund inorganic growth; the 1.44 ha land "at a cost comparable to building a new hospital" is the explicit "buy vs build" thesis — Kurtser repeatedly anchors to "a large hospital here and now, saving construction time and ramp-to-utilization." That is the real value claim: time-to-capacity, not a cheap price.
- De-PR flags: "strongest team of doctors recognized by the world medical community", "leading institution" — unverifiable marketing. The hard, checkable numbers: 276k specialist visits/yr capacity, 27,740 bed-days/yr, 662 staff (236 doctors), 14,088 sq m. NO disclosed revenue/EBITDA of the target → outside observers cannot independently check the ~4yr payback.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- Russian private multi-profile / hospital peers: **EMC (European Medical Center, MOEX: GEMC)** — the premium Moscow in-patient incumbent and the closest listed comp; **Medси** (Sistema/АФК "Система"); **Доктор рядом, СМ-Клиника, К+31, Hadassah Medical Moscow**. MDMG historically led in obstetrics/IVF/perinatal; it is now buying its way into general multi-profile + oncology + surgery in-patient — i.e. moving onto EMC/Medси turf.
- Kurtser's claim: this makes MDMG "the largest bed fund in the Russian Federation" and "the most diversified commercial in-patient provider in the Moscow region." Plausible by raw bed count but unverified vs Medси's larger out-patient footprint — treat as a marketing superlative (analysis).
- **Why the landscape looks this way:** Russian private healthcare is consolidating because (a) foreign and individual owners are exiting (Kulibaev here), creating cheap-ish quality assets, and (b) the listed players (MDMG, EMC) have the balance sheets and cheap rouble debt access (Sberbank) to roll up. Second-order: a listed company paying ~4yr-payback for a built, staffed, da-Vinci-equipped hospital is rational vs multi-year greenfield risk in a high-rate environment.

## [2] Company history / fit
- MDMG: 2025 FY revenue ~RUB 43.5bn, net profit ~RUB 10.8bn (per market data); Q1 2026 revenue +32% to ~RUB 11.8bn, but ~RUB 2bn of that is the consolidation of the May-2025 "Expert" network — organic growth ~9% (per smart-lab/finam). Recommended FY2025 final dividend ~RUB 47/share; share ~RUB 1,212 (26 Jun 2026).
- This is the SECOND major RUB-8.5bn M&A inside ~13 months (Expert network May 2025; Ilyinskaya Jun 2026). Clear strategic pivot: **from obstetrics/IVF brand → diversified hospital/in-patient platform**, with management explicitly calling hospital/in-patient ("госпитальный формат") the main revenue-growth driver.
- **Why the company acts this way:** its core obstetrics/IVF market is mature and birth-rate-exposed (a structural demographic headwind in Russia). To keep double-digit growth and defend its premium multiple, MDMG must add adjacencies (oncology, surgery, general in-patient) — hence buy-and-integrate. Notably, management says it will NOT open a maternity ward at Ilyinskaya — a deliberate signal it is buying diversification AWAY from its birth-rate-dependent core.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- Real, not announced: this is a closed/closing deal (FAS-approved, registering), with an operating asset (existing patient base, 662 staff). That is acquired traction, not a pilot. The asset's own traction (276k visits, 27,740 bed-days capacity) is capacity, not disclosed actual utilization — the gap matters for the payback claim.
- Value-add thesis: synergy in surgery + oncology, leveraging MDMG's existing referral/brand and "centralized medical processes." Genuine if MDMG can lift the hospital's utilization toward capacity faster than Kulibaev did. **Who captures the margin:** MDMG captures it only if integration raises occupancy/case-mix; otherwise it has bought fixed cost (662 salaries) at a premium. The da Vinci + oncology/targeted-chemo lines are higher-margin than commodity out-patient — that is the upside.
- **What could break it:** if the RUB 1.1bn target debt + RUB 6bn Sberbank loan land in a high-rate environment, interest drag could push real payback well past the guided ~4 years; oncology/surgery ramp requires retaining the "expert" doctors who are the actual asset.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Plans: keep the historic "Ilyinskaya Hospital" brand and service level; integrate specialists + centralized processes; develop the 1.44 ha for a future phase; explicitly NO maternity ward there.
- Sentiment: brokerage coverage (smart-lab "positive review") frames MDMG as a growth + dividend name; the deal extends the in-patient-led growth story. Risk backdrop: Russian sanctions/macro, high interest rates raising the cost of the Sberbank line, birth-rate decline pressuring the legacy core, and execution risk on a second large integration in a year.
- **Why the market goes this way / second-order:** consolidation of foreign-owner exits is a structural tailwind for the few well-capitalized listed roll-ups. Counterintuitive second-order: stacking two RUB-8.5bn debt-funded deals in 13 months makes MDMG's growth increasingly *acquisition-dependent* and *leverage-sensitive* — the durable question shifts from "can the obstetrics brand grow" to "can MDMG integrate roll-ups and lift occupancy faster than rates erode the payback."

9. **Seller motivation / discount?** Kulibaev (foreign owner, Kazakh) exiting a Russian asset amid the 2022-26 divestiture wave — could imply a motivated/discounted seller, supporting MDMG's "buy vs build" math. **Analysis.**
10. **How acquisition-dependent is MDMG's growth?** Two RUB-8.5bn deals in 13 months; Q1'26 +32% headline but ~9% organic ex-Expert. Growth is increasingly inorganic + leverage-driven. Answered (structural risk).

13. **Does the deal change MDMG's multiple story?** It deepens the shift from "obstetrics premium" to "hospital roll-up" — arguably a lower-quality, more capital-intensive growth mode. **Analysis — central-question shift.**
14. **Counterparty/related-party concerns?** OOO "Klinik Management Group" also acquired — opco/propco split typical; no red flags disclosed. **Open.**
15. **Macro/sanctions exposure?** Russia-only, rouble-funded, domestic demand — insulated from sanctions but exposed to rouble rates, inflation and demographic decline. Answered.

Importance: 3/5 — A real, sizeable (RUB 8.5bn), FAS-approved domestic M&A by a MOEX-listed leader, with a clear strategic pivot (obstetrics → diversified hospital roll-up) and concrete financing (Sberbank RUB 6bn line). **Sector & drivers.** Russian private multi-profile healthcare is mid-consolidation, and the structural fuel is foreign/individual-owner exits. MDMG itself reports 2025 revenue RUB 43.5bn (+31% y/y), EBITDA RUB 13.3bn (+24%, ~30.5% margin) and net profit ~RUB 10.8-11.0bn (per company 12M'25 IFRS release) — i.e. a well-capitalised listed roll-up with the cash flow to fund inorganic growth. No public Russian-private-hospital TAM is verifiable here → market-size = "no data" (do not invent). Structure: fragmented at the asset level but consolidating around the two listed names (MDMG, EMC) plus Sistema's Medси; capital intensity (a built-and-equipped hospital) and regulation (FAS clearance, licensing) are the entry barriers. Why now: (a) a 2022-26 wave of foreign-owner divestitures (here the Kazakh seller Kulibaev, per Kommersant) puts built, staffed assets on the market; (b) listed players with rouble debt access (Sberbank) can buy-vs-build and skip multi-year greenfield ramp in a high-rate environment. Second-order: every deal funded by debt rather than equity raises the leverage-sensitivity of the whole growth story.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: revenue/EBITDA margin, bed-days & out-patient visits (capacity vs occupancy), case-mix (higher-margin oncology/surgery vs commodity out-patient). Key players: **EMC (United Medical Group, MOEX:GEMC)** — the premium Moscow in-patient incumbent and closest listed comp; **Medси (АФК Система)** — larger out-patient footprint; plus К+31, СМ-Клиника, Hadassah Moscow. Basis of competition: brand/clinical reputation + location + breadth of in-patient capability, not price. Recent moves: MDMG's May-2025 RUB 8.5bn "Expert" clinic-network buy (consolidated from Q4'25), and now Ilyinskaya (announced 24-25 Jun 2026, FAS-approved, in registration). Protagonist's position: historically the obstetrics/IVF leader, now **buying its way into general multi-profile + oncology + surgery in-patient** — i.e. moving onto EMC/Medси turf. Kurtser's "largest bed fund in RF / most diversified Moscow in-patient" is a marketing superlative — no third-party bed-count ranking cited (analysis). Moat: brand + referral network + scale; the durable asset of the target is the 236 doctors, so the moat is partly intangible/people-based and retention-dependent (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** The deal is RUB 8.5bn EV (incl. RUB 1.1bn target debt; equity portion ~RUB 7.4bn). Crucially, the context-enrichment said target financials were undisclosed — they are NOT: per Vedomosti/smart-lab, **AO Ilyinskaya Hospital 2025 RAS (РСБУ) revenue RUB 3.89bn, net profit RUB 690m.** This permits real arithmetic:
- **Deal EV/Revenue = 8.5 / 3.89 = 2.2x** (RAS revenue).
- **Deal EV / target net profit = 8.5 / 0.69 = 12.3x**; equity/NP = 7.4 / 0.69 = 10.7x.
- **Naive payback on current net profit = 8.5 / 0.69 ≈ 12 years** vs management's guided "~4-5 years" → the guidance implicitly assumes MDMG roughly TRIPLES the asset's profit (to ~RUB 2bn+/yr). That gap is the central skeptical point; EBITDA of the target is not disclosed, so an EV/EBITDA on the target = "no data".

Parent & peer trading comps (for context; third-party screeners, treat as indicative):
- **MDMG (MOEX:MDMG):** 75m shares; share ~RUB 1,212 (26 Jun 2026, per note) → mkt cap ~RUB 91bn. Trailing P/E ~8.4x, EV/EBITDA ~6.4x, P/S ~2.1x (per financemarker/blackterminal screeners) `[UNSOURCED-quality]`. On the deal's RUB 2.2x EV/Rev, MDMG is paying for the target ~in-line with its own ~2.1x P/S — i.e. NOT obviously cheap on revenue.
- **EMC (MOEX:GEMC):** 2024 revenue RUB 25.3bn, EBITDA RUB 11.5bn (45.5% margin — far higher than MDMG's ~30% and likely far above a commodity hospital), net profit RUB 10.4bn; mkt cap ~RUB 57.7bn (Aug-2025) → ~5.5x P/E, ~0.5-0.6x P/S — the premium-niche comp trades CHEAPER on revenue than MDMG.

Read-through: deal EV/Rev ~2.2x is in-line with MDMG's own valuation and is within the EV/Rev sanity range — not a multiple outlier; the risk is in the PROFIT/payback bridge, not the headline multiple.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Payback bridge is the whole bet (execution/valuation).** Target earns RUB 690m net today; ~12x current net profit / ~12-year naive payback vs guided ~4-5y means MDMG must ~triple the asset's profit via occupancy + oncology/surgery case-mix. If integration underdelivers, MDMG has bought RUB 8.5bn of mostly fixed cost (662 salaries) at ~2.2x revenue. Why it matters: it converts a "growth" deal into a multi-year integration risk.
2. **Leverage shift on a historically net-cash name (financing).** Company materials describe MDMG as debt-funding-light; the RUB 6bn Sberbank line (4.5y) + RUB 1.1bn assumed debt is a real change in funding mode. At elevated rouble rates, interest drag is material and is NOT netted into the guided payback → the ~4y figure is likely flattered. Second large debt-funded deal in 13 months makes growth increasingly leverage- and acquisition-dependent.
3. **Foreign-seller exit + no audited target IFRS (information/governance).** Seller is an exiting foreign owner (motivated seller could mean a fair price OR a stressed asset); the only target numbers are RAS, not IFRS, and from press, not MDMG disclosure — so margin/EBITDA and true occupancy remain unverifiable. Why it matters: the buyer's edge depends on data outsiders can't yet check.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is consolidation, not a re-rating: MDMG is converting itself from an obstetrics-premium story into a debt-funded hospital roll-up, structurally lower-quality, more capital-intensive growth (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if the next 2-3 quarters of MDMG IFRS show the consolidated in-patient segment lifting occupancy/case-mix and the asset tracking toward ~RUB 2bn cash flow, the ~4-5y payback is credible; trigger to watch = MDMG's H2'26/FY'26 segment disclosure on Ilyinskaya utilisation and net-debt/EBITDA. What breaks it: flat target profit + rising interest cost would stretch real payback well past 5 years and confirm growth is now bought, not earned.
