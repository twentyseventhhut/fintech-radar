# Russian central bank revokes ALOR+ depository licence

> [!info] 2026-06-26 · 3 упоминаний · 2 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: Финтехно

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[Финтехно] Разбираемся в ситуации с депозитарной лицензией ООО «АЛОР +», которую сегодня аннулировал Банк России. На практике это значит, что компания больше не может нормально оказывать услугу учёта прав на ценные бумаги клиентов как лицензированный депозитарий. Им, скорее всего, предложат перевести бумаги в другой депозитарий или к другому брокеру.

[Финтехно] ▶️ «АЛОР» в своих регламентах пишет, что при недостатке обеспечения брокер может без дополнительного согласия клиента продать бумаги или закрыть позиции по текущим, в том числе невыгодным, ценам, а убытки ложатся на клиента.

[Финтехно] Клиентам с обычным портфелем без плеча, вероятно, будет достаточно понимания, куда переводить бумаги, сколько это займёт, будут ли временные ограничения на сделки, переводы, дивиденды, купоны и корпоративные действия. При переводе ИИС важно соблюдать тип счёта и сроки закрытия старого договора, иначе могут возникнуть налоговые последствия.

# Context-enrichment: Russian central bank revokes ALOR+ depository licence
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **25–26 June 2026** the Bank of Russia (ЦБ РФ) **annulled the DEPOSITORY (custody) licence ONLY** of broker ООО «АЛОР +» ("Алор брокер", OGRN 1027700075941). This is a **forced sanction for conduct violations**, not a voluntary surrender and not a solvency/AML event. Crucially, the **brokerage licence (077-04827-100000) and ALOR's other live licences survive** — so the firm can still route orders; it just can no longer record/custody clients' securities rights itself.
- **De-PR'd reality:** ALOR's own client message ("assets are in safety, we will ensure a correct transition") is reassurance-framing. The CBR likewise says the move "does not create risks of blocking client funds and securities." Both are partly true (assets transferable, not lost) and partly spin: ALOR **asked clients to close margin/leveraged positions** the same day, which implies real operational disruption during the wind-down. The note's own aggregated text underscores this — under ALOR's broker regulations, if collateral is insufficient the broker may, **without further client consent, sell securities or close positions at current (even unfavourable) prices, with losses borne by the client**. So the "everything is safe" frame conceals a forced-deleveraging risk for margin clients.
- **Why structured this way:** Pulling ONLY the depository licence (not the broker licence) is a **calibrated, proportionate sanction** — it punishes the specific failure (custody/accounting) while avoiding a disorderly broker collapse that would freeze 38k clients' market access. → That is why the CBR could simultaneously frame it as "no risk to clients": the architecture of the penalty is designed to be contained.

## [1] Competitors / peers (precedent class: regulator pulls a financial licence)
Direct corpus precedents for a regulator revoking a licence / conduct enforcement:
- [[Eurasian Bank loses Uzbekistan licence over capital shortfall]] — closest structural peer (CIS central bank revokes a licence, liquidation, client-handling) — but that was **capital shortfall + compulsory liquidation**, i.e. a solvency death; ALOR is **conduct-only, single-licence, firm survives**. ALOR is materially less severe.
- [[Interactive Brokers fined nearly $12 million for sanctions breaches]] — broker punished by a regulator (OFAC) for **sanctions-related conduct**; parallels the "sanctions-regime violation" theme, but IBKR paid a fine and kept operating, whereas ALOR lost a whole licence.
- [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]] — Russian-market regulatory backdrop / CBR posture context.
**Position:** Among licence actions, ALOR sits in the **middle band** — harsher than a fine (IBKR), far milder than forced liquidation (Eurasian Bank). The distinctive feature vs all peers: the violated rule is **Russia's own counter-sanctions decrees** (No. 95/2022, No. 138/2023, No. 840/2024 — the non-resident "Type C" segregated-accounting regime), plus **backdated depository entries**. (analysis)
- **Second-order:** This is enforcement of the *exit-of-frozen-foreign-assets* perimeter, not a generic broker cleanup. A Dec-2025 CBR board decision already tightened depositories' segregated-accounting duties (cbr.ru/rbr/dir_decisions/rsd_2025-12-09_34_01) → ALOR may be the first visible enforcement of that tightening, raising read-across risk to other depositories. (hypothesis — not confirmed)

## [2] Company history / fit
ALOR ("Алор брокер", entity ALOR+) is **one of Russia's oldest brokers** — brand lineage to **1993**, entity registered **1997**. Scale: **~1.5 trln ₽ client assets at end-2025** (Vedomosti/press); ALOR self-reports **>2 trln ₽ assets and >20 trln ₽ annual turnover** (promo, treat skeptically) and **~38,000 clients** across ~17 regional offices. NRA rated it **AAA** (Aug 2024). Businesses: brokerage, (former) depository/custody, dealer; access to Moscow Exchange, SPB Exchange, SPbMTSB; well-known proprietary trading software (ALOR Trade / ALOR-API / OpenAPI) strong with the algo/active-trader community.
- **Fit / why it happened this way:** A multi-trillion-₽, AAA, 30-year legacy broker losing a licence for **conduct, not solvency** is the tell. → The failure is **compliance-cultural** (repeatedly ignoring CBR directives + backdating records), not financial distress. → For a legacy custodian whose edge is active/algo traders and non-standard operations, the structural pressure was to keep serving complex (incl. non-resident/foreign-creditor) flows — exactly the perimeter the counter-sanctions decrees now police. (analysis)
- **Headcount vs AUM tension:** 38k clients is **modest** vs bank-affiliated mega-brokers (Sber/T-Bank/VTB with millions). So ALOR is **large by AUM/legacy, mid-tier by retail headcount** → systemically minor even if individually notable.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction (what's actually material)
What is genuinely new here is **not** "a broker lost a licence" (routine) but **the stated cause**: repeated breach of the non-resident/counter-sanctions securities regime **plus backdated depository records** — a fraud-adjacent accounting failure. That is the real signal: the CBR is **policing the custody/accounting layer used to move frozen foreign-held securities**, and willing to pull a licence from a large, AAA-rated incumbent to do it.
- **Who captures the impact in the stack:** The depository (custody/record-keeping) layer is where the violation sat, so that is the layer the CBR amputated — leaving brokerage (order routing) intact. → Client securities migrate to **another depository** (NSD/НРД plausible but **unconfirmed**) over a **~6-month** wind-down; assets re-registered, not lost. (Standard CBR practice: 3–12 months to return/transfer client assets.)
- **Traction caveat (anti-PR):** "Assets safe" is the *announced* outcome; the *live* friction is forced margin-position closure and a custody migration with possible temporary limits on trades, transfers, dividends, coupons and corporate actions — and, for ИИС accounts, tax pitfalls if account type/closing-deadline rules are mishandled (per the note's own primary-source memo). So value-loss is operational/transitional, not principal. (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Process:** ~6-month wind-down; ALOR transfers client securities to another licensed depository; clients with plain unleveraged portfolios mainly need to know **where** to move and **timing**; margin clients face deleveraging risk.
- **Sentiment / market reaction:** Reports of a **~3–5% intraday jump** in the Moscow Exchange index around the news, but analysts attribute this to a **short squeeze / oversold bounce and large-client repositioning**, NOT to fundamentals. **Causation is contested** — do not present the index move as caused by the ALOR action. (skeptical)
- **Regulatory backdrop:** Anti-sanctions / counter-sanctions enforcement is intensifying; the Dec-2025 CBR tightening of depository segregated accounting suggests **more enforcement could follow** against depositories that mishandled non-resident assets — the key second-order watch-item. (hypothesis)
- **Counterintuitive second order:** Because the penalty was deliberately **contained to one licence**, the systemic impact is small — but the *signal* is large: even an AAA, multi-trln-₽ legacy custodian is not too big to be sanctioned for breaching the non-resident-securities perimeter. The fragility revealed is **compliance/perimeter**, not capital.
- **Open / unconfirmed:** exact CBR order (приказ) number / direct press-release URL not retrieved; receiving depository not named; no evidence ALOR is on EU/US/UK sanctions lists (this is *Russian domestic* counter-sanctions enforcement, not a Western designation); AUM figure is a 1.5–2 trln ₽ range; "7th by revenue" ranking unverified.

# Challenge / red-team: Russian central bank revokes ALOR+ depository licence

Second-order questions that actually decide the item's weight. Each answered from sources or marked **open**.

NRA-rated AAA pre-event → conduct, not distress. (Interfax, RG)

**Sector & drivers.** Russian retail brokerage / depository (custody) layer. *Size/growth:* retail now makes up ~three-quarters of domestic share trading on Moscow Exchange, with the retail-investor count up ~50x between 2013 and 2025 (per CBR, via brokerlistings/scribe roundups); net retail contributions to brokerage accounts ~RUB 574bn in the period cited, ~2x the prior year, driven by falling deposit rates (web, secondary — treat the headline figures as approximate, not from a single audited source). The custody/record-keeping (depository) function sits *behind* the broker in the value chain; NSD/НРД is the central settlement depository above firm-level depositories. Entry barriers are regulatory (CBR licence per function) more than capital. *Drivers / why now:* (1) intensifying CBR enforcement of the counter-sanctions / non-resident "Type C" segregated-accounting perimeter — a Dec-2025 CBR board decision tightened depositories' segregated-accounting duties, and ALOR looks like an early visible enforcement of it (hypothesis, see [1]); (2) sanctions-driven asset migration — Russians have moved record sums to foreign brokers, raising the policy premium on policing where frozen foreign-held securities are recorded. Why-ladder: CBR tightens custody accounting → first incumbent loses the custody licence → signals the *perimeter*, not capital, is now the binding constraint for Russian custodians.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: client assets (AUM), client count, share of MOEX retail turnover; for a *depository* specifically — securities under custody and segregated-accounting compliance (the KPI ALOR failed on, not a financial one). Key players & basis of competition: bank-arm brokers (Sber/T-Bank/VTB) compete on distribution + ecosystem reach and win retail headcount; legacy independents (ALOR, Finam, BCS, Aton) compete on platform/API and active-trader/algo product. **What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is *not* a re-rating or a deal — it is the first visible **enforcement signal** that even an AAA, multi-trln-₽, 30-year incumbent custodian is not too big to lose its custody licence for breaching the frozen-foreign-asset perimeter.
