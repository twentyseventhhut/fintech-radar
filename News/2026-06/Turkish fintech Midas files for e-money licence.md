Midas files for e-money licence to launch digital wallet
Sinziana Albu
22 Jun 2026 / 5 Min Read
Turkey-based investment platform Midas has applied for an electronic money institution licence to expand into digital payments.
According to Bloomberg , the company filed the application as part of a broader strategy to introduce digital wallet and prepaid card capabilities alongside its existing investment products. Founder Egem Eraslan outlined the initiative in a statement published on 17 June 2026, noting that the new features would allow users to spend funds directly from balances held within their Midas accounts, creating a more direct link between investing and everyday spending.
From investment app to financial platform
Midas offers users access to stocks listed on Borsa Istanbul, US equities, cryptocurrencies, mutual funds, and additional asset classes through a single mobile app. A low-cost model and mobile-focused design have helped the platform attract millions of customers seeking affordable access to both local and international markets.
The company has strengthened its financial position through several funding rounds, completing a fundraise in 2025 that was reported as a record for Turkey's fintech sector, bringing total investment to more than USD 100 million. The capital has supported product expansion and infrastructure development.
In addition, the payments expansion signals a move towards a broader financial super-app model. Through the process of adding spending capabilities, Midas aims to encourage users to maintain higher balances and engage with the platform more frequently. A digital wallet would support transfers and payments, while a prepaid card would allow direct access to account funds for purchases, without routing through traditional banks. Moreover, this approach reflects a trend seen among investment-focused apps globally, including US platforms such as Robinhood, which began offering card products in 2024 to consolidate financial activity within a single ecosystem.
Regulatory pathway and 2026 product roadmap
Securing the licence requires meeting requirements established by Turkey's central bank, covering customer fund protection, operational security, and compliance standards. Regulatory approval remains pending.
Eraslan has indicated that the wallet and card features form part of a broader 2026 product roadmap, which also includes advanced trading tools and expanded market access. If approved, Midas would gain formal authority to issue electronic money and offer related payment services, potentially unlocking new revenue from payment volumes while deepening its relationship with its existing user base.
Turkey's fintech sector continues to develop, driven by rising digital adoption and demand for low-cost financial services. Midas's application positions the company to compete with both traditional banks and other digital service providers in an increasingly competitive domestic market.

# Context-enrichment: Turkish fintech Midas files for e-money licence
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**Freshness: FRESH.** This is a genuinely new event — an EMI (electronic-money institution) licence application filed with Turkey's central bank, founder statement dated 17 Jun 2026, reported by Bloomberg 19 Jun 2026. It is NOT a re-run of the prior Midas funding coverage ([[Midas raises $80 million Series B, Turkey's largest fintech round]], Aug 2025): different event type (regulation vs funding), different date. Note a corpus naming collision: the 2026-03/04 "Midas $50m Series A for on-chain/tokenised investment infrastructure" notes ([[Midas raises $50m for tokenised investment infrastructure]], [[Midas closes $50m Series A for on-chain investment products]]) are a DIFFERENT, global tokenization company mis-sharing the `company/midas` tag — not the Turkish investing app.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Midas — an Istanbul retail investing app ("Turkey's Robinhood", founded 2020, founder/CEO Egem Eraslan) — **applied** to the Central Bank of the Republic of Türkiye (CBRT/TCMB) for an electronic-money-institution licence. Status is **PENDING; nothing is live or approved.** If granted, it would let Midas issue e-money and run a **digital wallet** (transfers/payments) plus a **prepaid card** so users can spend balances held in their Midas account "without routing through traditional banks" (Bloomberg/The Paypers).
- **De-PR'd:** this is an *application*, not a launch. The Paypers itself states "Regulatory approval remains pending." The "super-app" and "deepening relationship / higher balances" language is the company's framing of intent, not realized traction.
- **Why structured this way → what it reveals:** Midas's core is a brokerage (commodity, transaction-based take rate on trades). A wallet+card converts idle account cash into a *spend* graph and a payments-volume revenue line, and raises switching costs. (analysis) The licence is the gating step because in Turkey you cannot legally issue e-money or a prepaid card without CBRT authorisation under Law No. 6493 — Midas is buying a regulated rail, not building product first.

## [1] Competitors / peers
**Turkish e-money / payments (the licence pool Midas is joining):** Papara (>18M users, was the dominant EMI) — **CBRT REVOKED its licence Oct 2025** in an illegal-betting probe; Ininal, Aypara, PayFix and others had licences suspended in spring 2025 in the same crackdown; Tosla (Akbank-owned), Param, iyzico/Ödeal (recently authorised for open banking, [[Turkey authorizes iyzico and Odeal for open banking]]). **Second-order read:** the Turkish EMI space is mid-crackdown — CBRT approval in 2026 is slower and politically sensitive, so Midas's "pending" status carries real regulatory-timeline risk, not a formality. The Papara revocation and the Ozan seizure ([[Turkey's TMSF seizes fintech Ozan over laundering allegations]]) show the licence can be *removed*, making compliance posture a moat-or-death variable.
**Global investing-app → super-app analogue:** Robinhood — launched **Gold Card in March 2024** (>700K cardholders, >$10B annualised spend by 2026) and a Platinum card in Mar 2026, explicitly framed as becoming a "financial super-app". Midas's move is the same playbook ~2 years later; Revolut/Nubank/eToro are directional comparators. Revolut is also entering Turkey directly via the Fups acquisition talks ([[Revolut in talks to acquire Turkish neobank Fups]]) — so Midas is racing a global super-app onto its home turf.
**Position:** catching up / parity-seeking, not pioneering. The investing-to-spending bundle is a proven global pattern; Midas is localizing it.

## [2] Company history / fit
Trajectory: founded 2020; single-app access to Borsa Istanbul equities, US stocks, mutual funds and crypto; first commission-free platform in Turkey; **$80M Series B Aug 2025** (led by QED, with IFC, QuantumLight, returning Spark Capital/Portage/Bek), Turkey's largest-ever fintech round, valuation reported near $1B, ~3.5M customers then / ~4M by mid-2026 ([[Midas raises $80 million Series B, Turkey's largest fintech round]]). Derivatives/US options launch flagged Sep 2025.
- **Why Midas acts this way (analysis):** a pure brokerage in Turkey earns a thin, transaction-dependent take rate that swings with trading volume; to justify a ~$1B valuation it needs durable, recurring engagement and a bigger share of wallet. Holding spendable balances + a card is the standard route from "trading app" to "primary financial account" — it converts episodic traders into daily users and creates float/interchange economics. The 2025 capital ("security infrastructure to international standards") plausibly pre-funded exactly this compliance build.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Novelty: low.** Wallet+card-on-top-of-brokerage is not new globally (Robinhood, Revolut). What is *locally* meaningful is doing it natively from a Turkish-equity/US-equity/crypto investing base under a CBRT licence.
- **Traction: effectively zero today** — application pending, no wallet/card live, no payments volume, no merchant/interchange numbers. All upside is prospective.
- **Who captures the margin (analysis):** value here is interchange + float on held balances + retained AUM. BUT the card rails run on Mastercard/Visa, so Midas takes the issuer slice, not the network economics; and prepaid-card interchange in Turkey is regulator-capped. The real prize is *retention/AUM* (keeping investing balances and cash in-app) rather than fat payments margin. (hypothesis) If the wallet mainly recycles money users would have invested anyway, the incremental revenue is modest; the value is defensive (stickiness) more than a new profit pool.
- **What breaks it:** licence denial/delay; Turkey's EMI crackdown sweeping in new entrants; lira/inflation volatility hitting the very balances the card is meant to spend.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Eraslan frames wallet+card as part of a 2026 roadmap (also advanced trading tools, expanded market access). Next gate is **CBRT approval** (Law No. 6493: customer-fund protection, capital adequacy — EMI minimum capital cited around TRY 55M but verify against current regulation, qualified-shareholder tests, operational/data security). Sentiment is constructive but the credible base case is a multi-quarter regulatory wait with a non-trivial denial/conditions risk given 2025–26 enforcement.
- **Why the market goes this way (analysis):** Turkey's structural inflation (peaked >75% in 2024, still elevated; lira ~48–55 USD/TRY) pushes households into US-equity and crypto hedges via apps like Midas, and into spending balances fast before they devalue — which paradoxically makes a *spend-from-balance* card attractive (users want to deploy/spend, not park lira). High fintech/crypto adoption (~⅓ of Turks hold crypto) is the tailwind. **Counterintuitive second-order:** the same macro that fuels Midas's investing growth (devaluation) also undermines the "hold higher balances" thesis — Turks don't want to *hold* lira; the card's appeal is spending fast, which doesn't build the float Midas wants.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Turkish e-money/payments is a sizeable, fast-cashless market: Turkey FinTech Payments ecosystem ~$15bn, monthly card-payment volume ~TRY 2.21tn in 2025, and EMI+PI transaction volume ~TRY 5tn in 2024 heading toward ~7tn (per Ken Research / Turkish Business World, as of early 2026). 86 licensed payment+e-money institutions, 6 digital banks (per the same sources). Structure: fragmented at the EMI layer (many small issuers) but value concentrates with scale players holding balances + card-issuing rails; entry barrier is now primarily **regulation+capital, not product** — you cannot issue e-money without CBRT authorisation under Law No. 6493. "Why now": (1) the regulatory regime is mid-cleanup (2025-26 enforcement sweep), so a clean new EMI licence is more valuable and harder to get; (2) structural inflation (lira ~48-55/USD) drives both retail investing (USD-equity/crypto hedging) and fast spend-from-balance behaviour — the macro that fuels Midas's brokerage growth also stresses its "hold higher balances" thesis (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: TPV/payment volume, take rate, interchange + float on held balances, MAU/transactions per user; for the brokerage base, AUM and commission take rate. Basis of competition = distribution + balance/float capture, not price (interchange is regulator-capped). Key players & recent moves: **Papara** (~23M users, ~$2bn valuation per PitchBook/Dealroom) — licence revoked Oct 2025, then **REINSTATED Jan-2026 under state trusteeship** after a court suspended the cancellation (Türkiye Today / AML Intelligence) — i.e. it is operationally back, a sharper competitor than the bare "revoked" read; **Param** — Turkey's first licensed EMI, raised $78M Series B Apr-2025 at ~$875M (Tracxn/press); **Tosla** (Akbank/AkÖde); **iyzico/Ödeal** (open-banking authorised); plus the **Trendyol–Baykar–ADQ–Ant** super-app MoU ([[Trendyol, Baykar, ADQ, Ant International sign fintech MoU]]) and **Kaspi.kz** expanding into Turkey ([[Tencent invests in Kazakhstan fintech Kaspi.kz]]). Midas's position: **catching up / parity-seeking** — investing→spending is a globally proven pattern (Robinhood Gold Card 2024) being localised ~2 yrs late. Moat (analysis): switching costs from in-app AUM + a clean post-2025-crackdown compliance posture, not network effects.

**Comps & multiples.** No EV/Revenue can be computed for Midas — it is private, no disclosed revenue (multiple = no data). Internal/round comps:
- [[Midas raises $80 million Series B, Turkey's largest fintech round]] — $80M Series B Aug-2025 (QED-led), valuation **~$1bn**, total funding >$140M (Series A $45M), ~3.5M users. Per-user implied value ≈ $1.0bn / 3.5M ≈ **~$285/user (round valuation, not market cap)**.
- **Param** (closest domestic EMI comp): ~$875M at $78M Series B — confirms a ~$0.9–1.0bn band for Turkey's top private fintechs.
- **Robinhood (HOOD)** — public super-app analogue: market cap ~$95.3bn (22-Jun-2026), TTM revenue ~$4.61bn → EV/Revenue ≈ $95.3bn / $4.61bn ≈ **~20.7x** (gurufocus cited ~19.4x as of Feb-2026); Gold subs 4.3M (+36% YoY Q1-26). Rich multiple, justified by ~41% revenue growth (growth-multiple link) — a directional read on where a scaled investing-super-app trades, NOT a Midas comp.
- Distribution not computed (only 2 truly comparable private figures, Midas + Param); qualitative read: Midas's ~$1bn round valuation is **in-line** with Param's ~$875M for top Turkish fintechs, neither rich nor cheap on the (sparse) evidence.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Regulatory timeline/denial (highest).** Licence is PENDING; the 2025-26 CBRT/BRSA crackdown (Papara revoked then reinstated, Ininal/PayFix suspended, Ozan seized) means approval is slow and scrutiny-heavy — second-order: a multi-quarter wait or conditional grant delays the whole 2026 payments revenue line and can re-rate the super-app thesis.
2. **Margin captured by another stack layer.** Card rails are Mastercard/Visa; prepaid interchange is regulator-capped in Turkey — so the new "payments revenue" is thin issuer/float economics, not a fat new pool. Real value is defensive (AUM/retention), meaning the upside narrative may be over-stated vs realised P&L (analysis).
3. **Stronger-than-assumed competition + macro contradiction.** Papara is back operational (~23M users) and Revolut/Kaspi are entering — Midas is no longer racing a wounded incumbent; and lira devaluation pushes users to spend-fast/hold USD-assets, undermining the "park higher balances / build float" pitch the card depends on.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is a strategic signal, not a re-rating — Turkey's leading retail-investing app buying a regulated payments rail to convert episodic traders into daily primary-account users; it pulls the brokerage layer toward the super-app battle (Papara, Param, Trendyol-Ant, incoming Kaspi/Revolut). Falsifiable thesis: if the CBRT grant lands and Midas ships a live card with disclosed float/interchange revenue within ~12 months, it re-rates from "trading app" toward "primary financial account" and supports/extends the ~$1bn valuation. What breaks it: licence denial or onerous conditions, or a launch that merely recycles balances users would have invested anyway (no incremental revenue, only stickiness). Trigger to watch: the CBRT decision and any first card-spend/interchange disclosure.
