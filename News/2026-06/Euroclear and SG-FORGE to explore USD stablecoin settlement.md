Euroclear and SG-FORGE to explore USD stablecoin settlement
Claudia Pincovski
29 Jun 2026 / 4 Min Read
Euroclear and Societe Generale-FORGE (SG-FORGE), the digital asset subsidiary of France-based Societe Generale group, have announced a collaboration to explore how regulated digital cash could support the issuance and settlement of short-term funding instruments denominated in USD.
The initiative centres on assessing the potential use of USD CoinVertible, a stablecoin issued by SG-FORGE and compliant with the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), to settle tokenised USD-denominated Negotiable European Commercial Paper (NEU CP). NEU CP is a short-term financing instrument widely used across European markets.
Bridging the gap in multi-currency DLT settlement
The collaboration is positioned against the backdrop of broader modernisation efforts in European short-term debt markets. Project Pythagore, an existing market initiative, aims to migrate EUR-denominated NEU CP onto distributed ledger technology (DLT) with settlement in central bank money. However, because the NEU CP market operates across multiple currencies, that framework does not extend to non-euro transactions, a gap the Euroclear–SG-FORGE collaboration seeks to address for USD-denominated instruments.
By introducing a MiCA-regulated stablecoin as a potential settlement asset for USD transactions, the two organisations aim to maintain the safety, resilience, and transparency standards expected of financial market infrastructure while supporting liquidity conditions across the market. The collaboration will assess whether this model can function alongside, rather than separately from, the central bank money settlement approach being developed for euro instruments.
A company official at Euroclear noted that tokenising NEU CP could create more efficient funding conditions for issuers and contribute to overall market liquidity, framing the work as part of a broader evolution of the NEU CP market toward DLT infrastructure. SG-FORGE's chief executive, in turn, pointed to the role of MiCA-compliant digital cash solutions in addressing cross-currency settlement within existing regulatory and market standards.
The collaboration reflects a wider pattern in European financial market infrastructure, where the arrival of MiCA has given regulated stablecoins a clearer legal status, opening the door to their use in institutional settlement contexts. For the NEU CP market specifically, which has historically relied on conventional cash settlement mechanisms, the assessment marks a practical step toward evaluating whether tokenised money instruments can meet institutional requirements at scale.
No timeline for the assessment's completion or any subsequent implementation phase has been disclosed.

_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On **25 June 2026** (Euroclear's own press release date; The Paypers re-reported it 29 Jun) Euroclear and **SG-FORGE** (Societe Generale's digital-asset arm) announced a **collaboration to "explore"** whether SG-FORGE's MiCA-compliant **USD CoinVertible (USDCV)** stablecoin can settle **tokenised, USD-denominated Negotiable European Commercial Paper (NEU CP)** — a short-term funding instrument. This is an **assessment/feasibility study, not a live product**: "no timeline for the assessment's completion or any subsequent implementation phase has been disclosed." Classic "to explore / aims to" framing — announced, not launched, no volumes, no clients.

**+ Why structured this way:** The honest hook is the **currency gap**, not the stablecoin. **Project Pythagore** (the existing market initiative to move *EUR* NEU CP onto DLT) settles in **central-bank money**, the gold standard for safety. But NEU CP is multi-currency, and CeBM is **EUR-only** — there is no USD central-bank money rail in Europe. So for USD legs, the choice is commercial-bank money or a **regulated stablecoin**. SG-FORGE's USDCV is positioned as the "next-best to CeBM" USD settlement asset. The framing "alongside, rather than separately from" the CeBM-for-EUR approach is the tell: this is a **patch for the non-euro corner** of a EUR-first DLT design, not a wholesale stablecoin endorsement.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Deutsche Borse / Clearstream + SG-FORGE** ([[Deutsche Borse integrates SocGen CoinVertible stablecoins]], 2025-11-21): the SAME issuer (SG-FORGE), the SAME stablecoins (EUR+USD CoinVertible), for **securities settlement, collateral and treasury** at Clearstream — Euroclear's direct CSD rival. Euroclear is here **catching up to its arch-rival** on the same rail.
- **Deutsche Borse + Circle** ([[Deutsche Börse and Circle collaborate on stablecoins in Europe]], 2025-10-01): USDC/EURC for European post-trade — Circle is the obvious USDC alternative to USDCV.
- **Clearstream tokenised securities platform** ([[Clearstream launches tokenized securities platform]], 2025-11-07) and **DTCC** (July-2026 pilot, Oct-2026 launch) — the broader tokenised-securities land grab the three giant CSDs (DTCC/Euroclear/Clearstream) are running, having co-authored a 2026 interoperability white paper.
- **ClearToken CT Settle** ([[ClearToken launches CT Settle multi-currency settlement solution]], 2025-12-15) — regulated multi-currency stablecoin settlement on Canton, the same multi-currency-PvP problem from the challenger side.

**+ Why the lay of the land:** SG-FORGE has become the **default European bank-issued stablecoin counterparty** (Clearstream, now Euroclear, plus MetaMask distribution). Its moat is **MiCA EMI licence + bank credibility**, which is exactly what an FMI needs to touch a stablecoin. The second-order point: Euroclear and Clearstream are **both** routing through the same issuer, so USDCV's credibility, not either CSD, is the shared dependency.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **EURCV**: launched Apr-2023 (first institutional stablecoin on a public chain), restructured Jul-2024 to full MiCA EMT/free-transfer; deployed across Ethereum, Solana, XRPL, Stellar, Optimism ([[Societe Generale-FORGE launches EUR stablecoin on XRP Ledger]]). Market cap modest (~tens of millions of EUR; one source cites a higher figure — **discrepancy, treat with caution**).
- **USDCV**: launched **June 2025** — SocGen the first global bank with a USD stablecoin; **BNY as reserve custodian**; brought into **MetaMask** via Consensys, Apr-2026 ([[Societe Generale brings USDCV stablecoin to MetaMask]]).
- **Euroclear**: ~EUR 40T+ assets under custody; pushing DLT (Pythagore, D-FMI, the DTCC/Clearstream interoperability paper).

**+ Why they act this way:** Euroclear's structural pressure is **defensive** — Clearstream already signed SG-FORGE for the same job seven months earlier, and DTCC's tokenised platform lands in 2026. A CSD's value is **network/incumbency**; if settlement migrates to DLT+stablecoins and Euroclear isn't on the rail, it risks disintermediation by tokenisation-native venues. SG-FORGE's pressure is **demand-side**: a stablecoin is only worth its bank backing if it gets **institutional settlement use-cases** (vs sitting in wallets), so FMI deals are its real distribution.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Genuinely new element:** using a **MiCA stablecoin to plug the USD gap in a CeBM-anchored EUR DLT market (NEU CP)** — a specific, regulator-friendly use-case rather than generic "stablecoin for settlement." That said, **the building blocks all pre-exist**: USDCV (Jun-2025), SG-FORGE↔CSD settlement (Clearstream, Nov-2025). So novelty is **incremental** (new counterparty Euroclear + new instrument NEU CP), not a breakthrough.
**Traction: effectively zero** — no pilot live, no participants named, no notional, no date. This is a feasibility announcement.

**+ Who captures the margin:** If this scales, the value accrues to **SG-FORGE** (float income on USDCV reserves + becoming the standard European USD-settlement asset) and to **Euroclear** (defends its post-trade fees by moving them on-chain). USDCV reserves at BNY earn the carry — the stablecoin issuer, not the CSD, holds the new economic rent. The risk that breaks it: a **US-regulated USD stablecoin (USDC, or a GENIUS-Act bank coin)** or eventual **wholesale CBDC / tokenised CeBM** could displace a EUR-bank's USD coin for USD legs.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Next step is an internal assessment (no date). Backdrop is strongly favourable: **MiCA** gives regulated stablecoins clear legal status for institutional settlement; **Citi (Jun-2026)** projects tokenised securities at ~$5.5T by 2030 with a ~$1.9T stablecoin float; DTCC pilots its tokenisation platform Jul-2026. Risks: (a) regulators may still prefer **CeBM/tokenised-deposits** over stablecoins for systemic settlement; (b) **liquidity fragmentation** across competing stablecoins/chains — the very problem the DTCC/Euroclear/Clearstream interoperability paper flags; (c) USDCV's thin circulating supply means **settlement-scale liquidity is unproven**.

**+ Second-order:** The counterintuitive read is that "explore" announcements like this are **option-buying** by incumbents — cheap signalling to stay relevant while the real settlement standard (stablecoin vs tokenised deposit vs CBDC) is undecided. Weight comes only when one converts to a **named, dated pilot with notional**.

_Red-team questions (second-order). Each answered from sources or marked open._

1. **Is this live or just "explore"?** Explore/assessment only. No pilot, no notional, no participants, no date. (source: Euroclear PR, The Paypers — "no timeline disclosed").
2. **Is it actually new vs Clearstream?** Largely incremental: Clearstream (Deutsche Borse) signed the SAME SG-FORGE CoinVertible coins for securities settlement/collateral in **Nov 2025** — seven months earlier. New here = Euroclear as counterparty + NEU CP as instrument. [[Deutsche Borse integrates SocGen CoinVertible stablecoins]]
3. **Why a stablecoin and not central-bank money?** Because Project Pythagore's CeBM rail is **EUR-only**; there's no USD CeBM in Europe, so USD legs need a commercial alternative. The stablecoin fills the **non-euro gap**, it is not preferred over CeBM. (source: note text)
4. **What is USDCV's actual circulating supply / settlement-scale liquidity?** Open — likely thin (USDCV launched Jun-2025, retail/MetaMask distribution). Settlement at institutional notional is unproven. Sources conflict on CoinVertible market cap (€80m vs ~$452m) — treat as uncertain.
5. **Who earns the economics?** SG-FORGE captures the **reserve carry** (USDCV reserves custodied at BNY) and the "standard USD settlement asset" position; Euroclear defends post-trade fees. The issuer holds the new rent, not the CSD. (analysis)
6. **Why not USDC (Circle)?** Open — Circle already partners Deutsche Borse for European post-trade. A bank-issued, MiCA-EMT, ACPR-licensed coin may be more palatable to a French/EU FMI, but USDC has vastly deeper liquidity. Choice of USDCV may favour relationship over depth. [[Deutsche Börse and Circle collaborate on stablecoins in Europe]]
7. **How big / liquid is the NEU CP market and the USD slice?** Open — NEU CP is a large French/European short-term debt market, but the USD-denominated, tokenised slice that this would address is unquantified.
8. **Does the regulator actually want stablecoins for systemic settlement?** Contested. ECB/central banks lean toward CeBM/tokenised deposits for systemic safety; MiCA legalises stablecoins but doesn't make them the preferred FMI settlement asset. Regulatory acceptance for *scale* is open.
9. **What breaks the model?** A US-regulated USD coin (USDC / GENIUS-Act bank coin) or future tokenised CeBM/wholesale CBDC could displace a EUR-bank's USD stablecoin for USD legs. (analysis)
10. **Is this defensive positioning by Euroclear?** Strongly appears so — Clearstream beat it to SG-FORGE, DTCC launches its tokenisation platform in 2026. "Explore" = cheap optionality to stay on the rail. (analysis)
11. **Fraud / failure-mode liability silent?** Yes — nothing on what happens if USDCV de-pegs or BNY-custodied reserves are impaired during settlement. Open.
12. **Atomic DvP or just cash leg?** Open — note describes USDCV as the cash/settlement asset for tokenised NEU CP, but doesn't confirm atomic delivery-vs-payment on a single ledger.
13. **Does it interoperate with Pythagore's EUR-CeBM rail?** Stated aim is to function "alongside, rather than separately from" the CeBM approach, but mechanism unspecified — cross-rail interoperability is the same problem DTCC/Euroclear/Clearstream flagged in their 2026 white paper. Open.
14. **What converts this from announcement to weight?** A named, dated pilot with a notional figure and a real issuer/dealer participant. Until then it's signalling.
15. **Date discrepancy?** Euroclear PR dated **25 Jun 2026**; the captured Paypers item says "29 Jun 2026." Original event = 25 Jun; note retells it 29 Jun. Not a duplicate of any existing note.

**Importance: 3/5** — A specific, regulator-coherent use-case (MiCA stablecoin plugging the USD gap in a CeBM-anchored EUR DLT market) from two heavyweight names, riding a real structural wave (tokenised securities, MiCA). But it is **explore-stage, no traction, no novelty in the building blocks** (USDCV and SG-FORGE↔CSD settlement both pre-exist; Clearstream did the near-identical deal in Nov 2025). Fresh, not a duplicate, but weight is capped by "announced, not launched."

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: tokenized securities settlement / regulated stablecoins as settlement cash in regulated capital-markets infrastructure (FMI). The protagonist is an (I)CSD (Euroclear, post-trade settlement) pairing with a MiCA-licensed stablecoin issuer (SG-FORGE) to settle tokenized USD NEU CP. Sector signals: tokenized RWAs (ex-stablecoins) ~$28.9bn at May 2026, a record, with tokenized Treasuries ~$16.1bn the largest sub-segment; total stablecoin cap ~$320bn (per CoinDesk Research / RWA.xyz, via coindesk.com, May 2026). Long-run TAM figures cited by banks (McKinsey ~$2tn by 2030; BCG ~$16tn; StanChart ~$30tn by 2034) are projection ranges, not realized volumes — treat as scenario, not size (analysis). Structure: post-trade is highly consolidated/oligopolistic — a handful of (I)CSDs (Euroclear, Clearstream, DTC) sit on the rails; barriers are regulatory licensing, network effects and integration into existing market plumbing, not technology. "Why now": MiCA gave EUR/USD stablecoins a clear legal status in the EU, and the EUR leg of NEU CP is already being moved to DLT (central-bank-money settlement) under Project Pythagore — but Pythagore is EUR-only and central-bank money, leaving a structural gap for non-euro (USD) legs that a regulated stablecoin can fill (per Euroclear press, via euroclear.com / ledgerinsights.com, 25 Jun 2026).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: settled value / on-chain AUM, stablecoin tokens-in-circulation, and breadth of FMI integrations (not classic take-rate — settlement infra monetizes on fees per settled instruction, undisclosed here). This is an *exploration/assessment*, not live volume: no timeline, no size, no economics disclosed (de-PR flag). Players & basis of competition: (a) incumbent CSDs racing to DLT — [[Clearstream launches tokenized securities platform]] (Nov 2025) and [[Deutsche Borse integrates SocGen CoinVertible stablecoins]] (Nov 2025, where SG-FORGE already supplies CoinVertible to Clearstream for collateral/settlement); (b) US side — [[DTCC selects Canton Network for Treasury tokenization]] and [[DTCC subsidiary receives SEC no-action letter for tokenized securities]] (Dec 2025); (c) new infra entrants — [[LayerZero builds Zero blockchain with Citadel, DTCC and ICE]] (Feb 2026), [[ClearToken launches CT Settle multi-currency settlement solution]] (Dec 2025). Basis of competition is distribution/regulatory standing and which "cash leg" wins (central-bank money vs regulated stablecoin), not price. Protagonist's position: Euroclear is an incumbent CSD playing catch-up to Clearstream/DTCC on live tokenized rails but with a strong moat (FMI licensing, network effects, embedded market position) (analysis). Notably, SG-FORGE is now supplying the *same* USD CoinVertible into competing venues (Clearstream/Deutsche Börse) — it is a neutral arms-dealer, not exclusive to Euroclear.

**Comps & multiples.** This is a partnership/exploration with no round, valuation or disclosed economics, so trading multiples are **not computable here** — no data. Reference points only: Euroclear is a privately/cooperatively held (I)CSD — not listed, no public market cap (no data). SG-FORGE is a subsidiary of Société Générale (public, ticker GLE.PA) — segment-level financials for FORGE are not separately disclosed (no data); group-level multiples are not a meaningful comp for this initiative. Settlement-asset comps (stablecoin scale, as a proxy for adoption, NOT a valuation multiple): USD CoinVertible ~26.3m tokens in circulation (per The Block / SG-FORGE, as of 15 Apr 2026) vs USDC ~$75.1bn and USDT ~$186.6bn (per CoinDesk, Jan 2026). Arithmetic of relative scale: $75.1bn / 26.3m ≈ ~2,850x — i.e. CoinVertible is sub-scale by ~3+ orders of magnitude vs USDC, so liquidity/depth is the binding constraint, not regulatory status. Listed pure-play stablecoin comp = Circle (CRCL); EV/Revenue or P/E not pulled here → `[UNSOURCED]`. Distribution not computed — qualitative only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **De-PR / vaporware risk:** announced "exploration/assessment" with explicitly **no timeline and no implementation phase disclosed** — many such CSD-DLT pilots have not converted to live, scaled settlement; second-order: easy to over-read as adoption.
2. **Settlement-asset liquidity / sub-scale:** USD CoinVertible (~26.3m tokens) is orders of magnitude smaller than USDC/USDT; thin secondary liquidity undermines its usefulness as a USD money-market settlement asset and could force reliance on the issuer's own balance sheet, reintroducing the counterparty/credit risk that DvP-in-cash is meant to remove.
3. **Cash-leg standard fragmentation / disintermediation:** the EUR leg settles in *central-bank money* (Pythagore) while the USD leg would settle in a *commercial stablecoin* — a two-tier safety standard; if regulators or a future digital-USD (or a deeper rival like USDC) is preferred for the dollar leg, SG-FORGE's CoinVertible could be displaced as the settlement rail, capturing the economics elsewhere.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Marginal, not re-rating: it extends the "regulated stablecoin as settlement cash inside an FMI" thesis from collateral (Clearstream/Deutsche Börse) to short-term funding (NEU CP), and stakes a USD-leg position that Pythagore's central-bank-money model can't reach (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if CoinVertible becomes the default USD settlement asset for tokenized NEU CP, USD CoinVertible circulation should climb materially above ~26m tokens within ~12 months. Trigger/what breaks it: watch for a named go-live date and first settled USD NEU CP; absence of either by end-2026 — or selection of central-bank-money/USDC for the USD leg — falsifies it. (analysis)
