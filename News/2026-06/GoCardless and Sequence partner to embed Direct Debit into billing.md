GoCardless and Sequence partner to embed Direct Debit into billing workflows
Paula Albu
25 Jun 2026 / 5 Min Read
GoCardless has partnered with Sequence to deliver a native Direct Debit integration within the Sequence billing platform for growing businesses.
UK-based GoCardless has announced a partnership with Sequence, an AI billing and quote-to-cash platform, to embed Direct Debit payment collection directly into Sequence's billing engine. The integration allows businesses to automate payment collection across one-off invoices and recurring billing schedules without switching between separate tools.
When a company sends a one-off invoice or sets up a recurring subscription through Sequence, customers can authorise Direct Debit payments through the Sequence portal in a one-time setup. All subsequent payments are then collected automatically, removing the need for manual chasing or separate payment collection workflows.
Operational benefits and cash flow visibility
The integration addresses a specific operational pain point for growing businesses: the friction of managing billing and payment collection across disconnected systems. By connecting billing and payments within a single platform, the partnership reduces the number of tools finance teams need to manage and provides a clearer, more predictable view of cash flow. Direct Debit's high success rates as an account-to-account payment method reduce the failed payment risk associated with card-based collection.
 Tom Metcalfe , Director of Global Partnerships at GoCardless, noted that chasing late payments and managing failed card transactions represents a significant operational burden for growing businesses, and that embedding Direct Debit directly into the billing process allows merchants to collect payments automatically while offering customers a more secure and straightforward way to pay.
 Riya Grover , CEO and Co-Founder at Sequence, described billing for complex contracts as a major challenge for fast-growing teams, adding that embedding GoCardless into Sequence means businesses can now handle the full revenue workflow from signed contract to cash collected in one place, without finance teams needing to wire up separate tools once an invoice is issued.
Integration model and target market
Sequence serves businesses managing complex, contract-based billing - a segment where invoice complexity, variable payment schedules, and revenue recognition requirements make manual payment collection particularly costly and error-prone. The GoCardless integration is designed to cover the full quote-to-cash cycle within Sequence, from contract signature through to automated payment collection, without requiring businesses to adopt a separate payments platform.
No details on the number of businesses currently using Sequence or the markets in which the Direct Debit integration is initially available have been disclosed.

# Context-enrichment: GoCardless × Sequence — Direct Debit embedded into billing
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS VERDICT: fresh.** No prior note covers a GoCardless–Sequence tie-up; the corpus has GoCardless billing/A2A partnerships but not this counterparty (see [[multifi partners GoCardless to streamline small-business payments]], [[MRI Software selects GoCardless for flexible UK tenant payments]], [[GoCardless signs Clay as a customer]]). It is, however, the latest in a *repetitive* series of "embed DD into billing/repayment SaaS" announcements — low novelty, not a duplicate.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **25 Jun 2026:** GoCardless announced a **native Direct Debit integration inside Sequence**, an AI billing / quote-to-cash platform. Customers authorise DD once via the Sequence portal; subsequent one-off invoices and recurring schedules collect automatically. External sources say the connector is described as **live now** (Finextra, Financial IT). DD scheme coverage maps to GoCardless's footprint (Bacs UK / SEPA EU / ACH-pull US, 30+ countries).
- **What's actually disclosed:** nothing quantified. **No named launch customers, no TPV routed, no client count, no commercial terms, no markets-live list.** The note itself concedes "no details on the number of businesses currently using Sequence or the markets... have been disclosed." → "live" = *connector shipped*, not adoption.
- **Why framed this way:** it is a standard embedded-payments / channel-distribution release. The $130bn-TPV, 100k-business figures are **GoCardless-wide, not integration-specific** — do not attribute them to Sequence. The press anchors to the operational pain ("chasing late payments", "failed card transactions") because there is no traction number to lead with.
- **Second-order tell:** GoCardless ran a near-identical billing embed with **Intelligent Billing** (telecoms/MSPs) in the same week (Crowdfund Insider, Jun 2026). This is a *campaign* of swappable billing-SaaS connectors, not a Sequence-specific bet — which itself signals low strategic weight per deal.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Stripe** is the sharpest threat: Stripe Billing bundles native ACH/SEPA/Bacs collection in one stack for exactly the dev-first B2B-SaaS segment Sequence targets — making a separate GoCardless rail arguably redundant for many of those customers.
- **Billing-platform field Sequence competes in:** Chargebee, Maxio, Zuora (enterprise), usage-billing natives Metronome and Orb — several with their own or embedded collection.
- **GoCardless rivals on the rail side:** Stripe, Modulr, open-banking PISPs (Yapily, TrueLayer, Plaid) — see [[UK Payments Initiative launches new open banking scheme]] and [[Visa completes first A2A variable recurring payment in UK]].
- **Position:** parity / catching up, not ahead. The pattern (DD-in-billing) is well-trodden; GoCardless's edge is A2A breadth (Bacs/SEPA/30+ countries) and bank-debit economics vs cards — but it sits *behind* Sequence's UI (authorisation "through the Sequence portal"), i.e. as a commoditisable back-end rail.

## [2] Company history / fit
- GoCardless is a recurring-collection / A2A specialist: **100k+ businesses, >USD $130bn processed annually, 30+ countries** (company-wide). It has a clear strategic thesis — A2A/open banking as the card alternative — executed via [[GoCardless launches Recurring Pay by Bank on UKPI rails]], [[GoCardless delivers first VRP payment with Jellyfish Energy]] and [[GoCardless launches AI tool Same Day Settlement+]].
- **Why it acts this way:** DD is a commodity take-rate business; growth comes from *distribution* — embedding the rail at the point of invoice (highest-intent moment) inside finance software is cheaper than direct sales. Hence a stream of billing-SaaS partners (multifi, MRI, Clay, Intelligent Billing, now Sequence).
- Sequence is a small but well-backed AI-billing startup (Riya Grover / Eamon Jubbawy; ~$38–40m raised, a16z + 645 Ventures; "hundreds of finance teams", 15+ countries). Fit is genuine but asymmetric: Sequence adds a marquee payments logo; its routable volume is immaterial to a $130bn-TPV firm.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Novelty: low.** "Embed DD into a billing tool" already exists across GoCardless's own partner book and is natively bundled by Stripe. Nothing mechanically new.
- **Traction: unproven.** Zero adoption metrics. Treat as **announced capability**, not validated traction.
- **Who captures the margin:** Sequence owns the customer UX and the sticky finance-team relationship; GoCardless is the back-end rail. The durable value accrues to whoever owns the billing surface — a disintermediation risk for GoCardless if billing platforms multi-home rails or build their own.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **The material event around this note is the Mollie acquisition** of GoCardless (~€1.1bn, announced 11 Dec 2025; see IR `funding_announcement`), pending regulatory approval, expected to close mid-2026. Post-close, partnership priorities (including Sequence) sit under Mollie's "complete payment platform" (350k+ businesses). The Sequence connector is minor next to this.
- GoCardless fundamentals are improving (FY25 +22% to £160.9m; first EBITDA-positive quarter Q4 FY25) — context, not driven by this deal.
- A2A/open-banking tailwind (UKPI scheme, Recurring Pay by Bank/VRP) supports the broader DD-collection thesis, but this specific integration rides that wave rather than advancing it.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** A2A / open-banking recurring collection and the adjacent quote-to-cash / subscription-billing SaaS layer. No clean TAM disclosed in-note → no data on size for the integration; directionally, UK A2A is being institutionalised via the **UK Payments Initiative scheme, launched 3 Jun 2026** ([[UK Payments Initiative launches new open banking scheme]]) backed by Yapily, TrueLayer, GoCardless, Plaid, Revolut, Monzo. Structure: the rail layer (DD/A2A) is a commoditised take-rate business; the billing-software layer is sticky and finance-team-owned and sits *upstream* of money movement. Drivers + "why now": (1) regulatory push toward A2A as a card alternative (National Payment Vision / VRP); (2) finance-ops consolidation — buyers want contract-to-cash in one tool. Second-order: value migrates to whoever owns the billing surface, not the rail (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: TPV/payment volume, take rate, payment success/failure rate (DD's pitch), and for billing SaaS — ARR/NRR, time-to-cash. Key players: rail side — GoCardless vs **Stripe, Modulr**, PISPs (Yapily/TrueLayer/Plaid); billing side — **Stripe Billing, Chargebee, Maxio, Zuora, Metronome, Orb** (Sequence's competitors). Basis of competition: distribution + breadth of schemes (rail) and depth for complex contract billing (SaaS). Recent moves: GoCardless billing/repayment embeds with multifi (Jul 2025, [[multifi partners GoCardless to streamline small-business payments]]), MRI Software (Dec 2025, [[MRI Software selects GoCardless for flexible UK tenant payments]]), Clay (Oct 2025, [[GoCardless signs Clay as a customer]]), Intelligent Billing (Jun 2026), now Sequence (25 Jun 2026).
