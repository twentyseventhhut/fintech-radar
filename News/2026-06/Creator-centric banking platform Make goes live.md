# Context-enrichment: Creator-centric banking platform Make goes live

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
A platform calling itself **"Make"** — an *"influencer and creator-centric banking platform"* — was reported to have **"gone live"** by **FinTech Futures on 2026-06-29** (single trade-newsletter item; the underlying article returns 403 to direct fetch, so all Make-specific facts are effectively single-sourced).

De-PR'd reality:
- **Anguilla-based** (Caribbean special economic zone — an offshore financial center), **~50 staff**, hiring toward 150+.
- **No banking license** — the company itself says it is *"looking to obtain banking licenses to support international growth"* → it currently operates as an overlay, **not a deposit institution**.
- **No disclosed funding** — it is *"beginning to explore fundraising"*, which strongly implies **no institutional round raised yet**.
- **No named BaaS / sponsor bank.** No product specifics (cards, accounts, payouts, invoicing, tax tools) are confirmed by any reachable source.
- Founder cited only as a first name, **"Lazaro (Laz)"**; idea dated to 2023. Co-founders/full name **open**.

**Why this framing matters:** the press hook is the word *"live"*, but "live" with no license, no funding, no named bank partner, and no disclosed feature set is **closer to a brand/soft-launch than a regulated banking go-live**. The **Anguilla domicile** is itself a tell — it points to light-touch offshore structuring rather than a US/UK/EU-regulated product, which lowers the credibility of "banking platform" framing. The central question is not "what did Make ship" but **"is there anything here yet beyond a positioning statement in a niche that has repeatedly failed?"** — and the honest answer is: not visibly.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Creator/influencer banking is a **graveyard niche**, well-documented in our own corpus and externally:
- **Karat Financial** — founded 2019, raised ~$70M Series B (~2021); **cut ~10%+ of staff Oct 2023** in the creator-economy slowdown; survived and pivoted into creator **business banking powered by Grasshopper Bank** plus a Visa "Karat Agent" pilot (see corpus [[Visa unveils 2025 Creator Report and Karat creator-agent pilot]]). Survivor, bruised.
- **Creative Juice** (MrBeast/Justin Kan-backed, ~$20M) — **absorbed by Rho** business banking (talks Nov 2023, deal ~Jul 2024). A distressed exit, not independent success.
- **Willa** — $18M Series A (FinTech Collective, Jul 2021); **shut down its payments & banking features** and retreated.
- **Stir** (a16z, ~$100M valuation) — pivoted away from banking toward monetization tooling.
- **Lumanu / Pietra** — alive but they are **payout infra / e-commerce ops**, not consumer creator banks (see [[Visa and Lumanu partner for faster influencer payments]]).
- Incumbent/big-co moves the corpus already covers: [[Visa and TikTok launch UK Creator Card]] (Apr 2026), [[TerraPay partners with MilX to power global payouts for content creators]] (Jan 2026).

**Position of Make: well behind.** Better-funded, better-located, better-partnered peers either retreated (Willa, Creative Juice→Rho) or shrank (Karat) or are owned by Visa-rail partners. Make enters with **less capital, no license, no named rail** than the cohort that already struggled. **Why the niche keeps failing (second-order):** creator income is seasonal/platform-dependent → underwriting and lending are risky; **interchange margins are thin** and don't cover high creator **CAC**; creators already use Stripe/Wise/Mercury → a standalone "creator bank" has weak structural pull.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Creator/influencer banking is a vertical neobank niche inside the broader creator economy. Per Goldman Sachs Research, the creator economy TAM is ~$250bn today and could approach ~$480bn by 2027 (~14-20% CAGR), across ~50m global creators (per Goldman Sachs, via goldmansachs.com / TechCrunch, as of 2026). Make's own framing cites a ~665m "creators/influencers/digital entrepreneurs" universe — a far looser definition than Goldman's 50m monetising creators, so treat the addressable banking base as much smaller than the headline (analysis). Structure: highly fragmented and contested — incumbents (Novo, Mercury-style SMB neobanks), creator-specialists (Karat), and card networks moving down-stack (Visa+TikTok Creator Card, Apr 2026; Visa Creator Report + Karat agent pilot, Nov 2025). Value in the chain accrues to whoever owns issuing/BaaS rails and the underwriting data; a thin app layer captures little. "Why now": short-form-video monetisation + multi-currency cross-border payouts make creators look like micro-SMBs underserved by retail banks — the demand is real, but so is the crowding.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: funded accounts, deposit/balance per user, interchange take rate (≈0.5-1.5% on debit), payout/FX volume, and CAC-payback (creator acquisition is expensive). Key players & basis of competition: Karat (credit-card wedge → banking via Grasshopper; ~$100m raised over 5 rounds, Series A $26m led by USV 2021) competes on creator-specific underwriting (social stats as credit signal); Visa+TikTok and Visa's creator initiatives compete on distribution/network; generalist neobanks (Novo) compete on price/breadth. Make's position: late niche entrant, self-funded (~$5m injected, team ~50, based in Anguilla's SEZ), differentiating on multi-currency accounts (USD/GBP/EUR), Visa-powered local/international staff payouts across 27+ currencies, and revenue-splitting for collaborators. Moat: weak/unproven (analysis) — no proprietary underwriting data moat like Karat, no platform distribution like Visa/TikTok; differentiation is feature-level (FX + revenue-split), which is copyable.

**Comps & multiples.** No valuation/round/metrics disclosed for Make (self-funded, ~$5m total injected — a round-equivalent, not a market cap), so multiples = no data; comparison is qualitative. Internal comps: [[Visa and TikTok launch UK Creator Card]] (Apr 2026, network+platform distribution), [[Visa unveils 2025 Creator Report and Karat creator-agent pilot]] (Nov 2025, incumbent + specialist), [[Upward raises Seed+ round and partners with Mastercard]] (Nov 2025, embedded card/banking infra). External reference points: Karat ~$100m raised cumulatively / Series A $26m (per TechCrunch, 2021); Creative Juice (creator neobank, ~$20m raised) did NOT survive standalone — acquired by Rho in Jul 2024 (per Crunchbase/PitchBook/LinkedIn), a precedent that the pure creator-banking model struggles to reach standalone scale. Make at ~$5m self-funded is sub-scale vs both. [UNSOURCED]: Make's user count, deposits, FX volume, take rate, CAC.

**Risk flags.**
1. Thin-margin niche-neobank economics — debit interchange (~0.5-1.5%) plus FX spread on a small, hard-to-acquire creator base rarely covers creator-marketing CAC; at ~$5m self-funded with 50 staff, burn-vs-unit-economics is the core question (the Creative Juice→Rho 2024 precedent shows standalone creator banking failing to reach escape velocity).
2. BaaS / sponsor-bank dependency — "Powered by Visa" plus an unnamed sponsor bank means fraud liability, compliance, and the actual deposit licence sit with third parties; the note is silent on the sponsor and on the Anguilla SEZ regulatory/deposit-protection posture, a second-order risk if a rail or sponsor is pulled (cf. the BaaS sponsor-scramble theme).
3. Disintermediation by networks/platforms — Visa+TikTok and Visa's creator pushes can embed banking-grade tools directly at the distribution layer, squeezing thin independent apps; Make's copyable FX/revenue-split features offer little defence (churn risk if a platform bundles equivalents).

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) Marginal: a self-funded late entrant validates that creator banking is still seen as a white space, but the signal from Karat (raising/pivoting) and Creative Juice (absorbed by Rho) is that the durable winners are either data-moat underwriters or network/platform incumbents, not thin app-layer neobanks. Falsifiable thesis: Make does not reach standalone scale and either raises external capital, gets acquired (Rho-style), or quietly narrows — watch for a funding round, disclosed user/deposit numbers, or a sponsor-bank/SEZ regulatory disclosure as the trigger that would confirm or break this.
