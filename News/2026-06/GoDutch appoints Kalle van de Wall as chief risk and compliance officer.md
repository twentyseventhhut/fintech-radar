# GoDutch appoints Kalle van de Wall as new chief risk and compliance officer

Kalle van de Wall previously led risk and compliance for embedded payments provider Modulr and served as chief compliance officer for Brex.

Business banking start-up GoDutch has appointed Kalle van de Wall as the company's new chief risk and compliance officer, effective immediately. He will work under co-founders Matias Tesolin and Thomas Vles, who confirmed the new board member addition via a statement posted to LinkedIn this week.

In his statement, Vles says GoDutch is currently experiencing "rapid growth", with van de Wall to "play a key role in building the foundations for the next chapter". The company closed €3.6 million in a seed funding round led by Luxembourg Finance House in December, when Vles reported 12,500 active business users, 40% month-on-month growth on average, and €1.5 billion in transactions processed annually.

A former criminal investigator for the Dutch national police force, van de Wall has a background that is heavily rooted in managing European market entry and expansion for global companies that process billions in annual transactions.

Related:Former HMRC chief Sir Jon Thompson named Vocalink chair

He previously led risk and compliance for embedded payments provider Modulr, where he helped the firm secure an electronic money institution (EMI) licence from the Dutch central bank in 2022.

Van de Wall then moved to expense management platform Brex, serving first as chief compliance officer before ascending to head of compliance and risk management for Europe at the end of 2023. In this most recent position, van de Wall worked to secure the regulatory approvals necessary to expand the American fintech giant's services to Europe.

A brief job description by the company states that van de Wall has been hired in this latest role to "lead GoDutch’s EMI licensing and build robust, scalable risk and compliance frameworks to enable safe growth across Europe".

The company's attempts to scale operations across the Eurozone have so far included the rollout of international IBANs in Belgium in April.

The Compliance Jobs Report this week has two new hires at Takeda, plus more hiring news at BAE, John Deere, S&P Mobility, and the Supreme Court of Ohio, and elsewhere. Promotions to note at AbbVie, Bilfinger, Global Fund, and more; job leads are in pharmaceuticals, data centers, and aerospace (no, not SpaceX).  
 We gather the Jobs Report news items from LinkedIn, press releases, and wherever else we can find tips — and thank you to everyone who sends them in, you make the Jobs Report possible!  If you have any gossip to share (even confidentially) email me at [email protected] or find me on LinkedIn . 
Compliance Jobs
 This week we begin with several appointments at Takeda Pharmaceuticals. First, the company has hired Zoe Philippides as chief ethics and compliance officer. Philippides previously worked at Moderna for four years as chief ethics, compliance, and privacy officer. Moderna and Takeda’s offices are three blocks apart in Cambridge, Mass., so one assumes Philippides’ commute will not change much. 
 Second, Amy Bentsen has joined the company as head of ethics and compliance investigations for the Americas region. Bentsen previously worked as a partner at law firm McDermott Will & Schulte , and at King & Spaulding for a decade before that.  
 Umid Yakubov has a new job at John Deere as global director of investigations and hotline operations. Yakubov comes to the farm equipment manufacturer after 17 years at General Motors , most recently as senior counsel for global compliance and investigations.  
 GoDutch , a business banking and payments firm that quite naturally is based in Amsterdam, has hired Kalle van de Wall as chief risk and compliance officer. Wall joins the firm after two years as head of compliance and risk management for Brex , another fintech firm. 
 BAE Systems has hired Andrew Daniels as head of export control. Daniels comes to the British defense contractor after six years at Astellas Pharma , where he most recently had been chief of staff to the general counsel (and had been VP of ethics and compliance for a few years before that).  
 We have an update on Lisa Bowers , who rather cryptically announced several weeks ago that she had a new job lined up. She’s now lead compliance analyst at S&P Mobility. Bowers had previously been a senior manager in compliance and ethics at Fannie Mae , until she and her colleagues were all purged by Trump Administration loyalists trying to dig up dirt on President Trump’s political opponents. Glad to see the Fannie Mae diaspora, who were treated terribly, are starting to find their footing again. 
 In Ireland, Robert Whyte has joined Parexcel International , which does contract clinical research around the world, as EMEA compliance officer. Whyte previously worked at Illumina for three years and most recently had been EMEA compliance officer and global head of channel partner compliance.  
 Sunflower Bank has hired Sid Slonaker as a level II compliance analyst . Slonaker comes to the Dallas-based bank from First Western Trust , where he had been a senior mortgage compliance analyst since 2021. 
 Spanish medical equipment manufacturer Werfen has hired Ekaterina Egorova as compliance officer for global governance and engagement. Egorova comes to Werfen after six years in various roles at Novartis , most recently as global director of ethics, risk, and compliance operations. 
 Nearwater Capital , a specialty financing business in New York, has hired Rachel Gardezi as director of enterprise risk management and controls assurance. Gardezi previously worked at Tradeweb as global head of enterprise risk management. 
 Marc Shriver has joined Storage Asset Management , a provider of management services to self-storage businesses, as vice president of risk and compliance. Shriver previously worked at Northmarq , a real estate firm in Minneapolis, as chief compliance officer since 2024. 
 Here’s one we don’t see too often: the Supreme Court of Ohio has created a chief compliance officer role, and appointed Hilary Burgess-Jackson to fill it . Burgess-Jackson previously worked at Nationwide Children’s Hospital in Columbus, Ohio, as a senior compliance associate. (Radical Compliance has never heard of courts appointing chief compliance officers before. Does your state do this? Email us at [email protected] with tips!) 
 And President Trump has nominated Brian Johnson to be director of the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau . Technically this is a Jobs Report item because Johnson is vice president of U.S. card compliance at Capital One ; but Johnson has only held that job for two years. Prior to that he hung out as a managing director at Patomak Global Partners , the consulting firm run by Paul Atkins , now head of the Securities and Exchange Commission . Johnson also worked at the CFPB in the Trump 1.0 Administration. 
Moving Up the Ladder
 Well here’s a power grab: The Pedernales Electric Cooperative , based in Johnson City, Texas, has promoted its chief compliance officer to be chief executive officer . Christian Powell has been chief compliance officer at the electric utility since 2024, and held several compliance and regulatory affairs roles at PEC before that. Compliance officers seizing the means of production — I like it! 
 AbbVie has promoted Mariette Culter from manager of enterprise third-party risk to associate director of enterprise supplier risk management. 
 Bilfinger has promoted Tobias Krämer from compliance officer for Bilfinger’s rotating equipment division to director of group compliance governance and anti-fraud operations.  
 Daniel Petrescu has a new role at the Global Fund , moving from head of audit and advisory to head of strategic advice, insights, and prevention.  
 Daniel Agra has been promoted at Vtex , from global risk and compliance manager to senior global compliance manager.  
 TD Bank has promoted Lauren Henry , from senior AML financial crimes risk investigator to manager of AML financial crimes investigations. 
 Greek financial services firm Quant has promoted Christos Lygeros from compliance head to compliance director. 
 Raymond Burgin has been promoted to compliance manager at the U.K. Chartered Institute of Export & International Trade , a nonprofit that fosters global trade. Burgin had previously been “responsible officer” for academic quality and compliance.  
 Figure , a fintech doing home mortgage lending on the blockchain, has promoted Jalyn Williard from senior fulfillment associate to financial crimes investigator. 
 And Kroo Bank in Britain has promoted Muhammad Asghar from detection analyst to senior financial crimes analyst. 
Vendors & Service Providers
 Remember Sean O’Rielly , chief compliance officer at Humana until the end of last year? He just turned up at FTI Consulting as senior managing director in the firm’s healthcare practice. 
 Tina Seale has joined Berkeley Research Group as a senior managing consultant on export controls and U.S. government compliance. Seale previously worked at NASA , TE Connectivity , and elsewhere as a senior compliance executive handling export control and compliance issues. 
Open Req Orders
 Vertex Pharmaceuticals is looking for a director of business partner compliance . Job is based in Boston, with a salary range of $200,000 to $300,000.  
 Honeywell Aerospace is looking for a senior export compliance officer . Job is based in Minneapolis, with a salary range of $88,000 to $133,000. (Honeywell Aerospace is actually hiring for multiple export control and trade compliance staffers , both in the United States and around the world.)  
 NScale , a British provider of data centers and similar AI services, is seeking for a vice president of ethics and compliance for its U.S. operations . Job is based in New York, with a salary range of $240,000 to $290,000.
