ВТБ стал залогодержателем крупного пакета Ozon, пишет РБК со ссылкой на источник на финансовом рынке и еще одного собеседника, близкого к сделке. По словам одного из источников, банк в результате серии финансовых сделок «получает в залог крупный пакет акций Ozon». Ранее, 30 июня, ТАСС сообщил, что совладелец Ozon АФК «Система» в ближайшее время заключит финансовую сделку более чем на 300 млрд рублей с группой инвесторов и при участии ВТБ. По данным агентства, это будет структурная инвестиционная сделка. Средства, полученные от нее, корпорация может направить на расчеты по кредитам. «Сделка подписана. Она позволит группе погасить банковский долг, существенно снизить долговую нагрузку компании и кардинально улучшить кредитный профиль», — подтвердил агентству пресс-секретарь АФК Иван Макушок.
Как уточняет Forbes , на 1 марта 31,8% акций Ozon принадлежали холдингу АФК «Система», бенефициаром которого выступает миллиардер Владимир Евтушенков (№94 в рейтинге 155 миллиардеров России — 2026 Forbes, состояние — $1,6 млрд).
Согласно данным на сайте раскрытия корпоративной информации, АФК «Система» выдала в пользу бенефициара независимую гарантию, по которой компания будет обязана выплачивать по его требованию деньги «в рамках обеспеченных денежных обязательств принципала, имеющихся перед бенефициаром». Кроме того, АФК «Система» передала в залог «ценные бумаги в обеспечение исполнения денежных обязательств залогодателя по выданной независимой гарантии». Сумма сделки, согласно раскрытию, составила до 350 млрд рублей, а срок исполнения обязательств по гарантии — до конца 2030 года, обратил внимание РБК.
Первый вице-президент АФК «Система» Илья Филатов сообщил изданию, что компания привлекла «инвестиционный ресурс» на 320 млрд рублей, заложив неназванные ценные бумаги. По его словам, средства будут «в приоритетном порядке направлены на погашение корпоративного долга и публичных обязательств», но также на развитие текущего портфеля активов и новые перспективные проекты. В марте глава АФК «Система» Тагир Ситдеков говорил «Интерфаксу», что корпорация в рамках оптимизации долга прорабатывает различные инструменты, включая «структурные сделки с банками».

# Context-enrichment: VTB takes large Ozon share stake as collateral
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 29–30 June 2026, AFK Sistema (the conglomerate of billionaire Vladimir Evtushenkov, controlled via his son Feliks; also parent of MTS) raised RUB 320bn in a "structural investment deal" arranged with VTB, pledging a package of securities that includes its stake in Ozon. Disclosure filings put the ceiling of the deal at up to RUB 350bn, with obligations running to end-2030. First Vice-President of Sistema Ilya Filatov confirmed the RUB 320bn raise against pledged (unnamed) securities; press-secretary Ivan Makushok confirmed the deal is "signed" and will let the group repay bank debt, cut leverage and improve its credit profile.

De-PR'd reality — the framing gap:
- The parties (and much of the press) call it a "structural/investment deal," NOT a loan. VTB's Dmitry Pyanov explicitly stressed it is a collateral agreement, "not a credit." That distinction is deliberate: a structured pledge/repo-style transaction with a non-bank investor syndicate keeps it off the simple "VTB lent Sistema RUB 320bn" balance-sheet story and lets VTB tout it as capital-markets structuring. **Why this way:** it broadens funding beyond VTB's own balance sheet (several non-bank financial partners participate), and it monetizes an illiquid strategic stake without an outright sale — Sistema keeps the upside/voting while getting cash. (analysis)
- **The pledge is essentially the whole Ozon stake.** As of 1 March 2026, Sistema held 31.8% of Ozon; at Ozon's ~RUB 1tn market cap that ~31.8% is worth roughly RUB 300–390bn — i.e. the RUB 320bn raised is ~1:1 against the stake's market value, an aggressive loan-to-value with essentially no haircut cushion. That is the single most important de-PR'd fact: this is not "a large stake as collateral," it is **Sistema pledging its crown-jewel Ozon holding almost in full to service debt.** (partly analysis on LTV)
- VTB reportedly received a proportional share of governance rights, "including one of two Class A shares" (Pyanov) — i.e. the pledge carries control features, not just economic exposure.
- Vagueness flagged: exact number of shares, the identity of the "non-bank financial partners," and whether MTS-chain entities are co-pledgors are undisclosed. CNews framed it as "the owners of MTS pledged their Ozon stake" — consistent with the Sistema/Evtushenkov ownership chain, but the precise pledgor structure is opaque.

## [1] Competitors / peers
This is a corporate-finance/collateral event, not a product; the relevant peers are (a) other Russian oligarch-conglomerate deleveraging deals and (b) share-pledge financing generally.
- **VTB–Wildberries (RVB):** VTB is simultaneously building deep ties to the other Russian e-commerce champion — a strategic partnership announced May 2026, VTB selling its ex-Otkritie HQ (Vivaldi Plaza) to Wildberries-Russ [[VTB to sell former Otkritie office to Wildberries-Russ]], and RVB's own M&A (Eapteka) [[Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka]]. **Second-order:** VTB is positioning itself as the house bank/financier to BOTH leading marketplaces (Ozon via the Sistema pledge, Wildberries via partnership) — a barbell on Russian e-commerce credit. Ozon also runs its own bank (Ozon Bank) now registering a DFA platform with the CBR [[Ozon Bank registers digital financial assets platform with CBR]], so the ecosystem is increasingly financialized.
- **Sberbank** is the natural rival creditor and is pushing its own frontier collateral (crypto-backed corporate loans) [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]]; VTB's "first deal of this scale and structural complexity in Russia" claim is partly a competitive flex against Sber.
- **Share-as-collateral globally:** using listed equity to raise liquidity is routine (JPMorgan accepting crypto/ETF collateral [[JPMorgan to offer financing against crypto ETFs as collateral]], Robinhood pledging Apple shares [[Robinhood wants Apple shares as crypto loan collateral]]). The Russian novelty is scale + the sanctioned-market context, not the mechanism.
**Position:** ahead on scale within Russia (self-described first of its kind), parity on mechanism vs global share-pledge finance.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Sistema is a serial debt-heavy holding; its standalone corporate net debt has long been the market's central concern, forcing repeated asset monetizations, IPOs (it took Ozon public in 2020, MTS long ago) and "structural deals with banks." In March 2026 CEO Tagir Sitdikov told Interfax the group was working through "structural deals with banks" for debt optimization — this June deal is the concrete execution of that stated plan, so it **fits logically** as flagged deleveraging, not a surprise.
- Sistema–Ozon history: Sistema (with MTS) has backed Ozon since ~2014, IPO'd it in 2020. Ozon re-domiciled from Cyprus to Russia (MKPAO), completing around end-2025/2026, which made the Russian-listed shares pledgeable onshore — a **precondition** that made this deal mechanically possible. (Redomiciliation → onshore, unrestricted shares → usable as domestic collateral.)
- **Why Sistema acts this way:** its structural pressure is a high standalone-debt / high-rate environment (elevated Russian policy rate) crushing carry on leveraged holdings; monetizing the Ozon stake without selling lets it cut cash interest while retaining the equity option — a classic "raise against the winner to survive the rate cycle" move. (analysis)
- Note a governance wrinkle: reporting indicates Sistema was no longer the single largest Ozon holder by mid-2026 (Alexander Chachava's O23 reportedly lifted its stake to ~35% in April 2026), which sharpens Sistema's incentive to extract cash value from a stake whose control premium is eroding. (per web reporting; treat as open on exact figures)

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new:** the *scale and structuring* (up to RUB 350bn, multi-year to 2030, syndicate of bank + non-bank investors, control-share features) — VTB claims it as a first-of-its-kind Russian structured pledge. That claim is plausible for the domestic market.
- **What's NOT new:** the underlying act — pledging listed equity to raise liquidity — is old. The "structural deal / not a loan" label is largely presentational; economically Sistema has a secured obligation to repay ~RUB 320–350bn by 2030 with its Ozon stake on the line.
- **Traction / substance:** this is a *closed, signed* transaction with cash actually raised (Filatov confirmed RUB 320bn attracted) — so it is real execution, not an MoU. That materially raises its weight vs a typical announcement.
- **Who captures the value:** VTB captures a well-collateralized, control-linked exposure to Russia's #1/#2 e-commerce name plus fee/structuring economics, and cements itself as Sistema's key creditor. Sistema captures survival liquidity but at ~1:1 LTV cedes most of the Ozon stake's downside protection to VTB. **The real question shifts** from "Sistema is deleveraging" to "at what price has Sistema mortgaged its best asset, and does VTB now effectively control the fate of the Ozon stake if Ozon's price falls?" (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Watch:** margin/top-up mechanics if Ozon shares fall (near-1:1 LTV means little cushion → forced-sale or step-in risk on the pledged block, which could pressure Ozon's float); confirmation of the pledgor chain (MTS-linked entities?); whether VTB syndicates/offloads the exposure to the non-bank partners.
- **Sentiment:** Russian market read it as a credit-positive deleveraging catalyst for Sistema (improved credit profile per management) but a red flag on how stretched the balance sheet is — one commentator framed it as a "harbinger of PAO Ozon's demise," i.e. bearish tail-risk chatter. Mixed.
- **Regulatory backdrop:** enabled by Ozon's redomiciliation (onshore MKPAO shares) and occurring under Western sanctions on VTB/Sistema — a domestic-capital solution to a domestically-funded problem.
- **Second-order (counterintuitive):** the deal makes VTB *more* entangled with, not insulated from, Russian consumer-tech equity risk right as it also backs Wildberries — concentration in e-commerce credit could make VTB fragile to a sector downturn, not safer, despite the "well-collateralized" framing. (analysis)

## Red-team / challenge questions

1. **Loan or "structural deal"?** Parties insist it is a collateral agreement, "not a credit" (Pyanov). Economically Sistema owes ~RUB 320–350bn secured by Ozon shares to 2030. What is the *legal* structure — repo, secured guarantee, forward? — and does the label change who bears price risk? Answer: disclosure shows an independent guarantee by Sistema + pledge of securities backing it; the "not a loan" framing appears largely presentational. Partly open.

2. **How big is the pledged stake, exactly?** Sistema held 31.8% of Ozon at 1 Mar 2026. Is the *entire* stake pledged or a slice? Reporting implies effectively the whole holding. Exact share count: **open**.

3. **What is the loan-to-value?** RUB 320bn raised vs a ~31.8% stake worth ~RUB 300–390bn implies ~0.8–1.0x LTV — aggressively little haircut. Confirm Ozon market cap on deal date and the true LTV. Partly open (mktcap sources vary RUB 219bn–393bn quoted for different bases — needs the MKPAO whole-company figure).

4. **What happens on a margin call?** With near-1:1 LTV, an Ozon price drop could trigger top-ups or forced sale of the pledged block — pressuring Ozon's float. Are there top-up/cure provisions or a liquidation waterfall? **Open.**

5. **Did VTB get control, not just economics?** Pyanov mentioned "one of two Class A shares" and rights proportional to funds. Does the pledge convey voting/board control over the Ozon stake, and could VTB step in? Likely partial control features — needs confirmation.

6. **Who are the "non-bank financial partners"?** The deal reportedly includes a syndicate beyond VTB. Who are they, and does VTB retain the risk or distribute it? **Open** — material for whether this is truly VTB balance sheet.

7. **Is MTS in the pledgor chain?** CNews headline says "owners of MTS pledged their Ozon stake." Are MTS-level entities co-pledgors, or is it purely Sistema holdco? **Open.**

8. **Was redomiciliation the enabler?** Could this pledge have happened before Ozon moved from Cyprus to Russia (MKPAO)? Almost certainly not at this scale onshore — confirm the redomiciliation completion date vs the pledge date. Answer: redomiciliation completing ~end-2025/2026 is the plausible precondition. Confirmed in direction, exact date open.

9. **Does it actually cut Sistema's leverage or just reshuffle it?** RUB 320bn goes to repay corporate debt/public obligations — but Sistema now owes a secured RUB 320–350bn instead. Net leverage may barely fall; what changes is tenor (to 2030) and creditor. Is the "credit profile improvement" real or cosmetic? **Partly open** — depends on rate/tenor swap.

10. **Is Sistema still the largest Ozon holder?** Reporting says Chachava's O23 rose to ~35% in April 2026, above Sistema's 31.8%. If Sistema is no longer control shareholder, the "crown jewel" framing weakens — and its incentive to cash out is higher. Confirm current cap table. **Open.**

11. **VTB e-commerce concentration risk:** VTB is simultaneously financier to Ozon (pledge) and Wildberries/RVB (partnership, HQ sale). Is it over-concentrated in Russian consumer-tech credit? Does the "well-collateralized" framing mask correlated sector risk? Analysis — **open**.

12. **Is VTB's "first of its scale/complexity in Russia" claim true?** Or marketing vs Sberbank? Plausible for scale; unverifiable in absolute terms. **Open.**

13. **What is the pledge's seniority vs other Sistema creditors?** If Sistema hits distress, where does VTB rank on the Ozon shares vs bondholders? **Open** — decides recovery.

14. **Freshness:** Is this a re-report of an earlier Sistema debt-optimization announcement (March 2026 "structural deals with banks" comment)? No — March was intent; June 29–30 is the *signed, funded* execution with a concrete RUB 320bn figure and pledge. This is a genuine "talks → signed deal" follow-up, hence **fresh**.

15. **Downside trigger to watch:** an Ozon share slide + a Sistema liquidity event simultaneously = forced sale of the pledged block into a thin domestic float, cascading Ozon price damage and VTB step-in. Low-probability tail, high impact. **Open.**

Structure: the lender side is highly consolidated (VTB is the #2 Russian bank; FY2025 IFRS net profit RUB 502.1bn, −8.9% YoY, per TASS via web) and the collateral is a single concentrated equity block; barriers to this kind of deal are capital scale and sanctions-era domestic-only rails.

**Competitive landscape.** KPIs the sector runs on: for Ozon — GMV, take rate, and increasingly Fintech revenue / Fintech adj. EBITDA (Fintech revenue was RUB 93.3bn in FY2024, +191% YoY, with Fintech adj. EBITDA RUB 32.4bn; FY2025 guidance Fintech revenue +>80% YoY — Ozon IR, drive_url above and Q4/FY2024 release drive_url https://drive.google.com/file/d/1llWzry-0---2k3ZTIU_BXjVqEJXZd100/view). Basis of competition on the financing side is balance-sheet capacity and willingness to warehouse concentrated equity risk. Recent related moves: VTB is reported to also be pursuing a Wildberries-linked deal (per RBK/note) and Russian banks are broadly building marketplace-fintech ties. Protagonist positions: AFK Sistema (Ozon's 31.8% holder as of 2026-03-01 per Forbes, via note) is the borrower deleveraging; VTB is the creditor/pledgee taking the Ozon block as security. Ozon itself is a passive underlying — its cash flows are not directly encumbered, only Sistema's shares (de-PR: the marketplace's operations are unaffected; this is a holdco financing). Moat: VTB's scale and state backing (analysis); Ozon's moat is marketplace network effects + a fast-growing captive fintech arm.

**Comps & multiples.** Deal terms: RUB 320bn attracted (up to RUB 350bn disclosed), guarantee obligations running to end-2030 (RBK/TASS via note + web).
