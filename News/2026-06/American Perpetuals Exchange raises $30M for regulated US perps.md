New York Senator Kirstin Gillibrand’s son, Theodore Gillibrand, has raised $30 million in funding for a new perps trading venue.

Fortune reported Thursday the round was led by New York City-based venture firm Lux Capital at an estimated $300 million valuation.

Gillibrand’s startup, dubbed American Perpetuals Exchange Corporation, plans to file for a Designated Contract Market license with a special exemption to list perps on single-name equities under joint CFTC and SEC oversight, according to a June 4 memo.

The Commodity Futures Trading Commission and Securities Exchange Commission are currently collaborating on a "harmonization" strategy to unify their approach to novel markets and asset classes like crypto and perps.

In May, the CFTC approved Kalshi’s request to list the first official bitcoin perp in the U.S., and permitted Coinbase to list long-dated "perp-style" futures. More recently, Kraken announced it would launch crypto perps on Kraken Pro.

This comes as CME Group sues the CFTC over those approvals, arguing that perps are legally swaps under the Dodd-Frank Act, not futures, and that Kalshi and Coinbase were essentially permitted to sidestep stricter swap rules meant to protect against systemic risks.

"The absence of a regulated U.S. venue does not eliminate demand for equity perpetual futures," according to the memo log for the June 4 meeting. "It redirects that demand to offshore platforms outside the reach of U.S. oversight, where participants have no recourse and regulators have no visibility."

In addition to Gilibrand, representing APEC, and SEC and CFTC officials, the meeting notes representatives from Gibson, Dunn & Crutcher LLP and BGR Group were in attendance. Rebecca Rettig, a prominent crypto lawyer, also attended on behalf of Arktouros PLLC.

The memo notes APEC also intends to apply for a Derivatives Clearing Organization (DCO) license, which would allow it to clear its transactions in-house.

"APEC seeks significant capital infusion as part of the licensing process, as the DCM/DCO registration requires demonstrated financial resources and extensive legal expenditure," the memo states. "Capital is allocated towards regulatory capital and compliance infrastructure, building out the compliance team, technical systems, and legal framework required for exchange operations."

"The company that most aptly navigates the licensing process will have a considerable market edge, and as the first regulated perpetuals exchange there is endless potential for value capture."

Gilibrand is a recent graduate from Stanford University, and an alum of venture capital firm Paradigm, a significant Kalshi backer, as well as a former Andreessen Horowitz intern, according to his LinkedIn page.

Sen. Gillibrand (D-NY) is a significant crypto industry backer. She is a frequent collaborator with Republican Sen. Cynthia Lummis, who together have introduced efforts like the Responsible Financial Innovation Act in 2022 and 2023, and more recently the GENIUS Act, covering stablecoins, which became law last summer.

# Context-enrichment: American Perpetuals Exchange raises $30M for regulated US perps
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

**FRESHNESS VERDICT: FRESH.** This is a NEW event — a $30M seed/early round for a NEWLY founded company (American Perpetuals Exchange Corporation, "APEC"). The corpus already covers the surrounding regulatory wave (CFTC approval of Kalshi's BTC perp, Coinbase, Kraken's launch, the SEC/CFTC harmonization push), but none of those are this round or this company. No duplicate. The de-PR'd hook below is what actually distinguishes it.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- APEC raised **$30M led by Lux Capital** at a **~$300M valuation** (Fortune, Jun 18 2026; confirmed The Block, Inc., Washington Times). Founder: **Theodore Gillibrand** — son of Sen. Kirsten Gillibrand (D-NY), Stanford grad, **ex-Paradigm**, former a16z intern.
- **The real differentiator (often blurred in the "US perps" framing): APEC targets PERPETUALS ON SINGLE-NAME EQUITIES / stock indices — not crypto perps.** Per the June 4 regulator memo, it plans to file for a **Designated Contract Market (DCM)** license with a *special exemption* to list perps on single-name equities under **joint CFTC + SEC oversight**, and separately to apply for a **Derivatives Clearing Organization (DCO)** license to clear in-house.
- Status: **pre-license, pre-product.** Nothing is live. The $30M is explicitly framed in the memo as capital to satisfy the *regulatory capital requirement* and fund the compliance/legal build-out for DCM/DCO registration — i.e. table stakes to even apply, not growth capital.
- **Why structured this way / what it reveals:** The single-name-equity angle is the whole bet. Crypto perps already have onshore approvals (Kalshi, Coinbase, Kraken). Equity perps under *dual* CFTC/SEC oversight do NOT yet exist in the US — that is the white space APEC is trying to claim "first-mover" on. The dual-regulator ask is necessary precisely because single-name equity derivatives straddle the SEC (securities) / CFTC (futures) jurisdictional line — the exact seam the agencies' "harmonization" effort is trying to stitch. The PR/memo language ("endless potential for value capture", "considerable market edge" for whoever navigates licensing first) is a tell: the moat is regulatory/licensing, not technology or liquidity.
- **Skeptical read:** a $300M valuation for a company with no license, no product, and no users is a bet on (a) the regulatory window opening for equity perps and (b) the founder's access/positioning. The senator-son angle is unavoidable: the founder met SEC + CFTC officials (June 4 memo, with Gibson Dunn, BGR Group lobbyists, and crypto lawyer Rebecca Rettig/Arktouros present). This is a regulatory-access play as much as a product play.

## [1] Competitors / peers
The US "onshore perps" land-grab is crowded — but mostly on the CRYPTO side, where APEC is NOT playing:
- **Kalshi** — first CFTC-approved US bitcoin perp (approved May 2026) [[CFTC permits first US-listed bitcoin perpetual contract]]. Note: APEC's founder is ex-Paradigm, a *significant Kalshi backer* — overlapping network.
- **Coinbase** — permitted to list long-dated "perp-style" futures; built on its 2025 perpetual-style futures launch [[Coinbase and Kalshi bring perpetual crypto futures to US]], [[Coinbase Derivatives will launch US Perpetual-Style Futures]].
- **Kraken** — launched CFTC-regulated US perps June 2026 [[Kraken launches CFTC-regulated perpetual futures in the US]]; already does 24/7 perps on tokenized US stocks (offshore) [[Kraken rolls out 24 7 perpetuals for tokenized US stocks]]; acquired a DCM via Small Exchange/IG ($100M, 2025) [[Kraken acquires exchange in $100M derivatives deal]].
- **CME Group** — the incumbent and the spoiler: **suing the CFTC** (filed ~Jun 18 2026) arguing perps are *swaps* under Dodd-Frank, not futures, so the DCM fast-path Kalshi/Coinbase used is illegitimate. CFTC calls the suit "frivolous."
- **Hyperliquid** — the offshore reality APEC's memo cites: ~70% of on-chain perp DEX volume, ~6% of total perp volume, ~$200B monthly; no legal entity, no KYC, no US license — and *expanding into non-crypto assets* (commodities, equities exposure). This is the "demand redirected offshore" APEC says it wants to onshore.
- **Position:** APEC is *behind* on existence (it's pre-license while rivals are live) but potentially *ahead* on the specific equity-perp niche IF dual oversight materializes. Crypto-perp incumbents (Kraken, Coinbase) could trivially extend into equity perps if the rules clear — APEC's lead is a head start in licensing, not a structural moat.
- **Why the landscape is this way:** Perps are the dominant global crypto derivative ($60T+ 2025 volume) but were historically banned/constrained onshore, sending volume to Binance/Hyperliquid/offshore. The 2025–26 regulatory thaw (GENIUS Act, SEC/CFTC harmonization, pro-crypto administration) reopened the door. Everyone is racing for the licensed-onshore version of an already-proven offshore product. The CME suit is the incumbent defending its swap-rules turf and could reset the entire path.

## [2] Company history / fit
- APEC is **recently founded** (New York). No prior product history; this is effectively a formation + seed event.
- Founder fit: Gillibrand's pedigree (Paradigm, a16z, Stanford) gives crypto-derivatives credibility and investor access; the Lux Capital lead is a serious deep-tech/frontier VC, not a crypto tourist. The senator parentage gives unusual regulatory reach (the June 4 meeting with both agencies).
- **Why the company acts this way:** It is structuring itself entirely around the *licensing-first* thesis — raising to meet DCM/DCO capital floors before building product. That's the inverse of a typical startup (build → users → revenue). It only makes sense if you believe the binding constraint is regulatory approval, and that being the *first* licensed equity-perp DCM is worth more than product/distribution advantages competitors hold.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Genuinely novel niche:** perps on single-name US equities under dual CFTC/SEC oversight. That specific product does not exist onshore today. (Crypto perps onshore = no longer novel.)
- **Traction: effectively zero.** No license, no product, no users, no volume. All "value capture" claims are prospective. This is the single biggest caveat — the breakdown should not pass the round off as adoption.
- **Where value/margin sits (analysis):** If APEC gets DCM *and* DCO licenses, it captures both the trading venue *and* the clearing economics — a vertically integrated, hard-to-replicate position (clearing is the deep moat, à la CME/ICE). That is the real prize and why in-house clearing is in the memo. **But** the value depends entirely on a regulatory outcome APEC does not control: the SEC/CFTC harmonization landing in a form that permits equity perps, AND the CME suit not blowing up the DCM path. If perps get reclassified as swaps (CME's argument), APEC's whole DCM strategy needs rework.
- **Second-order (hypothesis):** Even if approved, an incumbent (Kraken with its DCM, Coinbase, ICE/CME themselves) could fast-follow into equity perps with existing licenses, liquidity, and users — eroding the first-mover premium. The $300M valuation prices in a clean win on a contested, multi-variable regulatory bet.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Next:** file DCM application (+ exemption for single-name equity perps); apply for DCO; build compliance/legal/tech stack with the raised capital. Watch the SEC/CFTC harmonization timeline and any rulemaking on perps.
- **Regulatory backdrop:** broadly favorable (GENIUS Act became law summer 2025; pro-crypto stance; harmonization underway). **Key risk: the CME v. CFTC lawsuit** — if perps are ruled swaps, the fast DCM path that makes APEC viable is undercut.
- **Sentiment:** coverage is heavy but driven substantially by the celebrity/political angle (senator's son) rather than product substance — a yellow flag for over-indexing on the news.
- **Why the market may go this way (analysis):** Onshoring offshore perp demand is a real structural driver, and equities are the obvious next asset class after crypto. But **counterintuitive second-order effect:** the more attractive the equity-perp prize looks, the more likely well-capitalized incumbents (CME, ICE, Kraken, Coinbase) crowd in — so being the named "first" startup may be more fragile than it appears. The durable winner is whoever pairs a license with liquidity and clearing, and APEC has only (prospectively) the license.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** APEC sits at the seam of two markets: (1) the now-proven *crypto* perpetual-futures market, and (2) the *unbuilt onshore equity* perp market it actually targets. Sizing the proven side: crypto perp/futures volume hit ~$61tn in 2025 (KuCoin); the top-10 perp venues alone cleared ~$92.9tn in 2025, +64.6% YoY (KuCoin), and CEXs cleared ~$86tn in perp volume last year (a16z crypto). Perps are now ~75–90% of crypto derivatives volume (Datawallet) — i.e. the dominant instrument, not a niche. **DEX perps are the fast-growing slice:** monthly DEX perp volume crossed ~$1tn by late 2025 (CoinMarketCap), with share rising from ~2.0% (Jan-2024) to ~10.2% (Jan-2026) (Datawallet). For the *equity*-perp market APEC wants — perps on single-name US stocks/indices under dual CFTC/SEC oversight — there is **no data / no TAM**: it does not exist onshore today; demand is inferred only from offshore crypto-perp appetite, not measured. Structure: heavily concentrated and high-barrier. The barriers are exactly what APEC is buying — a DCM requires demonstrated financial resources (CFTC guidance cites >12 months of operating expenses as evidence of sufficient capital, per Katten/CFTC Part 38), and a DCO adds clearing-grade capital + risk infrastructure. **Why now:** the 2025–26 regulatory thaw (GENIUS Act law summer 2025; pro-crypto administration; live SEC/CFTC "harmonization") cracked open the onshore door that historically pushed perp volume offshore — second-order effect: every venue is now racing for the *licensed onshore* version of an already-proven offshore product, and equities are the obvious next asset class after crypto.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs for a perp venue: **notional/perp volume, open interest, and (if integrated) clearing fees** — none of which APEC has (pre-product). Basis of competition = regulatory license + liquidity + clearing, in that order of difficulty. Key players, recent dated moves:
- **Kalshi** — first CFTC-approved US BTC perp (approved May 29 2026; CryptoBriefing/Seeking Alpha). APEC founder is ex-Paradigm, a major Kalshi backer (overlapping network).
- **Coinbase** — permitted to list long-dated "perp-style" futures (Jun 2026), built on its Jul-2025 US perpetual-style futures launch; already runs 24/7 stock perpetual futures (Mar 2026) [[Coinbase launches 24 7 stock perpetual futures]] — i.e. *already in the equity-perp adjacency APEC claims as white space*.
- **Kraken** — launched CFTC-regulated US perps Jun 16 2026 [[Kraken launches CFTC-regulated perpetual futures in the US]]; owns a DCM via the $100M Small Exchange/IG deal (Oct 2025) [[Kraken acquires exchange in $100M derivatives deal]]; already offers 24/7 perps on tokenized US stocks offshore [[Kraken rolls out 24 7 perpetuals for tokenized US stocks]].
- **CME Group** — incumbent + spoiler: sued the CFTC (~Jun 2026) arguing perps are *swaps* under Dodd-Frank, not futures, so the DCM fast-path is illegitimate; CFTC calls it "frivolous" (Seeking Alpha, crypto.news).
- **Hyperliquid** — the offshore reality APEC's memo cites: >70% of perp-DEX volume, ~$180–200bn 30-day volume (Apr 2026), $619bn Q1-2026; no legal entity, no KYC, no US license (CoinGecko, Yellow). This is the demand APEC says it wants to onshore.

Protagonist's position: **behind on existence** (pre-license while Kalshi/Coinbase/Kraken are live) but potentially **ahead in the narrow equity-perp-under-dual-oversight niche** IF harmonization lands. Moat (analysis): the only durable prize is the *DCO clearing license* (regulatory/intangible barrier, à la CME/ICE); the venue itself has no network effect, switching cost, or scale yet. Licensing lead = head start, not a structural moat.

**Comps & multiples.** APEC: $30M round at ~$300M post-money (Fortune, Jun 18 2026) — a **round valuation, not market cap**, on **zero revenue / zero license / zero users**, so EV/Revenue, P/S and per-user multiples are all **"no data" (no denominator exists)**. The only honest read is qualitative: $300M prices an uncontrolled multi-variable regulatory outcome. Internal comps from the base (all live or licensed peers, which makes APEC look richly priced for its pre-license stage):
- [[Kraken acquires exchange in $100M derivatives deal]] — Kraken paid **$100M to acquire an actual DCM license + operating exchange** (Oct 2025). Direct read-across: a *working* DCM changed hands for ~1/3 of APEC's *pre-license* paper valuation. Outlier flag: **rich** — APEC is valued at 3x a real, licensed DCM while holding only the *intent* to apply.
- [[Coinbase launches 24 7 stock perpetual futures]] / [[Kraken rolls out 24 7 perpetuals for tokenized US stocks]] — incumbents already shipping the equity-perp adjacency; precedent that the "white space" is contestable, not owned.
- [[Coinbase and Kalshi bring perpetual crypto futures to US]] / [[Kraken launches CFTC-regulated perpetual futures in the US]] — onshore crypto-perp path already paved by funded incumbents.
Distribution not computed (no comparable revenue/EV figures across these); qualitative comparison only. **Verdict: rich vs the one true comparable (a $100M live DCM).**

**Risk flags.**
1. **Pre-license / pre-product valued at $300M** — the round funds *regulatory capital + legal burn* (memo), not growth; if the DCM/DCO approval slips years or is denied, the $30M is pure compliance burn with no offsetting revenue, and the $300M mark has nothing to support it. Second-order: long-runway down-round risk.
2. **CME v. CFTC lawsuit is an existential, binary threat to the path** — if perps are ruled *swaps* under Dodd-Frank, the DCM fast-track APEC's entire strategy depends on is undercut and must be reworked. APEC does not control this outcome.
3. **Incumbent fast-follow erodes any first-mover premium** — Kraken (owns a DCM), Coinbase (already runs 24/7 stock perps) and CME/ICE can extend into equity perps with existing licenses, liquidity, and users the moment the rules clear; APEC must build all three from zero. The "first regulated perpetuals exchange" claim is fragile.
4. **Regulatory-access / governance optics** — celebrity-driven coverage (senator's son), founder-led meetings with both agencies plus lobbyists; an access play, not a moat — vulnerable if the harmonization politics shift.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** (analysis) This is a *marker of the equity-perp frontier opening*, not a re-rating event — the signal is that VC money (Lux lead) is now pricing onshore single-name-equity perps as a near-term category, one step beyond the proven crypto-perp onshoring. Falsifiable thesis: **APEC's value is dominated by regulatory outcome, not execution** — the bet is wrong if a funded incumbent (Kraken/Coinbase) launches CFTC/SEC-blessed single-name equity perps first, which would collapse the first-mover premium before APEC even files. Triggers to watch: (a) APEC's actual DCM/DCO filing acceptance; (b) the CME v. CFTC ruling; (c) any SEC/CFTC harmonization rule explicitly covering equity perps. Until one lands concretely, treat this as a celebrity-tinged formation bet, not a venue story.
