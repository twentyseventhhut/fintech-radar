Expands Estimating AI to materials distributors, announces partnership with US LBM
 DURHAM, N.C. , June 30, 2026 /PRNewswire/ -- Higharc, the homebuilding AI company for the full design-to-construction lifecycle , today announced it has raised a $95 million Series C led by global software investor Insight Partners. The round brings Higharc's total funding to more than $170 million.
Simultaneously, Higharc announced an agreement with US LBM, the largest private distributor of lumber and building materials in the United States, marking the expansion of Higharc's platform to the building materials supply chain.

 
 
 AI that actually works for the built world 
Homebuilding is undergoing a fundamental shift as complex design, 3D modeling and estimating workflows move to AI. Across homebuilder and distributor teams, work that once required weeks of manual effort can now be automated.
"AI isn't just assisting builders. It's reshaping how builders work, cutting time and cost per job while giving buyers a more personalized experience," said Marc Minor, CEO and co-founder of Higharc. "The builders and distributors leading the way use AI to automate their most complex workflows unlocking teams to focus on product improvement and customer experience."
While AI is reshaping technical workflows across industries, most AI systems fail on basic spatial reasoning. In homebuilding, these errors mean delayed starts, materials overage, change order and frustrated buyers. Higharc's AI platform is different. Higharc generates homes as spatial databases — capturing code requirements, construction standards and geometry — and creates the structured data foundation required for production-grade AI.
Higharc replaces AutoCAD and point solutions and enables builders to automate design, estimating and sales workflows. Using Higharc's generative system , homebuilders produce precise and build-ready homes, complete with construction documents, real-time estimates and fully shoppable 3D models.
Builders across North America use Higharc to accelerate their design-to-construction workflows. Higharc customers report substantial impact and time savings including:
Compressing product development timelines from months or years to weeks or days
Cutting time to community open by 25-50%
Increasing margin by 10-15% 
"We evaluated a lot of AI tools, but most produce outputs that require so much correction they're unusable. Higharc is different. It's grounded in how homes are actually built, so the outputs are usable from day one," said Kyle Bear, VP of Research and Development at Signature Homes. "Higharc helps us move faster and operate with more precision and fewer downstream surprises. And as a result, we're breaking ground on more homes each year while protecting margins and pricing power."
 Expanding AI estimating to the supply chain 
The new funding will be used to scale AI product development and expand the platform to a broader segment of the homebuilding market.
As part of its expansion, Higharc today announced its AI Estimating product for building materials distributors, with US LBM as its first partner. Until now, estimating buildings was a famously complex, manual process prone to errors and delays. The new offering gives LBM distributors and dealers the ability to generate accurate material takeoffs from builder plan sets at enterprise scale.
At the center of this capability is Higharc's AutoTranslate AI, which can translate any floorplan image into a rich 3D spatial data model. It combines proprietary AI vision models with deterministic construction logic to generate precise quantity estimates and align them directly to purchasable materials. The results are quantity estimates that reflect how homes are actually designed and built — not approximations.
"With advanced technology accelerating the building materials industry, what sets Higharc apart for US LBM is the ability to go from a static 2D plan to a precise and dynamic 3D data model in one step. That unlocks not just fast, accurate quoting but smarter material planning, the ability to sell the whole home and real-time collaboration with our customers — powering better outcomes for us, the builder and ultimately the homeowner," said Jonathan Greene, Chief Digital and Technology Officer at US LBM.
 Investing in the future of homebuilding AI 
"Everything we're building is in service of one outcome: to make homebuilding seamless so builders can build better homes more affordably. This next phase is about scaling that impact by deepening our AI capabilities and bringing suppliers onto the same trusted system builders already rely on," explained Minor.
"We evaluate AI companies across every sector, and Higharc stands out as a clear leader in applying AI to residential construction," said Josh Fredberg, Managing Director at Insight Partners. "Higharc is setting the standard for how homes will be designed, permitted and built in the age of AI. Leading builders are already standardizing on the platform, and we have conviction that this extension into supplier workflows will transform the broader builder supply chain."
The round was led by Insight Partners, with participation from Wellington Management and existing investors including Fifth Wall, Spark Capital, Lux Capital, SE Ventures (Schneider Electric's venture arm), Simpson Strong-Tie, PSP Partners, RXR Arden Digital Ventures, Suffolk Technologies, Vertex Ventures, NC Tweener Fund and MetaProp.

# Context-enrichment: Higharc raises $95M Series C for AI homebuilding software
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5. Freshness: FRESH._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **Facts (confirmed):** Higharc (Durham, NC; founded 2018 by Marc Minor et al.) raised a **$95M Series C** on **2026-06-30**, led by **Insight Partners**, with Wellington Management + existing investors (Fifth Wall, Spark Capital, Lux Capital, SE Ventures/Schneider, Simpson Strong-Tie, PSP Partners, RXR Arden, Suffolk Technologies, Vertex Ventures, NC Tweener Fund, MetaProp). **Total raised now >$170M**. Prior rounds: Series A $21M (Apr 2021, Spark), Series B $53M (Feb 2024, Spark + Pillar VC).
- **Bundled news:** simultaneously launched **AI Estimating for building-materials distributors**, with **US LBM** (largest private US building-materials distributor) as first partner. Core tech: **AutoTranslate AI** — converts any 2D floorplan image into a 3D spatial data model, then generates material takeoffs mapped to purchasable SKUs.
- **No valuation disclosed** (marketing-vague). A stale third-party datapoint (GetLatka, 2023) put ARR ~$12M / valuation ~$36M — not confirmed, almost certainly outdated; treat as noise, not a current anchor.
- **Why structured this way (analysis):** the round is deliberately co-announced with the US LBM deal and a product launch. That's the tell — **the story the company is selling is not "we raised money" but "we're jumping from the builder side of the value chain to the supplier/distributor side."** A Series C alone in a legacy vertical wouldn't warrant Insight leading; the supply-chain expansion is the thesis. The de-PR'd read: capital is being raised to fund a **land-grab into distributor workflows** before incumbents (Hyphen, ECI) wake up.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Legacy homebuilder software:** **Constellation HomeBuilder Systems** (trusted by ~half of top-200 NA builders), **Hyphen Solutions** (BuildPro scheduling, BRIX ERP — strongest in supply-chain/supplier coordination), **ECI BuildTools** (project mgmt/ERP). These are configurable ERP/scheduling stacks — NOT generative design.
- **Higharc's position:** it attacks a *different* layer — **generative design + spatial database** that replaces AutoCAD + point solutions, then extends downstream into estimating/sales/3D. So vs. the ERP incumbents it's **ahead on design-AI, behind on entrenched ERP/back-office footprint.** With the US LBM move it now steps directly onto **Hyphen's turf (supplier coordination).**
- **Corpus peers (adjacent, not same deal):** [[Pillar raises €12 million for AI construction operating system]] (EU, AI construction OS), [[Handle closes $27 million Series B for construction finance]] (construction finance rails), [[Construction AI startup Mason hires founding team]] (construction workforce AI), [[Tidalwave raises $22M Series A for AI mortgage platform]] (D.R. Horton-backed AI mortgage — adjacent homebuilder-tech capital).
- **Why the landscape is this way (analysis):** homebuilding is one of the last big verticals where design still runs on general-purpose CAD + manual estimating. The incumbents own the *back office* (ERP/scheduling) but not the *design-to-data* front end. Higharc's bet is that whoever generates the home as structured 3D data **owns the source-of-truth** the rest of the stack must reference — a classic "control the schema, control the workflow" play. Second-order: US LBM as first distributor partner is a **wedge to make Higharc's spatial model the estimating standard for suppliers**, which would disintermediate incumbents' supplier-coordination modules.

## [2] Company history / fit
- 2018 founded → 2021 Series A $21M → Feb 2024 Series B $53M → Jun 2026 Series C $95M. Strategic/industry investors have been baked in since Series B (Home Depot, Ferguson, Simpson Strong-Tie, SE Ventures) — i.e. **distributors and manufacturers were already on the cap table.**
- **Why the company acts this way (analysis):** the presence of Ferguson/Home Depot/Simpson on the cap table since 2024 makes the US LBM distributor expansion the *logical next step*, not a pivot — Higharc is monetizing relationships it seeded two years ago. Structural logic: pure builder-side design SaaS has a **capped TAM (builders)**; opening estimating to distributors multiplies the addressable seats and gives a **network effect** (builder plans → distributor quotes on the same spatial model). This is the software-multiple move: escape a single-segment tool, become the **shared data layer between builders and suppliers.**

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **What's genuinely new:** AutoTranslate (2D plan image → 3D spatial DB → SKU-mapped takeoffs) applied to the *distributor* side is the real novelty. Most construction AI "fails on spatial reasoning" (their claim, plausible) — Higharc's differentiator is a **deterministic construction-logic layer on top of vision models**, which is a credible moat if it holds up.
- **Traction (mixed evidence):** Company-reported — customers build **40,000+ homes/yr = ~$19B new-home sales volume**; claimed **25-50% faster time-to-community-open, 10-15% margin uplift**. 167 employees. Deloitte Fast 500 top-50, Fast Company Most Innovative 2026. **Caveat:** these are *customer-reported* impact figures and volume-through-customers, NOT disclosed ARR, seat count, or NRR — the durable-revenue picture is undisclosed.
- **Why the value-add is real-but-unproven (analysis):** the spatial-DB-as-source-of-truth thesis is genuinely differentiated vs. ERP incumbents. BUT — **who captures the margin?** If Higharc becomes the design + estimating layer, distributors (US LBM) and manufacturers (Ferguson/Home Depot on cap table) may capture most of the material-sales upside while Higharc collects SaaS seats. Risk: Higharc ends up the **"plumbing" that enables others' margin** rather than the taker of it. The 10-15% margin claim is the builder's margin, not Higharc's economics.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Plans:** scale AI product dev; roll AI Estimating across more distributors beyond US LBM; deepen the builder↔supplier shared-data platform.
- **Sentiment:** strong — Insight (a $90B+ AUM software specialist) leading a Series C in a *legacy vertical* signals conviction that construction is a real AI-software category, not a niche. The round size (nearly doubling Series B) at presumably a step-up valuation reflects that.
- **Risks / what's silent:** (1) no valuation → can't judge if it's a genuine up-round or a defensive raise; (2) housing is **rate-sensitive/cyclical** — a construction slowdown hits seat expansion; (3) incumbents (Hyphen, Constellation) with entrenched ERP footprints can bolt on AI estimating and out-distribute; (4) undisclosed net-revenue-retention and gross margin — the whole "software multiple" thesis rests on numbers not shown.
- **Why the market goes this way (analysis):** capital is concentrating on the *design-to-data* control point because that's where a durable moat can form. Counterintuitive second-order effect: **the more distributors/manufacturers Higharc onboards, the more it depends on partners who also fund it and could in-source or squeeze it** — cap-table strategics are both a moat and a leash.

Global construction software market is put at ~$11.6–11.8bn in 2026 growing ~9–10% CAGR, with the residential segment cited as the fastest-growing sub-segment (~11.8% CAGR) (per Fortune Business Insights / Mordor Intelligence, via secondary market-report citations, as of 2026 — treat sizing as vendor-report figures, not audited). Structure is fragmented at the residential end: point solutions (estimating, CAD/BIM, sales visualization, scheduling) sit alongside horizontal incumbents (Procore, Autodesk), and no single vertical stack owns the full homebuilder design→estimate→sell→build lifecycle — which is the white space Higharc claims. "Why now": generative/vision AI makes floor-plan-to-3D-data translation cheap, and Higharc's stated wedge is that generic LLMs "fail on basic spatial reasoning," so it stores homes as structured spatial databases with deterministic construction logic (analysis) — a data-moat argument more than a pure-AI one.
