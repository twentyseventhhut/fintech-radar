 ГК «Свеза» купила «Центр фармацевтической упаковки» 
Группа компаний «Свеза» — один из лидеров российского лесопромышленного комплекса — объявляет о приобретении 100% уставного капитала ООО «Центр фармацевтической упаковки» (ЦФУ). Предприятие специализируется на упаковочных решениях для фармацевтической отрасли и является одним из лидеров своего сегмента.
Открытое в 2017 году в Обнинске Калужской области производство упаковки для фармацевтической отрасли сегодня входит в число лидеров своего сегмента и сотрудничает с крупнейшими российскими фармпроизводителями. Компания выпускает широкий спектр продукции, включая складные картонные коробки для лекарственных средств и листки-вкладыши различных форматов сложности.
На производственной площадке работает порядка 100 человек. Сумма сделки и ее структура не раскрываются.
Приобретение ЦФУ стало очередным шагом «Свезы» по диверсификации бизнеса и расширению присутствия на рынке упаковочных решений. Ранее компания вошла в число производителей бумаги и картона после покупки КБК «КАМА» в Пермском крае, а также начала работу в сегменте картонной и бумажной упаковки, приобретя предприятия «ММПОФ ПЭКЭДЖИНГ» и «ММПОФ РОТОГРАВЮР» в Ленинградской области.
По словам Дмитрия Колчина, генерального директора группы компаний «Свеза», приобретение ЦФУ является логичным шагом в реализации стратегии диверсификации бизнеса и расширения присутствия группы в новых рыночных сегментах. Сделка позволяет «Свезе» не только выйти на рынок упаковки для фармацевтической индустрии, но и ускорить развитие собственных решений для отрасли за счет синергии производственных и R&D-компетенций группы. Дополнительным преимуществом является расположение производства в Обнинске, которое обеспечит «Свезе» близость к ключевым клиентам в Центральном федеральном округе.
«Для дивизиона упаковки — это приобретение в первую очередь означает усиление и расширение кластера «ММПОФ» в сегменте упаковочных решений для фармацевтики. Наши компании близки по подходам к работе: мы выстраиваем долгосрочные отношения с клиентами, соблюдаем международные стандарты качества и деловой этики, а также последовательно внедряем принципы устойчивого развития на всех этапах производства», — отметил Евгений Савченко, генеральный директор дивизиона упаковки группы компаний «Свеза».

# Context-enrichment: Sveza acquires "Center of Pharmaceutical Packaging" (ЦФУ)
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

This is an industrial / packaging M&A bolt-on, not fintech. It only sits in this corpus as part of same-week Russian deal flow (cross-linked from [[Russian prosthetics maker Motorica raises 1bn rouble soft loan]]). Treated accordingly: low strategic weight for a fintech reader, but a clean illustration of an oligarch-adjacent group rolling up the domestic packaging supply chain under sanctions.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- Sveza Group (ГК «Свеза») bought **100% of LLC "Center of Pharmaceutical Packaging" (ЦФУ)**, a maker of pharma packaging (folding cartons for drugs + patient-information leaflets/inserts) based in **Obninsk, Kaluga oblast**. Founded 2017, **~100 employees** (Mergers.ru primary, 2026-06-29).
- **Deal size and structure NOT disclosed** — this is the central PR tell. ЦФУ is small (≈100 staff, one site), so the ticket is almost certainly immaterial to Sveza; non-disclosure here reads as "too small / not strategic enough to quantify," not "too big to reveal."
- Framed by Sveza's CEO Dmitry Kolchin and packaging-division head Evgeny Savchenko as "diversification" and "strengthening the ММПОФ cluster" in pharma packaging, with claimed synergy of "production + R&D competencies" and proximity to Central-Federal-District clients. These are generic acquirer talking points; no revenue, capacity, multiple or accretion figure given.
- **Why structured / framed this way:** Sveza is a commodity plywood producer (a price-taker in a cyclical, export-hit market) buying its way up the value chain into higher-margin converted/branded packaging. Pharma packaging is the most regulated, stickiest, highest-spec sub-segment of folding cartons — long qualification cycles and GMP-adjacent requirements create switching costs. Buying an existing qualified producer (vs. building) buys **time and customer relationships**, which is the whole rationale for an undisclosed small-cap tuck-in.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- Domestic pharma/folding-carton converters: ЦФУ competes with the likes of Gotek, regional carton plants, and Sveza's own newly-acquired ММПОФ assets. Post-deal these become one cluster rather than rivals.
- Comparable **buyer behaviour** in this corpus is cross-border financial M&A ([[Poland's Zen buys PINbank as Asvio Bank acquires Motor-Bank]], [[TBC Bank Group acquires majority stake in Uzbek firm Billz]]) — different sector, but the same "buy capability rather than build" logic. The closest thematic neighbour is the Russia-exposure cluster: [[Citigroup to sell remaining Russian operations at $1.2bn loss]] shows foreign capital exiting while domestic groups like Sveza consolidate — two sides of the same sanctions-driven re-domiciling of Russian industrial ownership.
- **Why the lay of the land is this way:** with Western exits and import substitution, the structural opportunity for a cash-generative Russian group is to roll up fragmented domestic suppliers cheaply. Pharma packaging specifically benefits from localisation pressure (regulators favour domestic supply of drug packaging).

## [2] Company history / fit
- Sveza is the **world's largest birch-plywood producer** (~7 mills: Tyumen, St. Petersburg/Leningrad, Kostroma, Vologda, Sverdlovsk, Perm). Historically part of **Alexei Mordashov's Severgroup**; control was transferred to management (For Invest LLC) in **Oct 2022**, with Severgroup reportedly retaining ~49% (the post-sanctions ownership is contested in Russian press — treat "fully management-owned vs. still Mordashov-adjacent" as **open/disputed**).
- Diversification arc (consistent, dated): **KBK "КАМА"** pulp/paper & board mill in Perm krai (reported ~47 bln rub, LWC paper + folding boxboard used for drug packaging) → **ММПОФ Пэкэджинг + ММПОФ Ротогравюр** in Leningrad oblast (Jan 2025; 2023 revenue ≈7.9 bln + 4.2 bln rub; food/hygiene and tobacco cartons) → **ЦФУ** (2026-06; pharma cartons).
- **Why the company acts this way (analysis):** plywood is a low-multiple, cyclical, sanctions-exposed export commodity (EU/US closed birch-plywood markets after 2022). Moving downstream into paper → board → converted/branded pharma packaging is a classic **take-rate → software-like-multiple** migration: capture more of the value chain, sell into the resilient, regulated domestic pharma sector, and re-rate the group away from a pure commodity. ЦФУ is the most specialised, smallest, highest-spec piece of that ladder.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Not novel as an event** — it is the third step of an already-running roll-up, and the smallest. The real signal is *continuation/cadence* of Sveza's packaging build-out, not the asset itself.
- **Traction is real but modest:** ЦФУ is an operating, revenue-generating plant ("works with the largest Russian pharma producers"), so this is live business, not an announcement. But with ~100 staff and undisclosed (presumably small) terms, the financial value-add to a multi-mill group is marginal in the near term.
- **Where value is captured (analysis):** the durable margin sits in *qualified, regulator-approved pharma packaging supply* — high switching costs, localisation tailwind. The risk is that this remains a collection of small bolt-ons that never reach the scale/integration needed to actually re-rate the parent; "cluster synergy" is asserted, not demonstrated.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Expect Sveza to keep buying small/mid converters to fill out the ММПОФ packaging division (food, hygiene, tobacco, now pharma) — building a vertically integrated forest → paper/board → converted-packaging champion for the localising Russian market.
- **Risks / open questions:** (a) contested ownership and Mordashov/sanctions overhang could complicate financing and any future export ambitions; (b) commodity plywood cash flows funding the diversification are themselves under sanctions pressure; (c) integration/synergy claims unproven. **Why the market may go this way (analysis):** sanctions + import-substitution policy structurally favour domestic consolidation of packaging supply, so the strategy is directionally sound even if any single deal (like ЦФУ) is immaterial.
- **Counterintuitive second-order effect:** the more Sveza re-domiciles and verticalises domestically, the more it ties its fate to the Russian regulatory/sanctions regime — de-risking on supply-chain control but increasing single-jurisdiction concentration risk.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russia pharmaceutical-packaging market: ~$1.4bn projected revenue by 2030 at ~8.7% CAGR 2025–30 (per Grand View Research, "Russia Pharmaceutical Packaging Market," via grandviewresearch.com) — a small but above-GDP-growth niche inside the much larger, near-flat Russian paper-packaging market (~$3.7bn 2025, low-single-digit CAGR per Grand View / Mordor; figures vary by source, treat as order-of-magnitude only). Structure: fragmented downstream conversion (folding cartons + leaflets), consolidating upstream — leading forestry/board players are vertically integrating into conversion to secure supply and capture margin (per seamless.legal / law.asia M&A reviews, undated). "Why now": import substitution after the 2022 Western exit lets domestic producers capture share and pulls capital into downstream packaging; this deal is one such roll-up step (analysis).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: capacity/utilization of converting lines, customer concentration with pharma OEMs, proximity-to-customer (logistics cost on bulky low-value cartons), GMP/quality-standard compliance. Sveza is one of Russia's largest forestry groups (world's largest birch-plywood producer; 7 plywood mills + a pulp & paper mill) building a packaging division through serial M&A: KAMA pulp-and-paper (Perm), then MMPOF Packaging + MMPOF Rotogravure (Leningrad, completed 10 Jan 2025, total acquired assets est. ~₽12bn per KKMP/whatwood). ЦФУ (Obninsk, founded 2017, ~100 staff, folding cartons + leaflets for major Russian pharma) extends that into the pharma sub-segment. Position: incumbent forestry leader entering pharma-packaging as a new entrant — niche bolt-on, not market leadership in pharma (analysis). Moat candidate: vertical integration (own board feedstock → in-house conversion) + Central-Federal-District location near pharma clients; switching costs in qualified pharma packaging (audited/validated suppliers) are real but ЦФУ, not Sveza, holds those relationships.

**Comps & multiples.** Deal value and structure NOT disclosed — no purchase multiple computable (no data). 100% equity stake; ~100 employees; revenue/EBITDA undisclosed → EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, price-per-employee all `[UNSOURCED]`. Internal comps: the news corpus is a fintech radar; semantic search and grep surfaced NO comparable Russian industrial / packaging / pharma-M&A deals (top semsearch hits were unrelated bank/payments M&A). Closest in-base adjacency is the same group's prior packaging roll-up referenced in this story (KAMA, MMPOF), but those are not separate notes here. Sveza's own ₽12bn MMPOF deal (Jan 2025, ~3 sites) is the only sized precedent (per whatwood/KKMP) but covers different assets/scale, so it is a directional anchor, not a clean comp — distribution not computed, qualitative comparison only. Rich/cheap: cannot assess.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disclosure opacity** — no price, no multiple, no target financials; impossible to judge whether Sveza overpaid or what incremental EBITDA it bought. Pattern of undisclosed bolt-ons makes the diversification ROIC unverifiable to outsiders.
2. **Customer/qualification concentration & integration risk** — value sits in ЦФУ's validated relationships with "major Russian pharma producers"; pharma packaging requires supplier re-qualification, so mishandled integration or key-account loss erodes the thesis. ~100-person target is small enough that the synergy claim ("production + R&D") could be aspirational PR rather than realized.
3. **Macro/sectoral** — thesis leans on import-substitution demand and Russian pharma volumes; sanctions, rouble swings, and rate-driven capex constraints could compress margins on a low-value, freight-sensitive product. Roll-up acquirers also carry execution risk if deals outpace integration capacity.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Continued vertical-integration roll-up by Russian forestry majors into specialty converting — Sveza is assembling a diversified packaging platform (board → FMCG/tobacco → now pharma) under one roof (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if Sveza keeps acquiring CFD converters within ~12 months, this is a deliberate pharma-packaging platform play, not a one-off; trigger = next disclosed Sveza packaging acquisition or a stated divisional revenue target. What breaks it: no further deals and ЦФУ run as a standalone bolt-on — then it's diversification flavor, not consolidation.
