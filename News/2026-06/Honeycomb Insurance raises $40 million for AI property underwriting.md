Itai Ben-Zaken, Co-Founder And CEO Of Honeycomb Insurance

**Honeycomb Insurance** has secured an additional **$40 million** in funding as the digital insurer accelerates its expansion across the commercial real estate insurance market, a sector increasingly seeking data-driven underwriting solutions amid evolving risk conditions.

The financing round was led by **Zeev Ventures**, with participation from existing investor Ibex Investors and new investors Peakline, Alpha Partners, Meitar Partners, Practical VC and former San Francisco 49ers player Harris Barton. The latest investment brings Honeycomb’s total funding to $95 million.

The Chicago-based company plans to use the capital to expand into additional states, broaden its insurance product portfolio, enhance agent-facing technology and continue investing in its proprietary AI-powered underwriting platform. The funding comes as Honeycomb scales rapidly within the apartment building and condominium association insurance segments, where traditional underwriting models have faced growing challenges in assessing increasingly complex property risks.

Honeycomb exited 2025 with $275 million in gross written premium and has continued expanding both its geographic footprint and insured asset base. The company currently operates across 22 states, covering more than 65% of the U.S. population, and manages more than $100 billion in insured assets.

The investment reflects growing investor confidence in technology-enabled insurers that are modernizing commercial property coverage through automation, advanced analytics and artificial intelligence. As climate risks, rising replacement costs and shifting property market conditions reshape the insurance landscape, carriers capable of underwriting at a more granular level have gained increased attention from brokers, property owners and investors.

Honeycomb’s strategy centers on evaluating individual properties rather than relying on broad underwriting categories commonly used throughout the industry. The company’s platform analyzes hundreds of structured and unstructured data points, including environmental factors, geospatial information, property characteristics and historical performance metrics. Those inputs are combined with proprietary AI models and high-resolution imagery to assess risk on a building-by-building basis.

The approach allows Honeycomb to identify and price risks that may be overlooked or inaccurately assessed under traditional underwriting frameworks, particularly for well-maintained multifamily and condominium properties that may not fit conventional risk profiles.

“We are building Honeycomb to be the category leader in commercial real estate insurance,” said Itai Ben-Zaken, co-founder and chief executive officer of Honeycomb Insurance. “We didn’t add AI to a legacy offering. Our platform is centered on proprietary data and AI models to underwrite each property individually, with competitive and fair pricing and terms. This funding accelerates our expansion into new states and new product lines as we move toward that goal.”

The commercial real estate insurance market represents a significant opportunity for technology-driven carriers. Multifamily properties, condominium associations and other residential assets continue to require increasingly specialized coverage solutions as insurers adapt to rising catastrophe exposure, inflationary pressures and changing regulatory requirements.

Honeycomb’s AI-native infrastructure also supports portfolio-level risk management by helping the company monitor geographic concentration, adjust exposure and respond more quickly to changing market conditions. That capability has enabled the insurer to pursue growth while maintaining underwriting discipline, a balance that has proven difficult for many emerging insurance companies.

“Honeycomb has built something I rarely see — an insurance company that has scaled rapidly while maintaining a lean operation,” said Oren Zeev, founding partner of Zeev Ventures. “That combination is exceptionally rare in insurance, and it reflects the strength of both the team and the technology. The commercial property market is massive, underserved by legacy carriers, and Honeycomb is uniquely positioned to become a category leader.”

Founded by insurance and real estate industry veterans, Honeycomb specializes in property and casualty insurance products tailored to landlords and condominium associations. The company’s digital platform eliminates the need for physical inspections and supports both admitted and non-admitted insurance offerings, streamlining underwriting and policy issuance for property owners and insurance agents.

With fresh capital in place, Honeycomb is positioned to accelerate its national expansion and deepen its presence in a commercial real estate insurance market that continues to demand more flexible, data-driven underwriting solutions. As property owners navigate increasingly complex risk environments, the company is betting that AI-powered insurance infrastructure can deliver a competitive advantage in one of the industry’s largest and most fragmented segments.
