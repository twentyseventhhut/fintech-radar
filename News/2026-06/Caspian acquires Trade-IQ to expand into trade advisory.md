# Context-enrichment: Caspian acquires Trade-IQ to expand into trade advisory
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**Disambiguation (critical):** This **Caspian** is the **San Francisco AI customs/duty-optimization startup** (meetcaspian.com), founded 2024 by Justin Sherlock (CEO) and Matt Ebeweber, which raised a **$5.4M seed in July 2025 led by Primary Venture Partners** — see [[Caspian raises $5.4 million seed for AI duty optimization]]. It is **NOT** the institutional crypto-trading "Caspian" (Tora/Kenetic JV) nor Caspian Impact Investments. The target **Trade-IQ** (trade-iq.com) is a **boutique Michigan customs-consulting firm** founded by Shannon Bryant (LCB, ex-Harley-Davidson trade compliance) — and is **NOT** AKS iQ's "Trade iQ" TBML/AML bank-screening product (a pure name collision). Do not conflate.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On ~2026-06-24 Caspian announced it "acquired" Trade-IQ and, the same day, launched two products — **Trade Audit** and **Tariff Calculator** — reframing itself from duty-drawback recovery into a "full-lifecycle" trade play (recover → audit → optimize → advise). De-PR'd reality:
- **Every economic term is undisclosed**: price, cash/stock, structure (asset vs equity), earnout, headcount. Only one person — founder Shannon Bryant, who becomes "VP of Trade Advisory" — is named.
- **No tier-1 independent coverage.** The story exists only on wire/PR republishers (VentureBeat business desk, National Law Review *press-releases* path, citybiz). It is a **company-issued announcement, not independently verified M&A.**
- **→ Why structured this way:** A boutique with one named credentialed broker, no disclosed team/clients/revenue, plus same-day product launches = this reads as an **acqui-hire of a single customs veteran wrapped as a "platform expansion"** and timed for marketing momentum. The verb to watch: Caspian says it has "filed $40M in refunds" — *filed* ≠ *collected/approved by CBP*.
- **Metrics don't reconcile**: acquisition release cites ~$100B recoverable TAM and "$40M filed"; Caspian's own site says "$130B" and "$500M+ recovered." Unaudited, internally inconsistent.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Caspian's **real** lane is importer-facing customs/duty recovery, not bank trade finance. Direct comps: **Pax** (AI tariff refunds), **Zollback** (YC S24, AI duty drawback + brokerage), **Flexport** (customs suite + drawback algorithm), **SAP Global Trade Services**, **J.M. Rodgers** (legacy drawback). The corpus's adjacent trade-finance M&A — [[United Fintech acquires Trade Ledger]] and bank-facing names (Mitigram, Surecomp, Traydstream, Coriolis, [[CredAble appoints Ashutosh Taparia as fintech CEO|CredAble]]) — are a **different buyer (banks/LCs/AML)**, NOT direct rivals.
**→ Why this matters (second order):** The "trade advisory" framing borrows credibility from the much larger bank trade-finance world while Caspian actually competes against a cluster of similarly funded AI-drawback startups (Pax, Zollback) with the *same* pitch and no obvious moat. The differentiator claimed here is human advisory headcount — exactly the thing that doesn't scale and that an acqui-hire of one broker barely moves.

## [2] Company history / fit
Trajectory: founded 2024 → obtained own US customs-broker license + ABI connection (claimed in first ~15 months) → $5.4M seed July 2025 ([[Caspian raises $5.4 million seed for AI duty optimization]]) → June 2026 Trade-IQ acqui-hire + Trade Audit/Tariff Calculator launch. The fit is **logically coherent**: duty drawback is transactional/contingency-fee revenue; to earn a software multiple and land mid-market importers, Caspian needs recurring advisory + audit surfaces. Buying one credentialed LCB and bolting on calculators is the cheapest way to *claim* a full lifecycle.
**→ Why the company acts this way:** Contingency-fee recovery is lumpy and commoditizing fast (Pax/Zollback/Flexport all chasing it). Structural pressure → move "upstream" into proactive optimization/advisory to defend a recurring-revenue story — but this is the *announcement* of that pivot, not evidence it works.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Little is genuinely new. The acquisition adds human expertise, not technology; Trade Audit and Tariff Calculator are **brand-new, same-day, maturity unproven** (announced, not adopted). No client transfer, revenue, or usage disclosed. Compare the *pattern* to other small advisory/tech bolt-ons: [[Fiserv acquires Smith Consulting Group for advisory services]] (embedding advisory headcount) and [[EquiLend acquires Trading Apps for front-office automation]] (capability tuck-in) — both larger, with disclosed strategic logic. Here the value-add is **unverified**.
**→ Who captures the margin:** Duty recovery's margin sits with whoever owns the importer relationship + filing automation. An acqui-hire of one broker doesn't durably capture that; it adds a name and a credential. The recurring-revenue claim (advisory/audit) is the part that *would* justify a multiple — and it's exactly the part with zero disclosed traction.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Backdrop is favorable to the *narrative*: 2025–2026 tariff volatility (US tariff/duty churn) makes "duty optimization" topical, which is why several startups are funded here. Expect Caspian to push the lifecycle story toward a follow-on raise; a $5.4M seed is thin to fund acquisitions + multi-product expansion, hinting the Trade-IQ deal was nominal/all-stock.
**→ Counterintuitive second-order:** The tariff-news tailwind that makes this story sellable is the same force commoditizing the category — every competitor gets the same tailwind, so the durable winner is decided by distribution/automation, not by acquiring scarce advisory headcount. The acqui-hire may *raise* fragility (key-person dependence on one VP) rather than reduce it.

## Market Research

_Market layer on top of context-enrichment. Skeptical analyst; cite or "no data". This Caspian = SF AI customs/duty-optimization startup, NOT crypto-trading Caspian._

**Sector & drivers.** Two adjacent sizings frame Caspian's space, neither a clean TAM for it. (a) **Duty-drawback services** (the recovery business Caspian started in): ~**$21.96bn (2024) → $43.69bn by 2033, ~3.1% CAGR** per Business Research Insights — but this is a *services* market (broker/consulting fees), low-growth and commoditizing, not a software TAM (BRI, via businessresearchinsights.com, as of 2025). (b) **Trade-management software** (the "platform" Caspian is *re-rating into* via Trade Audit/Tariff Calculator): only **~$1.31bn (2025) → ~$2.11bn by 2030, ~9.9% CAGR**, trade-compliance the largest slice at ~30.8% (The Business Research Company / Mordor, as of 2025–26). The headline "$100B+/$130B unclaimed duty" figures Caspian cites are an *opportunity pool*, not a captured market; an independent estimate puts unclaimed tariff refunds at **~$15bn/yr with ~80–85% of eligible importers never filing** (J.M. Rodgers / Pax, via jmrodgers.com & paxai.com, 2025). **Structure:** fragmented and bifurcated — legacy contingency-fee brokers (J.M. Rodgers, Livingston) on one side, a cluster of newly-funded AI-drawback startups on the other; near-zero entry barriers beyond a customs-broker license + ABI connection, which is exactly why ≥4 lookalikes exist. **Why now:** 2025–26 tariff churn raises both the refund pool *and* the number of funded entrants chasing it — the tailwind is shared, not proprietary; second-order, it accelerates commoditization of the recovery layer and pushes everyone "upstream" to advisory/audit (the move Caspian is announcing).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: refunds *filed vs collected/approved by CBP*, recovery rate vs traditional (peers claim "+15–20%"), turnaround time, and (for the SaaS story) recurring ARR/attach — Caspian discloses none of these in audited form. Players & basis of competition (product+automation+distribution, not price): **Pax** (YC; **$4.5M seed**, Initialized Capital, Flexport-linked angels; claims ~+15% recovery), **Zollback** (YC S24; **launched 2026-03-17**, claims "$10M refunds processed", revenue tripling, +15–20% recovery in 10–15 days), **Flexport** (drawback inside a full customs suite — distribution moat), **SAP GTS / Oracle / Thomson Reuters / E2open** (enterprise GTM incumbents), **J.M. Rodgers / Livingston** (legacy; Livingston launched AI duty recovery Jan 2025). **Caspian's position: niche / catching-up.** It has the *largest disclosed seed* of the AI-drawback pure-plays ($5.4M vs Pax $4.5M) but the same pitch, and its claimed differentiator after this deal — *human advisory headcount* (one LCB, Shannon Bryant) — is the one input that doesn't scale and is the weakest moat (intangible/key-person, not network effects or switching costs) `(analysis)`.

**Comps & multiples.** No deal economics disclosed (price, stock/cash, earnout, target revenue) → **EV/Revenue, P/S, price-per-claim all "no data"; multiple not computable.** Sanity peers for the *narrative* (all private, round valuations, not market caps):
- [[Caspian raises $5.4 million seed for AI duty optimization]] — $5.4M seed, Jul 2025 (Primary VP). The same balance sheet now funding M&A + 2 product launches → supports the nominal/all-stock acqui-hire read.
- Pax — $4.5M seed (Initialized) — closest direct comp; near-identical stage/model `[UNSOURCED post-money]`.
- [[Zamp raises $17 million for AI sales tax compliance platform]] — **$30M total** (Acrew + Thomson Reuters Ventures), 120+ firms, $300M+ remitted — the *adjacent-vertical proof* of the "AI + humans owning the full compliance lifecycle" thesis Caspian is borrowing, but at ~5–6x the capital and with disclosed traction; post-money `[UNSOURCED]`.
- [[Outpost raises $17.5m Series A for cross-border commerce]] — $17.5M Series A, cross-border tax/compliance infra — adjacent, larger, Series-A-stage.
Internal trade-context anchor: [[Global trade hit record $33 trillion in 2024, says UNCTAD]]. **Read:** distribution not computed (no disclosed figures for the subject); qualitatively, Caspian is *seed-stage among seed-stage peers*, with Zamp/Outpost showing what a funded, traction-backed version of this thesis looks like one stage up.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disintermediation / shared-tailwind commoditization** — Pax, Zollback, Flexport and legacy brokers chase the identical refund pool with the identical AI pitch; the tariff tailwind benefits all equally, so margin accrues to whoever owns importer distribution + filing automation, not to an acqui-hire of one broker. Caspian risks being a feature, not a platform.
2. **Key-person fragility from the "moat"** — the differentiator is one credentialed VP (Bryant); advisory headcount doesn't scale and concentrates risk in a single hire — the acqui-hire may *raise* fragility, not reduce it.
3. **Capital adequacy / re-rating risk** — a $5.4M seed funding acquisitions + two same-day launches in a low-growth (~3% CAGR) services market while pitching a higher-growth (~10% CAGR) software multiple. If the recurring advisory/audit revenue (zero disclosed traction) doesn't materialize, the valuation story breaks and a follow-on is needed on a thin balance sheet.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a *narrative re-rating attempt* (recovery-services → trade-software multiple), not a consolidation wave — the category is too early/fragmented to consolidate. **Falsifiable thesis:** if Caspian can't show disclosed recurring ARR or a named multi-client base for Trade Audit/Advisory within ~2–3 quarters, the "full-lifecycle platform" framing collapses back to lumpy contingency-fee recovery. **Triggers to watch:** a Caspian follow-on raise (would validate the pivot or expose the thin-seed read), Pax/Zollback raising larger rounds (would confirm commoditization), or any Flexport/SAP bundling of AI drawback (would disintermediate the pure-plays).
