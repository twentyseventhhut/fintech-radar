# Context-enrichment: Russian central bank maps hybrid payment-instrument usage
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
The Bank of Russia published its **annual** sociological survey "Attitude of the population of the Russian Federation to various means of payment — 2025 results" (results_2025.pdf, released ~2026-06-23). This is a **recurring** publication: prior editions exist as results_2024.pdf, results_2023.pdf. So the genre is not new; the **data period (calendar 2025) is** — hence fresh, not a reprint.

De-PR'd headline numbers (vs 2024):
- 94% hold payment cards (92% in 2024); 50% have a virtual card; debit card is the #1 cashless method (79% use it; 82% of cardholders pay with it).
- Alternative rails: SBP QR pay 38%, mobile/online-bank transfers 36%, e-wallet 16%.
- Cash, counterintuitively, **strengthened on every consumer-preference metric**: "prefer cash for everyday payments" 24%→27%; "primarily pay cash" 24%→27%; "can't imagine life without cash" 43%→48%; "fully gave up cash" 10%→5%; "never pay cash" 10%→3-5%; cash savings 32%→36%. Average single cash purchase rose 1,731→1,999 RUB (+268).

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the post framing ("after years of digitalization we see a hybrid model — digital for convenience, cash for reliability") is essentially **accurate de-PR'd**, but it buries the actually interesting second-order fact: at the **macro/transaction** level Russia is among the most cashless economies on earth — cashless share of retail turnover reached **88%** in 2025 (85.8% in 2024), 88.9% by latest CBR data, with card share *falling* as SBP/QR/wallet/biometric "alternative" rails grow to ~14% of digital volume. Yet this **attitudinal** survey shows cash *attachment rising*. The gap = a structural insight, not a contradiction: cash is being squeezed out of transaction *count* but retained as a **resilience/store-of-value hedge** (the survey's own framing: 41% withdraw cash "in case cashless is unavailable"). That redundancy demand is plausibly amplified by 2025's payment outages and internet-shutdown episodes the CBR itself flagged. The "hybrid" label is real, but driven by **distrust/resilience**, not consumer indifference.

## [1] Competitors / peers
This is a regulator demand-side study, so "peers" = other central-bank payment-habit surveys (a recurring genre in the corpus):
- [[Card payments remain top choice for South Koreans, BOK survey]] — BOK: cards 46.2%, cash 15.9% of transactions (2025).
- [[Chile central bank study maps cash and card payment habits]] — BCCh "Diario de Pagos", cash dominant in informal/transit.
- [[Colombia central bank survey shows electronic payments growth]] — Banrep: transfers most-used digital instrument.
- [[Connecting the Dots how consumers pay in Brazil]] and [[This Week in Fintech Pix's reach and Cumbuca's access]] — Pix overtook cash; sharp cash decline.
- [[Cash usage holds steady at 14% of US payment transactions]] and [[Survey finds 85% of Americans want cash acceptance laws]] — US: cash ~14% of transactions but politically sticky.

**Position:** Russia sits at the **far digital end** of this peer set on transaction share (~88% cashless vs ~84% US, vs cash still ~16% in Korea), but its consumer *cash attachment* is **rising**, which is closer to the US "right-to-cash" sentiment than to Brazil's near-total Pix migration. **Why the lay of the land:** Russia leapfrogged via state-built rails (SBP, NSPK/Mir) after 2022 sanctions cut Visa/Mastercard — a top-down digitalization that pushed transaction share up fast, but the same geopolitical/operational fragility (outages, sanctions, internet shutdowns) is what keeps the cash-as-insurance demand alive. So Russia is simultaneously more cashless (rails) and more cash-attached (sentiment) than Western peers.

## [2] Company history / fit
The CBR runs an annual cadence of payment-system publications. In this same June 2026 window it also released a **new** companion metric — payment-service satisfaction — see sibling note [[Russian central bank measures payment-service satisfaction metric]] (results_assessment_2025.pdf), and is rolling out the **digital ruble** (mass adoption mandated from 1 Sep 2026; see [[Финтехно Russian central bank sets digital ruble payroll tariffs]]). **Why the CBR acts this way:** demand-side attitudinal data feeds two policy priorities — (a) cash-circulation infrastructure planning (banknote denominations, ATM/cash-on-till coverage), and (b) building the evidence base and consumer-readiness narrative ahead of the digital-ruble rollout and the universal-QR push. The survey is an instrument of regulatory prioritization, not a market event.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Novelty: low.** It is the next annual edition of an existing series; the figures are incremental year-on-year moves, not a discontinuity. The only genuinely **new** analytical signal is the **divergence** between rising macro cashless share (88%) and rising consumer cash attachment — and even that the CBR itself has reported before in softer form. **Value-add / who captures it:** this is regulator survey output, so there is no commercial "stack" or margin to capture; the value is informational. For fintechs the durable read-through is that **SBP/QR (38%) is now firmly the #2 instrument behind debit cards and ahead of in-app bank transfers**, confirming the state-rail disintermediation of card networks — the same trend the macro data shows as card-share decline. **Traction is real and measured** (these are usage %s, not announcements), but it's confirmatory, not directional.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
The CBR will repeat this survey annually and use it to (a) defend cash-infrastructure investment despite 88% cashless share — Nabiullina/CBR have publicly said cash will not disappear, share may fall toward ~10% of retail while absolute cash volume still grows; (b) underpin the digital-ruble mass rollout from 1 Sep 2026 (retailers >120M RUB revenue must accept) and the universal QR (SBP + BNPL + later digital ruble). **Why the market goes this way / second-order:** the more the state pushes a single mandated digital rail (digital ruble + universal QR), the **stronger** the cash-as-insurance reflex documented here is likely to get — concentration of rails increases systemic fragility and surveillance concerns, which sustains, not eliminates, cash demand. Counterintuitive read: a more digital Russia is also a more cash-hedged one. Risks to any clean "cashless" narrative: outages, sanctions, internet shutdowns, and the privacy overhang of a CBDC.

## Market Research

**Sector & drivers.** This is a regulator demand-side survey, not a company event, so the "sector" is the Russian retail-payments rails. Size/structure: cashless share of retail turnover reached ~88% in 2025 (85.8% in 2024; Nabiullina cited 87.5% at end-Q1 2025, CBR targeting ~90% by year-end — per Interfax/TAdviser, as of 2025), placing Russia among the most cashless large economies. The rails are state-controlled and concentrated: Mir holds ~80% of the card market after Visa/Mastercard's 2022 exit, and SBP (Faster Payments System) processed ~13.4bn transactions / 69.5tn RUB in 2024 with ~88.6% of C2C transfers running through it (per AFA, via central-banking/Bisq sources). Entry barriers are near-absolute: rails are built/owned by CBR-NSPK, not private networks. Two drivers + "why now": (1) top-down state digitalization post-sanctions (Mir/SBP/digital ruble) keeps pushing transaction share up; (2) operational/geopolitical fragility (outages, internet shutdowns) sustains a cash-as-insurance reflex — the survey's own 41% "withdraw cash in case cashless is unavailable" quantifies it. "Why now": the data lands ~10 weeks before the mandated digital-ruble rollout (1 Sep 2026), making it consumer-readiness evidence, not a market signal.

**Competitive landscape.** For an attitudinal survey the relevant KPIs are instrument-share and adoption %s (not GPV/take-rate). Per the survey: debit card #1 cashless method (79% use; 82% of cardholders pay with it), SBP QR #2 at 38%, mobile/online-bank transfers 36%, e-wallet 16%; 94% hold cards, 50% a virtual card. "Players" here = peer central-bank payment-habit surveys (recurring corpus genre), basis of comparison = transaction-share and cash-attachment trajectory:
- [[Card payments remain top choice for South Koreans, BOK survey]] — cards 46.2%, cash 15.9% of transactions (2025).

**Risk flags.**

Falsifiable thesis: after the 1 Sep 2026 digital-ruble + universal-QR rollout, the 2026/2027 edition of this survey will show cash-attachment metrics flat-to-up, not down — what would break it is a clean YoY decline in "can't imagine life without cash" (48%) alongside rising digital-ruble usage.
