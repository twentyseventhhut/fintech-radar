**[0] What happened.** A "Connecting the Dots in Fintech" digest item (2026-06-26) re-surfaces the headline numbers from Stripe's **2025 annual letter**: Stripe processed **$1.9 trillion** in total payment volume (TPV) in 2025, equal to **~1.6% of global GDP**, now powering **5M+ businesses**, including **80% of the Nasdaq 100** and **90% of the Dow Jones Industrial Average**. Note: this is a newsletter re-circulation of data Stripe first disclosed in **February 2026**; it is not new disclosure.

- [[Stripe reaches $159 billion valuation in employee tender offer]] (2026-02-24) — the tender-offer/valuation side of the same letter.

**[2] Numbers (sourced, not invented).** TPV $1.9T, **+34% YoY** vs $1.4T in 2024 (Stripe-stated). Valuation **$159B** (€135B) via Feb 2026 tender — up **~74%** from the **$91.5B** Feb-2025 mark. Stripe states it was "robustly profitable" in 2025. Revenue suite (Billing/Invoicing/Tax/Sigma) on a **$1B annual run-rate**. New 2025 business cohort: **57% non-US**, growing **~50% faster** than the 2024 cohort. Stablecoin volume ~doubled to ~$400B (Bridge platform). Sources: stripe.com/newsroom/news/stripe-2025-update; stripe.com/annual-updates/2025; CNBC 2026-02-24; TechCrunch 2026-02-24.

**[3] Why it matters.** Stripe remains the private benchmark for global online-payments scale. $1.9T TPV is materially larger than Adyen's full-year processed volume (~€1.4T) and well above PayPal's quarterly TPV (~$475B in Q4-25). The 1.6%-of-GDP framing and Nasdaq-100/Dow penetration signal deep enterprise entrenchment, not just SMB/startup reach.

**[4] What to watch.** No audited financials (private; "robustly profitable" is unquantified). TPV growth decelerating in absolute % is plausible at this scale. IPO timing remains open — the recurring tender offers are a deliberate substitute for going public.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
**Red-team questions**

1. Is this story *new*? The $1.9T figure was disclosed in Stripe's Feb-2026 annual letter and is already in this vault ([[Stripe surpasses $1.5 trillion total payment volume in 2025]]). The June digest is a re-circulation — does it add anything beyond newsletter reach?
2. "TPV" ≠ revenue. $1.9T is volume Stripe *processes*, not money Stripe *keeps*. Stripe's net revenue is a small take-rate fraction of this. Does any reader conflate the two?
3. Is "1.6% of global GDP" a meaningful KPI or a marketing frame? GDP is value-added; TPV is gross flow (double-counts intermediated transactions). The two are not comparable categories.
4. "Robustly profitable" is unaudited and unquantified. On what basis (GAAP net income? FCF? adjusted EBITDA?) and verified by whom?
5. The headline says "5 million businesses" but the letter says 5M+ "directly or via platforms." How many are direct Stripe merchants vs. counted through platform customers (e.g., Shopify-style)? Risk of inflated count.
6. Is the $1.9T net of refunds, chargebacks, and reversals, or gross? Stripe doesn't disclose the netting methodology publicly.
7. The +34% YoY is Stripe-reported. Without audited TPV reconciliation, is the 2024 $1.4T base itself comparable (same definition, same FX treatment)?
8. Valuation is a *private tender* mark ($159B) set with insider participants (Thrive, Coatue, a16z) who are existing holders — not an arm's-length public market. How much is this a true price vs. a managed liquidity event?
9. Nasdaq-100/Dow penetration: does "powers 80% of Nasdaq 100" mean those firms run material payment flow through Stripe, or merely use *some* Stripe product (e.g., one subsidiary, one billing line)? Likely the latter.
10. FX/mix effect: with 57% of new businesses non-US, how much of the $1.9T growth is volume vs. a weaker-USD translation tailwind?
11. Stablecoin "~$400B doubled" — is this Stripe-processed or Bridge-platform throughput that includes large self-custody/treasury transfers that aren't real commerce?
12. Competitive read: Adyen's volume grew only ~8% in 2025 and its stock fell on a cautious outlook. Is Stripe's +34% a share gain, or category-wide online-payments deceleration that Stripe is simply outrunning for now?
13. Why no IPO? Recurring tenders let Stripe set its own valuation and avoid public disclosure/scrutiny. Is the absence of an IPO a strength signal or an avoidance of audited transparency?
14. Source independence: the headline numbers all trace to Stripe's own letter. Is there any third-party-verified figure here at all?

**Importance: 2/5 — rationale.** The underlying event (Stripe's 2025 annual letter) is genuinely significant, but this specific note is a *duplicate re-circulation* of data already captured in the vault four months earlier (Feb 2026). No new disclosure, no audited financials, and the metrics are self-reported marketing frames. Material as a reference data point, low as net-new signal.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector context — global online/enterprise payments.** Stripe sits at the top of the merchant-acquiring / payment-facilitation stack for internet-native and increasingly enterprise commerce. The 2025 read-through is bifurcation: Stripe and the platform-scale acquirers keep compounding volume while legacy/consumer-skewed players decelerate. Stripe's $1.9T TPV (+34% YoY, Stripe-reported) is an outlier growth rate at this absolute scale.

**Comparables (full-year / latest 2025, sourced):**
- **Adyen (AMS:ADYEN):** FY2025 processed volume ~€1.4T, up only ~8% YoY (vs +33% in 2024); net revenue €1.09B in H2, +20%. Shares fell sharply (reports of up to ~20%) on a cautious 2026 outlook and softer H2 volumes. Closest direct enterprise-acquiring comp; its deceleration sharpens Stripe's relative momentum.
- **PayPal (NASDAQ:PYPL):** Q4-2025 TPV ~$475.1B, +9% reported (Q3 ~$458.1B, +8%). Larger consumer/branded-checkout franchise, structurally slower growth.
- **Block (NYSE:XYZ/SQ):** Square GPV ~$67.2B in Q4-2025, +~10-12% YoY; full-year gross-profit growth raised to ~15% (Cash App gross profit +24%, Square +9%). SMB/consumer-skewed; not a like-for-like TPV comp.
- **Stripe (private):** $159B valuation (Feb-2026 tender), +~74% off the $91.5B Feb-2025 mark; prior public marker $106.7B (Sep-2025).

**Read:** On growth-adjusted scale Stripe is outperforming the listed peer set. Adyen is the cleanest comp and is decelerating; PayPal and Block grow in high-single/low-double digits. Stripe's +34% implies share gains plus mix (57% of new logos non-US, faster-growing cohort).

**Risk flags:**
1. **Disclosure asymmetry.** All Stripe figures are self-reported and unaudited ("robustly profitable" unquantified) — not comparable to peers' SEC/IFRS filings.
2. **Private-mark valuation.** $159B is an insider-tender mark (Thrive/Coatue/a16z are existing holders), not an arm's-length public price; rerating risk at any eventual IPO.
3. **Category deceleration signal.** Adyen's cautious outlook and PayPal's high-single-digit growth suggest the online-payments category is slowing; Stripe's +34% may compress in 2026.
4. **TPV vs. economics gap.** Headline volume tells nothing about take-rate compression, which is the live competitive battleground in enterprise acquiring.
5. **Concentration / platform-counting.** "5M+ businesses" and "80% of Nasdaq 100" likely include thin/platform-mediated usage; revenue concentration in large accounts is undisclosed.
6. **Stablecoin optimism.** The ~$400B stablecoin volume and Tempo/Bridge bets are early and regulatorily exposed; counting them as commerce growth is generous.

Sources: stripe.com/newsroom/news/stripe-2025-update; adyen.com H2-2025 results; PYMNTS; CNBC; TechCrunch; SEC 8-K filings (PYPL, Block).
