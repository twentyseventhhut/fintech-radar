# Context-enrichment: MyOffice reports nearly 9bn rouble loss
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 2026-06-29 RBC reported that **New Cloud Technologies (NCT / НОТ, brand "МойОфис")** — the Russian Microsoft-Office substitute — posted a **2025 net loss of ~RUB 8.82bn (~"nearly 9bn")**. Key de-PR'd facts:
- This is a **restated** figure. The company first filed a 2025 net loss of **~RUB 4bn**, then **republished corrected accounts on 2026-06-18** that **roughly doubled the loss to 8.82bn**. So the headline "9bn" is an accounting restatement, not a fresh deterioration in trading.
- **The entire incremental ~RUB 4.9bn comes from a one-off impairment**: a revaluation of intangible assets (capitalised R&D/IP) written down by ~4.9bn and routed through "Other expenses." Strip that out and the operating loss is ~4bn.
- **Revenue ≈ RUB 1.02bn, down ~49.5% YoY** (from ~2bn in 2024). The revenue halving is the real economic signal; the 8.82bn is mostly a non-cash IP write-down.
- The company frames the restatement as "technical accounting correction... no impact on operational/strategic plans." That framing is self-serving but partly true: the cash burn is the 4bn operating loss, not the 8.82bn headline.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** a ~4.9bn intangible impairment is management conceding that the capitalised value of MyOffice's own software/IP is no longer supportable at book value — i.e. the future cash flows those products were expected to generate have been marked down. That is a more damning admission than the loss number itself: it says the import-substitution demand thesis that justified the R&D capitalisation has weakened. The "technical" framing buries that.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Russian office-suite market (~RUB 40bn in 2023 per Stream Consulting; Microsoft still ~a quarter even post-sanctions; R7 + MyOffice together ~20%):
- **Р7 / "Р7-Офис" (R7-Office)** — the direct domestic rival and the winner. **R7 revenue ~RUB 3.28bn in 2025** (vs ~3.2bn 2024), i.e. **~3x MyOffice's revenue** and beating MyOffice on revenue for the **second year running**. In Feb 2025 **VK (VK Tech) bought 25% of R7 for ~RUB 2.5bn**, giving R7 a deep-pocketed strategic backer and distribution.
- **Microsoft (legacy)** — still installed/available; management itself blames "continued ability to buy Microsoft Office" for weak substitution demand. The incumbent never fully left, which is the structural drag on both Russian vendors.
- **MyOffice market share fell from ~5% to ~3% (by value) in 2025.**

**Why the lay of the land is this way:** R7 won by being the lighter-weight, more-deployable, OnlyOffice-derived suite with broader compatibility, then secured VK as backer/distributor (RuStore, VK ecosystem). MyOffice, by contrast, over-invested in an ambitious in-house stack (mail, messenger, collaboration) and is now retreating. Second-order: VK's stake makes R7 the consolidation anchor of Russian office software; MyOffice risks becoming the distressed asset, not the consolidator.

## [2] Company history / fit
- MyOffice was the early **flagship of Russian Microsoft-Office import substitution**, heavily promoted into the public sector after 2022.
- **Ownership:** Kaspersky Lab ~**68.8%**, Andrey Cheglakov ~**22.7%**, founder Dmitry Komissarov ~**5.0%** (remainder minor holders). Kaspersky is the controlling parent — relevant because a deep-pocketed strategic owner can keep funding losses, but also signals MyOffice is now a captive bet inside Kaspersky rather than an independent growth story.
- **Trajectory of losses:** net loss ~RUB 1.2bn (2024) → first reported ~4bn (2025) → restated **8.82bn (2025)**. Revenue trajectory: ~2bn (2024) → ~1.02bn (2025), i.e. revenue **halved** the same year losses tripled+.
- **Restructuring underway:** development **halted on Mailion, МойОфис Mail, Squadus and Documents Online**; **staff cuts** notified to employees by email in mid-May 2026.

**Why the company acts this way:** structural pressure — substitution demand and state-sector budgets came in below the level needed to amortise a broad in-house product portfolio. The IP write-down + product shutdowns + layoffs are the same decision viewed three ways: management is **shrinking back to the core office suite** and abandoning the "Russian Microsoft 365 ecosystem" ambition (mail/messenger/collab) it couldn't fund.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Not a new event in substance** — it is a **restatement of an already-known 2025 result** (the ~4bn loss was reported earlier, e.g. May 2026). The genuinely new datum is the **+4.9bn intangible impairment / 8.82bn restated figure**, published 2026-06-18 and re-reported by RBC 2026-06-29.
- **Traction (negative):** revenue down ~50%, share down 5%→3%, products discontinued, layoffs. This is contraction, not growth.
- **Value-add of the news:** the impairment is the substantive signal — capitalised software/IP marked down ~4.9bn = management no longer believes those assets will earn out. That is a real, not cosmetic, statement about MyOffice's competitive position vs R7/Microsoft.

**Why it matters, deeper:** the question shifts from "how big is the loss" to **"is MyOffice still a going concern as an independent franchise, or a Kaspersky-subsidised runner-up being wound back to its core?"** The product shutdowns + write-down + layoffs all point to the latter. Who captures the value in Russian office software is increasingly **R7/VK**, not MyOffice.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Management:** restatement is "technical," strategy unchanged. Sceptically read: continued retrenchment to the core suite, reliant on Kaspersky funding and mandated public-sector substitution.
- **Sector tailwind that didn't materialise as hoped:** import-substitution mandates exist but Microsoft remains de-facto available and budgets tightened — the policy push is not translating into the revenue the capitalised R&D assumed.
- **Risks:** further impairments if revenue keeps falling; going-concern dependence on Kaspersky; losing the public-sector tender battle to R7/VK; talent loss from layoffs.
- **Counterintuitive second-order:** a deep-pocketed parent (Kaspersky) can keep a loss-making substitute alive far past the point a standalone firm would fold — so the "8.82bn loss" is less an existential signal than a marker that MyOffice is becoming a **strategic captive subsidy**, not a market-winning product, while R7+VK consolidate the actual market.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian office-software (productivity-suite) sub-segment of the import-substitution software market. Market size per BusinesStat: ~RUB 77bn in 2024, +18% YoY, roughly doubled from RUB 38bn (2020) (per BusinesStat via TAdviser, as of Mar-2025 — note this is the *whole* office-software market, not just suites). Pre-war foreign share was dominant: per J'son & Partners, in 2020 ~77.5% was foreign (MS Office, WPS, etc.), ~15.5% free (LibreOffice/OpenOffice), ~7% domestic — so the post-2022 substitution wave is the entire bull case. Structure: state-mandated demand (Mincifry deadlines for gossector / critical-infrastructure migration off foreign software), Russian-software registry gating subsidised credit and procurement eligibility. **Why now / the headwind:** the substitution tailwind is fading — analysts flagged saturation after 2025 once most of the addressable gossector base has migrated (per TAdviser). MyOffice's own numbers are the clearest evidence: revenue did not just slow, it *fell* (see below), which is consistent with a one-off migration bulge rather than recurring growth.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs for a SaaS/licence office vendor: recurring revenue/ARR, seat count, gross/EBITDA margin, registry inclusion. Key Russian players: **R7 (P7-Office)**, **MyOffice (New Cloud Technologies / NOT)**, plus AlterOffice, Tsirkon; free LibreOffice/OpenOffice remain a zero-price alternative that caps pricing power. Basis of competition: government/enterprise procurement, registry status, format compatibility, ecosystem bundling — not consumer brand. **Recent moves:** R7 has out-earned MyOffice on revenue two years running — R7 FY2024 revenue >RUB 3.2bn (+12.6%) vs MyOffice ~RUB 2bn, gap widening from 1.5x (2023) to 1.6x (2024) (per ComNews, Apr-2025). Strategic event: RBC reported (Jan-2026) Astra Group is in talks to buy a controlling (>50%) stake in NOT from majority owner Kaspersky (68.8%) "after long attempts to bring it to profit" — **both Astra and Kaspersky publicly denied any sale talks** (Kommersant), so treat as unconfirmed `(hypothesis)`. **MyOffice position: losing share / catching down** — niche #2 behind R7 and shrinking. Moat is weak: switching costs exist (format lock-in, certified deployments) but are eroded by R7's lead and by free suites; no network effect, scale disadvantage vs R7.

**Comps & multiples.** Protagonist (NOT / MyOffice, FY2025, per RBC via Sostav/Habr, 29-Jun-2026): revenue **RUB 1.02bn, –49.5% YoY**; net loss **RUB 8.82bn** — *restated up* from an initially-reported RUB 4.02bn loss. The restatement is the actual story: intangible assets were written down from RUB 10.16bn to RUB 5.29bn (a ~RUB 4.87bn impairment), which is what blew the loss out; the company calls it a "technical procedure" (de-PR: an ~RUB 4.9bn intangibles write-down is an impairment, not a clerical edit). Debt to majority owner Kaspersky ~**RUB 24.96bn**, +33% YoY — i.e. the loss is funded by intercompany debt, not external capital. Loss/revenue ratio ~8.6x; equity multiples are meaningless on a private, deeply loss-making, sub-scale name → P/E, EV/EBITDA = **no data** (private, EBITDA-negative).
- **Internal comps:** none in base — semsearch and `grep "company/myoffice"` return only this note; no Russian office-suite or import-substitution peers indexed. Distribution not computed; qualitative comparison only.
- **External peer scale (per AKM/VK/ComNews, FY2025/FY2024):** [[R7]] (P7-Office) — closest comp, FY2024 rev >RUB 3.2bn, ~1.6x MyOffice and growing. [[VK Tech]] — FY2025 rev RUB 18.8bn (+38%), segment EBITDA RUB 4.8bn (+22%), 31.9k clients (2.7x) — profitable, ~18x MyOffice's revenue (broader cloud/B2B, not pure office). [[Astra Group]] (potential acquirer) — FY2025 IFRS rev >RUB 20bn (+18%), EBITDA >RUB 8bn, net profit ~RUB 6bn, MOEX-listed. The contrast is the point: comparable Russian software vendors (Astra, VK Tech) are *growing and profitable*; MyOffice is shrinking and loss-making — it is the outlier, not the sector.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Demand saturation / revenue reversal.** Revenue halved YoY (–49.5%) — the import-substitution one-off has rolled off and there is no evidence of recurring/renewal revenue replacing it. Second-order: undermines any "structural domestic-software growth" thesis for this name.
2. **Subsidy / single-sponsor dependence.** The business is sustained by ~RUB 25bn (+33%) of intercompany debt to Kaspersky, not by operating cash or external investors; Kaspersky reportedly wants out. If the sponsor stops funding, going-concern is at risk regardless of registry status.
3. **Competitive displacement by R7 + free suites.** R7 has out-earned MyOffice two years running with a widening gap; LibreOffice caps pricing. A #2 that is shrinking while the leader grows has a structural, not cyclical, problem.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` Read-through for the Russian office-suite segment: the substitution wave is a pull-forward, not a perpetual annuity — vendors that didn't win the gossector land-grab (MyOffice) now face flat-to-declining demand while leaders (R7) consolidate. Likely path is **consolidation**: a strategic (Astra) absorbing a distressed asset for the product/registry slot rather than its P&L. **Falsifiable thesis:** MyOffice cannot return to growth standalone; watch for (a) a confirmed Astra/NOT transaction, or (b) a fresh Kaspersky capital injection vs. a funding cut — either resolves the going-concern question. **What breaks it:** a genuine revenue re-acceleration in FY2026 (renewals + new mandated-migration cohorts) would show the decline was a reporting/timing artefact, not saturation.

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Verdict (headline read).** MISS / deep deterioration · revenue ₽1.02bn (-49.5% YoY) · net loss ₽8.82bn (restated; vs ~₽1.2bn loss in 2024 — loss widened ~7x) · key drivers: import-substitution demand burn-out + a June-2026 restatement that ~doubled the loss (intangible-asset reclass) · no guidance, only a "sustainable operating model by 2026" restructuring promise. This is a private RU software entity (RAS statutory accounts in the state reporting registry), not an IR/EDGAR filer — figures are from the resubmitted statutory report via RBC/Habr/CNews/Sostav.

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Revenue ₽1.02bn, **-49.5% YoY** (2024 ≈ ₽2.04bn) — revenue roughly halved.
- Net loss **₽8.82bn** (restated 18 Jun 2026), up from a net loss of ~₽1.2bn in 2024 — loss widened ~7x. The originally filed FY2025 loss was ₽4.02bn (already ~3.3x the 2024 loss); the restatement more than doubled it.
- Net margin deeply negative: net loss / revenue ≈ -864% (₽-8.82bn on ₽1.02bn).
- Negative net assets ₽13.7bn (2024: ₽9.7bn) — equity hole deepening.
- Debt to majority owner Kaspersky Lab ₽24.96bn, +33% YoY (2024 short-term obligations ~₽3bn) — the company is funded by its 68.8% shareholder, not by its market.
- Headcount 1,013 (2024: 1,070); mass layoffs announced May 2026 (a former-employee chat reportedly >600 people; "many departments cut almost in full"). Management comp rose ₽59.6m → ₽70.5m.

**By segment / driver.** Top line collapsed because the corporate-software market "passed the peak of 'hot' import substitution" — customers turned conservative, postponed projects and cut purchase volumes. MyOffice's monetary market share fell 5% → 3% over 2025, trailing R7-Office (~10%) and the Yandex/VK ecosystems; this is the second consecutive year it lost the revenue race to R7. The loss is driven less by operating burn than by the restatement: intangible assets were cut from ₽10.16bn to ₽5.29bn and ~₽4.87bn reclassified into "Other expenses" (₽7.39bn restated vs ₽335m originally). De-PR: the company framed the restatement as a "technical procedure," but a legal expert (cited by CNews) said moving ~₽5bn of capitalised IT assets to expenses "exceeds a simple technical correction" — i.e. prior years likely over-capitalised R&D, and the write-down crystallised it now.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public consensus exists for a private RAS filer — beat/miss is qualitative vs prior year [consensus UNSOURCED]. Trajectory is unambiguously negative: net loss ~₽1.2bn (2024) → ₽4.02bn (FY2025 original) → ₽8.82bn (FY2025 restated); revenue ~₽2.04bn (2024) → ₽1.02bn (2025). No prior MyOffice report exists in the corpus (this is the first), so no internal `[[wikilink]]` trend; the in-base comparables are other loss-widening fintech/software names (e.g. [[Freecharge FY25 revenue falls 35%, slips into loss]]) — but those are revenue-declining minnows, not a near-9x equity-eroding blow-up.

**Guidance / forward.** No formal guidance (private filer). Management/owner commentary only: a restructuring "to concentrate resources on the highest-growth-potential directions" and reach a "sustainable operating model by 2026"; CEO Evgeny Kaspersky described the asset as in "slow-burn mode" with reduced development funding while existing products are maintained. (analysis) With Kaspersky support volumes cut (since June 2025), revenue halving, headcount falling and a ₽25bn intragroup debt, FY2026 hinges entirely on continued shareholder funding rather than a market recovery; absent a demand rebound, a return to operating viability in 2026 looks optimistic.

**Thesis-flags.**
1. **Import-substitution tailwind is over.** Fact: revenue -49.5%, share 5%→3%. Why: the 2022-24 forced-migration demand spike has normalised and buyers are rationing budgets. Why it matters: the core bull case for RU office-software substitutes was a captive, growing post-sanctions market — that thesis is now empirically broken for the #2 player. Second-order: consolidation toward R7/Yandex/VK; weaker independents become shareholder-dependent.
2. **Restatement doubled the loss and questions accounting quality.** Fact: ₽4.02bn → ₽8.82bn via ₽4.87bn intangible-asset → expense reclass. Why it matters: prior R&D capitalisation flattered earlier results; the "technical" label is contested. Second-order: reduces trust in any reported figure and signals impaired product/IP value.
3. **Solvency rests on Kaspersky, not customers.** Fact: negative net assets ₽13.7bn, owner debt ₽24.96bn (+33%). Why it matters: this is an owner-subsidised entity; with Kaspersky reducing development support and labelling it "slow-burn," continued funding is a judgment call, not a contract. Second-order: layoff-led cost cuts (1,070→1,013 plus the May-2026 cuts) are a survival, not a growth, posture.
