_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)

The Bank of Russia added **Ozon Bank** (the marketplace's licensed banking subsidiary) to its **register of operators of information systems (OIS) in which DFAs are issued** — confirmed by CBR ~18 June 2026. This is the *issuer/issuance-platform* licence (Federal Law 259-FZ, CBR Regulation 746-P), **not** the separate *DFA exchange operator (ООЦФА)* register that authorises secondary trading across other platforms. An OIS can also organise trading, but only of DFAs it issued itself. So Ozon can now mint and run primary placement of its own digital financial assets in-house.

**Why it is structured this way / what it reveals:** Until now Ozon placed its DFA through **MTS's "Blockchain Hub" (ЦФА Hub)** — an OIS in the CBR register since July 2023 — buyable inside the Ozon app. Both Ozon and MTS sit under **AFK Sistema** (≈31.8% of Ozon, ≈42% of MTS), so this was an intra-group arrangement, not arm's-length. The registration **internalises a capability Ozon already used via an affiliate**: it removes dependence on MTS's rails, captures the operator economics itself, and lines up with Ozon's broader push for brokerage/depository licences in 2026 (and the separately-announced "Ozone Capital" / "Ozon Asset Management" entities). Ozon's prior DFA issues were tiny — 2bn and ~3bn rubles. The CBR's own framing matters: the regulator is courting **marketplace audiences** as the next distribution channel for DFA, so a marketplace getting its own licence is a policy-aligned move, not a breakthrough. Related: [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]] (Sber's regulated DFA platform context).

## [1] Competitors / peers

There are **20+ DFA platforms** in Russia; ~**14 OIS operators** were registered by end-2024 (Sber, Atomyze, Lighthouse, MTS Blockchain Hub, Alfa's A-Token, NSD/НРД, plus exchange operators Moscow Exchange and SPB Exchange). Position of this item: **catching up / late entrant**, joining an already-crowded field.

- **Sber** — clear market leader: **408bn rubles** issued in 2025 (×5.6 vs 2024), and **231bn rubles in January 2026 alone**. ([[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]])
- **Alfa-Bank (A-Token)** + VTB accounted for ~57% of issuance in 2024; A-Token ~60% of placements, Masterchain ~20%, Tokeon ~5%. Alfa's CEO claims Russia holds ~20% of the *global* DFA market.
- **Atomyze** — first Russian DFA issuer (incumbent pioneer).
- **MTS Blockchain Hub / ЦФА Hub** — Ozon's prior host; now a direct peer it is exiting.

**Why the landscape is this way (analysis):** DFA in Russia is overwhelmingly a **bank-bond substitute** — ~98% of issuances are debt-like instruments and ~60% are <1-month tenor. Issuance volume therefore concentrates among balance-sheet-heavy banks (Sber, Alfa, VTB) that already have the corporate borrowers and treasury demand. A marketplace's edge is **distribution to retail**, not origination — which is exactly the gap the CBR wants filled, but it does not move Ozon up the issuance league table on day one.

## [2] Company history / fit

Ozon's fintech arm (Ozon Bank) has scaled fast: client funds on accounts/deposits exceeded **620bn rubles** by Q1 2026. It is building out a full financial-services stack — banking, planned brokerage and depository licences in 2026, asset management ("Ozon Asset Management", reg. mid-2025), and now an in-house DFA operator (the "Ozone Capital" plan announced Jan 2026, realised here via Ozon Bank's OIS registration). **Fit: logical.** A high-frequency marketplace with a captive app audience and a banking licence is a natural DFA *distribution* channel; owning the issuance platform lets it keep the fee stack and cross-sell investment products to existing customers rather than renting MTS's rails.

**Why it acts this way (analysis):** marketplace + bank margins are thin and commoditised; layering investment/DFA products onto an existing customer base is a classic move to lift monetisation per user without new customer-acquisition cost. The intra-Sistema MTS arrangement was a stopgap; with its own banking licence and audience scale, vertical integration is the predictable next step.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction

**Low novelty.** This is a licence/registry entry, not a product launch — there is no new instrument, UX, or numbers attached. Issuing DFA on a CBR-registered OIS is already done by ~14 operators; Ozon was already *issuing* DFA via MTS. The real change is **ownership of the rail**, not a new capability for the market.

**Traction = essentially none yet (announcement, not adoption):** prior Ozon DFA issues were 2–3bn rubles, a rounding error against Sber's 408bn. No disclosed first in-house issuance, volume target, instrument set, or user numbers. **Who captures the margin (analysis):** by becoming its own OIS, Ozon captures issuance/operator fees previously shared with MTS and can bundle DFA into its app's investment offering — but the binding constraint is *demand/origination*, where banks dominate. Unless Ozon converts marketplace traffic into meaningful retail DFA buying, the licence is optionality, not value realised.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment

Russian DFA market is large and fast-growing: ~**1.5 trillion rubles (~$13bn)** in 2025 vs 44bn in 2023 (≈×34); 2026 forecasts ~1.3–1.8trn. CBR is explicitly steering DFA toward **marketplace/retail distribution**, which is the structural tailwind behind this registration. Expect Ozon to (a) migrate issuance off MTS, (b) push DFA into its app alongside the coming brokerage/depository products, (c) target retail investors with short-tenor debt-like DFA.

**Why / second-order (analysis):** the bull case is distribution — Ozon's app reach could widen DFA's retail base faster than a pure bank could, which is precisely the CBR's aim. The bear case: DFA is a **domestic, sanctions-era, regulated workaround** (~98% debt, <1-month tenor, no cross-border liquidity), so Ozon is entering a crowded, low-margin, structurally domestic market as a late mover, while Sber/Alfa control issuance. **Counterintuitive risk:** intra-Sistema dynamics — Ozon exiting MTS's platform could pressure MTS's Blockchain Hub volumes, and persistent Sistema/Sber ownership chatter around Ozon adds corporate-structure uncertainty over who ultimately controls the rail. Net: a sensible, logical, but incremental step.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian DFA (ЦФА) is a fast-growing but structurally domestic market. CBR estimates it grew >1/3 to ~$13bn in 2025; new issuance hit **231bn rubles (~$3bn) in January 2026 alone** — more than half the prior full year (per CBR/DL News, Jan-Feb 2026). The market is a **sanctions-era bank-bond substitute** (~98% debt-like, ~60% sub-1-month tenor), so growth is driven by treasury/funding demand, not tokenization innovation. Two policy drivers are the real "why now": (1) from 2026 **non-qualified retail investors can buy select DFA up to 600k rubles/yr** (CBR rule), opening the retail channel a marketplace is built to serve; (2) Moscow has flagged DFA as a **2026 economic priority**, targeting tax parity with bonds (per TASS/CBR coverage). Net: tailwinds favour *distribution* (Ozon's edge), but origination remains the bottleneck.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: **issuance volume**, **AUC/assets-on-platform**, and **secondary trading share** — Ozon discloses none of these for its new OIS yet `[UNSOURCED]`. Basis of competition is **balance-sheet origination + corporate-borrower access**, not UX. Recent dated moves: Sber **408bn rubles issued 2025** (×5.6 vs 2024) with on-platform assets rising 25bn→185bn rubles in 6 months; **Alfa A-Token ~60% of trading volume**, Masterchain ~20%, Tokeon ~5%; **Alfa+VTB ~57% of 2024 issuance** (Ledger Insights, CCN, Feb-2026). Protagonist position: **late niche entrant** — Ozon's prior issues (2bn / ~3bn rubles via affiliate MTS) are a rounding error vs Sber. Moat (analysis): not origination but **distribution/intangibles** — captive marketplace app + banking licence; a real but unproven retail funnel.

**Comps & multiples.** No DFA-platform multiples exist (private, fee-based rails) → "no data" on EV/issuance. But the protagonist itself is listed: **Ozon Group** completed Cyprus→Russia redomiciliation (Sep 2025) and trades on MOEX with a market cap reported ~**$12bn** (companiesmarketcap, Apr 2026). The DFA registration is **immaterial to that valuation** — prior issuance ~$50–80m of notional vs a ~$12bn equity base = **<1% (analysis)** — so this is optionality, not a re-rating event. Internal comps (corpus): [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]] (Sber DFA 408bn rubles 2025, the issuance benchmark Ozon is ~100x below); cross-region tokenization comparators on a *different* model (equities/RWA, not debt substitutes) — [[B3 plans tokenization platform and real-linked stablecoin]], [[SBI Holdings forms tokenized stock venture with Startale]], [[ECB launches Appia tokenization roadmap]] — useful only to underline that Russian DFA is **not** the same instrument class as Western tokenization. Distribution not computed; qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
