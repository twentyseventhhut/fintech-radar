The Ethereum Foundation is cutting roughly 20% of its workforce, eliminating 54 positions as part of a broad restructuring that comes amid sustained senior leadership turnover and growing fragmentation across the wider Ethereum ecosystem.
The layoffs, announced Tuesday in a blog post , conclude a months-long internal reorganization tied to the implementation of the Foundation’s updated mandate and treasury policy. The EF said the reduction leaves it “leaner and more focused,” with a structure aligned around what it described as the “critical tasks” needed to support Ethereum’s long-term development.

While the EF shrinks, a separate ecosystem effort backed by some of Ethereum’s largest corporate holders is expanding.
On Monday, BitMine Immersion Technologies and SharpLink Gaming, two of the largest publicly traded Ethereum treasury companies, alongside Ethereum co-founder Joseph Lubin, announced support for ETHLabs, a new non-profit research and development initiative aimed at accelerating Ethereum’s technical roadmap and institutional adoption.
As part of the restructuring, the EF has grouped its work into five clusters, including a dedicated institutional layer focused on enterprise engagement, financial infrastructure, and policy coordination.

# Context-enrichment: Ethereum Foundation cuts 20% of workforce in restructuring
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
On 2026-06-23 the Ethereum Foundation (EF) announced via blog post the elimination of **54 positions, ~20% of staff** (i.e. base of ~270 FTE), and a **~40% cut to its 2026 operating budget** (per CoinDesk / Vitalik Buterin; via [decrypt.co](https://decrypt.co/371864/ethereum-foundation-cuts-20-of-workforce-in-leaner-reorganization), [coindesk.com](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/06/23/ethereum-foundation-cuts-20-of-staff-amid-leadership-exodus)). The org is regrouped into **five domain clusters** plus ops/management, with research narrowed to a "CROPS" agenda (censorship-resistance, resilience, openness, privacy, security).
- **The headline is the wrong frame.** "20% layoffs" is the symptom; the real action is a **financial-model change**: EF is converting from a finite-runway, drawdown-funded org into an **endowment** that aims to spend only treasury returns. The stated glide-path is from ~15% of treasury spent/year today → **~5% by 2030** (per Buterin, via [coindesk.com](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/06/23/vitalik-buterin-says-ethereum-foundation-will-cut-budget-40-in-major-reset)). At a treasury reported around **~$270m total / ~102,000–172,000 ETH** through 2026 (Arkham/CoinDesk; figures vary by date and ETH price), a 15%→5% cut is a structural spend reduction, not a one-off.
- **Why structured this way:** EF's treasury is ETH-denominated and ETH is **down ~44% YTD** (per [cryptoslate.com](https://cryptoslate.com/ethereum-foundation-cuts-20-of-staff-as-eth-sinks-44-ytd-despite-record-usage/)). A 15%/yr burn on a falling, single-asset treasury is a melting-ice-cube; the endowment reframe is the only way to claim "viable indefinitely" without selling more ETH into a weak market. The PR ("leaner and more focused") buries the fact that **this is forced discipline, not strategic choice**.
- **What got killed (the substance):** wind-down of the **Privacy & Scaling Explorations (PSE)** unit / ZK research lab, smaller/cheaper Devcon, narrower institutional strategy, more reliance on AI-assisted formal verification ([techtimes.com](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318949/20260623/ethereum-foundation-cuts-54-jobs-shuts-zk-research-lab-slashes-budget-40.htm)). Severance: ≥1 month/year of service + retirement payment + placement support.
- **The simultaneous split (one day earlier):** on 2026-06-22, **five former EF researchers** (Ansgar Dietrichs, Barnabé Monnot, Caspar Schwarz-Schilling, Josh Rudolf, Julian Ma) launched **ETHLabs**, a separate non-profit R&D org **funded by BitMine (NYSE: BMNR), SharpLink (NASDAQ: SBET) and co-founder Joe Lubin** (plus Anchorage, Octant, SNZ) ([prnewswire.com](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ethlabs-founded-by-former-ethereum-foundation-contributors-and-funded-by-bitmine-sharplink-and-joe-lubin-launches-to-accelerate-ethereums-institutional-supercycle-302806705.html), [theblock.co](https://www.theblock.co/post/405694/bitmine-sharplink-and-joe-lubin-back-ethlabs-nonprofit-to-advance-ethereums-next-phase-of-growth)). This is the load-bearing fact: EF shrinks while **the corporate ETH-treasury bloc stands up a rival, better-funded dev org**. Development funding is migrating from a neutral foundation to balance-sheet-driven public companies.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Two competitive frames, both relevant:
- **Crypto-org layoff wave (peers by event):** EF joins a heavy 2025–26 cadence of crypto retrenchment: [[Coinbase cuts about 700 jobs in AI-driven restructuring]] (~14%, May 2026), [[Gemini exits UK, EU, Australia and cuts staff]] (~25%, Feb 2026), [[Polygon Labs lays off ~30% of staff in stablecoin pivot]] (Jan 2026), [[Kuda cuts jobs in company-wide restructuring]] (Mar 2026), [[Rapyd restructures with hundreds of layoffs in AI-driven shift]] (Jun 2026), [[Pipe drastically reduces workforce]] (Nov 2025), [[PrimaryBid cuts 40% of staff amid GBP 18m loss]] (Jan 2026). EF's distinction: this is a **non-profit standard-setter**, not a revenue-driven company — the cut is treasury-discipline + governance fallout, not P&L survival or an "AI productivity" story.
- **Chain-ecosystem competition (the deeper frame):** Ethereum faces intensifying rivalry from **Solana, BNB Chain, Polygon** for stablecoin/payment liquidity. Corpus: [[EU explores Ethereum and Solana for digital euro]], [[Connecting the Dots on Europe eyeing Ethereum for euro stablecoins]] (Solana/BNB each ~$5bn stablecoin supply vs ETH's lead; ETH app fees ~$2.61bn/yr — still dominant), [[Polygon surpasses $2.5 trillion in stablecoin transfer volume]]. **Why it matters:** ETH usage/fees remain category-leading, yet the *funding base of its core R&D* is being cut at the exact moment rivals out-market it. The competitive risk isn't tech parity — it's **mindshare and dev-funding momentum** while ETH price (and treasury) sags.

## [2] Company history / fit
EF has spent 2025–26 telegraphing exactly this. In June 2025 it published a formal **Treasury Management Policy** (blog.ethereum.org) targeting 15%→5% spend. In Feb–Apr 2026 it **staked 70,000 ETH** to generate operating yield rather than sell principal (CoinDesk) — the endowment thesis in action. The 2026 governance churn is acute: **~9 senior figures left/transitioned in ~6 months**, including co-EDs **Tomasz Stańczak** and **Hsiao-Wei Wang**; board member **Bastian Aue** runs the transition. **Why EF acts this way:** an ETH-only treasury in a -44% YTD tape forces a choice between (a) selling ETH (bearish signal, dilutes the network's biggest aligned holder) or (b) cutting burn + earning staking yield. EF chose (b). The layoffs are the lagging, visible consequence of a treasury decision made a year earlier.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
The genuinely new development is **not the layoff** — it's the **bifurcation of Ethereum's development funding**. For a decade EF was the de-facto central R&D and grants engine. Now:
- EF goes lean/endowment, narrows to CROPS protocol stewardship;
- **ETHLabs** captures the institutional-adoption / scaling / settlement agenda, funded by the **corporate ETH-treasury bloc** (BitMine, SharpLink, Lubin) that has a direct balance-sheet incentive to pump ETH utility.
- **Who captures the value / who pays (the second-order question):** development is shifting from a **mission-neutral foundation** to **for-profit-adjacent backers whose ETH holdings benefit from the roadmap they fund**. That's a real conflict-of-interest / credible-neutrality risk for a base-layer that markets itself as neutral. Traction check: ETHLabs is *announced* (launched 2026-06-22) with named researchers and named backers, but **no shipped output yet** — treat as a credible org formation, not yet adoption.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Price/flows:** ETH ~$1,670, **down ~44% YTD**; ~$157m liquidations in 24h post-announcement (≈$140m longs); **7 straight weeks of US spot-ETH-ETF net outflows ≈ $1bn** (per [cryptoslate.com](https://cryptoslate.com/ethereum-foundation-cuts-20-of-staff-as-eth-sinks-44-ytd-despite-record-usage/), [beincrypto.com](https://beincrypto.com/ethereum-foundation-20-reorganization/), [benzinga.com](https://www.benzinga.com/Opinion/26/06/60084875/ethereum-foundations-20-staff-cut-adds-pressure-as-eth-loses-key-support)). Kalshi: only ~39% odds of closing >$1,690 by Fri 2026-06-26.
- **Sentiment:** read as a **governance/leadership-credibility event** layered on weak price, not a tech failure — usage and fees are at records even as price/funding fall (the "ETH's reckoning" framing). 
- **Why the market goes this way (counterintuitive second-order):** a leaner, endowment-funded EF is *financially* healthier and arguably *better* for long-run sustainability — but the market reads "20% cut + 9 execs gone + rival org spun up" as **fragmentation and loss of central direction**, which compresses the speculative narrative ETH needs. The fix (discipline) and the perceived problem (loss of momentum) point opposite directions short-term.

## Top challenge / red-team questions (second-order)

1. **Is the "20% layoff" the real story, or a symptom?** Symptom. The load-bearing change is the **financial-model shift to an endowment** (15%→5% spend by 2030) on an ETH-only treasury that's down ~44% YTD. The layoff is the lagging visible consequence of a treasury policy set in mid-2025.

2. **Was this forced or strategic?** Mostly forced (analysis). A single-asset treasury falling 44% makes a 15%/yr burn a melting-ice-cube; cutting burn + staking yield (70,000 ETH staked Feb–Apr 2026) avoids selling ETH into weakness. PR frames discipline as choice.

3. **Is EF actually financially distressed?** No — that's the counterintuitive point. Endowment math makes it *more* durable long-term. The market mis-reads durability-discipline as loss-of-direction. The risk is reputational/momentum, not solvency.

4. **Who captures the development agenda EF is vacating?** ETHLabs — funded by the corporate ETH-treasury bloc (BitMine, SharpLink, Lubin). Core R&D funding migrates from a neutral non-profit to balance-sheet-incentivized backers. This is the most important second-order fact.

5. **Does ETHLabs create a credible-neutrality conflict?** Open / yes (analysis). Backers hold large ETH positions and benefit directly from the roadmap they fund. For a base layer that markets neutrality, dev funding aligned to corporate holders is a structural governance tension, even if intentions are aligned.

6. **Announced vs live for ETHLabs?** Announced only (2026-06-22). Named org, named researchers, named backers — but no shipped output. Credible org formation ≠ adoption.

7. **Is the 9-exec exodus cause or coincidence of the restructuring?** Both feed each other. Co-EDs Stańczak and Wang departed; ~9 senior figures gone in 6 months. Some of those people *became* ETHLabs founders — so the "exodus" is partly a controlled re-housing of talent into a better-funded vehicle, not pure attrition.

8. **Does this signal Ethereum losing the chain race?** Not on fundamentals — ETH usage and app fees (~$2.61bn/yr) are at records and lead Solana/BNB (~$5bn stablecoin supply each) on stablecoin liquidity. The threat is **mindshare + dev-funding momentum** while price/treasury sag, not tech displacement.

9. **How big is the treasury actually?** ~$270m total / ~102,000–172,000 ETH through 2026 (Arkham/CoinDesk; varies by date and ETH price). Numbers move with ETH price, which is itself the problem.

10. **What exactly got cut on the research side?** PSE / ZK research lab wound down, Devcon shrunk, narrower institutional strategy, more AI-assisted formal verification. Cutting privacy/ZK research is notable given CROPS explicitly names privacy — tension between stated priorities and what was axed. (open)

11. **Is "AI-assisted formal verification" a real efficiency lever or PR?** Open. Plausible for client/spec work, but presented without metrics; treat as direction, not proven savings.

12. **Did the market price governance risk or price risk?** Mostly price/flow risk amplified by governance optics: ~$157m liquidations in 24h (~$140m longs), 7 weeks of spot-ETF outflows ≈$1bn. The reorg was the catalyst on an already-weak tape, not the root cause.

13. **Is this comparable to the for-profit crypto layoffs (Coinbase/Gemini/Polygon)?** Only loosely. Those are P&L/AI-productivity driven at revenue companies. EF is a non-profit standard-setter; its cut is treasury-discipline + governance, with no revenue line to defend. Don't equate.

14. **Who's silent about what?** EF didn't respond to comment requests by publication; no detail on which teams beyond PSE, no disclosure of exact severance cost or treasury sale (if any) to fund it. ETHLabs hasn't disclosed funding size.

15. **What would change the assessment?** Upgrade to 5/5 if EF is forced to sell ETH principal (signals real distress) or if ETHLabs and EF visibly diverge on protocol direction (fork-of-governance risk). Downgrade to 3 if it proves a clean, well-managed transition with talent simply re-housed and roadmap intact.

Importance: 4/5 — A foundational governance/funding restructuring of the second-largest crypto network, with a genuine structural twist (dev funding migrating from neutral foundation to corporate-treasury-backed ETHLabs) and material market impact (ETH -44% YTD, ~$1bn ETF outflows, liquidations). Not 5/5 because there's no solvency event and the "competitive decline" framing is narrative, not fundamentals (usage/fees at records); the real novelty is the funding bifurcation, which is still announced-not-proven.

Arithmetic illustration: if mkt cap < (ETH held × ETH price), mNAV < 1x. Distribution not computed (only 2 clean comps); qualitative: the funders of ETHLabs are themselves trading at/under NAV in a -44% ETH tape.

**Risk flags.** (1) **Credible-neutrality / conflict** — dev funding migrating from a neutral foundation to corporate ETH-holders (BitMine/SharpLink/Lubin) who benefit from the roadmap they fund; second-order governance risk for a base layer that sells neutrality. (3) **Momentum/mindshare loss** — cutting PSE/ZK research and Devcon while Solana/BNB spend to win liquidity risks a self-reinforcing narrative decline despite leading fundamentals.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a **governance re-architecture, not a decline event**: Ethereum is splitting into a lean neutral steward (EF) + a corporate-funded growth engine (ETHLabs). Falsifiable thesis (analysis): if EF and ETHLabs stay aligned on the roadmap, this is bullish-durable (endowment math = indefinite runway); the thesis **breaks** if the two diverge on protocol direction (a "fork of governance") or if EF is forced to **sell ETH principal** — watch for either. Catalyst to watch: ETHLabs' first shipped output and EF's first post-reorg treasury report.
