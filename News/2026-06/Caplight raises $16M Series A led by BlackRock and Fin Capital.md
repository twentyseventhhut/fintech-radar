SAN FRANCISCO , June 24, 2026 /PRNewswire/ -- Caplight Technologies has closed a $16 million Series A led by BlackRock and Fin Capital, with strategic participation from UBS Investment Bank. The round extends its lead in venture secondary data and fuels expansion across private market data and agentic workflows for research and transactions.
Caplight's data shows VC has tripled into a $12+ trillion asset class in three years, outpacing the infrastructure built to navigate it. Caplight unifies private markets data and investing in one platform: 100,000 company and investor profiles, $4 trillion in funding round data, $300+ billion in proprietary secondary data, and $5+ billion in daily live transaction flow. Customers collectively manage over $52 trillion in assets and access Caplight via platform, API, and MCP server.
"Transparency unlocks private markets for institutional investors," said Javier Avalos, CEO of Caplight. "We give the world's best investors the data they need to understand the venture market and the infrastructure to participate in it. In an age where proprietary data is at a premium, we're proud to be their source of truth."
BlackRock joins as a strategic investor, supporting the company's mission to improve private market transparency, data infrastructure, and secondary market liquidity. The investment establishes a framework for future collaboration across BlackRock's private markets and technology ecosystem, including Aladdin and Preqin.
"With private markets becoming a growing part of portfolios, investors are increasingly demanding better data, transparency, and efficient secondary market infrastructure," said Kunal Khara, Senior Managing Director and Global Head of Aladdin Product at BlackRock. "We believe Caplight is well positioned to serve these evolving client needs and are excited to support the company as it continues to scale its business."
Fin Capital, a leading fintech -focused investor and longtime Caplight backer, led the round alongside BlackRock. New investor LEAP Global Partners co-led. UBS Investment Bank joined as a strategic investor. Existing backers DB1 Ventures, the corporate venture arm of Deutsche Börse Group, Better Tomorrow Ventures, Clocktower Ventures, and Dash Fund increased their positions.
 About Caplight Caplight Technologies, Inc. is building data and transaction infrastructure for venture capital. The platform combines proprietary private company data with a marketplace that connects investors and broker-dealers for secondary market liquidity. The company was founded by Javier Avalos and Justin Moore in 2021, and is based in San Francisco.
Securities transactions are offered through Caplight Markets LLC, member FINRA/SIPC.

# Context-enrichment: Caplight raises $16M Series A led by BlackRock and Fin Capital
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5. Freshness: **fresh**._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- On **2026-06-24** Caplight Technologies (SF, founded 2021 by Javier Avalos & Justin Moore) closed a **$16M Series A**, **co-led by BlackRock and Fin Capital** (a longtime backer), with **LEAP Global Partners** co-leading as a new investor and **UBS Investment Bank** as a strategic investor. Existing backers **DB1 Ventures (Deutsche Börse's corporate VC)**, Better Tomorrow Ventures, Clocktower Ventures and Dash Fund increased positions.
- What Caplight actually is — **two linked businesses around private-stock secondary pricing**: (1) a **data product** (near-real-time pricing feed / "MarketPrice" pitched as an alternative to stale 409A marks; sold via platform, API and an MCP server) and (2) a **regulated marketplace** — securities offered through **Caplight Markets LLC (FINRA/SIPC)**. Its distinctive product is **synthetic, cash-settled options/derivatives on private stock** (long/short/hedge without touching the cap table), aggregating a network of broker-dealers rather than running its own ATS.
- **What the PR is silent on (the red-team core): valuation, revenue/ARR, take-rate, and actual *executed* transaction volume.** The headline "$5B+ in **daily live transaction flow**" is order/indication **flow, not settled trades** — a deliberately flattering metric. "Customers collectively manage over **$52T** in assets" is the **clients' AUM, not Caplight's**, and the paying-customer count is withheld. All six traction metrics ($52T AUM, 100k profiles, $4T funding-round data, $300B+ secondary data, $5B+ daily flow) are **PR claims, none independently verified**.
- **Why structured this way:** $16M is tiny — the headline is the **investor list, not the dollars**. The signal being sold is the *cap table* (BlackRock + UBS + Deutsche Börse), which substitutes for disclosed traction. The Aladdin/Preqin language ("establishes a framework for future collaboration… including Aladdin and Preqin") is **directional framing with zero integration specifics** — a strategic option, not a signed commercial deal.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Forge Global** — registered broker-dealer + SEC/FINRA ATS; full execution venue, >$17B private shares traded. Being **acquired by Charles Schwab (~$660M, $45/sh, announced 2025-11-06, close H1 2026)** — note that price is a markdown vs Forge's ~$2B 2022 SPAC value [[Charles Schwab to acquire Forge Global]].
- **EquityZen** — SPV-bundling of accredited investors; **acquired by Morgan Stanley (Nov 2025)** [[Morgan Stanley to acquire EquityZen]].
- **Nasdaq Private Market** — issuer-controlled liquidity programs + "authoritative" data; now the exclusive resolution-data provider behind [[Polymarket launches private-company prediction markets with Nasdaq data]].
- **Hiive / Zanbato** — early-stage live price discovery / institutional ATS, respectively.
- **Position (analysis):** The execution venues (Forge, EquityZen) are being **absorbed by wirehouses/custodians**; the data layer is being **rolled up by data giants** (BlackRock/Preqin, Nasdaq). Caplight is the **independent data-first + derivatives** wedge — neither a pure ATS nor a pure data vendor. Its second-order edge is the **synthetic derivatives** product none of the venue-incumbents center on; its second-order risk is that this same independence makes it an **acquisition/strategic-partner target** (which the BlackRock/UBS/Deutsche Börse cap table all but telegraphs).

## [2] Company history / fit
- Founded **2021**; ~$10M prior seed capital across ~3 rounds per third-party trackers (Tracxn/Crunchbase — **unverified**, not company-confirmed), so total raised likely ~$26M. Fin Capital and DB1 Ventures are repeat backers, so the Series A is a **continuation of an existing relationship, not a fresh validation** — and the upsizing of existing positions partly fills the round.
- **Why it acts this way (analysis):** a data-only business is commoditizable (409A/pricing feeds are a crowded space); pairing **proprietary secondary pricing** with a **regulated derivatives marketplace** is the move from a low-multiple data subscription to a transaction-revenue + network-effect business. The "agentic workflows / MCP server" framing rides the AI-distribution narrative to justify a software multiple it does not yet disclose revenue for.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Genuinely differentiated:** synthetic, cash-settled options on private names (hedge/short exposure without the cap-table friction of SPVs or direct secondaries) is a real product gap vs Forge/EquityZen/Hiive. The patent ("Data Interpolation Platform for… Predictive and Interpolated Pricing Data") supports a defensible-data claim.
- **Traction is unproven:** every headline number is a PR aggregate, and the one volume metric is *flow*, not executed trades. **No revenue, no take-rate, no executed-derivatives volume** disclosed → value-add is **plausible but not confirmed**.
- **Who captures the margin (analysis):** if BlackRock/Preqin or Nasdaq build/buy comparable secondary-pricing data, Caplight's **data moat thins** and value concentrates in execution + the derivatives book. The strategic investors are positioned to **either distribute Caplight's data (upside) or internalize it (disintermediation risk)** — the same parties are both customers and potential competitors.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Why BlackRock leads (the real story):** BlackRock bought **Preqin for ~$3.2B (announced 2024-07-01, closed 2024)** to dominate private-markets data, sizing the data TAM at ~$8B→~$18B by 2030. **Preqin covers fund/primary data; Caplight covers secondary transaction pricing — the gap Preqin doesn't fill.** This is **both offensive** (extend the data moat into secondaries) **and defensive** (keep a fast-growing secondary-data source inside the ecosystem rather than feeding rivals). (analysis)
- **Tailwind is structural and real:** global secondary volume hit a **record ~$226B in 2025 (+~41% y/y)**, with a path toward ~$300B; ~30,000 companies / ~$3.7T unrealized value await exits; AI mega-tenders (OpenAI ~$500B, Anthropic ~$350B, SpaceX ~$400B→~$800B, Stripe ~$159B) keep employees and funds demanding liquidity and pricing. Companies staying private longer makes private-stock *data* a scarce, monetizable asset.
- **Counterintuitive second-order effect (analysis):** the same consolidation wave that validates the thesis (Schwab/Forge, MS/EquityZen) makes an independent Caplight **fragile, not safe** — a $16M round buys runway, but with BlackRock, UBS and Deutsche Börse already on the cap table, the most likely "next" is a **strategic acquisition**, not an IPO. The risk is **disintermediation by its own strategic backers** if the Aladdin/Preqin "framework" never converts to a real integration.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Sub-vertical: private-markets *secondary* infrastructure — splitting into a data layer (pricing/analytics) and an execution layer (marketplaces/ATS). Caplight straddles both. Size/growth: global secondary volume hit a record ~$226B in 2025 (+~41% y/y), per Jefferies' 2025 review (via note) — a structural tailwind as companies stay private longer (~$4T→~$13T forecast private-capital AUM by 2032, per fintech.global/Preqin framing, as of 2026-06). The *data*-vendor TAM is the more relevant frame for Caplight: BlackRock sized private-markets data at ~$8B→~$18B by 2030 when it bought Preqin (note). Structure: rapidly **consolidating** — execution venues are being absorbed by custodians/wirehouses (Schwab/Forge, MS/EquityZen, Goldman/Industry Ventures), while the data layer is being rolled up by data giants (BlackRock+Preqin, Nasdaq). Why now: AI mega-tenders (OpenAI, Anthropic, SpaceX, Stripe) force employee/fund liquidity and make private-stock *pricing* a scarce, monetizable asset; on Caplight's own data, AI/ML names are now ~31% of its secondary activity (per PR/web, 2026), 2025 secondary volume $3.5B (+75% y/y), 76 first-time-traded companies. (analysis) Entry barriers: proprietary transaction data + network of broker-dealers + FINRA registration — moderate but not deep.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector runs on: (1) for data — coverage/freshness of pricing vs stale 409A marks; (2) for marketplace — *executed* secondary volume (not order flow) and take rate; (3) network of broker-dealer counterparties. Key players & basis of competition: **Forge Global** (registered BD + SEC/FINRA ATS, >$17B traded — full execution venue) and **EquityZen** (SPV-bundling for accrediteds) compete on *execution*; **Nasdaq Private Market** (~$80B executed for 200,000+ individuals, per [[Polymarket launches private-company prediction markets with Nasdaq data]]) and **BlackRock/Preqin** compete on *data/authority*; Hiive/Zanbato on price discovery / institutional ATS. Recent moves: Schwab–Forge ~$660M (announced 2025-11-06, close H1 2026); Morgan Stanley–EquityZen (announced Nov 2025, ~$350–450M est., terms undisclosed); MS subsequently *halved* private-share trading fees — a margin-compression signal for execution venues. Protagonist's position (analysis): **niche/independent data-first + derivatives wedge** — neither a pure ATS nor a pure data vendor. Moat = proprietary secondary-pricing dataset + the only player centering *synthetic cash-settled derivatives* on private stock (intangibles/data + nascent network effects). Catching up on execution scale; ahead on the derivatives product gap.

**Comps & multiples.**
- **Forge Global (external, public, acquired):** Schwab deal ~$660M / TTM revenue $92.9M (as of 2025-09-30) = **~7.1x price/revenue** (acquisition price ≈ EV proxy, not clean EV). This is a *markdown* vs Forge's ~$2B 2022 SPAC value — repricing of an execution-only venue.
- **Preqin (external, data-vendor benchmark):** BlackRock $3.2B / ~$240M 2024 revenue = **~13.3x revenue** (per WatersTech/web). A *pure-data* asset clears ~13x vs an *execution* asset at ~7x — the spread Caplight is trying to sit on the rich side of.
- **EquityZen (external):** ~$350–450M est. (Morgan Stanley); revenue undisclosed → **multiple: no data**.
- **Caplight (protagonist):** $16M Series A; post-money valuation **undisclosed**; total raised ~$26.2M (PitchBook, unverified vs company). Revenue/ARR **[UNSOURCED]** → no Caplight multiple computable.
- Internal comps: [[Charles Schwab to acquire Forge Global]] · [[Morgan Stanley to acquire EquityZen]] · [[Polymarket launches private-company prediction markets with Nasdaq data]].
- Distribution not formally computed (only 2 verifiable revenue-anchored multiples). Qualitative read: the ~7x (execution) vs ~13x (data) gap is the whole investment logic — a data-first positioning *aspires* to the higher multiple, but with no disclosed Caplight revenue this is narrative, not arithmetic. In-line/cheap/rich flag: **not assessable** for Caplight (valuation withheld).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disintermediation by its own backers (primary).** BlackRock/Preqin (and Nasdaq) can build or buy comparable secondary-pricing data; the strategic investors are simultaneously customers and potential competitors. If they internalize the data, Caplight's thin data moat collapses to the execution/derivatives book — where it is sub-scale.
2. **Margin compression / value capture moving downstream.** MS halving private-share fees post-EquityZen signals execution economics are commoditizing; if the durable margin sits with custodians (distribution) and data giants (authority), a mid-stage independent risks being squeezed at both ends.
3. **Traction opacity → re-rating risk.** No revenue, take-rate, or *executed*-derivatives volume disclosed; headline "$5B+ daily flow" is order/indication flow, not settled trades, and "$52T AUM" is clients'. A $16M round (partly existing-backer upsizing) buys runway, not validation — leaving the equity story dependent on the cap table, not the P&L.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is **consolidation-stage capital, not a fresh entrant**: the BlackRock/UBS/Deutsche Börse cap table reads as acquisition optionality more than an IPO path — the more likely "next" is a strategic tuck-in (like Forge/EquityZen) than independence. Falsifiable thesis (analysis): if the Aladdin/Preqin "framework" converts to a *signed* commercial/integration deal within ~12 months, Caplight re-rates toward the data-vendor (~13x) end and the moat hardens; absent that, it stays a sub-scale independent and gets absorbed or out-competed. Trigger to watch: a disclosed BlackRock/Aladdin–Caplight commercial agreement (not "framework" language), or any disclosure of *executed* derivatives volume. What breaks it: Preqin/Nasdaq shipping native secondary-pricing data.
