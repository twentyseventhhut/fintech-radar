Pine Labs-owned Setu co-founder Nikhil Kumar steps down; says, 'It is hard to put into words'
 
 Follow Us
Nikhil Kumar, co-founder of fintech infrastructure startup Setu , has stepped away from his day-to-day role at the company, nearly eight years after helping establish the business and four years after its acquisition by Pine Labs .
Kumar announced the move in a LinkedIn post on 3 June, saying it was time to leave his operational responsibilities after years of building the company.
"After many years of building Setu, it is time for me to step away from my everyday role," he wrote.
Reflecting on his time at the Bengaluru-based startup, Kumar described Setu as one of the most significant chapters of his life.
"It is hard to put into words what Setu has meant to me. It has given me far more than I ever expected. It has been one of the most important chapters of my life," he said.
 
Founded in 2018 by Kumar and Sahil Kini, Setu provides application programming interface (API) infrastructure for digital financial services, including bill payments, UPI transactions, KYC verification, digital signatures, savings and credit products.
It also provides account aggregator-related services, while Setu AA, operated by Agya Technologies, is licensed by the RBI to support consent-based financial data sharing.
In 2024, Setu launched Sesame, which it describes as India's first large language model designed specifically for the banking and financial services sector.
"We set out to build infrastructure. Somewhere along the way, a company became a community. Work became a way of looking at the world, and of doing our small part to move the ecosystem forward," Kumar wrote.
Setu, which has raised more than $18 million from investors including Lightspeed, Falcon Edge and Alpha Wave Global, was acquired by Pine Labs in June 2022 as part of the payments company's efforts to expand its financial infrastructure and digital payments offerings.
The startup strengthened its leadership team last year, appointing former Razorpay executive Prashanth Nimmagada as chief technology officer and Vijeth Pandit as chief product officer.
Kumar, a graduate of Visvesvaraya Technological University in Karnataka, previously co-founded customer feedback platform Voyce, which was acquired by Exotel in 2015.
He did not disclose his next plans, but said he was leaving with confidence in the company's future and its leadership team.
