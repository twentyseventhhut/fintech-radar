ZA Bank has launched a Cross-boundary Wealth Management Connect Southbound Scheme service in partnership with Industrial Bank, allowing eligible investors in mainland China’s Greater Bay Area to invest in eligible wealth management products in Hong Kong through the ZA Bank app.
 

 ZA Bank has launched a Cross-boundary Wealth Management Connect Southbound Scheme service in partnership with Industrial Bank, allowing eligible investors in mainland China’s Greater Bay Area to invest in eligible wealth management products in Hong Kong through the ZA Bank app. 
 
Under the arrangement, eligible investors can first open a mainland remittance account, complete qualification verification and undergo ZA Bank’s risk assessment through Industrial Bank. They can then open and operate a Southbound Scheme Dedicated Investment Account with ZA Bank in Hong Kong.
The service is available only to eligible residents of the nine mainland cities in the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area. Applicants must have more than two years of investment experience and meet income and asset requirements set by the relevant regulatory authorities.
Investors assessed by Hong Kong banks as vulnerable customers are not eligible for the Southbound Scheme.
Users can conduct cross-boundary renminbi remittances between their mainland remittance account at Industrial Bank and their dedicated investment account at ZA Bank. The individual investment quota under the Southbound Scheme is up to RMB 3 million.
After opening an account, investors can use the ZA Bank app to subscribe to eligible Hong Kong wealth management products. The initial product range includes RMB, Hong Kong dollar and foreign currency time deposits, as well as low- to medium-high-risk funds authorised by Hong Kong’s Securities and Futures Commission, subject to the scope permitted by regulators.
ZA Bank said the partnership follows a clear division of responsibilities, with Industrial Bank managing the mainland remittance account and ZA Bank managing the dedicated investment account in Hong Kong.
Funds under the Southbound Scheme are subject to closed-loop management through the Cross-border Interbank Payment System. They can only be remitted between the two dedicated accounts through the original route and cannot be used for other purposes.
The dedicated investment account and product sales are governed by Hong Kong laws and relevant regulators, including the Hong Kong Monetary Authority and the Securities and Futures Commission. ZA Bank noted that Hong Kong’s investor protection mechanisms differ from those in mainland China.
Calvin Ng, chief executive officer of ZA Bank, said the launch marked a milestone for the digital bank and reflected its participation in Greater Bay Area financial connectivity.
Ng said ZA Bank aimed to simplify the traditional cross-boundary wealth management process and provide eligible Greater Bay Area users with a wider range of compliant products through its digital platform.
