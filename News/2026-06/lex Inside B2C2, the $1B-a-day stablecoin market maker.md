# Context-enrichment: lex — Inside B2C2, the $1B-a-day stablecoin market maker
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**Freshness verdict: FRESH** (but low-novelty). This is a lex podcast *profile/commentary* of B2C2 with US-Americas CEO Cactus Raazi — not a deal or a new disclosure. It is **not** a duplicate: prior B2C2 notes in the corpus cover discrete events — [[Standard Chartered partners B2C2 on digital asset liquidity]] (2026-02, distribution deal) and [[Robinhood adds B2C2 sales head Zeke Vince for institutional crypto]] (2026-01, a talent loss). This note is a long-form company portrait, so it is a distinct artifact, but it surfaces no NEW figures beyond B2C2's own 2025 marketing. Weight comes from the franchise, not the news.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
A podcast interview, not an event. B2C2 = institutional **OTC principal market maker** in digital assets (founded 2015, London, by ex-Goldman FX trader Max Boonen; ~90% owned by Japan's **SBI Holdings** since Dec 2020). It quotes two-way prices off its own book, it is not an exchange.
- The headline "**$1B/day**" is specifically **stablecoin-swap** volume (CoinDesk, 2025-10-23), *not* total trading volume — the lex title blurs this. The "**~1,500 institutions**" and "**40+ exchanges**" claims are *not corroborated in B2C2's own current materials* — treat as marketing-deck numbers (open/unverified). B2C2's own hard self-reported figures: **>$2T cumulative** volume since 2015, **~$200B** client volume in 2025, ">1,000 settlements/day," "~25% of DeFi perps" (self-reported, unverified).
- **Why framed this way:** B2C2 is mid-repositioning from "crypto OTC desk" to "stablecoin settlement infrastructure." Leading with "$1B-a-day stablecoin" recasts a cyclical trading P&L as durable payments plumbing — a deliberately more investable narrative ahead of a capital raise (see [2]).

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Wintermute** (London) — larger by volume (widely cited $5B+/day; record single-day OTC ~$2.24B Nov 2024); the closest *bigger* peer. B2C2 looks structurally #2-or-lower on raw flow.
- **Cumberland/DRW**, **GSR** (bought broker-dealer Equilibrium 2025 for a US license), **Galaxy Digital (GLXY)** — the cleanest public comp: Q1-2026 Global Markets adj. gross profit only $31M, GAAP net loss $216M as industry volumes fell >25% q/q. Shows how brutal a down-quarter is for these desks.
- **Flow Traders** (public, Amsterdam) — FY2025 net trading income €485.8M; in Mar-2026 launched 24/7 OTC liquidity for tokenized stocks/gold — a TradFi MM pushing into the same tokenization/stablecoin turf.
- **Jane Street / Jump** — both retreated from US crypto in May-2023; post-GENIUS-Act re-entry is **open/unverified** and a real latent threat (vastly larger balance sheets).
- **Crossover Markets / CROSSx** — execution-only ECN, [[Crossover Markets raises $31m Series B at $200m valuation]] led by Tradeweb (with DRW, Wintermute, Virtu, XTX). Signals TradFi RFQ/ECN market structure encroaching on the principal-OTC model B2C2 relies on.
- **Why this matters (2nd order):** B2C2's edge is balance-sheet + relationships, not a tech-platform moat. If TradeFi liquidity (Flow, Jane Street) and ECNs (CROSSx/Tradeweb) commoditize execution, the principal MM risks being squeezed to a price-taking liquidity layer.

## [2] Company history / fit
- 2015 founded → 2020-07 SBI takes $30M minority → **2020-12 SBI completes ~90% takeover** (valuation never disclosed). 2024-09 **Cactus Raazi** (ex-Goldman MD, ex-Amber Group US CEO) appointed Americas CEO. 2025-07 reported raising **up to $200M**, partly to let **SBI sell down its 90% stake** (CoinDesk 2025-07-17; valuation/buyers undisclosed).
- **Hard financial (SBI disclosure):** FY ended Mar-2024 revenue **¥57.1B (~$380M), +88.5% y/y**, pre-tax profit **~¥8.4B (~$55M)** — turned profitable on the post-election rally. Skeptic's note: this is cyclical, rally-driven; the Galaxy Q1-2026 loss shows how fast it reverses.
- **Why act this way:** SBI sitting on a cyclical 90% asset wants outside capital + a "settlement infrastructure" story to justify a software-style valuation rather than a commodity trading-desk multiple. The stablecoin pivot is the multiple-rerating play. Fits SBI's broader Japan crypto build-out — [[SBI Holdings in talks to acquire stake in Japan's Bitbank]], [[Ripple and SBI launch RLUSD stablecoin in Japan]], [[SBI CEO pledges up to $1.25 billion for a future Ripple IPO]].

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- Real 2025-26 story = **PENNY** (launched 2025-10-23): instant, *zero-fee* stablecoin↔stablecoin swaps (USDT/USDC/USDG/RLUSD/PYUSD/AUSD) with on-chain settlement; **Solana named primary settlement network**; market-maker role in Paxos's **USDG / Global Dollar Network** (passed 100+ partners, >$1B mcap, 2025-12-04).
- **But:** zero-fee → monetization is spread/order-flow capture, not fees (unconfirmed). USDG at ~$1B is tiny vs USDT/USDC (hundreds of billions); B2C2's role there is small/early. The "$1B/day" is largely **intra-crypto settlement plumbing**, not proven real-economy payments.
- **Who captures the margin (2nd order):** the rails are owned by **Visa, Circle, Paxos, Solana, Nomura** — B2C2 is a liquidity *layer beneath* them, not the network owner. That is a commoditizable position; the durable question is "does B2C2 own anything, or is it a price-taker?"

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Regulatory tailwind:** **GENIUS Act** signed 2025-07-18 (federal stablecoin framework, 1:1 reserves); OCC NPRM early 2026. MiCA live (B2C2 has Luxembourg VASP). This legitimizes regulated USD stablecoins for institutional settlement → directly favors PENNY/USDG/Solana strategy.
- **Sentiment:** institutional stablecoin-settlement is the consensus growth wave (Circle/Nomura Japan FX platform; Visa USDC settlement Dec-2025). B2C2 is riding it but does not own the rails.
- **Counterintuitive risk:** the same wave invites TradFi giants (Jane Street/Jump re-entry, Flow Traders, Visa/Circle) onto B2C2's turf. A successful "settlement infrastructure" narrative could attract the very players who commoditize the liquidity layer. The central question is therefore **not** "is B2C2 big?" but "can a balance-sheet-heavy principal MM defend a durable margin as execution commoditizes and rails are owned upstream?"

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Institutional crypto OTC / stablecoin-settlement is the live growth vector beneath the headline. Per CoinLaw (crypto OTC statistics 2026, via coinlaw.io) the crypto OTC market is cited at **$50–60bn average daily volume in 2026**, with institutional OTC activity "growing >110% y/y" — secondary/aggregator figures, treat as directional, not audited. Stablecoin circulation is "projected to exceed $1tn by late-2026" with 2025 volume +72% y/y (per StablecoinInsider/Plasma compilations, as of 2026 — unaudited aggregator data). Structure: the trading/MM layer is **fragmented and balance-sheet-intensive** (Wintermute, Cumberland/DRW, GSR, B2C2, Galaxy, FalconX) while value is migrating *upstream* to rail/issuer owners (Circle, Paxos, Visa, Solana). Barriers are capital (inventory/warehousing) + relationships + licensing, NOT a defensible tech platform — low switching costs for a price-taking liquidity layer. **Why now:** GENIUS Act (signed 2025-07-18) + MiCA legitimize regulated USD-stablecoin settlement, pulling TradFi MMs (Flow Traders, and a possible Jane Street/Jump re-entry — unverified) onto the same turf. The "$1B/day" recast of a cyclical OTC P&L as durable "settlement infrastructure" is the multiple-rerating pitch ahead of B2C2's raise.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: **daily notional / annual client volume**, **principal spread capture (bps)**, and **inventory/balance-sheet turnover** — there is no recurring ARR/NRR here; revenue is transactional and cyclical. Players & basis of competition (price + balance sheet + relationships, not product):
- **Wintermute** — >$5bn/day, "$3.5tn-a-year"; reportedly raising **$100–300m at ~$2bn** (PitchBook/press, 2026); record single-day $2.24bn (Nov-2024). The closest bigger peer; also *funds B2C2's disruptors* (CROSSx). B2C2 is structurally **#2-or-lower on raw flow** (analysis).
- **Galaxy Digital (GLXY)** — public comp, but increasingly an AI/data-center story (Q1-2026: total revenue $10.0bn, GAAP net loss $216m on MTM; **Global Markets adj. gross profit only $31m**, +3% q/q). Shows how thin trading economics are even at scale.
- **Flow Traders (AMS:FLOW)** — TradFi MM; 4Q25 net trading income €123.8m (-20% y/y); pushing 24/7 OTC liquidity for tokenized assets — direct encroachment.
- **Cumberland/DRW, GSR, FalconX, Ripple Prime, Coinbase Prime** — named as the largest North-American stablecoin-flow desks (per 2026 OTC compilations).
B2C2's position: **niche-to-#2 institutional principal MM**, mid-pivot to settlement; moat is balance sheet + SBI backing + 1,000+ settlements/day relationships, NOT network ownership — `(analysis)`. PENNY economics (zero-fee → spread capture) `[UNSOURCED]`.

**Comps & multiples.** Comparability is weak: only Galaxy is public and its valuation is now dominated by data-center/AI, so a clean trading multiple is unsound.
- **GLXY** mcap **~$8.4bn** (companiesmarketcap/macrotrends, ~2026-06-18; range $7.9–11.0bn across the month). Annualizing Global Markets adj. gross profit ($31m × 4 = ~$124m) → **mcap / GM-gross-profit ≈ $8.4bn / $0.124bn ≈ 68x** — but that's a *segment* number inside a multi-line co, so the ratio is **not meaningful** as a trading-desk multiple (flagged, not used).
- **Wintermute** private: **~$2bn round valuation** vs "$3.5tn/yr" volume (round valuation, not market cap; revenue undisclosed → revenue multiple = **no data**).
- **B2C2** itself: SBI-disclosed FY3/2024 revenue **¥57.1bn (~$380m)**, pre-tax **~¥8.4bn (~$55m)**; 2025 raise **up to $200m**, valuation/buyers **undisclosed** → implied multiple **no data**.
Internal comps from the base: [[Wintermute secures bitcoin credit line from Cantor Fitzgerald]], [[Astriax receives $30m from Wintermute for on-chain trading]], [[Deutsche Bank, Galaxy, Flow Traders launch euro stablecoin EURAU]], [[Crossover Markets raises $31m Series B at $200m valuation]], [[Zodia Markets raises $18M backed by Circle Ventures]]. **Distribution not computed** (<3 comparable disclosed multiples); qualitative read: Wintermute's ~$2bn round sets a rough ceiling; B2C2 as #2-or-lower on flow plausibly sits below unless the "settlement infrastructure" narrative earns a software-style premium (the whole point of the pitch).

**Risk flags.**
1. **Cyclicality / earnings volatility** — FY3/2024's +88.5% revenue and ¥8.4bn profit were rally-driven; Galaxy's $31m/qtr trading gross profit and -25% q/q industry volumes show how fast principal-MM economics reverse. A raise priced off a peak is exposed to mean-reversion.
2. **Disintermediation / margin captured upstream** — rails are owned by Circle/Paxos/Visa/Solana; ECNs (Tradeweb/CROSSx) and TradFi MMs (Flow Traders) commoditize execution. B2C2 risks being squeezed to a price-taking liquidity layer that owns nothing.
3. **Re-entry / balance-sheet asymmetry** — post-GENIUS Jane Street/Jump re-entry (unverified) brings far larger balance sheets; the same settlement narrative that lifts the multiple also invites the players who erode the spread.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` The pitch is a **trading-desk → settlement-infrastructure re-rating** — if it works, B2C2 (and Wintermute) get software-style multiples on what is still cyclical principal flow. Falsifiable thesis: *the re-rating is unjustified unless PENNY/USDG settlement produces recurring, spread-stable revenue independent of the trading cycle.* Trigger/what breaks it: the next down-quarter (a Galaxy-style volume drawdown) hitting B2C2's P&L, or the raise closing at/below a commodity trading multiple — either would confirm "still a trading desk." Watch: disclosed raise valuation + buyers, and whether USDG/PENNY volume grows independent of crypto beta.
