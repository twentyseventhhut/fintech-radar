AppsFlyer has raised more than $1 billion in Series E funding at a $2.7 billion post-money valuation, Axios has learned from sources familiar with the financing.

**Why it matters:**The investment is from four of the world's top advertising platforms, which are betting on the need for independent measurement as AI reshapes digital advertising.

**Driving the news:**Moloco, Google, Meta and Unity have each acquired a minority stake in AppsFlyer, CEO and co-founder Oren Kaniel tells Axios. Kaniel did not comment on specifics of the deal.

*   **"**They believe what we believe: that attribution and measurement must be independent, unbiased and trusted," Kaniel says. "As AI takes over more of how advertising gets bought and optimized, the signals feeding those systems become the most consequential infrastructure in the industry."
*   The investments are non-exclusive and while each company is integrated with AppsFlyer, they will not receive preferential treatment, he says.
*   The transaction is subject to regulatory approvals.

**Context:** AppsFlyer provides omnichannel measurement across mobile, the web and connected TV along with deep linking and data collaboration. The marketing tech company was founded in 2011.

*   The SF-based company works with more than 15,000 brands globally and has about 1,300 employees.
*   The company has previously raised $300 million. Its General Atlantic-led 2020 Series D was $210 million at a $1.6 billion post-money valuation, Kaniel says.
*   Existing investors include Magma, Pitango, Eight Roads, Goldman Sachs Growth Equity, Qumra Capital, DTCP, Salesforce Ventures and General Atlantic.

**What's next:**Kaniel says the investment will help the business move faster on improving omnichannel measurement and building agentic workflows.

*   AppsFlyer also intends to go public.
*   "This is a milestone, not a destination," Kaniel says. "We have always believed AppsFlyer should one day stand as a public company, and this investment is a step on that path."

**Behind the scenes:**Goldman Sachs served as exclusive financial advisor and Meitar Law Offices and Latham & Watkins as legal advisors to AppsFlyer.

*   JPMorgan served as financial advisor and Freshfields and Herzog Law as legal advisors to Moloco. Fenwick served as corporate legal advisor to Meta. H-F & Co. served as legal advisor to existing investors.

# Context-enrichment: AppsFlyer secures $1B+ Series E from Moloco, Google, Meta, Unity ($2.7B post-money)
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 4/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- AppsFlyer (mobile measurement partner / MMP, founded 2011, SF + Israel) raised **>$1B in a Series E at a $2.7B post-money valuation**. First reported by Axios on **2026-06-22** ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/22/appsflyer-billion-moloco-google-meta-unity)); StrictlyVC/Crunchbase carried it 2026-06-23.
- Buyers: **Moloco, Google, Meta, Unity** — four ad/adtech platforms — each taking a "minority stake." Deal **subject to regulatory approvals**. Goldman Sachs was exclusive financial advisor to AppsFlyer.
- **De-PR flag #1 — this is mostly secondary / cash-out, not growth capital.** AppsFlyer is profitable, positive cash flow, ~$500M ARR (per coverage). A >$1B raise on a $2.7B post-money for a profitable company that "had previously raised $300M" and last raised in 2020 strongly implies a large **secondary** component (existing investors/employees selling), not $1B of new balance-sheet money. The note's "raised $1.3B total" conflates primary + secondary.
- **De-PR flag #2 — "minority, non-controlling" is contested.** AppsFlyer's PR and Kaniel insist the stakes are minority, non-exclusive, with contractual non-preferential treatment of APIs/signals/attribution logic. BUT **Globes reports the investor group bought a combined ~48% stake** led by Moloco ([globes.co.il](https://en.globes.co.il/en/article-moloco-leads-group-buying-48-stake-in-appsflyer-1001546734)). 48% collectively is "minority" only on a strict >50% definition; in practice it is a near-controlling bloc and reframes the whole "independence" story (analysis).
- **Why structured this way:** the buyers are the *measured parties* (Google/Meta/Unity sell ads; Moloco is a DSP). Them funding the *referee* is the structural tension Axios itself flags. The "non-preferential treatment" contract clauses exist precisely because the conflict of interest is obvious — they are governance band-aids, not neutral capital.
- IPO framing: Kaniel calls it "a step on that path" to going public — i.e., this is also a **pre-IPO liquidity / cap-table cleanup** event.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- MMP market: **AppsFlyer + Adjust ≈ 45% of global MMP revenue (2024); AppsFlyer + Adjust + Kochava >60%**. Other peers: **Branch** (deep-linking leader, ~32% growth in web-to-app last year), **Singular** (cost aggregation/ROAS), **Airbridge**, **Kochava** ([airbridge.io](https://www.airbridge.io/en/blog/appsflyer-vs-airbridge-vs-adjust-vs-branch-best-mmp-for-subscription-app-in-2026), [mobileaction.co](https://www.mobileaction.co/blog/best-mobile-measurement-partners-mmp/)).
- AppsFlyer position: **clear category leader on integration breadth (10,000+ network integrations) and ecosystem depth**; Adjust competes on SKAN implementation + flat transparent pricing; Singular on cross-channel ROAS; Branch on deep links.
- **Why the lay of the land is this way (2nd order):** Apple's ATT/SKAN and Google's Privacy Sandbox destroyed deterministic user-level attribution → MMPs pivoted to privacy-safe aggregated + incrementality measurement. The winner is whoever has the most network integrations and trust as a *neutral* clearinghouse. The $1B raise is partly a moat-buy: by taking equity, the walled gardens entrench AppsFlyer as the de-facto neutral layer over Adjust/Singular — competitors can now plausibly argue AppsFlyer is *no longer* neutral. The deal could be a competitive *gift* to Adjust/Singular on the independence pitch (analysis).

## [2] Company history / fit
- Founded 2011 (Tel Aviv/SF). Series C 2017; **Series D 2020: $210M led by General Atlantic at $1.6B post-money** ([appsflyer.com PR](https://www.appsflyer.com/company/newsroom/pr/appsflyer-raises-210m-series-d/)). ARR >$150M in 2019, ~850 employees then.
- Now: ~15,000 brands, ~1,300 employees, ~$500M ARR, profitable. Prior backers: General Atlantic, Salesforce Ventures, Pitango, Goldman Sachs Growth, DTCP, Qumra, Magma, Eight Roads.
- Valuation path: $1.6B (2020) → $2.7B (2026) = **~69% over ~6 years (~9%/yr)** — modest for "tech-deal-of-the-year" framing; reflects the 2021 bubble unwind and that this is a profitable, capital-efficient business, not a hyper-growth one.
- **Why now:** 6 years since last raise, profitable, employees/early VCs need liquidity, IPO not yet open → a strategic-investor secondary at a controlled valuation is the obvious move. The "AI/agentic measurement" framing is the growth narrative to justify the round and a future IPO.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Not a new product** — this is a financing/cap-table event. The genuine *new* thing is the **alignment of Google+Meta+Unity+Moloco behind a shared independent-measurement utility**, which is strategically novel (rivals co-investing in their own referee).
- Traction is **real and proven** (unlike most "AI" rounds): 15,000 brands, $500M ARR, profitable, 10,000+ integrations. This is the antithesis of the speculative AI-marketing rounds Crunchbase notes (sales/marketing VC ~$4.1B YTD 2026).
- **Value-add / who captures the margin:** AppsFlyer sits between advertisers and walled gardens as the trusted measurement layer. Its durable moat is *integration breadth + neutrality reputation*. The threat: if neutrality is perceived as compromised by the 48% strategic bloc, the very asset they paid for erodes (second-order risk). Also, walled gardens increasingly push their own first-party measurement (Google's GA4/Privacy Sandbox conversions, Meta's CAPI/AEM) — disintermediation risk: the platforms could route around the MMP layer they just invested in.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Plans:** faster omnichannel measurement (mobile/web/CTV), **agentic AI workflows**, and an **eventual IPO** (no date; "a step on the path").
- **Regulatory:** deal needs approvals. Given the active ad-tech antitrust climate (Google search/adtech rulings, Meta scrutiny), three direct ad-dollar competitors co-investing in the firm that grades their performance is a plausible antitrust/conflict flag. Watch for remedy conditions on the ~48% bloc / governance ([ppc.land](https://ppc.land/google-meta-moloco-and-unity-buy-into-appsflyer-to-keep-measurement-neutral/)).
- **Sentiment:** Israeli press (Globes, JPost) frame it as "tech deal of the year." Sober read: a high-quality, profitable category leader getting a strategic pre-IPO secondary — substantive, but the valuation step-up is modest and the "independence" narrative is in tension with a ~48% strategic stake.
- **Counterintuitive 2nd-order effect:** the capital meant to *cement* neutrality may *undermine* the perception of neutrality — handing Adjust/Singular a marketing wedge and inviting regulators in.

**Sector & drivers.** The mobile-measurement-partner (MMP) niche is small in absolute dollars but growing double-digit: per Intel Market Research, the global MMP market was ~$284M in 2024, projected to ~$639M by 2032 at ~12.5% CAGR ([intelmarketresearch.com](https://www.intelmarketresearch.com/mobile-measurement-partner-market-3547)). Structure: **consolidated** — AppsFlyer + Adjust ≈45% of global MMP revenue (2024) and AppsFlyer + Adjust + Kochava >60% (note [1]); barriers are network-effect integration breadth (10,000+ ad-network integrations) + the "neutral referee" trust asset, not capital. That secular shift is the real reason walled gardens are paying to own a stake in the referee now rather than build their own.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Independence/conflict (governance).** Three direct ad sellers (Google/Meta/Unity) + a DSP (Moloco) co-owning a reported ~48% bloc of the firm that *grades their ad performance* is a structural conflict no contractual "non-preferential treatment" clause fully cures; if neutrality perception erodes, the core moat — trust — erodes with it. Second-order: invites antitrust/remedy conditions (deal is explicitly subject to approval).
2. **Disintermediation by the same buyers.** The walled gardens funding the MMP also push first-party measurement (Meta CAPI/AEM, Google GA4/Privacy Sandbox; cf. PayPal/Mastercard commerce-media measurement in-base) — they could route around the layer they just bought into, capturing the measurement margin themselves.
3. **Secondary vs primary / valuation framing.** A >$1bn raise on a $2.7bn post-money for a profitable, last-raised-2020 company implies a large secondary/cash-out, not $1bn of new growth capital (note [0]); the headline "$1.3bn raised total" overstates fresh funding, and the ~9%/yr step-up + sub-IPO-range mark suggests a controlled pre-IPO liquidity event, not a growth re-rate.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Not a re-rating and not a new entry — it's a **strategic consolidation of the neutral-measurement standard** ahead of agentic ad-buying, with a pre-IPO secondary attached (analysis).
