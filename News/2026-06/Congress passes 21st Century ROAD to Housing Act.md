## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Both chambers passed the **21st Century ROAD to Housing Act (H.R. 6644)** — Senate **85-5 on 2026-06-22**, House **358-32 on 2026-06-23** — sending it to the President's desk (this is the event already covered in [[US Senate passes housing bill with four-year Fed CBDC ban]]). The genuinely NEW wrinkle in THIS pick: on **2026-06-24**, hours before a scheduled noon Capitol Hill signing ceremony, **Trump abruptly canceled it via Truth Social**, saying he won't sign until Congress passes the **SAVE America Act** (a.k.a. SAVE Act — Safeguard American Voter Eligibility Act), which requires documentary proof of citizenship / voter ID. He called it "a National Emergency" and derided the housing bill as "Warren centric." [nbcnews.com/.../trump-cancels-plan-sign-major-housing-bill](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/donald-trump/trump-cancels-plan-sign-major-housing-bill-fights-congress-act-rcna351558) · [axios.com/2026/06/24/trump-delays-housing-bill-save-act](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/24/trump-delays-housing-bill-save-act)

**The digest's procedural claim is mostly right but loosely worded.** Under the Constitution (Art. I, Sec. 7), a bill becomes law **10 days (Sundays excepted) after presentment** if the President neither signs nor vetoes — but ONLY if Congress remains in session; if Congress adjourns within that window, the bill dies via **pocket veto**. So "automatically becomes law 10 days after passing out of Congress" is imprecise on two counts: the clock runs from **presentment** (not passage), and the pocket-veto adjournment risk is real and unmentioned. [time.com/.../trump-housing-bill-save-america-act](https://time.com/article/2026/06/24/trump-housing-bill-save-america-act-voting-restrictions/) · [pbs.org/.../trump-says-he-wont-sign-major-housing-bill](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/trump-says-he-wont-sign-major-housing-bill-until-congress-passes-save-act)

**→ Why structured this way:** Trump is using a popular, must-not-fail bipartisan bill as **leverage** to extract an unrelated, stalled voting-rights priority — even GOP leadership says the SAVE Act lacks the votes. The veto-override math (85-5 / 358-32 are both well above two-thirds) makes a formal veto futile, so the canceled ceremony is **theater/leverage, not a real blocking mechanism** — the bill is very likely to become law on its own timeline. Warren ("sign the damn bill") and the bipartisan sponsors are calling the bluff.

The US runs ~2.5m loan originations/year (secondary citation, unverified primary). **Why now for THIS bill:** the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act (H.R. 6644) is a **supply-side + investor-restriction** statute, not a fintech bill. Its operative levers for housing fintech: (a) Title-2 NEPA/zoning streamlining → more new-construction units → more purchase-origination volume over time; (b) institutional-investor cap — restricts new single-family purchases by "large institutional investors" (≥350 SF homes), with HUD reporting within 180 days of enactment and civil penalties of $1m/violation or 3x purchase price (per Greenberg Traurig / Latham insights). The House softened the Senate's stricter 7-year forced-sale of newly-built BTR homes (per Greenberg Traurig). **Why it matters:** the binding fintech channel is volume (more homes built → more loans), not a new product mandate; the bill does not create or fund any mortgage-tech rail.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: origination volume / gain-on-sale margin (origination), servicing UPB & MSR value (servicing), ARR/NRR (SaaS tooling like nCino). **Risk flags.**
