Jet Bank launches as Albania's first digital-only bank
Sinziana Albu
25 Jun 2026 / 5 Min Read
Jet Bank has launched Albania's first digital-only bank, built entirely on Backbase's AI-Native Banking OS.
The institution received its banking licence as a greenfield entity in March 2026, meaning the licence was newly granted rather than acquired through an existing institution. A team of 70 professionals built the bank over six months, a timeline that contrasts with the years typically required to launch a licensed bank from scratch. The bank was developed without any legacy code, operating entirely on Backbase's AI-native infrastructure from the outset.
Albania's financial services sector has historically been characterised by substantial cash usage, and public demand ahead of the launch reflected latent appetite for digital banking alternatives. More than 75.000 people had joined the bank's waiting list before operations began, with registered users now receiving invitations to activate accounts on a rolling basis. In addition, account opening and management are conducted entirely through a mobile application, with core banking services available in real time.
At launch, customers have access to multi-currency current accounts, savings accounts, term deposits, and JetPlans dedicated savings goals, as well as virtual and physical debit cards and foreign exchange services. In-app live chat and secure messaging are also available. Loans and credit cards are expected to be added in the coming months, and conversational and agentic AI capabilities are planned for a later stage.
Technology platform and strategic framing
The Backbase AI-Native Banking OS underpins Jet Bank's full operational stack, enabling customer, employee, and AI agent interactions to be handled from a single unified layer, rather than across the disconnected systems that characterise many established financial institutions. The model reflects an approach to bank architecture that prioritises platform unification over modular legacy integration.
 Fatbardha Rino , chief executive of Jet Bank, stated that the decision to enter the Albanian market was informed by confidence in the country's economic potential and its population. Rino noted that the partnership with Backbase made it possible to bring the bank to market within months, with a model designed for continuous improvement.
 Jouk Pleiter , chief executive of Backbase, indicated that launching a licensed bank with a small team in such a short timeframe would, until recently, have been considered unrealistic. Pleiter described the approach as building on a single AI-native foundation rather than inheriting fragmented legacy systems, enabling a unified operational front-end for customers, employees, and AI agents from day one.
Jet Bank is the first digital-only licensed bank in Albania and, according to both companies, represents a reference point for digital banking development across the Western Balkans.

# Context-enrichment: Jet Bank launches as Albania's first digital-only bank on Backbase
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS VERDICT: FRESH** — but a thin "go-live" follow-up. This is the actual launch (operations + app live, 25 Jun 2026) of a bank whose Backbase partnership we already covered at [[Jet Bank taps Backbase to launch Albania's first neobank]] (19 Jan 2026, "gearing up to go live"). Per the skill's own rule, a genuine "announced → launched" transition counts as a NEW development, so this is fresh, not a duplicate. The weight, however, is low: same companies, same "first digital-only bank in Albania" claim, same 6-month/70-people build narrative — what is genuinely new is only (a) the licence date (Bank of Albania, March 2026), (b) the live launch + 75k waiting-list conversion, (c) the disclosed product set at launch.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- Jet Bank went live on 25 Jun 2026 as Albania's first fully digital-only licensed bank, on Backbase. Licence was granted by the Bank of Albania in **March 2026** as a **greenfield** entity (newly issued, not acquired). Owner: Jet Holding B.V., Amsterdam. CEO: Fatbardha Rino (24y banking, ex multi-country).
- Build: ~70 people, ~6 months; setup → UAT in ~3 months (per the Jan press release). No legacy code; core banking implemented by **Facilization** (Jet Bank's core-banking partner), with Backbase as the engagement/front-end layer — a detail the June framing buries.
- **Live products at launch:** multi-currency current accounts, savings, term deposits, JetPlans savings goals, virtual + physical debit cards, FX, in-app chat/secure messaging. **Loans and credit cards are NOT live** ("coming months"); conversational/agentic AI is "planned for a later stage."
- Traction signal: 75,000+ waiting-list sign-ups, now converting on a rolling basis. This is a registration list, not 75k funded/active accounts — do not read it as adoption.
- **Why the PR is framed this way / the de-PR'd reality:** The release leans on "AI-native Banking OS." But Backbase only *launched/branded* the "AI-Native Banking OS" on **22 Apr 2026** — after the Jan partnership. So calling the Jan build "AI-native from the outset" is retroactive packaging; the actual live AI capability is admitted to be future ("planned for a later stage"). The real, shippable story is "fast greenfield neobank on a SaaS engagement layer + Facilization core" — a configuration sale, not an AI breakthrough.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Core/engagement-platform peers powering "first neobank in market X":** Mambu (e.g. [[Mambu selected as core banking provider by Nyla]], "Africa's first Islamic neobank", Mar 2026), 10x Banking ([[10x Banking and audax partner to speed bank upgrades]], same "6 months / fraction of cost" pitch), Thought Machine, Temenos, Codebase Technologies ([[Codebase Technologies and Fuze partner on digital asset access]]). Backbase itself ran the same playbook with [[Libyan Islamic Bank modernizes banking with Backbase]] (Aug 2025).
- **In-market (Albania):** Jet Bank is genuinely first as a 100%-digital licensed bank, but it competes with incumbents that already digitized — Raiffeisen Albania reports 30k+ active digital-wallet users; ~93% of Albanian banks have digital-transformation units. So "first digital-only" ≠ "uncontested digital greenfield."
- **Position:** First-mover on label, follower on substance. The "6-month build" is now an industry-standard vendor claim (10x, Mambu, Backbase all make it), so it is not a differentiator — **why:** the speed comes from the vendor's pre-built stack, which means the moat sits with the vendor, not the bank.

## [2] Company history / fit
- Jet Bank is a brand-new, foreign-owned (Dutch Jet Holding B.V.) greenfield with no prior history — the trajectory is entirely forward-looking. CEO Rino's multi-country banking background fits the "import European standards into an underbanked market" thesis.
- **Why this structure:** Albania is a high-cash, under-penetrated market joining SEPA (Nov 2024) with electronic payments +24% YoY (H1 2025) and the first increase in bank count in 20 years expected in 2026 — a window where a greenfield digital entrant can grab share cheaply before incumbents fully convert. Dutch ownership + Backbase (Amsterdam) is a tidy Netherlands-Albania bridge.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **Genuinely new vs the Jan note:** the licence (Bank of Albania, Mar 2026), the live app, and the converting waiting list. That is the only delta — everything else is restated.
- **Value-add, one level deeper:** Real value is regional reference-selling for Backbase ("Western Balkans benchmark"), i.e. Jet Bank is as much a Backbase case study as a bank. **Who captures the margin:** the platform vendor (Backbase recurring SaaS + Facilization core) captures durable economics; Jet Bank carries the licence, capital, CAC and credit risk. Until lending/cards ship, Jet Bank monetizes mainly deposits + FX + card interchange — thin. The multiple here is the *vendor's*, not the bank's.
- **What breaks it:** if the 75k list doesn't convert to funded, transacting accounts, or if lending (the actual revenue engine) slips, this stays a deposit-gathering app in a small (~2.8m population) market.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Roadmap: loans + credit cards "coming months"; conversational/agentic AI "later." Long-term stated ambition: scale into a regional Western Balkans digital platform beyond Albania.
- **Backdrop:** Albania SEPA accession, BoA building a TIPS-clone instant-payments rail (Jan 2025), regulator visibly pro-digital. Tailwind is real.
- **Counterintuitive second-order:** "first digital-only in a cash market" cuts both ways — low digital-banking competition, but also low baseline digital trust/literacy and a thin addressable affluent base. First-mover advantage in a 2.8m-person, cash-heavy market may be a small prize; the more bankable optionality is the regional-platform expansion, which is unproven and years out.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** This is a vendor-led greenfield neobank launch in a small EM market, so the investable sector is twofold: (a) the Western Balkans/Albania digital-banking *market* (the bank's TAM) and (b) the core/engagement-platform *vendor* market (where the durable economics sit). Albania TAM is small: ~2.8m population, high-cash — cash-on-delivery still ~78% of e-commerce orders, though wallet transactions grow ~17% CAGR (per Madres Travels/Fintech Times, May 2026). Tailwinds are real and dated: SEPA accession (Oct 2025, lowering cross-border euro-transfer cost ~10x per World Bank, Mar 2026), BoA instant-payments rail expected later 2026, electronic payments +24% YoY (H1 2025), and the first increase in Albania's bank count in ~20 years expected in 2026 — a genuine "why now" window for a digital entrant (per Raiffeisen Albania / Fintech Times, 2026). The vendor sector is consolidating around an "AI-native banking OS" framing: Backbase rebranded its platform on 22 Apr 2026, after this engagement began. Structure: the bank layer is capital-/regulation-intensive with thin near-term economics; the vendor layer is a fragmented but scaling SaaS oligopoly (Backbase, Temenos, Mambu, Thought Machine, 10x) with recurring revenue and high switching costs — value accrues there, not to a sub-scale licensee.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs that matter here: for the *bank* — funded/active accounts (not waiting-list signups), deposits, and eventually NIM once lending ships; for the *vendor* — ARR, NRR, and reference-customer count. Players: in-market, Jet Bank is first as a 100%-digital *licensed* bank but competes with already-digitized incumbents (Raiffeisen Albania ~30k+ digital-wallet users; ~93% of banks have digital units) plus EMI/lending fintechs (EasyPay, Pago, Iute) — so "first digital-only" is a label, not an uncontested market. Recent vendor moves: Backbase ran the identical "first neobank in market X on our stack" playbook with [[Libyan Islamic Bank modernizes banking with Backbase]] (Aug 2025); peers do the same — [[Mambu selected as core banking provider by Nyla]] ("Africa's first Islamic neobank", Mar 2026), [[10x Banking and audax partner to speed bank upgrades]] (same "6 months/fraction of cost" pitch), [[Thought Machine signs Shawbrook Bank as core banking customer]] (Sep 2025). Basis of competition: distribution + reference proof, not price. Jet Bank's position: first-mover on the *label*, follower on *substance* — the 6-month build is now a commoditized vendor claim, so the moat sits with Backbase (recurring SaaS) and Facilization (core), not the bank (analysis).

**Comps & multiples.** Jet Bank itself has no disclosed valuation, capital base, or backers (Jet Holding B.V.) — bank-level multiples = no data. The relevant comps are the *platform vendors* whose multiple this launch actually expands. EV/Revenue arithmetic (all figures publicly verifiable; private names are round valuations, not market caps):
- **Temenos** (public, the only clean comp): EV ~$5.81bn / FY25 rev ~$1.07bn = **~5.4x** EV/Rev; ARR ~$860m growing ~12%. In-line for a mature, profitable banking-software name.
- **Thought Machine** (private): $2.7bn round valuation / ~$70.6m rev (2024) = **~38x** — rich, justified only by growth + scarcity, not profit.
- **Mambu** (private): ~$5.5bn 2024 Series E / ~$128.6m rev = **~43x** — rich and *stale* (peak-2024 raise; treat with caution, valuation likely re-rated since).
- **Backbase** (private, the protagonist's vendor): ~€2.5bn pre-money / ~$345m ARR (2024, up from ~$247m in 2023) ≈ **~7–8x ARR** — strikingly *cheaper* than the VC-backed private peers, consistent with bootstrapped, profitable-ish growth rather than blitz-scaling.
Distribution across 4 comps spans ~5x–43x, so no single median is meaningful (growth/profitability drive the spread). Internal comps: [[Jet Bank taps Backbase to launch Albania's first neobank]] (Jan 2026, the partnership), [[Stablecoin neobank Fasset raises $51 million for emerging markets]] (May 2026, EM neobank funding marker). Outlier note: the wide private/public gap is a *growth-multiple* effect, not a flag — but Mambu's ~43x is the one to discount as stale.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Disintermediation / margin captured by the vendor stack.** Jet Bank holds the licence, capital, CAC and (future) credit risk, while Backbase + Facilization capture the durable recurring economics — the bank is as much a vendor case study as a business; second-order, its equity story is structurally thinner than the vendor's.
2. **Traction risk — "registrations ≠ adoption."** 75k is a waiting list, not funded/transacting accounts; in a high-cash, low-digital-trust market, conversion is unproven and the real revenue engine (lending/cards) is not yet live, so near-term monetization is just deposits + FX + interchange (thin).
3. **Small-prize / single-market concentration.** A 2.8m-person cash-heavy market caps the first-mover prize; the bankable optionality (Western Balkans regional platform) is unproven and years out, so execution risk is concentrated on an expansion that hasn't started.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** For the *vendor* sector, this is a reference-sell, not a re-rating: it strengthens Backbase's "AI-native, 6-month greenfield in EM" pitch and pressures Mambu/10x/Thought Machine to match the deployment-speed narrative in Balkan/EM tenders (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: if Jet Bank's waiting list converts to a disclosed base of funded, transacting accounts AND lending ships on schedule (H2 2026), it validates the playbook and Backbase reuses it across the region; the trigger to watch is the *first disclosed active-account/deposit figure*. What would make it wrong: another licensed digital-only applicant at BoA, or the list failing to convert — in which case this stays a deposit-gathering app and a vendor logo, not a market event.
