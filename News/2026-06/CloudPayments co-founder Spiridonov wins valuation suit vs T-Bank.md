# Context-enrichment: CloudPayments co-founder Spiridonov wins valuation suit vs T-Bank
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**Freshness: fresh** — first coverage of CloudPayments / Tinkoff(T-Bank) and of this dispute in the corpus (no prior `company/cloudpayments` or `company/t-bank` notes exist). The 2026-06-29 item reports a NEW event: the ~4-year substantive arbitration (МКАС/ICAC) finally concluding. Earlier phases of the saga (Jan-2023 non-payment claim, 2023 disclosure suits) were never in the corpus, so nothing to dedupe against.

**Headline caveat up front (the anti-PR gate):** the note's title — "Spiridonov WINS valuation suit" — is **Spiridonov's own self-report** via his Telegram, relayed verbatim by Русский венчур. The independent press (Vedomosti, via sostav.ru, 2026-06-29) confirms only that the dispute *concluded*; it does NOT independently confirm a win. Worse, earlier reporting (CNews 2026-02-06) and the documented track record show Spiridonov **lost** every related state-court proceeding. **No awarded amount is disclosed anywhere.** Treat "won" as unverified / contested.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- A >4-year corporate dispute between CloudPayments co-founder **Dmitry Spiridonov (Дмитрий Спиридонов)** and **T-Bank** (formerly Tinkoff / TCS Group) concluded ~2026-06-29. It was heard at the **International Commercial Arbitration Court (МКАС / ICAC) at the RF Chamber of Commerce** — a confidential arbitral forum, so there is **no public ruling text**.
- What was actually disputed: **the price paid for Spiridonov's residual 5% stake** when Tinkoff exercised a buyout option in **January 2023**. Per the option agreement, price = a **formula tied to trailing-12-month EBITDA** (audited by "Технологии Доверия — Аудит", the ex-PwC Russia entity). Spiridonov claimed underpayment of **~116M rubles** (alleged shortfall + interest) and argued the calculation ignored the company's strategic/growth value. (CNews, 2026-02-06.)
- The de-PR'd reality: Spiridonov says he "proved his stake was mis-valued." Press says T-Bank "**fulfilled its obligations in full**" (with reported findings of abuse of rights by Spiridonov). These two accounts are **irreconcilable**, and only the founder-friendly one reached the digest. **(analysis)** Why the framing is this way: МКАС awards are confidential, so the only narrator on the record is the losing-everywhere-else founder — a textbook case of a PR-skewed single-source item.
- **Why it matters / second order:** the dispute hinges on a *trailing-EBITDA buyout formula* exercised exactly when CloudPayments' profit had collapsed (net profit 864M→467M→**36M** rubles in 2021→2022→2023; CNews). A formula that keys off the trailing year mechanically minimizes the payout if the option is exercised in a trough — the real grievance, beyond "mis-valuation," is *timing of the option call*.

## [1] Competitors / peers (founder-vs-acquirer valuation disputes)
- Direct corpus peer on the *mechanism* (control/valuation fight): **[[CAB Payments agrees 287M US takeover deal]]** (2026-04) — a public shareholder–board battle over a buyout price, i.e. the same "is the exit price fair" question, but fought in the open vs. in confidential arbitration here.
- On *minority-stake / earn-out litigation* broadly: **[[Tether pays $299.5 million to settle Celsius dispute]]** (2025-10) — a settled valuation/liquidation dispute; contrast: that one produced a public, quantified outcome, this one produced neither.
- Valuation-mechanics reference points in the corpus: **[[Plaid completes tender offer at $8 billion valuation]]** (2026-03), **[[Kraken parent Payward raises capital at $20 billion valuation]]** (2026-05) — show how Western private-stake repricing happens via tender offers/markups; Russian post-2022 exits instead run through opaque formulaic options with little founder disclosure leverage.
- **Position:** this is a small, idiosyncratic, individual dispute (5% stake, ~116M rub ≈ ~$1.3M scale), not a market-moving deal. **(analysis)** The pattern it fits — founders alleging undervaluation in post-2022 forced/structured exits — also describes the **Tinkov→Potanin** TCS sale dispute; same actor (Tinkoff/T-Bank), recurring "founder says he was shortchanged" motif.

## [2] Company history / fit
- **CloudPayments**: online-acquiring / payment-gateway "constructor" for e-commerce, founded **2014** by Spiridonov and **Konstantin Yan (Константин Ян)**.
- Acquisition was **staged**: **Oct 2017** Tinkoff bought **55%** (reported ~290M rub ≈ ~$5M — note: this price is NOT in the primary Kommersant/Vedomosti deal announcement and should be treated as reported-not-confirmed), with a 2-year option on the rest; **May 2019** bought another **35%** → 90%; **2019** Yan exited; **Jan 2023** the option on Spiridonov's final ~5% was exercised → **100%**.
- Scale (CNews): 2021 revenue +119.8% YoY to >4.5B rub; the **net-profit collapse to 36M rub in 2023** is the load-bearing fact for the dispute (the EBITDA formula bit exactly here).
- **Why structured this way:** acquirers prefer staged option-based buyouts (control early, cheap formula-priced tail later, contractual confidentiality). Efficient for Tinkoff; left the founder with minimal disclosure rights — which is precisely what the years of failed disclosure suits exposed.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- Genuinely *new event* (arbitration concluding), but **low informational value-add**: outcome is contested, the award (if any) is secret, and the proceeding sets **no public precedent** (МКАС is confidential).
- Traction of the underlying "win" claim: **weak.** Documented record is a string of **losses** — Moscow Arbitration Court rejected Spiridonov's disclosure suit (May 2023), appeal rejected (Aug 2023), cassation rejected (Nov 2023); his Jan-2023 "they didn't pay me" claim was walked back after he confirmed receiving the money. (Forbes.ru; vc.ru; CNews.) Against that backdrop, an unverified self-declared arbitration "win" deserves skepticism.
- **Who's silent about what:** the amount. No source — including Spiridonov — states what, if anything, he was awarded. A "win" with no number is the tell.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Procedurally near-terminal: МКАС/ICAC awards are generally **final**, set aside only on narrow grounds in state court — so this likely ends the saga regardless of who "won."
- **Backdrop:** Tinkoff Bank rebranded to **T-Bank** (June 2024) under Potanin/Interros control after Oleg Tinkov's forced ~2022 exit. Post-2022 Russian fintech M&A skews toward opaque, formula-priced, confidentiality-bound founder exits.
- **Risks / second order (analysis):** the lasting signal is *cautionary for founders*, not market-moving: a trailing-EBITDA option call timed to a profit trough can gut a minority founder's exit value, and confidential arbitration leaves them with neither leverage nor a public record. Importance is capped by the small size, the contested/secret outcome, and the absence of systemic impact.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Subvertical: Russian online payments / merchant-acquiring (payment-gateway / "constructor" for e-commerce), inside the T-Bank (T-Technologies / formerly TCS / Tinkoff) ecosystem. Market size: Russia cards-and-payments est. ~$0.78tn in 2025, projected ~$2.19tn by 2033 at ~13.7% CAGR (per Market Data Forecast / Mordor Intelligence, as of 2025 — secondary analyst figures, treat as order-of-magnitude). The structural shift is the central bank's Faster Payments System (SBP/СБП): 13.4bn transactions / RUB 69.5tn in 2024; SBP share of C2C transfers ~88.6% in 2024 (up from ~80% a yr earlier) per Bank of Russia / TAdviser. Why now: the disclosed news is NOT a market move — it is a 4-year corporate/valuation dispute concluding (arbitration at the ICAC, RF Chamber of Commerce). Structure: domestically consolidated around a few ecosystems (Sber, T-Bank, YooMoney/VK, Mir/SBP rails); high barriers = bank licensing + acquiring infra + post-2022 sanctions-driven walling-off from Visa/Mastercard, which forces all card rails onto Mir + SBP. Second-order: SBP's zero/low-fee push structurally compresses acquiring take rates — relevant to what a payments-constructor like CloudPayments is worth over time.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs (acquiring): TPV/turnover, take rate (merchant discount), per-transaction margin, merchant attach. None disclosed for CloudPayments at segment level (T-Technologies does not break it out) → `[UNSOURCED]`. Key players & basis of competition: Sber (scale + default distribution), T-Bank/CloudPayments (product + SMB e-com distribution), YooMoney (VK), plus the regulator-owned SBP rails competing on price (low/zero fee) against card acquiring. CloudPayments' position: niche-but-embedded — a SMB/internet-business payment constructor that became a 100%-owned T-Bank subsidiary (timeline: T-Bank 55% in 2017 for RUB 290m; +35% in 2019; remaining stake bought out by Jan 2023, reaching 100%). Moat `(analysis)`: distribution + switching costs via T-Bank's SME base, not a standalone network effect. The note's own primary text and a same-month corpus item ([[Финтехно Russian fitness clubs shift to SBP subscription billing]]) both point the same way — recurring/subscription billing is migrating to SBP rails, i.e. disintermediation pressure on card-acquiring economics.

**Comps & multiples.** This is a litigation/governance event, not a financing — there is no fresh CloudPayments valuation marker. Only anchor: 2017 entry at RUB 290m for 55% ⇒ implied 100% ≈ RUB 290m / 0.55 ≈ RUB 527m (~$9m at 2017 FX) `(analysis)` — a 2017 round/control valuation, NOT current market value, and the very figure now disputed by the founder. Forward multiples (EV/Rev, EV/EBITDA) = **no data** (segment financials not disclosed). Parent for materiality context: **T-Technologies (T-Bank)** — tracked in our IR coverage (`tinkoff`, 49 filed docs). Filed FY2025 IFRS results (via T-Bank IR / AKM / bne IntelliNews): total revenue >RUB 1.4tn (+49% YoY), operating net profit RUB 174bn (+43% YoY), 54.1m total customers, 34.3m MAU. Against that, the entire disputed CloudPayments stake (founder's residual ~5–10%) is immaterial to the parent — sub-0.1% of any reasonable group value. Internal acquiring/M&A comps from the base: none directly comparable for Russian acquiring earn-out disputes (semsearch top hits were Western M&A — Euronet/CrediaBank, Santander/TSB — not comparable; geo/model fail the comparability test) → distribution not computed, qualitative only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Governance / earn-out precedent (primary).** A co-founder publicly winning a 4-year suit alleging his stake was deliberately mis-valued under the option agreement signals weak minority-holder protection in Russian ecosystem buyouts — second-order: raises the perceived "founder-dilution risk" discount on future T-Bank/Sber-style acqui-buyouts of SMB fintechs, and invites copy-cat claims from other bought-out founders.
2. **Disintermediation of acquiring economics.** SBP's ~88.6% C2C share and low-fee mandate erode card-acquiring take rates; a payments-constructor's long-run value is captured by the regulator's rails layer — the "what's the stake even worth" question that underlies the suit is itself a symptom.
3. **Disclosure opacity / reputational, not financial, to T-Bank.** T-Technologies never broke out CloudPayments economics; the loss is immaterial to FY2025 numbers (RUB 174bn net profit) but the headline ("founder says Tinkoff just took it / didn't pay") is a governance-reputation flag, not a P&L event.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` Not a re-rating of T-Bank — materiality is trivial. The real signal is governance: this is a watch-item precedent for how Russian banking ecosystems settle with founders of acquired fintechs. Falsifiable thesis: if the arbitration award/terms become public and are sizeable, expect (a) more founder claims against ecosystem acquirers and (b) tighter option-agreement valuation language in future Russian SMB-fintech buyouts. Trigger to watch: disclosure of the award amount, any appeal, and whether other ex-founders (e.g. CloudPayments co-founder K. Yan, who exited in 2019) follow. What breaks the thesis: terms stay sealed and no copy-cat suits surface within ~12 months.
