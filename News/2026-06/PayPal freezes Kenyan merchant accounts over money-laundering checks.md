# PayPal Freezes Accounts of Kenyan Users Over Compliance Checks

*   by Kenyans.co.ke Writer on Wednesday, 3 June 2026 - 12:32 pm 

File image of Kenyans using smartphones

Photo

Kenyans.co.ke

PayPal has frozen funds and restricted the accounts of an unknown number of Kenyan users amid heightened compliance checks.

According to reports, the move is linked to Kenya's placement on the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) grey list, which identifies countries at increased risk of money laundering and terrorist financing, prompting global financial firms to enforce stricter verification measures.

Affected users have reportedly been asked to provide documents such as proof of employment, bank statements and utility bills to verify their identity and residential address.

The company is also requiring Kenyan users who receive money from abroad to explain the source and purpose of the funds, with some accounts facing restrictions until the requested information is provided.

paypal-kenyan-foreign-policy

Photo

Further, those who do not comply have revealed that they have been blocked from moving or withdrawing their money for at least six months.

Some of the affected individuals, including freelancers who earn income through digital gigs, have revealed that their funds have been held for extended periods, with the firm warning that accounts that remain non-compliant for more than six months risk being permanently deactivated.

According to reports, the PayPal restrictions come amid a broader trend of heightened scrutiny of financial accounts in Kenya over money laundering concerns, with cryptocurrency platforms such as Binance and other fintech firms also facing increased regulatory oversight.

PayPal has told affected users that it may hold remaining balances for up to 180 days before releasing them, to cover possible chargebacks or other liabilities.

Once an account is restored, future payments can still be held for up to 21 days.

In Kenya,PayPal is widely used by freelancers, remote workers receiving payments from overseas clients, and online shoppers seeking a secure alternative to sharing their card or bank details across multiple websites.

According to reports, one of the most challenging requirements for affected users has been providing proof of a physical address.

PayPal reportedly requires documents such as electricity, water, gas, or internet bills linked to a formally registered residential address.

However, many users argue that the requirement does not align with the reality of Kenya's addressing system, where landmarks and informal location descriptions are often used in place of structured street addresses common in countries such as the United States and those in Europe.
