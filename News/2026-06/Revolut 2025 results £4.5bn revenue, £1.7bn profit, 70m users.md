# Context-enrichment: Revolut 2025 results — £4.5bn revenue, £1.7bn profit, 70m users
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

> **Framing flag up front.** This pick is the **GBP framing of the SAME FY2025 results already in-base** as [[Revolut reports record $2.3bn profit and $6bn revenue for 2025]] ($6bn rev / $2.3bn PBT). £4.5bn ≈ $6bn at FX; £1.7bn = the PBT line ($2.3bn). It is a **digest re-run of a March-2026 print, not a new event** — that alone caps importance. The detailed [1]/[3] economics already live in this note's `## Market Research` and `## Earnings Review` sections; this Context layer focuses on the **de-PR'd reconciliation** and second-order reading.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
The headline "£4.5bn revenue, £1.7bn profit, 70m users, >£50bn deposits" needs three corrections before it can be trusted:
- **Revenue £4.5bn — CONFIRMED audited.** Audited consolidated revenue is **£4,515.8m** (FY ended 31 Dec 2025), +46% YoY vs £3,090.0m in 2024. BDO LLP signed a **clean/unqualified opinion** (12 Mar 2026). This is the solid part of the claim.
- **"£1.7bn profit" is PRE-TAX, not net.** Audited **PBT £1,713.3m** (+57%); **net profit is £1,304.6m** (+65%). The digest (and much PR) blurs PBT and net — a ~£0.4bn difference. → Why it matters: a casual reader double-counts margin. Note the in-base `Earnings Review` section even cites "net profit $1.7bn" — that figure conflates USD-PBT with net; the audited GBP net is £1.3bn.
- **"70m users" is a post-period PR milestone, not the audited count.** Audited **retail customers at 31 Dec 2025 = 68.3m** (+30% from 52.5m), plus 767K business. "70m" is a **January 2026 milestone** (see [[Revolut surpasses 70 million customers globally]]) and is **total registered customers, not active/MAU** — Revolut historically reports cumulative customers, never monthly-active. The funded/active share is **not disclosed** (open).
- **">£50bn deposits" includes partner-held balances.** PR "total customer balances £50.2bn (+66%)" bundles balances at partner institutions; the **on-balance-sheet customer liabilities are ~£36.9bn**. Use £36.9bn for any like-for-like bank comparison.
- **De-PR'd revenue mix (audited):** card/interchange £1,003.9m (22%), interest income £974.2m (22%), subscriptions £708.5m (16%), wealth incl. crypto £663.5m (15%), FX £605.8m (13%), rewards £192.8m (4%). **Durable lines (interest + subscriptions) ≈ £1.68bn ≈ 37%** of revenue.

**+ Why structured this way:** the loud framing (USD $6bn, "£1.7bn profit", 70m, £50bn) is **IPO-marketing optics** — bigger numbers, an active-bank narrative — ahead of a $75bn→$100bn+ secondary and an eventual US listing (see [[Revolut completes share sale at $75B valuation]], [[Revolut plans secondary share sale at over $100B valuation]]). The audited GBP/net/registered figures are smaller but real.

## [1] Competitors / peers
On-topic peers (latest reported): **Nubank** FY2025 ~$16.3bn revenue (+45%), ~$2.9bn net, **131m+ customers** ([[Nubank turns 13 with 135 million Latin America customers]], [[Nubank posts record Q1 2026 revenue above $5 billion]]) — bigger by users and revenue, public at ~3.1x EV/Rev. **Monzo** FY2025 ~£1.0–1.7bn revenue, £87.3m PBT, ~12.5m users ([[Monzo's profit jumps to £87.3 million as users grow 25%]]). **Chime** IPO'd Jun-2025 at ~$11.6bn (well below its $25bn private mark). **Wise** £1.65bn revenue, £565m PBT, 15.6m active.

**Position:** Revolut leads private European neobanks on profit-at-scale (£1.7bn PBT, 38% margin) and on **revenue-per-registered-user** (~£66), but Nubank dwarfs it on absolute users (131m vs 68.3m) and revenue (~$16bn vs $6bn). **+ Second-order:** the cleanest reality-check is the **multiple gap** — Revolut's $75bn private mark ≈ 12.5x P/S vs Nubank's ~3.1x EV/Rev as a *larger, profitable, public* peer; Chime's IPO already repriced a neobank below its private mark. The premium is partly justified by +46% growth + 38% margin, but the public market is the unforgiving test.

## [2] Company history / fit
Trajectory: 2021 ~$0.6bn rev (≈55% from crypto fees) → 2024 $4.0bn / 52.5m customers → 2025 $6bn / 68.3m. Valuation $33bn (2021) → $45bn (Aug-2024) → **$75bn (Nov-2025)** ([[Revolut valued at up to $65B in new funding round]], [[Revolut completes share sale at $75B valuation]]) → $100bn+ secondary / up-to-$200bn IPO ambition ([[Revolut targets up to 200B valuation in IPO two years out]]).

**Critical history caveat:** Revolut's **2021 accounts were filed ~6 months late and BDO issued a rare QUALIFIED opinion** — it could not verify completeness of ~£477m (three-quarters) of revenue due to weak IT controls (crypto-heavy mix). 2022 also delayed. **2024 and 2025 accounts were on time and clean** — the verification flag appears remediated, though the auditor is **still BDO**. **+ Why:** a commodity-ish, transaction-led model needs a software/banking multiple to justify a $75bn→$200bn mark → hence the push into licensed banking (full UK licence granted 11 Mar 2026, after FY2025 close, so **zero FY2025 contribution**; US charter filed; Mexico bank live) and durable subscriptions/interest income.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**No new event-novelty** — this is a digest re-surfacing of the March print. The *substantive* story already de-risked in the corpus: revenue is **genuinely diversified** (interest 22% + subscriptions 16% are recurring; **11 product lines each >£100m**; crypto no longer dominant vs 55% in 2021). **+ Who captures the margin / what breaks it:** the durable lines are interest income (rate-sensitive — net interest ~£891m on a £37bn deposit base; **compresses if central banks cut**) and subscriptions (+67%, the highest-quality line). Crypto/wealth (+31% after +298% in 2024) is the swing factor and is **not separately disclosed** (open). The lending pivot (loan book +120% to £2.15bn; credit losses roughly doubled to ~£61m) is unproven through a cycle. Traction is real, but the *quality/durability* of the 38% margin as lending scales is the silent variable.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Plans: full UK bank now live (FY2026 lending upside), Mexico bank live (Jan-2026), **US national-bank charter applied 5 Mar 2026** (OCC+FDIC), India/Brazil/Ukraine expansion ([[Revolut reaffirms interest in entering Ukrainian market]], [[Revolut plans 2027 US bank launch with FDIC-insured and stablecoin accounts]]). 2026 ambition ~$9bn revenue / ~$3.5bn profit ([[Revolut expects $9B revenue and $3.5B profit in 2026]]); 100m customers by mid-2027. Secondary at >$100bn planned H2-2026 ([[Revolut weighs H2 2026 secondary share sale near $150B valuation]]); IPO up to $200bn, US/Nasdaq, ~2 years out.

**+ Why / counterintuitive second-order:** a $75bn secondary is a **mark, not primary capital** — it sets a price, doesn't strengthen the balance sheet. The IPO band (12–33x trailing revenue) only holds if growth stays >35% AND the lending pivot proves margin-accretive; a sub-30% print or a credit-cost spike in the young loan book collapses the premium toward listed-peer multiples (~5x). Capital/valuation concentration around a private mark makes the eventual listing **fragile to repricing**, not safe — Chime is the cautionary base-rate.

**Sector & drivers.** Global neobanking is a large, fast-growing pool — per multiple secondary trackers, ~350m neobank users globally in 2025, up from ~301.7m in 2024 (per electroiq/coinlaw aggregators, as of 2025); market-size estimates diverge widely ($211bn–$383bn for 2025 depending on methodology) so treat any single TAM as soft — `[UNSOURCED]` on a clean figure, the dispersion itself is the signal. Structure: still fragmented by geography (Revolut/Monzo/N26 in Europe, Nubank in LatAm, Chime in the US) — no single global winner, and the value chain is shifting from interchange-only models toward full balance-sheet banking (deposits + lending). Barriers are now primarily regulatory (banking licences) and capital, not product. Why now: Revolut secured a full UK banking licence in March 2026 and filed for a US bank charter, the gating step to lend off its own balance sheet — the structural reason its revenue mix is diversifying (interest income $1.3bn, +23%; credit book +120% to $2.9bn, per Revolut FY25 report, as of 2026-03-24).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: revenue/user (ARPU), deposit/customer-balance base, transaction volume, take rate, and increasingly net-interest income as licences land. Key players & basis of competition: Revolut (super-app breadth + FX/subscriptions), Nubank (LatAm scale + credit), Monzo (UK), Chime (US, interchange-led), N26 — competition is on product breadth and licence reach, not price. Recent peer moves: Chime IPO'd 2025-06-12 at ~$11.6bn (well below its $25bn private mark, per CNBC/Morningstar); Monzo raised £340m at a ~£4bn valuation and is prepping a London IPO at £6–7bn (per corpus [[Revolut wins UK approvals for private bank targeting £500k clients]]); Nubank posted Q1 2026 revenue near $5bn (+53% YoY, per web). Protagonist position: ahead on profitability-at-scale among private peers — $6bn revenue (+46%), $1.7bn net profit, 68.3m retail customers, $67.5bn balances, 5th consecutive profitable year, 38% PBT margin (per Revolut FY25 report). Moat `(analysis)`: scale + multi-product cross-sell (11 product lines >£100m revenue; 63% of new customers via referral → low CAC) — switching costs are moderate, the real moat is the licensed-banking footprint (30+ of 40 markets).

**Comps & multiples.** Note that this pick is the GBP framing (£4.5bn rev / £1.7bn profit) of the SAME FY25 results already in-base as [[Revolut reports record $2.3bn profit and $6bn revenue for 2025]] ($6bn rev / $2.3bn PBT / $1.7bn net) — not a new event; the £4.5bn ≈ $6bn at FX.

Median of the three sourced peers ≈ 5.5x P/S. Revolut at 12.5x trailing (and ~33x on the IPO target) is RICH vs every comparable — but it is the only one combining $6bn revenue, +46% growth AND a 38% PBT margin, so the premium is partly growth/margin-justified rather than an automatic flag. **Risk flags.**
1. **Valuation gap to public comps.** $75bn private mark = 12.5x P/S vs Nubank's 3.1x EV/Rev and Chime's ~5.5x P/S as listed peers. The $200bn IPO ambition implies the public market must underwrite ~33x trailing revenue — Chime's IPO already repriced a neobank to <half its private mark, the base-rate risk that Revolut's listing clears below the last round.

De-PR: "record profit" is real, but the quality/durability of the 38% margin as lending scales is the silent variable.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** `(analysis)` This is a re-rating-and-IPO-setup story, not a new datapoint — Revolut is positioning a 2027–28 listing at a multiple (12–33x) that only holds if growth stays ~40%+ AND the lending pivot proves margin-accretive. What breaks it: a sub-30% growth print or a credit-cost spike in the young loan book would collapse the premium toward listed-peer multiples (~5x).

## Earnings Review

<!-- enrichment:earnings_review -->
**Verdict (headline read).** BEAT (vs company's own prior-year ambition / sustainable, broad-based) · Revenue £4.5bn / $6.0bn (+46% YoY; no public sell-side consensus — private company, so beat is judged vs prior year + management's own guidance) · PBT £1.7bn / $2.3bn (+57%), net profit $1.7bn (+65%), PBT margin 38% (vs 35%) · drivers: revenue diversification (11 product lines >£100m) + operating leverage at scale · guidance: not formally given in the FY release, but management reiterated 100m customers by mid-2027; a Dec-2025 interview flagged a ~$9bn revenue / ~$3.5bn profit ambition for 2026 (see [[Revolut expects $9B revenue and $3.5B profit in 2026]]). Fifth consecutive year of net profitability.

**Key figures (with growth).**
- Revenue: $6.0bn / £4.5bn, +46% YoY (2024: $4.0bn / £3.1bn). Total transaction volume $1.7trn, +65%.
- Profit before tax: $2.3bn / £1.7bn, +57% YoY (2024: $1.4bn / £1.09bn). PBT margin 38% vs 35% in 2024 — driver is operating leverage ("disciplined financial steering", CFO), not a one-off.
- Net profit: $1.7bn, +65% YoY (2024: $1.0bn / £790m).
- Retail customers: 68.3m, +30% (added 16m in the year); business customers 767K, +33%. Transactions per customer +24%; paid-plan adoption +42%. RevPoints loyalty 17m users (vs 6.6m a year earlier).
- Customer balances / deposits: $67.5bn / £50.2bn, +66% YoY. Savings balances more than doubled to £20.4bn.
- Credit: total credit portfolio $2.9bn, +120% YoY (unsecured personal loans, credit cards, small mortgages book).

**By segment / driver.** Broad-based, no single engine carrying the print:
- Card payments $1.3bn, +45% — largest stream, ~22.2% of revenue.
- Interest income $1.3bn, +23% — ~21.6%; lagging the group on rate normalisation but cushioned by the +66% balance growth and +120% loan book.
- Subscriptions $936m, +67% — ~15.7%; fastest-growing major line, highest-quality (recurring) revenue.
- Wealth (equities + crypto) $876m, +31% — ~14.7%.
- FX $800m, +43% — ~13.4%.
- 11 product lines each >£100m (>$135m) annual revenue. Revolut Business = 16% of total income, $365bn transaction volume.

**vs expectations / prior period.** No public sell-side consensus (Revolut is private; no FactSet/Street estimate) → judged vs prior year and management's own framing, exact "consensus surprise" is [UNSOURCED]. Acceleration on the bottom line (PBT +57%, margin +3pts) and deposits (+66%) is the standout; revenue growth decelerated from +72% in 2024 to +46% in 2025 (law of large numbers as the base scales past $4bn), but profit growth re-accelerated relative to revenue. Internal trend over the corpus: clear up-and-to-the-right trajectory — 2024 was $4.0bn rev / $1.4bn PBT / 52.5m customers (see [[Revolut reports record $2.3bn profit and $6bn revenue for 2025]]), and customer count has since pushed past 70m / 75m post-period (see [[Revolut surpasses 70 million customers globally]]). vs UK neobank peer: Monzo FY pre-tax profit £87.3m (+44%) on £1.7bn revenue (see [[Monzo's profit jumps to £87.3 million as users grow 25%]]) — Revolut's £1.7bn PBT is roughly its entire revenue line and ~20x Monzo's profit, underscoring the scale gap.

**Guidance / forward.** No formal numeric FY-ahead guidance in the release. Management reiterated the 100m-customers-by-mid-2027 target and the transition to a "truly global bank" (licensed in 30+ of 40 markets; full UK banking licence secured March 2026; full Mexico launch Jan 2026; US bank charter filed). Prior public ambition (Dec 2025 interview): ~$9bn revenue / ~$3.5bn profit for 2026 — implies ~+50% revenue and ~+52% PBT, broadly in line with the 2025 growth rate; plausible given the diversification but assumes no rate-cut hit to the 21.6% interest line and sustained deposit inflows. Management tone: confident, emphasising "profitable at scale", margin discipline, and structural resilience of the multi-engine model. CFO framed geographic expansion beyond Europe as the "next frontier of focus."

**Thesis-flags.**
1. Margin expansion is structural, not cyclical (38% PBT margin, +3pts) → fact: profit grew faster than revenue → why: operating leverage + 63% of new customers via word-of-mouth (low CAC) → why it matters: validates the "profitable at scale" thesis that underpins the $75bn valuation → second-order: strengthens the case for a higher-multiple US IPO (2-3 yrs out) rather than a forced raise.
2.
