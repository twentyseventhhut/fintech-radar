# Gemabank places shares unsold under pre-emptive rights

> [!info] 2026-06-30 · 1 упоминаний · 0 источника(ов) с текстом
> Каналы: Mergers.ru

## Агрегированный текст (из дайджестов)

[Mergers.ru] 🩸 Гемабанк выставил на внебиржевое размещение акции, не выкупленные по преимущественному праву. По итогам завершившегося этапа реализации преимущественного права ряд акционеров, направивших заявления на приобретение акций, не смог исполнить обязательство по оплате в установленный срок. В соответствии с условиями размещения такие акции не признаются размещенными в рамках преимущественного права и подлежат доразмещению в порядке открытой подписки. Для завершения процедуры размещения Гемабанк открывает сбор оферт на приобретение 1 217 оставшихся акций. Размещение пройдет во внебиржевом порядке, без предварительного сбора оферт, с 29 по 30 июня 2026 года включительно. Цена одной обыкновенной акции – 116 рублей.

# Context-enrichment: Gemabank places shares unsold under pre-emptive rights
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 1/5._

**HEADLINE CORRECTION (de-PR / de-error).** "Gemabank" is NOT a bank. It is ММЦБ / MMCB (International Medical Center for Processing and Cryopreservation of Biomaterials, ticker **GEMA** on the Moscow Exchange), a **biotech / stem-cell storage company** in the **Artgen Biotech** (ABIO) group, operating the "Gemabank" cord-blood/stem-cell bank brand. The note's `region/ru` + `industry/capital-markets` + `type/funding` tags are directionally fine, but the framing as a "Russian bank equity raise" is wrong — this is an equity-market (SPO) event by a small-cap biotech, not a banking capital raise. (Sources: akm.ru, vedomosti.ru, artgen.ru, investfunds.ru.)

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
The aggregated note captures only the **administrative tail end** of a larger event. Gemabank ran a **secondary public offering (SPO)** on MOEX in mid-June 2026: up to **3,370,080 ordinary shares** offered at **RUB 116/share**, book open **15–18 June 2026**, trading resumed **19 June 2026**, raising **~RUB 400m** with the book **oversubscribed** (confirmed by Vedomosti, AKM, Artgen). The company targeted RUB 200–400m; it landed at the top of the range.

This specific note is about the **pre-emptive-rights cleanup**: during the pre-emptive stage, some existing shareholders filed purchase applications but **failed to pay on time**. Under the terms, those shares are **not deemed placed** under pre-emptive rights and are re-offered by open subscription. Gemabank opened collection of offers for **1,217 remaining shares**, placed **over-the-counter (внебиржевое), 29–30 June 2026**, at **RUB 116/share** (same SPO price). Total value of this residual tranche ≈ **RUB 141k** (1,217 × 116) — a rounding error next to the ~RUB 400m SPO.

**+ Why structured this way / what it reveals:** Russian additional-issue law (Law on JSCs, art. 40–41) grants existing shareholders a mandatory pre-emptive right; unpaid subscriptions revert to open subscription. So this is a **standard, legally-mandated mop-up**, not a "shares went unsold because demand was weak" story. Crucially, the *main* SPO was oversubscribed — so the "unsold under pre-emptive rights" phrasing is a legal-procedure artifact (a few shareholders didn't pay), NOT a sign of failed demand. Reading it as a capital-raising struggle would be exactly the wrong inference.

## [1] Competitors / peers
Peer set is **small-cap MOEX SPOs/placements**, not banks. In the corpus, the nearest analogues are equity placements by micro/small-caps: [[Stakk secures $15M placement to expand SaaS in US and Australia]] (single-tranche institutional placement at a fixed price) and the pre-IPO/strategic-investor mechanics in [[Gemini taps Nasdaq as $50M strategic investor ahead of IPO]]. Russian-market context: [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]] (unrelated business, but same sanctioned/isolated capital-market backdrop). None of these are the same event.

**+ Why the landscape looks this way:** Post-2022 sanctions closed foreign capital to Russian issuers, so small-cap growth funding is a **retail-driven domestic MOEX phenomenon** (Digital Broker / ALOR+ as organizers here). The pool of buyers is Russian retail + local funds; ticket sizes are tiny by global standards (~RUB 400m ≈ ~$5m). That structurally explains both the small size and the retail-friendly allocation rules (full fill up to RUB 50k, then pro-rata).

## [2] Company history / fit
GEMA IPO'd on MOEX in **July 2019** (opened +34% vs IPO price). It is the Russian market leader in personal stem-cell storage; controlling shareholder is **Artgen Biotech (ABIO)**. 2025 financials: revenue **RUB 376.7m** (+8%), net profit **RUB 158.4m** (+4.7%), EBITDA **RUB 180.4m**. Market cap ~**RUB 1.7bn** (Mar 2026). Dividends: RUB 2.8/share for 2025 (≈2.3% yield). This SPO fits: a profitable but tiny biotech topping up equity for "strategic development, liquidity and a long-term investor base."

**+ Why the company acts this way:** With a ~RUB 1.7bn cap and modest profit, organic growth + gene-therapy R&D needs external capital; debt is expensive in a high-rate RUB environment, so a small equity top-up plus a **parent backstop** (below) is the cheapest way to fund without over-diluting. Artgen retains control and signals confidence via the buyback offer.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Novelty of THIS note: essentially **zero** — it is a legal mop-up of 1,217 unpaid shares. Traction/substance sits in the **parent-backstop structure** of the main SPO, not in this note: **Artgen Biotech offered to buy back SPO shares at RUB 150.8/share after 3 years** (≈+30% on the RUB 116 price, ~9% CAGR) for investors who put in **≥RUB 500k** and **hold continuously for 3 years** — plus a **180-day lockup** on Artgen's own stake and a **30-day price-stabilization** window.

**+ Why it matters (2 levels deep):** The RUB 150.8 put is what actually de-risked the SPO and drove oversubscription — it converts an illiquid small-cap equity bet into something closer to a **~9% RUB "structured note"** with equity upside, attractive vs. deposits only if you trust Artgen's 3-year solvency. So the real story is a **parent underwriting its subsidiary's raise**, i.e. capital is effectively being recycled inside the ABIO group. The "unsold under pre-emptive rights" note captures none of this and is the least informative slice of the event.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Near term: the 1,217 residual shares clear OTC by 30 June; SPO proceeds fund "strategic development." Watch (a) whether free float / liquidity actually improves, (b) the 3-year Artgen buyback obligation (a real balance-sheet claim on the parent), and (c) gene-therapy pipeline progress that justifies the multiple.

**+ Second-order / counterintuitive:** The parent-backstop that made the SPO succeed also **transfers downside risk up to Artgen** — if GEMA underperforms and holders exercise the RUB 150.8 put in 3 years, ABIO absorbs the loss. Concentration of the safety net in one controlling shareholder makes the *group* the real credit, not the SPO buyers. For a Fintech Radar audience this is a minor, non-fintech, sub-$5m Russian small-cap event; weight is low.
