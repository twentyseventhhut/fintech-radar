## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened.** Reform Technologies, a UK/Belgium-linked AML regtech founded in 2024, revamped its board of directors and named **Elizabeth McCaul** — former ECB Supervisory Board member and former New York State Superintendent of Banks — as **chair**. The appointments follow a **€1 million equity raise** in which all incoming board members personally participated.

**[1] Who / the new board.**
- **Elizabeth McCaul (Chair)** — former member of the ECB Single Supervisory Mechanism Supervisory Board (2019–2024, non-renewable 5-yr term); former NY State Superintendent of Banks (acting from 1997, through 2003, incl. during 9/11); ex-Goldman Sachs banker; ex-CEO/Chair of Europe at Promontory Financial Group (IBM). Heavyweight banking-supervision credibility on both sides of the Atlantic.
- **Larry Sobin (Vice-Chairman)** — former COO of BNP Paribas CIB North America; ex-IBM Promontory partner.
- **Louis Wittock (Vice-Chairman / CEO co-founder)** — co-founder and CEO of Reform (with Filip Verbeke).
- **Jeff Lavine (Non-Executive Director)** — 30+ yrs at PwC; former Risk Management & Regulatory partner and Global Financial Crimes Leader.
- **Markus Schulz (Advisory Board Chair)** — ex-Group Financial Crime Officer at ING; currently at K2 Integrity; prior leadership at ABN Amro, Zurich Financial Services, GE Capital, Standard Chartered.

**[2] What Reform does.** Sells the **"Reform Digital Twin"** — a digital replica of a bank's legacy AML/transaction-monitoring stack that lets institutions **simulate, validate and optimise** their financial-crime detection frameworks (rules/thresholds/models) **before pushing changes to production**. Positioning: reduce false positives and modernise legacy TM without ripping out core systems. Sector = **RegTech / financial crime (AML/transaction monitoring)**, not payments or embedded finance.

**[3] Why it matters.** Very early-stage (founded 2024, only €1M raised) but the board is disproportionately senior — a deliberate credibility play to sell into regulated Tier-1/Tier-2 banks, where procurement is trust-gated. McCaul's ECB/NYDFS pedigree is the asset being monetised: regulatory-grade legitimacy for a startup with almost no track record. Timing aligns with EU AMLA (the new EU anti-money-laundering authority) standing up, raising bank appetite for defensible, validated TM frameworks.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector.** RegTech / financial crime, specifically **AML transaction monitoring (TM) & model validation**. Reform's "Digital Twin" is a sandbox/parallel-run layer for tuning legacy TM systems before production — a niche adjacent to (not competing head-on with) full TM engines.

**Market size / tailwind.** Global AML/KYC compliance spend is large and rising — see [[Global AML KYC spend to hit record $2.9 billion in 2025]]. Structural driver in Reform's core (EU/UK) market: the **EU AMLA** anti-money-laundering authority standing up 2025–26, which raises supervisory expectations for effectiveness and auditability of TM frameworks — directly the pain Reform's validation/simulation pitch addresses. McCaul is being marketed on exactly this angle ("9/11 to AMLA" webinar).

**Competitive landscape / comps.**
- **Full TM / AI monitoring engines** — ThetaRay ([[ThetaRay appoints Brad Levy as Chief Executive Officer]], [[I&M Group inks partnership deal with ThetaRay for AML platform]]), Flagright ([[OnePay adopts Flagright's AI-powered AML monitoring platform]]), plus incumbents (NICE Actimize, SAS, Oracle, Feedzai). These are the systems Reform's Digital Twin sits *beside*, not necessarily replaces.
- **AI-agent AML/KYC challengers** — [[Castellum.AI raises $8.5M for AML KYC platform]] (claims 94% false-positive reduction, ex-regulator-trained agents). Direct thematic peer on the "modernise legacy AML" narrative — and a useful benchmark: Castellum raised $8.5M Series A vs Reform's €1M, i.e. Reform is a full stage earlier.
- **Integrated risk/compliance consolidators** — [[CUBE acquires Acin to launch integrated risk and compliance solution]] (UK RegTech, control-to-regulation mapping); Valsoft/Alessa; K2 Integrity (where advisor Markus Schulz sits) — signals ongoing consolidation and that credibility/advisory networks are the moat.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Stage vs. story mismatch.** €1M raised, founded 2024, product = pre-production simulation tool with no disclosed customer logos or ARR. The board pedigree vastly exceeds the commercial evidence — classic "credibility-led" early startup; execution risk is high.
2. **Related-party governance.** All new board members are also the investors in the €1M round. Board oversight independence is structurally weak when directors are the funders and are being marketed as the product's selling point.
3. **"Digital Twin" is a thin wedge.** Simulating legacy TM before production is a feature many TM vendors (and banks' own model-validation/MRM teams) already do internally; defensibility and willingness-to-pay for a standalone tool are unproven.
4. **Long, trust-gated sales cycles.** Selling into regulated banks is slow; €1M runway is modest for enterprise GTM. Marquee directors open doors but do not close 12–18-month procurements.
5. **Reputation dependency on one name.** Value proposition leans heavily on McCaul; key-person concentration on both credibility and network.

**Net.** Legitimate, well-timed AML angle (AMLA tailwind) with an exceptionally senior board for its size, but this is a governance/credibility story at a pre-commercial company, not a validated-traction story. Watch for: named bank customers, a larger Seed/Series A, and independent (non-investor) board members.
