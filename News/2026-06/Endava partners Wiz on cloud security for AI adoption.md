Endava partners with Wiz on cloud security for AI adoption
Sinziana Albu
30 Jun 2026 / 5 Min Read
Endava has partnered with Wiz to deliver integrated cloud security for enterprise AI adoption.
The collaboration is intended to combine AI-enabled defence, multicloud security and agentic AI governance into a shared foundation for enterprise transformation. Building on its existing status as a Google Cloud Premier Partner and Google Security Partner, Endava has joined the Wiz Partner Alliance, a global network of partners working with customers on cloud security and operations.
As enterprises increasingly operate across multiple cloud platforms, SaaS ecosystems and AI models, maintaining consistent security policies has become more complex. Through the partnership, Endava will support enterprises in building cloud security foundations using Wiz capabilities, with the aim of providing greater visibility across multicloud environments and supporting the secure adoption of AI-enabled workloads.
According to data cited by Google Cloud, the time between an initial breach and the next stage of a cyberattack has shrunk from eight hours to 22 seconds, as attack surfaces extend beyond the traditional network perimeter. The partnership is intended to help customers move from fragmented security checks toward a continuous model in which risks are identified, prioritised and addressed earlier across the AI lifecycle.
Services and capabilities
Under the partnership, Endava will provide cloud risk assessments aimed at identifying vulnerabilities and misconfigurations, managed detection and response services to support monitoring, triage and investigation of critical issues, and advisory and implementation support to embed Wiz into development pipelines and AI workflows. The intention is to help organisations identify and address risks earlier, before systems reach production.
 Nick Ross , VP of EMEA and LATAM Channels and Alliances at Wiz, said security has become a foundational layer of the AI era, noting that enterprises want to innovate with AI and cloud while having confidence that the services they build are secure, resilient and trusted by their customers. He added that Endava’s experience within the Wiz Partner Alliance is expected to help organisations turn cloud security visibility into action across the multicloud environments they operate.
 Matt Cloke , CTO at Endava, said that as enterprises adopt agentic AI, autonomous agents are able to access data repositories, systems and permissions across interconnected environments. He noted that this is increasing pressure on organisations to strengthen data governance, identity controls and consistent security policies across their AI ecosystems, and that Wiz contributes the visibility, context and risk prioritisation needed to embed security into AI and cloud programmes from the outset, alongside Endava’s cloud engineering and managed services capabilities.

# Context-enrichment: Endava partners Wiz on cloud security for AI adoption
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS: fresh.** No prior corpus note covers an Endava–Wiz tie-up; the only prior Endava note is [[Endava and Paysafe partner on payments technology]] (Nov 2025), a different partner/topic. ## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Endava (LSE/NYSE: DAVA), a UK-based IT/software-engineering services firm, **joined the Wiz Partner Alliance** and will resell/implement Wiz's cloud-security platform. Announced 30 Jun 2026 (Businesswire; Endava IR press release 121). Wiz is now part of Google Cloud (Google closed its ~$32bn Wiz acquisition earlier). Endava already holds Google Cloud Premier Partner + Google Security Partner status, so this is a **channel/alliance enrollment layered onto an existing Google relationship**, not a new bilateral product.
- Concrete deliverables named: cloud risk assessments (find misconfig/vulns), managed detection & response (MDR), and advisory/implementation to embed Wiz into dev pipelines and "agentic AI" workflows.
- **All framing is announcement-stage.** Zero named launch customers, zero contract value, zero revenue commitment, no exclusivity. The "8 hours → 22 seconds" breach-to-next-stage stat is Google Cloud's own marketing number, not Endava data.
- **Why framed this way (red-team):** Endava is a *services* partner, not a security vendor. The value it adds is billable consulting hours around someone else's product. The PR foregrounds "agentic AI governance" because that is the highest-multiple narrative available — and (analysis) because Endava badly needs an AI story: this lands mid-crisis (see [2]).

## [1] Competitors / peers
The Wiz Partner Alliance is a **crowded channel**. Accenture is a flagship alliance member (dedicated Wiz Services Program) and in Mar 2026 separately expanded its Google Cloud security partnership — it operates at a scale (100k+ security staff) Endava cannot match. Other GSI/IT-services peers competing for the same Wiz+Google security implementation work: EPAM, Globant, HCLTech, Capgemini, Deloitte. Position: **Endava is a small, catching-up channel partner**, not a differentiated one. Being in the Alliance is table stakes, not an edge.
- **Why the landscape is this way / second order:** Wiz (now Google) wins by maximizing certified implementers; each partner is largely interchangeable. Margin in this stack accrues to Wiz/Google (the platform) and to the largest GSIs (deal access, scale). A sub-£1bn-revenue, currently-shrinking services firm has weak bargaining power here — it is a price-taker on a commodity implementation service.

## [2] Company history / fit
Endava is a legit fit *on paper*: FS/payments is a core vertical, and it has 250+ Google Cloud projects. BUT the timing is the real story. FY2026 has been **one of Endava's worst periods**:
- Q3 FY2026 revenue £178.5m, **-8.4% YoY**, missing consensus (£183.9m); FY2026 guidance cut to ~£721.8–725.8m (constant-currency -5% to -6% YoY).
- **£364.6m non-cash goodwill impairment** in Q3 → large pre-tax loss (~£372m).
- Stock fell ~21% on the print; **JPMorgan downgraded to Underweight, PT cut to $5 from $9**, and cut FY26/27 EPS estimates >40%, citing delayed deal conversions, extended cycles, client spend scrutiny.
- Management: "one of the more challenging periods Endava has faced in recent years."
- **The one bright spot: AI-driven revenue is now ~15% of total (Q3), up from ~5% a year ago.**
- **Why the company acts this way (analysis):** With core demand shrinking and margins under pressure, Endava is repositioning every relationship it has (Google, now Wiz) around the *only* growth line it can point to — AI. Joining the Wiz Alliance is cheap, PR-friendly, and lets it attach "agentic AI security" to its sell. Structural logic: a discretionary-spend IT-services model in a spending downturn needs a demand narrative that survives budget cuts; "secure AI adoption" is that narrative.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Novelty: low.** Neither the capability (Wiz on multicloud) nor the partner model is new; Accenture and others were already doing this. For Endava specifically, joining an alliance and adding a resell/implement motion is incremental, not transformative.
- **Traction: none disclosed.** No customers, no bookings, no revenue tied to Wiz. This is "announced," firmly not "live/adopted." Contrast with, e.g., HCLTech–Thought Machine which named Vault-certified delivery teams and CoEs.
- **Who captures the margin (why the value-add is thin):** Wiz/Google own the platform economics and the customer relationship's security layer; Endava captures only its services margin on implementation hours — a commoditized, cyclical, headcount-bound revenue that gets *its own* low multiple, not a software multiple. So even if the AI-security line grows, it does not fix Endava's core problem (shrinking, low-multiple services revenue). **The central question is not "does Endava have an AI story" — it clearly does (15% of revenue) — but "can any partnership offset an 8% top-line decline and a JPM Underweight," and one commodity channel enrollment plainly cannot.**

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- Watch for named client logos or a stated Wiz-linked bookings figure in coming quarters; without those, treat as PR.
- Sentiment on the name is **negative**: post-Q3 downgrade, PT $5, >40% estimate cuts, goodwill writedown signalling prior-acquisition overpayment. The AI-revenue mix shift (5%→15%) is the bull case; the demand/margin trajectory is the bear case.
- **Why / second-order:** As Google absorbs Wiz, Google can steer security-implementation demand to whichever partners it favors; Endava's leverage as one of many alliance members is limited, and the same Google relationship that gives it access also commoditizes it. Risk: the AI narrative masks that Endava remains a discretionary-spend services firm in a client-caution cycle. Regulatory/backdrop tailwind: enterprise pressure to govern agentic-AI identity/permissions is real and durable — the demand is genuine, but Endava is one of many undifferentiated ways to buy it.
