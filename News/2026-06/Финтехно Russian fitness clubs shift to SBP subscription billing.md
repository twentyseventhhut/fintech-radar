[Финтехно] РБК выпустил спецпроект про то, как фитнес-клубы — а за ними подтягивается и бьюти-индустрия — уходят от разовых абонементов к автосписаниям через СБП.

# Context-enrichment: Russian fitness clubs shift to SBP subscription billing
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
This is an **RBC special project (native/sponsored format)** describing a real, slow secular trend, not a single launch event. The de-PR'd facts (all from FitnessData via the primary sources):
- Recurring's share of **club-card sales doubled in two years to ~15%** by end-2025 (Moscow ~20% on 2024 data). So 85% of card-value is still annual/one-off — recurring is a minority, not the dominant model.
- **65.3% of full-format clubs opened in H1-2025 run a subscription model** (vs 48% a year earlier) — but the sources are explicit that this number is **dominated by one operator, DDX Fitness** (DDX alone supplied ~53% of all 2025 sector investment, >19bn RUB). Strip DDX out and the "65%" is not representative of the market.
- Fitness penetration in RF >5.1%; FitnessData's *hope* is recurring could lift this to 15–20% over **10–15 years** — a forecast, not traction.
- The commercial payload: **Paygine's "Method" (methodpay.ru) "subscriptions" module is now embedded in YCLIENTS** as a turnkey block, putting the same recurring tooling federal chains use into small studios' hands.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals:** the piece bundles a genuine payments-infra story (A2A recurring via SBP undercutting cards) inside a *fitness-market* feature, with a single vendor (Paygine/Method) as the soft-sell beneficiary. The headline metric (65%) is inflated by survivorship/concentration (DDX). The real, defensible fact is narrow: recurring is now a packaged module distributed through YCLIENTS, the dominant RU booking/CRM rail for beauty+fitness SMBs — distribution, not novelty, is the news.

## [1] Competitors / peers
The global pattern — **A2A/instant-rail recurring debits displacing card subscriptions to dodge interchange** — is well-trodden in the corpus:
- [[Smart Fit partners Belvo to enable direct bank debit payments]] (2025-12, LatAm) — the *closest analogue*: a gym chain moving subscriptions onto open-banking direct debit specifically to cut card decline/churn. Same use-case, different rail (PIX/OB vs SBP).
- [[Revolut launches PayTo in Australia]] (2026-03) and [[Visa completes first A2A variable recurring payment in UK]] (2025-11) — A2A VRP for recurring bills.
- [[PPRO adds Pix Automatico to its payments platform]] (2025-06) — Brazil's central-bank PIX Automático, the canonical "regulator-built A2A recurring rail replaces cards," directly parallel to SBP.
- [[Acquired.com partners with Visa to unlock transparent, trusted, and flexible payment experiences]] (2026-01), [[payabl launches SEPA Direct Debit for business payments]] (2025-07), [[Ecommpay upgrades subscriptions to cut failed recurring payments]] (2025-09), [[Revolut Business launches recurring billing tool]] (2026-01) — multi-rail subscription tooling.

**In RU specifically:** YuKassa, Robokassa, MixPlat, Prodamus, Yandex Pay all already offer SBP-recurring with ~0.7% fees (external search). Paygine/Method is **one of several** — its edge is the YCLIENTS-native integration, not the capability.

**Why the lay of the land is this way / second order:** the global driver is identical everywhere — card interchange/acquiring (1–3.5% in RU) vs central-bank A2A rails (SBP capped ~0.7%, 2.5–3x cheaper). RU is *behind* Brazil's PIX Automático (a true mandated recurring A2A standard) — SBP recurring is still vendor-tokenized rather than a uniform CBR-standardised VRP, which is why distribution (YCLIENTS) matters more than the rail itself here.

## [2] Company history / fit
**Paygine** = RU payment-orchestration/BaaS provider; "Method" is its merchant subscription-billing product. Fit is logical: an acquirer-adjacent vendor monetizes the card→SBP migration by selling the *billing logic and retry/dunning layer* on top of the cheaper rail, and reaches SMBs by piggybacking on **YCLIENTS** (the dominant RU appointment/CRM platform for fitness+beauty). **Why this way:** the rail (SBP) is a near-commodity priced by the regulator; a standalone PSP can't earn on interchange, so the margin must come from software (subscription management, churn/dunning, embedded checkout) and from owning distribution. Embedding in YCLIENTS is the cheapest path to thousands of small studios that would never integrate an API themselves.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Genuinely new:** almost nothing at the rail level — SBP-recurring and card-recurring both predate this. The only real delta is **packaging + distribution**: a turnkey subscription module inside YCLIENTS for studios too small to build it. **Traction:** thin and indirect — the cited 15% recurring share and 65% of new clubs are *market-wide FitnessData stats inflated by DDX*, **not** Paygine/Method adoption numbers. No Method GMV, merchant count, or churn-reduction figure is disclosed (the PR is silent here). **Who captures the margin:** the SBP rail is regulator-priced (~0.7%), so value accrues to whoever owns (a) distribution (YCLIENTS) and (b) the retention/dunning software — a commoditizing rail means the billing layer is where any durable take-rate lives. **What breaks it:** if CBR standardises SBP recurring as a uniform VRP (PIX-Automático style), the vendor's tokenization moat erodes and YCLIENTS could internalise billing itself, disintermediating Paygine.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Sentiment on the *fitness market* is cautious: FitnessData projects 2026 growth slowing to 10–14% (from ~20%), margins down to 7–9% (Silina) / 14% net (Borovikov), continued consolidation and "washing-out" of non-chain operators. **On payments:** recurring should keep gaining share — the structural pull is churn-reduction + predictable revenue for clubs and 2.5–3x lower fees vs cards. SBP volumes nationally are up ~2x YoY (4.1bn tx / 22.6tn RUB in Q1-2025; ~2.4m accepting merchants). **Counterintuitive second-order:** the same consolidation that drives recurring adoption (big chains like DDX standardising on it) makes the *vendor* market fragile — large chains tend to build/own billing in-house, so the long-tail-via-YCLIENTS SMB channel is Paygine's real (and contestable) prize, not the chains. Regulatory risk cuts both ways: CBR pushing A2A helps volume but a mandated VRP standard would commoditize the very layer Paygine sells.

**Freshness verdict: FRESH.** No prior corpus note covers the Russian fitness/SBP recurring story or Paygine/Method; closest is [[Smart Fit partners Belvo to enable direct bank debit payments]] (different region/rail/company) — a peer, not a duplicate.

**Sector & drivers.** Two stacked sectors meet here: the RU **fitness market** (the demand) and **SBP/A2A recurring acquiring** (the rail Paygine monetizes). Fitness: ~316.5bn RUB in 2025, +20% YoY — but decelerating sharply (growth fell to 8% by Dec-2025; FitnessData base case for 2026 is +10–14%), with 14.6k objects, 5.10% penetration, 7.45m clients (per FitnessData via cre.ru, as of end-2025). Payments backdrop: RU cards-and-payments market ~$0.78tn in 2025, growing ~13.6% CAGR (per Mordor Intelligence / MarketDataForecast, 2025), with SBP as the structural growth vector — 2.4m accepting merchants and 4.1bn tx / 22.6tn RUB in Q1-2025 (~2x YoY, per CBR). **Structure:** the *rail* is consolidated and regulator-priced (CBR/NSPK own SBP; fee capped 0.4–0.7% vs 1–3.5% card acquiring → 2.5–3x cheaper, per CBR), so no PSP earns on the rail itself; the *billing-software + distribution* layer above it is fragmented (YuKassa, Robokassa, MixPlat, Prodamus, CloudPayments, Yandex Pay, Paygine/Method all offer SBP-recurring). Value migrates to whoever owns distribution and the dunning/retry logic — a near-textbook commoditized-rail / software-margin split. **Why now:** SBP now natively supports account-binding "subscription" (recurring) via static-QR + bank-app consent (per nspk.ru/CBR), and YCLIENTS — the dominant RU fitness/beauty booking-CRM — is the cheap distribution wedge into the long tail of studios that would never self-integrate an API.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: for the rail/billing vendor — merchant count, processed GMV/TPV, take-rate on the software layer, churn/decline-recovery uplift; for fitness clubs — recurring share of card-card value (~15%, doubled since 2023), member churn, payback (clubs w/ pools >5yr, "dry" 3.5–5yr per Kommersant). **Key players & basis of competition:** SBP-recurring is a commodity at ~0.7% across YuKassa/Robokassa/Yandex Pay/MixPlat/CloudPayments; competition is therefore on *distribution and integration*, not price or the rail. Paygine/Method's only differentiator is the **YCLIENTS-native turnkey module** — same tooling federal chains (DDX, Sprit.Fitness) use, now packaged for SMB studios. **Recent moves:** DDX Fitness — the recurring standard-bearer — supplied ~53% of all 2025 sector investment (>19bn RUB, per cre.ru), and 65.3% of H1-2025 new full-format clubs ran subscription (vs 48% a year earlier, per Kommersant) — but both metrics are DDX-concentrated, not market-wide. **Protagonist position:** *niche / catching-up* — one of 6+ vendors with no disclosed Method GMV or merchant count; moat = distribution/switching-cost via YCLIENTS embedding `(analysis)`, not technology or scale. **Risk flags.**
1. **Rail commoditization → no durable take-rate.** SBP is regulator-priced at ~0.7% with ≥6 vendors offering identical recurring; any margin lives in software/distribution, which is contestable — second-order: YCLIENTS can internalize billing and disintermediate Paygine entirely.
2. **Regulatory single point of control (CBR/NSPK).** A future CBR-standardized SBP VRP (PIX-Automático style) would lift volume but erase the vendor-tokenization moat Method sells — the same regulator that creates the opportunity can collapse the margin.
3. **Demand concentration + market deceleration.** The headline adoption (65% / 15%) is DDX-inflated and the underlying fitness market is slowing to 10–14% with margins compressing to 7–9% (Silina, per Kommersant); large chains build billing in-house, so Paygine's real prize is the fragile long-tail SMB channel, not the chains that actually drive the recurring trend.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a RU instance of the global card→A2A-recurring re-rating, not a new mechanism `(analysis)`: the durable value is migrating from the (commoditized) rail to the *billing/dunning + distribution* layer, and the contest is platform-level (YCLIENTS) not vendor-level. Falsifiable thesis: Paygine/Method captures the SMB long tail *only if* it stays the default YCLIENTS billing block before CBR mandates a uniform SBP VRP or YCLIENTS internalizes billing. Trigger to watch: a CBR/NSPK standardized recurring-VRP announcement, or YCLIENTS launching native subscription billing — either would break the thesis.
