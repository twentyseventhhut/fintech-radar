# Context-enrichment: Bank of Russia rejects Sber/Alfa/T-Bank payment-system project
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 3/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
Per RBC (relayed by Meduza, 2026-06-22), the Bank of Russia (CBR/ЦБ) told the three banks at a May 2026 meeting that it would **not support** their project for a private payment system that could complement or replace the National Payment Card System (NSPK/НСПК). Crucially, the regulator rejected **both versions** the banks floated over ~a year:
- **v1 — a card-based circuit** (an "alternative contour"), which evolved from an earlier **unified/universal QR + "payment roaming"** idea so different QR solutions could interoperate. The banks went as far as **legally incorporating an operator** (a subsidiary — Interfax, 2024–25, reports Sber/Alfa/T-Bank, holding >80% of acquiring, signed a memorandum/consortium for a unified QR).
- **v2 — a full payment system without a card focus** — QR + biometrics + Bluetooth + other "alternative" form-factors.

CBR's counter-offer: instead of a parallel system, let the big banks **take an equity stake in NSPK or in its individual services**. Reported rationale (RBC): the **card version wasn't technologically stronger** than the existing market; the **QR/multi-form-factor version overlapped** with rails CBR is already pushing — the **universal QR**, the **SBP (СБП / Faster Payment System)**, the **digital ruble**, and biometrics.
- **Why structured this way / what it reveals:** this is a **monopoly-defense move dressed as a technology critique**. The "not technologically stronger" line lets CBR reject without admitting the real issue: NSPK is **state-controlled critical infrastructure** (sanctions-era backbone after Visa/Mastercard exit), and a bank-owned parallel rail would fragment control and acquiring economics the regulator wants centralized and fee-capped. The equity-stake offer is the tell — CBR will give the banks **upside inside the tent**, not a competing tent. (analysis)

## [1] Competitors / peers
On-topic = Russian domestic payment rails, all CBR/NSPK-owned or CBR-mandated:
- **NSPK / Mir** — national card scheme + switch, built 2014–15 after the first sanctions threat; now the mandatory domestic switch.
- **SBP (СБП)** — instant A2A/QR system on NSPK rails, fee-capped, dominant for QR retail payments. [[Финтехно Russian fitness clubs shift to SBP subscription billing]] shows live SBP recurring-billing traction.
- **Universal/single QR** — NSPK's "one QR for everything" (SBP + bank pay-services + digital ruble), legislated 2025; draft law fixing the single NSPK QR went to CBR in Jan 2025; banks must offer it free (no operator fee). This is **exactly the lane the banks' QR-roaming idea tried to occupy** — and lost.
- **Digital ruble** — CBR CBDC; systemically-important banks must process it from **2026-09-01**, with payroll tariffs already set from Jan 2026 ([[Финтехно Russian central bank sets digital ruble payroll tariffs]]).
- **+ Why the lay of the land is this way / second-order:** the banks (Sber/Alfa/T-Bank = ~80%+ of acquiring) wanted **standards-based interoperability ("roaming")** so multiple operators could coexist; CBR wants a **single fee-capped NSPK operator**. The structural conflict: every CBR rail (SBP zero/low fee, universal QR no operator fee, digital ruble) **erodes interchange and balances** that fund the big banks' acquiring and current-account economics. The banks weren't building tech — they were defending a P&L line. (analysis)

## [2] Company history / fit
The three banks have ~a year of escalation: **summer 2023** Gref (Sberbank) first floats an "alternative/additional payment instrument"; Nabiullina says an *additional* instrument is fine but not an *alternative* one (this June rejection is the literal cash-out of that 2023 caveat). **Summer 2024** Sber/Alfa/T-Bank sign a memorandum/consortium for a **unified payment QR** (MultiQR tech, used by ~18 banks / ~88% of issuance), then **incorporate an operator subsidiary**. 2025–26 the project broadens to a full multi-form-factor system. **May 2026** CBR's firm "no"; **2026-06-22** RBC reports it.
- **+ Why they act this way:** these are the incumbents most exposed to disintermediation by CBR rails — biggest acquiring books, biggest balances. Owning the QR/payment-system layer would let them **set standards and keep economics** rather than route everything through a zero-fee state operator. It fits their pattern of probing the regulatory perimeter (cf. Sber's crypto-backed-lending and DFA push: [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]]). (analysis)

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Low genuine novelty, near-zero traction.** Nothing launched: the "operator" was incorporated but the system was never live; this is **rejected-at-the-proposal stage**. The QR-roaming idea is essentially a private mirror of NSPK's universal QR — which is **already legislated and live-tracked** — so the value-add CBR was asked to credit didn't exist ("not technologically stronger"). 
- **+ Who captures the margin:** the whole fight is about **where acquiring/QR margin and float sit** — with the banks (private operator, standards) or with NSPK (single fee-capped operator). CBR's rejection routes the margin to the **state rail**; the equity-stake offer hands the banks **dividends, not control**. The real story is **traction CBR is building on its own rails** (SBP recurring billing live; digital-ruble mandate dated Sep 2026), not anything the banks shipped. (analysis)

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Expect the banks to take the **NSPK equity/service stake** CBR offered (the face-saving compromise), or to keep co-developing the **universal QR** under NSPK standards while lobbying on economics. CBR's posture is consistent across its June 2026 actions: it is **measuring and steering** the whole payments market (new payment-satisfaction metric: [[Russian central bank measures payment-service satisfaction metric]]; hybrid cash/digital usage mapping: [[Финтехно Russian central bank maps hybrid payment-instrument usage]]) and pushing the digital ruble.
- **+ Why the market goes this way / second-order:** under sanctions, **payments are national-security infrastructure**, so CBR will not tolerate a parallel rail it doesn't control, regardless of the banks' weight. Counterintuitive effect: the banks' very dominance (~80% acquiring) is what **dooms** the project — a bank-controlled alternative looks like private capture of critical infra, exactly what the regulator must prevent. The durable risk for the big banks is **margin compression** as CBR's free/low-fee rails (SBP, universal QR, digital ruble) keep advancing; this rejection accelerates that. (analysis/hypothesis)

## Challenge / red-team questions

1. **Did CBR "reject" a launched system or a proposal?** → A proposal. The operator was incorporated but nothing went live — this is a no at the design stage, not a shutdown of a working rail.
2. **Was it really one project or two distinct asks (card scheme vs QR/multi-form-factor)?** → Two iterations of the same effort; CBR rejected both the card version and the QR/biometrics/Bluetooth version. Important because the banks pivoted to dodge the regulator and still lost.
3. **What is CBR's stated vs real rationale?** → Stated: card version "not technologically stronger," QR version overlaps with universal QR/SBP/digital ruble. Real (analysis): defend NSPK as state-controlled critical infrastructure and protect fee-capped centralization. Open whether CBR ever published the reasoning itself or it's only RBC sourcing.
4. **Is this consistent with prior CBR statements?** → Yes — Nabiullina in summer 2023 told Gref an *additional* instrument was fine, an *alternative* one was not. This is the literal enforcement of that line.
5. **What share of acquiring do the three banks hold, and why does that matter?** → ~80%+ (Interfax). Their dominance is precisely why a private rail reads as capture of critical infra — the dominance dooms the project rather than empowering it.
6. **What economics were the banks actually defending?** → Acquiring fees, QR routing, and current-account float that CBR's zero/low-fee rails (SBP, universal QR free operator, digital ruble) erode. The "payment system" was a P&L-defense, not a tech play. (analysis)
7. **Is the equity-stake counter-offer a real concession or a token?** → Likely token control: dividends/upside inside NSPK, not governance over their own rail. Open — terms unpublished.
8. **What is the universal QR's status, and does it pre-empt the banks' idea?** → Legislated 2025 (draft law fixing single NSPK QR to CBR Jan 2025), free operator, covers SBP + pay-services + digital ruble. It occupies exactly the lane the banks' "QR roaming" targeted.
9. **Digital-ruble timeline — does it pressure the banks?** → Systemically-important banks must process digital ruble from 2026-09-01; payroll tariffs live Jan 2026. Yes — it directly compresses payroll-balance economics, a key bank motive.
10. **Could the banks relaunch under a different frame?** → Plausibly only as standards work *inside* NSPK; a parallel operator is foreclosed under sanctions-era security logic. (hypothesis)
11. **Is there a sanctions/security dimension the PR framing hides?** → Yes — NSPK is the post-Visa/Mastercard backbone; CBR cannot allow a rail it doesn't fully control. This reframes the story from "tech rivalry" to "infrastructure sovereignty."
12. **Any numbers on the project's size/investment?** → Not disclosed; only the operator incorporation and >80% acquiring share are concrete. Most figures are about the rejection's context, not the project itself. Open.
13. **Who benefits from the rejection?** → NSPK (margin and standard-setting centralize on the state rail); smaller banks (equal access to free universal QR vs a big-bank-controlled standard). The losers are the three incumbents' fee lines.
14. **What's the second-order effect for the big banks?** → Accelerated margin compression as free CBR rails advance; their lobbying shifts from "build our own" to "shape NSPK economics from within." (analysis/hypothesis)
15. **Is sourcing single-thread?** → Largely RBC → relayed by Meduza/Finтехно; CBR has not (per available sources) issued its own statement on the rejection. Treat the rationale as reported, not regulator-confirmed.

Importance: 3/5 — A genuinely consequential regulatory line (no parallel to NSPK; ~80% acquiring incumbents told no), and a clean illustration of CBR's sovereignty-over-payments doctrine under sanctions. But it caps at 3 because nothing launched (proposal rejected at design stage), zero traction/novelty, single-thread RBC sourcing without a CBR statement, and the substantive rails (universal QR, SBP, digital ruble) were already known/legislated. It is a meaningful "what didn't happen and why," not a market-moving event.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Russian domestic card-and-payments market valued at ~$0.69tn (2024) → est. ~$0.78tn (2025), with a quoted ~13.7% CAGR to 2033 (per Market Data Forecast — third-party report, treat as directional, not regulator-confirmed). Mir holds ~80% of the card market after the 2022 Visa/Mastercard exit; >86bn Mir transactions and ~$1.2tn cumulative volume over a decade (per Right Line/secondary, as of 2025). SBP turnover exceeded RUB 22tn in Q1 2025 alone (secondary, as of 2025). Structure: **highly consolidated and state-centralised** — NSPK is the single mandatory domestic switch and CBDC/QR operator; the only "private" margin pool (e-payment aggregators ex-NSPK/SBP) was just RUB 70.1bn in 2025 (TAdviser), i.e. a rounding error vs the rails themselves. Entry barriers = regulatory licence + the fact that the rails are critical sanctions-era infrastructure CBR will not let fragment. Drivers / why-now: (i) digital-ruble go-live **2026-09-01** for systemically-important banks (CBR), with one analyst (Freedom Finance, via DL News) projecting it slows card-market growth 7–9%/yr — a direct structural threat to the very banks behind this project; (ii) the universal QR (legislated 2025, free NSPK operator) already occupies the lane the banks tried to claim. The rejection is the enforcement event of a multi-year doctrine: an *additional* instrument is tolerated, an *alternative* rail is not.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: acquiring take rate / interchange, QR routing fees, A2A (SBP) volume, current-account float. Basis of competition is **regulatory, not product** — every domestic rail is CBR-owned or CBR-mandated, so "competition" is really the banks lobbying for economics inside NSPK. Key players: NSPK/Mir (mandatory switch), SBP (fee-capped A2A/QR), universal QR (free operator), digital ruble (CBDC, no interchange). Protagonist = **Sber + Alfa + T-Bank, jointly >80% of acquiring** (Interfax), who incorporated an operator subsidiary ("Fintech Platform LLC", registered Jan 2026 per Interfax). Recent moves: 2024 unified-QR memorandum/consortium (Interfax); Jan 2026 subsidiary registration; May 2026 CBR's firm "no" at a bankers' meeting (RBC, via Meduza); 2026-06-22 RBC report. Position: these are the incumbents **most exposed to disintermediation**, not challengers with an edge — CBR's "not technologically stronger" line is the tell. Moat (analysis): their only durable moat is scale/distribution inside the state rails; the project was a P&L defence against CBR eroding interchange and float, not a new moat. Their ~80% dominance is precisely what dooms a private rail (reads as capture of critical infra).

**Comps & multiples.** No deal/valuation/round attached to this news — it is a rejected proposal at design stage, so trading multiples are **not applicable** (no data on project investment, no transaction value; only the operator-subsidiary incorporation and >80% acquiring share are concrete). Internal comps (Russian payments-sovereignty / sanctions-rail precedents from the base): [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]] (Sber probing the regulatory perimeter; DFA issuance hit RUB 408bn in 2025, +5.6x YoY — shows where Sber's "alternative" energy is now channelled, inside CBR-sanctioned DFA rather than a parallel card rail); [[Russia's A7 cross-border payments firm plans global expansion]] and [[Sanctioned Russian crypto network expands across Africa]] (state-aligned alternative cross-border rails — the *permitted* form of payment innovation). External multiples: not computed — listed peers are state-controlled rails (NSPK is unlisted, CBR-owned) with no public EV/Revenue; Sberbank is listed but this news carries no segment financials to anchor a multiple. Distribution not computed; qualitative comparison only.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Margin compression on the big banks (structural, accelerating).** CBR's free/low-fee rails — SBP, universal QR (free operator), digital ruble (2026-09-01, projected −7–9%/yr on card growth) — erode the interchange, QR-routing fees and float that fund Sber/Alfa/T-Bank acquiring. The rejection forecloses the banks' main defence, so this risk now compounds with no offset. Second-order: lobbying shifts from "build our own rail" to bargaining over NSPK economics from a weaker seat.
2. **Regulatory disintermediation / no recourse.** Even ~80%-of-acquiring incumbents cannot build a parallel rail under sanctions-era infrastructure-sovereignty logic. CBR's equity-stake counter-offer hands dividends, not governance — control over the rail stays with the state regardless of bank weight.
3. **Single-thread sourcing.** The entire rejection and its rationale trace to RBC (relayed by Meduza/Финтехно); no published CBR statement on the rejection in available sources. Treat the "not technologically stronger" rationale as reported, not regulator-confirmed.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a **consolidation-toward-the-state-rail** event, not a re-rating: it confirms NSPK as the single locus of domestic payments margin and standard-setting, and routes the big banks' upside into NSPK equity/dividends rather than an owned rail (analysis). Falsifiable thesis: the banks abandon the parallel operator and pivot to (a) taking the NSPK stake and (b) channelling "alternative payment" ambition into CBR-sanctioned lanes (DFA, cross-border crypto rails). Trigger to watch: terms of any NSPK equity stake, and digital-ruble adoption post 2026-09-01 — heavy CBDC uptake would confirm the float/interchange compression that motivated the rejected project. What breaks the thesis: a relaunch framed as standards work *inside* NSPK, or CBR publishing its own rationale (would upgrade sourcing).
