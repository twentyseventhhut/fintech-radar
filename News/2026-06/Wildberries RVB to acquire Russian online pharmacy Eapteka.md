Сейчас РВБ укрепляет позиции как раз в тех сегментах, где профильная экспертиза и офлайн-составляющая по-прежнему важны. По этой причине маркетплейсом была приобретена косметическая сеть «Рив Гош», добавляет эксперт.

 Весной 2026 года он занялся регистрацией товарных знаков «Ozon Аптека» и «Ozon Ветаптека». В компании поясняли, что рассматривают возможность запуска доставки безрецептурных лекарств на дом, но не в ПВЗ.
Аптечные активы сейчас интересны маркетплейсам в первую очередь потому, что это частотная и устойчивая категория спроса, отмечает гендиректор Atomic Capital Александр Зайцев. «Лекарства, БАДы, косметику и товары для здоровья покупают регулярно и не только в периоды высокого потребления»,— поясняет он. Для онлайн-площадки это способ зайти в более маржинальные сегменты, говорит господин Зайцев.

# Context-enrichment: Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
RVB LLC (ООО РВБ) — the holding entity of the merged Wildberries & Russ group — has *agreed to* buy the online-pharmacy service Eapteka. This is **not a closed/announced deal**: it was first reported by the Telegram channel "Свободная касса" and only *confirmed by anonymous investment-market sources* to Kommersant. Both Eapteka and Wildberries declined to comment; there is **no official confirmation, no signed terms, no price, no structure disclosed**. Market sources put Eapteka's value at "≥8 bn RUB" — a single unverified estimate, not a transaction price.
- Eapteka 2024 (SPARK): revenue 32.9 bn RUB (+12%), **net loss 4.1 bn RUB** (loss narrowed 8%). 2025 financials unpublished. End-2025 (DSM Group): 15.5k pickup points, ~32 bn RUB turnover, **1.42% online-pharma market share — only 4th place**, behind Apteka.ru, Yuteka and Zdravcity.
- **Why this framing matters:** the "≥8 bn RUB" valuation against a chronically loss-making (~12% net loss margin) #4 player is the tell. Sber paid 5.7 bn RUB for ~45% in 2020–21, then **exited entirely by autumn 2025**, handing its stake to structures of Alexey Repik (ex-owner of R-Pharm). A controlling strategic walking away after 4–5 years is the loudest fact in the story (analysis): Eapteka is a distressed, sub-scale asset being recycled, not a prize.

## [1] Competitors / peers
**E-pharma rivals (ahead of Eapteka):** Apteka.ru (Katren) #1, Yuteka #2, Zdravcity (R-Pharm-adjacent) #3. **Marketplace rivals entering pharma:** Ozon registered "Ozon Аптека"/"Ozon Ветаптека" trademarks (spring 2026), exploring OTC home delivery; Yandex runs apteki.yandex.ru. Russian online-pharma penetration is **only ~15.9%** of the 367 bn RUB e-pharma turnover (DSM, 2025) — low vs other categories, which is *why* it is framed as "headroom."
- **Why the lay of the land is this way (analysis):** pharma is heavily regulated (Rx delivery restrictions, licensing, the offline/PVZ requirement), which is exactly why pure marketplaces have been slow. Buying Eapteka buys a **license + 15.5k pickup points + compliance stack**, not technology. The deal makes Wildberries the largest marketplace in pharma *by acquisition*, leapfrogging Ozon/Yandex — but in the absolute e-pharma ranking it stays #4. Position: catching up via M&A, not leading.

## [2] Company history / fit
RVB is on an aggressive **vertical/embedded-finance roll-up**: it absorbed cosmetics chain Riv Gauche («Рив Гош»), launched a leasing subsidiary and a consumer-credit MCC, runs **WB Bank** (see [[WB Bank launches savings accounts and consumer loans]] — savings accounts + consumer loans up to ₽500k, launched June 2026), and in May 2026 signed a strategic partnership with state-owned **VTB**, which is taking an initial 5% of WB Bank with an option to grow.
- **Why the company acts this way (analysis):** marketplace take-rates are commoditized and margin-thin. RVB's logic = move into **high-frequency, high-margin, sticky categories** (cosmetics, pharma, health/БАД) that drive repeat visits — and monetize the resulting traffic + 80M+ customer base through **finance** (credit, savings, BNPL, leasing). Eapteka fits as a frequency/loyalty + data engine feeding WB Bank, *not* as a standalone profit center.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
**Not novel.** Wildberries already had Eapteka on its storefront via a 2025 *partnership* (a "demo" already ran). The same buyer rumor circulated in **summer 2025 and Wildberries denied it then** — this is a year-old, on-again relationship now reportedly maturing into ownership, not a fresh strategic move. Traction of Eapteka itself: declining relevance (#4, persistent losses, lost its Sber backer).
- **Why the value-add is thin (analysis):** the real value isn't Eapteka's P&L (loss-making) but (a) the pharma **license + PVZ footprint** as a regulatory shortcut and (b) feeding **WB Bank's** embedded-finance flywheel. **Who captures the margin?** Likely *finance*, not pharma retail — drug retail margins are slim and price-regulated. If true, Eapteka is an acquisition cost RVB pays to expand the lending/data graph. Risk: integrating a sub-scale, loss-making asset can dilute rather than accrete (analysis).

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Expect a quiet close (or a denial — note WB denied in 2025). RVB was also a bidder for pharmacy chain «Апрель» (owners shopping it for 4 years; a source says Apriori is *not* currently of interest). Analyst sentiment is mildly positive on the *logic* (Infoline's Burmistrov, Atomic Capital's Zaytsev: pharma is frequent/durable demand, a route into margin) but no one defends Eapteka's standalone economics.
- **Why the market goes this way / second-order (analysis):** the durable story is **embedded finance, not pharma**. The VTB tie-up (state bank into a private marketplace's bank, 80M customers) reframes the central question from "can WB win e-pharma?" to "**is RVB building a state-aligned super-app/financial conglomerate** using verticals like pharma as customer-frequency feedstock?" Counterintuitive downside: heavy state-bank entanglement (VTB) + roll-up of distressed assets = **balance-sheet and concentration risk**, not safety.

## Top challenge / red-team questions

1. **Is the deal even real?** It is sourced to a Telegram channel + anonymous "investment circles," both parties declined to comment, and WB *denied the same rumor in 2025*. Status: announced-by-leak, **not confirmed**. (open)
2. **What is the actual price and structure?** Only a third-party "≥8 bn RUB" *valuation* exists — no transaction price, no terms, no equity %, no consideration mix. (open)
3. **Why did Sber walk away?** Sber paid 5.7 bn RUB for ~45% in 2020–21 and **fully exited by autumn 2025** (stake to Repik's structures). A strategic seller exiting a flagship asset is a strong negative signal on Eapteka's standalone value. (answered — bearish)
4. **Can a loss-making asset accrete value?** Eapteka lost 4.1 bn RUB in 2024 (~12% net loss margin) at flat ~32 bn RUB turnover. Does RVB have a credible path to profitability, or is this distressed-asset recycling? (open)
5. **Is the "leapfrog" claim real?** WB becomes #1 *among marketplaces* in pharma, but stays **#4 overall** (1.42% share) behind Apteka.ru/Yuteka/Zdravcity. How material is marketplace-leadership when the category leaders are pharma-native? (answered — marginal)
6. **What is actually being bought — tech or a license?** The real asset looks like the **pharma license + 15.5k PVZ + compliance**, a regulatory shortcut, not technology or brand. (answered — likely license/footprint)
7. **Who captures the margin in the stack?** Drug retail is low-margin and price-regulated; the monetization is plausibly **WB Bank** (credit/data), not pharma. Is pharma a loss-leader for embedded finance? (open — hypothesis)
8. **Novelty?** Eapteka was already on WB's storefront via a 2025 partnership; the buy-rumor is a year old. This is relationship maturation, not a new strategic bet. (answered — not novel)
9. **How does the VTB tie-up change the thesis?** With VTB taking 5% of WB Bank + access to 80M customers, is RVB building a **state-aligned financial conglomerate** where pharma is just frequency feedstock? (open — reframes central question)
10. **Concentration / political risk?** Deep state-bank (VTB) entanglement + roll-up of distressed assets (Eapteka, Riv Gauche, leasing/MCC) — does scale create fragility rather than safety? (open)
11. **Regulatory overhang:** Rx-drug e-delivery in Russia remains restricted (Ozon limits to OTC, no PVZ). What share of Eapteka's GMV is exposed to delivery rules, and does WB ownership change anything? (open)
12. **What did the 2025 partnership actually produce?** Was Eapteka's WB-storefront integration a success (GMV, orders) or weak — which would explain buying vs. continuing to partner? (open)
13. **Why not «Апрель» instead?** WB bid on pharmacy chain Apriori; a source says it's "not interested now." Is Eapteka a fallback/second-choice asset? (open)
14. **Pro-forma 2025 numbers are missing** — Eapteka hasn't published 2025 financials. Decision-relevant data is unavailable. (open)
15. **Counterfactual:** if VTB/WB Bank embedded-finance is the real story, does Eapteka even matter to the weight of this item, or is it a footnote to a bigger thesis? (open — likely a footnote)

Importance: 2/5 — rationale: An **unconfirmed, leak-sourced, price-undisclosed** agreement to buy a **loss-making #4** e-pharmacy that the previous strategic owner (Sber) just exited. Not novel (year-old relationship, prior storefront partnership). The genuinely interesting thread — RVB's embedded-finance roll-up and the VTB/WB Bank alliance — lives in adjacent notes, not in this M&A item itself. Region-specific (Russia), modest scale (≤8 bn RUB ≈ <$100M), low fintech novelty → 2/5.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector & drivers.** Two overlapping sectors: Russian e-pharma and marketplace embedded finance. E-pharma turnover ~367 bn RUB in 2025 = 15.9% of total pharmacy market (DSM Group, Annual Report 2025) — low penetration vs general e-commerce, which is the "headroom" thesis. Growth is real: online drug sales +28% YoY in value, +15% in packs in 2025, well above offline (AlphaRM, via DSM/expopharmtech, 2025). Structure: still fragmented-but-concentrating at the top — Apteka.ru (Katren) alone ~33% consumer reach in 2025, with Yuteka and Zdravcity next; Eapteka is #4 at 1.42% share. Entry barriers are regulatory, not technological: pharma license, Rx-delivery restrictions, mandatory offline/PVZ footprint — which is exactly why pure marketplaces (Ozon, Yandex) have moved slowly and why buying a licensed operator is a shortcut. "Why now": marketplace take-rates are commoditized, so RVB and Ozon are both pushing into high-frequency, high-margin, regulated verticals (pharma, cosmetics) to drive repeat traffic that can be monetized through their captive banks.

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: for e-pharma — GMV/turnover, market share, online penetration; for the embedded-finance layer that actually drives the thesis — fintech revenue, deposits, loan book, customer base, fintech EBITDA share. Key players & basis of competition: e-pharma natives compete on assortment + price + PVZ density (Apteka.ru #1, Yuteka #2, Zdravcity #3, Eapteka #4); marketplaces compete on traffic + embedded finance + delivery. Recent moves with dates: VTB framework agreement to take an initial 5% of WB Bank "and other RVB fintech assets," with an option to grow (announced 2026-05-27; further talks slated after VTB's 2026-06-30 AGM — TASS/Moscow Times/Reuters). Ozon registered "Ozon Аптека"/"Ozon Ветаптека" trademarks (spring 2026) for OTC home delivery. RVB earlier bought cosmetics chain Riv Gauche and was a bidder for pharmacy chain «Апрель». Protagonist's position: catching up via M&A — the Eapteka buy would make WB the largest *marketplace* in pharma but it stays **#4 overall** (1.42%) behind pharma-native leaders. Moat (analysis): not in Eapteka itself (loss-making, sub-scale) but in RVB's distribution + 80M-customer base + WB Bank — a network-effects/scale moat on the finance side, with Eapteka supplying frequency and a regulatory license, not technology.

**Comps & multiples.** Internal comps: [[WB Bank launches savings accounts and consumer loans]] (WB Bank consumer loans up to ₽500k, savings accounts, June 2026 — the monetization endpoint); [[Ozon Bank registers digital financial assets platform with CBR]] (the closest live marketplace-bank peer, June 2026). External comp — Ozon's Fintech segment is the read-across: Q2 2025 fintech revenue **46.2 bn RUB = 17% of group revenue but 37.5% of group EBITDA** (adj. fintech EBITDA 14.7 bn RUB, +2.5x YoY); fintech customers **34.4M (+43%)**, loans issued 100.2 bn RUB (+73%), deposits **348.6 bn RUB** (~4x YoY) (Ozon IR, Q2 2025). This validates the embedded-finance flywheel as the value driver, *not* pharma retail. Eapteka deal multiple — **no data**: only a single anonymous "≥8 bn RUB" *valuation* exists (no price, no terms, no equity %, no consideration mix). Against 2024 SPARK figures (revenue 32.9 bn RUB, **net loss 4.1 bn RUB**, 2025 unpublished), an EV/Revenue on the rumored value would be ~8 / 32.9 = **0.25x** revenue — sub-1x, consistent with a distressed, loss-making asset (P/E/EV-EBITDA undefined: EBITDA/earnings negative). Reference point: Sber paid 5.7 bn RUB for ~45% in 2020–21 (implying ~12.7 bn RUB whole-company then) and **exited entirely by autumn 2025** — a lower implied value now plus a strategic seller walking away both read bearish on standalone economics.

**Risk flags.**
1. **Deal is unconfirmed / leak-sourced (execution + reputational).** Sourced to a Telegram channel + anonymous "investment circles"; both parties declined comment; WB *denied the same rumor in 2025*. No price, terms, or structure disclosed — decision-relevant data (2025 financials) is unpublished. Why it matters: re-rating any thesis on an unsigned deal is fragile; a denial/collapse is a live outcome.
2. **Distressed, sub-scale target (margin/accretion).** #4 player, 1.42% share, persistent ~12% net-loss margin on flat ~32 bn RUB turnover, and the prior strategic owner (Sber) just fully exited. Why it matters: integrating a loss-maker can dilute rather than accrete; the real asset is the license + 15.5k PVZ, not a profitable P&L.
3. **State-bank entanglement + roll-up concentration (balance-sheet/political).** VTB (state bank) taking 5%+ of WB Bank "and other RVB fintech assets," stacked on a roll-up of distressed/sub-scale assets (Eapteka, Riv Gauche, leasing/MCC). Why it matters: deep state-bank dependence + asset accumulation can create fragility and concentration risk, not safety — and pharma margins are price-regulated, so the lending/data layer (WB Bank) must carry the economics.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** This is a footnote to a bigger thesis, not a standalone e-pharma story (analysis): Eapteka is customer-frequency + regulatory-license feedstock for RVB's embedded-finance flywheel, mirroring the Ozon model where fintech is 17% of revenue but 37.5% of EBITDA. Falsifiable thesis: the durable value is **WB Bank monetizing pharma traffic via credit/data**, not pharma retail margin — wrong if WB Bank's loan book/deposits fail to scale on the back of these verticals (watch WB Bank disclosures) or if the deal is denied/never closes. Trigger/catalyst: a confirmed close + WB Bank growth metrics, or the post-2026-06-30 VTB talks defining how far the state bank's stake in RVB fintech extends.
