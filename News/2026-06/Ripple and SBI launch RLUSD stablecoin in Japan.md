Ripple and SBI launch RLUSD stablecoin in Japan
Paula Albu
25 Jun 2026 / 5 Min Read
The Japan Financial Services Agency (JFSA) has approved RLUSD as the country's first "Type 4" electronic payment instrument, a regulatory classification introduced to accommodate fiat-backed tokens that do not fit existing categories. The stablecoin is now available to both institutional and retail users through SBI VC Trade, the regulated exchange arm of Japan-based SBI Group. Although SBI VC Trade began distributing RLUSD on a limited basis in March 2026, full approved availability came into effect on 24 June 2026.
RLUSD is issued by Standard Custody & Trust Company, a New York-chartered subsidiary of Ripple, and is backed by US dollar deposits and short-term US Treasuries. An independent firm publishes monthly reserve verification reports, a structure aligned with JFSA's compliance expectations. Rick Maeda of Presto Research noted that Japan's market places a premium on compliance, suggesting that the reserve transparency underpinning RLUSD works in its favour within this regulatory environment.
Transaction limits and blockchain constraints
Despite the regulatory approval, the Japan launch carries notable constraints. RLUSD is available exclusively on Ethereum, and not on Ripple's own XRP Ledger, as the majority of RLUSD supply and liquidity already resides on Ethereum. Each transaction is also capped at one million yen, equivalent to approximately USD 6,200, a ceiling that sits uncomfortably alongside Ripple's stated positioning of RLUSD as infrastructure for payments, tokenisation, and collateral management.
The cap becomes more pronounced in context. On the same date, SBI VC Trade launched its own yen-denominated stablecoin, JPYSC, with no transaction cap and an explicit institutional focus. The parallel launch positions Ripple's dollar-denominated stablecoin within a narrower retail space, while SBI's homegrown offering occupies the institutional segment.
Adoption remains the central challenge
Regulatory clearance does not guarantee uptake. A survey of 518 Japanese investment professionals, published in April 2026 by Nomura and Laser Digital, found that 63% see practical applications for stablecoins, including treasury management and cross-border payments. Respondents expressed the greatest trust in stablecoins issued by established financial institutions, a category that RLUSD, issued by a crypto company, does not currently occupy.
The competitive landscape adds further pressure. Circle's USDC entered Japan in March 2025, while Tether's USDT has not received approval. Japan's three largest banks are also developing a shared yen stablecoin targeted for 2027. Against a total market capitalisation of approximately USD 1.7 billion, RLUSD faces considerably larger incumbents, with USDT alone standing at around USD 186 billion.
Potential use cases for RLUSD in Japan include settlement of tokenised securities and remittance flows into Southeast Asia through SBI's existing corridors, though both scenarios involve transaction sizes that exceed the current per-transaction cap.

## Контекст

<!-- enrichment:context -->
**[0] What happened (the fact).** On 24 June 2026, Japan's Financial Services Agency (JFSA) approved Ripple USD (RLUSD) as the country's **first "Type 4" electronic payment instrument** — a new regulatory class for foreign fiat-backed stablecoins under the revised Payment Services Act. RLUSD became fully available to institutional and retail users via **SBI VC Trade**, SBI Group's regulated exchange arm, after a limited distribution start in March 2026. RLUSD is issued by Standard Custody & Trust Company (a NY-chartered Ripple subsidiary), backed 1:1 by USD deposits and short-term US Treasuries with monthly third-party reserve attestations. Launch is **Ethereum-only** (not the XRP Ledger) and capped at ~¥1M (~USD 6,200) per transaction.
- Ripple official: https://ripple.com/ripple-press/ripple-and-sbi-group-partner-to-launch-ripple-usd-in-japan/
- crypto.news: https://crypto.news/ripple-and-sbi-launch-rlusd-in-japan-after-jfsa-approval/
- CryptoBriefing: https://cryptobriefing.com/ripple-rlusd-stablecoin-japan-launch/

**[1] Prior chapter — this was pre-announced.** The Japan launch was telegraphed nearly a year earlier. SBI VC Trade (a licensed Electronic Payment Instruments Exchange Service Provider) said in Aug 2025 it expected RLUSD to go live in Q1 2026.
- [[Ripple and SBI to distribute RLUSD stablecoin in Japan by 2026]] (2025-08-25)

**[2] RLUSD's regulatory track record elsewhere.** RLUSD has been collecting approvals and institutional rails globally, which feeds Japan's "compliance-first" narrative: UAE/ADGM regulatory green light, BNY custody of reserves, AMINA (first bank to support it), and an RLUSD off-ramp for BlackRock/VanEck tokenised funds via Securitize.
- [[Ripple's RLUSD stablecoin wins UAE regulatory approval]] (2025-11-28)
- [[Ripple selects BNY to custody RLUSD stablecoin reserves]] (2025-07-10)
- [[AMINA becomes first bank to support Ripple USD]] (2025-07-04)
- [[Ripple makes RLUSD off-ramp for BlackRock, VanEck funds]] (2025-09-26)

**[3] The Japan regulatory backdrop.** Japan amended a Cabinet Office Ordinance under the Payment Services Act so that foreign trust-type stablecoins handled by registered Electronic Payment Service Providers can be treated as "electronic payment instruments" (not securities). New rules took effect **1 June 2026**, opening the market to foreign issuers under strict reserve/disclosure/consumer-protection conditions. RLUSD is the first under the new "Type 4" sub-class.
- Cryptobriefing (foreign trust framework): https://cryptobriefing.com/japan-stablecoin-regulations-foreign-trust/
- The Coin Republic (June 1 opening): https://www.thecoinrepublic.com/2026/05/19/stablecoin-news-japan-opens-stablecoin-market-to-foreign-issuers-from-june-1/

**[4] Competitive / parallel context.** Same day, SBI VC Trade + Startale launched **JPYSC**, Japan's first trust-bank-backed yen stablecoin (issued by SBI Shinsei Trust Bank), with **no transaction cap** and an explicit institutional focus — splitting the market: RLUSD (USD, retail, capped) vs JPYSC (yen, institutional, uncapped). Circle's USDC was the first foreign dollar stablecoin approved in Japan (live via SBI VC Trade 26 Mar 2025); Tether USDT remains unapproved. Japan's three megabanks (MUFG, SMBC, Mizuho) launched a joint stablecoin consultative body on 10 June 2026, targeting interbank real transactions by ~31 Mar 2027 on the Progmat platform.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
**Red-team questions**

1. **"First Type 4" vs "first foreign stablecoin" — which is true?** USDC was approved in Japan in March 2025 (first foreign dollar stablecoin). RLUSD's claim is narrower: first under the *new* "Type 4" sub-class for foreign trust-type tokens. Is the press conflating the two? (The note already disambiguates this, correctly.)

2. **Is the ¥1M (~$6,200) per-transaction cap a regulatory limit or a self-imposed one?** Sources don't clarify whether the cap is a JFSA condition on RLUSD specifically, a general retail-EPI rule, or SBI's own risk choice. Why does JPYSC, launched the same day under the same Act, carry *no* cap?

3. **The cap directly contradicts the use cases.** Ripple positions RLUSD for tokenised-securities settlement and remittances into Southeast Asia — both routinely exceed $6,200/tx. Is the Japan listing effectively retail-only theatre while real institutional flow goes to JPYSC?

4. **Why Ethereum-only, not the XRP Ledger?** Stated reason: most RLUSD supply/liquidity is on Ethereum. But this undercuts Ripple's own XRPL payments pitch. Does it signal weak XRPL stablecoin demand?

5. **Adoption gap — trust in issuer type.** Nomura/Laser Digital (Apr 2026, n=518 pros) found 63% see use cases but greatest trust goes to *bank-issued* stablecoins. RLUSD (crypto-company-issued) does not fit that category. Does regulatory approval move the trust needle at all?

6. **Scale mismatch.** RLUSD total mcap ~$1.7B vs USDT ~$186B. In a market where USDT isn't even approved, does RLUSD's small float matter, or is the relevant comparison only the approved set (USDC, RLUSD, JPYSC)?

7. **Is SBI conflicted?** SBI distributes RLUSD *and* launched its own competing JPYSC *and* is a Circle/USDC partner. Whose volume does SBI actually steer? Is RLUSD a hedge or a genuine priority?

8. **Reserve attestation ≠ audit.** Monthly "reserve verification reports" by an independent firm — is this a full attestation or a limited agreed-upon-procedures report? JFSA "compliance expectations" are cited but not the exact standard.

9. **Standard Custody & Trust (NY-chartered) as issuer into Japan** — does the cross-border issuer structure create redemption/legal-recourse ambiguity for a Japanese retail holder? What happens in a de-peg or issuer distress?

10. **What is actual day-1 liquidity/volume on SBI VC Trade?** "Available" ≠ traded. No volume figures cited. Free deposits/withdrawals suggest SBI is subsidising to seed adoption — a tell that organic demand is unproven.

11. **Megabank stablecoin (FY2026/Mar-2027, Progmat) is the real institutional threat** — once MUFG/SMBC/Mizuho ship a bank-issued yen coin, does RLUSD's retail-USD niche shrink further?

12. **Timeline integrity:** limited distribution Mar 2026 → new Act in force 1 Jun 2026 → full approval/availability 24 Jun 2026. Was the March activity pre-approval grey-zone, or under a prior provision? Worth confirming the legal basis for the March start.

13. **Single source for the note.** The base note rests on one Paypers article (709 words). External corroboration (Ripple PR, crypto.news, CryptoBriefing, CryptoTimes) confirms the core facts — but verify the $6,200 cap and "Type 4" framing aren't echoing one upstream wire.

**Importance: 3/5** — Genuinely notable as the first foreign stablecoin admitted under Japan's new Type 4 foreign-trust framework, a milestone in one of the world's strictest regimes, and it extends Ripple's decade-long SBI alliance. But impact is structurally capped: Ethereum-only, ¥1M/tx retail ceiling, a tiny ~$1.7B float, and same-day overshadowing by SBI's own uncapped institutional JPYSC plus a looming megabank coin. It's a regulatory-milestone story more than a volume/market-moving event.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector context.** Japan's stablecoin market opened to foreign issuers via a revised Payment Services Act + Cabinet Office Ordinance effective **1 June 2026**, creating a "Type 4" electronic-payment-instrument class for foreign trust-type tokens distributed by registered Electronic Payment Service Providers. This is a deliberately compliance-heavy regime (full 1:1 reserves, monthly attestations, consumer protection, per-tx limits), positioning Japan as a "regulated-stablecoin" market rather than an open one. The June 2026 cluster of launches (RLUSD, JPYSC, plus the megabank consortium forming) marks the regime's first wave of live products.

**Comparables (approved/adjacent in Japan):**
- **Circle USDC** — first foreign dollar stablecoin approved in Japan (live via SBI VC Trade 26 Mar 2025); Circle Japan KK + SBI JV. Direct USD competitor to RLUSD and far larger float. (https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250324944828/en/Circle-Strengthens-Commitment-to-Japan-with-New-Investment-and-Expanded-USDC-Access)
- **SBI/Startale JPYSC** — launched same day (24 Jun 2026), Japan's first trust-bank-backed yen stablecoin (SBI Shinsei Trust Bank), **no transaction cap**, institutional focus. Splits the market vs RLUSD's capped retail USD slot. (https://www.cryptotimes.io/2026/06/24/sbi-and-startale-launch-japans-first-trust-backed-yen-stablecoin-jpysc/)
- **MUFG/SMBC/Mizuho megabank coin** — joint consultative body launched 10 Jun 2026 on the Progmat platform, targeting interbank live transactions by ~Mar 2027. The eventual bank-issued benchmark.
- **Tether USDT** — not approved in Japan; the largest global stablecoin (~$186B) is structurally absent from this market.
- RLUSD global footprint as credential: UAE/ADGM approval, BNY reserve custody, AMINA bank support, BlackRock/VanEck tokenised-fund off-ramp.

**Market sizing.** RLUSD total mcap ~$1.7B at launch — a fraction of USDC and ~1% of USDT (~$186B). Within Japan's *approved* set the comparison is tighter (USDC, RLUSD, JPYSC), but RLUSD enters as a small-float late mover behind USDC's ~15-month head start.

**Risk flags:**
1. **Transaction-cap risk** — ¥1M (~$6,200) per-tx ceiling structurally excludes the institutional settlement/remittance use cases Ripple markets; confines RLUSD to retail while uncapped JPYSC takes institutional flow.
2. **Distribution-conflict risk** — sole distributor SBI also issues competing JPYSC and partners with rival Circle/USDC; SBI's incentives may not favour RLUSD volume.
3. **Issuer-trust gap** — Nomura/Laser Digital survey (Apr 2026): users trust bank-issued coins most; RLUSD is crypto-company-issued. Megabank + JPYSC (trust-bank) coins exploit this.
4. **Liquidity/chain risk** — Ethereum-only listing (no XRPL); day-1 trading volume undisclosed; free deposits/withdrawals imply SBI subsidy to seed unproven organic demand.
5. **Competitive-encroachment risk** — USDC incumbency above, JPYSC and forthcoming megabank yen coin alongside/below; RLUSD's addressable niche (regulated retail USD) is narrow and contestable.
6. **Regulatory/structural risk** — cross-border issuer (NY-chartered Standard Custody & Trust) raises redemption/recourse questions for Japanese retail holders in a de-peg or issuer-distress scenario; "monthly reserve verification" standard not specified as full audit.
7. **Headline-vs-substance risk** — "first Type 4" is a real but narrow milestone; easily overstated as "first foreign stablecoin" (USDC predates it). Single base source (Paypers) externally corroborated but watch for upstream-wire echo on the cap/Type-4 framing.
