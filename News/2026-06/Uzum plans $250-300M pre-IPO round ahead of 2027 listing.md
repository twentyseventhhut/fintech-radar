Uzum plans USD 250-300 mln pre-IPO round ahead of potential 2027 listing
Paula Albu
25 Jun 2026 / 5 Min Read
Uzbekistan-based super-app Uzum is planning a USD 250-300 million funding round ahead of a potential IPO targeted for 2027.
Uzbekistan-based Uzum, a digital ecosystem combining e-commerce, logistics, digital banking, and lending, is planning to raise between USD 250 million and USD 300 million in a pre-IPO funding round, with a public listing targeted as early as 2027. The company is reportedly considering listing venues including Hong Kong, London, Abu Dhabi, and Nasdaq. The timing for the pre-IPO round is targeted for the second half of 2026 or early 2027.
Founded in 2022, Uzum reached a valuation of USD 2.3 billion in March 2026, a 53% increase from USD 1.5 billion in August 2025 . Its March 2026 funding round raised USD 131.5 million , attracting participation from Oman's sovereign wealth funds, Tencent, VR Capital, and FinSight Ventures. Total funding raised to date exceeds USD 250 million, meaning the planned pre-IPO round would approximately double that lifetime total.
Financial performance and market penetration
Uzum's 2025 financials reflect substantial scale for a company founded three years prior. The platform processed USD 11 billion in payment volume, generated approximately USD 691 million in revenue, and reported net income of USD 176 million, with fintech operations driving the majority of profitability. The company reports 20 million monthly active users - equivalent to more than half of Uzbekistan's total population of approximately 36 million.
In 2025, Uzum issued 4.1 million debit cards, representing approximately half of all debit cards issued in Uzbekistan during the year. The concentration of card issuance within a single platform reflects the degree to which Uzum has become the primary vehicle for consumer-facing digital finance in the country.
Regional context and IPO positioning
The planned pre-IPO raise serves a dual purpose: funding continued expansion and establishing a valuation benchmark ahead of the public offering. A successful raise at USD 3 billion or above would create a pricing reference point for the eventual listing.
Uzum's trajectory reflects broader digitalisation across Central Asia, where informal trade networks and cash-heavy economies are transitioning toward regulated digital platforms. Uzbekistan has been actively promoting economic modernisation, and Uzum has positioned itself as the dominant consumer digital platform in the country.
The primary risk identified is single-country concentration. Uzum's commercial and financial performance is closely tied to Uzbekistan's economic stability, regulatory environment, and government policy towards foreign-backed technology companies.

## Челлендж / ред-тим

<!-- enrichment:challenge -->
**Red-team questions (verify before treating as fact):**

1. "Pre-IPO" intent is reported, not confirmed by a closed term sheet. Is this a sourced leak or an on-record company statement? The Paypers attributes it to "reportedly" — provenance is second-hand.
2. Listing venue list (HK + London + Abu Dhabi + Nasdaq) is suspiciously broad. A company genuinely close to filing typically narrows to 1–2 venues. Breadth suggests early-stage optionality / signalling, not imminence.
3. The note claims "total funding raised to date exceeds USD 250 million." But the March 2026 round alone was USD 131.5M and the 2024/2025 rounds add USD 114M + USD 70M + others — lifetime likely well above USD 250M. Is the note's lifetime figure understated/stale?
4. The March 2026 round included USD 50M convertible "tied to Uzum's next funding round." Is this pre-IPO round that "next round"? If so, part of the 250–300M may be conversion, not fresh cash — inflating the headline.
5. USD 2.3B valuation is a private mark set by insiders (Tencent, VR Capital, Oman SWFs) who are repeat participants. How much is independent price discovery vs. round-tripping among aligned holders?
6. USD 11B "payment volume" vs >USD 500M e-commerce GMV — a 20x gap. Payment volume can be inflated by low-margin throughput (P2P, bill pay). Which metric actually monetizes? Revenue/payment-volume take rate is only ~6%.
7. Net income USD 176M on USD 691M revenue = ~25% net margin for a 3-year-old EM fintech. Is this IFRS-audited, or management figures? Loan-loss provisioning on a USD 400M unsecured book in a young credit market is the obvious soft spot.
8. Single-country concentration: ~100% of revenue is Uzbekistan. Soum FX risk, sovereign/regulatory risk, and policy toward foreign-backed tech are existential, not peripheral. How would a London/Nasdaq book-build price that?
9. ~20M MAU on ~36M population (and ~4.6M annual transacting users per intellinews) — a large gap between "users" and "paying users." Is the 20M figure registrations or genuine actives?
10. Tencent is an anchor investor; does Chinese ownership complicate a Nasdaq/London listing (CFIUS-style scrutiny, audit/PCAOB issues)? This may explain the HK/Abu Dhabi inclusion.
11. Unsecured loan book grew to USD 400M and total disbursed USD 1.2B — rapid credit expansion in a thin-file market is a classic pre-IPO vintage risk. Has the book been through a full default cycle?
12. The "USD 3B+ pre-IPO sets a pricing floor" framing is the company's narrative. Down-rounds happen; what if EM/frontier IPO windows stay shut into 2027?
13. Is the 2027 IPO timeline credible given the company keeps pushing the same "as early as 2027" line since at least Sep 2025? Repeated identical guidance can indicate slippage, not progress.
14. Comparable: Kaspi (Kazakhstan) listed on Nasdaq then faced short-seller scrutiny. Does Uzum face the same EM-fintech disclosure skepticism?
15. Are debit-card "issuance" share (~half of national issuance) figures reflecting active cards or cumulative plastic? Issuance share != usage share.

**Importance: 4/5 — rationale.** A flagship Central Asian fintech super-app moving toward a USD 3B+ pre-IPO and a 2027 cross-border listing is a genuinely significant frontier-market event with strong fundamentals (profitable, scaled, dominant). Not 5/5 because it is incremental within a long, already-covered arc (intent, not a closed round), single-country, and the IPO remains conditional and repeatedly-guided rather than filed.

## Market Research

<!-- enrichment:market_research -->
**Sector context.** Uzum sits at the center of Central Asia's fastest-growing digital-finance market. Uzbekistan's payment infrastructure has scaled sharply: card issuance rose from ~34M (2023) to ~70M; the Fast Payment System processed >USD 10.2B in a single month (Jul 2025); and the Central Bank pushed a universal QR standard (rollout targeted early 2026). The regional digital-payments economy is projected to surpass ~USD 250B by 2026. The thesis driving Uzum's valuation: the next leg of growth shifts from raw payment rails to integrated ecosystems bundling commerce + banking + lending, with consumer credit cited as the key monetization frontier as the population formalizes financially.

**Comparables / peers.**
- **Kaspi.kz (Kazakhstan)** — the regional blueprint and the single most relevant comp. Central Asia's first fintech unicorn and super-app; Nasdaq-listed (2024). Validates both the model and the cross-border-listing path Uzum is pursuing — but also a cautionary tale (post-listing short-seller scrutiny over related-party and disclosure questions). A natural valuation anchor for Uzum's IPO book-build.
- **Sea Group (Shopee/SeaMoney), MercadoLibre** — EM e-commerce + fintech flywheel comps used to justify super-app multiples, though at far larger scale and multi-country.
- **Nubank** — profitable EM neobank reference for the "credit as the profit engine" narrative.
- **Domestic:** Uzum competes against bank-led wallets (e.g., established Uzbek banks digitalizing) and other local e-com/payment players, but currently holds dominant share (~half of national debit-card issuance; ~20M MAU).

**Risk flags (K=7):**
1. **Single-country concentration (highest).** Effectively 100% Uzbekistan revenue. Sovereign, regulatory, FX (soum), and policy-toward-foreign-tech risk is undiversified and existential for an international listing.
2. **Credit/asset-quality risk.** ~USD 400M unsecured loan book, USD 1.2B disbursed, in a thin-file market that hasn't been through a full default cycle. Provisioning adequacy is the key unknown behind the USD 176M net income.
3. **Valuation/round-quality risk.** USD 2.3B mark set by repeat insiders (Tencent, VR Capital, Oman SWFs); USD 50M of the March round was convertible "tied to the next round" — potential overlap with the pre-IPO headline. Limited independent price discovery; down-round risk if windows shut.
4. **Listing-venue ambiguity / Tencent overhang.** Four candidate venues (HK/London/Abu Dhabi/Nasdaq) signals early-stage, not imminent. Chinese (Tencent) anchor ownership may complicate a US/UK listing (audit/PCAOB, geopolitical scrutiny) — possibly why HK/Abu Dhabi remain in play.
5. **Metric quality / disclosure.** "Users" (~20M) vs annual transacting users (~4.6M); USD 11B payment volume vs >USD 500M e-com GMV. Heavy reliance on management (likely non-PCAOB-audited) figures invites EM-fintech skepticism (cf. Kaspi).
6. **Execution/timeline slippage.** "IPO as early as 2027" has been the standing line since ~Sep 2025; repeated identical guidance can indicate delay rather than momentum. Pre-IPO round itself is intent, not closed.
7. **Macro/window risk.** Frontier-market IPO appetite is cyclical; a closed 2027 EM-listing window or soum depreciation could force a delay or down-round regardless of fundamentals.

**Net read.** Fundamentally one of the strongest EM-fintech stories in the region — profitable, scaled, dominant share — but the bull case is heavily contingent on (a) credit-book seasoning, (b) credible independent IPO pricing, and (c) navigating the Tencent/venue/disclosure friction that the Kaspi precedent makes salient.
