 «Неофарм» закрыл сделку по покупке астраханской сети «На здоровье» 
Фармритейлер «Неофарм», работающий под брендом «Столички», завершил процесс приобретения астраханской сети аптек «На здоровье». Изменения отражены в СПАРК-Интерфакс, указывает Vademecum . Ранее московский оператор, по данным реестра Росздравнадзора, не был представлен в субъекте. Астраханская область – родной регион для сети «На здоровье», однако несколько ее аптек также представлены в Севастополе.
О процессе приобретения аптечной сети Vademecum сообщал в мае 2026 года. Тогда же директор по развитию отраслевой аналитической компании RNC Pharma Николай Беспалов оценил сумму сделки в 280–350 млн рублей. Эксперт считает, что причина выхода «Столичек» на рынок Астраханской области «заключается в наличии в регионе интересного для компании предложения по привлекательной цене».
Руководитель аптечной сети «Столички» Маргарита Массалютина подчеркнула в разговоре с Vademecum, что присоединение аптек «На здоровье» – логичный шаг в укреплении позиций компании в Южном федеральном округе. «Теперь из почти 2 тысяч аптек «Столички» – 69 будут работать для жителей Астрахани и Астраханской области», – отметила Массалютина. Она также добавила, что смену вывесок планируется проводить постепенно.
ООО «На здоровье», по данным СПАРК-Интерфакс, зарегистрировано в 2021 году в Астрахани, учредителями выступали Светлана и Илья Батаевы, а также Мария Смутная. В 2025 году из капитала аптечной сети вышла Светлана Батаева, а ее доля перешла Илье Батаеву.
«Неофарм» же стал единственным учредителем одноименного юрлица – ООО «На здоровье», – зарегистрированного в 2025 году Ольгой Федоровой. По данным реестра Росздравнадзора, последней компании принадлежит 79 лицензий на осуществление аптечной деятельности. Адреса точек совпадают с местоположением учреждений компании Батаева. У юрлица осталось шесть лицензий.
Сеть «На здоровье» по итогам 2025 года заняла 112-е место в традиционном рейтинге Vademecum «ТОП200 аптечных сетей России». За указанный период выручка игрока составила 1,2 млрд рублей. На начало 2026 года ритейлер управлял 89 точками.
«Неофарм» в том же рейтинге занял пятое место с выручкой 114,3 млрд рублей. Показатель сети вырос на 25% год к году. На конец 2025 года компания управляла 1,9 тысячи аптечных точек. ООО «Нео-Фарм» было зарегистрировано в 2003 году в Москве. В 2022 году 99,99% актива перешли во владение комбинированному закрытому паевому инвестиционному фонду «ЗдравИнвест», управляющей компанией которого выступает ООО «Современные фонды недвижимости». Еще 0,01% ООО «Нео-Фарм» принадлежит Лидии Нифантьевой. ООО «Сеть социальных аптек «Столички» было зарегистрировано в мае 2016 года. По данным СПАРК-Интерфакс, в 2025 году юрлицо заработало порядка 29 млрд рублей, чистая прибыль компании оценивается в 756,4 млн рублей. «Столички» на 100% принадлежат «Неофарм».
В мае 2025 года «Неофарм» стал бенефициаром ООО «Солнечное здоровье» и ООО «Аптечная сеть «Будьте здоровы», расположенных в Нижнем Новгороде. До этого владельцем компаний выступала Светлана Иорданова. По информации источника Vademecum, в периметр сделки вошли 40 аптек.

# Context-enrichment: Neofarm (Stolichki) closes acquisition of Astrakhan pharmacy chain «Na Zdorovye»
_Analytical notes (not a post). Importance: 2/5._

**FRESHNESS/DUPLICATE VERDICT: FRESH.** This is the *closing* of the deal (reported by Vademecum on 2026-06-26, confirmed completed by ABN on 2026-06-30), which is a genuinely distinct event from the 2026-05-19 Vademecum report that framed it as *intent/talks* ("Stolichki plans to enter Astrakhan via acquisition of ~62 Na Zdorovye points"). ~6-week gap between "talks" and "closed" — a real talks→signed follow-up, not a reprint. No prior internal note covers this specific deal (Na Zdorovye / Astrakhan / Neofarm). The internal RU-pharmacy notes ([[Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka]], [[RWB buys majority stake in Eapteka pharmacy service]]) are a *different* deal (online Eapteka), so no duplication. Note only 1 mention / 1 source in the corpus — a low-salience item.

## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
De-PR'd: Neofarm — Russia's #5 pharmacy retailer (RUB 114.3bn 2025 revenue, ~1.9k outlets), operating its discount banner «Stolichki» — **closed** the purchase of «Na Zdorovye», an Astrakhan-based chain (RUB 1.2bn 2025 revenue, ~89 outlets, #112 in Vademecum TOP200). Mechanically the deal was done via a *newly registered* (2025) OOO «Na Zdorovye» (registrant Olga Fedorova) into which **79 Roszdravnadzor licenses** were transferred; the sellers' old Bataev-structure entity retained **only 6** licenses — i.e. near-total asset absorption. Stolichki head Margarita Massalyutina: "69 of Stolichki's ~2,000 pharmacies will now serve Astrakhan"; rebranding to be done *gradually*.
- **Price is UNVERIFIED.** No official figure. The only number in circulation — **RUB 280–350m** — is an *estimate by RNC Pharma's Nikolay Bespalov*, not a transacted or SPARK-confirmed price. Every multiple below is therefore indicative, not real. Both parties declined to disclose (ABN). **Why this matters:** the "attractive price" narrative is Bespalov's framing being laundered into "fact"; treat the range as a plausible floor, not a mid-point.
- **Store-count fog.** 3 figures float around: May 19 spoke of ~62 points *being acquired*; closing transferred *79 licenses*; the network is described at *89 outlets*; Massalyutina cites *69* Astrakhan points. Licenses ≠ storefronts, and 6 were retained by the seller — so the actual operating footprint taken over is fuzzy (~69–79), not a clean 89.
- **Why structured this way:** buying through a fresh SPV + license transfer (rather than an equity purchase of the old OOO) lets Neofarm cherry-pick the operating assets/licenses and leave residual liabilities/points with the seller — standard for a distressed/opportunistic regional roll-up.

## [1] Competitors / peers
RU pharmacy retail is consolidating fast. DSM Group 9M2025: retail market RUB 1,663bn, +12% YoY (growth mostly price/inflation, not volume); top-20 share **74.3%** (up from 67.4% a year earlier). Leaders (context, mixing DSM 9M2025 + Forbes FY2025): **Aprel** ~RUB 259bn / ~9,976 outlets; **Rigla** (Protek, vertically integrated with wholesale) ~RUB 237bn / ~6,766; **Planeta Zdorovya** ~RUB 144bn; then 36.6, Implozia, and **Neofarm #5–6** (~RUB 114bn Vademecum FY / RUB 81bn DSM 9M — different periods, not a contradiction). Online is a *separate* axis: **Apteka.ru (Katren)** #1 aggregator, **Zdravcity (Sber-linked)**, and **Eapteka** — recently 100% acquired by Wildberries/Russ (RVB) ~2026-07-01 for an estimated RUB 7–12bn (see [[Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka]], [[RWB buys majority stake in Eapteka pharmacy service]]).
- **Who is consolidating whom:** Rigla (Protek) is the most aggressive *offline* roll-up (FAS-approved multi-region deals, «Aliya» 174 pharmacies, «Novaya Apteka» 55). Neofarm runs *smaller* regional tuck-ins. **Why the landscape looks this way:** per-pharmacy barriers are low (licensing), but real scale barriers sit in procurement leverage + logistics → favours roll-ups; leaders buy cheap sub-scale regional players to enter new geographies. **Second-order:** Aprel — the #1 chain — is itself reportedly seeking buyers (valued ~RUB 58–65bn), an exit signal at the very top of the market.

## [2] Company history / fit
Neofarm (OOO «Neo-Farm», est. 2003, Moscow) runs a dual-brand strategy: premium «Neofarm» + discount «Stolichki» (OOO «Set sotsialnykh aptek Stolichki», est. May 2016, ~RUB 29bn 2025 revenue, RUB 756.4m net profit). Since July 2022 ~99.99% is held via ZPIF «ZdravInvest» (managing co. «Sovremennye Fondy Nedvizhimosti»); 0.01% by Lidia Nifanteva — a fund-wrapped ownership after ex-owner/State Duma deputy Evgeny Nifantev sold out. This is a **serial regional roll-up**: May 2025 it took Nizhny Novgorod's «Solnechnoye Zdorovye» + «Budte Zdorovy» (~40 pharmacies; rebranded into Stolichki by Oct 2025, 67 institutions folded in); now Astrakhan. **Why:** a fund-owned commodity retailer under a concentration land-grab grows fastest by buying cheap sub-scale chains and rebranding them into the discount banner — expanding density/procurement scale in new federal districts (here the Southern FD, a first-time entry for the buyer).

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
Low novelty. This is a **~1%-of-revenue tactical bolt-on** (RUB 1.2bn target vs ~RUB 114bn parent) to plant a flag in the Southern Federal District — not a needle-mover. The value-add is real but modest: 69 operating points + entry into a region where Neofarm had *zero* prior presence (per Roszdravnadzor). Traction is confirmed by the *closing* (licenses transferred), not just an announcement — a genuine execution point. But rebranding is "gradual" and integration synergies (procurement, logistics) are unproven. **Who captures margin:** at Stolichki's ~2.6% net margin, absorbing a sub-scale chain risks near-term dilution until procurement scale + rebranding land; the value is in *footprint/density*, not immediate earnings.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
Sentiment: consolidation continues; watch whether Neofarm files *further* small acquisitions in the Southern FD `(analysis)`. Regulatory backdrop: FAS approvals routine for these small deals; no antitrust friction at this scale. Risks: (1) price unverified — multiples indicative only; (2) target's Sevastopol/Crimea points carry sanctions/title-risk overhang and it's unclear how many transferred cleanly (open); (3) margin dilution during integration. **Why the market goes this way:** concentration is climbing toward ~75%, so cheap regional tuck-ins at ~0.2–0.3x sales are the leaders' default expansion mode. **Counterintuitive second-order:** the *strategically* material 2026 RU pharma-retail story is the Wildberries/RVB–Eapteka online deal, not this — this deal matters mainly as another data point on the offline roll-up trend.

## Indicative multiples (all rest on the UNVERIFIED RUB 280–350m estimate)
- Price/Sales = RUB 280–350m / RUB 1,200m = **~0.23x–0.29x** (analysis) — cheap, consistent with "attractive price" framing.
- Price-per-outlet = RUB 280–350m / 89 = **~RUB 3.1–3.9m** per point (analysis).
- Internal comp: Eapteka valued ~RUB 7–12bn on RUB 32.9bn 2024 revenue → P/S ~0.2–0.4x — in-line, though Eapteka is a loss-making online model.
- **Falsifiable trigger:** if SPARK/disclosure later show a paid multiple materially above ~0.5x sales, the "opportunistic/attractive price" thesis breaks and the deal looks defensive.

## Market Research

**Sector & drivers.** Russian pharmacy retail is a large, fast-consolidating market. 2025 retail drug sales were ~RUB 1.89tn, +16% YoY (per DSM Group, via web reporting on the 2025 results). Structure is concentrating hard: by end-2025 the top-50 chains held ~75% of the market and the top-2 players' combined share exceeded 20% for the first time (per DSM Group / Forbes.ru); the top-10 chains alone did ~RUB 1.245tn = 56.9% of the market (per DSM Group). Note prior-run figure "RUB 2,313bn / +14%" is not corroborated by fresh DSM 2025 data and is dropped as `[UNSOURCED]`. Entry barriers are low per-pharmacy (Roszdravnadzor licensing) but real at scale (procurement leverage, logistics, private-label), which is precisely what favours roll-ups. "Why now": leaders buy small regional chains cheaply to enter new geographies — RNC Pharma's Bespalov explicitly framed this deal as Neofarm/Stolichki entering Astrakhan because an "attractively priced" target existed in a region where the buyer had zero prior presence (per Vademecum, via mergers.ru).

**Competitive landscape.** Sector KPIs: revenue per outlet, number of points, regional density. Top players by 2025 revenue (per DSM Group, figures vary by source): April #1 (leader 2nd year running, +34%), Rigla #2 (Protek-owned, vertically integrated with wholesale, +40%, ~6,766 outlets), Planeta Zdorovya #3 (~RUB 144bn, +19%). Neofarm ranked #5 with group revenue RUB 114.3bn (+25%) and ~1.9k outlets at end-2025 per the deal's own primary source (mergers.ru/Vademecum); some 2025 web rankings place it #6 and quote divergent revenue — treat the exact rank as `(disputed across sources)`. Its Stolichki social-pharmacy unit earned ~RUB 29bn in 2025 with RUB 756.4m net profit (per SPARK, via mergers.ru). Differentiator worth flagging: Neofarm's ~RUB 60m revenue-per-outlet is reportedly ~2x Rigla and ~3x April (per Forbes.ru) — a Moscow/CFD high-purchasing-power concentration, i.e. it competes on outlet productivity and price segmentation (premium Neopharm vs discount Stolichki), not footprint size. Recent buyer moves: May 2025 Neofarm became beneficiary of Solnechnoye Zdorovye and "Budte Zdorovy" in Nizhny Novgorod (~40 pharmacies, since rebranded to Stolichki, ~67 outlets); now Na Zdorovye (89 points, RUB 1.2bn 2025 rev, #112 in Vademecum TOP200) adds 69 Astrakhan outlets. Position: established top-5/6 incumbent running a serial regional roll-up; moat = outlet productivity + dual-brand price coverage + procurement scale `(analysis)`. 79 licences transferred vs only 6 left at the seller entity → near-full asset absorption (per Roszdravnadzor registry, via mergers.ru).

**Comps & multiples.** Deal price RUB 280-350m for a chain with RUB 1.2bn 2025 revenue — this is an RNC Pharma *estimate*, not a disclosed/confirmed price. Price/Sales `(analysis)` = RUB 280-350m / RUB 1,200m = **0.23x-0.29x** — cheap on revenue, consistent with a small sub-scale regional target and the buyer's "attractive price" framing. Price-per-pharmacy `(analysis)` = RUB 280-350m / 89 points = **RUB 3.1m-3.9m per outlet**. EV/EBITDA = no data (target margins undisclosed). Internal comp: [[Wildberries RVB to acquire Russian online pharmacy Eapteka]] / [[RWB buys majority stake in Eapteka pharmacy service]] — Eapteka valued at "≥RUB 8bn" (source estimate) and RNC Pharma's Bespalov later put its business at ~RUB 7-12bn ex-debt, on RUB 32.9bn 2024 revenue → P/S ~0.24x-0.37x `(analysis)`, broadly in-line despite Eapteka being a loss-making (~RUB 4.1bn net loss) online #4 model. Both point to ~0.2-0.4x revenue for Russian pharmacy assets currently — but note the comparability is weak: Na Zdorovye is a profitable-scale-unknown offline regional chain, Eapteka a chronically loss-making national e-pharmacy; the shared cheap multiple partly reflects *distress/sub-scale* rather than a clean sector benchmark. Distribution not computed (2 comps, both indicative). Flag: in-line/cheap vs a thin, low-quality comp set.

**Risk flags.**
1. Price unverified — only RNC Pharma's RUB 280-350m estimate exists; no confirmed deal value, so every multiple here is indicative `(analysis)`, not transacted. Second-order: the "attractive price" thesis is untestable until SPARK/disclosure surfaces the real figure.
2. Target geographic concentration — Na Zdorovye is Astrakhan-centric (home region) plus a few Sevastopol points; Sevastopol exposure carries sanctions/political overhang for a buyer that had no prior presence there, and same-region density gains are capped in a thin regional market.
3. Margin/integration execution — Stolichki's 2025 net margin is ~2.6% (756.4m/29bn); rolling up sub-scale chains at low revenue-per-point can dilute margins before rebranding/procurement synergies land, and rebranding is being done "gradually" (per Massalyutina), delaying synergy capture.

**What this changes (idea-lens).** Reinforces the consolidation thesis: top-5/6 players use cheap regional tuck-ins (~0.2-0.4x sales) to expand footprint while concentration climbs to ~75% and the top-2 cross 20% share `(analysis)`.
