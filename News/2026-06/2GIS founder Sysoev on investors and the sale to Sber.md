## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)
- **Not a new deal.** This is Alexander Sysoev (founder of 2GIS, Russia's #2 maps/local-search service) reflecting in Forbes on (a) why he took outside money and (b) why he sold to Sber and left. The headline metaphor — "захлопнул себе мышеловку" ("I snapped the mousetrap shut on myself") — is self-criticism of the **2020 deal structure**, not an announcement.
- **Two events conflated in coverage:** (1) **Aug 2020** — Sber's "Цифровые активы" buys **71.94%** control, Sber/Mail.ru JV O2O Holding 3%, three founders keep **25%**; total **valuation 14.3 bln RUB (~$208M)**. (2) **July 28 / Aug 1 2025** — Sber buys out the residual **25%** AND, as a *condition*, the three founders (Sysoev, Vera Garmash, Boris Berkhin) terminate their contracts and leave. **(analysis)** The real story is that a minority-retention stake converted into a **forced founder exit** — and that exit clause did not exist in 2020.
- **Numbers are soft / undisclosed.** The 2025 price was not disclosed. Secondary reports: "several billion RUB but under 5 bln" vs an expert estimate ~5.5 bln RUB. A 25% stake at a claimed ~$1bn valuation would imply 9–14 bln RUB — the figures don't reconcile, so treat price/valuation as **reported-but-unconfirmed**.
- **Stated reason:** "different values" with the strategic partner (Sber); compromise worked early, became untenable. **(analysis)** Polite gloss for: a 25% minority with no governance leverage cannot stay if the 71.94% owner wants you gone. Proceeds reportedly went ~half into an Altai hotel project, rest into AI/IT startups.
- **Why framed this way:** founder rehabilitates his legacy ("first Forbes interview in ~20 years") by owning the structural mistake rather than disputing the outcome — costless candor now that he's fully cashed out.

## [1] Competitors / peers
- **Yandex Maps + Yandex Navigator** are the dominant rivals. At the 2020 deal: Yandex Maps ~67M users, 2GIS ~48.6M. Yandex leads Moscow/European Russia; **2GIS dominates Siberia, Far East, Central Asia.** 2GIS's moat is its detailed **business directory** (org listings, floors, entrances, hours) + offline maps — local-search / Yellow-Pages economics, not Yandex's ride/ads flywheel.
- **GPS/GLONASS-jamming resilience** is now the live competitive axis. 2GIS shipped step-by-step offline navigation ("Шаг за шагом") on **2026-04-09**, but Yandex shipped a comparable "По шагам" in **Dec 2025** — 2GIS was the **follower**. 2GIS is on Russia's "white lists" of services that keep working during mobile-internet shutdowns. **(analysis)** Sber's strategic value in 2GIS is precisely as the **anti-Yandex map asset** — owning local-intent/mobility data outside Yandex's orbit.
- Google Maps is a distant factor in Russia; it's effectively a two-horse race (Yandex vs 2GIS).

## [2] Company history / fit
- **1999** launched (Novosibirsk, Sysoev family); **2004** legal entity DoubleGIS/ДубльГИС. Bootstrapped ~15 years ("develop without outside money to the last moment").
- **Oct 2015** — first equity raise: **$40M from Baring Vostok + ru-Net (Boguslavsky)**, valuation "≥$150M." Earlier approaches (2010/2013) declined.
- **June→Aug 2020** — Sber buys 71.94% control at **14.3 bln RUB (~$208M)**; Baring Vostok and RTP Global (ru-Net) **exit** here. Forbes had valued 2GIS ~$320M in Feb 2020, so Sber bought *below* that mark.
- **July/Aug 2025** — Sber consolidates 100%; founders exit. **2026-06-25** — Forbes retrospective.
- **Fit:** the trajectory is a classic Russian-tech ending — independent product → strategic-bank control → full absorption into a super-app ecosystem; founder optionality compresses at each step.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction
- **MAU:** ~50M (2020) → **86.9M (July 2025)**; ~4.5M daily search queries (2025). Real growth, but verify methodology (people vs devices vs installs).
- **Genuinely new in the June 2026 interview:** (1) the forced-exit clause was a 2025 condition, absent in 2020; (2) explicit "different values" conflict with Sber; (3) the "mousetrap" self-criticism of the 2020 minority structure; (4) reinvestment into an Altai hotel + AI startups. These are *disclosures/colour*, not market-moving facts → low news weight.
- **Where value is captured (analysis):** with founders and VC investors gone, 2GIS's value-add now accrues entirely to **Sber's ecosystem** — local data feeding SberAuto, logistics (SberLogistics), delivery (Samokat), ad targeting. The standalone "honest справочник" brand identity is now subordinate to ecosystem KPIs.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment
- **Sber owns 100%** and will fold 2GIS deeper: maps/local data into mobility, delivery, ad targeting, and likely **GigaChat/Salute** AI integration into search/routing (mirroring Yandex putting Alice into Maps, Dec 2025).
- **(analysis)** Counterintuitive second-order effect: full consolidation removes the friction that kept 2GIS a distinct consumer brand. Watch whether it survives as a standalone brand or gets co-branded/absorbed under Sber. FAS/regulatory angle on 100% consolidation is a tail risk.
- **Sentiment:** founder-departure stories rarely move a market; weight here is *narrative* (a cautionary tale on minority-stake structuring under a dominant strategic), not financial. Adjacent Sber corpus items show Sber's broader ecosystem/regulatory posture — see [[Russia's central bank rejects Sber, Alfa, T-Bank payment system project]] (regulatory pushback on Sber-led infrastructure) and [[Russia's Sberbank plans crypto-backed loans for corporate clients]] (ecosystem expansion drive).

**Key figures (with growth, Sber FY2025 IFRS, filing 2026-02-26).**
