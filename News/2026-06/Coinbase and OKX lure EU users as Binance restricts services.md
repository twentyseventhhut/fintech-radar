## [0] What exactly happened (de-PR'd)

Two things are bundled under one headline, and only one is a genuine new event:

- **The trigger (confirmed follow-up).** With MiCA's transitional/grandfathering period ending **1 July 2026**, Binance — which failed to secure a MiCA CASP licence (it **withdrew** its Greek application on ~24.06.2026 after the reported rejection risk in [[Binance faces loss of EU access over Greece MiCA licence]], and now says it will re-apply in **France** — filing not yet confirmed) — emailed EU users (~25.06.2026, named markets: France, Italy, Poland, Spain) that from 1 July it stops new sign-ups, spot orders, deposits and Earn/staking. **Withdrawals stay open; existing assets remain accessible.** So "temporarily restricts *some* services" is precise: this is a service freeze for new activity, **not a full ban or a fund lock-up**.
- **The actual new event: the competitor land-grab.** Two already-licensed exchanges launched dated, capped switch-in incentives aimed at Binance's displaced EU base:
  - **Coinbase** — **5% bonus paid in BTC** on up to **€1,000,000** of crypto **transferred in** (not fiat deposits/purchases/conversions), for **new** users, gated behind a paid **Coinbase One** subscription; markets Germany/France/Italy/Belgium/Poland/Sweden (+UK); deadline ~13–14 July 2026.
  - **OKX** — **8% deposit bonus** (up to **€20,000 in USDC**) on net deposits for EEA residents who opt in, window **29 June–31 July 2026**, min €10; plus welcome bonus up to €400 and 30-day VIP.

**Why structured this way / what it reveals.** These are classic **acquisition-cost promos disguised as generosity**, engineered to look bigger than they are: Coinbase's 5% is on *transferred-in crypto* only, and requires a paid Coinbase One sub — i.e. it filters for high-balance, sticky, fee-paying users, not tyre-kickers. OKX's 8% is USDC-denominated (steering into a MiCA-compliant stablecoin) and capped at €20k. The "€1,000,000 / 450M users" framing in press coverage inflates the stakes: CoinDesk's "Binance's 450M EU users" figure is the **EU population**, not Binance's userbase — Binance's real EU user count is **unconfirmed**, and its EUR pairs are ~**1% of its global spot volume**. So the addressable prize is far smaller than the headline implies. The core de-PR point: this is a **regulatory-arbitrage marketing war over a modest EUR order-flow pool**, not a fight over 450M users.

The prompt-flagged items (**Bybit EEA authorisation = Austria FMA 28.05.2025; FalconX/Sygnum = institutional MiCA grants late June/early July 2026**) are **distinct events**, not this story — this note is specifically the Coinbase/OKX **retail incentive** land-grab.

**Why the lay of the land / 2nd-order dynamics.** The gap here is **not product, it's supervisory cleanliness** — the same premium-to-the-clean-player dynamic identified in [[Binance faces loss of EU access over Greece MiCA licence]]. But note two counter-moves that muddy the "everyone wins from Binance's exit" narrative: (a) **Gemini holds licences yet quit EEA retail (Apr 2026)** — a licence is necessary, not sufficient; EU retail economics are marginal for some. (b) The regional order book already isn't Binance's: single-source CryptoQuant data (indicative, not audited) puts **EUR spot share at Bitvavo ~44% / Kraken ~20% / Binance ~8%** — Europe is the one region where Binance never dominated, so the flow up for grabs is smaller and more contested than the headline suggests. Coinbase and OKX are fighting over a pool where **local incumbents (Bitvavo, Kraken) already lead**.

## [2] Company history / fit

- **Coinbase** — a US-listed, compliance-first exchange whose whole EU thesis is "regulated venue for the post-MiCA era." Luxembourg CSSF licence (June 2025) → 27-state passport → a €1M-cap switch-in bonus is a logical, on-brand customer-acquisition play for exactly this moment. Structural driver: as a public company under fee compression, Coinbase needs **EU user growth + subscription (Coinbase One) attach** to justify its multiple — hence a promo that *forces* a paid sub.
- **OKX** — was among the **first** fully MiCA-authorised (Malta, Jan 2025), positioning early precisely to be the "compliant destination" when rivals stumble; the 8%/USDC bonus operationalises that first-mover bet. Caveat: a reported **€1M Malta AML penalty** (Finance Magnates, uncorroborated here) sits awkwardly against the "clean" framing.
- **Binance (the departing party)** — this fits its multi-year EU friction arc (Netherlands €3.3M fine + 2023 exit; German/Austrian application withdrawals; the $4.3bn DOJ resolution, 2023) documented in the sibling note. Post-settlement Binance defaults to **fast, hard geo-restriction** the moment a regulator pushes.

## [3] Novelty / value-add / traction

- **What's genuinely new vs the 06-18 note:** that note was the *threat/report* ("set to be rejected," Binance disputing). This is the **realised outcome** — Binance's own user-facing restriction emails + the first **concrete competitor incentive campaigns** with dated terms and caps. That is a real "talks → action" progression, so **fresh**, not a re-run.
- **Traction is thin, by design.** These are launched *offers*, not proven inflows — **no switch-in volume, converted-user, or GMV numbers exist yet** (open). The mechanics reveal low conviction on breadth: caps (€1M / €20k), a paid-sub gate (Coinbase), and short windows (to mid/end July) target a **narrow, high-value cohort**, not mass migration.
- **Where the margin is captured / what breaks the case.** The durable prize isn't the promo BTC/USDC — it's **retained fee-paying order flow + subscription revenue** after the bonus lapses. The risk: bonus-chasers arbitrage the incentive and leave, and the actual displaced flow is small (Binance ≈8% EUR share, ≈1% of its global spot) and already contested by **Bitvavo/Kraken**, who didn't even need a promo. So value-add is **real but modest** — a tactical share grab in a mid-sized regional pool, not a franchise-defining shift.

## [4] What's next / market sentiment

- **Scenarios:** (a) Binance re-files in France and returns within months → the incentive window was a short arbitrage blip; (b) Binance's EU absence persists → Bitvavo/Kraken/Coinbase/OKX split the flow, with **local incumbents likely capturing most** and the US names paying up for the visible slice. Sentiment: negative for Binance's EU narrative, mildly positive for licensed rivals — but the market reads the promos as **tactical, not transformative**.
- **Regulatory backdrop.** This is MiCA's single-passport dedline biting in practice: **280 authorised CASPs** on ESMA's 03.07.2026 register (+37 in a week; BaFin/Germany 58, France 31, Netherlands 26 lead). The deadline is doing what it was meant to — forcing licensure or exit.
- **Counterintuitive 2nd-order effect.** Aggressive fiat-value bonuses to poach a departing rival's users can **train a mercenary, low-loyalty cohort** and compress everyone's EU unit economics — the same reason Gemini *exited* EEA retail. So the promo war may prove Binance's ~8% EUR flow was **marginally profitable to fight over**, and the real long-run winners are the **already-dominant local venues (Bitvavo, Kraken)** that spent nothing (hypothesis). Retail pushed offshore/outside the EU perimeter remains a tail risk (open).
