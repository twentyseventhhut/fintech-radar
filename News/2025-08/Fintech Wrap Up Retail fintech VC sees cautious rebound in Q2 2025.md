# Retail Fintech VC Trends

August 8, 2025

| By Rudy Yang

**A quarter of big wins—but also big warnings for consumer fintech**

Retail fintech delivered a tale of two markets in Q2 2025. While VC funding climbed 17% quarter-over-quarter to $2.2 billion, the sector still trails 49% behind last year’s pace as investors continue favoring B2B over consumer plays. The standout story was the long-awaited return of fintech IPOs, with eToro’s $4 billion and Chime’s $9.1 billion public debuts driving exit value up a staggering 546% year-over-year to $13.2 billion.

Wealthtech emerged as the clear winner, capturing $1.3 billion across 82 deals and benefiting from higher AI adoption rates among consumer segments. However, a concerning trend persists: Retail fintech’s median deal size of $5.1 million still lags enterprise fintech’s $6.5 million, highlighting how the AI premium remains concentrated in B2B models.
