Indonesia’s QRIS payment system is now accepted in Japan as of August 17, 2025, marking its first expansion outside ASEAN.

QRIS, Indonesia’s national QR code payment platform, can be used at 35 merchants in Japan, allowing Indonesian visitors to pay by scanning JPQR Global with their domestic apps.

Bank Indonesia developed this cross-border payment initiative in partnership with the Indonesian Payment System Association.

Bank Indonesia announced it has started trialing QRIS connectivity with China, in cooperation with the People’s Bank of China, UnionPay International, ASPI, and representatives of payment system operators.

The central bank said cross-border QRIS has facilitated 994,890 transactions with Thailand since August 2022 and 4.3 million with Malaysia since May 2023.

Expansion to more merchants in Japan and reciprocal acceptance for Japanese visitors in Indonesia are planned.

The launch involved cooperation with Japan’s Ministry of Economy, Trade, and Industry, Payment Japan Association, Netstars, and participating financial institutions.

## 🧠 Food for thought

### 1️⃣ National payment systems require years of domestic maturity before successful international expansion

QRIS’s Japan launch on August 17, 2025, marks exactly six years since its domestic debut on August 17, 2019 1, demonstrating how digital payment infrastructure requires substantial time to mature before cross-border scaling.

This timeline reflects the complex technical and regulatory groundwork needed for international interoperability.

The system had to first prove its domestic viability, growing from Indonesia’s fragmented landscape of 38 registered e-wallet apps in 2019 to a unified platform serving 57 million users today.

The sequential rollout pattern shows strategic market selection, starting with neighboring ASEAN countries where regulatory alignment and economic ties were stronger, before expanding to more complex markets like Japan.

### 2️⃣ Cross-border QR payment adoption varies significantly by market characteristics

Transaction data from QRIS’s existing international implementations reveals stark differences in usage patterns across countries.

Malaysia leads with 4.31 million transactions worth Rp1.15 trillion since May 2023, while Singapore recorded only 238,216 transactions worth Rp77.06 billion since November 2023, despite launching earlier.

Thailand falls in the middle with 994,890 transactions worth Rp437.54 billion since August 2022.

These variations likely reflect different factors including tourist flow patterns, existing payment infrastructure maturity, and bilateral economic relationships between Indonesia and each country.

The Malaysia numbers are particularly striking, generating nearly 18 times more transactions than Singapore despite launching only about six months earlier, suggesting either stronger Indonesian tourist presence or better merchant adoption in Malaysia.

Indonesia’s QRIS Payment System Now Accepted in Japan
 Jakarta. Bank Indonesia has officially expanded the Quick Response Indonesian Standard (QRIS) cross-border digital payment system to Japan, with the service now available at 35 merchants through a partnership with the local payment app JPQR Global.
Bank Indonesia Governor Perry Warjiyo said the launch, which took place on Sunday, marked a milestone in QRIS’s regional expansion and coincided with Indonesia’s 80th Independence Day.
“Since its adoption six years ago, QRIS has become a game changer in the digital payment ecosystem that drives the Indonesian economy, with 57 million users today,” Perry said in a statement.
The expansion is the result of a collaboration between Bank Indonesia, Japan’s Ministry of Economy, Trade, and Industry, the Payment Japan Association (PJA), and the Indonesian Payment System Association (ASPI).
Perry added that the number of Japanese merchants accepting QRIS is expected to grow significantly in the near future.
Bank Indonesia data shows that cross-border QRIS transactions have grown steadily across Southeast Asia. As of June 2025, QRIS facilitated 994,890 transactions worth Rp 437.54 billion ($27 million) in Thailand, 4.31 million transactions worth Rp 1.15 trillion ($71 million) in Malaysia, and 238,216 transactions worth Rp 77.06 billion ($4.8 million) in Singapore.
