## Stripe buys three firms in nine months to build AI-powered payments

San Francisco — Stripe is stitching together a next-generation payments stack by buying the pieces it says traditional finance still lacks.

The privately held fintech has now closed three deals in nine months—stablecoin rails provider Bridge, embedded-wallet startup Privy and, most recently, real-time-payments specialist Orum—as it chases the twin “gale-force tailwinds” of artificial intelligence and blockchain, chief executive Patrick Collison told developers at the company’s Sessions conference in May.stripe+2

## Building instant money movement

Stripe processed more than $1.4 trillion in payments last year—roughly 1.3 percent of global GDP—yet its merchants still wait days for bank transfers to clear. Orum’s machine-learning APIs, which predict fund availability and route payments across FedNow, RTP and same-day ACH, aim to cut settlement to seconds.finovate+1

Founder Stephany Kirkpatrick said joining Stripe will let Orum “transform payment speed and certainty” at global scale. Analysts see the acquisition, announced July 21, as Stripe’s answer to intensifying pressure from Adyen and the card networks to deliver instant payouts.linkedin

## Extending into crypto rails

Speed is also the lure behind stablecoins. Stripe paid $1.1 billion for Bridge in February, gaining infrastructure to let businesses hold, send and receive dollar-denominated tokens worldwide without intermediary banks. Two months later the company launched stablecoin financial accounts in 101 countries, positioning the dollar-pegged USDC and Bridge’s own USDB as low-friction hedges against local currency volatility.themiddlemarket+2

Privy fills the last mile: its developer toolkit spins up secure, app-native wallets so end users never juggle private keys. The deal, disclosed June 11, gives Stripe custody and identity tech powering more than 75 million wallets, broadening its crypto on-ramp just as Washington debates stablecoin rules.cryptobriefing+1

## An AI core to tie it together

Under the hood, Stripe is rewriting its own software with AI. A new “Payments Foundation Model,” trained on tens of billions of historical transactions, has already lifted fraud-attack detection 64 percent “practically overnight,” the company claims. Collison argues that blending the model with Bridge’s programmable money and Orum’s rail-switching logic will let Stripe automate everything from payroll splits to multicurrency treasury in real time.techcrunch

Investors appear convinced: Stripe’s latest funding round in May pushed its valuation to $65 billion as it earmarked 22 percent of the new cash for acquisitions. Whether the shopping spree ends there is unclear—Collison quipped in May that the market for “useful, boring infrastructure” remains vast.coinlaw+1

Yet for merchants, the strategy is straightforward: if money can move instantly, securely and on whatever rail is cheapest, it scarcely matters whether it’s riding ACH, FedNow or a stablecoin ledger. By owning the APIs that decide, Stripe hopes to be the unseen layer stitching them all together.
