Open Asset, a blockchain fintech company with stablecoin issuance technology, announced on the 8th that it has attracted a strategic investment worth 5 billion won from the country's leading financial value-added network (VAN) operator.
With this investment, the two companies decided to jointly develop an integrated service that directly links OpenMint, Open Asset's stablecoin issuance service, and Dussen's financial payment infrastructure.
Open Asset directly issues general-purpose won stablecoins using Dussen's financial linkage network, and also provides a full-fledged white label issuance agency service for companies that want to issue stablecoins under their own brand.
Specifically, it will jointly develop an integrated service that links Dussen's firmware banking and Kakao authentication systems with Open Asset's stablecoin issuance system.
Users are expected to experience a one-stop financial service that allows real-time conversion from stablecoins to bank accounts from existing bank accounts to stablecoins.
In addition, Open Asset's stablecoin will be applied to Dusen's foreign currency exchange kiosk infrastructure to create an environment where various virtual asset-based charging and currency exchange can be widely used not only by domestic users but also by foreign customers.
Kim Kyung-up, CEO of Open Asset, said, "We will build a foundation for stablecoins to be connected to real-life finance based on Dudgeon's dual stable financial network."
