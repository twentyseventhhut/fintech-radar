# Fiinu to acquire FX firm Everfex in £12m reverse takeover

The proposed acquisition comes as Fiinu prepares for its Plugin Overdraft platform to go live with an undisclosed UK bank in Q4 2025.

[](https://www.fintechfutures.com/author/tyler-pathe)

Tyler Pathe,Reporter, FinTech Futures

UK banking start-up Fiinu has announced a proposed reverse takeover of Everfex, a foreign exchange (FX) brokerage which, despite only recently being incorporated through the January 2025 acquisition of Polish currency hedging solutions provider Stały Kurs, has already tipped into profitability, recording over £600,000 in pre-tax profits in the first four months of this year.

The deal is valued at "up to £12 million", Fiinu says, comprising an initial £8 million consideration alongside a possible additional earnout for Everfex owners if its order book surpasses £650 million by year-end, payable in new equity at 20 pence per ordinary share.

Fiinu previously completed a similar reverse takeover of Immediate Acquisition, formerly Immedia Group, enabling its listing on the Alternative Investment Market (AIM) of the London Stock Exchange (LSE) in July 2022.

According to an LSE filing, Fiinu plans to raise £800,000 by way of a conditional cash subscription to fund the acquisition of Everfex, and had its AIM securities suspended from trading on AIM, as per reverse takeover rules, on 7 August.

Related:Barclays to buy UK money app GoHenry

Profitable Everfex makes for a desirable acquisition target, having executed over $1 billion in spot, swap, and forward contracts trades last year, when its SME client base is said to have expanded 1,300%, with 2,500 clients already using the platform in Poland. Fiinu will use the purchase to broaden its fintech portfolio beyond Banking-as-a-Service, and "add immediate commercial momentum via Everfex’s established regional operations".

Fiinu is currently preparing to roll out its Plugin Overdraft platform with an undisclosed third-party bank in the UK, a project it says "remains on schedule for Q4 2025". The platform lets users use open banking to access regulated third-part overdraft services without having to change banks. The company adds that it's "in active discussions with a number of other banks to white-label its proprietary AI-powered underwriting engine, which enables its Bank Independent Overdraft model".

On top of this, Fiinu has been attempting to launch itself as a bank for at least the past seven years, and though already completing its mobilisation phase with the Bank of England, admits in its latest statement that the project "remains on pause due to capital requirements". This echoes a similar announcement made by the company in April 2023, when shallow funding pools led to operations being scaled back.

#### Latest board appointments: Mark Wallace and Sami Kalliola

On completion of the proposed acquisition of Everfex, Fiinu will appoint Mark Wallace, former chief risk officer at UBS Investment Bank, and Sami Kalliola to the board.

Wallace will join as an independent non-executive director of Fiinu Plc, and chair of both Everfex and the group risk committee. Kalliola will join the Fiinu Plc board as executive director, while also assuming the position of chief strategy officer.

Meanwhile, Feyzullah Egriboyun, who was appointed as group CFO of Fiinu in March this year, has served his notice to the company, effective 22 August, according to the LSE filing. Chief operating officer Michael Hopton will succeed Egriboyun as interim CFO, supported by audit committee chair Simon Leathers, while a permanent successor is sourced.

Fiinu Plc has announced it will acquire foreign exchange brokerage Everfex P.S.A. for up to £12 million in a deal that advances its push into the FX market and banking-as-a-service (BaaS) infrastructure. The acquisition is structured as a reverse takeover, triggering a temporary suspension of Fiinu’s shares on the AIM exchange pending shareholder approval.

Everfex, a profitable FX broker, executed more than $1 billion in foreign exchange contracts during 2024. The firm specializes in institutional and high-volume corporate trading, positioning it as a key acquisition for Fiinu, which is seeking to scale its financial services ecosystem beyond its core Plugin Overdraft® product.

## Fiinu plans to raise around £0.8 million

The announcement comes as Fiinu pursues a broader strategy to embed its technology stack into digital banking infrastructure. The acquisition is expected to unlock opportunities for deeper integration between FX execution, BaaS rails, and the consumer-facing capabilities already present in the company’s platform.

Fiinu said it plans to raise around £0.8 million through a conditional subscription of new ordinary shares to support the transaction. The subscription will be priced and allocated following shareholder approval of the deal. If approved, the acquisition will represent a significant milestone in Fiinu’s shift toward becoming a vertically integrated fintech, combining regulated payment and credit services with proprietary banking infrastructure.

According to statements issued with the transaction, the acquisition will provide Fiinu with enhanced FX capabilities that complement its existing consumer and SME offerings. In addition to diversifying revenue, the deal is expected to add a profitable line of business and expand the group’s footprint in foreign exchange services.

As part of the reverse takeover process, Fiinu’s shares will remain suspended from trading on the London Stock Exchange’s AIM market until the company publishes a new admission document detailing the acquisition terms, governance changes, and revised corporate structure.

This deal comes amid a period of rising interest in BaaS infrastructure among fintech players seeking to bundle deposits, lending, payments, and FX within a single regulatory perimeter. Everfex’s operations could allow Fiinu to embed international payments, hedging, and cross-border treasury services into its offering, with potential benefits for partner banks and white-label clients.

Fiinu has indicated that integrating Everfex’s execution infrastructure will support broader monetization of its platform via payment margins, FX spreads, and institutional trading fees. With the Plugin Overdraft® product targeting underbanked consumers, and BaaS APIs being rolled out to third-party partners, the company is seeking to widen its unit economics by adding high-margin financial services atop its licensing and core tech layers.

As of the latest filings, Fiinu’s market capitalization stood at £27.57 million, suggesting that the acquisition, valued at up to £12 million, would substantially reshape the balance sheet and shareholder structure. The deal remains subject to customary closing conditions and due diligence.

Fiinu’s leadership said the move reflects a clear focus on building a “connected financial services ecosystem,” in which digital overdrafts, embedded banking tools, and cross-border capabilities can be deployed in tandem.
