Indonesia’s central bank, Bank Indonesia (BI), plans to launch the Indonesian Standard QR Code payment system (QRIS) in China by the end of 2025.

The central bank has tested QRIS in China since August 2025 with partners including the Indonesian Payment System Association, UnionPay International, and payment providers.

Bank Indonesia said it is in the final phase of sandbox testing and will move to full implementation once all requirements are met.

The rollout will allow users to make payments in China using QRIS, after similar launches in Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, and Japan.

In Thailand, QRIS has recorded nearly 995,000 transactions worth 437.5 billion rupiah (US$26.8 million) since August 2022.

Malaysia has seen 4.3 million QRIS transactions valued at Rp 1.2 trillion rupiah (US$73.6 million) since May 2023, while Singapore recorded 238,216 transactions worth 77.1 billion rupiah (US$4.7 million) since November 2023.

QRIS digital payment transactions rose 148.5% year-on-year in Q2 2025, driven by more users and merchants.

## 🧠 Food for thought

### 1️⃣ Indonesia taps into China’s massive mobile payment ecosystem

Indonesia’s QRIS expansion into China represents entry into the world’s most advanced mobile payment market.

China leads globally with 92% of consumers using QR-code-driven digital wallets, and approximately 954 million people actively use mobile payment services as of 202423.

This creates enormous market potential compared to Indonesia’s existing cross-border implementations, where Thailand recorded 994,890 QRIS transactions worth Rp 437.54 billion since August 2022, and Malaysia reached 4.31 million transactions worth Rp 1.15 trillion since May 20231.

The scale difference is significant. China’s mobile payment giants Alipay and WeChat Pay processed transaction volumes of RMB 118.19 trillion and RMB 67.81 trillion respectively in just Q3 20232.

Indonesia’s partnership with UnionPay International for the sandbox testing phase positions QRIS to integrate with China’s existing infrastructure, potentially accessing this vast user base of Chinese consumers both domestically and when traveling abroad.

### 2️⃣ Cross-border QR systems deliver substantial cost savings and financial inclusion

The Indonesia-China QRIS implementation follows a proven model that significantly reduces transaction costs and barriers.

Similar cross-border QR payment systems between ASEAN countries have demonstrated cost reductions of up to 30% while providing exchange rate buffers for users4.

These systems address a critical need in Southeast Asia, where 50% of the population remains unbanked, with Indonesia specifically having 50% of its population unbanked5.

Traditional cross-border payments face high costs due to multiple intermediaries and currency conversion fees, while QR-based systems enable direct transactions between central banks, reducing both fees and processing complexity67.

The financial inclusion impact extends beyond individual consumers to benefit micro, small, and medium enterprises that often operate in the informal sector and struggle with traditional banking access58.

BI to roll out QRIS payments in China by end of 2025
 
 
 
 

 Published on 21/08/2025 GMT+7 
 

 Reading time 2 minutes 
 

 Author: Julian Isaac 
 

 Editor: Imanuddin Razak 
 
Published on 21/08/2025 GMT+7 Reading time 2 minutes Julian Isaac Imanuddin Razak
The Indonesian Central Bank (BI) has confirmed that the expansion of its Quick Response Code Indonesian Standard (QRIS) payment service to China will be implemented by the end of this year. Deputy Governor of BI, Filianingsih Hendarta, said the system has entered its trial phase as of 17 August 2025.
“The system has entered its trial phase as of 17 August 2025Hopefully, God willing, by the end of the year we can implement Indonesia–China QR payments in both directions, inbound and outbound,” Deputy BI Governor, Filianingsih Hendarta, told BI’s August Board of Governors press conference on Wednesday, August 20, 2025.
She cited that BI is currently finalizing connections between Indonesia’s national switching providers − PT Rintis Sejahtera, PT Alto Network, PT Artajasa Pembayaran Elektronis, and PT Jalin Pembayaran Nusantara − and Union Pay International (UPI). 
“Our switching with UPI involves two dominant ecosystems in China that control 88 percent of users and 80 percent of merchants,” she said.
The deputy governor noted that cross-border QRIS implementation requires several steps, starting from signing a central bank memorandum of understanding, securing agreements with industry players, developing interlinking systems, and running sandbox trials. 
“Now we are in the final stage of sandbox testing, after which we will move to implementation,” she added.
Meanwhile, BI reported that Indonesia’s digital financial transactions remained robust in July 2025, supported by a secure and reliable payment system. The total value of digital payment transactions in July grew 45.30 percent year-on-year (yoy) to 4.44 billion transactions. QRIS usage showed even stronger growth, surging 162.77 percent yoy, driven by the rapid increase in users and merchants.
