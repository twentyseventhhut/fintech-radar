Japan’s Credit, Charge Card Payments Set To Rise 9.4% In 2025: GlobalData
 # Japan 
 # Credit 
 # Card 
 # Payments 
 # GlobalData 
Japan’s credit and charge card payments market is expected to grow by 9.4 per cent in 2025 to reach JPY129.6 trillion (USD 857.3 billion), driven by a shift away from cash and increased merchant acceptance of credit cards, according to a forecast by data and analytics firm GlobalData.
The latest figures from GlobalData’s Payment Cards Analytics show that credit and charge card payment values rose 12.7 per cent in 2023, followed by a further 12.1 per cent increase in 2024 to reach JPY118.5 trillion (USD 783.8 billion), indicating sustained momentum in card-based transactions.
Ravi Sharma, Lead Banking and Payments Analyst at GlobalData, said Japan’s traditionally cash-reliant payment ecosystem is seeing an accelerated transition to digital and card-based payments. “Japan’s payment landscape is predominantly cash-based. However, payment card usage has grown in recent years, mainly driven by the higher adoption of credit and charge cards,” Sharma said.
In 2024, the frequency of credit and charge card usage reached 70.7 transactions per card annually, far outpacing the 2.4 transactions per card recorded for debit cards. GlobalData attributes this trend to the stronger incentives and value-added features associated with credit cards, such as flexible repayment options and reward programmes.
These benefits, coupled with expanded card acceptance infrastructure, are encouraging more consumers to opt for credit and charge cards over other forms of payment. “Another important factor driving this usage is the wider acceptance of credit cards among merchants, with credit cards now accepted across all types of merchants,” Sharma added.
Credit and charge cards currently dominate Japan’s card payments market, making up 96.2 per cent of total card payment value in 2024. Debit and prepaid cards remain marginal in comparison, underscoring the popularity of reward-linked credit card spending.
Ecommerce is one of the key drivers behind this growth. Credit and charge cards accounted for over half of Japan’s ecommerce transaction value in 2024. This is largely due to the advantages credit cards offer in online transactions, including fraud protection, promotional discounts, and loyalty points.
The country’s public transport systems are also contributing to the uptake of credit card payments. In September 2024, Keikyu Line stations introduced a contactless ticket payment system, enabling passengers to pay at automated gates using their credit cards—another example of how digital infrastructure is being adapted to reduce cash dependence.
In 2018, Japan’s Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI) published a cashless strategy aimed at increasing the share of cashless payments to 40 per cent by 2025, with a long-term target of 80 per cent. Measures introduced under this plan include point-based reward schemes, promotion of self-service payment kiosks, and the collection of more granular data on digital payment trends.
GlobalData’s outlook suggests this momentum will continue. The firm projects a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 7.4 per cent for Japan’s credit and charge card market between 2025 and 2029, with the market expected to reach JPY172.3 trillion (USD 1.1 trillion) by 2029.
“The ongoing shift from cash to electronic payments, availability of pricing benefits on credit and charge cards and rising adoption of contactless payments are expected to further boost credit and charge card usage,” Sharma said.
