UK-based Vennre has launched a venture capital co-investment product, allowing qualified individual investors to participate in private market startup deals with a minimum entry of US$5,000.

Vennre is a digital wealth creation platform that offers access to private market investments.

The company reported reaching over US$25 million in total transaction value from both income- and growth-focused deals.

The new product is Shariah-compliant and available to a global user base, including investors in Saudi Arabia and the Gulf region.

Vennre said the platform is fully digital and aims to lower traditional barriers to venture capital investing, such as high entry minimums and limited access.

## 🧠 Food for thought

### 1️⃣ Shariah-compliant investing momentum supports Vennre’s strategic positioning

Vennre’s focus on Shariah-compliant venture capital investments aligns with significant market growth in Islamic finance.

The global Islamic finance market is projected to reach $4.94 trillion by 2025, driven by digitalization and growing demand for ethical investment options 1.

This growth is particularly relevant to Vennre’s target demographic, as 67% of high-net-worth individuals in the Middle East specifically value Shariah compliance in their investment decisions 2.

The timing appears strategic given that younger investors are increasingly prioritizing ethical investment practices, with sustainable investing and ESG principles becoming integral to Islamic finance 1.

This demographic shift suggests Vennre’s combination of venture capital access with Shariah compliance could capture both the growing Islamic finance market and the broader trend toward values-driven investing.

### 2️⃣ Lowering entry barriers addresses institutional dominance in venture capital

Vennre’s $5,000 minimum investment represents a significant departure from traditional venture capital accessibility.

Venture capital has historically been an institutional asset class, with traditional VCs typically seeking returns of 3x to 5x over a 10-year horizon—returns that were previously accessible only to large institutions and ultra-high-net-worth individuals 3.

By offering co-investment opportunities at this lower threshold, Vennre is effectively democratizing access to an asset class that could generate substantial returns for qualified individual investors.

This approach addresses a real market gap, as HENRYs (High Earners, Not Rich Yet) have traditionally been excluded from venture capital despite having sufficient capital and risk tolerance for these investments.

The company’s achievement of $25 million in total transaction value with over half of investors choosing to reinvest suggests there’s genuine demand for this type of accessible private market investing.
