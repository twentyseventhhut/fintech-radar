Paytm block deal: China's Ant Group to exit fintech major, suggest reports
Ant Group is set to fully exit One 97 Communications, Paytm's parent company, by selling its 5.84% equity stake in a transaction valued at approximately ₹ 3,800 crore on Tuesday.
 One 97 Communications , the parent company of Paytm, is expected to see China’s Ant Group fully exit the Indian payments firm. According to sources cited by NDTV Profit, Chinese e-commerce major Alibaba group firm Antfin plans to sell its remaining 5.84% equity stake in Paytm through block deals on Tuesday, with the transaction estimated to be worth around ₹ 3,845 crore.
The floor price has reportedly been fixed at ₹ 1,020 per share, representing a 5.4% discount to Paytm’s last closing price of ₹ 1,078.20 on August 4 on the NSE.
As of the June quarter, Antfin (Netherlands) Holding B.V. held a 5.84% stake, or 3.77 crore shares, in the company, according to exchange data. Antfin has been steadily trimming its stake in the fintech major in recent quarters, having sold a 4% stake in May. Foreign investors currently hold the largest stake in the company at 54.9%, followed by general shareholders, who collectively own a 29.3% stake.
This development comes at a time when Paytm’s stock has been on an upward trend, closing each of the last five months in positive territory and delivering a stellar gain of 52%.
Even as the broader Indian stock market remained under significant pressure in July, Paytm’s stock closed the month with an 18% gain , marking its largest monthly rise since November 2024. After nearly six months, the stock crossed the ₹ 1,000 mark in mid-July, fueled in part by reports of its potential inclusion in the MSCI Standard Index.
Meanwhile, Mutual funds have shown growing confidence in the company’s growth prospects. According to the latest shareholding data for Q1FY26, domestic mutual funds collectively held a 13.86% stake in Paytm , up from 13.11% in the March quarter.
Posts first-ever profit in Q1
The company ₹ 122.5 crore in the quarter ended June 2025">reported its first-ever consolidated net profit of ₹ 122.5 crore in the quarter ended June 2025 , driven mainly by cost optimization and an increase in payment revenue.
Marketing and promotional expenses were cut by more than half to ₹ 99.8 crore during the reported quarter, down from ₹ 221.4 crore a year ago and ₹ 142.7 crore in the March 2025 quarter. Employee benefit expenses were also reduced by about ₹ 300 crore, or one-third, to ₹ 643 crore from ₹ 952.5 crore on a year-over-year basis.
Consolidated revenue from operations in the reporting quarter rose about 28% to ₹ 1,917.5 crore from ₹ 1,501.6 crore in the June 2024 quarter, mainly due to improved payment processing margins. During the quarter, Paytm reported that its average monthly transacting users (MTU) base reached 7.4 crore.

### thearcweb.com

*   Chinese payments major Ant Financial has sold its remaining 5.8% direct stake in Paytm-owner One97 Communications. The shares were sold for Rs 3,980 crore ($454 million) through block deals. It sold roughly 37.3 million shares at a price of about Rs 1,067.

*   The latest transaction brings Ant and parent company Alibaba’s total proceeds from their decade-old bet on Paytm to Rs 16,000 crore ($1.85 billion). Ant and Alibaba invested Rs 7,043 crore ($851 million) in the company through multiple entities, according to The Arc Research.

*   In 2023, Anttransferred the voting rights equivalent to a 10.3% stake to Paytm founder Vijay Shekhar Sharma by moving the shares to a new entity called Resilient Asset Management. That was a “no-cash” deal, where Ant retained the economic value of the stake. These shares have not been sold yet and are worth about Rs 7,000 crore ($797 million).

*   Ant and its parent company, Chinese ecommerce giant Alibaba, have been paring their holdings in Paytm since the IPO in 2021. They became strategic investors in the company in 2015. An amount of $680 million was first committed through multiple tranches in 2015 at a valuation ranging from $1.5 billion to $3.5 billion.

*   The Indian fintech’s shares were trading at Rs 1,063 on Tuesday, August 5, down by 1.42%. Its market capitalisation stood at Rs 67,811 crore, or $7.7 billion. But the shares debuted on the exchanges at an issue price of Rs 2,150 in November 2021, and have been recovering since then. Ant had previously offloaded shares earlier this year and in 2023, besides during the IPO.

*   In 2024, Paytm faced a major setback when the banking regulator RBI barred its affiliate, Paytm Payments Bank, from running several core operations, significantly impacting its revenues. Paytm[](https://www.thearcweb.com/article/paytm-vijay-shekhar-sharma-esop-loss-5xocjaHQHd7hx0py)swung back into net profit recently, having logged topline growth over the past few quarters.

*   At one point, Alibaba and Ant owned nearly 40% of Paytm. In 2020, Ant was unable to transfer $200 million (out of a planned $400 million) during Paytm’s last private-market round because of government curbs stemming from India-China border issues. As Paytm wanted to reduce Chinese shareholding, Alibaba could offload a substantial chunk in the offer for sale in the IPO, when the fintech was still valued at $19 billion.
