# Exclusive: German investech Upvest bolsters technical expertise with new engineering SVP and board director

Adyen's Roberto Fajardo has been named SVP of engineering, while Parloa's CTO Alexander Matthey has joined Upvest's board as a non-executive director.

German investment infrastructure provider Upvest has appointed Roberto Fajardo as SVP of engineering, and welcomed Alexander Matthey to its board as a non-executive director, FinTech Futures can reveal.

Fajardo joins from Dutch payments processor Adyen, where he has served as VP of engineering since November 2023. Before this, he worked at Backbase as VP of technology for over seven years, along with software engineering roles at Reply and Goldman Sachs.

In his new position at Upvest, Fajardo will oversee both engineering strategy and organisational development as the company continues to expand its product offerings. Last year, Upvest tapped Deutsche Bank to access banking infrastructure that enables the delivery of automated payment processing, virtual IBANs, end-user cash management tools, and foreign exchange services.

Speaking with FinTech Futures, Fajardo states that "API-first platforms will continue to reshape how financial institutions integrate investment products, enabling faster go-to-market, customisation, and scalability", adding that "the pragmatic and intentional use of AI will play a key role in enabling financial institutions to integrate, migrate, and adopt new functionality fast".

Related:Former HMRC chief Sir Jon Thompson named Vocalink chair

In addition, Parloa's chief technology officer, Alexander Matthey, is joining Upvest’s board as a non-executive director. According to Upvest, Matthey will provide "strategic guidance on scaling technology and applying AI across the organisation".

These appointments follow Upvest's €100 million Series C funding round in 2024 and its recent expansion into the UK market, marked by the appointment of former Starling Bank head of SME banking, Symmie Swil, as UK general manager in March.

Founded in 2017 by CEO Martin Kassing, Upvest currently operates in 20 markets, serving major clients such as N26, bunq, and Revolut.Already employing over 220 staff, the company says it is "investing heavily in technology and talent" to further develop its API-first investment infrastructure.

Upvest strengthens engineering leadership to drive its next stage of growth
Julieta Varsano
 Roberto Fajardo , ex- Adyen and Backbase , joins to lead engineering as Upvest scales product delivery across Europe
 Alexander Matthey , CTO of AI-unicorn Parloa , joins the board as Non-Executive Director, further strengthening engineering’s voice in Upvest’s growth journey
The addition of these top engineering leaders reinforces Upvest’s ambition to lead Europe in investment infrastructure innovation
 Berlin, 13. August 2025 – Upvest, the leading investment infrastructure provider in Europe, today announces two key leadership hires to support its next phase of growth. Roberto Fajardo joins as SVP Engineering and Alexander Matthey joins the board as Non-Executive Director. Together, they bring deep experience in scaling financial infrastructure in regulated spaces and building world-class engineering cultures.
Roberto Fajardo joins Upvest from listed payments giant Adyen where he led a global engineering team. Prior to that, he spent over seven years at AI-powered banking platform Backbase during a phase of hyper-growth, where he scaled the engineering team responsible for modernising digital banking infrastructure.
At Upvest, Roberto will lead engineering strategy and organisation as the company continues to rapidly expand its product portfolio. 
 Upvest is uniquely positioned to transform how investment products are built and delivered. The opportunity to reimagine investing infrastructure from the ground up, powered by scalable and reliable technology, is what excites me most. Roberto Fajardo SVP Engineering
With a strong track record in leading high-growth tech teams, Roberto will be responsible for driving engineering excellence, accelerating delivery, and embedding a culture of continuous improvement. “Delivering customer value through fast, pragmatic iterations over chasing perfection is a mindset I truly believe in”, he added. “Upvest shares that mindset. It’s an environment where engineers move fast, learn quickly, and build with purpose.” 
In addition, Alexander Matthey is joining Upvest’s board as a Non-Executive Director. Alex brings invaluable expertise in building global platforms, leading through scale, and adopting AI for efficiency gains. As part of the board, he will provide strategic guidance on scaling technology and applying AI across the organisation.
 “Upvest has established itself as the leading player in Europe’s investment infrastructure space,” said Alex. “It’s rare to find a company that combines deep infrastructure capabilities with a culture that’s both ambitious and grounded. I’m excited to support this next chapter in an advisory role.” 
These strategic hires follow a period of rapid growth for Upvest, which secured its €100m Series C funding in 2024 and expanded into the UK market in 2025. With demand rising for modern, API-first investment infrastructure, Upvest is investing heavily in technology and talent to meet the needs of Europe’s leading financial institutions.
 “Roberto and Alex bring a rare combination of infrastructure know-how, hands-on scale-up experience, and visionary thinking,” said Martin Kassing, CEO and co-founder of Upvest. “They share our belief that strong engineering is the foundation for long-term leadership and we’re thrilled to have them on board.” 
 About Upvest ‍ 
Upvest is on a mission to make investing money as easy as spending it. The company empowers businesses to offer a wide range of investment products and the best user experience in the fields of capital market investment and retirement planning. Upvest’s Investment API is easy to integrate so that fintechs and enterprises alike can save resources and fully focus on their core business.
Upvest is one of the few fintechs in Europe that can provide its B2B clients with the full range of investment opportunities in the areas of principal broking business, proprietary trading, and safe custody business. The company was founded in 2017 by Martin Kassing and currently employs more than 220 people across its hubs in Berlin, London and Tallinn.
