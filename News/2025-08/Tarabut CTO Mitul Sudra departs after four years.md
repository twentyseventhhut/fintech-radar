# Tarabut CTO Mitul Sudra departs after four-year tenure

Sudra says he is "exploring new ideas and gearing up to build something new again", while also advising start-ups through his company, F7&Partners.

Mitul Sudra has stepped down from his role as chief technology officer (CTO) of Middle Eastern open banking platform Tarabut, having led the firm's technology strategy since 2021.

In a LinkedIn announcement earlier this month, Sudra explains that he made the decision to leave Tarabut in early 2025.

Describing his time at Tarabut, Sudra states: "Together, we expanded across the region, launched and scaled full-stack open banking infrastructure alongside a universal API platform powering hundreds of banks and fintechs, secured regulatory licences across the UAE, Bahrain, and Saudi Arabia, and earned the backing of Visa and major regional banks and investors. We laid the groundwork for data-driven lending, payments, and BaaS, all within one of the world’s most dynamic regulatory landscapes."

Sudra was the company's first C-suite hire and was part of the leadership team when Tarabut secured $32 million in Series A funding in 2023. The firm also acquired UK fintech Vyne last year for an undisclosed sum to enhance its account-to-account (A2A) capabilities.

Related:UK banks and fintechs unite to launch new open banking payment framework

On his future plans, Sudra writes: "I’m advising early-stage ventures and growth-stage companies across AI, fintech, and healthtech through my firm, F7&Partners, while also exploring new ideas and gearing up to build something new again."

Before joining Tarabut, Sudra co-founded UK-based open banking platform OpenWrks in 2015, which was later acquired by Tink in 2020.
