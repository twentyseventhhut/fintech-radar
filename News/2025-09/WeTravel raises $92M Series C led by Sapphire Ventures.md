Pillsbury advised WeTravel, a San Francisco-based operating system for multi-day travel businesses, in raising $92 million via a Series C funding round.
Sapphire Ventures led the funding round, with participation from Left Lane Capital, Base10, Cross Creek and other angel investors.
WeTravel is a comprehensive operating system designed for multi-day group travel businesses. It integrates itinerary building, booking tools, global payment processing and trip management into a single platform—empowering operators to boost direct bookings, offer high-end travel experiences and streamline every aspect of their operations.
Additionally, according to recent coverage, the funds will be directed toward accelerating innovation across its platform, expanding global payment infrastructure, building AI-driven tools that automate manual workflows and delivering new solutions for operators to scale their businesses in real time.
Click here to learn more.
The Pillsbury deal team was led by Corporate partner Justin Hovey and included special counsel Caitlin Bloom Stulberg and associates Olivia Fong and Carissa Lee; Executive Compensation & Benefits partner Laura McDaniels; Tax partner Nora Burke; and Intellectual Property partner Christopher Kao.
