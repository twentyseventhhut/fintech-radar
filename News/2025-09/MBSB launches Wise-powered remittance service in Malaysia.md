MBSB BANK LAUNCHES GET – GLOBAL EASY TRANSFER, BECOMES FIRST BANK TO PARTNER WITH WISE PLATFORM IN MALAYSIA
 PETALING JAYA, 28 August 2025 – MBSB Bank Berhad has launched GET – Global Easy Transfer, a new international remittance service built in partnership with Wise, the global technology company specialising in cross-border payments. 

With this launch, MBSB Bank becomes the first bank in Malaysia to partner with Wise Platform, Wise's global infrastructure for banks and enterprises, enabling customers to send money abroad in a simpler, faster, and more transparent way, directly through the Bank’s M Journey retail banking app. 

GET – Global Easy Transfer is designed to solve real pain points that have long frustrated Malaysians: hidden fees, slow delivery times, unclear exchange rates, and lack of transfer visibility. At launch, customers can now send money in 12 currencies with real-time tracking, competitive exchange rates, and no hidden markups. This includes the United States (USD), the United Kingdom (GBP), Europe (EUR), Singapore (SGD), Indonesia (IDR), Australia (AUD) and many more. 

 Rafe Haneef, Group Chief Executive Officer of MBSB Bank said: “With GET – Global Easy Transfer, we are giving families, professionals and business owners the confidence to send money abroad knowing exactly how much will arrive and when. This is what customer-first innovation looks like.” 

Transfers are instant or same-day, depending on the destination, and can be tracked end to-end—just like a parcel, from the moment it is initiated. 

For thousands of Malaysian families, remittance is not optional, it is essential. With an estimated 55,000 Malaysians currently studying abroad in countries such as Australia, the UK and the US, international transfers are part of everyday life. 

Whether it is tuition fees, accommodation, living allowances or emergencies, GET – Global Easy Transfer offers families a faster, clearer and more reliable way to support their loved ones from home. 

 Usman Ghouse, Group Chief Consumer Banking Officer of MBSB Bank added: “Remitting money should not be ambiguous. GET – Global Easy Transfer was designed to address the real frustrations we have heard from customers—extensive delays, hidden charges, and poor transparency. This partnership with Wise Platform is about offering world-class capability with local accountability.”

 Samarth Bansal, APAC General Manager at Wise Platform said: “This partnership with MBSB Bank represents a significant milestone for Wise Platform - our first collaboration in Malaysia, enabling at least 22 million individuals in Malaysia to send money in a fast, secure, transparent and convenient way. With local payments in Malaysia and other parts of Asia Pacific being instant, customers now expect the same from cross-border transfers. We are excited to partner with MBSB to redefine cross-border payments and make international transfers as fast and convenient as domestic ones. MBSB Bank's commitment to customer-first innovation aligns perfectly with our mission, and together we're setting a new standard for cross-border payments in Malaysian banking."

 About Wise Platform

Thanks to Wise Platform, banks and large companies can leverage Wise’s technology and embed the best way to send, receive, and manage money internationally into their existing infrastructure. With a wide variety of partners worldwide, Wise Platform saves banks and businesses time and money by allowing them to seamlessly bring fast, cost-effective, transparent and convenient cross-border payments to their customers.

 MBSB Bank has introduced Global Easy Transfer (GET), a new international remittance service developed in partnership with Wise .
With this launch, MBSB Bank becomes the first Malaysian bank to partner with Wise Platform, the company’s global infrastructure for financial institutions.
The service allows customers to send money abroad directly through the Bank’s M Journey retail banking app in what it describes as a faster, more transparent and straightforward process.
Global Easy Transfer aims to address common challenges faced by Malaysians when sending money internationally, including hidden fees, slow processing times, unclear exchange rates and limited visibility of transfers.
At launch, the service supports transfers in 12 currencies, including the United States dollar, the British pound, the euro, the Singapore dollar, the Indonesian rupiah and the Australian dollar.
Customers are able to track their transfers in real time, with delivery times ranging from instant to same-day depending on the destination.
Rafe Haneef, Group Chief Executive Officer of MBSB Bank, said:
 “With GET, Global Easy Transfer, we are giving families, professionals and business owners the confidence to send money abroad knowing exactly how much will arrive and when. This is what customer-first innovation looks like.” 
International transfers are a necessity for many Malaysian households.
It is estimated that around 55,000 Malaysians are currently studying overseas, particularly in Australia, the UK and the US.
