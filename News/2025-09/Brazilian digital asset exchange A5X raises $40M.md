# Brazilian digital asset exchange A5X raises $40M

A5X, the new Brazilian exchange focused on derivatives and futures, has completed a Series C funding round worth R$200 million, bringing its total capital raised to R$385 million. The company's valuation has now reached R$1.35 billion, five times its value of R$235 million a year ago, when the current group of investors first joined. 
 The new funds will serve two purposes: around R$100 million will be used to meet capital requirements set by Brazil’s Central Bank, while the other half will support A5X’s launch and sustain operations for the next two years. 
 “We were approached by major international private equity funds interested in investing, but our existing shareholders decided to make the new contribution themselves, which shows their confidence in the company’s future,” said A5X CEO Carlos Ferreira Filho, one of the exchange’s four co-founders. 
 The other co-founders are Karel Luketic, the exchange’s CFO; Nilson Monteiro, founder and current president of brokerage Ideal (now controlled by Itaú Unibanco); and Julian Chediak, a partner at law firm Chediak Advogados. Despite the new capital, these four founders remain in control of the business.

 The first round (Series A) took place in June 2024, with investment from Ideal. Six months later, the Series B round brought in ABN Amro Clearing—the clearing and custody arm of Dutch bank ABN Amro—along with IMC Trading, Jump Trading Group, Optiver, and XTX Markets, all major market makers in the Brazilian and global derivatives market. 
 XP also holds a call option that gives it the right to acquire a stake in the new exchange, which is expected to be exercised in the medium term. 
 Testing phase 
 According to CFO Karel Luketic, A5X is on track with its original timeline: to begin testing with the Central Bank and Brazil’s Securities and Exchange Commission (CVM) between the end of this year and early 2026, with full operations expected between the second and third quarters of next year. 
 With the exchange infrastructure now in place, the next step is to define the first products to be listed. In equities, A5X plans to offer a full suite of futures and options contracts on both Brazilian and international stocks, as well as a new equity index designed to compete with B3’s Ibovespa, currently Brazil’s leading stock market benchmark. 
 In fixed income, A5X intends to roll out a range of interest rate-linked products. In the currency market, the exchange plans to launch a futures contract tied to the Ptax, the Central Bank’s daily reference exchange rate for the dollar. And in crypto, A5X aims to start with a bitcoin futures contract. 
 New competitors 
 A5X is one of three new exchanges being built to challenge B3, currently Brazil’s only operational stock exchange. The other two are CSD—backed by banks Santander and BTG Pactual—and ATG, backed by Mubadala, the sovereign wealth fund of Abu Dhabi. 
 On Monday (15), A5X also signed a licensing agreement with B3 for access to its market data. The deal allows A5X to use B3’s licensed data for internal processes such as collateral pricing, risk management, trading operations, and index creation. In practice, this means A5X is purchasing the right to access B3’s vast database as it ramps up its platform.
