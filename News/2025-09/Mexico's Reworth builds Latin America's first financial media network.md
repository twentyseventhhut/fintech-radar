 Reworth is the fintech building the Financial Media Network (FMN) in Latin America, transforming banks into high-ROI media channels for merchants by using transaction data to deliver personalized, merchant-funded cashback directly into bank apps. 
 Q: What was the business opportunity that gave origin to Reworth back in 2022? 
A: In 2022, we saw two converging forces: the rise of fintechs and neo-banks in Mexico with limited benefits programs, and data from the United States showing that 68% of consumers preferred cashback as their primary card incentive. We began by offering cashback for fintechs, but quickly realized the bigger opportunity: building the first Financial Media Network (FMN) in Latin America. By 2023, we pivoted fully to this model, where banks become high-ROI media channels, consumers get personalized cashback, and merchants only pay for verified sales.
 Q: What added value does Reworth bring to the Mexican financial ecosystem, both for banks and for merchants? 
A: We help banks turn their benefits into a new source of income. We do this by using transaction data to run merchant-funded cashback offers, so banks earn money every time a customer redeems an offer. For merchants, Reworth replaces risky ad spending with a simple model: they show offers directly to the right bank customers and only pay when a real purchase happens. This way, they know exactly what results they’re getting.
 Q: Why do merchants see higher ROI with Reworth compared to traditional online advertising platforms like Google or Meta ads, and how do you measure this impact? 
A: Merchants see higher ROI because we eliminate attribution uncertainty by linking offers directly to verified transactions. We distinguish between new customer acquisition and increased engagement from existing clients, while also analyzing a merchant’s share of wallet across categories. This enables precise targeting of incremental revenue opportunities. On average, we deliver six pesos in verified sales for every peso a retailer invests in our network.
 Q: How does Reworth’s international presence enable scalable, customized solutions for local banks and retailers? 
A: Mexico has been our proof of scale, as cashback drove a 78% increase in engagement and increased lifetime expenditure among participating customers 2.5 times. With millions of cardholders onboarded, we now replicate this model in markets like Argentina and soon Colombia, with Europe planned for 2026. Our API-first architecture allows fast integration into any bank system, enabling rapid deployment.
 Q: How is Reworth changing the perception of cashback, from a bank expense to a merchant-funded growth channel, in Mexico? 
A: We have redefined cashback from a bank cost into the foundation of a new advertising channel. FMN means merchants fund rewards from their media budgets, and banks earn new revenue by becoming the channel. For merchants, cashback becomes a performance-marketing tool with dashboards that mirror digital ad platforms.. Consumers respond to 10–15% cashback, and merchants are willing to fund it when ROI is guaranteed.
 Q: What initiatives is Reworth taking in order to lead the creation of a FMN in Mexico and Latin America? 
A: We are building the first FMN in the region, targeting a share of Mexico’s advertising market that is projected to grow from US$10 billion to US$28 billion in 2030 with retail media as a key driver. This mirrors global benchmarks: Chase Media drove US$12 billion in spend and Klarna generated about US$180 million in ad revenues in its first year. Our platform uniquely integrates transaction analysis, data enrichment, personalization, and cashback distribution for banks, while giving merchants a self-service campaign dashboard. With the largest contracted card base in Mexico, we are now scaling this leadership across Latin America.
 Q: How does Reworth automate cashback while ensuring transaction accuracy and security? 
A: We developed a proprietary model that correctly identifies up to 95% of transactions in Mexico’s fragmented payments environment, where aggregators complicate attribution and transaction recognition. Our infrastructure is PCI and ISO 27001 certified, and we only process anonymized data. By not requiring personally identifiable information, we protect banks, reduce systemic risk, and guarantee consumer privacy.
 Q: What real-time dashboards and insights do you provide for banks and merchants to evaluate ROI and consumer behavior? 
A: Banks can access dashboards with enriched transaction data, including a recency, frequency, and monetary (RFM) analysis, enabling them to segment high-value, at-risk, or inactive users for targeted campaigns. Merchants use a dashboard comparable to Google Ads, but instead of clicks they track spend, monitor campaign results, and analyze metrics like share of wallet, repeat purchase frequency, and average ticket size relative to competitors.
 Q: How do you reduce integration pain points so that any bank or merchant can join the FMN quickly? 
A: For merchants, onboarding is fully self-service through our platform or via a bank partner, requiring only brand assets and campaign setup. Banks vary in complexity, but our API adapts to both modern and legacy systems. For consumers, the process is frictionless: once their bank joins, cashback is automatically applied at participating merchants.
 Q: Why is FMN the next big revenue stream for banks and merchants? 
A: An FMN allows banks to earn advertising fees on merchant-funded cashback transactions, effectively creating a new interchange model. The success of global benchmarks like Chase, which drove US$12 billion in consumer spend, and Klarna, which generated US$150 million in its first year, demonstrates the model’s scalability. With Mexico’s ad market growing from US$10 billion to US$28 billion by 2030, FMNs will capture an increasing share.. By adopting this approach, banks monetize data ethically while deepening customer engagement with tangible rewards.
 Q: How will AI and transaction data make FMNs as personalized as a traditional streaming platform, but for shopping? 
A: By applying AI to rich transactional data, we can predict future behavior and deliver hyper-personalized offers that cut through digital noise. Instead of generic ads, consumers receive timely, relevant cashback incentives that function like streaming recommendations, but with direct monetary value. This turns offers into relevant actions that consumers actually use.
 Q: In a market where consumer loyalty is volatile, why will FMNs be the foundation for sustainable loyalty? 
A: Traditional loyalty is outdated; consumers are loyal to value. Cashback creates immediate gratification: when a customer receives 10% back instantly, it reinforces engagement. In Mexico, where local retailers face global e-commerce pressure, FMNs give customers a direct financial reason to keep choosing local merchants over global e-commerce giants..
 Q: What new markets is Reworth evaluating in the short term?  
A: Our priority is Mexico, aiming to cover 70% of cards by 2026. We’re also expanding to Colombia, with Brazil, Europe and the Middle East on the horizon through our partnership with a major card network. For merchants, exclusivity in categories creates a powerful first-mover advantage for the ones joining us now. 
 Q: What innovations will you launch in the next 12 to 18 months to make FMNs even more valuable to banks, merchants, and consumers? 
A: Our roadmap is built on three pillars. The first is the use of AI-driven personalization to automate segmentation and campaign recommendations for our bank partners. The second is enabling advanced analytics for merchants, including audience size and reach, aligned with digital advertising benchmarks.  Finally, our cross-border capabilities allow cashback offers to follow cardholders internationally, making our FMN indispensable for global commerce.

 Raphael Kappeler

Five Questions With: Reworth 

 Por Contxto 
 
November 1, 2022
 Reworth, a Mexican startup using cashback to enable banks and retailers to understand their customers’ behavior better, recently increased its seed round to US$6.6 million. In addition to Magma, Actius, K5, and NOA Capital funds, CEOs of prominent Mexican startups (Bitso, Nowports, and Jüsto) participated in the round. With the new capital, Reworth—which already works with fintechs such as Clara, Castor, and Dinn—plans its regional expansion. Raphael Kappeler, its CEO and founder answered Contxto’s key questions. 
 
 
 1. In simple terms, what is Reworth? 
 Reworth simplifies how banks and retailers interact with their customers, enabling them to understand, predict and influence consumer behavior through cashback. We hyper-personalize and deliver retailer-funded offers to increase interaction and revenue. By delivering frictionless cashback rewards, customers are immediately incentivized, increasing their loyalty and allegiance to brands.
Raphael Kappeler, CEO and founder of Reworth (Photo: Reworth)
 
 2.  How is it different from others that have tried this idea before? 
Our key differentiator is our technology and greenfield approach to how banks and retailers interact with their customers. We started from the ground up to understand how we can align the incentives of banks and retailers to deliver unique value to the end consumer. In this way, the bank can generate “hyper-personalized” offers to the right customers at the right time. This increases average ticket size by more than 30%, and card usage by more than 48%. With our business model, we reduce process friction and maximize profits. In this way, we help generate “frictionless loyalty,” rewarding customers and increasing the number of transactions.
 3. What company achievement are you most proud of? 
We are excited to be the first Latin American startup to be part of the Barclays Rise Growth Academy acceleration program, one of the most prestigious for fintech companies. In this way, we demonstrate that our solution is backed by one of the most innovative banks in the world to be exported. We are also very proud that bold and innovative investors see our product as redefining the game’s rules, which has resulted in the expansion of our seed funding round and brought our total capital raised to US$6.6 million to date. Finally, we are pleased to have added major financial institutions such as Castor, Clara, and Dinn in just four months.
 4. Tell us a Latin American startup you admire. 
At Reworth, we recognize and admire the visionary investors in the Latin American startup ecosystem. Thanks to them, the first great successes have been created, attracting top-tier talent and funding in the region. For example, Brazil put Latin America on the international funding map thanks to Patrick Sigrist’s iFoods, Sergio Furios’ Creditas, and, of course, the huge success of David Velez’s Nubank. Also, Linio and Rappi have contributed enormously to the startup ecosystem in Mexico and inspired a new generation of successful entrepreneurs in the country.
 5. What is Reworth’s next step? 
We are working hard after our funding round and are striving to close a bank monthly to reach 6 million cardholders and reduce integration time. We are also preparing to expand throughout Latin America. Finally, cashback is just the beginning, as we are developing innovative solutions for the future.
