 Sundaram Home Finance forays into Emerging Business segment in Karnataka
 
In the first phase of its expansion, Sundaram Home Finance plans setting up 8-10 Emerging Business branches in the smaller towns in Karnataka
 By Sindhu Hariharan 
Share

 The Emerging Business segment comprises of small business loans of up to ₹20 lakh and affordable housing finance of up to ₹40 lakh.
 
 Sundaram Home Finance has made a foray into Karnataka in the Emerging Business segment and is targeting disbursements of ₹60 crore in the first year of its operations in the state.
The Emerging Business segment comprises of small business loans of up to ₹20 lakh and affordable housing finance of up to ₹40 lakh.
In the first phase of its expansion, the company plans setting up 8-10 Emerging Business branches in the smaller towns in Karnataka.
Commenting on the foray, D Lakshminarayanan, MD, Sundaram Home Finance, said, “In line with our stated intent of expanding the Emerging Business outside Tamil Nadu, we have forayed into Karnataka and are hopeful of establishing a good presence in the Tier 2 and 3 towns in this state over the next 12-18 months.”
He added, “We believe there will be a high demand for credit in the affordable housing segment and hope to tap into this growing opportunity. Also, the industrial growth in smaller towns in the state will lead to opportunities in the small business loans segment with entrepreneurs looking for working capital finance to expand their businesses.”
Sundaram Home Finance has over 50 branches in Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh in the Emerging Business segment and registered disbursements of over ₹200 crore in this segment last year. The company registered a net profit of ₹62 crore in the first quarter ended June 30, 2025, on disbursements of ₹1,488 crore.
