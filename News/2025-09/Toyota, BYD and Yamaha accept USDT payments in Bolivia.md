El mercado automotor de Bolivia sumó criptomonedas a sus métodos de pago y **Toyota**, **BYD**y **Yamaha**confirmaron que reciben USDT como forma de transacción.

La iniciativa se da en un escenario de crecimiento de las operaciones con criptomonedas en el país, que **aumentaron 630%**, alcanzando los **u$s294 millones**en solo seis meses de 2025.

El avance se consolidó luego de una resolución del Banco Central de Bolivia, del 25 de junio de 2024, que **eliminó la prohibición** sobre el uso de criptomonedas y reconoció oficialmente los "activos virtuales".

De esta forma, las instituciones financieras quedaron habilitadas para canalizar operaciones hacia exchanges regulados, lo que abrió la puerta a que empresas internacionales incorporen pagos con stablecoins.

**Yvette Espinoza**, representante de la**Autoridad de Supervisión del Sistema Financiero (ASFI)**, apuntó que **la medida permite darle un marco de transparencia y control** a la cada vez más crecientedemanda de transacciones digitales.

La aceptación de USDT de parte de fabricantes internacionales suma a la estrategia que impulsa el uso de un "**dólar digital**" como **vehículo de inclusión financiera**.

## Stablecoins y tecnología: la apuesta de Toyota en Bolivia

**Toyota**, una de las automotrices que se sumó a esta propuesta, ya tiene experiencia en la **adopción de stablecoins**. En 2024, la compañía lanzó el proyecto **Toyota Blockchain Lab**, enfocado en crear **identidades digitales** para vehículos con el estándar Ethereum ERC-4337.

Además, su división financiera también avanzó en la **emisión de bonos tokenizados**, integrados en la billetera digital **Toyota Wallet**.

Estas iniciativas **muestran que la aceptación de USDT en Bolivia forma parte de un plan más amplio de digitalización y exploración** de blockchain en la industria automotriz.

La adopción de USDT por parte de Toyota, BYD y Yamaha representa un paso clave en la incorporación de stablecoins en sectores industriales tradicionales.

Para Bolivia, refuerza su estrategia de innovación regulatoria y lo sitúa como un actor creciente dentro del mercado cripto latinoamericano.

Mientras que para la industria automotriz, este movimiento abre la **posibilidad de un futuro donde los pagos digitales, la tokenización y la tecnología blockchain puedan revolucionar****la forma de comprar y los modelos de negocio** a nivel mundial.

Toyota, BYD, and Yamaha dealerships in Bolivia have officially begun accepting USDT (Tether) as payment, signaling a major milestone for both the country’s automotive industry and its evolving financial sector. The announcement was made by Tether CEO Paolo Ardoino, who shared promotional images from local dealerships advertising USDT as an “easy, fast, and safe” payment method. This move highlights how global brands are adapting to local economic pressures while boosting the visibility of stablecoins in real-world commerce.

## **Automotive adoption of stablecoins**

The integration of USDT into Bolivia’s automotive market comes at a time when traditional financial systems are under strain. Customers at select Toyota, BYD, and Yamaha dealerships can now complete purchases directly with Tether’s stablecoin, avoiding the need for scarce U.S. dollars or reliance on traditional banking channels. BitGo, a leading digital asset custodian, is reportedly providing secure self-custody solutions to facilitate these stablecoin transactions and ensure safe settlement.

This development follows a pivotal policy change in June 2024, when Bolivia’s central bank lifted a long-standing ban on cryptocurrencies, allowing banks to process both Bitcoin and stablecoin transactions. The regulatory shift opened the door for local businesses to adopt digital assets as a hedge against instability and as a more efficient transaction medium. Automakers now appear to be among the first major industries in the country to embrace stablecoin payments at scale.

## **Economic context and broader implications**

Bolivia has been grappling with high inflation, shrinking foreign reserves, and persistent U.S. dollar shortages, placing significant pressure on consumers and businesses alike. In this climate, stablecoins such as USDT are increasingly being seen as a reliable alternative for preserving value and conducting cross-border or large-scale transactions. For international automakers, the ability to accept USDT helps maintain sales while offering customers a modern payment option insulated from local currency fluctuations.

The adoption of USDT payments by globally recognized brands like Toyota, BYD, and Yamaha underscores the mainstream potential of stablecoins. While details remain limited on whether the acceptance applies nationwide or only at specific dealerships, the move represents one of the clearest examples yet of stablecoins transitioning from niche use cases into everyday commerce.

Industry analysts suggest Bolivia’s embrace of USDT could act as a blueprint for other emerging markets across Latin America and beyond. Countries facing similar liquidity shortages or foreign exchange pressures may look to stablecoins as a bridge between local economies and the global financial system. For consumers, the ability to purchase vehicles with USDT reduces friction and expands access to stable, dollar-backed value in an uncertain environment.

At the same time, the development raises important questions around regulatory oversight, consumer protections, and long-term sustainability. While stablecoin adoption provides short-term relief from dollar shortages, its integration into national economies will likely require clearer policy frameworks to safeguard users and ensure compliance. Nonetheless, Bolivia’s automotive sector has now taken a decisive step in showcasing how stablecoins can function as a practical tool in high-value transactions, signaling broader changes on the horizon for digital finance in the region.
