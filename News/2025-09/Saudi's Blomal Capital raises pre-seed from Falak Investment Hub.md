**Riyadh:**Saudi fintech Blomal Capital has secured a pre-seed round investment at an undisclosed amount from Falak Investment Hub, according to a press release.

Blomal was founded in 2022 by Abdulaziz Baraqabah, Munthir Alrehaili, Abdulrahman Akram, and Bilal Abduljawad. It is building a digital-first platform to simplify access to private and public investment funds.

العربية 
Saudi-based fintech Blomal Capital has secured a pre-seed funding round from Falak Investment Hub. While the investment amount remains undisclosed, the funding marks a strategic step toward transforming the Kingdom’s investment fund distribution landscape.
Founded in 2022 by Abdulaziz Baraqabah, Munthir Alrehaili, Abdulrahman Akram, and Bilal Abduljawad (CFA, CAIA), Blomal is developing a digital-first platform designed to streamline investor access to both private and public funds. The platform aims to simplify complex investment processes while maintaining efficiency, transparency, and accessibility for both individual and institutional investors.
Aligning with Saudi Arabia’s Fintech Vision
Falak Investment Hub emphasized that this investment reflects its broader strategy to support early-stage fintech startups driving financial innovation in the Kingdom. By backing Blomal Capital, Falak seeks to strengthen Saudi Arabia’s digital investment infrastructure and foster technologies that empower investors and institutions alike.
“We’re excited to support Blomal Capital’s mission to democratize fund access,” a Falak spokesperson said. “Their innovative platform complements Saudi Arabia’s rapidly evolving financial ecosystem and aligns with national efforts to digitize investment services.”
Expanding Access for Regional Investors
Blomal’s vision goes beyond simply digitizing investment — it aims to create a comprehensive ecosystem that connects investors, fund managers, and institutions through one seamless platform. The company is positioning itself to address growing demand for digital-first wealth management solutions amid Saudi Arabia’s accelerating fintech adoption under Vision 2030.
With Falak’s backing, Blomal Capital is expected to scale its platform capabilities, onboard new fund providers, and deepen its role in transforming how investors interact with both public and private investment opportunities.

UAE-Based FinTech Startup Sovra Raises Over $2M to Scale Self-Custody Financial Services Across MENA
The funding will support product development and the upcoming launch of Sovra’s platform, which offers dollar-based accounts, global payments, and self-custody financial services.
Published
on
By
Sovra, a UAE-based fintech startup focused on self-custody financial services, has raised more than $2 million in a pre-seed funding round led by Pharsalus Capital. The round also attracted participation from regional and international angel investors, including Kareem Atiyeh, founder of Ramp, Hisham Al-Falih, founder of Lean Technologies, Hany Rashwan, founder of 21Shares, and Naguib Sawiris, Chairman of Orascom Development Holding AG.
Founded in 2025 by Ahmed Wahby, Sovra is building a platform that enables users to hold dollar-based accounts, earn returns on savings, send money globally within seconds, and spend through payment cards supported by Visa and Mastercard. The company’s core proposition centers on self-custody, allowing users to maintain direct control over their funds without relying on traditional financial intermediaries.
The newly secured funding will be used to strengthen the platform’s technology infrastructure, accelerate product development, and support preparations for the company’s official market launch.
Building Financial Access Beyond Traditional Banking
Sovra enters the market at a time when large segments of the Middle East and North Africa remain underserved by traditional financial institutions. According to the company, nearly two-thirds of adults across the region are either unbanked or lack access to sufficient banking services.
Even among those with bank accounts, challenges such as inflation, currency depreciation, withdrawal restrictions, and limited access to savings products continue to affect financial security. Cross-border transfers also remain costly and time-consuming in many markets.
The company aims to address these issues by providing users with access to digital dollar accounts that can be managed directly through a mobile application. Through the platform, users can save, spend, transfer funds internationally, and access yield-generating financial products while maintaining ownership and control of their assets.

A Self-Custody Approach to Financial Services
Unlike traditional financial platforms, Sovra operates on a self-custody model in which users retain exclusive access to their accounts. The company provides the underlying infrastructure but does not control customer funds or have the ability to restrict access.
The platform is built around USDC, the dollar-backed stablecoin issued by Circle. According to the company, each digital dollar is backed by equivalent reserves held in U.S. dollars and subject to independent audits.
Sovra also partners with global financial service providers to offer payment cards, yield-generating opportunities, and instant transfers between accounts without additional fees.
Chief Executive Officer Ahmed Wahby said the company was founded on the belief that technology now makes it possible to remove many of the barriers that traditionally separate people from their money. He emphasized that users should retain ownership of their funds regardless of the status of the platform itself, describing self-custody as a foundational principle rather than simply an operational feature.
Targeting a Large Regional Opportunity
Sovra is primarily targeting three customer segments: young professionals across the Middle East and North Africa, university students, and expatriates from the region living abroad.
The company’s origins are closely tied to the experiences of its founder, who witnessed the collapse of Lebanon’s banking system in 2019, when depositors lost access to their savings and the local currency suffered a dramatic decline in value.
Today, Sovra operates with a team spread across the Middle East and Europe, bringing together talent from organizations including McKinsey, Revolut, and JumpCloud, alongside specialists in decentralized finance.
Anthony Ghossn, Managing Director of Pharsalus Capital, said Sovra offers individuals an alternative to fragile financial systems by providing self-custody tools designed to strengthen financial security. He added that the company’s leadership team has demonstrated both the vision and determination required to address a longstanding challenge across the region.
With fresh capital and plans for an official launch, Sovra is positioning itself to serve a growing market of consumers seeking greater control over their savings, payments, and access to global financial services.

Saudi Logistics Company SAL Establishes Netherlands Subsidiary to Expand Air Cargo Operations Across Europe
The new Amsterdam-based entity will serve as a platform for SAL’s global growth strategy and expansion of air cargo ground handling operations outside Saudi Arabia.
Published
on
By
SAL Saudi Logistics Services has established a wholly owned subsidiary in the Netherlands as part of its efforts to strengthen its international presence and expand its air cargo ground handling operations beyond the Saudi market.
The new entity, named SAL International Ground Services BV, will be headquartered in Amsterdam and is expected to play a central role in supporting the company’s international growth plans. The move reflects SAL’s broader strategy of building a direct presence in key global logistics markets while enhancing connectivity with international freight and cargo networks.
The establishment of the subsidiary marks another step in the company’s efforts to evolve from a domestic logistics provider into a regional and global player within the air cargo and logistics sector.
Amsterdam Chosen as European Hub
SAL selected Amsterdam as the headquarters for its new subsidiary, leveraging the city’s position as one of Europe’s most important logistics and transportation gateways.
The Netherlands has long served as a strategic hub for international trade, air cargo operations, and supply chain management, making it an attractive location for companies seeking access to European markets and global shipping routes.
By establishing a presence in Amsterdam, SAL aims to strengthen its ability to participate directly in international logistics activities while positioning itself closer to customers and partners across Europe.
The company stated that the new subsidiary will function as an international holding platform designed to support the execution of its overseas expansion strategy and identify growth opportunities in air cargo ground handling and related logistics services.

Supporting Long-Term International Growth
The launch of SAL International Ground Services BV comes amid continued growth in the global logistics industry, driven by increasing e-commerce activity, expanding international trade flows, and rising demand for air freight services.
As logistics providers seek to expand their operational footprints, major industry players are investing in strategic locations that offer access to high-volume cargo corridors and international transportation networks.
SAL believes the new structure will provide greater flexibility in managing future international operations while supporting the company’s ambitions to expand into foreign markets.
The company confirmed that all incorporation procedures have been completed, including the issuance of the articles of incorporation and commercial registration. The new entity has been officially registered in the Netherlands under establishment number 42077718 and remains wholly owned by SAL Saudi Logistics Services.
Expected Impact on Future Operations
While SAL stated that the incorporation of the new subsidiary will not have a material direct financial impact in the immediate term, the company expects the move to contribute positively to consolidated financial performance as international operations expand and overseas business activities begin to scale.
The company added that it will continue to disclose any material developments related to the project in accordance with applicable Saudi capital market regulations.
The establishment of a European subsidiary underscores SAL’s commitment to building an operational network that extends beyond Saudi Arabia. As global freight volumes continue to grow and demand for specialized ground handling services increases, the company is positioning itself to capture opportunities in international markets while building on its experience in managing air cargo operations within the Kingdom.
With Amsterdam now serving as a strategic base for future expansion, SAL is taking a significant step toward strengthening its role in the global logistics and air cargo ecosystem.
